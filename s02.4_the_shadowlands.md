## 2.4 The Shadowlands

The Shadowlands are not a passive threat. They press constantly against the Empire’s southern border, and the only thing standing between Rokugan and annihilation is the Kaiu Wall — and the Crab Clan that mans it. This section defines the Shadowlands as a living mechanical system: the Wall, the Towers that hold it, the seasonal pressure that never relents, and the discrete horde assaults that represent existential crises for the Empire.

### 2.4.1 The Kaiu Wall — The Twelve Towers

The Kaiu Wall runs from the Sea of the Sun Goddess in the east to the Plains above Evil near the Shinomen Forest in the west. Twelve great towers stand at intervals along its length, numbered from the First Tower in the southeast to the Twelfth in the northwest. Each tower is responsible for the wall section it anchors. If a tower fails, its section falls.

### 2.4.2 Tower Mechanics — Fortification Settlement

Each of the Twelve Towers is classified as a special Fortification Settlement. Unlike normal settlements, Towers produce nothing — they are pure consumers of resources. They cannot be taxed or developed. They have no civilian population. Repair and construction actions are exclusive to Kaiu family units. A Tower’s condition directly shields the adjacent Crab province from Shadowlands Taint bleed.

Each Tower tracks three values:

- Structural Integrity (SI) — the physical condition of the tower and its wall section. Maintained by Koku spending. If SI reaches 0, the wall section no longer provides any fortification bonus.

- Garrison — troops stationed at the tower. Tracked by unit type, not abstracted PU. Maintained by Rice. A tower may hold any combination of universal units and Crab clan elite units.

- Supply (Rice) — the stockpile feeding the garrison. Consumed each season. If Supply runs dry, Garrison units begin taking Health attrition.

SI provides a flat Defense bonus to all units manning the tower. This bonus applies on top of each unit’s own base Defense stat — SI never modifies the unit’s personal Defense, only the fortification bonus it receives.

| **SI** | **Defense Bonus** | **State** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 10 | +12 | Pristine — the Wall at its most formidable |
| 8–9 | +10 | Well maintained |
| 6–7 | +7 | Functional but showing wear |
| 4–5 | +4 | Deteriorating — repairs overdue |
| 2–3 | +1 | Crumbling — barely operational |
| 1 | +0 | Ruin — Tower contributes nothing to defense |
| 0 | +0 | Breached — wall section gone, troops fight in the open with no fortification bonus |

For reference: a standard castle provides a total of +8 Defense (Urban +3, fortification +5). A fully maintained Tower at SI 10 provides +12 — significantly harder to crack. A Hida Bushi (base Defense 7) at a SI 10 Tower reaches an effective Defense of 21 against Shadowlands creatures: 7 (base) + 2 (Hida Bushi special vs Shadowlands) + 12 (SI 10 bonus). PTL Integration: All provinces directly adjacent to the Shadowlands accumulate +0.1 PTL per season as a baseline — the Wall slows Jigoku’s corruption but does not stop it entirely. This is constant and unavoidable regardless of Tower condition. A Tower whose SI drops to 5 or below is considered “degraded” and adds +0.5 PTL per season to adjacent Crab provinces on top of the baseline. Without Kuni Shugenja intervention (Section 2.4.17), even well-maintained Wall provinces slowly accumulate Taint over years of service. Minimum Garrison Threshold — PROVISIONAL: the minimum defensible garrison is 1 full Company (153 Health worth of combat-capable troops). A tower below this threshold is flagged as undermanned and triggers the Garrison Shortage topic pipeline (Section 2.4.12). Note: this value will likely be revised upward after playtesting — the true defensible minimum probably needs to account for tower SI condition, SS tier, and expected horde scale.

### 2.4.3 Seasonal Pressure — LOCKED

The Shadowlands press against the Wall constantly. Every season, every tower loses SI unless actively maintained with Koku, and Rice consumption feeds the garrison regardless of whether a major assault occurs. This is the baseline drain the Crab must absorb indefinitely.

Seasonal pressure operates on two mechanics simultaneously: Option B (seasonal variation — different seasons hit the Wall differently) and Option C (adjacent tower bleed — a weakened tower puts pressure on its neighbours).

| **Season** | **SI Decay** | **Koku to Maintain** | **Rice Modifier** | **Notes** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Spring | −1 SI | 2 Koku | ×1.0 | Thaw damages foundations |
| Summer | −0 SI | 1 Koku | ×1.0 | Best repair season — cheapest maintenance |
| Autumn | −1 SI | 2 Koku | ×1.2 | Stockpiling pressure before winter |
| Winter | −2 SI | 3 Koku | ×1.5 | Frost cracks stone, supply lines difficult |

Summer is the critical repair window. A Crab player who invests in summer maintenance can offset the spring and autumn decay. Neglecting summer means entering winter already degraded — and winter is unforgiving.

Adjacent Tower Bleed: A tower whose SI drops to 4 or below begins bleeding pressure into adjacent towers. Each season it remains at SI 4 or below, adjacent towers take an additional −0.5 SI decay. A collapsing section destabilises its neighbours — the Wall fails in chains, not in isolation.

### 2.4.4 Discrete Horde Attacks

Beyond the constant seasonal pressure, the Shadowlands periodically musters a full horde — a massive force that forms deep in the Shadowlands and marches toward the Wall. These events are the primary existential threat to the Empire.

- Target selection is random. The Shadowlands is not organised enough to exploit specific weaknesses — Jigoku is chaos, not a chess player. Any of the Twelve Towers may be targeted.

- Repeat targeting is weighted. The tower most recently attacked has a higher probability of being targeted again in the next horde event. The Shadowlands keeps pressing a wound.

- The Crab cannot have every tower at peak condition simultaneously. The Wall is always under-resourced somewhere. The strategic question is never “is everything perfect” — it is “which tower is weakest, and is that where the horde is heading?”

**The Scout Loop — Hiruma Scouts in the Shadowlands:**

Intelligence on incoming hordes is obtained exclusively through active scouting. Hiruma Scouts — named characters, not unit abstractions — must be deployed into the Shadowlands to gather this information. Scouting has two distinct objectives:

- Detect: Is a horde forming at all? A Crab player with no scouts deployed receives no warning. The horde arrives at the target tower with the garrison already in combat.

- Track: Which tower is the horde heading for? Knowing a horde exists but not its target forces the Crab to make a hard choice about where to concentrate forces. Knowing the target allows specific reinforcement — moving troops, rushing Koku for emergency SI repairs, pulling garrison from quieter sections.

Scouting is a real trade-off. Hiruma Scouts are named characters operating in the most hostile terrain in Rokugan. They can die in the Shadowlands. The Hiruma Scout school is purpose-built for this role — Rank 1 always knows direction of the Empire in Shadowlands — but they are not expendable.

**Horde Frequency — LOCKED:**

Every 2 seasons a horde roll fires. Base probability: 50% (subject to playtesting adjustment). The roll is purely calendar-driven — Tower condition, SI, and garrison strength do not affect whether a horde forms, only how badly a Tower suffers when one arrives.

- Roll succeeds: a horde forms. Invasion type is rolled using the weighting table below. Target tower is randomly selected with repeat weighting applied. Strength counter resets to 0.

- Roll fails: no horde this cycle. Strength counter increments by 1. Each point of Strength adds one additional Company to the generated horde when it finally fires, drawn from the invasion type’s normal unit pool. A Jigoku Horde with Strength 3 arrives with 3 extra Companies of Bakemono or Ogres on top of the baseline army. The counter is uncapped — a very long quiet period produces a very large horde. The counter does not reset on a failed roll — it only resets when a horde fires. A quiet period is not a safe period.

**Invasion Type Weighting — LOCKED:**

When a horde forms, invasion type is determined by weighted roll. Strength counter does not affect type — it only affects army size and quality.

| **Invasion Type** | **Weight** | **Notes** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Jigoku Horde | 60% | Most common. Bakemono and Ogres, pure chaos. |
| Undead Legion | 25% | Maho-tsukai commanded. Undead only, never rout. |
| Oni-Led Horde | 15% | Rare. Generated Oni at head of Bakemono and Ogre army. Spawn variant possible. |

### 2.4.5 Tower Combat — Horde Assault

When a horde reaches a tower, the engagement resolves using the standard Army Combat System (Section 11.7) in siege mode. The Shadowlands always storms — it cannot starve a garrison out. Every assault is a direct storm assault.

- The defending garrison fights from the tower. The SI Defense bonus applies to all units. Garrison units receive their standard +2 Defense for fighting in their own settlement on top of the SI bonus.

- The attacking force is a Shadowlands army composed of units from the Shadowlands Unit Roster (Section 2.4.6). Army composition depends on invasion type.

- A breach occurs when SI reaches 0 and the garrison is destroyed or routed. At that point the horde enters Rokugan and a full Shadowlands Incursion crisis triggers (Section 16.3).

- Troops remain effective at SI 0 — they fight with their own base Defense stats, simply without any fortification bonus. A SI 0 tower still has whoever is standing in the rubble.

**Assault SI Hit — LOCKED:**

Every horde assault damages the Tower structurally, regardless of outcome. The hit is determined by battle outcome and applied immediately after the engagement resolves. Winning costs the Wall. The question is only how much.

| **Outcome** | **SI Hit** | **Notes** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Decisive defender victory (horde routed quickly) | −1 SI | Minimal structural damage. A pristine tower barely notices. |
| Contested battle (horde fought hard before routing) | −2 SI | Meaningful damage at any SI level. Pushes a healthy tower toward deterioration. |
| Narrow defender victory (garrison badly damaged) | −3 SI | Severe. A tower at SI 4 drops to SI 1 after a hard-won defense. |
| Breach (garrison destroyed or routed) | −4 SI + breach condition | Catastrophic. Triggers Shadowlands Incursion crisis (Section 16.3). Rebuilding requires a dedicated Kaiu repair action. |

Post-assault recovery: after the battle resolves, normal seasonal SI maintenance resumes. The Taisa spends Koku to repair as usual — the assault hit is treated as additional decay on top of the seasonal table. If the tower was breached (SI 0), routine maintenance is insufficient — a dedicated Kaiu repair action is required: higher Koku cost, takes a full season, requires Kaiu engineers physically present at the tower. Garrison Health/PU reconciliation follows the existing mass combat recovery rules (Section 11.7).

### 2.4.6 Types of Invasion — LOCKED

Not all hordes are alike. The composition of a Shadowlands assault determines its tactical character and the specific threats the Crab must prepare for. Three invasion types exist. The first two never mix with each other. The third is its own distinct category.

**Jigoku Horde — Bakemono and Ogres:**

The most common invasion type. Chaotic and disorganised, reflecting the Shadowlands’ nature as pure entropy. Bakemono in large numbers, supported by Ogres as wall-breakers. May include a Bakemono Shaman (rare). No undead. No necromancer required — this horde moves on instinct and the dark pull of Jigoku.

**Undead Legion — Maho-tsukai Raised:**

An army of undead raised and directed by a maho-tsukai. Never mixes with Bakemono or Ogres — the necromancer’s control does not extend to living Shadowlands creatures, and living creatures avoid the dead. The undead never rout. The maho-tsukai commanding them is the strategic priority: their death disrupts revenant coordination and renders the horde far less dangerous. A Kuni Witch Hunter or skilled Hiruma Scout identifying and targeting the maho-tsukai is the correct counter.

**Oni-Led Horde:**

A generated Oni marches at the head of a Bakemono and Ogre army. The Oni never appears alone — it is always accompanied by standard Shadowlands forces. The Oni occupies its own grid slot on the mass battle grid; the rest of the horde is composed of Bakemono and Ogre Companies as normal. No undead are present. The Oni and its accompanying forces share the same chaotic, aggressive nature — this is not a disciplined army, it is a force of destruction with something uniquely terrible at its centre.

**Oni-Led Horde — Spawn Variant:**

If the generated Oni has the Spawn special ability (Pool 3), the horde includes Spawn Companies — lesser creatures born of the Oni itself. Spawn are not standard Bakemono or Ogres. They are unique to their parent Oni and recognisably its offspring: same body form, heavily diminished stats. Their presence makes every Spawn-variant horde visually and mechanically distinct from all others.

Spawn Company stat generation — LOCKED: All values derived from the parent Oni’s generated mass battle stats. Health: 153 (standard Company pool). Attack: parent Oni Mass Battle Attack ÷ 2, round down, minimum 3. Defense: parent Oni Mass Battle Defense ÷ 2, round down, minimum 1. Morale: 10. Morale Defense: 3. Body Form: inherits the parent Oni’s form and its ASCII movement modifier exactly. Special Attack: if the parent Oni has Breath Weapon or Taint Spit (Pool 3), Spawn inherit a weakened version at half damage or effect. All other Pool 3 abilities are not inherited — they do not translate coherently to a Company formation. Morale is deliberately low relative to the parent — Spawn are lesser creatures and can rout, unlike the Oni itself which fights until destroyed.

### 2.4.8 Oni — Procedural Generation System — LOCKED

Every Oni that appears in the Shadowlands is unique. No two are alike in form, capability, or weakness. When an Oni event triggers, the game generates a new entity from the parameters below, in order. The result is a named, persistent creature with a full stat block for both the Mass Battle layer and the ASCII individual combat layer. Its generated traits are locked at creation and do not change. See Section 40 for the base Ugulu no Oni stat block, which serves as the design reference for the generation ranges.

Oni never have Void. They are creatures of Jigoku — the spiritual balance that Void represents is antithetical to their nature.

**STEP 1 — SIZE**

Size is rolled first. It anchors Health on the Mass Battle grid, tile footprint on the ASCII map, and the Ring point budget used in Step 3.

| **Size** | **Mass Battle Health** | **ASCII Footprint** | **Ring Budget** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Small | 50 | 1×1 | 9 points |
| Medium | 100 | 2×2 | 12 points |
| Large | 175 | 3×3 | 15 points |
| Massive | 300 | 4×4 | 19 points |

**STEP 2 — BODY FORM**

Body form determines how the Oni moves on the ASCII map and its inherent combat modifiers. Winged is a secondary modifier that can apply to any base form.

| **Form** | **Description** | **ASCII Movement** | **Mass Battle Modifier** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Humanoid | Bipedal, man-shaped | Standard movement | No modifier |
| Serpentine | Snake lower body, humanoid upper | Slow, ignores difficult terrain | +1 Attack, −1 Defense |
| Bestial | Quadruped, animal-like | Fast, extra movement tiles | +1 Attack when charging |
| Towering | Enormous biped, multiple stories | Slow, 3-tile reach | +2 Attack, −1 Defense |
| Amorphous | Shifting, formless | Squeezes through any gap | Immune to flanking |
| Insectoid | Multi-limbed, carapaced | Fast, can climb walls | +2 Defense |
| +Winged | Flight added to any base form | Can leap over units | Ignores SI bonus on first assault round |

**STEP 3 — DOMINANT RING**

One Ring is designated dominant and receives the highest allocation from the Ring budget. The remaining points are distributed across the other three Rings, with no Ring dropping below 1 and none reaching the dominant Ring’s value. Void is never available.

| **Dominant Ring** | **Archetype** | **Character** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Earth | Immovable brute | High Wounds, high Reduction. Extremely difficult to kill. Low speed and offense. |
| Water | Powerful charger | High Strength, high damage output. Built to break through lines. |
| Fire | Fast aggressor | High Agility, high attack rate. Glass cannon — hits hard, takes damage easily. |
| Air | Evasive hunter | High Armor TN, hard to hit. Low raw damage but difficult to pin down. |

Ring budget allocation: the dominant Ring receives between 35–45% of the total budget (rounded). The remainder distributes randomly across the other three Rings within the constraints above.

**STEP 4 — DERIVED STATS**

All stats flow automatically from the generated Ring values. No additional rolls required.

| **Stat** | **Formula** |
| --- | --- |
| Wounds | Earth × 16. Wound penalty thresholds at 25%, 50%, 75%, Dead at 100%. |
| Armor TN | Air × 5. |
| Reduction | Earth × 4. Halved (Earth × 2) against jade, crystal, or obsidian. |
| Initiative | Air k Air. |
| Attack | Fire k Fire + weapon skill (claws, bite, or weapon by form). |
| Damage | Strength k (Strength ÷ 2, rounded up). |

Mass Battle translation — LOCKED: All values are flat, not roll-based, for grid combat resolution. Mass Battle Health scales with Size: Small = 50, Medium = 100, Large = 175, Massive = 300. These pools are significantly larger than a standard Company (153) to reflect an Oni’s supernatural durability — bringing one down requires sustained coordinated effort from multiple Companies. Mass Battle Attack = Size floor + Fire ring value. Size floors: Small = 5, Medium = 7, Large = 9, Massive = 11. This guarantees every Oni is a genuine threat on the grid regardless of Ring composition — Fire adds offensive bonus on top of the floor. Example: a Small Oni with Fire 2 has Attack 7; a Massive Oni with Fire 6 has Attack 17. Mass Battle Defense = Earth + Air (combined physical toughness and evasion). No special damage rules apply — the standard combat system is used without modification. The Oni’s durability is expressed entirely through its Health pool and Defense stat. Morale — LOCKED: Oni have no Morale stat and cannot rout. They fight until their Health reaches zero. This is distinct from the Undead’s lack of Morale — Undead do not rout because they are mindless, Oni do not rout because they are incapable of fear. The mechanical result is identical: an Oni on the grid must be destroyed, it will never flee.

**STEP 5 — SPECIAL ABILITIES**

Three ability pools. One result from each pool per generated Oni.

**Pool 1 — Always Present (Fear):**

Every Oni has Fear. The rating scales with size: Small = Fear 1, Medium = Fear 2, Large = Fear 3, Massive = Fear 5. All characters within Fear × 5 feet must make a Willpower check (TN 10 + Fear rating × 5) or suffer −1k0 to all rolls while within range.

**Pool 2 — Invulnerability (one rolled randomly):**

| **Invulnerability** | **Effect** |
| --- | --- |
| Arrow Immunity | Immune to non armor-piercing arrows. |
| Blade Immunity | Reduce all blade damage by half before Reduction. |
| Fire Immunity | Immune to fire-based attacks and spells. |
| Spell Immunity | Immune to 1d3 randomly determined spells (rolled at generation). |
| Poison Immunity | Immune to all poison effects. |

**Pool 3 — Special Attack (one rolled, rarity weighted):**

| **Special Attack** | **Rarity** | **Effect** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Breath Weapon | Common | Ranged line attack hitting all targets in a straight path. Damage = Fire k Fire. |
| Crushing Grip | Common | Grapple attack. On hit, target cannot act until they beat the Oni’s Strength in an opposed roll. |
| Taint Spit | Uncommon | Ranged single-target attack. On hit, target gains 1 Taint Rank immediately. |
| Regeneration | Uncommon | Recovers Earth Wounds per round at the start of its initiative. |
| Spawn | Rare | Once per combat, produces 1d3 Bakemono Companies that enter from the Oni’s grid slot. |
| Taint Aura | Rare | Passive. All characters within Earth × 5 feet gain +0.5 Taint Rank per round of exposure. Jade provides full protection. |

**STEP 6 — WEAKNESS**

Every Oni has two weaknesses. The first is universal and never generated: jade, crystal, and obsidian always halve Reduction. The second is specific and procedurally generated — unknown to the Crab unless discovered through active scouting by a Hiruma Scout or Kuni Witch Hunter before the battle.

| **Weakness** | **Effect when exploited** |
| --- | --- |
| Fire | Takes double damage from fire-based attacks and spells. |
| Water | Movement halved in or near water. −2 to all rolls in water terrain. |
| Specific Spell School | Full damage from one element’s spells, ignoring Reduction entirely. |
| Specific Weapon Type | Reduction halved against one weapon type (spears, bows, no-dachi, etc.), rolled at generation. |
| Sunlight | −2 to all rolls during daylight hours. |
| Sound | Must make Earth check (TN 20) or lose action when exposed to a specific sound, rolled at generation. |
| Named Individual | One character type (e.g. Kuni Witch Hunter, Hida Bushi) deals full damage ignoring Reduction. |

Intelligence and the Specific Weakness: A Hiruma Scout or Kuni Witch Hunter who successfully investigates the Oni before combat (requires a dedicated scouting action and a successful Lore: Shadowlands roll, TN based on Oni size) reveals the specific weakness to the Crab player. Without this intelligence, the weakness remains unknown and the Crab fights the Oni at full Reduction. The investigation carries risk — the Oni may detect the scout.

CROSS-REFERENCE: The Ugulu no Oni stat block in Section 40 is a pre-generated example using this system. Earth-dominant, Large-equivalent, Humanoid form, Arrow Immunity (Pool 2), Spell Immunity (Pool 3, two spells). Its specific weakness is not listed — it must be discovered in play.

### 2.4.10 Shadowlands Strength — LOCKED

Every Shadowlands province adjacent to the Wall has a Shadowlands Strength (SS) value. SS represents how much dangerous activity is massing in that province — creatures gathering, Taint thickening, pressure building against the adjacent tower. High SS makes the Wall harder and more expensive to maintain. The only way to reduce it is sorties.

**SS Accumulation — LOCKED:**

SS rises passively at random intervals. Each tick fires at an unpredictable time and adds 1–3 SS. The Crab never know exactly when the next tick will hit or how much it will add. There is no ceiling — SS accumulates indefinitely if sorties are neglected. SS is per-province and does not affect horde targeting.

**SS Tiers and Seasonal Pressure Modifiers — LOCKED:**

| **SS Tier** | **Additional SI Decay** | **Additional Koku** | **Rice Modifier** |

### 2.4.11 Taisa AI Decision Logic — LOCKED

The Taisa AI manages seven competing demands simultaneously: SI maintenance, sortie timing, scout coverage, supply requests (Rice, Koku, troops), jade stockpile, Jade Petal Tea stockpile, and Taint monitoring. All seven cost resources, risk troops, or have catastrophic consequences if neglected. The Taisa cannot do everything perfectly at once — triage is the core of the decision loop. The guiding principle is proactive not reactive: a Taisa who waits until crisis before acting is failing their duty.

**Decision 1 — SI Maintenance:**

- Standing rule: always repair if SI drops below 6. This floor is non-negotiable. SI maintenance is the first Koku spend regardless of other pressures.

- Threat aware modifier: if scouts report a horde forming, emergency repairs activate regardless of current SI. All available Koku is directed toward pushing SI as high as possible before the assault arrives.

- Quiet periods: if no horde detected and SI is above 6, repair spending is reduced. Koku is conserved for other demands.

**Decision 2 — Sortie Timing:**

- SS Low: no sortie. Conserve garrison.

- SS Medium: order a Small sortie (10–20%) to prevent further SS accumulation.

- SS High: order a Medium sortie (21–40%) unless garrison is below safety threshold, in which case escalate to Shireikan for reinforcement before sortying. Never commit to a Medium sortie if SI is below 6 simultaneously — the tower cannot survive a horde hit while undermanned and degraded.

- Large sorties (41–60%) are never ordered by the Taisa alone. They require Shireikan approval.

- Jade gate: all sortie decisions above are subject to jade availability (Decision 5). If jade stockpile is critical, no sortie fires regardless of SS level. SS management waits for jade supply to be restored.

**Decision 3 — Scout Deployment:**

- Standing patrol: always maintain at least one scout in the adjacent Shadowlands province. A Taisa with no scouts deployed is operating blind — this is never acceptable Crab doctrine.

- Escalate coverage: if SS is Medium or High, deploy a second scout. Higher SS means more activity in the province and greater intelligence value.

- Horde detected: if a scout reports a horde forming, immediately deploy all available scouts to track direction. Intelligence on the target tower justifies the risk.

- Scout lost: replace as soon as a qualified character is available. Flag the coverage gap to the Shireikan immediately.

**Decision 4 — Supply Requests:**

- Rice: request resupply when stockpile drops below 2 seasons of garrison consumption. Early requests give logistics time to respond before garrison Health attrition begins.

- Koku: request additional allocation when current budget is insufficient to maintain SI above 6 through the next season. Calculate projected decay against available Koku and request the shortfall proactively.

- Emergency request: if a horde is detected and both SI and stockpile are below safe levels simultaneously, send an urgent priority request directly to the Shireikan. Bypasses normal logistics timing. A Taisa who hides a crisis from their Shireikan is committing a serious failure of duty.

- Troops: if garrison drops below minimum defensible threshold, flag to Shireikan immediately via the garrison shortage report. Do not wait for the shortage to compound. A Taisa who silently absorbs garrison attrition without reporting it is creating a crisis their Shireikan cannot see coming.

**Decision 5 — Jade Stockpile Request:**

- Routine threshold: request jade resupply when stockpile drops below two sortie seasons of coverage — enough for one Small and one Medium sortie before running critically low. Early requests give the supply chain time to respond.

- Critical threshold: if jade drops to zero or below minimum Small sortie allocation, flag as emergency to Shireikan immediately — same urgency as horde detection. All sortie orders suspended. Taisa notes jade critical status in their Shireikan report.

- Blocked sorties: no sortie orders fire while jade is critical regardless of SS level. A Taisa who sends warriors into the Shadowlands without jade allocation is sending them to die from Taint, not in battle.

**Decision 6 — Tea Stockpile Request:**

- Routine threshold: request Tea resupply when stockpile drops below one season of coverage for current Tainted troop count. Early enough for Brotherhood logistics to respond before rationing becomes necessary.

- Partial supply: if Tea is insufficient to cover all Tainted troops, ration immediately — highest Taint Rank characters receive Tea first. Report rationing situation to Shireikan including which characters are going without.

- Empty stockpile: flag as emergency to Shireikan immediately. Unassisted Taint growth rolls begin firing. Taisa notes which characters are closest to Rank 4 in their report — the Shireikan needs to know who is at risk.

- Rank 4 override: if any character reaches Rank 4 while Tea is unavailable, request Kuni assessment and execute removal protocol immediately. Does not wait for Tea supply to resolve first. The clock does not pause for logistics.

**Decision 7 — Taint Monitoring:**

- Standing requirement: always maintain a Kuni or Taint-detection capable character at the Tower. Garrison Taint assessment — checking individual characters for Taint — can be performed by either a Kuni Shugenja or a Kuni Witch Hunter. Province purification — cleansing Tainted land — requires a Kuni Shugenja specifically (Section 2.4.17). If no Kuni of either type is present, flag to Shireikan immediately — a Tower without Taint monitoring is operationally blind to one of its most dangerous threats.

- Routine monitoring: each season the Kuni assesses all named garrison characters. Results reported to Taisa. Any character above Seeds of Darkness goes on the watch list.

- Escalation ladder: Rank 1–2 — monitor, administer Tea, note in seasonal report. Rank 3 — flag to Shireikan, increase Tea priority, reduce sortie exposure for that character. Rank 4 — immediate removal protocol, autonomous Taisa action under Kuni doctrine. Does not require Shireikan authorisation.

- Reporting discipline: Taisa includes full Taint watch list in every Shireikan report. A Taisa who conceals Taint status from their Shireikan is committing the same failure of duty as one who hides a garrison shortage or a horde detection.

### 2.4.12 Wall Manpower — Troop Pipelines & the Garrison Shortage Topic — LOCKED

The Kaiu Wall is permanently hungry for men. Two distinct pipelines feed it, operating at different levels of the system. Neither is sufficient alone — the Wall requires both the steady trickle of conscripts and the deliberate commitment of trained warriors.

**Pipeline 1 — Ashigaru (Automatic, Seasonal):**

Every season, all Crab provinces automatically generate a small number of Ashigaru PU that flow directly to the Wall. This requires no lord action — it is the structural consequence of the Wall’s existence. It is funded by the population growth malus: Crab provinces grow at half the normal rate because their men go to the Wall instead of to families and farms. The malus is permanent and applies to all Crab provinces without exception. The Ashigaru produced this way are assigned to towers by the Shireikan based on current need.

**Pipeline 2 — Elite Bushi (Deliberate, Lord-Driven):**

Crab clan elite units — Hida Bushi, Hiruma Scouts, Crab Berserkers — are trained in the provinces and assigned to the Wall by deliberate lord action. This is not automatic. The lord physically sends them: a conscious decision to detach valued units from provincial forces. Once assigned, command transfers permanently to the receiving Tower Commander. The lord gives them up. Bushi can be requested through the shortage topic system (below) but cannot be compelled — the decision belongs entirely to the lord.

**Wall Garrison Shortage — Topic Type (Tier 3):**

When a Tower’s garrison drops below minimum defensible threshold, a Wall Garrison Shortage condition is flagged internally. This does not generate a topic automatically — topics do not self-propagate. The condition must be communicated by characters through the existing letter system and in-person visits.

The Taisa or Shireikan writes letters to Crab lords informing them of the shortage. The letter plants a Wall Garrison Shortage opinion topic in the recipient’s awareness and sets their starting position based on their virtues and disposition. A character who travels in person to make the case directly is significantly more persuasive — face-to-face interaction can push a resistant lord’s position across the +50 commitment threshold that letters alone cannot reach. A dedicated envoy travelling the clan’s provinces to rally support for the Wall is a natural and meaningful role for player characters.

The opinion topic spawns an action topic: “Send bushi to [Tower X].” A lord whose position on this action topic crosses +50 commits troops. Below that threshold they are aware of the shortage but not yet acting.

### 2.4.13 Shireikan AI Decision Logic — LOCKED

The Shireikan AI operates on two simultaneous layers. At the tower layer, the Shireikan manages their own tower using the full Taisa decision logic (Section 2.4.11) — the Southern Shireikan commands Tower 3, the Northern commands Tower 10. At the command layer, they oversee all six towers in their half, manage the reserve Koku and jade pools, oversee Jade Petal Tea supply and Brotherhood relationship maintenance, authorise Large sorties, coordinate garrison shortages through letter campaigns and escalation, coordinate with the other Shireikan, and escalate to the Clan Champion when their resources are exhausted.

**Decision 1 — Triage: Monitoring Six Towers:**

- Priority 1: any tower with SI at or below 3 and a confirmed horde incoming. Immediate response required. If the Shireikan cannot address this alone, escalate to the Champion immediately.

- Priority 2: any tower with SI below 6 and SS High simultaneously. Two compounding problems cascading together. Act before they become Priority 1.

- Priority 3: any tower that has flagged a shortage but not yet reached crisis. Proactive reinforcement while time still exists.

- Priority 4: the Shireikan’s own tower. They do not prioritise themselves. Their tower is last in the queue unless it is the confirmed horde target.

- Priority 5: any tower with jade stockpile critical. Sorties suspended there; SS will climb unchecked until jade is restored. Address before SS compounds into a higher-tier crisis.

- Priority 6: any tower with Tea stockpile below one season of coverage or a garrison member at Taint Rank 3+. Taint cascades are slow to start and fast to become unrecoverable. Act while time still exists.

**Decision 2 — Troop Redeployment:**

Receiving priority follows the triage order above. The Shireikan sends reinforcements to the highest priority tower first.

- Source selection: always pull from the tower with the highest garrison surplus above minimum threshold. Never pull from a tower with SS High, SI below 6, or active scout reports of nearby Shadowlands activity — even if it has surplus troops.

- Transfer cap: maximum 30% of a source tower’s garrison per redeployment order. Prevents overcorrection creating a new crisis where stability existed.

- Own tower constraint: the Shireikan’s own tower is subject to the same minimum threshold as any other. They may send their own garrison troops to a crisis tower and should be more willing to do so than to strip another Taisa’s command.

**Decision 3 — Reserve Koku Pool Management:**

The Shireikan receives a seasonal Koku allocation from the Clan Champion and distributes it across their six towers, retaining a portion as a personal reserve pool. Reserve sizing is dynamic — determined by current Wall conditions, not a fixed formula.

- Healthy Wall (low SS, SI above 6 everywhere, no horde detected): distribute generously, retain small reserve. Stability does not require hoarding.

- Mixed conditions (one or two towers showing strain): retain moderate reserve, distribute conservatively to stable towers.

- Stressed Wall (multiple SI concerns, horde detected, SS elevated): retain large reserve, distribute only minimum needed for basic maintenance.

**Decision 4 — Koku Authorisation:**

- Automatic approval: tower has SI below 6 and horde confirmed incoming. No deliberation. Draw from reserve immediately.

- Conditional approval: SI below 6 but no confirmed horde. Approve and draw from reserve. If this occurs for two consecutive seasons flag it to the Clan Champion — a structural budget problem, not a temporary one.

- Deferred approval: SI above 6 but Taisa wants extra Koku for aggressive repairs. Approved only if reserve pool is healthy and no other tower has a higher priority claim.

- Denied: reserve pool is empty and no tower is in worse shape. Taisa manages within their allocation. Shireikan escalates to Champion to request additional resources for the half.

**Decision 5 — Escalation to Clan Champion:**

- Immediate escalation: any tower reaches SI 3 or below with a confirmed horde incoming. The Shireikan cannot fix this alone in time.

- Resource escalation: two or more towers simultaneously require emergency Koku and the reserve pool cannot cover both.

- Garrison escalation: a tower is at minimum garrison with a confirmed horde incoming and no surplus troops exist anywhere in the half to redeploy.

- Structural escalation: a tower requires emergency Koku for two consecutive seasons. Flagged as a budget problem requiring Champion-level resolution.

- Jade escalation: jade reserve is exhausted and one or more towers remain critical. Jade shortage across multiple towers simultaneously is a Champion-level supply problem requiring mine province purchasing (Section 2.4.14 Decision 7).

- Tea escalation: Tea critically low across multiple towers simultaneously, or a monastery province reaches PTL 6+ ending cultivation. Beyond Shireikan authority to resolve alone — Champion must address Brotherhood relationship or initiate purification campaign.

**Decision 6 — Cross-Half Coordination:**

- Troop request: if a crisis in one half requires troops from the other, the affected Shireikan contacts their counterpart directly through the letter and order systems before escalating to the Champion. Direct coordination is always attempted first.

- Ambiguous horde direction: if scouts report a horde forming but direction is unclear — potentially threatening towers near the boundary between halves — both Shireikan share intelligence and pre-position troops accordingly. Neither waits for the Champion to coordinate this.

- Failed coordination: if direct Shireikan-to-Shireikan coordination is insufficient, both escalate to the Champion together. A crisis that spans both halves of the Wall is precisely the situation the Rikugunshokan appointment exists to resolve.

**Decision 7 — Jade Distribution Across Six Towers:**

- Distribution logic: towers with active sorties or SS Medium/High receive priority jade allocation. Towers with no Shadowlands activity receive minimum maintenance supply. The Shireikan retains a jade reserve using the same dynamic sizing model as their Koku pool — healthy Wall distributes generously, stressed Wall retains more in reserve.

- Critical flag: if any Tower flags jade critical, draw from the Shireikan’s jade reserve immediately — same automatic approval logic as emergency Koku. No deliberation when a tower has no jade and SS is elevated.

- Escalation: if jade reserve is exhausted and a tower is still critical, escalate to Champion to acquire more from mine provinces. Jade shortage across multiple towers simultaneously is a Champion-level supply problem.

**Decision 8 — Tea Supply Oversight:**

- Tower monitoring: if any tower reports Tea rationing or empty stockpile, prioritise Tea resupply to that tower from the half’s Brotherhood allocation. Higher Taint Rank concentration in a tower’s garrison increases its Tea priority.

- PTL awareness: monitor PTL in provinces containing monasteries within the half. If any monastery province reaches PTL 4+, flag to Champion — cultivation is degrading and supply will thin within one to two seasons. PTL 6+ is an immediate escalation — that monastery is lost as a Tea source.

- Brotherhood relationship maintenance: fund material support to monasteries in the half as part of normal seasonal resource allocation. Assign patrol coverage to Brotherhood roads when military protection is needed. These are not optional — neglecting them directly threatens Tea supply within one to two seasons.

- Escalation: if Tea is critically low across multiple towers simultaneously, or a monastery province hits PTL 6+, escalate to Champion immediately. This is a structural supply problem beyond Shireikan authority to fix alone.

**Decision 9 — Large Sortie Approval:**

Large sorties (41–60% of garrison) require Shireikan approval. They are the most dangerous commitment a Tower can make — nearly half the garrison leaves the Wall for an extended operation into the Deep Shadowlands. The Shireikan approves only when all four conditions are simultaneously met:

- SS is High (9+): the threat level justifies the risk. A Large sortie against Medium SS is wasteful and exposes the Tower to unnecessary danger.

- SI is at or above 6: the Tower can absorb a horde hit if one arrives while the garrison is depleted. A degraded Tower cannot survive a horde assault at half garrison.

- Jade stockpile is sufficient for 3 fingers per warrior for an extended mission: no exceptions. A Large sortie into the Deep Shadowlands without full jade allocation is a death march.

- No active horde detected: if scouts report a horde forming, deny regardless of all other conditions. You do not split your force when the enemy is moving toward you.

- Immediate denial override: if the Shireikan’s own Tower or any adjacent Tower in their half is under threat simultaneously, deny automatically. The Wall’s survival takes priority over SS reduction regardless of how high SS has climbed.

**Decision 10 — Garrison Shortage Letter Campaign:**

When a Tower’s garrison drops below minimum and redeployment within the half cannot fill the gap, the Shireikan does not wait for the Champion to act. Section 2.4.12 grants the Shireikan authority to write letters directly to Crab lords to propagate the Wall Garrison Shortage topic. This is the Shireikan’s own letter campaign — separate from and parallel to the Champion’s pipeline.

- Trigger: garrison shortage flagged by a Taisa AND redeployment from other towers in the half is insufficient or would create a new shortage elsewhere.

- Action: write letters to Crab Family Daimyo with troops available in their provinces. Each letter plants the Wall Garrison Shortage opinion topic in the recipient’s awareness and sets starting position based on their virtues and disposition. Target lords with high Chugi, Yu, or Meiyo first — they are most likely to respond without courtier escalation.

- Escalation to Champion: if one full season of Shireikan letters has not moved any lord to the +50 commitment threshold, escalate to the Champion. The Champion then takes over the pipeline — letters first, then DISPATCH_COURTIER if letters fail, then Wall-wide emergency if courtier is refused (per Section 55.23a).

- Parallel operation: the Shireikan’s letter campaign and the Champion’s pipeline can run simultaneously. The Shireikan does not wait for Champion authorisation to write letters — this is within their standing authority. The Champion’s courtier dispatch and emergency declaration are escalation tools the Shireikan cannot access themselves.

### 2.4.14 Clan Champion & Rikugunshokan AI — LOCKED

The Clan Champion holds ultimate authority over the Wall. In normal operations they oversee both Shireikan directly. When war or extreme crisis demands their attention elsewhere, they delegate Wall authority to a Rikugunshokan — one Shireikan elevated with full Champion-level Wall decision-making power. The Rikugunshokan AI is identical to the Champion AI for all Wall purposes. The Champion retains override authority at all times but is functionally absent from day-to-day Wall decisions while delegated.

**Decision 1 — Seasonal Koku Allocation Between Shireikan:**

Each season the Champion allocates the Wall’s total Koku budget between the two Shireikan. Three factors determine the split:

- Current Wall health: the half with more degraded towers (lower average SI, higher average SS) receives a larger share. The Champion does not distribute equally when conditions are unequal.

- Threat intelligence: if scouts report horde activity concentrated near one half, that half receives more. Known threats justify pre-emptive investment.

- Historical drain: a half that consistently exhausts its allocation or requires emergency Koku receives a structural increase. Two consecutive seasons of emergency draws flags a systemic budget problem requiring Champion-level resolution.

**Decision 2 — Reserve Army Commitment:**

The Crab maintain five armies, two permanently on the Wall. The Champion controls the remaining three as a strategic reserve. Committing reserve armies to the Wall is the Champion’s most powerful Wall intervention — and the most costly, as it strips the clan’s field capability.

- Trigger: a Shireikan escalates a garrison crisis that redeployment within the Wall cannot resolve. Or a confirmed massive horde (high Strength counter) is heading for a tower already at low garrison.

- Constraint: if the clan is at war, reserve armies may be committed to the field instead. The Champion weighs Wall crisis severity against field campaign needs. This is the core tension of the Crab Champion’s position — the Wall and the rest of the Empire pull in opposite directions.

**Decision 3 — Rikugunshokan Appointment:**

- Wall-wide emergency: a crisis spans both halves simultaneously and direct Shireikan coordination has failed. Unified command is required. The Champion elevates one Shireikan to Rikugunshokan.

- War delegation: the Crab are at war and the Champion cannot focus on the Wall. They appoint a Rikugunshokan to oversee the entire Wall with full delegated authority, freeing the Champion to manage the military campaign. The Rikugunshokan inherits all Champion-level Wall decisions until the delegation is revoked.

- Selection: the Champion chooses the Shireikan whose half is currently more stable — the one who can best manage the whole Wall while their own section is in better shape. In a war delegation, the Champion may also factor in which Shireikan is the stronger political operator, as the Rikugunshokan will need to coordinate cross-clan aid requests.

**Decision 4 — Courtier Dispatch to Family Daimyo:**

When a garrison shortage cannot be filled by redeployment alone, the Champion engages a three-step escalation pipeline. Step 1: write letters to Family Daimyo propagating the Wall Garrison Shortage topic — attempted for at least one full season before escalating. Step 2: if letters have not moved any lord to the +50 commitment threshold, dispatch a courtier directly to the most promising Daimyo. The courtier visit is harder to refuse than a letter, carries the Champion’s personal authority, and demands a same-season decision. Step 3: if the courtier is refused and the crisis remains critical, proceed to Decision 6 (Wall-wide emergency declaration). Note: the Shireikan runs their own parallel letter campaign (Section 2.4.13 Decision 10) and does not wait for Champion authorisation to write letters. The Champion’s pipeline is the escalation layer above the Shireikan’s.

- Compliance: the Daimyo commits bushi. Honor gain scaled to contribution size (per Section 2.4.12 table). Disposition improvement with the Champion — a permanent positive historical modifier.

- Refusal: the Daimyo declines. Honor loss applied immediately — publicly refusing the Champion’s representative is a visible act of defiance. Severity scales with Wall urgency: minor loss during quiet periods, serious loss during active horde assault. Permanent negative disposition hit with the Champion — historical modifier applied. The Champion remembers who helped and who did not.

- Courtier cost: dispatching courtiers is not free. It consumes the Champion’s political capital and removes a courtier from other duties. This tool is reserved for genuine emergencies, not routine shortages.

**Decision 5 — Requesting Aid from Other Clans:**

- Trigger: a breach is imminent or has occurred and Crab resources alone cannot contain the threat. This is the Shadowlands Incursion crisis path — see Section 16.3 for the full court commitment system.

- The Champion uses the existing topic and court systems to request military aid, shugenja support, or supplies from other clans. Clans with high disposition toward the Crab or strong Chugi/Yu/Meiyo personalities respond more readily. The Champion’s own disposition network and past favors determine how much leverage they have when making this request.

**Decision 6 — Wall-Wide Emergency Declaration:**

- Trigger: a breach has occurred or is imminent across multiple sections simultaneously. The Champion formally declares a Wall-wide emergency, which elevates the Shadowlands Incursion to maximum personal relevance for all Crab lords and generates an immediate court agenda item. This declaration cannot be ignored — every Crab lord is compelled to respond.

- A Wall-wide emergency is the most serious declaration a Crab Champion can make short of the clan going to war. It supersedes all other clan priorities. The Champion makes this call only when the Wall genuinely risks catastrophic failure — not as a precaution, but as a last resort before the horde pours into Rokugan.

**Decision 7 — Jade Acquisition:**

- Routine purchasing: each season the Champion allocates Koku to jade purchases from available mine provinces. Crab-territory mines take priority — shortest supply chain, most reliable. Non-Crab mines are purchased when Crab supply is insufficient. The Yasuki handle the commercial transactions; the Champion authorises the expenditure.

- Stockpile target: maintain a clan-level jade reserve sufficient for at least two full seasons of Wall consumption across all twelve towers simultaneously. Below that threshold, increase purchasing until restored.

- Forced sale enforcement: if a non-Crab province is withholding jade — claiming production shortfalls or delays — the Champion escalates through court action. Direct confrontation with the offending lord before appealing to Imperial authority.

- War constraint: if the clan is at war, jade expenditure competes with campaign costs. The Wall takes priority unless the campaign is existential. A Champion who lets Wall jade supply fail to fund a border skirmish is making the wrong trade.

**Decision 8 — Brotherhood Relationship Management:**

The Brotherhood are not asking for much. Their asks are modest, reasonable, and entirely within the Crab’s means. The danger is not cost — it is neglect. The Champion manages three pillars specifically in relation to the handful of Tea-producing monasteries the Crab depend on, not the Brotherhood as a whole.

- Material support: a small seasonal budget covers food and basic maintenance for the specific Tea-producing monasteries only — a handful of locations whose output the Crab depend on. The Koku cost is genuinely minimal. There is no excuse for failing this. Treat it as a fixed Wall operational cost, non-discretionary.

- Military protection: include the roads connecting Tea monasteries to Crab supply lines in existing provincial patrol orders. This costs nothing extra — it is a matter of including those routes in patrol sweeps the Crab are already conducting. Not Brotherhood roads in general. Just the Tea supply routes.

- Political goodwill: publicly honour the Brotherhood once per year and do not treat them as servants. The relationship that matters is with the abbots of the Tea-producing monasteries specifically. Maintain positive disposition. Do not interfere with their operations. This is not demanding. Losing goodwill requires active neglect or deliberate insult — a Champion who fails this has failed something that cost almost nothing to maintain.

- Escalation trigger: if all three pillars are simultaneously stressed — budget cut, roads unprotected, disposition damaged — treat this as a Tea supply emergency equivalent to a jade critical flag. Two seasons before supply degrades. Act now.

**Decision 9 — PTL Monitoring Near Tea Monasteries:**

- Standing awareness: track PTL in provinces containing Tea-producing monasteries as a separate priority from general provincial PTL monitoring. A monastery province climbing toward PTL 4 is an early warning requiring action before cultivation degrades.

- Intervention threshold: PTL 3 in a monastery province triggers a purification campaign order — shugenja AP committed to reducing PTL before it reaches the cultivation failure threshold at PTL 4. Early intervention is cheap. Waiting until PTL 5 or 6 is expensive and risks losing the monastery entirely.

- Purification priority: if war or other demands compete for shugenja AP, monastery province purification takes priority over non-critical PTL reduction elsewhere. A monastery province at PTL 3+ is treated as more urgent than a standard province at PTL 5 — the downstream cost of losing Tea supply outweighs the immediate governance cost.

- Lost monastery: if a monastery province reaches PTL 6+ and cultivation fails, immediately request Brotherhood assistance in relocating the affected monks to a safer monastery. Replacement cultivation takes at least two seasons to establish — the Wall will feel this supply gap. Flag it to Shireikans immediately so they can begin rationing.

### 2.4.15 Jade — Lore, Mechanics & Wall Supply — LOCKED

### Lore

Jade is formed from the tears of Lady Sun, shed at the dawn of creation. It is not merely a material — it is a physical manifestation of celestial purity, a fragment of Tengoku that fell into the mortal world and hardened into stone. Its intrinsic spiritual nature makes it antithetical to Jigoku in the most fundamental sense: where Jigoku corrupts, jade purifies; where the Taint spreads, jade resists; where oni manifest, jade burns. The Shadowlands and jade cannot occupy the same space without one destroying the other.

This purity is not without cost. Jade does not merely repel the Taint — it absorbs it. A piece of jade carried into the Shadowlands is fighting a constant battle against Jigoku’s influence, and over time that battle leaves its mark. What begins as a brilliant green stone slowly turns soft and brittle, discolors to grey and brown, and finally dissolves into black sludge. The jade has not failed — it has sacrificed itself, taking the Taint into its own substance so the warrior carrying it does not have to. The Crab understand this better than any clan in the Empire. A Hida bushi who returns from the Shadowlands with blackened jade fingers knows exactly what that jade died doing.

The Crab’s relationship with jade is unlike any other clan’s. For other clans jade is a sacred substance, venerated and rarely touched. For the Crab it is a consumable military resource — as necessary as rice and iron, and far harder to obtain. A Crab warrior who ventures beyond the Wall without jade is considered not merely reckless but negligent. The clan’s culture around jade is entirely practical: how much do we have, how long will it last, and where do we get more.

### Mechanical Rules — Individual Protection

Jade provides protection against Taint acquisition. A character carrying jade gains +10 to all Earth rolls made to resist gaining Taint. Touching jade to a Tainted wound within a minute of injury provides an additional +10 to resist being Tainted by that wound. Jade does not protect against Taint that is already present — it cannot reverse infection, only resist new acquisition.

**Jade Fingers in the Shadowlands — LOCKED:**

Small pieces of jade carried on the person are referred to colloquially as “fingers of jade” and are standard issue for any Crab warrior entering the Shadowlands. Each finger absorbs Taint over time and eventually degrades. Multiple fingers extend the protection period, but with diminishing returns — they absorb the Taint simultaneously rather than sequentially.

| **Number of Fingers** | **Length of Protection** |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | 7 days |

**Jade Powder — LOCKED:**

Ground jade mixed into a paste and carried in single-dose pouches. Applied to a weapon as a Complex Action. Grants the weapon jade’s full effect against Shadowlands creatures for three hits, with diminishing returns: first hit deals full damage, second hit deals half damage, third hit deals quarter damage. After three hits the powder is exhausted and cannot be recovered. In the Shadowlands the Taint consumes exposed jade powder within two hours — Crab bushi keep pouches sealed until immediately before combat. Jade powder is a stopgap for warriors who cannot carry jade weapons proper.

**Jade Petal Tea — LOCKED:**

Despite its name, Jade Petal Tea contains no actual jade. It is an herbal concoction cultivated by the Brotherhood of Shinsei in a handful of monasteries. Daily consumption causes most physical and mental symptoms of the Taint to go into remission, temporarily deactivates any Shadowlands Powers the character has acquired, and significantly slows Taint growth. It does not reverse serious mutations or reduce existing Taint Rank. A Tainted character who drinks it faithfully is not cured — they are managed. The Kuni Witch-Hunters coordinate with the Brotherhood to track distribution. Unauthorized possession is considered evidence of Taint. On the Wall, Jade Petal Tea is the standard treatment for garrison troops who have accumulated low-level Taint from prolonged proximity to the Shadowlands.

### Jade as a Strategic Resource

Jade is a Legendary-tier material (Section 42), produced only at Imperial-controlled mine locations. Even clan capitals must receive it via supply chains. It moves along trade routes at 2% chance per IC day — slower and less reliable than any other material in the game. A clan that neglects jade acquisition faces increased Taint vulnerability during Shadowlands Incursion events, and reduced capacity to protect sortie parties and garrison troops on the Wall.

At clan level, active jade stockpiles reduce PTL adjacent bleed into Crab provinces by half (per Section 2.6.3 and PTL rules). This is the primary strategic reason jade acquisition matters beyond individual character protection — a Crab lord who secures jade for their clan reduces Taint pressure across the entire southern border.

### Wall Jade Supply — LOCKED

Each Tower maintains a jade stockpile separate from the clan-level jade reserve. Tower jade is consumed by sortie parties and garrison troops facing Tainted enemies. It is tracked at Tower level, not clan level — a well-supplied clan can still have individual towers running critically low if logistics are disrupted.

**Sortie Jade Allocation — LOCKED:**

Every sortie party receives a standard jade finger allocation before departure. The allocation scales with sortie size and expected duration. A Small sortie (10–20% garrison, 1–3 day mission) receives 1 finger per warrior. A Medium sortie (21–40%, up to 7 days) receives 2 fingers per warrior. A Large sortie (41–60%, extended mission) receives 3 fingers per warrior. Jade powder pouches are issued separately as a combat supplement — one pouch per warrior for expected combat encounters. Warriors returning from a sortie with degraded jade fingers return them to the tower — blackened jade is useless but confirms the warrior carried it. Missing jade on return triggers a Taint inspection by the Kuni or their equivalent.

**Low Jade Supply Consequences — LOCKED:**

- Sorties cannot be ordered at Small size or above when Tower jade stockpile is critically low. The Taisa AI will not send warriors into the Shadowlands without minimum jade allocation — doing so without jade is a death sentence from Taint accumulation.

- Garrison troops facing Tainted enemies without jade protection make standard Taint exposure rolls at full TN with no bonus. Over seasons this accumulates into low-level Taint in the garrison, degrading their effectiveness and eventually generating Taint management costs.

- A Tower with critically low jade escalates immediately to the Shireikan. Jade shortage is treated with the same urgency as Rice shortage — a garrison that cannot protect itself from Taint is not a garrison, it is a slow casualty list.

**Two Supply Chains — LOCKED:**

- Jade fingers and jade powder — physical jade, mined at Imperial-controlled locations, moving through the trade network at Legendary tier rates. Subject to supply chain disruption, market availability, and political access to Imperial stockpiles. The Crab’s most critical and most fragile material supply line.

- Jade Petal Tea — Brotherhood of Shinsei cultivation, distributed through monastery networks. Separate from the jade trade entirely. Availability depends on the Crab’s relationship with the Brotherhood and the monasteries in their territory. Disrupting the Brotherhood’s network — through war, political action, or Taint contamination of monastery lands — cuts off the garrison’s primary Taint management tool.

### Jade Petal Tea — Acquisition & Supply — LOCKED

The Brotherhood of Shinsei is not a servant organisation. They supply Jade Petal Tea to Wall garrisons because they believe in protecting the Empire from the Taint — but that goodwill must be actively cultivated. The Crab cannot compel Tea delivery. They can only earn it.

**The Three Pillars of Brotherhood Support — LOCKED:**

- Material support: funding monastery upkeep, donating Rice, contributing to Brotherhood construction and maintenance. A Crab lord who provides regular material support to monasteries in their territory builds a reservoir of goodwill that sustains Tea supply even during difficult periods.

- Military protection: keeping Brotherhood roads and monasteries safe from banditry, Shadowlands threats, and the general violence of a border province. A monastery that loses monks to Bakemono raids reduces its cultivation capacity. A Brotherhood that feels unprotected reduces its cooperation.

- Political goodwill: maintaining positive disposition with Brotherhood leadership at court. The Brotherhood has influence in the Imperial court and among all clans. A Crab clan that publicly respects and supports the Brotherhood builds political capital that translates into reliable Tea supply and Brotherhood advocacy at court when the Crab need it.

Neglecting any pillar long enough quietly degrades Tea supply. The Brotherhood does not announce reductions — supply simply thins until the Taisa notices the stockpile is not being replenished at the expected rate.

**PTL Effect on Tea Cultivation — LOCKED:**

The Tea blossom cannot grow in Tainted soil. Monasteries in provinces with elevated PTL lose cultivation capacity as the land itself becomes inhospitable to the plant. This creates a direct feedback loop between Wall failure and Tea supply failure.

| **Province PTL** | **Cultivation Effect** | **Notes** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0–3 | Full supply | Normal cultivation. Tea flows to garrison at standard rate. |
| 4–5 | Partial supply (half) | Soil showing corruption. Blossoms wither early. Taisa must ration. |
| 6+ | Cultivation fails | No Tea produced from this monastery. Brotherhood may relocate monks to safer territory. |

The feedback loop: Wall degrades — PTL rises in adjacent Crab provinces — monasteries lose cultivation capacity — Tea supply falls — Tainted garrison troops go unmanaged — Wall harder to defend — PTL rises further. The Crab have a direct mechanical incentive to keep PTL low in their own provinces, not just for governance but to protect their Taint management infrastructure.

**Tea as Tower Stockpile — LOCKED:**

Jade Petal Tea is tracked as a Tower-level stockpile alongside Rice and jade. Each Tainted garrison member consuming Tea costs 1 dose per season. The Taisa tracks how many Tainted troops are present and whether the stockpile can cover them.

- Full supply: all Tainted garrison members receive their daily dose. Taint growth is suppressed. Symptoms are managed.

- Partial supply: the Taisa prioritises. The most heavily Tainted troops receive Tea first — those at higher Taint Rank are closest to becoming Lost and take priority. Lower-ranked troops go without and make unassisted Taint growth rolls.

- Empty stockpile: all Tainted garrison members make unassisted Taint growth rolls each season. Over time this generates genuine Taint Rank increases in named characters, potentially leading to Lost conversions that remove warriors from the garrison entirely. A Tower running dry on Tea is losing its veteran troops to corruption.

- Tea shortage escalates immediately to the Shireikan — same urgency as jade shortage. A garrison losing warriors to Taint corruption is degrading just as surely as one losing them to battle.

**Tea Shortage Consequence Cascade — LOCKED:**

When Tea supply runs out and Tainted garrison members go unmanaged, Taint accumulates on an accelerating timeline. The consequences compound over time through four stages.

- **Short term: **Garrison troops with existing Taint begin accumulating more. Named characters start failing Earth rolls (once per month at low Ranks). Low-level symptoms emerge — nightmares, mood changes, early physical mutations. The Kuni begin monitoring flagged individuals.

- **Medium term: **Taint Ranks climb. Roll frequency increases — Rank 2 characters check once every two weeks, Rank 3 once per week. Troops become visibly symptomatic. Other garrison members notice. Morale deteriorates. The Kuni are now managing multiple at-risk individuals simultaneously.

- **Long term: **Characters reach Rank 3 and 4. Rank 4 triggers daily Earth rolls — the character is in freefall. At Rank 4 the Kuni act: the character is removed from active garrison duty immediately. They are either sent to a Brotherhood monastery for management, executed if too dangerous to transport, or — if they have already turned hostile — treated as a Lost threat inside the tower. From the Tower’s perspective the outcome is the same in all cases: the unit is gone from the garrison roster.

- **Worst case: **If the cascade goes unaddressed and a character reaches Rank 5 before the Kuni can act, they become Lost — hostile to all former allies, adding +0.5 PTL per season to the province by their presence, and representing an active threat inside the tower’s walls. A Lost character inside a garrison is an ASCII map crisis, not a bookkeeping entry.

Removal threshold — LOCKED: Taint Rank 4 triggers automatic removal from the garrison. The Crab do not wait for Rank 5 confirmation. A Rank 4 warrior on the Wall is a liability the garrison cannot afford — the Kuni doctrine is preemptive, not reactive. This is not considered dishonourable for the removed warrior if they have served faithfully — the Taint is the enemy’s doing, not their own weakness. It is, however, final.

### Jade Acquisition — The Imperial Monopoly — LOCKED

By Imperial decree, jade may only be purchased by the Crab Clan. This is not a trade preference or a gentleman’s agreement — it is law, backed by the full authority of the Emperor. Any person, merchant, or lord who mines jade, finds jade, or comes into possession of jade has one legal option: sell it to the Crab. Selling jade to any other clan, hoarding it, or moving it without Crab authorisation is a criminal act. The Kuni Witch-Hunters treat suspicious jade possession as evidence of either Taint concealment or black market activity — neither of which ends well for the holder.

The rationale is straightforward: jade is Rokugan’s primary defence against the Shadowlands, the Wall consumes it constantly, and the Crab are the ones standing on that Wall. The Empire decided long ago that concentrating jade supply in the hands of the clan that needs it most is preferable to a free market that might route it to decorative jewellery in Crane provinces while Crab warriors enter the Shadowlands without protection.

**Mine Locations — LOCKED (IMPLEMENTATION NOTE: exact map placement during world build):**

- Jade mines exist across Rokugan but are rare and unevenly distributed. The majority are located in Crab lands — the southern mountains and the territories closest to the Shadowlands have the highest jade concentrations, as if the land itself responds to the proximity of Jigoku by producing its antithesis.

- A smaller number of mines exist in other clan territories. Those clans mine the jade and are legally obligated to sell it to the Crab at a price set by the Yasuki. They have no choice in the matter — but this creates a political dynamic. A clan that controls a jade mine has leverage: they can delay sales, claim production shortfalls, or use jade access as a bargaining chip in negotiations with the Crab. The Crab cannot compel delivery without Imperial enforcement, which requires court action.

- IMPLEMENTATION NOTE: Exact mine locations on the World Map are assigned during world build. Principle is locked: most jade is in Crab territory, some is scattered across other clans, all of it flows legally to the Crab.

**The Forced Sale Mechanic — LOCKED:**

- Any province that contains a jade mine generates jade as a special resource each season. That jade is automatically offered to the Crab Clan for purchase at the Yasuki-set price. The lord of that province cannot sell it elsewhere — the game system enforces the Imperial decree. They can delay the transaction by claiming the jade is unprocessed or in transit, which is a political action that creates a disposition hit with the Crab and risks Imperial censure if sustained.

- The Crab must actually pay for it. The Imperial decree grants them the right to purchase — not to seize. A cash-strapped Crab clan that cannot afford to buy its own jade supply faces a genuine crisis. This is where the Yasuki become essential: their Koku generation funds the jade purchases that keep the Wall operational.

**The Yasuki Family — Wall Financing ****&**** the Koku Bonus — LOCKED:**

Originally a Crane family that defected to the Crab during the First Yasuki War, the Yasuki are the finest merchants in the Empire — and the most commercially minded samurai family in Rokugan, which is itself a remarkable distinction given the samurai caste’s official contempt for commerce. The Yasuki do not share that contempt. They are the Crab’s economic backbone: managing the jade trade, negotiating supply contracts, maintaining the trade networks that keep rice, iron, and Koku flowing to the Wall, and handling the diplomatic relationships that keep other clans selling their jade rather than hoarding it.

The Hida hold the Wall. The Hiruma scout beyond it. The Kaiu build and repair it. The Kuni cleanse the Taint it faces. The Yasuki pay for all of it.

Yasuki provinces generate Koku at a boosted rate reflecting their commercial sophistication and trade network density: 0.35 Koku per town PU per season, compared to the baseline of 0.25. This 40% bonus represents the Yasuki’s mercantile expertise, their control of jade trade income, and the density of commercial activity in their territory. The Yasuki Koku bonus is the primary source of Wall financing — without it, the Crab could not sustain jade purchases, SI maintenance, and garrison upkeep simultaneously.

**The Crab Tension — LOCKED:**

The Crab naturally out-perform other clans economically and militarily. The jade monopoly gives them exclusive access to the Empire’s most critical supernatural resource. The Yasuki generate more Koku than comparable provinces elsewhere. The Hida are the toughest warriors in Rokugan. But every advantage the Crab possess is perpetually consumed by the Wall. The jade they acquire is ground into powder and left on Shadowlands battlefields. The Koku their merchants generate is spent on maintenance, repairs, and garrison wages. The soldiers they train die on the Wall or return Tainted. The population growth malus bleeds their provinces of the men who would otherwise farm, trade, and grow. The Crab are strong enough to hold the Wall. They are never strong enough to do anything else as well as they could without it.

### 2.4.16 Kaiu Engineer Actions — LOCKED

The Kaiu Engineers are the Wall’s builders and its doctors. Section 2.4.2 establishes that “repair and construction actions are exclusive to Kaiu family units” — these two actions formalize that principle. Koku-based maintenance (Section 2.4.3) prevents seasonal SI decay as already locked — a Taisa who pays the listed Koku cost holds SI steady. But Koku alone cannot restore lost SI. Only a Kaiu Engineer can push SI upward through FORTIFY_WALL_SECTION, and only a Kaiu can rebuild a breached wall section through SEAL_WALL_BREACH. The Kaiu Reinforcement modifier also provides a buffer against extraordinary decay sources — horde assault SI hits (Section 2.4.5) and seasons where the Taisa cannot afford full Koku maintenance — that Koku spending alone does not cover.

**FORTIFY_WALL_SECTION (1 AP)**

Requirements: Kaiu Engineer school, physically present at a Tower, Tower SI must be 1 or above — a breached Tower (SI 0) requires SEAL_WALL_BREACH instead. Roll: Engineering + Intelligence, TN 20 + (10 − current SI) × 2. A pristine Tower (SI 10) has TN 20; a crumbling Tower (SI 2) has TN 36. Worse condition means more complex structural work.

Immediate effect: +1 SI restored. Each Raise: +0.5 additional SI. Maximum SI remains 10.

Lingering effect: Sets a Kaiu Reinforcement modifier on the Tower. This modifier reduces all seasonal SI decay (from Section 2.4.3) by a flat value per season, holding at full strength for a fixed duration and then dropping to zero. Overwrite rule: a new FORTIFY_WALL_SECTION action replaces the existing modifier only if the new decay reduction value is equal to or greater than the current one. A Rank 1 Kaiu visiting a Tower already carrying a Rank 5 reinforcement does not degrade it — the existing modifier persists with its remaining duration. If the new value equals or exceeds the existing one, the new modifier replaces it with a fresh full duration.

Kaiu Reinforcement by School Rank: Rank 1: −0.25 SI decay/season, holds 2 seasons. Rank 2: −0.25 SI decay/season, holds 3 seasons. Rank 3: −0.50 SI decay/season, holds 3 seasons. Rank 4: −0.50 SI decay/season, holds 4 seasons. Rank 5: −0.75 SI decay/season, holds 5 seasons.

Rotation implications: A Rank 3 Kaiu can maintain 4 towers on a seasonal rotation — visit one per season, each reinforcement lasts 3 seasons, so 3 towers are always buffered plus the one just visited. A Rank 5 can cover 5. The Crab never have enough Kaiu Engineers to cover all 12 simultaneously. The rotation is the game.

**SEAL_WALL_BREACH (2 AP)**

Requirements: Kaiu Engineer Rank 3+, physically present at Tower, Tower SI = 0. This is rebuilding a destroyed wall section — ashigaru cannot do this, nor can Koku alone. Roll: Engineering + Intelligence, TN 35. Koku cost: 5 Koku (materials for breach reconstruction, paid by the Taisa from Tower funds). Duration: full season commitment — both AP slots committed on the day of the action, representing sustained work across the season.

Success: SI restored to 2. The wall section is back but barely functional. Normal FORTIFY_WALL_SECTION actions can raise it from there. Failure: no SI restored. Koku still spent (materials wasted in the attempt). May retry next season. This formalizes the “dedicated Kaiu repair action” referenced in Section 2.4.5 post-assault recovery rules.

NPC Engine Integration: Both actions require ActionID enum entries, Annex D skill map entries, and objective alignment entries in Annex A. FORTIFY_WALL_SECTION aligns to the existing MAINTAIN_FORTIFICATION NeedType (Section 57.8.1). SEAL_WALL_BREACH aligns to MAINTAIN_FORTIFICATION with an urgency override when SI = 0. Personality leans: Chugi (Duty) +15, Meiyo (Honor) +8 for both actions. Engine table entries: LOCKED. See Section 57.19.

Taisa AI Integration: The Taisa AI (Section 2.4.11) adds an eighth decision: Kaiu Engineer Request. If no Kaiu Reinforcement modifier is active and the Tower’s SI is below 8, the Taisa requests a Kaiu Engineer from the Shireikan. If SI is below 5, the request is flagged as urgent. If SI = 0, the request is treated with the same priority as a horde detection — a breached Tower without a Kaiu Engineer cannot be repaired at all.

### 2.4.17 Kuni Province Purification — LOCKED

The Kuni Witch Hunters range across Crab territory (and beyond) hunting Taint in people — investigating maho use, monitoring Tainted individuals, and eliminating the Lost. Their work is the INVESTIGATE_THREAT motivation weight against Maho_Cult_Spreading and Low_level_Taint_Discovered topics. Province purification is a different discipline. Cleansing Tainted land requires sustained ritual work by a Kuni Shugenja — a priest, not a hunter. The two roles are complementary but distinct: the Witch Hunter finds the infected; the Shugenja heals the ground they walked on.

**Baseline Shadowlands Bleed — LOCKED:**

All provinces directly adjacent to the Shadowlands accumulate +0.1 PTL per season. This is constant and unavoidable regardless of Tower condition — the price of being the Empire’s southern border. The Wall contains the worst of Jigoku’s influence but cannot eliminate it entirely. At +0.1/season without intervention: PTL 1 after 10 seasons (2.5 IC years), PTL 3 (Tea cultivation degradation threshold) after 30 seasons (7.5 IC years). Slow on its own but compounds with degraded tower bleed (+0.5 PTL/season when SI ≤ 5, per Section 2.4.2) and any maho use in the province (Section 56.14.6). Without periodic Kuni purification, even well-maintained Wall provinces eventually become spiritually inhospitable.

**PURIFY_TAINTED_GROUND (1 AP)**

Requirements: Kuni Shugenja school, physically present in the province. Roll: Lore: Shadowlands + Intelligence, TN 15 + (current PTL × 5). At PTL 1 the TN is 20; at PTL 4 the TN is 35; at PTL 6 the TN is 45 — only a skilled Kuni succeeds reliably at high contamination levels.

Immediate effect: −0.5 PTL. Each Raise: −0.25 additional PTL reduction.

Lingering effect: Sets a Kuni Ward on the province. The ward reduces all incoming PTL accumulation (baseline bleed and degraded tower bleed) by a flat value per season, holding at full strength for a fixed duration and then dropping to zero. Only one Kuni Ward can be active per province at a time. Overwrite rule: a new PURIFY_TAINTED_GROUND action replaces the existing ward only if the new bleed reduction value is equal to or greater than the current one. A Rank 1 Kuni visiting a province already carrying a Rank 5 ward does not degrade it — the existing ward persists with its remaining duration. If the new value equals or exceeds the existing one, the new ward replaces it with a fresh full duration.

Kuni Ward by School Rank: Rank 1: −0.1 PTL bleed/season, holds 2 seasons. Rank 2: −0.1 PTL bleed/season, holds 3 seasons. Rank 3: −0.2 PTL bleed/season, holds 4 seasons. Rank 4: −0.2 PTL bleed/season, holds 5 seasons. Rank 5: −0.3 PTL bleed/season, holds 6 seasons.

At baseline bleed (+0.1/season) only: A Rank 1 ward halves it (net +0.05/season — still climbing, slowly). A Rank 3 ward more than neutralizes it (net −0.1/season — the province improves while the ward is active). A Rank 5 ward pushes the province down by −0.2/season.

At baseline + degraded tower bleed (+0.6/season total): A Rank 5 ward reduces incoming by 0.3, netting +0.3/season — still climbing but at half speed. Without the ward, the province hits PTL 3 (Tea cultivation degradation) in 5 seasons. With the ward: 10 seasons. That buys time to fix the Tower.

**Group Effort — Cooperative Purification:**

Uses the standard L5R 4e cooperative roll framework: one primary roller, assistants make TN 15 rolls on Lore: Shadowlands + Intelligence, each success grants the primary roller +1k0 on their PURIFY_TAINTED_GROUND roll. This improves the immediate PTL reduction (more Raises achieved), not the ward value. The ward is always based on the highest-ranking Kuni present — wards do not stack additively. Multiple Kuni working together produce a better single purification result, not multiple independent wards.

**Rotation Implications:**

A Rank 3 Kuni Shugenja can maintain wards on 4 provinces (visit one per season, each ward holds 4 seasons). A Rank 5 can cover 6. The Crab have multiple Shadowlands-adjacent provinces requiring periodic purification. The Kuni Shugenja are also needed for garrison Taint assessment (Section 2.4.11 Decision 7), Tea monastery PTL monitoring (Section 2.4.14 Decision 9), and general spiritual duties across Crab territory. This creates the same personnel tension as the Kaiu Engineers — there are never enough Kuni to be everywhere at once.
