## 55.11b Named Monk Objective Assignment

Named monk characters — player and NPC alike — run through the same seven-phase decision loop as all other named characters. What differs is their objective assignment chain, their available action pool, and their typical context flags.

**Objective Assignment — LOCKED:**

Monks do not have a feudal lord in the traditional sense. They answer to the Brotherhood of Shinsei hierarchy — their abbot, their order’s senior figures, or the Brotherhood’s regional leadership. ASSIGN_OBJECTIVE for a named monk flows from this hierarchy rather than from a clan lord. A Clan Champion cannot order a monk the way they order a samurai vassal. A lord may request a monk’s assistance and the monk may choose to comply — but compliance is driven by the monk’s own objectives and disposition, not by feudal obligation.

Monks who are player characters may receive objectives from their abbot through the letter system, from crisis events that personally engage them, or from their own standing objectives. The priority cascade is identical to all other characters: Reactive Event > Crisis Override > Primary Objective > Standing Objective.

**Standing Objective Types for Named Monks — LOCKED:**

Five standing objective types cover the range of named monk behavior on the World Map. All five use existing NeedTypes and ActionIDs — no new engine components required.

1. Help People — NeedType: RAISE_DISPOSITION (local population targets), ACQUIRE_RESOURCE (sharing with those in need). Actions: SHARE_SUPPLIES, LISTEN_REFLECT, social actions directed at provincial population. Outcome: Stability gain in target province. A monk with this standing objective gravitates toward provinces in crisis, famine, or unrest — wherever people need help most.

2. Fight Bandits — NeedType: INVESTIGATE_THREAT, PATROL_PROVINCE. Actions: ORDER_BATTLE, CONDUCT_RAID against active banditry or insurgency events. Primarily Sohei and Yamabushi monks. Outcome: Criminality reduction, Stability gain. This standing objective routes the monk toward provinces with active bandit events rather than toward population centers.

3. Meditate — NeedType: PERFORM_RITUAL. Actions: MEDITATE, PERFORM_WORSHIP. The monk seeks quiet locations — temples, remote monasteries, sacred sites. Generates small Honor gain and personal spiritual development. A monk with this standing objective will use BEGIN_TRAVEL to reach suitable locations before settling into sustained meditation.

4. Train — NeedType: TRAIN_SKILL. Actions: TRAIN, MENTOR (if the monk has sufficient rank to teach). Monks advance through Kiho rather than Techniques but the progression mechanic is identical. A monk pursuing mastery trains regularly and seeks Sensei above their current rank. If their rank is high enough they may also generate MENTOR_CHARACTER objectives — seeking out younger monks to teach.

5. Worship the Kami — NeedType: PERFORM_RITUAL. Actions: PERFORM_WORSHIP at dedicated shrines and temples. The traveling worshipper pattern: the monk moves between worship locations generating steady WP wherever they go. Unlike Shugenja, named monks do not roll for bonus WP — their contribution is flat but consistent and does not require a seasonal check. A monk following this standing objective effectively becomes mobile worship infrastructure, filling gaps that fixed Shugenja cannot cover.

**Context Flags — LOCKED:**

Named monks do not have a home holding in the feudal sense. Their most common context flags are TRAVELING (moving between locations) and AT_TEMPLE (present at a worship location). They may also fire at AT_OWN_HOLDINGS if they are resident at a monastery, or AT_COURT if they have been invited or have reason to attend. The available action pool for AT_TEMPLE includes PERFORM_WORSHIP, MEDITATE, TRAIN, MENTOR, and social actions with other characters present. TRAVELING context includes BEGIN_TRAVEL routing and social actions with characters encountered en route.

**Monastery Context — LOCKED:**

Distant monasteries with no significant civilian population generate WP primarily from their resident monk population rather than background PU devotion. Named monks resident at a monastery contribute their flat active worship each AP alongside the background staff generation. The monastery is their AT_OWN_HOLDINGS equivalent — it is where they train, meditate, and receive objectives from their abbot. A named monk at their home monastery has full access to all non-military actions.
