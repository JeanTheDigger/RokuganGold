## 57.42 Sailing, Captains, and Passage

### 57.42.1 Design Intent

The Sailing skill (Section 24.3) and its mechanics are already plumbed into Section 11.9 (ship types, naval combat, navigation, weather) and Section 11.11 (pirate suppression). What Section 11.9 does not formalize is who makes the Sailing rolls on behalf of a ship, and how a samurai without their own vessel arranges transport on someone else’s. This section fills both gaps. It defines the captain as a formal role with a minimum Sailing requirement, distinguishes ship ownership from ship command, handles captain incapacitation, and introduces REQUEST_PASSAGE — the 0-AP social request by which a samurai arranges to be carried to a destination on a ship they do not own.

Rank 5 Mastery Ability (+5 on Cooperative or Cumulative Sailing rolls) per Section 24.3 applies identically to the Engineering Rank 5 mastery treated in Section 57.41: the bonus fires on multi-character or multi-session Sailing tasks (navigating a long voyage across multiple sub-tiles, coordinated crew actions during naval combat, multi-session ship repair). It does not apply to single-roll single-character checks. The same deferred Cooperative Skill Roll framework note from Section 57.41 applies here.

### 57.42.2 Captain Role and Ship Ownership

Every ship has two distinct character references: owner_id (who owns the vessel as property) and captain_id (who commands it day-to-day). Ship ownership follows one of two patterns depending on how the vessel came to exist.

**Command-appointment pattern — military and clan-owned vessels:** the clan or daimyo owns the ship. A captain is appointed through the military chain of command (Section 11.7 Army Combat). For Mantis kobune fleets, the captain is a member of the Kobune Captain Advanced School (Section 29), appointed by the Mantis Clan Champion or a delegated commander; the crew are typically Rank 1 Yoritomo Bushi. For Crab Koutetsukan and large Kaiu Shipmaster vessels, the captain is a Kaiu Shipmaster (Sailing 5 prerequisite) appointed by Crab military authority. For Crane Doji Marines vessels, a Sailing 3+ Marine captain is appointed by Crane military authority. For Imperial Legion warships, captain appointment follows the Imperial military hierarchy. In all command-appointment cases, owner_id points to the clan/daimyo entity, captain_id points to the appointed officer, and the captain holds their position at the owner’s discretion — they can be reassigned or removed by the appointing authority.

**Owner-patron pattern — private merchant vessels:** an individual samurai owns the ship as a business asset. The samurai is a merchant-patron — typically a Daidoji merchant (Crane), Yasuki merchant (Crab), Tortoise-family member (Kasuga Smuggler), or an independent Mantis trader. The owner hires a captain to operate the vessel; captains under this pattern are often heimin sailors, lower-status samurai, or ronin with relevant experience. The owner sets standing orders (“trade these goods,” “refuse Scorpion passage”) that the captain follows. In owner-patron cases, owner_id points to the merchant-patron samurai, captain_id points to the hired captain (who may themselves be a samurai or a named heimin character), and the captain serves at the owner’s pleasure. The owner retains override authority over the captain’s decisions.

**Self-owned edge case:** a ronin or minor samurai with a single small vessel (sampan, fishing kobune) may be both owner and captain — owner_id and captain_id point to the same character. This covers individual operators but is mechanically identical to the owner-patron case with owner and captain being the same entity.

### 57.42.3 Captain Sailing Requirements

Minimum Sailing rank required to legitimately captain a ship of each class:

**Small coastal/river vessels (Sampan, Merchant Barge, Kobune):** Sailing 1 minimum. A broad pool qualifies; heimin sailors with basic experience suffice for a captain-for-hire role.

**Oceangoing merchant (Sengokobune):** Sailing 3 minimum. The open-ocean capability demands more skill. The Mantis Kobune Captain school (Sailing 4 prerequisite) produces most named Sengokobune captains.

**Heavy warships (Koutetsukan, Atakebune):** Sailing 4 minimum. Professional-grade handling of iron-clad or fortress-class vessels. Kaiu Shipmaster (Sailing 5 prerequisite) is the canonical Koutetsukan captain path.

The owner has no Sailing requirement. A Daidoji merchant with Commerce 5 and Sailing 0 legitimately owns a Kobune; they simply must employ a qualified captain. An owner attempting to personally captain a ship for which they do not meet the Sailing requirement operates at a −2k0 penalty on all Sailing rolls for that vessel, representing inadequate training. Engine validation: when a captain is appointed to a ship, the engine verifies the captain’s Sailing rank meets the ship class’s minimum. Appointments that fail this check are either rejected (for military chain appointments, blocked by the appointing authority’s review) or permitted with the −2k0 penalty (for owner-patron self-captaining, the owner’s choice to accept the risk).

### 57.42.4 Captain Mechanical Duties

The captain is the character who commands the ship. During routine travel, the captain does not roll — ships move at their listed rate and arrive at their destination. The captain’s Sailing skill matters during naval combat: the Ride the Waves Heroic Opportunity (Section 11.7 Category 9) uses Sailing (Navigation) + Intelligence, and the captain’s Battle skill governs the ship’s Company Attack/Defense contributions per the standard military command rules (Section 11.7). Ship-level tools enhance combat navigation rolls: a Mantis direction finder (+1k0 on Navigation rolls during combat) or a shugenja aboard who successfully communes with water kami (+2k0 on the captain’s next Navigation roll during combat).

In naval combat (Section 11.9), the captain is the Company commander for the ship. Their Battle skill governs the Company’s Attack/Defense contributions per the standard military command rules (Section 11.7), while their Sailing skill governs maneuvering rolls specific to ships (weather adjustment, ramming positioning, boarding approach). In the Ship Boarding ASCII template (Section 56.18), the captain fights on the quarterdeck and is the “high-value target” per existing lore — killing or capturing the captain breaks the opposing crew’s morale.

### 57.42.5 Captain Incapacitation and Succession

If a captain is Down, Out, killed, or otherwise incapacitated while the ship is at sea, temporary command devolves to the crew member with the highest Sailing skill rank aboard the vessel (ties broken by Sailing emphasis match to the current need, then by Insight Rank, then by Status). This acting-captain state persists only until the ship returns to port, at which point the appointing authority (military chain for command-appointment ships, owner for owner-patron ships) selects a permanent replacement via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (for owner-patron appointments) or through the military chain (for command-appointment ships). If no crew member aboard has any Sailing rank, the vessel is in genuine peril — the engine rolls a catastrophic-incident check at each sub-tile transition until reaching safe harbour or sinking. A ship in combat whose captain falls continues fighting under the acting-captain for the duration of the engagement; morale effects for the specific loss-of-captain event apply per the Ship Boarding template when implemented.

### 57.42.6 REQUEST_PASSAGE ActionID

REQUEST_PASSAGE is the action by which a samurai (or ronin, or other named character) asks to be carried aboard a ship to a destination port. Category 11 (Personal Development). 0 AP cost — a brief conversational request, not an engagement requiring sustained effort. Design principle: the 1-AP social actions (CHARM, NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE, PROBE, READ_CHARACTER) are the courtier’s active toolkit and cost AP because they represent mechanical work to change state. REQUEST_PASSAGE is logistics, not courtier work — arranging transport with a captain is a routine travel request, not a deliberate political or social maneuver. Different category, different cost. This principle is authoritative for adjudicating the AP cost of similar routine-travel and logistics actions elsewhere in the system. Primary skill: Etiquette, secondary Courtier. Throttle: maximum 2 REQUEST_PASSAGE actions per character per IC day, to prevent spamming captains across a busy port.

**Preconditions:** the requester is in a settlement that has port facilities (coastal or major river settlement). The target of the request is one of: a captain currently in that same port (co-located, the normal case); an owner-patron accessible via letter (for advance arrangement through the owner rather than the captain directly); or a daimyo who owns ships, via formal petition (see below). The requester must know of the target’s existence through met_characters or topics per Section 22.3 and Section 29.15.24. Command-appointment military vessels (Section 57.42.2) cannot be targeted by REQUEST_PASSAGE directly — their captains serve the military chain and do not accept passengers on their own authority. Passage on a military ship requires petitioning the daimyo or military authority who controls the vessel; on acceptance, the authority issues ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to the captain directing them to carry the passenger.

**Parameters of the request:** the requester specifies a destination province (typically a port settlement on the ship’s feasible route), and optionally a koku offer as compensation. The koku offer is a meaningful input to the decision logic; a larger offer improves acceptance probability but does not guarantee it — a captain with standing orders to refuse Scorpion passengers, for example, does not change their answer for silver. Compensation guidance: 1–2 koku for a short coastal hop (1–2 sub-tiles), 3–5 koku for longer coastal voyages, 5–15 koku for open-ocean crossings. These are guidance values, not enforced rates; the requester may offer any amount including zero.

### 57.42.7 Captain and Owner Accept-Passage Logic

When REQUEST_PASSAGE fires, the target captain (or owner, if the request bypasses the captain to go to the owner directly) evaluates acceptance. The decision is engine-resolved through disposition, compensation, schedule compatibility, standing orders, and personality.

**Acceptance factors:** disposition toward the requester (Acquaintance or higher is the baseline threshold for free passage; lower dispositions require compensation to overcome); schedule compatibility (the ship must plausibly be travelling toward or via the requested destination as part of its normal operations — captains do not make special trips for passengers); compensation offered (meaningful but not decisive alone); standing orders (owner-set constraints on who the captain may accept — these are hard refuses); captain personality (Jin/Compassion +5 lean on accepting struggling travelers; Seigyo/Control +3 lean when proper koku is offered; Rei/Courtesy +2 lean on accepting high-Status or polite requests).

**Owner override via direct appeal:** a requester may bypass the captain entirely by petitioning the owner directly (in person or by letter). When an owner-patron grants passage, the captain complies — they do not refuse their owner. When a daimyo who owns military ships grants passage as a personal favour, the appointed captain complies. Owner-granted passage creates a soft Obligation on the requester (Section 22 Advantages — Obligation Advantage) at a 3-point value, representing the favour extended. Obligation accumulated through owner-granted passage is repaid through normal favour mechanics over time — a future letter of introduction, a political alignment vote, an artistic commission.

**Owner-override letter flow:** when the owner is not co-located with the requester or the captain, the authorization travels by letter. The full sequence: (1) the requester fires REQUEST_PASSAGE via WRITE_LETTER to the owner; (2) the letter travels to the owner at standard delivery time (Section 12.7); (3) the owner evaluates acceptance; (4) on acceptance, the owner sends two letters — one to the requester (acceptance response with departure_tick, embarkation location, and destination per Section 57.42.7 Acceptance response) and one to the captain (order to accept this passenger); (5) both letters travel at standard delivery time. Total delay is the round-trip letter time plus the owner’s decision. If departure_tick arrives before the requester’s confirmation letter or the captain’s order letter, the ship sails without the passenger and the authorization applies to the next voyage at that port.

**Lord naval asset visibility:** a lord who controls ships (owner-patron or command-appointment authority) has visibility of their naval assets through the same subordinate-tracking system used for land-based vassals. The lord knows which ships they control, where each ship currently is (the captain’s location_province_id = the ship’s current port or sea position), what each captain’s current assignment is, and each ship’s departure_tick if in port. For player lords, this information is available in the MUD interface as part of the existing vassal and holdings display. For NPC lords, the decision engine queries this data when evaluating transport requests. This parallels ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS for land holdings — no new ActionID is required because the information is a lord’s standing visibility of their own subordinates, not an active intelligence-gathering action.

**Ship selection for directive transport:** when a daimyo or owner with multiple ships accepts a transport request, the NPC decision engine selects the best ship based on: proximity to the requester (a ship already at the requester’s port is preferred over one that must sail to collect them), schedule compatibility (a ship not on another mission or deployed for military operations), route compatibility (a ship whose existing route passes through or near the requested destination), and ship class suitability (a Kobune for a single passenger, not a Koutetsukan). The daimyo issues ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to the selected captain with parameters: passenger identity, destination province, and optionally a specific departure_tick. The captain receives the directive, adjusts their departure_tick if needed, and the passenger is notified through the standard acceptance response flow (Section 57.42.7 Acceptance response). For player lords, the selection is a manual choice — the player sees their naval assets and picks which ship to assign. The directive is issued through the same ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE mechanic.

**Owner-override timing — port-only:** owner-override applies only at voyage setup, while the ship is in port and the captain has not yet sailed. Once the ship is at sea, the captain’s authority aboard is absolute — they cannot be overridden by owner letter reaching them mid-voyage (and in practice no letter can reach a ship at sea anyway). A passenger who wants to change the ship’s course, accept/decline a new passenger, or alter captain decisions mid-voyage must NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE, or BRIBE_FOR_INFO with the captain directly. The captain’s judgement at sea is law. Owner dissatisfaction with the captain’s mid-voyage decisions is resolved at the next port — the owner may reprimand, replace, or dismiss the captain after the fact, but the voyage’s outcome stands.

**Refusal:** a captain or owner may decline without Honor or Glory consequence. Refusing passage is a prerogative, not a slight — Rokugani norms do not obligate a captain to serve all comers. If the refusal was politely delivered, no disposition shift occurs. If the refusal was rude or contemptuous (context-dependent), a small negative disposition shift applies (−1 to −3 on the requester’s side, per the specific framing). A refused requester may REQUEST_PASSAGE again with the same captain only after 1 IC day has passed, and only if their circumstances have changed (new compensation offered, or letter from the captain’s lord).

**Acceptance response:** when the captain (or owner) accepts REQUEST_PASSAGE, the requester receives three pieces of information: the departure_tick (the IC day the ship sails), the embarkation location (the port Lesser Zone or dock zone where the ship is moored), and the confirmed destination province. This information is stored on the character as a pending voyage notice visible in the MUD interface. For the co-located case (requester and captain at the same port), the response is immediate. For the letter case (owner approves remotely), the information travels via return letter subject to standard delivery timing (Section 12.7) — the requester learns the departure date when the letter arrives.

**Departure timing:** every ship in port has a departure_tick — the IC day it sails. Ship field: departure_tick (IC day, stored on the ship object). Set by the captain’s NPC decision engine when the ship arrives at port, based on the captain’s purpose and standing orders. Default port stay durations by pattern: owner-patron merchant vessels stay 2–4 IC days in port (loading cargo, conducting business, accepting passengers); command-appointment military vessels stay 1–2 IC days (faster turnaround, following orders). A lord who issues ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to a captain can set a specific departure_tick, overriding the default. Departure_tick is public information at the port — any character in the port settlement can see which ships are docked, their destinations, and their departure dates. Sailors talk, dock workers know, the harbor is a public space. When departure_tick arrives, the engine sets aboard_ship_id on all accepted passengers who are co-located in the port settlement at that moment. Characters who accepted passage but are not in the port settlement at departure_tick are left behind — aboard_ship_id stays null. No departure reminder or notification is sent to accepted passengers — the player was told the date on acceptance (Section 57.42.7 Acceptance response) and it is their responsibility to be at the dock on time. Directive transport exception: when the ship’s purpose at this port is specifically to carry a particular passenger (a daimyo ordered ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE directing the captain to transport this person), the captain delays departure_tick by up to 2 IC days beyond the original date, waiting for the passenger to arrive. If the passenger has not arrived after the extension, the captain sails and fires WRITE_LETTER to the ordering daimyo reporting the passenger’s failure to appear. Incidental transport (a merchant vessel running a trade route, passenger accepted opportunistically) departs on schedule with no delay — the passenger was told the date and is one of several reasons the ship was at port. Port stay durations and directive transport extension PROVISIONAL pending balancing.

### 57.42.8 Passenger State

On acceptance, the requester has confirmed passage. Character sheet field: aboard_ship_id (default null, set to the ship’s ship_id when the ship’s departure_tick fires and the character is co-located at the port settlement). Acceptance is not embarkation — the character receives the voyage details (Section 57.42.7 Acceptance response) but does not board until departure_tick. Between acceptance and departure, the character remains free to act in the port settlement normally. At departure_tick, the engine checks co-location: if the character is in the port settlement, aboard_ship_id is set and the voyage begins. If not, the character is left behind. While aboard_ship_id is non-null, the character’s location_province_id tracks with the ship’s current location, updating as the ship moves between sub-tiles per Section 11.9 travel rates. The passenger is not in a province—they are at sea with the ship, and actions requiring province presence (e.g., AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, VISITING, AT_COURT) are not available. Social actions with other passengers or crew remain available if co-located on the same ship. When the ship reaches the agreed destination port, aboard_ship_id clears and the passenger arrives in that province. If the ship is attacked mid-voyage, the passenger is aboard during combat resolution and may participate per the Ship Boarding ASCII template when implemented; if the ship is captured or sunk, the passenger’s fate follows the ship’s fate (captured → Hostage System per Section 22.9; sunk → survival check at GM/arbiter discretion, drowning typical for characters without Athletics + Swimming skill).

### 57.42.9 Sailing Emphasis Index

Sailing has two emphases: Navigation and Knot-work. Both have mechanical uses distributed across multiple sections. This subsection consolidates every roll where each emphasis fires, serving as an implementer lookup index. No new rules are defined here — all entries reference their authoritative source section.

**Navigation emphasis — mechanical uses:** Tortoise Escape Attempt: captain rolls Navigation + Intelligence contested by enemy captain’s Battle + Intelligence (Section 11.9 Tortoise Oceangoing Vessel). Ride the Waves Naval Heroic Opportunity: Sailing (Navigation) + Intelligence TN 20 (Section 11.7 Category 9). Navigation on land: standard Sailing roll with +10 TN (Section 24.3, RAW). Pirate fleet detection via naval patrol: Sailing + Perception with Navigation emphasis applicable (Section 57.17 amendment). Standard L5R emphasis bonus: +1k0 on any Sailing roll where determining position, reading currents, or charting course is the relevant task.

**Knot-work emphasis — mechanical uses:** Secure the Rigging Naval Heroic Opportunity: Sailing (Knot-work) + Agility TN 20 (Section 11.7 Category 9). Cut the Grapples Naval Heroic Opportunity: Sailing (Knot-work) + Agility TN 15 (Section 11.7 Category 9). Rescue the Fallen Naval Heroic Opportunity (rope option): Sailing (Knot-work) + Agility TN 15 (Section 11.7 Category 9). Patch the Hull Naval Heroic Opportunity (sailor’s repair option): Sailing (Knot-work) + Strength TN 20 (Section 11.7 Category 9). Sleight of Hand Escape defense: contested vs Sailing (Knot-work) — the character who tied the bonds rolls Knot-work to resist the escape attempt (Section 24.4). Standard L5R emphasis bonus: +1k0 on any Sailing roll where working with ropes, sails, rigging, or ship hardware is the relevant task.

**Schools with Sailing as starting skill:** Yoritomo Bushi (Sailing, no emphasis specified), Yoritomo Shugenja (Sailing with Navigation emphasis), Mantis Brawler (Sailing, no emphasis specified).

**Schools requiring Sailing as prerequisite:** Kaiu Shipmasters (Sailing 5), Doji Marines (Sailing 3), Isawa Seaguard (Sailing 3), Yoritomo Scoundrel (Sailing 2), Mantis Navigator (Sailing with Navigation emphasis 3), Kobune Captain (Sailing 4).
