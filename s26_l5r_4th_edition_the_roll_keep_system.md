# 26. L5R 4th Edition — The Roll & Keep System

The Roll & Keep system (R&K) is the core dice mechanic of L5R 4th Edition. All rolls are expressed as XkY — X dice rolled, Y dice kept. The total of the kept dice is compared against a Target Number (TN) to determine success or failure.

## 26.1 Core Mechanic

**How Rolls Work:**

- Roll a number of ten-sided dice equal to the relevant Trait + Skill Rank. Keep a number of dice equal to the Trait value. Add the results of the kept dice together and compare to the TN.

- Example: A character with Agility 4 and Kenjutsu 3 attacking with a katana rolls 7 dice and keeps 4 (7k4). They add the four highest results and compare to the target's Armor TN.

- Standard TN values: TN 5 (trivial), TN 10 (easy), TN 15 (moderate), TN 20 (challenging), TN 25 (hard), TN 30 (very hard), TN 40 (extremely difficult), TN 50+ (near impossible).

**Dice Explosions:**

- Any die that results in a 10 is re-rolled and the new result added to the 10. This can chain — if the re-roll also results in a 10, it explodes again. There is no limit to how many times a die can explode.

- Exploding dice only apply to rolled dice — not kept dice that were not rolled (i.e. bonus kept dice from certain abilities).

**Raises:**

- Before rolling, a character may declare Raises. Each Raise adds 5 to the TN of the roll. If successful, each Raise produces a beneficial effect — extra damage, better outcome, narrative advantage.

- Free Raises: granted by certain abilities or circumstances. They reduce the TN by 5 each (or produce a beneficial effect without increasing TN). Free Raises do not stack with regular Raises for TN purposes.

- Called Raises: specific Raises declared for a specific purpose (e.g. Disarm, Knockdown, Extra Attack). These follow the standard +5 TN rule.

- Maximum Raises: a character cannot declare more Raises than their Ring in the relevant element, unless a School Technique or ability states otherwise.

**Unskilled Rolls:**

- When making a roll without any ranks in the relevant Skill, the character rolls only half the normal number of dice (rounded down) and keeps the same number. They also cannot make Raises on Unskilled Rolls.

- Void Points can temporarily raise a Skill from 0 to 1, eliminating Unskilled penalties for one roll.

## 26.2 Contested Rolls

When two characters directly oppose each other — in social confrontations, stealth vs. detection, grappling — both roll and compare totals. The higher total wins. Ties go to the character who initiated the action.

## 26.3 Skill Roll Formula

The standard formula for all skill-based actions: (Trait) + (Skill Rank) dice, keep (Trait) dice vs TN.

- Example: Awareness 3 + Courtier 3 → roll 6 dice, keep 3 (6k3).

- Example: Agility 4 + Kenjutsu 4 → roll 8 dice, keep 4 (8k4).

## 26.4 Wound System

Characters track damage through Wound Ranks. Each Rank has a penalty that applies to all rolls while the character is at that Rank.

- Healthy (0 penalty) → Nicked (−3) → Grazed (−5) → Hurt (−10) → Injured (−15) → Crippled (−20) → Down (−40) → Out (incapacitated).

- Wound thresholds are determined by Earth Ring: each Wound Rank holds Earth × 2 Wound Points.

- A character who reaches the Out Wound Rank is incapacitated and will die without immediate medical attention.

## 26.5 Initiative

At the start of combat, all participants roll Initiative once. Initiative is rolled as Reflexes + Insight Rank dice, keeping Reflexes dice, adding the result to a base of 10. The character with the highest Initiative acts first each Round. The Initiative Score persists for the entire skirmish unless modified by abilities or situational effects.

## 26.6 Armor TN

Every character has an Armor TN — the TN attackers must meet or beat to hit them. Base Armor TN is 5 + (Reflexes × 5). Armor, certain stances, and abilities can increase this value.
