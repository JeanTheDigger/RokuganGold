# 25. L5R 4th Edition — The Five Rings & Traits

The most basic representations of a character's abilities are the Five Rings. All things — living and nonliving — are composed of differing proportions of the five elements. Each Ring is made up of two Traits, one mental and one physical. Characters begin with all Rings and Traits at 2 out of a possible 10. Rank 2 represents normal capability with no particular training. Rank 10 is reserved for the most supremely gifted individuals and supernatural beings.

**How Rings Work:**

- Rings are never increased directly. A Ring equals the lower of its two constituent Traits.

- Example: if a Ring's Traits are 2 and 4, the Ring Rank is 2. When the lower Trait rises to 3, the Ring rises to 3.

- To raise a Ring, both Traits must be raised — the Ring follows the lower one.

## 25.1 Air

Air is enigmatic and mercurial — the element of empathy and intuition. Spirits of Air are capricious but their wrath is as a storm. Those aligned with Air possess superior instincts and reaction speed. Those at odds with Air are physically sluggish and oblivious to nuanced behavior.

**Awareness (Mental):**

Intuition and general ability to empathize with others. Allows perceiving feelings and motivations even with little evidence. Also allows better portraying those feelings — winning over others who believe the character is sympathetic. Persuasion, charisma, and manipulation are its hallmarks. The most important Trait for Social Skills.

**Reflexes (Physical):**

Ability to instantly react to events. A character with high Reflexes is fleet of foot with lightning-fast response time. Important for Initiative Score and Armor TN determination.

## 25.2 Earth

Earth is eternal, unchanging, and unmoving — the element of endurance and resistance. Spirits of Earth are quiet and impassive but capable of terrible violence. Those aligned with Earth have tremendous fortitude and resist the Shadowlands Taint. Those at odds with Earth are frail and weak-minded.

**Willpower (Mental):**

Ability to focus mental energies on the task at hand. High Willpower means decided and persistent — capable of close attention for hours with no wavering. Primarily used to resist manipulation and intimidation attempts.

**Stamina (Physical):**

Ability to endure constant physical activity and recover from wounds. High Stamina means exerting oneself for long periods with no fatigue and recovering from near-mortal wounds remarkably quickly. Primary benefit is influence on Wound recovery rate.

## 25.3 Fire

Fire is the element of dynamic change, energy, and destruction — motion and illumination. Spirits of Fire can mend what is broken or destroy all in their path. Those strong in Fire are great warriors, brilliant scholars, or both. Those weak in Fire are physically uncoordinated and sluggish of thought.

**Intelligence (Mental):**

Ability to gain new knowledge and put it to use. High Intelligence means understanding new information quickly, applying it instantly, and recalling it with clarity. Those with lower Intelligence have difficulty with new material and recall it imperfectly. Common for non-combat oriented characters.

**Agility (Physical):**

Hand-eye coordination and general physical athleticism. Virtually any physical feat not dependent on speed or strength depends on Agility. Used for almost every Weapon Skill — one of the most important Traits for combat.

## 25.4 Water

Water is the element of rapid change and alteration — the inexorable force that carves mountains and the capricious storm that batters ships. Those strong in Water possess incredible strength and perception. Those weak in Water are slow to move with dulled senses.

**Perception (Mental):**

Attention to events unfolding around the character. High Perception notices everything happening nearby. Low Perception misses even obvious things in immediate vicinity.

**Strength (Physical):**

Pure physical power with no consideration for finesse. High Strength means devastating warrior capability. Low Strength means ineffective combat power. Considerably affects weapon and unarmed damage, lifting capacity, and taxing physical activities like climbing and swimming.

## 25.5 Void

The fifth and final element — both the presence and absence of the other four elements simultaneously. The state of all elements existing together and the emptiness between them. An almost uniquely human trait — a result of the divine spark from the Celestial Heavens. Very few other creatures can even perceive Void, let alone manipulate it.

**Void Points:**

There are no Traits associated with the Void Ring. Instead, a character gains Void Points equal to their Void Ring. Void Points represent a moment of enlightened insight — when a character is briefly at one with the universe. Pool refreshes to full once per OOC day at the OOC Day Tick (Section 57.32.2). Meditation can recover them mid-session (Section 57.32.3).

**Void Point Expenditure (one per Round unless otherwise specified):**

- +1k1 to a Skill, Trait, Ring, or Spell Casting roll (declared before the roll). Damage Rolls cannot be enhanced this way.

- Temporarily increase a Skill rank from 0 to 1 — avoiding Unskilled Roll penalties.

- Reduce Wounds suffered from one damage source by 10 (declared immediately after damage total announced).

- Increase Armor TN by 10 for one Round (declared at beginning of Round).

- Exchange Initiative Score with one willing target for the remainder of the skirmish (beginning of Round — only one character needs to spend Void).

- Increase Initiative Score by 10 for the duration of the current skirmish (beginning of Round).

- Activate certain School Techniques — this does not count against the once per Round restriction.
