## 54.7a The Kolat — Master Selection at World Generation

The ten Kolat Masters are not pre-written characters. They are selected at world generation from the full pool of named NPCs produced by the world population pass (Section 52). Anyone can be a Master — a Clan Champion, a provincial lord, a magistrate, a sensei, a physician, a geisha master, or an obscure lord nobody would ever suspect. The only absolute exclusion is the Emperor himself.

This means no two game worlds will have the same Kolat. The conspiracy’s shape, its vulnerabilities, its reach, and its blind spots are all emergent properties of who happened to be generated and who happened to be selected.

### The Selection Process

Masters are selected one Sect at a time in a fixed processing order that also serves as conflict resolution priority. If the same NPC qualifies as a top candidate for multiple Sects, the Sect processed first claims them.

Every eligible NPC enters a single weighted pool. Tier determines weight but lower tiers are never excluded — they are simply less likely. A T1 candidate is never guaranteed to be picked. A T3 candidate can always surprise. Pool size matters: a Sect with only two T1 candidates and thirty T3 candidates will frequently land a T3.

| **Tier** | **Selection Weight** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal | Weight 5 — appears five times in the weighted draw pool |
| T2 — Acceptable | Weight 2 — appears twice |
| T3 — Last Resort | Weight 1 — appears once |

With a typical pool of 3 T1 candidates, 8 T2, and 20 T3, the actual odds are roughly 29% T1, 31% T2, and 38% T3. The prime candidate is never favored by more than 3:1 over anyone else. A crowded T3 pool can easily beat a sparse T1.

### Universal Filters

The following conditions cut the pool before any tier logic runs. An NPC that fails any of these cannot be considered for any Sect, regardless of their skills or status.

- Insight Rank 3 minimum. No junior samurai holds a Master seat regardless of skill.

- Not the Emperor. The one absolute exclusion in the system.

- Not already selected as another Master. One seat per NPC.

- Must meet Sect minimum skill thresholds. These are the floor for T3 eligibility. An NPC that does not meet the minimums is not in the pool at all.

### Processing Order and Conflict Resolution

Sects are processed in the following order. Earlier Sects have priority in conflict resolution. If the same NPC wins the weighted draw for multiple Sects, they are assigned to the first Sect processed and removed from all others.

| **Order** | **Sect** | **Role** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Tiger | First among equals. Processes first. |
| 2 | Chrysanthemum | Imperial City surveillance. |
| 3 | Silk | Intelligence gathering. |
| 4 | Coin | Economic warfare. |
| 5 | Cloud | Knowledge preservation. |
| 6 | Jade | Supernatural opposition. |
| 7 | Dream | Sleeper agent creation. |
| 8 | Lotus | Assassination. |
| 9 | Roc | Inactive. Seat exists; no unique mechanics at launch. |
| 10 | Steel | Hidden Temple guard. |

If a Sect’s weighted pool is entirely empty after universal filters are applied, that seat goes vacant at world generation. The Kolat opens the game with a missing Master for that Sect. This is rare but possible, particularly for Roc and Dream.

### Sect Profiles

Each Sect has a distinct candidate profile, tier criteria, skill boosts applied to whoever is selected, and a special rule that fires at world generation. The expected outcome column reflects typical results given the pool sizes at a standard world start.

#### Master Tiger — Internal Security

Master Tiger is first among equals. Identifies and eliminates traitors. Knows confirmed identities of at least one member from every other Sect. The Empire is full of magistrates and Scorpion courtiers — T1 is plentiful but so is T3, making the outcome unpredictable despite a strong pool.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Scorpion Clan Champion, any Scorpion Family Daimyo, Emerald Champion, Jade Champion |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Rank 4+ magistrate with Investigation 5+, Kuroiban operative, Seppun Hidden Guard Captain |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ courtier with Courtier (Manipulation) 4+ and Investigation 3+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Courtier (Manipulation) 3+, Investigation 3+

**Skill Boosts:** Courtier (Manipulation) 8, Investigation 7, Intimidation (Control) 7, Sincerity (Deceit) 7

**Special Rule:** At world generation, Tiger receives one confirmed NPC identity from every other Sect’s weighted pool. These are stored as hidden data links. If Tiger is Broken, this cross-knowledge becomes available to investigators.

#### Master Chrysanthemum — Imperial City

The most politically exposed Master. Operates inside Otosan Uchi itself, surrounded by Imperial Magistrates and the Seppun Hidden Guard. T1 is rare and extremely high-value. A Seppun Hidden Guard Commander winning this seat means the Kolat has a mole inside the organization designed to catch them. Expect T2 or T3 most games.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Otomo Family Daimyo, Seppun Family Daimyo, Miya Family Daimyo, Seppun Hidden Guard Commander, any Imperial Advisor |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Otomo, Seppun, or Miya courtier at Rank 4+, any Jeweled Champion, Imperial Herald at Rank 4+ |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ character with Status 5+, regular Imperial Court access, and Courtier 4+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Status 5+, Courtier 4+, Etiquette 3+

**Skill Boosts:** Courtier 8, Etiquette 8, Sincerity (Deceit) 7, Investigation (Notice) 6

**Special Rule:** Passively receives a defined category of topics within 1 IC day, regardless of physical location. No roll required. No detection risk. The information appears in their known_topics at the next engine firing. Topic collection scope: all Tier 3+ topics generated in Otosan Uchi or wherever the Imperial Court is currently convening (the subscription follows the institution, not the city — see below); all topics of any tier naming an Imperial family member (Otomo, Seppun, or Miya) or a holder of an Imperial appointment (Emerald Champion, Jade Champion, Imperial Herald, Imperial Advisor, Seppun Hidden Guard Commander); all succession topics anywhere in the Empire; all topics recording an Imperial policy commitment or decree; all topics recording a deployment or movement order issued by the Imperial Court; all topics recording a Jade Magistrate or Seppun Hidden Guard investigation targeting any named character. This is an intentionally broad scope. Chrysanthemum’s value to the conspiracy is total coverage of the Imperial seat — when in doubt, the subscription includes it. Institutional tracking: the subscription follows the Imperial Court as an institution, not a fixed location. When the Emperor travels or holds winter court at a provincial castle, the subscription relocates with him. The engine tracks “Imperial Court location” as a world-state field updated whenever the Emperor’s court formally convenes at a new settlement. Chrysanthemum’s passive subscription queries this field rather than a fixed settlement ID. Agents of the Chrysanthemum Sect stationed at the Imperial Court extend this collection. Each Chrysanthemum field agent with an active Kolat objective passively collects all topics they personally witness at court, then routes them to Master Chrysanthemum via DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE targeting the Master’s code name. Topics are flagged kolat_distributed after successful routing so they are not double-reported. Agent silence detection: Master Chrysanthemum maintains a network record in their special_data — a Dictionary[agent_code_name → agent_last_contact: int] storing the IC day of each registered Chrysanthemum field agent’s last successful DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE. At each Seasonal Tick the Kolat maintenance pass checks every entry. If the gap between current IC day and agent_last_contact exceeds the per-agent threshold (default 30 IC days for an agent in Otosan Uchi; 60 for an agent in a remote province), two things fire: a Tier 4 personal concern topic enters Master Chrysanthemum’s known_topics — “[Agent code name] has not reported in [X] days” — and LOCATE_CHARACTER fires at priority 2 targeting that agent’s NPC ID. The Master investigates before assuming compromise. Master Chrysanthemum can update individual thresholds via cipher directive. The network record is stored in special_data on Master Chrysanthemum’s character sheet and is also registered in Section 54.7h as a hidden field.

#### Master Silk — Intelligence

Runs the largest spy network in the Empire. Courtiers, geisha, and social manipulators are everywhere in Rokugan, making this the largest pool of all. T1 wins frequently by sheer volume, but the specific NPC selected is usually unexpected. Master Silk generates a pre-placed network of contacts at world start.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Shosuro Actor Rank 5, Doji Courtier Rank 5, Geisha Master (any clan), Kitsuki Investigator Rank 5 |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Rank 4+ courtier with Acting 4+ and Sincerity (Deceit) 4+ |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ character with Courtier 3+, Acting 3+, Awareness 4+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Acting 3+, Courtier 3+, Awareness 3+

**Skill Boosts:** Acting 8, Sincerity (Deceit) 8, Courtier (Manipulation) 7, Forgery 6

**Special Rule:** Generates 1d6+2 pre-placed sleeper agent contacts at world start, scattered across the Empire. These NPCs are not Broken — they are conscious agents. Their identities and locations are stored in the Silk Sect’s hidden network record.

#### Master Coin — Economic Warfare

Controls vast sums through manipulation of trade networks, the Yasuki, Daidoji, Yoritomo, and Ide trade families. Family Daimyos with mercantile connections are uncommon. T1 is extremely specific. A T3 Coin Master is often a surprisingly obscure provincial lord nobody would suspect. Expect T2 or T3 most games.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Yasuki Family Daimyo, Ide Family Daimyo, Daidoji trade administrator Rank 5 |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Family Daimyo with Commerce 4+, Yoritomo Courtier Rank 4+, Mantis merchant captain (lord-tier) |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ lord with Commerce 4+ and a province generating above-average Koku |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Commerce 4+, must be lord-tier (has at least one province)

**Skill Boosts:** Commerce (Appraisal + Mathematics) 8, Courtier 7, Sincerity (Deceit) 6, Forgery 6

**Special Rule:** Has hidden Koku reserves of 2d10 x 10 not reflected in any official record. These reserves are untraceable through normal Commerce investigation (Section 51) and do not appear in the province tax cascade. They fund Kolat operations without touching the Master’s visible wealth.

#### Master Cloud — Knowledge Preservation

The Kolat’s archive. Knows everything the organization has ever learned. Phoenix and Dragon produce the ideal candidates but are a small fraction of the NPC pool. The shugenja requirement further limits candidates. Often T2. A Dragon monk rather than a Phoenix elder makes for a subtle and interesting variation.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Phoenix Family Daimyo, Isawa Elder Rank 5, Agasha or Tamori Family Daimyo, Togashi Tattooed Monk Rank 5 |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Rank 5 shugenja with Spellcraft 4+ and three or more Lore skills at Rank 4+ |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ shugenja with Spellcraft 3+ and Calligraphy 3+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Spellcraft 3+, Calligraphy 3+, at least two distinct Lore skills Rank 3+, must be Shugenja school

**Skill Boosts:** Spellcraft 8, Lore: History 7, Calligraphy (Cipher) 7, Meditation 6, Lore: (Clan-specific) 7

**Special Rule:** Has the Kolat spell Master Cloud’s Eyes (Air 3) memorized and always available without a scroll. Also knows the location of the Hidden Temple’s archive chamber — a settlement-level reference stored in their special_data.

#### Master Jade — Supernatural Opposition

Opposes Jigoku, the Lying Darkness, and anything that would corrupt the mortal realm before humanity can claim it. Witch hunters and Phoenix Inquisitors are rare NPCs. A Kuni as Master Jade — hunting the Shadowlands while secretly serving the conspiracy that opposes divine rule — is exactly the emergent fiction this system is designed to produce.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Kuni Witch Hunter Rank 4+, Phoenix Inquisitor Rank 4+, Jade Champion |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Jade Magistrate Rank 3+, Asahina shugenja with Lore: Shadowlands 4+, Hiruma scout specialist Rank 4+ |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ shugenja with Investigation 4+ and Lore: Shadowlands 3+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Lore: Shadowlands 3+, Investigation 3+, must be Shugenja school or Witch Hunter path

**Skill Boosts:** Investigation 8, Lore: Shadowlands 7, Medicine 6, Spellcraft 6

**Special Rule:** Can anonymously feed intelligence to the Kuni Witch Hunters, Phoenix Inquisitors, and Jade Magistrates without exposing themselves. Investigation trails die at the institutional level. This is the mechanism by which the Kolat keeps Jigoku’s servants from corrupting the Empire they intend to inherit.

#### Master Dream — Sleeper Agents

The most disturbing Sect. Creates human weapons through sustained psychological torture and memory erasure. Sensei with institutional access to isolated people are genuinely uncommon. The T3 pool opens to anyone with the right skill combination — a Brotherhood abbot, a Crane physician, a remote dojo sensei. This Sect should never have an obvious face. Frequently T3 by design.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Head Sensei at a major Clan Dojo, Kuroiban operative with Medicine 4+, senior Brotherhood of Shinsei Abbot |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Rank 4+ character with Temptation 4+ and institutional access to isolated people, any Rank 4+ physician |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ character with Temptation 3+, Intimidation (Control) 3+, Medicine 2+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Temptation 3+, Intimidation (Control) 2+, Medicine 2+

**Skill Boosts:** Temptation 8, Intimidation (Control) 8, Medicine 7, Sincerity (Deceit) 6

**Special Rule:** Generates 1d6+2 active sleeper agents at world start. World-generation handling: at world generation, the engine rolls 1d6+2 to determine the starting sleeper count. Each sleeper is selected from the NPC pool using the same candidate criteria as the self-initiated scanner (Willpower 2 or below, no Kolat awareness, useful social position). Their trigger phrase and sleeper command are set by the world generator using a randomised but thematically appropriate command set — typically: eliminate a named high-Status NPC, deliver a cipher document to a specific location, or open a specific gate or door at a specific time. These commands are written into dream_sleeper_registry at world start with sleeper_status: “dormant” and conditioning_stability: 100.0. Master Dream knows all world-start sleepers from the first moment of play — they are part of the inheritance from the previous Dream Master. Their trigger phrases are stored only in this NPC’s hidden data. If this Master is killed before being Broken, all sleeper connections are permanently severed — the triggers die with them. These are the Sleeper Agent disadvantage holders described in Section 54.7.

#### Master Lotus — Assassination

Commands the deadliest killers in the Empire. True ninja-school graduates are rare. T2 expands to skilled bushi with stealth capabilities, which is more available. Lotus operatives never know the identity of their Master — by institutional design, the entire Sect communicates exclusively through dead drops.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Shosuro Infiltrator Rank 5, Shosuro Actor Rank 5, any Ninja Advanced School graduate |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Rank 4+ bushi with Stealth 4+ and weapon skill 5+, Daidoji Iron Warrior Rank 4+ with covert skills |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ ronin or bushi with Stealth 3+, weapon skill 4+, and Acting 2+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Stealth 3+, weapon skill 4+

**Skill Boosts:** Stealth (Sneaking) 8, primary weapon skill 8, Acting 7, Knives 6, Ninjutsu 5

**Special Rule:** Gains the Silent Advantage. Lotus operatives never know this Master’s identity. Communication flows exclusively through dead drops — settlement IDs stored in special_data — with NPCs in the Silk Sect acting as unwitting couriers. This Master coordinates targeting with Master Silk, never with their own operatives directly.

**Cross-reference: RESPOND_TO_SUPERNATURAL_THREAT standing objective.** Kuni Witch Hunters, Phoenix Inquisitors, and Jade Magistrates require a dedicated standing objective — RESPOND_TO_SUPERNATURAL_THREAT — that drives them to independently investigate and respond to Taint, maho, oni, and spirit-related topics in their jurisdiction. This standing objective is not part of the Kolat system; it is a base-game NPC archetype mandate. When this objective is designed for those NPC archetypes, it should: (1) fire on the same supernatural topic triggers that ASSESS_SUPERNATURAL_THREAT uses; (2) use the existing INVESTIGATE_THREAT decomposition (Section 8.3) as its skeleton with supernatural-specific branching for Lore: Shadowlands checks; (3) score ANONYMOUS_TIP receipts from registered jade_asset_network contacts as priority-raising events — an incoming tip increases the threat’s urgency in the objective queue by +20. Until this is formally designed, Jade Magistrate, Witch Hunter, and Inquisitor NPCs will not proactively respond to supernatural threats through their own engine — they will only respond when a tip or direct encounter forces it. This is a known design gap. CROSS-REF: Section 54.7c (ASSESS_SUPERNATURAL_THREAT), Section 54.7c (CULTIVATE_JADE_ASSET), Section 8.3 (INVESTIGATE_THREAT decomposition).

#### Master Roc — Inactive Seat

The Roc seat exists at world generation and a Master is selected following normal selection rules. The seat historically maintained the Kolat’s connection to its foreign counterpart but has no unique ActionIDs, NeedTypes, or engine pipeline at launch. The Master operates as a standard Kolat agent using only the shared Kolat ActionID pool. Unicorn NPCs with gaijin connections are the primary candidate pool. There may be zero T1 candidates in some world generations; the seat frequently lands at T3.

#### Master Steel — Hidden Temple Guard

Commands the ronin army that protects the Hidden Temple. A pure warrior in an organization of shadows. Famous ronin exist in the NPC pool at world start. The T3 pool is any competent bushi willing to appear as or be a ronin — an exiled Clan Champion’s disgraced yojimbo, a former Lion officer cast out after a failed campaign. If a lord-tier NPC wins this seat, they require a ronin cover identity.

| **Tier** | **Candidate Pool** |
| --- | --- |
| T1 — Ideal (Weight 5) | Named ronin with Glory Rank 5+, former yojimbo of a Clan Champion now masterless, Wolf Legion veteran |
| T2 — Acceptable (Weight 2) | Any Rank 4+ bushi willing to appear as or become ronin, any Rank 4+ ronin with Battle 4+ and Leadership Advantage |
| T3 — Last Resort (Weight 1) | Any Rank 3+ bushi or ronin with Battle 3+ and weapon skill 5+ |

**Minimums:** Insight Rank 3+, Battle 3+, weapon skill 4+. Strongly preferred to be ronin or able to appear as one.

**Skill Boosts:** Battle 8, primary weapon skill 8, Defense 7, Intimidation (Bullying) 6

**Special Rule:** Gains the Guardian of the Hidden Temple technique (Section 54.7). If a lord-tier NPC wins this seat, a hidden second identity is generated — a ronin persona with a separate name and history. This cover identity is tracked separately from the Master’s public character record. If the cover is blown, the public identity is not automatically implicated.

The following table summarizes the typical outcome of the weighted selection across all ten Sects at a standard world start. Variation is intentional. No two game worlds should feel like they have the same Kolat.

| **Sect** | **T1 Pool** | **Expected Outcome** | **Design Intent** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tiger | Large | Even spread | The enforcer is often unexpected |
| Chrysanthemum | Small | Often T2 or T3 | Imperial moles are rare and shocking |
| Silk | Very Large | Often T1, but varied individual | The spymaster is rarely the obvious one |
| Coin | Small | Often T2 or T3 | The banker is sometimes an obscure provincial lord |

### What Every Master Receives

Regardless of Sect, every selected Master receives the following additions to their character sheet. These are applied on top of their normally generated sheet and the Sect-specific skill boosts.

- is_kolat_master flagged true. Hidden from all public character data.

- kolat_superior_id: int or null. Hidden from all public character data. The Kolat's parallel operational hierarchy. Points to the character ID of this agent's Kolat superior --- for a Sect operative, this is their Sect Master; for a Sect Master, this is Master Tiger (or null if they report directly to the Conclave). Functions identically to operational_superior_id (Section 11.3.18) but is invisible to any non-Kolat system. SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE uses this field to route orders. A character may have both a standard operational_superior_id (their public military or legal assignment) and a kolat_superior_id (their secret Kolat chain) simultaneously --- the two hierarchies coexist without conflict because the Kolat hierarchy operates covertly through separate communication channels (dead drops, Tears, cipher letters) rather than through ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE.

- is_unique flagged true. This NPC will never be replaced by a generic generated character if they die — their death is a permanent event.

- Dark Secret (Kolat) Disadvantage added.

- Forbidden Knowledge (Kolat) Advantage added.

- Spy Network Advantage added at the Kolat-discounted cost of 5 points.

- Perceived Honor (4 points) Advantage added. Their public face is immaculate.

- Skill boosts applied. Sect-specific skills are raised to the values listed above. The max() rule applies — a skill is never lowered, only raised. A character who was already Investigation 8 when selected as Master Jade stays at 8, not 7.

### Integration with the NPC Objective System

Every Master NPC runs the standard NPC Decision Engine (Section 55) for their public role. A Yasuki Family Daimyo who is Master Coin still pursues economic objectives, attends courts, manages their provinces, and behaves as a Yasuki lord should. Their Kolat role does not replace their public behavior — it runs in parallel.

When a Master is elevated, their standing objective is replaced by the Kolat mandate for their Sect. The Master no longer has a public standing objective of their own — the Kolat mandate is their standing objective, full stop. Their public behaviour as a Yasuki lord, a Kuni Witch Hunter, or an Imperial courtier continues to emerge from that mandate naturally: a Master Coin whose standing objective is “ensure the Kolat always has sufficient untraceable operational funds” will still pursue economic strength, maintain their provinces, and attend courts — because those actions serve the mandate. The tension investigators can detect arises when the mandate and the lord-assigned primary objective pull in different directions: the magistrate who inexplicably drops an investigation, the merchant who funds a politically inconvenient expedition, the abbot who never seems to be in his cell at night.

The full design of the Kolat objective layer — including standing objectives per Sect, the standing objective replacement mechanic, how Masters communicate, and the mechanics by which players can expose and Break a Master — is detailed in Section 54.7b.
