## 54.7c The Kolat — New NeedTypes and ActionIDs

The ten Kolat Sects map mostly onto existing NeedTypes and ActionIDs in the NPC Decision Engine (Section 55). Five Sects require new NeedTypes and sixteen require new ActionIDs to cover operational actions the standard engine does not model. All new entries follow the same schema as the existing system (Section 55.3). New NeedTypes are added to the NeedType enum in enums.gd, the same file as all other NeedTypes. New ActionIDs are added to the ActionID enum and their scoring data added to the objective alignment annex.

### New NeedTypes

**SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE.** “I need to transmit an instruction, assessment, or warning to another part of the organisation.” Fires when Tiger needs to assign a primary objective to another Sect, when Tiger needs to allocate operational_koku from temple_vault_koku to a Master’s operational budget (decrements vault, credits receiving Master’s operational_koku), when Cloud needs to route intelligence to a specific Master, or when any Master needs to flag a threat upward to Tiger. Routes through the Tears rather than the physical courier network. Tears operate independently once created — the Eye is not involved in transmission. ImmediateNeed fields: target_npc_id (receiving Master by code name only — Tiger knows the real NPC ID; others do not), target_clan_id (used here to carry the destination Sect identifier, e.g. “kolat_silk”, “kolat_tiger”), priority 1–3. Objective alignment: TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR 100, SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT 60, RUN_COURIER_ROUTE 75, DO_NOTHING 0.

**Targeted surveillance trigger.**Tiger directive override: incoming Tiger directives replace existing Kolat objectives immediately — see universal rules (Section 54.7d).

**MAINTAIN_KOLAT_NETWORK.** “The physical infrastructure of the Kolat — courier routes, dead drop locations, safe houses, contact chains — needs to be kept operational.” Fires for Master Silk as a persistent standing need. The network degrades if not actively maintained. ImmediateNeed fields: target_settlement_id (the specific network node requiring attention), target_npc_id (a courier or contact needing servicing), threshold (network integrity score; fires at priority 2 below 80%, priority 3 below 60%), threshold_type = “network_integrity”. Objective alignment: RUN_COURIER_ROUTE 95, ESTABLISH_DEAD_DROP 90, DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE 85, ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION 60, PROBE 50, WRITE_LETTER 30, DO_NOTHING 0. Vault monitoring: Tiger does not directly read temple_vault_koku between Conclaves — the vault is a physical chest at the Hidden Temple and Tiger is not always there. Between Conclaves, Tiger knows the vault balance only from Master Coin’s seasonal reports (received via Silk cipher letter). When Coin’s report indicates the vault is below the minimum threshold (default 50 koku), Tiger fires SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Master Coin at priority 3 requesting a deposit. At the annual Conclave, Tiger reads temple_vault_koku in person — the only time Tiger sees the physical vault directly. Tiger is the only Master with read access to the vault balance.

**MANAGE_KOLAT_FUNDS.** “The Kolat requires untraceable operational Koku. I need to generate it, clean it, move it, or assess whether current reserves are sufficient.” Fires for Master Coin when kolat_koku (local working reserve) falls below the laundering threshold, when Tiger sends SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE requesting a vault deposit (Tiger knows from Coin’s seasonal report — Coin has write-only vault access and cannot read the balance independently), or when a new laundering opportunity arises. ImmediateNeed fields: target_resource = “kolat_koku”, threshold (minimum reserve level set seasonally by Tiger directive), threshold_type = “kolat_reserve”, target_settlement_id (destination for a requested transfer). Objective alignment: UNDERREPORT_KOKU 100, LAUNDER_KOKU 95, TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS 85, PURCHASE_MARKET 40, ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS 30, DO_NOTHING 0.

**CONDITION_SLEEPER.** “I have identified a candidate for sleeper conditioning. I need to isolate them, conduct the conditioning process, install a trigger, and return them to normal life without evidence of what happened.” A multi-step NeedType persisting until conditioning is complete or abandoned. Each step is a separate action. Full conditioning requires sessions equal to the target’s Willpower × 3, conducted over consecutive or near-consecutive IC days. ImmediateNeed fields: target_npc_id (the candidate, previously assessed via PROBE or READ_CHARACTER), target_settlement_id (the isolation facility — must be confirmed accessible before CONDUCT_CONDITIONING fires), trigger_phrase (string — set by the Dream Master when the NeedType is created; this is the phrase that will activate the sleeper; it must be determined before conditioning begins and cannot be changed without re-conditioning), sleeper_command (ImmediateNeed — the structured command the sleeper will execute on activation; fully resolved at conditioning time using the Dream Master’s current IntelDB; stored as a NeedType plus target fields), threshold (conditioning progress 0–100%), threshold_type = “conditioning_progress”, priority = 2 normally, 3 if Tiger has specifically requested this candidate. Decomposition prerequisite: before generating CONDUCT_CONDITIONING as an ImmediateNeed, the decomposition function must verify that an isolated facility is accessible within reach — a location where the target can be held without witnesses for the required number of sessions. If no such facility exists or is reachable, the ImmediateNeed falls back to BEGIN_TRAVEL (targeting a settlement with a suitable facility) or ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION (to locate a suitable contact who can provide one). CONDUCT_CONDITIONING is never generated against a target who cannot currently be isolated. Completion handler: when conditioning_progress reaches 100.0 on an AP firing, the decomposition function executes a completion sequence before returning: (1) installs the four hidden sleeper fields on target_npc_id’s character sheet — trigger_phrase (from ImmediateNeed), sleeper_command (from ImmediateNeed), conditioning_stability = 100.0, active_sleeper_command = null; (2) marks CONDITION_SLEEPER as complete and removes it from the Dream Master’s active NeedTypes; (3) generates a new MAINTAIN_SLEEPER ImmediateNeed targeting the same npc_id, firing at priority 1 on the next seasonal tick. The target is released from isolation with no memory of the conditioning. From the world’s perspective they simply reappear in their normal life. Objective alignment: CONDUCT_CONDITIONING 100, PROBE 50, READ_CHARACTER 45, BRIBE_FOR_INFO 40, EAVESDROP 35, DO_NOTHING 0. Note: BEGIN_TRAVEL and ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION appear in the decomposition prerequisite fallback path as replacement ImmediateNeeds, not as scored options. They are not in this alignment table because they are never generated alongside CONDUCT_CONDITIONING — they replace it when the prerequisite fails. Scanner trigger: CONDITION_SLEEPER fires under two conditions. Condition A — Tiger directive: Tiger sends SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Master Dream naming a specific candidate NPC and requesting conditioning. This is the primary trigger — Tiger identifies who needs to be conditioned based on strategic need. Priority = 3 when Tiger-directed. Condition B — network capacity: if dream_sleeper_registry contains fewer than 3 active sleepers with conditioning_stability above 50 and no Tiger directive is pending, Master Dream self-initiates a recruitment scan. The scanner evaluates all NPCs in Master Dream’s known_contacts with Willpower 2 or below (low Willpower = easier to condition), no Kolat awareness (must never have encountered Kolat evidence), and a social position that makes them useful as a sleeper (access to a high-Status character, presence at court, proximity to a military commander). The highest-scoring candidate generates a CONDITION_SLEEPER NeedType at priority 2. Master Dream assesses the candidate via PROBE and READ_CHARACTER before beginning isolation. Priority = 2 when self-initiated.

**MAINTAIN_SLEEPER.** “An existing sleeper requires ongoing contact to keep their conditioning stable, verify the trigger is intact, and confirm they have not been exposed or compromised.” Sleeper conditioning degrades without maintenance. A sleeper uncontacted too long may develop unreliable triggers or observable psychological symptoms. ImmediateNeed fields: target_npc_id (the specific sleeper), threshold (IC days since last contact; fires at priority 1 when overdue one season, priority 2 when overdue two seasons, priority 3 when the sleeper shows behavioural anomalies), threshold_type = “sleeper_contact_overdue”. Relationship to conditioning_stability: sleeper_contact_overdue and conditioning_stability are two separate metrics tracking the same underlying concern from different angles. sleeper_contact_overdue counts IC days since the last MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT action against this target. conditioning_stability is a float 0–100 that degrades passively at a rate of 5 points per IC season without contact. The MAINTAIN_SLEEPER NeedType fires when sleeper_contact_overdue exceeds the season threshold. When MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT executes successfully, it resets sleeper_contact_overdue to 0 AND restores conditioning_stability by 10 points (capped at 100.0). The two metrics are always maintained together by the same action — a coder should never update one without updating the other. MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT is the only action that services both. Objective alignment: MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT 100, PROBE 50, EAVESDROP 35, BEGIN_TRAVEL 70, DO_NOTHING 0. Note: BEGIN_TRAVEL fires when the sleeper’s location is stale or unknown; the decomposition generates LOCATE_CHARACTER at priority 2 before attempting MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT. Tiger-initiated activation pipeline: when Tiger wants a sleeper activated, Tiger sends SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Master Dream containing: target_sleeper_id (the sleeper_id from dream_sleeper_registry), activation_urgency (priority 1–3), and optionally a revised sleeper_command if Tiger wants the command updated before activation. On receipt, Master Dream checks dream_sleeper_registry: if conditioning_stability is above 50 and sleeper_status is “dormant”, ACTIVATE_SLEEPER fires at the directed priority. If conditioning_stability is below 50, Master Dream sends a return directive to Tiger flagging the risk and requesting guidance — Tiger decides whether to proceed anyway or delay for re-conditioning. If the sleeper’s sleeper_status is “post_activation”, Master Dream notifies Tiger and awaits a re-conditioning or retirement decision before proceeding.

**LOCATE_CHARACTER.** Added to enums.gd: NeedType.LOCATE_CHARACTER. “I need to find where a specific character currently is.” A general-purpose NeedType that fires whenever another decomposition function determines that its primary objective is meaningfully blocked by not knowing a target NPC’s location. It is never generated in isolation — always triggered from within another decomposition function that has hit an unknown-location wall. The triggering decomposition assigns the priority based on how essential the target’s physical presence is to the parent objective.

Priority assignment by the triggering decomposition: Priority 3 — the parent objective is completely blocked without the target’s physical presence (assassination, duel challenge, personal delivery, conditioning). Priority 2 — the parent objective would be significantly advanced by finding them but has partial alternatives (building disposition with a specific target, extracting intelligence from a specific person). Priority 1 — finding them is desirable but solid alternatives exist; LOCATE_CHARACTER competes against those alternatives and will often lose. If the triggering decomposition determines the parent objective can be meaningfully advanced without the target at all, LOCATE_CHARACTER is not generated.

ImmediateNeed fields: target_npc_id (the character to find), threshold (not used — this NeedType has no progress metric; it resolves the moment a Fresh or Recent location enters IntelDB for the target), priority (assigned by triggering decomposition as above).

Decomposition function: the LOCATE_CHARACTER decomposition walks available methods in order of escalating commitment, generating action candidates that compete normally in Phase 5. It does not pick one path — it generates all viable candidates and the engine scores them. (1) Ask present characters — if any characters are currently co-located and might plausibly know the target’s movements, generate PROBE and LISTEN_REFLECT targeting them. Low cost, no travel required. (2) Write and inquire — generate WRITE_LETTER to known contacts in cities the target frequents. Cheap but subject to letter delivery delay (Section 12.7). (3) Social graph inference — walk the target’s known relationships in order: lord first (a loyal samurai is most likely near their Daimyo), spouse second, home province third. For each anchor whose location is Fresh or Recent, generate BEGIN_TRAVEL with target_settlement_id set to that anchor’s settlement. A Recent anchor applies ObjAlign −10 at scoring time per the staleness rules in Section 55.12. (4) Network inquiry — for Kolat Masters, generate RUN_COURIER_ROUTE to push a sighting request through the Silk network. For non-Kolat characters, generate WRITE_LETTER to the widest available contact network. (5) Eye observation — for Kolat Masters physically present at the Hidden Temple with no AP spent, generate OBSERVE_VIA_EYE targeting the best available anchor settlement from the social graph walk. (6) Report back to lord — if no viable candidates exist at priority 2+, generate WRITE_LETTER or ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE flagging the target as currently unfindable and requesting updated orders or intelligence from the lord. A vassal who has exhausted all paths without finding the target does not sit idle — they surface the problem.

Objective alignment: PROBE 80, LISTEN_REFLECT 70, OBSERVE_VIA_EYE 75, BEGIN_TRAVEL 85, WRITE_LETTER 60, RUN_COURIER_ROUTE 70, ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE 55, DO_NOTHING 0.

Resolution: LOCATE_CHARACTER resolves automatically the moment a Fresh or Recent location entry for the target enters the character’s IntelDB through any channel — a letter, a daily conversation, a successful PROBE, an OBSERVE_VIA_EYE observation, or a Silk network report. The triggering decomposition then re-fires with the new location data and generates its normal ImmediateNeed. LOCATE_CHARACTER does not linger once its condition is met.

Triggering decompositions: the following existing decomposition functions must add a location check and generate LOCATE_CHARACTER at the appropriate priority when IntelDB.get_known_location() returns null or STALE for the target NPC. ELIMINATE_CHARACTER (priority 3 — an assassination requires the target to be found); CHALLENGE_TO_DUEL (priority 3 — a duel requires physical presence); CONDITION_SLEEPER (priority 3 — conditioning requires the candidate to be reachable and isolatable); RAISE_DISPOSITION when the ImmediateNeed specifies a particular target NPC (priority 2 — other contacts may be cultivated in the meantime); GATHER_INTELLIGENCE when the target is a specific named NPC rather than a general information-gathering action (priority 2); MENTOR_CHARACTER (priority 2 — mentoring requires co-location but alternatives exist); MAINTAIN_SLEEPER (priority 2 — contact requires physical presence with the sleeper, but the degradation timeline is not immediately critical). Any future decomposition function that requires co-location with a specific NPC should follow the same pattern: check IntelDB.get_known_location() first, generate LOCATE_CHARACTER if the location is absent or stale, and assign the priority appropriate to how blocking the absence is.

Personality effects: the personality lean values on the generated ActionIDs naturally differentiate behavior. A Ketsui character travels to the most likely anchor immediately. A Chishiki character probes present contacts and writes letters before committing to travel. A Dosatsu character assesses all known information first. A Chugi character who runs out of leads reports back to their lord rather than improvising. A Yu character travels toward the most probable location without hesitation. The engine produces all of these behaviors from the same decomposition function through the existing Phase 5 scoring — no special cases required.

Generality note: LOCATE_CHARACTER is not Kolat-specific. Any named character — courtier, bushi, shugenja, magistrate — can have this NeedType generated when their parent objective requires a target whose location is unknown. The Eye and RUN_COURIER_ROUTE branches simply do not generate for non-Kolat characters because those ActionIDs are not in their available option pool for their ContextFlag.

### New ActionIDs

Each entry lists: AP cost, availability context, skill mapping, and any special rules. Entries follow the format established in Section 55.3.

**TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR.** 1 AP. Any context. Requires: NPC holds a Tear. The Master transmits a directive through their Tear to the target’s Tear. Tears operate independently once created by the Eye — they do not require the Eye to be actively operated during transmission. The Eye was needed to create the Tears; it is not needed for them to function. The transmission takes no observable time from the outside. The Master briefly loses focus — a moment indistinguishable from distraction. The directive arrives as a felt impression: a direction, a name, an urgency level. Complex instructions cannot be transmitted; the Tear carries intent, not paragraphs. Skill mapping: primary Calligraphy (Cipher), secondary Awareness. Tiger’s TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR carries routing authority — Tiger’s transmissions can be addressed to any Master by code name and the Eye’s keeper routes it to the correct Tear. Other Masters can only transmit to Tiger or to the Temple directly. Detection: Investigation (Notice) vs TN 35 to notice the brief absence of focus. By itself meaningless; combined with other signals it becomes significant.

**OBSERVE_VIA_EYE.** All AP for the IC day. VISITING (at the Hidden Temple settlement) only. Requires: physical presence at the Eye. Cannot be used if any AP has already been spent that IC day — the Eye must be the first and only action taken. The Master selects a target location and dedicates the rest of the day to observation. No other actions are possible once the Eye is activated. For the remainder of the IC day, the observer receives every topic that fires at the target location at full fidelity — same tier, same momentum, same confidence as if the observer were physically present. This is Direct Observation (Section 55.12) achieved remotely. Topics enter the observer’s known_topics immediately as they fire, tagged eye_sourced: true. No roll, no skill check, no chance of misreading — the observer sees. If an observed event is itself topic-generating — an army mobilising, a public declaration, a daimyo disgracing a retainer — the topic is created by that event, not by the Eye. The Eye is the mechanism of presence. A private meeting that would not generate a topic for a physically-present observer also generates none through the Eye. The Eye cannot observe through scrying wards, cannot observe the Shadowlands reliably, and cannot observe inside the Hidden Temple itself. Detection risk: none. The observation is undetectable by the subject. No skill mapping — this action requires no roll.

Contention rule: only one Master may use the Eye on a given IC day. If multiple Masters have OBSERVE_VIA_EYE as their highest-scoring action on the same day, access resolves as follows. First: highest ImmediateNeed priority wins (priority 3 before 2 before 1). Second: if two Masters tie on priority, Tiger wins regardless of all other factors. Third: remaining ties resolve by public Status Rank, descending. The losing Master’s OBSERVE_VIA_EYE is removed from their option pool for that day only — the engine re-scores their remaining actions and selects the next best. The Eye’s keeper (the Cloud archivist present at the Temple) enforces the schedule and notifies displaced Masters through the Tear network. Implementation note: this check runs in context_visiting.gd’s option generator for the Hidden Temple settlement. Before adding OBSERVE_VIA_EYE to the option pool, the generator queries whether any other Master has already used the Eye during this IC day (tracked via the shared action log). If yes, OBSERVE_VIA_EYE is not added for that AP firing.

Executor failure state: at the end of the IC day, the executor checks whether each NPC in the observer’s IntelDB who had a last_known_location entry pointing to the observed settlement was actually confirmed present (i.e. generated at least one topic at that location during the observation window). Any NPC not confirmed present has their location entry downgraded to confidence: STALE.

Location inference fallback: when a target NPC’s location is STALE or unknown, the GATHER_INTELLIGENCE decomposition function generates LOCATE_CHARACTER as the ImmediateNeed (Section 54.7c — New NeedTypes) rather than continuing to score OBSERVE_VIA_EYE against an invalid target. LOCATE_CHARACTER’s own decomposition then walks the social graph and may generate OBSERVE_VIA_EYE on an inferred anchor settlement if the Master is already at the Temple. The full location inference logic is defined in the LOCATE_CHARACTER NeedType above.

**SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT.** 1 AP. VISITING (at the Hidden Temple) for a full report; any context for a condensed Tear transmission. Intelligence is formally submitted to Cloud’s archive. A full report requires physical presence. A condensed report transmitted via Tear carries only the most critical summary. Intelligence submitted enters the Cloud archive and becomes available to Tiger for routing to other Masters — the mechanism by which intelligence from one Sect reaches another. A report containing information about a compromised Master or active investigation automatically triggers Tiger assessment on the next engine firing. Skill mapping: primary Calligraphy (Cipher), secondary Lore: Kolat.

**RUN_COURIER_ROUTE.** 1 AP. TRAVELING, AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, or VISITING (any settlement — dispatching only). The Master travels a segment of the Silk network’s courier route — delivering coded messages, collecting drops, verifying locations are still clean, confirming couriers are operational — or dispatches a trusted agent to run the route instead. Running it personally is faster and more reliable but creates observable movement patterns. Dispatching costs hidden resources but leaves no personal trace. Skill mapping: primary Stealth (Shadowing), secondary Investigation (Notice). Failure does not lose the message — it flags route_integrity_reduced for that segment, elevating MAINTAIN_KOLAT_NETWORK priority until the segment is repaired or abandoned. Used by: Silk.

**DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE.** 1 AP. VISITING (at the Hidden Temple) or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS via network dispatch. Master Silk routes intelligence to agents who need it — updating field agents on changed circumstances, alerting specific contacts to new risks, forwarding Cloud archive entries to operatives with relevant assignments. Agents without recent intelligence updates make outdated assumptions. Skill mapping: primary Courtier, secondary Calligraphy (Cipher). Intelligence distributed to an agent on a compromised route has a chance of interception: Stealth vs the investigator’s Investigation to determine whether interception occurs.

**ESTABLISH_DEAD_DROP.** 1 AP. Any settlement context. The Master identifies and sets up a new dead drop location — a physical site where messages, Koku, or materials can be left and retrieved without the parties meeting. A good dead drop is in a location both parties have legitimate reason to visit, has natural concealment, and is not observable from any vantage an investigator would naturally use. A dead drop is assigned a concealment rating (1–5) at establishment. Each visit after the third in a single IC season reduces concealment by 1. A drop reaching concealment 0 is assumed compromised and abandoned immediately. Skill mapping: primary Investigation (Notice), secondary Stealth (Sneaking).

**UNDERREPORT_KOKU.** 1 AP. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS only. Lord-tier only. Master Coin adjusts domain financial reporting — reducing stated Koku output before it passes up the tax cascade. The difference accumulates in dirty_koku on Master Coin’s character sheet (Section 54.7h). It is not yet spendable — it must be laundered through LAUNDER_KOKU before entering kolat_koku. This is not a single dramatic fraud but dozens of small adjustments across dozens of line items, each individually explainable as rounding or estimation error. Skill mapping: primary Commerce (Mathematics), secondary Forgery. Roll: Commerce (Mathematics) + Intelligence vs TN 20 baseline, increasing by 5 for each consecutive season of underreporting and by 5 if the domain has been investigated this season. Honor cost: −0.3 per use, automatic, not public unless the fraud is discovered. Detection: a character with Commerce (Mathematics) 4+ examining the records rolls Commerce (Mathematics) + Intelligence vs TN equal to the original forger’s roll result; success reveals a discrepancy, each Raise reveals one additional fraudulent line item.

**LAUNDER_KOKU.** 1 AP. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or VISITING (requires a commercial environment). Master Coin moves extracted Koku through legitimate economic activity — purchasing goods through intermediaries and selling at slight loss, receiving clean Koku in return. Any legitimate transaction can serve as a vehicle if structured correctly. Conversion rate: each AP spent laundering converts up to 5 Koku from hidden reserves into clean funds. Clean funds can be transferred via TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS or held locally. Unlaundered funds cannot be used for any action requiring explanation of their source. Skill mapping: primary Commerce (Appraisal), secondary Sincerity (Deceit).

**TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS.** 1 AP. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or VISITING (at the Hidden Temple). Master Coin moves clean Koku from kolat_koku into temple_vault_koku (the Hidden Temple world-state field, Section 54.7h). Deposit direction only — Master Coin funds the vault. Tiger allocates from the vault to Sects via SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE, which decrements temple_vault_koku and credits the receiving Master’s operational_koku. Physical movement through Silk couriers or personal delivery at seasonal meetings. Transfers above 50 Koku generate a detectable movement of resources — a caravan, a chest transported under guard — existing physically in the world. Characters along the route with Investigation 3+ may notice unusual cargo movement. Below 50 Koku the funds move indistinguishably from normal commercial activity. Skill mapping: primary Commerce (Mathematics), secondary Stealth.

**ANONYMOUS_TIP.** 1 AP. Any context. Master Jade feeds intelligence to an institutional anti-Shadowlands organisation — Kuni Witch Hunters, Phoenix Inquisitors, or Jade Magistrates — without identifying themselves as the source. The tip arrives as an unsigned letter, a planted rumour, or evidence left where the right investigator will find it. Generates a Tier 4 topic at the target organisation: “[Organisation] has received intelligence on [subject].” The topic does not identify the source. Tracing the tip’s origin: Investigation vs TN 30 per step; success reveals only the delivery method, not Master Jade directly. Skill mapping: primary Calligraphy (Cipher), secondary Stealth.

**CONDUCT_CONDITIONING.** 1 AP per session. Multiple sessions required. VISITING or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, requires an isolated location with privacy. One session of the conditioning process applied to the target. The subject must be physically present and isolated — not of their own free will. A full conditioning requires Willpower × 3 sessions conducted over consecutive or near-consecutive IC days. Interrupting the process before completion leaves the subject psychologically damaged but without a functional trigger, and potentially able to remember fragments. Roll per session: contested Willpower (target) vs Temptation + Intelligence (Dream). Three consecutive target successes mean the conditioning fails for this session — the Master must wait before attempting again. Three consecutive Master successes mean full progress for the session. Skill mapping: primary Temptation, secondary Intimidation (Control) and Medicine. Honor cost: −3.0 applied once, on the AP firing where conditioning_progress reaches 100.0 and the completion handler runs. No Honor cost is applied for individual sessions that do not complete the full conditioning. This is the highest Honor cost of any single action in the engine, exceeding RAID_HARVEST (−2.0). Applied automatically regardless of rationalisation, ideological conviction, or the Master’s standing objectives. The game makes no exception for Kolat true believers. Detection: the subject has no memory of the conditioning but may have been absent from normal life during sessions — a gap investigators can find. Longer conditioning creates larger gaps.

**MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT.** 1 AP. Any context where the sleeper is physically present. Physical presence is mandatory — this action cannot be performed by letter. Conditioning stability is assessed through direct behavioural observation, which is only possible in person. Master Dream makes discreet contact with an existing sleeper to assess conditioning stability, refresh key elements of the installed behaviour pattern, and verify the trigger is intact. The sleeper does not know this contact is anything other than a normal social interaction. A sleeper whose conditioning is degrading will show subtle behavioural anomalies during contact — unusual responses, unexpected emotional reactions, partial memories surfacing. If the Master cannot reach the sleeper in person, the sleeper goes unmaintained and their sleeper_contact_overdue threshold continues to increment. Roll: Medicine + Perception vs TN 20 to detect degradation. If degradation is detected, the Master must choose between emergency re-conditioning (CONDUCT_CONDITIONING, requires isolation, carries all its risks) or deactivating the sleeper. Skill mapping: primary Acting, secondary Medicine.

**ACTIVATE_SLEEPER.** 1 AP. Any context where the sleeper can hear the trigger phrase. Master Dream speaks the sleeper’s trigger phrase in the subject’s presence, embedded in normal conversation. The sleeper’s conditioning activates. They carry out the attached command regardless of their normal personality, values, or awareness of consequences. They will have no memory of receiving or following the instruction. The command is limited to five words or fewer — the same constraint as the Sleeper Agent disadvantage (Section 54.7). Complex operations cannot be commanded directly. Skill mapping: primary Acting, secondary Sincerity. Detection: an observer who notices the phrase and recognises it as a trigger requires both knowing that sleeper triggers exist as a mechanic and rolling Investigation (Notice) vs TN 40 in the moment. Practically undetectable. Investigators may work backward from bizarre subsequent sleeper behaviour, but this takes IC weeks minimum. Post-activation handling: once the sleeper completes their command (active_sleeper_command resolves or expires), the sleeper’s hidden fields reset: active_sleeper_command returns to null, the sleeper’s normal goal resolution loop resumes. The sleeper has no memory of executing the command. Their sleeper_status in dream_sleeper_registry updates to “post_activation”. Master Dream is notified via the registry change on the next MAINTAIN_SLEEPER check. Re-use: a sleeper can be activated again only if conditioning_stability is above 50 at time of re-activation. A second activation on a sleeper with low stability risks a failed command or partial memory recovery — the sleeper may retain fragments of both commands, producing erratic behaviour. Re-conditioning (full CONDUCT_CONDITIONING sequence) resets stability and installs a new command, replacing the old one. The trigger phrase may be changed during re-conditioning. A sleeper should not be activated more than twice without re-conditioning — cumulative psychological damage becomes visible to close observers. Tiger is responsible for deciding whether a post-activation sleeper is re-conditioned, held in reserve, or quietly retired. Execution model: activation is not instant resolution. When ACTIVATE_SLEEPER fires and active_sleeper_command is populated, the sleeper’s engine does not immediately resolve the command. Instead, on every subsequent AP, the sleeper’s goal resolution loop runs with active_sleeper_command overriding all standing objectives and personal priorities at maximum weight. The sleeper pursues the command through the normal action system — BEGIN_TRAVEL to reach a target, RAISE_DISPOSITION to gain access, GATHER_INTELLIGENCE to find an opportunity, or whatever sequence of standard actions the command requires. No special fast-path exists. The sleeper is functionally a different character: single-minded, overriding all other concerns, unable to be distracted by social obligations, personal fears, or competing objectives. They will not stop for meals, rest, or relationships unless those activities directly serve the command. Their sleeper_status in dream_sleeper_registry updates to “executing” immediately on activation and remains there until the command resolves. Resolution conditions: the command resolves when (a) the target action is completed (target NPC is dead, gate is opened, disclosure is delivered), (b) the sleeper dies, or (c) conditioning_stability drops to zero mid-execution, at which point the sleeper snaps out of the command state with partial memories and severe psychological distress. A high-Willpower target may defend against an ELIMINATE attempt normally — the sleeper is not supernaturally powerful, only supernaturally focused. Failure is possible. The command may take multiple IC seasons to resolve if the target is well-guarded, has left the region, or if circumstances make the command difficult to execute.

**SECURE_ONI_EYE.** 1 AP. VISITING (at the Hidden Temple) only. Master Steel conducts a security assessment of the Eye’s physical housing — verifying access controls are intact, no unauthorised person has been near it, the crystal shows no signs of damage or tampering, and the Hidden Guard rotation covering the Eye’s chamber is current and trusted. Roll: Investigation (Notice) + Perception vs TN 20. On a successful roll the Eye is confirmed secure for this season. On a failed roll the assessment is inconclusive — Master Steel cannot confirm or deny that the Eye is uncompromised. Two consecutive inconclusive assessments automatically trigger SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT to Tiger on the next AP, flagging a security review request. If a player faction has planted a surveillance device near the Eye without being caught, a failed roll leaves them undetected for another season. Success at TN 25+ detects the presence of tampering. Success at TN 35+ identifies how long it has been in place. There is no separate eye_security_unknown data flag — the engine tracks this through the action log (two consecutive failed SECURE_ONI_EYE entries in the same season trigger the reporting behaviour). Skill mapping: primary Investigation (Notice), secondary Battle.

**CONDUCT_PERIMETER_PATROL.** 1 AP per patrol leg. Available to Steel Sect scouts (kolat_sect = “kolat_steel”, role = “scout”) operating in provinces within 3 provinces of the Hidden Temple settlement. The scout travels their assigned route under mundane cover (hunter, travelling monk, minor merchant) and observes: strangers asking unusual questions, unusual military movements, magistrate activity, unfamiliar caravans. At the end of the patrol, the scout sends a report letter back to Master Steel. The letter is tagged source: steel_scout_report in Master Steel’s IntelDB on receipt. Report content: any NPCs encountered who are not in the scout’s known_contacts, any topics active in the patrolled province, garrison and magistrate movements observed. Roll: Stealth (Sneaking) vs TN 15 to avoid drawing attention during the patrol. Failure: the scout is noticed and questioned — a Tier 4 personal topic fires in the province (“a stranger was seen watching the road”). Critical failure: the scout’s presence is linked to their cover identity — MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT fires. Cover requirement: each scout must have an established cover identity registered in steel_scout_record before firing this action. A scout who has just joined the garrison and has no cover identity generates RAISE_DISPOSITION with regional contacts and establishes cover first. Skill mapping: primary Stealth (Sneaking), secondary Investigation (Notice).

**ARCHIVE_TOPIC.** 0 AP. Automatic trigger — fires immediately when MAINTAIN_CLOUD_ARCHIVE identifies a topic for archival. Available only to Master Cloud (kolat_sect = “kolat_cloud”). Writes the topic’s full data structure — tier, parties named, content summary, source, IC day recorded, original momentum — into cloud_archive in special_data. The topic is tagged is_archived: true. Archived topics are never deleted by normal momentum decay. They persist indefinitely in the archive regardless of whether they remain active in any NPC’s known_topics. An archived topic retains all its original data including the character relationships it names, making it permanently accessible for counter-intelligence queries and future resurrection. Honor cost: none. This is a clerical action, not a deceptive one. Skill mapping: primary Calligraphy (Cipher) — the topic is encoded before storage.

**RESURRECT_TOPIC.** 1 AP. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or VISITING any settlement with a Silk courier connection. Master Cloud re-injects an archived topic back into the active topic pool. The resurrected topic enters at its original tier but attributed to “historical records” or “a recently uncovered document” rather than to Cloud or the Kolat. To a receiving character it appears as a freshly surfaced historical revelation, not a Kolat operation. Requires: a Tiger directive (SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE containing: target_npc_id of the character to be damaged, archive_topic_category as a hint for which topic type to resurrect, and urgency priority 1–3) OR Master Cloud’s own assessment that a high-leverage archived topic (leverage_value 2–3) has become newly relevant due to a change in the target’s circumstances. Self-initiated resurrections are reported to Tiger via TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR before execution — Tiger has 7 IC days to veto. Tiger-directed resurrections proceed immediately. Roll: Calligraphy (Cipher) + Intelligence vs TN 20 to encode the resurrection document convincingly. Failure: the document is traceable to its origin — a skilled investigator with Calligraphy (Cipher) 3+ who examines it can identify the cipher family. Partial disguising of the source is possible with 1 Raise: reduce traceability by one tier. The resurrected topic propagates normally through the conversation and letter systems from the point of re-injection. It behaves exactly like a new topic — it decays, it travels, it influences NPC decisions. The difference is its content is historical fact, not current event. A scandal that was buried fifteen seasons ago is now back in circulation with full documentary support. Honor cost: −0.5 per use — deliberately surfacing old dirt to damage a reputation is dishonorable even by Kolat standards. Skill mapping: primary Calligraphy (Cipher), secondary Sincerity (Deceit).

**USE_CLOUDS_EYES.** 1 AP. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS (requires the caster to be stationary and uninterrupted). Master Cloud casts the Kolat spell Master Cloud’s Eyes (Air 3) on a bird, insect, or small animal near a target settlement, observing the current situation there in real time. Engine effect: generates a real-time topic snapshot of the target settlement’s current ambient topic pool. Topics received this way are tagged source: clouds_eyes in Master Cloud’s IntelDB and bypass the normal intelligence travel delay — they represent the situation right now, not what a courier would have reported weeks ago. Range limit: the target settlement must be within the spell’s effective range (Air Ring × 10 miles — for a Master Cloud with Air 4, this is 40 miles, roughly 2–3 provinces depending on terrain). Topics received at confidence: high (direct magical observation is more reliable than rumour). Roll: Spellcraft + Air Ring vs TN 15 (casting roll). Success: 1d3 topics received from the target settlement. Each Raise: one additional topic. Failure: no topics received; the caster suffers −2k2 to Perception-based rolls for the remainder of the AP (disorientation from failed scrying). The spell cannot be cast on a settlement where strong jade wards or Kuroiban barriers are active — these sever the link. Trigger: USE_CLOUDS_EYES fires when (a) Tiger sends a SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE naming a specific settlement for real-time observation, or (b) MAINTAIN_CLOUD_ARCHIVE identifies a Category A or B topic originating from a specific settlement and Cloud’s IntelDB on that settlement is stale (last updated more than 2 seasons ago). The spell is not cast speculatively — it requires either a directive or a specific information gap to justify the AP cost. Skill mapping: primary Spellcraft, secondary Lore: Shugenja.

**DELIVER_SEALED_LETTER.** 1 AP. VISITING the destination settlement or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS with an established letter channel to the recipient. The Silk operative carries a sealed cipher document from Master Silk and delivers it to a named recipient. The operative does not open or read the letter — this is Kolat operational discipline, not inability. They know it is Kolat business. They do not know the sender, the content, or why it is being delivered. Requires: the operative is in or can travel to the destination settlement within a reasonable timeframe (BEGIN_TRAVEL fires first if not already present). On delivery: the letter enters the recipient’s IntelDB tagged source: silk_delivery. The delivery is confirmed back to Master Silk via a brief acknowledgement letter (also sealed). If the recipient is not present at the named settlement, the operative waits up to 7 IC days before reporting non-delivery to Master Silk. Non-delivery triggers a new assignment — Master Silk either re-routes through a different operative closer to the recipient’s current location, or alerts the original sender that delivery failed. Detection risk: VISITING a settlement and asking for a specific person by name can attract attention. Roll: Sincerity (Deceit) vs TN 10 to appear as ordinary correspondence. Failure: a Tier 4 personal topic fires in the destination settlement — “a courier was asking after [recipient name]”. Critical failure: the delivery is witnessed and noted by an investigator-type NPC. Skill mapping: primary Sincerity (Deceit), secondary Stealth (Sneaking).

**APPROACH_FOR_RECRUITMENT.** 1 AP. Any context where the target is physically present; isolated setting preferred (AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or private VISITING grants +5 to roll TN). Requires: target disposition ≥ Friend (+31), RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT assessment stage complete. The recruiting agent makes their case for the Kolat — the philosophy, the purpose, the offer of purpose beyond the feudal order. Roll: contested Sincerity (Temptation) vs target’s Willpower + (Gi rank × 2 if target holds Gi virtue). Success: target agrees. Completion handler fires immediately — kolat_sect set, standing objective shifted, target registered in Master’s network record. Failure: attempt fails. Tier 4 personal topic fires in target’s known_topics: “[Agent] made an unusual proposition.” RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT is abandoned for this candidate — they cannot be re-approached by the same agent. Critical failure (failed by 10+ on TN): target reports the contact. Tier 3 threat topic fires. The agent’s cover is at immediate risk. Honor cost: −0.5 on success only. Skill mapping: primary Sincerity (Temptation), secondary Courtier (Manipulation). Personality filter: Gi always_blocked. Makoto blocked_when “target has no existing Kolat awareness”. Coin exception: Master Coin recruits merchants, eta bankers, and minor clan traders — not samurai. The pitch is not ideological — it is transactional. When kolat_sect = “kolat_coin” and RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT is active, the APPROACH_FOR_RECRUITMENT decomposition uses Commerce (Merchant) + Sincerity (Temptation) rather than raw Sincerity (Temptation). The offer: guaranteed access to Kolat trade networks, protection from magistrate interference, and a share of profits from operations. The candidate understands they are joining a clandestine merchant network; they do not need to understand it is the Kolat specifically. Their motivation is profit and security, not conspiracy. Failure consequence is adjusted: a merchant who refuses the offer is less likely to report it than a samurai — they understand discretion. Failure fires a Tier 4 personal topic in the candidate’s known_topics but does not automatically generate a Tier 3 threat. Critical failure still generates a Tier 3 threat if the candidate has magistrate connections. Lotus exception: Master Lotus never approaches a candidate under their real identity. When kolat_sect = “kolat_lotus” and RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT is the active NeedType, APPROACH_FOR_RECRUITMENT requires a prerequisite: Master Lotus must have established a cover persona maintained across at least 1 prior meeting with the candidate. The approach proceeds under this persona. The candidate knows only their handler’s cover identity, never the real NPC behind it. The cover persona is registered in Master Lotus’s special_data; if the persona is ever compromised, all operatives recruited under it must be treated as exposed. The roll uses Acting + Sincerity (Temptation) for the persona rather than base Sincerity alone.

**ROUTE_VIA_DEAD_DROP.** 1 AP. VISITING or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS near a Silk-managed dead drop settlement (settlement ID stored in Lotus special_data). Master Lotus deposits an encrypted cipher package into the Silk network’s courier queue at the nearest dead drop node. The package is opaque to Silk — it is an encoded cipher document indistinguishable from ordinary Silk courier traffic. The Silk operative who runs that route picks it up and delivers it to the destination dead drop without knowing the content. Requires: the target dead drop settlement must be within the operational range of at least one active Silk courier route. If no Silk route currently services the destination: first fallback — Master Lotus generates a SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE at priority 2 to Master Silk requesting a route extension to the target settlement. This may take one or more seasons. Second fallback — if Tiger’s directive is priority 3, Master Lotus travels personally to deposit within reach of a functional Silk node, and generates BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the nearest serviceable dead drop. Personal deposit is slower but does not depend on Silk cooperation. Roll: Stealth (Sneaking) vs TN 15 to avoid drawing attention during the deposit. No roll required if AT_OWN_HOLDINGS with a private courier arrangement. Skill mapping: primary Stealth (Sneaking), secondary Calligraphy (Cipher). Honor cost: none. This is a logistics action, not a violence action.

**CHECK_DEAD_DROP.** 1 AP. VISITING the assigned dead drop settlement. The Lotus operative visits the predetermined dead drop location — a loose stone, a merchant’s stall, a temple offering box — and checks for a waiting package. Roll: Investigation (Notice) vs TN 15 to detect the package. The drop is designed to be missed by casual observation; the operative knows exactly where to look, so the TN is low. On success with a package waiting: the kolat_objective slot is populated with ELIMINATE_CHARACTER targeting the encoded NPC ID, at the priority specified in the package. The package is destroyed. The operative has no knowledge of who sent the assignment — only the target and the priority. On success with no package: the NeedType resets its check timer. On failure: the operative misses the package if one is present. MAINTAIN_DEAD_DROP_SCHEDULE will generate another CHECK_DEAD_DROP on the next AP. Cover: the operative must have a plausible reason to be in the dead drop settlement. If they are VISITING for another purpose, no cover story is required. If BEGIN_TRAVEL brought them specifically to this settlement with no other business, investigation-minded characters may notice the purposeless travel (Tier 4 personal topic risk on critical failure of the Investigation roll). Skill mapping: primary Investigation (Notice), secondary Stealth (Sneaking).

**ROTATE_DEAD_DROP.** 1 AP. VISITING any settlement reachable by Silk courier routes. Master Lotus establishes a replacement dead drop node at the current settlement, replacing a compromised entry in lotus_network_record. The new settlement ID is submitted to the Silk network as a routine new courier route request — Silk processes it as ordinary infrastructure expansion, unaware of the security reason. Requires: at least one operative entry in lotus_network_record with dead_drop_settlement_id or confirmation_drop_settlement_id that is flagged as compromised. Execution: (1) update lotus_network_record for the affected operative entry — replace the compromised settlement ID with the current settlement ID; (2) mark any package currently in transit through the old drop as undeliverable in the Silk routing record — the operative assigned that package receives no assignment until a new package is routed through the replacement drop; (3) the old settlement ID is flagged drop_compromised: true in lotus_network_record and is never reused. Roll: Stealth (Sneaking) vs TN 10 to avoid drawing attention during the establishment. Low TN reflects that this looks like ordinary courier business. Cover story maintained throughout. Skill mapping: primary Stealth (Sneaking), secondary Courtier (acting as a merchant arranging logistics). Honor cost: none.

**ARRANGE_PROXY_DUEL.** 1 AP. AT_COURT or VISITING (requires social context where the proxy can be cultivated). The Lotus operative engineers a situation where a skilled third party — who genuinely believes they have cause to fight — will issue or accept a duel challenge against the target. The proxy duelist is never told they are serving the Kolat. They fight believing the grievance is real. Three sub-steps executed over multiple APs: Sub-step 1 — identify a suitable proxy. Scan known_contacts for a character with dueling capability (weapon skill 4+, willing to fight) and a latent grievance against the target or susceptibility to a manufactured one. If none exists, generate RAISE_DISPOSITION with a skilled bushi in the target’s social circle and build toward this sub-step over several sessions. Sub-step 2 — build the narrative. The operative uses GOSSIP, FABRICATE_ACCUSATIONS, or PERSUADE to create or amplify a believable grievance between the proxy and the target. The grievance must be plausible given both characters’ histories — the decomposition checks for narrative compatibility in IntelDB before generating this action. Sub-step 3 — trigger the confrontation. Once the grievance is established and the proxy’s disposition toward the target is in Rival or Enemy range, a Tier 3 confrontation topic is generated. Normal social mechanics take over — the target must respond to the challenge or suffer honor consequences. Roll: Courtier (Manipulation) + Acting vs TN 20 for sub-step 2. Failure means the narrative doesn’t land — no topic fires, the operative must try a different angle. The proxy duelist fights genuinely. If they win, the target dies with no visible Kolat connection. If they lose, the operative’s cover is intact but the method has failed — REASSESS_ELIMINATION fires again. Honor cost: −1.0 to the operative for manufacturing a false grievance that leads to a death. Skill mapping: primary Courtier (Manipulation), secondary Acting.

**ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE.** 1 AP. Any context. Master Jade composes and routes a detailed threat assessment to a specific registered jade_asset_network contact — or to a named institutional body (Jade Magistrates, Kuni Witch Hunters, Phoenix Inquisitors) — through anonymous channels. More powerful than ANONYMOUS_TIP: carries a structured assessment including threat location, observed manifestations, recommended response methods, and supporting evidence from Master Jade’s IntelDB. The recipient has no way to identify the source; the information appears to come from a well-informed anonymous informant. Intent: this action supplements an investigation the asset is likely already conducting under their own standing objective, not initiates one. If the asset has not yet encountered the threat, the tip brings it to their attention and raises its priority in their objective queue. If they are already investigating, the tip provides specific intelligence — a location, a named character, a method — that accelerates their work. Either way the asset acts through their own engine. Master Jade does not command them. Requires: target is registered in jade_asset_network OR is an identifiable institutional contact (Jade Champion, Inquisitor Commander, Kuni Daimyo). If routing to a specific asset, the asset’s disposition toward Master Jade’s cover persona improves by +5 on receipt — the relationship is sustained by useful intelligence. Roll: Calligraphy (Cipher) vs TN 15 to encode the document securely. Failure means the document is readable but not strongly encrypted — a smart investigator who intercepts it could deduce patterns. Critical failure: the document contains a traceable element; a Tier 4 personal risk topic fires for Master Jade. Skill mapping: primary Calligraphy (Cipher), secondary Lore: Shadowlands (ensuring the assessment is accurate and credible). Honor cost: none — reporting supernatural threats is honourable regardless of the method.

**SPONSOR_INSURGENCY.** 1 AP. VISITING the target province or a settlement within 1 province distance (the operative contacts proxies rather than travelling into the target province themselves). Coin operative covertly funds, equips, and directs a bandit or criminal group in the target province, seeding or strengthening a Ronin Bandit Uprising insurgency. Mechanically: if no insurgency is present, generates a new Ronin Bandit Uprising at Strength 1, Concealment 8 in the target province. If an insurgency is already present, adds +2 to its Strength (capped at 10). The funding is untraceable — it moves through the Yasuki, Kaeru, or Unicorn merchant networks as legitimate commerce payments before reaching the bandit group. Koku cost: 10 koku per Strength point seeded, drawn from temple_vault_koku if Master Coin is at the Hidden Temple, or from kolat_koku (local working reserve) if operating remotely. Roll: Commerce (Merchant) + Sincerity (Deceit) vs TN 20 to successfully route the funds without leaving a commercial trail. Failure: funds are routed but with a detectable pattern — a Tier 4 investigation topic fires in the target province naming suspicious merchant activity. Critical failure: the bandit group is traced back to a Coin operative’s cover identity. MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT fires. Skill mapping: primary Commerce (Merchant), secondary Sincerity (Deceit).

**BRIBE_GARRISON_COMMANDER.** 1 AP. VISITING the target province or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS with a letter channel to the target (requires at least Acquaintance disposition). Coin operative bribes the garrison commander of a target settlement to under-report troop strength, divert garrison maintenance funds, or look away from growing criminal activity. Effect: triggers the under-garrison Stability penalty (−2 Stability per season per Section 11.11) in the target province on the next Seasonal Tick, sustained for as long as the bribe is maintained. Ongoing cost: 5 koku per season, drawn from temple_vault_koku if Master Coin is at the Hidden Temple, or from kolat_koku (local working reserve) if operating remotely. Decrements automatically at each Seasonal Tick as long as the bribe is active. The commander receives their payment through the same merchant network laundering that covers all Coin transactions. Requires: target commander identifiable in IntelDB with Sincerity rank below 3 OR confirmed financial pressure in their history topics. Roll: Commerce (Appraisal) + Sincerity (Temptation) vs target commander’s Willpower × 5. Failure: commander refuses. No topic fires — they pocket the money and do nothing, or return it silently. Critical failure: commander reports the approach. Tier 3 threat topic fires implicating a merchant contact. If the bribe is stopped (koku depleted, operative goes dark), the penalty ends at the next Seasonal Tick. Reserve depletion handling: both SPONSOR_INSURGENCY and BRIBE_GARRISON_COMMANDER check kolat_koku before firing. If kolat_koku ≤ 0 at the point of execution, the action is suppressed and MANAGE_KOLAT_FUNDS fires at priority 3 instead — Master Coin replenishes the reserve before continuing disruption operations. For BRIBE_GARRISON_COMMANDER specifically: if an ongoing bribe misses a seasonal payment due to depletion, the garrison commander stops cooperating silently. Their garrison behaviour returns to normal on the next Seasonal Tick. No topic fires — the commander simply stops looking the other way. This is invisible to Master Coin unless they notice the stability penalty has ended. A sustained disruption operation therefore requires sustained reserve maintenance. Skill mapping: primary Commerce (Appraisal), secondary Sincerity (Temptation).

**CONTRIBUTE_TO_RESERVE.** 0 AP. Automatic trigger. Fires for a Coin Sect operative (kolat_sect = “kolat_coin”) immediately after a successful CONDUCT_COMMERCE action, on the same AP. The operative diverts a portion of their personal koku yield into a local hidden reserve rather than their personal holdings. Diversion rate: skim_rate field in coin_network_record (default 25% of the CONDUCT_COMMERCE personal yield; adjustable by Master Coin via cipher directive). Example: a Commerce 4 merchant in a crossroads settlement (×1.3) earns 10.4 koku from CONDUCT_COMMERCE. At 25% skim rate, 2.6 koku is diverted to local_reserve_koku; 7.8 koku enters personal holdings. The diverted funds are logged as legitimate commercial costs in the operative’s bookkeeping — they exist physically as coins in a hidden cache, not as a line item that can be detected by normal Commerce (Mathematics) examination. The reserve accumulates season over season until COLLECT_OPERATIVE_FUNDS retrieves it. If the operative goes dark or is compromised, their local_reserve_koku is noted in coin_network_record as unrecovered — Master Coin will attempt retrieval as part of MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT handling. Honor cost: none. Skill mapping: none — this is an automatic accounting action, not a contested roll.

**CHECK_CONFIRMATION_DROP.** 1 AP. VISITING the confirmation drop settlement for a given assignment. Master Lotus checks the confirmation drop node for an execution token deposited by the operative. Fires for Master Lotus on a schedule driven by MONITOR_ASSIGNMENT_COMPLETION (see below). Roll: Investigation (Notice) vs TN 10. On success with token present: assignment marked confirmed in lotus_network_record, operative status returns to idle, lotus_network_record assignment fields cleared. The confirmation token is destroyed on retrieval. Tiger is notified via SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE that the target has been eliminated. On success with no token present: Master Lotus records a check timestamp; if elapsed days since assignment_dispatched_ic_day exceed the escalation threshold, REASSESS_ELIMINATION fires. On failure: revisit on next AP. Skill mapping: primary Investigation (Notice), secondary Stealth (Sneaking).
