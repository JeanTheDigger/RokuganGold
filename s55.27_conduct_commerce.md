## 55.27 CONDUCT_COMMERCE

**CONDUCT_COMMERCE.** 1 AP. VISITING or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS in any settlement with town PU present. Available to any character with Commerce 3+ and a merchant role, merchant vassal status, or town-based trading infrastructure. Represents one season of active trading in the current settlement — buying, selling, moving goods, cultivating commercial relationships. One CONDUCT_COMMERCE per settlement per character per season maximum. Cannot be used during active War Status in the province.

**Effect 1 — Settlement boost.** The character’s trading activity adds a seasonal bonus to the settlement’s Koku generation modifier: +0.05× to the settlement’s existing location modifier for the current season only. This is not permanent — it represents the increased market activity their presence and trading creates. Maximum cumulative bonus from all CONDUCT_COMMERCE activity in a single settlement in a single season: +0.15×. Three or more active merchants in the same settlement cap the bonus collectively. The settlement’s base location modifier (Section 4.3.8) is unchanged — only the seasonal output is affected. Example: a crossroads settlement at ×1.3 with two active merchants becomes ×1.4 for that season.

**Effect 2 — Personal yield.** The character earns personal Koku equal to: Commerce rank × settlement_modifier × 2. Settlement modifier used is the base location modifier from Section 4.3.8 — the seasonal boost from Effect 1 does not compound into personal yield. Examples: Commerce 5 in a port city (×1.5) = 15 Koku. Commerce 3 in a standard settlement (×1.0) = 6 Koku. Commerce 3 in a remote village (×0.7) = 4 Koku. Commerce 5 in a Ryoko Owari-equivalent (port city ×1.5 × crossroads ×1.3 = ×1.95) = 19.5 Koku. Personal yield is deposited directly into the character’s personal koku holdings. It is real money earned through real economic activity and is fully visible as legitimate income. Honor cost: none for characters with a merchant role or merchant vassal status. Samurai without a merchant role designation who use this action receive the full personal yield but at −0.5 Honor per use — direct commerce is beneath their station. Most samurai NPCs will score DO_NOTHING higher than the honor cost justifies.

**Effect 3 — Ambient intelligence.** After the primary commerce roll, the character makes a secondary Investigation (Notice) + Awareness roll vs TN 15. Success: one topic from the settlement’s ambient topic pool is added to the character’s known_topics, flagged source: commerce_overheard. The topic is selected by the engine based on current momentum in the settlement (higher-momentum topics are more likely to surface) and the character’s existing IntelDB (topics already known are skipped). Critical success (beat TN by 10+): two topics received. Topics received through commerce are one confidence tier lower than if the character had witnessed the event directly — overheard rumour is not confirmed fact. Topics are not restricted to events that originated in the current settlement. A topic that originated elsewhere may have propagated to this settlement through normal momentum bleed — the merchant hears about events from other provinces through the merchants, travellers, and minor samurai they deal with. Topic travel distance corresponds to momentum: a high-momentum event (Tier 1 or 2) may have propagated across several provinces; a low-momentum Tier 4 event may only be known locally. This makes settlement centrality doubly valuable — crossroads and port cities hear more, and hear it faster, than remote villages. A merchant who consistently trades in a major hub over multiple seasons builds one of the best ambient intelligence pictures in the Empire.

**Standing objective connection.** CONDUCT_COMMERCE is the primary decomposition of the “Grow my commercial enterprise” standing objective for merchant-archetype NPCs. Full decomposition: CONDUCT_COMMERCE in the highest-modifier accessible settlement → BEGIN_TRAVEL toward better markets when the current one is saturated or the character has traded there this season already → SECURE_TRADE_ROUTE when a route serving their primary settlement is threatened. A merchant NPC following this standing objective naturally migrates toward major commercial hubs, which concentrates ambient intelligence in those locations as a realistic emergent consequence. Skill mapping: primary Commerce (Merchant), secondary Investigation (Notice).

**COLLECT_OPERATIVE_FUNDS.** “One or more of my operatives has accumulated a local reserve above threshold. I need to collect it, bring it into the main laundering pipeline, and ensure the funds are clean before depositing them into the Kolat reserve.” Fires for Master Coin at each Seasonal Tick when any coin_network_record entry has local_reserve_koku above the collection threshold (default 20 koku — adjustable per operative via directive). Priority = 1 normally; priority = 2 if Tiger has requested a funding transfer and kolat_koku is below operational threshold. ImmediateNeed fields: target_npc_id (the operative with the highest local reserve), threshold (local_reserve_koku ≥ 20), threshold_type = “operative_reserve_threshold”. Decomposition: Step 1 — collection. Two paths. PATH 1 (remote): generate WRITE_LETTER (cipher) directing the operative to route their local reserve through the Silk merchant network as a standard commercial transfer. The operative’s local reserve is decremented; Master Coin’s dirty_koku field is incremented by the same amount after a 7–14 IC day transit delay. PATH 2 (personal): if Master Coin is already VISITING the operative’s province, or the reserve exceeds 50 koku (too large for courier safely), generate BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the operative’s settlement and collect directly. Direct collection has no transit delay and no detection roll. Step 2 — laundering. After collection, LAUNDER_KOKU fires immediately at priority 2 to clean the incoming dirty koku. Until laundered, funds sit in dirty_koku and cannot be transferred or used for normal operations. Emergency path: if Tiger sends a priority 3 funding directive and kolat_koku is insufficient but dirty_koku has adequate funds, Master Coin may invoke an emergency transfer. The dirty koku moves directly via TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS without laundering. Risk: dirty koku moving through the Silk courier network at volume leaves a detectable financial pattern. A Commerce (Mathematics) 4+ investigator who examines the relevant transaction records can identify the movement. Each emergency dirty transfer generates a risk accumulator: if three or more emergency transfers occur within two seasons, a Tier 4 investigation topic fires naming suspicious merchant activity in the transfer region. Master Coin accepts this risk consciously — the engine scores LAUNDER_KOKU 95 and emergency transfer 40 in the COLLECT_OPERATIVE_FUNDS alignment, meaning laundering is always preferred. Emergency transfer only fires when Tiger has explicitly flagged priority 3 and time does not permit laundering. Laundering rate: 5 koku per AP per LAUNDER_KOKU action. Step 3 — deposit. Once laundered, clean koku is added to kolat_koku. If Tiger has requested a specific amount, TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS fires at priority 2 immediately. Objective alignment: LAUNDER_KOKU 100, WRITE_LETTER 90, TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS 85, BEGIN_TRAVEL 70, DO_NOTHING 0. Vault balance reporting: at each Seasonal Tick, as part of the collection and laundering cycle, Master Coin includes the current temple_vault_koku balance in their seasonal report to Tiger via WRITE_LETTER (cipher) through Silk. This is the primary mechanism by which Tiger knows the vault balance between Conclaves. Master Coin has write access to temple_vault_koku (via TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS) and therefore knows its current value. Tiger does not independently read the vault field — they rely on Coin’s reported figure. If Coin’s report is absent or delayed, Tiger does not know the current vault balance until the next report arrives.

**MONITOR_TEMPLE_PERIMETER.** “I need my scouts out in the surrounding region, watching for anything unusual — strangers asking the wrong questions, magistrates moving too close, military units without a clear destination. The Temple must have warning before a threat arrives at its door.” A standing NeedType that fires continuously for Master Steel while any scout entry in steel_scout_record has last_report_ic_day exceeding the reporting threshold (default 14 IC days). Priority = 1 normally; priority = 3 when a steel_scout_report letter arrives containing a threat indicator in any province within 3 provinces of the Hidden Temple, or during the annual Conclave window (the 7 IC days before and after the Conclave date). ImmediateNeed fields: target_province_id (the province with the oldest scout coverage or the province named in the incoming steel_scout_report threat indicator), threshold_type = “perimeter_coverage”. Decomposition: Step 1 — assign patrol routes. Master Steel generates CONDUCT_PERIMETER_PATROL for each scout whose last_report_ic_day is overdue. Scouts operate autonomously once assigned — they travel their designated route and return a report via letter. Step 2 — assess incoming reports. When a scout’s letter arrives (tagged source: steel_scout_report in Master Steel’s IntelDB), the content is parsed for threat indicators: unknown characters in the region, Jade Magistrate movement, Imperial troop deployment, unusual merchant caravans. If a threat indicator is found, a Tier 3 perimeter alert topic fires in Master Steel’s known_topics. Step 3 — threat response. SHALLOW threat (unknown traveller, curiosity): assign a garrison ronin to observe from concealment. MODERATE threat (Jade Magistrates moving with clear direction toward the Temple’s region): alert Tiger via SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE at priority 2, increase patrol frequency. DEEP threat (military unit or organised investigation closing in): alert Tiger at priority 3, prepare garrison for defensive posture, generate EMERGENCY_LOCKDOWN (see below). Conclave mode: during the Conclave window, all patrol routes activate simultaneously, all available scouts are deployed, and garrison goes to full readiness. No scout misses a report during the Conclave window — if one does, MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT fires for that scout immediately. Objective alignment: CONDUCT_PERIMETER_PATROL 100, ASSIGN_GARRISON 85, SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE 80, WRITE_LETTER 60, DO_NOTHING 0.

**MAINTAIN_STEEL_GARRISON.** “My garrison must be at strength, paid, and ready. A gap in the line is a vulnerability the Temple cannot afford.” A standing NeedType firing for Master Steel at each Seasonal Tick. Checks three conditions in priority order. Condition 1 — operational_koku sufficiency: garrison ronin require stipend payment each season (base rate: 3 koku per ronin per season). If operational_koku falls below seasonal payroll, Master Steel generates SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Tiger at priority 3 requesting resupply before paying. If Tiger cannot resupply in time, the garrison still gets paid from whatever operational_koku exists — Master Steel never lets the garrison go unpaid if avoidable. Ronin who go unpaid for two consecutive seasons check morale: Willpower roll vs TN 20 to remain loyal. Failure: the ronin deserts. Since desertion means a permanent-posting ronin has left with knowledge of the Temple’s location, MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT fires immediately at priority 3 for that NPC, and Tiger is alerted. Condition 2 — garrison headcount: minimum garrison is 6 ronin (enough to maintain a 3-shift watch rotation with one in reserve). If headcount drops below 6 due to desertion, death, or injury, SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE fires to Tiger at priority 2 flagging the vacancy and requesting a candidate referral. Tiger identifies and routes the candidate; Master Steel does not recruit independently. See Steel exception in RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT (Section 54.7c) for the full pipeline. Condition 3 — readiness assessment: SECURE_ONI_EYE fires once per season to confirm the Temple’s physical security systems are intact. ImmediateNeed fields: threshold (operational_koku ≥ seasonal_payroll), threshold_type = “garnison_payroll”. Objective alignment: SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE 100, RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT 90, SECURE_ONI_EYE 80, DO_NOTHING 0.

**seasonal_payroll computation:** Computed dynamically at each Seasonal Tick as the count of steel_garrison_record entries with role “garnison” or “scout” AND morale_status not “desertion_risk”, multiplied by 3 koku. Not stored as a static field — always recomputed from the current garrison record at tick time.

**MAINTAIN_CLOUD_ARCHIVE.** “Topics decay. Scandals are forgotten. Secrets lose their momentum and vanish from living memory. My job is to make sure the Kolat never forgets anything that might matter.” A standing NeedType firing for Master Cloud at each Seasonal Tick. Checks two conditions. Condition 1 — incoming topics and intake: topics enter Cloud’s known_topics through three defined channels, all of which require either action or an incoming letter. Channel A — Tiger directive: Tiger sends SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE explicitly routing a topic to Cloud as archive-worthy. The topic arrives via letter through Silk. Channel B — USE_CLOUDS_EYES: Cloud’s own scrying action produces topics directly from observation. Channel C — normal social and letter channels: Cloud is a high-Status shugenja with an active social life. Succession topics, legitimacy topics, and news of powerful figures reach Cloud the same way they reach any senior court figure — through conversations, letters from contacts, and court attendance. Cloud does not automatically receive these topics. Cloud must be socially active, maintain correspondence, and attend institutions where such topics circulate. The collection scope (succession, Status 6+, Tier 1–2, Kolat-flagged) defines what Cloud prioritises archiving from what they actually learn — not what they magically receive. A season in isolation with no social contact and no USE_CLOUDS_EYES is a season with no new intelligence. Cloud evaluates each incoming topic Topics flagged with any of the following properties are automatically archived: tier 1 or 2 (major events never decay from the archive), topics naming a Clan Champion, Family Daimyo, Imperial family member, or Jade Magistrate (powerful persons’ histories are always preserved), topics involving succession, inheritance, or legitimacy claims (leverage with indefinite future value), topics involving confirmed Kolat operations or Kolat-connected characters (institutional memory). Cloud exercises discretion on tier 3 and 4 topics: if the topic involves a character with Status 5+ or names a figure likely to become powerful, ARCHIVE_TOPIC fires. Condition 2 — archive maintenance: the physical records caches require periodic cipher updates as encoding systems evolve. WRITE_LETTER (cipher) fires every two seasons to send encoded additions to the Hidden Temple via Silk courier, to be added to the master archive under Steel’s custody. The Silk courier carries a sealed cipher document and does not know the content. Steel adds the sealed packet to the archive chests without opening it. ImmediateNeed fields: threshold_type = “archive_maintenance”. Objective alignment: ARCHIVE_TOPIC 100, USE_CLOUDS_EYES 90, WRITE_LETTER 70, DO_NOTHING 0.

**RESPOND_TO_ARCHIVE_QUERY.** “Tiger needs historical intelligence on a specific character or family. I need to search the archive and send back everything we have.” Fires for Master Cloud when Tiger sends a SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE requesting a lookup on a specific NPC, family, or topic category. Priority matches the directive priority (1–3). ImmediateNeed fields: target_npc_id or target_family_id (the lookup subject), priority from directive. Decomposition: Cloud queries cloud_archive for all stored topics that name or implicate the target NPC or family. Assembles a summary document in cipher. Fires WRITE_LETTER (cipher) to Tiger via Silk courier. Transit time: 7–14 IC days depending on Silk route availability. If the archive contains a topic that directly contradicts the target’s current public standing — a buried scandal, a false lineage claim, a recorded past crime — Cloud flags it in the summary as leverage_available: true. Tiger decides whether to act on it. Cloud does not act on leverage independently. Cloud compromise protocol: if MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT fires for Master Cloud, a Cloud-specific Stage 1 action fires before the standard go-dark directive — Cloud immediately destroys their personal working copy of the archive (the copy at their holdings). The master copy at the Hidden Temple is unaffected and remains under Steel’s custody. Tiger is alerted via TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR at priority 3. The archive is not lost — but it is inaccessible until a new Cloud Master is elevated and granted access to the Temple. This is the primary security reason why Cloud’s working copy is cipher-encoded with a personal key known only to Cloud and Tiger. A secondary check: if the query reveals that the target has a connection to a known Kolat operative or Master, Cloud immediately flags this to Tiger via Tear rather than waiting for the letter. This is the counter-intelligence function — Cloud is the institutional memory that catches things no currently-active Master would know. Empty archive result: if cloud_archive contains no topics naming the queried NPC or family, Cloud sends Tiger a null result letter noting the absence and recommends one of two actions: (1) USE_CLOUDS_EYES targeting the NPC’s last known settlement for a real-time snapshot, or (2) a Silk intelligence-gathering directive to accumulate topics about the target before a future query. An empty result is itself intelligence — a powerful figure with no archive entry means the Kolat has never encountered them, which is worth knowing. Objective alignment: WRITE_LETTER 100, TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR 95 (counter-intelligence flag only), DO_NOTHING 0.

**GATHER_AND_REPORT.** “I am positioned to see things others miss. My job is to pay attention to what matters and make sure Master Silk knows about it.” A standing NeedType firing for Silk Sect operatives (kolat_sect = “kolat_silk”) at each Seasonal Tick. Silk operatives do not need to travel to gather intelligence — their value is their position. A courtier at a Clan Champion’s court, a merchant at a major crossroads, a monk at a temple that receives pilgrims from across the Empire. The operative gathers through their normal daily life. Mechanics: at each Seasonal Tick, the operative’s known_topics are scanned for topics that have entered their pool since the last report. Topics meeting any of the following criteria are flagged for reporting: Tier 1 or 2 (always report), topics naming a character with Status 5+ (powerful persons’ movements are always significant), succession or legitimacy topics (any tier), military deployment or troop movement topics, topics involving Jade Magistrate or Hidden Guard activity. Flagged topics are compiled into a cipher report letter via WRITE_LETTER (cipher) addressed to Master Silk. The letter travels through normal channels — the operative uses their existing social and commercial networks to send it, not a dedicated Kolat infrastructure. Transmission time: 3–14 IC days depending on distance from Master Silk’s location and available letter routes. Additionally, when a Silk operative encounters a topic through CONDUCT_COMMERCE, RAISE_DISPOSITION conversations, or any other social action that produces a source: commerce_overheard or directly observed topic of the flagged categories, a report fires immediately rather than waiting for the Seasonal Tick — urgent intelligence does not wait for the season’s end. Priority = 1 normally (reporting is background activity alongside normal life); priority = 2 if the operative holds a Tier 1 or 2 topic that Master Silk has not yet received. Objective alignment: WRITE_LETTER 100, DELIVER_SEALED_LETTER 90, DO_NOTHING 0.

**MAINTAIN_SILK_NETWORK.** “Intelligence is flowing in from my operatives. I need to evaluate it, decide what Tiger and the other Sects need to know, and assign delivery tasks to the right operatives for any pending letters.” Master Silk’s standing NeedType. Fires at each Seasonal Tick and immediately when a report letter from any silk_network_record operative arrives. Two standing functions. Function 1 — intelligence routing: incoming report letters from operatives are parsed for topic content. Master Silk evaluates each flagged topic against Tiger’s standing intelligence priorities (maintained as a field in silk_network_record: tiger_priority_topics, updated by Tiger directive). Topics matching Tiger’s priorities are immediately routed to Tiger via WRITE_LETTER (cipher) at priority 2. Topics of exceptional significance (Tier 1 or 2, or naming a known Kolat Master or operative in a threat context) are routed via TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR at priority 3. Topics that are significant but not immediately urgent are batched and sent in a seasonal intelligence summary letter to Tiger. Topics that are neither priority-matching nor significant are filed in Master Silk’s own IntelDB only — background awareness. Function 2 — delivery assignment: when any Kolat Master or Tiger sends a delivery request (via SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE naming a target recipient and a sealed letter or package), Master Silk scores available operatives in silk_network_record by proximity to the destination and current assignment load. The best-available operative receives a DELIVER_SEALED_LETTER assignment via cipher directive from Master Silk. The operative does not know the origin, content, or final significance of what they carry. They know only: deliver this sealed letter to this named person at this settlement. Objective alignment: WRITE_LETTER 100, TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR 95, SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE 80, DO_NOTHING 0. Gap 1 — Geographic coverage check: at each Seasonal Tick, MAINTAIN_SILK_NETWORK cross-references silk_network_record operative positions against tiger_priority_topics’ associated provinces. If a priority province has no operative within 2 provinces, a coverage gap flag fires in Master Silk’s known_topics: “no coverage in [province]” at Tier 4. RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT fires at priority 2 targeting the Silk Sect profile for a candidate in or near the gap province. This creates a natural recruitment expansion driver — intelligence gaps pull new operatives into under-covered regions. Gap 2 — tiger_priority_topics update mechanism: Tiger updates the priority list via SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Master Silk, containing a revised Array[string] of topic categories and province names to watch. On receipt, MAINTAIN_SILK_NETWORK immediately pushes the updated list to each operative in silk_network_record via cipher directive (WRITE_LETTER at priority 2 to each operative). Operatives update their internal scanning criteria on the next AP after receiving the update letter. Gap 3 — operative repositioning: when a coverage gap requires moving an existing operative rather than recruiting a new one — typically because the gap is urgent and recruitment would take too long — Tiger or Master Silk can send a cipher directive to a specific operative with a new position assignment. The operative generates BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the new province. Their silk_network_record entry’s position field updates when they arrive. The old province loses coverage for the duration of travel. Master Silk tracks travel status in silk_network_record via a reposition_in_transit bool field. Gap 5 — Silk-to-Cloud routing: when MAINTAIN_SILK_NETWORK routes a topic to Tiger, Tiger’s engine evaluates whether to forward to Cloud for archival (per Cloud’s passive subscription criteria — succession topics, Status 6+ topics, Tier 1–2 topics). This forwarding uses Tiger’s standard SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Cloud. The Silk operative who gathered the topic has no awareness of the Cloud routing — they reported to Master Silk; what happens after is above their operational knowledge.

### Sleeper Command Taxonomy

The sleeper_command field is a structured data object, not a free-form string. It is resolved at conditioning time using Master Dream’s current IntelDB and stored on the sleeper’s character sheet. The command is limited to five words of spoken instruction (matching the Sleeper Agent disadvantage, Section 54.7e) but the full data structure below contains all the engine information needed to resolve the command correctly when activation fires. Five command types are defined.

**ELIMINATE.** The sleeper kills a named character by whatever means are available to them. The sleeper uses their full Action economy and available skills to execute the kill on the next available AP after activation. They have no memory of the command afterward. This is functionally equivalent to a Lotus assassination except the agent is unwitting and the method is improvised — the sleeper uses what they have access to, not a pre-planned weapon. A sleeper with low combat skills may fail or be overpowered. Tiger must select sleepers for elimination commands who have plausible means of execution. Schema fields used: target_npc_id.

**DISCLOSE.** The sleeper delivers a specific statement to a named character or to any present audience, as if it were something they genuinely know or believe. The disclosure is injected as a new topic into the active topic pool at the moment of delivery, attributed to the sleeper as its source. The topic may be true, partially true, or entirely fabricated — the engine does not validate it. It fires at the tier specified in the disclosure_topic struct. A fabricated Tier 2 political disclosure from a trusted source can generate a crisis indistinguishable from a real one. The sleeper believes what they are saying in the moment of activation — they speak with complete conviction, which makes them credible to observers. Their sincerity is not feigned because they are not consciously aware it is false. After activation they return to normal and may be confused about what they said. Schema fields used: target_npc_id (optional — null means the sleeper discloses to whoever is present), disclosure_topic (full TopicStruct: tier, category, content_summary, parties_named, confidence).

**GRANT_ACCESS.** The sleeper opens, unlocks, or grants physical access to a specific location or object — a gate, a door, a sealed chamber, a vault, a chest. The sleeper uses their legitimate access credentials to perform this action, making it appear intentional and authorised. The most powerful application is siege scenarios: a sleeper gatekeeper who opens a castle gate mid-siege immediately collapses the defender’s fortification bonus and forces a direct combat resolution, bypassing the full siege attrition model (Section 11.8). The gate breach is treated as a forced entry event. Garrison defenders must immediately respond to the open gate rather than continuing their defensive posture. The Kolat does not need an army if they have the right person asleep at the right gate. Schema fields used: target_location_id (the settlement gate, door, or object ID), target_object_id (optional — for chests or vaults rather than structural access points).

**SUPPRESS.** The sleeper fails to perform an action they would normally take — a guard who does not raise the alarm, a physician who does not administer an antidote, a magistrate who does not file a report, a herald who does not announce an arrival. Negative commands are the hardest to detect: the sleeper simply does nothing, which looks like inattention, error, or negligence rather than conspiracy. Post-activation, the sleeper has no memory of the suppression and may genuinely believe they simply forgot or were distracted. Investigators working backward from the consequences may eventually reach the sleeper, but the chain of evidence is weak. Schema fields used: suppress_action (string — the specific action type the sleeper will not take, e.g. “RAISE_ALARM”, “ADMINISTER_MEDICINE”, “FILE_REPORT”), target_npc_id (optional — specifies a particular person the suppression applies to, e.g. “do not report this specific person” rather than anyone).

**DELIVER.** The sleeper hands a specific object to a named character. The object must already exist and be accessible to the sleeper at the time of activation. Typical objects: a poisoned gift, a forged document, a weapon pre-planted for a subsequent user, a cipher letter the sleeper believes is ordinary correspondence. The delivery appears entirely normal — the sleeper presents it naturally, with whatever social framing they would normally use. The recipient has no reason to be suspicious unless the object itself reveals something unusual. Schema fields used: target_npc_id, target_object_id (the item to be delivered; must exist in the world state and be accessible to the sleeper).

### Sleeper Command Data Schema

sleeper_command: {

  command_type: string

    -- "ELIMINATE" | "DISCLOSE" | "GRANT_ACCESS" | "SUPPRESS" | "DELIVER"

  target_npc_id: int or null

    -- the NPC targeted by the command; null for DISCLOSE to open audience

  target_object_id: string or null

    -- object involved in DELIVER or GRANT_ACCESS; null otherwise

  target_location_id: string or null

    -- gate, door, or structural target for GRANT_ACCESS; null otherwise

  disclosure_topic: TopicStruct or null

    -- full topic data for DISCLOSE commands; null otherwise

    -- TopicStruct: { tier: int, category: string, content_summary: string,

    --   parties_named: Array[npc_id], confidence: string }

  suppress_action: string or null

    -- action type the sleeper will not take for SUPPRESS; null otherwise

}

The five-word spoken trigger constraint applies to the verbal activation phrase only, not to this data structure. The data structure is resolved silently by the engine when the trigger fires. The spoken phrase is the key; this struct is the lock mechanism behind it. Execution timing: no command type resolves instantly. The struct defines what the sleeper will do — the normal action system determines how and when they do it. A GRANT_ACCESS command does not open a gate the moment of activation; it makes the sleeper single-mindedly work toward opening that gate through whatever actions are available to them. An ELIMINATE command does not kill the target immediately; it turns the sleeper into a focused assassin who pursues the target through travel, social access, and opportunity until the kill is possible or the sleeper dies trying. The command sets the destination. The engine plots the route.

**OVERSEE_KOLAT_NETWORK.** “I have received reports from my Masters. I need to read them, assess the Kolat’s current state, identify what requires my attention, and issue directives for the coming season.” Tiger’s primary standing NeedType. Fires at each Seasonal Tick and immediately when a report letter or Tear transmission arrives from any Master. Tiger is the only member of the Tiger Sect — there are no agents, no network to maintain, only the nine other Masters reporting upward. Tiger’s awareness of the Kolat’s state is entirely derived from what Masters choose to report. Tiger cannot independently verify any report without directing another Sect to investigate. Exception — personal investigation: Tiger’s Investigation 7 and Sincerity (Deceit) 7 make them one of the most capable investigators in the conspiracy. When a situation warrants direct involvement — typically when delegating to Silk would reveal too much about what Tiger suspects, or when urgency requires faster resolution than a letter chain permits — Tiger may personally conduct surveillance on a target using GATHER_INTELLIGENCE and PROBE under their cover identity. This is never Tiger’s first choice: personal exposure is the conspiracy’s greatest risk. But it is available and is used when no other option exists. This is a structural feature, not a flaw — it is why Tiger’s trust in each Master must be calibrated carefully. Assessment loop: Step 1 — read incoming reports. At each Seasonal Tick Tiger reads all cipher letters received from Masters via Silk and all Tear impressions received since the last tick. Each report updates Tiger’s IntelDB for the relevant Sect: treasury balance from Coin, perimeter status from Steel, archive additions from Cloud, intelligence summaries from Silk, sleeper registry status from Dream, court intelligence from Chrysanthemum, supernatural threat assessment from Jade, Lotus network status from Lotus. Step 2 — assess. Tiger evaluates the combined picture against three standing questions: (a) Is any Sect compromised or going dark? Fire MONITOR_KOLAT_SECURITY. (b) Does any Sect need resources, direction, or support? Fire SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE at appropriate priority. (c) Is there a strategic opportunity or threat requiring a coordinated response across multiple Sects? Fire DIRECT_KOLAT_OPERATION. Step 3 — direct. For each identified need, Tiger generates SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE or TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR as appropriate. Urgent matters (priority 3) always use Tear first, letter second for detail. Routine matters use letter only. Step 4 — allocate resources. At the start of each season Tiger reviews temple_vault_koku and allocates operational_koku to Sects with active operations requiring funding. SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE decrements vault and credits the receiving Master’s operational_koku. Priority = 1 normally; priority = 2 if any Master has not reported in two or more seasons; priority = 3 if any Master is confirmed compromised or the vault is below minimum threshold. Objective alignment: SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE 100, TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR 95, MONITOR_KOLAT_SECURITY 90, DIRECT_KOLAT_OPERATION 80, BEGIN_TRAVEL 50, DO_NOTHING 0.

**MONITOR_KOLAT_SECURITY.** “Something is wrong inside the organisation. A Master has gone silent, or an investigation is getting too close, or I have reason to believe one of my Masters has been turned. I need to identify the threat and contain it before it unravels everything.” Fires under four trigger conditions. Trigger 1 — Master silence: any Master whose last report exceeds two full seasons without contact. Tiger has no direct way to verify if the Master is compromised, dead, or simply unable to communicate. Dead vs compromised disambiguation: Tiger fires LOCATE_CHARACTER for the cover identity and directs Silk to investigate. If Silk returns a confirmed death topic: fire EVALUATE_SUCCESSION. If Silk returns an investigation topic naming the cover identity: treat as Trigger 3. If nothing unusual: Tiger sends a Tear to the silent Master — no returning impression after one further season escalates to full compromise assessment. A silent Tear is the closest Tiger can get to confirmation that something is wrong.

Trigger 2 — investigation proximity: a Silk report or Cloud archive query returns a Tier 2 or Tier 3 investigation topic naming a character within two social degrees of any Master’s cover identity. Tiger assesses the threat tier: if the investigation is moving toward the Master’s known cover activities, Tiger sends SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE at priority 2 warning the at-risk Master to reduce visibility and suspend non-essential operations. If the investigation names the cover identity directly, Tiger treats it as Trigger 3 immediately. Tiger does not wait for the investigation to escalate before acting — early warning is the entire value of Silk’s intelligence network. Trigger 3 — confirmed compromise: MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT has fired for a Master or senior operative. Tiger’s three-tier response: SHALLOW (cover persona suspected, Kolat connection not established) — SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to go dark, assess via Silk intelligence, hold operational_koku until situation clarifies; MODERATE (Kolat connection suspected but not confirmed) — TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR at priority 2 ordering full go-dark, begin EVALUATE_SUCCESSION pre-assessment (evaluate heirs without formally triggering succession), withhold all operational_koku; DEEP (Kolat connection confirmed, arrest imminent or investigation has a name) — TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR at priority 3 to all other Masters warning of potential network exposure, direct Lotus to assess whether elimination of the compromised Master is necessary to protect the network, fire EVALUATE_SUCCESSION immediately. Tiger weighs elimination of a compromised Master against the risk that a dead Master whose cover is already known may leave more evidence than a living one who goes dark. This is Tiger’s most difficult judgment call. Trigger 4 — suspected turned Master: Tiger receives contradictory reports from two Masters about the same observable event, or a Master’s stated actions produce outcomes inconsistent with what those actions should produce. Tiger cannot confront a Master directly without revealing Tiger’s own identity or the broader network architecture. Response: Tiger directs Cloud to run a deep archive query on the suspect Master’s cover identity looking for prior compromise indicators; directs Silk to place a conscious operative near the suspect’s known position for passive observation; withholds operational_koku from the suspect Master with a cover explanation of temporary resource constraints. If the cloud query or Silk report returns a compromise indicator, escalate to Trigger 3. If both return clean results after two seasons, Tiger reassesses — the contradiction may have been genuine error or miscommunication rather than betrayal. Priority = 2 for Triggers 1 and 2; priority = 3 for Triggers 3 and 4. Objective alignment: SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE 100, TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR 95, LOCATE_CHARACTER 85, DIRECT_KOLAT_OPERATION 70, DO_NOTHING 0.

**DIRECT_KOLAT_OPERATION.** “This objective requires more than one Sect acting independently. Objective alignment: SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE 100, TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR 95, OVERSEE_KOLAT_NETWORK 80, DO_NOTHING 0.
