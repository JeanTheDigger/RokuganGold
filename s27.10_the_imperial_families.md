## 27.10 The Imperial Families

The three non-dynastic Imperial families occupy a unique position in the Empire — small in numbers and holdings but enormously powerful by virtue of their direct connection to the Throne. See Section 2.1.19 for full history and Section 2.1.20 for the Imperial Bureaucracy they administer. Imperial family membership is not available as a player character creation option — these families are restricted to NPCs. A player character may be adopted into an Imperial family or appointed to an Imperial position during play as a result of extraordinary achievement, but this is an in-game event, not a starting choice.

**Seppun:** Trait bonus: +1 Reflexes. The Emperor’s protectors. Seppun produce the miharu (guardsmen) and the Hidden Guard (anti-magical protection). Closest non-dynastic family to the Emperor. Their devotion to the Throne is absolute and deeply ingrained — not fanaticism, but a subtle, unshakeable duty. Vassal families: Hanako (magistrates and law reform), Yotsu (ronin family sworn to Seppun service).

**Otomo:** Trait bonus: +1 Awareness. The serpents in the garden. Otomo courtiers keep the Great Clans perpetually off-balance through manipulation, discord, and political maneuvering — ensuring no clan can ever unite against the Emperor. They fill many key positions in the Imperial bureaucracy and serve as the repository for the Imperial bloodline through the Ritual of Abdication. Vassal family: Reju (education and schooling).

**Miya:** Trait bonus: +1 Stamina. The Emperor’s heralds and peacemakers. The Miya carry Imperial edicts throughout the Empire, serve as impartial mediators in inter-clan disputes, and administer Miya’s Blessing — the annual charitable aid program. The most beloved of the Imperial families. The Imperial Herald position is traditionally held by the Miya daimyo (Section 2.1.17). Vassal families: Anou (architects and craftsmen for Miya’s Blessing), Satoshi (martial Miya — warriors preserving the martial traditions of their controversial namesake).
