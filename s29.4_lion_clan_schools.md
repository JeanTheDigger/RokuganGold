## 29.4 Lion Clan Schools

**BASIC SCHOOLS:**

**Akodo Bushi [CR]:**

Precision over power. The Akodo deliberately avoid the specialization seen in other clans' schools, believing perfect execution of fundamentals makes further narrowing unnecessary. Every Akodo also receives foundational training in tactics and strategy. Techniques transform marginal successes into greater ones and at higher ranks convert failures into successes.

- Benefit: +1 Perception

- Skills: Battle (Mass Combat), Defense, Kenjutsu, Kyujutsu, Lore: History, Sincerity, any one Bugei or High Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — The Way of the Lion: Choose per attack — ignore the portion of the opponent’s Armor TN derived from Armor (Reduction still applies), or gain a Free Raise. Also gain +1k0 to all attack roll totals on your first melee attack against an opponent in a skirmish, or against any opponent who has declared a Raise on an attack against you since your last Turn (only once regardless of how many Raises they called).

- Rank 2 — Strength of Purity: Once per Turn during a skirmish, add your Honor Rank to the total of any single roll. Cannot be used on damage rolls or while in Center Stance.

- Rank 3 — Strength of My Ancestors: Attack as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action with Samurai keyword weapons.

- Rank 4 — Triumph Before Battle: Once per skirmish, designate an opponent during the Reactions Stage. During the following Round you may ignore all Armor TN bonuses that opponent gains from their Stance.

- Rank 5 — Akodo’s Final Lesson: When making Raises on any Bugei Skill Roll, if you meet or exceed the original TN but fail to meet the Raised TN, you still succeed — without the benefits of the Raises.

**Ikoma Bard [Courtier] [CR]:**

Argument by historical precedent. Unlike most courtiers who traffic in deception, the Ikoma omoidasu demonstrate desired outcomes by citing historical heroes and ancestral examples. In a society bound by tradition, few opponents can cleanly refute a courtier who demonstrates their ancestors would have disapproved of their current course. Techniques allow the Ikoma to inspire allies, resist manipulation, and spread Glory for others.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Courtier, Etiquette, Lore: History (Lion Clan), Perform: Storytelling, Sincerity (Honesty), any one High Skill, any one Bugei Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Traditional Clothing, Wakizashi, Knife, Calligraphy Set, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — The Herald of Glory: Gain Precise Memory Advantage at no XP cost (refunded if already possessed). May use Perform: Storytelling to publicly brag on behalf of another — rolling Perform: Storytelling/Awareness (TN 20) awards that person Glory equal to your School Rank, up to School Rank times per person per month.

- Rank 2 — The Heart of the Lion: No Honor or Glory loss for displaying emotion in public on behalf of another Lion or an honorable cause greater than yourself. Anyone attempting to sway you with Intimidation or Temptation adds +5 to their TN per School Rank.

- Rank 3 — The Voice of the Ancestors: Prior to a battle or skirmish, inspire allies by speaking for a few minutes and rolling Perform: Oratory/Awareness (TN 15 + 5 per person inspired; non-Lion add +5 each). Each inspired target may add their Honor Rank to one Skill Roll total during that battle. Unused by battle’s end, it is lost.

- Rank 4 — The Strength of Tradition: School Rank times per session, spend a Simple Action and roll Perform: Storytelling/Awareness (TN 25). On success, the next time that ally fails a roll that day, they may reroll it, adding your Honor Rank in unkept dice. If they fail the reroll, you lose 2 Honor.

- Rank 5 — Every Lion is Your Brother: Five times per session, when making a Contested Social Roll, cite historical precedent and add unkept dice equal to your Perform: Storytelling Rank. GM has final say on appropriateness.

**Ikoma Lion’s Shadow [Bushi] [EE]:**

The first Ikoma was ruthless and pragmatic — most modern Ikoma omit this from the record. The Lion’s Shadow are a small, secretive group who celebrate that memory. They sacrifice their own honor willingly for the clan, while the broader Lion Clan remains ignorant of their activities. They regard themselves as a practical foil to the machinations of the Scorpion and similar operators.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Courtier, Etiquette, Kenjutsu, Investigation (Notice), Sincerity, Stealth, any 1 High or Low Skill

- Honor: 3.5 | Outfit: Traditional Clothing, Daisho, any 1 weapon, Calligraphy Set, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — No Boundaries: No Honor loss for using Low Skills when used directly for the goals or glory of the Lion Clan (GM’s judgement). Free Action — target a number of opponents equal to School Rank; gain +1k0 to all attack rolls and Contested Rolls against those opponents. Activatable School Rank times per day; lasts until end of day.

- Rank 2 — The Lion Cannot Fail: +1k0 to all Skill Rolls using School Skills.

- Rank 3 — The Spirit of Ikoma: Once per Round, lose 3 Honor as a Free Action to gain +2k1 to attack, damage, and Contested Social Skill Rolls until end of Round.

- Rank 4 — The Quiet Lion’s Claws: Make melee weapon attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action.

- Rank 5 — Ferocious Determination: Spend a Void Point as a Free Action — Contested Courtier/Awareness vs opponent’s Etiquette (Courtesy)/Awareness. On success, opponent subtracts a penalty equal to your Awareness plus their own Honor Rank from all attack and Contested Social Skill Rolls against you for hours equal to your School Rank. Does not work against creatures with no Honor Rank.

**Kitsu Shugenja [CR]:**

Ancient rituals unchanged since the family’s founding. The Kitsu’s non-human origins give them unparalleled insight into the bonds between the living and ancestral realms. Their technique disrupts Spiritual Advantages possessed by opponents. A secondary focus on Battle-keyword spells makes them unusually effective on the battlefield for shugenja.

- Benefit: +1 Perception

- Skills: Battle, Calligraphy (Cipher), Etiquette, Lore: History, Lore: Theology, Spellcraft, any one High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, Knife, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Water / Fire | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Water, 2 Air, 1 Earth

- Technique — Eyes of the Ancestors: Make a Spell Casting Roll (as a Water spell) against a target’s Willpower ×5. On success, learn all Spiritual Advantages or Disadvantages that individual possesses. Spend a Void Point to negate any one Spiritual Advantage an opponent possesses for minutes equal to School Rank. Free Raise on any spell with the Battle keyword.

**Lion Elite Spearmen [Bushi] [NP]:**

A holdover from the dawn of the Empire, when the spear dominated tribal warfare. The Lion broadly shifted to the sword under Hantei Genji, but Matsu traditionalists preserved the spear tradition. Matsu Shujo formally organized their training. Maintained by the Matsu family, the school is considered a relic by other clans but remains a useful component of Lion armies.

- Benefit: +1 Reflexes

- Skills: Battle, Defense, Kenjutsu, Lore: History, Polearms, Spears (Magari-Yari), any one Bugei or High Skill

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Normal Clothing, Daisho, bundle of ten nage-yari, magari-yari, any one other spear or polearm, Light or Ashigaru Armor, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — The Way of Magari-Yarijutsu: +1k0 to all Spear and Polearm Skill Rolls. When wielding a spear or polearm in Center, Defense, or Full Defense Stance, gain Reduction equal to half your Skill Rank in the weapon.

- Rank 2 — Strike Like the Lion: When wielding a spear or polearm (melee or ranged), ignore enemy Reduction equal to half your current Honor Rank (rounded up).

- Rank 3 — Strike Through the Eagle: Ready/draw a nage-yari as a Free Action any number of times per Round. Ranged hand-hurled attacks with nage-yari are Simple Actions.

- Rank 4 — Strike Like the Wind: Make melee attacks as a Simple Action when wielding a spear or polearm.

- Rank 5 — Strike With the Soul: Extra Attack Maneuver costs only 3 Raises with spear or polearm. When hurling spears as missile weapons, make one attack as a Free Action.

**Matsu Beastmaster [Bushi]:**

Unique in all of Rokugan. Beastmasters bond with lion warcats not as tools but as equals — members of their own pride. Communication between Beastmaster and warcat approaches the mystical; a trained warcat can sense what her Beastmaster wants without words or gesture. Most Beastmasters prefer the company of their pride to that of other samurai. Only the Matsu, they insist, are strong enough to maintain Bushido under such pressures.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Animal Handling 2, Battle, Defense, Iaijutsu, Kenjutsu, any 1 Bugei Skill

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, 4 koku

- Rank 1 — One with the Pride: Bond with one lion warcat — it accompanies you everywhere and follows basic commands without compulsion. No lion will attack you without being compelled. Direct one warcat to Attack as a Complex Action. Any warcat not attacking uses Guard to protect you.

- Rank 2 — Heart of the Beast: A second lion joins your pride. You and all companion lions gain Swift 2. All lions gain an additional Wound Rank (final progression: 36: +15, 60: Dead).

- Rank 3 — The Ferocity Within: When wielding a katana, wakizashi, magari-yari, nage-yari, any knife, or fighting unarmed, make melee attacks as a Simple Action. Direct one warcat to Attack as a Simple Action.

- Rank 4 — All as One: Pride members make two claw attacks per Round. You and all pride members gain +2k1 to damage rolls when fighting alongside one another. When you direct a warcat to Attack, it may also make a Simple Move Action that Round.

- Rank 5 — With the Soul of a Lion: A third lion joins your pride. All warcats gain +10 Armor TN, +2 Reduction, and +2 Fear.

**Matsu Berserker [Bushi] [CR]:**

Absolute, unwavering commitment to battle. A true Matsu has no fear of death and the Techniques embrace this completely — trading defensive positioning for overwhelming offensive power. An opponent who has been destroyed cannot retaliate. The Matsu Berserker is most dangerous in Full Attack Stance, and generates Fear effects that destabilize opponents and create easier targets for allies.

- Benefit: +1 Strength

- Skills: Battle, Jiujutsu, Kenjutsu (Katana), Kyujutsu, Lore: History, any two Bugei Skills

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Light or Heavy Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, Heavy Weapon or Polearm, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — The Lion’s Roar: Add Honor Rank to all damage rolls. When assuming Full Attack Stance, move an additional 5 feet per Turn beyond the Stance bonus (cannot exceed maximum movement).

- Rank 2 — Matsu’s Fury: When assuming Full Attack Stance, choose an opponent within 30’. If you hit them this Turn, they are frozen with fear and cannot take Move Actions. Each Reactions Stage, the affected opponent may roll Willpower (TN = damage dealt from your latest attack, ignoring Wound Rank penalties) to negate. Immune-to-Fear targets cannot be affected.

- Rank 3 — The Lion’s Charge: Make melee attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action.

- Rank 4 — Matsu’s Courage: Ignore TN penalties from Wound Levels equal to your Honor Rank — or twice your Honor Rank while in Full Attack Stance.

- Rank 5 — The Lion’s Victory: Once per encounter, after rolling damage, spend a Void Point as a Free Action. All kept dice explode — reroll each and add results to your damage total.

**ADVANCED SCHOOLS:**

**Akodo Tactical Master [Bushi]:**

The greatest strategists of Rokugan. Tactical Masters study every notable battle in the Empire’s history — both how the Lion succeeded and how enemies found weaknesses. They adapt and change plans to meet any eventuality and do not disdain knowledge from outside Rokugan’s borders if it helps prepare for future threats.

- Requirements: Water 4, Intelligence 5 | Skills: Battle (Mass Combat) 5, Games: Shogi 4 or Games: Go 4

- Rank 1 — The Eyes of the General: Reroll any one die each Battle Turn of Mass Combat. Also reroll any one die during an attack roll in which you called at least one Raise.

- Rank 2 — Malleable as the Sea: Spend a Void Point to choose any one Heroic Opportunity from the corebook to perform during a Mass Battle Turn (subject to GM approval).

- Rank 3 — The Soul of the Army: Spend a Void Point to gain +5k2 on any Battle Skill Roll (instead of the normal +1k1), or +2k2 on any Bugei Skill Roll (instead of +1k1).

**Kitsu Sodan-Senzo [Shugenja] [IH]:**

True-blooded Kitsu — those with direct blood lineage to the original non-human kitsu founders — develop powers far beyond ordinary Kitsu shugenja. They can project their souls into the spirit realms to interact directly with the dead. Some manifest a pale shadow of the original kitsu’s physical capabilities, a surprise that has proven fatal to many Lion enemies. Soul projection is regarded as the most sacred act the Kitsu perform.

- Requirements: Water 4, Awareness 3 | Skills: Lore: Spirit Realms 4, Meditation 5 | Other: Must be true-blooded Kitsu with ability to cast Water spells of Mastery Level 4 or higher

- Rank 1 — Senses of the Kitsu: Detect spirit portals within School Rank ×10 miles (GM rolls Spellcraft/Perception TN 25 secretly; automatic if within School Rank miles). Previous School Rank increases by 1 for casting purposes.

- Rank 2 — Hand of the Kitsu: Unarmed damage increases by +0k1 (spiritual talons). Roll Meditation (TN 30) to project your soul into Meido or Yomi to interact with ancestral spirits using Social Skill Rolls as normal.

- Rank 3 — Soul of the Kitsu: When projecting into spirit realms, bring one other person if they also succeed at the Meditation roll. Passenger sees and hears everything but cannot speak. Ancestral spirits can sense the passenger. Gain one additional rank in your previous School for casting purposes.

**The Lion’s Pride [Bushi] [CR]:**

One of the most feared military units in the Empire. Female Matsu samurai-ko only — they live, train, and fight together at all times. On the battlefield the Lion’s Pride seeks out and kills enemy generals, destroying their battlefield efficiency. Entry requirements are among the most demanding of any school in Rokugan.

- Requirements: Agility 5, Strength 5 | Skills: Battle 5, four weapon Skills at Rank 3 | Other: Honor Rank 6, female Matsu family member

- Rank 1 — The Fury of Matsu: Add +10 to Armor TN while in Full Attack Stance.

- Rank 2 — Paragon of Honor: Opponents with lower Honor Rank within 30’ may not reroll 10s. An opponent may ignore this effect for the encounter by spending 2 Void Points during the Reactions Stage of any Combat Round.

- Rank 3 — Matsu’s Technique: When an opponent declares a melee attack against you and you have not yet taken your Turn this Round, spend a Void Point as a Free Action to immediately make a Simple Action attack — resolving before their attack. You may only perform one Simple Action on your Turn this Round. Usable in Full Attack Stance.

**ALTERNATE PATHS:**

**Akodo Kensai [Bushi]:**

Absolute devotion to the katana above all other weapons. Thousands of hours of kata and iaijutsu until the blade is an extension of the body itself.

- Replaces: Any Lion Bushi 4. Requires: Iaijutsu 4, Kenjutsu 5, Prodigy Advantage (may waive Prodigy by increasing all other requirements by 1).

- Technique — The Heart of the Sword: While using a sword in Attack Stance, increase Armor TN by Honor Rank against the first melee attack of each Combat Round. Once per skirmish, activate a Kata as a Free Action.

**Akodo Siege Strategist [Bushi]:**

Founded by Akodo Keiichi. Specialized siege assault training — less flexible than the Kaiu Engineers but fully capable in its specific role. The Lion’s rapid capture of fortifications like Shiro no Yogin over the centuries is largely credited to these strategists.

- Replaces: Akodo Bushi 4 or Akodo Tactical Master 2. Requires: Battle (Mass Battle) 6, Engineering (Siege) 4.

- Technique — Broken by Tactics: When on the attacking side of a siege, always receive a Heroic Opportunity when rolling on the Mass Battle Table regardless of the normal result. Opposing structures suffer a 50% Reduction penalty (applied after any defensive bonuses).

**Bishamon’s Chosen [Shugenja] [CR]:**

Kitsu shugenja who march into battle alongside their bushi brethren. Their fervor and devotion to the Celestial Order remains pure whether in a temple or in the chaos of battle.

- Replaces: Kitsu Shugenja 3. Requires: Battle 3.

- Technique — Scion of Strength: Expend a spell slot as a Free Action to gain +1k0 to any Strength-based roll including melee damage, or +1k0 to the Spell Casting Roll of any spell with the Battle descriptor.

**Deathseeker [Bushi] [CR]:**

When a Lion bushi shames his ancestors, seppuku is the typical path. The Lion offer another: the Deathseekers, lightly armored warriors who charge headlong into battle seeking redemption through death. Most do not survive a year. They pray to Nagiroko-to — the Fortune of Forgiveness — that their heroic deaths will earn the forgiveness of their ancestors.

- Replaces: Any Lion Bushi School 1. Requires: Honor Rank 5, Dishonored Disadvantage.

- Technique — Honor of the Lion: Gain the Benefit, Skills, Honor, and Outfit of a Rank 1 Lion bushi (any Lion school). Add Honor Rank to the total of one damage roll per Turn; if you do, Armor TN is reduced by 5 until the next Round’s Reactions Stage. +1k0 to attack rolls while in Full Attack Stance.

**De Bellis Legionnaire [Bushi]:**

Born from the Lion’s acquisition and translation of De Bellis Yoditorum — an ancient treatise on the Yodotai, a vast warrior culture far north and west of Rokugan. The Lion study the text with a mixture of appreciation and disgust, training units to both employ and counter its tactics. Some scholars are troubled by references implying the book is part of a larger series.

- Replaces: Akodo Bushi 4, Matsu Berserker 4. Requires: Battle 3, Lore: Gaijin (Yodotai) 1.

- Technique — I Stand with My Brothers: At the start of a skirmish, roll Battle/Awareness (TN 15) as a Free Action. On success, gain unkept bonus dice equal to the number of allies in your group plus one per Raise (max twice School Rank total; max School Rank on any one roll). Use on attack or damage rolls, or on Defense Skill Rolls when declaring Full Defense. Unused dice are lost at end of skirmish.

**Disciples of the River [Shugenja]:**

Founded by Kitsu Sonosuke for talented Kitsu shugenja who lack the pure bloodlines for Sodan-Senzo or the militant spirit for Bishamon’s Chosen. They venerate the spirits of the Empire’s rivers and their power serves the clan from irrigating rice paddies to moving troops safely along waterways.

- Replaces: Kitsu Shugenja 3. Requires: Way of the Land Advantage for an area with a river.

- Technique — Servant of the River: When casting a Water spell while standing within one hundred feet of a naturally occurring river, gain one Free Raise on that spell.

**Ikoma Historians [Courtier]:**

Elite specialists charged with supervising the Empire’s official histories — a duty they regard as sacred. They use access to historical secrets to protect both Imperial and Lion interests, and most members of the hidden Ikoma-Shosuro alliance are drawn from their ranks.

- Replaces: Ikoma Bard 3, Ikoma Lion’s Shadow 3. Requires: Lore: History 5.

- Technique — The Past and the Present: Acquire the Forbidden Knowledge (Empire’s True History) Advantage. Free Raise on all Calligraphy rolls. Roll Lore: History/Intelligence (TN 25) to accurately recount any incident witnessed or story heard, including details missed at the time.

**Ikoma Orator [Courtier]:**

Adaptation of the Lion battlecry tradition for courts — credited to Ikoma Tasuki during the Gozoku era, whose speeches on behalf of Empress Yugozohime helped break the conspiracy.

- Replaces: Ikoma Bard 3. Requires: Sincerity 4, Lore: Bushido 3, Perform: Oratory 4.

- Technique — The Voice of Bushido: Give a speech of at least five minutes before a court audience. Targeting an ally: Perform: Oratory/Awareness (TN 30) — on success, grant that ally one Paragon of Bushido Advantage for one week. Targeting an enemy: same roll if unopposed, or Contested Perform: Oratory/Awareness vs Sincerity/Awareness if they respond — on success, inflict one Failure of Bushido Disadvantage for one week (or deny the corresponding Paragon Advantage if held). Cannot affect the same person twice in one month.

**Ikoma Scrapper [Bushi]:**

Ikoma was not only a storyteller but a vicious hand-to-hand fighter. The Lion’s Shadow preserves his less reputable legacy, but this public dojo teaches a simpler, more honorable technique inspired by the same source.

- Replaces: Akodo Bushi 4, Matsu Berserker 4, Ikoma Lion’s Shadow 4, Ikoma Bard 4. Requires: Strength 4, Jiujutsu 5.

- Technique — Every Scar Has a Name: Attack as a Simple Action with unarmed strikes and improvised weapons (if not already possessed). Once per skirmish, when striking unarmed or with an improvised weapon, call two Raises to inflict a Fear effect on the target with Rank equal to your Insight Rank.

**Ikoma Warden [Bushi]:**

The finest horsemen in the Lion Clan. Ikoma Wardens patrol Lion provinces along established routes with internal magistrate status. They routinely supply cavalry support to Akodo and Matsu forces, making their active numbers far larger than expected for a patrol and law enforcement unit.

- Replaces: Akodo Bushi 2 or Ikoma Lion’s Shadow 2. Requires: Horsemanship 3.

- Technique — To Race the Wind: While Mounted, +1k0 to all Bugei Skill Rolls. When spending a Void Point on a Horsemanship or Investigation Skill Roll, gain +2k2 instead of +1k1.

**Kitsu Spirit Legion [Shugenja]:**

Formed in the twelfth century. A rare handful of Kitsu shugenja who can reach across the spirit realms and summon spirits to serve. The ability cannot be taught — one must be born to it.

- Replaces: Kitsu Shugenja 4. Requires: Must be born with the spiritual gifts required — a character development and roleplay constraint discussed between player and GM.

- Technique — The Legions of the Dead: Complex Action — expend 2 spell slots of one chosen Element and spend a Void Point. A spirit warrior appears with the Spirit trait; all Rings at 2 except the sacrificed Element’s Ring (which starts at 3). The spirit has Kenjutsu 3, Defense 2, spectral daisho and light armor. Remains in the mortal realm for hours equal to your Kitsu Shugenja School Rank or until dismissed.

**Lion Paragon [Bushi]:**

The Paragons strive to be the ideal samurai, well known throughout the clan with their techniques passed down across generations. Honor is not merely philosophical here — it is a direct combat multiplier.

- Replaces: Any Lion Bushi 3. Requires: Void Ring 4, Kenjutsu 5, Honor Rank 7.

- Technique — Pure and Dedicated: Make melee attacks as a Simple Action, but only one attack per Turn (no Extra Attack Maneuver). Once per skirmish, spend a Void Point as a Free Action — your Honor Rank counts as double for all other School Techniques for the remainder of the encounter. Does not stack with other Honor Rank modifiers; use the highest.

**Lion Scout [Bushi]:**

Akodo’s treatise on warfare emphasized that knowledge determines battle before it begins. Hiding is dishonorable, but Lion Scouts accept this necessary sacrifice for the clan. Trained by both Akodo and Ikoma traditions.

- Replaces: Any Lion Bushi 2. Requires: Battle 2, Hunting 3.

- Technique — Shadow Unseen: Move at a normal rate while using the Stealth Skill. +1k0 to Stealth Skill Rolls and all Agility-based Bugei Skills.

**Lion Tactician (Ikoma Tactician) [Bushi]:**

The Akodo War College also offers specialized training to lower-ranking officers — gunso and chui — to enhance smaller unit performance. During the Akodo disbandment era many Ikoma trained in this technique, generating the alternate name Ikoma Tactician.

- Replaces: Any Lion Bushi 4, Ikoma Bard 4. Requires: Battle 4, War Fan 4, must be at least Gunso in rank.

- Technique — The Commander’s Fan: +1k1 to Battle (Mass Battle) rolls when your war-fan aids in conveying orders (GM’s discretion). While carrying a war-fan, add half your War Fans Skill Rank (rounded up) to Armor TN. In Defense or Full Defense Stance, or when carrying only a war-fan, add full Skill Rank instead. Does not stack; two war-fans do not double the bonus.

**Lioness Legion [Bushi]:**

Founded centuries ago by a legendary Matsu sensei. The Lioness Legion combines the speed of cavalry with the ferocity of the Matsu — those who have faced them describe it as nightmarish. Composed primarily of young women chosen for natural athleticism and trained ruthlessly. A male equivalent, the Lion’s Mane, has begun to take the field.

- Replaces: Matsu Berserker 2, Akodo Bushi 2, or Matsu Beastmaster 2. Requires: Athletics 3.

- Technique — Charge of the Pride: While in Full Attack Stance, all movement during the Round gains an additional 5 feet (once per Round only). As a Complex Move Action, move Water Ring × 25 feet.
