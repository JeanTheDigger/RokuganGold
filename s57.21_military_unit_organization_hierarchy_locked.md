## 57.21 Military Unit Organization Hierarchy --- LOCKED

This section formalises the organizational belonging of military units. Section 11.3.18 defines the person-to-person command chain through operational_superior_id (Chui → Taisa → Shireikan → Rikugunshokan). This section defines the unit-to-unit organizational structure — which Company belongs to which Legion, which Legion belongs to which Army. These are separate concepts: operational_superior_id tracks who commands whom, the unit hierarchy tracks which units are organizationally grouped together. A Company can be geographically separated from its parent Legion (garrisoned at a remote fort) while remaining organizationally part of that Legion.

**57.21.1 Unit Entities**

Five organizational levels exist as tracked entities in the simulation. Kaisha (Company): the base tactical unit. 7 squadrons (guntai), ~153 officers and soldiers. Commanded by a Chui (named NPC) and a command staff of 5 additional officers. Has its own Company stat block (Health, Attack, Defense, Morale, Morale Defense). Fields: company_id, parent_legion_id, commander_id (Chui character_id), current_location_id (settlement or sub-tile), deployment_status (enum: WITH_LEGION, GARRISONED, DETACHED, ON_CAMPAIGN), is_reserve (bool). Yobihei Kaisha (Reserve Company): a smaller specialized company of 6 squadrons containing shugenja trained in military applications, engineers, scouts, or cavalry. Commanded by its own Chui and command staff. Every Legion has exactly one Reserve Company alongside its 6 regular Companies. Uses the same data structure with is_reserve = true. Daibutai (Legion): 6 regular Companies + 1 Reserve Company (Yobihei Kaisha) = 7 units, ~750 troops. Commanded by a Taisa. The largest autonomous unit in an army — the primary operational formation. Companies are sometimes deployed as discrete units for garrison or detachment, but for administrative purposes they are always grouped together as legions. Legions are typically stationed at the same duty post for extended periods. Fields: legion_id, parent_section_id (links to the Shireikan’s section), commander_id (Taisa character_id), home_province_id (where the Legion musters), constituent_companies (array of 7 company_ids — 6 regular + 1 reserve). Go-hatamoto (Army): the largest military organization maintained by the Great Clans. A standard go-hatamoto consists of up to 48 legions, divided into sections or wings commanded by Shireikan (4–12 legions per Shireikan). Every Great Clan has at least one standing army; the larger and more martial clans (Lion, Crab) can maintain three or four simultaneously. Fields: army_id, clan_id, commander_id (Rikugunshokan character_id), constituent_sections (array of section_ids). Section (Wing): the intermediate organizational level between Legion and Go-hatamoto. A Shireikan commands one section of 4–12 legions. The section is a tracked entity. Fields: section_id, parent_army_id (links to the Go-hatamoto), commander_id (Shireikan character_id), constituent_legions (array of legion_ids). The full organizational chain is therefore: Kaisha (parent_legion_id) → Daibutai (parent_section_id) → Section (parent_army_id) → Go-hatamoto (clan_id) → Clan. Every unit at every level has exactly one parent. No unit is orphaned. No level is skipped. The Rikugunshokan commands the entire go-hatamoto. It is not uncommon for family daimyo or Clan Champions to serve as Rikugunshokan; daimyo or Champions who are not inclined toward military command may appoint a Rikugunshokan who answers directly to them. Typically a clan has between one and three samurai of this rank at any given time.

**57.21.2 Organizational Belonging vs Deployment**

A Company always belongs to exactly one Legion (parent_legion_id is never null for a standing Company). A Legion always belongs to exactly one Section (parent_section_id is never null). A Section always belongs to exactly one Go-hatamoto (parent_army_id is never null). Deployment does not change belonging — it changes location and status. When a Company is garrisoned at a remote fort, its deployment_status is GARRISONED and its current_location_id is the fort, but its parent_legion_id still points to its Legion. The Company is organizationally part of that Legion even though it is geographically elsewhere. When a Taisa issues a recall (muster order), all Companies with that parent_legion_id receive the order regardless of their current location. Garrisoned Companies must travel back to the Legion’s muster point. DETACHED status means the Company has been temporarily assigned to another commander — for example, lent to an ally or assigned to a different Taisa for a specific campaign. The Company’s parent_legion_id does not change (it still belongs to its original Legion), but its operational chain temporarily routes through the borrowing commander. When the detachment ends, it returns to its parent Legion. WITH_LEGION means the Company is physically with its Legion at the Legion’s current location. This is the default state. ON_CAMPAIGN means the Company is deployed as part of its Legion in an active military operation — moving toward an objective, besieging, or fighting.

**57.21.3 Commander Assignment and Vacancy**

Each unit has a commander_id pointing to the named NPC who commands it. The person-to-person command chain (operational_superior_id) mirrors the unit-to-unit organizational chain (parent IDs) at every level. Chui’s operational_superior_id = the Taisa who commands their Company’s parent Legion (via parent_legion_id → legion.commander_id). Taisa’s operational_superior_id = the Shireikan who commands their Legion’s parent Section (via parent_section_id → section.commander_id). Shireikan’s operational_superior_id = the Rikugunshokan who commands their Section’s parent Go-hatamoto (via parent_army_id → army.commander_id). Rikugunshokan’s operational_superior_id = null. They receive objectives from their lord_id (Clan Champion or Family Daimyo). The Rikugunshokan is the top of the military operational chain — above them is the feudal chain, not the military chain. When a unit is reassigned to a different parent (a Company detached to a different Legion, a Legion transferred to a different Section), the commander’s operational_superior_id updates to match the new parent unit’s commander. The person chain always follows the unit chain. When a commander dies, is captured, or is reassigned, the unit’s commander_id becomes null — the position is vacant. A vacant command triggers the FILL_VACANCY NeedType (Section 57.20.3) on the lord with appointment authority. Until filled, the unit operates at reduced effectiveness: a Company without a Chui loses the commander Battle bonus from Section 11.6. A Legion without a Taisa cannot execute coordinated manoeuvres (no flanking bonus for Legion-mate Companies). A Reserve Company without a Chui loses its specialist function entirely. A Go-hatamoto section without a Shireikan cannot initiate new campaigns — it holds position until a new commander is appointed.

**57.21.4 Commander Location Tracking**

A unit commander’s current_settlement_id always matches their unit’s current_location_id. When a Company is garrisoned at a fort, the Chui is at that fort. When a Legion moves to a new province, the Taisa moves with it. This is automatic — the commander does not spend AP to travel with their unit. They are inseparable from their command unless they are personally reassigned (which removes them from command and triggers a vacancy). A Chui who is personally ordered to court by their daimyo must relinquish command of their Company — they cannot command a Company in Province A while attending court in Province B. The lord must either appoint a temporary replacement or accept the Company operating without a named commander.

**57.21.5 Fleet Organization**

Ships use the same organizational hierarchy. A ship is a Company (ship Company stats in Section 11.9). Ships are grouped into Squadrons (equivalent to a Legion, 3–8 ships under a fleet captain). Squadrons are grouped into a Fleet (equivalent to a Go-hatamoto section). The same parent_legion_id / parent_army_id pattern applies. Most clans have minimal naval organization — a handful of Kobune with no formal squadron structure. The Mantis and Crab have standing fleets with formal squadron organization. A ship’s deployment_status follows the same enum: WITH_LEGION (with its squadron), GARRISONED (assigned to a port), DETACHED (lent to another commander), ON_CAMPAIGN (deployed for naval operations).
