## 29.14 Miscellaneous Schools — Special Appointments, Gaijin & Cross-Clan Paths

Schools and paths available across clan lines, by Imperial appointment, through rare gaijin training, or through membership in cross-clan organizations. Sources: [CR] Core Rulebook, [IH] Imperial Histories, [EE] Enemies of the Empire, [BoV] Book of the Void, [SE] Secrets of the Empire.

**BASIC SCHOOLS:**

**Kenku Swordsman School [Bushi]:**

The Kenku are extremely discerning regarding their techniques. A human student must either prove his worth to a Kenku sensei or be selected by one to receive training. This school teaches the student to harness the speed of wind and the fury of lightning, striking with the unpredictability of a storm.

- Benefit: +1 Reflexes

- Skills: Defense, Hunting, Kenjutsu (Katana), Meditation, Tea Ceremony, any one Lore Skill, any one Bugei Skill

- Honor: N/A (Kenku do not use the standard Honor system)

- Outfit: Light or Heavy Armor, Daisho, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — Wing of Thunder: Add the total of your Reflexes and School Rank to your Armor TN against any enemy with lower Initiative.

- Rank 2 — Heaven’s Claw: Any time you inflict more than 10 points of damage with a single attack, your opponent loses one Void Point. Against opponents with no Void Points remaining (or no Void), re-roll a single damage die and take the better result.

- Rank 3 — Lightning Kiss: You may attack as a Simple Action while using any melee weapon in which you possess a Skill Emphasis.

- Rank 4 — Churning Skies: If you delay your Turn until after all opponents have acted, ignore all Wound Penalties (including Down and Out) and spend any amount of Void Points on damage rolls this Round.

- Rank 5 — The Same Breeze: At the beginning of your Turn, spend a Void Point as a Free Action to copy one bushi Technique of any character within 20’ for a number of Rounds equal to your Air Ring. You must abide by all costs and restrictions of the copied Technique. If not a Kenku yourself, you must have seen the Technique used at least once. You may only copy one Technique at a time.

**Kshatriya Warrior [Bushi] — Ivory Kingdoms:**

The standard fighting school of the Ivory Kingdoms’ kshatriya warrior caste, whose social role was roughly equivalent to a samurai. The kshatriya are now largely extinct, but a few survivors still carry on their fighting traditions. Under normal circumstances available only to Ivinda natives of the kshatriya caste, although some techniques are being studied by the Rokugani group calling itself the Rajya ke Varisa.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Archery, Defense, Hand-to-Hand, Horsemanship, Lore: Theology (Ivory Kingdoms), Spears, Swordsmanship, any one appropriate High Skill

- Honor: 5.0 (Integrity)

- Outfit: Sword, shield, spear, bow, light armor, steed, elegant clothing, 10 koku (equivalent)

- Rank 1 — The Strength of Indra: Gain +1k0 with all School Weapon Skills. When rolling to resist a Fear effect, your Willpower is considered one Rank higher.

- Rank 2 — The Ward of Vishnu: While carrying a shield, take a Simple Action to make a Contested Defense / Agility roll against one opponent’s Weapon Skill / Agility. If successful, that opponent must call three extra Raises to strike you until the start of your next Turn.

- Rank 3 — The Speed of the Mongoose: Make melee attacks as a Simple Action when fighting with a sword, spear, or unarmed.

- Rank 4 — The Virtue of the Gods: Once per skirmish after a successful attack, spend a Void Point to add unkept dice equal to your Lore: Theology (Ivory Kingdoms) rank to your damage roll.

- Rank 5 — The Courage of Shiva: Gain +1k1 to resist Fear effects. If reduced to Out or Dead, on your next Turn you may take one final Simple Action which suffers no Wound penalties.

**Sainika Fighter [Guru] — Ivory Kingdoms:**

The martial arts tradition of the Ivory Kingdoms, dating back before the land was unified by the Maharajah. Maintained by the gurus — wise men of the brahmin caste — who saw it as religious devotion as much as fighting art. Since the fall of the Kingdoms, Sainika has been fading as the remaining gurus age and die without students. The handful of surviving gurus are actively seeking students to preserve their techniques.

- Benefit: +1 Void

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Hand-to-Hand, Knives, Lore: Theology (Ivory Kingdoms), Medicine, Meditation, any one High Skill

- Honor: 6.0 (Integrity)

- Outfit: Simple clothing, knife, sandals

- Special: Students of Sainika are expected to take up an Ascetic lifestyle.

- Rank 1 — Harmony: Add your Void Rank to your Armor TN. Add your Void Rank in unkept dice to any Contested Roll to resist Social Skill Rolls, Social Techniques, and Fear effects.

- Rank 2 — Vision: Spend a Void Point and take a Simple Action to assess your opponent. For the remainder of the skirmish, gain +Xk0 to attack rolls against that opponent, where X is the opponent’s lowest Trait.

- Rank 3 — Understanding: Roll additional dice on all Perception-based rolls equal to your Void Rank.

- Rank 4 — Unity: Make attacks as a Simple Action when fighting unarmed or with a knife.

- Rank 5 — Purity: When attacking with an unarmed blow or knife, spend a Void Point to also strike the opponent’s spiritual being. If your attack hits, make a Contested Void roll vs the opponent. On success, the opponent suffers -Xk0 (where X is your Void Rank) on Skill Rolls until the third Reactions Stage from now. Your attack still deals normal physical damage. Spiritual effects cannot be stacked, though duration can be extended.

**ADVANCED SCHOOLS:**

**Dragon Channeler [BoV]:**

Reserved for shugenja of supreme elemental mastery who are personally chosen by the Elemental Dragons or trained by an existing Dragon Channeler.

- Technique Rank: 6

- Replaces: N/A (must be a Rank 5 shugenja)

- Requirements: At least one Ring at Rank 5+, at least two other Rings at Rank 4+, Meditation 5, Spellcraft 5, Lore: Theology 5

- Special: A character can only become a Dragon Channeler if personally chosen by the Elemental Dragons or personally trained by an existing Dragon Channeler. GMs may connect this to the Chosen by the Oracles Advantage (four Dragon Spell Slots rather than three if applicable).

- Technique — Beseech the Dragons: Lose any Affinities and Deficiencies, gaining instead an Affinity for Dragon Spells. Normal spell slots are reduced by 2 in each Ring; in return, gain three Dragon Spell Slots usable only to invoke the powers of the Dragons. Spend Void Points to cast additional Dragon spells when slots are exhausted. Dragon spell slots do not recover from rest — regain one slot only through two hours of uninterrupted meditation at Meditation / Void TN 30.

**Kolat Master [EoE]:**

The Ten Kolat Masters are among the most devious and ruthless individuals in the Empire. Only ten exist at any one time, achieving position by rising through the conspiracy ranks or forging alliances with other Kolat.

- Technique Rank: 6 (may also be taken at Insight Ranks above 6)

- Replaces: Any Technique (or none) at Insight Rank 6 or higher

- Requirements — Rings/Traits: Two mental Traits at 5 or higher

- Requirements — Skills: Courtier (Manipulation) 5, Intimidation (Control) 4

- Requirements — Other: Must be a member of the Kolat conspiracy and chosen to lead one of the ten Sects. Cannot be Tainted or corrupted by the Nothing.

- Technique — Will of the Master: You are Status 10 to any character who is a Kolat member of your Sect, and Status 9 to any Kolat of another Sect. Gain +2k0 on all Courtier and Intimidation Skill rolls.

**ALTERNATE PATHS — IMPERIAL APPOINTMENTS:**

**The Amethyst Champion [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any — may voluntarily take upon advancing in Rank or wait until a later Rank

- Requirements: Courtier only. Must have won the Amethyst Championship (Section 11.5).

- Technique — The Emperor’s Voice: The Amethyst Champion brings the voice of the Emperor to those who cannot attend Imperial Court. As a Free Action, spend a Void Point to temporarily gain the ability to use any courtier Technique that has been used against you in the past seven days.

**The Emerald Champion [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any — may voluntarily take upon advancing in Rank or wait until a later Rank

- Requirements: Bushi only. Must have won the Test of the Emerald Champion (Section 11.5).

- Technique — The Emperor’s Hand: Gain +1k1 on all High and Bugei Skill Rolls and an additional +1k0 on all School Skills. Gain bonus Void Points per day equal to your highest Ring; these may be granted to any one subordinate or ally in line of sight as a Free Action (once per Round). You may not use these bonus Void Points yourself.

**Emerald Magistrate [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any Bushi, Courtier, or Shugenja School, Rank 4

- Requirements: Investigation 3, Lore: Law 3; must receive an Imperial appointment as Emerald Magistrate

- Special: Members of the Imperial families may ignore one Skill Rank requirement. GMs may allow this Path at Rank 3 or 5 for schools that already grant Simple Action attacks at Rank 4.

- Technique — Honor Is My Shield: Gain Reduction equal to half your Honor Rank (rounded up). This stacks with Reduction from all other sources including armor.

**The Jade Champion [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any — may voluntarily take upon advancing in Rank or wait until a later Rank

- Requirements: Shugenja only. Must have won the Test of the Jade Champion (Section 11.5).

- Technique — The Emperor’s Will: Gain a Free Raise on all Spell Casting Rolls. When confronting an opponent who practices outlawed magic (maho, yobanjin shamanism, gaijin sorcery, etc.) or possesses corrupt supernatural abilities (Shadowlands Taint or corruption by the Lying Darkness), gain an additional Free Raise on your Spell Casting Roll.

**Jade Legionnaire [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any Bushi or Shugenja School, Rank 2

- Requirements: Glory 2+; must receive an appointment to the Jade Legion

- Technique — Purity in Purpose & Deed: If your Honor Rank is higher than your opponent’s, add the difference to all attack and damage rolls against that opponent. All creatures and opponents without an Honor Rank are considered to have Honor Rank 0.

**Jade Magistrate [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any Bushi, Courtier, or Shugenja School, Rank 4

- Requirements: Lore: Law 3, Spellcraft 3; must receive an Imperial appointment as Jade Magistrate

- Special: Members of the Imperial families may ignore one Skill Rank requirement.

- Technique — Scent of the Kami: Make a Perception / Spellcraft roll to identify any spells cast in an area during the past 48 hours. TN equals the basic TN to cast the spell (Mastery Level x 5, plus 5).

**Imperial Legionnaire [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any Bushi School, Rank 2

- Requirements: Glory 2+; must receive an appointment to the Imperial Legions

- Technique — Strength of the Empire: When fighting alongside other Imperial Legionnaires, or alongside Emerald or Jade Magistrates, when you spend a Void Point to enhance a roll you also gain a bonus to the roll equal to the number of allied legionnaires and/or magistrates within 25 feet.

**Imperial Explorers:**

- Technique Rank: 2

- Replaces: Any Bushi School at Rank 2

- Requirements: Lore: Cartography 3

- Technique — Seeking New Paths: Gain Absolute Direction if you did not already possess it. Gain +1k0 to all Bugei Skill Rolls in a region for which you possess the Way of the Land Advantage. After spending at least two days in any city or province-sized area, make an Intelligence / Lore: Cartography roll (TN 20) to create a map of that region. Anyone who possesses the map gains the Way of the Land Advantage for that region as long as they keep the map.

**The Ruby Champion [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any — may voluntarily take upon advancing in Rank or wait until a later Rank

- Requirements: Bushi only. Must have won the Test of the Ruby Champion (Section 11.5).

- Technique — Master of the Dojo: When fighting alongside allies under your command, spend a Void Point as a Complex Action to allow any one ally within line of sight to use one of your Techniques as if that ally were a member of your School at equivalent rank. This effect lasts until the start of your next Turn.

**The Topaz Champion [CR]:**

- Replaces: Any — may voluntarily take upon advancing in Rank or wait until a later Rank

- Requirements: Must have won the Topaz Championship

- Technique — Soul of Promise: When you advance in Insight Rank and into this Path, you gain the Technique it would normally replace. You also gain a number of bonus Void Points per day equal to your highest Ring. These may be spent like normal Void Points but only to enhance School Skill Rolls.

**Second City Guardsman [Bushi]:**

- Technique Rank: 2

- Replaces: Any Bushi School at Rank 2

- Requirements: Defense 4, any one Weapon Skill at 4

- Technique — The Governor’s Armor: Your Status Rank is considered one higher throughout the Colonial territories. When fighting within the Second City or another city-sized Colonial settlement while in Defense or Full Defense Stance, if your opponent attacks and misses you before your Turn for the Round, you may immediately change your Stance to Attack (losing all Armor TN bonuses from your previous Stance).

**Serpent Hunter [Bushi]:**

A small sect of warriors in the Colonies dedicated to the eradication of the feral Nagah. Frequently sought out by explorers and merchants requiring protection in remote jungle regions.

- Technique Rank: Variable

- Replaces: Any Bushi School Rank that awards an attack as a Simple Action

- Requirements: Lore: Naga 3, Hunting 3

- Technique — Blood of the Serpent: Make attacks as a Simple Action with Samurai-quality melee weapons. When facing a Naga opponent of any kind, make attacks as a Simple Action with any weapon.

**ALTERNATE PATHS — CONSPIRACY ****&**** POLITICAL:**

**The Bloodspeaker Technique:**

The secret methods of the Bloodspeaker Cult, pioneered by Iuchiban himself and passed through his chief apprentice Yajinden. Only high-ranking Bloodspeakers who understand that Maho is the cult’s true source of power are allowed to learn this. The Bloodspeakers make no distinction between social caste — they have taught their secrets to samurai of all types, peasants, and even eta.

- Rank: Any

- Replaces: Any

- Requirements: Can only be learned from a senior member of the Bloodspeaker Cult

- Special: Can be taught to anyone, samurai or commoner. If learned at Rank 1, replaces the Rank 1 Technique and reduces starting Honor to 1.0 (does not affect Outfit or starting Skills).

- Technique — Iuchiban’s Method: Learn two Maho spells of your choice and gain a Free Raise for Spell Casting Rolls with Maho spells. When casting a Maho spell, reduce the Taint you gain by 1 for every 2 additional Wounds you take (minimum 1 point of Taint). If using someone else’s blood to power the spell, you may instead transfer the Taint onto that person at the rate of 1 point per 2 extra Wounds inflicted — reducing your own Taint gain to 0.

**Agents of the Second Gozoku:**

- Technique Rank: 2

- Replaces: Any School 2

- Requirements: Honor Rank 4 or less; must have Obligation to the Second Gozoku or its leader

- Technique — To Shield the Empress: The Second Gozoku believes they do what they must to protect the Empress from the harsh realities of rule. Whenever you take an action that would result in Honor loss, if by taking this action you spare Honor or Glory loss to someone of higher Status Rank than you, the loss is halved (rounded down).

**Chrysanthemum Conspirator:**

- Technique Rank: 2

- Replaces: Any Bushi or Courtier School at Rank 2

- Requirements: Must have the Disadvantage Dark Secret (Rebel)

- Note: Historically relevant to the reign of the Steel Chrysanthemum. GMs may adapt for any campaign involving rebellion against a tyrant.

- Technique — No Course but One: Those who conspire against the Steel Chrysanthemum place greater value on Honor than on social status or Imperial authority. Gain a Free Raise on any attack roll made against an opponent whose Status Rank exceeds their Honor Rank.

**Gozoku Agent [Courtier]:**

- Technique Rank: 2

- Replaces: Any Basic School at Rank 2

- Requirements: Must have the Forbidden Knowledge (Gozoku) Advantage

- Note: Technique dies out after the Gozoku’s purge but is revived by Bayushi Atsuki upon his return through Oblivion’s Gate. GMs may also use it for any multi-clan political conspiracy such as the Kolat.

- Technique — Overwhelming Presence: The agents of the Gozoku conspiracy were feared for the power they could bring to bear on any who dared oppose them in court. Add your Intimidation Skill rank to the total of all Social Skill Rolls you make against individuals who are not members of the Gozoku.

**Order of the Stone Crab [Bushi]:**

Followers of Hida Tsuneo, the Steel Chrysanthemum’s personal champion, who committed horrific atrocities in loyal service to his Emperor. The Technique did not long survive Tsuneo’s death — all known members were dead within a month from seppuku or execution. GMs may adapt this Path for ruthless followers of any villain or anti-hero in other eras.

- Technique Rank: 4

- Replaces: Any Bushi School (of appropriate clan) at Rank 4

- Requirements: Must have an Honor Rank lower than 5.0

- Technique — Scorn the Weak: When attacking human opponents, gain +1k1 to attack rolls for each Physical Disadvantage they possess.
