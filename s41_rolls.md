# 41. Rolls

**SKILLS:**

Skills represent what a character learns through training and experience, ranked 1–10. Rank 1 = introduced to the most basic principles. Rank 10 = one of the greatest masters on the planet (or a supernatural being). When a Skill Roll is called for, it lists the Skill first, then the Trait: e.g. Athletics/Agility means roll (Athletics Rank + Agility Trait) dice and keep (Agility Trait) dice.

**RAISES:**

When a player declares a Raise, they voluntarily increase the TN of the task by +5 per Raise. Common uses include combat Maneuvers and creative actions. Maximum Raises per roll = Void Ring.

- Free Raises: Grant the benefit of a Raise without increasing the TN, and do not count toward the maximum. May also be used to reduce the TN by 5 instead.

- Risk: If Raises are declared and the final roll fails to meet the new TN, the roll fails — even if the original TN would have been met.

**TYPES OF ROLLS:**

**Skill Rolls**

The most common roll. Roll (Trait Rank + Skill Rank) dice, keep (Trait Rank) dice. Example: Reflexes 3 + Kyujutsu 4 = roll 7 dice, keep 3.

**Trait Rolls**

Used when attempting a task based solely on innate ability, without benefit from training (e.g. holding one’s breath, holding onto a moving wagon, memorizing material quickly). Roll and keep dice equal to Trait Rank.

**Ring Rolls**

Uncommon. Roll and keep dice equal to Ring Rank. Typically involve magical or supernatural effects (e.g. resist a spell, roll Earth to resist Shadowlands Taint).

**Damage Rolls**

Made after a successful attack. Every weapon has a Damage Rating (DR) formatted as Xk Y.

- Melee weapons: Add Strength to the rolled dice of the DR. Example: Strength 3 + dai-tsuchi DR 5k2 = roll 8k2.

- Bows: The bow itself has a Strength rating added to the rolled dice of the arrow’s DR. Example: Yumi Strength 3 + willow leaf arrow DR 2k2 = roll 5k2.

- Unarmed: Base DR is 0k1. Roll Strength dice, keep 1.

**Spell Casting Rolls**

Roll (Shugenja School Rank + relevant Ring) dice, keep (relevant Ring) dice. Example: Fire 3 + Rank 1 Isawa Shugenja = roll 4 dice, keep 3.

**Contested Rolls**

Two characters roll in direct opposition; only one can succeed. Both make the designated roll and compare results — higher total wins. Any roll type can be contested. Ties where a tie is not viable require an immediate reroll (repeat until resolved).

- Declaring Raises on a Contested Roll: The roll must exceed the opponent’s total by +5 per Raise declared, or the roll fails.

**SKILL ROLLS, EXPANDED:**

**Unskilled Rolls**

A character with no ranks in a Skill may still attempt a Skill Roll, effectively making a Trait Roll against a Skill Roll TN. Two conditions apply:

- Dice never explode on an Unskilled Roll.

- Unskilled Rolls may not benefit from Raises of any kind (called or Free).

**Failed Rolls**

When a Skill Roll fails, a second attempt is usually possible (unless the GM rules otherwise). The TN for the second attempt increases by +10.

- Intelligence or Perception rolls usually cannot be re-attempted without a change in circumstances (e.g. new information becoming available).

- Attack rolls (Weapon Skills) may not be re-attempted at +10 TN — a miss is simply a miss.

**Cooperative Rolls**

Multiple individuals working together toward a single result. Two types:

- Low-consequence cooperation (e.g. shipwrights building a vessel): One individual makes the roll and receives a bonus to the total equal to the combined Skill Ranks of all other participants.

- High-consequence cooperation (e.g. samurai roped together climbing a mountain): Each participant makes their own roll. The participant with the highest Skill Rank grants a bonus equal to their Skill Rank to all others’ rolls.

**Cumulative Rolls**

Require multiple successes over time to complete a task. The TN is typically very high but is achieved by deducting each successful roll’s total from the remaining TN. Example: TN 60 sculpture — a roll of 24 leaves TN 36; a roll of 22 on the next attempt leaves TN 14.

- Each listing specifies how long must pass between attempts.

- GM may require a minimum TN (15, 20, or 25) for a roll to count toward the cumulative total.

- A very low roll may subtract from the cumulative total (representing a correctable mistake).

- Cumulative Rolls may also be Cooperative Rolls — generate the Cooperative total first, then deduct it from the Cumulative TN.

**SPECIAL DICE RULES:**

**Exploding Dice**

When a die comes up as 10, roll it again and add the new result to the die’s total. If the re-roll is also 10, roll again. Continue until a non-10 result is achieved.

- Example: Roll 10, then 3 — die result is 13.

- Example: Roll 10, 10, then 7 — die result is 27.

**The Ten Dice Rule**

No roll may ever use more than ten dice at a time. When rolled dice exceed 10, convert the excess:

- Extra rolled dice (beyond 10): Become kept dice at a ratio of 1 kept die per 2 extra rolled dice. If there is an odd extra rolled die remaining after conversion, it becomes +2 to the roll total.

- Extra kept dice (beyond 10): Each extra kept die becomes +2 to the roll total.

- Example: 12k4 → 10k5 (2 extra rolled dice = 1 extra kept die).

- Example: 13k9 → 10k10 +2 (2 extra rolled dice = 1 extra kept die; 1 odd extra rolled die = +2; kept dice are now at 10).

- Example: 10k12 → 10k10 +4 (2 extra kept dice = +4).

- Example: 14k12 → 10k10 +12 (4 extra rolled dice = +8; 2 extra kept dice = +4; total bonus +12).
