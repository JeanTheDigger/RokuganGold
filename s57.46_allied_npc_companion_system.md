## 57.46 Allied NPC Companion System

### 57.46.1 Overview

This section defines how allied NPCs behave on the ASCII tactical map when accompanying a player character during missions. The system covers all companion types: doshin (Section 11.3.19e), yoriki (Section 11.3.6), yojimbo (Section 11.3.18), hunting party members (Section 57.38), named NPC allies in battlefield bubbles (Section 11.7), and shugenja support in spirit realm expeditions (Section 56.16). Every companion type shares the same underlying framework — grid presence, movement logic, command interface, morale — with type-specific behavior layered on top.

Design intent: companions are tools, not pets. They serve specific tactical functions (blocking exits, identifying suspects, protecting the player, providing local knowledge) and have specific limitations (morale, noise, refusal to fight above their station). The player manages them through a simple command menu. The system is deliberately lightweight — the player is playing a samurai, not an RTS.

### 57.46.2 Companion Slots and Mission Launch

Hard cap: 6 companion slots per ASCII map mission. More than 6 companions turns the tactical layer into a management game — engagements at that scale use the mass battle grid (Section 11.7) instead. Each companion occupies one slot. Doshin teams (Section 57.46.3) occupy one slot per team, not per individual.

Mission launch screen: before entering the ASCII map, the player sees all available companions with their stat summaries, noise contribution (Section 57.46.13), and any type-specific notes (doshin local knowledge coverage, yojimbo combat capability, yoriki investigation bonuses). The player selects up to 6 slots. Trade-offs are visible: more bodies means better coverage of escape routes and faster room clearing, but also more noise, more chaos in tight spaces, and higher risk of casualties. A solo mission is quiet and precise. A full team locks down the building but announces itself on entry.

Companions who are not selected remain at the staging area and do not participate. The player cannot summon additional companions once the mission has launched.

### 57.46.3 Grid Representation

Each companion slot occupies one tile on the ASCII map. Companions are rendered as distinct characters: @ for the player, numbers 1–6 for companions (matching the command menu order). Companion tiles block movement for enemies but not for the player or other companions.

**Doshin teams:**

City doshin operate in teams of 2–3 (Section 11.3.19e.v). A doshin team occupies a single tile on the grid — the team moves, acts, and is commanded as one unit. This keeps the map manageable while preserving the tactical doctrine described in the stat blocks. A team uses the best individual’s stats with a teamwork bonus: +5 to grapple/restrain rolls when the team has 2+ members. If a team member is killed, the teamwork bonus is lost when the team drops to 1 member. Village doshin do not form teams — each village doshin occupies one slot individually.

**Named NPC companions:**

Yoriki, yojimbo, and other named NPCs each occupy one slot individually. They use their full character sheet stats. Their school techniques apply normally.

### 57.46.4 Command Menu Interface

The command menu is accessible at any time during the player’s turn via a dedicated key (default: TAB). The menu displays all active companions in a numbered list showing: companion number (1–6), name or type (e.g. “Doshin Team A”, “Yorik Daidoji Kenji”), current command, current health/wounds, and morale state (steady, shaken, broken). The player selects a companion by number, then selects a command from the available list for that companion type.

Issuing a command is free — it costs no action and does not end the player’s turn. The companion begins executing the new command on their next action. Commands are persistent: they remain active until the player changes them, the companion’s morale overrides them, or the command completes (MOVE TO reaches its destination and converts to HOLD). The player can change commands as often as they want within a single turn — no limit on reissuing orders.

When a companion’s morale breaks, their entry in the command menu changes to BROKEN and their command changes to FLEE. The player cannot override a broken companion’s retreat. The entry remains visible so the player knows what happened.

### 57.46.5 Universal Commands

All companion types have access to these four commands. These form the baseline behavior layer.

**FOLLOW (default):**

Stay 1–2 tiles behind the player, matching the player’s movement. In corridors (width 1–2 tiles), companions trail single-file behind the player. In open areas (width 3+ tiles), companions offset to the player’s flank. If the player moves faster than the companion can follow (player has higher movement speed), the companion moves at maximum speed along the player’s path. FOLLOW is the default command assigned to all companions at mission start.

**HOLD:**

Stop at current tile. Do not move under any circumstances. If attacked in melee, defend (reaction style depends on companion type — doshin restrain, bushi counterattack, shugenja ward). Do not pursue retreating enemies. HOLD is the command for anchoring a companion at a specific position — a chokepoint, a cleared room, a flanking position.

**MOVE TO [tile]:**

Pathfind to a player-designated tile and HOLD on arrival. The player selects the target tile from the map (cursor targeting). The companion moves along the shortest safe path, avoiding revealed traps and hostile tiles. If the path is blocked by enemies, the companion engages them en route (fight-through, not stealth). On arrival, the command converts to HOLD. If the target tile is unreachable (no valid path), the companion holds at the closest reachable tile and the command menu flags the obstruction.

**RETREAT:**

Move toward the nearest map exit and leave the mission. The companion pathfinds to the closest exit tile and departs. Irreversible once they reach the exit — a retreated companion cannot be recalled. The companion fights defensively while retreating (parry/dodge only, no attacks) unless cornered with no escape route, in which case they fight normally. RETREAT is available at all times. It is the only command that can be issued to a companion whose morale is shaken but not yet broken — a proactive withdrawal before they rout.

### 57.46.6 Doshin-Specific Commands

These commands are available only to doshin companions (village doshin, city doshin teams, doshin headman). They reflect the doshin’s role as local law enforcement — guides and informants, not soldiers.

**GUARD EXIT [tile]:**

Move to and block a designated exit point (door, window, alley mouth, ladder). The doshin positions at the exit tile and enters a blocking stance. Any enemy attempting to flee through the guarded tile must beat a contested Athletics (Running) / Strength vs doshin Jiujutsu (Restraining) / Strength check. On success, the enemy passes through. On failure, the enemy is grappled and held. A city doshin team with the +5 teamwork bonus to grapple is significantly harder to break through than a single village doshin. This is the doshin’s highest-value tactical action — in a raid, the player clears rooms while doshin block escape routes. The command persists until cancelled. A doshin guarding an exit does not abandon position to chase or pursue. Samurai avoidance applies (Section 57.46.11): a samurai fleeing through a guarded exit triggers the grapple at −10 penalty.

**IDENTIFY [NPC]:**

Player targets a visible NPC on the map (any NPC within line of sight). The doshin rolls Lore: Local Area / Perception vs TN 15 (auto-succeed for city doshin in their home district with Lore: Local Area 3). On success, the NPC’s name, occupation, and criminal status (if any active CrimeRecord per Section 11.3.14e targets this NPC) are revealed to the player. On failure, the doshin does not recognize the person. Costs the doshin’s action for the round. Can only target NPCs in the doshin’s home settlement — outside their district, the roll is at +10 TN (unfamiliar territory).

**SEARCH AREA:**

The doshin spreads out and searches a 3×3 area centered on their current tile. Investigation / Perception roll vs TN 20. Reveals hidden items, concealed passages, stashed contraband, and forensic evidence (bloodstains, footprints — connects to Kitsuki Powder from Section 2.2 Dragon Clan, Tamori kagaku). Takes 2 rounds to complete — the doshin cannot move or act during the search. A doshin team searches faster: 1 round instead of 2 (multiple sets of eyes). A doshin headman with Investigation 2 and Lore: Underworld 2 adds +5 to the search roll — they know where criminals hide things.

### 57.46.7 Yojimbo-Specific Commands

**PROTECT [target]:**

Stay adjacent to a designated character (the player or another companion). The yojimbo moves to maintain adjacency at all times, matching the target’s movement. When the target is attacked in melee, the yojimbo intercedes — the attacker must defeat the yojimbo’s Defense before reaching the target. This is the Guard action (Section 40) applied to the companion system. A yojimbo on PROTECT who is also adjacent to an enemy automatically counterattacks — they do not passively absorb hits. If the target moves faster than the yojimbo can follow, the yojimbo sprints to close the gap (double movement, no attack that round). PROTECT persists until cancelled or the target leaves the map.

### 57.46.8 Yoriki-Specific Commands

**INVESTIGATE [tile/area]:**

The yoriki examines a specific tile or 3×3 area for evidence. Investigation + Perception roll with full school technique bonuses (Kitsuki Method grants additional raises per Section 2.8.5). Reveals forensic details doshin would miss: weapon type from wound analysis, time of death, forged documents, magical residue. Takes 1 round for a single tile, 2 rounds for a 3×3 area. A yoriki with EXAMINE_CRIME_SCENE bonuses (Section 11.3.13d) applies them here. This command is functionally identical to the player performing EXAMINE_CRIME_SCENE but delegated to the companion — the player can fight or move while the yoriki investigates.

### 57.46.9 AI Behavior Priority Stack

Each companion evaluates behavior through a four-level priority stack. Higher priorities override lower ones. The stack is checked every round before the companion acts.

Priority 1 — SURVIVAL: If morale is BROKEN, override all commands with FLEE. Non-negotiable. The companion pathfinds to the nearest exit at maximum movement speed. They will fight only if cornered with no escape route. This priority cannot be overridden by the player.

Priority 2 — PLAYER COMMAND: Execute the player’s last issued command (FOLLOW, HOLD, MOVE TO, RETREAT, or a type-specific command). This is the normal operating mode. The companion does what they were told.

Priority 3 — DEFAULT STANCE: If no command has been issued and morale is steady, default to FOLLOW. The companion trails the player silently.

Priority 4 — REACT TO THREATS: If attacked while executing any command other than RETREAT, the companion defends. Defense style depends on type: doshin attempt to grapple and restrain (Jiujutsu vs attacker’s Athletics). Bushi (yojimbo, yoriki with bushi school) counterattack with their best weapon skill. Shugenja fall back 2 tiles and cast a defensive ward if available, or dodge. Reaction does not change the active command — after the immediate threat is resolved, the companion resumes their current order.

### 57.46.10 Morale and Breaking

Companions track morale as a three-state system: STEADY (normal), SHAKEN (near breaking), BROKEN (routing).

**STEADY:**

Normal operation. The companion executes commands without hesitation. No penalties.

**SHAKEN:**

Triggered when allied casualties reach 50% of the companion’s morale threshold (e.g. 15% casualties for village doshin with a 30% threshold). The companion’s combat rolls take −5 (fear penalty). The command menu flags the companion as SHAKEN. The player can issue RETREAT to withdraw them before they break. SHAKEN state clears if the threat is eliminated (all visible enemies defeated) or the companion moves 10+ tiles from the nearest enemy.

**BROKEN:**

Triggered when allied casualties reach the companion’s morale threshold. The companion’s command is forcibly overridden to FLEE. The player cannot issue new commands to a broken companion. The companion pathfinds to the nearest exit at maximum speed, fighting only if cornered. Once a companion breaks, they do not recover during the current mission — they are gone.

Morale thresholds by companion type: Village doshin: 30% allied casualties (flees toward nearest settlement). City doshin team: 40% (disciplined withdrawal toward map exit). Doshin headman: 50% (holds longer than regular doshin). Yojimbo: does not break. Fights until dead or until their PROTECT target leaves the map. Duty overrides survival. Yoriki: school-dependent. Bushi-school yoriki use their school’s Yu weighting — high Yu breaks at 50%, low Yu at 30%. Non-bushi yoriki break at 35%. No yoriki retreats before the player (Chugi prevents it while their operational superior is engaged). Named NPC allies: personality-driven per Section 19. High Yu (7+): 50%. Mid Yu (4–6): 35%. Low Yu (1–3): 20%.

“Allied casualties” means companions killed or broken during this mission, counted as a fraction of the total companions who started the mission. If 2 of 6 companions are down, that is 33% — enough to break village doshin but not city doshin.

### 57.46.11 Doshin Samurai Avoidance

Doshin will not engage samurai targets unprompted. This is a hard behavioral gate, not a soft lean — doshin have authority over peasants and eta only (Section 11.3.1). Three specific interactions:

Movement: if commanded to MOVE TO a tile occupied by a samurai enemy, the doshin moves to the nearest adjacent tile instead and enters HOLD. They will not path through a tile containing a samurai.

GUARD EXIT: if a samurai flees through a doshin’s guarded exit tile, the doshin attempts the grapple check at −10 penalty (terrified of laying hands on a samurai). On success, the samurai is momentarily delayed (1 round) but not held — the doshin releases immediately and apologizes. On failure, the samurai passes through unimpeded. Either way, the doshin does not pursue.

Combat: if a samurai attacks a doshin in melee, the doshin defends (dodge/parry only, no counterattack) and immediately moves 2 tiles away if possible. They will not strike a samurai. Exception: if the local magistrate has issued an explicit arrest warrant for the samurai (active CrimeRecord with arrest_authorized = true per Section 11.3.14e), the doshin fights normally — they have legal authority. Even then, city doshin are reluctant (−5 penalty) and village doshin refuse entirely unless the doshin headman is present and orders it.

### 57.46.12 Doshin Local Knowledge Passive

While a doshin is following the player (FOLLOW command or within 5 tiles on any command), the doshin automatically generates text prompts when the player enters or approaches areas the doshin knows. This is a passive ability — it fires without player input and costs no action.

Trigger: the player enters a tile within 3 tiles of a point of interest the doshin has knowledge of. The doshin rolls Lore: Local Area / Perception vs TN 15. City doshin in their home district auto-succeed (Lore: Local Area 3). Village doshin in their home village auto-succeed (Lore: Local Area 2, but the village is small enough that TN 15 is trivial). Outside their home area, the roll is at +10 TN (unfamiliar territory) and most prompts will fail.

Prompt content is procedurally generated from the settlement’s zone data (Section 57.36) and NPC roster. Examples: “This alley connects to the docks — two exits at the far end.” “That building belongs to Honest Jin. He runs a gambling den. Two guards, usually armed.” “The back door opens onto the canal. Smugglers use it at night.” “That shrine is dedicated to Inari. The monks keep to themselves.” “I’ve seen a ronin hanging around this corner for the past week. Didn’t recognize him.”

Frequency cap: maximum 1 prompt per 5 tiles of player movement. The doshin does not narrate every tile — they speak up when something is worth mentioning. Prompts that repeat information the player has already received are suppressed.

### 57.46.13 Noise Impact

Each companion increases the party’s noise footprint per Section 56.6.3. This is the primary mechanical trade-off of bringing companions — a full team of 6 adds significant detection radius to every noise event.

Base noise contribution per companion: +1 tile to noise radius for every noise event the party generates. Stealth skill reduces this: Stealth 1 = +0.5 (rounded up per event). Stealth 2 = +0.25 (rounded up). Stealth 3+ = +0 (the companion is trained to move silently).

Noise contribution by companion type: Village doshin (no Stealth): +1.0. City doshin team (Stealth 1): +0.5 per team. Doshin headman (no Stealth, but experienced): +0.75. Yojimbo (varies by school — Kakita Bushi Stealth 0 = +1.0, Shosuro Infiltrator Stealth 3+ = +0). Yoriki (varies — Kitsuki Investigator typically Stealth 0–1 = +0.5 to +1.0). Named NPC: use their Stealth skill rank.

Example: a raid team of the player + 2 city doshin teams + 1 yoriki + 1 yojimbo = 4 companion slots. Noise contribution: +0.5 + 0.5 + 1.0 + 1.0 = +3.0 tiles to every noise event. A Moderate noise action that normally carries 12 tiles now carries 15 tiles. This is significant — the patrol two rooms over hears the door being forced.

### 57.46.14 Companion Death Consequences

Companion death on the ASCII map is permanent and has world-state consequences.

Doshin: doshin_losses on the settlement increments per Section 11.3.19e.iii. Recovery rate: 1 doshin per IC season as replacements are trained. A settlement that loses its entire doshin force in a botched raid has no ground-level law enforcement for multiple seasons. The criminals are emboldened and the province’s suppression capability drops.

Named NPC (yoriki, yojimbo, named ally): permanent death per Section 22 (Named Character System). The character is gone. Their position becomes vacant (FILL_VACANCY fires on the appointing lord per Section 57.20.3 and UPHOLD_LAW per Section 57.46 if the position was law enforcement). A Tier 4 topic generates: “[Character] died during [mission type] in [province].” If the dead character was someone’s yojimbo, the protected character is now unprotected — the Crane dueling deterrent (Section 2.2) loses its teeth until a replacement is assigned.

Hunting party members: permanent death per Section 57.38 casualty rules. The hunt generates a Tier 4 topic. Glory loss for the hunt host.

### 57.46.15 Pathfinding

Companions use A* pathfinding on the ASCII grid. They avoid revealed traps (traps the player or any companion has detected), hostile tiles (tiles containing enemies, unless the companion is fighting through to a MOVE TO destination), and impassable terrain. On FOLLOW, companions walk the player’s proven path — following the exact tiles the player has already traversed, which guarantees safety from unrevealed traps. On MOVE TO, companions pathfind independently and may trigger unrevealed traps en route. The player can mitigate this by scouting a path first, then issuing MOVE TO along the cleared route.

Companion movement speed: base 1 tile per round for heimin companions (doshin). Named samurai companions use their character sheet movement rate (typically 1–2 tiles per round depending on Water Ring and armor). A companion on FOLLOW whose movement speed is lower than the player’s falls behind gradually — the gap between them and the player grows by 1 tile per round of faster player movement. The player can wait (end turn without moving) to let slower companions catch up.

### 57.46.16 Companion Types Summary

Doshin (village): 1 slot per individual. Low combat stats. Breaks at 30%. +1.0 noise. Local knowledge passive. GUARD EXIT, IDENTIFY, SEARCH AREA. Will not engage samurai. Primary value: blocking exits and identifying NPCs in their home village.

Doshin (city team): 1 slot per 2–3 doshin. Better combat stats with +5 teamwork grapple bonus. Breaks at 40%. +0.5 noise. Local knowledge passive. GUARD EXIT (stronger), IDENTIFY, SEARCH AREA (faster — 1 round). Will not engage samurai. Primary value: locking down raid sites in their home city.

Doshin headman: 1 slot. Best doshin stats. Breaks at 50%. +0.75 noise. Local knowledge passive (enhanced — Lore: Underworld 2). All doshin commands with bonuses. Will not engage samurai unless arrest warrant issued. Primary value: investigation support and doshin coordination.

Yojimbo: 1 slot. Full samurai stats and school techniques. Does not break. Noise varies by school. PROTECT command. Primary value: keeping the player or a VIP alive in combat.

Yoriki: 1 slot. Full samurai stats and school techniques. Breaks at 30–50% (school-dependent). Noise varies. INVESTIGATE command. Will not retreat before the player. Primary value: delegated investigation on the map while the player acts elsewhere.

Named NPC ally: 1 slot. Full character sheet. Breaks at 20–50% (personality-dependent). Noise varies. Universal commands only (no type-specific commands). Primary value: additional combat power or specialized skills.
