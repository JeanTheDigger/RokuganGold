# 17. Personal Visits System — LOCKED

Personal Visits are the intimate, unstructured layer of diplomacy that happens outside of formal courts. Every named character is physically located somewhere in the game world — in a specific zone on the MUD map. When two named characters are in the same zone they can interact directly in the ASCII map layer. When they are in different zones, interaction requires travel or the Game of Letters. A Personal Visit is when a named character deliberately travels to another named character's location to interact with them.

## 17.1 How a Visit Happens — LOCKED

There are three ways a Personal Visit occurs, each with different social weight and right-to-refuse implications.

**1. Invitation Sent:**

- The host sends a formal invitation to a specific character via letter.

- The recipient can accept or decline. Declining a formal invitation carries a small disposition cost — it is a mild social slight.

- If the recipient accepts and travels to the host's location, the visit begins.

- Refusing entry after sending an invitation: significant Honor and disposition loss — you invited them and then turned them away.

**2. Letter Announcing Arrival:**

- The visitor sends a letter informing the host they intend to visit. Less formal than an invitation — the initiative comes from the visitor, not the host.

- The host still has the right to refuse entry. Refusing someone who sent a letter of arrival: small disposition cost — slightly less than refusing a formal invitation.

- If the host accepts, the visitor travels to their location and the visit begins.

**3. Showing Up Uninvited:**

- The visitor simply arrives at the host's location without prior notice.

- The host can refuse to receive them. Refusing an uninvited guest: minimal social cost — it is entirely within their rights.

- If the host receives them despite no prior notice, it signals significant goodwill — a positive disposition modifier.

## 17.2 What Happens During a Visit — LOCKED

A Personal Visit is an intimate 1-on-1 or small group interaction. There is no formal agenda, no crisis to manage, and no public audience. The available actions reflect this — targeted personal interactions only. No broadcast actions are available since there is no audience for public displays.

**Available Action Categories During a Visit:**

- Category 1 — Raise disposition with one person: All Direct Conversation approaches, Deliver Gift, Offer a Favor, Perform Personally For. The intimate setting makes these actions more impactful than at court — no political theater, just two people.

- Category 3 — Lower someone's disposition with a specific person: Gossip, Disclose, Reveal a Secret Privately. Private setting means lower risk of exposure than court.

- Category 5 — Intelligence: Read a Character, Probe. A visit provides sustained observation opportunity — multiple days of interaction reveal more than a single court encounter.

- Category 2 and 4 are not available — no public audience means no broadcast actions.

**Action Points During a Visit:**

- The standard 2 AP per in-game day applies. A visit lasting multiple days gives multiple days of action points in the intimate setting.

- Both host and visitor spend their daily action points on the visit — it is an active engagement for both parties, not just the visitor.

**The Intimate Setting Advantage:**

- Category 1 actions performed during a Personal Visit carry a bonus compared to the same action at court — the absence of political theater and the sustained presence over multiple days creates deeper connection.

**Intimate Setting Bonus — LOCKED:** All Category 1 actions performed during a Personal Visit receive a flat +3 disposition bonus added to the action’s normal disposition result. This stacks with Raise bonuses and gift quality modifiers. The bonus reflects the absence of political theater, sustained multi-day presence, and genuine personal attention that a visit provides over a single court encounter. A CHARM that would produce +4 disposition at court produces +7 during a visit. A DELIVER_GIFT of Fine quality (+5) becomes +8. The bonus does not apply to Category 3 (sabotage) or Category 5 (intelligence) actions — those benefit from the visit’s privacy and sustained observation respectively, but through qualitative advantages (lower exposure risk, more observation time) rather than a numeric disposition bonus. Value PROVISIONAL.
