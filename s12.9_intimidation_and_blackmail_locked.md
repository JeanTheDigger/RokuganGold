## 12.9 Intimidation and Blackmail — LOCKED

Intimidation operates across three distinct contexts, each with different mechanics, costs, and social consequences. All three use the Intimidation Low Skill (Willpower + Intimidation). Honor Rank resists all three — high-Honor characters are harder to intimidate in any context, making Honor mechanically valuable beyond court credibility.

**Blackmail — Using a Secret as Leverage (Private):**

You possess a secret about the target. You threaten to expose it unless they give you something in return. Available as a Personal Visit action (1 AP) or by letter (no AP, subject to delivery delay).

Roll: Willpower + Intimidation (contested) vs target’s Etiquette + Willpower roll total + their Honor Rank as a flat bonus (per The Strength of Honor, Section 32). Disposition toward you adjusts the defender’s total: Friend or above +5, Enemy or below −5.

The secret’s severity determines starting Free Raises:

- Tier 4 (minor embarrassment): 0 Free Raises.

- Tier 3 (significant compromise): 1 Free Raise.

- Tier 2 (severe threat): 2 Free Raises.

- Tier 1 (existential): 3 Free Raises.

Each Raise achieved beyond the TN = one Favor extracted from the target. The Favor System (Section 12.10) defines what a favor is worth mechanically — two types (Specific/General), three tiers (Minor/Moderate/Major), with disposition values and breaking consequences at each tier. The number of favors is determined here.

Failure or pushing too far: If you declare more Raises than you can meet, the blackmail fails entirely. The target calls your bluff or resists. You can still expose the secret — but you get nothing from the blackmail. Exposing a secret after a failed blackmail attempt generates a Betrayal Tier 4 topic about you specifically — you revealed you had the secret and then used it anyway, marking you as someone who cannot be negotiated with even when shown what you know.

Compliance is not genuine: A target who yields to blackmail has not changed their position, their objective, or their disposition. They are complying under duress. Compliance tracked separately from genuine agreement. If the pressure is removed — the secret is exposed anyway, the blackmailer loses leverage, or the target’s circumstances change — compliance ends immediately.

- Blackmailer Honor loss: −0.3 regardless of success. Using leverage against someone is dishonorable even when it works.

- Blackmailer Infamy gain: +0.1.

**Private Intimidation — No Secret Required:**

Applying direct personal pressure — implied consequences, veiled threats, making clear what you are capable of. No secret needed. Available in three settings:

In person — Personal Visit or private court meeting (1 AP): Roll Willpower + Intimidation (contested) vs target’s Etiquette + Willpower roll total + their Honor Rank as a flat bonus (per The Strength of Honor, Section 32). Success raises the TN on their next action toward you or your objective this session by +10. Each Raise adds +5 to that TN. Effect lasts until end of session or until they successfully push back with their own Willpower roll (TN 15 + your Intimidation rank).

By letter (no AP, delivery delay): Roll Willpower + Intimidation vs TN 15 + target’s Honor Rank (flat bonus, per The Strength of Honor, Section 32). Success raises TN on their next relevant action by +5. No Raises available by letter — the absence of physical presence limits the pressure. Weaker than in-person intimidation but usable across distance.

- Actor Honor loss: −0.2.

- Actor Infamy gain: +0.05.

**Public Intimidation — Court Context (1 AP):**

Openly threatening someone in a court context to suppress their position on a topic or prevent them from acting. Witnessed by everyone present. The most powerful form of intimidation and the most socially costly.

Roll: Willpower + Intimidation (contested) vs target’s Etiquette + Willpower roll total + their Honor Rank as a flat bonus (per The Strength of Honor, Section 32).

Success does not move their genuine topic position — their internal position stays the same. Instead it raises the effective TN they must overcome to act on that position. They want to speak, vote, or commit but find it harder to do so. Each Raise increases the difficulty by +5.

Effect duration: Remainder of the court session. Once court ends the pressure dissipates entirely. This is temporary suppression, not genuine conversion.

Witnesses react: every character present with Rei (Courtesy), Gi (Honesty), or Meiyo (Honor) virtue applies their negative reaction to the actor. The Disgrace topic about the actor’s conduct may be generated if the act was sufficiently crude. High-Honor clans (Lion, Crane, Phoenix) characters present will lose disposition toward the actor.

- Actor Honor loss: −0.3.

- Actor Infamy gain: +0.1.

- Actor disposition loss with all Rei/Gi/Meiyo witnesses: −2 each.

**Compliance Tracker — LOCKED:**

All three intimidation contexts produce compliance rather than genuine agreement. Compliance is tracked as a separate value distinct from topic position and disposition. A character can be at +60 genuine position on a topic and simultaneously under compliance pressure suppressing their ability to act on it.

- Compliance is binary: active or inactive. It is active while the pressure condition holds.

- Compliance ends when: the intimidation effect duration expires (end of session for court actions), the blackmail leverage is removed (secret exposed or revealed as fabricated), the target successfully resists with their own Willpower roll, or the intimidating character’s disposition toward the target crosses into Friend range (genuine intimidation of someone you care about becomes impossible).

- When compliance ends: the character immediately acts on their genuine position. If their genuine position was suppressed in court, they now make their Public Declaration. If their compliance was blocking a commitment, that commitment now happens or doesn’t based on their genuine position.

- Sustained compliance: if a character is kept under pressure across multiple sessions through repeated intimidation actions, the compliance persists — but each repetition costs the actor the same Honor and Infamy. Long-term blackmail is expensive for the blackmailer.
