## 4.5 L5R 4th Edition Rules Integration

The core resolution system is based on Legend of the Five Rings 4th Edition (L5R4e). The following rules are the primary reference for mechanical adaptation into Godot.

### 4.5.1 Core Dice System — Roll & Keep

- All checks use the Roll & Keep system: roll a number of d10s equal to Trait + Skill, keep the highest X dice (X = Trait value), sum the kept dice against a Target Number (TN).

- Exploding dice: a result of 10 on any die explodes — reroll and add the new result.

- Raises: Players may declare Raises before rolling, adding +5 to the TN per Raise for additional narrative or mechanical effects.

**IMPLEMENTATION NOTE:** *Dice system implementation in Godot (custom dice roller node or abstract to math) is resolved during engine development.*

### 4.5.2 The Five Rings & Traits

- Air (Reflexes + Awareness): social grace, perception, ranged combat.

- Earth (Stamina + Willpower): endurance, resistance, raw toughness.

- Fire (Agility + Intelligence): precision, knowledge, spellcasting.

- Water (Strength + Perception): movement, strength, adaptability.

- Void: the fifth Ring, used for Void Points — a meta-resource spent for re-rolls or bonuses.

**✓ RESOLVED:** All 10 Traits tracked individually: Stamina, Willpower (Earth), Strength, Perception (Water), Agility, Intelligence (Fire), Reflexes, Awareness (Air), Void. Ring = min(Trait1, Trait2) per L5R 4e. Both Traits must be separate values — they appear independently in roll formulas throughout (Stamina + Willpower for Taint resistance, Reflexes for initiative, Agility for attacks, etc.).*

### 4.5.3 Combat in the ASCII Map

- Initiative: rolled at combat start using Reflexes + Insight Rank, determines action order each round.

- Actions: each character gets one complex action (attack, full move, cast spell) or two simple actions per round.

- Attack resolution: Attack Roll (Trait + Skill, keep Trait) vs. target's TN to Be Hit (TNBH = 5 + Armor + Reflexes × 5).

- Damage: on hit, roll damage dice (weapon-dependent, keep set number), subtract Reduction (armor), apply remainder to Wounds.

- Wound levels: Healthy, Nicked, Grazed, Hurt, Injured, Crippled, Down, Out — each level imposes stacking penalties.

- Maneuvers: Raises on attack rolls can trigger special effects — knockdown, disarm, called shots, etc.

**Stances — LOCKED:** A character’s Stance determines what actions they may take during their Turn. Stances reflect the basic combat postures every samurai is taught. During the first Round, characters adopt their Stance during Stage 1 (Initiative). On every subsequent Round, they may change or maintain their Stance at the beginning of their Turn in Stage 2 (Actions). Verbatim from L5R 4e Core Rulebook.

**Attack Stance (Water): **The standard combat Stance. No restrictions on Actions. Fluid and versatile.

**Full Attack Stance (Fire): **+2k1 to attack rolls. Armor TN reduced by 10. May only take Simple or Complex Actions that are attacks. Move Actions permitted only to close distance with enemies. Cannot deliver ranged attacks. Cannot be used while mounted (unless Horsemanship Rank 3+, per Section 2.2 Unicorn). A character who takes a Move Action in Full Attack gains +5 feet beyond normal movement, once per Round.

**Defense Stance (Air): **Adds Air Ring + Defense Skill Rank to Armor TN. No restrictions on Actions other than that the character may not attack. Useful for Skill Rolls, Spell Casting, or non-combat actions during a skirmish while remaining defensively prepared.

**Full Defense Stance (Earth): **Upon declaring, the character rolls Defense / Reflexes and adds half the total (rounded up) to Armor TN until their following Turn. This roll is a Complex Action — the character may only take Free Actions while in Full Defense. The most protective Stance but entirely passive.

**Center Stance (Void): **The character forfeits all Actions, focusing energy for the following Round. On the Round following adoption of Center Stance, the character gains +1k1 plus their Void Ring on any one roll made during their Turn. The character also adds 10 to their Initiative Score for that Round only. Primary function is in iaijutsu dueling (Section 4.8.1) but provides benefits in skirmishes as well.

⚙️ CROSS-REF: Defense Skill mastery abilities modify Stance behavior (Section 24.2 — Rank 3: retain previous Defense roll in Full Defense; Rank 5: +3 Armor TN in Defense/Full Defense; Rank 7: one Simple Action in Full Defense). Horsemanship Rank 3 unlocks Full Attack while mounted (Section 2.2 Unicorn). Berserker’s Rage (Crab alternate path) prohibits Center, Defense, and Full Defense Stances. Iaijutsu dueling uses Center Stance exclusively (Section 4.8.1). Multiple school techniques reference specific Stances — all are defined here as the authoritative source.

**Attack ****&**** Defense — LOCKED:** Attacks require a Skill Roll using a Weapon Skill as one component (melee or ranged depending on weapon). The attack roll total is compared to the target’s Armor TN. Armor TN = (Reflexes × 5) + 5 + bonuses from Stances and armor. If the attack roll meets or exceeds the Armor TN, the attack hits and damage is rolled. Verbatim from L5R 4e Core Rulebook.

**Attack roll penalties and bonuses: **Wound Rank penalties apply to attack rolls as they do to all Trait rolls (Section 22.3). Any ranged attack against an opponent within melee range suffers −10 to the attack roll total — firing at someone who is a direct threat is awkward. Stance bonuses and penalties (Full Attack +2k1 attack / −10 Armor TN, Defense adds Air Ring + Defense Rank to Armor TN, etc.) are defined in the Stances section above. Additional situational bonuses and penalties (attacking while balancing, fighting in darkness, etc.) are applied per encounter context.

**Damage: **Every weapon has a Damage Rating (DR) expressed as XkY. The katana, the most common samurai weapon, has DR 3k2. For melee attacks, add the attacker’s Strength to the first number of the DR. A character with Strength 3 wielding a katana (DR 3k2) rolls 6k2 for damage. The player selects the two highest dice from the six rolled and totals them — this is the number of Wounds inflicted. Certain ranged weapons also add Strength to damage (see Equipment); others do not. Armor provides Reduction — a flat number subtracted from the damage total before Wounds are applied. Reduction values are defined per armor type in Section 42.

**✓ RESOLVED:** *Wound Level Display — LOCKED: Wound levels are not displayed passively on the ASCII map. The player uses a Look command (similar to Dwarf Fortress adventure mode) to select any visible character or creature on the map. Selecting a target displays their current wound level (Healthy through Out), active wound penalties, and any visible status effects. This keeps the map visually clean and puts health information behind a deliberate player action. Named characters also display name and clan affiliation on Look. Anonymous soldiers display unit type only.*

Blood Splatter — LOCKED: When a character or creature takes damage that moves them one or more Wound Ranks in a single hit, blood splatter tiles appear on the map. Blood is rendered as a red-tinted overlay on existing floor tiles — the underlying terrain symbol remains visible but the tile colour shifts to dark red. The splatter does not affect movement or combat. It is purely visual feedback.

Splatter scales with severity. A hit that crosses one Wound Rank marks the target's tile only. A hit that crosses two Wound Ranks marks the target's tile and 1–2 random adjacent floor tiles. A hit that crosses three or more Wound Ranks marks the target's tile and 2–4 adjacent tiles. Killing blows always produce maximum splatter regardless of damage. Splatter direction is biased away from the attacker — a sword strike from the east produces splatter mostly to the west and on the target's own tile.

Splatter tiles persist for the remainder of the scene. They accumulate — a prolonged melee in a corridor produces a visible trail of where the fighting was heaviest. A battlefield that has seen a full Heroic Opportunity plays out leaves the 20×20 bubble visibly marked. On outdoor terrain (grass, dirt, sand), splatter renders as dark patches. On indoor terrain (wood floors, stone, tatami), splatter renders as pooling. On water tiles, blood disperses and does not persist.

Splatter is cosmetic only. It has no mechanical effect on movement, combat, stealth, or any other system. It exists to make violence feel real on a text map where a single character symbol represents a person.

**Grappling — LOCKED**

Initiation: Grappling is a Complex Action. The attacker must be adjacent to the target (exception: sasumata, see below). The attacker rolls Jiujutsu (Grappling) / Agility against the target’s Armor TN. If the roll hits, a contested Strength roll follows — attacker rolls Jiujutsu / Strength, defender rolls Jiujutsu / Strength (or raw Strength if Jiujutsu 0). If the attacker wins the contested roll, the grapple is established and the attacker is the controller. If the defender wins, the grab fails and the attacker’s Complex Action is spent. Jiujutsu Rank 5 grants a Free Raise toward the initiation roll (Section 24). Chain weapons at Rank 3 can initiate grapples using the chain weapon skill instead of Jiujutsu (Section 24).

Grapple state: Both characters are locked to the same tile. Neither can move. Both are considered engaged in melee. Large weapons cannot be used by either participant — katana, no-dachi, polearms, bows, and any weapon requiring two hands or significant reach are unusable while grappling. Small weapons can be used by either participant — tanto, knife, jitte, and any weapon classified as concealable or one-handed short. Stances do not apply during a grapple — both participants are locked in close-quarters struggle. Wound penalties from the character’s current Wound Rank apply normally to all grapple rolls.

Controller’s turn: The controller spends a Complex Action and chooses one option. Damage — deal unarmed damage (Strength k1, modified by Jiujutsu rank bonuses: +1k0 at Rank 3, +0k1 at Rank 7) or attack with a small weapon at normal damage. The target cannot apply Defense Stance bonuses. Throw — push the target 1 tile in any direction of the controller’s choice. Both characters go prone. The grapple ends. The thrown character takes no falling damage (1-tile throw) but loses their next Simple Action standing up. Pin — the target is fully immobilized. A pinned character cannot attempt to break free on their next turn — they can only speak, cast spells (verbal component only, Mastery 1 maximum per the Complex Action limit), or wait. Pin is a prerequisite for Bind. Bind — requires the target to be currently Pinned and requires the controller to have rope or restraints in inventory. The controller applies the Bound condition (Section 4.5.3 Bound state). The grapple ends. The target is now Bound per the existing rules: cannot move, attack, or use items, but CAN speak, cast spells, and use social ActionIDs.

Defender’s turn: The defender spends a Complex Action and chooses one option (unless Pinned, in which case only Speak and Cast are available). Break free — contested Strength roll, defender’s Jiujutsu / Strength vs controller’s Jiujutsu / Strength. If the defender wins, the grapple ends and the defender may immediately take a Free Move Action (1 tile in any direction). If the defender fails, the grapple continues and the controller retains control. Small weapon attack — the defender attacks the controller with a small weapon (tanto, jitte) at the controller’s Armor TN. Normal damage. Chain weapons at Rank 5 grant +1k0 on contested rolls against grappled opponents (Section 24). Cast spell — a grappled shugenja can cast spells with verbal components only. This follows the standard Complex Action limit — only Mastery Level 1 spells can be completed in a single round while grappled.

Third-party interaction: Characters adjacent to a grappling pair can attack either participant. However, attacking a specific participant in a grapple requires a called shot — +10 to the attack TN to ensure the blow hits the intended target and not their entangled opponent. An attacker who fails the called shot TN but would have hit the base Armor TN of either participant strikes the wrong target (randomly determined). Ranged attacks against grappling pairs follow the same +10 called shot rule. An ally who does not want to risk friendly fire should wait for the grapple to resolve or attempt to join the grapple (initiate a new grapple against the enemy participant, following normal initiation rules — if successful, the enemy is now grappled by two characters and suffers −10 on all break-free attempts).

Sasumata (man-catcher) special rule: The sasumata is a polearm with a forked head designed to pin a target at distance. It initiates grapple at 2-tile range instead of adjacent. On a successful initiation, the target is pinned at distance — the controller and target remain on separate tiles with 1 empty tile between them. The target cannot close to melee range. The target can only attempt to break free (contested Strength roll) or cast spells. The controller cannot use Damage or Bind from range — the controller must release the sasumata pin (Free Action, ends the grapple) and close to adjacent range to escalate. If the target breaks free from a sasumata pin, the controller may attempt one free re-grab as a Free Action (one retry per round, same Jiujutsu / Agility vs Armor TN roll). The sasumata is the doshin’s primary arrest tool — it allows heimin enforcers to restrain a suspect without entering sword range.

Doshin teamwork grapple: When 2 or more doshin are adjacent to the same target, they gain +5 on the grapple initiation roll (Section 57.46). Additionally, each doshin beyond the first who is adjacent to a grappled target adds +5 to the controller’s contested Strength roll to maintain control. Three doshin grappling one target: +5 initiation, +10 to maintain. This is the core doshin arrest mechanic — individually weak heimin who overwhelm through coordinated numbers.

Oni Crushing Grip: Oni and other supernatural creatures with the Crushing Grip ability use their own grapple rules as defined in their stat blocks (Section 40). The standard grapple flow applies except where the creature’s ability explicitly overrides it — typically by replacing the contested Strength roll with a fixed TN or by preventing all defender actions until the grip is broken. The Crushing Grip ability supersedes standard grappling rules for the creature that possesses it.

NPC grapple behavior: Doshin always prefer grapple over lethal attack against non-Shadowlands targets — the samurai avoidance hard gate (Section 57.46) still applies. Magistrate NPCs and yoriki grapple when the mission objective is arrest, not kill. Bushi NPCs only initiate grapples when they have a significant Strength advantage (+2 or more Strength over the target) and the objective is capture rather than combat. Shugenja and courtier NPCs never initiate grapples — they lack the Jiujutsu training and physical build.

⚙️ CROSS-REF: Jiujutsu skill and rank bonuses: Section 24. Chain weapons grapple: Section 24. Doshin teamwork +5: Section 57.46. Bound condition: Section 4.5.3. Oni Crushing Grip: Section 40 creature stat blocks. Sasumata in doshin stat blocks: Section 11.3.1. Weapon classifications (large vs small): Section 39.

**✓ RESOLVED:** *Mass combat and skirmish are linked through the Battle Table System (Section 11.7). The Army View resolves Company-level combat. When a character’s Battle Table roll triggers a Duel or Heroic Opportunity, a 20×20 Battlefield Bubble ASCII map generates for player characters. NPCs resolve through engine dice rolls.*

### 4.5.4 Movement in the ASCII Map

- Movement uses L5R 4th Edition RAW with 1 tile = 5 feet. Free Action: Water Ring in tiles per round (always available, on top of other actions). Simple Move Action: Water Ring × 2 tiles. Full Move (Complex Action): Water Ring × 4 tiles, cannot attack that round. A Water 3 character moves 3 tiles free every round, 6 tiles with a Simple Action, or 12 tiles at a full sprint.

- Difficult terrain (mud, rubble, water) costs double movement per tile.

- A Full Move action doubles movement but prevents attacking that round.

- Entering a character's threat zone triggers disengagement rules (possible free strike opportunity).

- Athletics interaction with tile movement: a character with Athletics Rank 3 ignores Moderate Terrain movement penalties; Rank 5 ignores Difficult Terrain movement penalties; Rank 7 adds +1 tile (5 feet) to one specific Move Action the character takes in a Round — the Round’s maximum total movement is unchanged. Full base rules for Move Actions, terrain, and stances are in Section 40.

**✓ RESOLVED:** *1 tile = 5 feet. All L5R 4e distance measurements convert at this rate. Spell ranges, movement, throwing distances, and weapon reach all use this conversion.*

### 4.5.5 Social & Skill Checks

- Social actions (Persuasion, Intimidation, Courtier, Etiquette) are resolved with Roll & Keep, using the relevant Trait + Skill.

- Honor and Glory affect social TNs — a dishonorable character faces higher TNs in formal settings.

- Contested checks: both parties roll, higher result wins (or sets the TN for the other).

**✓ RESOLVED:** *Social Check Triggers — LOCKED: On the ASCII map, a Social Menu is available alongside the Combat and Movement options. The Social Menu displays all available social actions (CHARM, NEGOTIATE, INTIMIDATE, PROBE, READ_CHARACTER, etc.) and prompts the player to select a target from visible characters. Standard social skill rolls apply — the ASCII map provides the spatial context (who is present, who is within conversation range) but the resolution is the same Roll and Keep system used everywhere else. The MUD view also provides social action options for interactions that do not require spatial positioning — conversations, letters, court actions, and any social exchange where physical tile placement is irrelevant.*

### 4.5.6 Climbing on the ASCII Map

Climbing covers vertical or near-vertical movement that cannot be handled by standard Move Actions — cliff faces, castle walls, steep cave shafts, ruined building facades, rope lines, tree trunks, and similar surfaces. Climbing is resolved per tile on the ASCII grid, using Athletics (Climbing) / Strength.

This subsection governs general climbing. Two specific contexts retain their own locked rules: the Kaiu Wall Horde Assault (Section 11.7) uses its own mass-combat-adapted climb model (ladders 2 tiles/round, raw strength 1 tile/round, 3k3 damage for ladder drops), and naval boarding (Section 11.9) uses its own rules for climbing from water onto a ship deck (TN 20, Full Round Action, provokes free attacks from the deck edge above). Both remain authoritative in their contexts. All other ASCII map climbing uses the rules below.

**Climb Action type:** Climbing one tile upward is a Complex Action. A character cannot attack, cast a spell, or take a second climb in the same Round. A Free Move Action may still be used before or after the climb (e.g., moving laterally along a ledge), subject to whether the surface permits it.

**Climb speed:** 1 tile per Round on success, regardless of surface difficulty. A character with a ladder, rope, or similar climbing aid that functions as an Easy surface may ascend 2 tiles per Round if they declare a Full Climb (trades the normal Complex-Action climb for doubled progress, at a flat +5 TN penalty for that Round).

**Climb TN by surface:**

- **Easy (TN 10):** Rope, ladder, cargo net, knotted line, cargo rigging, gnarled tree with abundant holds, a wall with exposed rebar or timber ties. No roll required on a successful declaration as long as nothing disrupts the climber. Roll only if the climber is attacked, heavily wounded, or subject to exceptional conditions (darkness, fog, wet surfaces).

- **Moderate (TN 15):** Rough stone wall, steep slope with natural holds, timber palisade, the outside of a typical peasant house, a well-constructed wooden fence. The baseline “climbable surface for a trained bushi.”

- **Hard (TN 20):** Smooth stone, wet timber, ship’s hull from the waterline, cave shaft with sparse holds, castle curtain wall. Matches the TN 20 anchor from Section 11.9.

- **Very Hard (TN 25):** Cliff face with minimal holds, slick rock, a castle wall that has been deliberately smoothed against climbers.

- **Extreme (TN 30):** Overhanging rock, icy cliff, glassy surfaces, the interior of a creature’s gullet (per Muduro no Oni, Section 54.5).

**Failure consequences:**

- **Miss TN by less than 5 — Stuck:** The climber makes no progress this Round but does not fall. The tile is held. Roll again next Round.

- **Miss TN by 5 to 9 — Slip:** The climber loses 1 tile of progress. If the climber is at the base (ground level), they are simply off the wall — no damage, no tile movement downward. Next Round’s climb roll is at +5 TN from strain and scraped hands. The strain penalty clears when the climber either reaches a ledge, resumes solid footing, or rests for a Round taking no climb action.

- **Miss TN by 10 or more — Fall:** The climber falls from their current height. Falling damage = 1k1 per 2 tiles (10 feet) of height fallen, rounded down to the nearest 2 tiles. A fall from 1 tile is ignored (character lands upright). A 4-tile fall = 2k2. A 6-tile fall = 3k3. A 10-tile fall = 5k5. Falls from above 20 tiles use RAW massive-damage conventions per Section 40. The falling character lands Prone on the tile directly below their last held position. A character who is Hurt or worse after the fall may not climb again this skirmish without magical or medical assistance.

**Armor penalties:**

- Light armor: −1k0 to climb rolls.

- Heavy armor: −2k0 to climb rolls. A character in heavy armor moves at 0.5 tiles per Round rounded down — the climber makes a climb roll every Round but only ascends 1 tile every second Round on success. Heavy armor cannot be worn on Very Hard (TN 25) or Extreme (TN 30) surfaces — the physical restrictions of the armor preclude the necessary range of motion. A character in heavy armor who wishes to climb such a surface must first remove their armor (Full Round Action × armor don time per Section 39).

These penalties match the existing naval boarding rule (Section 11.9) and apply identically across all climbing contexts.

**Wound penalties:** The universal Wound Rank penalty (Section 22.3) applies to all climb rolls as it does to any Trait roll. A Grazed character climbs at −5, a Hurt character at −10, and so on. No additional climb-specific wound rule is required — the existing system produces the correct outcome: a wounded character attempting a difficult climb will fall, and a heavily wounded character cannot realistically climb anything above Moderate difficulty.

**Void spend:** Standard — spending 1 Void Point grants +1k1 on a single climb roll, per Section 25.5. Particularly useful on the final tile of a long climb where one bad roll can undo the entire ascent.

**Interaction with Athletics Mastery Abilities:** The Rank 3, 5, and 7 Mastery Abilities in Section 24.2 apply to overland movement through terrain, not to climbing. Climbing is resolved through this subsection and does not interact with the movement-penalty reductions those Mastery Abilities grant. The Athletics (Climbing) Emphasis grants its standard benefit per Section 24.0 — re-roll any dice that resulted in a 1, once per roll.

**Descending:** Climbing downward is mechanically identical to climbing upward, but uses one TN step easier than the surface normally indicates (a Hard surface is TN 15 to descend; an Easy surface requires no roll). Downward climbing carries the same fall risk on miss-by-10+. A character may also attempt a “controlled fall” — deliberately dropping from any height below 6 tiles to the ground, taking falling damage per the standard formula. This is sometimes faster than descending carefully and is a legitimate tactical choice when pursued.

**Assisted climbing:** A character already at the top of a climb may lower a rope to a climber below (Simple Action). From that Round forward, the climb is treated as Easy (TN 10). Throwing a rope to a climber on an adjacent tile (e.g., the deck-edge rope rule in Section 11.9) uses Athletics (Throwing) / Agility TN 10 if the target is willing and stationary.

**Cooperative climbing:** The cooperative rule from Section 41 applies — roped-together climbers each roll their own Athletics (Climbing) / Strength, and the participant with the highest Skill Rank grants a bonus equal to their Skill Rank to all others’ rolls. This represents the experienced climber setting holds, anchoring rope, and calling out the route.

**Attacks against climbers:** A climber is physically committed to supporting their own weight and cannot defend normally. A defender attacking a climber gains +2k0 on the attack roll, and the climber cannot use any Defensive Stance benefits during the exchange. If the climber takes damage from the attack, they must immediately make a reactive Athletics (Climbing) / Strength roll at TN = (the climb’s normal surface TN) + (damage dealt). Failure by less than 5 holds position; failure by 5 to 9 causes a slip (as standard); failure by 10 or more causes a fall (as standard). Ranged attacks follow normal ranged attack rules against the climber’s base Armor TN — no Defense Stance adjustments apply. Dropping heavy objects on a climber (rocks, pots of oil, furniture) from above auto-hits the climber’s tile (no attack roll — gravity does the work) for the object’s damage, and the climber makes the same reactive climb roll. A defender adjacent to a ladder, grappling hook, or similar climbing aid may destroy it as a Simple Action with Strength TN 15 or one successful weapon strike against the aid — all climbers currently on that aid take falling damage per the fall formula.

**Climbing while stealthed:** A character maintaining Stealth during a climb rolls Athletics (Climbing) / Strength normally against the surface TN. Any failed climb roll (stuck, slip, or fall) automatically breaks Stealth for the climber — scraped stones, dropped gear, or caught breath give them away. Other characters in the zone make Perception rolls to notice per standard Stealth rules (Section 24.4). A successful climb roll does not break Stealth — a skilled climber in soft-soled shoes can ascend silently if they execute cleanly.

**Cross-references:** Kaiu Wall Horde Assault climbing — Section 11.7. Naval boarding climb from water to deck — Section 11.9. Athletics skill entry and Mastery Abilities — Section 24.2. Void spending — Section 25.5. Cooperative skill rolls — Section 41. Falling damage general reference — Section 40.

### 4.5.7 Leaping on the ASCII Map

Leaping covers jumping across gaps — a rooftop to an adjacent rooftop, across a collapsed section of floor in a dungeon, between boulders on a cave shaft. RAW does not assign leaping to a skill; it is resolved as a Reflexes roll, with the TN scaling to the width of the gap.

**Leap TN by gap width:**

- **1 tile (5 feet):** No roll. A trivial step-across.

- **2 tiles (10 feet):** Reflexes TN 15.

- **3 tiles (15 feet):** Reflexes TN 20. Matches the Wall of Flame “leap clear” anchor (Section 35) and the Hare Clan “Leap of the Hare” school technique range (Section 29.11).

- **4 tiles (20 feet):** Reflexes TN 25.

- **5+ tiles:** Beyond unaided human capability. Requires kiho, spell, or creature ability (e.g., Kharma’s Kiss kiho at Air Ring × 10 feet, Section 38; Hasaiki no Oni’s Leaping ability, Section 54.5).

**Running leap:** A character with at least 2 tiles of clear runup before the gap may leap at −5 TN (so a 3-tile gap becomes TN 15 with runup). The runup is a Free Move Action; the leap itself is the Simple Move Action that follows.

**Failure:** Miss by less than 5, the character lands at the far edge but Prone (they barely made it). Miss by 5 or more, the character fails to clear the gap and falls — falling damage per Section 4.5.6 (1k1 per 2 tiles fallen). Heavy armor adds +5 to the TN on any leap.

### 4.5.8 Swimming on the ASCII Map

Swimming covers crossing or moving through water tiles — a river in a dungeon, a flooded cave chamber, a moat at the base of a castle wall, a pond in a stealth approach. This subsection governs general swimming. Naval boarding water rules (Section 11.9, subsection 56.18.3) remain authoritative for ship-to-ship contexts.

**Stay afloat:** A character in a water tile must make an Athletics (Swimming) / Strength roll each Round to stay afloat. TN 15 in calm water. Failure means the character sinks and begins drowning per RAW. On a successful roll, the character remains at the surface and may also take one Simple Action.

**Crossing water tiles:** On a successful stay-afloat roll, the character may move 1 water tile that Round as a Simple Action. No faster swim mode is available — water always costs a full Simple Action per tile.

**TN modifiers by water conditions:**

- **Calm water** (pond, slow river, flooded room): TN 15.

- **Moderate current** (normal river, tidal flow): TN 20.

- **Strong current** (fast river, storm waves): TN 25.

- **White water / rapids:** TN 30. Each failed roll also sweeps the character 1 tile downstream.

**Armor penalties:** Light armor −1k0 to swim rolls. Heavy armor −2k0. A character who enters water while wearing heavy armor and fails the first stay-afloat roll sinks immediately and cannot recover without removing the armor (Full Round Action) — once below the surface, drowning begins per RAW.

**Wound penalties:** The universal Wound Rank penalty (Section 22.3) applies to swim rolls as it does to any Trait roll. A Hurt character swimming in strong current is at −10 before the current modifier, and will likely drown without assistance.

**Combat in water:** Characters swimming cannot attack or defend normally. Melee attacks suffer −2k0. Ranged attacks into or out of the water follow normal rules but the swimming character has no Defensive Stance benefits. This matches the naval boarding rule.

**Climbing out:** Climbing out of water onto a bank, ledge, or platform uses Athletics (Climbing) / Strength at TN 10 for a low bank, TN 15 for a high bank, TN 20 for a ship deck (the naval rule). See Section 4.5.6 for general climb rules. An ally may lower a rope (Simple Action), reducing the climb TN to 10 regardless of bank height.

**Void spend:** Standard — 1 Void Point for +1k1 on a single swim roll.

**Cross-references:** Naval boarding water rules — Section 11.9, 56.18.3. Climbing out of water — Section 4.5.6. Falling into water from a climb failure — Section 4.5.6. Athletics skill entry — Section 24.2.

### 4.5.9 Tracking on the ASCII Map

Tracking covers following a trail left by a creature or person through a wilderness or dungeon environment — bandits fleeing into the forest, an oni’s path through a ruined village, a fugitive’s route through cave tunnels. Tracking is resolved in segments of 5 tiles: one Hunting (Tracking) / Perception roll per segment of trail followed.

**The roll:** Hunting (Tracking) / Perception, against a TN set by trail age with terrain and weather modifiers. The roll is a Complex Action that produces no other activity that segment. Tracking cannot be combined with combat or stealth (see “Tracking while stealthed” below).

**Base TN by trail age:**

- **Fresh (same OOC day, under 24 real hours):** TN 15.

- **1 OOC day old:** TN 20.

- **2–3 OOC days old:** TN 25.

- **Week or older (7+ OOC days):** TN 30.

**Terrain modifiers:**

- **Soft ground** (mud, snow, sand): −5 TN.

- **Forest floor or grassland:** no modifier (baseline).

- **Rocky or hard ground:** +5 TN.

- **Paved road or stone floor:** +10 TN.

**Weather modifiers:**

- **Clear:** no modifier.

- **Recent rain or snow since the trail was made:** +10 TN.

- **Heavy rain or snowstorm since the trail was made:** trail lost. No roll is possible — the weather has erased the trail.

**Failure consequences:**

- **Miss TN by less than 5 — Stalled:** The tracker cannot identify the trail’s continuation this segment but does not lose it. Re-roll next segment at the same TN.

- **Miss TN by 5 to 9 — Trail Lost (recoverable):** The trail is lost for this segment. The tracker may pick it up on the next segment at +5 TN from the added difficulty of re-finding the line. If that re-find roll also fails by 5+, the trail is lost entirely per the tier below.

- **Miss TN by 10 or more — Trail Lost (entirely):** The tracker has lost the trail and cannot recover it from this point. They must backtrack to the last successfully-tracked segment and attempt a new approach — a different route, an ally’s assistance, or wait for new signs.

**Wound and armor penalties:** The universal Wound Rank penalty (Section 22.3) applies to tracking rolls. Armor does not penalise tracking — the difficulty is sensory, not physical.

**Void spend:** Standard — 1 Void Point grants +1k1 on a single tracking roll.

**Cooperative tracking:** The cooperative rule from Section 41 applies. Multiple trackers working together, with the highest-Hunting-Rank tracker leading, grants a bonus equal to the leader’s Hunting Skill Rank to all others’ rolls.

**Tracking while stealthed:** A tracker moving at full Stealth cannot also track — the concentration required for each is incompatible. A character may alternate: track a segment (5 tiles) openly, then move stealthily to the next vantage point, then track the next segment. The Rank 5 Athletics Mastery (+1k0 Stealth in wilderness environments, per Section 24.2) does not apply to tracking rolls — it applies only to Stealth rolls. This is correct and intentional: Hunting and Stealth are separate skills.
