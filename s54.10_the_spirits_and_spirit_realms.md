## 54.10 The Spirits and Spirit Realms

Rokugan has always been a highly religious culture that maintains links to matters beyond the merely physical. The world they inhabit is strongly connected to realms beyond, especially to the ten other Spirit Realms that coexist with the mortal realm. Most intelligent creatures from the Spirit Realms are fully aware of their own nature and will often consciously attempt to cross from one Realm to another. Ningen-do seems to exert a particular attraction to these creatures, and all manner of them have interacted with mortals over the course of the Empire’s thousand-year history. In general, travel between the different Spirit Realms and between those Realms and the mortal world of Ningen-do is difficult. In most cases, passing between the Spirit Realms requires some manner of portal, and certain locations within Ningen-do are attuned to the Spirit Realms where portals can be opened more easily.

### The Eleven Spirit Realms

CHIKUSHUDO, THE REALM OF ANIMALS: Of all the Spirit Realms, Chikushudo has the closest ties to the mortal world. Much of Chikushudo overlaps with Ningen-do, and its inhabitants frequently and easily pass over the border. The Realm of Animals looks almost exactly the same as Ningen-do, albeit without any signs of human habitation. Many shapeshifters call Chikushudo home – badgers, bats, cranes, dogs, foxes, hares, and monkeys, among others, who can change their form at will and even take on human bodies. Shapeshifters who venture out into the human world are bound by powerful laws known as taboos which restrict their actions and choices. One of these taboos is to never reveal these laws to their spouses. Because of this ignorance the laws are frequently broken, and when that happens the shapeshifter must return to Chikushudo. The most famous such marriage united the future Crane Clan Thunder, Doji Hoturi, to a kitsune woman named Doji Ameiko who later committed jigai when she learned of her husband’s apparent infidelities.

GAKI-DO, THE REALM OF THE HUNGRY DEAD: Those whose overwhelming depravities make them unworthy to immediately reenter the Celestial Order are ejected to Gaki-do, whose negative energies only increase the spirits’ insanity. Once a spirit enters Gaki-do, it is surrounded by demons and other spirits intent on devouring everything around them. These creatures hate everything and everyone without prejudice, and hold no thoughts in their minds other than to satisfy their eternal hunger. Gaki-do has weak borders with Toshigoku, Jigoku, and Ningen-do, allowing gaki to travel to those places with ease. The undead known as pennaggolans feed on the blood of mortals, as do the spirits known as o-toyo. The spirits called buruburu feed off the adrenaline and exhaustion of their victims, while the average gaki feeds on unclean substances – or simply eats its prey.

JIGOKU, THE REALM OF EVIL: Jigoku has been the enemy of Rokugan since the dawn of the Empire. It corrupted one of the Kami to lead its armies against humanity, and constantly threatens mankind with endless armies of demons. The Festering Pit is the single largest spirit portal in the mortal realm, a direct link between Ningen-do and Jigoku. The demons of Jigoku have distinct ranks, a pecking order that derives from strength and size – weaker oni will always stay out of the way of stronger oni and will slavishly follow their orders in battle. Mortals often say that Tengoku is the Realm of Good and Jigoku is the Realm of Evil, but this is not completely accurate – the forces of Tengoku represent the proper order and balance of all things, not simple goodness.

MAIGO NO MUSHA, THE REALM OF THWARTED DESTINY: Maigo no Musha is a unique Spirit Realm because it did not exist before man – it was the cataclysmic events initiated by mortals that shook the Celestial Order and gave birth to it. The twelfth-century conflict known as the War of Spirits was responsible for thousands of deaths that should never have happened, leaving souls in limbo because they had been blocked from fulfilling their destiny. A new Spirit Realm, the Realm of Thwarted Destiny, formed to house these poor creatures as they struggled to find a way back into the world. Over time, Maigo no Musha adopted a more expansive role and became the Realm of Lost Heroes. The Legion of the Dead – formed from the souls of fallen heroes who fought to preserve the natural balance – guards the realm from any intruders.

MEIDO, THE REALM OF WAITING: The Celestial Order reincarnates souls, sending them back to the world in another form, or to one of the other Spirit Realms in reward or punishment for their conduct in life. Before that can happen, however, these souls must go to Meido to be judged by Emma-O, the Fortune of Death. Meido is a flat, uninteresting place with few distinguishing features – it serves as an interim place, a pause before spirits pass onwards. Its borders are jealously guarded, as Meido must remain in a constant state to keep the Celestial Order from collapsing. To the creatures of Meido, however, it is not a curse – centuries of existing within the uneventful realm are enough to make any spirit desire change. Some creatures from Meido might be able to bestow their curse of mediocrity and ennui onto others: a “demon” that sucks enthusiasm from its surroundings could actually be a native spirit of Meido.

NINGEN-DO, THE REALM OF MORTALS: Ningen-do is the focus of attention from all the other Spirit Realms because mortals determine the future of everything around them. Actions taken on mortal soil have consequences that ripple out to the Spirit Realms. Those who wish to affect other Spirit Realms often find it easiest to accomplish this by altering the people of Ningen-do. The inhabitants of Ningen-do have become accustomed to the constant assault from outsiders and are generally determined to repel them all.

SAKKAKU, THE REALM OF MISCHIEF: Sakkaku is unique among all the Spirit Realms for one reason: it serves no higher purpose. The Realm of Mischief is filled with light-hearted spirits who exist only to play pranks on those unfortunate enough to stand in their way. The realm itself reflects the chaos of its inhabitants – the landscape follows no rhyme or reason and doesn’t seem to follow the laws of physics. As a whole, the creatures of Sakkaku do not intend to harm their victims – they derive pleasure in frustrating their opponents. Unfortunately, their definitions of “joke” do not often follow human conventions, and from a human perspective many of these pranks are deadly dangerous or exceedingly cruel.

TENGOKU, THE CELESTIAL HEAVENS: The rulers of the Celestial Pattern gather in Tengoku to hold court over all. Gods, Fortunes, Elemental Dragons, the Sun, and the Moon all reside within the beautiful world of Tengoku. The borders of this blessed realm are guarded by Fortunes, lesser kami, and powerful warrior spirits. Tengoku is forced to defend the other realms – not in the name of good, but in the name of the proper order of the universe.

TOSHIGOKU, THE REALM OF SLAUGHTER: Those who die with rage in their hearts are often consumed by their powerful emotions. These blighted souls enter Toshigoku, the Realm of Slaughter, where they wage war on each other for the rest of eternity. The land of Toshigoku resembles Rokugan in every way except one: those who die on its battlefields return on the sunrise of the next day, ready to continue their senseless fight. Packs of Toshigoku slaughter spirits often appear in Ningen-do with little to no warning in places of great conflict. The realm of Toshigoku is also the primary home base of the vicious Tsuno, an ancient off-shoot of the Kitsu.

YOMI, THE REALM OF BLESSED ANCESTORS: Yomi is the reward for a mortal who fulfills his destiny – it is the highest reward a soul may achieve without attaining divinity. The Realm of Blessed Ancestors closely resembles Ningen-do, with cities and landscapes that appear exactly like their counterparts from the mortal realm, but free of the pain and misery of mortal life. Ancestor spirits, known as shiryo, reside in Yomi and watch over their descendants. Residents of Yomi stay out of most conflicts between the other realms but are also the quickest to rise to the defense of any Spirit Realm that comes under assault. The Blessed Guard, an elite unit of shiryo comprising the finest heroes ever produced by the human race, guards Yomi itself.

YUME-DO, THE REALM OF DREAMS: The borders of Yume-do are paper thin and creatures of every Spirit Realm access it nightly. Whenever anyone, man or beast, falls asleep and dreams, he is accessing the Realm of Dreams. Many of the inhabitants of Yume-do do not have a defined form – they change and adapt as each mind affects the reality of the realm. Other inhabitants, such as the Nezumi Transcendents, have adapted to the nature of their home and are able to use its power freely. Yume-do is one of the most dangerous Spirit Realms to enter because of its chaotic and unpredictable nature, since monsters from Jigoku, ghosts from Gaki-do, and tormented spirits of Toshigoku can also access Yume-do.

### New Mechanics: Shapeshifters and Advantages

CHILD OF CHIKUSHUDO [Spiritual Advantage, 7 Points]: One of your ancestors was a spirit from beyond Ningen-do, and you have inherited some of his vast abilities. You may choose a Minor Shapeshifter Ability (subject to GM approval). However, your abilities were never intended to be contained within a human form, so you suffer one of the following special penalties (determine randomly or collaboratively with GM): 1-2: Unsettling Visage – you suffer a –2k1 penalty on all Social rolls; 3-4: Crippling Stutter – every few sentences, your words come out garbled and nonsensical; 5-6: Painful Change – every time you use one of your Shapeshifter Abilities, you suffer 5 Wounds; 7-8: Base Instincts – once a week, the animal within compels you to perform humiliating acts that would seem normal to an animal, and if others witness your shameful display, you lose 6 Glory points; 9-0: Unstable Form – each time you use a Shapeshifting Ability, you must make a Void roll at TN 20 or you cannot use your Shapeshifting Ability and cannot use Void Points until the next sunrise.

MEDIUM [Spiritual Advantage, 4 Points]: You have a strong connection with the Spirit Realms beyond your own. Though you may not physically travel to the other Realms, you may project your spirit into them by entering deep concentration. Your spiritual projection is malleable, and whenever you encounter other spirits while in this state they will treat you as one of their own unless you choose to reveal your status as outsider to them.

THE SHAPESHIFTER SYSTEM: A shapeshifter may choose a number of Abilities from the lists below. For each Ability the Shapeshifter must also choose a Minor or Major Penalty – a Minor Ability can be compensated with a Minor or Major Penalty, but a Major Ability always requires a Major Penalty. If a Shapeshifter ever breaks any of the rules set by its Penalties, it immediately loses all the benefits of its Shapeshifter Abilities until the next sunrise.

MINOR SHAPESHIFTER ABILITIES: Animal Empathy (understand chittering of natural animals); As the Fish (breathe underwater, swim at walking speed); Eyes of the Owl (see in darkness as well as daylight); Humanity (turn human for one full day – if used consecutively for at least one year and one day, you lose your shapeshifting abilities permanently and become entirely mortal); Flight (gain ability to fly, gain Swift 1); Legendary Healing (once per skirmish, heal 2k1 Wounds as a Complex Action); Mimic (alter form to another form of roughly the same size as a Complex Action, lasts 5 minutes or 5 Rounds in battle); A Panther’s Moves (+3k0 bonus to all Stealth rolls); Piercing Howl (once per skirmish, emit an ear-shattering howl as a Complex Action, creating a Fear 2 effect on every character within 10’); Speaker of the Dead (understand words spoken by creatures and monsters of other Spirit Realms, though you may not speak their languages).

MAJOR SHAPESHIFTER ABILITIES: Bound by the Heavens (three times a day, gain +1k1 bonus on any single roll while directly following a promise; if you violate the letter or spirit of a promise, suffer –1k1 penalty on all rolls until the next sunrise); Ephemeral Form (once per day, make your body insubstantial for 10 Rounds – you may pass through other physical objects with ease); Possession (once per day, leave your body behind and attempt to enter another person as a Complex Action, requiring a Contested Willpower roll; if the target fails, you inhabit the target’s mind and control his actions until the next sunrise, but you may not compel your victim to harm himself or his loved ones); Protection of Tengoku (Partial Invulnerability to all mundane weapons); Protection of Yomi (Reduction 5, not cumulative with armor but stacks with other natural sources of Reduction); Speed of a Predator (Swift 2); Strength of Jade (your attacks may ignore Invulnerability as if using a weapon of jade, but suffer –2k0 penalty on the damage roll).

MINOR SHAPESHIFTER PENALTIES: You cannot drink alcohol.

MAJOR SHAPESHIFTER PENALTIES: You cannot be the target of any elemental spell (if involuntary subject of a spell, cannot use one Shapeshifter ability until next sunrise); you cannot kill another living being; you cannot cross running water; you cannot eat meat of any kind; you must always dress in the colors of your natural form; your shadow always reveals your true form; you cannot bathe; you cannot lie; you cannot answer any question with a straight answer (must answer with another question); you cannot wear clothes other than the bare minimum to cover your modesty; you cannot enter a shrine or temple; you cannot allow a human to touch you; you cannot tell anyone your true nature or your true name; you may not speak; you may never touch an item created by humans; you must marry a worthy human within one year and treat your spouse with loyalty and respect (if your spouse proves unworthy, you must return to your own Realm and never come back to Ningen-do).

### Spirits of Chikushudo

KITSUNE. Fox spirits are both the most numerous and the most famous creatures from the Realm of Animals. The kitsune’s natural form is that of a fox with multiple tails, ranging between two (a minor fox spirit) to nine (a goddess of the forest). Kitsune have a zest for life which pushes them to explore as much of the world as possible. They are mischievous and curious creatures that relate to humans more than any other spirit, interacting with them as often as they can. Some have fallen in love and married humans, living with them for decades before returning to their own realm. The Kitsune Mori near the Fox Clan has a very close bond with Chikushudo, and many kitsune reside within that deep forest.

Special Abilities: Shapeshifter – A typical Kitsune will have Mimic, A Panther’s Moves, Protection of Yomi, and Ephemeral Form. Spirit. Swift 3.

KITSUNE-TSUKI. When a kitsune dies it attempts to clear its mind and soul of the troubles of the world. Those who fail to do so sometimes become consumed by the failures of their life and transform into a kitsune-tsuki – a deadly and tormented mockery of the noble creature they once were. Kitsune-tsuki take the form of ghostly foxes that haunt the land where they passed away. They remain there and prey on passersby in a futile effort to feel the same sensations and pleasures they did while alive. The kitsune-tsuki possesses mortal bodies, taking control of their actions and using them to vicariously experience life. The kitsune-tsuki cannot force a possessed human to harm itself, but this does not protect the victim from other dangerous activities. The kitsune-tsuki’s possession does not last long – it must exit its host twenty-four hours after the initial possession.

### Spirits of Gaki-do

BURUBURU. The buruburu is a malevolent spirit that loves to torture its victims while it feeds on their fear. It attaches to its victim undetected and does its best to remain unobtrusive – it is very easily killed by any who recognize its presence, so it must rely on subterfuge and deception to maintain its survival. The buruburu possesses its target and begins to slowly destroy him over weeks of painful manipulation. It forces its victim to suffer through the worst of its fears – every night the target is asleep, the buruburu tortures him to make sure he never gets a peaceful rest. Every nightmare seems brutally real. The victim finally dies from terror – or if he can overcome his fears, sheer exhaustion. This process takes weeks to finish, but the effects of the long descent are painful to behold.

O-TOYO. The o-toyo resembles a human-sized cat with exaggerated fangs and sharp claws. It can take the form of a much smaller cat to fool its prey; however, anyone can spot the o-toyo’s true nature by peering at the beast from the corner of their eye. The o-toyo normally maintains a meek and docile nature to fool others into approaching, but once its prey is in range it pounces with supernatural speed. Its favored style of attack is to pin the target to the ground with its claws, then bite into the victim’s neck, feeding on its essence. The o-toyo resides in forests and less populated areas and feeds almost exclusively on humans.

It has a very specific nesting ground: the mokumokuren only appears in the ripped holes of a paper wall. The easiest way to banish a mokumokuren is to fix all the holes in the paper walls – it will simply move on in search of another ill-maintained wall.

### Chikushudo Spirit Animal Overlay --- Encounter Variants

The following overlay applies to any base animal stat block from Section 54.1 when encountered inside a Chikushudo overlap (Section 56.16.1). Spirit animals are not new species. They are the existing wolves, bears, boars, stags, eagles, and snakes of the province --- perfected. Bigger, faster, smarter, more beautiful. They are the ideal of their kind. They fight to defend territory, not out of malice. Killing one feels wrong. They are not evil. They are not corrupted. They are simply more than their mortal counterparts.

- **Ring Increase:** All Rings +2 over the base animal stat block. A mortal wolf with Earth 2 becomes Earth 4. A bear with Strength 4 becomes Strength 6. The increase applies to all derived statistics --- Attack rolls, damage, Armor TN, Wound thresholds.

- **Swift Increase:** Swift rating increased by +1 over base. A wolf already at Swift 2 becomes Swift 3. They are faster than any mortal animal.

- **Intelligence:** Spirit animals fight with real intelligence, not animal instinct. Packs coordinate flanking maneuvers. A spirit wolf pack sets ambushes, retreats to draw pursuers into killzones, and disengages when the fight is not worth it. They do not flee from wounds like mortal animals --- they withdraw deliberately when territory is secure.

- **Territorial:** Spirit animals do not pursue beyond their territory. If the player retreats, the animals let them go. The forest does not chase you. You come to it. This means the player can always disengage --- but disengaging means giving up ground toward the heart.

- **No Fear Effect:** Spirit animals are not scary. They are majestic. There is no mechanical Fear. The psychological pressure is guilt --- the player is killing beautiful creatures that are defending their home. This is not represented mechanically. It is felt through the encounter’s tone.

- **Spirit:** All spirit animals have the Spirit trait. They cannot be permanently killed --- they reform in Chikushudo. Closing the overlap stops them from manifesting.

- **Cannot Be Controlled:** Spells and abilities that summon, bind, or control animals do not function against Chikushudo spirit animals. They are in their native domain. They cannot be commanded by mortals. Animal Handling receives a Free Raise for communication but cannot compel obedience.

- **No Taint:** Spirit animals have Taint Rank 0. There is no corruption in Chikushudo. Jade has no special effect against them.

The guardian at the heart of a Chikushudo overlap is an ancient animal spirit of immense power --- a hengeyokai spirit lord whose age and wisdom approach that of a minor Fortune. The specific form depends on the province’s terrain and wildlife: a great white bear in mountain provinces, a nine-tailed fox in forested provinces, a massive stag in plains provinces, an enormous serpent in river provinces. A party that fights its way to the heart and attacks the spirit lord faces 200 Wounds of Partially Invulnerable ancient beast while performing a ritual. A party that communicates and negotiates might complete the ritual peacefully. The shugenja’s social skills matter as much as the bushi’s sword.

When in Rokugan they wear resplendent kimono and large hats, partly because the tennyo appreciate beauty and enjoy Rokugani styles, but mainly because the hat helps conceal their face – while the tennyo holds a human form, its face is completely blank of all features, including eyes, nose, and mouth. Several mortals have mistaken tennyo for creatures of the Lying Darkness at first glance, but their noble actions quickly disabuse that notion. The tennyo does not appreciate combat, though it will defend itself. The only exception is when the tennyo encounters a creature of Jigoku – then the tennyo strikes with rapid precision, devastating the target in mere moments.

### Spirits of Yomi

SHIRYO (Ancestor Spirits). In their natural state within Yomi, the shiryo are corporeal and real, retaining much the same armor and weapons they held or favored during their natural lives. They resemble humans with one large exception: to human eyes, the shiryo appear to wear large masks that cover their entire face, decorated to reflect the soul within. When shiryo visit Ningen-do, they are incorporeal and can only interact with those of great spiritual power – unless speaking to their own descendants. A shiryo will have the same Traits, Rings, Skills, and other abilities it possessed in life, but also has the Spirit creature quality. Shiryo may gain additional knowledge and wisdom during their eternity in the Realm of Yomi, typically expressed as increases in mental Traits, Void, and Skills.

SHUTSUDOHIN (Awakened Item Spirits). When powerful nemuranai are finally destroyed or cast out of the physical world, the spirits within the items ascend into Yomi as shutsudohin. They retain a powerful connection with the human race and have humanoid forms, yet none can mistake a shutsudohin for a normal ancestor – their angles are sharp and jarring and the spirit as a whole has a peculiar boxy form. They can change form into the item they once were, change their size to suit the needs of the moment, or fight as an animate weapon on their own. Shutsudohin love Rokugan and humanity and will do whatever needed to serve them – sometimes they will volunteer to be reformed into a new weapon so their power and energy can continue to serve Rokugan in the mortal world. Thus, it is actually possible for a shutsudohin to ascend to Yomi more than once, stronger each time as it infuses one legendary weapon after another.

BLESSED GUARD. Every member of the Blessed Guard was once a normal ancestor, yet each has earned great importance in Yomi. They are the shiryo of Rokugani heroes – the finest heroes ever produced by the human race – and dress uniformly in heavy lamellar armor in the fashion of samurai from the dawn of the Empire, their armor and weapons created with a special metal found within Yomi itself that dims glow at all times. The Blessed Guard rarely leave Yomi, but on a few occasions have traveled to the other Spirit Realms to fight for a just cause, including traveling to Rokugan during the Battle at Oblivion’s Gate and defending Tengoku when it came under attack by Fu Leng. The Blessed Guard will not shy away from the greatest of dangers because they know they are the best there ever was.

### Spirits of Yume-do

NEZUMI TRANSCENDENTS. The Nezumi shamans have the peculiar ability to transcend reality and become creatures of pure Name, ascending into Yume-do to live forever. Though they inhabit the Realm of Dreams, the Transcendents always keep a careful eye on the welfare of their race in Ningen-do. They love puzzles and riddles, often invoking them when others ask for their advice. The Transcendents are powerful beyond imagining – their magic is rarely invoked but has tremendous potency.

BAKU. The baku is the heart of the Realm of Dreams. Without the baku, the dreamers of the other worlds could never access Yume-do during their sleep. Most baku are very short-lived – they die and disappear as soon as the dreamer wakes up. Once in a while, however, a dream is uniquely powerful and the baku of these dreams survive and grow stronger, drawing individuality from the dream and the surrounding Realm. These baku become spirits able to change form to anything they desire, with their natural form being that of a large dragonfly. Within Yume-do, the baku has near limitless power – outside its native realm, the baku maintains its shapeshifter abilities but loses most of its power. The baku are passive creatures that do not wish harm on anyone; they will strike to defend themselves but will not move beyond those instincts.
