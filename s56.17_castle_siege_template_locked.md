## 56.17 Castle Siege Template --- LOCKED

The ninth map template. Generates when a storm assault fires with a player character present — either as attacker or defender. The Army View (Section 11.7) resolves the overall siege at Company level. The Castle Siege Template generates the ASCII map for the player’s personal experience of the assault. Same world state, different resolution path per Section 9.

**56.17.1 Size Categories**

Map size scales with the settlement being assaulted. Fortification: 20×25. A military structure — outer wall, single gate, small courtyard, command building. Two layers (wall, interior). Castle Town: 25×30. Outer wall with gate, outer bailey with buildings, inner wall with gate, inner bailey, tenshu (main keep). Three layers. City: 30×40. Extensive outer wall, multiple gates, outer district, inner wall, castle compound, tenshu. Four layers. Each layer is a defensive line the attacker must breach to advance.

**56.17.2 Map Layout**

Japanese castle architecture funnels attackers through kill zones. The map reflects this. Walls are impassable solid tiles. Gates are reinforced but not the primary breach point — a well-defended gate is a kill zone, not an opportunity. The primary assault method is ladders against the walls, overwhelming the defenders above, and opening the gate from the inside once the walkway is taken. Ladders can be placed against walls (attacker Simple Action, one ladder per round per character). Gates can also be set on fire (requires fire arrows or a deliberate arson action — Complex Action, gate burns through in 5–10 rounds depending on size, defenders can douse the fire with water from above). Defenders can push ladders away (contested Strength roll, TN 15). Elevated walkways run along the inside of each wall — defenders on the walkway gain the Mounted/Higher attack bonus (+1k0) against enemies below and hard cover from ranged attacks (+10 Armor TN). Murder holes above gates allow attacks on enemies assaulting the gate below — 3k2 damage per round, no attack roll, as long as a defender occupies the murder hole tile. Arrow slits in walls allow ranged attacks outward at +0 modifier while granting +15 Armor TN from return fire. Baileys are open courtyards between wall layers — once the attacker breaches a gate, the fighting moves to the bailey where defenders can mass. The tenshu (main keep) is the final objective for attackers and the last retreat for defenders.

**56.17.3 Assault Mode (Player Attacking)**

The player starts outside the outer wall with 10–15 friendly soldiers from their army. Objective: reach the tenshu. The player must breach each gate or scale each wall to advance through the layers. Friendly soldiers fight alongside the player — they batter gates, place ladders, and engage defenders. The Army View state feeds the map: if the overall assault is succeeding, friendly soldiers push harder and defenders thin out on the walkways. If the assault is failing, friendly soldiers take casualties and the defenders hold firm. Once attackers take a section of wall walkway, they can descend into the bailey and open the gate from within — allowing the main assault force to pour through without climbing. This is the standard method of breaching a Rokugani castle. Fire is the alternative — fire arrows and arson against wooden gates and buildings. Kaiu Engineers (Crab) provide a significant advantage: they can identify structural weaknesses in walls, construct better siege ladders faster, and direct fire attacks more effectively. Armies without siege specialists rely on numbers, ladders, and courage.

**56.17.4 Defense Mode (Player Defending)**

The player starts on the outer wall walkway with 10–15 friendly garrison soldiers. Objective: hold until the assault fails. Enemy soldiers spawn at the bottom edge each Battle Turn in waves, same as the Horde Defense variant. They place ladders against walls, set fires at the gates, and attempt to breach. If attackers take a section of walkway, they descend to open the gate from within. The player decides where to concentrate defenders — at the gate, along the wall pushing ladders, or at murder holes. If the outer wall falls, the player retreats to the inner wall and the defense continues. If all layers fall, the player retreats to the tenshu for a last stand. The Army View state feeds the map: if the garrison is winning overall (attackers taking heavy casualties), waves shrink and enemies become less aggressive. If losing, waves intensify and breaches happen faster. Garrison soldiers receive the full defensive bonuses from the Army View rules: +3 Defense (Urban terrain) and +5 Defense (castle fortification) on top of their base stats when fighting from the walls.

**56.17.5 Friendly and Enemy Force Composition**

Both sides use the individual soldier stat blocks from Section 11.6. The specific unit types are drawn from whatever Companies are involved in the Army View — a Lion army assaulting a Crane castle means Akodo Bushi attacking and Daidoji Heavy Spearmen defending. Named commanders (Gunso+) appear with their full character sheets. The defending garrison commander is the high-value target for attackers — reaching and killing or capturing them can break the defense. The attacking army’s general is not on the map — they direct from behind.

**56.17.6 Resolution and Consequence**

The Castle Siege Template resolves when the Army View resolves the storm assault. Assault succeeds (attacker reaches the tenshu and kills or captures the garrison commander): the castle falls. Ownership transfers. The castle’s garrison is destroyed or captured. The defending lord is captured or killed depending on the situation. Assault fails (attacker’s Companies rout or retreat): the castle holds. The attacking army takes casualties and must decide whether to maintain the siege or withdraw. A player who personally reached the inner bailey but whose army failed around them must retreat — holding a position alone inside a defended castle is not survivable. Heroic Opportunity rewards from the Battle Table apply normally — the player rolls the Battle Table each Battle Turn during the assault.
