## 11.2 Flagged System — Travel Papers & Movement Control (RESOLVED — FLAVOR ONLY)

Source: Chapter Six (Trade Routes), Chapter Seven (Magistrates). Travel papers are required to cross clan borders — their absence is a crime. Clan magistrates and wardens check these at key road crossings. This system is not a current priority, but one element is worth noting early: between allied clans, travel papers would likely be freely granted and largely a formality. Between rival or hostile clans, obtaining papers becomes a genuine obstacle and a political tool.

- Allied clans: papers are a formality — granted easily, primarily an information trail that lords can use to track movement.

- Hostile or neutral clans: papers may be refused entirely, effectively functioning as an embargo on individual movement and trade.

- The primary mechanical relevance: a clan can halt trade with another clan not by military action but simply by refusing to issue or honor travel papers. This is a courtier/political action on the World Map.

- Player character relevance: forging papers, obtaining papers through political favors, and confiscating papers are all potential gameplay actions — but this is deferred.

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Law ****&**** Order):** The travel paper signing hierarchy is documented in Section 11.3.6 (Charter of the Emerald Magistrates). Emerald Magistrates are the lowest Imperial rank authorized to sign cross-clan travel papers. Clan Champions and family daimyo have similar authority. Clan magistrates and governors can only sign papers for travel to a specific location in another clan’s territory. Checking travel papers is a core duty of clan magistrates (Section 11.3.3).

**☑ RESOLVED — NO MECHANICS:** *Travel papers are background flavor only. They exist in the lore as part of Rokugan’s bureaucratic texture and are referenced in magistrate duties (Section 11.3) and criminal activity (forgery), but no dedicated travel paper mechanic will be designed. Movement control between clans is handled through existing diplomacy and trade route systems. No further design work required.*
