## 54.7i The Kolat — Win Condition, Secrecy, and the Imperial Counter-Response

### The Kolat Win Condition

The Kolat’s ultimate objective is the installation of a Kolat-aligned Regent or equivalent power-behind-the-throne in the Imperial court. Not necessarily the Emperor themselves — a Regent, Imperial Advisor, Voice of the Emperor, or similar position of unchecked proximity to the throne is sufficient. The candidate may be a conscious Kolat member or a Dream sleeper with an appropriate command installed. The goal is not public revolution. It is not the destruction of the Celestial Order by force. It is the quiet capture of the Imperial decision-making apparatus — the Emperor continues to perform his rituals, the Clan Champions continue to bow, the Celestial Order continues to function on the surface. But the person whispering in the Emperor’s ear serves the Kolat.

**Win condition trigger.** The engine registers a Kolat victory when: (a) the primary candidate (kolat_primary_candidate_npc_id) holds a position of Regent, Imperial Advisor, or equivalent court role with direct access to the Emperor, AND (b) the candidate has held this position for one full IC year without their Kolat affiliation being discovered, AND (c) imperial_awareness_level is below 70 (the Imperial family does not have enough knowledge to act decisively). If all three conditions are met simultaneously, the Kolat win condition fires as a world-state event. The game does not end — this is a persistent world. But the political landscape shifts dramatically: the Kolat’s influence over Imperial policy becomes a background constant, and the player community faces a new challenge of exposing and dismantling an entrenched conspiracy with Imperial cover.

**Candidate pipeline.** Tiger maintains kolat_primary_candidate_npc_id on their character sheet — the NPC currently being groomed for the Regent position. Tiger also maintains kolat_backup_candidate_npc_ids: Array[npc_id] — up to two backup candidates being prepared in parallel. If the primary candidate dies, is exposed, or becomes politically unviable, Tiger immediately elevates the highest-scored backup. The Kolat always has a plan B. Each Sect’s role in advancing the candidate: Silk monitors the candidate’s political environment and reports threats. Cloud archives everything about the candidate’s rivals and maintains leverage over obstacles. Coin funds the candidate’s political rise through strategic merchant patronage. Chrysanthemum builds the candidate’s reputation at court and undermines rivals. Dream conditions or recruits the candidate if they are not already a conscious Kolat member. Lotus eliminates competitors for the position when subtler approaches fail. Steel protects the conspiracy’s infrastructure while all of this unfolds. Tiger coordinates the entire pipeline and sets the pace.

### Secrecy Architecture

**kolat_exposure_level: int (world state, 0–100).** How much the general population, non-Imperial institutions, and player characters broadly know about the Kolat’s existence. Starts at 0 at world generation — nobody outside the Kolat knows the conspiracy exists. The Imperial family actively suppresses this value even as their own awareness grows, because public knowledge of a conspiracy threatening the throne damages the legitimacy of the Celestial Order. The Empire will not announce what it knows. Events that raise kolat_exposure_level: a Kolat operation traced publicly to a named merchant network (+5), a Master’s cover identity publicly associated with criminal activity (+10), a Kolat-linked assassination attributed to an organisation rather than a lone actor (+15), a player community discovering and publishing significant evidence of the Kolat’s existence (+10–25 depending on scope). Events that lower kolat_exposure_level: Lotus eliminating investigators before they report publicly (−5), Coin bribing the right magistrate to bury a report (−3), Cloud resurrecting cover topics that muddy evidence (−5), time passing without fresh evidence (−2 per season naturally). The Imperial family’s covert suppression actively decrements this value when imperial_awareness_level is above 30: the Empire knows and is deliberately containing the story.

**imperial_awareness_level: int (world state, 0–100).** How much the Imperial family privately knows about the Kolat. Tracked separately from public exposure because the Empire fights the Kolat covertly — raising their own knowledge while suppressing public knowledge. Starts at 0. Events that raise imperial_awareness_level: a Jade Magistrate investigation reaching a Kolat operative and reporting internally (+5), a player presenting evidence directly to Imperial authorities (+10–30 depending on quality), a captured Kolat Master being interrogated by the Seppun Hidden Guard (+20), the tiger_succession_key or kolat_master_identities being discovered (+40). The Imperial family never shares this information with Clan Champions or the public except at threshold 90+, and even then under the highest oath of secrecy. The throne’s legitimacy depends on appearing inviolable.

**imperial_response_active: bool (world state).** Set to true when imperial_awareness_level reaches 30. Triggers specific NPC behaviours in Imperial-aligned characters across the Empire. While true, certain Emerald Magistrates receive expanded mandates described only as investigating “anomalous merchant activity and succession irregularities.” Certain Seppun Hidden Guard units are reassigned to provinces with known Kolat activity. Certain succession candidates receive unexpected Imperial protection. None of these characters are told they are fighting the Kolat — they are given deniable cover mandates and act accordingly.

### Imperial Response Thresholds

The Imperial family’s response escalates as imperial_awareness_level rises. At every threshold the response is covert, institutional, and deniable. The Emperor never publicly names the Kolat.

**0–29 — Unaware.** No response. The Kolat operates freely. The Imperial court sees no pattern in what appears to be unrelated criminal activity, merchant disputes, and unfortunate deaths.

**30–49 — Suspicious.** imperial_response_active fires. A trusted Imperial advisor privately investigates anomalous patterns. One or two Seppun Hidden Guard captains are briefed that something organised may be operating against Imperial interests. They are told nothing specific. They look for patterns. Standard Emerald Magistrate investigations in affected provinces receive quiet resource increases.

**50–69 — Confirmed threat.** The Emperor is personally briefed in sealed session. A small covert task force assembles: selected Emerald Magistrates with expanded authority, Hidden Guard operatives, one or two Phoenix Inquisitors. They are told only that a criminal conspiracy threatens the succession — not that it is the Kolat, not its full scope. The task force begins systematic investigation of merchant networks, unusual deaths, and succession anomalies. Tiger’s kolat_strategic_priorities field gains a new entry: “degrade Imperial task force.”

**70–89 — Partially mapped.** Imperial investigators have identified at least one Master’s cover identity or mapped at least one Kolat operation. Targeted surveillance begins on identified individuals. The task force expands. The Emperor considers privately briefing the most loyal Clan Champions. The Kolat’s win condition is now significantly harder to achieve — any candidate advancement is under scrutiny. Tiger activates emergency protocols across all Sects.

**90–99 — Significantly mapped.** Multiple Masters identified. The Emperor privately briefs the most loyal Clan Champions under sealed Imperial oath. A coordinated covert purge is planned: simultaneous arrests, asset seizures, sealed trials. The Kolat enters full emergency mode. All non-essential operations suspended. Masters with confirmed cover exposure are ordered to flee or go dark permanently. The win condition is effectively blocked until exposure is reduced.

**100 — Full Imperial knowledge.** The Emperor acts. A sealed Imperial decree authorises simultaneous arrest of all identified Kolat members. Assets are seized. Operations are shut down. No public announcement is made — the Kolat is broken quietly, the same way it operated. The throne’s legitimacy is protected. History records nothing. This is the Kolat’s catastrophic failure state — not a public defeat, but an effective end to the conspiracy’s current generation. Seeds may survive. The next generation of the Kolat may rebuild. But the current operation is over.

### Tiger’s Candidate Fields

**kolat_primary_candidate_npc_id: int or null.** Stored in special_data on Tiger’s character sheet. The NPC currently being groomed for the Regent or equivalent Imperial position. Set by Tiger’s OVERSEE_KOLAT_NETWORK when a viable succession opportunity is identified. Null at world start — Tiger must identify and begin cultivating a candidate before this field is populated. Updated if the primary candidate becomes unviable. Flagged is_hidden: true.

**kolat_backup_candidate_npc_ids: Array[int].** Up to two backup candidates being prepared in parallel. If the primary candidate dies, is exposed, or becomes politically unviable, Tiger immediately elevates the highest-scored backup to primary. The array is ordered by Tiger’s preference. Flagged is_hidden: true.

**kolat_candidate_pipeline_stage: string.** Tracks the primary candidate’s current advancement stage: “identified” (Tiger has selected the NPC as a potential candidate but no active cultivation has begun), “cultivating” (Sects are actively advancing the candidate’s position and removing obstacles), “positioned” (the candidate holds a senior court role with Imperial proximity), “installed” (the candidate holds the target position — win condition check active), “compromised” (the candidate’s Kolat affiliation has been discovered — pipeline reset). Flagged is_hidden: true.
