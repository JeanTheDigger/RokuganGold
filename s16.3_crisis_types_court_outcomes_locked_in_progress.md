## 16.3 Crisis Types & Court Outcomes — LOCKED (in progress)

For every crisis type, three things are documented: the possible court commitments, the world systems those commitments engage, and the crisis end condition. Court does not resolve crises directly — it produces commitments. The actual resolution happens through the game's existing military, resource, and character systems. Momentum does not drop when someone pledges help. It drops when the committed actions actually happen in the world.

**Tier 1 Crisis Types — Existential / Supernatural:**

**Shadowlands Incursion:**

A Shadowlands force has breached the Kaiu Wall or manifested within Rokugan's borders. The most serious crisis in the Empire. The reason the Crab Clan exists. Every lord's personal relevance minimum is 50 regardless of geography — a Shadowlands incursion threatens all of Rokugan.

- Tier: 1. Momentum gain: +3/tick base × scope multiplier. Personal relevance minimum: 50 for all lords.

- End condition: The Shadowlands army is defeated in battle through the military combat system. Momentum resets to 0 when the last Shadowlands force in the affected province is destroyed.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Send military aid: Commit troops to reinforce the Crab or join the fighting directly. Engages army movement and combat systems. The committed army must physically travel to the affected province and engage the Shadowlands force.

- Send shugenja: Commit shugenja characters to assist with purification, magical combat, and Taint detection. Engages character movement system. Shugenja provide support in combat and can slow Taint spread in affected provinces.

- Send supplies: Commit Rice and Arms without committing troops. Engages resource transfer system. Sustains Crab forces longer without committing military assets.

- Request Imperial Intervention: Escalate to Winter Court agenda. If court level is insufficient to compel all clans, this adds the crisis to the top three Imperial topics. The Emperor may issue an Edict binding all clans to contribute.

- Do nothing: Acknowledge the crisis and commit nothing. Legally valid but carries severe Honor and disposition consequences given the existential nature of the threat. Crab disposition toward non-contributing clans drops permanently — historical modifier applied.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- The Shadowlands force advances automatically — moves into adjacent provinces on the World Map each tick.

- Affected provinces lose PU and Rice to Shadowlands depredation each tick.

- Taint spreads — affected provinces may generate new Tier 1 crises of their own.

- The crisis does not resolve on its own. Only military defeat ends it.

**Maho Cult Spreading:**

A practitioner or group of practitioners of Maho — blood magic — has established themselves within a province and is actively corrupting the population, performing dark rituals, and potentially summoning oni or spreading Taint from within Rokugan itself. Unlike a Shadowlands incursion from outside the Wall, this threat operates in secret, making it more insidious and harder to detect.

- Tier: 1. Momentum gain: +3/tick base × scope multiplier. Personal relevance minimum: 50 for all lords.

- End condition: The cult is destroyed — all known Maho-tsukai in the affected province are killed or captured, and the corruption is purified through shugenja ritual. Both conditions must be met. Momentum resets to 0 when both are satisfied.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Send magistrates: Commit Emerald or Clan Magistrates to investigate and root out the cult. Engages character movement and investigation systems. Magistrates must travel to the affected province and conduct the investigation on the ground.

- Send shugenja: Commit shugenja to detect Taint, perform purification rituals, and combat any summoned oni. Engages character movement system. Essential for the purification end condition — the cult cannot be considered fully resolved without ritual cleansing.

- Send military support: Commit troops to support magistrates and shugenja, containing the affected area and preventing cult members from fleeing. Engages army movement system.

- Issue a Travel Warning: Formally warn other clans that movement through the affected province is dangerous. Reduces momentum gain slightly by limiting the cult's ability to spread through travelers. Does not resolve the crisis.

- Request Imperial Intervention: Escalate to Winter Court. The Emerald Champion may be dispatched — the highest authority for dealing with criminal and supernatural threats within the Empire.

- Do nothing: Politically dangerous given the supernatural nature of Maho. Honor loss. Significant disposition damage with all who know. The cult spreads.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- The cult spreads to adjacent provinces — scope multiplier increases, momentum gain accelerates.

- Corrupted PU in affected provinces — population units become unreliable and may generate random negative events.

- Summoned oni may manifest as a secondary Shadowlands Incursion crisis within the province.

- The crisis does not resolve on its own — it only grows.

**Imperial Succession Crisis:**

The Emperor has died, been incapacitated, or been removed from the throne. The most politically destabilizing event possible in Rokugan — the Celestial Order itself is disrupted. If the Emperor designated an heir and the Imperial families accept the succession, the crisis may resolve quickly. If no heir is designated, if the heir is contested, or if the Emperor died under suspicious circumstances (especially assassination), the crisis escalates dramatically. A Warlike Emperor killed in battle triggers the most severe form — the Empire’s military leader is dead on the field, and every army in the Empire must immediately reassess its command structure.

Tier: 1. Momentum gain: +5/tick base × scope multiplier. Personal relevance minimum: 80 for all lords — every samurai in the Empire cares who sits on the throne. End condition: a new Emperor is confirmed and enthroned. If succession is clean (designated heir accepted by Imperial families), resolves within 14–30 daily ticks. If contested, may persist for an entire season or longer.

Possible Court Commitments: Support a specific claimant — publicly declare support for one candidate, generating disposition bonuses with that candidate and penalties with rivals. Propose mediation — offer to host a succession court or negotiate between claimants. Commit military forces — back a claimant with arms (escalates the crisis toward Imperial Civil War if multiple clans back different claimants). Invoke divine authority — request the Brotherhood of Shinsei to interpret the Celestial Order’s will (generates a topic but does not mechanically resolve the dispute). Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100): the Imperial institution fractures. Tax cascade suspends at the Emperor tier — no Imperial tax is collected. Koku flow from the Emperor’s pool ceases. All Imperial appointments (Chosen, Champions) lose their authority — Emerald Magistrates cannot enforce Imperial law, the Imperial Legions have no clear commander. Clan Champions begin acting as independent sovereigns. If unresolved for two full seasons, automatically escalates to Imperial Civil War.

**Imperial Civil War:**

Multiple Great Clans are in open military conflict with the Imperial institution or with each other over control of the Empire itself. This is the most severe political crisis in the game — the Celestial Order is being actively torn apart. Two triggers exist. First: the Tyrant Emperor breaking point — average clan disposition toward the Emperor drops below Enemy (−31) across three or more Great Clans simultaneously (Section 55.10.1). The clans have decided the Emperor must go. Second: an unresolved Imperial Succession Crisis that persists for two or more seasons with multiple clans backing different claimants by force of arms.

Tier: 1. Momentum gain: +5/tick base × scope multiplier. Personal relevance minimum: 100 for all lords — no one is neutral in a civil war. End condition: one side achieves decisive military victory AND a new Emperor is enthroned (or the existing Emperor is restored to uncontested power). This requires both military resolution through the War Status System (Section 53) and political resolution through court. A military victory without political legitimacy does not end the crisis — it merely suppresses it.

Possible Court Commitments: Declare for a faction — formally commit the clan to one side. Generates permanent disposition modifiers toward all other clans based on their alignment. Propose a compromise candidate — suggest a new Emperor acceptable to multiple factions. Declare neutrality — refuse to take sides. Legally valid but earns the contempt of both factions. Commit military forces — deploy armies to support the chosen faction through the War Status System. Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100): the Empire fragments. Each Great Clan operates as an independent state. Trade between factions ceases. The Crab Wall may be left undermanned as forces redeploy for the civil war — creating a secondary Shadowlands Incursion risk. The crisis does not resolve on its own and will consume the game world until resolved by force and politics combined.

**Clan Champion Succession Crisis:**

A Great Clan Champion has died or been removed, and the succession is contested. Per the succession system (Section 22.5), a Clan Champion’s successor must be confirmed by the Emperor. If the Emperor is unavailable (dead, incapacitated, or occupied with a higher-priority crisis), or if the Emperor’s disposition toward the highest-priority candidate is Rival (−11) or below, the succession cannot be confirmed through normal channels. The stability of an entire Great Clan is at stake — without a confirmed Champion, the clan’s entire feudal hierarchy is paralyzed.

Tier: 1. Momentum gain: +3/tick base × scope multiplier. Personal relevance minimum: 60 for lords of the affected clan, 30 for all other clans — who leads a Great Clan affects everyone. End condition: the Emperor (or in his absence, the Imperial Advisor or Chancellor acting with emergency authority) confirms a successor. Alternatively, the clan may resolve the dispute internally through an iaijutsu duel between claimants (Section 4.8.1) — the Emperor ratifies the outcome retroactively.

Possible Court Commitments: Support a claimant — publicly advocate for one candidate at court. Generates disposition bonuses with the supported candidate and penalties with rivals. Propose a duel — suggest the succession be resolved by iaijutsu. Offer mediation — a neutral clan Champion offers to arbitrate. Apply leverage — use secrets, favors, or military pressure to influence the outcome. Petition the Emperor — formally request the Emperor intervene and confirm a successor. Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100): the clan fragments. Family Daimyo begin acting independently — each controls their own territory and military without central coordination. The clan’s tax cascade breaks at the Champion tier. External enemies exploit the chaos. If unresolved for 60 daily ticks, the Emperor (or highest available Imperial authority) intervenes and appoints a successor directly per Section 22.5, ending the dispute but generating significant political fallout.

**Oni Manifestation:**

An Oni has manifested within Rokugan proper — not as part of a broader Shadowlands incursion, but as a singular powerful supernatural entity operating independently. Could be a summoned Oni that broke free of its summoner's control, one that slipped through a weakness in the Wall, or one called forth by a Maho ritual. Unlike a Shadowlands incursion which involves an army, an Oni Manifestation is a single devastating presence — harder to track, harder to fight, and capable of enormous localized destruction.

- Tier: 1. Momentum gain: +3/tick base × scope multiplier. Personal relevance minimum: 50 for all lords.

- End condition: The Oni is destroyed. Oni cannot be negotiated with or driven away — only killed. Additionally, if the Oni was summoned, the summoner must be found and dealt with or a new Oni may be called. Both conditions must be met for full resolution.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Send elite military forces: Ordinary troops are largely ineffective against an Oni. Elite samurai, preferably equipped with jade weapons, must be committed. Engages army movement and combat systems. Jade weaponry increases Arms cost above standard military deployment.

- Send shugenja: Essential. Shugenja with Earth and Water magic can weaken an Oni significantly. Without shugenja support, even elite troops struggle. Engages character movement system.

- Investigate the summoning: If the Oni was summoned, finding and eliminating the summoner prevents recurrence. Engages magistrate and investigation systems. Without this step, the crisis may recur even after the Oni is destroyed.

- Seal the province: Formally quarantine movement into and out of the affected province while the Oni is active. Reduces civilian casualties and prevents the Oni from being drawn to populated areas by travelers. Does not resolve the crisis but limits damage.

- Request Imperial Intervention: Escalate to Winter Court. The Jade Champion may be dispatched — their specific mandate covers supernatural threats within the Empire.

- Do nothing: The Oni continues to rampage. PU losses in the affected province accelerate each tick. Honor loss for all who declined to act against a supernatural threat of this magnitude.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- The Oni expands its territory — adjacent provinces begin suffering attacks each tick.

- PU losses in affected provinces accumulate rapidly — an Oni can devastate a province's population in weeks.

- If the Oni was summoned, the summoner grows bolder — may attempt a larger ritual, potentially triggering a full Shadowlands Incursion crisis.

- The crisis does not resolve on its own.

**Tier 2 Crisis Types — Regional / Clan-Level Problems**

**Nezumi Colony Crisis**

A Nezumi warren has grown beyond provincial suppression capacity. At Strength 8+, the suppression TN (40–50) exceeds what most provincial-level characters can achieve. The colony steals 0.8–1.0 Rice per season, threatening the province's food security. At Strength 10, the infestation transitions to the Army Combat System (Section 11.7) — a clan army is required to dislodge a permanent colony. Nezumi are always hostile, Taint-immune, and breed rapidly. They cannot be detected by shugenja (no spiritual signature), cannot be negotiated with, and their Rice theft compounds each season. This is a purely military problem requiring clan-level resources. Uniquely among insurgency types, Nezumi spread into Stable provinces — adjacent lords face infestation risk regardless of governance quality.

- Tier: 2. Momentum gain: +2/tick base × scope multiplier. Personal relevance: high for the affected lord and all adjacent lords (Nezumi spread into Stable provinces); moderate for the Clan Champion; low for geographically distant lords.

- End condition: Nezumi Infestation Strength in the affected province reaches 0 — either through character-led suppression at Strength 1–9, or military destruction through the Army Combat System at Strength 10. If the Chieftain escapes during suppression or battle, a new infestation may spawn in an adjacent province at Strength 3 within 1–4 seasons, entering at Phase 3 (Detection) as a new insurgency instance with its own crisis lifecycle.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Send military forces: Commit troops to reinforce the affected province for suppression operations or, at Strength 10, to destroy the colony through the Army Combat System. Engages army movement system. The committed army must physically travel to the province.

- Send elite characters: Commit high-Rank bushi or scouts with strong Bugei skills to attempt suppression at the elevated TN. Engages character movement system. A Rank 5 bushi can plausibly hit TN 40–50 where a provincial Rank 3 cannot.

- Send supplies: Commit Rice to offset the colony's seasonal theft and prevent the starvation cascade from compounding the military problem. Engages resource transfer system.

- Request inter-clan assistance: If the infestation affects provinces belonging to different clans (possible because Nezumi spread into Stable provinces across clan borders), request military aid from the adjacent clan. Engages disposition system. The adjacent clan's AI evaluates the request using their own military feasibility — they will not commit forces if doing so leaves their own provinces undefended.

- Do nothing: No Honor cost for uninvolved lords. The affected lord and Clan Champion bear responsibility. Adjacent lords who do nothing accept the risk of spread into their own provinces.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- Nezumi spread continues — new infestations spawn in adjacent provinces each season, including Stable provinces. Each new infestation is an independent insurgency instance with its own Strength and crisis tier.

- Rice theft compounds: each active infestation drains Strength × 0.1 Rice per season from its host province. Multiple simultaneous infestations can push an entire region into the starvation cascade.

- At Strength 10, the infestation transitions to the Army Combat System (Section 11.7) — the colony must be destroyed through formal military campaign. Nezumi Colony Force Roster in Section 54.2 defines the enemy army composition.

- The crisis does not resolve on its own. Nezumi breed. The problem only grows.

**Tier 3 Crisis Types — Local / Regional Problems**

Locally significant, limited broader relevance. Most lords outside the affected region have low personal relevance scores. Five Tier 3 crisis types are defined below, each following the same format as Tier 1 and Tier 2 entries.

**Border Raid**

A provincial lord has launched a raid against a neighbouring province — stealing Rice or Arms stockpiles, burning farmland, or driving off population. Below the threshold of formal war. Only the directly involved lords have standing at court.

- Tier: 3. Momentum gain: +1/tick × scope multiplier. Personal relevance driven entirely by clan affiliation and direct adjacency. Lords more than one province away have near-zero relevance.

- End condition: The raiding lord formally ceases hostilities AND makes reparations acceptable to the raided lord, OR the raided lord retaliates successfully and the situation is contained at the same level.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Demand reparations: Publicly call on the raiding lord to compensate the raided province. Engages court pressure mechanics and disposition system. The raiding lord’s response is shaped by their Honor, their current war readiness, and the watching court’s reaction.

- Pressure the raiding lord to stand down: Disposition-based persuasion action. More effective if the pressuring character has high disposition with the raider or holds leverage over them.

- Offer to mediate: Position yourself as a neutral arbiter between both lords. Generates disposition gains with both parties if successful. Requires both parties to accept mediation.

- Request Family Daimyo intervention: Escalates the dispute upward. If accepted, the Family Daimyo takes over as the relevant party — the crisis may escalate to Tier 2 Border Conflict if the Daimyo formally endorses either side.

- Do nothing: Legally valid at this scale. No Honor penalty for uninvolved lords. The dispute is between the two lords and the Empire is not obligated to intervene.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- Escalates to Border Conflict (Tier 2 trending). The Family Daimyo is drawn in whether they want to be or not.

- Raided province loses PU and Rice each tick the raiding continues.

- Raiding lord’s clan-to-clan disposition with the raided clan takes a permanent historical modifier. The insult is on the record.

**Provincial Famine**

A single province is experiencing food shortage — distinct from the multi-province Famine that rises to Tier 2. Locally catastrophic but contained enough that the rest of the Empire can look away.

- Tier: 3. Momentum gain: +1/tick. High personal relevance for the affected lord and their clan. Near-zero for geographically distant lords unless they hold Rice surplus that could be sold.

- End condition: The affected province returns to positive Rice balance for 5 consecutive daily ticks.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Offer Rice sale or intra-clan sharing: A lord with surplus commits Rice to the affected province. Engages the Rice market system directly. Intra-clan transfers generate Honor; cross-clan sales generate Koku.

- Dispatch a relief mission: Commit a named character to travel to the affected province and manage distribution on the ground. Engages character movement system. Provides a bonus to Rice efficiency for the duration.

- Investigate the cause: Determine whether the shortage stems from poor harvest, mismanagement, or deliberate sabotage. Roll Commerce (Mathematics) or Investigation skill. Identifying sabotage may generate a new Criminal Organization or Border Raid crisis against the responsible party.

- Request neighbouring lord to lift blockage: If a trade route disruption is causing the shortage, court pressure can compel the blocking party to allow Rice passage. Engages disposition system.

- Do nothing: No Honor loss for uninvolved lords. The affected lord bears the full weight of their province’s starvation.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- PU starvation cascade activates per Section 4.3.6 — Shortage → Hunger → Famine stages with permanent PU loss.

- Peasant Revolt insurgency spawns in severely affected provinces (Section 11.11).

- Province Stability collapses. May escalate to multi-province Famine (Tier 2) if adjacent provinces are already under stress.

**Criminal Organization**

A criminal network — smuggling ring, bandit confederation, opium trade operation, or minor secret society — has established itself in a province. Not yet a full Insurgency (Section 11.11) but generating instability, disrupting trade, and corrupting local officials.

- Tier: 3. Momentum gain: +1/tick. Personal relevance concentrated on the hosting province’s lord and their clan magistrates. Neighbouring lords gain relevance if the network crosses their borders.

- End condition: The organization is dismantled — its leadership captured or killed and its operations disrupted — confirmed by magistrate investigation.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Dispatch magistrates: Commit Clan Magistrates to investigate and dismantle the network. Engages character movement and investigation systems.

- Request military assistance: Commit troops to patrol the affected province and suppress bandit activity. Engages garrison and army systems. Reduces the trade route Koku drain while active.

- Pressure the hosting lord to act: If the hosting lord is suspected of tolerance or involvement, court pressure forces them to visibly act. Engages disposition system. Failure to act after public pressure is an Honor violation.

- Request Emerald Magistrate involvement: If the network crosses clan lines, escalate to Imperial jurisdiction. The Emerald Champion’s magistrates have authority that overrides clan boundaries.

- Do nothing: No immediate Honor cost for uninvolved lords. The problem is the hosting lord’s to solve.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- Criminal network grows into a full Insurgency per Section 11.11. Province Stability degrades each tick.

- Trade route Koku drain activates and compounds each season.

- If the organization connects to a larger threat (Kolat, Shadowlands corruption), may escalate to a Tier 1 crisis.

**Trade Route Disruption**

A specific trade route has been severed or made unsafe — by banditry, a local lord’s embargo, war damage, or natural obstacle. Rice and Koku flow between connected provinces is interrupted.

- Tier: 3. Momentum gain: +1/tick. Personal relevance highest for lords whose provinces depend on the disrupted route for Rice purchase access or Koku income. Lords on unaffected routes have low relevance.

- End condition: The route is restored — the disrupting cause is removed AND normal merchant traffic resumes for 3 consecutive daily ticks.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Dispatch military patrol to clear bandits: Commit troops to patrol the disrupted route. Engages garrison and army systems. Resolves banditry-caused disruption directly.

- Pressure the blocking lord to reopen passage: Court pressure backed by disposition mechanics and, if necessary, leverage. A lord who is deliberately using a trade blockade as a political weapon may demand concessions before reopening.

- Negotiate an alternative routing arrangement: Broker an agreement for goods to move through a different province. Requires disposition building with the alternative route’s lord and compensation for the additional duty.

- Request Imperial Magistrate intervention: If the blockage is being used as a deliberate political weapon, escalate to Imperial jurisdiction. The Emperor’s authority over trade routes is clear — a deliberate embargo in defiance of Imperial will is a serious Honor violation.

- Do nothing: No Honor cost for uninvolved lords. The affected lords absorb the Koku loss.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- Koku income loss compounds each tick for all lords dependent on the route.

- If the disrupted route carried food, Provincial Famine may trigger in dependent provinces.

- If the disruption is deliberate and sustained, a formal Embargo escalates to Tier 2 War Status Tier 3 trending toward Clan War.

**Minor Clan Dispute**

Two lords — within the same clan or between neighbouring clans — have a formal grievance that has reached court but not yet escalated to military action. Could be a disputed inheritance, an insult requiring satisfaction, a broken betrothal, or a competing land claim.

- Tier: 3. Momentum gain: +1/tick. Relevance is nearly entirely personal — the directly involved lords and their immediate families. Third parties only gain relevance if they have prior disposition ties to either party or a stake in the outcome.

- End condition: Both parties reach a mutually accepted resolution — declared publicly at court — OR one party formally yields.

Possible Court Commitments:

- Mediate between the parties: Position as neutral arbiter. Requires both parties to accept mediation. Success generates significant disposition gains with both lords and a Glory gain for the mediator.

- Advocate publicly for one side: Commit your court credibility to one party’s position. Disposition gain with that lord, disposition loss with the other. The weight of your advocacy depends on your own Glory and Status.

- Offer to facilitate an Iaijutsu duel: Propose formal resolution through the dueling system (Section 4.8.1). Both parties must agree. Resolves the grievance cleanly with a clear winner and loser. Honor is satisfied regardless of outcome.

- Deliver a gift to one party: Shift their disposition toward settlement through the gift-giving system (Section 12.3). More effective if the gift is calibrated to the recipient’s clan tastes and the severity of their grievance.

- Apply pressure or leverage: Use a held secret, a favor owed, or an intimidation action to compel one party toward settlement. High risk — if the target resists or the leverage becomes public, the applying character absorbs Honor loss.

- Do nothing: No Honor cost for uninvolved lords. The dispute is between the two parties.

Consequences of inaction at maximum momentum (100):

- Dispute escalates — may trigger a formal Duel challenge, a Border Raid, or a Succession Dispute depending on the nature of the grievance.

- Honor damage for both parties if no resolution is reached and the dispute becomes common knowledge — allowing a grievance to fester publicly is itself a failure of conduct.
