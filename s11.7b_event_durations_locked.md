## 11.7b Event Durations — LOCKED

Major events in Rokugan take real time to resolve. The game uses Option C for event pacing: events simply take as long as they take in OOC days. There are no special time bubbles or compressed phases. A siege is a siege — it consumes the days it consumes. A player committing to a court season is committing their character's time for that OOC period. The 4:1 ratio (1 OOC day = 4 IC days) makes this weight tangible. Missing an OOC day during a siege means your character lost 4 IC days of the engagement.

All durations below are in OOC days. IC days = OOC days x 4.

**Event Duration Reference Table**

- Mass Battle: 1 OOC day (4 IC days). A single military engagement from approach through resolution. All AP that day is consumed by the battle. The player either survives or does not.

- Siege: 15–30 OOC days (60–120 IC days). A sustained military operation against a fortified position. Duration varies based on castle strength, garrison size, supply situation, and whether a storm assault is attempted. See Section 11.7 for full siege mechanics.

- Court Season / Winter Court: 30 OOC days (120 IC days). A full seasonal court. Committing to Winter Court means committing a real month of play to that context. This is intentional — in Rokugan, Winter Court was the whole winter.

- Festival: 3 OOC days (12 IC days). Empire-wide canonical festivals and local village festivals. See Section 10 for the full festival calendar.

- Diplomatic Summit: 5–7 OOC days (20–28 IC days). A formal multi-party negotiation convened outside of a full court season. Shorter than Winter Court but still a significant commitment.

- Tournament: 3–5 OOC days (12–20 IC days). Iaijutsu competitions, archery contests, sumai bouts, poetry contests. Duration varies by tournament size and number of events.

The weight of these commitments is a feature, not a bug. A player whose character is besieging a castle for 20 OOC days is making a real strategic choice. A character attending Winter Court for 30 OOC days is unavailable for military action during that period. Time is the game's scarcest resource.
