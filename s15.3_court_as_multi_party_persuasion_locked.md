## 15.3 Court as Multi-Party Persuasion — LOCKED

Court is not a simple voting game. Every delegation present has its own agenda — what outcome it wants on each topic. Those agendas can align, conflict, or be entirely orthogonal. A court session is a multi-party persuasion arena where delegations use their daily action points to move other attendees toward their preferred outcome through every tool available — gifts, performances, private meetings, insults, gossip, and alliances.

**Topic Positions:**

- Topics have a spectrum of positions, not a binary vote. An ongoing war has positions ranging from 'continue aggressively' through 'ceasefire' to 'negotiate peace' to 'Imperial intervention'.

- Each lord present has a position on the spectrum that can be moved by political action.

- The final outcome of a court session on a topic is determined by where the aggregate weighted opinion of attendees lands when the session closes.

- Lord opinions are weighted by their Status — a Clan Champion's position carries more weight than a Local Daimyo's.

- If the Emperor is present, Imperial approval is the dominant weight — a lord who has the Emperor's ear can swing the outcome even against a majority.

**Multi-Party Dynamics:**

- A delegation does not just need to persuade its direct opponents — it must outmaneuver everyone else simultaneously.

- Example: A weaker clan trying to persuade the Emperor to stop a war faces not only the winning clan trying to argue against peace, but potentially a third clan trying to exploit the war for trade leverage, a fourth trying to appear neutral while secretly helping the aggressor, and a fifth genuinely undecided and open to whoever makes the best case.

- Alliances formed between delegations multiply their effectiveness — two delegations coordinating their action points toward the same outcome are far more powerful than two delegations working independently.

- Sabotage is a valid strategy — a delegation that cannot win on a topic can still prevent their opponent from winning by disrupting their political actions, spreading damaging gossip, or goading them into a duel that removes them from the court floor.
