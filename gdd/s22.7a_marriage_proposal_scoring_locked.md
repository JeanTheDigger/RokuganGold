## 22.7a Marriage Proposal Scoring — LOCKED

This section formalises three numeric weights in `MarriageSystem.evaluate_proposal()`.
GDD s22.7 lists these factors qualitatively (favors improve prospects, military
alliance makes marriage attractive, Benten Festival is auspicious) without numbers.

---

### Context

The proposal acceptance formula in `action_executor.gd`:

```
acceptance_score = proposing_clan_disposition
                 + character_value                      (= int(status × 2 + glory))
                 + favor_tier × favor_tier_multiplier
                 + military_objective_bonus (if active)
                 + BENTEN_FESTIVAL_BONUS (if today)
```

Proposal accepted when `acceptance_score >= 0`.

**Typical base scores:**

| Proposing lord's disposition | Typical char_value | Base score |
|-----|------|------|
| Enemy (−50) | 8 | −42 |
| Rival (−20) | 8 | −12 |
| Stranger (0) | 8 | +8 |
| Ally (+20) | 8 | +28 |

---

### Calibration

**Calibration anchors:**

| Factor | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Moderate Favor break | −1.0 Honor | s12.10 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Major Favor break | −2.0 Honor | s12.10 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Disposition tier onset (Friend) | +31 | s12.2 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Disposition tier onset (Ally) | +11 | s12.2 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Gossip damage | −5 | s12.6 (GDD-confirmed) |

**Design principles:**

- A **binding political obligation** (MAJOR favor) is the strongest single lever — it should be capable of overcoming Rival-tier opposition when the candidate is otherwise acceptable.
- **Military urgency** increases attractiveness but does not constitute an obligation. It should weigh the same as a MODERATE favor — meaningful but deniable.
- The **Benten Festival** is the most auspicious marriage day per year. Its bonus should exceed military urgency (it is a sacred occasion) but fall below a Major obligation.

---

### Values — LOCKED

| Constant | Value | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| `favor_tier_multiplier` | 10 | MINOR=0, MODERATE=+10, MAJOR=+20. A MAJOR favor can offset Rival-tier opposition (−20) with a good-value candidate — binding obligation is the strongest lever available. |
| `military_objective_bonus` | 10 | Pressing military need (SECURE_ALLIANCE, RAISE_ARMY, DEFEND_PROVINCE) is as weighty as a MODERATE favor. Urgency helps but doesn't obligate. |
| `BENTEN_FESTIVAL_BONUS` | 15 | Most auspicious marriage day — stronger than military urgency, below Major obligation. Can tip a borderline Rival-tier proposal on this one day per year. |

---

### Notes

`favor_tier` parameter uses `FavorData.FavorTier` enum values: MINOR=0, MODERATE=1,
MAJOR=2. MINOR favors produce zero bonus (tier × 10 = 0) — a minor favor represents
too small an obligation to materially influence a marriage decision.

`BENTEN_FESTIVAL_DAY = 9`, `BENTEN_FESTIVAL_MONTH = 11` are GDD-confirmed calendar
data (unchanged).
