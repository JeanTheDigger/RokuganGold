## 53.2 — Intra-Clan Civil War

**STATUS:** LOCKED

An intra-clan civil war occurs when a faction within a clan takes up arms against the clan's legitimate authority structure. Unlike inter-clan wars, civil wars destroy a clan from within — the same army splits, the same provinces become contested, and the same family ties are severed. Civil wars are always Group A vs Group B. No three-way splits. Every character and unit in the clan must choose a side or leave.

### 53.2.1 Civil War Trigger

An intra-clan civil war is triggered when a character holding a lordship position (Family Daimyo or above) formally refuses a lawful removal order, lawful directive, or lawful succession ruling from their superior within the clan hierarchy. The refusal itself is the trigger — no additional conditions are required. The moment a lord says "no" to lawful authority and has the military or political power to back it up, the civil war begins.

The trigger generates a Tier 2 crisis topic: "[Clan] Civil War — [Rebel Lord] refuses [Authority's] [order type]." The topic propagates empire-wide through normal channels.

### 53.2.2 Faction Formation

At the moment of trigger, every named NPC in the clan evaluates loyalty. This is a one-time evaluation — once a side is chosen, it is fixed for the duration of the civil war unless specific defection conditions are met (53.2.8).

**Three outcomes are possible per NPC:**

**Legitimacy Faction** — the side holding lawful authority (the Clan Champion, or whoever issued the order being defied).

**Rebel Faction** — the side refusing lawful authority.

**Ronin Departure** — the NPC refuses to fight clanmates and leaves the clan entirely. This is only available when both the Legitimacy pull and the Rebel pull score below PROVISIONAL 40 — the NPC has low Chugi (no strong loyalty to the chain of command) AND low disposition toward the rebel lord (no personal allegiance to the rebel). Ronin departure costs −1.0 Honor (abandoning the clan) and the character becomes a ronin permanently. This is rare but historically authentic.

**Loyalty Evaluation — per NPC:**

| Factor | Weight | Legitimacy-Favoring | Rebel-Favoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chugi (Duty) | 30% | High Chugi → Legitimacy | Low Chugi → Rebel |
| Disposition toward rebel lord | 25% | Low → Legitimacy | High → Rebel |
| Rebel lord's recent competence | 20% | Poor results → Legitimacy | Strong results → Rebel |
| Legitimacy of the original grievance | 15% | Grievance is weak → Legitimacy | Grievance is strong → Rebel |
| Personal ambition (Ishi) | 10% | Low → Legitimacy (safe choice) | High → Rebel (opportunity) |

**Legitimacy of the original grievance** is scored mechanically: if the removal or order being defied is connected to an active crisis that the rebel lord was objectively failing to address (crisis tier active on one of the rebel lord's assigned objectives, province stability declining under their governance), the grievance is weak (2/15 points toward Rebel). If no visible failure exists in the rebel lord's `known_topics` — the removal appears politically motivated or arbitrary — the grievance is strong (12/15 points toward Rebel). NPCs who lack information about the context (the topic is not in their `known_topics`) default toward Legitimacy — the safe choice when you don't know the details (5/15 points toward Rebel).

**Family Daimyo** evaluate first. Their decision carries their direct household vassals (retainers, hatamoto) automatically. Provincial Daimyo within each family evaluate independently — a Family Daimyo who joins the rebel faction may find that some of their Provincial Lords chose Legitimacy.

**Player characters** always choose freely. No automatic evaluation — a reaction menu presents the choice. Players may also choose the ronin departure regardless of score thresholds.

**Feudal chain reassignment:** When a vassal and their immediate lord choose opposite factions, the vassal's `lord_id` is broken — they cannot report to an enemy. Vassals who split from their immediate lord are reassigned to the highest-ranking member of their own faction in their original chain of command. If no one in their chain chose the same faction, they report directly to their faction leader. Example: a Provincial Daimyo sides with Legitimacy but their Family Daimyo sides with the Rebel Faction — the Provincial Daimyo's `lord_id` reassigns to the Clan Champion (Legitimacy faction leader). This reassignment is temporary — if the civil war resolves and the clan reintegrates, the original feudal chain is restored where possible.

**Rebel lord's recent competence** is scored mechanically: objective completion rate over the last 4 seasons (percentage of assigned primary objectives the rebel lord achieved). 75%+ completion = strong competence (full 20 points toward Rebel). 50–74% = moderate competence (10 points toward Rebel). Below 50% = poor competence (3 points toward Rebel).

### 53.2.3 Army Reconstitution

The clan's existing Go-hatamoto structure dissolves the moment civil war triggers. Both factions must reconstitute armies from the units whose commanders chose their side.

**Step 1 — Commander evaluation.** Every military commander (Rikugunshokan, Shireikan, Taisa, Chui) evaluates loyalty using the table in 53.2.2. Units follow their commander's choice. A Taisa who joins the Rebel Faction brings all Companies in their Legion whose Chui also joined the Rebel Faction. Companies whose Chui chose the other side detach from the Legion and are recovered by their faction.

**Step 2 — Unit recovery.** Each faction collects all units whose commanders chose their side. Units physically located in territory controlled by the opposing faction must march to friendly territory — this takes time and may be intercepted. Units that cannot reach friendly territory within 1 season are considered trapped and must either surrender, defect, or attempt to fight their way out.

**Step 3 — Reassembly.** Each faction assembles recovered units into new Go-hatamoto structures. The faction's senior military commander (highest-ranked Rikugunshokan or Shireikan who joined) organizes the army. New parent IDs are assigned. Sections and Legions are reformed from available units. Understrength formations are consolidated — two half-strength Legions merge into one. The resulting armies will almost certainly be smaller and less organized than the original clan military.

**Step 4 — New commander appointments.** Vacant command positions (created by commanders who chose the other side or departed as ronin) are filled through FILL_VACANCY. Both factions are simultaneously rebuilding their command structures while the civil war is active.

### 53.2.4 Resource Partition

At the moment of civil war trigger, clan-level resources split based on physical control.

**Settlement stockpiles** (Rice, Iron, Arms, Koku) remain with whoever controls the settlement. Provincial stockpiles stay with the Provincial Daimyo who holds the province, regardless of faction.

**Champion-level treasury** remains with the Legitimacy Faction — the Clan Champion's stockpile is held at the Champion's seat of power. The Rebel Faction starts with only what their controlled provinces hold.

**Tax cascade breaks** between factions. Provinces controlled by the Rebel Faction stop sending revenue to the Legitimacy Faction's champion-level treasury. Each faction collects taxes only from provinces they control. The standard tax cascade (Section 4.3.7) operates normally within each faction's territory — provincial lords pay their family daimyo, family daimyo pay their faction leader.

### 53.2.5 War Score

Civil wars use the same War Score system as inter-clan wars (Section 53), starting at 50/50. All existing War Score shift events apply. Additional civil war-specific shifts:

- Family Daimyo defects to the other side mid-war: +12 / −12
- Provincial Daimyo defects: +5 / −5
- Rebel lord's Honor drops below 1.0: +15 to Legitimacy faction
- Imperial Edict issued condemning one side: +10 to the side the Emperor supports
- Foreign clan intervenes militarily on one side: +8 to the supported side (per existing allied clan rules in Section 53)

### 53.2.6 Consequences During Active Civil War

**Province stability penalty:** All provinces belonging to the clan suffer −3 Stability per season for the duration of the civil war. The clan is tearing itself apart — peasants suffer regardless of which lord they serve.

**Stalemate escalation:** If the civil war persists beyond PROVISIONAL 8 seasons, the province stability penalty increases to −5 per season. At 12 seasons, −7 per season. The clan is bleeding out. This creates mechanical pressure toward resolution.

**Economic disruption:** Internal trade routes within the clan are severed between the two factions. Tax cascade breaks per 53.2.4.

**Diplomatic credibility collapse:** All non-clan entities apply PROVISIONAL −10 disposition toward the clan collectively for the duration. A clan at war with itself cannot be trusted as an ally. Individual NPC dispositions toward specific characters are unaffected — only clan-level collective disposition.

**Honor hemorrhage for the Rebel Faction leader:** −0.3 Honor per season. Open rebellion against lawful authority is dishonorable. Does not stop until the civil war resolves. Other rebel faction members do not suffer this automatically — they chose a lord, which is legitimate feudal behavior. Only the lord who defied their superior bears the ongoing Honor cost.

**Outside exploitation:** Other clans may intervene. Foreign military intervention on either side reclassifies the crisis from Tier 2 (internal) to Tier 1 (multi-clan war involving clan sovereignty). An allied clan joining the Rebel Faction is endorsing rebellion against lawful authority — most Champions would refuse on principle, but political opportunism exists. An allied clan joining the Legitimacy Faction is supporting the existing order — less politically costly but still entangles them in another clan's internal affairs.

### 53.2.7 Resolution

**Legitimacy Victory:** The rebel lord capitulates (voluntarily surrenders authority), is killed, has Honor drop below 0.0 (Disgrace — loses all vassals automatically per Section 4.6), or loses military control of their seat of power (castle captured). Standard succession fires for the rebel lord's former position (Section 22.5). The legitimate authority selects or approves the replacement. Rebel faction members who survived face consequences scaled by their role: Family Daimyo who joined the rebellion face removal from position (new succession) and PROVISIONAL −1.0 Honor. Provincial Daimyo face reassignment and −0.5 Honor. Rank-and-file samurai face no mechanical penalty — following a lord's orders is fulfilling duty, even if the lord was wrong. Clan military reconstitutes into standard Go-hatamoto structure from whatever units remain.

**Rebel Victory:** The rebel lord must hold their position for PROVISIONAL 6 consecutive seasons after the civil war triggers without: Honor dropping below 1.0, losing their seat of power, or losing all allied Family Daimyo support simultaneously. If all three conditions hold for 6 consecutive seasons, the legitimate authority concedes. If any condition is broken mid-count, the consecutive season counter resets to 0.

What "concession" means depends on the specific civil war — the generalized system does not prescribe the outcome. The specific crisis type (e.g., Dragon Schism, Section 55.10.2.8) defines what the rebel wins. If no specific crisis type applies, the default outcome is: the legitimate authority accepts the rebel lord's continued position and the original order is rescinded. Both factions reintegrate. Clan military reconstitutes from remaining units.

**Championship Seizure:** If the rebel is a Family Daimyo rebelling against their Clan Champion, and the rebel achieves decisive War Score victory (PROVISIONAL 90+), the rebel may claim the Clan Champion position directly. The previous Champion must be dead, captured, capitulated, or have Honor below 0.0 (Disgrace) — the rebel cannot claim the title while the incumbent still holds the field. No Emperor confirmation is required. This is a military fait accompli.

The rebel assumes the Clan Champion position. Standard succession fires for the rebel's vacated Family Daimyo position, with the new Clan Champion as confirming authority — she appoints her own successor.

The Emperor chooses whether to recognize or denounce the new Champion — this is an archetype-driven reaction (Section 55.10.1), not a gate. Recognition grants normal diplomatic standing. Denouncement applies PROVISIONAL −15 disposition from the Emperor and reduced Imperial diplomatic access, but the rebel still holds the position.

The Precedent Effect (53.2.10) fires with increased severity: PROVISIONAL +5 to defy score empire-wide instead of +3. This was not merely a successful defiance — it was a successful seizure. The political landscape of the entire Empire shifts.

**Clan-specific Championship Seizure exceptions:**

**Dragon Clan:** Championship Seizure cannot apply. Togashi cannot be killed, captured, or disgraced through normal game mechanics. If Togashi vanished after an assault on the High House of Light, he is gone — not dead. The Dragon-specific resolution path (Section 55.10.2.8) is the only route to FC victory, and it grants autonomous rule, not the championship itself.

**Phoenix Clan:** Championship Seizure cannot apply. Phoenix succession is supernatural — when the Shiba Champion dies, the soul of the Kami Shiba reincarnates into a randomly selected living Shiba character (Section 55.10.3.8). An outsider claiming the title holds a hollow position — the real Champion (the reincarnated soul) exists somewhere else and the Elemental Council recognizes only the reincarnated vessel. This exception is absolute and cannot be overridden by War Score. Full Phoenix governance exception in Section 55.10.3.

**Post-resolution disposition scars:** All characters who fought on opposite sides of the civil war receive a permanent −10 disposition modifier toward each other. This decays at PROVISIONAL 1 point per season — full recovery takes 10 seasons. Characters who lost family members during the fighting receive an additional −15 modifier that does not decay. Civil wars leave generational hatred.

### 53.2.8 Defection

During an active civil war, NPCs may switch sides. Defection is not routine — it requires a trigger:

- The NPC's faction's War Score drops below 25 (Desperate)
- The NPC's lord within the faction is killed
- An Imperial Edict condemns the NPC's faction
- The NPC's disposition toward their faction leader drops below PROVISIONAL −20 (Enemy)

When triggered, the NPC re-runs the loyalty evaluation from 53.2.2 with current-state values. If the result now favors the other side, they defect. Military units under the defecting commander follow them. Defection mid-war generates: −0.5 Honor for the defector (betraying a chosen lord, even a rebel lord, is dishonorable), War Score shift per 53.2.5, and −15 disposition from all former faction members toward the defector.

### 53.2.9 Interaction with Emperor

The Emperor evaluates the civil war through his existing archetype behavior (Section 55.10.1). No new mechanic needed:

- Benevolent Emperor: intervenes to restore order, likely supporting the Legitimacy Faction
- Iron Emperor: demands compliance with lawful authority — strong Legitimacy support
- Cunning Emperor: may let it burn, or support whichever side serves his interests
- Warlike Emperor: cares about military impact — supports whichever side restores the clan's fighting capacity faster
- Tyrant Emperor: sees an opportunity to extract concessions from whichever side wants Imperial backing

Imperial Edicts condemning one side carry the War Score shift in 53.2.5. Imperial military intervention (sending Imperial Legions) escalates the crisis to Tier 1.

### 53.2.10 Precedent Effect

A successful rebel victory — regardless of which clan — sends ripples through the Empire. Every Family Daimyo in every clan observes that a subordinate defied their Champion and survived.

After a successful rebel victory resolves, all Family Daimyo empire-wide gain PROVISIONAL +3 to their defy score when evaluating compliance with their own Champion's directives. This modifier lasts for PROVISIONAL 5 seasons, then decays. If a second successful rebellion occurs while the first modifier is still active, the modifiers stack.
