## 12.8 Secret System — LOCKED

Secrets are a subtype of Tier 4 topic. A secret is information that the subject is actively suppressing — they know it exists and are deliberately keeping it out of normal circulation. It does not spread through daily conversation or letters because the subject controls it. When a secret is exposed, it cannot be used against the subject again — it becomes an ordinary topic in public circulation.

The Secret System operates in the Low Skills space. Using it costs Honor, Glory, and potentially Infamy. This makes secrets a genuine trade-off — a high-Honor courtier can use these mechanics but pays steeply. A Scorpion with low Honor and high Infamy pays almost nothing. This asymmetry is intentional and reflects Rokugan's social reality.

**Secret Data Structure — LOCKED:**

A secret is a topic object with three additional fields:

- is_secret: true — the subject is actively suppressing this topic. It does not enter normal conversation or letter circulation.

- severity: 1–4 — how damaging exposure is. Determines disposition damage, Honor loss, and Infamy gain for the subject on exposure.

- fabricated: true/false — whether the secret was created through Forgery rather than discovered. Hidden from targets. Detectable through Investigation vs the Forgery roll TN.

A character ‘possesses’ a secret when the secret object is in their knowledge pool. They can deploy it via court actions (Reveal a Secret Privately, Expose a Secret Publicly) or hold it as leverage.

**Physical Proof — LOCKED:**

Physical proof is a tangible object — a letter, a document, a signed agreement, a seal impression, a forged decree — that exists in a character’s inventory. Physical proof is categorically more powerful than topic knowledge:

- It cannot be denied the way a rumor can. A letter in hand is evidence. A whispered accusation is not.

- It can be presented to a lord as evidence in the betrayal consequence chain (Section 18.9).

- It can be stolen, destroying the possessor’s leverage.

- It can be destroyed, permanently eliminating the threat.

- Its authenticity can be challenged through Investigation/Perception vs the Forgery roll TN.

Physical proof is tracked through the Inventory System (Section 12.11). Proof items carry the is_evidence: true flag and belong to the Evidence item category. They exist in one of three storage tiers: on person (vulnerable to Pickpocket), current quarters (vulnerable to Search Quarters), or home storage (safe unless someone travels there specifically). Physical proof grants 1 Free Raise when used in Expose a Secret Publicly or Reveal a Secret Privately. Proof can be sent by letter (subject to interception risk per Section 12.11 transfer mechanics) or presented directly in person. A character can destroy proof in their own inventory at any time with no roll required — permanently eliminating the evidence. To destroy proof held by another character, it must first be acquired through covert means (Section 12.8).

**Secret Severity Tiers — LOCKED:**

Severity determines the damage done when a secret is exposed. Values apply to the subject of the secret.

Tier 4 — Minor Embarrassment: Uncomfortable but not career-ending. A samurai seen gambling. A courtier who lied about knowing an art form. A lord who overpaid for a horse. ✅ RESOLVED: The Crime Severity Classification (Section 57.47) provides the comprehensive tier-by-tier classification of offenses. Secret severity tiers align with 57.47's four-tier structure — a secret's severity is determined by the underlying act or fact being concealed, classified per the same system. Context modifier: a secret moves up one tier if it involves a character of higher Status than the subject (any Status advantage counts), or if it occurred within the last 4 IC seasons rather than in the distant past.

- Disposition damage on private exposure (Reveal a Secret Privately): −8 with the recipient toward the subject.

- Disposition damage on public exposure (Expose a Secret Publicly): −5 per witness.

- Subject Honor loss: 0.

- Subject Glory loss: −0.1.

Tier 3 — Significant Compromise: Genuinely damaging to reputation or standing. A past failure of duty. A hidden debt. A family scandal. A poor decision that cost lives.

- Private exposure: −15 disposition with recipient toward subject.

- Public exposure: −10 per witness.

- Subject Honor loss: −0.3.

- Subject Glory loss: −0.3.

Tier 2 — Severe Threat: Would destroy reputation and possibly position if widely known. Hidden Taint. A dishonorable act covered up. A betrayal of a lord. Criminal conduct.

- Private exposure: −30 disposition with recipient toward subject.

- Public exposure: −20 per witness.

- Subject Honor loss: −1.0.

- Subject Glory loss: −0.5.

- Subject Infamy gain: +0.3.

Tier 1 — Existential: Exposure ends the character’s political existence and possibly their life. Treason. Maho practice. Kolat membership. A secret implicating the entire family.

- Private exposure: −50 disposition with recipient toward subject.

- Public exposure: −35 per witness.

- Subject Honor loss: −2.0.

- Subject Glory loss: −1.0.

- Subject Infamy gain: +0.5.

- Automatically generates a Betrayal or Criminal Organization Tier 4 topic.

**Covert Acquisition Methods — LOCKED:**

Each covert acquisition action costs 1 AP and uses a Low Skill. Each carries an Honor cost for the actor. All Low Skill use in this context also generates Infamy gain — being known as someone who operates through these methods builds a dark reputation. Honor and Infamy changes apply immediately.

**Bribe a Servant (Temptation/Awareness):**

Offer money or favors to a member of the target’s household in exchange for information. The servant’s resistance is based on their loyalty to their lord.

- TN: 10 + (lord’s disposition toward the servant ÷ 5, rounded down). A servant their lord trusts deeply is very hard to bribe.

- Success: Acquire one secret from the target’s suppressed topic pool at random.

- Each Raise: Choose which secret rather than random selection.

- Critical failure: The servant reports the attempt to their lord. A Spy Uncovered Tier 4 topic is generated. The actor’s identity may or may not be revealed depending on the servant’s knowledge.

- Actor Honor loss: −0.2. Actor Infamy gain: +0.1.

- Detection: The lord receives a notification that someone attempted to bribe their household. The actor’s identity is not automatically revealed — discovering who requires a Probe or investigation action.

**Eavesdrop on a Private Meeting (Stealth/Agility):**

Every IC day, the system checks whether any private meetings occurred in your settlement. If yes, you receive a notification — characters X and Y had a private meeting. Not what was discussed. You then choose whether to spend 1 AP to investigate.

- TN: Contested vs the participants’ Perception + Investigation. They were being careful.

- Success: Learn what topic was exchanged in that meeting. It enters your topic pool as a suppressed topic — you know something they didn’t intend for you to know.

- Each Raise: Learn one additional topic from the meeting.

- Failure: Nothing learned. AP spent.

- Critical failure: You were noticed. The participants know someone tried to spy on their meeting. This generates a Spy Uncovered Tier 4 topic. They do not automatically know who — discovery requires their own intelligence action.

- Actor Honor loss: −0.1. Actor Infamy gain: +0.05.

**Intercept a Letter (Stealth/Agility + Forgery/Agility):**

Intercept a letter in transit before it reaches its recipient. Two rolls required — Stealth to intercept without detection, Forgery to reseal convincingly.

- Stealth roll TN: 15 + geographic distance modifier (letters moving through more territory are harder to intercept).

- Forgery roll TN: 15. Sets the TN for the recipient to notice the broken seal.

- Success on both: Actor reads the letter’s topic. Letter continues to recipient with no knowledge of interception. The topic is now in the actor’s pool.

- Success Stealth / Fail Forgery: Actor reads the letter but the broken seal is noticed. Recipient knows the letter was opened. A Spy Uncovered topic is generated.

- Fail Stealth: Interception detected. Sender and recipient both notified. Spy Uncovered topic generated.

- Actor Honor loss: −0.3. Actor Infamy gain: +0.1.

**Search Quarters / Pickpocket (Sleight of Hand/Agility):**

Steal a physical object from the target’s quarters or person. Requires access to the location or proximity to the target. If successful, the object moves from their inventory to yours — giving you physical proof rather than mere topic knowledge.

- TN: 15 + target’s Investigation rank for quarters search. Contested vs target’s Perception + Investigation for pickpocket.

- Success: One physical object transfers from their inventory to yours. You choose the target object if you know what they carry. Otherwise random.

- Each Raise: One additional object.

- Critical failure (quarters): Evidence left behind. Target knows someone searched their rooms. Spy Uncovered topic generated.

- Critical failure (pickpocket): Target notices immediately. Confrontation is likely.

- Actor Honor loss: −0.3. Actor Infamy gain: +0.1.

- Physical proof note: A stolen letter or document is worth more than knowing its contents. It can be shown as evidence (1 Free Raise per Section 12.11), cannot be denied, and can be stolen back through the same covert acquisition mechanics. Stolen items carry the is_evidence: true flag in the acquiring character’s inventory. See Section 12.11 for full storage, transfer, and destruction rules.

Pickpocket vs concealed items: when Pickpocket targets a character carrying concealed items (CONCEAL_ITEM, Section 12.8), a two-step process applies. Step 1 — find the item: the pickpocket rolls Investigation / Perception vs the item’s concealment_tn. Each concealed item is checked independently. If Step 1 fails, the pickpocket finds nothing and does not know whether the target had anything concealed or simply had nothing worth taking. If Step 1 succeeds, proceed to Step 2 — steal the item: the standard Pickpocket contested roll (Sleight of Hand / Agility vs target’s Perception + Investigation) determines whether the theft is noticed. If the pickpocket has prior knowledge of the concealed item (saw the target hide it, intelligence from SHADOW_TARGET, information from PROBE), Step 1 is skipped — the pickpocket knows where the item is and goes straight to Step 2. Non-concealed items (visible coin purse, scroll case on the obi) require only Step 2 as normal.

**Fabrication — LOCKED:**

Creating a false secret and deploying it as real. Mechanically available to any character. Honor cost makes it prohibitive for high-Honor characters. Virtue profiles make it impossible for Gi and Makoto NPCs — they will never fabricate regardless of stakes.

Fabricate a Secret or Document (Forgery/Agility):

- Tier 1 secret: TN 15.

- Tier 2 secret: TN 20.

- Tier 3 secret: TN 25.

- Tier 4 secret or forged official document: TN 30.

Success creates a secret topic object marked fabricated: true (hidden from targets). The object enters the actor’s possession and can be deployed via court actions or presented as physical proof. Detection TN = base TN + (Raises called × 5). Each Raise the forger called and succeeded on adds +5 to the detection TN. Rank 3 mastery (+1k0 to the Forgery roll) and Rank 7 mastery (+0k1 to the Forgery roll) help the forger hit the base TN and succeed on more Raises.

Actor Honor loss by tier:

- Tier 1: −0.3 Honor.

- Tier 2: −0.5 Honor.

- Tier 3: −0.8 Honor.

- Tier 4: −1.5 Honor.

Actor Infamy gain: +0.2 regardless of tier. The act of fabricating is always dishonorable.

Exposure of fabrication: If the target or their allies roll Investigation/Perception against the detection TN (base TN + Raises × 5) and succeed, the secret is exposed as fabricated. The Rank 5 Forgery mastery bonus (+1k0 to detect forgeries made by others) applies to this roll. This generates a Tier 4 Political topic — Betrayal variant — against the fabricator. The fabricator suffers the permanent historical disposition modifier from Section 12.2: −25 start / −25 floor, no decay. Their credibility as a source of intelligence is destroyed.

**Forge an Impersonation Letter (Forgery (Documents)/Agility):**

Write a letter that appears to come from Person A. The forger fabricates Person A’s handwriting and personal seal, embedding a topic of the forger’s choosing as if Person A communicated it. The recipient processes the letter as if Person A sent it — topic transfer, disposition effects, and NPC decision engine evaluation all reference the recipient’s relationship with Person A, not with the forger. ActionID: FORGE_IMPERSONATION_LETTER. Category 6 Covert Action. Cost: 1 AP.

Preconditions: the forger must know Person A exists (met_characters per Section 22.3 and Section 29.15.24). The forger must know the recipient exists (met_characters). The forger must have a topic in their active topic pool to include. The forger needs Forgery 1+ to attempt. Having a physical sample of Person A’s seal or handwriting (obtained through INTERCEPT_LETTER, SEARCH_QUARTERS, or other means that put a letter bearing Person A’s seal into the forger’s possession): +1 Free Raise on the Forgery roll.

Roll: Forgery (Documents) / Agility vs a base TN scaled by document type. Simple personal letter: TN 15. Official document with clan seal: TN 20. Imperial document or Great Clan Champion’s seal: TN 25. Failure (did not hit base TN): the forgery is obviously flawed, no letter is produced, AP spent. Success: the forgery is passable. Detection TN = base TN + (Raises called × 5). Each Raise the forger called and succeeded on adds +5 to the detection TN. A master forger calling 3 Raises on an official document (TN 20 base + 15) produces detection TN 35. Rank 3 mastery (+1k0 to the Forgery roll) and Rank 7 mastery (+0k1 to the Forgery roll) help the forger hit the base TN and succeed on more Raises. The seal sample Free Raise counts as a called Raise that automatically succeeds — it adds +5 to the detection TN without increasing the TN the forger must beat.

On send: the letter enters the Section 12.7 delivery system. Delivery time is normal (land courier rate, ocean letter queue per Section 12.7). The letter carries no Calligraphy quality bonus — the forger is imitating another’s hand, not displaying their own artistry. The letter arrives as serviceable but unremarkable.

Detection on receipt: the detection check fires only if the recipient has previously received at least one authentic letter from Person A — without a reference sample, the recipient has no basis to judge the handwriting or seal and the letter is automatically believed. If the recipient does have prior correspondence from Person A, they automatically roll Investigation / Perception vs the detection TN (base TN + Raises × 5, as set by the forger’s roll). The Rank 5 Forgery mastery bonus (+1k0 to detect forgeries made by others) applies to this auto-check if the recipient has it. Success: the recipient knows the letter is forged and disregards it. The topic does not transfer. The forger’s identity is not automatically revealed — the recipient knows the letter is fake but not who sent it. Identifying the forger requires a separate investigation (tracing the letter’s physical origin, ink, courier route, or cross-referencing with other intelligence). Failure: the recipient believes the letter is authentic. The topic transfers with Person A as the apparent source.

The reply problem: if the recipient believes the forged letter and replies, the reply routes to the real Person A per normal letter delivery (Section 12.7). Person A receives an unexpected reply to a letter they never sent. This generates a confusion flag on Person A — their NPC decision engine notes the anomaly. A Person A with high Intelligence or Investigation skill may fire INVESTIGATE_RUMOR or PROBE to determine what happened. This is the forger’s primary exposure risk — they cannot intercept the reply unless they also run INTERCEPT_LETTER on Person A’s incoming mail. A reply that references the forged topic is strong evidence that someone impersonated Person A.

Honor cost: “Using a Low Skill” per the Honor table (Section 4.6). School exemptions apply per the cross-reference note. Glory cost on discovery: −0.3 Glory per the “Caught using a Low Skill” Glory table entry (Section 4.6). Fires only if the forgery is detected AND the forger is identified through investigation. Detection alone reveals a fake letter — it does not reveal who sent it.

**Forge an Order (Forgery (Personal Seals)/Agility):**

Create a forged directive that appears to come from a lord or authority figure in the target’s chain of command. The target processes it as a legitimate ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE from their lord — adjusting priorities, generating the appropriate ImmediateNeed or StandingNeed, and executing the forged order. ActionID: FORGE_ORDER. Category 6 Covert Action. Cost: 1 AP.

Preconditions: the forger must know the target exists (met_characters per Section 22.3 and Section 29.15.24). The forger must know who commands the target — the target’s lord_id or operational_superior_id (obtainable through PROBE, READ_CHARACTER, OBSERVE_COURT_ATTENDEES, or general knowledge of the feudal chain). The forged order must appear to come from someone in the target’s actual chain of command — an order from someone outside the chain is immediately discarded without a roll. The forger needs Forgery 1+ to attempt. Having a physical sample of the authority figure’s seal: +1 Free Raise on the Forgery roll.

Roll: Forgery (Personal Seals) / Agility vs a base TN scaled by the authority level of the order being forged. Order from a local or provincial daimyo: TN 20. Order from a Family Daimyo or Clan Champion: TN 25. Order from the Emperor or Imperial authority: TN 30. Failure (did not hit base TN): the forgery is obviously flawed, no order is produced, AP spent. Success: the forgery is passable. Detection TN = base TN + (Raises called × 5). Rank 3 mastery (+1k0 to the Forgery roll) and Rank 7 mastery (+0k1 to the Forgery roll) help the forger hit the base TN and succeed on more Raises. The seal sample Free Raise counts as a called Raise that automatically succeeds — it adds +5 to the detection TN without increasing the TN the forger must beat.

Detection: the detection check fires only if the target has previously received at least one authentic written order or letter from the authority figure whose seal is forged — without a reference sample, the target has no basis to judge the seal or hand and the order is automatically believed. If the target does have prior correspondence from the authority figure, the engine silently rolls Investigation / Perception for the target vs the detection TN (base TN + Raises × 5, as set by the forger’s roll). The Rank 5 Forgery mastery bonus (+1k0 to detect forgeries made by others) applies to this auto-check if the target has it. The target is unaware the check was made. Success: the target recognizes the forgery and disregards it. The forger’s identity is not automatically revealed — the target knows the order is fake but not who sent it. Failure: the target believes the order is authentic and executes it. The real lord has no idea this happened.

Scope of forgeable orders: anything within ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE scope — deploy to a location, transport a person, patrol a route, hold position, withdraw from a position, investigate a target, reassign priorities. The forged order cannot command seppuku, resignation, or anything requiring formal legal proceedings rather than operational directives.

Exposure paths: the real lord discovers the forgery when they notice the vassal doing something unexpected — through subordinate-tracking, a vassal report of objective completion the lord never assigned, another vassal reporting the unusual activity, or the vassal requesting clarification about a contradicting follow-up order. At that point the lord has the forged document as physical evidence — Investigation / Perception vs the Forgery TN can be attempted again by the lord or a magistrate to confirm the forgery. If the lord never notices (the order was subtle enough to not contradict existing priorities), the forgery may never be discovered.

Good faith defence: a vassal who executed a forged order in genuine belief that it was authentic is protected from treason charges and insubordination charges. The forged document serves as evidence of the vassal’s innocence in any investigation or legal proceeding (Section 11.3). The investigation shifts from “why did you disobey?” to “who forged this order?” — the vassal’s Testimony Weight (Section 11.3.10) is intact because they acted in good faith. The vassal’s lord may be angry about the outcome but cannot punish the vassal for following what appeared to be a legitimate order.

Honor cost: “Using a Low Skill” per the Honor table (Section 4.6). School exemptions apply per the cross-reference note. Glory cost on discovery: −0.3 Glory per the “Caught using a Low Skill” Glory table entry (Section 4.6). Fires only if the forgery is detected AND the forger is identified through investigation.

**Shadow a Target (Stealth (Shadowing)/Agility):**

Tail a target character for 1 IC day, observing their movements, contacts, and actions from a distance. This is MUD-layer surveillance — the shadow operates at the zone level, following the target through a settlement without entering the ASCII map. ActionID: SHADOW_TARGET. Category 6 Covert Action. Cost: 1 AP.

Preconditions: the shadow and the target must be in the same settlement at the start of the IC day. The shadow must know the target exists (met_characters per Section 22.3 and Section 29.15.24).

Roll: Stealth (Shadowing) / Agility vs target’s Investigation (Notice) / Perception. The target is unaware the check is being made (silent roll, same pattern as FORGE_ORDER detection).

Success: the shadow learns the target’s zone_event_log entries for that IC day — which zones they visited, who they spoke with (Daily Conversations and Deliberate Conversations), what ActionIDs they fired (CHARM, PROBE, WRITE_LETTER, etc.), and who they met. The shadow does not learn the content of conversations or letters — just that they happened and with whom. This is observational intelligence: the shadow knows where the target went, who they talked to, and what kind of actions they took, but not what was said or written.

Failure: the target notices someone is following them. They do not automatically know who — just that they are being tailed. A suspicious target may fire PROBE or READ_CHARACTER on people they encounter that day to identify the shadow. Critical failure (miss TN by 10+): the target identifies the shadow specifically. Disposition hit (−5). The target knows who was following them and can act on that knowledge.

Honor cost: “Using a Low Skill” per the Honor table (Section 4.6). School exemptions apply per the cross-reference note. Glory cost on discovery: −0.3 Glory per the “Caught using a Low Skill” Glory table entry (Section 4.6). Fires only if the shadow is identified (critical failure, or subsequent investigation by the target).

**Conceal an Item (Sleight of Hand (Conceal)/Agility):**

Hide a small object on your person. ActionID: CONCEAL_ITEM. Category 6 Covert Action. Cost: 0 AP (quick physical action). On the ASCII map, CONCEAL_ITEM is a Simple Action. Roll: Sleight of Hand (Conceal) / Agility vs a base TN scaled by item size. Tiny item (coin, seal, small vial, ring): TN 10. Small item (document, letter, medicine kit): TN 15. Small weapon (tanto, knife, shuriken): TN 20 — hard gate: requires Rank 5 Sleight of Hand mastery. A character without Rank 5 cannot attempt to conceal a weapon at all, regardless of skill rank or roll. This is a training gate, not a difficulty gate — concealing a blade in clothing without specialized technique is physically unreliable and the character does not know how to do it. Failure: the item is not properly concealed and remains visible. Success: the item is concealed. Detection TN = base TN + (Raises × 5). The concealment_tn is stored on the item in inventory with a concealed: true flag.

Detection: in normal Rokugani social contexts, no one searches a samurai’s person. Searching a guest is an insult — it says “I do not trust you.” A samurai entering a castle, court, or private residence is presumed honest. There is no passive gate check at zone entry. The concealment_tn matters only when another character fires SEARCH_PERSON (Section 12.8) targeting the carrier — a deliberate, costly action with social consequences if nothing is found. Each concealed item is checked independently. The concealment_tn does not degrade — the item stays concealed at the original TN until found or voluntarily revealed. Exceptional contexts where routine searches ARE culturally justified: the Kaiu Wall and Shadowlands-adjacent fortifications (Taint is existential, not social), prisons and magistrate holding areas during active investigation, and wartime restricted zones under martial law. These locations carry a security_search: true zone flag — guards at these locations passively roll Investigation / Perception vs each concealed item’s concealment_tn on zone entry. This is rare and specific, not the default.

Honor cost: “Using a Low Skill” per the Honor table (Section 4.6). School exemptions apply. Glory cost: −0.3 Glory per “Caught using a Low Skill” if a concealed item is found during a search or zone entry check.

NPC Behavior — CONCEAL_ITEM: this action is automatic and bypasses the normal NPC Decision Engine scoring. Any NPC carrying an item flagged as contraband (poison, maho scroll, Kolat Tear, gaijin artifact, stolen evidence) fires CONCEAL_ITEM before entering a settlement or moving to a new zone. Any NPC who has just completed SEARCH_QUARTERS or Pickpocket fires CONCEAL_ITEM on the stolen item before leaving the target’s location. Assassination preparation (Section 12.8 Phase 1) includes CONCEAL_ITEM as part of the equipment chain. No personality filter applies — this is self-preservation, not a moral choice. Honor thresholds do not gate CONCEAL_ITEM — a character who has already decided to carry contraband has already accepted the dishonor. School lean: Shosuro Infiltrator, Kasuga Smuggler, and Kolat operative SchoolTypes receive +1k0 on the Sleight of Hand (Conceal) roll per their school’s familiarity with covert logistics.

**Search a Person (Investigation/Perception):**

Physically search another character’s person for concealed items. ActionID: SEARCH_PERSON. Category 5 Intelligence. Cost: 1 AP. Roll: one Investigation / Perception roll. That result is compared against each concealed item’s concealment_tn independently — a result of 22 finds all items with concealment_tn 22 or below and misses all items above. Requires co-location — the searcher and target must be in the same zone or on the same ASCII map.

If an item is found: justified search. No consequences for the searcher. The target suffers “Caught using a Low Skill” Glory loss (−0.3) for having concealed the item. The item is revealed. If the item is contraband (a weapon at court, stolen documents, poison), the crime system (Section 11.3) takes over.

If nothing is found — two outcomes depending on authority. With magistrate authority (the searcher is a magistrate or acting on magistrate orders per Section 11.3): no Honor loss for the searcher. The search was a lawful exercise of duty. The target may resent it (disposition hit −3 to −5 toward the searcher) but has no legal grounds for complaint. Without magistrate authority (one samurai searching another on suspicion): the search is an insult. The searcher loses Honor (“Accusing a samurai without evidence” — −0.3 Honor). The target gains a provocation flag — grounds for ISSUE_DUEL_CHALLENGE. Disposition from the target toward the searcher drops −10. A samurai does not submit to being searched by a peer without consequence.

NPC Behavior — SEARCH_PERSON: this action requires suspicion. NPCs do not randomly search people. Triggers: the NPC is a magistrate or yoriki with an active investigation (Section 11.3.13) targeting the character and has evidence or testimony suggesting concealed items; the NPC witnessed the target acting suspiciously (failed Stealth roll near a restricted area, observed near a crime scene, caught leaving someone’s quarters without explanation); the NPC received intelligence through PROBE, SHADOW_TARGET, or a topic suggesting the target is carrying something hidden; the assassination suspicion accumulator (Section 12.8) on the settlement has reached 20+ and the NPC is the assigned bodyguard or household security — at this level of suspicion, the social barrier against searching a guest is overridden by genuine fear for the target’s safety, and the bodyguard or household head fires SEARCH_PERSON against the suspected character. At suspicion 30+, the search is near-automatic — the household is in crisis mode and any character under suspicion will be searched regardless of social cost. Personality lean: Dōsatsu (Insight) characters are more likely to act on subtle suspicion — they read the situation and act. Gi (Righteousness) magistrates search on principle when evidence warrants it. Rei (Courtesy) characters are reluctant — the social cost weighs heavily and they require stronger evidence before acting. Chugi (Loyalty) characters fire SEARCH_PERSON only if their lord explicitly orders the investigation. Without magistrate authority, NPCs almost never fire SEARCH_PERSON — the disposition and Honor costs make it prohibitively expensive unless the NPC has high confidence (intelligence from multiple sources, direct observation of suspicious behavior) that the search will find something.

**Bound Condition and Escape (Sleight of Hand (Escape)/Agility):**

A character who is subdued (reduced to Down wound level, surrendered, or grappled and pinned in combat) can be bound by an enemy as a Simple Action. The enemy uses rope, chains, or other restraints. This is a post-combat action — binding cannot occur during active combat unless the target is already immobilized through grapple. This condition is distinct from the Hostage System (Section 22.9) — hostages are honored guests held by political obligation, not prisoners in chains. The Bound condition applies to: characters arrested by doshin or magistrates, characters captured by bandits or enemy soldiers, characters imprisoned after sentencing (Section 11.3.15), characters restrained on an ASCII map during a mission.

Binding quality sets the Escape TN. Two methods: flat TN by material — simple rope TN 15, quality rope TN 20, chains TN 25, high-grade chains or manacles TN 30; or the binder rolls Sailing (Knot-work) / Intelligence and the result sets the Escape TN. The Knot-work contested method is used when a named character does the binding — their skill at tying knots directly determines how hard the bonds are to escape. Generic enemies (doshin, bandits) use the flat TN by material.

While Bound, a character: cannot move, cannot attack, cannot use items, cannot perform any physical action. They CAN speak, attempt Sleight of Hand (Escape), cast spells (L5R spells are prayers spoken to the kami — the verbal component is primary and does not require free hands; a bound shugenja can cast any spell that requires only speaking), and use social ActionIDs targeting characters present in the same zone (CHARM, NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE, INTIMIDATE — a bound samurai can still talk their way out).

A bound character has two options. Accept captivity: no roll. The character remains bound and enters the criminal justice pipeline (Section 11.3) or prisoner status. They await trial, sentencing, ransom, or rescue. This is not the same as hostage acceptance (Section 22.9) — a prisoner has no political protection and no honored guest status. Attempt escape: Sleight of Hand (Escape) / Agility vs the binding TN (flat material TN or contested vs binder’s Knot-work). On the ASCII map, this is a Complex Action — concentrated effort to work free, twisting and flexing against the bonds. Can be attempted once per IC day — the character waits for guard changes, nightfall, or distraction. Each attempt generates Quiet noise (the character is struggling). If a guard is within Quiet noise range (3 tiles), the guard rolls noise detection per Section 56.6.3. If the guard detects the noise and investigates, the escape attempt is discovered and the character is re-bound with tighter restraints (+5 to the binding TN).

Successful escape from bonds does not mean the character is free. They have slipped their restraints but are still in the location where they were held — a cell, a bandit camp, a magistrate’s holding area. Escaping the location requires further action: Stealth (Sneaking) to slip past guards, combat to fight through, or social means to talk past a guard. The Bound condition and the location escape are separate problems with separate rolls. Honor cost: “Using a Low Skill” per the Honor table (Section 4.6). School exemptions apply. Escaping from lawful custody (magistrate arrest, sentencing) carries additional Honor consequences through the legal system (Section 11.3).

Freeing an ally from bonds: another character can free a bound ally instead of the bound character escaping on their own. Cutting rope (simple or quality): Simple Action with any bladed weapon, no roll required. Breaking chains: Strength roll vs TN 25 (raw force, requires a tool such as a hammer, chisel, or heavy weapon) or possession of the key (Simple Action, no roll). Freeing an ally generates the same noise as the bound character’s own escape attempt — Quiet for cutting rope, Moderate for breaking chains (hammering is louder than twisting).

**Seduction Actions (Temptation (Seduction)/Awareness):**

Five ActionIDs share the same core roll and entanglement mechanic but produce different outputs. All are Category 6 Covert Actions, cost 1 AP, and require co-location with the target. Orientation precondition: the target’s orientation (Section 22.3) must be compatible with the actor’s gender. A straight target can only be seduced by the opposite gender, a gay target only by the same gender, a bisexual target by either. Targeting an incompatible orientation auto-fails with no roll — the system checks before the action fires, same pattern as the met_characters precondition. Core roll: Temptation (Seduction) / Awareness vs target’s Etiquette (Courtesy) / Willpower + Honor Rank (per The Strength of Honor, Section 32). Target with Lechery disadvantage: the actor gains +1k0 (applies when the actor’s gender matches the target’s attraction). Rank 5 Temptation mastery: +5 to the actor’s roll total. On success, all five actions create an entanglement — a romantic relationship between the actor and target that persists until broken. The entanglement generates a secret object at a severity tier determined by the participants’ circumstances: unmarried samurai of similar status = Tier 4 (minor scandal), married samurai = Tier 3 (adultery), cross-clan affair during political tension = Tier 2 (politically explosive). The secret is initially known only to the two participants and can be discovered through EAVESDROP, SHADOW_TARGET, or observation by others. Failure on any Seduction action: the target is not interested, disposition −3. Critical failure (miss TN by 10+): the target recognizes the seduction as calculated manipulation, disposition −10, the target may spread a topic about the attempt. Honor cost: “Using a Low Skill” per the Honor table (Section 4.6). School exemptions apply. Glory cost on discovery: −0.3 per “Caught using a Low Skill” plus the affair secret’s own consequences when exposed — the Low Skill penalty and the scandal penalty stack. Repeated use against the same target on subsequent IC days produces additional outputs and +3 disposition per success, deepening the entanglement. If the actor’s true intent is later revealed (the romance was manipulation), the target suffers the Betrayal disposition modifier (−25/−25, no decay, per Section 12.2).

Entanglement maintenance: a seduced target expects ongoing romantic attention. After the first successful Seduction action, the actor must make contact with the same person within every 16 IC days (4 OOC days). Contact that resets the maintenance window: any Seduction ActionID (any of the five) targeting the same person, or WRITE_LETTER to the same person. A love letter maintains the romance across distance without requiring physical presence — this is authentically Rokugani, as the Game of Letters is how most courtship operates across the Empire. If the window passes without contact, the target’s disposition toward the actor decays at −2 per missed window — the target feels neglected and the romantic interest cools. If three consecutive windows pass without contact, the entanglement breaks — the relationship flag clears, the target’s disposition drops −10 (feeling used and abandoned), and the affair secret remains in existence but no longer generates ongoing access or leverage. A formal breakup (the actor explicitly ends the relationship through conversation or letter) produces a larger hit: disposition −15, the entanglement breaks, and the former target may spread the affair as a topic out of spite. The maintenance cost is the hidden price of Seduction — each entanglement requires at minimum one letter every 16 IC days, consuming the actor’s limited daily letter budget (Section 12.7) on the entanglement instead of on political correspondence. An actor sustaining multiple simultaneous entanglements must dedicate a significant portion of their letter output to romantic maintenance, reducing their capacity for intelligence, diplomacy, and lord-service correspondence. Maintenance window values PROVISIONAL pending balancing.

Breakup severity: when the actor ends a romance, the target’s current disposition toward the actor determines the cost. Target disposition below Friend (+31): mutual agreement is possible — the target is not deeply invested and accepts the end gracefully. Disposition −3 to −5. The affair secret still exists but neither party is hostile. This is the summer court affair that ended when everyone went home. Target disposition at Friend+ (+31 or higher): the target is emotionally attached. They do not want the romance to end. This becomes a one-sided breakup — disposition −15, the target may spread the affair as a topic. The player does not choose “mutual” or “one-sided” — the target’s attachment level determines it automatically. For NPC-NPC entanglements, the decision engine evaluates both characters’ dispositions and applies the appropriate severity.

Marriage and exposed affairs: an exposed affair does not end a marriage. Rokugani marriages are political arrangements between families — dissolving an alliance over personal shame is more costly than enduring it. Marriage dissolution exists but follows its own system (Section 57.49.7, four pathways) and is not triggered automatically by affair exposure. Marital consequences fire only when the wronged spouse personally receives the affair topic through normal propagation channels (Daily Conversation, letters, court gossip). An affair exposed at a distant court does not automatically reach the wronged spouse — the topic must travel. Once the wronged spouse receives the topic: the wronged spouse’s disposition toward the cheating spouse drops −15 to −20 (processed through the existing disposition system, no new mechanic). The wronged spouse or their lord may demand PUBLIC_ATONEMENT from the cheating spouse through existing court actions. The marriage continues — cold, resentful, but politically intact. Duty over feelings. The wronged spouse’s lord evaluates the scandal independently — the lord’s concern is political damage to their position, not the personal betrayal. The lord may demand consequences for the cheating spouse’s family through disposition pressure and court actions, but this is lord-level political calculation, not spousal grievance.

Organic romance (non-manipulative): not all romances are schemes. Two NPC characters may develop genuine romantic interest without any Seduction ActionID firing. The simulation runs a passive romantic interest check each IC season for any pair of NPC characters (not PC-NPC pairs) who meet all of the following conditions: both at Friend+ disposition toward each other, co-located for extended time (the Cohabitation Disposition Gain per Section 12.2 is actively firing), and neither character’s personality hard-blocks romantic attachment (Ishi blocks it — their course is set and immovable; pure Gi may block it — romantic entanglement outside marriage is dishonest), and both characters’ orientations are compatible with each other’s gender (per Section 22.3 orientation field). Base chance: 5% per season. If the check fires, the entanglement flag sets on both characters. The affair secret generates at the appropriate severity tier — a genuine romance is still a scandal if the participants are married or the political context makes it explosive. No Honor cost — this is not a Low Skill use, it is the simulation generating organic human relationships the same way it generates insurgencies, trade patterns, and political alliances. The same maintenance rules apply once the entanglement exists. This mechanic ensures the world feels alive with human connections that exist independent of player scheming. A player who wants to romance an NPC must actively pursue it through the Seduction ActionIDs and accept the associated Honor and entanglement costs — player romance is always a deliberate choice with mechanical consequences, never a passive event.

NPC Behavior — Seduction ActionIDs: all five Seduction actions pass through the existing four-filter covert action gate (Section 12.8 NPC Decision Logic). Seduction adds a specific Honor cost calculation before scoring: the engine evaluates the Honor loss from “Using a Low Skill” against the NPC’s current Honor level AND whether their school provides protection. Full exemption (Shosuro Infiltrator R1, Bitter Lies R1, Kasuga Smuggler R1): no Honor cost, engine scores Seduction normally as another tool in the covert arsenal. Half Honor loss (Daidoji Harriers R1, Spymaster R3, Ikoma Lion’s Shadow R1, Scorpion Reduced Honour Bleed): Honor cost halved, engine discounts the penalty and considers Seduction usable under objective pressure. No exemption (everyone else): full Honor cost per the Honor table. The engine suppresses Seduction scoring for any NPC above Honor 3.5 without school exemption unless the objective is critical priority (lord-assigned, war-level stakes). In practice, Scorpion agents dominate Seduction use because they can afford it. A Lion bushi NPC at Honor 6.5 with no school exemption will essentially never fire a Seduction action because the Honor cost outweighs any objective value. Personality filters: Gi and Makoto NPCs will never fire any Seduction action — it is fundamentally dishonest, same restriction as fabrication. Rei NPCs fire Seduction reluctantly and only under extreme objective pressure. Chugi NPCs fire only if their lord explicitly assigns the objective. Seigyo (Control) and Dōsatsu (Insight) NPCs are the most likely to use Seduction proactively. Target selection: the NPC selects based on which Seduction ActionID matches their current objective. SEDUCE scores higher than CHARM when Temptation exceeds Courtier and Honor cost is acceptable. SEDUCE_FOR_INFO scores against BRIBE_FOR_INFO — Seduction wins when the actor lacks koku or the target has Lechery. SEDUCE_FOR_ACCESS fires during assassination Phase 1 when a household member can grant physical access. SEDUCE_FOR_LEVERAGE fires when creating an affair secret is more achievable than discovering an existing secret. SEDUCE_TO_COMPROMISE fires when the objective targets a third party and a vulnerable household member is available.

SEDUCE_FOR_INFO: extract information through romantic attention. On success, the target shares one topic from their active pool (same output as BRIBE_FOR_INFO) and disposition toward the actor shifts +3. The topic transfer is processed with the target’s genuine engagement — they are sharing willingly because they are romantically interested, not because they were paid.

SEDUCE_FOR_ACCESS: gain physical access to restricted areas through a seduced household member. On success, the seduced NPC grants the actor passage past checkpoints, into private quarters, or past guards. The access persists as long as the entanglement does — the seduced NPC continues to let the actor through for as long as the relationship holds. This connects directly to assassination Phase 1 (Section 12.8) — SEDUCE_FOR_ACCESS replaces the vague “Temptation (befriending a household member)” in the inner household infiltration chain with a specific mechanic. If the entanglement breaks (the target learns the truth, or the actor abandons the relationship), access is revoked immediately and the target becomes hostile.

SEDUCE_FOR_LEVERAGE: create the affair AS blackmail material. The actor’s goal is not information or access — it is the secret itself. On success, the entanglement secret object enters the actor’s inventory as leverage. The actor can then deploy it through BLACKMAIL (Section 12.9) against the seduced target: “do what I want, or your lord learns about us.” The target does not know the actor is treating the affair as a weapon — they believe the romance is genuine. This is the most cynical use of Seduction and the most devastating when revealed.

SEDUCE_TO_COMPROMISE: target a rival’s spouse, family member, or close associate. The seduced person is the tool, not the ultimate target. On success, the entanglement creates a secret aimed at a THIRD PARTY — the rival whose household the actor has penetrated. When exposed, the rival suffers Glory loss (−0.5 for a spouse’s infidelity, −0.3 for a family member’s), disposition damage from allies and subordinates, and a Tier 3 or Tier 2 political topic. The seduced person is collateral — they suffer the affair scandal consequences, but the political damage lands on the rival. The actor must specify the intended third-party target at the time of firing the action — the system tracks who the compromise is aimed at. Exposure note: the actor cannot expose the affair through EXPOSE_SECRET_PUBLIC without implicating themselves as a participant. The actor typically arranges third-party exposure — leaking evidence to a character who discovers and exposes the secret through their own EXPOSE_SECRET_PUBLIC or REVEAL_SECRET_PRIVATE action. The actor’s identity as the seducer may or may not be revealed depending on how the exposure is orchestrated.

SEDUCE: pure disposition fast-track through romantic attention. No information extracted, no access gained, no leverage created, no third party targeted. On success, disposition toward the actor shifts +5 (higher than the +3 side effect of the other Seduction actions, because disposition IS the primary goal). The entanglement secret is still created at the appropriate severity tier. Repeated SEDUCE against the same target on subsequent IC days produces +5 disposition each time, rapidly advancing the relationship toward Friend+ threshold. Three successful SEDUCE actions over three IC days produces +15 disposition — enough to jump from Stranger to near-Friend. This is the seduction equivalent of CHARM but faster and riskier: the disposition gain is higher per action, but the entanglement secret and the Betrayal modifier on exposure make it a gamble that CHARM does not carry. A courtier who needs a daimyo at Friend+ disposition quickly and cannot wait for months of CHARM and letter exchanges uses SEDUCE — and accepts that the affair secret will exist forever as a vulnerability.

**THE SCORPION SHINOBI — LOCKED**

The word “ninja” is a myth. This is common knowledge in Rokugan, repeated so often it has become a social reflex — and like most things the Scorpion have engineered, it is simultaneously true and false.

The myth is true in the sense that the black-masked, shuriken-throwing, wall-climbing figure of popular imagination does not exist as a serious threat. That figure was invented by the Scorpion Clan in the fifth century as deliberate misdirection — young Shosuro Infiltrator students were equipped with flashy, largely ineffective weapons and black costumes and sent to draw attention while real operatives worked quietly beside them. The Gauntlet, as this training exercise is called, continues to this day. It serves two purposes: filtering out students not yet skilled enough for real work, and keeping magistrates and investigators focused on the wrong thing.

The myth is false in the sense that the real threat — the Scorpion shinobi — absolutely exists. They simply look nothing like the myth.

**What a Shinobi Actually Is — LOCKED**

A true shinobi is a small and specialized group within the Scorpion Clan, numbering at most a few hundred. Most are recruited from the Shosuro family — the Shosuro Infiltrator and Shosuro Actor schools train all such operatives. A minority come from the Soshi family. Rarely are shinobi recruited from the Bayushi or Yogo.

A professional shinobi is rarely, if ever, going to dress as a ninja. A true shinobi is more likely garbed as a peasant, a servant, an ordinary ji-samurai, or a similarly unremarkable person. Skilled shinobi may disguise themselves as specific named individuals — including clan samurai — if such an appearance will further their mission. The Shosuro family’s deep involvement in acting and oratory is not coincidental. The ability to convincingly portray others is the foundation of infiltration.

The real tools of a shinobi are:

- Acting and Sincerity (Deceit) — becoming someone else convincingly enough to pass at close range, over extended time, under scrutiny

- Investigation — reading a situation, identifying guards, understanding routines, finding vulnerabilities

- Forgery — false travel papers, forged letters, counterfeit seals

- Stealth — moving without being noticed, not through supernatural ability but through patience and knowledge of routines

- Poison — the preferred lethal tool. A corrupt samurai is more likely to die from poison dripped into his sake by a house-servant than from any blade. The Scorpion employ poison sparingly and only give it to their most skilled and trusted agents, as a careless operative might allow poison to fall into the wrong hands.

A shinobi does not announce themselves. A successful operation leaves no evidence that a shinobi was involved at all — the target dies of apparent illness, or an accident, or a grudge from a known enemy. The ninja myth exists precisely so that when something does go wrong and a black-clad figure is spotted, it becomes a Scorpion trick or peasant superstition rather than evidence of real intelligence operations.

**The Ninja Myth as Mechanical Reality — LOCKED**

In game terms, this means:

- True shinobi operations use the Social skill space first — Acting, Sincerity (Deceit), Investigation, Forgery — to achieve access. The covert action and Low Skill space (Stealth, poison) comes after. You cannot skip the social infiltration step and go straight to the killing.

- A character investigating a suspicious death faces false trails by default. The Scorpion have engineered deniability into their operations from the start. Proving shinobi involvement requires Investigation rolls against a Forgery or Acting TN, not finding a shuriken at the scene.

- Non-Scorpion characters attempting assassination without shinobi training operate at a severe disadvantage. They lack the social camouflage. They are using only the Low Skills without the social infrastructure that makes those skills safe to deploy.

- The Scorpion’s key Advantages reflect this reality: Bland (unremarkable appearance), Crafty (reads situations), Perceived Honor (appears trustworthy), Silent, Wary. These are social and awareness advantages, not combat ones.

Other clans have covert operators — the Kolat, the Kitsuki family’s investigators, ronin hired for dirty work — but none match the Scorpion’s systematic, generational investment in the social infrastructure of assassination. A Scorpion shinobi is not better at fighting. They are better at getting into the room without anyone knowing they were there.

**ASSASSINATION SYSTEM — TEMPLATE LOCKED, VALUES PENDING**

Assassination is a three-phase covert operation. It is not a single action — it takes multiple real days and requires sustained skill across all three phases. An NPC assassination attempt is resolved through the engine. A player character performing or receiving an assassination attempt experiences it as an ASCII Map mission (Section 4.4.2).

Equipment preparation (before Phase 1): the assassin must get their tools into position before beginning the access phase. Poison vials, secondary weapons, forged credentials, and other contraband must be concealed on the assassin’s person using CONCEAL_ITEM (Section 12.8) before entering the target’s settlement. Poison is a tiny item (TN 10). A tanto or knife requires Rank 5 Sleight of Hand mastery (TN 20) — an assassin without Rank 5 cannot conceal a blade and must rely on poison, arranged accident, or the daisho they carry openly as a samurai. An assassin disguised as a servant or commoner (Phase 1 social camouflage) does not have the right to carry a daisho — any weapon they carry must be concealed via CONCEAL_ITEM with the Rank 5 gate. Normal Rokugani etiquette means guests are not searched (Section 12.8 CONCEAL_ITEM detection rules), so the concealment_tn only matters if someone has specific reason to suspect the assassin and fires SEARCH_PERSON. This is why assassins succeed — the social contract IS the security.

**PHASE 1 — ACCESS (Social Infiltration)**

The assassin must establish a credible presence near the target — past the outer perimeter, into the inner household, close enough to act. Minimum duration: 3 real days for a well-protected target. Less-protected targets may be accessible in 1–2 days.

Each day of the access phase requires at least one skill roll. The approach and skill used depends on the assassin’s chosen method and what the location affords — there is no fixed sequence. Viable approaches per checkpoint include:

- Outer perimeter: Forgery (credentials), Temptation (bribery), Stealth (alternate entry), social introduction through an existing contact

- Inner household: Acting + Sincerity Deceit (sustained disguise), Forgery (fake contract as craftsman or servant), SEDUCE_FOR_ACCESS (seducing a household member for physical access per Section 12.8 Seduction Actions)

- Close proximity: Investigation (reading the target’s routine to find the isolated moment), Acting (maintaining cover under close scrutiny), arranging a distraction

The approach is chosen by the assassin based on their skill ranks, the location type, and available opportunities. A castle interior affords different approaches than a court setting or a traveling caravan.

Difficulty is set by: target’s Status (higher Status = higher base TN), household loyalty (loyal servants notice more), and accumulated suspicion from previous failed attempts.

Suspicion accumulator: each failed roll increases the TN on all subsequent Phase 1 rolls. Suspicion is tracked as a numeric value on the target settlement. Suspicion decay rate: −1 per daily tick while the assassin is absent from the settlement. −0.5 per daily tick while the assassin remains present but inactive (not making Phase 1 rolls) — the household does not fully relax with the suspicious person still around. Minimum 14 daily ticks (3.5 real days) before any settlement returns to baseline, regardless of how little suspicion accumulated — even a minor scare does not vanish overnight. A moderately failed operation (~15 suspicion from 2–3 failed rolls) clears in ~15 daily ticks absent (~4 real days). A heavily botched operation (~40–50 suspicion) clears in ~40–50 daily ticks (~10–12 real days). Per-roll suspicion accumulation: standard failure (missed TN by 1–9): +5 suspicion. Notable failure (missed TN by 10–19): +10 suspicion. Critical failure (missed TN by 20+): +15 suspicion plus immediate detection check by the nearest household member. Household response thresholds: 0–9 suspicion — no response, normal activity. 10–19 — household watchful, passive Investigation rolls gain +5. 20–29 — active patrols, bodyguard assigned if not already present. 30+ — target warned, routine changes, all Phase 1 TNs increase by +10 (stacks with the permanent per-roll TN penalty). Two parallel tracks operate: the per-roll TN penalty is permanent for the current operation (each failure makes subsequent rolls harder, cannot be reduced except by aborting and restarting Phase 1 from scratch), while settlement suspicion decays per the rates above (the household relaxes but the assassin’s cover remains compromised). Values PROVISIONAL pending playtest.

Detection signals during Phase 1: household members with high loyalty generate Investigation roll opportunities for the target or their bodyguards each day the assassin is present. A player character target who actively investigates can contest the assassin’s Acting or Forgery roll TN. Failure to detect does not mean the threat is gone — it means it wasn’t found today.

**PHASE 2 — EXECUTION (The Act)**

The assassin has access and has identified the moment. Three execution methods are available, each with different risk and concealment profiles:

**Poison:** The preferred Scorpion tool. Deniable, leaves no evidence of violence, requires no direct combat. Delivery uses Stealth + Sleight of Hand — concealing the act of poisoning food, drink, or a personal item. The target does not die immediately — poison takes effect over hours or days, giving the assassin time to be elsewhere when death occurs. The most concealable method and the hardest to trace.

**Blade in private:** Requires physical proximity and a moment alone with the target. Stealth + relevant weapon skill. Higher risk than poison — noise or struggle can alert nearby guards. A body with blade wounds is harder to explain as illness. Faster but less deniable.

**Arranged accident:** The most complex method. Investigation (reading the environment for opportunities) + Acting (setting up the scenario convincingly over multiple days). A tampered saddle, a weakened bridge, a fire set. Requires advance preparation during the access phase. Highest concealment potential if successful — an accident looks like an accident. Also the hardest to execute reliably.

**Bodyguard Encounter — LOCKED:**

When a named Bushi bodyguard is assigned to the target and present at the moment of execution, the assassin faces a real-time decision on the ASCII Map:

- Option A — Engage the bodyguard first, then the target. Fight the bodyguard, defeat them, then attempt the assassination. The fight generates noise and potentially alerts more guards. A dead bodyguard makes concealment nearly impossible — someone will ask questions.

- Option B — Go straight for the target and run. Ignore the bodyguard, strike the target, flee. The bodyguard gets a free interception opportunity. If the target survives or the assassin is caught fleeing, the operation is blown entirely.

- Option C — Abort. Withdraw without acting. The operation survives but the moment is lost. The assassin must wait for a window when the bodyguard is absent, which may require days of additional Phase 1 work or a change in method.

For NPC assassins the engine evaluates which option scores highest based on competence, target value, and personality. A Ketsui (Determination) assassin pushes through. A Seigyo (Control) assassin aborts and waits for the right moment.

**Player Character Target — LOCKED:**

If the execution phase reaches a player character while offline, it does not kill them instantly. A crisis event fires on their next login. The specific event depends on the method:

- Poison: the player wakes with symptoms. They are poisoned and have limited time to find an antidote or medical help. An ASCII Map mission begins immediately.

- Blade: the player finds evidence of a break-in, a wounded or dead bodyguard, or wakes injured but alive if a bodyguard intervened. An ASCII Map security mission begins.

- Accident: the player discovers the setup before it triggers, or survives the attempt injured. They know something is wrong but may not immediately know it was deliberate.

In all cases the player is alive and able to respond. The grace period before the situation resolves against them varies by execution method. Poison: 48 daily ticks (12 real days). Slow-acting — the player has time to find an antidote or medical help. The longest window, making poison the fairest method against players and the most lore-accurate Scorpion choice. Blade: 16 daily ticks (4 real days). A bodyguard intervened or the wound is not immediately fatal, but the situation is urgent. The shortest window. Arranged Accident: 32 daily ticks (8 real days). The setup is in motion but has not fully triggered — a weakened bridge, a tampered saddle. If the player does not log in and respond within the grace period, the assassination succeeds — the poison kills them, the blade wound proves fatal, or the accident completes.

If the player is online when the execution phase fires, it triggers an ASCII Map mission immediately. Finding the assassin, identifying the poison, preventing the accident — all play out in real time with the full skill system.

**Player-vs-Player Blade Edge Case — LOCKED:** For NPC assassins targeting an offline player, the engine resolves the NPC’s Phase 2 and the player receives the crisis event on login — no issue. For player assassins using blade against an offline player target, the attacking player needs the ASCII map to execute the strike but the target is not present. The player assassin may choose to resolve the attempt through the engine at NPC resolution quality, forfeiting the tactical advantage of personal ASCII map play. Alternatively, the player assassin may wait — remaining in the target’s settlement, accumulating suspicion each daily tick, with detection checks firing daily against them. If the target logs in before the player assassin withdraws, the ASCII map confrontation fires immediately for both players. If the player assassin withdraws before the target logs in, the opportunity is lost and they must re-enter Phase 1 to try again. Poison and arranged accident do not have this constraint — the assassin can be elsewhere when the effect triggers.

**PHASE 3 — CONCEALMENT (Covering Tracks)**

A successful execution that is not concealed generates a Death topic (variant: suspicious or violent murder) immediately, triggering the succession process (Section 22.5) and potentially an investigation. Concealment attempts to prevent this — making the death appear to be illness, accident, or the act of a known enemy rather than assassination.

Concealment is not guaranteed even for skilled operators. The method used in Phase 2 constrains what concealment is possible:

- Poison death — easiest to conceal. Illness is a credible explanation. Forgery (false medical record or witness account) + Acting (convincing the household the target was unwell before death). Contested against Investigation of anyone examining the body or the scene.

- Blade death — harder to conceal. Wounds require explanation. The narrative must point to a known enemy or a robbery. Forgery (plant evidence of another party) + Acting (establishing false witness accounts). If a bodyguard was killed, explaining two bodies is significantly harder.

- Accident death — concealment is built into the method. If the accident was set up well enough in Phase 2 it requires minimal additional concealment work. A failed accident that the target survived becomes a visible crime scene with evidence of tampering.

**Concealment Outcomes — LOCKED:**

- Full success: Death topic generated with no suspicious variant. The death enters public knowledge as natural causes or accident. Investigation is possible but must begin from zero without obvious leads.

- Partial success: Death topic generated with suspicious variant but no direct evidence pointing to the assassin. Rumors circulate. Characters with high Investigation skill can pursue leads but the assassin’s identity is not immediately exposed.

- Failure: Death topic generated as violent murder. The assassin’s trail — their Forgery TN, their Acting roll result, physical evidence — becomes the starting point for investigation. The commissioning lord is potentially implicated.

**Investigation Authority — ✅ DESIGNED:** The Magistrate System (Section 11.3) is now fully designed. Investigation authority for suspicious deaths follows the jurisdiction rules in Section 11.3.9d: same-clan killing is handled by the victim's clan magistrate, cross-clan killing invokes Imperial jurisdiction. Concealment failure generates a CrimeRecord (Section 11.3.14e) with discovered: true. The magistrate's INVESTIGATE_CRIME objective (Section 57.16) decomposes into the investigation loop (Section 11.3.13). The concealment_tn on the CrimeRecord is the TN that investigators must beat via EXAMINE_CRIME_SCENE (Section 11.3.13d).

**Magistrate System Interface Contract:** The Magistrate System (Section 11.3, fully designed) interfaces with the assassination system at three points. First: concealment failure generates a CrimeRecord (Section 11.3.14e) with crime_type = murder and the assassin’s concealment roll stored as concealment_tn. Investigators must beat this TN via EXAMINE_CRIME_SCENE (Section 11.3.13d) to uncover the trail. The concealment_tn is the mechanical bridge between Phase 3 output and investigation input. Second: a commissioning lord’s identity is never directly attached to the evidence. Tracing the kill order requires a separate investigation chain starting from the assassin’s identity — the assassin must be identified first (added to CrimeRecord.suspects), then connected to their employer through the conspiracy to murder rules (Section 11.3.9l). This is at minimum a two-step process and may require covert acquisition methods (Section 12.8) to complete. Third: the investigating authority determines jurisdiction per Section 11.3.9d. Assassination within a single clan’s territory is a clan matter — the victim’s Clan Magistrate has authority. Assassination crossing clan lines — a Scorpion agent killing a Crane lord — invokes Imperial jurisdiction and the Emerald Magistrates (Section 11.3.4). This jurisdictional split creates political complications: a clan investigating its own assassination may have reasons to suppress the truth (bribery pipeline, Section 11.3.11f), while Imperial investigation strips the clan of control over the outcome.

**HONOR AND INFAMY CONSEQUENCES — LOCKED**

- Ordering an assassination: Honor loss for the lord who commissioned it, scaled by the target’s Status. Target Status 1–2 (minor samurai, ji-samurai): −2.0 Honor. Target Status 3–5 (provincial officials, family daimyo retainers): −3.0 Honor. Target Status 6–7 (family daimyo, prominent clan figures): −4.0 Honor. Target Status 8+ (Clan Champion, Imperial family): −5.0 Honor. Always private initially — becomes public if concealment fails.

- Executing an assassination: Honor loss for the assassin character. Scorpion pay almost nothing. Other clans pay steeply.

- Failed assassination that is traced: generates a Betrayal Tier 2 topic about the commissioning lord. Permanent reputation damage. Possible Imperial consequence if target was high Status. If the target was killed, the confirmed successor (Section 22.5) automatically receives “Avenge [Death of Character X]” as a crisis-override primary objective per Section 18.6, targeting the identified commissioning lord. This objective justifies all war tiers against the responsible party per Section 53. If the target survived, they receive the same objective directly. The −50/−50 historical disposition modifier (killed a family member) applies to the commissioning lord from all members of the victim’s biological family (Section 22.6), potentially generating the “Seek vengeance” standing objective in multiple characters simultaneously.

**IMPERIAL PROTECTION MODIFIER — LOCKED**

Assassination attempts against the Emperor or members of the Imperial dynasty who are under Seppun protection (Section 2.1.19, Section 27.10) receive significant TN increases across all phases. Phase 1 (Approach): +15 TN to all social camouflage and infiltration rolls — the Seppun miharu are trained specifically to detect threats, and the Hidden Guard monitors for supernatural intrusion. Phase 2 (Execution): +20 TN to the execution roll — the Emperor is never unguarded; at least two miharu are physically present at all times, and Hidden Guard shugenja maintain active wards against magical assassination methods (poison detection, spirit intrusion, maho). Phase 3 (Concealment): +10 TN — the Seppun investigate any death or illness affecting the Imperial family with extreme thoroughness. These modifiers stack with standard assassination TNs. An assassination attempt against the Emperor is not impossible — the Steel Chrysanthemum was killed by his own miharu — but it requires either extraordinary skill, inside assistance from the Seppun themselves (a near-impossible betrayal given their institutional devotion), or a catastrophic failure in the Hidden Guard’s magical protections. Members of the Imperial family not currently under direct Seppun guard (traveling alone, posted to distant provinces) receive half these modifiers (+8/+10/+5).

**NPC Decision Logic — When to Use Covert Methods — LOCKED:**

⚙️ CROSS-REF: This four-filter model maps to the Seven-Phase Decision Loop (Section 55.4). Filter 3 hard blocks (Gi/Makoto never fabricate) are entries in personality_filter.json (Phase 4 — Personality Filter). Filter 1 clan baselines, Filter 2 Honor thresholds, and Filter 3 soft reluctance levels feed into Phase 5 scoring as Personality Lean inputs. Filter 4 objective and disposition requirements map to Phase 5's Objective Alignment and Disposition Modifier components. The four filters described here are the covert-action-specific view of the same architecture — they do not constitute a separate decision system.

Covert methods are deeply frowned upon in Rokugani society. They are the tools of the dishonorable, the desperate, and the Scorpion. A character who uses them pays not just in Honor but in social standing. The decision to use covert methods is governed by four stacking filters. All four must align before an NPC will act.

**Filter 1 — Clan Baseline:**

- Scorpion: No reluctance. These methods are their identity and cultural inheritance. Honor cost is priced in.

- Unicorn: Low reluctance. Eight hundred years of wandering produced a pragmatic relationship with survival methods. Less constrained by Rokugani social norms, still not comfortable with it.

- Crab: Moderate reluctance. Pragmatic under extreme circumstances — the Wall justifies things other clans won’t touch. But they prefer direct methods.

- Mantis: Moderate reluctance. Merchant and pirate heritage gives some comfort with irregular methods. Not proud of it.

- Dragon: High reluctance. Detached and observant by nature — they may possess information gathered through observation but rarely deploy it covertly.

- Crane: Very high reluctance. Deeply uncomfortable. Would consider using these methods only under catastrophic circumstances and would feel the Honor cost acutely.

- Phoenix: Very high reluctance. Spiritually and philosophically opposed. Sees covert manipulation as a corruption of proper conduct.

- Lion: Near-total reluctance. Honor code forbids it in almost all circumstances. A Lion who uses these methods has already compromised something fundamental about themselves.

**Filter 2 — School Starting Honor:**

Each school has a starting Honor value (see Section 22.4 generation templates). The Honor cost of covert methods hits high-Honor characters far harder than low-Honor ones. NPCs evaluate whether the cost is acceptable given their current Honor level. A character at Honor 1.5 losing 0.3 Honor is a minor cost. A character at Honor 4.0 losing the same 0.3 Honor crosses a threshold that changes how others perceive them.

General thresholds: characters with starting Honor below 2.0 will consider covert methods when objective and disposition justify it. Characters with starting Honor 2.0–3.5 will consider them only under significant pressure. Characters with starting Honor above 3.5 will almost never use them regardless of circumstances.

**Filter 3 — Virtue Profile:**

- Gi (Honesty/Righteousness): Will never fabricate. Will never deploy a secret through covert means even if they legitimately possess it. Cannot be overridden by objective or disposition.

- Makoto (Sincerity): Same as Gi. Cannot bring themselves to operate through deception.

- Meiyo (Honor): Strong reluctance. Personal code forbids it in most circumstances. Only under direct threat to their existence or their family.

- Chugi (Duty): Will use these methods only if their lord explicitly assigns it as a primary objective. Otherwise will not act independently in this space.

- Jin (Compassion): Reluctant. The harm caused to others troubles them even when the target deserves it.

- Seigyo (Control): Natural territory. Will deploy when it serves their objective and the cost is acceptable.

- Dōsatsu (Insight): Comfortable gathering through covert means. Slightly more reluctant to deploy — prefers to hold information as leverage rather than spend it.

- Chishiki (Knowledge): Comfortable with covert acquisition. Will deploy when the intelligence value of the act is high.

- Yu (Courage): Strong reluctance. Covert methods are indirect — the opposite of confronting an enemy openly. Will consider covert methods only when no direct path to the objective exists and the stakes are existential. A Yu character who resorts to subterfuge feels diminished by it.

- Rei (Courtesy): Strong reluctance. Covert methods violate the social order and proper conduct between samurai. Same unwillingness as Gossip — too crude. Will not deploy unless under extreme objective pressure from a lord assignment, and even then with visible discomfort (Honor cost weighted heavily in scoring).

- Ketsui (Determination): Moderate reluctance. No inherent moral objection — the method is secondary to achieving the goal. Once committed to a covert approach, pursues it relentlessly and will not abandon it regardless of setbacks. More willing than Yu or Rei but less natural than Seigyo or Dōsatsu.

- Kyōryōku (Strength): Strong reluctance. Prefers direct confrontation in all situations. Covert methods feel like weakness — a substitute for the force that should resolve the problem. Will consider covert methods only when direct force is clearly impossible and the objective demands action.

- Ishi (Will): Moderate reluctance based on established pattern. If their initial approach to a conflict includes covert methods, they use them without hesitation. If their approach does not, they will not adopt covert methods regardless of how circumstances change. Rigidity, not morality, governs their position.

- Kanpeki (Perfection): Conditional. Will use covert methods only when confident of flawless execution — competence in the relevant skill must be high (Rank 4+ in the governing skill). Will withdraw from a covert operation rather than execute it poorly. An imperfect covert action is worse than no action at all.

**Filter 4 — Objective and Disposition:**

Even a Scorpion with no compunctions won’t waste a valuable secret on someone they’re indifferent to. The target must be in the Enemy or Blood Enemy range (−31 or below), or the primary objective must specifically require damaging them. The stakes must justify the cost.

**Reputation Consequences of Covert Methods — LOCKED:**

Using covert methods is itself a reputation-generating event. Being known as someone who operates this way has ongoing social consequences independent of any specific act being proven.

Each covert action that becomes known adds to the character’s Infamy track. When Infamy accumulates sufficiently from covert method use, the game automatically generates a Personal Disgrace topic about the character — specifically a reputation topic marking them as someone known to use spies, bribery, and manipulation. This topic spreads through the normal conversation and letter systems.

The social consequences of this reputation topic vary by who receives it:

- High-Honor characters (Gi, Makoto, Meiyo virtue profiles, Lion and Phoenix clan) treat it as a Disgrace. Disposition drops significantly. They may refuse to deal with the character entirely.

- Neutral characters treat it as interesting intelligence — it makes them more cautious but doesn’t necessarily damage disposition.

- Pragmatic characters (Scorpion, some Crab, some Unicorn) may treat it as neutral or even positive — a capable operator is sometimes more valuable than an honorable one.

Infamy threshold for automatic reputation topic generation: +0.5 Infamy accumulated from covert method use specifically (tracked separately from other Infamy sources). Each time the threshold is crossed, a new reputation topic is generated. The topic is harder to suppress than ordinary topics — the subject cannot easily push it out of circulation because it reflects a real pattern of behavior.
