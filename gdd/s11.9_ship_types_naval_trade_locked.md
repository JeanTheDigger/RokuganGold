## 11.9 Ship Types & Naval Trade — LOCKED

Source: The Book of Water, Chapter Five. Naval trade uses the same province adjacency model as land trade routes (§4.3.18), with one modification: water sub-tiles count at half cost for trade radius purposes. Two water sub-tiles = one effective adjacency hop, reflecting the historical ease of moving goods by sea compared to overland. A coastal province can therefore trade with provinces up to two water sub-tiles away — a longer reach than any land route.

### Ship Types — LOCKED

Nine vessel types exist in Rokugan. Taraibune and Yakatabune have no world-map mechanical role — they are lore entries only. The Tortoise oceangoing vessel is a seventh mechanical class, Tortoise-exclusive, fully designed below. The remaining six participate in trade or military systems.

**Kobune: **The standard Rokugani vessel. ~75’ length, 6’ draft, crew of ~20. Flat-bottomed with no keel. Quarterdeck for archers at the stern (and sometimes bow). Small cabin. Little structural armature — fares poorly in strong waves or storms, but excels in rivers and lakes. Coastal ocean capable; deep ocean is extremely dangerous. Some river variants are oar-only with no sails. Functions as both transport and warship. Cargo: 0.3 Rice or Koku. Movement: 1 real day per coastal sub-tile. Cannot cross open ocean sub-tiles.

**Sampan: **Small river and coastal vessel, ~20’ long, crew 1–2. Two subtypes: kamo-sampan (duck — raised square prow, rivers) and niwatori-sampan (chicken — pointed bow, coastal). Cargo: 0.1 Rice or Koku. Movement: 1 real day per river or bay sub-tile. Cannot venture into open ocean.

**Merchant Barge: **Primary bulk goods carrier on inland waterways. 40–120’ long depending on merchant wealth. Turtle-shaped, broad-bellied. Propelled by large square sails supplemented with oars or poles. Merchant lives aboard. Used in rivers, lakes, and occasionally coastal waters. Cargo: 0.5 Rice or Koku. Movement: 1 real day per river, lake, or coastal sub-tile. Cannot venture into open ocean.

**Sengokobune: **Mantis-designed improved kobune, invented by shipbuilder Watanabe at the end of the eleventh century. 90’ long, crew of ~40, internal braces, lateen sails (technically a violation of Imperial Edict, kept hidden by the Mantis for decades). The only Rokugani vessel capable of sustained ocean voyages. Cargo: 0.5 Rice or Koku. Movement: 1 real day per ocean sub-tile. Can traverse all water sub-tile types.

**Koutetsukan: **Crab Clan iron turtle warship. Invented by Kaiu Sunshin as a weapon against Shadowlands sea-borne horrors. ~80’ wide, crew of ~100, fully enclosed, hull armored with iron plates, jade-tipped rams, “dragon’s maw” capable of projecting smoke or hot tar. Military unit only — no cargo capacity. Movement: 2 real days per coastal sub-tile (slow and heavy). Cannot cross open ocean sub-tiles — prone to capsizing in rough water.

**Atakebune: **Mantis secret giant floating fortress. Originally designed during secret wars with the Ivory Kingdoms during the Four Winds era; incorporates gaijin design elements. Hidden at the port Aramasu’s Vigilance; rediscovered during the Destroyer War as the Mantis “Fourth Storm.” Four segmented sails plus lateen sail. Military unit only — no cargo capacity. Available only to the Mantis Clan at game start. Movement: 1 real day per ocean sub-tile.

**Taraibune: **Barrel-shaped heimin fishing vessel. Oversized wooden barrel design, holds ~3 adults, propelled by rod or oar. Used for fishing and simple transport in riverside and island villages. No world-map mechanical role — lore flavor only.

**Yakatabune: **Luxury entertainment barge. Named for the tilted roof running two-thirds of its length. No mast or sails — propelled entirely by oars or poles. Interior lined with tatami mats; used for private gatherings, sake, and geisha. Prized as proof of high prestige and wealth. No world-map mechanical role — lore flavor only.

**Tortoise Oceangoing Vessel --- LOCKED: **The seventh mechanical ship class. The Minor Clan Tortoise (Kasuga) operates oceangoing vessels that incorporate gaijin design elements — keel, fore-and-aft rig, multiple sail shapes — making them functionally distinct from Rokugani-designed vessels. This knowledge is kept strictly secret from the rest of the Empire per the Section 11.9 Lore Notes on the Tortoise Fleet. Ship class enum: TORTOISE_OCEANGOING. Tortoise-exclusive --- no other clan may legitimately own or operate one. Approximately 90 feet long, roughly comparable in hull scale to a Sengokobune but with fundamentally different rigging and hull design. Crew: approximately 25–30 heimin sailors --- the fore-and-aft rig is more efficient than Rokugani square and lateen rigs and requires fewer hands to work, with the incidental benefit of reducing the number of witnesses who might speak of the ship’s unusual construction. All crew are carefully vetted Kasuga-associated heimin. Movement: 1 real day per ocean sub-tile, matching Sengokobune. Ocean-capable; ignores the deep ocean penalty (10% catastrophic loss per ocean sub-tile) that applies to non-oceangoing Rokugani vessels, same treatment as Sengokobune and Atakebune. Navigation bonus: +1k0 on Navigation rolls in open ocean sub-tiles, representing the gaijin design’s superior deep-water handling through the deep keel and fore-and-aft rig. No bonus in coastal or river waters; the design’s advantage is specifically a deep-water advantage. Cargo: no cargo value listed --- per Section 4.3’s resolved deferral of the physical goods transport layer, cargo values on ships are flavor only and do not connect to any game mechanic. Captain Sailing requirement: 3 minimum, matching Sengokobune. The Kasuga Smuggler school provides the gaijin-rigging training as part of its curriculum; non-Kasuga characters are excluded by clan-exclusivity before Sailing rank comes into play. Captain appointment follows the Kasuga Smuggler tradition (owner-patron pattern per Section 57.42.2). Crew follows the owner-patron rule from Section 57.43.4: the Kasuga captain is the only named character aboard, and all other crew are heimin to preserve the secrecy of the ship’s gaijin construction. The ship does not carry standing named samurai crew and does not carry a Yoritomo Shugenja crew slot (the Yoritomo Shugenja crew slot is Mantis-exclusive per Section 57.43.4). Construction: 10 Koku units (PROVISIONAL pending balancing), 1 season to build, available only at Tortoise-family shipyards. The commissioning cost exceeds the Sengokobune’s 8 Koku units because gaijin-design specialization requires Kasuga shipwrights and specialized materials. Naval combat stats (see Naval Combat Stats subsection below for full entry): H:130, A:3, D:4, M:14, MD:5. Lower Attack than Sengokobune (A:3 vs A:4) reflects that the Tortoise vessel is not configured for warfighting --- it carries archers but not the aggressive warship crew of a Mantis Sengokobune. Hull strength, defense, morale, and minimum damage match Sengokobune because the structural hull is comparable (in fact, the gaijin construction is marginally more sound than Rokugani designs, which is the whole point of the secret program). Special ability --- Escape Attempt: the Tortoise vessel’s purpose is trade, not combat, and its design philosophy is flee-before-fight. Mass Battle action (Army View layer), Tortoise Oceangoing Vessel only, once per engagement, consumes the ship’s Company action for that round. The captain rolls Navigation + Intelligence, contested by the enemy captain’s Battle + Intelligence. Weather modifiers: +1k0 in Wind weather, +2k0 in Storm weather, +3k0 in Typhoon weather --- the gaijin design handles weather better than Rokugani ships, and rough seas favor the escaping vessel. Success disengages the Tortoise ship from the engagement at the Army View level; if a Ship Boarding ASCII template had been generated, that map resolves with the ships separating and the Tortoise ship sailing away. Failure leaves the ship engaged; combat resumes next round; Escape Attempt cannot be retried in the same engagement. Escape Attempt is a captain-initiated action only, not a passenger action --- a player aboard who wants the captain to attempt escape must NEGOTIATE or PERSUADE the captain at the ASCII layer, and the captain evaluates the request as a personality-weighted decision. A player who IS the captain commands Escape Attempt directly as part of the Army View round. Secret System exposure: recognition that a Tortoise vessel is gaijin-built is treated as a single Tier 2 clan-level secret --- “The Tortoise Clan operates gaijin-built ships as a regular practice.” Specialists (Kaiu Engineer, Mantis Kobune Captain Rank 3+, Sailing 5+ characters) recognize the gaijin construction on sight with no roll required --- keel, fore-and-aft rig, and multi-shape sails are unmistakable to anyone trained in naval construction. Non-specialist characters may attempt recognition through a skill roll on proximity: Sailing, Engineering, or relevant Lore (Heraldry or History) against a TN scaled by access --- TN 25 at a distance (glimpsed at sea, passing in port), TN 20 aboard as a passenger, TN 15 with detailed inspection (examining the hull in drydock, climbing the rigging). A successful recognition surfaces the Tier 2 secret through the standard Secret System per Section 12.8 and propagates per its normal rules. The Tortoise do not maintain per-ship deniability at a deeper tier --- one exposure implicates the clan as a whole, because multiple gaijin-built ships are incontrovertible evidence of organized practice rather than individual oddity. No Tier 1 Imperial-sanction secret is separately tracked; if a public Tier 2 exposure escalates to Imperial investigation, the fallout flows through existing political systems rather than a dedicated deeper secret.

### Naval Combat Stats — LOCKED

Naval combat uses the same Company stat format as the Army Combat System (§11.7). Each ship is treated as a Company. Combat resolves using the standard round structure with the modifications noted below. Flanking rules do not apply at sea — ships engage in broadside or bow-on attacks only. Naval deployment uses the same Forward Row / Reserve Row structure as land combat (Section 11.7). Ships in the Forward Row engage in boarding combat on Round 1. Ships in the Reserve Row provide ranged support or hold for commitment. Only the Kobune has ranged capability from the Reserve Row — its archer quarterdeck fires ranged support each round at the enemy ship paired with its front-row ally, rolling 1d5 + Attack stat, identical to the Ashigaru Archer mechanic on land. All other ship types must deploy in the Forward Row to contribute. A Kobune committed from the Reserve Row to the Forward Row loses its ranged support and fights as a boarding ship. Weather degrades or suppresses Kobune back-row ranged support per the Weather at Sea subsection below. Defense floor: a ship’s Defense cannot drop below 0 after all modifiers (weather global, ship-type-specific, Heroic Opportunity effects) are applied. Negative Defense values are floored at 0.

Battle Table engagement levels map to the physical space of the ship deck. The “front line” in naval combat is the rail where boarding happens. Reserves: below decks or in the cabin. Safe from the fighting. The character is not participating in deck combat. Heroic Opportunities at this level are non-combat (Patch the Hull). Disengaged: on deck but away from the rails — near the mast, mid-deck, or on the quarterdeck behind the captain. Can see the fight, not in it. Can shift to Engaged next turn. Engaged: at the rail, fighting boarders coming over or preparing to board the enemy ship. Standard combat risk. Heroic Opportunities at this level include Repel Boarders, Cut the Grapples, Rescue the Fallen, Secure the Rigging, Rally the Crew. Heavily Engaged: on the boarding planks or on the enemy deck. Maximum risk — fighting on a one-tile-wide bridge between ships, or surrounded by enemy crew on their own vessel. Wet deck penalties apply. Heroic Opportunities at this level include Lead the Boarding, Kill the Captain, Seize the Colors, Defend the Quarterdeck. The Battlefield Bubble generates the appropriate ship location for the character’s engagement level — a Reserves character gets a below-decks bubble, a Heavily Engaged character gets the boarding planks or enemy quarterdeck.

**Kobune (warship configuration): **H: 100 | A: 3 | D: 3 | M: 12 | MD: 4. Special: Archer quarterdeck — the only ship with Reserve Row ranged capability. In the Reserve Row, fires ranged support each round (1d5 + Attack) at the enemy ship paired with its front-row ally, identical to the Ashigaru Archer mechanic on land. In the Forward Row, +1 Attack on Round 1 only (archers loose before ships close to boarding range). Weather degrades back-row ranged: Rain (rough seas) reduces to 1d3 + Attack; Storm and Typhoon suppress ranged entirely.

**Sengokobune: **H: 130 | A: 4 | D: 4 | M: 14 | MD: 5. Special: Mantis crew bonus — when operated by the Mantis Clan, +1k0 to all combat rolls.

**Koutetsukan: **H: 200 | A: 6 | D: 8 | M: 20 | MD: 8. Special: Ram attack — once per battle, +8 Attack, deals 5 Health to self. Immune to boarding actions. Ship-type weather penalty: additional −1 Attack in Storm and Typhoon (top-heavy iron hull).

**Atakebune: **H: 250 | A: 7 | D: 6 | M: 18 | MD: 7. Special: Mobile fortress — grants +3 Defense to all adjacent friendly ships. Mantis Clan only at game start.

**Tortoise Oceangoing Vessel: **H: 130 | A: 3 | D: 4 | M: 14 | MD: 5. Special: Escape Attempt --- once per engagement, Mass Battle (Army View) action, consumes the ship’s Company action for the round. Captain rolls Navigation + Intelligence, contested by enemy captain’s Battle + Intelligence. +1k0 in Wind weather, +2k0 in Storm weather, +3k0 in Typhoon weather. Success disengages at Army View level, closes any generated Ship Boarding ASCII map. Failure leaves the ship engaged, cannot retry. Tortoise-exclusive; full stat block and design rationale in the Ship Types subsection above.

### Naval Trade Route Rules — LOCKED

Trade radius on water: two water sub-tiles = one effective adjacency hop. A coastal province can form a naval trade connection with any province within two water sub-tiles. This gives sea routes a longer reach than land adjacency at the same resource cost.

Naval trade routes generate the same Koku bonus per season as land routes under §4.3.18. The same disruption and embargo rules apply.

Piracy vulnerability: naval routes have no inherent piracy disruption unless a Pirate (Wako) Fleet insurgency (Section 11.11) is active in the province or an adjacent coastal province. When a fleet is active, trade route effects scale with fleet Strength per the insurgency type definition. See Section 11.4.3 for pirate lore.

Mantis exception: Mantis provinces have −10% pirate fleet spawn chance (Section 11.11) and the Mantis gain +3 to all pirate suppression rolls. The dominant naval power protects its own waters.

Ocean sub-tiles require Sengokobune or Atakebune to cross. Kobune, Sampan, and Merchant Barges are limited to river, lake, and coastal sub-tiles. A naval trade route crossing an ocean sub-tile cannot be established without Sengokobune access — effectively a Mantis monopoly at game start.

Route disruption by War Status, banditry, and embargo follows the same rules as land routes (§4.3.18). Naval blockade (a military unit controlling a coastal or ocean sub-tile) also disrupts all routes passing through that sub-tile.

### Navigation — LOCKED

Rokugani navigation is near-mystical in the view of most samurai. Ship captains do not chart new courses — they follow established routes laid down by bolder ancestors, staying close to shore and island-hopping when open water must be crossed. Routine travel requires no roll — ships move at their listed rate and arrive at their destination (Section 57.43.7). Navigation skill matters during naval combat through the Ride the Waves Heroic Opportunity (Section 11.7 Category 9) and the Tortoise Escape Attempt (this section). Two tools enhance combat navigation rolls:

Direction finder (lodestone device): A south-pointing lodestone suspended in a bowl of water. Found only on the largest and most seaworthy Mantis vessels — originally a gift from Merenae ambassadors. Grants +1k0 to Navigation rolls during naval combat (Ride the Waves, Tortoise Escape Attempt). Mantis only.

Shugenja kami assistance: A shugenja aboard may commune with water kami for navigational guidance. Perform a Water affinity roll vs TN 20. Success grants +2k0 to the captain’s next Navigation roll during naval combat.

Deep ocean penalty: Any ship attempting an open ocean crossing without Sengokobune, Atakebune, or Tortoise Oceangoing Vessel hull class has a 10% chance of catastrophic loss per ocean sub-tile crossed. The deep ocean is suicidal for standard Rokugani vessels.

### Boarding Actions — LOCKED

When two ships are adjacent in naval combat, either side may declare a boarding action instead of a normal attack. Boarding is the primary method of ship-to-ship combat in Rokugan — Rokugani vessels lack cannon or significant ranged weaponry beyond archers. The boarding ship's crew crosses to the defending ship via grappling hooks and planks. Both crews fight using their ship's Company stats (Attack, Defense, Morale). The boarding side suffers −2 Attack on the first round of boarding (disadvantage of crossing between ships). Subsequent rounds fight normally. A ship reduced to 0 Health through boarding is captured — its surviving crew surrenders or is killed. A captured ship can be claimed as a prize (Koku value = half its construction cost). CAPTURED SIGNATURE SHIPS --- SPECIAL RULE: Signature ships are vessels tied to a specific clan’s identity and design heritage --- the Atakebune (Mantis) and the Koutetsukan (Crab) at game start. When a non-native clan captures a signature ship, the default outcome is destruction. The captor sinks, burns, or scuttles the vessel as part of the capture resolution. The ship class remains clan-exclusive; capture does not create ongoing ownership by the rival clan. Destruction is the lore-consistent, low-political-risk outcome and is what most captors will choose. The captor may elect to override the default and keep the ship as a prize. This election is a deliberate act, not passive retention. Keeping a captured signature ship produces: a permanent Tier 1 or Tier 2 crisis topic (“Clan X holds a stolen [Atakebune/Koutetsukan]”); a Mantis or Crab Blood Feud (as appropriate) against the captor’s clan per Section 22 Advantages/Disadvantages; ongoing −1 disposition per season from all characters of the signature ship’s clan toward all characters of the captor’s clan, for as long as the ship is retained; and operational requirements that still apply (Sailing 4+ captain for an Atakebune, etc. per Section 57.42.3). The captor may also elect to return the ship gracefully under diplomatic pressure. Return generates: a major favor owed by the signature ship’s clan to the returner (Obligation Advantage at a high value, settled through the favor system); partial Honor recovery; and formal closure of the incident (no ongoing topic or disposition drift). For NPC captors, the choice is personality-driven. Most captors default to destroy (Yu, Chugi, Meiyo all score destruction highly --- honorable resolution, no lingering political complication). Seigyo, Kanpeki, and Kyoryoku captors may score retention highly if they judge the political cost acceptable. Makoto and Jin captors may lean toward return (gracious closure, restoration of right relations). For player captors, the three options are presented as a deliberate choice at the moment of capture, with consequences laid out; the player chooses. This rule applies only to signature ships. Non-signature ships (standard Kobune, Sengokobune not tied to a specific clan identity, Merchant Barge, Sampan) follow the normal prize rules above --- claimed at half construction cost, operated normally by whoever has the skill, no special consequences. The Koutetsukan is immune to boarding actions --- its fully enclosed iron hull prevents grappling. It can only be engaged through ramming or ranged fire. Player Resolution Path: Naval combat follows the same Section 9 design principle as land battles. The Army View resolves ship-to-ship combat at Company level using the ship stats above. NPC naval engagements resolve entirely through engine dice rolls. When a boarding action involves a player character’s ship, a Ship Boarding ASCII template generates --- the player fights on the deck while the Army View resolves the broader engagement. This is the naval equivalent of the Battlefield Bubble. The Army View state feeds the boarding map: if the player’s fleet is winning, enemy boarders are fewer and less aggressive. If losing, the player’s ship may face multiple boarding attempts. Ship Boarding ASCII template: LOCKED (Section 56.18).

### Weather at Sea — UNLOCKED v381

Weather is the naval equivalent of terrain. Weather is a unified sub-tile-level world state determined once per IC day (Section 56.6.2) — naval combat reads the current weather from the sub-tile where the engagement takes place. Weather does not change mid-battle except through the Shift the Storm Naval Heroic Opportunity (Section 11.7 Category 9). Weather applies global modifiers to all ships in the engagement, plus ship-type-specific modifiers on top. This mirrors how land terrain applies a global modifier plus unit-type-specific penalties. Weather does not affect routine travel — ships move at their listed rate regardless of conditions (Section 57.43.7). Weather matters only during naval combat.

Global modifiers (apply to all ships, both sides): Clear — no modifier. Wind — no modifier. Rain (rough seas) — all ships −1 Attack; Kobune back-row ranged support degraded to 1d3 + Attack. Storm — all ships −2 Attack; Kobune back-row ranged support cannot fire. Typhoon — all ships −3 Attack, −2 Defense; Kobune back-row ranged support cannot fire.

Ship-type-specific modifiers (on top of global): Flat-bottomed vessels (Kobune, Sampan, Merchant Barge) — additional −1 Defense in Storm, additional −2 Defense in Typhoon. Koutetsukan — additional −1 Attack in Storm and Typhoon (top-heavy iron hull). Sengokobune and Atakebune — no additional penalties (designed for heavy weather). Tortoise Oceangoing Vessel — no additional penalties (gaijin keel design).

Weather determination: weather is determined once per IC day per sub-tile. Roll 1d100 and cross-reference with the current season. This is the unified weather determination — the same roll governs both land ASCII maps (Section 56.6.2) and naval Army View engagements on that sub-tile. Five unified weather states exist: Clear, Wind, Rain, Storm, Typhoon. Each system reads the state through its own modifier table. Province biome converts certain states for land contexts (Rain → Snow in winter highland, Storm → Blizzard in winter highland; see Section 56.6.2 biome conversions). Coastal and ocean sub-tiles use the base table without biome conversion. Typhoon is restricted to coastal and ocean sub-tiles — it does not fire on inland sub-tiles. Spring: Clear 1–40, Wind 41–75, Rain 76–95, Storm 96–100, Typhoon impossible. Summer: Clear 1–45, Wind 46–80, Rain 81–95, Storm 96–100, Typhoon impossible. Autumn: Clear 1–20, Wind 21–45, Rain 46–80, Storm 81–95, Typhoon 96–100. Winter: Clear 1–15, Wind 16–35, Rain 36–70, Storm 71–95, Typhoon 96–100. Spring and Summer are 75–80% Clear or Wind — most days are fair weather. Autumn and Winter push toward Rain and Storm — weather becomes a meaningful tactical consideration. Typhoon only fires in Autumn and Winter at 5% — rare enough to feel dramatic, impossible in calmer seasons. At sea, weather can only change mid-battle through the Shift the Storm Naval Heroic Opportunity (Section 11.7 Category 9), which is available only to Yoritomo Shugenja or Storm Rider school characters. ### River Combat — LOCKED

Rivers constrain naval combat. Only Kobune, Sampan, and Merchant Barge can operate on rivers — Sengokobune, Koutetsukan, and Atakebune are too large. Maximum 2 ships abreast on a standard river, 3 on a major river. Flanking is impossible — ships can only engage front-to-front or front-to-side. Shore-based attacks are possible — archers and shugenja on the riverbank can fire on ships passing within range (shore-to-ship attack uses normal ranged attack rules, ship-to-shore is −2 Attack due to unstable platform). River current: the downstream ship gains +1 Attack (current momentum advantage); the upstream ship suffers −1 Attack (fighting against current). A ship that runs aground (Navigation failure on a shallow river) is immobilized until freed (crew Simple Action, Strength TN 15).

### Lore Notes — LOCKED

Ship construction: cedar planks, flat copper nails, cypress mast, fire-bent pine deck planks. Once introduced to water, a Rokugani vessel can never return to land — if removed from water the wood dries, shrinks, and the vessel falls apart.

Launching ceremony: a shugenja blesses the ship and dock, invoking Suitengu (Fortune of the Sea). Colorful flags are hung from the masts. A ginsei-ono (small silver-bladed axe) ceremony cuts the mooring rope. Rice is scattered overboard as offering to Isora (Fortune of the Seashore).

Ship naming convention: two-word names combining an honored ancestor's name with an admirable quality, or a natural phenomenon name (the Silent Bell, the Fortune’s Coin, the Deathless). Renaming a ship after it has left shore is considered severely bad luck.

Sailor life: sailors almost never wear armor at sea (salt air ruins it; impedes swimming if overboard). Preferred weapons are small and multi-use: kama and masakari. Diet is pre-cooked rice balls wrapped in dried seaweed, pickled vegetables, dried seaweed, and fish caught from trailing nets.

The Tortoise Fleet: the Minor Clan Tortoise has had covert Imperial permission to travel to gaijin lands since their founding. Their ocean-going vessels incorporate gaijin design (keel, fore-and-aft rig, multiple sail shapes) and are functionally gaijin ships. This knowledge is kept strictly secret from the rest of the Empire.
