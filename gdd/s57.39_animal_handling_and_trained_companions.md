## 57.39 Animal Handling and Trained Companions — LOCKED

### 57.39.1 Design Intent

The Animal Handling skill (Section 24.3) lets samurai and their retainers train animals — dogs, horses, falcons, and rarer creatures like warcats or war dogs — to serve them. This section implements the skill in the persistent world. Trained animals exist as lightweight companion entities that belong to a specific character, accompany them across the World Map, appear on ASCII maps as tile entities, and can fight, be wounded, and die. They are distinct from inventory items (swords, koku, scrolls) and from NPCs (full character-sheet social actors). They are a third thing: persistent living creatures bonded to their trainer. The system explicitly has no economic layer — trained animals are not sold as a trade. A character who wants a trained animal trains it themselves or is gifted one by another trainer. The companion entity persists for its natural lifespan or until killed in combat.

The skill’s three Mastery Abilities (Rank 3, Rank 5, Rank 7 per Section 24.3) are available to any character who reaches the rank, regardless of school. School techniques that grant animal packs or prides — Matsu Beastmaster warcats, Unicorn Wardog Master war-dog packs, Toritaka Bushi Falcon’s Eyes perception, Aerie Falconer spirit-falcon attack commands, Utaku Horse Master cross-skill bonuses — operate on top of the baseline skill. A Matsu Beastmaster with Animal Handling 5 may both command their warcat pride (school technique) AND maintain general companion animals (skill ability). A Scorpion courtier with Animal Handling 5 may command their one trained dog to attack but cannot have a pride of warcats. The two layers are additive and distinct: skill gives you the general capability, school gives you the specialist capacity.

### 57.39.2 Trained Animal as Companion Entity

Trained animals are companion entities, not inventory items. A companion is a persistent game-world record tied to exactly one owner character (the trainer). The companion record persists across sessions, survives the character logging out, and continues to exist in the world until the animal dies. Companions travel with their owner automatically — when the owner moves between provinces via BEGIN_TRAVEL (Section 4.4), the companion moves with them. When the owner enters an ASCII mission (hunt, combat, quest), their companions appear alongside them on the map as tile entities. Companions are not NPCs in the full sense: they have no dispositions toward other characters, no Honor or Glory or Status, no NeedTypes, no topics in their own name, and no ability to initiate social actions. They are living entities that follow their trainer and act on command.

Ownership is exclusive. A companion belongs to exactly one character at a time. Transfer of ownership can occur in two ways: voluntary gift (the trainer explicitly transfers the companion to another character) and death-of-owner (the companion transitions to the owner’s heir per standard inheritance rules, or becomes a free-roaming wild animal if there is no heir). On transfer, the companion’s training_progress and fully_trained fields are preserved — the animal retains what it was taught. What does NOT transfer is the bond: Mastery Abilities depend on the current owner’s Animal Handling rank, not on the animal’s training history. A fully trained war dog transferred to an owner with Animal Handling 3 retains its training but cannot be commanded to attack, because the new owner lacks Rank 5. A fully trained falcon transferred to an owner with Animal Handling 6 can be commanded to attack (Rank 5), but does not benefit from the Rank 7 no-flee override (the original trainer’s deeper bond does not transfer).

Rebonding: on ownership transfer to a character who has Animal Handling rank sufficient to theoretically command the companion (rank 5+ for commanding, rank 7+ for no-flee), the new owner must establish their own working bond before the Mastery Abilities function at full reliability. Rebonding requires 3 TRAIN_ANIMAL sessions between the new owner and the transferred companion. These sessions do not modify training_progress (the animal is already trained); they establish the owner-animal bond. During the rebonding window (after transfer but before 3 sessions complete), the animal follows and obeys basic presence but does not reliably respond to combat commands — it may comply on roll success but not automatically. After 3 rebonding sessions, the companion is fully bonded to the new owner and the Mastery Abilities function normally under their rank. Rebonding is tracked by a transient field rebond_sessions_remaining on the companion record, set to 3 on transfer and decremented to 0 through TRAIN_ANIMAL sessions.

### 57.39.3 Companion Fields

Each companion entity is a Dictionary with the following fields. companion_id: integer, unique identifier assigned at creation. name: string, the name the trainer has given the animal. species: enum — DOG, PIGEON, RIDING_HORSE, FALCON, WAR_DOG, WARHORSE, WARCAT. owner_id: integer, the character_id of the trainer; exactly one at a time. wound_total: integer, current wounds taken. wound_threshold: integer, derived from species (per Section 57.39.6 table). wound_rank: integer, derived from wound_total and threshold per standard creature wound rules (Section 54). training_progress: integer, accumulated progress points from TRAIN_ANIMAL sessions. training_threshold: integer, total points needed to complete training per Section 57.39.6 table. fully_trained: boolean, true once training_progress ≥ training_threshold. acquired_date_ic_day: integer, IC day when the TRAIN_ANIMAL track was begun. acquired_province_id: integer, the province_id where the animal was acquired. location_province_id: integer, current province — tracks with owner’s location automatically. is_alive: boolean, false on death.

An untrained animal that is in the process of being trained still has a companion record — fully_trained is false, and the Mastery Abilities (commanding to attack, no-flee behaviour) do not function on it until training_progress reaches training_threshold. The companion can still follow the trainer, appear on ASCII maps, and take wounds while in training, but it cannot be commanded into combat. The specific behaviour of a companion in training depends on how far along the training is, following a three-tier rule: Tier 1, “wild” — before the third TRAIN_ANIMAL session has occurred, the animal is effectively still wild. It will flee the scene of any combat (weapons drawn, beast present, hostile action). It cannot be commanded to do anything and remains with the trainer only out of proximity and feeding, not bond. Tier 2, “following” — from the third TRAIN_ANIMAL session onward, but before training completion, the animal is partially bonded. It stays near the trainer during combat rather than fleeing outright, but takes no combat action itself. It flees if wounded at any rank. It cannot be commanded to attack but will not abandon its trainer. Tier 3, “trained” — after training_progress reaches training_threshold (fully_trained true), the full Mastery Ability rules apply per the trainer’s rank. This three-tier progression gives the player-character or NPC meaningful behaviour from an animal partway through training: a dog in session 2 will flee any fight, a dog in session 10 will stay loyal but not engage, a dog fully trained can be sent to attack.

### 57.39.4 Companion Cap by Skill Rank

A character can maintain a number of trained companions that scales with their Animal Handling skill rank. The cap counts fully-trained companions and companions currently in training toward the same limit — a character cannot start training a fourth animal if they already have three. Cap ladder: Animal Handling 1–2 — 1 companion. Animal Handling 3–4 — 2 companions. Animal Handling 5–6 — 3 companions. Animal Handling 7 or higher — 4 companions. The cap is a total across all species — a character may have any mix of species up to their cap (e.g., at rank 5, one dog, one horse, and one falcon simultaneously). If a character’s skill rank increases, their cap increases immediately; if a character’s skill rank decreases (extremely rare in practice), the cap decreases and excess companions must be released or transferred.

School techniques that grant additional animals (Matsu Beastmaster pride, Unicorn Wardog Master pack) are separate from this cap. A Matsu Beastmaster with Animal Handling 3 has their school-granted pride (up to 3 warcats per school progression, per the Matsu Beastmaster technique entries in Section 29) AND a general-skill cap of 2 additional companions of any species. School-granted animals are recorded in the character sheet under the relevant school record field and do not count against the skill cap.

### 57.39.5 TRAIN_ANIMAL ActionID

TRAIN_ANIMAL is the action a character fires to either begin training a new animal or advance the training of an existing companion. Category 11 (Personal Development), alongside TRAIN, MENTOR, TREAT_WOUND, and the artisan actions.

**AP cost:** 1. Each training session consumes 1 AP (one Tick worth of focused effort in the Time System of Section 13). Training progresses over multiple sessions until the companion’s training_progress reaches the training_threshold.

**Roll:** Animal Handling + Awareness, keeping Animal Handling, against the species-specific TN in Section 57.39.6. On a successful roll, progress gained equals (result − TN) + 1, added to the companion’s training_progress. On a failed roll (result below TN), no progress is gained (the session was wasted but does not backslide). Progress cannot go below zero. Rank progression rolls follow the Section 24.0 skill system normally.

**Contexts:** AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, VISITING, AT_COURT. Training requires stable co-location — not valid in TRAVELING, ON_CAMPAIGN, or UNDER_SIEGE contexts (an animal cannot be trained while the trainer is crossing provinces or in active warfare).

**First-session preconditions (acquiring the animal):** The first TRAIN_ANIMAL session for a new animal has additional preconditions. The trainer must be AT_ZONE in a province where the target species is naturally present (per the terrain table in Section 57.39.6). The trainer must not already be at their companion cap. The first session creates the companion record with fully_trained false and training_progress set to the result of the first roll (or 0 on failure). From the second session onward, only the general context and companion-existence preconditions apply.

**Subsequent-session preconditions:** For non-first sessions, the trainer and the companion must be co-located (same settlement or zone). The companion must still be in training (fully_trained false). Once fully_trained is true, TRAIN_ANIMAL is no longer valid on that companion — training is complete.

**Completion:** When training_progress reaches training_threshold, fully_trained is set to true. The companion is now a trained animal with access to the Rank 5 command-to-attack Mastery Ability (if the trainer has the rank) and the Rank 7 no-flee override (if the trainer has the rank). No ceremony or additional action is required — the transition is automatic at session completion.

### 57.39.6 Training Progression Table

Each species has a training TN (the TN of the Animal Handling + Awareness roll per session), a training_threshold (total progress needed to complete), a wound_threshold (per-rank wound track per Section 54 creature rules), and terrain availability (the provinces where the species can be initially acquired).

**DOG:** training TN 10, training_threshold 20, wound_threshold 5, terrain any rural province (Plains, Forest, Hills, Mountain, Coastal) — excludes Urban and Shadowlands. The most common and accessible companion.

**PIGEON:** training TN 10, training_threshold 20, wound_threshold 2, terrain any settled province. Trained for delivery of short messages between fixed locations (detailed use in Section 12.7 letter system remains unchanged — pigeons do not replace the standard letter system, they are a character flavour layer for visual texture).

**RIDING_HORSE:** training TN 15, training_threshold 40, wound_threshold 10, terrain Plains provinces or any Unicorn Clan province. Horse is a common samurai companion even outside Unicorn territory, but acquiring an untrained horse for training requires access to horse-breeding regions.

**FALCON:** training TN 15, training_threshold 40, wound_threshold 3, terrain Mountain or Hills provinces, or any Crane/Crab/Dragon homeland province. Falcons are high-effort companions requiring patience; takagari tradition is culturally Crane-associated but the species is available elsewhere.

**WAR_DOG:** training TN 20, training_threshold 60, wound_threshold 8, terrain any Unicorn Clan province only. The Unicorn war-dog breed is a gaijin import and the breeding stock exists only in Unicorn lands. A non-Unicorn character may train a war dog only if they travel to a Unicorn province to acquire one — a realistic gate that matches the breed’s in-fiction provenance.

**WARHORSE:** training TN 20, training_threshold 60, wound_threshold 12, terrain Plains provinces or any Unicorn Clan province. Warhorse training goes beyond riding — the animal is conditioned to charge, stand under arrow fire, and remain steady in melee chaos. A riding horse cannot be “upgraded” to a warhorse through additional TRAIN_ANIMAL sessions — warhorse training is a separate track from initial acquisition; a character must choose WARHORSE as the target species from the first session.

**WARCAT:** training TN 25, training_threshold 100, wound_threshold 15, terrain Lion Clan provinces only. Warcat training through the general Animal Handling skill is technically possible but effectively prohibitive for non-Matsu Beastmasters — the investment is vast, and a Matsu Beastmaster achieves the same result via their school technique (Section 29). A determined non-Matsu with Animal Handling 5+, Awareness 4+, and extended access to Lion lands could in principle train a single warcat companion, but this is rare in practice and carries political complexity — the Matsu regard warcat bonding as their ancestral prerogative.

Training times scale with animal difficulty. A competent trainer (Animal Handling 3, Awareness 3) produces a trained dog in roughly 3 sessions, a trained falcon or riding horse in roughly 10–15 sessions, a war dog or warhorse in 20–30 sessions, and a warcat in 50–80 sessions. Session counts translate directly to AP and therefore to IC days — a focused trainer firing TRAIN_ANIMAL daily produces a dog in days, a warhorse in weeks, a warcat in months.

### 57.39.7 Commanding Trained Animals in Combat — Rank 5 Mastery

Per Section 24.3 Rank 5 mastery, a character with Animal Handling 5 or higher may command trained companions to attack a target of their choosing. The command requires the trainer to be co-located with the companion in the same scene (World Map engagement, ASCII mission, court incident — anywhere both entities are present) and to take a Simple Action to issue the command. The target of the attack must be identifiable to the companion (visible, at close or medium range, distinct from the trainer’s allies).

Once commanded, the companion engages the target using its species stat block (Section 54 creature entries). In ASCII map combat, the companion is an engine-controlled ally unit that attacks the designated target on its turn, takes damage from retaliation normally, and rolls initiative per standard combat rules. On the World Map at the strategic layer, a commanded companion contributes a small combat modifier to the owner’s immediate action but does not function as an independent actor. The companion cannot target the trainer’s allies; if the designated target is itself an ally (the trainer is confused or deliberately misusing the command), the companion refuses to engage and retreats to a neutral position.

By default (Rank 5 mastery, without Rank 7), a commanded companion that takes significant wounds — reaching Hurt wound rank — breaks off combat and flees to a safe position regardless of further commands. The trainer may attempt to re-command the animal once it has regained composure (after the current engagement resolves and the animal returns to its pre-combat baseline). This default behaviour reflects realistic animal psychology: a wounded animal prioritises survival over obedience.

### 57.39.8 The "Never Flees" Rank 7 Mastery

Per Section 24.3 Rank 7 mastery, animals trained by a character with Animal Handling 7 or higher will not flee from combat even when badly wounded. The Rank 5 default flee-at-Hurt behaviour is overridden. A commanded companion continues to engage the designated target until the target is dead, the companion itself dies, or the trainer issues a new command (including a withdrawal command). This represents the deepest bond between trainer and animal — the animal chooses loyalty over survival.

The no-flee override is a property of the trainer, not of the companion itself — if a trained companion is transferred to a new owner with lower Animal Handling rank, the override no longer applies. The animal reverts to standard wounded-flee behaviour under the new owner (or under no owner). This matters for the voluntary-gift case: a Rank 7 trainer gifting their companion to a Rank 3 owner gives up the no-flee property. The new owner has the animal but not the bond that produced the behaviour. The companion was trained by someone deeper; it no longer has that trainer’s presence.

### 57.39.9 Companion Death, Injury, and Healing

Companions take wounds during combat following the standard creature wound track rules (Section 54). Each species has its own wound_threshold per Section 57.39.6. A companion at 0 wounds is Healthy; at 1 to wound_threshold wounds it is Wounded; beyond that it progresses through standard wound ranks up to Out and Dead. Combat damage is applied per the beast’s stat block vs the companion’s Armor TN (default 15 for most trained animals; warcats and warhorses have higher values per their Section 54 entries).

Healing a wounded companion uses the same mechanisms as healing a wounded character. A character with Medicine (Wound Treatment) skill may use the TREAT_WOUND ActionID (Section 57.31) on a wounded companion, consuming a Medicine Kit charge and rolling as normal. Natural healing through rest proceeds on the same Stamina-driven schedule as for characters, using the companion’s own Stamina value (per Section 54 stat block). Shugenja healing spells that affect “a person” generally extend to trained animals at the caster’s discretion, following the same per-spell rules as in Section 54.

Companion death produces a Tier 4 Personal topic for the owner: “[Owner] lost their [species name] [companion name].” No Glory or Honor change attaches to the owner — losing a trained animal is a personal loss, not a reputation event. The companion_id is retired and the companion record is archived (is_alive set to false, not deleted). The owner’s cap recovers the slot immediately and may begin training a replacement if they wish. Time between companion death and a new training start is flavour, not mechanic — the system does not impose a mourning period.

### 57.39.10 School Techniques Versus Skill Abilities

The two layers of animal mastery operate independently and additively. The skill layer — Animal Handling with its Rank 3, 5, and 7 Mastery Abilities — is available to anyone who invests in the skill. The school layer — specific techniques that grant animal packs, prides, species-specific bonuses, or direct non-verbal commands — is restricted to characters in schools that teach those techniques. A character may access either layer, both layers, or neither.

**Skill-only examples:** A Bayushi courtier with Animal Handling 5 may train a dog and command it to attack. They have no school technique for animal work, but the skill alone gives them the capability. A Hida bushi with Animal Handling 3 may train a horse for riding or a dog for hunting. An Ichiro scout may train a tracking dog.

**School-only examples:** A Matsu Beastmaster has their school-granted pride of warcats per the Matsu Beastmaster technique (Section 29). These warcats are NOT companions under the Section 57.39 skill system — they are school assets with their own rules (pride mechanics, Swift, Fear, pride growth at higher ranks). The Matsu Beastmaster gains the pride by taking the school path, not by raising Animal Handling. A Unicorn Wardog Master has their school-granted pack of war-dogs (pack size = Awareness). These are also NOT Section 57.39 companions.

**Both layers together:** A Matsu Beastmaster with Animal Handling 5 has BOTH their school pride (warcats) AND up to 3 additional companions of any species under Section 57.39. The two systems do not interact mechanically — the pride operates under the Matsu Beastmaster technique rules, the companions operate under Section 57.39. A Matsu Beastmaster who wishes to add a tracking dog to their household may train one as a Section 57.39 companion, alongside their pride of warcats.

**Utaku Horse Master cross-skill bonus:** The Utaku Horse Master school technique (Master of the Open Plains, Section 29) grants +2k0 to all Animal Handling, Horsemanship, and Hunting rolls. This bonus applies to TRAIN_ANIMAL rolls, effectively giving the Utaku a large advantage in companion training. An Utaku Horse Master with Animal Handling 4, Awareness 3, and the school bonus rolls 6k4+3 (instead of 4k4+3) on TRAIN_ANIMAL sessions — significantly faster training progress, especially for warhorses.

### 57.39.11 NPC Decision Engine Integration

NPC characters may train and maintain companions through the TRAIN_ANIMAL ActionID as part of standard engine behaviour. Two patterns apply: school-driven training, and personal-lean training.

**School-driven training:** NPCs in schools with explicit animal techniques (Matsu Beastmaster, Unicorn Wardog Master) have a standing need to maintain their school assets. The Matsu Beastmaster’s pride and the Unicorn Wardog Master’s pack are bookkeeping handled by the school technique, not by TRAIN_ANIMAL — these NPCs do not need to fire TRAIN_ANIMAL to have their school animals. However, if they wish to acquire additional Section 57.39 companions beyond their school assets, they follow the standard pattern described below. Three other schools have companion expectations wired as standing ImmediateNeeds: Toritaka Bushi (trained falcon, priority 2), Aerie Falconer (trained falcon, priority 2), and Utaku Horse Master (trained riding horse or warhorse, character’s choice at creation, priority 2). These three schools all have Animal Handling either as a starting skill, a prerequisite, or a direct school-bonus specialty. Other wilderness-adjacent schools (Kitsune Ranger, Hiruma Scout, Ichiro) may fire TRAIN_ANIMAL through the standard personal-lean pattern described below, but do not have a hard standing need for a companion wired at the school level. At each AP tick, the engine checks NPCs in the three listed schools: if they lack a companion of their school’s expected species and have an open companion slot and AP available, TRAIN_ANIMAL fires at priority 2. Once they have the expected companion fully trained, the standing need clears. If the companion dies, the need re-fires 1–2 IC seasons later (the NPC grieves before seeking a replacement).

**Personal-lean training:** Non-school NPCs do not spontaneously decide to train animals as a background goal — the engine does not fire TRAIN_ANIMAL as a default standing need. TRAIN_ANIMAL fires for non-school NPCs under one of three conditions: (1) the NPC’s character sheet carries an explicit animal-trainer lean (a designer-set or world-state-generated flag indicating this character cares about animals); (2) the NPC is at AT_OWN_HOLDINGS with AP to spare, Animal Handling rank 2+, and a current companion slot open — a light background activity when idle; (3) the NPC has lost a companion to death and the engine fires a replacement need within 1–2 IC seasons.

**Objective alignment:** TRAIN_ANIMAL scores under the following NeedTypes: TRAIN_SKILL 70 (training an animal is a form of skill-practice; the character is exercising Animal Handling), REST 15 (as a quiet at-home activity it ranks above idle but well below explicit rest). It is not scored under combat or political NeedTypes — training is a long-term investment, not a tactical tool. The TRAIN_SKILL alignment is the primary entry point: a character with Animal Handling as their leaning skill may choose TRAIN_ANIMAL over generic TRAIN when they have a companion in progress.

**Personality filter:** School-type leans apply (Annex C). Matsu, Shinjo, Moto, Utaku, Hiruma, Toritaka, Ichiro, Kitsune, and other wilderness-adjacent schools receive a +10 lean on TRAIN_ANIMAL. Pure courtier schools (Otomo, Doji Courtier, Soshi) receive a −10 lean — they are less likely to personally train animals even if they theoretically have the skill rank. Shugenja leans are neutral except for Kitsune (animal affinity: +10) and Isawa Elementalist (abstract focus: −5).

**Skill mapping (Annex D):** action_id TRAIN_ANIMAL; primary_skill Animal Handling; primary_emphasis varies by species (Dogs, Horses, Falcons, War Dogs, Warcats); primary_trait Awareness; base_tn varies by species per Section 57.39.6 table; ap_cost 1; contexts AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, VISITING, AT_COURT; requires_target true (the companion record, or a new target species for a first session); requires_consumable none; validator_rules trainer has Animal Handling rank 1+ AND trainer below companion cap AND (has companion in progress OR has an available training slot), co-located with companion or (for first session) in terrain-available province; hostile false; honor_cost 0; glory_cost 0; output increments training_progress or creates new companion record. Note on TRAIN_SKILL alignment (Annex A, scored at 70): the validator_rules above gate this action properly — a character with no Animal Handling skill, at their companion cap, or without an open training task cannot fire TRAIN_ANIMAL even if TRAIN_SKILL selects it as a candidate. This prevents the engine from over-producing trained animals in the world.
