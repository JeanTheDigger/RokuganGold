## 12.10a Favor Dispute Witness Disposition — LOCKED

This section formalizes the witness disposition gain when a creditor wins a contested
favor dispute. GDD s12.10 line 47 specifies that witnesses gain disposition toward the
creditor but gives no numeric value.

---

### Source Derivation

**GDD text (s12.10):**
> "The roll is public if in a court setting, private if in a personal visit. Witnesses
> to a creditor winning a contested favor dispute gain disposition toward the creditor."

**Calibration anchors:**

| Event | Witness gain/loss | Source |
|-------|-------------------|--------|
| PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE success per witness | +2 | s12.4 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Public Debate "slight" per witness | +1 | s15.4 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Daily conversation | +1 | s12.6 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Witnessing a favor break | −5 | s12.10 (GDD-confirmed) |

**Design principle:**

Witnessing a creditor win a dispute is a secondary social event — the witness observes
someone asserting a legitimate claim and being vindicated. It is not a primary social
action directed at the witness. Secondary witness gains sit at the low end of the
positive scale (+1 to +2).

The break penalty (−5) is asymmetrically larger because social contract violations are
more memorable and threatening to community trust than someone correctly enforcing
their rights.

**Debtor-winning path:** GDD is silent. A debtor successfully contesting an overreach
is a private legal resolution. No witness disposition gain applies.

---

### Value — LOCKED

| Outcome | Witness disposition toward creditor | Rationale |
|---------|-------------------------------------|-----------|
| Creditor wins | +2 | Secondary social vindication. Matches PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE per-witness gain. Notable but not primary. |
| Debtor wins | 0 | GDD silent. No admiration event — a technicality resolved, not a public honor display. |
