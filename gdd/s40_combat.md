# 40. Combat

**DEFINITION OF TERMS:**

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Law ****&**** Order):** The iaijutsu duel serves as a formal legal resolution mechanic in Rokugan — any unresolvable legal dispute can be settled by steel. See Section 2.8.2 for the lore of iaijutsu as legal resolution, and Section 2.8.11 for the rules and consequences of illegal (unsanctioned) duels. When the magistrate system is fully designed, the duel-as-verdict mechanic should be integrated here.

- Action — What a character does when it is their Turn during a combat Round.

- Initiative — A measure of how quickly a given character reacts compared to other participants in a skirmish.

- Round — A unit of time, 3–10 seconds in length, during which all participants in a skirmish have an opportunity to take Actions.

- Turn — The opportunity for an individual character to take Actions during a combat Round. Under normal circumstances, every participant has one Turn per Round, which takes place on their Initiative.

**SEQUENCE OF EVENTS:**

**Stage 1: Initiative**

During the first Round of a skirmish, all participants make an Initiative Roll: Insight Rank / Reflexes (roll Reflexes + Insight Rank, keep Reflexes). The result is the Initiative Score, which determines action order for the entire skirmish. The Initiative Roll is made only once; the score persists unless modified by abilities or situational effects. Characters also select their Stance immediately before making their Initiative Roll on the first Round.

**Stage 2: Turns**

The character with the highest Initiative Score acts first. A character may delay their Turn, allowing the next-highest character to act. The delaying character then has the chance to act or delay again. Turns cannot be saved from one Round to the next. If every participant delays, the lowest-Initiative character must eventually act. After the first Round, characters may change their Stance at the beginning of their Turn before taking Actions. A character has only one Turn per Round regardless of how many Actions they take.

**Stage 3: Reactions**

At the end of each Round, after all Turns have been taken, certain abilities take effect as a result of the Round’s events (e.g. expired spell effects end here). All such effects happen simultaneously during Stage 3. Reaction effects generated by Techniques, Spells, or Advantages are always clearly labeled as such.

**ATTACK ****&**** DEFENSE:**

All attacks require a roll using a Weapon Skill. The result is compared to the target’s Armor TN.

- Armor TN = (Reflexes × 5) + 5 + bonuses from Stance + bonuses from armor.

- If the attack roll meets or exceeds the target’s Armor TN, the attack succeeds and damage is rolled.

- Ranged attacks against a target in melee range suffer -10 to the attack roll total.

- Damage Rating (DR): Every weapon has a DR. For melee attacks, add Strength to the first number of the DR (e.g. katana DR 3k2 with Strength 3 = roll 6k2 for damage). Some ranged attacks also add Strength — see Equipment.

**WOUNDS:**

Characters have Wound Ranks. Each Rank holds a maximum number of Wounds equal to Earth Ring × 2. Damage fills Wound Ranks in order. As soon as damage begins filling a Rank, the character suffers that Rank’s effects.

- Healthy — Default condition. No penalties. (Earth ×2 Wounds)

- Nicked — +3 TN to all rolls. A mild but distracting injury.

- Grazed — +5 TN to all rolls.

- Hurt — +10 TN to all rolls.

- Injured — +15 TN to all rolls.

- Crippled — +20 TN to all rolls. Any attempt to make a Move Action is increased one level of difficulty (Free → Simple, etc.).

- Down — +40 TN to all rolls. May speak only in a whisper. May only potentially take Free Actions (no Move Actions), and must spend a Void Point to do so.

- Out — Immobile, unconscious, and likely dying. When this Rank is filled, any additional damage kills the character instantly.

**STANCES:**

Assumed during Stage 1 (first Round) or at the start of a character’s Turn in Stage 2 (subsequent Rounds).

**Attack Stance (Water)**

The standard combat stance. No restrictions on Actions.

**Full Attack Stance (Fire)**

+2k1 to all attack rolls. Armor TN reduced by 10. May only take Actions to attack; Move Actions can only move toward enemies. Cannot make ranged attacks. Moving grants +5' beyond normal for that Move Action (once per Round; still cannot exceed the normal maximum). Cannot be used while mounted.

**Defense Stance (Air)**

Add Air Ring + Defense Skill Rank to Armor TN. No restrictions on Actions except may not attack. Ideal for performing Skill Rolls or Spell Casting Rolls during a skirmish while remaining ready to defend.

**Full Defense Stance (Earth)**

Upon declaring this Stance, make a Defense/Reflexes roll and add half the total (rounded up) to Armor TN until the following Turn. This roll is a Complex Action — the character may only take Free Actions while in this Stance.

**Center Stance (Void)**

Forfeit all Actions while in this Stance. On the following Round, gain +1k1 + Void Ring on any one roll made during the Turn. Also add +10 to the Initiative Score for that Round only. Primarily useful for iaijutsu dueling.

**ACTIONS:**

Each Turn, a character may take one of two options:

- One Complex Action + Free Actions

- Two Simple Actions + Free Actions

- Free Actions: Minor activities that do not disrupt other tasks. A character may perform each Free Action a maximum of once per Round unless otherwise specified.

- Simple Actions: More involved tasks requiring attention, but a character may take two per Turn.

- Complex Actions: Elaborate or time-consuming efforts requiring the character’s full attention.

**MOVE ACTIONS:**

- Free Move Action: Water Ring ×5 feet.

- Simple Move Action: Water Ring ×10 feet.

- Maximum movement per Round: Water Ring ×20 feet (unless a special ability increases this).

Terrain Types:

- Basic (city streets, plains, sparse forest): No movement penalties.

- Moderate (tall grass, foothills, beaches): Water Ring considered 1 Rank lower (minimum 1) for Move Action distance.

- Difficult (mountains, dense forest, hip-deep water): Water Ring considered 2 Ranks lower (minimum 1) for Move Action distance. GM may optionally impose -5 to -10 penalty on physical rolls in Difficult terrain.

- Athletics Mastery interaction: Athletics Rank 3 reduces Moderate Terrain to Basic and reduces Difficult Terrain’s Water Ring penalty from −2 Ranks to −1 Rank. Athletics Rank 5 removes all terrain movement penalties entirely. See Athletics, Section 24.2.

**MANEUVERS:**

Maneuvers are specialized attacks requiring Raises to perform. They are not an exhaustive list — GMs and players may improvise others using these as guidelines.

**Called Shot (Variable Raises)**

Targets a specific body part. Without a Called Shot it is assumed the torso is struck. With Raises: 1 Raise = specific limb; 2 Raises = hand or foot; 3 Raises = head; 4 Raises = eye, ear, finger, or similarly small part. No universal mechanical effect, but GM may rule that sufficient damage severs or destroys the targeted part.

**Disarm (3 Raises)**

Inflicts only 2k1 damage regardless of weapon used (Strength is not added to the damage dice). On a hit, both characters make a Contested Strength Roll. If the attacker wins, the target drops the weapon. Wooden-component weapons targeted by a steel blade may be broken (GM’s discretion). Recovering a dropped weapon is a Simple Action.

**Extra Attack (5 Raises)**

Make 5 successful Raises on the first attack roll. These Raises confer no other benefits. If successful, immediately make a second attack roll after the first is resolved (including damage). The initial attack must succeed; the second may miss without negative effects. May only be used once per Turn.

**Feint (2 Raises)**

Add half the amount by which the attack roll exceeded the target’s Armor TN (accounting for the 2 Raises) to the damage roll. Maximum bonus = 5 × Insight Rank.

**Guard (0 Raises)**

Simple Action. Not available in Full Attack Stance. Designate one person within 5 feet. Until the next Turn, whenever that person is within 5 feet: their Armor TN is increased by 10 and the guarding character’s Armor TN is decreased by 5.

**Increased Damage (1+ Raises)**

Each Raise adds +1k0 to the damage roll. Multiple Raises may be called. All Raises made in one Round for this purpose count as one effect for mechanics that reduce Raise requirements.

**Knockdown (2 Raises for bipeds, 4 Raises for quadrupeds)**

Targets the legs. Inflicts normal damage and forces a Contested Strength Roll. If the attacker wins, the target is knocked Prone.

**IAIJUTSU DUELING:**

The formal means of conflict resolution between samurai. Most often to first blood; can be to the death for grievous matters. Both duelists are in Center Stance throughout and may only take the actions outlined below.

**Stage 1 — Assessment**

Both characters enter Assessment on the Initiative Turn of the faster duelist. Each makes an Iaijutsu (Assessment)/Awareness roll (TN = 10 + opponent’s Insight Rank ×5). On success, learn one of the following (+ one more per Raise): opponent’s Void, Reflexes, Iaijutsu Skill, Iaijutsu Emphases, current Void Points, or current Wound Level. If one duelist’s roll exceeds the other’s by 10+, the winner gains +1k1 on the subsequent Focus roll. Either duelist may concede defeat after Assessment.

**Stage 2 — Focus**

Both duelists make a Contested Iaijutsu (Focus)/Void Roll. If one beats the other by 5 or more, that duelist earns the right to strike first, gaining one Free Raise toward the strike roll per additional increment of 5 by which they won. If neither beats the other by at least 5, a simultaneous kharmic strike occurs.

**Stage 3 — Strike**

On the third Round, on the Initiative Turn of the slower duelist. The first-striker makes an Iaijutsu/Reflexes attack roll against the opponent’s normal Armor TN (Free Raises from Focus apply). The attack is resolved normally including Wounds. The second duelist may then strike if still alive. In a duel to first blood: striking after first blood is considered extremely dishonorable. In a kharmic strike: both attack simultaneously — the cause of the duel is considered dropped, neither is victor or defeated. If both survive and the duel is to the death, it becomes a standard skirmish.

**CONDITIONAL EFFECTS:**

**Blinded**

-3k3 to all ranged attack rolls. -1k1 to melee attack rolls. Base Armor TN = Reflexes + 5 (armor still adds normally). Water Ring considered 2 Ranks lower for Move Action distance. Any Simple Move Action requires an Athletics/Agility roll (TN 20) or the character falls Prone.

**Dazed**

-3k0 to all actions. May only use Defense and Full Defense Stances. Cannot perform an Iaijutsu duel. Recover during the Reactions Stage with a successful Earth Ring Roll (TN 20). May attempt once per Round; TN decreases by 5 each failed attempt.

**Entangled**

May take no Actions other than attempting to break free (Strength roll, TN set by GM based on entanglement; Contested Roll if someone is actively maintaining it). Opponents may initiate a Grapple with an Entangled character without an attack roll.

**Fatigued**

+5 TN to all Skill rolls, physical Trait rolls, and Spell Casting Rolls after 24 hours without rest. Penalty increases by +5 per additional day without rest. After Stamina days without rest, must make Willpower Trait Rolls (TN 20) every 2 hours to avoid falling asleep. A Fatigued character may not take the Full Attack Stance.

**Grappled**

Armor TN reduced to 5 + any bonuses from worn armor.

**Mounted / Higher Ground**

+1k0 to attack rolls against unmounted/lower characters. A mounted character may not use the Full Attack Stance.

**Prone**

-10 to Armor TN against melee attacks (lasts until standing). Cannot use Move Actions. May only adopt Defense or Attack Stance. Cannot attack with Large weapons. -2k0 penalty to attacks with Medium and Small weapons. Standing up is a Simple Action.

**Stunned**

May take no actions. Armor TN = 5 + armor bonuses. Recover during the Reactions Stage with a successful Earth Ring Roll (TN 20). If the roll fails, the Stun ends at the end of the next Combat Round.

**GRAPPLING:**

Grapples occur within the normal skirmish rules (same Initiative, Actions, Rounds, and Turns).

- Initiating: Attack roll using Jiujutsu/Agility (Complex Action unless a special ability reduces this). Must meet or exceed the target’s Armor TN — this attack roll ignores armor’s Armor TN bonus. On success, both attacker and target are in a Grapple.

- Control: Initially the initiator is in control. At the beginning of each character’s Turn, all participants make a Contested Jiujutsu/Strength Roll. The highest result takes control until the next character’s Turn.

Actions available to the character in control:

- Hit (Complex Action): Inflict normal unarmed damage on any one other Grapple participant. No Raises allowed (no attack roll), but Free Raises can still apply.

- Throw (Complex Action): Throw one opponent, making them Prone anywhere within 5 feet. The thrown character is removed from the Grapple.

- Break (Simple Action): Immediately remove yourself from the Grapple.

- Pass (Free Action): Do nothing; maintain the Grapple and retain control.

Weapon Grapples: Chain weapons and certain polearms may initiate Grapples. Use the appropriate Weapon Skill in place of Jiujutsu for attack and control rolls; Hit uses weapon damage instead of unarmed damage. Risk: if the weapon-user loses control of the Grapple, their opponent gains 2 Free Raises on their next Turn toward a Disarm Maneuver against them.

**SUMAI TOURNAMENTS:**

For formal sumai tournament wrestling, use a Contested Roll of Jiujutsu (Sumai)/Strength. If one wrestler beats the other by 5 or more, they win the match (opponent knocked down or forced out of ring). If neither wins by 5+, the bout continues with another Contested Roll.

- Size advantage: A larger wrestler gains +1k0 to the roll vs a normal or smaller opponent.

- Stare-down (optional): Contested Intimidation/Willpower before the match. Winning by 5+ grants +1k0 to the subsequent Jiujutsu roll.

**OFF-HAND WEAPONS ****&**** MULTIPLE ATTACKS:**

Using two weapons simultaneously is uncommon in Rokugan. Exceptions include the Dragon daisho technique, the Mantis using paired small weapons, and the Lion katana-and-war-fan combination.

- Off-hand attack penalty: -5 (Small weapon), -10 (Medium weapon), -15 (Large weapon).

- Dominant-hand penalty: -5 to all attacks with the dominant hand while a secondary weapon is held in the off hand.

- Armor TN bonus: A character wielding two weapons adds their Insight Rank to Armor TN due to the larger area covered.
