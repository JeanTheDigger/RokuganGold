## 54.7e The Kolat — Sleeper Agent Activation Mechanics

Sleeper agents are unconscious assets. They do not know they have been conditioned, do not hold a kolat_sect value, and run normal engine loops until the moment their trigger phrase is spoken. At that moment their engine is temporarily replaced by a dedicated override loop. The normal Phase 2–5 scoring pipeline is suspended. The override executes the installed command on every AP firing until the command is complete or the sleeper dies. Nothing else stops them.

### Stored Data — Character Sheet Hidden Fields

Conditioning installs four hidden fields on the target NPC’s character sheet at the moment the final CONDUCT_CONDITIONING session completes. These fields are invisible to the NPC and to any character examining them through normal social interaction. They are visible to the engine, to investigators who successfully detect conditioning (Lore: Kolat or Medicine vs TN 35), and to players who have broken a Dream Master and extracted their records.

**trigger_phrase.** String. The spoken phrase that activates the sleeper. Set by the Dream Master at conditioning time. Cannot be changed after conditioning without re-conditioning. If the phrase is spoken by anyone who knows it — not only the Dream Master — activation triggers.

**sleeper_command.** Structured ImmediateNeed. The command the sleeper will execute on activation. Stored as fully resolved engine data: a NeedType and all relevant target fields (target_npc_id, target_settlement_id, etc.) resolved at conditioning time using the Dream Master’s current IntelDB. The five-word spoken command is the human-readable form. What is stored is the engine-readable form. This eliminates any command parsing problem at activation — the engine does not need to interpret natural language. If the Dream Master’s IntelDB contains stale location data for the target at conditioning time, that staleness is baked into the stored command and the LOCATE_CHARACTER decomposition will run to resolve it at execution time.

**conditioning_stability.** Float 0–100. Degrades without maintenance (MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT). A sleeper whose stability falls below 30 may show behavioural anomalies observable by a perceptive character. Below 10 the trigger may fire unreliably — activation does not guarantee execution. Stability degradation is a pre-activation concern. Once the sleeper is activated, this field is no longer evaluated — they execute regardless of stability. A partially degraded sleeper who activates may surface fragments of awareness during execution, but the override loop continues. Seasonal decrement: at each Seasonal Tick (Section 55.2), a Kolat maintenance pass runs over all characters whose character sheet carries sleeper hidden fields. Each such character’s conditioning_stability is decremented by 5.0 and their sleeper_contact_overdue is incremented by the IC days in the season just completed. This runs as background system processing, not as any NPC’s AP decision. It fires regardless of who is present or active. MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT is the only action that reverses both decrements.

**active_sleeper_command.** ImmediateNeed or null. Null when dormant. Populated with the sleeper_command contents when ACTIVATE_SLEEPER fires. Cleared when the command reaches COMPLETE state. This is the field the pre-Phase 2 check reads at the start of each AP firing to determine whether to engage the override loop.

### Activation — What Happens When ACTIVATE_SLEEPER Fires

The Dream Master speaks the trigger phrase in the sleeper’s presence (or within earshot — physical presence is mandatory per the ActionID definition). The executor validates the phrase against the target’s stored trigger_phrase. On match:

(1) active_sleeper_command is populated with the contents of sleeper_command. (2) No topic is generated. The activation is externally unobservable — it is a private mental event, not a social action. Characters physically present who choose to watch the interaction closely may make an active Investigation (Notice) roll vs TN 40 on their own initiative; success creates a personal IntelDB entry noting that something unusual occurred in that moment, but does not identify what or why. This is not a topic in the momentum system. It is a personal knowledge entry available only to the observing character. The Dream Master and the command are not identified regardless of roll result. (3) The sleeper has no subjective experience of receiving the command. From their perspective this was a normal conversation.

### The Override Loop

On the sleeper’s next AP firing, a pre-Phase 2 check reads active_sleeper_command before the normal goal resolution loop begins. If populated, the normal engine loop — Phase 2 goal resolution, Phase 3 option generation, Phase 4 personality filter, Phase 5 scoring — is bypassed entirely. A dedicated sleeper executor runs instead:

**Decomposition.** The stored ImmediateNeed is decomposed exactly as any other ImmediateNeed would be — the existing decomposition functions run normally. LOCATE_CHARACTER fires if the target’s location is stale or unknown, generating BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the best anchor. The sleeper will travel. They will wait. They will pursue across seasons if necessary. The command does not expire. Geographic limit exception: ELIMINATE_CHARACTER carries a reachability check in its decomposition. Before generating BEGIN_TRAVEL toward a target, the decomposition queries whether the target’s last known location is within the Emerald Empire’s boundaries. If the target has entered the Shadowlands or any territory beyond the Empire’s recognised borders, the operative’s ELIMINATE_CHARACTER objective is suspended — not cleared, suspended. The operative does not follow. They return to their normal life and the assignment remains dormant in lotus_network_record with operative_status = “standing_by”. MONITOR_ASSIGNMENT_COMPLETION continues checking the confirmation drop on schedule. If the target re-enters the Empire, the decomposition reactivates and BEGIN_TRAVEL fires toward the updated location. If the target never returns, the assignment remains dormant until Master Lotus clears it manually via SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Tiger requesting closure.

**Personality filter bypass.** The filter is not applied. The sleeper’s virtues, honor, and personality lean have no effect on action selection during the override loop. A Jin-virtue sleeper will assassinate. A Gi-virtue sleeper will fabricate. A Chugi-virtue sleeper will act against their lord’s known position. The conditioning installed the override precisely because the Dream Master knew these blocks would otherwise prevent execution. This is why CONDUCT_CONDITIONING carries the highest Honor cost in the engine.

**Cover maintenance.** Between APs, the sleeper continues to eat, sleep, and maintain the surface appearance of a normal life. The override loop governs AP-slot decisions and also suppresses all letter-writing. For non-lord sleepers, the free daily letter and any AP-batch letters are blocked. For lord sleepers, the Civilian Order pool cannot be spent on WRITE_LETTER for the duration. An activated sleeper does not write letters. Writing to their lord about unusual travel plans or unexplained absences would expose the mission. The letter suppression is total for the duration of the override — no letters out, regardless of standing correspondence commitments. Gaps in an otherwise regular correspondent’s letter schedule are detectable: a character who normally receives weekly letters from the sleeper and notices an unexplained silence may generate a Tier 4 personal concern topic. This is a secondary detection vector available to players who track correspondence patterns closely. They do not behave erratically unless their conditioning_stability was already critically low before activation.

**Action log.** Every action taken under the override loop is logged with memory_suppressed: true. The sleeper’s own engine never sees these entries — they cannot recall or report what they did. Investigators examining the action log through PROBE or READ_CHARACTER may notice gaps. A character with Medicine 4+ who examines the sleeper closely can detect signs of psychological suppression: Investigation (Notice) + Medicine vs TN 30.

### Resolution

The override loop ends under exactly two conditions.

**Command complete.** The ImmediateNeed resolves to a successful outcome. The active_sleeper_command field is cleared. The engine returns to the normal Phase 2–5 loop on the next AP firing. The sleeper resumes their life with no memory of what they did. The memory_suppressed: true entries remain in the action log. If they had a primary objective before activation, it is still there. If their lord was expecting them, they return to that expectation now.

**Sleeper dies.** The override clears with the NPC. No other condition terminates an active sleeper. A temporarily unreachable target, an intervening crisis, a lord’s direct order, a priest’s intervention — none of these stop the sleeper. They are a weapon in motion. The only way to stop a weapon is to destroy it.

### Investigator Implications

A sleeper who has been activated and is pursuing their command behaves in ways that are subtly, then dramatically, inconsistent with their established personality. They travel without apparent reason. They seek out individuals they have no professional connection to. They fail to respond normally to social pressure. Characters who have spent time with the sleeper and roll Investigation (Notice) vs TN 25 may generate a Tier 4 topic: “[Character] is not themselves.” This is not a Kolat topic. It is a personal anomaly topic. Connecting the anomaly to conditioning requires Lore: Kolat 3+ and a separate Investigation roll vs TN 30.

The investigators’ problem: by the time the anomaly is noticeable, the sleeper is probably already close to executing. Reactive investigation is almost always too slow. The only reliable counter is detecting conditioning before activation — finding the hidden fields through PROBE at TN 35+, or maintaining close enough observation of the Dream Master’s movements to intercept the activation event.
