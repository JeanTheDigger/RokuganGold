## 22.7 Marriage System — LOCKED

Marriage in Rokugan is a political and social institution. It seals alliances between families and clans, creates cross-clan biological connections, produces heirs who inherit from both sides, and generates the intricate web of relationships that defines Rokugani politics. Marriage is arranged by lords — rarely by the individuals involved.

**What Marriage Does — LOCKED:**

- Creates a permanent biological family link between two characters and their respective families.

- Generates cross-clan disposition modifiers — the married character's family gains small positive permanent modifiers toward the spouse's family, and vice versa.

- Produces children who belong to one clan institutionally but have biological connections to both — creating characters who sit at the intersection of two clan networks.

- Children inherit great-grandparent connections from both sides — extending the web of biological family across clan lines.

**Who Arranges Marriage — LOCKED:**

- Marriage is arranged by the lord of the character being married — their daimyo, family head, or Clan Champion for high-profile matches.

- Player characters can influence marriage arrangements through court actions and personal visits — but the decision belongs to the lord.

- A lord's NPC objective system (Section 18) drives when and to whom they arrange marriages — a lord whose objective is improving relations with the Crane might arrange a marriage into a Crane family.

**At Game Start — LOCKED:**

- The world is populated with a fully generated four-generation family web for all active named characters.

- Cross-clan marriages already exist at game start — creating immediate political and personal complexity.

- The family web is generated before positions are filled — ensuring that who holds a position is connected to who holds other positions in believable ways.

**MARRIAGE TYPES — LOCKED**

Three marriage types exist, all using the same mechanics but serving different purposes and generating different effects:

- Within the same Family — consolidates the family’s biological web and produces heirs. One partner is designated primary; the secondary partner relocates to the primary partner’s province and comes under their lord’s authority within the family hierarchy. No clan/family baseline boost applies — they are already of the same family. No Favor exchange — no family is losing a member to another group. Primarily genealogical and dynastic.

- Between Families within the same Clan — strengthens intra-clan cohesion. Generates a family-to-family baseline boost between the two families. No clan-to-clan effect. Common and politically useful.

- Between Clans — the dramatic case. Full clan-to-clan and family-to-family baseline boost. One character crosses clan lines permanently. Torn loyalty. Favor exchange between clans. Military alliance likelihood increases. Reserved for significant political moments.

**MECHANICAL EFFECTS — LOCKED**

- Clan-to-Clan baseline boost: +8 (cross-clan marriages only). Applies immediately. Decays at 1 point per 10 IC seasons — fully fades in 80 seasons (~20 IC years) without renewal. Cap: +20 maximum between any two clans from marriages. A new cross-clan marriage resets the decay clock.

- Family-to-Family baseline boost: +5 (between-families and cross-clan marriages). Applies immediately. Decays at 1 point per 8 IC seasons — fully fades in 40 seasons (~10 IC years) without renewal. Cap: +15 maximum between any two families. A new marriage between the same families resets the decay clock. Renewal is the political incentive for repeated dynastic marriages — alliance maintenance requires ongoing investment.

- Military alliance likelihood: the NPC engine scores alliance-related actions (REQUEST_AID, ASSIGN_OBJECTIVE toward military cooperation) higher when a marriage connection exists between the clans. This is emergent from the disposition boost, not a separate mechanic.

- Favor: the family gaining a roster member owes a Favor to the family losing one. Registered in the Favor system (Section 12.10) immediately on marriage completion.

**THE MARRIED CHARACTER — LOCKED**

The married character joins their new clan and family institutionally — new lord chain, new objectives, new roster. They are fully part of their new clan. However they retain a permanent connection to their birth family and clan that never decays:

- +15 permanent disposition floor toward their birth Family. This modifier never decays and cannot be removed. They will always feel warmth toward the family they came from.

- +8 permanent disposition floor toward their birth Clan. Broader but still meaningful — clan identity runs deep.

This creates genuine torn loyalty in the engine. When the married character’s new lord assigns objectives that conflict with their birth family’s interests — raiding a birth family province, opposing them at court, withholding Rice during famine — the permanent disposition modifier pulls against full compliance. The character will fulfill their duty, but the scoring formula reflects the tension. A CHUGI character complies regardless. A MEIYO or MAKOTO character may find certain actions against their birth family scoring so low they refuse them outright.

The individuals being married have no choice in the arrangement. Their lord decides. This is Rokugan.

**PROPOSAL MECHANICS — LOCKED**

A marriage is proposed and negotiated through three possible paths, in escalating order of formality:

Path 1 — Letters: The proposing lord sends a formal proposal letter to the target lord via the Letter System (Section 12.7). The letter specifies the proposed characters, the proposed direction of movement (who joins whose clan), and the implied Favor obligation. The target lord evaluates through the NPC engine — their disposition toward the proposing family/clan, their current objectives, the value of the proposed terms. They respond by letter: accept, decline, or counter-propose. If both sides agree through correspondence, the marriage is arranged. No court required. This is the quiet, efficient path.

Path 2 — Personal Visit: The proposing lord sends a representative or visits in person to the target lord’s holdings (Section 17). More personal than letters, faster to resolve. Uses the standard visit action menu. Both parties negotiate directly. Still private — no public topic generated unless one of them chooses to disclose.

Path 3 — Court Negotiation: If terms are disputed — who moves, what Favor is owed, which characters are selected — either lord may request a formal court session. The marriage negotiation becomes a public political event. A Tier 4 topic is generated: “[Family A] and [Family B] are negotiating a marriage arrangement.” Other lords become aware and may act — supporting, opposing, or attempting to leverage the negotiation for their own purposes. Court actions become available to both sides and to interested third parties. This is the dramatic path. A cross-clan marriage negotiated at Winter Court with the Empire watching is a political event in its own right.

**NEGOTIATION TERMS — LOCKED**

The terms negotiated between the two lords are:

- Which character from each family is being married.

- Direction of movement — which character joins the other’s clan. This is the primary bargaining point. A family losing a high-ranked or strategically valuable character will demand more.

- The Favor — what the gaining family owes the losing family. Registered immediately on completion. The losing lord evaluates whether the Favor offered is worth the roster loss.

A lord’s acceptance probability is influenced by: disposition toward the proposing family/clan (Section 12.2 and 12.2b), their current objectives (a lord seeking military alliance values the cross-clan boost more), the rank and utility of the character being offered versus the one being requested, and the significance of the Favor offered.

**BENTEN FORTUNE CONNECTION — LOCKED**

Marriage negotiations conducted during the Festival of the River of Stars (9th day of the Boar, Winter) gain a bonus modifier to acceptance rolls — Benten’s blessing on formal unions is recognized across Rokugan. Additionally, a province that has failed Benten’s worship threshold (Wrathful tier) causes marriage negotiations originating from that province to automatically fail — the Fortune of Romantic Love has withdrawn her blessing entirely.

**CHILDREN — LOCKED**

Children born from a marriage are tracked in the family web from birth as Insight Rank 0 characters. They are present and named — part of the biological network, visible in family records, relevant to inheritance — but not active on the World Map. They cannot be interacted with, assigned objectives, or played as characters. They simply exist as a fact of the family structure until adulthood.

**Pregnancy — LOCKED**

Each season after a marriage is completed — regardless of marriage type (within-family, between-families, or between-clans) — a pregnancy check fires based on the disposition between the two married characters. Disposition is checked at the personal level — how these two specific people feel about each other, seeded from their clan and family baselines and shaped by any personal history.

- Blood Enemy to Rival (−100 to −31): 0% chance per season. The relationship is too hostile.

- Stranger to Acquaintance (−30 to +30): 5% chance per season.

- Friend (+31 to +60): 15% chance per season.

- Close Friend to Devoted (+61 to +100): 25% chance per season.

On average a friendly marriage produces a child every 2–3 IC years. A hostile political arrangement may never produce children at all. The permanent +15 disposition floor the married character retains toward their birth family does not directly affect marital disposition — that floor applies toward birth family members, not toward their spouse. Marital disposition builds through the normal personal interaction system.

**Clan ****&**** Family Affiliation — LOCKED**

Children belong to the clan and family of the institutional parent — whichever clan the married character joined. A Crane character who married into the Lion clan produces Lion children. The child’s institutional identity is Lion: they answer to Lion lords, receive Lion school assignments at Gempuku, and appear on the Lion clan roster.

However the child carries biological connections to both sides through the family web — the Lion institutional identity plus the Crane biological ancestry from their parent’s birth family. This cross-clan biological link creates natural warmer relations with that Crane family across generations. A Lion character who is the grandchild of a Crane marriage has a legitimate biological reason for a positive disposition floor toward that specific Crane family.

**Gempuku — LOCKED**

At 18 IC years of age (72 seasons), the child undergoes Gempuku — the Rokugani coming-of-age ceremony. This is the trigger event that transitions them from Insight Rank 0 to active named character status on the World Map.

Gempuku generates a Tier 4 topic: “[Character Name] of [Family] has undergone Gempuku.” The event is noted by the social world. The character’s lord assigns them a school reflecting their intended role — bushi, courtier, shugenja, monk, or other. The character receives the full baseline template for that school (Section 22.4): standard starting Rings, skills, Honor, Status, starting outfit. No parental inheritance of stats — they begin as a fresh Rank 1 character of their school.

From Gempuku onward the character is fully active — interactable, assignable, playable if a player chooses that role, and subject to all normal character systems. Their lord now has a new vassal. The family web gains a new active node. The cross-clan biological connections they carry from their mixed heritage are present from birth and become politically relevant from the moment they enter the World Map.
