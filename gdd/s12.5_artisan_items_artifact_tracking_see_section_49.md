## 12.5 Artisan Items & Artifact Tracking — SEE SECTION 49

Art pieces, weapons, jewelry, and other crafted objects should be tracked individually in the game like named characters — with their own history, provenance, and identity. An item made from rare materials by a renowned artisan is not just mechanically superior; it is a named object with a story. Who made it, what materials were used, who owned it, what courts it passed through — all of this gives items the same flavor as characters.

- Items crafted from rare or special materials (gems, sacred wood, Kaiu steel, Phoenix-blessed paper) carry additional prestige and political weight.

- Renowned artisans — Kakita craftsmen, Kaiu smiths, Shiba artists — produce named items that carry the maker's reputation.

- A named item given as a gift carries more political weight than an anonymous equivalent.

- Items can accumulate history — a katana that won a famous duel, a painting that hung in the Emperor's court, a tea set that belonged to a deceased Clan Champion. These histories make items politically significant beyond their material value.
