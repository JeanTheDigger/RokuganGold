# 14. Action Point System — LOCKED

The Action Point system governs what a character can do with their time. It is the universal currency of player agency — covering court actions, training, personal interactions, travel, and everything else a character does personally in a day. Lord characters also have a separate Civilian Order budget for administrative directives (Section 57.34). Every character has the same daily pool regardless of Insight Rank, social standing, or clan. There are only so many hours in a day — being more skilled makes you better at what you do, not capable of doing more things.

**Action Point Budget — LOCKED:**

- Each character has 2 Action Points per in-game day, split into Morning and Afternoon slots.

- Since 1 real-world day = 4 in-game days, each player has 8 Action Points to spend per real-world day.

- Players can spend those 8 points anytime across the real-world day — no need to log in at specific times or split actions between sessions.

- Action Points do not carry over to the next real-world day — unspent points are lost. A character who does nothing has simply rested.

- The pool is flat — no scaling with Insight Rank, status, or any other character attribute. Skill makes actions more effective, not more numerous.

**Action Point Categories — LOCKED:**

Action Points can be spent across all aspects of a character's life. Categories include but are not limited to:

- Court actions — attending a performance, seeking a private audience, delivering a gift, making a political argument, spreading gossip, gathering intelligence, writing a letter.

- Training — practicing a weapon skill, studying a scroll, meditating, working on a craft.

- Travel — moving between locations on the ASCII map or World Map.

- Personal interactions — visiting another character's quarters, requesting a private meeting, delivering a message in person.

- Military actions — inspecting troops, issuing orders, reviewing supply reports.

- Economic actions — reviewing accounts, directing servants, managing holdings.

**Design Philosophy:**

- The Action Point system creates meaningful daily choices — spending points on court means not spending them on training or personal interactions.

- It respects all playtime habits equally — a player who logs in once for 3 hours gets the same 6 points as one who logs in six times for 30 minutes each.

- It connects to the narrative time decoupling from Section 13 — a player declares their mechanical actions (spending points) and then roleplays those actions as richly as they wish, independent of how long the roleplay takes.
