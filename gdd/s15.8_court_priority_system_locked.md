## 15.8 Court Priority System — LOCKED

When multiple courts occur simultaneously, characters must choose which to attend. This is a meaningful decision with real political consequences. The existing systems handle most of the mechanics — this section defines the decision logic and consequence framework.

**NPC Decision Logic — LOCKED:**

NPCs choose courts in strict priority order:

- Primary objective: The court that most directly serves the NPC’s active primary objective takes absolute priority. If their lord assigned them to attend a specific court, they attend that court. Full stop.

- Personal relevance score: If both courts serve the primary objective equally, the NPC attends the court with the higher personal relevance score for them.

- Standing objective: If still tied, the court that better serves their standing objective breaks the tie.

- Court status: If all else equal, the higher-status court wins — a Clan Champion court over a Provincial court, Imperial Winter Court over everything.

**Proxy Strategy — LOCKED:**

A character can attend one court themselves while sending a designated proxy to the other. The Designated Proxy System (Section 16.2) already covers how this works. This is the primary tool for managing court conflicts — a skilled lord with good courtiers can have a presence at multiple simultaneous courts through careful delegation.

**Sequential Courts — LOCKED:**

Courts have different durations (Section 15.1). A short Provincial court (a few IC days) may begin and end during a longer Clan Champion court. A character can attend the shorter court, then travel to join the longer court still in progress. Travel time applies — they miss whatever happened before they arrived. This is handled by the existing movement system.

**Leaving a Court Early — LOCKED:**

A player can leave a court at any time. The consequences depend on their role:

- Host leaving their own court: Significant Honor and Glory loss. This is a serious breach of hospitality.

- Guest leaving: Small disposition cost with the host — a mild social slight.

- Designated proxy leaving before mandate is fulfilled: Treated as mandate abandonment. Out-of-mandate consequences apply (Section 16.2). The lord must be notified.

**Objective Negligence and Court Choice — LOCKED:**

Court priority is part of objective fulfillment. If a lord assigns a primary objective that requires attending a specific court, choosing to attend a different court instead is objective negligence under Section 18.9.

- Attending the wrong court passively (chose a different one without clear conflict): −0.1 Honor per IC season of negligence.

- Attending the wrong court deliberately against the lord’s known interest: −0.5 Honor immediately. Treated as active opposition.

- Lord discovers the wrong choice: Full betrayal consequence chain from Section 18.9 may apply depending on disposition and evidence.

This also applies to proxies. A proxy who accepts a mandate to attend Court A and instead attends Court B has violated their mandate. The out-of-mandate system fires regardless of whether the proxy thought Court B was more important.

**Player Information — LOCKED:**

When multiple court invitations arrive simultaneously, the player sees: the court type and host, the primary topic on the agenda, which characters they know are attending, and any objective assignment from their lord. The player makes their choice with this information. There is no automatic recommendation — the decision is theirs.

Missing a court has natural consequences through existing systems: topics spread without the player’s influence, dispositions shift without their input, commitments are made they had no say in, and NPCs act according to their own objectives without the player’s counter-pressure. These consequences are not flagged to the player as penalties — they simply happen in the world.

**Otomo Institutional Behavior — LOCKED:**

Source: *Secrets of the Empire*, pp. 117–119 (Otomo family, seiyaku tradition). Cross-references: Section 2.1.19 (Otomo Family), Section 12.6 (Daily Conversation System), Section 12.7 (Letter System), Section 17 (Personal Visits System), Section 55.22b (Seiyaku System — Alliance Suppression).

The Otomo family’s seiyaku tradition is not a court behavior — it is an institutional directive that governs Otomo NPC behavior across every interaction channel. When the NPC Decision Engine generates actions for Otomo characters, the following leans apply regardless of context — at court, during personal visits, through letters, and in daily conversation. The Otomo are always the serpents in the garden, not merely when the garden happens to host a formal event.

**Core Behavioral Leans (apply in ALL contexts):**

Gossip +15 to any Gossip action in any setting — court, personal visit, or daily conversation. An Otomo does not idle; every social encounter is an opportunity to plant a seed of doubt. Disclose (information that damages a third party’s reputation) +10 in any setting. Any action that worsens disposition between two Great Clan representatives +10 in any setting. Otomo characters never use any action — court, visit, letter, or conversation — to strengthen alliances between Great Clans unless the Emperor has specifically ordered it. This prohibition is absolute. An Otomo NPC presented with a choice between two actions, one of which builds inter-clan goodwill and one which does not, always chooses the latter.

**Channel-Specific Behavior:**

At Court (Section 15): The behavioral leans above stack with the standard court action system. Otomo NPCs prioritize attending courts where representatives of multiple Great Clans are present — the more clans represented, the more opportunities for discord. When an active Seiyaku Directive exists (Section 55.22b), court attendance is targeted at the specific clan pair under directive.

During Personal Visits (Section 17): Otomo NPCs use personal visits as a precision tool. They visit lords of Great Clans to conduct Category 3 actions (Gossip, Disclose, Reveal a Secret Privately) in the intimate setting. The private nature of visits makes them ideal for sharing sensitive information — an Otomo visiting a Lion lord to privately disclose a Crane slight is more effective than the same disclosure at a crowded court. Otomo NPCs prioritize visiting lords whose disposition toward another clan is currently above +31 (Friend) — they seek out warmth to cool it. When a Seiyaku Directive is active, the assigned operative prioritizes personal visits to representatives of the targeted clan pair.

Through Letters (Section 12.7): Otomo NPCs use their letter allowance to sustain low-intensity pressure on inter-clan relationships. A non-lord Otomo writes one free letter per IC day; an Otomo who holds a lordship draws from their Civilian Order pool (Section 57.34) and may write multiple letters per IC day. An Otomo’s letter always carries a topic selected to cast one clan in a negative light to the recipient. Letter targets are chosen strategically: the Otomo writes to whichever Great Clan representative is most receptive to hearing criticism of another clan. When a Seiyaku Directive is active, the assigned operative sends letters exclusively to representatives of the targeted pair, alternating between Clan A and Clan B, each letter emphasizing the other clan’s recent actions in the most unfavorable light. This is a relentless drip of negative framing — an Otomo lord with a large Civilian Order pool can sustain multiple letters per IC day to multiple targets simultaneously. The Calligraphy roll for Otomo letters uses Intelligence (sharp, persuasive arguments) by default.

In Daily Conversation (Section 12.6): When Otomo NPCs share a settlement with Great Clan representatives, their daily conversation topic selection is weighted toward topics that emphasize inter-clan tension. The +10 lean to any action that worsens inter-clan disposition applies to topic selection in daily conversation — if the Otomo NPC’s topic pool contains a topic that could damage relations between two Great Clans, that topic is prioritized for sharing.

**Escalation at Rival Disposition:** Otomo characters at Rival disposition or below toward a specific clan will actively seek to Fabricate Accusations or spread damaging topics about that clan’s representatives through any available channel — court actions, personal visit disclosures, and letter campaigns. They use the Secret System (Section 12.8) if covert methods are available. This escalation is not limited to court — an Otomo at Rival disposition toward the Crane will fabricate accusations whether at Winter Court, visiting a Lion lord’s castle, or writing from their own holdings.

This behavior is driven by Seigyo (Control) Shourido virtue, which is the default Shourido assignment for generated Otomo courtier NPCs. This plugs into the existing personality_lean.json framework (Section 55.21.4). The world-map-level Seiyaku System (Section 55.22b) provides the institutional layer above this individual behavior — determining which clan pairs are targeted and assigning operatives.
