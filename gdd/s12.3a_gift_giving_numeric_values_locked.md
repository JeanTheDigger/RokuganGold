## 12.3a Gift-Giving Numeric Values — LOCKED

This section formalizes two numeric values left unspecified by GDD s12.3 and s15.4.
The main sections describe outcomes qualitatively ("small disposition loss", "an insult")
without assigning numbers. This addendum assigns the canonical values used by the simulation.

---

### Source Derivation

**GDD-confirmed anchors (from s15.4 and s12.2):**

| Anchor | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Charm critical failure | −3 | s15.4a |
| private_insult temporary modifier | −5 / 30 days | s12.2 temporary modifier table |
| Play a Game (pleasant recreation) | +3 | s15.4 |
| Gossip base damage (targeted, 1 AP) | −5 | s15.4a |

**Distinction between the two outcomes:**

- **Critical failure** — the roll missed TN by 10+. The gift itself has value; the presentation
  was so badly executed it caused offense. This is clumsiness, not aggression. GDD s15.4:
  *"Small disposition loss regardless of gift quality."*

- **Forbidden gift** — the giver chose a weapon (non-Legendary) or armor. GDD s12.3:
  *"Gifting a sword implies the recipient lacks one, which is an insult."* This is a
  deliberate choice that communicates contempt or ignorance, not an execution failure.

The two outcomes are intentionally different in severity. Clumsiness is embarrassing;
implying a samurai is unequipped is a genuine affront.

---

### Values — LOCKED

| Constant | Value | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| `CRITICAL_FAILURE_DISPOSITION_LOSS` | −3 | "Small disposition loss." Matches Charm critical failure (s15.4a). Does not cross a disposition tier on its own. Recoverable with goodwill. |
| `FORBIDDEN_GIFT_DISPOSITION_LOSS` | −5 | "An insult." Matches private_insult magnitude and Gossip base damage. The implied slight (recipient lacks a sword) is a deliberate social offense. Immediate hit, no lasting modifier — the damage is to the relationship, not a lingering stain. |

---

### Notes

Both outcomes apply an immediate `disposition_change` only — no temporary modifier is
created. The forbidden path never reaches the presentation roll, so no social obligation
(`gift_obligation`) is created either.

Inappropriate gifts (wrong taste for recipient — e.g. a poetry scroll for a Crab bushi)
are handled differently: they reduce or remove the Free Raises from gift quality, resulting
in a lower success outcome, not a dedicated negative value.
