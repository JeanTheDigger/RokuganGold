## 57.29 Ikebana System

### 57.29.1 Overview

Ikebana is the ephemeral display art. It is unique among the artisan sub-skills because the performance IS the creation IS the display object &#x2014; the artisan arranges flowers in front of an audience, the audience experiences the act of arrangement as a live performance, and the finished arrangement remains in the room afterward as a temporary display. Then the flowers die. No other artisan skill collapses creation, performance, and display into a single action with a mortal output.

Ikebana originated as Buddhist flower offerings (kuge) placed before altars at temples. During the Muromachi period, as the shoin-zukuri architectural style developed the tokonoma alcove, flower arrangements migrated from purely spiritual offerings to decorative art. In Rokugan, ikebana arrangements sit in the tokonoma alongside the kakemono painting &#x2014; the two arts are companions, not competitors. The arrangement complements the painting, and both contribute to the zone&#x2019;s aesthetic and spiritual environment.

Ikebana already qualifies as _performance_skill (Section 24), unlocking PERFORM_FOR and PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE. This section defines the additional mechanic: when the performance succeeds, a temporary display object is created as a byproduct, occupying the zone&#x2019;s ikebana_slot. The display generates visitor effects for its remaining lifespan, then degrades naturally as the flowers die. The cycle repeats when the artisan performs again.

### 57.29.2 Arrangement World Object

Each ikebana arrangement tracks: arrangement_id, creator_id, quality_tier (Normal through Legendary), season_created (the IC season during which the arrangement was made), materials_source (garden_id if materials came from a garden, null if gathered or purchased), date_created (IC day), lifespan_remaining (IC days), display_zone_id (zone_id or null), expired (boolean, false until lifespan reaches 0), composition_materials (list of 1–3 flower/branch entries selected from the seasonal material table, Section 57.29.6a), composition_description (generated text describing the arrangement — e.g. “A three-branch arrangement of weeping cherry (shidarezakura), purple iris (kakitsubata), and young willow (yanagi) in a celadon vessel, expressing the tension between spring’s exuberance and the quiet pull of water”). The composition_description is generated procedurally at creation time from the selected materials, the quality tier, and the creator’s personality profile. Higher quality arrangements receive more evocative descriptions. Materials, provenance, and history follow standard Section 49 rules.

### 57.29.3 Ikebana Slot

New zone slot: ikebana_slot. Holds one ikebana arrangement. Available at any eligible interior zone: Castle/Shiro, court locations, Temple/Shinden, estate/private quarters. Broader eligibility than sculpture (shrine/temple only) because ikebana is a secular court art as much as a spiritual one &#x2014; a Crane lord&#x2019;s court hall displays an ikebana arrangement for the same reason it displays a kakemono painting. The ikebana_slot coexists with wall_art_slot (kakemono), displayed_art_slot (by&#x014D;bu), and fusuma_slot (fusuma). A fully decorated zone can have all four painting slots plus an ikebana arrangement &#x2014; five simultaneous aesthetic features, each by a different artisan, each generating its own visitor effects.

When the arrangement&#x2019;s lifespan expires, the ikebana_slot empties automatically. No topic, no removal action &#x2014; flowers die. The expired arrangement_object is retained as a historical record (date_created, creator_id, quality_tier) but generates no further effects. A new performance by any ikebana artisan with permission fills the slot again.

### 57.29.4 Performance as Creation

Ikebana uses the existing PERFORM_FOR (Category 1, targeted) and PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE (Category 2, broadcast) ActionIDs with Artisan: Ikebana as the _performance_skill. No new ActionID is required. The roll is Artisan: Ikebana / Awareness vs TN 15, same as any _performance_skill action.

On success: two effects fire simultaneously. First, the standard performance disposition effects from PERFORM_FOR or PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE &#x2014; the audience experiences the live arrangement and gains disposition toward the performer, same as any court performance. Second, if the performer is AT_ZONE at an eligible interior zone, an arrangement_object is created and placed in the ikebana_slot. Quality tier is determined by performance Raises: 0 Raises = Normal, 1 = Fine, 2 = Exceptional, 3 = Masterwork, 4+ = Legendary. The arrangement begins generating visitor effects immediately.

On failure: the standard performance failure fires (small disposition loss toward the performer). No arrangement is created &#x2014; the flowers were placed poorly and the result is not worth displaying. The ikebana_slot remains empty or retains its previous occupant.

If the ikebana_slot is already occupied by a living arrangement: the new arrangement replaces the old one. The old arrangement_object is marked expired. No topic fires for the replacement &#x2014; refreshing an ikebana display is routine, not newsworthy. If the performer lacks ikebana_display_permission for the zone: the performance fires normally (audience sees the arrangement being made) but the arrangement is NOT placed in the ikebana_slot. The performer takes the arrangement with them as an inventory item &#x2014; a giftable object worth 2 bu (Section 49 canonical price). A performed arrangement that is not displayed has the same lifespan as a displayed one; it dies in the performer&#x2019;s inventory if not gifted before expiry.

### 57.29.5 Ikebana Display Permission

Same pattern as painting permissions (Section 57.27.4a). The zone lord grants ikebana_display_permission to specific artisans. Grant, revocation, and lordship change grace period (1 IC season) follow the existing permission infrastructure. The lord may perform ikebana at their own zone without a permission flag. An artisan who is a member of the court (AT_COURT status) and holds the lord&#x2019;s disposition at Friendly or above is typically granted permission implicitly &#x2014; a welcomed court artisan arranges flowers as part of court life without needing to request formal permission each time.

### 57.29.6 Seasonal Materials and Garden Synergy

Ikebana arrangements use seasonal flowers, branches, and grasses. The materials are inherently seasonal — you arrange what is blooming. There is no “wrong season” display issue as with seasonal kakemono because the arrangement IS the current season by nature of its materials. Material acquisition is a background activity that never consumes AP or requires explicit action. An ikebana artisan at any zone with natural plant life (any province with Ecology rating 1+ — effectively everywhere except the deep Shadowlands) is assumed to have access to seasonal flowers. The artisan walks outside, selects branches and blooms, and returns. This is not modelled as a discrete action because it is not interesting — the skill is in the arrangement, not in the sourcing.

Garden synergy: at any zone with a garden (Section 57.23) in the same Lesser Zone or an adjacent zone, the garden’s quality grants bonus Free Raises on the ikebana performance roll. Garden bonus: Normal garden = +0, Fine = +1, Exceptional = +1, Masterwork = +2, Legendary = +2. A garden provides cultivated flowers that are healthier, more varied, and more perfectly formed than wild-gathered materials — the arrangement benefits from the gardener’s sustained work. This is the cross-system synergy hook: the garden system (57.23) feeds the ikebana system (57.29). A court with a beautiful garden adjacent to the court hall provides the ikebana artisan with superior materials, which improves the arrangement quality, which improves visitor effects. Two artisan sub-skills feeding each other. Without a garden: no bonus. The artisan works with whatever is available. The arrangement may still be Legendary if the artisan’s skill is sufficient — quality is in the composition, not the rarity of materials. A master can make transcendent art from common pine and plum.

### 57.29.6a Seasonal Flower Composition Table

Each arrangement selects 1–3 materials from the current season’s table based on quality tier. Normal quality uses 1 material. Fine uses 1–2. Exceptional uses 2–3. Masterwork and Legendary use 3. The selection is weighted by the artisan’s personality (a contemplative Dragon monk gravitates toward pine and bamboo; a flamboyant Crane courtier toward peonies and wisteria) and by garden availability (a garden with specific species prioritises those species in the selection). Materials from neighbouring seasons (early spring materials available in late winter, late autumn materials in early winter) are valid at 10% probability — seasonal boundaries are soft, not hard walls. Rarity tags below (Common, Uncommon, Rare) indicate how frequently each material appears in arrangements across the Empire — they are flavour descriptors that affect the composition_description’s tone and the social perception of the arrangement, not mechanical gates. A Legendary arrangement of entirely Common materials is valid and achievable — quality is in the artisan’s skill, not the flowers’ scarcity.

Spring (Haru) materials: Sakura (cherry blossom — the defining spring flower, ephemeral beauty, Common), Shidarezakura (weeping cherry — cascading branches, dramatic form, Uncommon), Ume (plum blossom — early spring, perseverance through cold, Common), Momo (peach blossom — soft pink abundance, Common), Fuji (wisteria — cascading purple clusters, elegant, Uncommon), Kakitsubata (iris — deep purple, associated with water and poetry, Common), Ayame (sweet flag iris — similar to kakitsubata but drier habitat, Common), Botan (peony — the king of flowers, opulent, Uncommon in most provinces, Common in Crane territory), Suisen (narcissus — early spring, delicate, Common), Rengyō (forsythia — bright yellow branches, Common), Yanagi (willow — graceful drooping branches, Common near water), Tsubaki (camellia — late winter/early spring crossover, Common).

Summer (Natsu) materials: Hasu (lotus — the sacred Buddhist flower, purity rising from mud, Uncommon, Common at temple zones), Ajisai (hydrangea — rain season flower, shifting colours, Common), Asagao (morning glory — ephemeral bloom that opens at dawn and closes by midday, Common), Yuri (lily — fragrant elegance, Uncommon), Himawari (sunflower — bold and bright, Common in agricultural provinces), Hōsenka (balsam — delicate, Common), Take (bamboo — year-round but evokes summer coolness, Common), Hōzuki (Chinese lantern plant — orange pods, late summer, Uncommon), Ran (orchid — refined beauty, associated with scholars and monks, Rare in most provinces, Uncommon in Phoenix and Crane territory). Lotus note: lotus is spiritually significant in Buddhist contexts. An arrangement featuring lotus at a temple zone carries additional weight — the flower’s symbolism (purity, enlightenment) enhances the arrangement’s spiritual offering function (Section 57.29.9).

Autumn (Aki) materials: Kiku (chrysanthemum — THE autumn flower, imperially significant, symbol of longevity, Common), Momiji (maple branches — vivid red and gold autumn colour, Common), Susuki (pampas grass — swaying silver plumes evoking autumn wind, Common), Hagi (bush clover — the most frequently referenced plant in classical poetry, Common), Nadeshiko (dianthus/fringed pink — delicate, associated with feminine beauty, Common), Rindō (gentian — deep blue, late autumn, Uncommon), Ominaeshi (patrinia/maiden flower — golden clusters, Common), Kuzu (arrowroot vine — vigorous, wild, Common), Fujibakama (thoroughwort — subtle fragrance, Uncommon). Kiku note: chrysanthemum is the Imperial flower. An arrangement prominently featuring kiku carries political weight — displaying the Imperial flower is a gesture of loyalty to the throne. At courts where the Emperor’s authority is contested, a kiku-centred arrangement is a political statement.

Winter (Fuyu) materials: Matsu (pine — evergreen endurance, the backbone of winter arrangements, Common), Ume (plum blossom — late winter, courage to bloom in cold, Common), Tsubaki (camellia — winter red against snow, Common), Sazanka (sasanqua camellia — late autumn/winter, smaller than tsubaki, Common), Nanten (heavenly bamboo — bright red winter berries, auspicious, Common), Fukujusō (adonis/pheasant’s eye — golden bloom in snow, symbol of new year’s hope, Uncommon), Suisen (narcissus — winter-blooming, fragrant, Common), Sasa (bamboo grass — evergreen, modest, Common). Winter note: the classic winter combination is shōchikubai — pine (matsu), bamboo (take), and plum (ume). These Three Friends of Winter symbolise endurance, flexibility, and renewal. An arrangement using all three is the most prestigious winter composition and is considered auspicious for the new year.

Cross-regional materials: Mugunghwa (rose of Sharon — associated with the Unicorn Clan’s continental connections, summer/autumn, Rare outside Unicorn territory, Uncommon within). Bǎihé (white crane orchid — a continental variety prized for its elegance, Rare, occasionally available through Mantis or Unicorn trade). Materials at Rare availability require the artisan to actively seek them through CRAFT-equivalent gathering at specific terrain types or through purchase from specialised merchants. An arrangement featuring a Rare material is noteworthy — the provenance of the material itself becomes part of the arrangement’s story.

Composition generation: when an arrangement is created, the engine selects materials from the current season’s table using the following algorithm. Step 1 — determine material count by quality (1 for Normal, 1–2 for Fine, 2–3 for Exceptional, 3 for Masterwork/Legendary). Step 2 — weight selection by artisan personality. High Rei: weight toward elegant/refined materials (botan, fuji, ran, kiku). High Jin (Compassion): weight toward gentle/modest materials (nadeshiko, suisen, hagi). High Ketsui (Determination): weight toward enduring materials (matsu, ume, take). High Ambition: weight toward dramatic/showy materials (sakura, himawari, momiji). Step 3 — if a garden is the materials_source, prioritise materials the garden provides. Step 4 — check for canonical seasonal combinations. If the selected materials form a culturally significant combination (shōchikubai in Winter — pine, bamboo, plum; other canonical combinations to be defined per season), note this in the composition_description for social recognition. Step 5 — generate composition_description combining the selected materials, the vessel type (ceramic, bronze, bamboo, lacquerware — weighted by region and available resources), the structural form (rikka formal standing, nageire natural cascade, or simple offering depending on context and artisan emphasis), and an aesthetic phrase linking the materials to the season’s mood. The description is stored on the arrangement_object and visible through inspection.

### 57.29.7 Natural Degradation

Ikebana arrangements die. Lifespan by quality tier: Normal = 7 IC days (~1 IC week), Fine = 14 IC days (~2 IC weeks), Exceptional = 21 IC days (~3 IC weeks), Masterwork = 30 IC days (~1 IC month), Legendary = 45 IC days (~6 IC weeks). When lifespan_remaining reaches 0, the arrangement expires. The ikebana_slot empties automatically. Expired arrangements generate no topic and no social consequence &#x2014; flowers dying is natural, expected, and aesthetically correct. The impermanence of the arrangement is part of its beauty. This is Buddhist philosophy expressed through living art.

No maintenance action exists. Unlike gardens (seasonal MAINTAIN_GARDEN) or shide (seasonal degradation with replacement), ikebana arrangements cannot be maintained or extended. The artisan performs again to create a new arrangement. The replacement cycle is the mechanic &#x2014; an ikebana artisan at a busy court performs every 2&#x2013;4 weeks to keep the slot filled, generating recurring performance events and refreshing the visitor effects.

Degradation while in inventory: an arrangement that was not placed in a zone (because the performer lacked permission, or chose not to display it) degrades on the same schedule. It can be gifted through DELIVER_GIFT before expiry &#x2014; a fresh ikebana arrangement is a meaningful gift, especially if performed in front of the recipient (the performance IS the gift-giving moment). The standard quality-based Free Raises from Section 49 apply on DELIVER_GIFT. After expiry, the arrangement cannot be gifted &#x2014; dead flowers are not a gift.

### 57.29.8 Visitor Effects

A displayed ikebana arrangement generates visitor disposition bonuses toward the creator, same pattern as painting (Section 57.27.10). Quality scaling: Normal = +1, Fine = +2, Exceptional = +3, Masterwork = +4, Legendary = +5. Duration: 4 IC months (longer than the arrangement&#x2019;s lifespan &#x2014; the memory of beauty persists after the flowers die). Null/deceased creator handling: same rule as painting (Section 57.27.10a). Glory tick per 5 visitors, same as painting. Zone lord hosting bonus: +0.01 Glory per tick.

Stacking: the ikebana visitor bonus fires independently from all painting slot bonuses. A zone with a Fine kakemono (+2), Exceptional by&#x014D;bu (+4 with presence modifier), Fine fusuma (+2), and a Fine ikebana arrangement (+2) generates +10 total visitor disposition spread across four creators. Each slot has its own independent Glory tick counter.

Seasonal aesthetic synergy: when the ikebana arrangement is displayed alongside a seasonal kakemono whose season_affinity matches the current IC season, both the kakemono&#x2019;s seasonal harmony bonus AND the ikebana visitor effect fire. The arrangement and the painting reinforce each other &#x2014; a spring kakemono with spring flowers in the tokonoma is the ideal aesthetic combination. No additional mechanical bonus beyond the independent effects &#x2014; the synergy is that both are at their best simultaneously.

### 57.29.9 Shrine and Temple Spiritual Offering

At shrine and temple zones, a displayed ikebana arrangement functions as a temporary flower offering &#x2014; the original Buddhist kuge tradition that ikebana evolved from. The arrangement provides a temporary worship enhancement: quality-scaled Free Raises on PERFORM_WORSHIP performed at the same zone. Normal = +0, Fine = +0, Exceptional = +1, Masterwork = +1, Legendary = +1. This stacks with shide, gohei, religious painting, religious statue, and guardian statue worship enhancement. The bonus expires when the arrangement dies.

No passive Worship Points. Unlike religious paintings and statues which generate passive WP permanently, ikebana generates no passive WP &#x2014; the arrangement is a temporary offering, not a permanent installation. Its value is in the worship enhancement bonus (helping active worship succeed better) and in the visitor effects (aesthetic beauty for the sacred space). The absence of passive WP reflects the arrangement&#x2019;s ephemeral nature &#x2014; permanent spiritual credit requires permanent investment.

### 57.29.10 NPC Engine

Artisan-side: an NPC with Artisan: Ikebana rank 1+ who is AT_COURT evaluates ikebana performance through the existing SEEK_GLORY and ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION NeedTypes. Ikebana competes with other _performance_skill options (Acting, Perform sub-skills, Poetry recitation, Origami) in the standard performance scoring pool (Section 15.4, Category 2). The NPC selects whichever _performance_skill they hold at highest rank. If Ikebana is their highest, they perform ikebana.

Refresh evaluation: when the ikebana_slot at the NPC&#x2019;s current zone is empty (arrangement expired), ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION urgency increases by +10 &#x2014; the court&#x2019;s aesthetic environment has degraded and the artisan feels the gap. This drives a performance within the next few AP, refilling the slot. The cycle is self-sustaining: perform &#x2192; arrangement displayed &#x2192; arrangement dies &#x2192; urgency increases &#x2192; perform again.

Lord-side: lords do not commission ikebana. Instead, MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD (Section 57.27.17) evaluates the ikebana_slot as an additional condition. If the slot is empty AND the lord has cultural sophistication (Rei elevated or Crane/Phoenix clan), the lord evaluates whether an ikebana artisan is present at court. If yes: no action needed &#x2014; the artisan will perform naturally. If no: the lord evaluates SEND_INVITATION to attract an ikebana artisan, or increases the weight of court hosting to attract skilled visitors. This is a soft pull, not a formal commission.

Garden interaction: when an NPC ikebana artisan is AT_ZONE at a court with an adjacent garden, the garden material bonus applies automatically. The NPC does not need to explicitly gather materials &#x2014; the garden provides. This is the primary reason lords maintain gardens alongside courts: the garden feeds the ikebana tradition, which keeps the court beautiful.

### 57.29.11 World Generation

At game start, major courts with ikebana artisan NPCs in residence are seeded with a living arrangement in the ikebana_slot. Quality matches the artisan&#x2019;s skill. Lifespan is set to a random value between 1 and the quality&#x2019;s maximum lifespan &#x2014; the arrangement was made at some point before game start and has been aging. Crane Champion&#x2019;s court: Exceptional to Masterwork arrangement. Imperial Court: Masterwork. Provincial courts with ikebana artisans: Fine to Exceptional. Courts without ikebana artisans: empty slot. The arrangement will expire naturally during the first IC month and be refreshed (or not) depending on whether an artisan is present.

Ikebana artisan NPCs: Crane territories seed the highest concentration (ikebana is a core Crane court art). Phoenix temples seed ikebana-skilled monks and shugenja. Other clans: ikebana artisans are uncommon. A provincial court without an ikebana artisan has an empty ikebana_slot &#x2014; the slot exists but has no content, same as a wall without a painting.

### 57.29.12 Lifecycle Topics

Ikebana performance: the standard performance topic fires from PERFORM_FOR or PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE. No additional ikebana-specific topic needed &#x2014; the performance system already generates &#x201C;[Character name] performed [skill] at [zone name]&#x201D; topics. Arrangement expiry: no topic. Flowers dying is not news. Creator deceased: if the artisan who created the current arrangement dies while it is displayed, a Tier 5 topic fires: &#x201C;[Creator name]&#x2019;s final ikebana arrangement at [zone name] is fading.&#x201D; The arrangement completes its natural lifespan and then expires. No special handling &#x2014; the flowers don&#x2019;t know their creator died.

### 57.29.13 Ikebana Emphases

Two emphases defined. Rikka (formal standing arrangement &#x2014; the grand style for ceremonies and major court events): +1k0 when performing at a court or temple zone during a formal court session or ceremony. Nageire (informal natural arrangement &#x2014; the simple style for tea rooms and private quarters): +1k0 when performing at a private quarters zone, tea room, or intimate gathering (PERFORM_FOR with 3 or fewer witnesses). The emphasis reflects the two poles of ikebana aesthetics: formal grandeur and wabi-sabi simplicity.
