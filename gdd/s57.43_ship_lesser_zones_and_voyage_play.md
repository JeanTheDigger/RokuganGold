## 57.43 Ship Lesser Zones and Voyage Play

### 57.43.1 Design Intent

When a player character is a passenger or crew aboard a ship (aboard_ship_id non-null per Section 57.42.8), the ship itself becomes a Lesser Zone with its own ASCII map. The player cannot escape the ship — they walk the deck and below-decks, but every map edge is water. They remain aboard until the ship reaches a port and aboard_ship_id clears. This is not dead time; it is a constrained play window populated with named characters (the captain, other named passengers, crew) with whom the player spends their Action Points on social, developmental, and occasionally dangerous interactions.

Voyage duration is bounded by world-map design. The Empire’s coastlines and island chains are arranged so that most sea routes are reachable in 1–2 OOC days (4–8 IC days at the 1:4 ratio from Section 13). Longer open-ocean crossings exist (a Mantis Sengokobune voyage to the Islands of Silk and Spice may require 3–5 OOC days) but are deliberately rare — the world map does not force players into long idle voyages as a routine occurrence. When a player does take a long voyage, they know what they are signing up for and can prepare: bring companion players, bring crafting tools, secure good relations with the captain in advance.

AP math during voyage: per Section 14, a character has 2 AP per IC day = 8 AP per OOC day (4 IC days per OOC day). A 2-OOC-day voyage is 16 AP of play time for the passenger. That is a significant investment — roughly 28% of a full Winter Court week — and the player deserves meaningful things to spend those AP on while trapped aboard. This section defines what those are.

### 57.43.2 Ship Lesser Zone Architecture

A ship carrying at least one player character (or a player’s embarked NPC retinue generating interest in the map) materializes as a Lesser Zone with zone_type SHIP_INTERIOR. The parent is a Travel Route Greater Zone representing the current voyage leg (the water sub-tile the ship is currently crossing) — ships skip the Navigation Zone tier since there is only one play space aboard. The zone exits only to itself — the player cannot navigate away — until arrival at a port Lesser Zone (which occurs automatically when the ship reaches its destination sub-tile and docks).

The ASCII map is generated deterministically from ship_id + ship_class seed per Section 4.4.1 rules. Same ship always produces the same layout — a player returning to the same Yasuki merchant Kobune on a later voyage sees the same cabin configuration, same cargo arrangement, same deck layout. Physical changes (damage from combat, cargo transferred) are stored as deltas. Rough map dimensions by class: Sampan 6×3 tiles (single-deck, no cabin); Merchant Barge 10×4 (deck plus small cabin); Kobune 12×5 (deck, quarterdeck, cabin); Sengokobune 18×7 (deck, quarterdeck, cabin, below-decks cargo hold); Koutetsukan 15×8 (heavily armored interior, enclosed fighting spaces, no open deck); Atakebune 25×10 (multi-deck floating fortress, significant interior).

### 57.43.3 Ship ASCII Map Tile Set

Ship interior tiles reuse the existing Section 4.4.2 tile rendering conventions and align with the Ship Boarding template already drafted at Section 56.18. Key tiles: wooden deck (= in brown, standard walkable floor, +0 movement modifier); wet deck (= in dark brown, −1k0 on Athletics/movement checks per Section 56.18, does not affect Yoritomo Bushi, Storm Legion, or Sailing 3+ characters); mast (heavy vertical block in brown, impassable, LOS-blocking); rigging (% in white, climbable via Athletics/Strength TN 10, one tile up per Simple Action, grants Mounted/Higher attack bonus and +5 Armor TN vs ranged); quarterdeck (= on elevated platform, +1k0 attack bonus vs main deck below, the captain’s default position); cabin wall (wood wall per Section 4.4.2); cabin floor (tatami if an upscale cabin, wooden if modest); cargo/barrels (in brown, light cover, +5 Armor TN, does not block movement); brazier (* in orange, 1 tile, deck-mounted fire pot for lighting Bo-Hiya fire arrows — character must be adjacent and spend a Simple Action to light an arrow; extinguished in Storm and Typhoon weather, cannot be relit); boarding planks (= on 1-tile-wide bridge between ships, only during ship-to-ship boarding per Section 56.18); ship edge (~ water beyond, the character cannot walk off). Water beyond the edge is deep water (dark blue ~) per Section 4.4.2 — stepping onto it is effectively jumping overboard (see 57.43.6).

### 57.43.4 Ship Population — Captain, Crew, and Named Passengers

**Captain:** always present, positioned on the quarterdeck by default per Section 57.42.2. The captain is a named character (on the World Map per Section 22) and is co-located with all players and passengers in the ship Lesser Zone. Players may fire standard social actions toward the captain — CHARM, NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE, IMPRESS, LISTEN_REFLECT, DELIVER_GIFT, OFFER_FAVOR, DISCERN_NEED, PLAY_GAME, PROBE, READ_CHARACTER. A Mantis kobune captain is a Kobune Captain Advanced School member; a Yasuki or Daidoji merchant captain is a lower-ranked hired officer; a self-captained ronin is whoever the ship belongs to.

**Crew:** a ship has two tiers of crew. Bonge crew are the nameless sailor mass that populates every vessel: heimin sailors drawn from the ship’s seed, generated as local colour, same faces every voyage. They provide ambient interaction (shouts, work sounds, minor conversation), they row, they haul lines, they die in boarding actions. They do not participate in the World Map simulation. A player may converse with them informally (freeform chat, not an AP action) or EAVESDROP on their conversations (the Covert Action, Section 15.4 Category 6) to pick up rumours and gossip propagating through sailor networks. Named samurai crew are the second tier, and they are full-sheet named characters participating in the World Map simulation on standing assignment. A named samurai assigned to a ship is treated analogously to a named samurai assigned to a land Company per the Operational Hierarchy (Section 11.3.18) --- their operational_superior_id points to the ship’s captain, operational_hierarchy_type = ‘military’, and the assignment is standing rather than per-voyage. A new character sheet field tracks the assignment: assigned_ship_id (int or null, default null). When non-null, the character’s location_province_id tracks with the ship’s current sub-tile automatically when the ship sails, identical to the assigned_company_id pattern for serving-rank soldiers in land Companies. Between voyages, the named samurai crew reside in their home provinces and pursue their normal lives --- they have families, lords, skills, objectives, disposition relationships. When the captain sets departure_tick (Section 57.42.7), the captain notifies all named crew with assigned_ship_id pointing to their ship. If the crew member is co-located (same settlement as the port), notification happens through Daily Conversation (Section 12.6) --- free, automatic, the captain tells them in person. If the crew member is in a different settlement, the captain fires WRITE_LETTER with the departure_tick and port location. On receiving the notification (conversation or letter), the crew member’s NPC Decision Engine generates an ImmediateNeed: REPORT_TO_SHIP at priority 1, overriding all other objectives. The crew member fires BEGIN_TRAVEL targeting the port settlement. Travel time follows standard overland or sea rates. If the crew member cannot reach the port before departure_tick (too far away, travel time exceeds the window), they miss the ship. Their assigned_ship_id remains set --- they are still assigned to the ship, they just missed this voyage. The captain sails short-handed. A ship that departs without all named crew slots filled operates normally at the Army View level (Company stats are the ship’s stats, not crew headcount). The missing crew member’s absence matters at the Heroic Opportunity level --- fewer named characters aboard means fewer potential Heroic Opportunity triggers during naval combat, and fewer social interaction targets during voyage play. When the ship returns to port, crew disembark and resume their normal lives until the next departure notification. Reassignment off a ship follows standard operational hierarchy rules --- ordered by the appointing authority, rare except in cases of death, promotion, formal command change, or ship decommission. Which ships carry named samurai crew: command-appointment ships (Section 57.42.2) do --- military and clan naval vessels where the clan maintains a samurai naval tradition. Owner-patron ships (merchant vessels owned by a samurai patron and operated by a hired captain) do not --- the captain is the only named character on those ships, and all other crew are unnamed heimin. This matches the Rokugani social principle that samurai do not perform commercial labour. Mantis naval vessels (all Mantis ship classes) carry an additional standing crew slot: up to one Yoritomo Shugenja assigned to the ship for water kami commune (navigation bonus per Section 11.9), Storm Rider weather shifting via Heroic Opportunity (Section 11.7 Category 9), shrine tending, and voyage blessings. The one-shugenja cap is strict for crew slots only --- no Mantis ship may carry more than one Yoritomo Shugenja in its assigned crew. Passengers and retinue members who happen to be Yoritomo Shugenja are not crew and are unaffected by this cap; any number of Yoritomo Shugenja may be aboard simultaneously in passenger or retinue roles. This shugenja crew slot is exclusive to Mantis naval vessels. No other clan carries shugenja as standing ship crew. Non-Mantis ships may take a shugenja aboard as a passenger (with all the schedule and disposition complications that implies), but there is no standing crew slot for shugenja on Crab, Crane, Phoenix, Imperial Legion, or other clan vessels. Ship class scales the number of named samurai crew slots. Specific slot counts by ship class are PROVISIONAL pending balancing --- the design intent is that small warship Kobune carry a handful of officers, Sengokobune and larger carry more, Atakebune carry many, and merchant vessels carry none. Failure to fill all slots (clan stretched thin, recent losses) is possible and notable --- a Mantis Kobune sailing with only heimin crew because available Yoritomo Bushi are all mustered elsewhere is a real consequence of clan-wide military commitment.

**Named passengers:** drawn from the ship owner’s clan/family pool at voyage start. A Yasuki merchant vessel carries 1–3 other Yasuki merchants or Crab characters with merchant-adjacent business in the destination port. A Daidoji merchant vessel carries 1–3 other Daidoji merchants or Crane traders bound for the destination region. A Yoritomo vessel carries 1–3 other Mantis characters — often Yoritomo Bushi, Yoritomo Courtier, or Yoritomo Scoundrel on their own operations. A Tortoise (Kasuga Smuggler) vessel carries 1–3 other Tortoise family members or Imperial-sanctioned gaijin-facing courtiers. Named passengers are generated from the existing World Map NPC pool — when a named NPC fires REQUEST_PASSAGE for their own reasons (a Yasuki merchant needing to reach a Crab port to CONDUCT_COMMERCE, a Yoritomo Courtier attending a Mantis court session), the engine routes them onto a compatible ship. The player’s ship may therefore carry NPCs whose presence is driven by genuine world-state needs, not decorative filler.

**Passenger manifest generation at voyage start:** when a ship’s route is finalized (destination port set, departure_tick established per Section 57.42.7), the engine queries the NPC pool for characters whose decision engine would generate REQUEST_PASSAGE for that destination within the voyage window. Each candidate NPC fires REQUEST_PASSAGE through one of two paths: directly targeting the captain (standard accept logic per Section 57.42.7 — disposition, compensation, standing orders, personality), or targeting the ship’s owner or daimyo, who accepts and orders the captain to carry the passenger (owner-override or command-appointment directive per Section 57.42.7). No NPC is auto-assigned to the manifest — every manifest passenger went through acceptance evaluation by either the captain or the ship’s authority. Up to 3 accepted NPCs join the manifest, preferring clan/family matches to the ship owner. If fewer than 1 matching NPC is accepted, the engine may upgrade the search to include allied clans or bound-for-same-destination unaffiliated characters, so that the player rarely travels completely alone. The manifest is fixed at departure_tick; named NPCs who are not co-located at the port settlement when departure_tick fires are left behind and must wait for the next departure.

**Formal retinues are separate from the passenger manifest:** the 3-named-passenger cap applies to NPCs travelling on their own REQUEST_PASSAGE — unsolicited passage requesters. Characters travelling as part of a lord’s formal retinue (yojimbo, advisors, secretaries, named vassals accompanying their daimyo or Clan Champion by command) are not passengers in the REQUEST_PASSAGE sense and do not count against the manifest cap. A Clan Champion boarding an Atakebune flagship may bring an arbitrary retinue of named characters (practical cap bounded by ship class: Atakebune easily carries 10+ retinue; Sengokobune roughly 5–8; Kobune 3–4; smaller vessels correspondingly fewer). Retinue members are co-located with their lord in the ship Lesser Zone, interact normally, and leave the ship when the lord disembarks. The ship’s ASCII map accommodates them by ship class per Section 57.43.2.

**Multiple players aboard:** per standard Lesser Zone rules (Section 4.4.1), two or more player characters aboard the same ship share the same ASCII map and can see, approach, and interact with each other directly. Players travelling together is explicitly encouraged — it transforms the voyage from solo play into cooperative roleplay with shared investment in the journey’s outcome.

### 57.43.5 Passenger AP Availability During Voyage

A new context enum value, AT_SHIP, applies whenever aboard_ship_id is non-null. Action context availability is adjusted accordingly. Actions that require AT_COURT, AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, VISITING, or AT_CRIME_SCENE are not available at AT_SHIP (the ship is not a province, not a court, not the character’s holdings). The following specific action availabilities apply at AT_SHIP.

**Available — Social (Categories 1, 3, 4, 5):** CHARM, NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE, INTIMIDATE, LISTEN_REFLECT, IMPRESS, DELIVER_GIFT, OFFER_FAVOR, PERFORM_FOR, PLAY_GAME, GOSSIP, DISCLOSE, REVEAL_SECRET_PRIVATE, PUBLIC_INSULT (to a fellow passenger — though the captain’s authority on board can intervene), PUBLIC_DEBATE, EXPOSE_SECRET_PUBLIC, PROVOKE_EMOTION, READ_CHARACTER, PROBE, ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION (to another passenger via the captain), DISCERN_NEED. These all target co-located characters aboard the ship. Note: the ship is not AT_COURT, so PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE and PUBLIC_DECLARATION, which specifically require a court audience, are not available unless the ship happens to be carrying a visiting court (edge case — a clan champion aboard a fleet flagship might hold an informal court on the quarterdeck; arbiter discretion).

**Available — Covert (Category 6):** EAVESDROP (on crew conversations, captain and officers, or other passengers), INTERCEPT_LETTER (a passenger’s correspondence if accessible), SEARCH_QUARTERS (of any accessible cabin — the captain’s cabin is the highest-value and highest-risk target aboard), FABRICATE_SECRET. BRIBE_FOR_INFO applies to crew (a sailor might talk about the captain’s standing orders for a few bu of silver). All covert actions aboard carry the standard Honor and Infamy costs plus the additional risk that a confined space has few hiding places — detection is easier.

**Available — Personal Development (Category 11):** TRAIN (training on deck; subject to captain’s tolerance — sparring on a merchant Kobune is generally accepted, live-blade kata during rough seas is not), MEDITATE, MENTOR (teaching another character aboard), TREAT_WOUND (a wounded passenger or crewmember accessible), LEARN_THEATER_PIECE (if a teacher is aboard), COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE, COMPOSE_PAINTING or SCULPTURE or EMAKIMONO (if the character brought their tools — rigging a painting easel on a wet deck is plausible; sculpting stone is not, clay or wood is), CRAFT (for portable crafts — a Calligraphy commission, a small carving, a prepared Poison; bulk smithing or pottery is not practicable aboard). COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN and TEND_BONSAI are not available aboard (no terrestrial terrain; tending an existing bonsai brought along is permitted as a narrative action). APPLY_TATTOO with recipient consent is possible in a stable cabin on a calm-weather voyage; rough seas block the steady work. ANNOUNCE_HUNT, REQUEST_HUNT_INVITATION, CANCEL_HUNT — not available aboard (no hunt terrain). TRAIN_ANIMAL — available if the character brought an animal companion aboard and the companion’s species is ship-compatible (a falcon or dog aboard is plausible; a warhorse cannot be brought on kobune-class or smaller vessels; a Sengokobune with a dedicated stall may carry one warhorse).

**Available — Communication (Category 10):** WRITE_LETTER is available, but dispatch is deferred until the ship reaches a port. Letters accumulate in the character’s outbound queue and dispatch in a batch when aboard_ship_id clears. A shugenja aboard with Commune + pigeon spell variants or a rare falcon messenger may dispatch earlier. SEND_INVITATION follows the same queued-dispatch rule. ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (for lord characters) uses Civilian Orders rather than AP per Section 57.34 and queues identically. Queued items dispatch as letters from the port of arrival and follow the standard Section 12.7 letter delivery timing from that port to the recipient — they are not instantaneous on arrival. A lord who queues ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE on IC day 1 of a voyage, arrives at port on IC day 8, and whose vassal is 4 sub-tiles inland from that port, receives the order on approximately IC day 12 (8 voyage + ~4 overland delivery). Orders propagate from the port of arrival, not from the ship at sea. Implementers should model the sender’s effective location as the port of arrival, not as mid-voyage. Civilian Orders used to issue queued directives are consumed from the pool at the OOC day they were spent, per Section 57.34 reset rules.

**Available — Spiritual (Category 12) and Honor ****&**** Dueling (Category 13):** PERFORM_WORSHIP is available if a shrine icon or portable ofuda is aboard (a ship often carries a small Suitengu shrine to the Fortune of the Sea per Section 11.9 lore); worship targets the Fortunes or the sea kami. PUBLIC_ATONEMENT is available as a ritual observance. ISSUE_DUEL_CHALLENGE is available — a duel aboard ship is dangerous (wet decks, restricted space) and the captain’s permission is etiquette-required. When ISSUE_DUEL_CHALLENGE fires aboard, a pending_duel flag is set on both duelists pointing to the challenge record. The challenger (or the challenged) may then NEGOTIATE with the captain to obtain consent to duel aboard. Captain consent is a standard NEGOTIATE check with the captain’s personality weighing the request: Rei (Courtesy) leans against aboard-duelling (+5 to refuse); Yu (Courage) and Chugi (Duty) are neutral; a captain with Jin (Compassion) strongly refuses (+10 to refuse). A Rokugani captain also has to consider the political consequences — a duel between visibly opposed clan passengers on their ship reflects on them as host. If consent is granted, the duel proceeds on the deck with standard terrain penalties (wet decks, quarterdeck elevation usable by one duelist, restricted space reducing maneuver options). If consent is refused, the pending_duel flag persists and the duel is automatically queued to resolve at the next port — both duelists carry the pending_duel forward and it fires on arrival. SEEK_PRETEXT is available toward fellow passengers. INTERVENE_CAPTAIN is a captain-only reactive ActionID available aboard ship for stopping hostile actions between passengers. The captain decides case-by-case whether to intervene; the action is not autofire. Full reactive-path specification including trigger conditions, personality gate, roll resolution, and consequence structure is in Section 55.11 alongside ISSUE_DUEL_CHALLENGE and ACCEPT_TRAINING. A passenger who attacks the captain after a failed intervention faces the crew as a unified hostile force per Section 57.43.4 captain-authority provisions; this cost is generally prohibitive.

**Available — Travel and Passive (Category 15):** BEGIN_TRAVEL and CHANGE_DESTINATION are not passenger-firable aboard ship (the captain controls the ship’s route; a passenger who wants the ship to change course must NEGOTIATE with the captain). DO_NOTHING is available — rest aboard a calm voyage, or simply pass time.

**Blocked entirely:** all lord-tier actions requiring presence at own holdings — ORDER_LEVY, ORDER_DEPLOY, ORDER_FORTIFY, ORDER_BATTLE, ORDER_PATROL, ASSIGN_GARRISON, SET_TAX_RATE, SET_STIPEND_RATE, CONDUCT_COMMERCE, ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS, CALL_COURT, INVESTIGATE_PROVINCE, REQUEST_ART, REQUEST_PERFORMANCE. PURCHASE_MARKET is blocked (no market; though informal trade with a fellow merchant passenger is a narrative action resolvable through NEGOTIATE). CULTIVATE_GARDEN, MAINTAIN_GARDEN are blocked. Kolat operations at sea divide into two categories. The governing principle: if a Kolat action requires a specific settlement asset or zone infrastructure, it is blocked aboard; if it requires only co-located characters and their state, it is available. The complete enumeration of all 29 Kolat ActionIDs (Section 54.7c) follows. Person-to-person Kolat actions --- available AT_SHIP when the relevant characters are co-located (9 ActionIDs): TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR (any context, requires Tear inventory item), SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT (Tear condensed path only --- the VISITING/Hidden Temple path for full reports is blocked), ANONYMOUS_TIP (any context), ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE (any context), CONDUCT_CONDITIONING (requires isolated location with target present --- a ship cabin qualifies; the confined setting and inability to escape make voyages a thematically appropriate venue for conditioning), MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT (requires sleeper physically present), ACTIVATE_SLEEPER (requires sleeper within earshot), APPROACH_FOR_RECRUITMENT (requires target present, disposition ≥ Friend --- a voyage is a thematically appropriate venue for Kolat recruitment given the isolated, confined setting with a small named population), ARCHIVE_TOPIC (0 AP automatic, writes to character sheet special_data --- no physical infrastructure required). A voyage is an isolated, confined setting with a small named population and can be a thematically appropriate venue for Kolat recruitment and conditioning. Infrastructure-dependent Kolat actions --- blocked AT_SHIP (20 ActionIDs): OBSERVE_VIA_EYE (requires physical presence at the Hidden Temple), RUN_COURIER_ROUTE (requires the Silk network at a settlement), DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE (requires Hidden Temple or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS via network dispatch), ESTABLISH_DEAD_DROP (requires any settlement --- physical infrastructure for the drop site), UNDERREPORT_KOKU (requires AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, lord-tier), LAUNDER_KOKU (requires AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or commercial VISITING environment), TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS (requires AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or VISITING Hidden Temple), SECURE_ONI_EYE (requires VISITING Hidden Temple), ROUTE_VIA_DEAD_DROP (requires proximity to a Silk dead drop node), CHECK_DEAD_DROP (requires VISITING dead drop settlement), ROTATE_DEAD_DROP (requires VISITING any Silk-accessible settlement), CHECK_CONFIRMATION_DROP (requires VISITING confirmation drop settlement), SPONSOR_INSURGENCY (requires VISITING near target province --- contacts criminal proxies in or near the province), BRIBE_GARRISON_COMMANDER (requires VISITING the target province or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS with a letter channel to the target --- per Section 54.7c authoritative definition; AT_SHIP satisfies neither context), CONTRIBUTE_TO_RESERVE (transitively blocked --- fires only after CONDUCT_COMMERCE, which is itself blocked AT_SHIP), ARRANGE_PROXY_DUEL (requires AT_COURT or VISITING --- no court or social venue exists aboard ship), CONDUCT_PERIMETER_PATROL (requires provinces near Hidden Temple --- Steel Sect scouts only), RESURRECT_TOPIC (requires AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or VISITING any settlement with a Silk courier connection), USE_CLOUDS_EYES (requires AT_OWN_HOLDINGS --- the caster must be stationary and uninterrupted at their holdings), DELIVER_SEALED_LETTER (requires VISITING the destination settlement or AT_OWN_HOLDINGS with an established letter channel). Detection and exposure risks aboard ship are governed by the existing Secret System and zone_event_log --- the ship’s Lesser Zone tracks named-character actions and produces witness records normally, and the smaller population and confined tiles naturally increase exposure risk without requiring a separate shipboard modifier. All theater PERFORM actions requiring a court audience are deferred until port arrival (though rehearsal via LEARN_THEATER_PIECE continues).

### 57.43.6 Movement on the Ship and Jumping Overboard

The player moves tile by tile on the ship ASCII map per Section 4.4.2 standard rules. Water Ring determines base movement; wet decks impose the −1k0 Athletics penalty per Section 56.18 unless the character is Yoritomo Bushi, Storm Legion, or has Sailing 3+. Rigging is climbable. The quarterdeck elevation grants +1k0 attack against targets on the main deck below. Cabin walls are wood (Section 4.4.2), shoji screens where appropriate. The ship edge is always water beyond — deep water (dark blue ~) per Section 4.4.2 is impassable on foot.

**Jumping overboard:** a deliberate JUMP_OVERBOARD action (0 AP, Athletics or no skill) steps the character onto a water tile beyond the ship edge. Consequences depend on conditions: in a coastal sub-tile within 1 sub-tile of land, the character rolls Athletics (Swimming) + Strength TN 15 — success means they swim to the nearest shore over the next IC day (tracked as a drift-to-land state); failure means drowning or rescue by a ship if one is nearby. In open ocean, the roll is Athletics (Swimming) + Strength TN 25 each IC day; failure at any point is death by drowning. Characters without Athletics rank and without the Swimming emphasis suffer an additional +10 TN (they cannot swim). A shugenja with Water affinity may attempt Commune to call a water kami for aid; success saves the character. Deliberate JUMP_OVERBOARD is rare — this action exists primarily to permit desperate escapes from capture aboard hostile vessels (after pirate boarding, or to avoid being carried to an unwanted destination).

**Shipwreck drift and bounded survival:** when a ship is wrecked (sinks in combat, capsizes in typhoon, is destroyed by a Skull Tide, etc.) and characters are cast into the water, they begin an open-ocean drift state. The drift is bounded: the engine rolls a landfall/rescue check once per IC day, with cumulative chance increasing over time to reflect the geographic reality that Rokugan’s coasts and island chains are near most sea routes. Landfall-or-rescue chance: IC day 1 = 10%; IC day 2 = 25%; IC day 3 = 40%; IC day 4+ = 60% capped. The Mantis Islands and their archipelagoes raise this chance to 30% on day 1 for wrecks in Mantis waters. The chance resolves before the daily Swimming roll — a character who makes landfall does not need to roll Swimming that day. The drift has a hard ceiling of 6 IC days; beyond that, the engine resolves the character’s fate as death at sea (narrative discretion: body washes up somewhere, or is never found). Rescue chance increases if a named ally is searching (a companion player firing INVESTIGATE_PROVINCE or CONDUCT_RAID in the wreck sub-tile, or a friendly captain who is made aware). A drowning fellow passenger may be saved in the same IC day by another character firing a standalone rescue action (narrative, arbiter-resolved: usually Athletics + Strength TN 15 + drift TN modifier); this allows a group to save non-swimmer companions.

### 57.43.7 Voyage Events

Voyages are not inert. World state drives events that fire during the voyage based on the ship’s current sub-tile and any active pirate insurgencies in the region. Players are not shielded from the simulation while aboard — the world continues to produce content. Weather does not affect routine travel — ships move at their listed rate regardless of conditions. Weather matters only when naval combat triggers, at which point it functions as the naval equivalent of terrain per Section 11.9 Weather at Sea.

**Pirate interception:** when a ship enters or passes through a sub-tile with an active Pirate Fleet insurgency (Section 11.11), the engine checks interception. Interception chance = pirate fleet Strength × 10% per ship passing through. A Strength 3 fleet catches 30% of ships. A Strength 8 fleet catches 80%. This is a flat world-state probability, not a skill roll — the engine resolves it when the ship enters the sub-tile. If interception fires, resolution scales with the pirate fleet’s Strength:

**Low Strength (1–3) — Deck Skirmish:** one pirate boat pulls alongside, a handful of pirates climb the rail. Resolved on the Ship Boarding ASCII map as individual combat — the player fights 3–8 pirate NPCs (stat blocks from Section 54.8) on their own deck. No Mass Battle. No Army View. Just a fight on the deck. If the player defeats the boarders, the pirate boat withdraws. If the player is defeated, capture per Hostage System (Section 22.9) — pirates typically ransom samurai prisoners.

**High Strength (4+) — Naval Mass Battle:** multiple pirate ships engage the player’s fleet. Naval combat (Section 11.9) fires through the Army View at Company level. The pirate fleet fights using Kobune stats at a ratio of 2–3 Kobune per Strength point (per Section 11.11). Naval Heroic Opportunities (Section 11.7 Category 9) trigger through the Battle Table. If the player’s ship is captured, the player is captured per Hostage System (Section 22.9) or killed depending on circumstances. If the player’s ship sinks during combat (Koutetsukan ramming, major boarding collapse), Athletics (Swimming) survival checks apply at TN 20–30 depending on proximity to shore and debris availability.

Most voyages pass uneventfully. Pirate interception only fires when the simulation has produced an active Pirate Fleet insurgency in the sub-tile the ship is crossing — no pirates, no event. A routinely-uneventful voyage is the baseline; players do not expect combat every trip. Interception chance values PROVISIONAL pending balancing.

### 57.43.8 Arrival and Zone Transition

When the ship reaches its destination sub-tile and docks at the port, aboard_ship_id clears on all passengers. Characters arrive in the destination port’s Lesser Zone (typically a Docks/Waterfront zone per Section 4.4.1). The ship Lesser Zone persists in the background — the ship is still a physical object at the pier — but players who disembark transition to the port’s ASCII map. Queued letters, invitations, and deferred theater performances that were suspended during the voyage dispatch on arrival (Section 57.43.5). IC time elapsed during the voyage is 4 × OOC days spent aboard per the Section 13 ratio — a 2-OOC-day voyage consumes 8 IC days, and any topics, letters, or events that triggered on the World Map during those 8 IC days are waiting for the player to catch up on arrival.

**Dynamic route disruption on arrival:** world state evolves during the voyage, and the destination port may not be in the same condition it was when the voyage began. Possible disruptions on arrival: the port is blockaded by a hostile faction (naval blockade per Section 11.9), the port is under siege (the city or its harbour forts are engaged per Section 11.7), the port is hostile to the ship’s clan (political crisis broke out mid-voyage, the port now refuses to dock the ship’s flag), the port has been captured by pirates (a successful Pirate Fleet insurgency that seized it during the voyage window), or the port has been struck by a natural disaster (typhoon, tidal wave, fire) and cannot receive ships. When a destination port is non-dockable on arrival, the captain decides: divert to a nearby friendly port (if one is within safe sailing range of the ship’s class), attempt to run the blockade (naval combat per Section 11.9, captain rolls Battle and Sailing, high risk), retreat to the origin port, or hold offshore waiting for conditions to change (expensive in time, cargo begins to spoil, passengers grow impatient). The captain’s personality leans the decision: Yu (Courage) leans run-the-blockade; Seigyo (Control) leans divert safely; Chugi (Duty) leans retreat to fulfil the owner’s original intent. Passengers have no formal vote but may NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE, or BRIBE_FOR_INFO with the captain to shift the decision (per the port-only / mid-voyage authority rule in Section 57.42.7, the captain’s judgement remains final). A desperate passenger may attempt JUMP_OVERBOARD near shore per Section 57.43.6. Arrival disruption is a world-state event, not a routine occurrence — it fires only when the simulation has actually produced the relevant condition.

**Re-embarkation:** a character who wants to continue the voyage (the same ship onward to a further port) simply re-fires REQUEST_PASSAGE with the captain or owner at the new port; standard acceptance logic applies. A character who missed embarkation because they were off-ship when the ship sailed is left behind — their aboard_ship_id remains null and they pursue alternate transport.

### 57.43.9 Offline Characters on Ship

A player who logs off during a voyage remains aboard ship — their character stays at their last ASCII map position (or is moved to a safe cabin tile by the engine if their last position is dangerous, such as on a boarding plank). Voyage events continue to resolve via the engine; dangerous events affecting the offline character resolve by engine roll per Section 9 Design Intent. Other players aboard can continue to interact with the offline character as a transient NPC — the character’s disposition and responses follow their personality filter per Annex C. The offline character does not spend AP on autonomous decisions while offline — voyage time simply passes for them, and they arrive at port with unspent AP accumulated per standard offline rules (AP cap at 2 unspent AP per Section 14; voyage time beyond that is effectively DO_NOTHING for the offline character). Design note: voyages are play time, not transit dead time. The ship’s Lesser Zone is a full play venue --- AP may be spent on social actions with the captain and other named passengers, on TRAIN, MEDITATE, CRAFT for portable crafts, LEARN_THEATER_PIECE, PERFORM_WORSHIP at the ship’s shrine, and the other AT_SHIP actions listed in Section 57.43.5. A player who logs in during a voyage has 2 AP per IC day to spend on this content, same as any other play situation. The 2-AP offline cap applies to voyages identically to any other offline period; the voyage did not cost the player AP, logging off did. This is the intended design and no voyage-specific exception is required.

**Offline characters in active ASCII missions:** if a voyage event triggers an ASCII map mission (pirate boarding per Section 57.43.7) while another player aboard is still online, the online player plays directly and the offline character remains on the map as an engine-resolved participant. The offline character’s turns resolve by engine dice using their full character sheet stats: combat actions follow their school’s personality leans (Annex C) and their default tactical priorities, social actions follow personality filter logic, spell casting selects from their known spells by situational appropriateness. The offline character cannot be controlled by other players — their sheet runs autonomously per Section 9 Design Intent — but an online ally may interpose (defend, draw attention, intercept a blow) through their own actions, and may carry or drag the offline character to safety if incapacitated. If the offline character is killed during the mission, the death resolves normally (their player returns to find the character dead or Down); if captured, the Hostage System (Section 22.9) applies. Players who anticipate likely voyage danger and plan to be offline should coordinate with their companions before logging off — a Jin companion who knows the sleeper may prioritise defending them, while a Seigyo one might not.
