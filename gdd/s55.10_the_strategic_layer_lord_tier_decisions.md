## 55.10 The Strategic Layer — Lord-Tier Decisions

**STATUS:** LOCKED. Integrates GDD Sections 4.3.17 and 53.

At each seasonal tick, lord-tier NPCs run a Strategic Review producing directives: Reassign Vassal Objectives (with Information Transfer), Adjust Tax/Stipend, Initiate War Readiness Check, Seek Peace, Call Court, or No Change. Now also runs Province Triage (Section 9) as part of threat assessment. The Emperor NPC additionally checks for vacant Emperor’s Chosen and Jeweled Champion positions during the Strategic Review. A vacant Chosen position generates a FILL_CHOSEN_VACANCY high-priority task — the Emperor evaluates candidates using the Imperial Appointment weights defined in Section 11.5. A vacant Jeweled Champion position generates a CALL_CHAMPIONSHIP task — the Emperor schedules the relevant championship tournament, with timing determined by personality and current priorities (Section 11.5 Championship Trigger Conditions).

### 55.10.1 Emperor NPC Decision Profile — LOCKED

The Emperor is the most important NPC in the game. Unlike every other lord-tier NPC, the Emperor has no superior to assign objectives — he operates under Case 1 of the Primary Objective Self-Selection system (Section 55.26.1). His decisions shape the political landscape of the entire Empire. The Emperor’s behavior is driven by an Emperor Personality Archetype set on his character sheet at game start as part of the pre-written character roster (Section 22.8). The archetype is emergent from the Emperor’s Bushido and Shourido virtue combination — it is a design label, not a separate system. When an Emperor dies and a successor takes the throne, the new Emperor’s personality may be different, producing a dramatic shift in the political landscape. A Benevolent Emperor succeeded by a Tyrant changes everything. This is one of the most impactful events possible in the game.

**Emperor Standing Objectives (unique, all archetypes):** Maintain the Celestial Order — stability above all. Balance power among the Great Clans — no single clan dominates. Protect the Empire from the Shadowlands. Maintain the prestige and authority of the Imperial institution. Ensure the tax cascade functions — provinces feed, armies are supplied. These standing objectives are always present regardless of archetype. The archetype determines how the Emperor pursues them and which he prioritizes.

**Emperor Primary Objectives (generated from world state):** Fill vacant Chosen and Champion positions (Section 11.5). Resolve active Tier 1 crises. Select Winter Court host castle. Issue Imperial Edicts responding to aggregate court positions. Intervene in Succession Disputes that exceed 60 daily ticks (Section 22.5). Create the Shogun when conditions warrant (archetype-dependent). These are generated dynamically during the Emperor’s seasonal Strategic Review based on the current world state.

**Winter Court Host Selection — LOCKED:**

The Emperor selects which specific Family Castle hosts Winter Court each year during the Autumn Strategic Review. The selection evaluates every Family Castle in the Empire individually — Great Clan and Minor Clan alike. There is no two-stage clan-then-castle filter; a thriving Daidoji castle can outscore a struggling Doji castle. The competition for hosting is not a dedicated bidding mechanic — clans compete by maintaining prosperous provinces, building family prestige, cultivating the Emperor’s favor, and managing crises through existing systems. World state determines the outcome.

**Lore Constraint:** Winter Court is never hosted in the Imperial Capital (Otosan Uchi). It is always hosted at a Family Castle elsewhere in the Empire.

**Hard Disqualifiers (castle excluded before scoring):**

- Castle is located in Otosan Uchi.
- Province stability is below 0.3 (the province cannot sustain four months of hosting the Empire’s political elite — insufficient food, infrastructure, or security).
- Province is under enemy military occupation (active_crisis with occupation flag on the host province).

**Five Scoring Factors:**

Each factor is normalized to a 0–10 scale before weighting. The final score for each castle is the sum of (factor score × archetype weight) across all five factors.

*Factor 1 — Disposition:* The Emperor’s disposition toward the Clan Champion who controls this castle’s clan. For all archetypes except Cunning, higher disposition produces a higher score — linear mapping from disposition tiers to 0–10. For the Cunning archetype, disposition scores on an inverse bell curve: peak score (10) at Neutral/Acquaintance, tapering toward 0 at both extremes (Devoted and Enemy). The Cunning Emperor picks hosts who feel neither safe nor threatened — maximum political uncertainty.

*Factor 2 — Clan Recency:* The number of in-game years since any castle belonging to this clan last hosted Winter Court. All castles in the same clan share the same recency score. Normalized: 0 years since hosting = score 0; 7+ years since hosting = score 10 (cap). In the first year of the game, all clans score 10 (none have hosted). This is a clan-level factor applied identically to every castle in that clan — it prevents a single clan from monopolizing the honor by rotating between its own castles.

*Factor 3 — Province Stability:* The host province’s stability value, directly mapped to 0–10. Represents the province’s capacity to bear the logistical and economic burden of hosting the entire Imperial Court, clan delegations, retinues, and servants for 120 IC days. A province that passes the 0.3 hard floor but has low stability still scores poorly — the Emperor can host there, but conditions will be strained.

*Factor 4 — Crisis Relevance:* The highest momentum among qualifying crisis topics in the castle’s province, normalized: momentum 0 = score 0, momentum 100 = score 10. Whether high crisis relevance is attractive or repulsive depends on the archetype weight — a positive weight draws the Emperor toward crises, a zero weight ignores them. A per-archetype crisis type filter determines which crises count as qualifying:

- Benevolent: humanitarian crises only — famine, plague, spiritual insurgency, refugee displacement.
- Warlike: military crises only — active war, Shadowlands incursion, bandit/insurgent military threat.
- Cunning: all crisis types qualify.
- Iron: weight is 0; crisis type filter is irrelevant.
- Tyrant: weight is 0; crisis type filter is irrelevant.

*Factor 5 — Family Prestige:* The host family daimyo’s (Status + Glory) / 2, mapped to 0–10. Represents the social stature and political standing of the specific family being honored by the Emperor’s selection. Minor Clan families naturally score lower here but are not excluded — an extraordinary Minor Clan family head with high Status and Glory can compete.

**Archetype Weight Matrix:**

Each archetype distributes a total weight budget of 50 across the five factors. The weight determines how much each factor contributes to the final castle score for that archetype.

| Factor | Benevolent | Iron | Cunning | Warlike | Tyrant |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Disposition | 5 | 5 | 15 | 5 | 20 |
| Clan Recency | 15 | 25 | 0 | 5 | 0 |
| Province Stability | 5 | 10 | 5 | 0 | 10 |
| Crisis Relevance | 20 | 0 | 20 | 25 | 0 |
| Family Prestige | 5 | 10 | 10 | 15 | 20 |
| **Total** | **50** | **50** | **50** | **50** | **50** |

**Expected Behavior per Archetype:**

- Benevolent: Gravitates toward famine-struck or spiritually troubled provinces that have not hosted recently. Rotates fairly. Uses Winter Court as a tool for bringing Imperial aid and attention to suffering regions.
- Iron: Strict clan rotation dominates. Within the rotation, picks the most stable and prestigious castle. Predictable, orderly, and fair — no clan gains undue advantage.
- Cunning: Picks castles belonging to clans the Emperor has ambiguous relationships with, in provinces where crises force rival delegations into uncomfortable proximity. May select a Minor Clan castle if it maximizes political chaos among the Great Clans.
- Warlike: Drives toward active military fronts. Favors prestigious military families with high Glory. Does not care about hosting comfort — Province Stability weight is zero.
- Tyrant: Rewards loyal clans and prestigious families. Picks stable, wealthy provinces that fund a grand spectacle. Completely ignores rotation fairness and crisis needs. Winter Court as a display of Imperial power and patronage.

**Selection Output:**

The castle with the highest total score is selected as host. Ties are broken first by Family Prestige (higher wins), then by Clan Recency (longer gap wins). The result is recorded on the CourtSessionData as `host_settlement_id` and `host_clan`. A WINTER_COURT_ANNOUNCED topic is generated at the moment of selection, spreading through the topic momentum system to inform all clans.

**Timing:** Selection occurs once per year during the Emperor’s Autumn Strategic Review. This provides sufficient lead time for delegations to form and travel before Winter begins (IC day 241 of each year, per Section 13).

**Winter Court Invitation and Delegation System — LOCKED:**

Once the host castle is selected, the invitation pipeline proceeds in three phases: capacity calculation, delegation allocation, and personal Imperial invitations.

**Phase 1 — Delegation Capacity:**

Total hosting capacity is derived from the host family daimyo’s lord rank. Larger, more prestigious castles support more attendees. The capacity determines how many delegates each Great Clan may send.

| Host Lord Rank | Total Capacity | Emperor Retinue | Personal Invitation Pool | Great Clan Slots Total | Per Great Clan |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Provincial Daimyo | 70 | 10 | 3 | 57 | 8 |
| Family Daimyo | 105 | 10 | 4 | 91 | 13 |
| Clan Champion | 150 | 10 | 5 | 135 | 19 |

PROVISIONAL: specific capacity numbers are tuning values. The structure is locked.

Emperor Retinue (~10) includes the Emperor, Seppun guard, and Imperial advisors. These are fixed attendees and do not consume delegation or personal invitation slots.

The host family daimyo and their household are present automatically — they live in the castle and do not consume delegation slots. This gives the host clan a natural numerical advantage: their delegation allocation brings in vassals from the clan’s other families, while the host family’s own people are already on the ground.

**Phase 2 — Great Clan Delegation Allocation:**

Every Great Clan receives an equal number of delegation slots, calculated as: (Total Capacity − Emperor Retinue − Personal Invitation Pool) ÷ 7, rounded down. The Emperor’s personality is not a factor in delegation sizing — archetype preferences are already expressed through castle selection. Equal allocation is a matter of Imperial protocol.

Minor Clans receive a full delegation allocation only if their castle is the selected host. Non-host Minor Clans may only attend through personal Imperial invitations (Phase 3).

**Champion Delegation Selection:**

Each Clan Champion selects which vassals fill their clan’s delegation slots. This is a scoring pass over all available vassals using a universal weight vector — no personality influence on delegation selection. The rationale: personality already shaped the Champion’s vassal pool through years of objective assignments, school training, and political cultivation. Any competent Champion sends their strongest political agents to Winter Court regardless of personal preferences.

Universal scoring factors (total budget: 50):

- Court Skills (weight 15): aggregate of Etiquette, Sincerity, Courtier, and Perform skill ranks. Dominates because court skills are what win at Winter Court.
- Status + Glory (weight 10): candidate’s (Status + Glory) / 2. Higher-ranking vassals reflect clan prestige.
- Disposition toward Champion (weight 10): loyalty matters — the Champion sends people they trust to represent the clan.
- Agenda Relevance (weight 10): candidate has knowledge_pool entries or topic_positions relevant to any of the top 3 Winter Court agenda topics. Brings delegates who can contribute to the crises under discussion.
- School Type (weight 5): courtier schools score highest, shugenja moderate, bushi lowest. Reflects that Winter Court is fundamentally a political arena.

Candidates are scored individually. The top N are selected.

**Yojimbo Pull-In Rule:** After the top N are selected, any selected courtier whose assigned yojimbo (linked via `operational_superior_id`) did not make the cut is pulled in automatically. The yojimbo is added to the delegation, which may cause the final delegation size to slightly exceed the base allocation. This preserves the existing courtier-yojimbo pairing as an atomic operational unit.

**Phase 3 — Personal Imperial Invitations:**

The Emperor selects individuals from a candidate pool to fill the personal invitation slots (3–5 depending on castle capacity). Personal invitations are a direct Emperor-to-individual honor, separate from clan delegations.

**Candidate Pool:** Any named NPC in the Emperor’s `met_characters` list OR any named NPC the Emperor has a KnowledgeEntry about in his `knowledge_pool`. The Emperor acts on his actual information but is not limited to face-to-face acquaintances — reputation can reach him through letters, reports, and topic spread.

**Candidate Scoring Factors (total budget: 30):**

*Factor 1 — Disposition toward Emperor:* How the Emperor feels about this individual. For all archetypes except Cunning, higher disposition produces a higher score. For the Cunning archetype, disposition scores on an inverse bell curve: peak score (10) at Neutral/Acquaintance, tapering toward 0 at both extremes. Same logic as the Cunning host selection behavior.

*Factor 2 — Prestige:* Candidate’s (Status + Glory) / 2, mapped to 0–10. Social visibility and standing.

*Factor 3 — Crisis Relevance:* Candidate has knowledge_pool entries or topic_positions relevant to any of the top 3 Winter Court agenda topics. More relevant knowledge = higher score.

*Factor 4 — School Type:* Courtier schools score highest, shugenja moderate, bushi lowest. The Warlike archetype inverts this ranking — bushi score highest, courtiers lowest. All other archetypes use the standard ranking.

**Personal Invitation Archetype Weight Matrix (total budget: 30):**

| Factor | Benevolent | Iron | Cunning | Warlike | Tyrant |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Disposition | 5 | 3 | 10 | 3 | 15 |
| Prestige | 3 | 10 | 5 | 7 | 10 |
| Crisis Relevance | 17 | 10 | 12 | 10 | 0 |
| School Type | 5 | 7 | 3 | 10 | 5 |
| **Total** | **30** | **30** | **30** | **30** | **30** |

The top-scoring candidates are granted personal invitations up to the pool size for that castle.

**The Emperor’s Peace — LOCKED:**

Winter Court operates under the Emperor’s Peace. Wars between clans continue throughout the Empire — armies march, provinces burn, sieges persist. The war system, army movement, and all military ticks run normally. However, within the Winter Court settlement itself, hostile actions between attendees are forbidden.

Hostile-tagged ActionIDs targeting other attendees are blocked within the Winter Court settlement for the duration of the court session. An NPC or PC who violates the Emperor’s Peace — committing violence, orchestrating an assassination, or initiating a hostile act within the host castle — commits a crime against the Emperor personally. This is treated as the highest severity category in the Crime Severity system (Section 57.47).

The constraint is spatial, not temporal. It applies only within the host settlement. Delegates who leave the court settlement are subject to normal rules. The war does not pause — only the court is sacred ground.

**Sanctioned Duel Carve-Out:** CHALLENGE_TO_DUEL is exempt from the Emperor’s Peace block if the duel follows the formal sanctioning process (Section 4.8.1). The host family daimyo or the Emperor may authorize a sanctioned duel at Winter Court. An authorized duel is a judicial act under the Fortunes’ judgment, not a violation of the peace. The hostile-tag block checks for the sanctioned flag before firing — only unsanctioned violence triggers the Emperor’s Peace crime. This preserves the Kakita dueling deterrent (Section 2.2) and the formal duel as a legitimate dispute resolution mechanism during court.

**Covert Actions Permitted:** Covert ActionIDs (EAVESDROP, FABRICATE_SECRET, INTERCEPT_LETTER, SEARCH_QUARTERS, BRIBE_FOR_INFO) are not hostile-tagged and are not restricted by the Emperor’s Peace. Espionage at Winter Court is a core gameplay activity. The Emperor’s Peace forbids violence and overt hostility, not information gathering. Getting caught conducting covert operations at Winter Court is handled through the existing Secret System (Section 12.8) and crime detection mechanics — it is a separate offense from violating the Emperor’s Peace.

Crime severity for Emperor’s Peace violations is in Section 57.47. Knowledge pool and met_characters constraints are in Section 55.12 and 55.7.

**Winter Court Without an Emperor — Regent Substitution — LOCKED:**

If the Emperor dies before the Autumn Strategic Review, no host selection occurs. The Imperial Chancellor (Emperor’s Chosen, Section 11.5) acts as regent for Winter Court.

The Chancellor runs the host selection scoring pass using a fixed neutral weight vector: 10/10/10/10/10 across all five factors. The Chancellor is a caretaker, not a political actor — no archetype preferences apply. The Chancellor uses their own personality for any other decisions during court (e.g., agenda management), but host selection is mechanically neutral.

Regent Winter Court operates with reduced authority:

- No Imperial Edicts at close — only a sitting Emperor has edict authority. The top 3 crises are debated but produce no binding outcomes. Commitments are still made by individual lords per Section 15.2, but the Emperor’s unique power to issue binding edicts is absent.
- Court prestige reduced from 3 (Imperial) to 2 (equivalent to Clan Champion Court).
- All other mechanics — delegation allocation, personal invitations, host advantage, Emperor’s Peace, agenda ordering — function normally.

If the Imperial Chancellor position is vacant at the time the Emperor dies (or was never filled — a Cunning Emperor may have left it empty), Winter Court does not occur that year. A year without Winter Court is politically significant — crises continue accumulating momentum with no annual release valve, and no Imperial Edicts are issued.

A year without Winter Court does not reset Clan Recency counters — the gap still counts toward future host selection scoring.

**Winter Court Travel Logistics — LOCKED:**

The full timeline from host selection to court opening:

*~IC day 200:* Emperor selects host castle during mid-Autumn Strategic Review. Imperial summons letters are dispatched to every Great Clan Champion simultaneously via the LetterSystem.

*IC days 200–210:* Letters arrive at Champion seats. Delivery time varies by distance from the Emperor’s current location to each Champion’s seat — nearby Champions hear first and have more time to prepare. This is a real information asymmetry: a Champion close to the Emperor begins delegation selection days before a distant one.

*IC days 210–240:* Champions receive the summons, run the delegation selection scoring pass, and dispatch their delegations. Delegates begin travel toward the host castle. Personal Imperial invitations are also dispatched during this window.

*IC day 241:* Winter begins. Winter Court ceremonially opens at the host castle. The 15-day grace period begins — formal arrivals, greetings, ceremonies, and entertainment hosted by the host family. No agenda topics are debated during the grace period. Delegates who have not yet arrived continue traveling.

*IC day 256:* Grace period ends. Mechanical court actions begin. The three agenda topics are debated in the order set by the host Champion. Delegates who arrive after day 256 join court late — they missed some session days, which is a disadvantage but not a disqualification. They are added to the attendee list on the IC day they physically arrive at the host settlement.

*IC day 361:* Winter Court closes. Emperor issues Imperial Edicts on the top 3 crisis topics. Court session ends. Glory rewards are distributed to the host family and clan.

The total window from announcement to political start is approximately 55 IC days (~14 real-world days). This is sufficient for most of the Empire but creates genuine pressure for distant clans — a Crab delegation traveling from the Kaiu Wall to a Crane host castle in the northeast must leave promptly or risk arriving late. This geographic pressure is intentional and makes host castle location a strategic factor beyond the scoring system.

**Host Prestige and Advantage — LOCKED:**

Hosting Winter Court is the greatest political honor a clan can receive. The mechanical rewards and tactical advantages below are why clans compete for this honor.

**Prestige Reward (Glory):**

Hosting generates Glory for the host clan at the close of Winter Court:

- Host family daimyo: +0.5 Glory
- Host Clan Champion: +0.3 Glory
- All host clan delegates who attended: +0.1 Glory

No Status change — Status reflects permanent rank, not temporary honors. No disposition bonus from the Emperor or other Champions — the reward is pure social recognition through Glory.

PROVISIONAL: specific Glory values are tuning values. The structure is locked.

**Tactical Advantage — Home Ground Skill Bonus:**

All host clan delegates (including the host family daimyo and their household) receive a +5 flat bonus to Etiquette, Courtier, and Sincerity skill check roll totals during Winter Court. This represents familiarity with the environment — the host’s servants, the layout of the castle, the cultural setting, and the social rhythms all favor the home clan. The bonus is significant: roughly equivalent to a full extra kept die on an average roll. Visiting delegations must bring their best to compete on the host’s ground.

The bonus applies only during the active court session (IC days 241–361) and only within the host settlement.

**Tactical Advantage — Agenda Topic Ordering:**

Section 15.1 specifies that the top 3 highest-momentum global crises form the Winter Court agenda. The host clan’s Champion controls the order in which these three topics are debated. Earlier topics receive more court days:

| Agenda Position | Court Days | Share |
|---|---|---|
| First (host’s choice) | 45 | 43% |
| Second | 35 | 33% |
| Third | 25 | 24% |

Court days are counted from the end of the grace period (105 days of active court = 120 minus 15 grace). The first topic receives nearly double the third topic’s time. This is a significant strategic lever — the host Champion places the crisis they want the strongest response to in the first slot, and buries the topic that benefits their rivals in the third.

PROVISIONAL: the specific day split across agenda positions is a tuning value. The structure — host controls ordering, earlier topics get more days — is locked.

**WINTER_COURT_ANNOUNCED Topic — LOCKED:**

When the Emperor selects the host castle, a WINTER_COURT_ANNOUNCED topic is generated immediately. This topic serves as the information vehicle that tells NPCs where Winter Court is being held, enabling delegation selection and travel decisions.

Topic data:

- topic_type: WINTER_COURT_ANNOUNCED
- category: POLITICAL
- tier: Tier 3 (empire-wide significant political event, not a crisis)
- subject_character_id: the host family daimyo
- subject_role: NEUTRAL (the host is neither hero nor villain)
- clan_involved: host clan
- provinces_affected: host province
- momentum: starts high (PROVISIONAL — exact starting momentum value to be validated against the momentum scale in Section 16.1)

**Non-Positional Topic:** Unlike most topics, NPCs do not generate topic_positions on WINTER_COURT_ANNOUNCED. This is an Imperial decree already made — there is nothing to debate or take sides on. The topic spreads through the broadcast system so NPCs become aware of the information and can act on it (Champion delegation selection, delegate travel, GAIN_WINTER_COURT_INVITATION pursuit), but no position calculation runs. Implementations must skip position calculation for this topic_type.

The topic resolves (resolved = true) when Winter Court closes at IC day 361.

**THE FIVE EMPEROR PERSONALITY ARCHETYPES**

**Archetype 1 — The Benevolent Emperor (Jin-dominant):** Standing direction: protect the people, maintain harmony, avoid unnecessary conflict. Sends aid to famine-struck provinces without being asked. Fills vacancies quickly with competent candidates regardless of political debt. Hosts Winter Court in provinces that need attention rather than those that flatter him. Reduces tax burden when possible. Issues edicts that favor stability and civilian welfare. The Empire under a Benevolent Emperor is prosperous but may be militarily vulnerable — enemies see compassion as weakness. Shogun creation: reluctant. Will only create the Shogun if a Tier 1 military crisis persists for 3+ seasons AND diplomatic resolution has been attempted and failed. Mechanical effects: Imperial tax rate reduced by 5%. Vacancy fill delay: minimum (14 ticks). Disposition baseline toward all Clan Champions starts at Acquaintance (+15). Will not issue edicts condemning a clan unless presented with overwhelming evidence. Will never authorize Total War between clans unless the Shadowlands are involved.

**Archetype 2 — The Iron Emperor (Chugi/Meiyo-dominant):** Standing direction: enforce duty, maintain the Celestial Order absolutely, demand loyalty and competence. Fills vacancies based strictly on merit and skill scores — disposition matters less than capability. Hosts Winter Court on a strict rotation ensuring no clan gains undue favor. Enforces tax collection rigorously. Issues edicts frequently — laws, standards, expectations. The Empire under an Iron Emperor is orderly and strong but rigid — innovation suffers, dissent is crushed, and clans with independent streaks (Dragon, Unicorn) chafe. Shogun creation: as a matter of duty when the military situation demands it. Will create the Shogun if military readiness across the Empire drops below a threshold or if a Shadowlands incursion reaches Tier 1. Mechanical effects: Imperial tax rate unchanged (baseline). Vacancy fill delay: short (14–30 ticks), purely merit-based selection (skill relevance weight increased to 25, disposition weight reduced to 10). Disposition baseline toward Champions starts at Stranger (0) — respect is earned. Issues at least one Imperial Edict per Winter Court. Champions who fail to meet tax obligations face immediate Honor consequences.

**Archetype 3 — The Cunning Emperor (Seigyo/Dosatsu-dominant):** Standing direction: control information, balance clans against each other, maintain power through manipulation rather than force. Deliberately delays filling vacancies to keep candidates competing for his favor. Hosts Winter Court where it will create the most political chaos — inviting rivals into close proximity. Selects Chosen positions to maximize inter-clan tension (a Crane Advisor and a Lion Chancellor). May withhold tax redistribution from clans that displease him. Issues edicts that are ambiguous by design, forcing clans to interpret and compete. The Empire under a Cunning Emperor is politically vibrant but unstable — every court is a battlefield, alliances shift constantly, and the Emperor’s true intentions are never clear. Shogun creation: never. An autonomous military power is a threat to his control. Mechanical effects: Imperial tax rate unchanged but redistribution favors loyal clans (+10% to clans at Friend disposition, −10% to clans at Rival). Vacancy fill delay: deliberately long (30–60 ticks). Clan balance weight in appointment evaluation increased to 25. Disposition baseline toward Champions starts at Stranger (0) but shifts rapidly based on who serves him best. Extraordinary championships called more frequently — a tool for keeping Champions off balance.

**Archetype 4 — The Warlike Emperor (Yu/Kyoryoku-dominant):** Standing direction: expand the Empire’s military power, confront threats directly, reward martial achievement. Fills military positions (Emerald Champion, military commanders) first and fastest — political positions are secondary. Hosts Winter Court where military matters dominate the agenda. Redirects tax surplus toward Arms production. Issues edicts authorizing military campaigns, endorsing wars, and rewarding battle glory. The Empire under a Warlike Emperor is militarily dominant but economically strained — wars consume resources, provinces are levied heavily, and courtier-focused clans (Crane, Scorpion) lose influence. Shogun creation: never. The Warlike Emperor IS the military leader in spirit — he does not delegate autonomous military authority. Instead, he personally directs campaigns through the Emerald Champion and by issuing war endorsement edicts. In extreme cases, the Emperor NPC may relocate to an active war zone, generating enormous morale bonuses for his side (+15 War Score immediately, +5 Morale to all allied Companies) but creating a catastrophic risk if he is killed or captured (Tier 1 crisis: Imperial Succession fires immediately). Mechanical effects: Imperial tax rate increased by 5% (directed to Arms and Iron stockpiling). Vacancy fill delay: short for military positions (14 ticks), long for political positions (45+ ticks). Disposition baseline toward Champions: bushi-school Champions start at Acquaintance (+15), courtier-school Champions start at Stranger (0). Issues War Justification endorsements more readily — clan wars that might normally be condemned are instead tolerated or encouraged.

**Archetype 5 — The Tyrant Emperor (Seigyo/Ishi-dominant):** Standing direction: absolute personal control, crush dissent, demand total obedience, punish disloyalty with extreme prejudice. This is the Steel Chrysanthemum archetype (Section 2.1.3). Fills vacancies exclusively with personally loyal sycophants — disposition weight increased to 30, skill relevance weight dropped to 5. Hosts Winter Court as a display of power — attendees are watched, tested, and those who displease the Emperor face consequences. Tax rates are punitive. Issues edicts that strip clan autonomy, demand tribute, and impose loyalty tests. The Empire under a Tyrant is a pressure cooker — Stability drops across all provinces, insurgency risk rises, but open rebellion is suicidal because the Tyrant controls the Imperial Legions. Eventually, the pressure produces a breaking point — the game’s most dramatic political crisis. Shogun creation: only as a personal enforcer loyal to the Emperor, not an autonomous military leader. The Tyrant selects a Shogun with maximum disposition toward the Emperor and minimum independent ambition (Ishi or Ketsui Shourido virtue candidates are rejected — too independent). Mechanical effects: Imperial tax rate increased by 10%. Vacancy fill delay: variable — fills positions that serve him instantly, leaves positions that check his power vacant indefinitely. Disposition weight in all evaluations: 30 (loyalty is everything). Province Stability penalty: −2 per season Empire-wide (fear and oppression). Honor loss for disagreeing with the Emperor at court: −0.5 (normally 0). Champions at Rival disposition or below face manufactured Disgrace topics — the Tyrant fabricates accusations using the Secret System (Section 12.8). Breaking point: if average clan disposition toward the Emperor drops below Enemy (−31) across three or more Great Clans simultaneously, a Tier 1 crisis fires: Imperial Civil War.

**Shogun Creation Summary by Archetype:** Benevolent — reluctantly, only after prolonged Tier 1 military crisis and failed diplomacy. Iron — as a matter of duty when military readiness demands it. Cunning — never; autonomous military power threatens his control. Warlike — never; the Emperor IS the military leader and does not delegate that authority. Tyrant — only as a personal enforcer, not an autonomous commander; selects for maximum loyalty.
