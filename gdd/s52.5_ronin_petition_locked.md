## s52.5 Ronin Petition and Employment — LOCKED

This section expands s52 Part 5 ("Ronin Generation") with locked mechanics for
honor transitions, the petition-to-service process, lord evaluation criteria,
and permanent ronin eligibility. All values lock previously-zeroed PROVISIONAL
constants in `simulation/ronin_system.gd`.

---

### Part A — Becoming Ronin: Honor Transitions by Cause

When `RoninSystem.make_ronin()` fires, the character's Honor changes based on
`RoninCause`. Status always drops by −1.0 regardless of cause.

| Cause | Honor Change | Rationale |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| `LORD_DEATH_NO_HEIR` | 0.0 | Duty was fulfilled; death of lord was no failing of the retainer |
| `CLAN_DESTROYED` | 0.0 | Catastrophic loss absolves the retainer of dishonor |
| `VOLUNTARY_DEPARTURE` | −0.5 | Breaking loyalty to a living lord; calibrated to "Moderate breach of duty" (above minor social offences at −0.3, below major betrayal at −1.0) |
| `DISMISSAL` (formal release) | −0.3 | Lord found the retainer unneeded; public diminishment without specific fault |
| `DISMISSAL_DISGRACE` | −1.0 | Lord found the retainer unworthy; matches s57.49b dissolution spouse Glory loss (comparable public shame at Status scale) |

**Implementation note:** `RoninCause.DISMISSAL` and the new `RoninCause.DISMISSAL_DISGRACE`
are distinct enum values. Callers that currently pass `DISMISSAL` without distinction
default to the formal-release cost.

**HONOR_LOSS_ON_RONIN = 0.0** — applies to LORD_DEATH_NO_HEIR and CLAN_DESTROYED.
**HONOR_LOSS_VOLUNTARY = 0.5** — applies to VOLUNTARY_DEPARTURE.
**HONOR_LOSS_DISMISSAL = 0.3** — applies to DISMISSAL (formal).
**HONOR_LOSS_DISMISSAL_DISGRACE = 1.0** — applies to DISMISSAL_DISGRACE.

### Part B — The Petition Process

A ronin seeking a new lord must present themselves in person at the lord's seat of
government and petition formally. Letters of intent may precede a visit, but the
oath of service requires physical co-location.

#### Petition Roll

The ronin rolls **Etiquette / Awareness** vs a base TN modified by the receiving
lord's prior disposition toward the petitioner.

| Lord's Disposition toward Ronin | TN Modifier |
|----------------------------------|-------------|
| ≤ −1 (Stranger-cold or worse) | Lord does not receive the petition (automatic refusal before roll) |
| 0 (pure Stranger) | +5 (cold formal reception) |
| +1 to +30 (Acquaintance range) | 0 (standard) |
| +31 to +60 (Friend range) | −5 (favorable reception) |
| +61 or above (Close Friend/Ally) | −10 (warm welcome) |

**BASE_PETITION_TN = 20** — a standard formal social interaction with a lord.
Total TN = 20 + disposition modifier.

A disposition of exactly 0 (true Stranger who has no opinion) requires TN 25.
This reflects the real difficulty of convincing a stranger to take on a new
obligation. Ronin petitioning lords who already know them (positive disposition)
have a meaningful advantage.

#### What Success Means

A successful petition roll does not guarantee acceptance — it means the petition
was delivered with skill and sincerity. The lord then evaluates whether to accept
(see Part C). A failed petition roll means the ronin dishonored themselves in
the delivery; the lord's disposition drops by −3 toward the petitioner and no
further petition may be made to the same lord for one full season (90 IC days).

### Part C — Lord Evaluation: ACCEPT_RONIN_PETITION ActionID

A lord who receives a successful petition roll evaluates acceptance via the NPC
decision engine. This fires as a lord-tier action under the `FILL_VACANCY`
NeedType with ActionID **ACCEPT_RONIN_PETITION** (new).

#### Prerequisites (all must pass)

1. The petition roll succeeded (petitioner's Etiquette check cleared the TN).
2. The lord has at least one unfilled position in their retinue (`vacant_positions`
   is non-empty, or the lord is below their rank's maximum retainer count).
3. The lord is not under active ceasefire or Emperor's Peace restriction.
4. The petitioner is not flagged `permanent_ronin = true`.

#### Lord Acceptance Factors

The lord evaluates three factors, any of which can override the others:

**Hard reject conditions (lord refuses regardless of roll)**
- Lord's disposition toward petitioner is ≤ −11 (Rival or worse).
- Petitioner has a `TREASON` or `MAHO_USE` crime record with legal status
  `DECREED_GUILTY` or `FUGITIVE` (known to the lord via their topic_pool).
- The petitioner's former clan is at war with the lord's clan and the petitioner
  departed voluntarily (cause: `VOLUNTARY_DEPARTURE`).

**Personality-driven acceptance (lord accepts without further conditions)**
- Lord virtue is `Kyoryoku` (cooperative) and petition roll succeeded: accepts
  if disposition ≥ 0.
- Lord virtue is `Jin` (compassion) and petitioner's `seasons_without_income` ≥ 4:
  accepts the desperate ronin.

**Standard acceptance condition**
- Lord disposition toward petitioner ≥ 0 AND petition roll succeeded.
- Lord may voluntarily raise the petitioner's Status by 0 (retainer tier) or
  assign a specific role. Role assignment follows normal vacancy rules.

#### NPC Engine Wiring

`ACCEPT_RONIN_PETITION` is added to:
- `FILL_VACANCY` NeedType in `objective_alignment.json` at score **80**
  (below `APPOINT_TO_POSITION` (100) since accepting a ronin fills a lesser
  role than confirming a promoted vassal, but more direct than writing letters).
- `AT_OWN_HOLDINGS` context list only (oath of service requires the lord at
  their seat).
- `LORD_ONLY_ACTIONS` filter (retinue size decisions are governance powers).

AP cost: **1** (standard governance action).

Metadata: `target_npc_id` = petitioner's character_id (set by the lord's context
from `petitioning_ronin_ids`, a world_state key injected by DayOrchestrator
from characters co-located with the lord who are ronin and have made their roll).

### Part D — Formal Acceptance

When `RoninSystem.accept_into_service()` is called after a successful lord
evaluation:

1. **Clan change**: Character's `clan` becomes the lord's clan.
2. **Status restoration**: Status raised to 1.0 minimum (as currently implemented).
3. **Honor recovery**: **HIRING_HONOR_RECOVERY = +0.3**. Making a sincere oath of
   service is a virtue — calibrated at the same scale as "Showing sincere courtesy
   to enemies" (Table 2.3 social honor gain at that tier). It is not a full honor
   restoration — the taint of having been ronin remains unless further deeds are done.
4. **`original_lord_id` preservation**: The field retains its value for lineage
   tracking. It is not overwritten on second (or later) acceptance.
5. **Petition cooldown cleared**: `supply_ledger["petition_refused_until"]` is
   removed.

**Topic**: Acceptance creates a **Tier 4 POLITICAL** topic titled
"[Ronin name] enters [Clan] service." Subject: the ronin character. Subject role:
standard (not NEUTRAL — they are alive and acting). This is public social
information appropriate for court gossip.

### Part E — Permanent Ronin

A character is permanently ineligible for clan service (`permanent_ronin = true`)
under the following conditions. These are set by the simulation and cannot be
cleared.

| Trigger | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| Civil war RONIN faction | Already implemented: set in day_orchestrator civil war resolution |
| Treason conviction | `CrimeType.TREASON` with `LegalStatus.DECREED_GUILTY` |
| Maho conviction | `CrimeType.MAHO_USE` with `LegalStatus.DECREED_GUILTY` |
| Disgraced three times | Three `DISMISSAL_DISGRACE` events in character's crime_record (counted by `DISHONORABLE_CONDUCT` records where `source_action == "dismissal_disgrace"`) |

The third condition (triple disgrace) represents the social reality that a samurai
dismissed in disgrace three times is considered irredeemably flawed. No lord will
risk the social cost of taking on such a retainer.

Characters with `permanent_ronin = true` who attempt petitions always receive the
rejection result immediately. The lord receives no petition at all — the attempt
is recognized as hopeless before it begins.

### Part F — Ronin Seeking a Lord: NeedType Routing

Ronin NPCs pursue new employment through the existing objective system. When a
character is ronin (lord_id = −1, role_position = "") and has no active primary
objective, the NPC engine may self-select `FIND_NEW_LORD` as a standing objective.

**FIND_NEW_LORD** NeedType (new) maps to:
- `BEGIN_TRAVEL` (score 80) toward the settlement of the highest-disposition
  known lord who has `vacant_positions` — populated via `_inject_petition_targets()`
  in DayOrchestrator.
- `WRITE_LETTER` (score 60) to announce intent before traveling.
- `DO_NOTHING` (score 0) as fallback.

`_assign_ronin_standing_objectives()` fires daily alongside monk and magistrate
standing objective assignment. It assigns `FIND_NEW_LORD` as primary objective
for ronin characters who:
1. Are ronin (as defined by `is_ronin()`).
2. Have no active primary objective.
3. Are not `permanent_ronin`.
4. Have at least one known lord with disposition ≥ 0 and `vacant_positions` non-empty.
