## s52.5 Ronin Petition and Employment — LOCKED

This section expands s52 Part 5 ("Ronin Generation") with locked mechanics for
Glory transitions, the petition-to-retainer-service process, lord evaluation
criteria, and permanent ronin eligibility. All constants lock previously-zeroed
PROVISIONAL values in `simulation/ronin_system.gd`.

---

### Part A — Becoming Ronin: Glory Transitions by Cause

When `RoninSystem.make_ronin()` fires, the character's **Glory** changes based
on `RoninCause`. Status always drops by −1.0 regardless of cause. **Honor is
unaffected** — ronin status is a social-reputational event, not a moral failing.

| Cause | Glory Change | Rationale |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| `LORD_DEATH_NO_HEIR` | −0.3 | The samurai is now masterless through no fault of their own, but the public association with a failed line carries mild stigma |
| `CLAN_DESTROYED` | −1.0 | Catastrophic loss is visible to all; the samurai could not preserve their clan's survival |
| `VOLUNTARY_DEPARTURE` | Rank-scaled (Table 2.3 Disloyalty row) | Breaking loyalty to a living lord is a public act of disloyalty; severity scales with honor rank |
| `DISMISSAL` (formal release) | −0.3 | Lord found the retainer unneeded; public diminishment without specific fault |
| `DISMISSAL_DISGRACE` | −2.0 | Lord found the retainer unworthy; severe public shame at the court level |

**Implementation note:** `VOLUNTARY_DEPARTURE` calls `CrimeSystem.get_disloyalty_honor(character)`
and applies the result as a Glory change. Honor rank 0 = 0.0; rank 3–6 = −0.6 to −1.0;
rank 9–10 = −1.8.

**GLORY_LOSS_LORD_DEATH_NO_HEIR = 0.3** — applies to LORD_DEATH_NO_HEIR.
**GLORY_LOSS_CLAN_DESTROYED = 1.0** — applies to CLAN_DESTROYED.
**GLORY_LOSS_DISMISSAL = 0.3** — applies to DISMISSAL (formal).
**GLORY_LOSS_DISMISSAL_DISGRACE = 2.0** — applies to DISMISSAL_DISGRACE.

### Part B — The Petition Process

A ronin seeking a lord's service must present themselves in person at the lord's
seat of government and petition formally.

#### Step 1 — Auto-Rejection Check (before roll)

The lord refuses to grant an audience and the petition fails immediately if any
of the following conditions hold:

- The petitioner is flagged `permanent_ronin = true`.
- The lord's disposition toward the petitioner is ≤ −1 (any negative disposition).
  A lord must think at least neutrally of a ronin to grant an audience.
- The lord knows of a conviction (DECREED_GUILTY or FUGITIVE) for any of these
  crime types in the petitioner's record: `TREASON`, `MAHO_USE`, or
  `UNSANCTIONED_COVERT_KILLING`.

#### Step 2 — The Petition Roll (contested)

The ronin rolls **Courtier / Awareness** contested against the lord's
**Etiquette / Awareness**.

The ronin must also beat a minimum TN of 20 on their roll — even a contested
victory does not constitute a well-delivered petition if the presentation itself
fell below this floor.

**PETITION_MIN_TN = 20**

#### Step 3 — Presentation Modifier

The margin between the two roll totals determines a modifier to the lord's
effective disposition for the acceptance evaluation:

- Modifier = `(ronin_total − lord_total) / 5` (integer division, positive if ronin wins)
- Example: ronin rolls 28, lord rolls 18 → margin 10 → modifier +2
- Example: ronin rolls 22, lord rolls 30 → margin −8 → modifier −1

**PETITION_MARGIN_SCALE = 5** — +1 effective disposition per 5 margin.

#### What Success Means

A successful petition roll (ronin beat TN 20) means the petition was delivered
with skill. The lord then evaluates whether to accept the petitioner as a retainer
(see Part C). A failed roll means the ronin embarrassed themselves in the delivery;
the lord's disposition toward the petitioner drops by −3 and the ronin may not
petition the same lord again for one full season.

**PETITION_FAILURE_DISPOSITION_PENALTY = −3**
**PETITION_COOLDOWN_DAYS = 90**

---

### Part C — Lord Evaluation

A lord who receives a successful petition evaluates acceptance. The presentation
modifier from Part B Step 3 is added to the lord's base disposition for this
evaluation.

Any of the Part A auto-reject conditions that were not already caught (i.e., crimes
discovered since the audience was granted) also apply here.

Standard acceptance: effective disposition ≥ 0 (base + modifier ≥ 0).

---

### Part D — Formal Acceptance as Retainer

When `RoninSystem.accept_into_service()` is called after a successful lord
evaluation:

1. **Lord assignment**: Character's `lord_id` and `role_position` are set.
2. **Status restoration**: Status raised to 1.0 minimum.
3. **Glory recovery**: **HIRING_GLORY_RECOVERY = +0.3**. Being taken on by a lord
   is a minor public signal of worth. Glory recovers partially; full recovery
   requires deeds in service.
4. **Clan unchanged**: The petitioner's `clan` remains as-is. This hiring is
   acceptance as a retainer, not formal clan induction. Clan membership requires
   a separate formal induction ceremony (not yet designed).
5. **Petition cooldown cleared**: `supply_ledger["petition_refused_until"]` is removed.

### Part E — Permanent Ronin

A character is permanently ineligible for retainer service (`permanent_ronin = true`)
under the following conditions. These are set by the simulation and cannot be cleared.

| Trigger | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| Civil war RONIN faction | Already implemented: set in day_orchestrator civil war resolution |
| Five disgraces | Five `DISMISSAL_DISGRACE` events in character's crime_record |

**PERMANENT_RONIN_DISGRACE_COUNT = 5**

The five-disgrace threshold represents the social reality that a samurai dismissed
in disgrace five times is considered irredeemably flawed. Note: TREASON and MAHO_USE
convictions result in execution under Rokugani law, not ronin status — they appear
as auto-reject conditions in the petition system because a character with such a
conviction who somehow remains alive cannot receive service from any lord.

Characters with `permanent_ronin = true` who attempt petitions receive immediate
rejection. The lord does not receive a petition at all.

### Part F — Ronin Seeking a Lord: NeedType Routing

Ronin NPCs pursue new employment through the existing objective system. When a
character is ronin (`lord_id = −1`, `role_position = ""`) and has no active primary
objective, the NPC engine may self-select `FIND_NEW_LORD` as a standing objective.

**FIND_NEW_LORD** NeedType maps to:
- `BEGIN_TRAVEL` (score 80) toward a settlement where a potential lord resides.
- `WRITE_LETTER` (score 60) to announce intent before traveling.
- `DO_NOTHING` (score 0) as fallback.

**ACCEPT_RONIN_PETITION** appears under `FILL_VACANCY` NeedType at score **40**
(lords are cautious about ronin and accept them only when genuinely short-staffed;
this ranks below `APPOINT_TO_POSITION` (100) and `WRITE_LETTER` (60)).

---

### Part G — PETITION_RONIN Under FIND_NEW_LORD

Part F lists three actions for FIND_NEW_LORD: BEGIN_TRAVEL, WRITE_LETTER, and
DO_NOTHING. PETITION_RONIN also belongs under FIND_NEW_LORD at a higher score:

**PETITION_RONIN — score 90 under FIND_NEW_LORD.**

Part F describes the travel and announcement phase. PETITION_RONIN is the
terminal act: once the ronin has traveled to a lord's seat (VISITING context),
they execute the petition. Scoring it above BEGIN_TRAVEL (80) ensures that a
ronin co-located with an eligible lord petitions rather than immediately
traveling away to seek another.

**Scope:** The PETITION_COOLDOWN applies per-lord. Part B says "the ronin may
not petition **the same lord** again for one full season." The cooldown is
stored as `supply_ledger["petition_refused_until_<lord_id>"]`, keyed by the
specific lord's character ID. A failed petition against Lord A does not prevent
the ronin from petitioning Lord B on the same day.

**Auto-rejection vs roll failure:** The −3 disposition penalty and 90-day
cooldown apply only to **failed rolls** (Part B: "A failed roll means the ronin
embarrassed themselves in the delivery"). Auto-rejections (permanent_ronin,
negative lord disposition, known serious convictions) cause no consequences —
the lord simply refuses to grant an audience. No social damage occurs because
no performance was witnessed.
