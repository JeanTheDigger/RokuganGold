## 56.9 Retroactive Correction --- Failure Consequences

**CORRECTION (applies retroactively to Sections 56.3.7, 56.4.6, 56.5.6, 56.7.6):** The Failure state in earlier template resolution sections stated that Strength increases by +1 on player failure. This is incorrect for most quest seeds. A failed attack on a Ronin Bandit Uprising does not conjure more ronin out of thin air. The insurgency’s Strength grows through the existing Hidden Growth mechanic in Section 11.11 (+1 Strength per season automatically). A failed player mission means the status quo continues and that natural growth keeps ticking.

The corrected universal Failure rule is: no Strength reduction. The insurgency remains at current Strength. The failure consequence is political and personal --- the lord lost warriors, looks incompetent, wasted Action Points. Not a mechanical Strength boost.

Whether failure provides a Strength bonus is seed-dependent, not template-dependent. Some seeds may justify a +1 Strength on failure --- for example, a Maho Cult that repels an investigation may be emboldened, or a Taint Manifestation that feeds on violence and death might grow from a failed assault. These exceptions are defined per seed type, not per map template. RESOLVED: No. Regular failure never grants +1 Strength to any seed type. The universal failure rule applies to all seeds without exception: no Strength reduction, no Strength increase. The insurgency remains at current Strength and continues its natural Hidden Growth (+1 Strength per season, Section 11.11). Critical failure consequences (Section 11.11: +1 Strength, character wounds, Taint exposure for Maho) are unchanged and remain the only mechanical penalty beyond political and personal costs.
