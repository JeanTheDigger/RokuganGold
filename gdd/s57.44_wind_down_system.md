## 57.44 Wind-Down System

### 57.44.1 Overview

Every OOC day (every 4 IC days), each character — PC and NPC — must select a Wind-Down method. This is not an AP action. It fires on the OOC Day Tick alongside natural healing and Void Point refresh (Sections 57.31.7a and 57.32.2). The Wind-Down selection determines how much of the character’s Void Point pool is restored and what secondary effects occur. A character who does not select a method (or has no method available beyond the default) receives only partial Void recovery. The system creates a persistent incentive to seek settlements with good amenities, makes posting and location matter beyond resource production, and provides the mechanical foundation for the Geisha Intelligence System.

### 57.44.2 Timing and Selection

The Wind-Down selection fires once per OOC day, at the same OOC Day Tick that processes natural healing and Void Point refresh. For PCs, the player selects from the available menu — the menu is populated based on the settlement features present at the character’s current location. For NPCs, the NPC Decision Engine selects based on personality weights (Section 57.44.15). If no selection is made before the tick fires, the character defaults to Rest.

### 57.44.3 Rest (Default — Always Available)

Sleep in your quarters. 50% Void Point refresh. No cost, no benefit, no interaction. Available everywhere — castle, camp, ship, roadside, prison cell. Rest always fires regardless of the rested_last_night flag; even a character who fought all day and collapses into bed recovers something. The rested_last_night flag continues to gate the natural healing portion of the OOC Day Tick separately. A character running on Rest alone is permanently operating at reduced Void capacity — functional but never at peak.

### 57.44.4 Sake House

Drink at an inn or sake house. Requires: inn or sake house feature in settlement. 100% Void Point refresh. No Koku cost. Price — loose lips: one random topic from the character’s private known_topics pool is revealed to a randomly selected character who is present at the same sake house that evening. The character does not choose what they share or who hears it. The system selects, simulating the unguarded moment after the third cup. For NPCs, this creates a passive intelligence leak that players can exploit — parking a character at the same sake house as a target is a low-effort information-gathering method. For PCs carrying sensitive secrets, the tradeoff is real. Price — Dishonorable Conduct risk: percentage chance per visit scaled by Willpower. A high-Willpower samurai drinks and stays composed. A low-Willpower one may cause a scene. Consequences on triggering: Glory −0.2 (public embarrassment), disposition −3 with lord (your lord heard about it), Tier 4 topic generated (“[Character] caused a disturbance at [location]”). No Honor loss — getting drunk is undignified, not dishonorable. PROVISIONAL: exact Willpower threshold and Dishonorable Conduct percentage pending balancing.

### 57.44.5 Geisha House

Visit an okiya. Requires: okiya feature in settlement. 100% Void Point refresh. Koku cost scaled by okiya quality tier (PROVISIONAL: exact costs per tier pending balancing — see Section 57.45 for tier definitions). The geisha is trained to make the samurai feel at ease — conversation, music, dance, companionship. The samurai drops their On completely. Price — directed information exposure: 40% chance per visit that one topic from the character’s private known_topics pool is leaked. Unlike sake (leaked to a random person present), the geisha house leak is routed to the okiya’s intelligence pipeline — from the geisha to the okaasan to whoever controls the okiya’s handler channel (Scorpion, Kolat Silken Sect, or independent). The routing mechanics are defined in the Geisha Intelligence System (Section 57.45). The character does not know they leaked anything. Benefit — Glory +0.05 per visit. Frequenting a geisha house is a status symbol — it signals wealth and cultural refinement. Benefit — small disposition gain with the character’s assigned geisha NPC over time. Regular visitors build a genuine relationship, which makes the intelligence pipeline self-reinforcing: the more comfortable the samurai becomes, the more they eventually share. Comparison to sake: sake is free, guaranteed one topic leak to a random person (uncontrolled). Geisha costs Koku, 40% chance of one topic leak to a specific handler (controlled). Sake is sloppy and cheap. Geisha is expensive but the intelligence goes somewhere deliberate.

### 57.44.6 Shrine Prayer

Pray and make an offering at a shrine. Requires: shrine feature in settlement. 75% Void Point refresh. Prayer is discipline, not release — kneeling on stone floors in silence, confronting obligations to the Celestial Order. Spiritually grounding but not true decompression. Koku cost (small) — the offering. Smaller than a geisha house visit but not free, and over a full year of daily shrine visits the cost adds up for a character without wealth. Benefit — generates 0.5 WP toward the shrine’s Fortune, connecting to the Kami Worship System (Section 4.3.21). A character who prays at a Hachiman shrine every evening as their wind-down passively contributes to that Fortune’s worship tier. A fraction of what a full PERFORM_WORSHIP action produces, but AP-free and cumulative. No information leak. Prayer is private.

### 57.44.7 Temple Stay

Spend the evening with monks at a temple. Requires: temple feature in settlement. 75% Void Point refresh. Same spiritual discipline as shrine prayer — sitting with monks, listening to sutras, participating in the evening service. Benefit — +0.5 WP toward the temple’s Fortune (same as shrine prayer). Benefit — one piece of local information received from the Brotherhood network. A rumor from a traveling monk, a concern from a nearby village, news from a distant province that passed through the monastery. The information is unfiltered and may be trivial or valuable — the character does not control what they receive. This makes temple stay a low-grade intelligence option that shrine prayer cannot match. A magistrate posted to a remote province might choose temple stay specifically because the monks are the best information source in the area. Price — 25% chance one topic from the character’s private known_topics pool leaks into the Brotherhood network. Unlike the geisha pipeline (directed to a specific handler), temple leaks are undirected — the topic enters the general information flow of the Brotherhood and may surface anywhere. A monk in a Phoenix province might casually mention something a Crab samurai said three months ago. Slow, diffuse, unpredictable. No one is harvesting it deliberately. Comparison to shrine prayer: same Void (75%), same WP bonus, but temple stay is a two-way information exchange. You risk leaking a topic, but you receive something in return.

### 57.44.8 Bathhouse

Soak at a public bath or onsen. Requires: bathhouse feature in settlement. 100% Void Point refresh. Koku cost (small admission fee). Genuine physical decompression — hot water, steam, your body unwinds and your mind follows. Price — social exposure. Bathhouses are communal. met_characters fires with all characters present that evening. If a character you would rather avoid is soaking in the same pool, you have now met them — they can reference you in conversation, they know you were in this settlement on this date, you enter their known contacts. Price — uncontrolled disposition drift. +1 disposition with ALL characters present, no exceptions. Shared bathing is an equalizer — samurai and merchants, rivals and allies, all in the same water. The social armor comes off with the physical armor. The drift is mandatory. A character maintaining careful enmity toward a court rival softens by +1 per shared bathhouse evening. One point is trivial. But a character who frequents the same bathhouse as their rival over a full season drifts +12 toward them — enough to cross a disposition threshold and undermine a political stance. No information leak. You are soaking, not talking about secrets. The exposure is physical and social, not informational.

### 57.44.9 Garden Walking

Walk through a cultivated garden. Requires: garden feature in settlement (ties to the Garden System, Section 57.23). 75% Void Point refresh. Contemplation, not release — walking slowly through a designed space, appreciating the stones and moss and water. No Koku cost. No information leak. No social exposure. No secondary benefit. The clean, modest option for characters who want something better than Rest without any risk. The design hook: this makes the Garden System matter beyond aesthetics. A garden that a player or NPC cultivated is now a wind-down amenity for the settlement. A lord who invests in a beautiful garden is investing in their people’s wellbeing. A lord who lets the garden decay removes an amenity from their own garrison.

### 57.44.10 Tea House

Share tea with a companion at a tea house. Requires: tea house feature in settlement AND a willing companion present in the same settlement. 75% Void Point refresh. This is casual tea — two people sitting down with a pot and talking quietly — not the formal CONDUCT_TEA_CEREMONY action (Section 57.37). No Koku cost. No information leak — tea is formal and controlled; unlike sake, you do not lose composure. Benefit — +2 disposition with the companion. Sharing tea is intimate in Rokugani culture. Over repeated visits with the same person, the relationship deepens meaningfully. The tradeoff: reduced Void, no risk, relationship benefit — but you need someone willing to sit with you. A character without friends or allies in the settlement cannot use this option.

### 57.44.11 Go Parlor

Play go or shogi at a game house. Requires: game house or inn with a game room in settlement. 75% Void Point refresh — intellectual competition demands focus, not release. No Koku cost. No Honor loss (unlike gambling, this is a respected pursuit). No information leak. Price — public reputation. Go is a game of intellect, and when you play at a parlor your skill is on display. Contested Games (Go) or Games (Shogi) roll against whoever is present that evening. Win: +0.1 Glory (you demonstrated skill publicly). Lose: no Glory loss, but the winner gains the Glory, and everyone present knows who beat whom. Over time, a character’s win/loss pattern at the go parlor becomes known through Daily Conversation. A samurai who consistently beats a provincial lord gains quiet respect. A provincial lord who consistently loses to a visiting ronin loses quiet respect. Secondary effect — met_characters fires with the opponent. Go parlors cut across social boundaries — a merchant and a samurai sitting across a board are equals for the duration of the game.

### 57.44.12 Music

Play an instrument alone in your quarters. Requires: Perform (relevant instrument) Rank 1+ AND the instrument in inventory. No settlement feature required — available anywhere including wilderness camps, ships, and remote postings. 75% Void Point refresh. No Koku cost. No information leak. No social exposure — this is private, solitary practice. No secondary benefit, no secondary cost. The portable 75% option — a samurai posted to a remote watchtower with no shrine, no garden, no tea house can still play flute alone and get 75% instead of Rest’s 50%. The only requirement is character investment: training the Perform skill and carrying an instrument.

### 57.44.13 Incense Ceremony (Kōdō)

“Listen” to fragrances. One of the three classical Japanese refined arts alongside tea ceremony and flower arrangement. Rare incense woods (aloeswood, sandalwood) are heated and the practitioner identifies the scents — a meditative guessing game that demands total sensory presence. Kōdō genuinely empties the mind. 100% Void Point refresh. Completely private, zero social exposure, no information leak. Price — significant Koku cost. Rare incense woods are among the most expensive luxury goods in the Empire. The cost per session must be high enough that even a family daimyō pauses before doing it nightly — this option eats into political spending budgets. If the price is too low, every wealthy NPC picks incense every night and the entire information leak ecosystem dies for the upper class. PROVISIONAL: exact Koku cost per session pending balancing — the design intent is “genuinely steep, not merely expensive.” Requires: kōdō set (inventory item) AND rare incense materials (consumable, purchased at settlements with specialist merchants — major castle towns and cities only). The luxury tax on peace of mind.

### 57.44.14 Pleasure Quarter

Visit the entertainment district. Requires: pleasure quarter or entertainment district feature in settlement (only present in larger towns and cities). 100% Void Point refresh. Cheaper and cruder than a geisha house — the common entertainment district with courtesans, drinking, music, and spectacle. The floating world. Price — Honor loss. Visiting the pleasure quarter is beneath a samurai’s dignity. −0.1 Honor per visit. Small individually but persistent — a samurai who visits regularly bleeds Honor steadily. Unlike the geisha house (a refined cultural institution that grants Glory), the pleasure quarter is undignified indulgence. Price — high information leak. 60% chance of one topic from the character’s private known_topics pool leaked per visit. The character is drunk, off-guard, and the courtesans are less discreet than trained geisha. The leak is undirected — it goes to a random character present, same as sake house. Nobody is professionally harvesting it. Low Koku cost — cheaper than geisha, more than sake. The commoner’s luxury. Comparison to geisha house: geisha costs more, grants Glory, has a 40% directed leak to a handler. Pleasure quarter costs less, loses Honor, has a 60% undirected leak to a random person. The geisha house is a calculated investment. The pleasure quarter is a cheap indulgence.

### 57.44.15 NPC Wind-Down Selection

NPCs select their wind-down method based on personality weights. The selection fires automatically at the OOC Day Tick using the available menu at the NPC’s current location. Personality leans: high-Gi and high-Meiyo characters lean toward shrine prayer, garden walking, and go parlor (clean, disciplined options). High-Yu and high-Kyōryoku characters lean toward sake house and pleasure quarter (direct, unsubtle release). High-Rei characters lean toward tea house, garden walking, incense ceremony, and go parlor (refined, controlled). High-Seigyo characters lean toward bathhouse (physical, efficient) and geisha house (strategic social investment). Low-Honor characters lean toward pleasure quarter and sake house. Characters with Perform skill lean toward music when better options are unavailable. Scorpion clan NPCs carry an elevated geisha house weight regardless of personality — the clan’s culture normalises okiya patronage. Crab clan NPCs carry an elevated sake house weight. Crane clan NPCs carry an elevated tea house, garden walking, and incense ceremony weight. Phoenix and Dragon clan NPCs carry an elevated incense ceremony and shrine prayer weight. A character’s wind-down pattern becomes a behavioral signature that observant players can learn and exploit. PROVISIONAL: exact personality weight values pending balancing.

### 57.44.16 Character Sheet Field Additions

Section 22.3 character sheet amendment (added v484): (1) last_wind_down_method (string, default “rest”) — the wind-down method selected on the most recent OOC Day Tick. Used by NPC behavioral analysis (players can observe patterns). (2) wind_down_void_modifier (float, default 1.0) — the Void Point refresh multiplier applied on the most recent OOC Day Tick. Set by the selected wind-down method: 1.0 for full refresh options (sake house, geisha house, bathhouse), 0.75 for partial refresh options (shrine prayer, temple stay, garden walking, tea house), 0.5 for Rest. Read by the Void Point refresh calculation at the OOC Day Tick (Section 57.32.2).
