# Section 57.49 — Marriage Dissolution — LOCKED

## Overview

Marriage in Rokugan is a political institution arranged by lords, not a personal
choice. Dissolution is correspondingly rare and always carries social weight — it
signals that a political alliance has failed, that honour has been catastrophically
lost, or that extraordinary circumstances have intervened.

Four dissolution pathways exist. Each pathway specifies who triggers it, what
consequences fire, and what topic is generated. Children of the dissolved marriage
remain with the samurai parent (the spouse who does not move households). No
property transfer mechanic is modelled — property stays with the lord who owns it.

---

## 57.49.1 Pathway 1 — Lord's Command

A Family Daimyo or higher-ranked lord may order the dissolution of a vassal's
marriage for political reasons: alliance realignment, enemy-clan entanglement,
dynastic necessity.

**Prerequisite:** The ordering lord holds `lord_rank >= FAMILY_DAIMYO`.  
**Trigger:** `DISSOLVE_MARRIAGE` ActionID resolved successfully.  
**Targeting gate:** The ordering lord must have a current disposition of
`≤ −31` (Enemy tier, per s12.2) toward the other spouse or that spouse's
immediate lord. NPC selection uses the first vassal marriage meeting this gate;
falls back to `need.target_npc_id` if no gate-passing marriage is found.

### Consequences — Pathway 1

| Target | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Ordering lord | −1.0 Honor (Table 2.3 — Lord-Commanded Dissolution) |
| Each spouse | −1.0 Glory |
| Family-to-family baseline | −25 (PROVISIONAL) |
| Clan-to-clan baseline (cross-clan marriages only) | −10 (PROVISIONAL) |

The `−1.0 Honor` to the ordering lord is pre-applied at action time (Pattern B)
before the action result is returned. Glory losses and baseline penalties are
applied by the writeback pass in `DayOrchestrator._apply_dissolution()`.

### Topic — Pathway 1

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| `topic_type` | `"marriage_dissolved"` |
| `variant` | `"lord_command"` |
| `tier` | TIER_4 |
| `category` | POLITICAL |

## 57.49.2 Pathway 2 — Criminal Conviction

When a married character is convicted of a Tier 1 crime, their marriage is
automatically dissolved. No consent from the spouse's lord is required; the
conviction itself is grounds. No penalties fall on the non-convicted family.

**Trigger:** `DECREED_GUILTY` on a `CrimeRecord` with `crime_type` in
`{TREASON, MAHO}` (both Tier 1 per s57.47).  
**Evaluation point:** After conviction processing, before daily cleanup.

### Consequences — Pathway 2

| Target | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Ordering lord | None — no lord initiates Pathway 2 |
| Each spouse | No Glory or Honor loss |
| Family/clan baselines | No change |

The convicted character's social disgrace is already modelled by conviction
consequences in s57.47. No additional dissolution penalties are warranted.

### Topic — Pathway 2

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| `topic_type` | `"marriage_dissolved"` |
| `variant` | `"criminal_conviction"` |
| `tier` | TIER_4 |
| `category` | POLITICAL |

## 57.49.3 Pathway 3 — Monastic Retirement

When a samurai formally retires from samurai life by entering a monastery, their
marriage is dissolved automatically. Monastic vows are incompatible with the
social obligations of marriage.

**Trigger:** `is_retired_monastic = true` set on the retiring character.  
**Evaluation point:** Daily, after character state is updated.

**Current scope of `is_retired_monastic`:** As of implementation, this flag is
set only by the Phoenix Elemental Council enforcement path
(s55.10.3, Stage 4 acceptance). A voluntary RETIRE_TO_MONASTERY ActionID for
NPC self-initiated retirement is deferred — no GDD spec for NPC voluntary
monastic choice exists yet.

### Consequences — Pathway 3

| Target | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Retiring spouse | No Glory or Honor loss — monastic retirement is honourable |
| Other spouse | No Glory or Honor loss |
| Family/clan baselines | No change |

### Topic — Pathway 3

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| `topic_type` | `"marriage_dissolved"` |
| `variant` | `"monastic_retirement"` |
| `tier` | TIER_4 |
| `category` | POLITICAL |

## 57.49.4 Pathway 4 — Imperial Decree

During active wars, the Emperor may order the dissolution of cross-clan marriages
between belligerent clans. A marriage between a Crane and a Lion samurai is a
political liability when those clans are at war; the Emperor's Peace (s55.10) does
not extend to pre-existing diplomatic entanglements.

**Trigger:** Active war between `spouse_a.clan` and `spouse_b.clan`.  
**Evaluation point:** Quarterly strategic review (s55.10), via
`_evaluate_war_marriages()` which returns `IMPERIAL_DISSOLVE_MARRIAGE` directives.  
**Actor:** The Emperor (Imperial lord rank). The directive is processed by
`DayOrchestrator` the same day it is generated.

No human NPC lord initiates Pathway 4. The simulation generates it automatically.

### Consequences — Pathway 4

| Target | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Any lord | No Honor cost — Imperial Decree supersedes political accountability |
| Each spouse | No Glory or Honor loss |
| Family/clan baselines | No change |

The dissolution itself carries no social penalty because the Emperor's authority
is unimpeachable. The *war* (s53) is the source of diplomatic damage; Pathway 4
is a downstream consequence, not an independent political act.

### Topic — Pathway 4

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| `topic_type` | `"marriage_dissolved"` |
| `variant` | `"imperial_decree"` |
| `tier` | TIER_4 |
| `category` | POLITICAL |

## 57.49.5 Children

Children of a dissolved marriage remain with the **samurai parent** — the spouse
who did not move households on marriage (i.e., the one who held the family name
and clan before the marriage). The `children_ids` array on the `marriage` dict
is not mutated by dissolution; children stay in their existing family records.

No custody transfer mechanic is modelled. Children's `lord_id`, `clan`, and
`family` fields are not changed.

## 57.49.7 NPC Decision Logic

`DISSOLVE_MARRIAGE` is a lord-only action (Category 14 per s14, requires
`FAMILY_DAIMYO` rank).

**Objective alignment:** `DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP` NeedType, score 15.  
The low score reflects that dissolution is a slow, relationship-damaging tool
rather than a primary diplomatic instrument. Lords escalate to it when other
options have failed.

**Precondition gate (Phase 4c):** `lord_rank >= FAMILY_DAIMYO`.

**Metadata population:** `_build_dissolve_marriage_metadata()` scans vassals
for an active marriage where the lord's disposition toward the other spouse is
`≤ −31` (Enemy tier). Falls back to `need.target_npc_id` if no gate-passing
marriage is found.

**Context:** AT_OWN_HOLDINGS only. Dissolution is an administrative governance
act, not a court action.

## 57.49.8 Penalty Value Derivation

Numeric values for `DISSOLUTION_FAMILY_DISP_PENALTY`, `DISSOLUTION_GLORY_LOSS_SPOUSE`,
and `DISSOLUTION_CLAN_DISP_PENALTY` are formally derived in **Section 57.49b**
(s57.49b_dissolution_penalty_values_locked.md). Summary:

- `DISSOLUTION_FAMILY_DISP_PENALTY = −25` — Derived from s12.2 Rival tier bounds
  (−11 to −30): the penalty must produce structural Rival-tier hostility at the
  family level without reaching Enemy (−31+).

- `DISSOLUTION_GLORY_LOSS_SPOUSE = −1.0` — Derived from s46 Table 2.4 "Family
  Dishonor: Lose one Glory Rank." A forced dissolution equals Family Dishonor in
  social magnitude.

- `DISSOLUTION_CLAN_DISP_PENALTY = −10` — Derived from s12.2 Stranger/Rival
  boundary (−10/−11): the clan penalty caps at the boundary without forcing
  structural Rival status between entire clans.

## 57.49.9 Deferred

- **Voluntary monastic retirement ActionID (`RETIRE_TO_MONASTERY`):** Would set
  `is_retired_monastic = true` through the AP loop. No GDD spec for NPC
  voluntary choice to retire exists. Do not implement until specified.

- **Contested dissolution:** A spouse or their lord resisting the dissolution.
  Not modelled. All pathways are uncontested in the simulation.

- **Remarriage cooldown:** Whether a dissolved spouse may immediately remarry.
  Not specified in GDD. Currently no cooldown is enforced.
