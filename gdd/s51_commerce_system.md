# 51. Commerce System

Commerce is a Low Skill in Rokugan — samurai consider direct involvement in trade distasteful, leaving day-to-day transactions to merchant vassals and heimin. Yet Commerce knowledge is quietly essential. A courtier who cannot assess the value of a gift is politically blind. A lord who cannot read his domain's finances is vulnerable. A samurai who knows the price of things understands the world more clearly than one who pretends such matters are beneath him. The Commerce skill has four distinct mechanical applications in the game.

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Law ****&**** Order):** A contraband goods category exists alongside legitimate commerce. Opium, gaijin pepper, stolen goods, and smuggled luxury items generate Koku outside the formal tax cascade. See Section 11.4 (Criminal Economy) for the shadow economy layer, and Section 2.8.9 (gaijin pepper) for the most extreme contraband type. The Scorpion opium trade and Mantis piracy history are clan-specific economic features that interact with this system.

**COMMERCE (APPRAISAL) — ITEM VALUATION**

When a character examines an item — in Inspection Mode or through direct handling — they may make a Commerce (Appraisal) + Intelligence roll to assess its approximate market value. This roll represents the character's ability to recognize quality, identify materials, and situate the item within the economy of Rokugan.

Appraisal TN and result precision:

- TN 10: Rough estimate. The character knows whether the item is cheap, moderate, or expensive. No specific value.

- TN 15: Approximate value. The character knows the item's value within ±25% of its actual market price.

- TN 20: Accurate value. The character knows the item's value within ±10%.

- TN 25: Precise value. The character knows the item's exact market value and can identify its quality tier (Normal, Fine, Exceptional, etc.).

- TN 30+: Expert assessment. The character knows the exact value, quality tier, and can identify whether Rare or Legendary materials were used — even without a full provenance investigation.

Appraisal results appear in the Inspection Mode right panel alongside other known provenance information. A character who has successfully appraised an item knows its value — this knowledge is theirs and persists. They do not need to re-roll every time they look at the item.

Appraisal is relevant to gift-giving. A character who knows the precise value of a gift they are about to receive or present has a social advantage — they can calibrate their reaction appropriately and recognize political signals embedded in the gift's price. Receiving an unexpectedly expensive gift from a rival is itself a Tier 4 Political topic worth noting.

**COMMERCE (MATHEMATICS) — DOMAIN FINANCE**

Characters who oversee provinces, manage a lord's finances, or operate as merchant patrons use Commerce (Mathematics) + Intelligence for financial analysis. This application of the skill operates at the domain level rather than the item level.

Domain finance applications:

- Assessing a province's economic health from observable indicators — market activity, merchant traffic, population mood. Roll Commerce (Mathematics) + Intelligence vs TN 15 to gain a general assessment, TN 20 for specific estimates of Rice yield or Koku output.

- Detecting embezzlement or financial irregularities in a lord's accounts. Roll Commerce (Mathematics) + Intelligence vs TN 20. Each Raise reveals one additional specific irregularity.

- Estimating a rival lord's financial strength from their visible spending — quality of troops, gifts given at court, infrastructure improvements. Roll Commerce (Mathematics) + Intelligence vs TN 20 for a rough estimate, TN 25 for a detailed picture.

- Calculating tax obligations, tariff values, and tribute amounts accurately. No roll required for standard calculations — the skill simply enables the character to perform them without error.

Domain finance knowledge is valuable court intelligence. A character who can accurately estimate that a rival clan is running low on Koku has a significant political advantage. This information, once known, becomes a Tier 4 Economic topic that can be shared through the conversation and letter systems.

**COMMERCE AS COURTLY INTELLIGENCE**

A character with Commerce skill can recognize economically significant signals that others miss. This passive application of Commerce knowledge operates without a roll — it is simply part of what a commercially literate character notices.

Passive Commerce observations:

- Recognizing when a gift is priced significantly above or below the giver's apparent means. A minor provincial samurai presenting a 20-koku gift at court is spending approximately two months of stipend on a single gesture — this is immediately notable to anyone with Commerce knowledge.

- Noticing unusual trade patterns — a merchant caravan passing through at an unexpected time, unusual goods appearing in a market that typically does not carry them, prices spiking without obvious cause.

- Identifying when a lord's visible spending does not match their known income — new armor for all samurai retainers, lavish court gifts, extensive feasting — suggesting either hidden income sources or dangerous overspending.

Each of these observations generates a Tier 4 Economic or Political topic for the observing character. A courtier with high Commerce skill is quietly gathering economic intelligence through every interaction, building a picture of the political landscape that purely martial or social characters cannot access.

The rank 5 Commerce mastery ability — adjusting item prices by up to 20% — represents the pinnacle of this commercial literacy: the character so thoroughly understands market dynamics that they can manipulate them through timing, presentation, and knowledge of supply and demand.

**COMMERCE — PURCHASE PRICE REDUCTION**

When purchasing materials or items at a market, a character may attempt to secure a better price through commercial knowledge — recognizing fair market value, timing purchases when supply is high, or simply knowing what things actually cost versus what merchants ask.

This is not haggling. Samurai do not haggle — it is beneath their station. This is applied commercial knowledge: understanding the market well enough to pay the right price rather than the asking price.

Mechanic: Before purchasing, roll Commerce + Intelligence vs TN 15.

- Failure: Standard market price.

- Success: −10% on the purchase price.

- The reduction applies to the final price after any clan territory modifiers.

- This roll may only be made once per purchase. There is no retry.

- The roll cannot be made for Legendary materials — their prices are set by scarcity and political access, not market dynamics.

A character with Commerce rank 5 who successfully makes this roll and applies the mastery ability price adjustment stacks both effects — their intimate knowledge of commercial reality lets them consistently find the best possible price. This represents the practical advantage of maintaining commercial literacy in a culture that officially disdains it.
