## 55.11 The Reactive Decision Path

**STATUS:** LOCKED

Reactive events bypass normal scoring. Each type has a decision tree driven by personality + disposition. Duel: accept if Yu/Kyōryōku or disposition ≤ Rival. Favor: honor if Chugi/Makoto or disposition ≥ Friend. Court invitation: use Court Priority System (Section 15.8). Bushido-over-Shourido rule: in public contexts, Bushido virtue wins conflicts. In private/covert contexts, Shourido leads.

ISSUE_DUEL_CHALLENGE — Proactive Duel Trigger:

This is a reactive action, not a scored action. It lives in the reactive decision path alongside duel acceptance. The engine evaluates it after any of the following trigger conditions fire against the NPC:

Trigger 1 — Public insult received: A PUBLIC_INSULT, GOSSIP, or EXPOSE_SECRET_PUBLIC action has publicly damaged this NPC's reputation this session.

Trigger 2 — Family or clan dishonored: A Tier 3+ Dishonor or Betrayal topic involving the NPC's family or clan has generated and the NPC is aware of it.

Trigger 3 — Cornered in court: NPC has lost a PUBLIC_DEBATE (by any margin) and has no remaining court actions available this session that could recover their position.

Trigger 4 — Eliminate objective active: NPC has a primary objective of ELIMINATE_CHARACTER targeting the present character. Any slight may serve as sufficient justification.

When a trigger fires, the engine runs a secondary intelligence evaluation before committing:

Step 1 — Own fighting capability: Check Iaijutsu skill rank and whether a champion (high-Iaijutsu character, disposition ≥ Friend) is in retinue or present. If neither, suppress unless YU or KYORYOKU (they issue regardless of personal odds).

Step 2 — Target capability: Check known intelligence on target's Iaijutsu rank and whether target has a known champion. Acts on known information only — not meta-knowledge (Section 20). If no intelligence exists on target: DOSATSU and CHISHIKI suppress and route to GATHER_INTELLIGENCE first. ISHI and YU issue regardless.

Step 3 — Personality gate: YU issues readily. KYORYOKU issues if odds are even or better. MEIYO issues when the slight is public and Honor demands response. SEIGYO suppresses unless slight is severe (prefers other responses). DOSATSU suppresses without sufficient intelligence. REI suppresses in most cases. JIN suppresses strongly and seeks negotiated resolution first.

If all steps pass: ISSUE_DUEL_CHALLENGE fires as a reactive action consuming 1 AP. The target receives a duel challenge event triggering their own reactive path (accept or decline). The challenge specifies terms (first blood or death) and names a champion if applicable. Declining triggers Glory loss per Section 4.8.1 outcomes.

**ACCEPT_TRAINING — Reactive Training Response:**

When a Sensei NPC executes MENTOR, the targeted student receives an ACCEPT_TRAINING reactive event. This event bypasses normal scoring and is evaluated immediately using the following decision tree:

Step 1 — Co-location check: The student must be in the same location as the Sensei. If not, the event is invalid — the Sensei’s AP is wasted and no progress occurs. The MENTOR decomposition prevents this by verifying co-location before firing.

Step 2 — Skill relevance: The Sensei’s offered skill must be one the student can benefit from — the Sensei’s rank must exceed the student’s current rank in that skill. If not, the student declines gracefully — no disposition cost, no AP consumed.

Step 3 — Personality gate:

KANPEKI accepts only if the Sensei is 2+ ranks above the student in the relevant skill — a Perfectionist will not train under someone barely better than themselves.

KETSUI declines unless the student has a MENTOR_CHARACTER primary objective targeting this Sensei specifically — Determination values self-reliance and resists accepting outside instruction without a deliberate commitment.

CHISHIKI accepts eagerly — Knowledge never turns away a learning opportunity.

All other virtues: accept if the student has AP available and no higher-priority reactive event is pending.

Step 4 — Accept: Student spends 1 AP on TRAIN (targeting the offered skill). Accelerated progress applies per Section 48 Sensei multipliers. Both characters’ AP are consumed. The Sensei gains a small amount of progress at the reduced teaching rate.

**INTERVENE_CAPTAIN --- Reactive Captain-Authority Response:**

A captain-only reactive action available aboard ship. Fires via this reactive decision path; bypasses the normal Phase 2 Goal Resolution and Phase 5 Scoring loop; does not require a NeedType entry in Annex A. Category 11 (Personal Development). 0 AP cost. Context: AT_SHIP, restricted to the character currently in the captain_id slot of the ship. Primary skills: Intimidation or Courtier (captain’s choice, typically engine-selected to whichever scores higher for that captain). Paired trait: Willpower. Not hostile-tagged --- this is a containment action, not an attack. ActionID enum: total count 129 → 130 (99 standard + 31 Kolat).

Trigger 1 --- Immediate hostile action with co-location: a passenger fires a hostile-tagged ActionID against another passenger, and the captain is physically present within the same ASCII tile cluster or on an adjacent elevated position (such as the quarterdeck overlooking the action). The captain witnesses the attempt and has the opportunity to act before it resolves.

Trigger 2 --- Planned or announced hostile action: an action with resolution-lag writes a pending flag between passengers aboard (for example, ISSUE_DUEL_CHALLENGE writing pending_duel). News travels through the ship via crew and passenger information flow; the captain learns about the pending action regardless of immediate physical proximity. The evaluation fires during the window between declaration and resolution.

Personality gate (the captain decides case-by-case whether to intervene): Jin +10, Rei +10 --- both intervene reliably. Jin from compassion; Rei from the host’s duty to keep order among guests. Seigyo +8 --- intervenes when authority is publicly tested; may suppress when outcome serves their own interests, evaluated contextually. Gi +5, Chugi +5 --- intervene when intervention serves integrity or duty to the voyage. Kyoryoku −5 --- may see passenger conflict as useful or entertaining and stand aside. Ishi −3 --- interpersonal drama between passengers is beneath their attention. All other virtues: no automatic lean; the case-by-case context dominates. The captain’s personality evaluation is not required to produce intervention; a captain who chooses not to intervene simply lets the hostile action resolve normally with no engine event.

If the captain elects to intervene, INTERVENE_CAPTAIN fires. Contested roll: captain’s Intimidation or Courtier + Willpower versus the aggressor’s Willpower. Success: the hostile action is blocked. An action_block entry writes to the aggressor’s sheet per Section 29.15.24, preventing hostile-tagged actions against the specific victim for the remainder of the voyage. Break conditions for the action_block entry: (1) voyage_end — aboard_ship_id clears on disembarkation, the block dissolves with the captain’s authority; (2) captain_permission — the captain explicitly lifts the restriction, typically via NEGOTIATE from the aggressor requesting reinstatement of their freedom of action; (3) victim_initiates_hostile — if the protected character initiates a hostile-tagged action against the aggressor, the block breaks immediately under the self-defence principle (same pattern as Ide R5 peace_locked break condition); (4) captain_incapacitated — the captain reaches Down, Out, or is killed, dissolving their authority aboard and all action_blocks they wrote. Failure: the hostile action resolves normally. The captain suffers Glory −0.3. All named witnesses aboard suffer disposition −5 toward the captain (one-time temporary modifier, standard decay). The aggressor’s disposition toward the captain drops by −10 (one-time temporary modifier, standard decay). Critical failure: when the aggressor’s contested total exceeds the captain’s by 10 or more, the intervention is not just unsuccessful but humiliating. All standard failure consequences apply (Glory −0.3, disposition −5 witnesses, disposition −10 aggressor). Additional Glory −0.2 (total −0.5). A Tier 4 topic generates: “[Captain] was publicly defied aboard [ship name].” Propagates through normal topic channels once the ship reaches port. Subsequent INTERVENE_CAPTAIN rolls this voyage suffer −1k0 (cumulative per critical failure, clears on disembarkation). The event writes to zone_event_log regardless of outcome.

Aggressor’s response after failed intervention: the aggressor may attempt to attack the captain directly. Mechanically this is not blocked; practically it is self-limiting. Per Section 57.43.4, the ship’s crew responds as a unified hostile force against any passenger who attacks the captain, and the political and legal consequences (immediate Tier 2 crisis topic, severe Honor penalty, separate crime under Rokugani law adjudicated on arrival at port) make the action prohibitively costly. No special rule is required; the structure of shipboard authority handles it.
