## 54.8 Ronin of the Empire

Ronin are samurai with no clan of their own. Some were cast out from their clan for sins real or imagined, while others were born of samurai blood but outside the confines of an existing clan or family. They are just as susceptible to good and evil as any others. Unfortunately, the circumstances of a ronin’s life are often extremely difficult or perilous, and few are equal to the task of surviving without losing their honor and virtue. Many among their ranks turn to selfishness, greed, and even criminal villainy to survive. The ronin population waxes and wanes with the tides of war: during times of peace it slowly but steadily increases, while war both provides employment for ronin and kills many of them off.

The single most dangerous aspect of a ronin enemy is the simple fact that a wave man has nothing to lose. Their family honor and reputation cannot be threatened, and no rival can hold a lord’s favor over the head of such a man. A samurai who has truly earned the enmity of a ronin faces an enemy motivated solely by honor or vengeance, unconstrained in his actions and often quite willing to throw away his life and honor if doing so means his foe will be defeated.

Ronin brotherhoods (otokodate, or “manly fellows”) exist as small groups who band together for protection, mutual benefit, banditry, or out of devotion to some ideal. There are generally only a few such groups in existence in each generation, and they seldom survive beyond the lifetimes of their founders. Mechanically, ronin who are fortunate enough to find a second or third Path do not have to purchase Multiple Schools or any other Advantage in order to learn the Technique. The price of learning a Path is the time and effort in campaign play to track it down and persuade its masters to teach it.

### Rank 2 Ronin Paths

CLAWS OF THE WOLF [Bushi]. A small group of vicious vigilantes who subscribe to the ideals of Toturi Sezaru, an Imperial heir who lived during the mid to late twelfth century and whose death helped usher in the Race for the Throne. They wear ceramic masks similar to the one made famous by Sezaru himself. Requirements: Wary, Driven. Technique Rank: 2. HUNTING THE DARKNESS – You gain a bonus of +2k2 to the total of your first attack roll in a skirmish if the opponent you are attacking is an individual you know without question is guilty of a violation of Imperial law. Generalization: Can be used by any group of vigilantes or devotees to a ruthless figure.

EAST WIND [Bushi]. A benevolent ronin brotherhood devoted to protecting the common people of East Hub Village, a city on the outskirts of Otosan Uchi. They found gainful employment with wealthy merchants and used their pay to purchase holdings within the city. After the destruction of Otosan Uchi in the mid-twelfth century, the East Wind brotherhood endured and eventually the Unicorn Clan began revitalizing the city and sponsoring the ronin. Requirements: Commerce 1+, Horsemanship 2+, Honor 4+. Technique Rank: 2. SHIELDED BY THE EAST – When in the Defense or Full Defense Stances and performing the Guard Action, you increase both your Armor TN and that of your charge by 5.

EYES OF NANASHI [Bushi]. Nanashi Mura (“Anonymous Village”) sits on the edge of Dragon Clan territory. The Dragon have always permitted the ronin who call the city home to rule the village’s affairs. The village is overseen by a council of ronin and their will is enforced by a group of warriors and magistrates known as the Eyes of Nanashi. Requirements: Lore: Law 3, Honor Rank 4+. Technique Rank: 2. STRIKE THE CENTER – The Eyes of Nanashi prefer non-lethal means of defending the city. You gain a bonus of +1k1 on any attack roll intended to disable rather than kill.

MOONLESS RIDERS [Bushi]. An extremely small and specialized group of ronin comprised primarily of former Unicorn, Lion, and Dragon samurai who specialize in cavalry tactics – but unlike most samurai, they train at night. As a result, they are among the preeminent nocturnal scouts and couriers in the Empire. Requirements: Horsemanship 3+, Way of the Land. Technique Rank: 2. MOVING THE SHADOW – You do not suffer any penalties that might be incurred due to natural darkness. When active at night or in other forms of darkness, you gain a bonus of +2k1 instead of +1k1 when you spend a Void Point to enhance any non-Weapon Bugei Skill.

SILENT BLADES [Bushi]. Despite their self-image as elite hired killers, the Silent Blades are in truth little more than rank amateurs at the arts of infiltration and assassination. They are actually pawns of a more secretive and skilled order known as Shadowed Steel, which is in turn a pawn of yet another unknown force. Requirements: Ninjutsu 3+, Stealth 3+. Technique Rank: 2. BLACK HEARTS, RED BLADES – If you are attacking an opponent who is unaware of your presence (e.g. a foe you have defeated with a Contested Stealth Skill Roll to remain undetected), you may add your Stealth Skill Rank to the total of all attack and damage rolls made against that opponent for the duration of this skirmish.

### Rank 3 Ronin Paths

BROKEN GUARD [Bushi]. In the aftermath of the infamous Scorpion Clan Coup, when the Akodo family of the Lion Clan was dissolved, some Akodo took up the life of the ronin. A small group banded together for mutual defense under former officer Saigorei, a man with a deep grudge against the Unicorn Clan. They honed their skills with an emphasis on anti-cavalry maneuvers and saw regular employment throughout the Clan War era. When the Akodo family finally reformed, many rejoined it. Requirements: Polearms 3+, Heart of Vengeance (Unicorn). Technique Rank: 3. THE TIGER’S TEETH – You may make a melee attack as a Simple Action when using a polearm. You gain a bonus of +1k1 on attack rolls made against targets on horseback (or mounted on similarly-sized creatures). Generalization: Can represent any anti-cavalry group or a group with a grudge against the Unicorn Clan.

HIDDEN SWORD [Bushi]. A large group that patrols the outskirts of the Shinomen Mori, trying to protect the people in the lands around it from bandits and the various enigmatic creatures emerging periodically from the Shinomen. Their area of operation requires them to interact with the Scorpion, Crab, and Unicorn clans fairly regularly. The group has been completely wiped out by the Forest Killers on at least one occasion, but was reformed by its leader’s sons to carry on his work. Requirements: Hero of the People. Technique Rank: 3. KEEPING THE PEACE – When fighting against an opponent with lower Honor Rank than yours, you may make melee attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action when wielding weapons with the Samurai keyword.

MACHI-KANSHISHA [Bushi]. The guardians of the City of the Rich Frog, a major trade hub that sits in the narrow divide between the holdings of the Lion and Unicorn clans. They are named for their preferred weapon: a long iron smoking pipe. Because of the militant nature of the two neighboring clans, the Machi-kanshisha prefer to use their namesake smoking pipes as non-lethal weapons to subdue their foes rather than kill them. In the twelfth century, the City of the Rich Frog is annexed by the Lion Clan and the Kaeru ronin family become vassals of the Ikoma family. Requirements: Athletics 2+, Staves 3+. Technique Rank: 3. SMOKE AND MIRRORS – When using an iron pipe, you gain a Free Raise on any Disarm or Knockdown attack. You may also spend a Void Point to make a melee attack with a machi-kanshisha as a Simple Action (you may spend up to two Void Points per Round in this manner).

SERPENTS OF SANADA [Bushi]. One of the most successful and hated pirate groups in almost a thousand years. The Serpents have accumulated a small fleet of vessels and are a particular offense to the Mantis Clan, who have been attacked on their home islands by the Serpents – something no pirate ever before dared to do. Requirements: Knives 3+, Sailing 3+, Honor Rank 3 or lower. Technique Rank: 3. THE SERPENTS’ COILS – When wielding a wooden weapon or a weapon with the Peasant keyword, you may make melee attacks as Simple Actions rather than Complex Actions.

SNOW RIDERS [Bushi]. A group of ronin who served in Moto Chagatai’s famous winter campaign and survived the Lion’s vengeance, going on to sell their services as couriers, messengers, and supplemental cavalry, all with a specialty in winter tactics. They have ensured they are always employed during the winter – if nothing else because employers don’t want their enemies employing the Riders instead. Requirements: Athletics 3+, Horsemanship 4+. Technique Rank: 3. THE JOURNEY’S BEGINNING – You may ignore movement penalties based on terrain if the penalties are caused by snow or ice. You may spend a Void Point while fighting mounted in order to make a single attack as a Simple Action.

### Rank 4 Ronin Paths

SEVEN WAVES MERCENARIES [Bushi]. An older ronin group with a reputation for discrete and effective service, founded roughly a century after the return of the Unicorn Clan. Created by a group of seven ronin, each one an outcast from one of the original Great Clans. When the Seven Waves take a job, those of their number associated with the clans in question remain apart to avoid coloring the employer’s opinion, but still offer expertise and advice to the other members. Requirements: Clan Ronin only. Technique Rank: 4. ROARING TO SHAKE HEAVEN – When fighting alongside your allies, you gain a bonus of +2 to the total of all attack and damage rolls for each different Basic School represented among your group. You may not gain a total bonus greater than twice your Void Ring from this Technique.

SWORD OF YOTSU [Bushi]. Few ronin brotherhoods in the Empire are better known or more successful. Led by an actual family – one of only a handful of ronin families in Rokugan’s history – the founder of the Yotsu performed a glorious service for the Hantei Dynasty, rescuing the Emperor’s youngest son from the clutches of the Bloodspeaker Cult by leaving his own child in the boy’s place. In gratitude, the Emperor granted the ronin his father’s personal name as a family name. Over subsequent generations, Yotsu’s children expanded their family’s operations into a true ronin brotherhood, allowing other ronin to swear fealty to them in exchange for a vow to protect the defenseless. Requirements: Kenjutsu 4+, Hero of the People, Honor Rank 5+. Technique Rank: 4. SHELTER THE BLAMELESS – During any Round when you are actively defending or protecting another (GM’s discretion), you gain one bonus Void Point for use during that Round. Unspent Void Points gained from this Technique are lost at the end of the Round.

WEAVERS [Bushi]. Among the elite agents of the Kolat conspiracy and known only to its highest-ranking members. These men and women rarely associate with one another in person, communicating instead through carefully encoded messages, discrete tattoos, and secret hand signals. The abilities of the Weavers vary wildly from individual to individual, each having been recruited for a particular skill set. In general, most have a propensity for violence and an absolute mastery of deceit. Requirements: Allies (Kolat) OR Dark Secret (Kolat) OR Obligation (Kolat). Technique Rank: 4. TWIST THE WEAVE – If you have ever won a Contested Skill Roll against someone using your Sincerity (Deceit), Intimidation, or Temptation, if you subsequently face that person in a skirmish you gain a Free Raise that can be used on an attack roll during the first Round of the skirmish.

### Rank 5 Ronin Paths

IRON GAUNTLET BROTHERHOOD [Bushi]. The battered and scarred veterans who make up the Iron Gauntlet Brotherhood are among the most seasoned and fearsome ronin in all of Rokugan. Individual members are survivors of other ronin groups that have been wiped out by one force or another. In battle, they are utterly ruthless and without hesitation. Requirements: Earth 4, any one Weapon Skill at 5 or higher. Technique Rank: 5. FOR MY BROTHERS – When fighting alongside other members of the Iron Gauntlet Brotherhood, you may expend a Void Point to negate damage inflicted on an ally by an amount equal to your Void Ring x5.

SHADOWED STEEL [Bushi]. Former vassals of the Scorpion Clan, surreptitiously culled from the clan’s ranks over the course of generations or even centuries, and carefully cultivated into a completely deniable and disposable asset. The Shadowed Steel are few in number and answer only to the Shosuro family, who communicate with them through covert means such as blind drops. When an objective absolutely must be achieved or a foe eliminated at all costs, when the risk is simply too high to utilize the Shosuro family’s own assets, they call on the Shadowed Steel. Requirements: Athletics 4+, Ninjutsu 4+, Stealth 4+. Technique Rank: 5. DEATH’S DARK SHADOW – When you succeed at a Contested Stealth Skill Roll against an opponent attempting to detect you, you may spend a Void Point to add the amount by which your Stealth Skill Roll exceeded your opponent’s roll to the next attack roll you make against that opponent. This must be done within one hour of the Contested Roll – otherwise you must wait until the next Contested Stealth Skill Roll opportunity.

WOLF LEGION [Bushi]. During the epic conflict known as the Clan War, an unprecedented number of ronin gathered under the banner of Toturi, the Black Lion – the former Lion Champion cast out when the Akodo family was dissolved. Toturi’s Army fought to protect the people of the Empire and the stability of the Empire itself. After the war, many rejoined their former clans; those who remained formed the Wolf Legion, which for decades continued to protect the people of Rokugan, alternately ignoring or confronting the selfish politics of the Great Clans. Requirements: Honor Rank 5+. Technique Rank: 5. BLACK LION TALON – Whenever you spend a Void Point to add +1k1 to the total of an attack roll, you may add your Honor Rank to the total of that attack roll as well.

### Ronin Shugenja Schools

Priests of the kami are rare even in the ranks of Great Clan samurai. Finding a ronin with the ability to speak to the kami is truly an impressive feat. Ronin shugenja generally fall into a handful of broad categories: some are self-trained; others are fortunate enough to find a sensei; and a third group gain the favor of some independent order or monastic sect such as the Brotherhood of Shinsei, the Kanosei Furudera order, or the Order of Isoshi.

SELF-TAUGHT SHUGENJA (New Basic School). Benefit: +1 Willpower. Skills: Athletics, Hunting, Meditation, Spellcraft, any three non-High Skills. Honor: 4.0. Outfit: Sturdy Robes, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, Scroll Satchel, 5 bu. Affinity/Deficiency: You may choose any one non-Void Element in which to have an Affinity. You automatically gain a Deficiency in the opposing Element and in any one other Element of your choice. Technique: THE KAMI’S GIFT – Those who are self-taught possess an affinity for the kami that many among the clans would envy. When you spend a Void Point on a Spellcasting Roll, you add +2k2 instead of the normal +1k1. Unfortunately, you do not automatically gain additional spells when you advance in Rank; you must seek them out on your own. Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, and any five spells you can cast (no spells may be in Elements for which you have a Deficiency).

DUTIFUL DISCIPLE SHUGENJA (New Basic School). Benefit: +1 Intelligence. Skills: Calligraphy, Lore: Theology, Lore (pick one), Meditation, Spellcraft, any two Skills. Honor: 4.0. Outfit: Sturdy Robes, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, Scroll Satchel, 5 bu. Affinity/Deficiency: Disciple shugenja typically have the same Affinity and Deficiency of their mentor, which varies wildly. You may choose one non-Void element in which you gain an Affinity; you are automatically Deficient in the opposing element. Technique: SOUL OF STUDY – Disciples often idolize their mentors and regard their teachings as the most sacred of verses. When casting one of your beginning spells (the ones given you by your mentor), you roll an additional +1k0 on Spell Casting Rolls. Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 spells of your elemental Affinity, 2 spells of a non-Deficient element, and 1 spell of a second non-Deficient element. You gain additional spells for going up in Rank normally so long as you remain on good terms with your sensei.

RONIN ORDER SHUGENJA (New Basic School). Benefit: +1 Awareness. Skills: Calligraphy (Cipher), Divination, Lore: Theology, Lore (pick any), Meditation, Spellcraft, any one Skill. Honor: 5.0. Outfit: Sturdy Robes, Daisho, Traveling Pack, Scroll Satchel, 5 bu. Affinity/Deficiency: Affinity for Air spells, Deficiency for Earth spells. Technique: THE PATH OF DISCIPLINE – Orders such as the Brotherhood and other prominent ronin groups emphasize the most basic tenets of shugenja traditions. You gain one Free Raise on all Sense, Commune, and Summon spells for all elements except Void and Earth. Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Air spells, 2 Water spells, and 1 Fire spell.

### Non-Ronin Bandit Archetypes

The following archetypes represent non-samurai members of bandit gangs --- commoners who have turned to crime. They supplement the ronin core defined above. Their presence in a Ronin Bandit Uprising scales with province Stability per Section 56.10.1c.

**BANDIT RABBLE.** Air 2 | Earth 2 | Fire 2 | Water 2 | Void 1. Honor: 1.0 | Status: 0.0 | Infamy: 0.5 | Insight Rank: 0 (untrained). Skills: Athletics 1, Hunting 1, Knives 1 or Peasant Weapons 1 (improvised). Equipment: farm tools as weapons --- sickles, hatchets, sharpened stakes, stolen knives. Occasionally a stolen sword they do not know how to use. No armor. Straw sandals, rough clothing. The lowest tier of bandit. Desperate commoners who turned to theft to survive. Barely fighters --- they mob in groups, swing wildly, and flee when the fight turns against them. Morale breaks at 40-50% visible casualties in their immediate area. A lone Bandit Rabble encountering a visibly armed samurai without nearby allies will attempt to flee immediately. Fleeing Bandit Rabble drop weapons and do not fight during flight.

**BANDIT THUG.** Air 2 | Earth 3 | Fire 2 | Water 2 | Void 1 | Agility 3. Honor: 0.5 | Status: 0.0 | Infamy: 1.0 | Insight Rank: 1 (barely). Skills: Athletics 2, Hunting 2, one weapon skill at 2, Defense 1, Intimidation 1, Stealth 1. Equipment: a real weapon --- a stolen katana handled clumsily, a proper knife, or a spear. Crude leather or padded cloth for protection. A commoner who has committed to the bandit life. Been doing this long enough to pick up rough fighting skills from the ronin. Holds their ground, uses cover, fights with some competence. Works best in pairs or supporting a ronin. No match one-on-one for a trained bushi. Morale breaks at 60% visible casualties in their immediate area. When they flee, they flee with purpose --- they know the escape routes.

### Peasant Revolt Archetypes

The following archetypes represent civilians who have risen against their lord. They are not criminals --- they are desperate citizens. Their presence scales with Peasant Revolt insurgency Strength per Section 56.10.4.

**REBEL PEASANT.** Air 2 | Earth 2 | Fire 2 | Water 2 | Void 1. Honor: 1.0 | Status: 0.0 | Infamy: 0.0 | Insight Rank: 0 (untrained). Skills: Athletics 1, Hunting 1, Knives 1 or Peasant Weapons 1 (improvised). Equipment: farm tools as weapons --- sickles, hatchets, sharpened stakes, stolen knives. No armor. The bulk of any peasant revolt. Untrained but fighting for their homes and families. Morale breaks at 60% visible casualties in their immediate area --- higher than Bandit Rabble because they are defending something, not stealing. They mob targets, swing wildly, and rely on numbers. They do not flee as readily as criminals because surrender means execution.

**REBEL ASHIGARU.** Air 2 | Earth 2 | Fire 2 | Water 2 | Void 1 | Agility 3. Honor: 1.5 | Status: 0.0 | Infamy: 0.0 | Insight Rank: 1. Skills: Spears 2, Defense 1, Athletics 2, Battle 1. Equipment: yari (spear), light ashigaru armor. Peasants who previously served as ashigaru conscripts and retained their training and equipment. The backbone of the revolt --- they know basic formation, can hold a chokepoint, and fight with discipline. Morale breaks at 70% visible casualties. Not many per revolt --- a village only has so many men who have served.

**REBEL LEADER.** Air 2 | Earth 3 | Fire 2 | Water 2 | Void 1 | Agility 3. Honor: 2.0 | Status: 0.0 | Infamy: 0.5 | Insight Rank: 1. Skills: one weapon skill at 2, Battle 1, Intimidation 2, Athletics 2, Defense 1. Equipment: the best weapon available --- a stolen katana, a quality spear, whatever the revolt could acquire. Light armor if available. The village headman, an ashigaru veteran, or a charismatic farmer who organized the revolt. One per revolt. Commands through respect and desperation. Morale does not break --- they fight until killed or the revolt is clearly over. They know what happens to revolt leaders who surrender.
