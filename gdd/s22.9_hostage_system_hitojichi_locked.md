## 22.9 Hostage System (Hitojichi) — LOCKED

The custom of hitojichi — claiming hostages — dates to the dawn of the Empire. Hostages are named characters physically held in another clan's territory. They are treated as honored guests, not prisoners. Their release is negotiated at court, not ransomed privately. Source: Sword and Fan.

**How Hostages Are Taken — LOCKED:**

- Siege surrender: When a besieged castle surrenders, high-ranked named characters inside automatically become hostages of the victorious army. The rank of the hostage determines their leverage value — a Taisa gives more leverage than a Gunso.

- Battle capture: When a named character would die in battle but their side is losing, they may survive as a hostage instead. For now — if a capture situation is possible, it always happens automatically. The captor always takes the hostage rather than executing.

**Personality Affects Capture Likelihood — LOCKED:**

- A Yu (Courage) character fights to the last — less likely to become a hostage because their personality drives them toward dying in battle rather than surrendering.

- Other personality types may yield sooner — more likely to survive as hostages. Connects directly to siege end condition behavior in Section 19.3.

- A Will (Shourido) character will never change their declared end condition — if they committed to fighting to the last, they fight to the last regardless of odds.

**Cohabitation Disposition Gain:** See Section 12.2 for the full Cohabitation Disposition Gain system. All rules there apply to hostages — proximity breeds familiarity, and a hostage held long-term may develop stronger loyalty to their host clan than their home clan.

**Position and Orders During Captivity — LOCKED:**

- A hostage does not lose their title or position — they remain the Taisa, the daimyo, the courtier they were before capture.

- All bonuses and authority associated with their position are on hold while they are held — their subordinates continue to function but without their direct leadership bonus.

- They can still issue orders and communicate through the Game of Letters system — letters can be sent and received from captivity.

- Their position is not filled by another character while they are held — it waits for their return.

**Hostage Leverage — LOCKED:**

- The more important the hostage's position, the more political leverage they provide to the captor.

- Leverage works similarly to controlling provinces in war — a held family daimyo gives extraordinary leverage in peace negotiations.

- Leverage is a court resource — it strengthens the captor's position in any court session dealing with the relevant crisis.

**Release Through Court — LOCKED:**

- Hostages are not ransomed privately — their release is negotiated at court as part of broader peace or treaty discussions.

- If a war ends and hostages are still held, the captor must release them as part of the peace terms or face dishonor — holding hostages beyond the end of the conflict that created them is a breach of honor.

- Releasing a hostage is a court commitment made through the Public Declaration action — it is binding and public.

- The Emperor can issue an Imperial Edict forcing a hostage exchange between unwilling parties — the most powerful resolution available.

**If the Hostage's Lord Dies — LOCKED:**

- A hostage whose lord dies while they are held simply becomes a follower of the new daimyo who inherits the position.

- Their loyalty transfers automatically to their lord's successor — they remain bound by duty to their institutional family regardless of who leads it.

- The new lord may negotiate their release or continue to use them as leverage depending on the political situation.

**Children Born in Captivity — LOCKED:**

- Children born to a hostage during captivity belong to the hostage's parents' clan and family — not the host clan.

- They are part of the hostage's biological family web and will return home with the hostage when released.

- They do however carry biological family connections to anyone born or met during captivity — including potential host clan relationships if the hostage married during captivity.

**Hostage Restrictions — LOCKED:**

- A hostage cannot leave their captor's settlement freely — they are confined to that location on the MUD map.

- They may participate in local court actions and receive visitors within the settlement.

- They cannot work against their captor — court actions targeting their captor's interests are unavailable to them while held.

- They can send and receive letters through the Game of Letters system — communication with the outside world is permitted as honored guests.

**Escape Attempts — LOCKED:**

Attempting escape is deeply dishonorable — the hostage's presence represents the given word of their family and clan. Only Bushi characters with sufficient fighting skills would realistically attempt it. A hostage who flees will be executed even by their own family if caught.

- Escape is a Bushi action — only characters with combat training and relevant skills would attempt it. Courtiers and Shugenja would not.

- The attempt is resolved through the character's fighting and movement skills against the settlement's garrison and defenses.

- Success: the character escapes and returns to their home territory. Their family and clan suffer significant Honor loss for the breach of the hostage agreement. The captor clan gains a permanent negative historical modifier toward the escapee's family.

- Failure: the character is caught and executed — even their own family will not object. The attempt itself is the dishonor, not merely the escape.

- Critical failure: execution is immediate and public. The Honor loss for the escapee's family is catastrophic and permanent.
