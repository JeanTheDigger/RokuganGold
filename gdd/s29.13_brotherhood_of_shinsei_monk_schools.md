## 29.13 Brotherhood of Shinsei — Monk Schools

Monks begin with Void 3 (all other Traits at 2). All monks know 3 Kiho at character creation (must meet prerequisites) and gain 2 new Kiho each time they advance in School Rank. Additional Kiho may be purchased for XP equal to the Kiho’s Mastery Level; a character may never know more bonus Kiho than their cumulative School Rank in all Monk Schools. Monks possess Glory 1 and Status 0; Status may increase normally but Glory gains are halved. Starting outfit: Bo (or pair of jo), plain Traveling Clothing, Scroll Satchel with passages from the Tao, 2 zeni. Monk Schools are like shugenja Schools — one basic Technique learned at character creation; all further abilities come from Kiho.

**BASIC SCHOOLS (TEMPLES):**

**First Dawn Scholars [Monk]:**

Devotion: Fortunism. Scholars who believe enlightenment and understanding transcend both race and time.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence | Honor: 5.5

- Skills: Etiquette, Jiujutsu, Lore: Nonhumans, Lore: Theology (Fortunes), Meditation, Staves, any one Skill

- Technique — Way of the Ancient World: Learn one additional Mystical Kiho. +1k0 to all Etiquette and Lore Skill rolls.

**Four Temples Monk [CR]:**

Devotion: Shintao. Believe monks must remain connected to civilization rather than retreat into seclusion. Their largest monastery lies within the Imperial capital. May serve as advisers to generals and daimyo, or travel the road helping the unfortunate.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness | Honor: 6.5

- Skills: Courtier, Etiquette, Jiujutsu, Lore: Theology (Shintao), Meditation, any 2 Skills

- Technique — Soul of Civility: When spending a Void Point to augment a High Skill Roll or Social Skill Roll, gain +2k2 instead of the normal +1k1.

**Fudoist Order [Monk]:**

Devotion: None. Adherents of the resurgent Fudoist philosophy from the Colonies. Many are benevolent; some display the malevolent behaviors that doomed the first incarnation of the sect. Can also represent followers of the original early-Empire Fudoist heresy.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower | Honor: 1.5 or 4.5 (chosen at character creation)

- Skills: Courtier, Etiquette, Jiujutsu, Lore: Theology, Meditation, any two Skills

- Technique — For Each, One Path: At character creation choose low or high Honor Rank and specialize in either Contested Social Skill Rolls or Attack Rolls. Gain a bonus to your chosen roll type equal to twice the difference between your Honor Rank and your opponent’s.

**The Order of Eternity [Monk]:**

Devotion: Shintao. Believe time is an illusion and past, present, and future are coexistent. This causes strange behaviors but grants unusual insight into the world. The GM determines how long contemplation takes.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence | Honor: see Shintao standard

- Skills: Defense, Divination (Astrology), Jiujutsu, Meditation, Lore: Theology, any two High Skills

- Technique — The Touch of Eternity: Contemplate a non-combat situation, then roll Meditation/Void (TN 25, higher for complex situations). On success, gain +1k1 + Void bonus to Skill Rolls made to resolve that specific situation. May not apply to more rolls than your Void Rank without further contemplation.

**Order of the Five Rings [Monk]:**

Devotion: Shintao. Excel at seeking out imbalances within others and exploiting them.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness | Honor: 4.0

- Skills: Courtier, Defense, Etiquette, Jiujutsu, Lore: Elements, Lore: Theology (Shintao), Meditation

- Technique — Cracks Within the Elements: When making an attack roll or Social Skill Roll against a mortal opponent, gain a bonus equal to twice the difference between your opponent’s highest and lowest Ring.

**Order of Heroes Monk [CR]:**

Devotion: Shintao. A small, little-known sect devoted to the Thunder Dragon and Fortune Goemon. They quietly identify threats throughout the Empire and then find individuals with the right talents to address them — having quietly assisted in the births of hundreds of heroes and legends. Adherents consider it vital never to draw attention to themselves.

- Benefit: +1 Perception | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Jiujutsu, Lore: Theology (pick emphasis), Meditation, any 2 Skills

- Technique — Destiny’s Hand: Spend a Void Point as a Free Action to confer +1k1 to any one roll this Round by an ally who has not yet taken their Turn. Cannot enhance rolls that normally cannot benefit from Void Points (such as damage rolls).

**Order of the Nameless Gift [Monk]:**

Devotion: Shintao. An offshoot of the Shrine of the Seven Thunders. Training involves endless physical rigor and trials of endurance. They embrace what is obvious rather than seeking subtlety — many assume them simple-minded, and they do not bother to argue. Their serenity comes from keeping perceptions and expectations simple.

- Benefit: +1 Stamina | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Jiujutsu, Lore (pick one), Meditation, any two Skills

- Technique — Shinsei’s Gift: Gain bonus Void Points per day equal to your Earth Ring. These bonus Void Points may only be spent to reduce damage or raise Armor TN. Cannot be spent simultaneously with other Void Points or for any other purpose.

**Order of Peaceful Repose [SE]:**

Devotion: Fortunist (Emma-O, Fortune of Death). Found throughout Rokugan wherever warfare or spiritual conditions make ghosts more common. Devoted to laying ghosts to rest without regard for whether they are heimin or samurai spirits. In the twelfth century they form an alliance with the Brothers of Rebirth.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Athletics, Calligraphy, Jiujutsu, Lore: Ghosts, Lore: Theology (Emma-O), Meditation, any one Skill

- Technique — Emma-O’s Insight: Spend 10 minutes in meditation and roll Meditation/Awareness (TN 25) to detect the presence of ghosts (yorei or goryo, not gaki). Usable Void Rank times per day. Call Raises for additional information: ghost’s identity (1 Raise), manner of death (1 Raise), connection to the mortal world (1 Raise), specific insight into what will lay it to rest (3 Raises). GM determines the exact information revealed, but it is always directly useful.

**The Order of Rebirth [Monk]:**

Devotion: Fortunist. From a militant sohei tradition that once served the Order of Thunder devoted to Osano-Wo. Embraces new paths and philosophies to unlock new truths. Their disregard for tradition earns disdain from other sects, but the brothers ignore it — as they say, the first step upon a new path is always the most difficult.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence | Honor: 6.5

- Skills: Athletics, Jiujutsu, Lore: History, Lore: Theology, Meditation, any two Skills

- Technique — Changing the Way: Each time in a given encounter that you attempt to use a Kiho of a different Element than any Kiho previously used in the same encounter, gain +5 on the activation roll.

**Order of the Wind [BoA]:**

Devotion: Fortunism. The world is naturally chaotic and unpredictable — this cannot be changed. A Brother of Wind accepts this and remains resolute in the face of uncertainty. This grants control over themselves and, in a limited way, over the world around them.

- Benefit: +1 Reflexes | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Jiujutsu, Lore: Theology, Meditation, Staves, any one Skill

- Technique — Essence of the Wind: Any effect that targets you and attempts to influence your behavior (spell, courtier Technique, social Skill, Advantage, or any other mechanic) requires an additional Void Point to take effect. This can bypass the normal limit on spending one Void Point per Round for enhancement effects.

**Shinmaki Order [EE]:**

Devotion: Shintao. Spend much of their lives studying the controversial Diamond Sutra and meditating on the Seven Thunders. They remain in dark and isolated monasteries for years and have highly controversial practices. They emerge occasionally to gather for sectwide meetings — known for extreme spiritual purity and a complete lack of respect for conventional etiquette.

- Benefit: +1 Void | Honor: 6.5

- Skills: Divination, Jiujutsu, Lore: Theology (Diamond Sutra), Meditation 2, any two Skills

- Technique — The Diamond Sutra: Begin play with the Disturbing Countenance Disadvantage at no point cost. Gain +1k0 to Meditation rolls, Fear rolls, and all Social Skill Rolls involving heimin and hinin. No Honor loss for touching dead flesh (lose Glory or gain Infamy normally).

**Shrine of the Seven Thunders Monk [CR]:**

Devotion: Shintao. Honor the seven mortals who sacrificed everything to defeat Fu Leng by dedicating their lives to enlightenment through contemplation and tranquility. Rarely leave their monastery, choosing to become one with their environment.

- Benefit: +1 Stamina | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Athletics, Jiujutsu, Lore: Theology (Shintao), Lore (pick one), Meditation, any 2 Skills

- Technique — Walk with the Prophet: All three initial Kiho must be selected from the same element. Your Insight Rank is considered 1 higher for the purposes of meeting the Mastery Level of Kiho from that element.

**Temple of Kaimetsu-uo Monk [CR]:**

Devotion: Fortunist. Adherents believe Kaimetsu-uo performed legendary deeds to avoid the destruction that would have come from contesting the Crab Clan’s leadership. Enlightenment comes from conciliation and peace with others.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Defense, Jiujutsu, Lore: Theology (Fortunes), Meditation, any 3 Skills

- Technique — The Path of Purity: When making a Contested Social Roll initiated by your opponent, you may use any Ring in place of the normal Trait used (typically Awareness) for that roll.

**Temple of Heavenly Wisdom [Monk]:**

Devotion: Fortunist. One of the eldest monastic traditions within the Crane Clan’s provinces — ancient when Lady Doji first approached it. Their studies aided Lady Doji in the promotion of culture throughout the Empire, giving the brothers unexpected social prestige and influence, now long since accepted by the Brotherhood.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence | Honor: 6.5

- Skills: Courtier, Etiquette, Lore: History, Lore: Theology, Meditation, Sincerity, any one High Skill

- Technique — The Largesse of Fukurokujin: When making any Social Skill Roll, select any one Kiho you possess. You may not use that Kiho for the remainder of the day, but gain +1k1 to the Social Skill Roll. Each Kiho may only be selected once per day for this purpose.

**Temple of Osano-Wo Monk [CR]:**

Devotion: Fortunist. Perhaps the most militant of all Brotherhood sects. Adherents believe enlightenment comes through hard work and physical exertion. Notorious for ritually scarring their skin to mark the progress of their training, giving them an alarming appearance.

- Benefit: +1 Strength | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Battle, Jiujutsu 2, Lore: Theology (Fortunes), Meditation, any 2 Bugei Skills

- Technique — The Hand of Thunder: +0k1 to all damage rolls from unarmed attacks. Cumulative with any other effects that add rolled or kept dice to unarmed damage.

**Temple of Persistence [Monk]:**

Devotion: Fortunist. Devoted to unyielding defiance and endurance. Accept any initiates who show unflagging spirit and willingness to persist despite overwhelming odds. Seen as overly stubborn by the rest of the Brotherhood — members do not argue the point. Called upon when tenacity matters more than diplomacy or martial prowess.

- Benefit: +1 Stamina | Honor: 3.5

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Jiujutsu (Martial Arts), Lore: Theology, Meditation, any one Bugei Skill, any one Skill

- Technique — Unyielding Spirit: Spend a Void Point as a Free Action to ignore all penalties to an immediately subsequent die roll. If used on a Contested Roll you did not initiate or an Attack Roll, also add +Xk0 to the roll where X is your Earth Ring.

**Temples of the Thousand Fortunes Monk [CR]:**

Devotion: Fortunist. As diverse as the gods they revere. Almost impossible to categorize as one group, but most believe they must work diligently to prove worthy of enlightenment.

- Benefit: +1 Agility | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Jiujutsu, Lore: History, Lore: Theology (Fortunes), any 1 Lore Skill, Meditation, any 2 Skills

- Technique — The Thousand Forms: Begin the game knowing 4 Kiho rather than the normal 3. These extra Kiho do not count against the additional Kiho purchasable with Experience Points.

**Tengoku’s Fist [Monk]:**

Devotion: Shintao. An offshoot of the Shrine of the Seven Thunders. Sees violence not as evil but as a natural part of existence. Among the more serene sohei sects — they do not view violence as a first resort, merely as a viable option equal to all others. When violence is called for, they prove exceptionally gifted at dispensing it.

- Benefit: +1 Agility | Honor: 4.5

- Skills: Athletics, Battle, Jiujutsu 2, Lore: Theology, Meditation, any one Skill

- Technique — The Hand of the Heavens: When making an unarmed attack or an attack using a bisento, bo staff, or weapon with the Monk property, gain a Free Raise on the attack roll if your Fire Ring is higher than your opponent’s Fire Ring.

**Wind’s Grace Order [BoA]:**

Devotion: Shintao. Created in the aftermath of the Clan War for monks who answered the call to defend the Brotherhood but could not regain their former peace afterward. The Brotherhood seized upon archery as a path to spiritual focus and recovery. Strong ties to the Asahina family and other prominent archery traditions.

- Benefit: +1 Reflexes | Honor: 6.5

- Skills: Calligraphy, Jiujutsu, Kyujutsu, Lore: Theology (Shintao), Meditation, any two Skills

- Special: Members of the Asahina family may potentially attend this school.

- Technique — Pillars of the Mind’s Eye: Make an archery attack as a Complex Action; for every Kiho currently active, gain +1k0 to the roll (only one Martial Kiho can contribute this benefit; active Kiho may have no other effects during this Round). Any time this Technique directly results in the death of an intelligent being, lose 3 Honor.

**ALTERNATE PATHS:**

**Abbot [Monk]:**

Those honored by the Brotherhood with election to a position of leadership receive additional training and gain influence and social status within their Order. Ordinarily reserved for monks of Insight Rank 4+; unusual circumstances may result in earlier promotion.

- Replaces: Any Brotherhood Rank 4+ (possibly lower with GM adjudication).

- Technique — The Reverence of Wisdom: Within the Brotherhood, considered Status 4.0. +1k1 to all Social Skill Rolls (except Temptation) when interacting with members of your own Order; +1k0 when interacting with outsiders of all types.

**Barefoot Brethren [SE]:**

Devotion: Koshin, Fortune of Roads. Carry messages all across Rokugan, asking no price except food. Famed for their endurance, regularly outpacing even Unicorn horses over long distances.

- Replaces: Any Fortunist Brotherhood Monk 2 (not Order of Osano-Wo). Requires: Ascetic, Meditation (Fasting) 3, Athletics 4.

- Technique — Koshin’s Favor: Gain only one Kiho at this Rank. Cannot become Fatigued from travel or lack of sleep (but not from lack of food/drink). +1k1 to all Meditation rolls.

**Brotherhood Spy [Monk]:**

Devised when Emperor Hantei VI turned to the Brotherhood to help combat the Gozoku conspiracy. Predominantly former Scorpion samurai who had retired to contemplative life. Can represent Brotherhood monks drawn into political affairs in any era; a perverted form may exist in the Spider Clan in the twelfth century.

- Replaces: Any Brotherhood Monk School 2. Requires: Lore: Theology 3.

- Technique — Bearing the Emperor’s Shame: When acting against a known or suspected Gozoku agent, or when acting on orders from a legitimate Imperial authority, gain a Free Raise on all Stealth and Deceit Skill Rolls. No Honor loss for these specific rolls under these specific circumstances.

**Defender of the Brotherhood [Monk/Courtier]:**

Emissaries who attend courts to express the Brotherhood’s concerns — protection of common people and of the Brotherhood’s shrines and temples. Found across peaceful and contemplative sects, but even the sohei of Osano-Wo occasionally train in these methods.

- Replaces: Any Brotherhood Monk 2 (not Four Temples). Requires: Willpower 3, Courtier 3, Etiquette 4.

- Technique — Harmony in Chaos: When spending a Void Point on an Etiquette or Courtier Social Skill Roll, gain +Xk1 to the roll (where X is your Willpower) instead of the normal +1k1.

**Kaze-do Fighter [Bushi/Monk]:**

See also Section 29.12 (Ronin). The ancient techniques of Togashi Kaze, preserved among peasants and Brotherhood monks. Normally only available to peasants, monks, or in extremely rare cases a ronin or Tattooed Man with a suitable teacher.

- Replaces: Any Rank 2 for a character who qualifies. Requires: Jiujutsu (Kaze-do) 3.

- Technique — The Way of Air: When fighting unarmed, any opponent attacking you with a melee weapon suffers −Xk0 to their attacking Skill Roll where X equals their Air Ring. Cannot apply to more opponents per Round than your Insight Rank.

**Order of Ebisu [Monk/Courtier]:**

Devotion: Fortunist. Spend most of their time among the common folk, occasionally interacting with the samurai caste to offer counsel or report abuse of heimin. Teach by example and education rather than political negotiation. Extremely patient — known to labor endlessly awaiting the moment their work can bear fruit among the samurai.

- Replaces: Thousand Fortunes Monk 3, Jurojin’s Blessing Monk 3. Requires: At least two heimin Craft Skills at Rank 3+.

- Technique — The Voice of Humility: Spend time working alongside peasants at humble tasks (utilizing this Path’s Craft Skills). For each week spent laboring this way, gain one bonus Void Point usable only on those Craft Skills, on Medicine Skill Rolls made to help peasants/hinin, or on Social Skill Rolls made against samurai. Maximum bonus Void Points held equals your Void Rank.

**Pure Song Shrine [SE]:**

Devotion: Shintao. For this sect, purity is the road to Enlightenment. Refrain from eating flesh, eggs, or root vegetables whose harvest kills the plant. Wear hemp rather than silk. Those with full vows wear bells on ankles and staves to warn creatures of their approach. Will never take violent action — but will lay down their own lives to save others. Known also for their beautiful music, rivaling the finest Kakita artisans.

- Replaces: Shrine of Seven Thunders Monk 4. Requires: Willpower 4, Defense 4, Meditation 3, any one musical Perform Skill at 3. Special: May not learn or use Martial Kiho.

- Technique — The Pure Song: +1k0 to all musical Perform Skills. May never deliberately attack another living creature — if you ever do, permanently lose the ability to perform Kiho. **Silent Ones [SE]:**

Devotion: Fortunist (Tsugumu, Fortune of Secrets). A tiny order of never more than a dozen total, unknown outside the Scorpion lands and barely known within. The upper ranks of the Scorpion Clan find them deeply useful. When an initiate takes full vows, his tongue vanishes from his mouth.

- Replaces: Any Fortunist Brotherhood Monk 3. Requires: Willpower 4, Meditation 4. Special: The character becomes permanently mute upon taking full vows.

- Technique — Tsugumu’s Silence: A secret voluntarily told to a member of this order can never be given up. The knowledge is guarded by the Fortune of Secrets and cannot be taken from the monk’s mind in any way, even through torture or intrusive Air magic.

**Student of Hitsu-Do [Monk]:**

Hitsu-do is an extremely offensive martial art with few defensive maneuvers. Focuses on speed and power, infusing each strike with inner Fire. The cost is that using one’s own Fire chi is also harmful to the practitioner.

- Replaces: Any Brotherhood Monk 2, Kikage Zumi 2, Kuni Witch-Hunter 2, Asako Henshin 2. Requires: Jiujutsu (Hitsu-do) 4, Fire 3, access to a Hitsu-do dojo.

- Technique — The Way of Fire: When making an unarmed attack, call on your inner Fire — suffer Wounds equal to Fire ×2 (Wound Penalties from this damage are applied after the attack roll). In return, unarmed damage for that attack has a base DR of 0k[Fire Ring] instead of 0k1 (does not stack with Hands of Stone). Using this Technique counts as using a Mystical Kiho. Your Fire Ring is considered 1 Rank higher for the purpose of learning Fire Kiho.

**Student of Mizu-Do [Monk/Artisan]:**

Mizu-do is a Crane martial art from stage performance tradition — defensive and soft, using the enemy’s strength against him. Popular among Crane, the Brotherhood, and certain courtiers. Bushi (especially Crab and Lion) tend to look down on it as impractical. Courtiers from Unicorn and Phoenix sometimes exchange favors to arrange access.

- Replaces: Any non-martial Brotherhood Monk 3, Doji Courtier 3, Kakita Artisan 3, Asahina Shugenja 3. Requires: Jiujutsu (Mizu-do) 4, Water 3, access to a Mizu-do sensei.

- Technique — The Way of Water: When performing a Grapple, use your opponent’s Strength Rank in place of your own. While in Defense or Full Defense Stance, when a melee attack misses you, spend a Void Point as a Free Action and roll Jiujutsu (Mizu-do)/Strength (TN = opponent’s attack roll, using the opponent’s Strength). On success, immediately Throw the opponent or take control of a Grapple against them.

**The Taoist Archers [Bushi/Monk]:**

See also Section 29.12 (Ronin). Devotees of perfect harmony through kyujutsu, existing within the Brotherhood for centuries. In the Pre-Coup era a ronin named Gahei learned the Technique and began teaching it, thereafter making it available to ronin who can find a willing teacher.

- Replaces: Any Brotherhood Monk or Ronin 5. Requires: Void 4, Kyujutsu (Yumi) 5, Lore: Theology 3, Meditation 4.

- Technique — Flight of Innocence: When making archery attacks, ignore all TN and dice penalties for being Blind, Missing Eye, or the target being concealed by darkness, magical illusion, camouflage, or similar visual obstructions. Penalties for physical obstacles and obstructive conditions still apply. Spend a Void Point while wielding a bow for +1k1 to damage.
