## 15.1 Three Court Types — LOCKED

**Imperial Winter Court:**

- The grandest court. The Emperor is present or represented by a high Imperial official.

- The primary goal is gaining Imperial favor and influencing Imperial decisions on the most pressing crises of the year.

- Every major clan sends a delegation — typically 6 to 20 members depending on the host.

- The top three highest-momentum global crises are the agenda. Secondary topics emerge from who is present.

- Gaining the Emperor's approval is the highest-value political action available — an Imperial Edict can end wars, legitimize claims, and destroy reputations.

- The host clan has a significant advantage — they set the physical environment, control the entertainment schedule, and can use their court's culture to put other delegations at a disadvantage.

- Held during Winter. The Emperor selects a specific Family Castle as the host — never the Imperial Capital, always a castle elsewhere in the Empire. Fierce competition for this honor. Crane historically dominates. The full host selection mechanic, invitation pipeline, delegation allocation, and host advantage are specified in Section 55.10.

- Duration: Fixed at 120 in-game days (the full Winter season, ~30 real-world days). The Emperor issues Imperial Edicts on the top three crises at the close of Winter Court. These edicts bind everyone.

**Clan Champion Courts:**

- Hosted by a Clan Champion or their designated representative. Attended by the clan's vassals and delegations from other clans.

- The primary goal is clan-level politics — forming alliances, resolving inter-clan disputes, negotiating trade, debating military appointments and resource distribution.

- No Emperor present — the Champion is the highest authority. Gaining the Champion's favor is the highest-value action here.

- Alliances are primarily formed and formalized at this level.

- Can occur in any season, though Winter is most common for major sessions.

- Duration: Moderate — roughly one to two weeks of in-game days. Ends when commitments are made or time runs out.

**Provincial / Family Courts:**

- Smaller and more intimate. Called for specific local purposes — resolving a family dispute, negotiating a marriage, addressing a regional crisis, receiving a visiting dignitary.

- Topics are narrower and more immediate than higher courts.

- Less prestige but more accessible — lower-ranking characters operate primarily at this level.

- Can occur at any time of year with erratic scheduling — convened when the daimyo sees fit.

- A promising courtier who wins decisively at this level may earn an invitation to higher courts.

- Duration: Short — a few in-game days at most. Focused on a specific crisis or purpose.

**Crisis-Triggered Courts — LOCKED:**

Courts do not only happen on a fixed schedule. When a crisis reaches sufficient momentum in a region, it can trigger a court being called by the local lord. The crisis becomes the main topic automatically. This makes courts feel organic — they happen because something is happening in the world, not on an arbitrary calendar.

- The momentum threshold required to trigger a court depends on the lord's rank — a provincial daimyo calls court at lower momentum than a Clan Champion would.

- Secondary topics emerge naturally from who attends — a lord attending a court about a famine may also raise an ongoing border war if it is relevant to them.
