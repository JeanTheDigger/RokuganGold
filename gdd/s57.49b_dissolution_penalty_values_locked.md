## 57.49b Marriage Dissolution Penalty Values — LOCKED

This addendum formalizes numeric values for the three PROVISIONAL constants in Section 57.49.
The main section specifies consequences qualitatively ("family relations are damaged",
"Glory penalty for being dissolved from", "modest clan-level cooling") without numeric values.
This addendum derives canonical values from LOCKED GDD anchors.

---

### Source Derivation

**GDD-confirmed anchors used:**

1. **s12.2 disposition tier boundaries (LOCKED):**
   - Rival tier: −11 to −30 (inclusive)
   - Stranger tier: −10 to +10 (inclusive)
   - Stranger/Rival boundary: −10 (Stranger) / −11 (Rival onset)

2. **s57.49.1 Pathway 1 precondition (LOCKED):**
   - The ordering lord must hold ≤ −31 (Enemy tier) toward the other spouse's lord
   - The ordering lord's personal relationship is already past Rival — in Enemy territory

3. **s46 Table 2.4 — Gaining & Losing Glory (LOCKED):**
   - *"Family Dishonor: Lose one Glory Rank if a close relative commits an act worthy of seppuku"*
   - 1 Glory Rank = 1.0 in simulation units (L5R 4e: 10 points per rank)

### DISSOLUTION_FAMILY_DISP_PENALTY = −20

**Derivation:**

The family baseline penalty applies to the collective disposition of both families
(all members of Spouse A's family toward all members of Spouse B's family).

The penalty must satisfy two constraints:

1. **It must produce structural Rival-tier hostility at the family level.** The
   ordering lord's personal relationship was already at Enemy (−31), so the two families
   are not on neutral terms. A family penalty that leaves them in Stranger territory
   (−10 to +10) would be inconsistently mild. Rival territory (−11 to −30) is the
   appropriate outcome.

2. **It must not reach Enemy tier (−31+) at the family level.** Enemy tier is reserved
   for active hostility between individuals who have directly wronged each other. The
   broader families — most of whom had no role in the dissolution — should not be placed
   in structural Enemy territory by a single lord's administrative act.

**Conclusion:** The penalty must fall within Rival tier: −11 to −30. −20 (mid-Rival)
is chosen as the calibration point: significant damage placing both families solidly
in Rival territory, without pressing toward the Enemy boundary. A lower value (−11,
onset of Rival) would understate the political weight of a coercive lord's command;
a higher value (−25 or −30) would overstate it relative to events like assassination
vengeance (−50, biological family, a far more extreme provocation).

### DISSOLUTION_GLORY_LOSS_SPOUSE = −0.5

**Derivation:**

s46 Table 2.4 (LOCKED) establishes the reference magnitude:

> *"Family Dishonor: Lose one Glory Rank if a close relative commits an act worthy
> of seppuku."*

In simulation units: 1 Glory Rank = 1.0. This event is the closest comparable
public family-level humiliation in the Glory table.

**Why half (−0.5) rather than full (−1.0):**

Family Dishonor fires when a close relative **actively commits** an act of catastrophic
personal failure — treason, murder, extreme dishonor. The relative is the agent of the
shame; the innocent family member bears secondary consequences.

A forced dissolution is different in a key respect: the dissolved spouse did not
commit a dishonourable act. Their lord judged the marriage politically undesirable
and exercised administrative authority. The spouse is the subject of a political
decision, not a secondary victim of another person's dishonour.

This makes forced dissolution **comparable in visibility** to Family Dishonor (both
are public declarations of family-level inadequacy) but **less severe in cause**
(a lord's political calculus vs a relative's personal failure). −0.5 (half a Glory
Rank) captures the public humiliation without equating it to the worst form of
family-level shame specified in the Glory table.

Applied to **both spouses**: the spouse being removed (family publicly deemed
politically undesirable) and the spouse whose family chose this path (the alliance
they formed has been revoked as a liability).

### DISSOLUTION_CLAN_DISP_PENALTY = −10

**Derivation:**

The clan-to-clan penalty applies only to cross-clan marriages dissolved via Pathway 1.
It affects the collective disposition of all members of Clan A toward all members of
Clan B.

**Design constraint:** The clan-level penalty should produce measurable cooling without
forcing structural rivalry between entire clans. Individual lords choose marriages and
dissolve them; the thousands of samurai in both clans had no role in either decision.

**s12.2 anchor:** The Stranger/Rival boundary is precisely −10 / −11:
- Stranger tier ends at −10 (inclusive)
- Rival tier begins at −11

A clan penalty of −10 caps at this boundary. Clan members who were at neutral (0)
fall to −10 (lower Stranger, still neutral tier). Members already at the bottom of
Stranger (−10) stay at −10. The penalty does not push neutral clans into Rival
territory — it produces measurable cooling while preserving the Stranger relationship
between clans who had no personal stake in the dissolved marriage.

The deliberate contrast with the family penalty (−20, mid-Rival) reflects the
hierarchical scope: the families directly involved bear most of the social damage;
the broader clans feel a secondary ripple proportional to their distance from the
event.

---

### Design Rationale Summary

The three values form a coherent hierarchy scaled to social scope:

| Level | Penalty | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| Individual spouse Glory | −0.5 | Half of s46 "Family Dishonor" — comparable visibility, lesser cause |
| Family-to-family baseline | −20 | Mid-Rival (−11 to −30 range) |
| Clan-to-clan baseline | −10 | Stranger floor — capped at the Stranger/Rival boundary |

Smaller scope, larger penalty: the families directly involved bear the most damage;
the broader clans feel a secondary ripple. This mirrors how other multi-scope events
are treated in the GDD (assassination vengeance is −50 to biological family but does
not modify clan baselines directly).
