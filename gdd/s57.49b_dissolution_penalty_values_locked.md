## 57.49b Marriage Dissolution Penalty Values — LOCKED

This addendum formalizes numeric values for the three PROVISIONAL constants in Section 57.49.
The main section specifies consequences qualitatively ("family relations are damaged",
"Glory penalty for being dissolved from", "modest clan-level cooling") without numeric values.
This addendum derives canonical values from LOCKED GDD anchors.

---

### Source Derivation

**GDD-confirmed anchors used:**

1. **s12.2 disposition tier boundaries (LOCKED):**
   - Rival tier: −11 to −30 (inclusive)
   - Stranger tier: −10 to +10 (inclusive)
   - Stranger/Rival boundary: −10 (Stranger) / −11 (Rival onset)

2. **s57.49.1 Pathway 1 precondition (LOCKED):**
   - The ordering lord must hold ≤ −31 (Enemy tier) toward the other spouse's lord
   - This means the ordering lord's personal relationship is already beyond Rival — in Enemy territory

3. **s46 Table 2.4 — Gaining & Losing Glory (LOCKED):**
   - *"Family Dishonor: Lose one Glory Rank if a close relative commits an act worthy of seppuku"*
   - 1 Glory Rank = 1.0 in simulation units (L5R 4e: 10 points per rank)

### DISSOLUTION_FAMILY_DISP_PENALTY = −25

**Derivation:**

The family baseline penalty applies to the collective disposition of both families
(all members of Spouse A's family toward all members of Spouse B's family), not to
individual characters.

The penalty must satisfy two constraints:

1. **It must produce structural Rival-tier hostility at the family level.** The
   ordering lord's personal relationship was already at Enemy (−31), so the two families
   are not on neutral terms. A family penalty that leaves them in Stranger territory
   (−10 to +10) would be inconsistently mild. Rival territory (−11 to −30) is the
   appropriate outcome.

2. **It must not reach Enemy tier (−31+) at the family level.** Enemy tier is reserved
   for active hostility between individuals who have directly wronged each other. The
   broader families — most of whom had no role in the dissolution — should not be placed
   in structural Enemy territory by a single lord's administrative act.

**Conclusion:** The penalty must fall within Rival tier: −11 to −30. The high end (−25)
reflects that a lord-commanded dissolution is a coercive political act, not an amicable
separation — the GDD specifies that the precondition is Enemy-tier hostility, signaling
a genuinely antagonistic political context. A mid-Rival penalty (−20) would understate
this antagonism; −25 places both families in deep Rival territory while preserving
Enemy tier for individual-level violence.

---

### DISSOLUTION_GLORY_LOSS_SPOUSE = −1.0

**Derivation:**

s46 Table 2.4 (LOCKED) establishes:

> *"Family Dishonor: Lose one Glory Rank if a close relative commits an act worthy
> of seppuku."*

A lord-commanded dissolution is comparable in social magnitude to this event:

- **Family Dishonor (s46):** A close relative's catastrophic personal failure becomes
  public knowledge, marking your entire family as unable to produce honourable samurai.
  You bear secondary shame for the act of another.

- **Forced dissolution (s57.49):** Your own family is publicly judged politically
  undesirable by a ranking lord — not a failure of a relative, but a direct judgment
  that your own household's alliances are liabilities. The dissolved spouse is the
  subject of the lord's decision, not a secondary victim.

The forced dissolution is **at least as personally devastating** as Family Dishonor.
Both events are public declarations of family-level inadequacy. −1.0 (one full Glory
Rank) matches the Table 2.4 anchor precisely.

Applied to **both spouses** because the dissolution signals political failure for each
household: the spouse being sent away (family politically undesirable) and the spouse
remaining (the alliance they formed was unwise or weak).

### DISSOLUTION_CLAN_DISP_PENALTY = −10

**Derivation:**

The clan-to-clan penalty applies only to cross-clan marriages dissolved via Pathway 1.
It affects the collective disposition of all members of Clan A toward all members of
Clan B.

**Design constraint:** The clan-level penalty should produce measurable cooling without
forcing structural rivalry between entire clans. Individual lords choose marriages and
dissolve them; the thousands of samurai in both clans had no role in either decision.

**s12.2 anchor:** The Stranger/Rival boundary is precisely −10 / −11:
- Stranger tier ends at −10 (inclusive)
- Rival tier begins at −11

A clan penalty of −10 caps at this boundary. Clan members who were at the very bottom
of Stranger disposition (−10) would have their effective disposition fall to −10 (no
change); those at neutral (0) would fall to −10 (still Stranger); those already at
Rival would fall deeper within Rival.

Crucially, the penalty **does not push neutral clans into Rival territory**. A cross-clan
dissolution between clans with existing warm relations (positive baselines) would cool
those relations meaningfully but not structurally antagonize the broader clan populations.

The deliberate contrast with the family penalty (−25, deep Rival) reflects the
hierarchical scope: the families involved in the dissolution bear most of the social
damage; the broader clans feel a secondary ripple proportional to their distance from
the event.

---

### Design Rationale Summary

The three values form a coherent hierarchy scaled to the social scope of each consequence:

| Level | Penalty | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| Individual spouse Glory | −1.0 | Matches s46 "Family Dishonor" anchor |
| Family-to-family baseline | −25 | Deep Rival (high within −11 to −30) |
| Clan-to-clan baseline | −10 | Stranger floor (boundary, not Rival) |

Smaller scope, larger penalty — the two families directly involved bear the most
damage; the broader clans feel the secondary ripple. This mirrors how the GDD
treats other multi-scope events (assassination vengeance is −50 to biological
family but does not modify clan baselines directly).
