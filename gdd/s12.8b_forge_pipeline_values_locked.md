## 12.8b Forge Pipeline Numeric Values — LOCKED

This addendum formalizes four PROVISIONAL values for the forgery subsystem
(GDD s12.8): NeedType alignment scores for FORGE actions (A15), forged objective
priority (A17), impersonation detection topic tier (A18), and INVESTIGATE_THREAT
objective priority on impersonation discovery (A19).

### A15 — Forge NeedType Alignment Scores

**GDD source:** s12.8 specifies the purpose of each forge action without
assigning NeedType scoring weights.

**Derivation:** Scores calibrated against comparable actions in the same NeedType.

#### DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP

| Action | Score | Calibration |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| FORGE_IMPERSONATION_LETTER | 70 | Tied with PUBLIC_INSULT (70). A forged letter creates relational damage comparable to a direct public insult: it poisons another party's view of the impersonated person without requiring physical proximity or direct confrontation. |
| FORGE_ORDER | 55 | Below FORGE_IMPERSONATION_LETTER. Orders compel action rather than creating reputational damage directly. Tied with PUBLIC_DEBATE (55). |

#### ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE

| Action | Score | Calibration |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| FORGE_IMPERSONATION_LETTER | 50 | A forged letter is usable as fabricated evidence — documentary material that can be deployed against the impersonated party ("I have proof you wrote this"). Tied with COMMISSION_ASSASSINATION (50). Both are indirect, high-risk leverage approaches. |
| FORGE_ORDER | 30 | An order compels a vassal to act but does not produce leverage material. The outcome of a forge order is behavioural change in the target's subordinate, not evidence the forger holds over the target. Below CONDUCT_RAID (35). |

The distinction between 50 and 30 reflects that leverage requires *something to hold*:
a forged letter gives you a document; a forged order gives you an outcome.

#### SUPPRESS_INVESTIGATION

| Action | Score | Calibration |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| FORGE_ORDER | 60 | Redirecting an investigator via a plausible fake order from their lord is a highly effective suppression tactic. Tied with FLEE_JURISDICTION (60). Both remove the investigator from the scene. |
| FORGE_IMPERSONATION_LETTER | 45 | A forged letter creates confusion without directly neutralising the investigator. Below KILL_WITNESS (50) — confusion is less decisive than silencing. |

### A17 — Forged Objective Priority: 8

**GDD source:** No specification.

**Derivation:** The `priority` field on objective dictionaries is stored metadata
in the current implementation — the NPC decision engine selects `objectives_map[id]["primary"]`
directly without reading the `priority` key. The value is therefore inert for
gameplay purposes.

8 is retained as a documentation signal: it is higher than all regular assigned
objectives (ATTEND_COURT = 5, RESTORE_GOVERNANCE = 5, UPHOLD_LAW = 4) but below
crisis-override level. This reflects that a victim of a forged order believes they
are following a genuine lord command, which takes precedence over self-selected
objectives — without triggering the emergency escalation reserved for AVENGE_DEATH
crisis overrides.

### A18 — Impersonation Detection Topic Tier: TIER_3

**GDD source:** No explicit tier assignment. Topic fires when a reply letter exposes
that a prior letter was forged (s12.8 impersonation detection pipeline).

**Derivation:** The event is that a character discovers someone sent fake
correspondence in their name. Tier calibration against comparable events:

| Event | Tier | Rationale |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Spy Uncovered | TIER_4 | Spy identity unknown; personal/local signal |
| Suspicious assassination death | TIER_3 | Affects a family/individual; regional signal |
| Impersonation discovered | **TIER_3** | Political scandal affecting a person/family; confirms active forgery operation |
| Lord assassination | TIER_2 | Removes a major power; political consequence is empire-wide |

TIER_3 reflects that impersonation discovery is a significant political event —
it confirms an enemy is running a forgery operation against you — but its
consequences fall on a family rather than reshaping inter-clan power.

### A19 — Impersonation Detection Objective Priority: 6

**GDD source:** No specification.

**Derivation:** Like A17, the `priority` field on objective dictionaries is stored
metadata — the NPC engine follows the primary objective slot directly without reading
`priority`. The value is inert for gameplay purposes.

6 is retained as a documentation signal: it is above routine standing objectives
(UPHOLD_LAW = 4) and above normal court attendance objectives (ATTEND_COURT = 5),
signalling that a victim of impersonation treats investigation of the forger as an
urgent personal matter, but below the pseudo-crisis priority used for forged orders
(8) and well below the AVENGE_DEATH crisis override.

### Design Note: Priority as Documentation

A17 and A19 establish a readable priority ladder that could become mechanically
meaningful if a future system reads the `priority` field to adjudicate objective
conflicts. The current ladder:

| Priority | Objective class |
|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Edict response |
| 2–3 | Vacancy fills, training, monk objectives |
| 4 | Standing objectives (UPHOLD_LAW) |
| 5 | Court attendance, governance restoration |
| 6 | Reactive investigation (impersonation target) |
| 8 | Forged lord orders (victim believes it genuine) |
| crisis_override | AVENGE_DEATH (assassination vengeance) |
