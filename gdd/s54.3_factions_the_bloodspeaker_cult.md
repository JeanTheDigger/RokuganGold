## 54.3 Factions: The Bloodspeaker Cult

“What do you know of the Bloodspeakers? Or the name Iuchiban? Children’s stories no doubt, told to misbehaving boys. But Iuchiban is more than just a name. He is perhaps the greatest threat the Empire has ever seen, and he lives still.” – Kuni Vistan, Witch Hunter, late eleventh century

### Origins of the Bloodspeakers

The history of the sinister cult known variously as the Bloodspeakers or the Cult of the Blood Red Moon begins with the sorcerer called Iuchiban. Hantei Jama was the younger brother of the Hantei Emperor who reigned at the turn of the sixth century. When his brother took the throne he joined the Otomo family to eliminate any conflict of inheritance. However, Jama was deeply embittered, jealous of his brother’s power and hateful of the Rokugani power structure that had shunted him off as unimportant. In the final year of the fifth century, Jama discovered the long-forgotten journals of Kuni Nakanu, a Crab priest who had conducted forbidden research into the undead. These journals contained the basics of what the Empire would come to know as maho, forbidden blood magic. Jama studied them for years and began to recruit followers. His chief lieutenants became Jama Suru and Asahina Yajinden.

Jama’s obsession with power led him and several senior cult followers to travel into the Burning Sands, pursuing legends of gaijin sorcerers who could not be killed. The voyage claimed the lives of all but Jama, Suru, and Yajinden. Jama discovered the secrets of the Khadi, the immortal sorcerers who resided in the Senpet lands. He mystically removed his own heart from his chest, sealing it in a hidden box. The Khadi ritual made him immortal so long as his heart was intact, as well as greatly enhancing his sorcerous power. It was in the wake of this terrible act that he took the cult name Iuchiban, the Bloodspeaker.

### Iuchiban’s Early Plots and the Bloodswords

Yajinden’s most notable early contribution to the cult was the creation of porcelain masks which could reanimate corpses as obedient zombies. His relentless quest to create more powerful artifacts gave birth to the cult’s most sinister early plot. Using the terrible relic known as the Anvil of Despair – an artifact created from the blood of the First Oni and the souls of the lost Boar Clan – Yajinden created four swords of terrible power known as the Bloodswords: Ambition, Judgment, Passion, and Vengeance. Otomo Jama arranged for these swords to be gifted to the champions of the Scorpion, Crab, Crane, and Lion Clans, intending their power would destroy these leaders and plunge the Empire into chaos.

Three of the swords did their work all too well. The lord of the Crane confessed his love for a geisha and committed suicide by leaping into the Sea of Amaterasu. The lord of the Crab murdered his own family for their putative moral failings, then committed suicide. The Champion of the Lion went mad and led 15,000 troops in a disastrous assault on the Dragon Mountains. The one exception was the Scorpion Champion, Bayushi Rikoji, who was suspicious of the unexpected sword and placed it into storage while dispatching a trusted magistrate, Soshi Takasho, to investigate. Takasho and the Lion magistrate Akodo Minobe eventually uncovered the truth about Otomo Jama.

### The Battle of Stolen Graves (510)

In the year 510, Takasho and Minobe tracked down the headquarters of the Bloodspeaker Cult within Otosan Uchi itself. Iuchiban and his followers were hiding near the Imperial Crypts, weeks away from unleashing an army of undead reanimated from the Crypts themselves. When exposed, Iuchiban hastily animated as many corpses as he could. It was Minobe who rallied the Imperial forces with a furious shout of “Blasphemy!” as he cut down the first of the zombies. The Empire’s samurai rallied and defeated the undead horde while Takasho captured Iuchiban himself. This conflict is known as the Battle of Stolen Graves.

Horrified by the desecration of ancestral corpses, the Emperor proclaimed that henceforth all dead bodies would be cremated before the bones were buried – a tradition that continues in Rokugan to the present day. Iuchiban could not be slain, so a Crab engineer named Kaiu Gineza built a unique Tomb – with duplicate decoys and an array of impenetrable traps. When the final Tomb was complete, Gineza walked inside activating each trap and stayed within to die, taking its secrets with him. Asahina Yajinden was handed over to his own family, who wiped his memories. But Yajinden had recently learned a dark secret to near-immortality – swapping his spirit from body to body – and escaped to continue rallying what remained of his master’s cult.

### The Second Rise and Battle of Sleeping River (750)

Over two centuries after his imprisonment, Iuchiban finally escaped from the Tomb, leaping his soul into the body of a peasant. Traveling across the Empire from body to body, he contacted Yajinden and gathered his followers to prepare for another assault. However, his overconfidence was his undoing. In the summer of 750 he attempted to possess the body of Togashi Yamatsu, an Ise Zumi monk in the Dragon court. Yamatsu was able to call on the strength of his mystical tattoos to resist, and pointed one trembling finger at Iuchiban shouting “Maho!” Imperial forces located the cult’s remote headquarters, and an army drawn from all the Great Clans confronted Iuchiban’s forces in a bloody seven-day clash known as the Battle of the Sleeping River.

Togashi Yamatsu confronted Iuchiban once more with a new tattoo, capturing Iuchiban’s malign spirit within his own body. The Phoenix proposed a solution: a magical ritual to bind Iuchiban’s soul into the stone of the Tomb itself. For thirty days a circle of Phoenix shugenja chanted continuously, several of them perishing, until Iuchiban’s soul was bound into the Tomb.

### The Bloodspeaker Cult Without Its Master

In the aftermath of the Battle of Sleeping River, the Bloodspeakers became a less unified and more diverse organization, fissuring into many different cells and sub-cults scattered across the Empire. Despite his imprisonment, Iuchiban could still distantly interact with his followers through the Oracle of Blood – a sinister creation that could appear within the dreams of any cult member, manifesting as a shape rising from a dark pool of blood, speaking in a deep and terrifying voice, revealing secrets, conveying prophecies, and occasionally delivering concrete information such as the name of a particular enemy.

One of the most successful Bloodspeaker cult cells during this era was the so-called Demon’s Breath Acolytes in the lands of the Unicorn, founded by Iuchi Ryutaro. Their unusual level of unity and loyalty – founded on mutual respect rather than hatred or fear – allowed them to develop close ties even when separated by great distances. A unique figure also rose among the cult’s ranks in 1111: Daigotsu, born to the Hantei bloodline, whose unborn soul had been bound to an unnamed oni. Though he trained with the cult, Daigotsu ultimately betrayed the Bloodspeaker movement and sided with Fu Leng and Jigoku itself – the Bloodspeakers had effectively created one of their own future enemies.

### The Third Rise and Death of Iuchiban (1165–1166)

In 1165, a powerful Lost maho-tsukai named Iuchi Shahai manipulated samurai into breaking into the Tomb. Iuchiban possessed her lieutenant Mohai and, together with the re-imprisoned Yajinden, overpowered her. The Bloodspeaker freed his original body from its jade chains and he and Yajinden escaped. Iuchiban deposed Daigotsu and seized control of the City of the Lost, then used an ancient ritual to sacrifice the sacred Ki-Rin and unleash an Empire-wide Rain of Blood – Tainted blood that corrupted any who felt its touch too long. The hidden cult cells across the Empire emerged overnight, their ranks swollen by the effects of the Rain.

The Empire launched ruthless purges known as the Blood Hunt, cleaning out cult cells and killing many senior cult leaders including Jama Suru. The final battle in 1166 saw an unusual alliance between the Emperor’s brother Sezaru and the Dark Lord Daigotsu. It was Sezaru who finally located Iuchiban’s hidden heart and destroyed it, taking the life of the Bloodspeaker once and for all. The Oracle of Blood vanished after his death. Yajinden escaped once more, departing the cult to follow Daigotsu and his newly formed Spider Clan. Sezaru continued the Blood Hunt until his death in 1169, rooting out and exterminating any remaining cells.

### The Modern Bloodspeaker Cult

The modern Bloodspeaker Cult is only a shadow of its former strength, with fewer than two hundred surviving members. The survivors are hardened fanatics, determined to hide and endure as long as necessary to reclaim their power and find some way of bringing back their leader. Iuchiban has escaped imprisonment twice, after all – why should he not defeat death itself?

The cult’s cell structure is designed to be self-sufficient and independent, united only by devotion to Iuchiban. Each individual cell normally numbers no more than five to six members with one leader, though during peak activities they may have as many as eight to ten members. These cells are scattered over wide geographical areas and dispersed among the normal population of Rokugan, preferably hiding within towns or cities. Normally each cell knows only of its own members. Pseudonyms and false names are routinely used. All of this means that even if one cell is compromised, no more than one or two other cells will be lost. The Bloodspeakers are effectively impossible to entirely root out.

### Bloodspeaker Philosophy

Power is the main focus of the Cult of the Blood Red Moon. Iuchiban’s philosophies emphasized individualism: the right of every person to seek power and strength without regard for such inconveniences as birth, station, or tradition. These teachings have always had powerful appeal to those cut off from power – peasants, eta, lower-ranking samurai, junior noblemen without hope of promotion. The eta caste have been especially fertile ground for Bloodspeaker recruitment almost since the cult’s founding.

Very few Bloodspeakers are willing servants of Jigoku. In fact, for much of its history the cult denied that its power had any connection to the Realm of Evil at all. Although the cult’s senior leaders and most powerful sorcerers usually recognize they are actually using the power of the Taint, they try to conceal this from their followers and pretend they are accessing “innate energy” all mortals possess. They find ways to divert their Taint into others and to minimize their exposure to Jigoku even as they tap into its power. The result is a sort of demented tightrope act. Bloodspeakers are entranced by the powers the kansen grant them, but their leaders are often terrified of the consequences. They are both some of the most horrifyingly corrupt souls in Rokugan and some of the most fearful – obsessed with acquiring magical skill to escape death or limit the Taint’s hold on their souls.

### Bloodspeaker Technique (Alternate Path)

The secret methods of the Bloodspeaker Cult were pioneered by Iuchiban himself and passed down through Yajinden. Only those high-ranking enough to understand that Maho is the true source of the cult’s strength are allowed to learn this technique. The Bloodspeakers make no distinction between social caste.

Rank: Any. Replaces: Any. Requirement: Can only be learned from a senior member of the Bloodspeaker Cult. Special Notes: This technique can be taught to anyone, samurai or commoner, and can replace Techniques at any Insight Rank. If learned by a samurai at Rank 1, it replaces the samurai’s Rank One Technique and reduces starting Honor to 1.0, but does not affect the samurai’s Outfit or starting Skills.

TECHNIQUE: IUCHIBAN’S METHOD. The high-ranking Bloodspeakers realize they are meddling with the Taint, but have learned ways to mitigate this, in part by shunting the corruption off onto their misguided followers. You learn two Maho spells of your choice and gain a Free Raise for Spell Casting Rolls with Maho spells. When casting a Maho spell, you may reduce the amount of Taint you gain by 1 point for every 2 additional Wounds you take when casting the spell (this cannot decrease the Taint gain below 1 point). However, if you are using someone else’s blood to power the spell, you may instead use this technique to transfer the Taint for the spell onto that person, at the rate of 1 point of Taint for every 2 extra Wounds inflicted. This can reduce your own Taint gain to 0.

### Bloodspeaker Maho Spells: Bloodspeaker cult members draw on the following maho spells from the master spell list (Section 32): Heart of the Damned (Earth 1), Purge the Weak (Earth 1), Suck the Marrow (Earth 1), Symbol of Blood (Water 1), Ward of Divine Peace (Air 1), Curse of the Clan (Air 2), Curse of the Kansen (Air 2), Dark Wings (Water 2), Gift of the Maker (Fire 2), Dancing with Demons (Air 3), Mists of Fear (Air 3), Symbol of the Bloodspeaker (Air 3), Burning Blood (Fire 4), Tomb of Earth (Earth 4), Blood Armor (Earth 5), Fierce Blood of the Earth (Earth 5), Take the Body (Air 6).

### Artifact: Porcelain Masks

The infamous porcelain masks created by Yajinden and his undead student Fushiki were used to create undead armies during both the Battle of Stolen Graves and the later Battle of Sleeping River. They are full-face mempo of smooth white porcelain, sometimes painted or sculpted to display facial features but more commonly left blank except for a vague face-like shape. They have no visible method of attaching and normally have no immediate effect on a living person who puts one on. They are imbued with dangerous Tainted kansen, and those who carry them for long periods must roll Earth at TN 10 to avoid gaining a point of Taint.

The true power of these masks is manifested when placed on the face of a dead human body. The mask instantly fuses with the flesh of the corpse and the kansen within animates the body, creating a zombie that obeys whoever attached the mask, making it a reliable undead servant.

Special Ability: Khadi. Iuchiban has removed his heart with a sinister gaijin ritual. As a result, he is highly resistant to pain (Reduction 10), has no Wound penalties, and heals his Earth in Wounds during the Reactions Stage of each Round. Even if he is killed, dismembered, beheaded, or burned to ashes, his body will continue to heal and eventually reform, although it takes time.

Iuchiban, born the second son of the Emperor, was a man overlooked by almost everyone despite his high birth. Underappreciated and ignored, he grew into a man of bitterness and seething rage. No single human being in the history of the Empire has engendered such horror, such disgust, or such outright fear as the Bloodspeaker. He is the bogeyman of Rokugan, the terror who nearly destroyed the Empire not once, not twice, but three times. Even after his final defeat, his immortal black heart burned to a crisp and his soul sent screaming to Jigoku, the nightmare may not be over. His soul bides its time in whatever dark corner of Jigoku it resides, and one day it may yet find a way to return.

Yajinden, the master artificer of the Bloodspeakers and crafter of the legendary Bloodswords, remains an enigmatic figure. He has spent seven centuries in an endless search to achieve true and absolute perfection of his art, dwelling always in the shadow of Iuchiban’s power and greatness. As century mounted on century, Yajinden grew more bitter and jealous, culminating in his failed plan to enter the Tomb and consume Iuchiban’s soul. In the aftermath of Iuchiban’s third defeat, Yajinden is the only living member of the original three founders of the cult. He no longer takes a direct stake in the Cult’s ongoing activities, having departed to follow Daigotsu and the Spider Clan. Now, finally, the artist is free to create and experiment once more.

Special Ability: Jama Suru’s peculiar nature makes him strangely divorced from normal human behavior and weaknesses. He gains +3k3 on all rolls to resist mental or social influence from anyone other than Iuchiban, but suffers a –3k0 penalty to all Social Skill rolls made to influence others.

In a very real sense the name “Jama Suru” is more of a title than an actual person. For as long as there has been an Iuchiban, there has been a Jama Suru, and every time the Bloodspeaker returns a new general is created bearing the same name. The later versions are created through a blending of unique magic: a kansen is imprinted into the recipient’s body and soul, infusing them with absolute loyalty to Iuchiban and strong magical powers. This process re-forges the personality into one of rage and hate. Whether or not the original Jama Suru is still present in any way within these later versions is unknown. The Jama Suru personality does seem to have some glimmering of recognition of its past existences, and it seems at least remotely possible that Jama Suru might appear in a time when Iuchiban himself is not active.
