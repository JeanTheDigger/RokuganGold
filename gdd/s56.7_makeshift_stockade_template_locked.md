## 56.7 Makeshift Stockade Template --- LOCKED

The Makeshift Stockade template generates an improvised defensive position built by the occupants in open terrain. This is the only template where the occupants built their defensive position from scratch --- overturned carts, stacked timber, ditches, sharpened stakes. Crude but functional. It is not a real fort. It is what desperate men with no engineering training threw together. Primary terrain: Plains and Flatlands where no natural cover exists. Applicable to Ronin Bandit Uprising and potentially military field fortifications.

### 56.7.1 Size Categories --- LOCKED

Same Strength floor rule as other templates. Size determined independently, bumped up if Strength requires it.

- **Small Stockade:** A ring of carts and debris around a few tents. Barely a perimeter. One gap serving as an entrance. Can house up to Strength 3.

- **Medium Stockade:** Timber walls waist to chest height, a cleared interior with shelters, a crude gate or blocked gap. Can house up to Strength 6.

- **Large Stockade:** Full improvised palisade, multiple internal structures, a watchtower or raised platform, possibly a ditch around the perimeter. Can house up to Strength 9.

### 56.7.2 Entry and Flow --- LOCKED

The opposite of Forest. No concealment on approach. The player crosses open ground toward a defended position. The stockade is visible from far out, and so is the player.

**Entry Points:** The stockade has one main gap or gate --- the way the bandits come and go. That is the obvious entry and the most defended. But the stockade is improvised, not engineered. The walls have weak points --- sections where the timber is lower, a gap where carts do not quite meet, a spot where the ditch is shallow. The generator creates two or three weak points around the perimeter. The player can identify them by observing from distance before committing.

**Approach:** Open ground in every direction. The player decides: rush the gate head-on, circle the perimeter looking for a weak point, or wait for nightfall when visibility drops for both sides. There is no cover on the approach unless the terrain provides a fold in the ground, a lone tree, or tall grass. Mostly it is exposed movement.

**Interior Flow:** Simple. Once inside the perimeter, it is a single open space with shelters and obstacles scattered within. No rooms, no corridors, no branching paths. Everything is visible from everywhere inside. Fights here are fast and chaotic --- no retreating to a corridor, no picking enemies off room by room. The player is in the middle of everyone at once.

**Perimeter as the Challenge:** The walls are the entire tactical puzzle. Getting over, through, or around them while defenders hold the advantage. Once inside, the defenders lose their structural edge --- it is an open brawl.

**No Depth Rule:** There is no deep room or back area. The leader is simply in the most protected spot --- behind the most fighters, near the center, on the raised platform if one exists.

### 56.7.3 Population Placement --- LOCKED

Everyone is in one zone. No split between approach and objective. The stockade concentrates the entire roster inside the perimeter.

- **Wall Watchers:** Posted along the perimeter walls, spread out. Eyes on the open ground. They spot the player approaching from distance. One per wall section in Small, two to three per section in Medium and Large.

- **Gate Guard:** Two or three at the main entrance. The strongest position. They expect trouble to come through here. If the player obliges, it is a chokepoint fight on the defenders’ terms.

- **Watchtower/Platform:** In Medium and Large stockades. One or two enemies on an elevated position. Ranged advantage over both the exterior approach and the interior space. The most dangerous enemy position because they see everything and can raise alarm early. Taking them out first is ideal but they are hard to reach.

- **Camp Group:** Inside, around firepits and shelters. Not on the walls --- resting, eating, ready to respond when the alarm goes up. Most of the roster.

- **Leader Group:** Center of the stockade, near the largest shelter or the platform. Same 20-25% concentration. No separate room protecting them --- just bodies between them and the walls.

**No Patrols:** Nobody patrols outside the stockade. The walls are the security. No enemies in the approach zone at all --- the first contact is at the perimeter.

**Response Pattern:** When alarm sounds, Wall Watchers hold position. Camp Groups move toward the breach point. The entire interior reacts as one --- there is nowhere to isolate fights. This makes the stockade the most combat-dense template. Every fight pulls more enemies.

### 56.7.4 Environmental Features --- LOCKED

Minimal compared to other templates. The stockade is crude and the surroundings are open.

- **The Walls:** The defining feature. Not uniform. Some sections are stacked timber, some are overturned carts, some are piled earth and debris. Each section has a height and a durability. Taller sections block sightlines and require climbing. Shorter sections can be vaulted but defenders behind them have cover. The player can assess wall quality from distance.

- **Ditch:** Medium and Large stockades may have one. A shallow trench around part or all of the perimeter. Slows movement crossing it, makes climbing the wall harder from inside the ditch. Not deep enough to trap anyone --- just enough to break a charge and make the attacker vulnerable for an extra moment.

- **Sharpened Stakes:** Placed in the ditch or at the base of walls. Damage hazard for anyone climbing over or falling in. The player needs to spot them before committing to a breach point. Present in roughly 40% of Medium stockades, most Large ones.

- **Raised Platform/Watchtower:** A crude elevated position built from lashed timber. Provides height advantage for ranged attacks and observation. One in Medium, one or two in Large. Not in Small --- not enough resources or people to justify building one.

- **Firepits and Shelters:** Same as Forest camp zone. Soft cover, light sources at night, mark occupied areas.

- **Open Ground:** The approach space. Mostly featureless. The generator may place minor terrain --- a slight ridge, a dry streambed, scattered rocks --- that gives the player one or two brief cover positions during approach. But mostly it is flat and exposed.

- **Time of Day:** Critical here more than any other template. A daylight approach across open ground against a watched perimeter is near suicidal for a small force. A night approach reduces the Wall Watchers’ vision radius significantly. Rain and mist help the attacker by cutting visibility. The stockade is the template where weather most directly determines difficulty.

### 56.7.5 Objective Placement --- Makeshift Stockade Template --- LOCKED

Simplified by the single-zone structure. Everything is inside the perimeter.

- **Kill the Leader:** Leader is at the center, near the platform if one exists. No escape route --- the stockade walls that protect the bandits also trap them. The leader cannot flee without climbing their own walls and running across open ground. If the player breaches, the leader fights. This makes Kill the Leader more reliable here than any other template.

- **Recover Stolen Goods:** Stored inside, near shelters or in a specific cart that is part of the wall itself. The goods might literally be the wall --- a cart full of stolen rice doubling as a barricade section. Extracting them could mean dismantling part of the perimeter, which creates an exit route but also alerts everyone.

- **Burn the Camp:** Effective here. The whole structure is timber, carts, and tents. Highly flammable. The player could set fire from outside without breaching at all --- fire arrows, a torch thrown over the wall. This forces the bandits out into the open where the player has the advantage. But it destroys any goods inside. Burn and Recover are mutually exclusive objectives.

- **Rescue Hostages:** Held inside, location uncertain from outside. The player cannot scout interior positions from beyond the walls the way they can observe a village or a camp from the tree line. Going in blind is risky when hostages are involved. Talking to a captured or fleeing bandit outside might reveal the hostage location.

- **No Drive Them Out:** Unlike Occupied Village, there is nowhere for the bandits to be driven to. They are in a box. It is fight or die.

**Secondary Objectives:** Same principle. A letter, hidden koku, information about the wider insurgency. Found inside shelters or on the leader.

### 56.7.6 Resolution and Consequence --- Makeshift Stockade Template --- LOCKED

Same spectrum, shaped by the stockade’s trapped nature.

- **Full Success:** Primary objective completed. Strength reduction same as other templates by objective type. The stockade itself is destroyed or abandoned. Unlike Forest camps, the bandits cannot relocate and rebuild easily --- a stockade takes time and materials to construct. The insurgency loses not just people but infrastructure. Delays any rebuilding by at least a season.

- **Partial Success:** Player breached and did damage but did not complete the primary objective. Strength reduced by kills. Because escape is difficult from a stockade, kill counts tend to be higher here than Forest or Village even on partial success. Fewer enemies get away. The remaining bandits have a damaged stockade --- weakened walls, fewer defenders. Next mission against the same uprising is easier, not harder.

- **Retreat:** Player could not breach or got pushed back after breaching. Strength reduced only by kills, which may be minimal if the player never got inside. The bandits know they are targeted but their defenses held. They repair and reinforce. Next mission is harder --- patched weak points, more stakes, deeper ditch.

- **Failure:** Same as other templates. No reduction, Strength increases by 1.

**Template-Specific Consequences:**

- **High Casualty Template:** Both sides. The stockade forces decisive engagement --- no scattering into trees, no fleeing through village streets. Fights inside go until one side breaks. The player is likely to take more damage here than other templates, but also inflict more. Strength reductions tend to be larger per mission.

- **Destruction is Permanent:** A burned or demolished stockade cannot be reoccupied. The insurgency must find a new location and start from scratch. This makes Burn the Camp particularly effective --- even as a partial success, a fire-damaged stockade is useless to the survivors.

- **No Civilian Impact:** Same as Forest. This is bandit construction in open ground. No PU loss, no infrastructure damage to the province.
