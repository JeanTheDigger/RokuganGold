## 11.3.12a Violence — Repeated Offense and Brutal Attack Infamy — LOCKED

This addendum locks the numeric value for the `INFAMY_PER_REPEATED_OFFENSE` constant
in `ViolenceSystem`. GDD s11.3.12e states "Each additional offense adds Infamy" without
giving a magnitude. The constant also covers brutal attacks on the first offense.

---

### Source

**GDD text (s11.3.12e):**
> "Characters who repeatedly engage in such behaviour accumulate Infamy.
>  Each additional offense adds Infamy."

The GDD specifies the direction and trigger (repeat pattern) but no numeric value.
The brutal-first-offense case shares the constant because both represent comparable
degrees of antisocial aberration: one is a pattern, one is an extreme act.

---

### Calibration Anchors

| Event | Infamy | Source |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Public intimidation | +0.1 | s12.9 (LOCKED) |
| Blackmail acceptance (victim) | +0.1 | s12.9 (LOCKED) |
| Assassination execution (non-Scorpion) | +3.0 | s12.8a (locked) |
| Infamy scale floor | 0.0 | s4.6 (GDD-confirmed) |

Repeated violence and brutal first offense are comparable in stigma to public
intimidation and blackmail — hostile social acts that carry a social taint even
without a formal crime verdict. The minimum floor for infamy accrual in the system
is +0.1, which is appropriate here.

---

### Values — LOCKED

| Constant | Value | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| `INFAMY_PER_REPEATED_OFFENSE` | 0.1 | Minimum infamy floor. Matches public intimidation and blackmail (s12.9): a pattern of violence or a brutal assault earns the same social taint as threatening someone publicly. Consistent with the scale where assassination = 3.0 and floor = 0.0. |

---

### Application

The constant applies to two cases in `ViolenceSystem._determine_infamy()`:

1. **Brutal attack** (`is_brutal == true`): Any physical assault that goes beyond
   restrained striking — drawing blood, causing serious injury, using a weapon outside
   a duel context.

2. **Repeat offense** (`prior_offenses_in_window >= 1`): The second or subsequent offense
   within the REPEAT_WINDOW_SEASONS window (currently 4 seasons). First non-brutal offense
   produces no infamy (GDD s11.3.12e implies infamy begins with *repeated* offenses;
   the brutal carve-out is a structural code decision reflecting severity, not a second
   explicit GDD rule).

Both cases use the same constant because both represent the minimum threshold where
Rokugan society marks the character as someone with poor self-control.
