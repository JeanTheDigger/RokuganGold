## 22.9a Hostage Honor Losses and Capture Likelihoods — LOCKED

This section formalises the honor-loss constants for harming a hostage and for a
hostage escaping, and the personality-driven capture likelihood modifiers.
GDD s22.9 describes these effects as "catastrophic" and "significant" without numbers.

---

### Source Derivation

**GDD text (s22.9):**
> "Harming a hostage is a catastrophic breach of the political contract."
> "When a hostage escapes, the family that provided them suffers significant
>   Honor consequences for failing to honour their guarantee."
> "Characters of high courage or iron will may prefer death to captivity."

**Calibration anchors:**

| Event | Honor change | Source |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Breaking a Minor Favor | −0.5 | s12.10 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Breaking a Moderate Favor | −1.0 | s12.10 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Breaking a Major Favor | −2.0 | s12.10 (GDD-confirmed) |
| Assassination execution (non-Scorpion) | −3.0 | s12.8a (locked) |
| Honor scale floor | 0.0 | s4.6 (GDD-confirmed) |

---

### Values — LOCKED

#### Honor losses

| Constant | Value | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| `HARMED_HOSTAGE_HONOR_LOSS` | −3.0 | "Catastrophic." Matches assassination execution cost — violating the fundamental political contract of the hostage relationship. Sufficient to drive a 3.0-Honor lord to the floor. |
| `ESCAPE_FAMILY_HONOR_LOSS` | −1.0 | "Significant." Mirrors breaking a Moderate Favor — the family that provided the hostage failed to honour their guarantee. |
| `ESCAPE_CRITICAL_FAMILY_HONOR_LOSS` | −2.0 | Clean escape (margin ≥ `ESCAPE_CRITICAL_MARGIN`) is political embarrassment. Mirrors breaking a Major Favor — a complete failure to secure what the family promised. |

#### Capture likelihood modifiers

These are probability multipliers on the base 1.0 (always captured) baseline.
A value of 0.5 means the character is captured 50% of the time; the other 50% they
die fighting rather than submit.

The code evaluates: `rand_int_range(1, 100) <= int(likelihood × 100)`

| Constant | Value | Virtue | Rationale |
|----------|-------|--------|-----------|
| `YU_CAPTURE_LIKELIHOOD` | 0.5 | Yu (Courage/Bravery) | Courageous characters prefer death to submission as often as they accept capture. A 50/50 split represents genuine courage: not suicidal, but not passive either. |
| `ISHI_CAPTURE_LIKELIHOOD` | 0.3 | Ishi (Will/Determination) | Iron-willed characters reject captivity 70% of the time. Ishi is the hardest Shourido virtue — unbreakable self-reliance means most prefer death to the dependency of captivity. |

---

### Notes

`ESCAPE_CRITICAL_MARGIN = 10` is unchanged (standard L5R near-miss convention).
`ESCAPE_TN_BY_SETTLEMENT` and `LEVERAGE_RANK*` values are GDD-confirmed and unchanged.

The 1.0 default (normal capture likelihood) represents a samurai who submits
pragmatically when outnumbered — not cowardly, simply realistic. Personality
modifiers represent those who refuse this calculation.
