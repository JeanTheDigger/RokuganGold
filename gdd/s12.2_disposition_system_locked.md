## 12.2 Disposition System — LOCKED

Disposition is the underlying relationship layer of the entire diplomatic system. Every named character has a Disposition value toward every other named character they have interacted with. This value drives everything else — invitations, supply sharing, political support, duels, betrayals. Disposition is not a simple good/bad flag but a nuanced numerical value with multiple contributing factors that evolve over time.

**Scale — LOCKED:**

- Disposition ranges from −100 to +100.

- 0 = neutral, a stranger never met.

- Positive values represent goodwill, trust, friendship, alliance.

- Negative values represent suspicion, rivalry, enmity, blood feud.

**Three Categories of Disposition Modifiers — LOCKED:**

**Category 1 — Permanent Modifiers (never change):**

Set at character generation. Fixed for the life of the character. These represent things that are simply true about who two characters are relative to each other.

- Personality type compatibility: Some virtue combinations naturally mesh, others clash, some same-virtue pairings are positive and others negative. The compatibility table is a single 14×14 matrix covering all Bushido and Shourido virtues. Each character has two virtues. When two characters meet, up to four virtue-pair interactions fire and stack. The table is symmetric — X reacting to Y produces the same modifier as Y reacting to X. Maximum modifier per pair: ±20. See full table below.

**Virtue Compatibility Table — LOCKED:**

One table governs all virtue interactions. Values represent the permanent disposition modifier generated when the two virtues meet. 0 means no meaningful interaction. Multiple pairs stack. A character with Jin meeting a character with Gi generates the Jin×Gi modifier from whichever section of the table covers that pairing.

**Category 2 — Historical Modifiers (start high, decay to a floor):**

Generated by events during play. These modifiers start at a peak value and gradually decay over time toward a permanent floor value — the residual memory of the event. They never decay below the floor. Multiple historical modifiers stack.

- Structure: Starting value → decays over time → caps at floor value permanently.

- Example: Fought in same battle, same side. Starting value: +10. Floor: +5. The memory fades but the bond never fully disappears.

- Example: Fought in same battle, opposite sides. Starting value: −10. Floor: −3. Enmity softens but never fully vanishes.

- Example: Killed a family member. Starting value: −50. Floor: −50. No decay. This stays forever.

**Decay Rate — LOCKED:**

All historical modifiers decay at 1 point per 10 IC days unless flagged as no decay. A modifier starting at +20 with a floor of +10 reaches its floor after 100 IC days (25 real days). Modifiers flagged as no decay remain at their starting value permanently — they are facts of history, not fading memories.

**Cohabitation Disposition Gain — LOCKED:**

A passive background process separate from the three modifier categories. When two named characters spend time in the same settlement — whether freely or as a hostage — they slowly gain disposition toward one another. Proximity breeds familiarity.

- Every daily tick spent in the same settlement generates a small positive disposition gain between all named characters present.

- No action points required — this is entirely passive.

- Accumulates meaningfully over months and years of cohabitation.

- Applies the standard Family/Clan ripple effect — genuine friendship developed through cohabitation slightly improves view of the other character's family and clan.

- In rare cases can lead to a hostage developing stronger loyalty to their host clan than their home clan.

Cohabitation Disposition Gain Rate — LOCKED: +0.1 disposition per IC day spent in the same settlement. Since 1 real day = 4 IC days, this is +0.4 per real day. Over a full Winter Court of 120 IC days the total gain is +12 from cohabitation alone — meaningful over a long stay, negligible over a few days. This is the smallest disposition increment in the system, reflecting that proximity breeds familiarity but does not substitute for active relationship building.

**How Disposition Affects Gameplay — LOCKED:**

Disposition thresholds govern five categories of NPC behavior. These are not probability rolls — they are decision inputs. Each threshold defines the conditions under which a character considers a given action available or appropriate.

**Supply Sharing — LOCKED:**

When a lord receives a request for Rice or Arms, their Disposition toward the requester determines their response automatically at the World Map level. No roll required — this resolves as a background calculation.

- +61 and above (Trusted Ally / Devoted): Fulfills the request generously — sends up to the full requested amount without negotiation.

- +31 to +60 (Friend): Fulfills the request but automatically scales the amount down proportionally based on exact disposition value within the range. A disposition of +31 sends roughly half the requested amount; +60 sends nearly the full amount.

- −10 to +30 (Stranger / Acquaintance): Refuses by default. Will reconsider only if a Favor is offered or a political incentive exists. Even then sends less than requested. Requires an active court or visit action to unlock — not automatic.

- −11 to −30 (Rival): Refuses unconditionally unless compelled by Imperial Edict.

- −31 and below (Enemy / Blood Enemy): Refuses unconditionally. May use the request itself as intelligence about the requester's weakness or resource shortage.

Note on Imperial Edicts: An Imperial Edict compelling supply sharing cannot be refused without consequences that amount to political destruction. Refusal is mechanically possible but represents the effective end of that character's standing in the Empire — instant catastrophic Honor loss, loss of all court standing, and every other lord is now justified in acting against them. No rational NPC refuses an Imperial Edict. A player who does so has chosen to operate outside the political order entirely.

Note on Favor System: The Favor System is fully designed in Section 12.10. Favors have two types (Specific and General), three tiers (Minor/Tier 3, Moderate/Tier 2, Major/Tier 1), disposition values by tier, calling-in mechanics, and breaking consequences by tier.

**Court Invitations — LOCKED:**

Invitation eligibility is determined by two separate criteria that both apply. First, authority over the affected territory or parties — the invitation list for any court is built from the authority chain over what the court concerns: the Local Daimyo of the affected province, their Provincial Daimyo, their Clan Champion, neighboring lords whose territory borders the affected area, and lords with direct supply or military relationships to the situation. Personal relevance score determines how actively an invited character engages — it does not determine whether they are invited. Second, disposition — within the authority-eligible pool, disposition determines whether the invitation is extended.

- +31 and above (Friend+): Invitation sent automatically.

- −10 to +30 (Stranger / Acquaintance): Invitation sent only if the character holds authority over the affected territory or is directly relevant by position. No political reason, no invitation.

- −11 to −30 (Rival): No invitation unless the host specifically requires their presence for strategic reasons — a calculated deliberate choice, never automatic.

- −31 and below (Enemy / Blood Enemy): Never invited by the host. If both parties end up at the same court it is because a third party invited both independently.

Note — Court Priority System: Fully designed in Section 15.8. NPCs use primary objective as primary driver, personal relevance score as tiebreaker. Players have full freedom of choice. Proxy strategy, sequential court attendance, early departure costs, and objective negligence consequences all covered.

**Political Support at Court — LOCKED:**

When a character has no strong personal position on a topic (position between −15 and +15), disposition toward the parties involved acts as a tiebreaker for how they spend their action points. Note: this tiebreaker behavior is an emergent result of the NPC Decision Engine’s scoring formula (Section 55.4) — the topic_position_modifier fires at near-zero for a neutral NPC, leaving the disposition_modifier as the dominant component. There is no separate court-only decision system; all NPC behavior including court action selection flows through the same seven-component scoring pipeline regardless of context. Phase 5 tiebreaker rule (v588): when two or more ActionIDs produce identical final scores after all seven scoring components, the engine selects randomly among the tied candidates. This creates natural behavioral variety — a character whose scoring genuinely cannot distinguish two actions has no strong preference, and either is appropriate. Random selection is seeded per-character per-AP-slot to ensure deterministic replay.

- +31 and above (Friend+): Will spend AP supporting your position if otherwise uncommitted on the topic.

- −10 to +30 (Stranger / Acquaintance): Disposition alone does not drive AP spending. Their own topic position drives them entirely.

- −31 and below (Enemy / Blood Enemy): Will spend AP opposing your position if otherwise uncommitted on the topic.

**Duel Provocation — LOCKED:**

A duel challenge is never a random background event. It is a deliberate action — available in the character's action menu, costing 1 AP, that a character chooses to take. Players may also issue a duel challenge as a reaction to another character's action. Issuing a challenge as a reaction does not cost AP but must be declared in the Player Reaction Menu (Section 14.2).

For NPCs, the decision to issue a duel challenge is evaluated as part of their daily action selection. Disposition sets the precondition — it must be Enemy or below for a duel to enter the NPC's decision pool at all. Whether they actually choose it over other available actions depends on the full factor set:

- Disposition precondition: −31 to −60 (Enemy) — duel enters the decision pool. −61 to −100 (Blood Enemy) — duel is a high-priority option; NPC will actively seek a pretext if none exists.

- Personality: Yu (Courage) and Tan/Ketsui (Shourido: Determination or Strength) make dueling a preferred action. Rei (Courtesy), Jin (Compassion), and Seigyo (Shourido: Control) make it a last resort.

- Topic position on the duel itself: a positive position (+30 or above) makes the NPC more likely to choose it; a negative position (−30 or below) suppresses it even at Enemy disposition.

- Own dueling skill vs. opponent's: a character who assesses themselves as outmatched will not choose a duel unless personality overrides this (Yu characters may challenge regardless). A significant skill advantage makes dueling the attractive option.

- Assigned champion: if the NPC has a designated duelist, the personal risk calculation changes — they can issue challenges more freely since they are not personally fighting. However, high disposition toward their champion (above +60) makes them reluctant to risk them on a low-stakes or unfavorable challenge.

- Pretext: a legitimate pretext (public insult, court disagreement, honor slight) makes the challenge far more likely. A Blood Enemy NPC may manufacture a pretext if none exists, but this itself has Honor implications.

**Information Sharing — LOCKED:**

Deliberate active sharing of intelligence is handled by the court action menu — Disclose and Reveal a Secret Privately both cover intentional information transfer at 1 AP cost. The information sharing threshold governs only passive behavior: which topics a character is willing to include during daily conversation (Section 12.6), without deliberate intent or AP cost.

The daily conversation system already handles topic sharing automatically. This threshold adds a filter on which topics get shared based on sensitivity — a character will not spontaneously share intelligence that could be used against them with someone they barely trust.

- +61 and above (Trusted Ally / Devoted): Will share sensitive topics during daily conversation — intelligence, secrets, information that could be used against them. Proactive and open.

- +31 to +60 (Friend): Shares relevant topics freely but withholds anything genuinely sensitive or dangerous. Neutral and positive topics flow naturally; intelligence does not.

- −10 to +30 (Stranger / Acquaintance): Shares only neutral topics. Nothing that could be exploited. Safe social currency only.

- −11 and below (Rival / Enemy / Blood Enemy): Shares nothing of value. May deliberately include misleading topics — though feeding false intelligence has Honor implications depending on personality.

**Disposition Threshold Names — LOCKED:**

The disposition scale of −100 to +100 is divided into named thresholds. These names carry narrative weight and determine mechanical modifiers on court actions and other social interactions.

- −100 to −61: Blood Enemy. Open hatred. Violence is likely. Cooperation is impossible.

- −60 to −31: Enemy. Active opposition. Will work against you given any opportunity.

- −30 to −11: Rival. Suspicious and competitive. Will not actively help you.

- −10 to +10: Stranger. Neutral. No history, no attachment.

- +11 to +30: Acquaintance. Familiar. Mildly positive but no real bond.

- +31 to +60: Friend. Genuine goodwill. Will help when asked.

- +61 to +90: Trusted Ally. Deep trust. Will go out of their way for you.

- +91 to +100: Devoted. Absolute loyalty within the bounds of Bushido.

**Their Disposition Toward You — Roll Modifiers (LOCKED):**

When making a social action targeting a character, their disposition toward you modifies the difficulty of the roll. Free Raises reduce the effective TN. Additional required Raises increase it — you must achieve +5 above the base TN for each additional Raise required just to succeed.

- Blood Enemy (−100 to −61): +2 additional Raises required.

- Enemy (−60 to −31): +1 additional Raise required.

- Rival (−30 to −11): No modifier.

- Stranger / Acquaintance (−10 to +30): No modifier.

- Friend (+31 to +60): 1 Free Raise.

- Trusted Ally (+61 to +90): 2 Free Raises.

- Devoted (+91 to +100): 3 Free Raises.

**Your Disposition Toward Them — Action Authenticity Modifier (LOCKED):**

Your own disposition toward the target affects how authentically you can perform an action. It is hard to genuinely harm someone you care about, and equally hard to genuinely charm someone you despise. The modifier affects your dice pool — specifically the number of dice kept.

- Devoted (+91 to +100) performing a hostile action: −2 dice kept.

- Trusted Ally (+61 to +90) performing a hostile action: −1 die kept.

- Friend (+31 to +60) performing a hostile action: −1 die kept.

- Neutral (−30 to +30): No modifier on either type.

- Rival (−31 to −60) performing a positive action: −1 die kept.

- Enemy (−61 to −90) performing a positive action: −1 die kept.

- Blood Enemy (−91 to −100) performing a positive action: −2 dice kept.

- Note: Both modifiers can apply simultaneously. Trying to charm your Blood Enemy while they are also your Blood Enemy gives you −2 dice kept AND they require +2 additional Raises from you. This combination is nearly impossible without exceptional skill.

**Disposition Change Values ****&**** Raises System — LOCKED:**

These values govern how much disposition changes on a successful social action. Targeted actions (1 person) are more powerful than broadcast actions (everyone). Raises allow characters to gamble for better outcomes at the risk of failure.

**The Raises System:**

Before rolling, a player may declare any number of Raises. Each Raise adds +5 to the TN they must meet. If they meet the new higher TN — success with bonus effect. If they fail to meet it — regular failure, as if they never declared the Raises. Raises are a gamble: push for a decisive result at the risk of losing everything.

- Critical failure threshold: roll is 10 or more below the TN (including Raise additions).

- Regular failure: roll is 1–9 below the TN.

- Success: roll meets or beats the TN.

- Raised success: roll meets the TN including declared Raises.

**Category 1 — Targeted Disposition Values (1 person):**

- Charm: +8 disposition on success. +3 per Raise beyond TN. Critical failure: −5.

- Negotiate: +9 disposition on success. +3 per Raise. Critical failure: −6.

- Impress: +9 disposition on success, plus TN reduction on next action toward them this session. +3 per Raise. Critical failure: −6.

- Persuade / Listen and Reflect: +11 disposition on success. +3 per Raise. Critical failure: −7.

- Intimidate: compliance tracked separately, genuine disposition gain minimal. +3 per Raise on compliance. Critical failure: −8.

- Deliver Gift: base disposition gain determined by gift quality tier (Normal: +3, Fine: +5, Exceptional: +8, Masterwork/Legendary: +12 — see Section 12.2 temporary modifier table and Section 15.4 for full action mechanics). +3 per Raise on Awareness + Etiquette roll. Critical failure: small disposition loss regardless of gift quality.

- Offer a Favor: Disposition gain scales with favor tier — see Section 12.10. Minor Favor (Tier 3): +6 on success, +2 per Raise. Moderate Favor (Tier 2): +10 on success, +3 per Raise. Major Favor (Tier 1): +15 on success, +4 per Raise. Critical failure any tier: −5 disposition, no favor debt created.

- Perform Personally For: +10 disposition on success. +3 per Raise. Critical failure: −4 (awkward in an intimate setting).

**Category 2 — Broadcast Disposition Values (everyone present):**

- All broadcast actions: +2 per witness on success. +1 per Raise per witness. Critical failure: −2 per witness.

- Broadcast actions are deliberately weaker per person than targeted actions — a public performance touches everyone lightly, a private conversation moves one person deeply.

- Broadcast Family/Clan ripple: every witness gains +0.5 to their Family opinion of the performer's family and +0.5 to their Clan opinion. Rounds to +1 every two witnesses. Applies to both positive and negative broadcast actions — a Kakita performing brilliantly improves everyone's Kakita/Crane opinion, a Crab insulting the room damages everyone's Hida/Crab opinion.

**Family ****&**** Clan Opinion Ripple Effect — LOCKED:**

Personal relationships color how we see entire groups. Every disposition change between two characters creates a small ripple effect on the observer's general opinion of the target's Family and Clan. Having a trusted friend in the Crab Clan makes one generally more optimistic about Crabs. Having a Blood Enemy in the Dragon Clan generates a lingering distrust of Dragon in general.

- When Character A's disposition toward Character B changes by any amount: +2 to A's Family opinion of B's family. +1 to A's Clan opinion of B's clan.

- The ripple is proportional in direction — positive disposition changes generate positive ripples, negative changes generate negative ripples.

- The ripple is flat — it is always +2/+1 regardless of how large the direct disposition change was. A +50 shift from a life-saving act and a +8 shift from a Charm both ripple the same amount.

- Family opinion cap: cannot be moved more than ±30 through personal relationship ripples alone. Beyond this cap requires direct family-level events — a formal alliance, a war, a marriage.

- Clan opinion cap: cannot be moved more than ±15 through personal relationship ripples alone. Beyond this cap requires clan-level events — a major war, an Imperial edict, a formal treaty.

- Clan-to-clan permanent baseline modifiers (Section 12.2) are separate from and stack with ripple-accumulated opinion changes.

**Category 2 Actions — Positive Broadcast — LOCKED:**

Category 2 contains only positive broadcast actions — things that raise disposition with everyone or benefit the room. Hostile public actions (Publicly Insult, Public Debate) belong in Category 4.

**Publicly Praise Someone (1 AP):**

Single out a specific character in front of the court and extol their virtues, deeds, lineage, or contributions. Public praise is a genuine political gift in a society where Glory and reputation are everything.

- Roll: Awareness + Courtier vs TN 15.

- Success: Praised character gains +2 disposition with all witnesses. You gain +5 disposition with the praised character directly.

- Each Raise: +1 per witness for the praised character. +3 with the praised character for you.

- Failure: The praise fell flat — underwhelming. No effect on anyone.

- Critical failure: The praise came across as hollow, insincere, or condescending. −2 disposition loss for both you and the praised character with all witnesses.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] if the praised character is relevant to the main topic.

- Family/Clan ripple applies to all witnesses — praising a Crane in public nudges every witness's Family and Clan opinion of the Crane accordingly.

**Public Performance (1 AP):**

Present an artistic work to the entire court — a poem, a dance, a musical piece, a tea ceremony, a theatrical performance. Not targeting anyone specifically — offering something beautiful to the room.

- Roll: relevant Artisan or Performance Skill + Awareness vs TN 15.

- Skill varies by art form: Poem/prose = Awareness + Artisan (Poetry). Dance = Awareness + Perform (Dance). Theater = Awareness + Acting. Music = Awareness + Perform (instrument). Tea ceremony = Awareness + Tea Ceremony.

- Success: +2 disposition with all witnesses. Modest Glory increase.

- Each Raise: +1 disposition per witness.

- Failure: The performance was adequate but unremarkable. No effect — no embarrassment, just silence.

- Critical failure: −2 disposition with all witnesses. Public artistic failure is humiliating in Rokugan's court culture.

- Family/Clan ripple applies to all witnesses — every witness gains a small positive nudge toward the performer's Family and Clan opinion.

- No objective tag by default. Exception: a poem or performance that explicitly addresses the main court topic carries the [OBJECTIVE TAG].

- Performance fatigue (diminishing returns): a court audience tires of repeated performances from the same person. The first PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE by a given performer at a given court within an OOC day applies at full effect (+2 disposition, full Glory). The second PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE by the same performer at the same court within the same OOC day applies at half effect (+1 disposition all witnesses, +0.15 Glory, Raise bonus halved to +0 per witness). The third and all subsequent PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE attempts by the same performer at the same court within the same OOC day have no positive effect — the AP is consumed but the audience is saturated: “we have seen enough of this one’s art today.” Critical failure penalties still apply at full strength regardless of fatigue — a botched performance is always embarrassing. The fatigue counter resets at the next OOC Day Tick. Different performers do not share fatigue counters — two different performers can each give their full-effect first performance at the same court in the same OOC day. Tracked via a per-character, per-court, per-OOC-day counter: performance_fatigue_count on the zone_event_log or a character session variable. PERFORM_FOR (targeted, Category 1) is NOT subject to performance fatigue — a private performance for one person is always fresh because the audience is different each time.

- Venue requirement: PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE requires the zone to permit public artistic display. The validator checks the zone’s subtype for performance eligibility. Eligible zones: main court hall, banquet hall, garden party, festival ground, public shrine courtyard, street, plaza, any zone flagged performance_permitted: true. Ineligible zones: private audience chamber (use PERFORM_FOR instead), war council room, magistrate’s investigation chamber, prison, storage, barracks interior, any zone flagged performance_permitted: false. A character attempting PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE in an ineligible zone has the action rejected by the validator — “this is not the place for a dance.” PERFORM_FOR has no venue restriction beyond requiring the performer and target to be in the same zone — a private tea ceremony can happen in any setting where two people can sit together. The performance_permitted flag is set per zone subtype (both Lesser Zone and Navigation Zone subtypes) in the zone definition tables (Section 57.36 for Lesser Zones; Navigation Zone flag values PROVISIONAL pending Section 57.36 extension); it does not require per-session GM input.

**Public Declaration (1 AP):**

Announce your position on a matter to the entire court. No specific target — you are speaking to the room. Everyone present now knows exactly where you stand. A Public Declaration is as much an information action as a disposition action — it removes ambiguity and gives other characters something concrete to react to and work with.

- Roll: Awareness + Sincerity vs TN 15. Sincerity because you are genuinely committing to a position, not just arguing.

- Every witness compares your declared position to their own position on the matter. Those who share or are close to your position gain disposition toward you. Those who oppose your position lose disposition toward you.

- Success: +2 disposition from aligned witnesses. −1 disposition from opposed witnesses.

- Each Raise: +1 more from aligned witnesses. −1 more from opposed witnesses.

- Failure: No effect — the declaration was underwhelming and failed to land.

- Critical failure: −2 from all witnesses — you came across as insincere, confused, or foolish regardless of their position.

- Commitment cost: A Public Declaration is binding. Your position is now publicly known. Acting against your declared position later triggers the objective alignment malus AND a public Honor loss. The declaration locks you in.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] always applies.

- Family/Clan ripple applies to all witnesses.
