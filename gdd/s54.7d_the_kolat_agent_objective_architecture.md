## 54.7d The Kolat — Agent Objective Architecture

Kolat agents below the rank of Master navigate two loyalties simultaneously: their visible feudal role within Rokugan’s celestial hierarchy, and their covert role within the Kolat. The engine models this through a three-layer objective architecture. Each layer operates independently within the Phase 2 priority cascade, and the behavioral tension between feudal loyalty and Kolat service emerges entirely from cascade position and Phase 5 scoring within each winning objective — no special-case logic required.

### The Three Objective Layers

**Standing Objective.** Permanent. Shifts at the moment of Kolat recruitment to reflect the agent’s Sect mandate at their level. This is not the Master’s mandate — it is a formulation appropriate to the agent’s access and role. A Silk field agent’s standing objective is not “keep every active agent supplied with current intelligence and the physical network operational” (that is Master Silk’s mandate). It is something like “serve the Kolat’s interests through my position and protect the network I touch.” The standing influence bonus (Section 55.26.2) flavours every scored action toward that direction without overriding anything. When the Kolat objective slot is empty, this is the only Kolat layer running. The agent pursues their lord-assigned primary, but the standing objective’s influence means they naturally cultivate useful contacts, maintain Silk routes, and position themselves for future assignments without anyone explicitly directing them. Ambient. Deniable.

**Chrysanthemum scanner extension.** The opportunity scanner (Section 55.26.1) generates self-selected primary objectives by matching world-state against four fixed domains: Political, Military, Economic, Personal. Chrysanthemum’s standing objective does not cleanly map to any of these, so the standard scanner would never generate a self-initiated GATHER_INTELLIGENCE objective for a Chrysanthemum agent. To correct this, Chrysanthemum Sect agents (kolat_sect = “kolat_chrysanthemum”) receive a Sect-specific scanner extension that runs alongside the standard four-domain pass. The extension checks the agent’s known_topics for any topic within the Chrysanthemum collection scope (Section 54.7a) that names a specific NPC. For each such NPC, it evaluates whether the agent’s IntelDB on that character is shallow — defined as fewer than 2 known topics about them, or no IntelDB entry at all. If shallow, it generates a GATHER_INTELLIGENCE candidate targeting that NPC and adds it to the scanner’s output pool. The standing objective’s influence bonus applies a positive weight to this candidate during primary objective selection. The agent does not require a directive to initiate this. They noticed something worth digging into, and their standing objective drives them toward it. Winter court pursuit: a second Chrysanthemum-specific scanner rule runs alongside the shallow-IntelDB check. When a winter court announcement topic fires in the agent’s known_topics — naming a specific castle or settlement where the Emperor will hold court — the scanner generates a self-selected primary objective to seek placement at that winter court. This is a standard social primary (RAISE_DISPOSITION with relevant hosts, or BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the location as a prerequisite), not a Kolat objective. The agent pursues an invitation through normal feudal channels. Once placed at winter court, the passive subscription and all reporting pipelines function identically to court in Otosan Uchi. An agent who attends winter court is among the most valuable assets in the network for that season. If the scanner produces a GATHER_INTELLIGENCE self-candidate and it wins selection, the resulting primary objective is an unassigned investigation — kolat_objective: false, standard primary slot, no Kolat ActionID unlock. It reflects the agent acting on their own initiative, not on Master Chrysanthemum’s orders. Any discoveries made are still flagged by the collection scope rules and reported upward via REPORT_IMPERIAL_INTELLIGENCE exactly as passive discoveries are.

**Primary Objective.** Unchanged from the standard engine. Lord-assigned or standing-derived through the opportunity scanner (Section 55.26.1). Feudal behavior as normal. A well-placed Silk agent’s primary objective is whatever their daimyo needs done. The standing objective’s influence means this work often serves Kolat interests incidentally — the agent may not always know the difference, and this is intentional.

**Kolat Objective.** A new third slot. Dormant when no assignment has arrived from the Master. Active when the Master assigns a task through the Kolat delivery channel. When active, enters the Phase 2 priority cascade. At priority 3 it fires before the primary objective. At priority 1–2 it yields to the primary. Whichever wins in the cascade is decomposed into one ImmediateNeed and scored through Phase 5 as normal. Unlocks Kolat ActionIDs the agent could not otherwise access. When the task is completed, abandoned, or recalled by the Master, the slot returns to dormant and the agent reverts to running on standing and primary objectives only. Recruitment scanner: every Master runs the RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT candidate check on every AP as part of the opportunity scanner (Section 54.7c). This runs as a primary candidate, not a Kolat objective. GATHER_INTELLIGENCE discovery hook: when the active Kolat objective has NeedType = GATHER_INTELLIGENCE and target_npc_id is set, the decomposition function registers a discovery listener on the agent’s IntelDB. Any new topic added about that target_npc_id during that AP firing triggers REPORT_IMPERIAL_INTELLIGENCE at priority 3 on the agent’s next AP — one reporting AP, then surveillance resumes. This hook is evaluated by the decomposition function at the start of every AP where the Kolat objective is active; it requires no additional field and costs no additional engine complexity.

### Kolat Objective Delivery

The Kolat objective arrives through Kolat channels, not the feudal letter system. The delivery path depends on the agent’s access level.

**Agents who hold a Tear.** Senior agents and Hands of the Masters may carry a Tear of the Oni’s Eye. Their Kolat objective arrives via TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR. Instantaneous, undetectable, no physical courier required. Tear delivery works for simple single-target objectives that fit the Tear’s capacity — a NeedType, a target name, and a priority level. This is sufficient for objectives like “eliminate this person” or “watch this location.” Complex objectives with multiple sequenced steps, location specifics, or supporting documentation cannot be fully transmitted by Tear. Those arrive via the Silk physical channel as supplementary cipher documents, with the Tear carrying only the top-level directive and priority to alert the agent that a delivery is incoming.

**Agents without a Tear.** The majority of field agents. Their Kolat objective arrives through the Silk physical network — a dead drop, a courier meeting, a contact handoff. Encoded in Calligraphy (Cipher), routed through established Silk infrastructure rather than the Imperial postal system. Subject to the same delivery delay and interception risk as any physical communication, but travelling through channels specifically hardened against discovery.

Lateral communication: all intelligence routes through Tiger — see universal rules (Section 54.7d).

### Engine Integration

**Objective struct.** One new boolean field: kolat_objective (default false). All existing Objective fields — type, target_fields, source, assigned_ic_day, priority — are unchanged. The flag is set to true only by Kolat delivery. The source field carries the assigning Master’s code name (never their real NPC ID, consistent with Master anonymity architecture).

**Phase 2 — Goal Resolution.** The Kolat objective enters the Phase 2 priority cascade alongside the existing slots. The cascade becomes: Reactive Event → Crisis Override → Kolat Objective (if priority 3) → Primary Objective → Kolat Objective (if priority 1–2) → Standing Objective. The cascade produces exactly one winner per AP firing, which is decomposed into one ImmediateNeed as normal. No engine change to Phase 3, 4, or 5 is required. The organic tension between lord-work and Kolat-work emerges from the cascade position: a priority 3 Kolat objective fires before the primary naturally — the agent drops lord-work without being told to. A priority 1–2 Kolat objective yields to the primary — the agent attends to their daimyo first. Personality, honor, and urgency govern everything within each winning objective’s Phase 5 scoring. No mandatory minimum. No floor. Fully organic.

**Phase 3 — ActionID Pool Unlock.** Phase 3’s context generator adds Sect-gated Kolat ActionIDs to the option pool through two paths. Path 1 (Masters): if is_kolat_master: true on the character sheet, Kolat ActionIDs are always unlocked regardless of which objective slot is active. Masters are permanently in Kolat operational mode — every AP firing has the full Sect ActionID pool available. Path 2 (Field agents): if kolat_sect is set AND the active objective carries kolat_objective: true, Kolat ActionIDs are unlocked for that AP firing only. When the Kolat objective slot is dormant, no Kolat ActionIDs enter the pool for that AP. Both paths are gated by kolat_sect — only the Sect-appropriate ActionIDs are added. Sect-gated unlocks:

Chrysanthemum: MONITOR_IMPERIAL_COURT, SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT, DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE, TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR (if holds Tear). Silk: RUN_COURIER_ROUTE, ESTABLISH_DEAD_DROP, DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE, DELIVER_SEALED_LETTER, GATHER_AND_REPORT. Coin: UNDERREPORT_KOKU, LAUNDER_KOKU, TRANSFER_KOLAT_FUNDS, SPONSOR_INSURGENCY, BRIBE_GARRISON_COMMANDER, CONTRIBUTE_TO_RESERVE. Dream: CONDUCT_CONDITIONING, MAINTAIN_SLEEPER_CONTACT, ACTIVATE_SLEEPER. Jade: ANONYMOUS_TIP, ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE. Roc: No sect-specific ActionIDs. Cloud: ARCHIVE_TOPIC, RESURRECT_TOPIC, USE_CLOUDS_EYES. Steel: SECURE_ONI_EYE, CONDUCT_PERIMETER_PATROL. Lotus: ROUTE_VIA_DEAD_DROP, CHECK_DEAD_DROP, ROTATE_DEAD_DROP, ARRANGE_PROXY_DUEL, TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR (if holds Tear). ELIMINATE_CHARACTER is already in the standard pool and requires no Sect-gate. IDENTIFY_LOTUS_OPERATIVE uses only standard pool ActionIDs (ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION, PROBE, GATHER_INTELLIGENCE) under the cover persona — no additional Sect-gated actions needed for headhunting. Tiger: TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR (if holds Tear), ESTABLISH_DEAD_DROP. Tiger-exclusive system calls: EVALUATE_SUCCESSION (not a NeedType — a direct system call); vault read access (temple_vault_koku) unique to Tiger. Cloud: SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT, DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE. All: LOCATE_CHARACTER, BEGIN_TRAVEL, WRITE_LETTER, PROBE, ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (these are already in the standard pool; listed here for completeness).

**Personality filter and lean.** Unchanged. All existing filter entries apply to Kolat ActionIDs exactly as they apply to any other action. A Gi-virtue agent whose Kolat objective is active cannot reach for UNDERREPORT_KOKU or ESTABLISH_DEAD_DROP — the filter blocks them regardless of the kolat_objective flag. The agent is still who they are. This is the same constraint that governs which virtues can hold which Master seats, applied at the action level for all conscious agents.

### Universal Rules for All Sects with Agent Networks

**Agent network record (universal schema).** Every Master who maintains conscious registered field agents — Chrysanthemum, Coin, Jade, and Silk — keeps a [sect]_network_record in their special_data. Schema: Dictionary[string → Array[last_contact: int, threshold: int, agent_status: string]]. The string key is the agent’s code name. agent_status values: “active” (normal), “dark” (go-dark sent, silence expected), “suspended” (threat MODERATE, awaiting Tiger), “burned” (threat DEEP, entry retained one season then deleted). Chrysanthemum’s implementation (chrysanthemum_network_record, Section 54.7h) is the reference implementation. All other relevant Sects follow the same schema under their own field name. Dream does not use this record — sleepers are tracked through the sleeper hidden fields (Section 54.7h). Lotus does not use this record — its agents communicate exclusively through dead drops with no live check-in cadence. Silk does not use this record — Silk’s network consists primarily of unconscious assets not requiring active management.

**Network capacity ceiling (universal).** All Masters with an agent network record are subject to the same capacity limits regardless of Sect. Maximum 6 registered agents (active + dark + suspended combined; burned entries do not count toward the cap). Of the active 6, no more than 3 may carry an active Kolat objective simultaneously — the rest run on standing objective and self-initiative only. When the record reaches 6 entries, the RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT candidate scanner is suppressed for that Master. These limits are universal. A Jade Master with 6 field investigators is as constrained as a Chrysanthemum Master with 6 court spies.

**Silence detection (universal).** At each Seasonal Tick, the Kolat maintenance pass checks every [sect]_network_record entry for every Master. If the gap between current IC day and last_contact exceeds the per-agent threshold, two things fire: a Tier 4 personal concern topic in the Master’s known_topics (“[Agent code name] has not reported in [X] days”) and LOCATE_CHARACTER at priority 2 targeting that agent. Default thresholds: 30 IC days for agents in a major city, 60 for remote postings. Per-agent thresholds are adjustable via cipher directive from the Master.

**MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT trigger (universal).** Any Master whose [sect]_network_record contains an agent named in an investigation, arrest, or accusation topic fires MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT at priority 3. The three-stage response — immediate go-dark, threat assessment, response by classification — is identical across all Sects (Section 54.7c). The Master never directly eliminates their own agents. DEEP threat response always routes through Tiger → Lotus.

**Tiger directive override (universal).** When an incoming Tiger directive assigns a new Kolat objective to an agent already carrying an active Kolat objective, the new objective replaces the existing one immediately on receipt. The previous objective is abandoned without completion handling. The agent begins work on the new objective on their next AP. This applies to every Kolat objective slot in the engine, not just Chrysanthemum agents.

**Lateral communication constraint (universal).** No Master communicates directly with any other Master. All inter-Sect intelligence routing goes through Tiger. This applies even in emergencies — a Master who discovers a threat to another Sect’s operations has no direct channel to warn them. The only path is to route to Tiger at priority 3 and trust Tiger to act. This is a deliberate architectural constraint. The anonymity architecture that protects every Master is the same constraint that prevents emergency lateral communication. Tiger absorbs the coordination cost.

### The Organic Tension

The behavioral tension between feudal loyalty and Kolat service emerges entirely from the Phase 2 cascade and Phase 5 scoring. No special case is needed. A priority 1 Kolat objective sits below the primary in the cascade — the agent attends to their daimyo first, the Kolat task waits. A priority 3 Kolat objective fires before the primary — the agent drops lord-work without being told to. Once an objective wins the cascade, personality and urgency govern what the agent does with it through the normal Phase 5 formula. An agent with high Chugi lean, even pursuing a priority 3 Kolat task, will score toward actions that remain visible and defensible to their lord. An agent with low Honor may score cautiously within the Kolat task, choosing lower-risk actions even when bolder ones are available. None of this requires a rule. It emerges from the cascade position combined with the same seven-component Phase 5 formula that governs every other NPC in the engine.

When the Kolat objective slot is dormant, the agent’s behavior is indistinguishable from any other well-placed samurai. Their standing objective flavours their primaries in ways that serve Kolat interests quietly — maintaining the right contacts, staying in proximity to useful locations, keeping their skills sharp in relevant areas. This is by design. The most effective Kolat agents are the ones who have not been given a task yet.

### Conscious Agents and Unconscious Assets

Not every person serving Kolat interests knows they are doing so. The three-layer architecture applies only to conscious agents — those who know they serve the Kolat and have been assigned a kolat_sect value on their character sheet. Unconscious assets — unwitting informants, bribed contacts, low-level couriers who believe they are running merchant routes — have no kolat_objective slot, no standing objective shift, and no ActionID unlock. They are simply NPCs with relationships to Kolat agents, managed through the standard disposition and objective assignment systems. The Silk network moves information and resources through them without their awareness. From the engine’s perspective they are not Kolat. From the Kolat’s perspective they are the most secure assets in the network.

Sleeper agents occupy a special case. They are technically unconscious — they do not know they have been conditioned — but their engine must respond when ACTIVATE_SLEEPER fires. This is addressed in Section 54.7e.
