## 57.47 Crime Severity Classification and Consequence Mapping

### 57.47.1 Overview

This section consolidates all crime types into a single severity classification with authoritative Honor, Glory, Infamy, Status, and Topic consequences. It fills three gaps: (1) conviction-level consequences for Treason (Section 11.3.8) and Magistrate Corruption (Section 11.3.11) that had sentence but no numeric Honor/Glory values, (2) three crime types in the CrimeRecord crime_type enum (maho, skimming, dishonorable_conduct) that had no consequence specifications, and (3) a cross-reference table that unifies all crime consequences in one place. Resolves the deferred design note in Section 2.8.6.

Crime consequences fire in two phases, not one. At-act consequences fire the moment the crime is committed, whether or not anyone witnesses it. At-conviction consequences fire when the character is formally convicted through the legal system (Section 11.3.13). The distinction matters because Honor is internal — a samurai who murders someone in secret knows what they did, and their Honor degrades immediately — while Glory, Infamy, Status, Topics, and Sentences are public and require discovery. At-act Honor loss uses Table 2.3 (Section 4.6), scaled by the character’s current Honor Rank. A high-Honor samurai loses more Honor for the same crime because the betrayal of their own standards is greater. At-conviction consequences (Glory, Infamy, Status, Topic, Sentence) are flat values that do not scale by Honor Rank — public disgrace is public disgrace regardless of how honorable the criminal was before.

### 57.47.2 Consolidated Crime Consequence Table

Seven severity levels across six crime types. Two-phase consequence structure: at-act Honor loss references Table 2.3 (Section 4.6), scaled by the character’s current Honor Rank. At-conviction consequences (Glory, Infamy, Status, Topic, Sentence) are flat values in the decimal scale (Section 4.6): 1 point = 0.1, 1 rank = 1.0.

**MINOR — Dishonorable Conduct:**

At-act Honor: Table 2.3 “Breach of etiquette, minor” (Section 4.6), scaled by Honor Rank. At-conviction: Glory −0.1. Infamy: 0. Status: no change. Topic: none (disposition modifier of −2 with witnesses only). Sentence: verbal reprimand, possible temporary exile. Escalation: 3+ offenses in 4-season window generates Tier 4 topic. Detection: trivial. No investigation loop. Source: Section 57.47.5.

**MINOR — Violence Against Persons:**

At-act Honor: Table 2.3 “Breach of etiquette, major” (Section 4.6), scaled by Honor Rank. At-conviction: Glory −0.1. Infamy: 0. Status: no change. Topic: Tier 4. Sentence: house arrest, recompense. Escalation: per Section 11.3.12e. Detection: trivial. Source: Section 11.3.12a (LOCKED). RECONCILIATION NOTE: Section 11.3.12a specifies flat Honor −0.2. Table 2.3 mapping supersedes — flat value should be retired at implementation.

Infamy: +5.0. Status: → 0.0 (all Status stripped). Topic: Tier 1 ("[Character] violated the Emperor’s Peace at Winter Court"). Sentence: execution without seppuku. ### 57.47.3 Gap-Fill: Treason and Corruption Conviction Values

Section 11.3.8 (Treason) and Section 11.3.11 (Magistrate Corruption) specify sentence and topic tier but not exact Honor, Glory, Infamy, or Status loss values. Those sections are LOCKED. This amendment provides the missing values without modifying the locked content.

**Treason Against Lord — conviction consequences:**

At-act Honor: Table 2.3 “Disloyalty to lord, spouse, or superior” (Section 4.6), scaled by Honor Rank. Fires at the moment of the treasonous act, not at conviction — the traitor knows what they did. At-conviction: Glory −2.0 (complete public disgrace). Infamy +3.0. Status → 0.0 (all Status stripped). These fire at conviction. If the convicted accepts seppuku, the seppuku modifier applies (Section 57.47.4). If the convicted refuses seppuku and is exiled rather than executed: additional Infamy +1.0, permanent “exiled traitor” tag on the character sheet that imposes −15 baseline disposition with all samurai who learn of the conviction.

Co-conspirator naming consequences: if the convicting lord names co-conspirators in the Tier 2 public topic (per Section 11.3.8e), each named co-conspirator immediately receives a Tier 3 investigation topic. If the lord does not name them, the topic remains in the lord’s IntelDB only — quiet pursuit continues.

**Magistrate Corruption — conviction consequences:**

At-act Honor: Table 2.3 “Accepting a bribe” (Section 4.6), scaled by Honor Rank. Fires per bribe accepted (already implemented as hidden −0.5 in Section 11.3.11f — this is the Table 2.3 value at Ranks 4–5). The cumulative Honor loss from multiple accepted bribes is the at-act consequence. At-conviction: Glory −1.5 (public disgrace). Infamy +2.0. Status: stripped of magistrate office and all associated authority. If the convicted held an Emerald Magistrate position, the Emerald Champion is notified and a Tier 2 topic generates Empire-wide. If clan magistrate, the clan daimyo decides sentence. The appointing lord suffers −5 disposition from every character who learns of the conviction — the appointment reflected their judgment, and their judgment was wrong.

### 57.47.4 Seppuku Modifier — Cross-Cutting Rule

When seppuku is offered and accepted, two modifiers apply to the base conviction consequences:

Honor: +1.0 (partial redemption — the act of seppuku is itself honorable). Net Honor for Treason conviction with seppuku: −3.0 + 1.0 = −2.0. Net Honor for Corruption conviction with seppuku: −2.0 + 1.0 = −1.0.

Family disposition penalty: halved. The family still suffers reputational damage (Glory −1.0 per Table 2.4 “Family Dishonor”), but the convicted’s acceptance of responsibility softens the blow.

When seppuku is offered and refused:

Additional Honor: −1.0 (refusing the offered path compounds the original crime with cowardice). Additional Infamy: +1.0. The family suffers the full disposition penalty. The refusal itself generates a secondary Tier 4 topic: “[Character] refused seppuku.”

When seppuku is not offered (Maho only): no modifier applies. The convicted receives the base consequences with no possibility of redemption. The family suffers full consequences plus a Tier 3 investigation topic (were they aware?).

### 57.47.5 New Crime Type: Dishonorable Conduct

Public loss of composure, drunken behavior, verbal outbursts, destruction of property, or minor breaches of etiquette that cross from social embarrassment into the attention of authorities. The lowest tier of samurai crime. Described in Section 2.8.6 lore. This subsection provides the mechanical specification.

CrimeRecord crime_type: dishonorable_conduct. Added to the crime_type enum alongside the existing types (treason, murder, skimming, maho, magistrate_corruption, violence, other).

**Severity: Minor.**

At-act Honor: Table 2.3 “Breach of etiquette, minor” (Section 4.6), scaled by Honor Rank. Fires immediately. At-conviction: Glory −0.1. Infamy: 0. Status: no change. Topic: none generated — too minor for the topic system. Witnesses receive a one-time disposition modifier of −2 toward the offender. Sentence: verbal reprimand from the nearest authority figure (doshin, magistrate, or senior samurai present). Possible temporary exile from the settlement (1 IC season).

**Detection:**

Trivial. Dishonorable conduct is public by nature. The zone_event_log records the incident automatically. No investigation loop fires. Doshin in cities handle the immediate response: calming the situation, escorting the offender away, reporting to the local magistrate if the behavior was severe enough to warrant it. In most cases the magistrate does not prosecute — the social shame of the incident is considered punishment enough.

**Escalation:**

A samurai who commits dishonorable conduct repeatedly (3+ offenses in a rolling 4-season window) escalates to formal censure. The third offense generates a Tier 4 topic: “[Character] has become known for public misconduct.” The lord is pressured to act: reassignment to a remote posting, stripping of court privileges, or formal reprimand. The escalation mechanic mirrors Section 11.3.12e (Violence repeated offenses) at a lower threshold.

**Samurai-on-heimin note:**

A samurai verbally abusing or intimidating heimin is not dishonorable conduct — it is the normal exercise of caste authority, however unpleasant. Dishonorable conduct applies only to behavior that shames the samurai in the eyes of other samurai: loss of composure in front of peers, public drunkenness at a formal event, destruction of a host’s property, or emotional outbursts that violate the expected self-control of the samurai caste.

### 57.47.6 New Crime Type: Skimming (Tax Fraud / Embezzlement)

A lord or official secretly diverting tax revenue, tribute, or resources for personal use. Already in the CrimeRecord crime_type enum as “skimming.” Distinct from magistrate corruption (accepting bribes to pervert justice, Section 11.3.11) — skimming is theft from the lord’s coffers, not interference with the legal system. Distinct from treason (undermining the lord’s authority) — skimming is greed, not disloyalty, though a lord may interpret it as both.

**Severity: Moderate.**

At-act Honor: Table 2.3 “Accepting a bribe” (Section 4.6), scaled by Honor Rank. Fires at the moment of diversion. At-conviction: Glory −0.3. Infamy: +0.5. Status: stripped of the office through which the skimming was conducted. Topic: Tier 3 (“Official [X] convicted of embezzlement in [province]”). Sentence: full restitution (the entire amount skimmed must be returned). Demotion or stripping of office. Seppuku offered if the amount exceeds 10 koku (PROVISIONAL threshold — a sum large enough to represent deliberate, sustained fraud rather than petty pilfering). Execution if seppuku is refused and the amount is large.

**Detection:**

The audit system (Section 4.3.7). When a lord’s expected revenue does not match actual collections, the discrepancy surfaces through ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS or the seasonal tax review. The skimmer’s concealment depends on their Commerce skill vs the auditor’s Commerce skill. A contested roll: skimmer rolls Commerce (Mathematics) / Intelligence to set the concealment_tn on the CrimeRecord. The auditor rolls Commerce (Mathematics) / Perception to detect the discrepancy. The Kolat’s UNDERREPORT_KOKU ActionID (Section 54.7c) is mechanically identical to skimming but routes the diverted Koku to the Kolat treasury rather than the skimmer’s personal funds — same detection mechanic, different destination.

**Authority chain:**

The lord who appointed the official has jurisdiction. If the skimmer IS the provincial lord, jurisdiction escalates to their superior (family daimyo or clan champion). If the skimming involves Imperial taxes, Emerald Magistrate jurisdiction applies. The Ministry of Exchange (Section 2.1.20) may conduct independent audits of Imperial tax collection.

### 57.47.7 New Crime Type: Maho (Blood Magic)

Use of blood magic — spells powered by shed blood and kansen (corrupted kami). Any maho use is a crime regardless of intent. A shugenja who uses maho to heal a dying child is as guilty as one who uses it to kill an enemy. The crime is in the method, not the outcome. Maho is cosmic treason — it subverts the Celestial Order at its deepest level by invoking Jigoku’s corrupted spirits rather than communing with the pure kami. Described in Section 2.8.6 lore (most severe form of blasphemy). Maho spell mechanics in Section 43. Taint consequences in Section 42. This subsection provides the legal consequence specification.

**Severity: Capital.**

At-act Honor: Table 2.3 “Breach of etiquette, blasphemous” (Section 4.6), scaled by Honor Rank — the most severe Honor entry in the table. Fires at the moment of casting. At-conviction: Glory −3.0 (complete public revulsion). Infamy: +5.0. Status: → 0.0 (stripped of all position). Topic: Tier 1 (“Maho-tsukai discovered in [clan/province]” — an Empire-wide emergency). Sentence: execution without seppuku. Seppuku is never offered. The convicted has rejected the Celestial Order and is not entitled to an honorable death. The body is burned, not buried. The ashes are scattered so that no shrine or memorial can be erected.

The Tier 1 topic classification reflects the existential threat: a confirmed maho-tsukai means kansen have been invoked, Jigoku’s influence has been invited into the mortal realm, and anyone who had contact with the maho-tsukai may be compromised. The topic drives the political and investigative response through the court system (Section 15).

**Detection:**

Multiple channels, none reliable alone. PTL increase: any maho use raises the Province Taint Level whether detected or not (project rule). A province with rising PTL and no known Shadowlands exposure is a red flag for Jade Magistrate or Kuni Witch-Hunter investigation. Taint on the caster: the caster gains Shadowlands Taint Points equal to one less than the spell’s Mastery Level per casting (Section 43). A samurai with Taint Rank 2+ and no Wall service history has no innocent explanation. Physical evidence: blood at the casting site, kansen residue detectable by shugenja (Sense spell, Section 31), disturbed kami patterns. Behavioral signals: the maho-tsukai’s growing Taint manifests as mutations (Section 44) that must be concealed, personality changes (Taint-driven personality drift per Section 42), and increasingly desperate efforts to hide their condition.

**Jurisdiction:**

Jade Magistrate primary (Section 11.3.4). Emerald Magistrate and Seppun Hidden Guard also have authority. The three clan-level anti-maho organizations (Asako Inquisitors, Kuni Witch-Hunters, Scorpion Kuroiban, Section 11.3.5) can investigate and detain but must escalate to Imperial authority for formal conviction and sentencing. The local daimyo cannot pardon a maho conviction. Only the Emperor can. This parallels Kolat jurisdiction at kolat_exposure_level 60+ (Section 11.3.8f) — both maho and Kolat membership threaten the Celestial Order at a level that exceeds local authority.

**Family consequences:**

The convicted’s immediate family is investigated. If complicit (knew and concealed): they face the same charge. If ignorant: Glory −1.0 (family dishonor per Table 2.4), a Tier 3 topic naming the family. The family daimyo may choose to strike the convicted’s name from the family register — this severs the legal connection and limits further reputational damage but is itself a public admission of failure.

**Bloodspeaker Cult connection:**

Individual maho use and Bloodspeaker Cult membership are separate crimes that often co-occur. A maho-tsukai who is also a Bloodspeaker Cult member (Section 54.3) faces both the maho conviction and a treason charge for conspiracy membership. The maho conviction is the more severe of the two and drives the sentence. The Bloodspeaker connection generates an additional investigation topic targeting the local cell — where there is one maho-tsukai, there may be more.

### 57.47.7a New Crime Type: Defilement of Sanctioned Duel

Severity: Moderate. Using poison, drugs, or any undisclosed foreign substance on a weapon during a sanctioned duel. A duel is an appeal to the Fortunes to judge the righteous — poisoning your blade is telling the Fortunes you have rigged the outcome. This is not rudeness or loss of composure. It is a premeditated violation of the most sacred conflict resolution ritual in the Empire. At-act: Honor loss via Table 2.3 “Being an accomplice to a minor crime” — Rank 0: 0 | Ranks 1–2: −0.1 | Ranks 3–4: −0.4 | Ranks 5–6: −0.4 | Ranks 7–8: −0.8 | Ranks 9–10: −0.8. At-conviction: Glory −0.5. Infamy +0.5. Status: no change (lord decides). Topic: Tier 3 — “[Character] defiled a sanctioned duel with poison.” Sentence: duel result invalidated. Public reprimand. Further punishment at lord’s discretion. The poisoner’s daimyo takes a disposition hit from all witnesses for having sanctioned a fighter who cheats. Escalation: if the opponent died from the poison effects, the crime reclassifies to Unsanctioned Killing Tier 3 (covert killing — capital severity, seppuku offered, Section 11.3.9). The duel defilement conviction values are replaced by the killing conviction values, not stacked. Detection: poison in a duel is detected either before the duel (Investigation (Notice) / Perception vs coating concealment_tn, Section 57.48.9) or after the duel (physician diagnosis of the wounded opponent, Section 57.48.14). Pre-duel detection invalidates the duel before it begins. Post-duel detection triggers a retroactive CrimeRecord and investigation. CrimeRecord crime_type: duel_defilement.

### 57.48 Poison Catalog — PROVISIONAL

Poisons are created through the Craft: Poison skill and can be applied to a target in a variety of ways depending on the poison in question. Some poisons must be introduced into the bloodstream and thus require ingestion or application via a wound. Some of the most dangerous poisons require only contact with the skin. Some poisons can be alleviated or cured through use of the Medicine (Antidotes) skill, while others can only be cured with magic. The Craft entry for each poison lists the TN for creating the poison using Craft: Poison, assuming a wide range of herbal components are present. The Heal entry describes the TN necessary to alleviate the symptoms with Medicine (Antidotes). Supersedes v499 decision (no poison system). Source: L5R 4e core rulebook, pages 334–335.

#### 57.48.1 Poison Conditional Effect Persistence Rule

Unless otherwise specified, Conditional Effects caused by poisons — such as Fatigued, Stunned, and Dazed — do not expire as they normally do as described in the Book of Earth. Rather, these Conditions are not alleviated unless the poison is treated as listed under the Heal entry for that poison. This is the critical distinction between poison-caused conditions and combat-caused conditions: a poison holds its victim until treated or until the poison’s duration expires naturally.

#### 57.48.2 Dripping Poison

The preferred weapon of assassins everywhere. Named for its carefully formulated viscosity — administered by dripping the poison down a thread over the head of a sleeping target, onto the lips. Must be thick enough to cling to the thread but thin enough to drip and splatter on the target. Cannot be introduced through any means other than ingestion. Its distinctive flavour makes it highly noticeable if concealed in food. Requires four hours in the target’s system before effects become noticeable. Induces sharp stomach pain and overwhelming nausea, which many mistake for food poisoning or illness. Delivery: ingestion only. Onset: 4 hours. Duration: 24 hours (self-resolving). Effects: once symptoms begin, the target must make a Stamina Roll (TN 20) or the poison reduces their Stamina by 2. Regardless of whether the Stamina roll is successful or not, the target is considered Fatigued. The target must make another Stamina roll every hour with the same possible results, until the poison is treated or 24 hours pass. After 24 hours the poison is typically broken down by the body and all symptoms cease. It is possible for victims to die from Dripping Poison — each failed roll reduces Stamina by 2, and if Stamina reaches 0, Earth Ring and Wounds become 0 as well, killing the victim. Craft: TN 20. Heal: TN 15.

#### 57.48.3 Fire Biter

More crippling than some but less lethal than most. Must be introduced directly into the bloodstream — far too bitter to be consumed orally without being detected. Most frequent use is painted onto a weapon with a brush and then introduced into an opponent’s system via a wound. Causes extremely painful inflammation and swelling of joints throughout the body, most severely near the wound. Those who react particularly badly can suffer violent spasms. Delivery: bloodstream (weapon-applied or wound). Onset: 10 minutes. Duration: 6 hours. Effects: within ten minutes after introduction, the target is considered Stunned due to significant pain. This lasts for six hours, although a successful Stamina Trait Roll (TN 30) can reduce the severity of the effects — the target is then considered Fatigued rather than Stunned. Craft: TN 25. Heal: TN 25 (can upgrade the effect from Stunned to Fatigued).

#### 57.48.4 Night Milk

One of the first poisons exclusively developed by the Shosuro family, but has also been used by other groups — perhaps with the Scorpion Clan’s connivance, so as to divert attention away from their own use of it. A somewhat thick and milky compound that must be introduced into the bloodstream directly; ingestion is not a suitable method of delivery. The onset of symptoms is nearly instantaneous, and while they do not last long, the poison is especially brutal and virulent while in effect. Traditionally painted onto weapons, although this must be done carefully because it leaves a visible white residue. Delivery: bloodstream (weapon-applied). Onset: immediate. Duration: 10 minutes (self-resolving). Effects: the target must make a Stamina Roll (TN 25) immediately after the poison is introduced, and once per minute after that. Each failed roll results in the target becoming Dazed until the next roll. This effect lasts a total of ten minutes before ending on its own. Craft: TN 25. Heal: TN 20 (this will end the ongoing effects).

#### 57.48.5 Venom

One of the simplest tenets of the Shosuro family, who are without question the greatest poison masters in all of Rokugan, is that elaborate concoctions can be avoided by using the poison nature itself has provided — such as the venom of spiders or snakes. These compounds, while difficult to collect, have the advantage of being less suspicious than most and leaving signs that many will recognize as natural. In some cases, it befits an assassin to simply place the venomous creature in close contact with the intended target rather than attempting to apply the venom directly. Delivery: bloodstream (creature bite, weapon-applied, or direct application). Onset: 1 hour. Duration: 24 hours or until three consecutive saves, treatment, or 8 hours of uninterrupted rest. Effects: introduction of venom into the bloodstream reduces the target’s Agility, Reflexes, Stamina, and Strength by 1 each hour. At the beginning of the second and each subsequent hour, the target may make a Stamina Roll (TN 25) to prevent additional penalties for that hour. Otherwise, these penalties continue to accrue until the target succeeds on three consecutive rolls, the poison is treated, 24 hours pass, or the target has an uninterrupted eight-hour period of rest. If Stamina is reduced to 0 by the poison, Earth and Wounds become 0 as well, and the victim dies. Reduced Traits return at the end of 24 hours if the victim survives. Craft: TN 10 (to prepare the venom in a manner to apply it). Heal: TN 15 (prevents any further penalties).

#### 57.48.7 Poison Creation

Poison is created through the standard CRAFT ActionID (Category 11, 1 AP) using Craft: Poison / Intelligence against the poison’s Craft TN. Success produces 1 dose of the named poison type, added to the character’s inventory as a poison_item with fields: poison_type (enum: dripping_poison, fire_biter, night_milk, venom), doses (int, default 1), creator_id (int). Failure wastes the AP. The Craft: Poison skill assumes the character maintains a personal supply kit of herbal components and tools — no special location or market access is required. The skill itself represents the knowledge, the kit represents sustained preparation. Craftable in any context where CRAFT is available (AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, VISITING, AT_COURT). Dose concealment: a poison_item is a standard inventory item with no inherent concealment. If the character is targeted by SEARCH_PERSON (Category 6, Investigation / Perception), the dose is found automatically. If the dose is left in a room and the room is targeted by SEARCH_QUARTERS (Category 6, Sleight of Hand / Agility), the dose is found automatically. To avoid detection, the character must actively conceal the dose: on their person using CONCEAL_ITEM (0 AP, Sleight of Hand (Conceal) / Agility, Category 6) which sets a concealment_tn on the item, or in a room using Stealth to hide the object in the environment. Both actions are already designed in Section 12.8. A prudent poisoner uses CONCEAL_ITEM immediately after crafting.

#### 57.48.8 Poison Creation — Residue and Detection

Poison creation leaves physical residue in the zone where it was performed: chemical stains, unusual smells, discarded plant matter, stained tools. This residue persists in the zone for 1 IC season (same purge cycle as zone_event_log). The crafter automatically makes a Sleight of Hand / Agility roll immediately after the CRAFT roll — this costs no additional AP and is not a separate action. The roll total becomes the concealment_tn of the residue left in the zone. There is no TN to beat — higher is simply better concealment. A character with Sleight of Hand 0 rolls raw Agility (Agility dice, keep Agility, minimum result 5). Any character subsequently present in the same zone may roll Investigation (Notice) / Perception against the concealment_tn. Success reveals that poison was crafted in this zone. This does not identify the poisoner — the investigator knows what happened, not who did it. Identifying the poisoner requires cross-referencing the discovery with zone_event_log entries for the relevant period, narrowing the suspect pool to characters who performed CRAFT in that zone during the residue’s lifetime. The CRAFT entry in the zone_event_log does not distinguish between Craft: Poison and any other craft skill — it records only that a CRAFT action was performed. A zone with multiple crafters (a calligrapher, a weaponsmith, and a poisoner all working in the same IC season) produces ambiguous evidence. The risk of detection scales naturally with context: AT_OWN_HOLDINGS with controlled access is very safe because few characters have reason to investigate your private quarters. VISITING at another lord’s court is dangerous because patrols, servants, and yoriki pass through zones regularly. AT_COURT during Winter Court is extremely high risk — the court is dense with investigators, and residue in a guest room draws immediate suspicion.

#### 57.48.9 Weapon Coating — ASCII Map Application

Fire Biter, Night Milk, and Venom can be applied to a melee weapon blade or to arrows using a brush or cloth. On the ASCII tactical map, coating a weapon or a single arrow is a Complex Action. The character must not be engaged in melee (no adjacent hostile characters). One dose is consumed from inventory and applied to one melee weapon or one arrow. The coated weapon delivers the poison on the next successful attack that deals at least 1 Wound to the target — the poison must enter the bloodstream through a wound. If the attack misses or deals 0 Wounds (fully absorbed by Reduction), the dose remains on the blade or arrow (a missed arrow that lands in the ground retains the dose but is not recoverable). Once the poison is delivered (target takes at least 1 Wound), the dose is consumed and the weapon or arrow returns to its normal uncoated state. One dose = one successful wound delivery. Pre-combat coating: if the character coats their weapon or arrows before the ASCII map generates (on the world map layer, as preparation before entering a mission), no AP cost is incurred — it is preparation, equivalent to equipping armor. The weapon or arrows enter the ASCII map already coated. For arrows, pre-combat coating applies one dose to one arrow — the character must choose which arrow in the quiver is poisoned. Detection still applies once combat begins. Visibility and detection: a coated melee weapon can be detected by an adjacent character. A coated arrow can be detected by a character who examines the quiver (requires adjacency or SEARCH_PERSON). The observer rolls Investigation (Notice) / Perception against the coater’s Sleight of Hand / Agility roll (made at the time of coating). Night Milk imposes a flat −5 penalty on the coater’s Sleight of Hand roll due to its visible white residue. Fire Biter and Venom have no additional visibility penalty. Detection reveals that the weapon or arrow is coated with a foreign substance — it does not automatically identify the poison type. A character with Medicine 3+ or Craft: Poison 1+ who detects the coating can attempt a second roll (Medicine or Craft: Poison / Intelligence, TN 15) to identify the specific poison.

#### 57.48.10 Ingestion and Creature Delivery — World Map Application

Dripping Poison (ingestion-only) and Venom (via creature placement) are delivered through the existing assassination system. No new ActionID is required. The assassin executes EXECUTE_ASSASSINATION (Category 6, Stealth / Acting) with the method field set to “poison.” The assassin must possess at least 1 dose of the appropriate poison in inventory. The dose is consumed on execution. The assassination system’s concealment roll (Stealth / Acting) determines whether the act is traced back to the assassin. The 48-tick grace period defined in Section 12.8 applies — this is the window within which the target (if a player character) can seek medical help. Dripping Poison’s 4-hour onset maps naturally to this window. Venom’s creature placement variant (placing a venomous spider or snake near the target) is thematically distinct but mechanically identical — the same EXECUTE_ASSASSINATION pipeline, the same Stealth / Acting roll, the same 48-tick grace period. The creature is the delivery mechanism, equivalent to the thread used for Dripping Poison. NPC assassination targets resolve through the Decision Engine per Section 55.21.15 — the 48-tick grace period applies only to player character targets.

#### 57.48.11 NPC Poison Use — ASCII Map Entry

When an ASCII map mission generates and an NPC combatant has a poison_item in inventory and a melee weapon equipped, the weapon enters the map already coated. This is automatic — no Complex Action, no AP cost, no decision point. An NPC with poison always prepares before a fight, not during one. The pre-combat coating rule from Section 57.48.9 applies: the weapon is coated, one dose is committed, and the first successful wound delivers the poison. Detection rules apply normally — the player can roll Investigation (Notice) / Perception against the NPC’s Sleight of Hand / Agility (rolled once at map generation and stored as the NPC’s coating_concealment_tn) to spot the coating before engaging. Night Milk’s −5 visibility penalty applies. If the player detects the coating, they receive a text prompt: “You notice a foreign substance on [NPC name]’s blade.” Characters with Medicine 3+ or Craft: Poison 1+ who detect the coating can identify the poison type per Section 57.48.9. NPC allies on the player’s side (companions per Section 57.46) follow the same rule — if a Scorpion’s Claws companion has poison, their weapon enters coated. The player is not notified of allied NPC coatings unless they specifically examine the ally’s weapon. NPCs with multiple poison doses coat once per combat. They do not re-coat mid-fight — the Complex Action cost and the requirement to not be engaged make mid-combat coating impractical for AI-controlled characters. If the NPC has multiple weapon-applicable poisons in inventory, the engine selects the most lethal (Venom > Fire Biter > Night Milk, based on potential lethality). Dripping Poison is never selected for weapon coating — it is ingestion-only.

#### 57.48.12 NPC Poison Use — Assassination Decomposition Amendment

Amendment to the ELIMINATE_CHARACTER decomposition in Section 55.21.15. When the NPC Decision Engine selects ELIMINATE_CHARACTER as a NeedType and the decomposition reaches method selection for EXECUTE_ASSASSINATION, the engine evaluates whether poison is available as a method. Prerequisite check: the NPC must have Craft: Poison 1+ on their character sheet. If yes, poison is a candidate method alongside blade and arranged accident. If the NPC has Craft: Poison 1+ but no poison dose in inventory, the decomposition inserts a preparatory CRAFT step before EXECUTE_ASSASSINATION — the NPC spends 1 AP to produce a dose using Craft: Poison / Intelligence vs the selected poison’s Craft TN. If the CRAFT roll succeeds, the dose enters inventory and EXECUTE_ASSASSINATION proceeds with method “poison.” If the CRAFT roll fails, the NPC either retries next AP cycle or falls back to blade method. Method selection scoring: NPCs with Shosuro Infiltrator or Shosuro Actor school techniques score poison method highest (school training emphasis). NPCs with high Stealth but no Craft: Poison cannot select poison — they default to blade. NPCs with high Craft: Poison but low Stealth score poison method over blade because the 48-tick grace period (longest window) gives the assassin more time to establish distance from the target. Personality modifiers: Seigyo (Control) NPCs prefer poison over blade — it is the controlled, patient method. Kyoryoku (Strength) NPCs prefer blade — direct confrontation. Yu (Courage) NPCs avoid poison — it is the coward’s weapon. These modifiers adjust method selection scores, not hard gates — a Yu NPC with Craft: Poison 5 and Stealth 1 may still select poison if blade is mechanically unviable.

#### 57.48.13 NPC Poison Use — World Map Combat Resolution

When NPC-only combat resolves through engine dice rolls (no player character involved, per Section 9 design principle), poison applies as a first-wound modifier. If the attacking NPC has a poison dose in inventory and the attack deals at least 1 Wound, the poison effect fires on the target. The dose is consumed. The target must make the poison’s resistance roll (Stamina vs the relevant TN per Section 57.48.2–5). The poison’s ongoing effects (Trait reduction, Conditional Effects) are applied to the target’s character record and resolved over subsequent daily ticks. For Company-level combat (army engagements without player involvement), poison is not modeled — individual poison doses do not scale to formation-level combat. Poison is a named-character weapon, not a mass-combat tool. For NPC-vs-NPC duels resolved through engine dice rolls, poison applies identically to the named-character rule above: first wound delivers, dose consumed, resistance roll, effects tracked. A poisoned NPC in a duel suffers the Conditional Effects during subsequent duel rounds — a Stunned duelist cannot act normally, a Dazed duelist suffers TN penalties. This makes poison a powerful but dishonorable pre-duel preparation. If the poison is detected (Investigation vs Sleight of Hand, same as ASCII map), the poisoner faces Defilement of Sanctioned Duel charges (Section 57.47.7a) at minimum, and the duel is invalidated.

#### 57.48.14 Poison Diagnosis

A poisoned character displays symptoms — nausea, joint swelling, disorientation, Trait degradation — but the cause is not automatically known. Poison symptoms can mimic illness, exhaustion, or combat aftereffects. Identifying that the cause is poison requires a diagnosis check. Diagnosis is a Free Action with no AP cost. It is a knowledge check, not an activity. On the ASCII map: a character with Medicine 1+ who is adjacent to or examining a poisoned character (or themselves) can roll Medicine / Intelligence vs the poison’s Diagnosis TN. Success identifies that the character is poisoned and names the poison type. Failure means the observer does not recognize the cause — they see symptoms but cannot distinguish poison from illness. One diagnosis attempt per observer per poison instance. If the observer fails, they cannot retry — a different character with Medicine 1+ must attempt, or the poison must progress until the symptoms become unmistakable (see progressive TN reduction below). On the world map: diagnosis fires automatically when a character with Medicine 1+ is co-located with a poisoned character during any social interaction (Daily Conversation, Deliberate PC-Initiated Conversation, or any ActionID requiring co-location). A physician does not need to spend AP to notice someone sweating, shaking, and losing coordination — they see it and recognize it. Self-diagnosis: a character with Medicine 1+ can diagnose their own condition at 0 AP, 0 action cost. They recognize their own symptoms. A character with Medicine 0 knows they feel terrible but cannot identify why — they must seek someone with Medicine skill. Diagnosis TNs by poison type: Dripping Poison — TN 20 (symptoms mimic food poisoning, subtle onset, easily dismissed as illness). Fire Biter — TN 15 (extreme joint inflammation directly correlated with a recent wound, suggestive pattern). Night Milk — TN 10 (immediate onset after a wound, extremely fast, obvious cause-and-effect to any trained eye). Venom — TN 20 (multi-Trait reduction mimics natural illness, source material notes that symptoms leave signs many will recognize as natural). Progressive TN reduction: as the poison progresses and symptoms worsen, diagnosis becomes easier. For Venom: the diagnosis TN drops by 5 for each Trait point the victim has lost (cumulative across all affected Traits). A victim who has lost 4 total Trait points has a diagnosis TN of 20 − 20 = 0 (automatic). For Dripping Poison: the diagnosis TN drops by 5 after each failed Stamina resistance roll. A victim who has failed 3 hourly rolls has a diagnosis TN of 20 − 15 = 5. For Fire Biter and Night Milk: the diagnosis TN drops by 5 after the first failed resistance roll (symptoms fully manifest). Minimum diagnosis TN is 5 in all cases — even obvious poisoning requires a trained eye to confirm. This progressive reduction means a physician who was not present at onset can still diagnose a worsening case later — the body tells the story even if no one was watching when it started. A new observer encountering the victim for the first time rolls against the current (reduced) TN, not the original onset TN. Diagnosis is a prerequisite for treatment. A character cannot receive TREAT_WOUND with Medicine (Antidotes) emphasis unless the poison has been diagnosed — the healer must know what they are treating before they can treat it.

#### 57.48.15 Poison Treatment via TREAT_WOUND

Amendment to Section 57.31. Poison treatment uses the existing TREAT_WOUND ActionID (Category 11, 1 AP) with the Medicine (Antidotes) emphasis instead of Medicine (Wound Treatment). No new ActionID is required. The emphasis determines the roll and effect: when TREAT_WOUND targets a character whose poison has been diagnosed (Section 57.48.14), the healer rolls Medicine (Antidotes) / Intelligence vs the poison’s Heal TN (listed per poison in Sections 57.48.2–5). Success alleviates or ends the poison’s effects as described in each poison’s Heal entry. Failure means the treatment did not work — the poison continues its effects normally. One treatment attempt per poison instance per target. If the first attempt fails, the healer cannot retry the same poison on the same target — a different healer must attempt, or the poison must run its natural course. This mirrors the one-treatment-per-IC-day limit on Wound Treatment but is stricter: one attempt total per healer per poison instance, not one per day. Target restrictions: same as standard TREAT_WOUND (Section 57.31.3) — the healer treats an ally, not themselves. Self-treatment is not supported. A poisoned character who is alone must endure the poison’s effects until they reach someone with Medicine (Antidotes) or until the poison’s duration expires naturally. Medicine Kit: not consumed for poison treatment. Antidote preparation uses the healer’s knowledge and locally available herbal materials, not the standardized Medicine Kit. The Kit remains at its current charge count. Daily limit interaction: poison treatment and wound treatment are tracked separately. A character can receive one TREAT_WOUND (Wound Treatment) AND one TREAT_WOUND (Antidotes) on the same IC day — they are different medical procedures treating different conditions. The last_medicine_treatment_ic_day field (Section 22.3) gates Wound Treatment only, not Antidotes.

#### 57.48.16 NPC Reactive Behavior — Seek Poison Treatment

Amendment to the NPC Decision Engine (Section 55). When a named NPC’s character record has an active poison condition (any poison_type field is set with remaining duration > 0), the engine fires a reactive check at the start of each AP cycle. This is not a NeedType — it is a reactive trigger, same pattern as the combat wound check that drives TREAT_WOUND scoring in Section 57.31.7. The reactive check evaluates: (1) is the NPC poisoned? (2) has the poison been diagnosed? If the NPC has Medicine 1+, self-diagnosis fires automatically at 0 AP per Section 57.48.14. If the NPC has Medicine 0, diagnosis requires co-location with a character who has Medicine 1+ — the NPC seeks such a character through TRAVEL_TO or existing social interaction. (3) Once diagnosed, the NPC seeks treatment. The engine scans co-located characters for Medicine (Antidotes) 1+. If a qualified healer is present, the NPC requests treatment — the healer’s Decision Engine scores TREAT_WOUND (Antidotes) highly for a poisoned ally (alignment score 95 for allies with disposition ≥ Neutral, 50 for acquaintances, 0 for enemies). If no qualified healer is co-located, the NPC’s TRAVEL_TO scores the nearest settlement with a known physician NPC. Priority: this reactive check operates at priority 2 — below crisis response (priority 3) but above all standard objectives. A poisoned NPC drops what they are doing to seek help unless a crisis overrides. Lethal poisons (Dripping Poison with failing Stamina rolls, Venom with Stamina approaching 0) elevate to priority 3 — the NPC treats their impending death as a crisis. Failed diagnosis fallback: if the co-located physician’s diagnosis roll fails (one attempt per observer per poison instance, Section 57.48.14), the NPC’s next action depends on lethality. For non-lethal poisons (Night Milk, Fire Biter) or for lethal poisons where current Stamina is above 50% of starting value: the NPC endures. The reactive trigger remains active but does not push TRAVEL_TO — the poison will self-resolve within its duration (10 minutes for Night Milk, 6 hours for Fire Biter, 24 hours for Dripping Poison and Venom). The NPC resumes normal objectives with whatever Conditional Effects the poison imposes. For lethal-capable poisons (Dripping Poison or Venom) where current Stamina is at or below 50% of starting value: the NPC seeks a second physician. TRAVEL_TO scores the next nearest settlement with a known Medicine character, excluding the physician who already failed. This is a genuine race against the hourly Stamina reduction — the NPC may die in transit if the next physician is too far away. If no second physician is reachable within the poison’s remaining duration, the NPC has no recourse and endures the poison’s natural course. Reactive trigger clearance: the reactive trigger clears automatically when the poison’s remaining_duration reaches 0 (natural expiry), when treatment succeeds, or when the character dies. No manual clearance is required.

#### 57.48.17 NPC Pre-Duel Poison Decision

Amendment to the duel pipeline (Section 4.5.3, Section 55). When a duel is confirmed (challenge issued and accepted, both daimyo sanctioned), the engine inserts a pre-duel preparation evaluation for each participant. For most NPCs, this evaluation does nothing — a normal samurai prepares their blade, meditates, and waits. For an NPC with Craft: Poison 1+, the engine evaluates whether to coat their weapon with poison. Hard blocks (never considers poison, immediate skip, no further evaluation): Yu (Courage) is an active Bushido virtue for this character, Gi (Righteousness) is an active Bushido virtue, Makoto (Sincerity) is an active Bushido virtue, or current Honor is 5.0 or above. Any single hard block is sufficient — the character will not poison a duel weapon under any circumstances. Evaluation pool (may consider poison): the NPC must have Craft: Poison 1+ and must NOT have any hard block. The engine computes a poison_score from the following factors. Opponent threat: (opponent’s relevant weapon skill − own relevant weapon skill) × 5. An outmatched NPC is more tempted. If the NPC is the superior fighter, this contributes 0 (minimum 0, does not go negative). Honor modifier: (5.0 − current Honor) × 5. Lower Honor = higher score. An Honor 2.0 character adds +15. An Honor 4.9 character adds +0.5. Personality modifiers: Seigyo (Control) +20, Kyoryoku (Strength) +10, Ketsui (Determination) +10. School modifier: Shosuro Infiltrator or Shosuro Actor school +30 (institutional training in poison use). Detection risk: −(opponent’s Investigation skill × 5). A Kitsuki opponent with Investigation 5 contributes −25. Stakes modifier: duel to the death +15, duel to first blood +0. Desperation amplifies willingness to cheat. Threshold: if poison_score exceeds 50, the NPC decides to use poison. PROVISIONAL — threshold subject to balancing. If the NPC decides to poison, the engine checks inventory for a weapon-applicable dose (Fire Biter, Night Milk, or Venom — not Dripping Poison, which is ingestion-only). If a dose is present, proceed to pre-combat coating (Section 57.48.9). If no dose is present, the crafting location selection logic (Section 57.48.18) fires to determine where to craft one. If crafting is not feasible (no time, no safe location), the NPC abandons the poison plan and fights clean.

#### 57.48.18 NPC Poison Crafting — Location Selection

When an NPC has decided to craft poison (either for a pre-duel coating per Section 57.48.17 or for an assassination preparation per Section 57.48.12), the engine evaluates where to perform the CRAFT action. The primary factor is detection risk — the residue left by crafting (Section 57.48.8) can be discovered by any character with Investigation (Notice) in the same zone. The NPC should craft where discovery is least likely. Location scoring: AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, private quarters (own bedroom, private study, personal chambers): +30. The NPC controls access. Magistrates need a warrant to search. Patrols do not enter a lord’s private quarters. Servants in Rokugan do not question their lord’s activities. Lowest detection risk. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, shared spaces (dojo, garden, common area): +10. Other characters pass through. Zone_event_log records traffic. Residue discoverable by anyone with Investigation 1+ who enters the zone. Better than enemy territory, worse than private quarters. VISITING, guest quarters at another lord’s court: −10. Guest rooms may be patrolled. Yoriki may be assigned to guest wings. During Winter Court, Investigation-capable characters are abundant. The NPC is far from home with no control over who enters the zone. AT_COURT during active court session: −30. Never viable. Court zones are dense with observers, magistrates, and investigators. Crafting poison here is effectively impossible without detection. Risk comparison: the NPC compares their expected Sleight of Hand / Agility roll (Sleight of Hand + Agility dice, estimate as rank × 5 + 10 for average rolls) against the settlement’s highest known Investigation NPC (Investigation × 5 + Perception × 5 as estimated opposition). If the NPC’s expected concealment_tn exceeds the estimated opposition by at least 5, the location is considered safe enough. If not, the NPC prefers a safer location. Time pressure integration: if the NPC is VISITING and the duel date is imminent, the engine checks whether there is enough time to BEGIN_TRAVEL home, CRAFT at private quarters, and return before the duel. Travel time per Section 57.50.1 character travel rate (4 provinces per IC day, 2 provinces per AP slot). If travel home and back fits within the remaining time → travel home, craft, return. If not → the NPC evaluates the risk at the current location. If the risk comparison passes (expected concealment_tn exceeds opposition by 5+) → craft here despite the location penalty. If the risk comparison fails and there is no time to travel → abandon the poison plan and fight clean. The NPC does not craft poison in a location where they expect to be caught. A Shosuro with Sleight of Hand 4 and Agility 4 at a Crane court staffed with Kitsuki investigators will travel home if possible, or abandon the plan if not. A Shosuro with Sleight of Hand 4 at a minor Mantis port with no trained investigators will craft on-site without hesitation.

### 57.49 Player Character Death — LOCKED

#### 57.49.1 Core Rule — Permanent Death

When a player character’s Wounds reach the Dead Wound Rank, the character is dead. There is no resurrection, no divine intervention, no second chance. Death is permanent. This applies regardless of the cause of death: combat on the ASCII map, poison (Stamina reduced to 0), assassination (grace period expired or blade method), seppuku (voluntary or compelled), execution (criminal conviction), mass battle (Company destroyed with commander present), Taint (mutation cascade), or any other source. The 48-tick assassination grace period (Section 12.8) exists to give the player time to prevent death — it does not activate after death has occurred. Shugenja communion with the dead (Kitsu Sodan Senzo, Section 29.4) allows the living to speak with a dead character’s spirit in Meido — this is a narrative and intelligence tool, not a resurrection mechanic. The dead character remains dead. Their spirit can provide information about the circumstances of their death, deliver final messages, or offer counsel, but they cannot return to life.

#### 57.49.2 Corpse as World Object

When a character dies, a corpse object spawns at the death location. The corpse contains the character’s full inventory at the moment of death: weapons, armor, medicine kits, crafted items, poison doses, letters, koku, personal effects. Any item the character was carrying or had equipped transfers to the corpse’s inventory. On the ASCII map: the corpse occupies the tile where the character fell. Any player character in the same zone or adjacent tile can interact with it: examine (see inventory), take items (move items to own inventory), or leave it. On the world map: the corpse exists as an object in the Lesser Zone where the character died. Any player character co-located in that zone can interact with it through standard zone interaction (equivalent to examining an object in the environment). Corpse persistence rule: the corpse persists as long as at least one player character is present in the same zone (world map) or on the same ASCII map. When the last player character leaves the zone or the ASCII map despawns at mission end, the corpse despawns with it. The fiction is that local people — peasants, servants, monks, or passing samurai — handle the body off-screen once no player is present to witness it. Items on a despawned corpse are lost permanently. They are not recovered, redistributed, or added to any stockpile. A player who wants to retrieve items from a fallen ally must do so before leaving the zone or before the ASCII map mission ends. This creates a tactical decision during dangerous missions: do you spend actions looting a fallen companion while enemies are still present, or do you press forward and accept the loss?

#### 57.49.3 Character Record Archival

The dead character’s full record is never deleted. It is archived and flagged as deceased. The archived record includes: all character sheet fields (Section 22.3), full disposition history with every character they ever interacted with, all topics they held at death, all CrimeRecords (as perpetrator and as victim), provenance records for any items they created, met_characters list, operational_superior_id chain, and the complete active_poisons list at time of death (for forensic investigation — a physician examining the body can diagnose poison post-mortem). The archived record is accessible to staff and DMs for dispute resolution, historical research, and debugging. It is not accessible to the player. The dead character’s name, deeds, and legacy persist in the living world through topics, provenance records on items they created, and the memories of characters who knew them. A Masterwork painting by a dead artist retains its provenance — the artist’s name and history are part of the item’s permanent record. A topic about a dead character decays normally per the topic system (Section 16) — the world eventually forgets, unless the deeds were significant enough to generate Tier 1 or Tier 2 topics that persist longer.

#### 57.49.4 World Consequences of PC Death

The same systems that fire for NPC death fire for PC death. No special treatment. Succession: if the dead PC held a lord position (Local Daimyo or above), the succession system (Section 57.20) fires immediately. The position becomes vacant, FILL_VACANCY activates, and the feudal hierarchy processes the transition per the standard rules (suspended tax cascade, frozen stockpile, army command transfer). The dead PC’s NPC successor inherits the position — the player’s new character does not automatically inherit their old character’s holdings. Army command: if the dead PC commanded an army in the field, the commander death consequences from Section 11.7 fire immediately — Morale penalties, bonus removal, next officer in the chain assumes tactical command. Topic generation: a named character death generates a topic. The tier depends on the character’s Status and the circumstances: death in battle generates a Tier 3 or Tier 2 topic (Glory-positive — heroic death). Death by assassination generates a Tier 2 topic (political crisis). Death by seppuku generates a Tier 3 topic (Honor-positive if voluntary, Honor-negative if compelled). Death by execution generates a Tier 2 topic (criminal consequence, reflects on the lord who ordered it). Kolat: if the dead PC was a Kolat operative, MANAGE_COMPROMISED_AGENT fires if the death exposed the network. If the death was unrelated to Kolat activity, the operative is simply removed from the sect roster — one fewer asset. Bloodspeaker Cult: if the dead PC was a cultist, the cell loses a member. The cell leader adjusts operations per the standard Bloodspeaker AI (Section 54.3). Companions: any NPC companions (yojimbo, yoriki, doshin) assigned to the dead PC become unassigned. The operational_superior_id field clears. They revert to their lord’s general pool and await reassignment through FILL_VACANCY or APPOINT_TO_POSITION.

#### 57.49.5 New Character Creation

After death, the player creates a new character from scratch. New clan, new family, new school, new name, new starting position. Standard L5R 4th Edition character creation rules apply (Section 22.3 for the full field schema). The new character has no mechanical connection to the previous character — no inherited XP, no inherited items, no inherited relationships, no inherited position. The new character enters the world at Insight Rank 1 with starting equipment per their school. The world they enter is the one their previous character helped shape. A player whose Lion lord died in battle may create a Crane courtier and encounter the political fallout of their own former character’s death from the other side. This is the design intent — the persistent world carries forward, the player adapts. Starting Location Rules — PROVISIONAL: A new character’s starting location is determined by their character type. Great Clan and Minor Clan characters: the player selects a starting settlement from the unconquered holdings of their chosen family. A holding is eligible if it is controlled by the character’s family and has not been conquered by an enemy clan or destroyed. If the family’s primary holding has fallen but the family retains other holdings, the player selects from the remaining holdings. If the family holds no unconquered territory, that family is unavailable for character creation. Hiruma family exception: the Hiruma family’s ancestral holdings are canonically lost to the Shadowlands. Hiruma characters select their starting settlement from Hida family holdings instead. Ronin: the player selects any unconquered, non-access-gated settlement in Rokugan as their starting location. Settlements requiring special access (Ekohikei, Forbidden City) and Shadowlands territory are excluded. No clan or family restriction applies. Brotherhood of Shinsei monks: not available as a starting character type. The character begins play at the selected settlement at the start of the next game tick following character creation. They carry starting equipment per their school (Section 22.3) and no other possessions, relationships, or obligations.

### 57.49.7 Marriage Dissolution Mechanic — PROVISIONAL

Marriage dissolution is the formal ending of a living marriage. It is extremely rare. Death of a spouse is handled by the death system and does not use this mechanic.

Grounds for Dissolution: Four pathways exist, each with distinct authority requirements and consequences.

Pathway 1 — Lord’s Command: A Family Daimyo or above with authority over either spouse may order the marriage dissolved unilaterally. The other spouse’s family is not required to agree. This is a political act — it severs a negotiated arrangement and insults the other family. Precondition: the ordering lord must be the Family Daimyo (or higher) of one of the two spouses. Provincial Daimyo and below cannot order dissolution. Consequences: ordering lord suffers Honor −1.0 (breaking a sworn arrangement). Both spouses suffer Glory −1.0 (public embarrassment). The other spouse’s family suffers disposition −25 toward the ordering lord’s family (PROVISIONAL). If the dissolution is cross-clan, the clan-level disposition penalty is −10 toward the ordering lord’s clan (PROVISIONAL). Both spouses return to their respective family’s primary holding. Children belong to the father’s family per Rokugani custom and remain with the father’s household. T4-83 MARRIAGE_DISSOLVED fires.

Pathway 2 — Criminal Conviction: When a spouse is convicted of a Tier 1 crime (treason, maho, Kolat membership), the innocent spouse’s Family Daimyo may dissolve the marriage without the usual diplomatic cost. This is the justified dissolution — the convicted spouse’s family bears the shame, not the dissolving family. Precondition: active conviction in CrimeRecord for treason (Section 11.3.8), maho (Section 11.3.4), or Kolat membership (Section 54.7i). Consequences: no Honor loss for the ordering lord or the innocent spouse. The convicted spouse’s family suffers the standard criminal consequences per Section 57.47. No disposition penalty to the dissolving family — the conviction justifies the dissolution. The innocent spouse returns to their family’s primary holding. Children belong to the father’s family unless the father is the convicted party, in which case the innocent mother’s Family Daimyo may claim them. T4-83 MARRIAGE_DISSOLVED fires.

Pathway 3 — Monastic Retirement: When a spouse voluntarily retires from samurai life and takes monastic vows (Section 22.5), the marriage functionally ends. This is not a hostile act — retirement is a respected choice in Rokugani society. Precondition: one spouse’s status changes to retired with monastic vows. Consequences: no Honor loss, no Glory loss, no disposition penalty. The retiring spouse enters the Brotherhood. The remaining spouse is unmarried and eligible for remarriage. Children remain with the non-retiring parent’s household. T4-83 MARRIAGE_DISSOLVED fires with the monastic variant tag.

Pathway 4 — Imperial Decree: The Emperor may dissolve any marriage in the Empire by direct command. This is the rarest pathway — used only when the marriage itself is a political problem at the Imperial level. Precondition: Emperor NPC action, triggered by extreme political circumstances (two clans at war whose alliance marriage has become untenable, or as a punitive measure). Consequences: no Honor loss for either spouse — the Emperor’s word is law. No disposition penalty toward the Emperor (his authority is absolute). The political consequences of the decree depend on context and are handled by the topic system. T4-83 MARRIAGE_DISSOLVED fires.

NPC Engine Integration: DISSOLVE_MARRIAGE is a new ActionID (Category 14, 1 AP, Courtier / Awareness, Family Daimyo+ only). The NPC engine should only generate DISSOLVE_MARRIAGE as a Phase 3 option under extreme conditions. The alignment score for DISSOLVE_MARRIAGE is 0 against all NeedTypes except AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE, where it scores 15 (PROVISIONAL) — below every other hostile action. Prerequisite gate: the ActionID scores 0 and is excluded from Phase 5 unless at least one of the following is true: (a) the other spouse’s clan disposition is at Enemy (−31) or below; (b) the other spouse holds an active CrimeRecord for a Tier 1 crime; (c) the ordering lord has received an Imperial Edict that conflicts with the alliance the marriage represents. This prerequisite gate ensures the engine almost never considers dissolution under normal conditions.

### 57.50 Travel Event System — PROVISIONAL

#### 57.50.1 Design Principle

Travel is an active AP-spending process, not a passive timer. Each BEGIN_TRAVEL action (1 AP) moves the character up to 2 provinces, passing through all sub-tiles along the route. The character may land on any sub-tile within those 2 provinces. To continue further, the character must spend another BEGIN_TRAVEL (1 AP). With 2 AP per IC day and 4 IC days per OOC day, a character has 8 AP per OOC day. A character who dedicates all AP to travel covers a maximum of 16 provinces per OOC day (8 BEGIN_TRAVEL actions × 2 provinces each). This makes long-distance travel fast but costly — every AP spent moving is an AP not spent on court actions, crafting, training, or other activities. A character who travels 4 provinces (2 AP) still has 6 AP for other actions that OOC day. IC travel speed: each BEGIN_TRAVEL consumes one AP slot (half an IC day, Morning or Afternoon) and covers up to 2 provinces. Character travel speed is therefore 4 provinces per IC day when both AP slots are spent on travel. This is the authoritative character travel rate — all systems that reference travel time for named characters (hunt date calculations in Section 57.38, duel timing in Section 57.48.17–18, NPC travel decomposition in Section 55, fugitive pursuit in Section 11.3.18) should use this rate. PROVISIONAL. Travel events are generated exclusively by active world state conditions in the sub-tiles a character crosses during BEGIN_TRAVEL. No random encounter tables. If a sub-tile has no active condition, passage through it is uneventful. A peaceful, well-governed province with no insurgency, no war, no spiritual disturbance, and no Taint produces no travel events — the character passes through safely. Events fire only because something is happening in the world. This is consistent with the core design principle (Section 9): content is a physical manifestation of world state.

#### 57.50.2 Trigger Conditions

The engine checks each sub-tile the character crosses during a single BEGIN_TRAVEL action. Each province contains 4–5 sub-tiles (Section 4.4.1). A BEGIN_TRAVEL action covering 2 provinces crosses 8–10 sub-tiles. For each sub-tile, five conditions are evaluated independently. Multiple conditions in the same sub-tile stack — each fires or does not on its own roll. A sub-tile in a province with War Status, an active bandit insurgency, and a spirit realm overlap checks all three separately. A traveler could face multiple events crossing a single sub-tile. All percentages below are per sub-tile crossed, calibrated so that crossing one full province (4–5 sub-tiles) produces roughly the same overall encounter probability as the earlier per-province design. Military escort rule: a character traveling with 3 or more armed companions (Section 57.46) is immune to Condition 1 (bandit/criminal), Condition 4 (Tainted creatures), and Condition 5 (diplomatic hostility patrol) encounter checks. Armed companions include doshin (a single doshin team slot counts as 2–3 armed companions), yojimbo, yoriki, and named NPC bushi allies. Unarmed companions (courtier NPCs, servants) do not count. A character traveling with a yojimbo and a doshin team (3–4 armed companions total) meets the threshold. A character traveling alone or with a single yojimbo does not. Condition 2 (War Status) is not negated — a small escort is insignificant relative to an army in the field. Condition 3 (spirit realm overlap) is not negated — spiritual phenomena are environmental, not creature encounters.

Condition 1 — Bandit or Criminal Activity. Two sources trigger this condition: under-garrisoned provinces and active insurgencies. Under-garrisoned: if the province’s garrison PU is below 5% of settlement PU (Section 4.3.11), opportunistic banditry is present. Base chance: 3% per sub-tile. Active insurgency: if the province has an active insurgency (Section 11.11) with Strength greater than 0. Base chance: 2% × insurgency Strength per sub-tile. When both conditions apply in the same province, use the higher of the two percentages — do not stack. At insurgency Strength 1 (2% per sub-tile), the under-garrisoned flat 3% governs. At insurgency Strength 2+ (4%+ per sub-tile), the insurgency percentage takes over. This creates a clean handoff: poorly policed provinces produce petty banditry; organized insurgencies produce worse. Skill reduction: Hunting 3+ or Stealth 3+ halves the final percentage (the character knows how to read terrain and avoid trouble on the road). Both skills qualifying does not stack — the reduction is halved once, not quartered. Minor event: the character spots a bandit scout, finds a looted merchant cart, or hears rumors at a roadside tea house. A character with Status 5.0+ may attempt to intimidate opportunistic bandits into retreating without combat — Intimidation / Willpower TN 15 (scaled to insurgency Strength if applicable: TN 15 + Strength × 5). Success: the bandits flee, no combat, no ASCII map. Failure: combat proceeds normally. Major event (insurgency Strength 3+ only — under-garrisoned without an insurgency never produces major events): bandit ambush. Generates a small ASCII map encounter — 3 to 5 bandits (scaled to insurgency Strength), flee objective available, terrain drawn from the province’s terrain type. The character can fight or run. Companion NPCs traveling with the character (Section 57.46) participate in the encounter. RECONCILIATION NOTE: Section 4.3.11 (LOCKED) defines “Named character movement risk” as a 15% encounter check for under-garrisoned provinces, negated by 1.0 PU military escort. This condition absorbs and supersedes that mechanic at implementation. The 15% flat rate and the social resolution option are preserved here. The 1.0 PU military escort threshold is replaced by the companion-based escort rule (3+ armed companions, Section 57.50.2) — the PU abstraction does not map to the companion system that governs individual character travel. Section 4.3.11’s movement risk text should be read as a reference to this system.

Condition 2 — War Status. The province is in active War Status (Section 11.7). Base chance: 3% per sub-tile. Skill reduction: Battle 3+ halves the percentage (the character reads the terrain and avoids active formations). Minor event: the character encounters a patrol checkpoint (Etiquette / Awareness TN 15: success = pass without delay, failure = 1 IC day travel delay as the character is questioned and their papers inspected), witnesses a burned village or battlefield debris (procedural text based on the armies involved), or encounters deserters on the road (no combat — deserters flee rather than fight a samurai). Major event (active combat operations in the province — two armies within 2 sub-tiles of each other): hostile patrol confrontation. Generates a small ASCII map encounter — 3 to 6 ashigaru from the opposing faction, terrain from the province. If the character’s clan is one of the belligerents, the patrol from the enemy side attacks. If the character’s clan is neutral, both patrols may attempt to detain and question — Etiquette / Awareness TN 20 to talk out of it, failure means combat.

Condition 3 — Spirit Realm Overlap. The province has an active spirit realm overlap (Section 4.3.21). Base chance: 4% per sub-tile. Skill modification: Lore: Theology 3+ or the relevant Lore: [Realm] at rank 1+ converts the event to always minor — knowledge transforms danger into information. The character recognizes what is happening and can report it rather than stumbling into it unprepared. Minor event: the character witnesses a spiritual phenomenon appropriate to the overlapping realm. Gaki-do: whispers of the hungry dead, cold spots on the road, glimpses of translucent figures at dusk. Chikushudo: animals behaving strangely — wolves following without attacking, birds circling in unnatural patterns, insects swarming without cause. Toshigoku: a sudden urge toward anger or violence (Willpower TN 15 to resist — failure imposes −5 on the next social action as the character is shaken by the experience). Sakkaku: minor illusions — the road seems to fork where no fork exists, sounds from empty buildings, a companion’s face momentarily appears as someone else’s. Yume-do: drowsiness, vivid waking dreams, time distortion (the character arrives 1 IC day late without explanation). Meido: a sense of profound stillness, silence where there should be sound, and a brief vision of a recently deceased character the traveler knew (if any exist in their met_characters list). All minor spiritual events grant the character a topic about the overlap if they did not already have one — information as reward. Major event: does not generate an ASCII map. Spirit realm overlaps do not produce creatures that ambush travelers on the road — they warp the environment. The distortion is the event. Spiritual encounter ASCII maps generate through the worship failure quest seed pipeline (Section 4.3.21), not through travel.

Condition 4 — Elevated Province Taint Level. The province has PTL ≥ 3 (Section 42). Base chance: 1% × PTL per sub-tile. A province at PTL 5 has a 5% chance per sub-tile crossed. Skill reduction: Lore: Shadowlands 3+ halves the percentage. Minor event: the character notices signs of Taint — discolored vegetation, unnatural silence, sickly animals, a faint wrongness in the air. Grants a topic about the province’s Taint level if the character did not already have one. No Taint exposure from a minor event — awareness protects. Major event (PTL 5+): Tainted creature encounter. Generates a small ASCII map — 1 to 2 Tainted creatures appropriate to the PTL level (Section 40 creature roster, scaled by PTL). Goblin scouts at PTL 5, an Oni spawn at PTL 7+. Jade is effective against these creatures (Section 42, Jigoku only). The encounter also exposes the character to passive Taint — Earth TN 15 after the encounter, failure = +0.1 Taint points. Characters with jade on their person (jade finger, jade weapon) are immune to the passive exposure.

Condition 5 — Diplomatic Hostility. The traveler is crossing a clan’s territory while their own clan has War Status or an active hostile diplomatic state with that clan (Section 11.7). Base chance: 5% per sub-tile. Skill reduction: Etiquette 3+ or possession of travel papers from the host clan reduces the chance to 1% per sub-tile. Minor event: the character is challenged by a local patrol. Etiquette / Awareness TN 15: success = the character presents themselves correctly and is allowed to pass with a warning. Failure = 1 IC day delay as the character is detained and questioned. Critical failure (miss by 10+) = the character is escorted to the nearest magistrate and must explain their presence — further delay of 2 IC days and a disposition penalty (−5) with the province’s local lord. Major event (active War Status between the clans, not merely hostile diplomacy): the patrol attempts to arrest the character as a potential spy. Generates a small ASCII map — 4 to 6 bushi (drawn from the host clan’s army roster, Section 11.6) plus 2 to 3 doshin. The character can surrender (detained, escorted to magistrate, 3+ IC day delay, formal investigation), fight (combat with full consequences — killing soldiers of another clan is an act of war), or flee (Stealth / Agility contested vs the patrol’s Investigation / Perception, success = escape and resume travel, failure = combat).

#### 57.50.3 Major Event Resolution

When a major event generates an ASCII map encounter, the BEGIN_TRAVEL action is interrupted. The character resolves the encounter — fight, flee, or surrender depending on the situation — and then resumes travel from the sub-tile where the encounter occurred. The remaining sub-tiles in the current BEGIN_TRAVEL action are still crossed after the encounter resolves — the encounter does not consume additional AP. Wounds sustained in the encounter persist normally and carry into the destination. A character who reaches the Dead wound rank during a travel encounter dies (Section 57.49). Companions traveling with the character participate in the encounter per their AI behavior rules (Section 57.46). Companion deaths during travel encounters follow the standard companion death rules. Major events are rare by design. The per-sub-tile percentages mean a typical province (insurgency Strength 1–2, PTL 1–2, no war) produces minor events most of the time — crossing 4–5 sub-tiles at 2–4% per sub-tile gives roughly a 10–20% chance of any event per province. Major events require severe conditions — the simulation is telling the player that this province is genuinely dangerous.

#### 57.50.4 Minor Event Resolution

Minor events resolve as a single skill check with text output. No ASCII map. No AP cost. The event fires during travel and resolves immediately. The character sees the result as a travel log entry: what happened, what they noticed, what they learned. Success on the skill check produces useful information — a topic about the province condition, intelligence about insurgency strength, awareness of a spirit realm overlap. Failure produces either nothing (the character misses the sign) or a minor consequence (1 IC day delay for a failed patrol check, −5 on a social action for Toshigoku aggression). Minor events never inflict Wounds, never inflict Taint (except as explicitly noted), and never generate combat. They are the world telling the player what is happening in the provinces they cross.

#### 57.50.5 Positive Encounters — Information as Reward

Travel events never produce treasure, random loot, or material rewards. The reward for a successful travel event is information. A successful minor event skill check can grant: a Tier 4 topic about the province’s condition (insurgency strength, Taint level, spirit realm overlap, war status), which the character can share through the normal topic exchange system (Section 12.6, 12.6a, 12.7). Intelligence about a specific condition that the character did not previously know about — equivalent to learning through direct observation rather than through second-hand reports. Awareness of a threat that may affect their lord’s holdings or their mission — a courtier crossing Dragon territory who learns about a Gaki-do overlap can report it to their lord by letter. This makes travel an intelligence-gathering opportunity for characters with the right skills. A Shinjo Scout with Hunting 4 and Investigation 3 who crosses five provinces learns something about each one. A Bayushi Courtier with Etiquette 4 passes through checkpoints smoothly and picks up rumors at every tea house. A Kuni Shugenja with Lore: Shadowlands 5 reads the Taint level of every province like a book. Characters without relevant skills experience travel as a timer — they arrive safely (unless severe conditions generate a major event) but learn nothing along the way.

### 57.51 COMPETE_FOR_POSITION — General Decomposition Pattern — PROVISIONAL

A general-purpose decomposition pattern invoked whenever an NPC identifies a contested appointment as a path to satisfying a standing objective. Not a NeedType itself — it is a decomposition branch available to ambition-driven standing objectives (Section 18.4: Advance my family’s standing within the clan hierarchy, Elevate my family name, Advance my personal Glory and reputation). The pattern triggers from the standing objective layer (priority 4 in the NPC Decision Engine), not from lord-assigned primary objectives. A lord never assigns RAISE_STATUS — ambition is personal.

#### 57.51.1 Trigger

Two conditions must be met simultaneously: (1) the NPC has an active ambition-type standing objective, AND (2) the NPC receives a topic about a position vacancy. The topic is the information gate — an NPC who never hears about the vacancy never competes. Topic reach determines the candidate pool naturally.

#### 57.51.2 Phase 1 — Viability Assessment

Five checks run in order — any failure terminates the branch: (1a) Hard gate check — clan or school restrictions. (1b) Status appropriateness — position Status must be greater than current Status and must not exceed it by more than 2.0 ranks (PROVISIONAL). (1c) School type and personality filter — administrative positions weight courtier schools, military positions weight bushi schools. (1d) Distance and attachment penalty: viability_penalty = (distance_in_provinces × 2) + (has_active_assignment × 15) + (lord_disposition above Friend × 10). If penalty exceeds position prestige_value × ambition trait weight, terminate. (1e) Minimum interest threshold of 20 after all penalties (PROVISIONAL).

#### 57.51.3 Phase 2 — Decision-Maker Identification

The NPC determines who controls the appointment based on position type: Governorships → Sentaku Tribunal (5 character_ids). Military promotions → lord_id in operational chain. Court appointments → relevant daimyo. Succession → confirmation authority per Section 22.5. Marriage → prospective spouse’s lord. The decision-maker character_ids become the primary social targets for all subsequent phases.

#### 57.51.4 Phase 3 — Self-Assessment and Improvement

The NPC evaluates their own candidacy using the same weights decision-makers will use (v559 candidate evaluation weights). Produces a prioritized action list: (3a) Unknown to decision-makers → TRAVEL to location, seek introduction via ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION, attend court, engineer Daily Conversation. (3b) Low disposition with decision-makers → CHARM, DELIVER_GIFT, OFFER_FAVOR, PERFORM_FOR targeted at decision-makers. (3c) Skill gaps → TRAIN deficit skills (slowest path, only if nomination window is long enough). (3d) Already competitive → maintain position, shift to Phase 4.

#### 57.51.5 Phase 4 — Intelligence Gathering (Competition Discovery)

The NPC attempts to identify competitors. Not automatic — requires active information gathering. Five channels: (4a) READ_CHARACTER on decision-makers — reveals strongest dispositions, flags probable competitors. (4b) PROBE decision-makers — may reveal specific intentions. (4c) Topic monitoring — passive, no AP cost. Social actions targeting decision-makers generate topics that reveal other candidates. (4d) EAVESDROP — covert intelligence without target awareness. (4e) Social network inference — circumstantial evidence (Character X arrived at capital, appropriate Status, attending court). Once competitors are identified, the NPC ranks them by estimated threat.

#### 57.51.6 Phase 5 — Strategy Selection

Three strategy tiers determined by personality, not mutually exclusive:

Branch A — Merit (always active): Every competing NPC runs Phase 3 self-improvement regardless of personality. Training skills, cultivating decision-makers through legitimate social actions. Characters with high Gi and high Meiyo use only this branch.

Branch B — Political (gate: Gi is not primary Bushido virtue AND pragmatism weight exceeds 40): Strategic social maneuvering without dishonor. GOSSIP (true but unflattering information), strategic gift timing, NEGOTIATE with other candidates for mutual support or withdrawal. No Honor cost.

Branch C — Adversarial (gate: Gi weight below 30 AND ambition weight above 60 AND Honor below 5.0): Active damage to competitors. Subdivides by personality: C1 Covert (Yu below 40 or Seigyo above 50) — FABRICATE_SECRET, REVEAL_SECRET_PRIVATE, proxy deployment (PERSUADE third party to spread information, keeping hands clean), indirect harm (CONDUCT_RAID against competitor’s clan territory, COMMISSION_SABOTAGE). The full fabrication chain: FABRICATE_SECRET creates fake secret → deploy via REVEAL_SECRET_PRIVATE to one decision-maker, or EXPOSE_SECRET_PUBLIC for nuclear option, or hand to a known gossip via REVEAL_SECRET_PRIVATE for proxy spread. C2 Direct (Yu above 60) — PUBLIC_INSULT, SEEK_PRETEXT for duel, PUBLIC_DEBATE, EXPOSE_SECRET_PUBLIC. C3 Extreme (Gi below 15 AND Honor below 3.0 AND desperation threshold exceeded) — COMMISSION_ASSASSINATION, engineer lethal duel.

#### 57.51.7 Phase 6 — Proxy Operations

NPCs who cannot be physically present delegate through: (6a) Feudal chain delegation — WRITE_LETTER to lord or ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to a vassal at court. Disposition gains from proxy gifts accrue to the principal. (6b) Alliance coordination — allies at court put in a good word through Daily Conversation. (6c) Adversarial proxy — COMMISSION_SABOTAGE via hired agents, plant damaging information through intermediaries.

#### 57.51.8 Phase 7 — Termination

Five conditions: (7a) Position filled, NPC selected — success, standing objective partially satisfied. (7b) Position filled, NPC not selected — evaluate gap, maintain decision-maker cultivation for future vacancies or redirect. (7c) Higher-priority need fires — competition actions deprioritized, may re-enter if need resolves before window closes. (7d) Viability lost — circumstances changed, Phase 1 re-check fails. (7e) Nomination window closes without nomination.

#### 57.51.9 Scope of Applicability

Referenced by: Sentaku Tribunal governorships (19 positions). Sentaku Tribunal membership (5 positions — adversarial sub-tree applies to influencing Emperor’s choice). Military promotions (Chui through Rikugunshokan chain). Magistrate appointments. Court postings (ambassador, envoy, delegate). Clan administrative positions (non-hereditary Provincial Daimyo). Marriage competition (two suitors, decision-maker is prospective spouse’s lord). Extensible to any future contested appointment.

The ambition-type standing objectives (Advance my family’s standing, Elevate my family name, Advance my personal Glory and reputation) do not yet have formal decomposition trees mapping them to Phase 5 ActionID scoring in the NPC Decision Engine. COMPETE_FOR_POSITION is the first decomposition branch for these objectives. The standing-objective-to-NeedType bridge for all 28 standing objectives in Section 18.4 remains a design gap. ### 57.52 Standing Objective AP System — PROVISIONAL

This section defines when and how NPCs spend AP on their standing objective (Section 18) alongside lord-assigned duty. It replaces the hard priority stack where lord’s orders completely suppress standing objectives. Under this system, both duty and personal objectives contribute to Phase 5 scoring simultaneously, gated by four factors: urgency, personality, compatibility, and neglect.

#### 57.52.1 Factor 1 — Urgency Derivation

Each AP cycle, the engine derives an urgency level from world state. Derivation order — first match wins: CRITICAL: character is on an active ASCII map OR character’s province has War Status AND enemy within 2 sub-tiles. Fires for all characters regardless of lord_id. NONE: lord_id is null (Clan Champion, ronin, independent Minor Clan lord). Standing objective at full strength. HIGH: War Status in character’s province or adjacent province, OR primary objective references Tier 3 topic, OR primary objective deadline within 3 IC days. STANDARD: default — any lord-assigned primary objective not meeting CRITICAL or HIGH. LOW: no active primary objective, or objective completed with no new assignment, or posted to province with no topics above Tier 4.

#### 57.52.2 Factor 2 — Personality Gate (Bushido Virtues)

Chugi (Duty/Loyalty): Personal AP at Low/None only. Neglect override: never. Exception: compatible ActionIDs (Factor 3) enter at all urgency levels.

Yu (Courage): Personal AP at Standard and below. At Standard, martial/combat standing objectives only. Non-combat at Low/None only. Neglect override: 28 IC days.

Rei (Courtesy): Personal AP at Standard and below. At Standard, context-appropriate actions only per rei_context_filter (Section 57.52.6). Neglect override: 21 IC days.

Jin (Compassion): Personal AP at Standard and below. At Standard, altruistic standing objectives only (is_altruistic tag, Section 57.52.7). Self-serving at Low/None only. Neglect override: 14 IC days (altruistic objectives only).

Gi (Honesty/Righteousness): Personal AP at Standard and below. Special: justice-driven standing objectives (is_justice_driven tag, Section 57.52.7) activate even at High. Neglect override: never.

Meiyo (Honor): Personal AP at Standard and below. Neglect override: never. Special: if lord’s order requires dishonorable action, personal AP activates even at High (existing Section 19.3 rule). No further internal-code logic required — the simplified gate IS the design.

Makoto (Sincerity): Personal AP at Standard and below. Special: active multi-AP progress tracks (TRAIN, CRAFT, COMPOSE) continue to completion even if urgency rises to High — +50 scoring bonus on continuation ActionID. Neglect override: not needed.

#### 57.52.3 Factor 2 — Personality Gate (Shourido Virtues)

Seigyo (Control): Personal AP at Standard and below. At High, activates if neglect exceeds 14 IC days. Preferentially selects dual-scoring ActionIDs (Factor 3 compatibility).

Ketsui (Determination): Personal AP at Standard and below. Reserves 1 AP per IC day for active personal target at Standard. Neglect override: 7 IC days. At 14+ days, may activate at High.

Dōsatsu (Insight): Personal AP at Standard and below. At Standard, intelligence/observation actions only (READ_CHARACTER, PROBE, EAVESDROP, LISTEN_REFLECT). Neglect override: 21 IC days.

Chishiki (Knowledge): Personal AP at Standard and below. At Standard, learning actions only (TRAIN Lore skills, MEDITATE). Neglect override: 14 IC days.

Kanpeki (Perfection): Personal AP at Standard and below. At Standard, TRAIN (school skills), CRAFT (artistic), MEDITATE only. Special: if relevant duty skill is below Insight Rank, TRAIN activates even at High. Neglect override: 14 IC days.

Kyōryōku (Strength): Personal AP at Standard and below. At Standard, power-acquisition actions only (combat training, SEEK_PRETEXT, leverage). Neglect override: 21 IC days.

Ishi (Will): Personal AP at Low/None only. Special: if character was actively pursuing standing objective before current lord assignment, treats standing objective at Standard activation. Neglect override: never.

#### 57.52.4 Conflict Resolution

Each character has both a Bushido gate and a Shourido gate active simultaneously. When they conflict, the more permissive rule wins. The Shourido virtue is the darker drive that undermines Bushido discipline. A Chugi/Ketsui character activates personal AP at Standard because Ketsui cracks through Chugi. An Ishi/Seigyo character activates at Standard because Seigyo overrides Ishi’s commitment. Implementation: evaluate both gates independently, take the OR of their results.

#### 57.52.5 Factor 3 — Compatibility

When an ActionID appears in both the duty alignment table and the standing objective alignment table, it receives both scores summed. No special logic required — dual scoring happens naturally when both objective sources contribute to Phase 5 simultaneously. Seigyo characters sort the pool by combined score before selection, preferentially choosing dual-purpose actions.

#### 57.52.6 Rei Context Filter

Config table keyed by context tag. Applies only to Rei characters at Standard urgency, personal actions only. AT_COURT or VISITING (court active): social, artistic, intelligence actions appropriate. TRAIN combat, CRAFT non-artistic, covert and military ActionIDs filtered out. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS (no court): no filter — private time. VISITING (no court): social and artistic actions, TRAIN with dojo access. TRAVELING: MEDITATE, TRAIN, COMPOSE. AT_WALL_TOWER or military posting: TRAIN combat, MEDITATE, CRAFT military, PERFORM_WORSHIP.

#### 57.52.7 Standing Objective Tags

is_altruistic (Jin gate): TRUE — Protect those under my care, Maintain peace, Strengthen Kaiu Wall, Eliminate Shadowlands threat, Maximize province prosperity, Ensure no resource shortage, Strengthen Imperial institution, Maintain balance of power. FALSE — all others.

is_justice_driven (Gi gate): TRUE — Seek vengeance for [historical wrong], Live by Bushido without compromise, Honor my ancestors through my deeds. FALSE — all others.

#### 57.52.8 Factor 4 — Neglect Accumulation

Character sheet field: standing_objective_neglect (integer, starts 0). Increments by 1 each IC day with zero AP on standing-objective-aligned actions. Resets to 0 on any day with 1+ aligned AP. When neglect exceeds a virtue’s threshold, the gate upgrades one tier. Thresholds: Ketsui 7, Jin (altruistic only) 14, Chishiki 14, Kanpeki 14, Seigyo 14 (extends into High), Rei 21, Dōsatsu 21, Kyōryōku 21, Yu 28. Chugi, Gi, Meiyo, Makoto, Ishi: never.

Character sheet field: active_personal_target (string or null). Set by COMPETE_FOR_POSITION (Section 57.51) or similar. Used by Ketsui reserve-1-AP rule.

#### 57.52.9 Implementation Summary

Layer A: derive_urgency function (existing fields, one enum). Layer B: two config lookups (bushido_gate, shourido_gate) + neglect check + OR. Layer C: virtue-specific ActionID filter on personal pool (same pattern as personality_filter.json). Special cases: Ketsui reserve (pool swap, 1 AP), Makoto sequence (+50 bonus), Meiyo honor override (Table 2.3 lookup), Gi justice override (tag check), Kanpeki skill gap (skill vs Insight Rank comparison). New fields: standing_objective_neglect, active_personal_target.

#### 57.52.10 Standing Objective Decomposition Functions

### 57.53 Go-hatamoto Replenishment System — PROVISIONAL

This section defines how destroyed Go-hatamoto Companies are replaced, how the cost burden is distributed across families, and the pipeline from vacancy detection to integration. The Go-hatamoto is the clan’s standing professional army. When a Company is destroyed in battle, the clan must replace it. The replacement cost falls on the families proportional to their share of the clan’s territory.

#### 57.53.1 Unit Creation by Type

Two unit types require distinct creation mechanisms. Ashigaru Companies: created via ORDER_LEVY (existing ActionID). Converts civilian PU to military PU. Requires settlement with Garrison/Barracks infrastructure. Costs PU (province loses farming capacity) + 1.00 Arms per PU equip cost. Starts at Training Level 0 (Raw). Samurai Companies: created via MUSTER_SAMURAI_COMPANY (new ActionID, Section 57.53.5). Does NOT consume civilian PU — samurai exist outside the civilian population as a separate caste. Requires settlement with Dojo infrastructure. Costs Arms only (2.50 Clan Samurai / 3.50 Specialized / 5.00 Elite). Starts at Training Level 2 (Trained). Each Dojo has a muster capacity limiting how many samurai Companies it can produce per year: standard Dojo = 1 Company per year, major clan academy (Kakita Academy, Akodo War College, Hida Bushi School, etc.) = 2 per year. New fields on Dojo infrastructure: muster_capacity (int, 1 or 2), companies_mustered_this_year (int, resets at start of Spring). MUSTER_SAMURAI_COMPANY checks: companies_mustered_this_year must be below muster_capacity or the action is unavailable at this Dojo until next year. A family with 3 Dojo can produce at most 3 samurai Companies per year. Replacing heavy losses takes multiple years. Peasant Levy is not part of the Go-hatamoto and remains under the Provincial Daimyo’s direct lord_id authority per existing rules.

#### 57.53.2 Army Composition Ratio

The Clan Champion sets the desired samurai-to-ashigaru ratio for the Go-hatamoto. This ratio is applied evenly across all Legions — the Rikugunshokan cannot concentrate samurai in one Legion and ashigaru in another. Every Legion mirrors the army-wide ratio. Stored on the Go-hatamoto army data structure: samurai_ratio (float 0.0 to 1.0, set by Clan Champion), ashigaru_ratio (1.0 minus samurai_ratio). Rounding within a Legion uses the same remainder rule as family contribution. Example: 70/30 ratio with 7 Companies per Legion = 4.9 samurai → 5, 2.1 ashigaru → 2. Total 7. Changing the ratio is a political act — generates a Tier 4 topic. May only be changed at the seasonal Strategic Review. PROVISIONAL.

#### 57.53.3 Family Contribution Tracker

Each family’s expected contribution to the Go-hatamoto is proportional to the number of provinces they control relative to the clan’s total province count. A family controlling 30% of the clan’s provinces owes 30% of the Go-hatamoto’s Companies. The tracker distinguishes samurai and ashigaru obligations separately because their costs are fundamentally different.

Data structure on the Go-hatamoto army: family_contribution_record, Dictionary keyed by family_id. Fields per entry: expected_samurai (int), expected_ashigaru (int), actual_samurai (int, count of active samurai Companies with source_family_id matching this family), actual_ashigaru (int, same for ashigaru), samurai_balance (actual minus expected), ashigaru_balance (actual minus expected), source_family_id (StringName).

New field on each Company: source_family_id (StringName). Set at formation to the family that owns the province where the Company is formed — derived from the province’s feudal ownership chain, not the individual forming lord’s personal family_id. If an Akodo governor in a Matsu province fires ORDER_LEVY, source_family_id = Matsu because the province belongs to the Matsu. Never changes for the life of the Company. New field on each Company: unit_origin (enum: SAMURAI or ASHIGARU). Set at formation. Used by the tracker to count each category.

Rounding rule: floor all expected values, then assign remaining Companies one at a time to families in order of largest fractional remainder. Ties broken by family with fewer total provinces. Deterministic, always sums correctly.

Recalculation fires at the seasonal Strategic Review (Section 55.26.1) only — not mid-season. When province ownership changes through conquest, Imperial edict, or administrative transfer, the new contribution numbers take effect at the next Strategic Review. The gaining family benefits from the extra province’s resources for up to one season before their obligation increases. Natural administrative lag.

#### 57.53.4 Replenishment Pipeline

Step 1 — Vacancy detection. The Rikugunshokan’s ContextSnapshot shows a Legion with fewer than its target Company count. A FILL_VACANCY NeedType fires targeting the empty Company slot. The Rikugunshokan reads the family_contribution_record and identifies which family has the largest negative balance in the relevant category (samurai or ashigaru).

Step 2 — Report to Clan Champion. The Rikugunshokan fires WRITE_LETTER (or direct report if co-located) to the Clan Champion: “Legion X has lost a [samurai/ashigaru] Company. Family Y owes the replacement per the contribution tracker.” This is a military assessment, not an order. The Rikugunshokan is in the operational chain and cannot issue orders to the Family Daimyo through the feudal chain.

Step 3 — Clan Champion orders Family Daimyo. The Champion fires ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to the Family Daimyo: “Raise a replacement [samurai/ashigaru] Company for the Go-hatamoto.” Information Transfer (Section 55.6) carries the unit tier needed. The obligation is mandatory — refusing is insubordination, grounds for removal from office.

Step 4 — Family Daimyo executes formation. For samurai: fires MUSTER_SAMURAI_COMPANY at their Kyuden or delegates to a Provincial Daimyo at a Dojo settlement via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. Arms cost drawn from the forming settlement’s stockpile. For ashigaru: fires ORDER_LEVY at a settlement with Garrison/Barracks. PU cost borne by the province. The Company is formed at Training Level 0 (Raw). No drilling occurs at the family level — the Family Daimyo delivers bodies and equipment only. The Company enters the Go-hatamoto at Raw via the same Steps 5–7 handoff. Once integrated, the Taisa (Legion commander) fires DRILL_TROOPS to bring the Company to standard. The existing TRAIN_TROOPS NeedType already prioritizes units below Training Level 2 — the Taisa’s engine naturally targets the new Raw Company. Until drilled, the Company fights at Raw penalties (−2 Attack, −2 Defense, −4 Morale) if the Legion sees combat.

Step 5 — Report completion. Family Daimyo fires WRITE_LETTER to Clan Champion: “Company formed at [settlement], ready for integration.” Topic carries the Company ID and location.

Step 6 — Champion forwards to Rikugunshokan. WRITE_LETTER or direct order: “Integrate this Company.”

Step 7 — Rikugunshokan integrates. Fires ORDER_DEPLOY targeting the new Company. Sets parent_legion_id to the Legion with the vacancy. The Company’s operational chain activates. Chui appointment uses the existing military promotion system (Section 11.3.18). The Company physically moves to the Legion’s current location via standard army movement.

Between formation and integration, the Company sits under the Family Daimyo’s authority. If the Family Daimyo is under attack during this window, they may use the Company defensively before it transfers. Once ORDER_DEPLOY fires, it leaves.

#### 57.53.5 New ActionID: MUSTER_SAMURAI_COMPANY

Category 7 (Military Actions, Lord-tier). 1 AP. Available at: AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. Requirements: settlement must have Dojo infrastructure, settlement Arms stockpile must cover equip cost for the target unit tier. Roll: Battle / Intelligence vs TN 15. Success: Company formed. Failure: 1 AP wasted, Arms not consumed, retry next AP. Cost: Arms equip cost drawn at execution (2.50 Clan Samurai Tier 1 / 3.50 Specialized Tier 2 / 5.00 Elite Tier 3). Effect: creates a new Company with the appropriate Clan Elite unit type, Training Level 2 (Trained), Health 153, source_family_id set to the forming family, unit_origin = SAMURAI, commander_id = null (Chui appointed separately), parent_legion_id = null (Rikugunshokan integrates separately). Unit tier determined by Dojo type: standard Dojo produces Tier 1 (Clan Samurai). Clan-specific advanced Dojo (Kakita Academy, Hida Bushi School, etc.) produce their clan’s Tier 2 and Tier 3 units. Skill mapping: primary Battle, secondary Intelligence. Personality leans: Chugi +10, Yu +5, Ketsui +5. No blocks. Annex A alignment: FILL_VACANCY 90, DEFEND_PROVINCE 70, TRAIN_TROOPS 60. Annex D skill map: Battle / Intelligence.

#### 57.53.6 Failure to Meet Obligation

The obligation is mandatory. A family that cannot immediately deliver (insufficient Arms, no Dojo, PU depleted) is not exempted — they owe. Consequences escalate: Season 1 deficit: family scrambles. Forge Iron to Arms, purchase Arms with Koku, reallocate resources. Contribution tracker shows negative balance. Tier 4 topic fires: “[Family] has not met their military obligation.” Season 2 deficit: topic escalates to Tier 3. Family Daimyo’s disposition with Clan Champion drops. Other families who ARE meeting their obligation gain political leverage. Season 3+ deficit: Clan Champion may intervene. Options: send Arms from clan reserve (family owes debt), temporarily reduce expected contribution (political favor costing Champion credibility), or in extreme cases strip provinces from the underperforming family and reassign them — permanently changing the contribution ratio.

### 57.54 Clan Champion Strategic Evaluation System — PROVISIONAL

Every Clan Champion evaluates world state through their available information, filters it through personality and standing objective, and produces 2–4 strategic conclusions that set clan-wide policy for the season. These conclusions are broadcast to all Family Daimyo, who independently translate them into territory-specific actions using their own capabilities and local conditions. The Emperor is excluded from this system — Imperial strategic direction operates through existing mechanics (Imperial Edicts per Section 16.4, Emperor Personality Archetypes per Section 55.10.1, direct orders via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE). Minor Clan Champions run the same system at their smaller scale.

#### 57.54.1 Firing Triggers

Five triggers fire the strategic evaluation. Trigger 1 — Seasonal Strategic Review: fires once per season, after the seasonal tick resolves resource production, starvation, worship checks, and topic momentum updates, but before the Champion’s first AP decision. This is the primary evaluation point. Trigger 2 — Mid-season Tier 1/2 crisis eruption: a Tier 1 or Tier 2 topic enters the Champion’s known_topics mid-season. Forces partial reevaluation — the new crisis is evaluated against existing priorities and inserted if it outranks one. Trigger 3 — Priority achieved: the strategic objective was accomplished. The entry is removed from clan_strategic_priorities. Ketsui Champions immediately run Steps 2–4 to fill the empty slot. All other personalities leave the slot empty until the next Seasonal Strategic Review. Trigger 4 — Priority impossible: the target died, the crisis resolved without the clan, the ally betrayed. Same behavior as Trigger 3. Trigger 5 — Imperial Edict: new binding Edict from Winter Court or emergency decree. Forces COMPLY_EDICT into the candidate pool.

#### 57.54.2 Information Inputs

The Champion does NOT read raw world data. They know only what has reached them through legitimate channels. known_topics: the full set of topics received through court, letters, conversation, and intelligence actions. This is their view of the world. A Champion who neglects court or doesn’t read letters has a narrower view. Vassal reports: information via WRITE_LETTER from Family Daimyo, Provincial Daimyo, magistrates, military commanders. Resource stockpile numbers, military readiness assessments, provincial stability reports. Accuracy depends on whether vassals are honest — a skimming daimyo sends false numbers (Section 4.3.7 audit system). Direct observation: what the Champion sees in their own province and at court. Disposition readings from personal interactions. Characters in met_characters. Court atmosphere. Divination results: only if a shugenja vassal performs Divination (embedded in PERFORM_WORSHIP per v574) AND communicates the result via WRITE_LETTER or in-person report. If no shugenja reports, the Champion is spiritually blind. Active Imperial Edicts: binding policy from Winter Court (Section 16.4). War Status: active wars, War Score, alliance obligations (Section 53). Standing objective: the Champion’s own directional bias.

#### 57.54.3 The Six-Step Process

Step 1 — Threat Scan. Review known_topics sorted by tier. Tier 1 and Tier 2 topics are existential — non-optional. Any active Tier 1/2 crisis touching the clan’s territory or interests automatically becomes a forced strategic conclusion (is_forced = true, score 150 for Tier 1, score 140 for Tier 2). No personality gate — all Champions respond to existential threats. Active Imperial Edicts boost matching topics. Forced conclusions bypass slot limits (see Section 57.54.6).

Step 2 — Opportunity Scan. Review remaining known_topics (Tier 3, Tier 4) and world conditions for opportunities. Check each of the 14 strategic conclusion trigger conditions (Section 57.54.5) against available information. Generate a candidate pool of all conclusions whose triggers are satisfied.

Step 3 — Scoring. Score each candidate using the Gap 2 formula (Section 57.54.6). Standing Objective Match (0 or +30) + Topic Urgency (Tier +10/+25, Momentum −10/0/+10) + Convergent Topics (+5 per additional triggering topic beyond the first) + Personality Preference (−15/0/+15/+25 across four tiers: core affinity +25, natural weight +15, neutral 0, deprioritize −15; hard-block removes from pool) + Continuation Bonus (flat +10 if this conclusion was active last season and triggers still valid; Makoto +20, Ketsui +15; Ishi locks — active conclusions from last season cannot be displaced by scoring unless triggers become invalid, exception: forced crisis conclusions override Ishi lock).

Step 4 — Selection. Fill remaining slots after forced conclusions. Discretionary slots = max(0, (personality_base + conditional_expansion) − forced_count). Personality base: Ketsui 2, Yu/Kyoryoku 2, Jin/Gi/Chugi/Makoto/Meiyo/Rei 3, Seigyo/Dosatsu/Chishiki 3. Conditional expansion: +1 additional slot if any remaining candidate scores above 50. Select the top N scoring candidates to fill available discretionary slots. Ties broken by: first, continuation (active longer wins), second, random.

Step 5 — Communication. Write selected conclusions to clan_strategic_priorities array on clan data object. Family Daimyo present at seasonal court read immediately. Absent Family Daimyo receive via WRITE_LETTER (1 letter per absent daimyo containing all active conclusions, standard delivery timing per Section 12.7). Strategic conclusions persist on clan data until the next Seasonal Strategic Review replaces them.

Step 6 — Reactive mid-season handling. When a Tier 1/2 crisis forces in mid-season, the new conclusion displaces the lowest-scored non-forced entry in clan_strategic_priorities. WRITE_LETTER fires to all absent Family Daimyo. When a priority is achieved or becomes impossible, the entry is removed from the array immediately. WRITE_LETTER notifies absent Family Daimyo. Until the letter arrives, distant Family Daimyo continue pursuing the resolved objective — wasted effort, but that is the cost of distance.

#### 57.54.4 Strategic Conclusion Types and Data Schema

16 strategic conclusion types across 6 domains. MILITARY (5): AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE, LAUNCH_OFFENSIVE, SUPPRESS_INSTABILITY, SUPPORT_SHADOWLANDS, DEFEND_TERRITORY. DIPLOMATIC (5): PURSUE_ALLIANCE, SEEK_PEACE, UNDERMINE_POSITION, STRENGTHEN_COURT, COMPLY_EDICT. ECONOMIC (3): SECURE_RESOURCE, DEVELOP_INFRASTRUCTURE, RECOVER_DAMAGE. SPIRITUAL (2): RESTORE_WORSHIP, RESPOND_SPIRITUAL_CRISIS. SOCIAL (1): BUILD_CULTURAL_PRESTIGE.

LAUNCH_OFFENSIVE carries a war_objective parameter (enum: CONQUER, HUMILIATE, DESTROY, PUNISH). CONQUER: take and hold territory, casus_belli field determines legitimacy. HUMILIATE: inflict maximum War Score damage, extract punishing peace terms. DESTROY: eliminate a specific target (fortress, army, fleet), limited scope — on objective completion, auto-transitions to SEEK_PEACE with score 150 and peace willingness MAXIMUM; if enemy refuses peace, auto-transitions to defensive posture. PUNISH: proportional retaliation for a specific grievance, self-limiting scope.

StrategicConclusion data schema: conclusion_id (int, incrementing counter per clan per season), conclusion_type (16-entry enum above), domain (enum: MILITARY, DIPLOMATIC, ECONOMIC, SPIRITUAL, SOCIAL), target_clan_id (int or null — required for AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE, LAUNCH_OFFENSIVE, DEFEND_TERRITORY, PURSUE_ALLIANCE, SEEK_PEACE, UNDERMINE_POSITION; null for all others), war_objective (enum or null — CONQUER/HUMILIATE/DESTROY/PUNISH, only for LAUNCH_OFFENSIVE), war_objective_target (int or null — province_id for CONQUER, target_id for DESTROY, topic_id for PUNISH; null for HUMILIATE and all non-offensive conclusions), edict_id (int or null — only for COMPLY_EDICT), score (int — formula output for discretionary, 150/140 for forced Tier 1/2, 150 for auto-transition), source_topic_ids (array of int — audit trail), is_forced (bool — true if Tier 1/2 crisis; Family Daimyo weight forced above all non-forced regardless of numeric score), is_continuation (bool — true if active last season with valid triggers), season_originated (int — IC season when first selected).

Storage: clan_strategic_priorities array on clan data object, no hard length cap (forced conclusions bypass slot limits). strategic_evaluation_log on Champion character sheet (full scored candidate list for debugging, not read by any game system). Empty array is valid state — Family Daimyo default to standing objective and local conditions when no Champion direction exists.

#### 57.54.5 Trigger-to-Conclusion Mappings

#1 AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE toward [Clan X]. Domain: Military. Triggers (any): known_topics contains hostile ActionID result from [Clan X] (CONDUCT_RAID, RAID_HARVEST, BLOCKADE_TRADE_ROUTE, IMPOSE_EMBARGO); border dispute or territorial grievance topic involving [Clan X]; EVALUATE_CLAN_STRENGTH intelligence shows [Clan X] military buildup adjacent to own territory; War Status with [Clan X] at Tension (Section 53); standing objective “Expand territorial influence” and [Clan X] identified as weakest adjacent rival; standing objective “Keep military dominant” and intelligence shows [Clan X] closing military gap. Personality: Yu/Kyoryoku trigger on weaker signals (single incident). Jin/Makoto require sustained pattern. Seigyo triggers but delays (gathers intelligence first). Ketsui triggers and locks (does not downgrade without resolution). Precondition for escalation to #2: must be active for at least 1 IC season OR Tier 1 crisis (direct invasion) skips precondition.

#2 LAUNCH_OFFENSIVE against [Clan X], objective [war_objective]. Domain: Military. Triggers (all required): #1 AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE toward [Clan X] currently active (exception: Tier 1 crisis skips precondition); Feasibility Ledger (Section 55) returns All Green or acceptable Yellow; casus belli exists in known_topics (border incident, grievance, broken agreement, historical claim, Imperial Edict, formal insult). War objective selection by personality: Yu/Kyoryoku weight CONQUER and HUMILIATE. Ketsui weights CONQUER (permanent gains). Seigyo/Dosatsu weight PUNISH and DESTROY (controlled, calculable). Jin almost never fires #2 except defending against direct attack; when forced, weights PUNISH. Gi weights PUNISH when casus belli is genuine injustice; blocks HUMILIATE (disproportionate). Makoto weights PUNISH for broken agreements; blocks CONQUER without legitimate historical claim. LAUNCH_OFFENSIVE replaces matching-target AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE entry — escalation consumes the precondition slot. DESTROY completion: auto-transition to SEEK_PEACE at score 150. If enemy refuses peace, set defensive posture. Withdraw offensive operations. Existing War Status engine handles defense.

#3 SUPPRESS_INSTABILITY. Domain: Military/Internal. Triggers (any): known_topics contains insurgency topic (any Section 11.11 type) in own territory; vassal report shows Stability below 50 in any province; known_topics contains crime surge or repeated magistrate failure in own territory; multiple provinces report peasant flight. Priority scales: single Tier 4 = STANDARD, multiple insurgencies or Tier 3 (spreading) = HIGH, Tier 2 (clan-wide) = forced top priority. Personality: Jin prioritizes early (unrest means suffering). Seigyo prioritizes (instability threatens control). Yu deprioritizes unless military-grade insurgency. Kyoryoku treats insurgency as military problem.

#4 SUPPORT_SHADOWLANDS. Domain: Military/Spiritual. Triggers (any): known_topics contains Shadowlands Incursion, Oni Manifestation, or Taint Insurgency at Tier 1/2; active Imperial Edict directs Wall support; WRITE_LETTER from Crab Champion requesting aid; standing objective “Eliminate Shadowlands threat” (always available at STANDARD urgency). Personality: Chugi treats Edict trigger as top priority. Jin responds to humanitarian aspects. Yu responds to military challenge. Seigyo evaluates cost/benefit. Special: Crab Champions never fire #4 — Shadowlands in Crab territory is internal (#3). Non-Crab Champions fire #4 when committing to someone else’s problem.

#5 PURSUE_ALLIANCE with [Clan X]. Domain: Diplomatic. Triggers (any): standing objective “Maintain balance of power” and a third clan growing dominant; standing objective “Expand territorial influence” and [Clan X] shares an enemy; known_topics contains Tier 2+ threat that own clan cannot address alone; major war just ended and clan is weakened; incoming alliance proposal from [Clan X] that aligns with Champion’s interests. Personality: Jin/Rei weight higher (cooperation natural). Seigyo weights when partner can be controlled. Yu/Kyoryoku weight lower (reliance feels like weakness). Precondition: must have at least one [Clan X] member in met_characters. If no contacts exist, must first generate #8 (STRENGTHEN_COURT) or use ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION.

#6 SEEK_PEACE with [Clan X]. Domain: Diplomatic. Triggers (any): War Status Active and War Score below 40 (Even or Behind per Section 53); War Status Active and vassal reports show Rice/Iron critical shortfall; DESTROY war objective achieved (auto-transition); standing objective “Maintain peace” and War Status at Tension+; Imperial Edict directing cease hostilities; allied clan withdraws from war. Personality threshold modifiers: Jin/Makoto fire at higher War Score thresholds (seek peace earlier, while still Ahead). Rei fires at moderate thresholds. Gi fires when casus belli satisfied regardless of War Score. Yu fires only at very low thresholds (near-Desperate). Kyoryoku fires at the lowest thresholds. Ketsui fires at moderately low thresholds but demands harsh terms. PROVISIONAL: exact War Score threshold shift values per personality pending balancing. Relative ordering locked (Jin earliest, Kyoryoku latest).

#7 UNDERMINE_POSITION of [Clan X]. Domain: Diplomatic. Triggers (any): standing objective “Undermine [Clan X]” (always available); standing objective “Expand territorial influence” and direct military action infeasible (Feasibility Ledger returned Any Red); known_topics show [Clan X] gaining court dominance; known_topics show [Clan X] winning alliance the Champion opposes; standing objective “Maintain balance of power” and [Clan X] is dominant clan. Personality gate: Seigyo/Dosatsu/Kyoryoku weight naturally. Yu hard-blocks (fight openly or not at all). Gi blocks unless target has committed genuine injustice. Makoto blocks (dishonest warfare abhorrent). Jin blocks unless target actively causing harm to others. Meiyo generates #7 but Phase 5 personality filters restrict ActionIDs to honorable methods — PUBLIC_DEBATE, MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION, PERSUADE, superior alliances. No GOSSIP, EAVESDROP, EXPOSE_SECRET.

#8 STRENGTHEN_COURT. Domain: Diplomatic. Triggers (any): Winter Court within 1 IC season (preparation); known_topics show own clan losing court positions on multiple active topics; observable data shows own clan has fewer courtiers at major courts than rival clans; standing objective “Advance family standing” or “Strengthen Imperial institution”; clan just lost a war or experienced major scandal (rehabilitation); critical appointment upcoming. Personality: Rei weights highest (court IS their battlefield). Seigyo weights for control. Yu weights lowest (court is distraction). Even Yu fires before Winter Court.

#9 COMPLY_EDICT. Domain: Diplomatic/Imperial. Parameter: edict_id. Triggers: active Imperial Edict whose requirements apply to the Champion’s clan. Forced into candidate pool whenever applicable. Competes for priority slots. Personality: Chugi ranks first (duty to Emperor overrides clan interest). Gi/Makoto rank high. Seigyo evaluates cost. Yu complies only when non-compliance would be publicly visible. Kyoryoku complies when Edict aligns with strength projection. Non-compliance consequences handled by Topic and Momentum System (Section 16.4).

#10 SECURE_RESOURCE. Domain: Economic. Triggers (any): vassal reports show Rice projected below 1 season consumption; Iron reserves insufficient for military deployment; Koku reserves insufficient for stipends; any province in Starvation (Section 4.3.6); multiple provinces report simultaneous shortfalls. Priority: single province low Rice = STANDARD. Active Starvation or multi-province = HIGH. Multi-province starvation = forced top priority. Personality: Jin fires at lower thresholds (prevent suffering). Seigyo fires early (resource control is power). Yu deprioritizes until crisis-grade. Information channel: Champion knows own-province data directly but relies on vassal reports for other provinces. A dishonest Provincial Daimyo sending false numbers blindsides the Champion until crisis escalates to court topic.

#11 DEVELOP_INFRASTRUCTURE. Domain: Economic. Triggers (all): no active crisis occupying all priority slots; standing objective “Maximize prosperity,” “Control trade networks,” or “Ensure no resource shortage”; vassal reports show provinces with economic potential below capacity (undeveloped markets, unused ports, trade routes without commerce, mine capacity without Forge). Personality: Seigyo/Chishiki weight highest. Jin weights (improving lives). Ketsui weights (building toward a goal). Yu/Kyoryoku weight very low (infrastructure is boring — their clans suffer economically). War Status does not block this conclusion — a Champion at war on one border can still develop interior provinces. The priority scoring naturally suppresses it when military urgency is high.

#12 RECOVER_DAMAGE. Domain: Economic. Triggers (all): a war, famine, or major crisis resolved within previous 2 IC seasons; multiple provinces report Stability below 60 and resource shortfalls; the specific war or crisis that caused the damage has resolved. Distinct from #10: #10 addresses acute shortage during crisis, #12 addresses structural damage after crisis. Personality: all generate when conditions met. Differentiation in execution: Jin prioritizes population welfare, Seigyo prioritizes revenue recovery, Yu prioritizes military recovery, Ketsui prioritizes restoring whatever was lost specifically.

#13 RESTORE_WORSHIP. Domain: Spiritual. Triggers (any): known_topics contains worship failure at Family Tier 3 or higher; shugenja vassal’s WRITE_LETTER reports Divination showing Fortune at Restless or worse; known_topics contains Fortune malus effects (Bishamon Restless = Attack −1, Ebisu Displeased = Rice −20%, Hotei Wrathful = Stability −20/season); standing objective “Preserve spiritual health” (always available at STANDARD). CRITICAL INFORMATION CHANNEL NOTE: This is the narrowest channel. The Champion does NOT receive worship data automatically. Divination results reach the Champion only if a shugenja vassal performs Divination AND communicates results. Without shugenja communication, worship failure reaches the Champion only at Family Tier 3 (Displeased, malus already active). Champions who maintain shugenja communication catch problems at Province Tier 4 (Restless). This is a meaningful gameplay consequence. Personality: Gi/Chugi fire at earliest signal. Jin fires when malus affects people. Seigyo fires when malus affects systems. Yu deprioritizes unless Bishamon is failing.

#14 RESPOND_SPIRITUAL_CRISIS. Domain: Spiritual. Triggers (any): known_topics contains spirit realm overlap in own territory (Toshigoku foothold, Gaki-do bleed, Chikushudo disturbance, Sakkaku trickster, Maigo no Musha haunting); elemental imbalance topic; Tier 2/3 spiritual crisis not related to Jigoku (Jigoku handled by #4). Reactive only — never from standing objective. Priority: single province Toshigoku foothold = STANDARD. Tier 3 incursion or multi-province overlap = HIGH. Any Tier 2 spiritual crisis = forced top priority. Personality minimal: all respond because spiritual crises affect military, economic, and political systems. Differentiation in response type: Gi commits shugenja immediately, Seigyo gathers intelligence first, Yu treats Toshigoku as military problem, Jin prioritizes affected population.

#15 DEFEND_TERRITORY. Domain: Military. Triggers (any): same external threat indicators as AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE (border tension, rival buildup, War Status) AND Feasibility Ledger (Section 55) returns Red on offensive action; known_topics contains T4-39 ARMY_MOVEMENT by foreign clan adjacent to own territory AND offensive Feasibility is Red; known_topics contains T3-01 BORDER_RAID and Champion judges offensive infeasible; personality deprioritizes offense (Jin, Makoto) regardless of Feasibility result; standing objective “Keep military dominant” and intelligence shows rival military advantage (defensive response to gap). Distinct from AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE: AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE leads toward offense, DEFEND_TERRITORY leads toward fortification and garrison hardening. A Yu Champion with Green Feasibility selects AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE. The same Champion with Red Feasibility selects DEFEND_TERRITORY. A Jin Champion may select DEFEND_TERRITORY even with Green Feasibility — personality overrides strategic opportunity. target_clan_id: nullable — specific rival if identified, null for general border hardening. Personality: Jin/Makoto core affinity. Yu/Kyōryoku neutral (trigger only when offense genuinely infeasible). Seigyo natural weight (defensive control). Ketsui locks defensive posture once committed.

#16 BUILD_CULTURAL_PRESTIGE. Domain: Social. Triggers (any): known_topics show rival clan artisan output generating court momentum (T2-14 LEGENDARY_ARTISAN_COMPLETION, T2-15 MASTERFUL_PERFORMANCE, T3-27 STANDARD_PERFORMANCE from rival artisans); own clan’s artisan output is low relative to court presence (season with 0 artisan topic_types vs 3+ court attendance topics — the clan is present but not producing culture); Winter Court hosting active (host clan has strong incentive to showcase culture — artisan output reflects on host prestige); known_topics contains T3-28 ART_REMOVAL_SLIGHT or T4-88 ART_REMOVAL_MINOR targeting own clan artisans. No triggers hardcode clan identity. target_clan_id: null — cultural prestige is self-directed. Personality: Rei/Kanpeki core affinity (cultural refinement and artistic perfection). Makoto natural weight (sincere appreciation of art). Seigyo natural weight (cultural influence as soft control). Yu/Kyōryoku/Ishi deprioritize (martial and willpower virtues see art patronage as a distraction).

#### 57.54.6 Scoring Formula

Conclusion Score = Standing Objective Match (0 or +30) + Topic Urgency (Tier 3 = +25, Tier 4 = +10; Momentum rising = +10, stable = 0, falling = −10) + Convergent Topics (+5 per additional triggering topic beyond the first) + Personality Preference (core affinity +25, natural weight +15, neutral 0, deprioritize −15; hard-block removes from pool) + Continuation Bonus (flat +10 if active last season with valid triggers; Makoto +20, Ketsui +15; Ishi locks active conclusions entirely unless triggers become invalid or forced crisis overrides). Standing Objective Match: binary. Conclusion domain matches standing objective domain = +30. Non-matching = 0. No adjacency concept — the 30-point swing provides sufficient differentiation. Forced conclusions (Tier 1/2 from Step 1) receive fixed scores: Tier 1 = 150, Tier 2 = 140. Auto-transitions (DESTROY completion to SEEK_PEACE) receive score 150. Forced conclusions have no slot cap — the slot limit applies only to discretionary conclusions. If forced count exceeds personality base, all discretionary slots are consumed and the Champion is entirely crisis-bound. Ishi lock exception: forced crisis conclusions override Ishi lock. When the crisis resolves, the Ishi-locked conclusion re-enters with continuation bonus intact. Ties broken by: first, continuation (longer-active wins); second, random. All scoring values PROVISIONAL pending balancing.

#### 57.54.7 Broadcast Model

Model A — Broadcast. All Family Daimyo receive all active strategic conclusions. No per-family assignment. Each Family Daimyo independently decides how to translate the Champion’s directions into territory-specific actions based on their own capabilities, personality, and local conditions. The Champion does not micromanage family-level task assignment. The Champion’s contribution is strategic direction (what matters this season). The Family Daimyo’s contribution is operational translation (how their territory and people serve those priorities). Family Daimyo self-select by capability: a Doji courtier Family Daimyo naturally gravitates toward diplomatic conclusions because Phase 5 scores RAISE_DISPOSITION and CHARM higher for them. A Daidoji military Family Daimyo gravitates toward military conclusions because they score ORDER_PATROL and DRILL_TROOPS higher. No additional AI decision needed — existing engine mechanics produce differentiated family behavior. A Family Daimyo may deviate from the Champion’s priorities when local conditions demand it. A Daidoji Family Daimyo receiving “Pursue Dragon alliance” as top priority but facing an active bandit insurgency (Stability 40) prioritizes suppression over diplomacy. Local crisis overrides distant strategy. The Champion may not know about the local crisis if the Daidoji hasn’t reported it. The Champion IS a Family Daimyo simultaneously. The strategic evaluation produces the broadcast, then the Family Daimyo layer consumes it. Two hats, one character — no special case needed. Strategic conclusions are clan-level policy, not individual ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE firings. The Champion can still fire ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE for specific one-off tasks on top of the standing policy. Communication cost: Family Daimyo at court read clan_strategic_priorities directly. Absent Family Daimyo receive 1 WRITE_LETTER containing all conclusions (not 1 per conclusion). Champion death mid-season: array persists. New Champion runs evaluation at their first Strategic Review. Until then, old priorities remain (institutional momentum during succession).

#### 57.54.9 Decision Hierarchy — PROVISIONAL

Four tiers of decision-making, each with distinct scope. Tier 1 — Clan Champion: “What should our clan focus on?” Strategic conclusions, policy-level (Sections 57.54.1–57.54.8, fully designed). Output: clan_strategic_priorities array on clan data. Tier 2 — Family Daimyo: “What needs to happen across my family’s territory?” Translates Champion direction into territory-wide operational decisions. Output: specific ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE orders to Provincial Daimyo and direct vassals. Tier 3 — Provincial Daimyo: “What needs to happen in my province?” Receives direction from Family Daimyo, executes using the standard Decision Engine (Section 55). No additional system required. Tier 4 — Individual samurai: “How do I execute this task?” Receives NeedType via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE, Phase 5 scores ActionIDs. Standard Decision Engine. Tiers fire top-down in sequence during the Seasonal Strategic Review: Tier 1 first, then Tier 2, then Tier 3. Each reads the output of the tier above as input. A character wearing multiple hats (e.g. the Hida Champion is simultaneously Clan Champion, Hida Family Daimyo, and possibly Provincial Daimyo) runs each evaluation in sequence, not simultaneously. The character first decides clan policy, then puts on their Family Daimyo hat and asks what it means for their family territory, then puts on their Provincial Daimyo hat and executes within their province.

The same four-tier personality preference scale from Tier 1 (Section 57.54.6) applies: core affinity +25, natural weight +15, neutral 0, deprioritize −15. The Champion scored AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE at 65 using the Champion’s personality. A Jin Family Daimyo re-weights: 65 + (−15 Jin deprioritizes military) = 50. A Yu Family Daimyo re-weights: 65 + (+25 Yu core affinity military) = 90. Same Champion direction, different family interpretation. This makes each Family Daimyo an active interpreter of Champion direction, not a passive relay. A clan with diverse Family Daimyo personalities naturally distributes effort across the Champion’s priorities because each daimyo gravitates toward conclusions that resonate with their nature. The personality-to-conclusion_type mappings are the same as Tier 1 — AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE is core affinity for Yu at both tiers, deprioritized by Jin at both tiers. The relationship between personality and conclusion type does not change based on which tier evaluates it. Local needs from the Family Daimyo’s known_topics use the same scoring formula: Tier 3 topic = +25, Tier 4 = +10, momentum rising = +10, stable = 0, falling = −10. Both pools produce NeedType candidates simultaneously. The Family Daimyo selects the highest-weighted candidate they can serve based on their school_type and skill profile, regardless of whether it originated from the Champion’s direction or from local conditions. Viability check: a NeedType candidate is viable if the character can personally produce at least one ActionID for it (has a required skill at rank 1+) OR an unassigned vassal in met_characters can produce an ActionID for it (making ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE the execution path). Non-viable NeedTypes are skipped. A bushi Family Daimyo with no Theology never produces PERFORM_RITUAL for personal execution — but if they have an unassigned shugenja vassal, ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE targeting the shugenja IS viable, so the NeedType enters the pool with delegation as its execution route. This means: a Champion conclusion at 65 beats a local Tier 4 topic at 10 — Champion priority wins. A local Tier 3 rising insurgency at 35 beats a Champion conclusion at 30 — the Family Daimyo handles the local crisis. A forced conclusion (score 150) dominates all non-forced candidates from either pool. If no candidate from either pool produces a NeedType the Family Daimyo can pursue, they fall through to the Opportunity Scanner and standing objective — behaving exactly as they do in the current engine. The amended Phase 2 cascade for lord-tier characters: Reactive Event (Phase 1) → Crisis Override → Combined Pool: Champion Strategic Conclusions + Local Needs (highest-scored applicable NeedType wins) → Opportunity Scanner self-selected → Standing Objective. This makes the Family Daimyo a genuine operational decision-maker who balances Champion direction against local reality, not a passive relay. Local needs entry mechanism: Tier 3+ topics from the Family Daimyo’s known_topics are converted to NeedType candidates using the existing Opportunity Scanner’s (Section 55.26.1) topic-to-NeedType logic, then promoted from the Scanner’s output into the combined pool with their topic-derived score (Tier 3 = +25, Tier 4 = +10, plus momentum modifier). Tier 4 and below topics that do not reach the combined pool threshold remain at the Opportunity Scanner’s normal cascade position below the combined pool. This creates three urgency levels: Phase 1 reactive events for Tier 1/2 crises (highest priority), combined pool for Tier 3+ local needs competing with Champion conclusions (middle priority), Opportunity Scanner for routine Tier 4 maintenance (lowest priority). For non-lord-tier characters (Tier 4), the cascade is unchanged — they receive their NeedType directly from ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE in the primary objective slot.

57.54.10c Information Channel Constraints. The Family Daimyo’s local needs assessment can only draw from information actually in their possession. Sources: known_topics (topics that have reached the Family Daimyo through court, letters, conversation, intelligence actions), vassal reports (WRITE_LETTER received from Provincial Daimyo, magistrates, shrine keepers, military commanders), and direct province data (ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS on provinces where the Family Daimyo is physically present — context requirements per Annex D apply). Information the Family Daimyo does NOT have: conditions in provinces whose Provincial Daimyo has not reported (invisible until topic escalation or personal visit), vassal skills for characters not in met_characters (cannot match skills to tasks for unknown vassals), the full reasoning behind the Champion’s conclusions (they see the conclusion_type, domain, target, and score but not which topics triggered it unless the Champion included context in their WRITE_LETTER), conditions in other families’ territories. A Family Daimyo who stays at home governs well but is politically absent from court. A Family Daimyo at court is politically present but operationally dependent on vassal reports for territory data. Both are valid character behaviors with genuine gameplay consequences. A Family Daimyo who actively maintains communication with Provincial Daimyo — reading reports, visiting provinces, staying in contact — has a richer picture and makes better operational decisions. One who sits at court all season and never reads reports makes decisions based almost entirely on the Champion’s direction, blind to local conditions.

57.54.10d Lord-Tier Delegation Awareness — General Engine Enhancement. ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE scoring in Phase 5 gains a modifier based on whether unassigned vassals with relevant skills exist. This is a general enhancement applying to any character with direct subordinates — lord-tier characters (checking lord_id) AND characters with operational subordinates (checking operational_superior_id per Section 11.3.18 and Section 57.17). This includes Provincial Daimyo, magistrates directing yoriki, courtier delegation leaders directing members, and military commanders directing subordinate officers. The engine uses HierarchySystem.get_direct_vassals() which already reads both lord_id and operational_superior_id (Section 57.17). When a lord-tier character’s Phase 2 produces a NeedType, Phase 5 evaluates whether to execute personally or delegate. ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE score increases when: unassigned vassals with relevant skill rank 3+ for the NeedType exist in met_characters AND are within communication range (co-located or reachable by WRITE_LETTER). ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE score decreases when: all known vassals are already assigned, or no vassal has relevant skills, or the task requires the lord’s personal authority (lord-tier-only ActionIDs). The Family Daimyo can only assign vassals in met_characters. A Family Daimyo who has met all their Provincial Daimyo and key vassals delegates intelligently. One who hasn’t must either assign based on roster data alone (school_type and family, but not personal skill assessment) or execute personally. Sequential assignment: the Family Daimyo assigns highest-priority task to best available vassal, then next task to next best vassal, working down the priority list. Each ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE costs 1 Civilian Order (Section 57.34). The Civilian Order budget constrains how many assignments fire per day — assignments beyond the daily budget queue to subsequent days. Highest-scored Champion conclusions generate assignments first. Cycling mechanism: the Family Daimyo has multiple Champion conclusions to serve. The delegation modifier creates natural cycling. On the first AP decision, the highest-priority conclusion wins Phase 2 and produces a NeedType. If an unassigned vassal with relevant skills exists, ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE scores high in Phase 5 — the Family Daimyo delegates. That vassal is now assigned, reducing the delegation score for that NeedType (fewer unassigned vassals available). On the next AP decision, the second conclusion may now win Phase 2 because the first conclusion’s NeedType has a lower delegation score (the best vassal for it is already assigned). The Family Daimyo delegates again, staffing the second priority. This continues until all conclusions are staffed or all vassals are assigned. A Family Daimyo with 4 Provincial Daimyo and 3 Champion conclusions naturally distributes all 3 tasks across the first few AP decisions, then spends remaining AP on personal execution of whichever NeedType their own skills best serve. The cycling is emergent from the delegation scoring — no explicit priority rotation mechanism is needed. Civilian Order Budget for Operational Superiors: ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE costs 1 Civilian Order (Section 57.34). The Civilian Order budget is defined for lord-tier characters. Characters who are not lord-tier but hold operational_superior_id over subordinates (magistrates with yoriki, courtier delegation leaders with members) also require Civilian Orders to direct those subordinates. Rule: any character whose character_id appears as operational_superior_id on at least one other character receives a Civilian Order allocation. Budget: 1–3 subordinates = 2 Civilian Orders per IC day, 4+ subordinates = 3 Civilian Orders per IC day. This mirrors the existing lord-tier budget scale without creating a new cost mechanic. The allocation is recalculated whenever operational_superior_id assignments change (subordinate added, removed, killed, or reassigned). Characters with BOTH lord_id vassals AND operational_superior_id subordinates use the HIGHER of the two budget calculations, not the sum. Resolves the pre-existing tension between Section 11.3.17b (which assumes magistrates can fire ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE) and Section 57.34 (which defines the budget for lord-tier only). All budget values PROVISIONAL pending balancing.

57.54.10e Target Field Inheritance — ImmediateNeed Population Rule. When the Family Daimyo converts a Champion conclusion into a NeedType (via the mapping table in 57.54.10a), the resulting ImmediateNeed (Section 55.3.2) must have its target fields populated. Three sources, applied in order: (1) Inherited from conclusion: target_clan_id propagates directly from the StrategicConclusion. AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE toward Lion produces DEFEND_PROVINCE with target_clan_id = LION. PURSUE_ALLIANCE with Dragon produces RAISE_DISPOSITION with target_clan_id = DRAGON. Conclusions without a target_clan_id (SUPPRESS_INSTABILITY, DEVELOP_INFRASTRUCTURE, etc.) inherit nothing — all target fields come from local assessment. war_objective and edict_id propagate the same way when applicable. (2) Populated from local assessment: the Family Daimyo fills target_province_id and target_topic_id from their own information — known_topics, vassal reports, direct observation. A local insurgency topic names a specific province; the Family Daimyo populates target_province_id from that topic. A worship failure report names a specific Fortune; target_topic_id references the worship failure topic. Only information the Family Daimyo actually possesses can populate these fields (Section 57.54.10c information channel constraints apply). (3) Deferred to receiving character Phase 3: any target fields the Family Daimyo cannot populate (because they lack the specific information) are left null. The receiving vassal’s Phase 3 (Generate Options) resolves them using the vassal’s own information. Example: Family Daimyo assigns RAISE_DISPOSITION with target_clan_id = DRAGON but no target_npc_id (they don’t know which specific Dragon to approach). The receiving courtier’s Phase 3 scans their own met_characters for Dragon clan members and selects the best target. This three-layer population — inherited, locally assessed, deferred — means no single tier needs complete information. Each tier contributes what it knows. The Champion provides strategic context (which clan, which edict). The Family Daimyo provides operational context (which province, which topic). The executing vassal provides tactical context (which specific NPC, which specific location). Reassignment: when a vassal receives a new ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE, the new ImmediateNeed replaces their current primary objective per existing Section 18.2 rules. No special handling needed. Universality: this three-layer population rule applies to ALL ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE firings at any tier, not just Tier 2. When a Provincial Daimyo (Tier 3) delegates to a magistrate, the same rule applies: target_clan_id inherited from the Family Daimyo’s assignment, target_province_id populated from the Provincial Daimyo’s direct knowledge (their own province), target_npc_id deferred to the magistrate’s Phase 3. Each delegation step adds one more layer of specificity without requiring any tier to have complete information.

#### 57.54.11 Provincial Daimyo — No Additional System Required

Phase 1 reactive events override received objectives when local crises erupt. #### 57.54.13 Cross-Tier Interactions

Multi-hat characters: evaluations fire in tier order (1 → 2 → 3). Each tier reads the previous tier’s output. The Hida Champion first produces clan_strategic_priorities, then reads them as Hida Family Daimyo and assigns Provincial Daimyo, then reads those assignments as Provincial Daimyo of their personal province and delegates to local vassals. Local override: a Family Daimyo or Provincial Daimyo may deviate from received direction when local conditions demand it. An active insurgency (Phase 1 reactive event) overrides the Champion’s “Pursue Dragon alliance.” The Family Daimyo addresses the crisis first, then returns to Champion priorities when the crisis resolves. Reporting upward: when a lord at any tier deviates from received direction due to local conditions, they should report the deviation via WRITE_LETTER to their own lord. The Daidoji Family Daimyo who diverted resources from alliance-building to insurgency suppression writes to the Crane Champion explaining why. This generates a topic and enables the Champion to adjust clan_strategic_priorities at the next reactive evaluation if the information changes the strategic picture. Communication cost and delay: all tier-to-tier communication uses existing WRITE_LETTER (Section 12.7) or in-person ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (Section 57.34). Letter delivery timing (Section 12.7) applies — mountain provinces receive direction later. A distant Provincial Daimyo may operate on stale Family Daimyo direction for days or weeks. This is correct gameplay: geographic proximity to the decision-maker is strategically valuable. Multi-hat self-assignment (v588): when a character wearing multiple hats (e.g. Clan Champion who is also Family Daimyo and Provincial Daimyo) produces a NeedType at Tier 2 that they will execute personally at Tier 3, the NeedType enters their primary objective slot directly without firing ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. No Civilian Order is consumed. Self-assignment is internal decision-making, not communication — the character is telling themselves what to do, not sending an order.

#### Section 57.55 Artisan Gift Pipeline and Prerequisite Resolution Pattern — PROVISIONAL

**57.55.1 Prerequisite Resolution Pattern — Design Convention**

When a decomposition function generates ActionID candidates for a NeedType and a candidate has an unmet material prerequisite, the decomposition checks the prerequisite, scores the blocked ActionID at 0, and the remaining candidates in the Annex A alignment table (including prerequisite-resolution ActionIDs) compete normally in Phase 5. The resolution action wins naturally, produces the missing prerequisite on this AP, and the next AP re-evaluates the original NeedType cleanly. This is not a new engine mechanism. It is a design pattern that already exists in three places: (1) CONDITION_SLEEPER facility check (Section 54.7c) — before generating CONDUCT_CONDITIONING, the decomposition verifies an isolated facility is accessible; if not, BEGIN_TRAVEL or ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION replace the blocked action. (2) LOCATE_CHARACTER location check (Section 54.7c) — when a parent decomposition determines its objective is blocked by not knowing a target NPC’s location, LOCATE_CHARACTER fires as a prerequisite NeedType with its own decomposition. (3) Poison dose acquisition (Section 57.48.12) — the assassination decomposition checks for poison in inventory; if missing and Craft: Poison 1+, CRAFT is generated as a prerequisite step. Convention for future ActionIDs: any ActionID with a material requirement (item in inventory, troops levied, co-location with target, data structure present) may declare a prerequisite condition. The decomposition function for the parent NeedType checks the condition before scoring that ActionID. If unmet, the ActionID scores 0 and other candidates — including prerequisite-resolution actions already present in the Annex A alignment table — compete. No new engine infrastructure is required. Each specific case gets its own decomposition amendment following this established pattern. The prerequisite condition list is open for additions at implementation.

**57.55.2 DELIVER_GIFT Material Prerequisite — Decomposition Amendment**

DELIVER_GIFT (Category 1, 1 AP, Etiquette / Courtier) carries a material prerequisite: has_giftable_item. Before Phase 5 scores DELIVER_GIFT under any NeedType, the decomposition checks the character’s inventory for at least one item with giftable: true. If no giftable item exists, DELIVER_GIFT scores 0 for this AP. Other ActionIDs in the alignment table — CHARM (75), COMPOSE_PAINTING (45), COMPOSE_SCULPTURE (45), CRAFT (40), OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (40), PURCHASE_MARKET (35) — compete normally. Phase 5 skill modifiers determine which wins: a Kakita artisan with Painting 5 finds COMPOSE_PAINTING pushed above CHARM by the skill modifier. A Doji courtier with Etiquette 5 and no artisan skills finds CHARM wins every time. A Yasuki merchant with Commerce 4 at a market town finds PURCHASE_MARKET competitive. Each character type gravitates to their strongest disposition-raising path through existing Phase 5 scoring — no special routing logic.

**57.55.4 Generic Gift Item Type**

When PURCHASE_MARKET fires under the RAISE_DISPOSITION NeedType context, the character purchases a generic gift. This is an abstract item representing the appropriate court gifts defined in Section 12.3 (LOCKED): art objects, writing implements, tea ceremony implements, poetry collections, rare incense and perfumes. The engine does not enumerate specific items — the gift is a commodity from the “appropriate gifts” category. Schema: item_id (unique), item_type: “gift”, quality_tier: Normal or Fine (determined by market availability per Section 49 — villages produce Normal, towns and cities can produce Fine), koku_cost: market price for that quality tier, giftable: true, provenance: null (no creator, no subject, no history). Weapons and armor are NOT giftable per Section 12.3 (LOCKED): “Weapons and armor are not gifts in Rokugan. Gifting a sword implies the recipient lacks one, which is an insult.” DELIVER_GIFT reads the generic gift’s quality_tier and uses the existing disposition mapping from Section 15.4: Normal = +3 disposition, 0 Free Raises on the Awareness + Etiquette roll. Fine = +5 disposition, +1 Free Raise. By contrast, an artisan-crafted Exceptional painting = +8 disposition, +2 Free Raises. A Masterwork painting = +12 disposition, +3 Free Raises. Artisan-crafted gifts are always mechanically superior to purchased gifts — more AP investment produces more disposition return. This preserves the hierarchy described in Section 12.3 (“a Kakita-crafted painting carries far more weight than a common object”) and the Crane Cultural Prestige mechanic (Section 2.2).

**57.55.5 Giftable Item Tag**

The giftable boolean tag is added to the item data structure (Section 49 provenance record). An item is giftable: true if it was produced by CRAFT or COMPOSE_ with an artisan sub-skill (Painting, Sculpture, Gardening, Calligraphy, Ceramics, Ikebana, Origami, etc.), purchased as a generic gift via PURCHASE_MARKET under RAISE_DISPOSITION context, or received as a completed art commission delivery. An item is giftable: false (or tag absent) if it is a weapon or armor (Section 12.3, explicit prohibition), a consumable (medicine kit, jade powder, poison dose, kagaku item), basic traveling supplies or equipment, the character’s equipped personal items, or an item currently in transit. Bonsai are a special case: DISPLAY_BONSAI (Section 57.24.8) is the primary use path; gifting a bonsai is possible but the bonsai system already handles its own pipeline. Bonsai items carry giftable: true by default.

In that case, the artisan receives CRAFT_ITEM as their primary objective through the standard delegation system (Section 57.54.10d) and the Scanner amendment does not fire — the primary objective takes precedence. The Scanner amendment covers only peer commissions where no ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE was issued.**

**57.55.7 Post-Crafting Delivery — Automatic Item Transfer

When a commissioned item completes (commission_record status changes to “completed”), the artisan’s engine generates an automatic delivery action on the next AP. Delivery path depends on co-location with the commissioner. Co-located (same settlement): Give Directly per Item Transfer Mechanics (Section 22.3, LOCKED). Immediate. No roll. No AP cost. The item moves from the artisan’s inventory to the commissioner’s inventory. commission_record status updates to “delivered.” Not co-located: Send by Messenger per Item Transfer Mechanics (Section 22.3, LOCKED). The artisan fires WRITE_LETTER (0 AP, free daily letter or 1 AP) with the item attached. The item travels with the letter at standard delivery timing (Section 12.7). During transit the item exists in neither party’s inventory. On arrival, the item enters the commissioner’s inventory and commission_record status updates to “delivered.” If the letter is intercepted, the item is also intercepted per standard interception rules. The delivery is automatic — it fires as a completion handler on the crafting ActionID’s resolution, not as a separate Phase 5 decision. This follows the CONDITION_SLEEPER completion handler precedent (Section 54.7c): “when conditioning_progress reaches 100.0 on an AP firing, the decomposition function executes a completion sequence before returning.” The crafting completion handler executes the delivery before the NeedType closes. For the daimyo art display pipeline (Pipeline 3): after the commissioner (lord) receives the delivered item, DISPLAY_PAINTING (0 AP, Section 57.27.16) or DISPLAY_BONSAI (1 AP, Section 57.24.8) becomes available. DISPLAY_PAINTING carries its own prerequisite: has_painting_item in inventory AND current zone has displayed_art_slot = true (Section 57.36). The lord’s engine scores DISPLAY_PAINTING under CRAFT_ITEM NeedType (alignment 100 in the existing Annex A table) on the next AP after the painting enters inventory. If an eligible zone exists in the lord’s holdings, the painting is displayed. If no eligible zone exists, the painting remains in inventory as a giftable item available for DELIVER_GIFT. Delivery cost clarification (v588): the completion handler delivery is a system-level action. It does not consume AP, does not count against the character’s daily WRITE_LETTER budget or free letter slot, and does not require a Civilian Order. The WRITE_LETTER delivery timing (Section 12.7) applies to transit duration only — the sending is automatic and costless. This is consistent with other completion handlers (CONDITION_SLEEPER, commission_record status updates) which execute system-level state changes without AP cost.**

**57.55.8 Art Display Trigger — Opportunity Scanner Amendment and Annex A Addition

Annex A amendment. DISPLAY_PAINTING is added to the CRAFT_ITEM alignment table in Annex A (objective_alignment.json) at alignment score 100. This entry was referenced by Section 57.55.7 but was not present in the existing table. Without it, Phase 5 cannot score DISPLAY_PAINTING under any NeedType. The corrected CRAFT_ITEM alignment table: CRAFT 100, DISPLAY_PAINTING 100, PURCHASE_MARKET 30, DO_NOTHING 0. DISPLAY_PAINTING at 100 ties with CRAFT. When both are candidates, the Phase 5 context filter resolves: CRAFT requires artisan skills (Annex D skill gate), DISPLAY_PAINTING requires lord authority over a zone with an available art slot (AT_ZONE context per Section 57.27.16). A lord without artisan skills sees only DISPLAY_PAINTING. An artisan without lord authority sees only CRAFT. An artisan-lord sees both and the skill modifier on CRAFT pushes it ahead — the artisan-lord crafts first, displays later. Correct behavior.

Opportunity Scanner amendment for art display. When the Opportunity Scanner (Section 55.26.1) runs for a lord-tier character (any character with zone_lord_id authority over at least one zone), it checks two conditions: (1) the character’s inventory contains at least one painting whose format matches an available art slot in a zone they control — kakemono format and wall_art_slot empty, byōbu format and displayed_art_slot empty, or fusuma format and fusuma_slot empty (slot definitions per Section 57.27.4, eligible zone subtypes per Section 57.36 Flag Matrix); AND (2) the character is AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or physically present in a settlement containing the eligible zone. If both conditions are met, the Scanner generates CRAFT_ITEM as a candidate at Scanner priority. Phase 5 scores DISPLAY_PAINTING at alignment 100 (the only CRAFT_ITEM candidate available to a non-artisan lord). DISPLAY_PAINTING fires at 0 AP (Section 57.27.16), resolving instantly without consuming an AP slot. The painting occupies the matching slot in the lord’s best eligible zone. Zone preference when multiple eligible zones exist: the zone where the lord is physically present wins; if the lord controls multiple eligible zones and is not physically present in any of them, the zone with the highest visitor traffic (most named characters present) wins — the lord displays art where it will be seen. If no eligible zone exists in the lord’s holdings (no interior zones with art slots, or all slots occupied), the Scanner does not generate CRAFT_ITEM. The painting remains in inventory as a giftable item available for DELIVER_GIFT under RAISE_DISPOSITION.

Seasonal rotation. When a kakemono is currently displayed in a wall_art_slot with a season_affinity that does not match the current IC season (Section 57.27.6), AND the lord has a kakemono in inventory whose season_affinity matches the current IC season, the Scanner generates CRAFT_ITEM. DISPLAY_PAINTING fires, replacing the off-season kakemono with the correct one. The replaced painting returns to the lord’s inventory. This handles the seasonal rotation described in Section 57.27.6 (seasonal harmony bonus) without any additional ActionIDs or manual intervention. A lord with a full four-painting seasonal set rotates automatically at each season boundary. A lord with only one kakemono never rotates — the painting stays displayed year-round with no penalty (Section 57.27.6: “no penalty, but NPCs with elevated Rei or Artisan: Painting rank 2+ register a Tier 5 aesthetic observation”).

Scope. This Scanner amendment covers paintings only (kakemono, byōbu, fusuma formats). Emakimono are excluded — they occupy no slot and are read via PRESENT_EMAKIMONO (1 AP, Section 57.27.19), which has its own engine path. Bonsai display is excluded — DISPLAY_BONSAI (1 AP, Section 57.24.8) has its own system with distinct zone requirements (CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD, TSUBONIWA). Sculptures are excluded — Section 57.28 defines statue_slot and guardian_slot at shrine/temple zones, but statuary auto-places on composition completion (sculptor must be AT_ZONE throughout, so no inventory-to-display transition exists) and figurines enter the gift economy via DELIVER_GIFT. No Scanner amendment needed for sculpture.

**57.55.9 Commission Record Death Cleanup

On character death (Section 57.49 trigger), the death handler checks for commission_records where artisan_id matches the dead character’s character_id. For each record with status “accepted” or “in_progress”: status updates to “failed,” failed_reason set to “artisan_death.” Any partially completed craft items (in-progress paintings with craft_progress below completion, sculptures mid-composition) are lost — the artisan died before finishing them. No partial item enters any inventory. The work dies with the artist. Items already completed and in transit (commission_record status “completed,” delivery WRITE_LETTER already sent per Section 57.55.7) are unaffected — the letter arrives normally per Section 12.7 delivery timing. Death does not recall letters already in the courier network. If the item is in the artisan’s inventory at status “completed” but the delivery completion handler has not yet fired (artisan died on the same AP as craft completion, before the handler executed), the item follows standard corpse inventory rules per Section 57.49 — it becomes part of the corpse’s possessions and may be recovered by whoever handles the body. The commission_record status still updates to “failed” because the automatic delivery chain is broken, but the physical item exists and can be recovered and delivered manually. Commissioner notification relies on the existing death system. Section 57.49 already generates a Tier 4 death topic (“[Character] has died”) that propagates through court, letters, and conversation per normal topic channels. No special WRITE_LETTER fires for commission failure — the death topic carries the information. The commissioner’s engine detects the artisan’s death through known_topics. On detecting a failed commission (artisan’s death topic in known_topics AND a commission_record with that artisan at status “failed”), the commissioner may re-fire REQUEST_ART (Section 57.23.3) to seek a new artisan, or fire OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (Section 57.23.4) directly if another suitable artisan exists in met_characters. The failed commission_record remains as a historical record on the commissioner’s data. It does not block new commissions with other artisans — there is no limit on concurrent commission_records with different artisans. The declined_commissions 2 IC season expiry (Section 57.23.4) does not apply to failed commissions — declined and failed are distinct states with different causes. This follows the same death cleanup pattern as other systems in Section 57.49: positions create FILL_VACANCY, active objectives are cleared, operational_superior_id relationships are severed, lord_id vassals trigger succession evaluation. Commission_records are another data structure referencing the dead character that requires cleanup on death.

#### Section 57.56 Advisory Council System — PROVISIONAL

**57.56.1 Design Intent**

The feudal governance hierarchy (Clan Champion → Family Daimyo → Provincial Daimyo → Individual Samurai) routes objectives downward through ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE, each costing 1 Civilian Order. Under the current system, a Family Daimyo managing 5 provinces spends 5 CO to distribute a single strategic conclusion. A lord juggling military, diplomatic, and economic conclusions simultaneously exhausts their CO budget on distribution alone, leaving nothing for direct intervention or correspondence. The Advisory Council System addresses this by allowing lords to appoint trusted vassals to advisory positions with domain-scoped delegated authority. An advisor speaks with the lord’s voice within their domain — they can issue ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to the lord’s vassals for NeedTypes that fall within their domain_scope, without occupying the vassal’s operational_superior_id slot. This is delegated authority, not chain-of-command authority. The advisor is not the vassal’s boss; they are the lord’s authorized representative for a defined set of concerns. The magistrate system (Section 11.3, LOCKED) is the closest precedent. A magistrate has a position-derived standing objective (UPHOLD_LAW), a CO budget (v582), and operational authority over yoriki. The advisory system extends this pattern from law enforcement to governance, diplomacy, military coordination, spiritual oversight, and commerce. Eligibility: Clan Champion, Family Daimyo, and Provincial Daimyo tiers. The Emperor does NOT use this system — the Emperor’s Chosen (Section 2.1.17) are the Emperor’s advisory council and predate and supersede this system. Source: Way of the Daimyo (Emerald Empire 4e, pp. 276–294). CK2 council system as structural inspiration. Neither is transposed directly — the system is built from existing engine infrastructure.

**57.56.2 Advisory Position Types**

Five advisory position_types. A lord may appoint at most one character per position_type. Not all positions need to be filled — most lords fill only those relevant to their situation. No hard slot cap per lord tier; the natural constraint is vassal availability.

Karo (Chief Steward). Domain: Administration and Economy. The karo manages the lord’s household, finances, and civilian infrastructure. They coordinate provincial daimyo on tax rates, stipend levels, resource distribution, and construction priorities. The karo does not personally execute lord-tier actions (SET_TAX_RATE, SET_STIPEND_RATE, ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS etc. remain lord-tier) — they issue ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE directing provincial lords to execute within the economic domain. The karo’s value is coordination and proactive monitoring, not personal execution of governance actions. Standing objective: “Maintain the prosperity and stability of my lord’s holdings.” Preferred skills: Commerce, Courtier, Lore: Law. Any school_type eligible.

Hatamoto (Military Advisor). Domain: Local Defense. The hatamoto manages garrison allocation, patrol scheduling, doshin oversight, and insurgency suppression coordination across the lord’s territory. Distinct from the Rikugunshokan (who commands the field army via operational_superior_id). The hatamoto handles what the army does not — standing defense, internal security, and suppression of local threats. Like the karo, the hatamoto delegates lord-tier military actions (ORDER_PATROL, ASSIGN_GARRISON) rather than firing them personally. Standing objective: “Defend my lord’s holdings against internal and external threats.” Preferred skills: Battle, Investigation, Intimidation. Bushi school_type preferred but not required.

Gaikou-kan (Ambassador). Domain: Diplomacy. The gaikou-kan manages diplomatic correspondence, court delegation assignments, and courtier deployment to foreign courts. When the lord receives a diplomatic conclusion from the Clan Champion (PURSUE_ALLIANCE, SEEK_PEACE, UNDERMINE_POSITION, STRENGTHEN_COURT), the gaikou-kan translates it into specific assignments for courtiers and delegation leaders. The gaikou-kan is a coordinator-executor — they can personally fire non-lord-tier court actions (CHARM, NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE, RAISE_DISPOSITION) at foreign courts AND delegate courtier assignments via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. Standing objective: “Advance my lord’s diplomatic interests.” Preferred skills: Courtier, Etiquette, Sincerity. Courtier school_type preferred but not required.

Jisha-bugyō (Temple Commissioner). Domain: Spiritual. The jisha-bugyō manages the worship cascade, shrine construction advocacy, Fortune status monitoring, and spiritual health of the lord’s territory. They coordinate shugenja on worship schedules and respond to realm overlap warnings. Coordinator-executor — can personally fire PERFORM_WORSHIP (standard AP) and delegate spiritual assignments. Standing objective: “Preserve the spiritual health of my lord’s territory” (identical to standing objective #25 from Section 57.52.10). Hard gate: school_type must be SHUGENJA or MONK — same gate as spiritual standing objectives #25–#27 (Section 57.52.10, v572).

Shōnin-kata (Merchant Patron). Domain: Commerce. The shōnin-kata manages trade relationships, market development, and commercial operations. Coordinator-executor — can personally fire CONDUCT_COMMERCE (standard AP, Commerce 3+ required) and coordinate merchants and traders. Standing objective: “Expand the commercial prosperity of my lord’s holdings.” Preferred skills: Commerce, Courtier, Sincerity. Any school_type eligible, Commerce 3+ required. Clan cultural disposition: appointing a Merchant Patron generates a Tier 4 topic (“Daimyo [X] appoints Merchant Patron”). Clans with merchant traditions (Yasuki/Crab, Daidoji/Crane, Yoritomo/Mantis, Ide/Unicorn) treat this as neutral or positive. Other clans’ characters react through normal disposition mechanics based on personality. No special Honor penalty — the topic system handles social consequences naturally.

**57.56.3 Appointment, Dismissal, and Succession Cleanup**

Appointment uses the existing APPOINT_TO_POSITION ActionID (Category 14, 1 AP, lord-tier). The appointed character must be in the lord’s met_characters list. This creates a natural ramp-up: the advisor must then build their own met_characters relationships with the lord’s vassals before they can direct them via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. A lord who appoints someone who already knows the provincial daimyo gets immediate effectiveness; a lord who appoints a stranger accepts a slower start. Cross-clan appointments generate a Tier 4 topic. The five advisory position_types are registered in the COMPETE_FOR_POSITION system (Section 57.51) — ambitious NPCs may compete to become a lord’s karo. Dismissal uses the existing DISMISS_FROM_POSITION ActionID (Category 14, 1 Civilian Order, lord-tier). Generates a Tier 4 topic. Outstanding ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE directives issued by the dismissed advisor persist on their recipients but source transfers to the lord (the lord inherits responsibility for existing orders). Succession cleanup: on lord death or succession (Section 22.5), all characters with advisory_lord_id matching the departing lord have their advisory_position_type and advisory_lord_id cleared. The new lord starts with an empty advisory_council. They can reappoint the same people or choose new advisors. This parallels how magistrate appointments and other position_types reset on lord change. Advisory positions are opportunity-driven. They emerge from vassal ambition via COMPETE_FOR_POSITION (Section 57.51), not from proactive lord creation. No engine trigger causes a lord to create advisory positions unprompted. Lords who lack ambitious, capable vassals govern without advisors — an authentic outcome, not a gap.

**57.56.4 Domain Delegation — The Core Mechanism**

Advisory positions introduce a third authority path for ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE alongside lord_id and operational_superior_id. This is called domain_delegation. A character holding an advisory position can issue ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to any character whose lord_id matches the advisor’s lord_id, provided the NeedType being assigned falls within the advisor’s domain_scope (Section 57.56.6). The advisor draws from their own CO budget (Section 57.56.5), not the lord’s. Engine amendment to Section 57.17 (HierarchySystem): HierarchySystem.get_assignable_targets(character_id) currently checks two paths — (1) characters whose lord_id matches the assigning character’s character_id, and (2) characters whose operational_superior_id matches the assigning character’s character_id. A third path is added: (3) if character holds advisory_position_type != null, return characters whose lord_id matches the character’s lord_id AND the NeedType being assigned is within the character’s domain_scope. Path 3 does not modify any target’s operational_superior_id. Phase 3 validation: when the assigning character uses domain_delegation (path 3), the engine checks: (a) assigner holds a non-null advisory_position_type, (b) assigner’s lord_id matches target’s lord_id, (c) the NeedType of the objective being assigned is present in the assigner’s domain_scope list. If any check fails, the action is blocked. Delegation awareness cycling (Section 57.54.10d amendment): the cycling mechanism that prevents piling assignments on one vassal must extend to domain_delegation targets. When the advisor assigns a vassal, that vassal’s delegation score reduces on subsequent scoring rounds, causing the next vassal to win. This uses the same “has active objective” flag read that the existing cycling mechanism uses.

**57.56.5 Civilian Order Budget for Advisors**

Advisory positions grant a position-derived CO allocation: 3 CO per OOC day (0.75 per IC day average). This is sufficient for 3 delegation directives per OOC day. If the advisor also holds a lordship or has operational subordinates, they use the highest applicable CO budget, not the sum. A character who is both a provincial daimyo (8 CO) and a karo for their family daimyo uses 8 CO, not 11. The advisory CO allocation is a floor, not an addition. Amendment to Section 57.34.2: add after the operational-superior CO rule (v582): “Characters holding an advisory position (Section 57.56) receive a minimum CO allocation of 3 per OOC day from the position itself. If the character also qualifies for CO from lordship rank or operational-superior count, the highest single value applies.” Remote delivery: when the advisor issues ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE to a vassal who is not co-located, the advisor must also send a WRITE_LETTER carrying the directive. The CO covers the decision; the letter covers the delivery. Non-lord letter rules apply to the advisor (1 free per IC day + 1 AP for batch of 4 additional). Letter delivery delay per Section 12.7 applies. Co-located targets receive directives instantly for CO only. All values PROVISIONAL.

**57.56.6 Domain Scope Enumerations**

Each advisory position_type has a static domain_scope — a list of NeedTypes the advisor is authorized to assign through domain_delegation. Karo domain_scope (Administration/Economy): ACQUIRE_RESOURCE, ADJUST_TAX, EVALUATE_PROVINCES, BUILD_INFRASTRUCTURE, CONDUCT_COMMERCE, FILL_VACANCY (civilian positions only — scoped by sub-check on position_type, excludes military and magistrate vacancies). Hatamoto domain_scope (Local Defense): DEFEND_PROVINCE, PATROL_PROVINCE, INVESTIGATE_THREAT, TRAIN_TROOPS, UPHOLD_LAW (coordination with magistrate, not replacement), ACQUIRE_RESOURCE. Gaikou-kan domain_scope (Diplomacy): RAISE_DISPOSITION, DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP, GATHER_INTELLIGENCE, ATTEND_COURT, SEEK_PEACE, SEEK_GLORY (court contexts only). Jisha-bugyō domain_scope (Spiritual): PERFORM_RITUAL, MANAGE_TAINT, BUILD_INFRASTRUCTURE (limited to shrine/temple construction sub-types only). Shōnin-kata domain_scope (Commerce): CONDUCT_COMMERCE, ACQUIRE_RESOURCE, SECURE_TRADE_ROUTE, EVALUATE_PROVINCES. Cross-domain NeedTypes available to all advisors: ASSIGN_OBJECTIVE, WRITE_LETTER, LOCATE_CHARACTER. HONOR_COMMITMENT is never delegated through domain_scope. Shared NeedTypes across domains: ACQUIRE_RESOURCE appears in Karo, Hatamoto (military supplies), and Shōnin-kata scopes. When two advisors both assign the same vassal in the same IC day, the later assignment overwrites per existing Section 11.3.18g last-write-wins rules.

**57.56.7 Conclusion-Type to Advisor Routing**

When a lord’s Phase 2 evaluation produces a NeedType from a Champion conclusion (Section 57.54.10), and the lord has an advisory council, ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE targeting the relevant advisor scores with a +20 bonus (“domain expert available”) on top of normal ObjAlign. This makes the advisor the preferred delegation target for in-domain work. Routing map: AGGRESSIVE_POSTURE → Hatamoto. LAUNCH_OFFENSIVE → Not routable to advisors (army operations go through Rikugunshokan via military hierarchy). SUPPRESS_INSTABILITY → Hatamoto. SUPPORT_SHADOWLANDS → Hatamoto + Jisha-bugyō. PURSUE_ALLIANCE → Gaikou-kan. SEEK_PEACE → Gaikou-kan. UNDERMINE_POSITION → Gaikou-kan. STRENGTHEN_COURT → Gaikou-kan. COMPLY_EDICT → Depends on edict domain. SECURE_RESOURCE → Karo or Shōnin-kata. DEVELOP_INFRASTRUCTURE → Karo or Jisha-bugyō. RECOVER_DAMAGE → Karo. RESTORE_WORSHIP → Jisha-bugyō. RESPOND_SPIRITUAL_CRISIS → Jisha-bugyō.

**57.56.8 Advisor Phase 2 Cascade**

The advisor’s Decision Engine Phase 2 cascade for NeedType selection: Reactive Event (Phase 1) → Crisis Override → Lord’s Direct Assignment (ImmediateNeed from ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE) → Position-Derived Standing Objective → Opportunity Scanner → Personal Standing Objective. The position-derived standing objective is the advisor’s day job. The personal standing objective is their personal life. Both coexist. This formalizes the pattern established by UPHOLD_LAW for magistrates (Section 57.16.9) and extends it to all advisory positions. Standing objective decompositions separate personal execution from delegation. The karo’s “Maintain prosperity” decomposes to ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE with sub-NeedTypes EVALUATE_PROVINCES, ACQUIRE_RESOURCE, FILL_VACANCY, BUILD_INFRASTRUCTURE (all delegation targets — lord-tier ActionIDs the karo cannot fire personally). The karo’s personal AP actions are limited to WRITE_LETTER, PROBE, and other non-lord-tier ActionIDs. The gaikou-kan’s “Advance diplomatic interests” decomposes to both personal court actions (CHARM, NEGOTIATE, PERSUADE — standard AP) and delegated assignments (ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE directing courtiers to foreign courts). The jisha-bugyō’s decomposition is identical to standing objective #25 (Section 57.52.10). The shōnin-kata personally executes CONDUCT_COMMERCE and delegates ACQUIRE_RESOURCE and SECURE_TRADE_ROUTE.

**57.56.9 Priority and Override Rules**

Domain_delegation directives rank equally with operational_superior directives. Lord overrides both. Two-tier priority stack: lord > everyone else. If the karo and the Rikugunshokan give conflicting orders to the same provincial daimyo, the most recent one wins (existing Section 11.3.18g last-write-wins). The lord resolves persistent conflicts by intervening directly. Lord override: the lord can always issue ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE directly to any vassal, bypassing the advisor. The lord’s directive takes priority per existing Section 11.3.18f rules. Socially costly — generates disposition −3 between lord and advisor per bypass. Frequent bypasses (3+ per IC season) generate Tier 4 topic: “Daimyo [X] undermines Advisor [Y].” Advisor cannot override lord: the advisor’s domain_delegation is explicitly subordinate to the lord’s direct authority. An advisor who disagrees with the lord’s direction may reduce their compliance (personality-driven, per Section 11.3.18h), but they cannot countermand the lord.

**57.56.10 Vassal Awareness and Disposition**

The provincial daimyo knows the directive came from the advisor, not from the lord directly. The advisor’s character_id is recorded as the source on the ImmediateNeed. Disposition toward the advisor affects compliance weighting. A poorly chosen advisor who antagonizes vassals produces worse governance than one who is respected. Starting disposition baseline for advisory relationships: +8 (the advisor is a known, vetted appointment — higher trust floor than military +5 or legal +5 because the lord personally chose this advisor from their household). Subsequent disposition follows normal interaction rules per Section 11.3.18i.

**57.56.11 Advisor Death and Vacancy**

On advisor death: outstanding ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE directives issued by the advisor remain active. Source on each ImmediateNeed transfers from the advisor’s character_id to lord_id. This is engine-level cleanup (Section 57.49 death handler pattern), not a character-level decision — vassals continue executing and learn about the advisor’s death through normal Tier 4 topic propagation. The directives are target-dependent (“patrol province X,” “acquire iron”), not advisor-dependent, so they remain valid regardless of who issued them. The lord inherits oversight of the transferred directives, increasing their CO pressure until a replacement is appointed. The lord’s FILL_VACANCY NeedType fires. Assassinating a lord’s karo degrades their governance — meaningful consequence. On advisor dismissal: outstanding directives remain active but source transfers to lord_id on the ImmediateNeed. No orphaned objectives. Generates Tier 4 topic.

**57.56.12 Character Sheet Fields**

On the advisor character (Section 22.3 amendment): advisory_position_type: enum (karo, hatamoto, gaikou_kan, jisha_bugyo, shonin_kata) or null. Only one advisory position at a time. Cleared on dismissal, death, or lord succession. advisory_lord_id: int or null. The lord who appointed them. Should always match lord_id at appointment time. Tracked separately to handle succession edge case: if the lord dies and succession changes lord_id, advisory_lord_id preserves the original appointment for vacancy cleanup. On the lord character (Section 22.3 amendment): advisory_council: array of {position_type: enum, character_id: int}. Maximum 5 entries (one per position_type). Empty array is valid. Entries are added by APPOINT_TO_POSITION and removed by DISMISS_FROM_POSITION, advisor death cleanup, or lord succession cleanup. Advisory positions do not confer Status. The advisor’s Status derives from their existing feudal position. The advisory_position_type grants delegated authority and CO budget, not social rank.

Section 57.57 Topic Type Catalog — PROVISIONAL

57.57.1 Purpose and Schema

This section is the authoritative enumeration of every topic_type the engine can generate. Every system that generates a topic references a catalog entry here rather than defining its own string. Each entry carries the following schema fields: topic_type (enum string), tier (1–4), category (MILITARY, POLITICAL, ECONOMIC, SPIRITUAL, SOCIAL, or PERSONAL), persistence (CRISIS or EVENT — crises persist until end_condition is met, events have scoring weight that decays over time), propagation_target (ALL_LORDS, CLAN_LORDS, ADJACENT_LORDS, LOCAL, or NONE), propagation_urgency (starting value for the propagation scoring modifier), template (string with variables in square brackets), trigger (system + condition that generates the topic), source_section (cross-reference to full mechanics), decay_rate (IC seasons until scoring weight reaches zero — events only, by tier: Tier 1 = 8, Tier 2 = 4, Tier 3 = 2, Tier 4 = 1), end_condition (crises only — what resolves the crisis), mechanical_effects (what the topic does to the engine when active). Additionally, event topics carry a decay_start field: IMMEDIATE (default — scoring weight begins decaying at topic generation) or ON_RESOLUTION (scoring weight stays full while the condition is active, begins decaying when the condition ends — used by ongoing-condition topics like fugitive declarations and embargoes). Additionally, all topic_types carry valence and sharing fields: default_valence (POSITIVE, NEGATIVE, or NEUTRAL — the baseline framing of the event), subject_roles (list of named roles the topic carries, e.g., winner/loser, subject/accuser/accused, host — each role is tagged POSITIVE or NEGATIVE by the generating system at topic creation time), listener_effects (NeedType candidates for the Opportunity Scanner, universal data-level effects, and relationship-based effects — see 57.57.6b for scope rules). At the topic instance level (per character in known_topics), two additional fields: subject_valence_map (populated at creation — maps each subject character_id to their role valence, e.g., {winner_id: POSITIVE, loser_id: NEGATIVE}) and acquisition_source (WITNESSED if the character was present at the zone when the topic fired, RECEIVED if acquired through conversation, letter, or court — set once at acquisition, never changes). Secrets are NOT in this catalog. Secrets are a separate system (Section 12.8) with their own severity tiers. When a secret is exposed, the exposure generates a topic from this catalog. The secret system feeds the topic system but is not part of it.

57.57.2 Topic Propagation Mechanism

No topic propagates magically. A topic fires and enters the known_topics of characters who are present at the event or detected it. From there it travels through existing channels — Daily Conversation (Section 12.6), WRITE_LETTER (Section 12.7), court speech, intelligence actions. The propagation_target tag identifies who needs the information, not who receives it instantly. It creates urgency within existing channels, not a bypass around them. When a character first receives a topic with propagation_target != NONE, the topic instance on that character gains a propagation_urgency score. Starting values by target type: ALL_LORDS +50, CLAN_LORDS +40, ADJACENT_LORDS +30, LOCAL +20, NONE 0. Co-located sharing: during Daily Conversation or Deliberate PC-Initiated Conversation topic selection, if the conversation partner matches the propagation_target AND the NPC has not already shared this specific topic with this specific person, the topic gets the propagation_urgency value as a flat bonus to topic selection scoring. Remote sharing: when the NPC’s engine evaluates who to write their free letter to (1 free per IC day for non-lords, Section 12.7), targets in met_characters who match the propagation_target AND have not been informed by this NPC get a scoring bonus equal to propagation_urgency. The letter carries the topic in its topic exchange payload. This uses the free letter — no AP cost. Tracking: each character’s topic instance carries informed_targets (list of character_ids this NPC has personally shared this topic with). When a topic is exchanged, the recipient’s character_id is added. This is NPC-side tracking only. Propagation_target matching: CLAN_LORDS means “lord + same clan as the topic’s source_clan_id.” ADJACENT_LORDS means “lord + province adjacent to the topic’s source_province_id.” ALL_LORDS means any lord-tier character. LOCAL means characters in the same province/settlement.

57.57.3 Intermediary Routing

When an NPC holds a topic with propagation_target and evaluates their met_characters for sharing, the check runs in two passes. Pass 1 — Direct targets: does anyone in met_characters match the propagation_target? If yes, apply full propagation_urgency bonus. Share directly. Pass 2 — Intermediaries: if Pass 1 finds no valid targets (or all valid targets are already in informed_targets), the NPC looks for the best intermediary. An intermediary is someone in met_characters who the NPC can reasonably infer has access to a valid target: (a) position under a target lord (the NPC knows their magistrate friend serves under Provincial Daimyo X), (b) same clan and higher Status (a Status 4+ samurai from the Crab clan almost certainly has access to Crab leadership), (c) advisory or staff position (a karo or gaikou-kan serves a specific lord). Intermediaries receive half the propagation_urgency bonus (+25 instead of +50 for ALL_LORDS). The NPC prefers direct contact when available. Chain propagation: the intermediary now holds the topic with their own propagation_urgency score. They run their own Pass 1 and Pass 2. Each link acts independently. The NPC does NOT calculate multi-hop paths — they look one step out and trust the chain.

57.57.4 Propagation Urgency Decay

Two decay layers. Per-target decay (immediate): once the NPC shares the topic with a specific person, that person is in informed_targets. The urgency bonus no longer applies to that target. Base urgency decay (over time): propagation_urgency decreases by 10 per IC day. After 5 IC days at ALL_LORDS (+50), the urgency has faded to 0. The topic is no longer breaking news. The NPC still holds it in known_topics and can still share it organically through normal conversation, but the special scoring pressure is gone. They assume word has gotten around. This means a crisis topic in a remote province with poor social connectivity genuinely travels slower than one near a major court. A Shadowlands breach near Otosan Uchi is empire-wide news in 2 IC days. A breach in a remote Crab border province takes longer because the chain has more hops. Authentic.

57.57.5 Topic Scoring Weight Decay

Topics stay in known_topics permanently but their scoring weight in NPC decision-making decays over time. The topic becomes background knowledge — remembered but no longer driving decisions or conversation. Crisis topics: no decay until end_condition is met. On resolution, the topic converts to a resolved event and begins decaying at its tier’s event rate. Event topics: scoring weight decays linearly across the tier’s duration. Tier 1 events decay over 8 IC seasons. Tier 2 events decay over 4 IC seasons. Tier 3 events decay over 2 IC seasons. Tier 4 events decay over 1 IC season. Decay is linear — an NPC talking about a Tier 4 topic on day 1 applies full weight. The same NPC on the last day of the season barely cares. A Shadowlands Incursion that lasted 3 seasons and was then defeated remains active news for another 8 seasons after resolution. All decay values PROVISIONAL.

57.57.6 Topic Categories

Every topic_type carries exactly one category. The category is a tag the personality scoring system can read — not a new system, just a field existing scoring consumes. MILITARY: battles, incursions, troop movements, war declarations, levies. POLITICAL: edicts, appointments, dismissals, betrayals, succession, alliances, diplomacy. ECONOMIC: trade disruptions, embargoes, famine, financial irregularities, commerce. SPIRITUAL: worship failures, Taint, oni manifestation, realm overlaps, rituals. SOCIAL: marriages, duels, performances, gifts, hunts, public conduct, gempuku. PERSONAL: deaths, injuries, travel sightings, companion loss, secret exposure.

57.57.6a Sharing Bias and Valence System

When an NPC selects which topic to share during Daily Conversation (Section 12.6) or Deliberate PC-Initiated Conversation (Section 12.6a), each topic in their known_topics pool receives a sharing_bias score based on the relationship between the topic’s participants and the conversation partner. This is a scoring modifier on topic selection — not a hard gate, just a tilt toward what the NPC would naturally bring up. The sharing_bias formula has three components, all computed from existing data. Component 1 — Subject Relevance: conversation partner’s connection to anyone named in the topic. Same family as a topic subject: +15. Same clan as a topic subject: +10. Personal disposition ≥ Friend or ≤ Rival toward a topic subject: +10. No connection: +0. Clan face protection modifier: if the conversation partner’s clan-mate is tagged NEGATIVE in the topic AND the NPC’s bushido_virtue is Rei, the clan bonus inverts to −10 (the NPC protects clan face by suppressing embarrassing clan news). Component 2 — Personality Match: does the NPC’s bushido_virtue or shourido_virtue match the topic’s category? Check both; if either matches, +5. If both happen to match the same category, still +5 — no stacking. Bushido mappings: Jin → PERSONAL, Yu → MILITARY, Rei → SOCIAL, Chugi → POLITICAL and SPIRITUAL (duty encompasses both governance and spiritual obligations), Gi → POLITICAL, Meiyo → any Honor-relevant topic across categories (duels, disgrace, betrayal, seppuku). Makoto has no category mapping — sincere characters care about truth in all domains equally. Shourido mappings: Dōsatsu → PERSONAL (reading people), Kyōryoku → MILITARY (strength), Kanpeki → SOCIAL (perfection displayed publicly), Seigyo → POLITICAL (political operators), Chishiki → ECONOMIC and SPIRITUAL (knowledge collectors care about information in all forms). Ketsui and Ishi have no category mapping. No match from either virtue: +0. Component 3 — NPC Involvement: the NPC’s personal connection to the event. NPC is tagged POSITIVE in the topic (own achievements): +20 (default), +10 if NPC's bushido_virtue is Rei (modesty suppresses boasting), +30 if NPC's shourido_virtue is Kanpeki (excellence demands recognition). NPC is tagged NEGATIVE in the topic (own shame): −20 (default), −30 if NPC's bushido_virtue is Rei (courtesy makes them suppress shame harder), −10 if NPC's shourido_virtue is Kanpeki (perfection-driven characters rationalize or reframe). NPC’s lord is a topic subject: +15. NPC witnessed the event (acquisition_source = WITNESSED): +10. No involvement: +0. All three components stack. Final sharing_bias is additive with any propagation_urgency the topic carries. For Tier 1–2 topics, propagation_urgency (+50, +40) dominates and sharing_bias is minor. For Tier 3–4 topics, sharing_bias is the primary driver of which topic an NPC selects in conversation. The sharing_bias system applies to all tiers but its practical impact scales inversely with tier — the lower the tier, the more sharing_bias matters. All values PROVISIONAL.

57.57.6b Valence Rules

Each topic_type defines subject_roles — the named roles that participants fill in that topic. At topic creation, the generating system populates subject_valence_map by assigning each participant’s character_id to their role’s valence. POSITIVE roles: achievements, victories, heroic acts, generous acts, successful completions. The participant talks about this freely (+20 involvement). NEGATIVE roles: defeats, disgraces, failures, crimes discovered, shameful acts. The participant actively suppresses this topic (−20 involvement). Some topic_types have both: DUEL_OUTCOME has a POSITIVE winner and a NEGATIVE loser. Some have only one: PROMOTION has a POSITIVE subject only. Some have neither participant valence: ARMY_MOVEMENT is NEUTRAL — it’s information, not pride or shame. The default_valence on the topic_type indicates the overall framing for listeners who have no personal connection to the participants: POSITIVE topics generate mild goodwill toward subjects, NEGATIVE topics generate mild scrutiny, NEUTRAL topics generate no disposition shift. Listener_effects on each topic_type define the specific mechanical consequences for NPCs who learn the topic. These are specified per entry in the Tier catalogs and are limited to three categories: (1) NeedType candidates — what the topic makes available to the Opportunity Scanner (e.g., learning about banditry generates INVESTIGATE_THREAT as a Scanner candidate), (2) universal data-level effects — fire for everyone who learns the topic regardless of personality (e.g., disposition freeze on death, vacancy unlocks, objective reassignment), (3) relationship-based effects — depend on the listener’s relationship to the topic’s subjects, not the listener’s personality virtue (e.g., “if the disgraced character is the listener’s lord: −2 disposition”). Listener_effects do NOT specify per-virtue personality reactions. Personality-mediated responses to topics are handled entirely by the existing Decision Engine: the Personal Relevance Formula (Section 16.4) combines relationship and virtue profile to determine how much each NPC cares. The personality_lean system (Section 55.3.3) determines which ActionIDs the NPC pursues in response. Phase 5 scoring determines the specific action. This separation means the topic catalog provides clean data inputs and the engine handles personality differentiation through systems that are already designed and LOCKED.

57.57.6c Universal Death-Awareness Effects

All death topic_types across all tiers (T4-04 NATURAL_DEATH, T4-05 MISSION_DEATH, T4-06 SUSPICIOUS_DEATH, T4-43 UNSANCTIONED_DUEL_DEATH, T3-08 DEATH_BY_SEPPUKU, T3-09 BATTLE_DEATH, T3-10 OPEN_KILLING, T3-11 COVERT_KILLING_DISCOVERED, T2-07 LORD_DEATH, T2-08 DEATH_BY_ASSASSINATION, T2-09 DEATH_BY_EXECUTION, T2-19 HEROIC_DEATH) share a common set of listener_effects that fire when an NPC learns that another character has died, regardless of cause. Per-entry unique effects are additive on top of this base. Relationship unlocks: if deceased was listener’s lord (lord_id), REPORT_TO_NEW_LORD becomes available per Section 55.33. If deceased was listener’s operational_superior_id, listener knows their superior is gone and can seek new assignment. If deceased was listener’s advisory_lord_id, listener knows their advisory position is cleared. If deceased held a position the listener could fill, COMPETE_FOR_POSITION pipeline (Section 57.51) activates. If listener is lord-tier and the death created a vacancy in their domain, FILL_VACANCY scoring activates. Objective reassignment: if deceased was the target of any active objective on the listener (ELIMINATE_CHARACTER, RAISE_DISPOSITION, LOCATE_CHARACTER, etc.), the objective is marked impossible, cleared, and falls back to standing objective. Social effects: disposition toward the deceased freezes (no further drift). Topic personal relevance scaled by disposition: disposition ≥ Friend adds +15 personal relevance, disposition ≥ Devoted adds +25. If disposition toward deceased was ≥ Friend, PERFORM_WORSHIP generates as a low-priority Opportunity Scanner candidate — a grieving samurai goes to the shrine and prays. No special ancestor veneration variant; fires through the existing worship system. Political calculation: if deceased was a lord, the listener’s Opportunity Scanner evaluates succession implications — new lord may have different disposition, strategic priorities, and alliances. If deceased was from a rival clan and died in suspicious or violent circumstances, the topic feeds into existing threat assessment scoring. All death topic_types reference this shared rule. Per-entry listener_effects in the tier catalogs describe only the unique additions beyond this base. NATURAL_DEATH (T4-04) has no unique additions — standard death-awareness effects only.

57.57.6d Topic Upgrade Mechanism

A topic instance can change its topic_type, tier, template, and subject_valence_map when new information is discovered. The topic retains its topic_instance_id — it is the same topic, evolved. The upgrade happens at the source — the character who discovers the new information (e.g., the investigating magistrate) has their topic instance upgraded. The upgraded version then propagates through normal information channels (conversation, letters) with fresh propagation_urgency because it is new information. Characters in distant provinces who hold the old version do not magically learn the upgrade. They continue holding the old version until someone tells them. When a character who holds the old version receives the upgraded version through conversation or a letter, the matching topic_instance_id triggers replacement — the new version overwrites the old. Characters who never hear the upgrade keep the old version until it decays. Scoring weight resets to full on upgrade — the new information is fresh news. Decay rate changes to the new tier’s rate. Confirmed upgrade paths: T4-06 SUSPICIOUS_DEATH upgrades to T3-11 COVERT_KILLING_DISCOVERED when investigation attributes the death (template updates to name the perpetrator, subject_valence_map adds perpetrator as NEGATIVE, tier changes 4 → 3). T4-06 SUSPICIOUS_DEATH can also resolve to T4-04 NATURAL_DEATH if investigation confirms natural causes (scoring weight decays normally from that point). Other potential upgrade paths (BANDITRY → CRIMINAL_ORGANIZATION, PROVINCIAL_RAID → BORDER_RAID) are escalation events that generate NEW topics at higher tiers rather than upgrading existing instances — the escalation is a new event, not new information about the same event. The upgrade mechanism is reserved for cases where the underlying event is the same but understanding of it changes.

57.57.6e One-Topic-Per-Event Rule

When a single event would generate multiple topic_types, exactly one fires — the most specific. This prevents double-counting and ensures each event produces one clean topic for the social network to propagate. Established applications: One-topic-per-death (Section 57.57.9): assassination supersedes lord death, execution supersedes lord death, heroic death supersedes lord death, etc. One-topic-per-promotion: NEW_APPOINTMENT supersedes PROMOTION when a structural authority change includes a Status increase. One-topic-per-appointment: SHOGUNATE_CREATION (T1-10) supersedes IMPERIAL_APPOINTMENT. MERCHANT_PATRON_APPOINTED supersedes CLAN_APPOINTMENT for shōnin-kata. IMPERIAL_APPOINTMENT supersedes CLAN_APPOINTMENT supersedes NEW_APPOINTMENT based on position significance. Same hierarchy applies to dismissals. The generating system selects the most specific applicable topic_type at the moment of generation. If no specific type applies, the catch-all fires (NATURAL_DEATH for deaths, NEW_APPOINTMENT for appointments, DISMISSAL for dismissals).

57.57.7 Performance Topic Correction

RECONCILIATION NOTE: Section 57.33 states a masterful performance (2+ Raises) generates a Tier 1 topic. This is reclassified: a performance with 3+ Raises generates a Tier 2 topic (SOCIAL, EVENT, decay 4 IC seasons). A standard success or a performance with 1–2 Raises generates a Tier 3 topic (SOCIAL, EVENT, decay 2 IC seasons). The original “Tier 1” reflected “best possible outcome” in a local success/failure scale, not the crisis tier system. A good dance is not an existential threat. The 3+ Raise threshold marks a genuinely extraordinary performance. Section 57.33 text is superseded by this catalog for tier assignment.
