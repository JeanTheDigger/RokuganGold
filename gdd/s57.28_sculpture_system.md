## 57.28 Sculpture System

### 57.28.1 Overview

Sculpture in Rokugan is a relatively minor art. The demand is limited. Temples, shrines, palaces, and tombs are highly decorated with statues of Fortunes and guardian spirits, but private homes prefer unshaped rocks in their gardens, and Rokugani architecture makes houses more congenial to paintings than sculptures. Sculpture&#x2019;s mechanical identity is therefore narrow and specific: it is the art of spiritual infrastructure. Where painting fills interior zone slots and affects the social and political environment, sculpture fills shrine and temple zone slots and affects the spiritual environment &#x2014; worship enhancement, spiritual defense against improper realm overlap, and devotional presence.

Three formats exist. Religious statuary (butsuzo) &#x2014; Fortune, kami, and guardian spirit statues for shrine and temple interiors. Guardian statues (komainu) &#x2014; paired protective figures at shrine and temple entrances. Figurines &#x2014; small carved objects for the gift economy, strongly associated with the Mantis Clan. No format competes with painting for interior court display or with garden/bonsai for exterior aesthetic display. Sculpture occupies its own zone slots at shrine and temple zones only.

Distinction &#x2014; Asahina Fire Sculptors (Section 15955): the Asahina school technique creates ephemeral sculptures of fire, heat, and light through expended spell slots. These are live performances experienced in the moment, not physical objects. Fire sculpture qualifies as a court performance (Glory award on success) but produces no persistent world object, no zone slot entry, and no gift item. The Asahina technique and the COMPOSE_SCULPTURE system are mechanically unrelated &#x2014; one creates transient beauty, the other creates lasting spiritual infrastructure.

Distinction &#x2014; Netsuke: netsuke are heimin craft, not samurai artisan work. They use Craft: Sculpture, not Artisan: Sculpture. A samurai who sponsors a netsuke workshop benefits economically through Commerce, not artistically through Artisan skill. No dedicated netsuke mechanic exists in this section.

### 57.28.2 Sculpture World Object

Each sculpture tracks the following fields: sculpture_id, format (statuary, guardian, figurine), creator_id, quality_tier (Normal through Legendary), material (wood, stone, bronze), subject_type (fortune, guardian_spirit, kami, portrait, animal, scenic), subject_id (fortune_id, character_id, creature_type, or null), subject_description (text &#x2014; narrative description), date_completed, craft_progress (int or null), display_zone_id (null or zone_id), display_slot (statue_slot, guardian_slot, or null), paired (boolean &#x2014; true for guardian statues, false otherwise), pair_intact (boolean &#x2014; true when both statues of a guardian pair exist, false when one is destroyed). Materials, provenance, and history follow standard Section 49 rules. Figurines additionally track: theme (sea_fortune, sea_animal, person, sailing, other &#x2014; primarily for Mantis cultural weighting).

### 57.28.3 Zone Slots &#x2014; Shrine and Temple Only

Two new zone slots, available only at shrine and temple zones: statue_slot &#x2014; holds one religious statuary piece inside the shrine or temple. The devotional centre of the space. One statue per zone. Generates passive Worship Points and enhances PERFORM_WORSHIP. Coexists with the wall_art_slot (religious paintings can hang alongside the statue) &#x2014; painting and sculpture serve complementary spiritual functions. guardian_slot &#x2014; holds one guardian statue pair at the shrine or temple entrance or gate zone. Provides a spiritual ward against improper realm overlap. One pair per zone. The guardian_slot is at the entrance/gate, distinct from the statue_slot inside &#x2014; they are not competing for the same physical space.

Neither slot is available at castles, courts, private homes, or secular zones. Sculpture is a temple art, not a court art. Lords who want to decorate their halls commission paintings (Section 57.27), not statues.

### 57.28.4 COMPOSE_SCULPTURE

ActionID: COMPOSE_SCULPTURE. Category 11 (Personal Development). All three formats use this ActionID with format-specific parameters. Roll: Artisan: Sculpture / Awareness vs TN 15 per AP spent. Progress per AP: roll result minus 15, minimum 1 on success. Declared Raises add +5 progress per Raise achieved. On failure: 0 progress, AP spent. Context: any for figurines. AT_ZONE at a shrine/temple for statuary and guardian statues &#x2014; the sculptor must work on site because the finished piece is too large and heavy to transport. Skill gate: Artisan: Sculpture rank must equal or exceed the target quality tier at completion.

Declaration at start (cannot change mid-composition): format, material, subject_type, subject_id (if applicable), target quality tier. Declaration is 0 AP.

Progress thresholds by format and quality: Religious statuary &#x2014; Normal = 20 (~7 AP), Fine = 40 (~14 AP), Exceptional = 65 (~22 AP), Masterwork = 95 (~32 AP), Legendary = 130 (~43 AP). These are long &#x2014; carving a life-sized wooden Fortune statue is months of dedicated work. Guardian statues (pair) &#x2014; Normal = 25 (~8 AP), Fine = 50 (~17 AP), Exceptional = 80 (~27 AP), Masterwork = 110 (~37 AP), Legendary = 150 (~50 AP). The longest progress track in the artisan system &#x2014; carving a pair of stone guardian figures is an enormous undertaking. Figurines &#x2014; Normal = 5 (~2 AP), Fine = 10 (~4 AP), Exceptional = 18 (~6 AP), Masterwork = 28 (~10 AP), Legendary = 40 (~14 AP). Same as kakemono &#x2014; these are small objects.

Composition degradation: applies to religious statuary and guardian statues. If 1 full IC season (90 IC days) passes without COMPOSE_SCULPTURE AP spent, accumulated progress halves. Same pattern as by&#x014D;bu and emakimono (Section 57.27.5). Does not apply to figurines (short enough to resume without losing coherence).

Material modifiers: wood (Common) = standard. Stone (Common but heavy) = +5 TN on all COMPOSE_SCULPTURE rolls (stone is harder to work than wood). Bronze (Uncommon, requires access to a foundry) = +1 Free Raise on all rolls (bronze is cast, not carved &#x2014; a skilled sculptor can achieve finer detail through the casting process), but requires the zone to have a foundry or metalworking facility within the same province.

Raises at completion: same as Section 49 standard. Quality tier upgrades one step per Raise, capped at Legendary.

### 57.28.5 Placement Permissions

Shrine and temple zones require permission from the shrine custodian (the shugenja or monk responsible for the site) before a sculptor may place a statue or begin work on guardian statues. Two permission types: statue_placement_permission (for statuary) and guardian_placement_permission (for guardian pairs). Granting follows the same pattern as painting permissions (Section 57.27.4a) &#x2014; direct consent, commission acceptance, or OFFER_ART_COMMISSION. Permission persists until revoked. Lordship change grace period: 1 IC season. The shrine custodian is the permission authority, not the provincial daimyo &#x2014; though a daimyo who controls the province may pressure a shrine custodian, they do not have direct authority over temple art. This reflects the semi-independent status of religious institutions in Rokugan.

### 57.28.6 Format: Religious Statuary (Butsuzo)

Fortune, kami, bodhisattva, and guardian spirit statues placed inside shrine and temple zones. Occupies the zone&#x2019;s statue_slot. Materials: wood (most common &#x2014; hinoki cypress using the joined-block technique), stone, or bronze. Multi-AP composition via COMPOSE_SCULPTURE at AT_ZONE. The sculptor must be physically present at the shrine or temple for every AP spent.

Passive Worship Points: a religious statue generates passive WP each IC season directed at the fortune_id in the sculpture’s subject_id. Passive WP by quality: Normal = +0.1, Fine = +0.25, Exceptional = +0.5, Masterwork = +0.75, Legendary = +1.0 per season. These are roughly double the religious painting values (Section 57.27.12) because the sculptural medium is spiritually weightier — a carved wooden Fortune is a more potent devotional object than a painted image of the same Fortune. A shrine with both a Masterwork statue (+0.75) and an Exceptional painting (+0.25) generates +1.0 passive WP per season before any active worship occurs. This covers a meaningful fraction of a Fortune’s worship needs but not the majority — active PERFORM_WORSHIP by a shugenja remains essential. Art is a semi-requirement for healthy shrine maintenance: a shrine with no art infrastructure must rely entirely on active worship, while a well-equipped shrine has a comfortable baseline. PROVISIONAL: all passive WP values pending worship threshold calibration.

Worship enhancement: characters performing PERFORM_WORSHIP at the same zone targeting the same Fortune depicted by the statue receive quality-scaled Free Raises. Normal = +0, Fine = +1, Exceptional = +1, Masterwork = +2, Legendary = +2. This stacks with: religious painting Fortune focus bonus (+1 flat, Section 57.27.11), shide Free Raises (quality-scaled, Section 57.26.10), gohei Free Raises (quality-scaled, Section 57.26.13), guardian statue worship enhancement (quality-scaled, Section 57.28.7), and ikebana shrine offering (quality-scaled, Section 57.29.9). Worship enhancement ceiling: maximum effective Free Raises on PERFORM_WORSHIP from all external artisan sources combined is 5. The first 4 Free Raises apply at full value. The 5th Free Raise applies at full value. Any Free Raises beyond 5 are wasted — the shugenja’s spiritual focus cannot absorb more enhancement than this regardless of how much infrastructure surrounds them. This means the theoretical +8 from six fully stacked sources is capped at +5 effective. A shrine with +6 worth of sources has +1 wasted capacity — one source could be removed without loss. The ceiling creates diminishing returns on over-investment: the first few artisan installations at a shrine are valuable, but after the 5th effective Free Raise, additional infrastructure provides passive WP and visitor effects but no further worship enhancement. PROVISIONAL: ceiling value of 5 pending worship system calibration.

Fortune mismatch: same rule as religious paintings (Section 57.27.12). Passive WP is directed at the depicted Fortune, not the shrine&#x2019;s primary dedication. Worship enhancement fires only when the worship target matches the depicted Fortune. A statue of Daikoku at an Inari shrine generates WP for Daikoku and enhances worship of Daikoku, not Inari.

Durability: religious statuary is far more durable than paintings. Wood statues last indefinitely indoors (no degradation). Stone and bronze statues are effectively permanent. A statue placed at a shrine continues generating passive WP and worship enhancement for decades or centuries without maintenance &#x2014; unlike shide (seasonal degradation) or gohei (use-count degradation), statuary is a one-time investment with permanent return. This is the core economic advantage of sculpture over the consumable origami spiritual tools.

### 57.28.7 Format: Guardian Statues (Komainu)

Paired stone or wood figures placed at shrine and temple entrance or gate zones. Occupies the zone&#x2019;s guardian_slot. Always created as a pair &#x2014; the a-gy&#x014D; (open-mouthed, beginning of all things) and un-gy&#x014D; (closed-mouthed, end of all things). Together they form the a-un ward. A single statue has no effect; the pair_intact field must be true for the spiritual ward to function.

Subject varies by shrine type: komainu (lion-dogs) for standard shrines, kitsune (fox guardians) for Inari-equivalent shrines, dragon guardians for water/sea shrines, ox guardians for Tenjin-equivalent shrines. Subject is cosmetic &#x2014; it matches the shrine&#x2019;s deity and affects the sculpture&#x2019;s ASCII representation but does not alter the mechanical ward effect.

Materials: stone (the standard outdoor material &#x2014; weathers rain and snow), wood (cheaper but degrades outdoors &#x2014; loses one quality tier per 5 IC years of outdoor exposure, minimum Normal), bronze (expensive but permanent like stone). Stone and bronze guardian statues are effectively permanent. Wood guardians require eventual replacement.

Spiritual ward: while the guardian pair is intact and displayed at a shrine/temple entrance zone, the ward provides a passive modifier against improper spirit realm overlap at the zone. When non-Jigoku realm overlap progression ticks fire at this zone (worship failure &#x2192; realm bleed, per Section 2.5), the guardian statues subtract a quality-scaled value from the overlap progress: Normal = &#x2212;1, Fine = &#x2212;2, Exceptional = &#x2212;3, Masterwork = &#x2212;4, Legendary = &#x2212;5 per tick. This slows the bleed but does not stop it &#x2014; if worship fails completely, even Legendary komainu will eventually be overwhelmed. The statues buy time for the shugenja to address the worship deficit. The ward is a buffer, not a cure.

Jigoku exclusion: the spiritual ward has NO effect on Taint bleed, Jigoku corruption, or Shadowlands-sourced spiritual intrusion. Jigoku is fundamentally different from the other spirit realms &#x2014; it is a corruption of the Celestial Order, not a natural realm that occasionally overlaps. Guardian statues maintain proper boundaries between Ningen-do and the legitimate spirit realms (Gaki-do, Toshigoku, Sakkaku, Chikushudo, Yume-do, Meido). Combating Jigoku requires jade, prayers, and shugenja &#x2014; not stone lions. This follows the global design principle: jade is anti-Jigoku ONLY, and correspondingly, Celestial Order infrastructure (guardian statues, worship, shrine maintenance) addresses everything EXCEPT Jigoku.

Worship enhancement: guardian statues at the entrance of a shrine also contribute Free Raises to PERFORM_WORSHIP performed anywhere in the same shrine complex. A shrine complex is defined as all zones sharing the same parent shrine or temple identifier in the zone hierarchy (Section 56) — typically encompassing the entrance/gate zone, the outer courtyard, the inner sanctum, and any auxiliary buildings. The guardian pair’s ward protects the complex boundary; its worship enhancement extends inward from that boundary. If the zone system does not yet define shrine clustering, this enhancement fires at any zone within the same Lesser Zone as the guardian_slot zone. Quality scaling: Normal = +0, Fine = +0, Exceptional = +1, Masterwork = +1, Legendary = +2. This stacks with statue_slot statuary, religious paintings, shide, and gohei.

Pair destruction: if one statue of the pair is destroyed (siege, vandalism, earthquake), pair_intact becomes false. The spiritual ward ceases immediately. The surviving statue remains in the guardian_slot as a damaged pair &#x2014; it occupies the slot but provides no effect. The sculptor or a replacement sculptor must COMPOSE_SCULPTURE a replacement statue (half the original pair threshold, since only one needs to be made) and install it to restore the pair. The replacement inherits the surviving statue&#x2019;s quality tier if the replacement roll matches or exceeds it; if the replacement is lower quality, the pair functions at the lower tier. Topic: &#x201C;A guardian statue at [shrine name] has been damaged.&#x201D; Tier 3 for Exceptional+, Tier 4 for Fine and below.

### 57.28.8 Format: Figurines

Small carved objects in wood, bone, or shell. The portable gift format for Sculpture. Materials: wood (Common), bone (Common), shell (Common in coastal provinces, Uncommon inland). Short progress track &#x2014; same thresholds as kakemono (see Section 57.28.4 for values). Context: any &#x2014; figurines are small enough to carve anywhere. No zone slot &#x2014; figurines are inventory items that enter the gift economy through DELIVER_GIFT.

Subject types: fortune (small devotional figure), animal (sea creatures, birds, horses), person (portrait figure of a named character), scenic (a moment captured in miniature &#x2014; a wave, a mountain, a ship). Theme field tracks the figurine&#x2019;s cultural category for Mantis preference weighting: sea_fortune, sea_animal, person, sailing, other.

Mantis cultural weighting: the Mantis Clan has a strong figurine tradition rooted in the Yoritomo sailors’ wood-carving during long voyages. Mantis NPC lords and courtiers have elevated disposition modifiers when receiving figurine gifts (+3 above standard quality-based Free Raises on DELIVER_GIFT, applied when the recipient is Mantis Clan). Figurine collection topic: once per IC season during the standard court evaluation pass, the engine checks each Mantis lord’s personal inventory while AT_COURT. If the inventory contains 3+ figurines sharing the same creator_id OR the same theme field, a Tier 5 cultural topic fires: “[Lord name]’s figurine collection at [zone name] is admired.” The topic fires once per season per qualifying cluster — if the lord has 3 sea_animal figurines and 3 figurines by the same artist, both clusters qualify and the topic fires once for the most prominent cluster (highest quality average). This is a passive emergence from normal gift-economy behavior, not a tracked collection system — the engine counts inventory items with matching fields rather than maintaining a separate collection record. Yoritomo Sculptors school (Section 17807) characters receive their +1k1 technique bonus on all COMPOSE_SCULPTURE rolls for figurines.

### 57.28.9 Visitor Effects

Religious statuary in the statue_slot generates visitor disposition bonuses toward the sculptor, same pattern as painting (Section 57.27.10). Quality scaling: Normal = +1, Fine = +2, Exceptional = +3, Masterwork = +4, Legendary = +5. Duration: 4 IC months. Null/deceased creator handling: same rule as painting (Section 57.27.10a). Glory ticks per 5 visitors, same as painting. Guardian statues in the guardian_slot also generate visitor disposition bonuses toward the sculptor at the same quality scaling &#x2014; visitors entering through the gate notice the craftsmanship. Both slots fire independently, same as the three painting slots.

### 57.28.10 Commission Pipeline

Religious statuary and guardian statues are commissioned by shrine custodians and temple priests, not by provincial daimyo. The commissioning authority is the character holding operational responsibility for the shrine or temple &#x2014; typically a shugenja or Brotherhood monk assigned to the site through the operational_superior_id chain (Section 11.3.18). REQUEST_ART (art_type: statuary or guardian, subject_type, fortune_id, zone_id) fires when the statue_slot or guardian_slot is empty and the custodian has sufficient cultural motivation. Cultural motivation weighting: Phoenix (highest &#x2014; temple maintenance is a Phoenix cultural priority), Crab (moderate &#x2014; guardian statues at fortified shrine-temples along the Kaiu Wall), Lion (moderate &#x2014; ancestral shrines), Dragon, Crane (low &#x2014; prefer painting), Scorpion, Unicorn (lowest). PROVISIONAL.

Figurines are commissioned through standard OFFER_ART_COMMISSION or received unsolicited through DELIVER_GIFT. Mantis lords generate REQUEST_ART (art_type: figurine) when they hold fewer than 3 figurines in inventory and their cultural interest weight exceeds threshold. This is a low-priority commission that competes normally in Phase 5.

ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE: a shrine custodian who holds operational authority over a sculptor vassal may order them to produce statuary or guardian pairs. Delivery windows: figurine = 1 IC season, statuary = 3 IC seasons, guardian pair = 4 IC seasons. Same obligation mechanics as painting commissions (Section 57.27.14).

### 57.28.11 NPC Engine &#x2014; NeedType Decomposition

ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION (artisan-side): extended for COMPOSE_SCULPTURE. When an NPC with Artisan: Sculpture rank 1+ has no active composition and meets trigger conditions, COMPOSE_SCULPTURE enters the Phase 5 candidate pool. Format selection: AT_ZONE at a shrine/temple with empty statue_slot &#x2192; statuary (score 55). AT_ZONE at a shrine/temple entrance with empty guardian_slot &#x2192; guardian pair (score 55). Default or not at shrine &#x2192; figurine (score 50). Subject selection: for statuary and guardians, the shrine&#x2019;s primary Fortune/deity determines the subject. For figurines, same disposition-driven logic as Theater/Painting &#x2014; strongest opinion, framing matches direction. Mantis NPC sculptors default to sea themes.

MAINTAIN_SHRINE (shrine custodian NeedType &#x2014; parallel to MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD for daimyo): fires when a shrine/temple zone has an empty statue_slot or guardian_slot and the custodian has spiritual motivation. Personality weights: Theology rank &#xD7; 10, Phoenix/Dragon clan +15, Brotherhood monk +20. Decomposes into REQUEST_ART (statuary or guardian) at score 55. Lower urgency than painting&#x2019;s MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD because sculpture is a slower, heavier investment &#x2014; a shrine custodian commissions a statue once in their career, not seasonally. PROVISIONAL.

Post-completion decision: statuary and guardian pairs &#x2014; the sculptor is already AT_ZONE (required for composition). Statue automatically occupies the slot on completion. No DISPLAY equivalent needed. Figurines &#x2014; same three-path evaluation as painting (Section 57.27.18): commissioned delivery (score 90), self-initiated gift (score 55, +10 if recipient is Mantis), hold for future opportunity (score 40).

Annex A entries: ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION &#x2014; COMPOSE_SCULPTURE (statuary): 55, COMPOSE_SCULPTURE (guardian): 55, COMPOSE_SCULPTURE (figurine): 50. MAINTAIN_SHRINE &#x2014; REQUEST_ART (statuary): 55, REQUEST_ART (guardian): 55. RAISE_DISPOSITION &#x2014; figurine commission acceptance: 60, DELIVER_GIFT (figurine, Mantis recipient): 65. PROVISIONAL.

### 57.28.12 World Generation

Religious statuary: major shrines and temples are seeded with statuary at Fine to Masterwork quality. The Seven Great Fortunes&#x2019; primary shrines each have Masterwork or Legendary statues. Provincial shrines: Fine to Exceptional. Village shrines: Normal to Fine or empty (40% probability). Creator_id: historical unnamed sculptor (null) for ancient works. Phoenix temple zones seed at one tier higher than baseline.

Guardian statues: major shrines and temple complexes are seeded with guardian pairs at Fine to Exceptional quality. Provincial shrines: 60% probability of Normal to Fine guardian pairs. Village shrines: 30% probability. Material: stone for outdoor, wood for indoor. Creator_id: null for world-gen pairs.

Figurines: Mantis lords&#x2019; inventories are seeded with 1&#x2013;4 figurines at Normal to Fine quality. Other clans: no figurine seeding. Sculptor NPCs: Phoenix territories seed at least one NPC with Artisan: Sculpture rank 2+ per major temple province. Mantis territories seed Yoritomo Sculptor school NPCs. Crab territories seed one sculptor per Kaiu Wall shrine. Other clans: rare, 1 per 3&#x2013;4 provinces.

### 57.28.13 Lifecycle Topics

Sculpture completion: &#x201C;[Creator name] has completed a [quality tier] [material] statue of [subject] at [zone name].&#x201D; Tier by quality: Normal/Fine = Tier 4, Exceptional/Masterwork = Tier 3, Legendary = Tier 2. Three-ring propagation. Guardian pair completion: same format with &#x201C;guardian pair&#x201D; replacing &#x201C;statue.&#x201D; Figurine completion: &#x201C;[Creator name] has completed a [quality tier] figurine depicting [subject].&#x201D; Tier 4 always (figurines are minor). Guardian pair damage: &#x201C;A guardian statue at [shrine name] has been damaged.&#x201D; Tier 3 for Exceptional+, Tier 4 for Fine and below. Statue destruction (siege/natural disaster): &#x201C;[Creator name]&#x2019;s [quality tier] statue of [subject] at [zone name] has been destroyed.&#x201D; Tier 3 for Exceptional+. Creator deceased mid-composition: same pattern as painting (Section 57.27.21) &#x2014; craft_progress set to null, abandoned_incomplete: true.

### 57.28.14 Provenance Investigation

Standard Section 49 provenance rules apply. Layer 1 (surface): format, approximate quality, subject, material. Layer 2 (deep): creator identity, creation date, material origins. Creator identification: Lore: History or Artisan: Sculpture / Awareness vs TN by creator fame. Same TN scale as painting (Section 57.27.22). Artisan: Sculpture rank 2+ grants 1 Free Raise. Sculptures are generally easier to attribute than paintings because the sculptor&#x2019;s technique (carving style, tool marks, proportional choices) is more physically distinctive than brushwork &#x2014; reduce all creator identification TNs by 5 for sculptures compared to paintings.

### 57.28.15 Cultural Interest and Clan Weighting

Phoenix: highest engagement with religious statuary. Shiba Artisans and Phoenix temple custodians are the primary commissioners and creators of shrine statuary. Mantis: highest engagement with figurines. Yoritomo Sculptors school provides the technique bonus. Mantis courts actively seek figurines. Crab: moderate &#x2014; guardian statues at fortified shrines along the Kaiu Wall. The Crab value practical spiritual defense. Lion: moderate &#x2014; ancestral shrines maintained with quality statuary. Dragon: low-moderate &#x2014; mountain temple tradition, monk-sculptors. Crane: low &#x2014; the Crane prefer painting in all contexts. A Crane court will have paintings, not statues. Scorpion, Unicorn: baseline &#x2014; sculpture when practically needed, not proactively sought. PROVISIONAL.

### 57.28.16 Sculpture Emphases

Artisan: Sculpture emphases grant +1k0 on COMPOSE_SCULPTURE rolls when working in the relevant format or material. Four emphases defined. Format emphases: Statuary (religious statuary composition), Guardian (komainu pair composition), Figurine (small decorative objects). Material emphasis: Stone Carving (any sculpture in stone — the +1k0 partially compensates for the +5 TN stone penalty, improving the sculptor’s odds without fully negating the difficulty of stone. A sculptor with both Stone Carving and a format emphasis working in stone receives +2k0 total, which more than compensates for the +5 TN at moderate skill levels). Emphasis acquisition follows standard rules (Section 24).

### 57.28.17 Siege and Destruction

Religious statuary: on zone capture, the statue persists &#x2014; conquerors do not typically destroy shrine statuary (doing so is a spiritual offense). On zone sacking: quality-scaled survival. Wood: Normal 30%, Fine 40%, Exceptional 50%, Masterwork 60%, Legendary 70%. Stone: +20% to all survival rates. Bronze: +30%. Stone and bronze are far more likely to survive sacking than wood. On zone destruction: wood statues destroyed, stone statues 50% survival, bronze 70%. Destroyed statues generate provenance records with destruction_cause.

Guardian statues: on zone capture, persist (same as religious statuary). On sacking: one or both statues may be destroyed independently using the same material-scaled survival rates. If one is destroyed, pair_intact becomes false and the spiritual ward fails. On zone destruction: same logic. Stone and bronze guardians are remarkably persistent &#x2014; a stone komainu pair can survive the destruction of the shrine they protected.

Figurines: inventory items &#x2014; follow their owner. Same as emakimono in painting (Section 57.27.25).
