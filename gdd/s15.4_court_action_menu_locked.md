## 15.4 Court Action Menu — LOCKED

Each character has 2 Action Points per in-game day (8 per real-world day) to spend at court. Action Points are split into Morning and Afternoon slots. Actions fall into five categories. Actions marked with [OBJECTIVE TAG] are checked against the character's declared court objective — if the action contradicts the objective, its effect is reduced. Actions without the tag resolve normally with no objective check. The objective is a directional goal, not a specific prescription — most tactical actions serve the objective's direction even if indirect or aggressive. The malus only triggers when an action directly contradicts the declared goal.

**Objective Alignment System — LOCKED:**

- Every character declares a court objective at the start of a session — the outcome they want on the main topic. This is stored by the game system.

- Objective-tagged actions are evaluated: does this action serve the declared objective's direction? If yes — full effect. If it directly contradicts — reduced effect (malus).

- Changing your declared objective mid-court is a detectable action — intelligence-gathering characters may notice the shift.

- Actions with no objective tag are never penalized regardless of objective.

**Category 1 — Raise Disposition with One Person (Targeted):**

Personal, reliable, and intimate. The safest disposition action. Effect is narrow but dependable. Disposition threshold modifiers apply to all actions in this category.

**Direct Conversation (1 AP):**

The character approaches a specific person and engages them in a 1-on-1 exchange. Not necessarily behind closed doors — this can happen in the open court, in a garden, in a corridor, or in a private room. The environment affects observability and political signal.

- Open court: The conversation can be overheard. Intelligence-gathering characters nearby may pick up information. No political signal raised by the meeting itself.

- Semi-private (corridor, garden): Modest discretion. Harder to intercept but not invisible. Moderate political signal.

- Private (closed room): Fully protected from eavesdropping. The act of seeking a private meeting is itself noticeable — others may wonder what required such discretion. Strong political signal.

The player chooses their approach within the conversation. Each approach uses a different Ring and Skill and is contested by the target's defense. Six approaches covering Air, Earth, Fire, and Water — Void is intentionally absent from direct conversation, fitting its transcendent nature:

**1. Negotiate — Awareness + Courtier vs Awareness + Courtier:**

Seeking common ground, moving toward a shared position. Rational rather than emotional.

- Success: Moderate disposition gain. If the conversation touched a court topic, their position moves slightly toward yours — an opening, not a conversion. Future Negotiate actions with this person this session have slightly lower TN.

- Failure: No disposition change. Their position on any touched topic hardens slightly — they've heard your argument and weren't moved.

- Critical failure: Small disposition loss. Their position hardens significantly. The negotiation broke down badly.

**2. Persuade — Awareness + Sincerity vs Willpower + Sincerity:**

Appealing to their values, sense of duty, or personal interests. More emotional and personal than Negotiate. Higher risk — misreading what they value causes the attempt to fail completely or backfire.

- Success: Strong disposition gain — stronger than Negotiate. If touching a court topic, position moves more significantly and durably than Negotiate. A position moved by Persuasion is harder to move back.

- Failure: No disposition change, no position movement. You simply misjudged what appeals to them.

- Critical failure: You appealed to something they find offensive — a Lion courtier arguing self-preservation to a Matsu, for example. Disposition loss. Their position may harden in opposition.

**3. Intimidate — Willpower + Intimidation (contested) vs target’s Willpower + Honor Rank (flat bonus):**

Applying pressure — veiled or direct. Produces compliance rather than genuine warmth. The most complex outcome of the six approaches. Hostile action authenticity modifier applies.

- Success: They comply — position on a touched topic moves toward yours. However genuine disposition does NOT increase, or increases only minimally. Compliance and genuine goodwill are tracked separately.

- Failure: No compliance, no position movement. Small disposition loss — they resent the attempt.

- Critical failure: They refuse openly. Significant disposition loss. If your disposition toward them is high (Friend or above), critical failure is more likely — intimidating someone you genuinely care about rings hollow.

✓ DONE — Intimidate compliance/pressure tracker fully designed in Section 12.9. Compliance is tracked separately from genuine topic position. Three intimidation contexts: Blackmail (secret leverage, Private), Private Intimidation (no secret, in person or letter), Public Intimidation (court, 1 AP). Compliance ends when pressure condition expires, leverage is removed, target resists, or actor’s disposition crosses into Friend range. Full system in Section 12.9.

**4. Charm — Awareness + Etiquette vs Awareness + Etiquette:**

Pure social grace, making yourself agreeable and pleasant. The cleanest and most reliable disposition gain — but the shallowest. Has a ceiling and diminishing returns.

- Success: Clean disposition gain. No position movement on any topic — Charm changes feelings, not minds. Particularly effective against Strangers and Acquaintances.

- Failure: No disposition change. The charm simply didn't land.

- Critical failure: The attempt reads as insincere or forced. Small disposition loss. More likely against those with very negative disposition toward you — forced warmth is hard to fake.

- Ceiling: Charm cannot push disposition above +40 (Friend tier) with any single character. Deeper thresholds require a different approach at least once.

- Diminishing returns: Each successive successful Charm with the same character in the same court session yields less gain — full, then half, then minimal. Resets between sessions.

**5. Impress — Intelligence + Lore (relevant) vs Awareness + Etiquette:**

Demonstrating knowledge of the target's world — their clan history, family struggles, personal reputation, current events relevant to them. Signals both intelligence and respect. Disarming in a way pure Charm is not — it says 'I see you specifically.'

- Success: Moderate disposition gain. Target is receptive — slight TN reduction on your next action toward them this session.

- Failure: Nothing. You simply didn't know enough to make an impression.

- Critical failure: You got something wrong — misidentified an ancestor, confused their family history. Small disposition loss. Embarrassing.

**6. Listen and Reflect — Perception + Investigation vs Awareness + Sincerity:**

You let the other person talk. You ask the right questions. You demonstrate genuine understanding of their perspective before offering anything in return. In a court full of people trying to be heard, genuine attentive listening is surprisingly powerful.

- Success: Strong disposition gain. The target feels genuinely understood — rare in court. Opens them to future Persuade or Negotiate actions with a TN reduction this session.

- Failure: Nothing — you listened but didn't connect.

- Critical failure: Your questions revealed your own agenda too clearly. Small disposition loss — they feel manipulated rather than heard.

- Objective tag note: No Direct Conversation approach carries an objective tag by default. Exception: if the conversation explicitly attempts to move the target's opinion on a court topic, the [OBJECTIVE TAG] applies to that portion of the exchange only.

**Deliver Gift (1 AP):**

Present a gift to a specific character. A masterful courtier presenting a modest gift can outshine a clumsy lord presenting a priceless one — the presentation matters as much as the object itself.

- Roll: Awareness + Etiquette vs TN 15. The roll determines how gracefully and meaningfully the gift is offered.

- Gift quality contributes Free Raises to the roll — see Section 49 for full quality tier values. Normal: 0 Free Raises. Fine: +1. Exceptional: +2. Masterwork: +3. Legendary: +4. History Point bonuses stack on top of base quality (Section 49).

- Success: Full disposition gain from both gift quality and presentation. Social obligation created — the recipient owes goodwill or a future favor, tracked by the game system.

- Failure: Partial disposition gain from gift quality alone. The presentation was awkward or graceless — the object is appreciated but the moment was not.

- Critical failure: The presentation undermined the gift entirely. Small disposition loss regardless of gift quality — a priceless gift offered clumsily or inappropriately causes offense rather than goodwill.

- Appropriate gifts vary by recipient — a warrior values a fine weapon, a courtier values art or rare paper, a shugenja values ritual objects. Inappropriate gifts reduce the Free Raises from quality or remove them entirely.

- No objective tag.

**Offer a Favor (1 AP):**

Explicitly offer something of value — information, political support, a future action, access to resources. A promise made in court carries weight.

- Roll: Awareness + Sincerity vs target's Perception + Sincerity. The target evaluates whether you are credible and whether the offer serves their interests.

- Success: disposition raise and a tracked favor debt recorded in the game system. The favor must eventually be honored or the disposition gain reverses and becomes a loss.

- Failure: target does not believe your sincerity or does not value the offer. No effect.

- No objective tag.

**Perform Personally For (1 AP):**

Direct a personal performance — tea ceremony, poem, music, ikebana, calligraphy — specifically at one person rather than the whole court. More intimate than a public performance.

- Roll: relevant Artisan or Performance skill + Awareness vs TN 15.

- Success: strong disposition gain with the specific recipient. A masterful success also raises your Honor/Glory modestly.

- Failure: awkward. Small disposition loss — the performance fell flat in an intimate setting.

- No objective tag.

**Play a Game (1 AP):**

Invite another character to play a game — Go, Shogi, Kemari, Sadane, Fortunes & Winds, Letters, or any other Games sub-skill present on both characters’ sheets. Playing a game together is recreation, not political manoeuvring. The purpose is shared enjoyment and the small disposition bonus that comes from spending pleasant time together.

- Requirements: both participants in the same zone, both willing, and both possess the named Games sub-skill at rank 1+. The inviter specifies which game — if the invitee does not have that specific sub-skill, they cannot accept and the action cannot fire. There is no option to play a game one participant does not know.

- Roll: Contested Games (named sub-skill) / appropriate Trait. Trait pairing follows the sub-skill: Go and Shogi use Intelligence; Kemari uses Agility; Fortunes & Winds, Letters, Sadane, and most other sub-skills use Awareness. Both participants roll; higher total wins the game.

- Both participants: +3 disposition toward each other. Duration: 2 IC months (standard temporary modifier per Section 12.2). Fires regardless of who wins — the game was a pleasant shared experience. This is the primary point of the action.

- Winner bonus: the loser grants the winner an additional +1 disposition (respect for skill). Total for the loser toward the winner: +4. Total for the winner toward the loser: +3. On a tie (both rolls equal), both participants treat it as a draw — just the base +3 each direction, no winner bonus.

- No Honor effects. No Glory effects. No Raises mechanic. Declining an invitation carries no consequence — playing a game is purely voluntary recreation. No objective tag.

- NPC engine: PLAY_GAME scores low and fires when the NPC’s active objectives are peaceful and social, no urgent needs compete, and another character is present who possesses a matching Games sub-skill. A Crane courtier and a Dragon monk both with Games: Go 2 at the same court during a quiet afternoon may play together, generating disposition and a minor social bond. NPCs never fire PLAY_GAME during active crises, hostile contexts, or when higher-priority objectives are available.

**Category 3 — Lower Someone's Disposition with a Specific Person (Targeted Sabotage):**

Surgical damage to a specific relationship. Lower risk than public attacks — the target may not even know you were involved. Two core actions: Undermine (making someone dislike someone) and Disclose (making someone's opinion known).

**Gossip (1 AP):**

Share damaging but unverified information about Person C to Person B. The goal is to make B like C less. No proof required — that is what makes it gossip rather than a revelation. Weaker than a verified secret but deniable and low risk when it works.

- Roll: Awareness + Courtier (Gossip) vs TN = 10 + (target C’s Glory Rank × 5) − (gossiper’s Glory Rank × 5). The target of the gossip (C) is the character being slandered, not the listener (B). Glory Ranks are the whole-number portion of the Glory value (Glory 4.7 = Rank 4). The TN reflects the court’s willingness to believe bad things about C given their reputation. High-Glory characters have strong reputations that resist slander: the Imperial Chancellor (Glory 10) against a Glory 3 gossiper faces TN 10 + 50 − 15 = TN 45, nearly impossible. Low-Glory or unknown characters are easy to slander: a ronin (Glory 0) gossiped about by a Glory 5 courtier faces TN 10 + 0 − 25 = TN ≤ 0, automatic success barring critical failure. This asymmetry enforces the social hierarchy — attacking up is dangerous and usually futile, attacking down is cheap and effective. Minimum TN: 5 (even a ronin slandering another ronin requires some plausibility). Maximum TN: 60 (beyond this, the target’s reputation is fundamentally unassailable through gossip).

- Success: B's disposition toward C drops −5. Unverified, so weaker than a revealed secret.

- Each Raise: the gossiper chooses at declaration time whether each Raise applies to damage or concealment. Damage Raise — −2 additional disposition drop between B and C. Concealment Raise — the gossip enters the topic system with no attribution to the gossiper’s name. Without a concealment Raise, the gossip is attributable: B knows who told them, and the zone_event_log records the GOSSIP action with the gossiper as source. With at least one concealment Raise, B believes the gossip arrived from an anonymous or general source (“people are saying”) rather than from the gossiper specifically, and the zone_event_log entry marks source_concealed: true — future investigation must overcome additional TN to identify the gossiper. A gossiper calling 3 Raises could split them: 1 damage Raise (−2 additional) and 2 concealment Raises (deeper source concealment, harder to trace back), or all 3 to damage, or all 3 to concealment. Bayushi Courtier R1 (“Weakness is My Strength”) grants automatic concealment — Bayushi gossip is always source-concealed without spending a Raise.

- Failure: the gossip fails to land — C’s reputation absorbs the slander without damage. No disposition effect. B may have heard the gossip but dismisses it as inconsistent with C’s known character and standing.

- Critical failure: the gossip reflects badly on the gossiper — the court reads the attempt as transparent malice. Your disposition with B drops −5. B may relay what you said to C, creating a new enemy. Additionally, if C holds higher Status than you, consult the Status Gap escalation note below.

- Status Gap escalation: attempting to spread malicious rumours about a character with higher Status than yours is culturally dangerous. If C’s Status exceeds the gossiper’s by 2.0 or more AND the gossip action is discovered (either through failure with witnesses, critical failure, or later investigation via zone_event_log or Kitsuki’s Eye), C and their allies may escalate. The escalation is not mechanical in the GOSSIP action itself — it manifests through normal downstream systems: provocation flag fires if the gossip is detected, C’s family may issue a duel challenge or legal complaint, and in extreme cases (Status gap of 4.0+ against an Imperial or Great Clan figure) the lord of C’s family may demand seppuku from the gossiper’s lord. The cultural principle “attacking up is a death sentence” is enforced through the existing honor, legal, and social systems reacting to the gossip’s discovery — not through a new escalation subsystem.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] if C is relevant to the main topic.

- Authenticity modifier applies — gossiping about someone you genuinely like incurs a dice kept penalty.

**Disclose (1 AP):**

Share what you know — or claim to know — about what someone else thinks or has said about a specific person. About opinions and positions, not damaging personal information. Disclose is fundamentally an information transfer action. There is no direct disposition change from the roll itself — the disposition shift, if any, happens as a natural downstream consequence of what B now knows about C.

- Roll: Perception + Sincerity vs target's Perception + Sincerity. The target evaluates whether what you're telling them is credible and genuine.

- Success: B now knows C's opinion or position on a matter. If that opinion is negative toward B — B's disposition toward C will drop as a consequence of knowing. If positive — B's disposition toward C may rise. The magnitude depends on the significance of what was disclosed.

- Each Raise: The information lands more convincingly — B is more certain of what they've been told, amplifying the downstream consequence.

- Failure: B doesn't find your disclosure credible. No information transferred. No effect.

- Critical failure: B suspects manipulation. Your disposition with B drops −5. If the disclosure was false, B may investigate — creating risk of exposure.

- You can Disclose false information. If B later discovers this — significant disposition loss with B and potential Honor loss for you.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] if C's opinion is relevant to the main topic.

**Reveal a Secret Privately (1 AP):**

Share a damaging secret about Person C with Person B privately. Unlike Disclose which is about opinions and positions, this is about revealing genuinely compromising personal information. Requires possession of the secret — either discovered through intelligence actions or fabricated. More devastating than gossip because it carries weight as a known or believed fact rather than unverified rumor.

- Roll: Awareness + Courtier vs target’s Perception + Sincerity. The target evaluates how credible and significant what you’re sharing is. If you possess physical proof of the secret (a letter, document, or object — see Section 12.8 and Inventory System), you gain 1 Free Raise on this roll.

- Success: B's disposition toward C drops significantly — more than a rumor would produce, because this carries the weight of specific compromising information.

- Each Raise: Additional disposition drop between B and C.

- Failure: B is unconvinced or uninterested. No effect.

- Critical failure: B suspects you are spreading malicious lies. Your disposition with B drops. B may warn C — creating a dangerous new enemy.

- If the secret is fabricated and later exposed as false — significant disposition loss with B and broad Honor loss as you become known as someone who fabricates lies about others.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] if C is relevant to the main topic.

- Authenticity modifier applies — revealing damaging information about someone you genuinely like incurs a dice kept penalty.

**Category 4 — Lower Someone's Disposition with Everyone Present (Public Attack) — LOCKED:**

The most aggressive and highest-risk category. Everything here is public — the whole room watches. A success is devastating. A failure is embarrassing. A critical failure can end your political effectiveness for the session. The Art of the Insult rules from the sourcebook apply where relevant — keep it personal, keep it true, be amusing, never address the target directly, quote the sages.

**Publicly Insult Someone (1 AP):**

Attack a specific character's reputation in front of the court. A well-crafted insult is a weapon. A clumsy one is a gift to your enemy.

- Roll: Awareness + Courtier vs contested target's Awareness + Etiquette.

- Success: Target loses −2 disposition with all witnesses.

- Each Raise: −1 per witness for the target.

- Failure: The insult fell flat. You look petty. −2 disposition with all witnesses for you.

- Critical failure: The insult backfired badly. −2 disposition with all witnesses for you. The target may gain sympathy. A duel challenge becomes likely.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] if the target is relevant to the main topic.

- Hostile action authenticity modifier applies — insulting someone you have high disposition toward incurs dice kept penalty.

- Family/Clan ripple applies negatively to all witnesses toward the insulted character's Family and Clan.

**Public Debate (1 AP):**

Engage a specific person in a public argument about a topic. The audience watches and their opinions shift based on who makes the better case — but their existing dispositions toward both parties influence whose argument they find more convincing. Winning a debate against someone the room loves requires a decisive margin. Losing to someone the room distrusts is survivable.

- Roll: Awareness + Courtier vs target's Awareness + Courtier. The difference between the two results is the base debate outcome.

- Per-witness modifier: each witness applies their disposition tier toward each debater. Formula: (A's roll − B's roll) + (witness tier toward A) − (witness tier toward B). Positive = swayed toward A. Negative = swayed toward B. Zero = holds current position.

- Disposition tier scale for debate modifier: Blood Enemy = −3. Enemy = −2. Rival = −1. Stranger/Acquaintance = 0. Friend = +1. Trusted Ally = +2. Devoted = +3.

Per-witness outcome based on combined score:

- Score +1 to +2: Slight topic shift toward winner. Winner +1 disposition, Loser −1 disposition with that witness.

- Score +3 to +4: Moderate topic shift. Winner +2, Loser −2.

- Score +5 to +6: Strong topic shift. Winner +3, Loser −3.

- Score +7 or more: Decisive topic shift. Winner +4, Loser −4.

- Loser of the debate from each witness's perspective loses disposition toward the winner — challenging someone publicly and losing carries a social cost.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] always applies — a public debate is inherently about a topic.

- Family/Clan ripple applies to all witnesses based on the outcome.

Public Debate — Topic Position Shift — LOCKED:

Roll: Awareness + Courtier (keep Awareness dice) vs TN set by target’s Awareness + Courtier. Disposition modifiers apply as Free Raises or forced additional Raises per the threshold table in Section 12.2. The target’s disposition toward you: Friend gives you 1 Free Raise, Trusted Ally 2 Free Raises, Blood Enemy requires 2 additional Raises, Enemy 1 additional Raise.

Each witness applies their own disposition tier toward each debater to determine who they perceive as winning. The per-witness score formula already in the document above applies. Topic position shift per witness based on the winner’s margin:

- Base success (meets TN, no Raises): +2 position shift per witness toward winner’s side. Loser: −2 per witness.

- 1 Raise: +4 position shift per witness. Loser: −4 per witness.

- 2 Raises: +6 position shift per witness. Loser: −6 per witness.

- 3 or more Raises: +8 position shift per witness. Loser: −8 per witness.

Position shifts are applied individually to each witness — a court of 10 witnesses where you win with 2 Raises gives each of those 10 characters +6 position shift. Broadcast position movement is deliberately smaller per person than targeted actions (Negotiate: +8, Persuade: +12) because debate touches everyone lightly rather than moving one person deeply.

Failure: No position shift for any witness. No disposition change.

Critical failure: You lose the debate decisively. Apply the loser values above to yourself even though you initiated. The target gains the winner values.

**Expose a Secret Publicly (1 AP):**

Reveal compromising information about a character in front of the entire court. Requires possession of the secret — either discovered through intelligence actions or fabricated. The most devastating public action available. A true secret used well can destroy a reputation permanently. A fabricated secret that is exposed as false destroys your own credibility instead.

- Roll: Awareness + Courtier vs TN 15. The roll determines how effectively you present the information — a high roll makes it land more convincingly. If you possess physical proof of the secret (a letter, document, or object — see Section 12.8 and Inventory System), you gain 1 Free Raise on this roll. Physical proof makes the revelation harder to deny.

- Success: Target suffers disposition loss with all witSuccess: Target suffers disposition loss with all witnesses. Magnitude scales with the secret’s severity (Section 12.8): Tier 4 −5 per witness, Tier 3 −10 per witness, Tier 2 −20 per witness, Tier 1 −35 per witness. Their position on touched topics may shift as their credibility collapses.

- Each Raise: Additional disposition loss per witness. Additional credibility damage.

- Failure: The revelation fell flat — poorly presented, the room was unconvinced. Small disposition loss for you.

- Critical failure: The secret was exposed as false or your presentation collapsed entirely. Significant disposition loss for you with all witnesses. If the secret was fabricated, you are now known as a liar — broad lasting disposition penalties across all future social interactions.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] if the target is relevant to the main topic.

- Family/Clan ripple applies negatively to all witnesses toward the target's Family and Clan.

**Provoke Emotion (1 AP):**

Deliberately manipulate a specific character into losing composure publicly. hostile: true. Full specification in Section 29.15.24.

Roll: Awareness + Courtier (Manipulation) vs target’s Willpower + Etiquette (Courtesy).

Success: Target loses composure. −0.2 Honor, −0.1 Glory, −3 disposition with each witness.

Failure: No effect.

Critical failure: Manipulation was transparent. −5 disposition with witnesses toward the provoker.

**Category 5 — Intelligence (Imperfect, Detectable):**

Information is the foundation of effective court play. Without knowing what you are working with, you spend action points blindly. Intelligence actions are always imperfect — the quality of your roll determines what you actually learn. Most intelligence actions trigger a detection prompt to the subject, though not necessarily revealing who was asking.

**Intelligence Outcome Tiers — LOCKED:**

- Success: Learn the truth accurately.

- Partial success (beat TN but by less than 5): Learn something vague or approximate.

- Failure: Learn nothing. Action point spent.

- Critical failure (10+ below TN): Learn something false and do not know it. The most dangerous outcome — you may act on wrong intelligence and waste further action points.

- Detection: Most intelligence actions send a prompt to the subject that someone has been inquiring about them. They do not automatically know who — discovering the source requires their own intelligence action. Exceptions noted per action.

**Read a Character (1 AP):**

Observe a specific person's behavior, body language, and manner at court to infer information about them. No conversation required — you are watching them from across the room, noticing how they react to others, how they carry themselves, how they respond when topics arise. The safest intelligence action — no detection prompt unless you critically fail.

- Roll: Perception + Investigation vs target's Awareness + Etiquette. The target defends with Awareness + Etiquette — a skilled courtier knows how to present a composed, unreadable face.

Three possible pieces of information that can be learned:

- 1. Personality insight — a glimpse of their character type, temperament, or how they operate at court.

- 2. Disposition toward someone — approximate disposition tier (not exact number) toward a specific person present at court.

- 3. Attitude toward the main topic — general direction of their position, and whether they seem certain or uncertain about it.

Raises determine quantity. Randomness determines which pieces are revealed:

- Success with no Raises: 1 random piece of information from the pool.

- Success with 1 Raise: 2 random pieces of information.

- Success with 2 Raises: All 3 pieces of information.

- Partial success: 1 piece of information but vague — approximate rather than clear.

- Failure: Nothing learned.

- Critical failure: 1 false piece of information delivered with the same confidence as a real read. You do not know it is wrong. No detection prompt on failure — but target may notice your scrutiny.

- No objective tag — Read a Character is pure observation with no direct topic influence.

**Probe (1 AP):**

Engage a specific character in direct conversation with the intent of learning their position on a topic or their objective at this court session. Active rather than passive — you are asking questions, steering the conversation, reading between the lines of what they say. A skilled courtier can probe without revealing what they're looking for. The target always knows a conversation happened — they just don't know how much you actually learned.

- Roll: Perception + Courtier vs target's Awareness + Sincerity. Water because you're reading beneath the surface of what they say. The target defends with Awareness + Sincerity — social composure and either genuine or performed honesty managing what they reveal.

Two possible pieces of information that can be learned:

- 1. Their position on a topic — where they stand and how firmly.

- 2. Their objective at this court session — what outcome they are working toward.

The lying mechanic: the target does not roll separately to lie. Their Awareness + Sincerity defense already incorporates deflection and misdirection. A decisive roll beats their composure and reveals the truth. A weak roll means you believe whatever they chose to present — true or false.

- Success with no Raises: 1 random piece of information — accurate.

- Success with 1 Raise: Both pieces of information — accurate.

- Partial success: 1 piece of information — vague or partially obscured by their deflection.

- Failure: Nothing meaningful learned. They managed the conversation successfully.

- Critical failure: 1 false piece of information — you believe what they wanted you to believe. You do not know it is wrong.

- Detection: Target always knows you were probing them — it was a direct conversation. They do not know exactly what you learned.

- [OBJECTIVE TAG] if the probed information is relevant to the main topic.

**Discern Need (1 AP):**

Identify what a target currently wants — their active resource shortages, political goals, and court session priorities. Distinct from READ_CHARACTER (which reveals personality) and PROBE (which reveals topic positions and objectives). DISCERN_NEED queries the target’s current objective stack from the NPC Decision Engine. Full specification in Section 29.15.24.

Roll: Skill varies by school (Yasuki = Commerce/Perception, Doji = Courtier/Awareness, default = Investigation/Awareness) vs target’s Awareness + Etiquette.

Success: Learn the target’s highest-priority current objective or resource shortage.

Failure: No information.

Critical failure: Target becomes aware of probing. −3 disposition.

No objective tag.
