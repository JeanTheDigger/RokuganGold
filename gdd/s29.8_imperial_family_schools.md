## 29.8 Imperial Family Schools

The three non-dynastic Imperial families — the Seppun, the Otomo, and the Miya — each maintain their own schools reflecting their institutional role within the Empire (see Section 2.1.19 for full history and Section 27.10 for family trait bonuses). Seppun schools produce the miharu guardsmen and the Hidden Guard shugenja who protect the Emperor. Otomo schools produce the courtiers who keep the Great Clans divided through the seiyaku tradition of political manipulation. Miya schools produce the heralds who carry the Emperor’s word across the Empire and serve as peacemakers in inter-clan disputes. Imperial family schools are restricted to NPC characters — player characters cannot start as members of an Imperial family or in an Imperial family school. A player character may be adopted into an Imperial family or granted access to certain Imperial school techniques during play as a result of extraordinary achievement, but this is an in-game event requiring specific narrative conditions, not a character creation option. Characters holding Imperial appointed positions (Emperor’s Chosen, Jeweled Champions) may be granted access to certain Imperial school techniques as part of their office — see the individual school entries below.

**BASIC SCHOOLS:**

**Hantei Bushi (Owl) [Bushi]:**

Alternate timeline only (Togashi Dynasty). Not present in the main timeline. Hantei’s Celestial fighting style adapted for the Owl Clan’s duty of fighting nonhuman threats. In canonical history, these techniques died with Hantei after the First War. GMs may adapt as needed.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Etiquette, Kenjutsu, Kyujutsu, Lore: Nonhumans, any one High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Average Clothing, Light Armor, Daisho, Bow, any one weapon, Traveling Pack, 4 koku

- Rank 1 — The Blessings of Heaven: When facing a nonhuman opponent, add either your Earth Ring or Honor Rank (whichever is higher) to your Armor TN. +1k0 to all attack rolls with Samurai weapons.

- Rank 2 — Wrath of the Earth Dragon: At the start of your Turn, spend a Void Point as a Free Action — your attacks this Round ignore all Reduction of nonhuman opponents.

- Rank 3 — Speed of the Air Dragon: Attack as a Simple Action when wielding Samurai weapons or when attacking a nonhuman opponent.

- Rank 4 — Courage of the Thunder Dragon: After striking an opponent, spend a Void Point as a Free Action to ignore any one of that opponent’s Techniques, armor effects, spells, special qualities, or creature abilities (except Invulnerability) that reduce or mitigate damage.

- Rank 5 — Judgment of the Celestial Dragon: Once per skirmish, after hitting your opponent, spend four Void Points to automatically reduce that opponent to Down/Out/Dead (whichever applies first). Against nonhuman opponents, costs three Void Points instead. GM’s discretion whether this works against transcendent entities such as Fortunes; GM may optionally allow it against such foes if you voluntarily die to activate it.

**Miya Herald [Courtier] [CR]:**

Imperial messengers charged with spreading the words of the Emperor to even the most remote regions of Rokugan. Protected by Imperial status but required to travel to places of great conflict and danger. Their school focuses on building trust and cordiality — speaking with confidence to ease tensions, boost defenses, and spread peace throughout the Empire.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Courtier (Rhetoric), Defense, Etiquette (Courtesy), Horsemanship, Lore: Heraldry, Sincerity, any 1 High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Sensible Clothing, Wakizashi, Calligraphy Set, Horse (Rokugani pony), 10 koku

- Rank 1 — Voice of the Emperor: While inside the borders of the Empire, spend a Void Point to gain the Way of the Land Advantage for your current province until you next sleep. Any Rokugani who reveres the Emperor and knowingly attacks you automatically loses Honor equal to twice your School Rank.

- Rank 2 — Eyes of the Emperor: Add your Honor Rank to all Etiquette (Courtesy) Skill Rolls made to resist others’ attempts to influence you.

- Rank 3 — Hand of the Emperor: At the start of a skirmish or during the Reactions Step of any later round, spend a Void Point as a Free Action to prevent any Rokugani who reveres the Emperor from being able to harm you before the next Reactions Step (non-damaging capture or restraint is still permitted). Only active while you are not participating in the skirmish or a duel.

- Rank 4 — Blessing of the Emperor: School Rank times per session, take a Complex Action to forcefully demand an end to violence. Make a Contested Courtier (Rhetoric)/Awareness vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Willpower roll against all honorable samurai (Honor 1.0+) who revere the Emperor within hearing. All who lose must cease all aggressive action and cannot attack or cast offensive spells until they take damage or you give permission. Lasts one minute. Affects both enemies and allies. Cannot be used against criminals, bandits, the Lost, or nonhumans.

- Rank 5 — Glory of the Emperor: When rolling Courtier or Etiquette against a target with at least Honor 1.0, add +5k0 to the roll.

**Otomo Courtier [CR]:**

The Seiyaju — dedicated to the Emperor’s safety through underhanded methods. The Otomo sow suspicion and distrust among the Great Clans to prevent any faction from gathering enough strength to topple Imperial authority. Their Imperial blood protects them from the repercussions of methods that would ruin any other courtier.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Courtier (Manipulation), Defense, Etiquette, Intimidation (Control), Investigation, Sincerity, any 1 High Skill

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Traditional Clothing, Wakizashi, Knife, Calligraphy Set, 10 koku

- Rank 1 — The Voice of Heaven: No Honor loss for using Intimidation (Control). When in conversation with a clan member (Great or Minor), make a Contested Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Awareness roll to provoke a disagreement or argument between them and someone from another clan or faction of your choice.

- Rank 2 — Destiny Has No Secrets: Spend a Void Point and roll Courtier/Awareness (TN 25) to learn one piece of critically useful information from your information network (GM chooses the specific intelligence).

- Rank 3 — My Master’s Voice (Obiesaseru): Complex Action — shout an appropriate Imperial command. Make a Contested Intimidation (Control)/Willpower vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Willpower roll against one honorable samurai (Honor 1.0+) who reveres the Emperor (sincere belief in acting honorably grants target a Free Raise). On success, on their next Turn the target may not move, attack, cast spells, or take any action that would directly obstruct or threaten you or your immediate allies. Cannot be used against criminals, bandits, the Lost, or nonhumans.

- Rank 4 — The Emperor’s Protection: When in an argument or debate, spend a Void Point and make a Contested Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Awareness roll. On success, your opponent must either agree with your position or immediately lose 5 Honor.

- Rank 5 — The Virtues of Command: +5k0 on Contested Social Rolls against anyone who reveres Imperial authority.

**Otomo Diplomat (Owl) [Courtier]:**

Alternate timeline only (Togashi Dynasty). Not present in the main timeline. Diplomatic specialists in dealing with nonhuman races, smoothing misunderstandings between human and nonhuman parties. GMs may adapt as needed.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Calligraphy, Courtier, Etiquette (Courtesy), Intimidation, Lore: Nonhumans, Sincerity, any one High Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Court Robes, Fan, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — Wisdom of the Owl: +1k0 to Etiquette rolls. During any social interaction with nonhumans, Free Action to roll Lore: Nonhumans/Intelligence (TN 20) to recall an important aspect of social interaction with that nonhuman type (GM chooses what is remembered, always useful to the situation).

- Rank 2 — Unity of the Realms: When encountering a nonhuman in a non-hostile environment, roll Courtier/Awareness (TN 25). On success, the nonhuman treats you as an Ally with Devotion 2 for the remainder of the encounter (unless you actively threaten or harm it).

- Rank 3 — The Voice of Heaven: When spending a Void Point on an Intimidation roll, gain +2k2 instead of normal +1k1. Against nonhuman opponents, gain +3k2 instead. No Honor loss for using Intimidation in this manner.

- Rank 4 — Uphold the Peace: At the start of a skirmish or on your Turn in any later Round, Complex Action — spend a Void Point and demand cessation of hostilities. Make a Contested Intimidation/Willpower roll against all within hearing (+3k0 bonus against nonhumans). Those who lose must cease all offensive action until attacked or until three Rounds pass. Cannot work against Shadowlands creatures or denizens of Jigoku or Tengoku. No Honor loss for using Intimidation this way.

- Rank 5 — Hantei’s Mandate: Gain an additional +3k0 to Intimidation rolls with the Rank 4 Technique (stacks with the +3k0 bonus against nonhumans).

**Seppun Guardsman [Bushi] [CR]:**

The Miharu — born to protect the Emperor from any threat. A Seppun Guardsman sacrifices everything else in his life for this duty, knowing that nothing else is significant set against the safety of the Imperial House. The school holds defense as its highest priority: detection before the threat arrives, resistance to corruption, and interposing oneself between the Emperor and any harm.

- Benefit: +1 Perception

- Skills: Battle, Defense, Etiquette, Iaijutsu, Kenjutsu (Katana), Kyujutsu, any 1 High Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 Weapon, Traveling Pack, 10 koku

- Rank 1 — Never in Darkness: When rolling to resist a Social Skill roll that could tempt or lead you away from your duty (Temptation, Intimidation, Courtier), roll additional unkept dice equal to your School Rank. +1k1 to Investigation rolls to detect an ambush or surprise attack.

- Rank 2 — The Clouds Part: Spend a Void Point at the start of your Turn to add your Honor Rank to the total of all attack and damage rolls until your next Turn.

- Rank 3 — Sun’s Light Reveals: Spend a Void Point as a Free Action and roll Investigation (Notice)/Perception (TN 25). On success, immediately see through all disguises and illusions around you whether normal or magical. In the presence of the Emperor, immediate Imperial family, or a major Imperial Official, no Void Point is required. Once a specific disguise or illusion is pierced, it no longer affects you.

- Rank 4 — Speed of Heaven: Attack as a Simple Action when using weapons with the Samurai keyword.

- Rank 5 — Heaven Never Falls: When within 20’ of a person you are guarding, spend a Void Point to leap in front of a blow or spell targeting your charge — damage is automatically applied to you instead. If still standing (not Down or Out) afterward, immediately take one extra Simple Action for any purpose including an attack. Usable even while Blinded, Prone, Stunned, Dazed, Entangled, or at the Down Wound Rank (but not Grappled or Out).

**Seppun Shugenja [CR]:**

The Hidden Guard’s magical arm. Seppun shugenja stand vigil at all times for signs of attack, their wards among the best in Rokugan. They are the last magical line of defense for the Emperor — versatile with both offensive and defensive spells, provided they are cast in protection of the Son of Heaven.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Calligraphy, Defense, Etiquette, Investigation (Notice), Meditation, Lore: Theology, any 1 High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, Bo, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 10 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Fire / Water | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Fire, 2 Water, 1 Air

- Technique — In Defense of the Throne: Free Raise on any spell cast to protect the life, personal belongings, family, or reputation of the Emperor (provided doing so would not cause Honor loss). Also applies to members of the Imperial families (but not yourself). Free Raise on any spells known only to the Imperial families.

**Seppun Shugenja (Owl) [Shugenja]:**

Alternate timeline only (Togashi Dynasty). Not present in the main timeline. These shugenja both communicate with nonhumans and spirits and protect the Empire from their depredations when they act improperly. GMs may adapt as needed.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Calligraphy, Defense, Investigation, Lore: Nonhumans, Lore: Theology, Meditation, any one High Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, Bo Staff, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 4 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Fire / Water | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Fire, 2 Air, 1 Earth

- Technique — In Defense of Ningen-Do: Free Raise on any spell targeting a nonhuman. When casting a damaging spell, expend an additional spell slot (same Element or Void) to have the spell ignore Reduction equal to twice your Void Rank. Against nonhuman targets, this also ignores all damage mitigation from creature abilities (except Invulnerability).

**ADVANCED SCHOOLS:**

**Imperial Scion [Courtier] [EE]:**

Utterly ruthless individuals who have mastered manipulation of their own social standing to decimate enemies. Standing against a Scion in court is tantamount to ruination for all but the most skilled or highest-ranking samurai. Most often drawn from the Otomo family, though Miya and Seppun members are occasionally permitted to train at the same elite dojo.

- Requirements: Awareness 5, Perception 4 | Skills: Courtier 6, Etiquette 4 | Other: Imperial family member only; Status 4+.

- Rank 1 — The Awe of the Throne: When called upon to make a Skill Roll or to use Glory or Honor Rank, spend a Void Point to substitute your Status Rank instead.

- Rank 2 — The Strength of the Throne: After conversing with an opponent of lower Status Rank for a few minutes, force a Contested Courtier/Etiquette roll. On success, reduce their Status by up to twice your Insight Rank.

- Rank 3 — The Terror of the Throne: While conversing with an opponent of equal or lower Status Rank, force a Contested Void Roll. On success, permanently inflict any one Social Disadvantage on the opponent. Alternatively, force the opponent to become ronin (usable once every six months; costs you 5 points each of Glory, Honor, and Status).

**Tsudao’s Legion [Bushi]:**

Devised personally by Empress Toturi II (the Sword) for the finest warriors of the Legion she personally commanded. One of the most formidable military forces in the Empire’s history. Entry requirements are among the most stringent of any school.

- Requirements: Agility 5, Strength 4 | Skills: Battle 5, any one Weapon Skill at 5 | Other: Honor Rank 6+, Status Rank 2+, any one Paragon Advantage.

- Rank 1 — All Things Through Honor: When making a Skill Roll with a High or Bugei Skill in which you have at least 1 Rank, spend a Void Point to make that roll as though your Skill Rank equals your Honor Rank.

- Rank 2 — Wisdom of the Sensei: When rolling on the Mass Battle Table, add or subtract your Void Ring from the total as desired. Select one Maneuver — whenever you attempt that Maneuver, gain one Free Raise toward success.

- Rank 3 — Empowered By Her Name: If your Honor Rank exceeds your opponent’s Honor Rank, make one additional melee attack as a Free Action once per Round against that opponent.

**ALTERNATE PATHS:**

**Imperial Influencer [Courtier]:**

A subtler path than the typical Otomo — shaping entire courts through things as deceptively simple as hosting dinner parties. Sometimes credited to Otomo Madoka, author of The Subtlety of the Court.

- Replaces: Otomo Courtier 3. Requires: Awareness 4, Etiquette 5, Sincerity 5. Must have Status 4.0+.

- Technique — Follow My Lead: At any courtly event you are hosting, roll Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness (TN 30) to set the dominant tone of the event (positive or negative, with a specific flavor). Anyone making a Social Skill Roll acting against the tone must call two Raises to no effect — failing due solely to those Raises constitutes a Minor Breach of Etiquette. Anyone acting in concert with the tone gains +2k0. If not the host, your roll becomes Contested against the host. Other guests may oppose you with Contested Courtier (Manipulation) rolls — the character with higher Status gains +1k0 per Status Rank of difference. Effects last until the end of the event.

**Imperial Observer [Courtier]:**

Assigned by the Imperial Bureaucracy to oversee the honorable conduct of war. Only those with Imperial authority are empowered to deliver unquestioned reports to the Throne. The Technique is an Imperial secret taught in the Ruby Dojo.

- Replaces: Otomo Courtier 4, Miya Herald 4 (optionally any Courtier 4 with GM approval). Requires: Battle 4 or Lore: War 4, Sincerity 4, Status Rank 3.0+.

- Technique — Pierce the Fog of War: When reporting on the events or conduct of a war, you are considered to have the Sacrosanct Advantage. Make a Sincerity/Awareness roll (TN = 5 × the commander’s Glory Rank) to praise or damn a commander on either side — on success, increase or decrease their Glory by 1 Rank. Raise to inflict or remove Infamy at a rate of half a Rank plus an additional half-Rank per Raise. No individual may be affected by this Technique more than once per battle reported.

**Otomo Bureaucrat [Courtier]:**

Specialists in delay, obfuscation, and persistent obstruction — using the intricate complexities of the Imperial government to block and foil the goals of the Great Clans. The Otomo bureaucracy came to full flower after the Reign of the Gozoku, when the family was tasked with ensuring the clans could never again subordinate the Emperor.

- Replaces: Otomo Courtier 3. Requires: Etiquette (Bureaucracy) 5.

- Technique — Imperial Scrutiny: Any time another samurai is trying to accomplish something in the courts, employ the intricacies of the Imperial bureaucracy to delay him. This takes up to several hours and is represented by a Contested Etiquette (Bureaucracy)/Willpower roll (spending a Void Point grants +2k2 instead of +1k1). On success, the samurai cannot make any progress toward his goal for the next two weeks regardless of what he does.

**The Rising Sun [Bushi]:**

The most elite cavalry unit in the Imperial military. Trained by the finest mounted combatants from across the Empire — mastering tactics the Great Clans do not share with one another. Even the Unicorn hold the Rising Sun in extremely high regard.

- Replaces: Seppun Guardsman 5 (with GM’s permission, any Bushi Basic School 5 if enrolled in the Imperial Legions). Requires: Strength 4, Horsemanship 5, Spears 5. Grants a steed if not already possessed.

- Technique — The Storm of Heaven’s Wrath: While mounted and in Attack Stance, add your Honor Rank to Armor TN. Spend a Void Point to add half your Honor Rank (rounded up) in kept dice to your attack roll.

**Satoshi’s Legacy [Bushi] [SE]:**

Miya Satoshi died in disgrace, but his effort to militarize the Miya family left a legacy of trained warriors carried on by the Satoshi vassal family. An aggressive fighting style derived from Satoshi’s training with the Matsu family.

- Replaces: Miya Herald 4. Requires: Kenjutsu 5.

- Technique — Fire and Ice: Make attacks as Simple Actions when in the Full Attack Stance.

**Seppun Astrologers [Shugenja]:**

The Seppun watch the night skies constantly for portents to protect the Emperor and Empire. They supplement astrological knowledge with divination magic and are greatly insulted by any comparison to kawaru practitioners.

- Replaces: Seppun Shugenja 4. Requires: Divination (Astrology) 4, Lore: Omens 5.

- Technique — Wisdom of the Heavens: Once per month, clarify a person’s place in the tapestry of fate and confer one Spiritual Advantage or Spiritual Disadvantage on them (ritual only, cannot be used in a skirmish). Effect lasts one month or until you use this Technique on a different person. Conferring a Disadvantage requires a successful Contested Void Roll against the target.

**Seppun Hidden Guard [Shugenja] [SE]:**

The elite order charged with the direct magical and spiritual protection of the Emperor, his family, and the Imperial Palace. Many spend their entire lives doing nothing but endlessly watching, reinforcing, and maintaining the wards over their charges.

- Replaces: Seppun Shugenja 3. Requires: Must be chosen for service in the Hidden Guard.

- Technique — Harmony in All: Free Raise on all spells with the Wards or Defense properties.
