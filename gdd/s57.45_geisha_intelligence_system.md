## 57.45 Geisha Intelligence System

### 57.45.1 Overview

The Geisha Intelligence System models okiya (geisha houses) as passive intelligence-gathering nodes across the Empire. Samurai visit okiya through the Wind-Down System (Section 57.44.5), where they drop their On — the emotional mask samurai maintain in public — and confide things they would never share with another samurai. These confidences route through a pipeline: the geisha hears the topic, decides whether to pass it to the okaasan (house mistress), who decides whether to pass it to whoever controls the okiya’s intelligence channel. The system produces a slow, steady drip of intelligence that rewards patience and relationship investment over direct action. The Wind-Down System handles why samurai visit and what leaks. This section handles where it goes after it leaks.

### 57.45.2 Okiya as Settlement Feature

An okiya is a settlement feature present in settlements with a PLEASURE_QUARTER zone (Section 57.36). Villages and fortifications never have okiya. Most castle towns and all cities have a PLEASURE_QUARTER and therefore one okiya. Small or heavily militarized castle towns (remote Crab border fortifications, frontier Lion outposts) may lack the zone entirely. Each settlement with a PLEASURE_QUARTER has exactly one okiya, with one exception: the Imperial Capital has 3–4 okiya of varying quality tier, reflecting its status as the cultural and political center of the Empire.

### 57.45.3 Okiya Quality Tiers

Three tiers, assigned at world generation based on settlement size and clan cultural investment. Tier 1 — Provincial House: found in smaller castle towns. 3–5 geisha. Patrons are local — provincial daimyō, magistrates, passing merchants. Tier 3–4 secrets at best. Low Koku cost per Wind-Down visit. Geisha have modest Perform and Awareness. Most are Independent. Tier 2 — Established House: found in major castle towns and smaller cities. 6–10 geisha. Patrons include family daimyō, senior courtiers, military commanders. Tier 2–3 secrets flow regularly. Moderate Koku cost. Geisha are well-trained — solid Perform, good Awareness. Mix of Scorpion-controlled and Independent. Tier 3 — Famous House: found only in major cities and the Imperial Capital. 10–15 geisha. Patrons include Clan Champions, Imperial officials, the Emperor’s court. Tier 1–2 secrets pass through. High Koku cost. Geisha are exceptional — high Perform, high Awareness, trained in multiple arts. Almost always Scorpion-controlled. The most valuable intelligence targets in the game. The tier determines the Koku cost in the Wind-Down System (Section 57.44.5), the caliber of patrons the NPC engine sends there, and the average value of topics that flow through the pipeline.

### 57.45.4 Okaasan — House Mistress NPC

The okaasan is a full named NPC generated through the World Population System (Section 52), with a complete character sheet, personality (Section 19), and the full NPC Decision Engine running on her. She manages the okiya, assigns geisha to patrons, collects intelligence from her geisha, and routes it to her handler (if Scorpion-controlled) or uses it for her own purposes (if Independent). She can be investigated, bribed, intimidated, turned, assassinated, or replaced. Her disposition relationships with her handler, her geisha, and her patrons drive the quality and reliability of the intelligence pipeline. An okaasan with high Seigyo or Chugi routes reliably. An okaasan with high Ishi withholds valuable material for her own leverage. The okaasan carries a unique standing objective: protect and grow the okiya’s reputation and profit.

### 57.45.5 Geisha NPC Generation

Each geisha in an okiya is a full named NPC generated through the World Population System (Section 52), with a complete character sheet and personality. Geisha are non-samurai (bonge class) NPCs with elevated Perform skills, Awareness, and social skills appropriate to their okiya’s tier. Tier 1 geisha: Perform 2–3, Awareness 2–3. Tier 2 geisha: Perform 3–4, Awareness 3–4. Tier 3 geisha: Perform 4–5, Awareness 4–5, multiple Perform sub-skills. Geisha trained at the Silken Promises school (Section 29) are rare and appear only in Tier 2–3 houses with Scorpion connections. A geisha’s personality determines her behavior in the intelligence pipeline — high-Jin geisha protect patrons, high-Chugi geisha report faithfully to the okaasan.

### 57.45.6 Geisha Assignment

On a samurai’s first Wind-Down visit to an okiya, the okaasan assigns them a geisha. The assignment is persistent — that geisha is theirs for all subsequent visits to that okiya. The okaasan selects based on compatibility: matching the patron’s personality to a geisha whose temperament complements them. Mechanically, the okaasan picks the geisha with the highest natural disposition affinity with the patron. The assignment is stored on the patron’s character sheet as assigned_geisha_id (per okiya). If the geisha dies, leaves, or is removed, the okaasan assigns a replacement on the next visit. Disposition resets to baseline with the new geisha — the relationship must be rebuilt. A patron can request a different geisha, but the okaasan may refuse if she judges the current assignment profitable. Changing geisha requires the okaasan’s consent, resolved as a disposition check.

### 57.45.7 The Intelligence Pipeline — Geisha to Okaasan

When the 40% topic leak fires during a Wind-Down geisha house visit (Section 57.44.5), the assigned geisha receives one random topic from the patron’s private known_topics pool. The geisha then makes a personality-weighted roll to decide whether to route the topic to the okaasan or withhold it. Factors pushing toward routing: high disposition toward the okaasan (loyalty to employer), high Chugi (duty), high Seigyo (follows the system), low disposition toward the patron (just a client), fear of punishment. Factors pushing toward withholding: high disposition toward the patron (genuine attachment), high Jin (compassion — does not want to see him harmed), low disposition toward the okaasan (resentment), topic severity (a Tier 1 secret that could destroy the patron’s life is harder to hand over than a Tier 4 triviality). A geisha who has been visited regularly by the same patron for a full year — disposition climbing from the +1 per visit — may start protecting him without either party explicitly discussing it. The pipeline degrades naturally through human connection. PROVISIONAL: exact roll formula and weighting values pending balancing.

### 57.45.8 The Scorpion Handler Pipeline

In a Scorpion-controlled okiya, the okaasan reports to a Bayushi handler — a full named NPC, typically a Bayushi Courtier or Shosuro operative assigned to manage a network of okiya in a region. When the okaasan receives a routed topic from a geisha, she makes a second personality-weighted roll to decide whether to route it to the handler or withhold it. Factors pushing toward routing: high disposition toward the handler (reliable relationship), high Seigyo or Chugi, the topic has clear political value (higher-tier topics are more worth reporting), the handler has been reliable — payment on time, protection delivered. Factors pushing toward withholding: low disposition toward the handler (late payments, threats, disrespect), high Ishi (resents being controlled), the topic involves someone she personally likes or owes a favor to, she judges the topic is more valuable as personal leverage than as intelligence she gives away for a fixed fee. A Bayushi handler who neglects his okaasan network gradually loses intelligence quality as okaasan start keeping the best material for themselves. Topics that pass the okaasan’s filter enter the handler’s known_topics directly. PROVISIONAL: exact roll formula and weighting values pending balancing.

### 57.45.9 The Kolat Infiltration Layer

The Kolat Silk Sect parasitizes the okiya pipeline. A Kolat Silk agent is embedded in the okiya as a geisha, servant, or entertainer. She is a full NPC with a kolat_sect field, running the Kolat engine pipeline from Section 54.7. She does not control the okaasan. She does not receive routed topics through the official pipeline. Instead, she overhears. Every time the okaasan receives a routed topic (it passed through both the geisha filter and the okaasan filter and is now in the okaasan’s known_topics), the Kolat agent makes an Investigation (Notice) / Perception roll against a TN based on the okaasan’s Stealth or discretion. Success: the agent copies the topic into her own known_topics, tagged kolat_sourced: true. She routes it through the Silk network per Section 54.7. Failure: she missed it this time. The agent does not need to do anything suspicious — she lives in the house, does her job, keeps her ears open. Her cover is her employment. In a Scorpion-controlled okiya with Kolat infiltration, the same leaked topic can flow through both pipelines simultaneously. The Bayushi handler gets it from the okaasan. The Silk network gets it from the embedded agent. Neither knows the other received it. Detection requires active investigation — the okaasan or the Scorpion handler must suspect a leak and look for it.

### 57.45.10 Independent Okiya

No Scorpion handler. The okaasan collects topics from her geisha through the same routing mechanic but keeps the information herself. She uses it strategically: trading gossip with local lords to build disposition and secure patronage, withholding damaging information about best-paying patrons to keep them returning, selling particularly valuable topics to interested parties for Koku through the existing NEGOTIATE framework, and using knowledge of who visits and what they confide as social leverage to protect the house from magistrate interference or rival establishments. An independent okaasan is a small-scale political operator — not a spy network, but a madam who knows too much and uses it to survive. A player who befriends an independent okaasan gains a local intelligence source. A player who threatens one makes an enemy who knows secrets about everyone important in town. Independent okiya may still be Kolat-infiltrated through the separate Pass 3 at world generation.

### 57.45.11 Okaasan Replacement and Succession

When the okaasan dies, is removed, or is incapacitated, the okiya enters a transition period. Immediate effects: the routing pipeline freezes. Geisha continue to hear topics from patrons (the 40% leak still fires during Wind-Down visits), but nothing routes upward. Topics accumulate in individual geisha known_topics with no one collecting them. The Scorpion handler receives nothing. The Kolat agent can still overhear geisha conversations but the organized flow stops. Replacement selection by okiya type: Scorpion-controlled — the Bayushi handler selects the replacement, typically the most senior geisha in the house, but the handler may install an outside operative. Requires the handler to travel or send a letter directive. Pipeline down for 1–2 IC seasons minimum. Independent — the most senior geisha inherits by default. No outside authority to confirm. Transition is faster, roughly 1 IC season. The new okaasan inherits the patron list but not the previous okaasan’s known_topics — whatever the old okaasan knew dies with her (okaasan keep everything in their heads for exactly this reason). The Kolat infiltration layer is unaffected by okaasan turnover. Assassination consequence: killing an okaasan is effective counterintelligence — it blinds the Scorpion handler for at least a season and destroys accumulated intelligence permanently. But it is a crime (Section 11.3), generates a Tier 4 topic, and may draw investigation. Turning or bribing the okaasan is more subtle than eliminating her.

### 57.45.12 Geisha at Court

Geisha perform at banquets, court functions, and private gatherings hosted by lords. When a lord hosts a court session, banquet, or formal gathering, they may hire geisha from a local okiya to perform. Koku cost paid by the host as part of court hosting expense, scaled by okiya tier. The geisha are physically present at the court event. They are not participants in court mechanics — they do not take court actions or hold positions on topics. But they listen. Any topic discussed publicly during the court session enters the attending geisha’s known_topics automatically. This is not a secret leak — it is public information the geisha witnessed. The value is that it routes back through the okiya pipeline. The okaasan (and through her, the Scorpion handler) learns what was discussed at a court the handler was not invited to. The real prize is private conversation. If a patron engages in private conversation with their assigned geisha during the court event, the same 40% leak mechanic fires, filtered through the geisha’s personality-weighted loyalty roll. A samurai who retreats to a quiet corner with his geisha during a banquet is running the same risk as a Wind-Down visit, but in a politically charged setting with higher-value topics in his head. Hiring cost scales by okiya tier. A lord who regularly hires Tier 3 geisha for court functions is paying significant Koku but projecting wealth, taste, and status. A lord who never hires geisha looks provincial.

### 57.45.13 Player Interaction

Multiple angles depending on what the player is trying to do. As a patron (passive): the player visits through the Wind-Down System. The 40% leak fires. Their assigned geisha may or may not route it. The player does not know whether a topic leaked or where it went. They know the risk exists. As an intelligence gatherer (active): build disposition with an okaasan through visits, gifts, and favors until she shares topics from her pool directly, either for Koku or as a returned favor. Build disposition with a specific geisha and attempt to turn her — convincing her to report to the player instead of the okaasan. Park a character at the same okiya as a target to listen for ambient leaks. As a counterintelligence operative (defensive): PROBE or READ_CHARACTER on the okaasan to detect whether she has a handler. Investigate individual geisha to detect Kolat affiliation. Build disposition with the assigned geisha high enough that the personality-weighted roll starts protecting the player. Or stop visiting and accept the Void cost. As a Scorpion player (handler): receive routed topics from the okaasan network. Intelligence quality depends on maintaining the relationship — paying on time, visiting periodically, keeping the okaasan’s disposition high. Neglect the network and watch it dry up.

### 57.45.14 World Generation Distribution

Three assignment passes fire during world generation for each settlement with a PLEASURE_QUARTER zone. Pass 1 — Okiya existence and tier: every settlement with a PLEASURE_QUARTER gets exactly one okiya (exception: Imperial Capital gets 3–4). Tier assigned by settlement size: small castle towns Tier 1, major castle towns Tier 2, cities Tier 2 or Tier 3 (weighted by clan cultural investment — Crane and Scorpion cities lean Tier 3, Crab cities lean Tier 2), Imperial Capital Tier 3. Pass 2 — Handler type: each okiya assigned Scorpion-controlled or Independent. Scorpion territory: 90% Scorpion / 10% Independent. Crane territory: 40% / 60%. Imperial Capital: 60% / 40%. Lion, Phoenix, Dragon territory: 30% / 70%. Crab territory: 20% / 80%. Unicorn territory: 25% / 75%. Minor Clan holdings: 10% / 90%. Pass 3 — Kolat infiltration: separate from Pass 2, each okiya has a flat 15% chance of containing a Kolat Silk agent at world generation, regardless of handler type. A Scorpion-controlled Tier 3 house in the Imperial Capital has the same 15% infiltration chance as an Independent Tier 1 house in Crab territory. The Kolat targets based on access, not existing control. The 15% rate produces roughly one in seven okiya Empire-wide as Kolat-compromised. The Silk Master can expand the network during play through deliberate RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT actions. PROVISIONAL: all percentage values pending balancing.

### 57.45.15 Character Sheet Field Additions

Section 22.3 character sheet amendment: add the following fields: (1) assigned_geisha_id (Dictionary[okiya_id → npc_id], default empty) — maps each visited okiya to the geisha assigned to this character. Populated on first visit, updated if the geisha is replaced. (2) okiya_visit_count (Dictionary[okiya_id → int], default empty) — number of Wind-Down visits to each okiya. Used by the disposition gain calculation and the geisha’s personality-weighted routing roll.
