## 57.48 Musha Shugyo System — LOCKED

A musha shugyo ("warrior's pilgrimage") is a sanctioned period of wandering in which a newly graduated samurai leaves their lord's service to travel the Empire seeking wisdom and experience. During the pilgrimage, the samurai is considered free of feudal obligation — they have no lord-assigned objectives, move freely, and act independently. When the pilgrimage ends, they return to their lord's service. The custom is respected across the Empire: most daimyo willingly grant permission, and completing a musha shugyo is considered an honorable pursuit of self-improvement.

Source: L5R 4th Edition, "The Origins of Ronin" (Secrets of the Empire / core supplements). Some clans value the tradition more highly than others — Dragon and Crane place a higher value on individual accomplishment and are more likely to produce pilgrimage samurai. The Crab, who need every warrior on the Wall, rarely permit it.

### 57.48.1 Eligibility and Trigger — LOCKED

Musha Shugyo is evaluated once, at the moment of Gempukku — when a new NPC is generated and enters the world as a full samurai. The evaluation is a single probability roll. If it passes, the NPC begins their career on pilgrimage rather than entering immediate service. If it fails, the NPC enters their lord's service normally. The roll is never revisited — an NPC who entered service cannot later request Musha Shugyo.

**Dependency:** This system requires the annual Gempukku NPC spawning system (not yet designed). Musha Shugyo evaluates at the moment that system generates a new named NPC.

**PC Character Creation:** Players may choose Musha Shugyo at character creation as an optional starting condition. No probability roll — the player decides. Mechanics are identical to the NPC system. Duration is 1 IC year from the character's creation date.

### 57.48.2 Probability Calculation — LOCKED

**Musha Shugyo Probability = Base Chance + Clan Modifier + Personality Modifier**

Evaluated once at Gempukku, pass/fail on a single roll against this probability. The three components:

**Base Chance:** PROVISIONAL — exact value pending playtesting. Expected range: 5–15%. Most samurai enter service immediately.

**Clan Modifier:** Each clan has a modifier reflecting its cultural attitude toward individual pilgrimage.

| Clan | Modifier | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Dragon | +10% | Highest value on individual enlightenment and wandering (Togashi tradition, Kitsuki independence) |
| Crane | +5% | Value individual accomplishment and artistic growth; Kakita artisans seek inspiration |
| Phoenix | +5% | Spiritual growth and scholarly exploration are respected |
| Unicorn | +3% | Shinjo heritage of exploration; the clan was founded on a pilgrimage |
| Scorpion | +2% | Occasionally useful for building covert contacts across the Empire |
| Lion | −3% | Duty to the army comes first; personal quests are secondary to service |
| Crab | −5% | Every samurai is needed on the Wall; leaving is near-dereliction |
| Minor Clans | +0% | Varies; too few samurai to have a strong cultural pattern |
| Imperial Families | −3% | Imperial service demands immediate availability |

PROVISIONAL: all clan modifier values are tuning values.

**Personality Modifier:** The newly generated NPC's virtues affect the probability.

Bushido virtues:
- Yu (Courage): +3% — the courage to leave safety and seek the unknown
- Gi (Honesty): +1% — seeking truth through experience
- Jin (Compassion): +1% — desire to understand the lives of others
- Meiyo (Honor): +0% — neutral
- Makoto (Sincerity): +0% — neutral
- Rei (Courtesy): −1% — prefers structured social environments
- Chugi (Duty): −3% — duty-bound samurai enter service immediately

Shourido virtues:
- Dōsatsu (Insight): +3% — driven to see the truth behind things
- Ketsui (Determination): +2% — independent, self-reliant
- Chishiki (Knowledge): +2% — seeks knowledge through experience
- Ishi (Will): +1% — follows their own path
- Kyōryōku (Strength): +0% — neutral
- Seigyo (Control): −2% — prefers to operate within systems they can manage
- Kanpeki (Perfection): −1% — seeks the correct path, which is usually service

PROVISIONAL: all personality modifier values are tuning values.

### 57.48.3 Mechanical Status During Pilgrimage — LOCKED

**Duration:** 1 IC year (360 IC days), fixed. Cannot be ended early. The `musha_shugyo_end_ic_day` field on the character sheet records when the pilgrimage ends.

**Character Sheet Fields:**
- `musha_shugyo: bool` — true during pilgrimage, false otherwise
- `musha_shugyo_end_ic_day: int` — IC day the pilgrimage ends (sentinel −1 when not on pilgrimage)
- `original_lord_id: int` — the lord the NPC will return to after pilgrimage (sentinel −1 when not on pilgrimage)

**Feudal Status:**
- `lord_id` is suspended for the duration. The original lord is stored in `original_lord_id`. The NPC receives no objectives from any superior.
- Clan identity is preserved — the NPC is not treated as ronin. Other NPCs recognize them as a clan samurai on sanctioned pilgrimage. No disposition penalties, no access restrictions. The "considered to be a ronin" from source material is narrative flavor about freedom of movement, not a mechanical status change.
- The NPC retains their clan, family, school, and all character sheet values. Nothing is stripped or modified.

**NPC Engine Behavior:**

The NPC runs through the normal NPC Decision Engine with no lord directives. Their behavior follows the Wandering Togashi architecture (Section 2.2) adapted for pilgrimage:

**Standing Objective: SEEK_EXPERIENCE.** A broad objective that allows the NPC to take whatever actions the world presents — training, attending courts, investigating problems, helping strangers, fighting threats if encountered. The engine picks from the full action pool based on what is available at the NPC's current location.

**Destination Weighting by School Type:** The NPC's school type shapes where they gravitate, not what they do on arrival.

- Bushi: weighted toward settlements with dojos, military activity, martial training opportunities. Provinces with active security threats (banditry, insurgency) are mildly attractive — a chance to test skills in real conditions.
- Courtier: weighted toward settlements with active courts, high-Status residents, cross-clan social activity. Provincial and Family courts are accessible; attending a foreign clan's court as an observer is a natural pilgrimage activity.
- Shugenja: weighted toward settlements with temples, shrines, spiritually significant sites. Provinces with active spiritual disturbances (realm overlaps, elemental imbalances) are mildly attractive — a chance to study phenomena firsthand.

The destination weighting determines where the NPC travels. On arrival, the normal NPC engine scoring determines what they do — TRAIN_SKILL, ATTEND_COURT, INVESTIGATE, PATROL, daily conversations, all driven by the available opportunities at that settlement.

**No Special Rewards:** The NPC gains whatever they gain during the year through normal gameplay — skill ranks from TRAIN_SKILL actions, knowledge entries from encounters, met_characters from conversations, topics from travel. There is no lump-sum XP award or special bonus on return. The benefit of Musha Shugyo is organic: a broader contact network, more varied experience, and exposure to parts of the Empire they would never see in normal service.

### 57.48.4 Return to Service — LOCKED

On the IC day recorded in `musha_shugyo_end_ic_day`, the pilgrimage ends automatically:

1. `musha_shugyo` flag clears to false.
2. `lord_id` reactivates to the value stored in `original_lord_id`.
3. `original_lord_id` resets to sentinel −1.
4. `musha_shugyo_end_ic_day` resets to sentinel −1.
5. A BEGIN_TRAVEL need fires toward the lord's primary holding. The NPC physically travels back from wherever the pilgrimage left them. Normal travel time applies.
6. On arrival at the lord's holding, the NPC enters the normal objective assignment pipeline — available for lord-assigned objectives, vassal duties, and position appointments.

**Lord Death During Pilgrimage:** If the original lord died or lost their position during the NPC's absence, `original_lord_id` points to a dead or deposed character. On return, the NPC travels to their family's primary holding instead and enters the vacancy/assignment pipeline (Section 22.5). They are assigned to whoever currently holds authority over their family — which may be a different lord than the one who granted the pilgrimage.
