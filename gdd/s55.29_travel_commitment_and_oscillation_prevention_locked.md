## 55.29 Travel Commitment and Oscillation Prevention — LOCKED

STATUS: LOCKED

The stateless decomposition system fires fresh every AP, producing the best ImmediateNeed for the current game state. This is correct and produces natural multi-step behavior through repeated evaluation. However, stateless evaluation of travel decisions creates an oscillation vulnerability: an NPC pursuing a mobile target can issue CHANGE_DESTINATION every AP as the target’s known location updates, spending seasons in transit without ever arriving. This section defines three systems that prevent oscillation while preserving stateless decomposition: the Travel Frustration Counter (Phase 5 scoring modifier), Location Granularity (settlement + sublocation access model), and the Objective Progress Function (stall detection with personality-gated thresholds).

### 55.29.1 Travel Frustration Counter

**Problem: **CHANGE_DESTINATION costs 0 AP (Section 14, Action Categories). An NPC chasing a mobile target receives a FRESH location update via letter, Probe result, or daily conversation. The decomposition fires, sees the new location, returns TRAVEL_TO with a different target_settlement_id. CHANGE_DESTINATION fires. The NPC redirects. If the target moves again before arrival, the cycle repeats indefinitely. The NPC spends seasons in transit accomplishing nothing.

**Solution: **An objective-scoped counter, travel_redirects, increments each time CHANGE_DESTINATION fires while the NPC is TRAVELING and the destination change targets a different settlement for the same objective. The counter feeds a negative modifier into Phase 5 scoring for TRAVEL_TO and BEGIN_TRAVEL actions.

**Penalty schedule: **0 redirects: no penalty. 1 redirect: -5. 2 redirects: -15. 3+ redirects: -30. The penalty is applied as a component of the urgency_bonus calculation (negative urgency) in Phase 5. It affects only TRAVEL_TO and BEGIN_TRAVEL actions scored against the specific objective that accumulated the redirects.

**Effect on behavior: **At -30, TRAVEL_TO can still win if ObjAlign is high and other urgency bonuses apply, but it now competes seriously against alternatives: WRITE_LETTER (challenge or petition by letter, ObjAlign 60-70 for many NeedTypes), ATTEND_COURT (travel to a major court and wait for the target to come to you), LOCATE_CHARACTER (invest in better intelligence before committing to another journey). The NPC organically shifts from a "chase" strategy to an "intercept" or "lure" strategy.

**Personality interaction: **A Yu-primary character with vengeance urgency (+20 from vengeance_target_present equivalent bonuses) can absorb 2-3 redirects before the penalty overwhelms their action scores — they are stubborn, they keep chasing. A Dosatsu character hits the effective wall after 1 redirect and switches to letters and intelligence. The penalty interacts with existing personality leans without any personality-specific code.

**Counter reset: **The travel_redirects counter resets to 0 when the NPC arrives at a destination and spends at least 1 AP in a non-TRAVELING context (AT_COURT, AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, VISITING, ON_CAMPAIGN). Arrival means the chase succeeded at reaching a location — reset and start fresh if the NPC needs to travel again. The counter is stored on the objective, not the character. A new objective starts with travel_redirects = 0. If a lord reassigns the same objective to a different vassal via Information Transfer (Section 55.6), the counter transfers with the objective — the new vassal inherits the operational history.

**Counter scope: **The counter is per-objective, not per-destination. Redirecting for a different objective does not increment the Avenge objective’s counter. An NPC who is traveling for personal reasons and then receives a crisis reassignment does not accumulate frustration against the original objective — the crisis redirect uses CHANGE_DESTINATION but is driven by a different objective in the priority cascade.

### 55.29.2 Location Granularity — Settlement and Sublocation Access

**Problem: **IntelDB.get_known_location() currently returns a settlement_id. When an NPC arrives at the settlement, they are assumed to have access to the target. But Rokugan’s social structure means physical presence in a settlement does not grant access to all areas within it. A Dragon bushi in the town outside Bayushi Palace cannot enter the castle without invitation. A ronin at the Imperial Capital cannot access the Imperial Palace. Arrival should solve the travel problem and create a social access problem.

**Solution: **IntelDB location entries gain a sublocation field and the engine gains an access evaluation function. Location entries are structured as: {settlement_id, sublocation: enum, staleness, ic_day_recorded}. Sublocation values: PUBLIC (market, temple quarter, inn — accessible to anyone), COURT (castle court chamber — accessible by invitation, Status threshold, or open court), PRIVATE (lord’s personal quarters — household members and explicitly invited guests only), RESTRICTED (Imperial Palace, hidden temples — requires specific role, Imperial favor, or Kolat membership).

**Access evaluation: **When the decomposition reaches Phase 3 (REACH) and the NPC is in the same settlement as the target, a new check fires: _can_access_sublocation(ctx, target_sublocation). This function evaluates: PUBLIC — always true. COURT — true if: the NPC has an active invitation to this court, OR the NPC’s Status >= the court’s minimum Status threshold, OR the court is in open session (some courts are periodically open). PRIVATE — true only if the NPC is a household member or has explicit guest status. RESTRICTED — true only if the NPC holds a qualifying role (Imperial appointee, Emerald Magistrate, etc.) or has specific access flags on their character sheet.

**Access denial state: **When _can_access_sublocation returns false, the decomposition does not return TRAVEL_TO (the NPC is already in the right settlement). Instead, it evaluates local alternatives based on the denial reason. The denial is recorded in IntelDB as: {access_denied: true, settlement_id, sublocation, reason_code, denied_ic_day}. Reason codes: INSUFFICIENT_STATUS (Status too low for this court), NO_INVITATION (invitation-only court, NPC has no invitation), HOSTILE_CLAN (NPC’s clan is hostile to the host clan, access blocked), RESTRICTED_ROLE (requires a specific role the NPC does not hold), HOST_REFUSAL (the host NPC personally denied access — a social action result).

**Decomposition routing by reason code: **INSUFFICIENT_STATUS — long-term problem. The NPC cannot gain Status quickly. Routes to alternative approaches: SEND_LETTER (communicate through the gate), ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE on the host (find a reason they should grant access), ATTEND_COURT at a different venue where the target may appear. NO_INVITATION — solvable. Routes to RAISE_DISPOSITION targeting the host NPC (to earn an invitation), SEND_LETTER requesting an audience, or ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE. HOSTILE_CLAN — solvable through diplomacy or deception. Routes to RAISE_DISPOSITION with the host clan’s representatives, or for Scorpion/covert characters, DISGUISE actions (if implemented). HOST_REFUSAL — the host personally said no. Routes to ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE on the host, SEND_LETTER (escalate through formal channels), or WAIT (the target will leave eventually). RESTRICTED_ROLE — structural barrier. Routes to SEND_LETTER, or the NPC reports to their lord (REASSESS_OBJECTIVE) since this barrier may be unsolvable for their character.

**Arrival observation: **When an NPC arrives at or rests in a settlement, they become aware of other samurai present in the same access layer. An NPC staying at a public inn knows who else is in the public areas of the settlement. An NPC attending court knows who else is at court. An NPC does not automatically know who is in the castle’s private quarters unless they have access to those quarters. This observation happens at arrival and at each daily tick while present, updating the NPC’s IntelDB with FRESH location entries for observed characters. Characters in higher access layers are invisible to those in lower layers unless they venture into public areas.

### 55.29.3 Objective Progress Function

**Problem: **An NPC pursuing an objective may become structurally stuck — the target is unreachable, the approach is failing, or circumstances have changed to make the objective unachievable. The stateless decomposition will continue generating ImmediateNeeds indefinitely, cycling through available actions without recognizing that no progress is being made. The NPC needs a way to detect stall and escalate.

**Solution: **Every primary objective type has a progress function that returns a float 0.0-1.0 representing completion proximity. The function is evaluated once per seasonal tick. The result is stored on the objective as last_measured_progress. If the value has not increased since the previous seasonal tick, the objective’s seasons_without_progress counter increments. If it has increased (any positive delta), the counter resets to 0.

**Stored fields on Objective: **last_measured_progress (float, 0.0-1.0). seasons_without_progress (int, starts at 0). travel_redirects (int, starts at 0, Section 55.29.1). These fields represent a small concession to state within the otherwise stateless system — the decomposition itself remains stateless and fires fresh every AP, but the objective carries a progress snapshot for stall detection.

**Player visibility: **The progress value is displayed to the player on the vassal objective display as a percentage: "Bayushi Kei: Avenge Death of Togashi Haru — 35%." This gives lord-tier players visibility into which vassals are making headway and which are stuck, enabling informed reassignment decisions.

#### 55.29.3.1 Progress Function Definitions

Each of the 12 primary objective types (Section 55.28) has a type-specific progress function built from fields the engine already tracks. The pattern is consistent: query existing game state fields, weight them, return 0.0-1.0.

# 55.28.12 — Avenge Death/Disgrace of [Character X]

func _progress_avenge(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    if obj.culprit_known: score += 0.2

    var loc = IntelDB.get_known_location(obj.target_npc_id)

    if loc != null and loc.staleness != Staleness.UNKNOWN: score += 0.15

    if loc != null and loc.staleness == Staleness.FRESH: score += 0.05

    var leverage = SecretSystem.get_held_secrets_on(ctx.npc_id, obj.target_npc_id)

    score += min(leverage.size() * 0.1, 0.2)

    var same_settlement = ctx.current_settlement == loc.settlement_id if loc else false

    if same_settlement: score += 0.2

    var pretext = DuelSystem.has_pretext(ctx.npc_id, obj.target_npc_id)

    if pretext: score += 0.1

    if obj.wrong_type == WrongType.DISGRACE:

        var status_delta = _get_target_status_loss(obj.target_npc_id, obj)

        score += min(status_delta / obj.target_threshold, 0.3)

    return min(score, 1.0)

# 55.28.1 — Break the alliance between [X] and [Y]

func _progress_break_alliance(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    var contacts_x = ctx.known_contacts.get(obj.target_clan_id, [])

    var contacts_y = ctx.known_contacts.get(obj.target_clan_id_secondary, [])

    if contacts_x.size() > 0: score += 0.1

    if contacts_y.size() > 0: score += 0.1

    var anchor_x = _get_alliance_anchor(obj.target_clan_id, obj.target_clan_id_secondary, ctx)

    var anchor_y = _get_alliance_anchor(obj.target_clan_id_secondary, obj.target_clan_id, ctx)

    var disp_known = ctx.known_dispositions.has([anchor_y, anchor_x])

    if disp_known: score += 0.1

    var leverage = SecretSystem.get_held_secrets_on(ctx.npc_id, anchor_x)

    score += min(leverage.size() * 0.1, 0.2)

    var disp = ctx.known_dispositions.get([anchor_y, anchor_x], 50)

    var threshold = 25  # Friend threshold

    if disp <= threshold: return 1.0  # alliance broken

    score += max(0.0, 0.5 - (float(disp - threshold) / 100.0))

    return min(score, 1.0)

**Discovery Confidence Gate — General Principle: **Any progress function where completion depends on the NPC’s discovered information (not omniscient world state) should cap the near-completion threshold based on investigation thoroughness. The cap rises as the NPC invests more intelligence effort. Below the effort threshold (minimum 2 seasons of investigation AND a threshold count of intelligence actions), the near-completion cap stays at 0.85 instead of 0.95. This signals "I think I’m done but I haven’t been thorough enough to be sure." The confidence gate prevents premature completion claims when hidden allies, undiscovered secrets, or unmapped economic nodes might exist. Objectives that target known, specific entities (a single lord, a specific province, the Emperor) do not need the confidence gate. Objectives that target discovered networks (ally networks, economic infrastructure, secret collections) do. The gate is applied in: Isolate Character (55.28.2), Remove from Position (55.28.5), Expose Secret (55.28.8), and Sabotage Economy (55.28.11).

The remaining 10 progress functions are defined below. All follow the same structural pattern as _progress_avenge and _progress_break_alliance above: query existing game state fields, weight them, return 0.0-1.0.

**# 55.28.2 — Isolate [Character X] politically**

# Completion: X has zero allies at disposition Friend (+25) or above among Status 3+ characters.

func _progress_isolate_character(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    var target_x = obj.target_npc_id

    var known_allies = _get_known_allies_of(target_x, ctx, min_status=3)

    if known_allies.size() == 0:

        var intel = ctx.action_log.count_actions_on(

            target_x, ["PROBE", "READ_CHARACTER"], all_time=true)

        score += min(intel * 0.05, 0.1)

        return score

    score += 0.1  # network partially mapped

    var total = known_allies.size()

    var severed = 0

    for ally_id in known_allies:

        var disp = ctx.known_dispositions.get([ally_id, target_x], null)

        if disp != null and disp < 25:

            severed += 1

    score += (float(severed) / float(total)) * 0.6

    var remaining = known_allies.filter(

        func(id): return ctx.known_dispositions.get([id, target_x], 50) >= 25)

    var leverage_count = 0

    for ally_id in remaining:

        leverage_count += SecretSystem.get_held_secrets_on(ctx.npc_id, ally_id).size()

    score += min(leverage_count * 0.05, 0.15)

    var allies_present = remaining.filter(func(id): return id in ctx.characters_present)

    if allies_present.size() > 0: score += 0.1

    # Discovery confidence gate

    if severed == total and total > 0:

        var seasons = _objective_age_seasons(obj, ctx)

        var intel_count = ctx.action_log.count_actions_on(

            target_x, ["PROBE", "READ_CHARACTER", "OBSERVE_COURT_ATTENDEES"], all_time=true)

        if seasons >= 2 and intel_count >= 4:

            score = max(score, 0.95)  # thorough — confident

        else:

            score = max(score, 0.85)  # quick — might have missed allies

    return min(score, 1.0)

**# 55.28.3 — Gain a Winter Court invitation for my lord**

# Completion: Host issues formal invitation when disposition toward lord reaches Friend+ (+31).

func _progress_winter_court_invitation(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    var lord_id = obj.context_npc_id

    var host_id = obj.target_npc_id

    var host_disp = ctx.known_dispositions.get([host_id, lord_id], null)

    if host_disp == null:

        var host_clan = CharacterDB.get_clan(host_id)

        var contacts = ctx.known_contacts.get(host_clan, [])

        score += min(contacts.size() * 0.05, 0.1)

        return score

    score += 0.1  # baseline known

    var threshold = 31

    if host_disp >= threshold:

        return 0.95  # threshold met, awaiting formal invitation

    var distance = threshold - host_disp

    score += max(0.0, 0.5 * (1.0 - float(distance) / 61.0))

    var host_clan = CharacterDB.get_clan(host_id)

    var contacts = ctx.known_contacts.get(host_clan, [])

    score += min(contacts.size() * 0.03, 0.1)

    if host_id in ctx.characters_present: score += 0.15

    var leverage = SecretSystem.get_held_secrets_on(ctx.npc_id, host_id)

    score += min(leverage.size() * 0.05, 0.1)

    return min(score, 1.0)

**# 55.28.4 — Have [Character X] appointed to [Position]**

# Completion: X is formally appointed. Detected when X’s character sheet registers the position.

func _progress_appoint_character(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    var target_x = obj.target_npc_id

    var position = obj.target_position_id

    var appointer = _get_appointing_lord(position, ctx)

    if WorldState.is_position_vacant(position): score += 0.15

    var appointer_disp = ctx.known_dispositions.get([appointer, target_x], null)

    if appointer_disp == null:

        var contacts = ctx.known_contacts.get(CharacterDB.get_clan(appointer), [])

        score += min(contacts.size() * 0.03, 0.1)

        return score

    score += 0.05

    var threshold = 31

    if appointer_disp >= threshold:

        score += 0.4

    else:

        var distance = threshold - appointer_disp

        score += max(0.0, 0.4 * (1.0 - float(distance) / 61.0))

    var known_rivals = _get_known_rival_candidates(position, target_x, ctx)

    if known_rivals.size() > 0:

        var weakened = known_rivals.filter(

            func(id): return ctx.known_dispositions.get([appointer, id], 50) < 25).size()

        score += (float(weakened) / float(known_rivals.size())) * 0.15

    else:

        score += 0.15

    var lev = SecretSystem.get_held_secrets_on(ctx.npc_id, appointer).size()

    for r in known_rivals:

        lev += SecretSystem.get_held_secrets_on(ctx.npc_id, r).size()

    score += min(lev * 0.05, 0.1)

    if appointer in ctx.characters_present: score += 0.1

    return min(score, 1.0)

**# 55.28.5 — Remove [Character X] from [Position]**

# Completion: X no longer holds the position. Discovery confidence applies (hidden protectors).

func _progress_remove_from_position(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    var target_x = obj.target_npc_id

    var position_id = obj.target_resource

    var removing_lord = _get_appointing_authority(position_id, ctx)

    if removing_lord == null: return 0.05

    score += 0.1

    var lord_disp = ctx.known_dispositions.get([removing_lord, target_x], null)

    if lord_disp == null: return score + 0.05

    if lord_disp <= 0: score += 0.35

    elif lord_disp <= 15: score += 0.2

    elif lord_disp <= 25: score += 0.1

    var leverage = SecretSystem.get_held_secrets_on(ctx.npc_id, target_x)

    score += min(leverage.size() * 0.1, 0.2)

    var known_allies = _get_known_allies_of(target_x, ctx, min_status=3)

    if known_allies.size() > 0:

        var severed = known_allies.filter(

            func(id): return ctx.known_dispositions.get([id, target_x], 50) < 25).size()

        score += (float(severed) / float(known_allies.size())) * 0.15

    else:

        score += 0.1

    if removing_lord in ctx.characters_present: score += 0.1

    # Discovery confidence gate

    var seasons = _objective_age_seasons(obj, ctx)

    var intel = ctx.action_log.count_actions_on(

        target_x, ["PROBE", "READ_CHARACTER", "GATHER_INTELLIGENCE"], all_time=true)

    if lord_disp <= 0 and known_allies.size() == 0:

        if seasons >= 2 and intel >= 4: score = max(score, 0.9)

        else: score = min(score, 0.8)

    return min(score, 1.0)

**# 55.28.6 — Resolve [Clan War crisis] through negotiation**

# Completion: War Status formally ended through peaceful mechanism. Annihilation = failure.

func _progress_negotiate_peace(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    var clan_a = obj.target_clan_id

    var clan_b = obj.target_clan_id_secondary

    var war = WarSystem.get_active_war(clan_a, clan_b)

    if not war: return 1.0

    var contacts_a = ctx.known_contacts.get(clan_a, [])

    var contacts_b = ctx.known_contacts.get(clan_b, [])

    if contacts_a.size() > 0: score += 0.05

    if contacts_b.size() > 0: score += 0.05

    if contacts_a.is_empty() or contacts_b.is_empty(): return score

    score += 0.05

    var leader_a = _get_clan_war_leader(clan_a, war)

    var leader_b = _get_clan_war_leader(clan_b, war)

    var disp_a = ctx.dispositions.get(leader_a, 0)

    var disp_b = ctx.dispositions.get(leader_b, 0)

    var trust_a = clamp(float(disp_a + 30) / 60.0, 0.0, 1.0)

    var trust_b = clamp(float(disp_b + 30) / 60.0, 0.0, 1.0)

    score += ((trust_a + trust_b) / 2.0) * 0.25

    var score_diff = abs(war.get_war_score(clan_a) - war.get_war_score(clan_b))

    score += max(0.0, 0.2 - (score_diff * 0.01))

    if _knows_clan_seeks_peace(clan_a, ctx): score += 0.1

    if _knows_clan_seeks_peace(clan_b, ctx): score += 0.1

    if CourtSystem.get_peace_court(war) != null: score += 0.1

    if _has_imperial_support_for_peace(war, ctx): score += 0.1

    return min(score, 1.0)

    if status != null:

        if status.stability <= 25: score += 0.1

        elif status.stability <= 50: score += 0.05

    if not WarSystem.is_province_affected(target_province): score += 0.05

    if EconomySystem.get_active_merchants(target_province) > 0: score += 0.05

    return min(score, 1.0)

# Completion: Clan X’s aggregate output drops below threshold. NPC estimates from known info. Discovery confidence applies.

func _progress_sabotage_economy(obj, ctx) -> float:

    var score = 0.0

    var target_clan = obj.target_clan_id

    var known_provinces = _get_known_enemy_provinces(target_clan, ctx)

    if known_provinces.is_empty():

        var intel = ctx.action_log.count_actions_on(

            null, ["GATHER_INTELLIGENCE", "PROBE"],

            all_time=true, clan_filter=target_clan)

        score += min(intel * 0.03, 0.1)

        return score

    score += 0.1

    var estimated_total = 0.0

    var estimated_damage = 0.0

    for pid in known_provinces:

        var base = _estimate_province_max_output(pid, ctx)

        estimated_total += base

        var status = ctx.province_statuses.get(pid, null)

        if status != null:

            var current = _estimate_province_current_output(pid, status)

            estimated_damage += (base - current)

    if estimated_total > 0:

        score += (estimated_damage / estimated_total) * 0.4

    var active_insurgencies = known_provinces.filter(

        func(id): var s = ctx.province_statuses.get(id, null)

                  return s != null and s.active_insurgency_id != null).size()

    score += min(active_insurgencies * 0.05, 0.1)

    var disrupted = _count_disrupted_trade_routes(target_clan, ctx)

    score += min(disrupted * 0.05, 0.1)

    if TopicSystem.has_active_sanctions(target_clan, ctx): score += 0.1

    # Discovery confidence gate

    var seasons = _objective_age_seasons(obj, ctx)

    var intel_count = ctx.action_log.count_actions_on(

        null, ["GATHER_INTELLIGENCE", "PROBE", "INVESTIGATE_PROVINCE"],

        all_time=true, clan_filter=target_clan)

    if seasons < 2 or intel_count < 3:

        score = min(score, 0.7)

    return min(score, 1.0)

#### 55.29.3.2 Stall Detection and REASSESS_OBJECTIVE

The stall detector runs at each seasonal tick after the progress function:

func _check_objective_stall(obj, ctx):

    var current_progress = _get_progress_func(obj.objective_type).call(obj, ctx)

    var delta = current_progress - obj.last_measured_progress

    if delta <= 0.0:

        obj.seasons_without_progress += 1

    else:

        obj.seasons_without_progress = 0

    obj.last_measured_progress = current_progress

    var threshold = _get_stall_threshold(obj, ctx)

    if obj.seasons_without_progress >= threshold:

        return NeedType.REASSESS_OBJECTIVE

    return null

**Personality-gated stall thresholds: **The number of seasons an NPC tolerates zero progress before triggering REASSESS_OBJECTIVE varies by personality. Yu / Ketsui / Kyoryoku (perseverance cluster): 5 seasons. These characters do not give up easily. Yu may escalate (INITIATE_WAR_CHECK) rather than abandon. Dosatsu / Seigyo (adaptation cluster): 3 seasons. They abandon the direct approach and reframe — the objective is not dropped but the strategy shifts from physical pursuit to indirect methods (DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP, EXPOSE_SECRET_PUBLIC through intermediaries). Jin / Gi (acceptance cluster): 2 seasons. If circumstances have made the objective unachievable, they accept it and return to their standing objective. The honor debt remains narratively but the NPC has made peace with it. Chugi (duty cluster): if lord-assigned, never self-abandons — always reports back via SEND_LETTER to lord. If self-selected, 3 seasons, then abandons to serve their lord’s other needs.

**REASSESS_OBJECTIVE NeedType: **Added to enums.gd: NeedType.REASSESS_OBJECTIVE. When stall detection triggers, the decomposition returns NeedType.REASSESS_OBJECTIVE instead of the normal ImmediateNeed. This NeedType bypasses Phases 3–5 (Generate Options, Personality Filter, Score All Options) entirely — it does not produce a scored action list. Instead, it directly generates a specific response based on objective assignment source. No objective_alignment.json entry exists for REASSESS_OBJECTIVE because it never enters the scoring pipeline. This NeedType produces different actions based on objective assignment source. Lord-assigned objectives: the NPC writes a letter to their lord reporting the stall. "I cannot reach the target. Requesting new orders." The lord’s next Strategic Review (Section 55.10) evaluates the stalled objective and either reassigns it to someone with better access, modifies it (change approach parameters), or cancels it. The vassal receives a new primary objective or updated parameters. Self-selected objectives: the personality threshold determines the response. Perseverance personalities continue with modified approach. Adaptation personalities reframe. Acceptance personalities abandon and return to standing objective.

**Information Transfer on reassignment: **When a lord reassigns a stalled objective to a different vassal, the standard Information Transfer rules (Section 55.6) apply. The counter fields transfer with the objective: last_measured_progress, seasons_without_progress, and travel_redirects. The new vassal inherits the operational history — they know this has been attempted for N seasons without progress. However, the personality threshold is evaluated against the new character’s personality. A stalled objective transferred from a Jin vassal (threshold 2, triggered at season 2) to a Yu vassal (threshold 5) gives the Yu vassal 3 more seasons of patience before they would stall. The information transfers; the willingness to persevere is personal.
