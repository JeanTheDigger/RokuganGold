## 29.7 Unicorn Clan Schools

**BASIC SCHOOLS:**

**Horiuchi Shugenja:**

Masters of meishodo — a hybrid of gaijin name magic and Rokugani shugenja tradition developed during the Unicorn's centuries of travel. Rather than trapping kami in items as the original gaijin sorcerers did, Unicorn meishodo amulets appeal to surrounding elemental kami to perform effects. Each amulet holds one unalterable effect. Meishodo sacrifices power for speed — a jealously guarded Unicorn secret.

- Benefit: +1 Stamina

- Skills: Calligraphy, Craft: Meishodo, Defense, Lore: Theology, Meditation, Spellcraft (Spell Research), any 1 High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 4.5 | Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, Knife, Scroll/Amulet Satchel, Traveling Pack, 4 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Earth / Air | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Earth, 2 Water, 1 Fire

- Technique — Mysteries of Meishodo: Create a meishodo amulet by rolling Craft: Meishodo/Ring (TN 15 + 5 × spell Mastery Level), taking hours equal to the Mastery Level. On success, the amulet casts the chosen spell as one Complex Action regardless of Mastery Level — no Spell Casting Roll required but no Raises apply (not even Free Raises). Expend a spell slot as normal. May only create meishodo from spells of Mastery Level equal to or less than your Shugenja School Rank; can never create meishodo of Mastery Level 4, 5, or 6. Other meishodo-trained shugenja may use your amulets.

**Ide Emissary [Courtier] [CR]:**

The most pacifistic courtier tradition among the Great Clans. The Ide specialize in protecting themselves from the ramifications of their actions and defusing conflicts among others. Their Techniques are entirely non-offensive — adapting to new customs instantly, reading true emotions, and shifting attitudes toward peace.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Calligraphy, Commerce, Courtier, Etiquette (Conversation), Horsemanship, Sincerity (Honesty), any one High or Perform Skill

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Extravagant Clothing, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Calligraphy Set, Traveling Pack, 10 koku

- Rank 1 — The Heart Speaks: In any situation where you might inadvertently give offense (through accident or ignorance), roll Etiquette (Courtesy)/Awareness (TN 20) to avoid doing so. Free Raise on Sincerity (Honesty) rolls. Any time you roll Sincerity (Deceit), TN is increased by +5.

- Rank 2 — Piercing the Veils: Make a Contested Etiquette/Awareness vs target’s Etiquette/Willpower roll to read someone’s true emotions in a social situation. On success, gain a general sense of their true feelings. Raise for more specific information (GM’s adjudication).

- Rank 3 — The Heart Listens: Spend at least 10 minutes in conversation, then make a Contested Sincerity (Honesty)/Awareness vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Willpower roll to shift the target’s attitude in a positive direction. Two successful Raises make the target regard you as an Ally for the remainder of the day (GM approval). Cannot be used on Sworn Enemies.

- Rank 4 — Answering the Heart: When using your Rank 2 and Rank 3 Techniques, roll additional unkept dice equal to your School Rank.

- Rank 5 — The Immovable Hand of Peace: Spend a Void Point and make a Contested Sincerity (Honesty)/Awareness vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Awareness roll. On success, the target cannot take any hostile action against you (physical, social, or magical) for hours equal to twice your School Rank, as long as you likewise refrain from hostility. Does not work against those with Honor Rank below 2.0 or no Honor Rank.

**Iuchi Shugenja [CR]:**

Wild and carefree — as many lessons are taught in the open plains as in the temple. The Iuchi embrace meishodo and a diversity of magical styles born from centuries of travel. Their Technique enhances movement and speed, and they cast Travel spells with unmatched power.

- Benefit: +1 Perception

- Skills: Battle, Calligraphy (Cipher), Horsemanship, Lore: Theology, Meditation, Spellcraft, any one High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Extravagant Clothing, Wakizashi, Scroll Satchel, any 1 weapon, Calligraphy Set, Traveling Pack, Horse (Unicorn Riding Horse), 10 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Water / Fire | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Water, 2 Earth, 1 Air

- Technique — Spirit of the Wind: Complex Action — sacrifice one spell slot of any element to grant one target individual an additional Simple Move Action on their next Turn (lost if not used). Free Raise on any spell with the Travel keyword.

**Moto Bushi [CR]:**

The living stereotype of the Unicorn Clan — born of a union between Rokugani people and gaijin nomads of the northern steppes. Their unfettered fighting style earns them both awe on the battlefield and enmity from traditionalists. Heavy cavalry specialists with Techniques centered entirely on the Full Attack Stance and maximum damage output.

- Benefit: +1 Strength

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Horsemanship, Hunting, Kenjutsu (Scimitar), any one Bugei Skill, any one Skill

- Honor: 3.5 | Outfit: Riding or Heavy Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, Heavy Weapon or Polearm, Traveling Pack, Horse (Unicorn Riding Horse), any one weapon, 10 koku

- Rank 1 — The Way of the Unicorn: Wield any two-handed weapon with one hand (except bows). +1k0 to damage rolls while mounted, using a scimitar, or using a two-handed melee weapon (bonuses do not stack). Scimitars have the Samurai keyword for you.

- Rank 2 — Shinsei’s Smile: Gain a bonus to attack rolls equal to half your opponent’s Wound Rank TN penalties (rounded down). Example: opponent in Crippled Wound Rank grants +10 to attack roll totals.

- Rank 3 — Desert Wind Strike: Make attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action when using a melee weapon.

- Rank 4 — The Charge of Madness: Once per skirmish, if you bring a target down to the Out Wound Level, immediately make one additional attack as a Free Action against a different target. No Raises allowed on this attack.

- Rank 5 — Moto Cannot Yield: While mounted or in Full Attack Stance, keep additional damage dice equal to half your Strength (rounded down). Only applies when wielding a two-handed weapon or a weapon with the Samurai keyword.

**Moto Death Priest [Shugenja] [IH]:**

Adherents of the Shi-Tien Yen-Wang — the wrathful gaijin death gods worshipped by the Moto before joining the Unicorn Clan. After centuries of the Moto Curse (the Lords of Death’s wrath at being forsaken), a bargain was struck near the end of the War of Spirits: the Moto would resume worship in exchange for the gods staying their wrath. Death Priests bring the blessings of death to those deemed enemies of the Unicorn.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower

- Skills: Athletics, Horsemanship, Hunting, Lore: Theology, Meditation, Spellcraft, any 1 Skill

- Honor: 3.5 | Outfit: Sturdy Traveling Clothing, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, Horse (Unicorn Steed), 5 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Earth / Air | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Earth, 2 Fire, 1 Water

- Technique — Blessings of the Shi-Tien Yen-Wang: Simple Action — sacrifice a spell slot and target one enemy within School Rank × 100 feet. Make a Contested Willpower Roll. On success, the target may not benefit from exploding dice or Advantages that allow rerolling for Rounds equal to your School Rank. Free Raise on any spell with the Travel keyword.

**Moto Vindicator [Bushi]:**

Born from the shame of Moto Tsume’s fall to the Shadowlands. When Tsume led the majority of Moto forces into darkness and they became some of Jigoku’s greatest tools, the surviving Moto swore to eradicate all Tainted creatures bearing their name. Techniques train them to spot Taint, neutralize opponent advantages, and fight past physical limits against their sworn enemies.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower

- Skills: Horsemanship, Hunting, Investigation (Notice), Kenjutsu, Kyujutsu, Lore: Shadowlands, any 1 Bugei Skill

- Honor: 3.5 | Outfit: Heavy Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 Weapon, Traveling Pack, Riding Horse, 5 koku

- Special: Must have either the Higher Purpose (Defeat the Shadowlands) Advantage or the Driven (Defeat the Shadowlands) Disadvantage.

- Rank 1 — Purity of the Breath: At the start of each Round, choose one effect until end of Round: reduce TN penalties from Wound Ranks by School Rank + Willpower, or add School Rank + Willpower to Armor TN. Both bonuses are doubled when in a skirmish against Shadowlands creatures.

- Rank 2 — Facing the Dark Within: +2k0 to all Investigation rolls; increased to +2k1 when specifically attempting to notice the effects of Shadowlands Taint.

- Rank 3 — Justice of Our Ancestors: Make melee attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action.

- Rank 4 — Avenging Our Own: +2k0 to attack and damage rolls against any enemy who has attacked you in a skirmish, and against any creature with the Shadowlands Taint.

- Rank 5 — Bloodied but Unbowed: Once per skirmish, Free Action to activate. While making melee attacks, gain a bonus to damage roll totals equal to the TN penalties you are suffering from Wound Ranks (using default penalties, ignoring modifications). Lasts two full Rounds. May extend by one additional Round by taking 10 Wounds.

**Shinjo Bushi [EE]:**

Mounted tactics specialists who incorporate gaijin fighting methods encountered during the clan’s centuries of travel — active parry, riposte, and other oddities unfamiliar to most Rokugani. A unique defensive style that rewards those who mistake restraint for weakness.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Horsemanship 2, Kenjutsu, Kyujutsu, any 1 Skill

- Honor: 4.5 | Outfit: Ashigaru or Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, Bow, Knife, Traveling Pack, Horse, 10 koku

- Rank 1 — The Way of the Ki-Rin: When spending a Void Point to add +1k1 to any School Skill Roll, also add your Horsemanship Skill Rank to the total. Cannot be used in Center Stance.

- Rank 2 — Dance of the Blade: While in Full Defense Stance, when an opponent succeeds with an attack targeting you, take a Free Action to make a Contested Agility Roll against that opponent. On success, the attack misses. May be done School Rank times per Round.

- Rank 3 — The Four Winds Strike: Attack as a Simple Action with weapons with the Samurai keyword. While mounted, also attack as a Simple Action with a bow.

- Rank 4 — Spirit of the Blade Unleashed: While in Defense or Full Defense Stance, when an opponent makes a melee attack targeting you, after the attack resolves take a Free Action to make one melee attack against that opponent (immediately assumes Attack Stance). Usable School Rank times per skirmish, once per Round.

- Rank 5 — Dancing With the Fortunes: While mounted, when you wish to spend a Void Point, roll Void Ring (TN 20) as a Free Action. On success, gain the mechanical benefit of spending the Void Point without actually spending it. On failure, spend it as normal. Usable times per skirmish equal to Void Points at skirmish start. On foot, TN increases to 30.

**Utaku Battle Maiden [Bushi] [CR]:**

The finest and most respected cavalry in all the Empire. The Utaku alone have the right to breed, raise, and ride the legendary Utaku steeds — the finest horses in the world. Techniques focus on mounted combat with Honor as a key multiplier — the higher the Battle Maiden’s Honor, the greater her bonuses.

- Benefit: +1 Reflexes

- Skills: Battle, Defense, Horsemanship 2, Kenjutsu, Sincerity, any one High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Light or Riding Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, Horse (Utaku Warhorse), 10 koku

- Rank 1 — Riding in Harmony: Gain a bonus equal to your Honor Rank to one attack per Round. While mounted, may instead apply this bonus to one damage roll. Also add Honor Rank to all Horsemanship roll totals.

- Rank 2 — The Void of War: During Stage 1 of any Combat Round, add 5 to either your Initiative Score or your Armor TN. Lasts until you use this Technique again or the skirmish ends.

- Rank 3 — Sensing the Breeze: While mounted, make attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action.

- Rank 4 — Wind Never Stops: While mounted, spend a Void Point and take a Simple Action to charge and attack at the end of your movement (movement and attack use the same Simple Action, using the steed’s movement rate). On a successful hit, +2k1 to all damage rolls this Turn.

- Rank 5 — Otaku’s Blessing: At the start of your Turn, spend a Void Point as a Free Action. Add Honor Rank to all damage rolls and Bugei Skill Rolls this Turn. Stacks with Riding in Harmony.

**Utaku Mounted Infantry [Bushi]:**

Soldiers who ride horses while the army maneuvers but dismount to fight on foot — developed when the Unicorn encountered enemies who could not be defeated by a cavalry charge alone. Originated with Otaku Buki during the clan’s wandering years. Enrollment expanded sharply after the Shinjo disgrace during the Hidden Emperor era drove Utaku men away from the Shinjo Bushi School.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Athletics, Battle, Defense, Kenjutsu, Polearms, Spears, any one Bugei or High Skill

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Normal Clothing, Daisho, any one spear or polearm, Light Armor, Steed (Unicorn Riding Horse), Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — Choose Your Weapon: Select Kenjutsu, Polearms, or Spears — gain a free Emphasis of your choice in that Skill. +1k0 to attack rolls with the chosen Skill. If Spears or Polearms: in Defense or Full Defense Stance while wielding that weapon, add Skill Ranks to Armor TN. If Kenjutsu: add half Skill Rank (rounded down) instead.

- Rank 2 — Speed of My Sisters: On foot, move as though Water Ring is 1 Rank higher. +1k0 to Initiative rolls.

- Rank 3 — All-Fronts Attack: With your chosen weapon Skill, attack as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action.

- Rank 4 — Utaku’s Thunder: Once per skirmish while wielding your chosen weapon, spend a Void Point to add Honor Rank in unkept dice to your attack roll. Movement bonus from Rank 2 now treats Water Ring as 2 higher instead of 1.

- Rank 5 — Epic of My Name: Rank 1 Armor TN bonus now also applies in Attack Stance. Once per skirmish in Full Attack Stance, add +4k2 to a damage roll with your chosen weapon (Spears or Polearms may also use this in Attack Stance).

**ADVANCED SCHOOLS:**

**The White Guard [Bushi] [CR]:**

Elite heavy cavalry — the best warriors of the Moto family. They fight against gaijin forces threatening Rokugan and anything that might tarnish the Moto legacy. Their extreme pious dedication to the Lords of Death and their merciless tactics make them feared across the Empire.

- Requirements: Earth 5, Agility 5, Strength 4 | Skills: Horsemanship 5, Lore: Theology 5 | Other: Must be a devout follower of the Lords of Death.

- Rank 1 — Pale Face of Death: Spend a Void Point as a Free Action at the start of your Turn to choose one opponent within 30’. They suffer TN penalties to all actions equal to your Lore: Theology Skill Rank for the rest of the encounter. Only one target may be affected at a time.

- Rank 2 — Moto’s Strength: Ignore TN penalties from Wound Levels equal to double your Lore: Theology Skill Rank.

- Rank 3 — Fury of Heaven: When you make an attack, spend a Void Point to add five times your Lore: Theology Skill Rank to the total of your roll.

**ALTERNATE PATHS:**

**Calm Heart Duelist [Bushi]:**

The only notable traditional dueling dojo in Unicorn lands, based in Dark Edge Village. Ironically, most adherents come from the peaceful Ide family — born from the need to resolve conflicts without needlessly taking life.

- Replaces: Any Unicorn Bushi School 3. Requires: Iaijutsu 3, Lore: Law 1.

- Technique — The Calm Heart Conquers: +1k0 to any Iaijutsu Skill Roll when not striking to kill (duel to first blood or skirmish). Make melee attacks as a Simple Action when wielding a katana or wakizashi.

**Hand of Peace [Courtier]:**

Dedicated pacifists who follow Ide’s peaceful dictums to their logical conclusion. They still carry samurai weapons but refuse to ever use them. They serve as effective diplomats with the Crane and especially the Phoenix.

- Replaces: Ide Emissary 4. Requires: Honor 5.0+.

- Technique — Ide’s Ideal: When able to propose a peaceful solution to a diplomatic problem, roll Courtier (Rhetoric)/Awareness vs opponent’s Etiquette (Courtesy)/Willpower. On success, the opponent is forced to make a meaningful concession (GM chooses the specifics). Usable twice per encounter. If you commit an act of violence that inflicts damage on a fellow Rokugani, lose this Technique’s benefits for one month.

**Ide Caravan Master [Courtier]:**

The Ide are the Unicorn’s most numerous merchant patrons. Their famous caravans travel across the Empire selling the clan’s unusual wares — an incredibly profitable endeavor.

- Replaces: Ide Emissary 1 (uses same Benefit, Skills, Honor, and Outfit). Requires: Commerce 2.

- Technique — The Gilded Road: +1k1 on all Commerce Skill Rolls, and on all Social Skill Rolls made with your customers. Gain one level of the Wealthy Advantage and one instance of the Way of the Land Advantage for free.

**Ide Trader [Courtier]:**

Carves a mercantile niche with friendliness and eagerness to help — mostly a facade. The best Ide Traders are as cunning and ruthless as the most cutthroat Yoritomo.

- Replaces: Ide Emissary 2. Requires: Commerce 3, must pursue a career as a merchant patron.

- Technique — Brisk Economy: Cannot lose Honor or Glory for using the Commerce Skill in public. Persuade someone to become a mercantile partner by rolling Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness with a bonus equal to double your Commerce Skill Rank (TN 25 for commoners, Contested vs Etiquette/Willpower for samurai). On success, the target becomes a free Ally with 1 point of Devotion.

**Iuchi Couriers (Lord of the Plains) [Shugenja]:**

Jokingly called Lords of the Plains for their incredible movement speed. They soothe the spirits of the Earth as they ride, creating a path of least resistance for their steeds.

- Replaces: Iuchi Shugenja 2. Requires: Horsemanship 3, Way of the Land Advantage.

- Technique — Beyond the Wind: Five-minute ritual + Meditation roll (TN 15) to bless your horse or another rider’s horse. For hours equal to your Earth Ring, the horse suffers no movement penalties based on terrain and does not fatigue. Maximum horses blessed simultaneously equals your Earth Ring.

**Iuchi Traveler [Shugenja] [CR]:**

Children of the Four Winds who spend their lives on the road. They possess the most sacred Unicorn magic — teleportation. Rituals originally developed for exploration were later adapted for war.

- Replaces: Iuchi Shugenja 3 or Horiuchi Shugenja 3. Requires: Athletics 2, Horsemanship 2.

- Technique — Where the Wind Wills: Spend 1 Void Point and expend 1 Air or Water spell slot (not Void) as a Simple Action to instantly teleport up to School Rank × 100 feet. Affects only you and your carried materials. Expend a second Void Point to also teleport a horse-sized mount you are riding.

**Moto Fanatic [Bushi]:**

Full commitment to aggressive warfare — charging front lines with alarming frequency and suffering surprisingly few casualties through a mixture of skill and complete disregard for personal safety.

- Replaces: Any Moto Bushi School 4.

- Technique — Reckless Abandon: While in Full Attack Stance, spend a Void Point as a Simple Action to gain Reduction equal to your School Rank for as long as you remain in Full Attack Stance.

**Rajya ke Varisa [Bushi]:**

A cross-clan group of Crab, Unicorn, and Mantis samurai who study the fighting techniques of the Ivory Kingdoms kshatriya, adapting them to Rokugani combat.

- Replaces: Hida Bushi 4, Shinjo Bushi 4, Moto Bushi 4, Yoritomo Bushi

- Technique — Legacy of the Kshatriya: Twice per skirmish, take a Free Action to spend a Void Point before an attack. If the attack succeeds, add unkept dice to the damage roll equal to your Lore: Theology Skill Rank.

**Shinjo Magistrate [Bushi]:**

The Unicorn tradition of upholding the law and protecting common folk — often putting them in conflict with the Scorpion and criminal organizations. After Lady Shinjo’s purge of the Kolat, the Shinjo Magistrates remade themselves as specialists in rooting out hidden corruption.

- Replaces: Shinjo Bushi 2, Utaku Battle Maiden 2, Moto Bushi 2, Moto Vindicator 2. Requires: Investigation 3, Lore: Law 3.

- Technique — Eyes of the Vigilant: +1k0 to Investigation rolls when locating clues or testing truthfulness. +1k0 to Hunting rolls when tracking known criminals or traitors. +1k0 to all Weapon Skill Rolls when attacking such persons.

**Shinjo Scout [Bushi] [CR]:**

The advance unit of the Unicorn during their centuries of travel outside Rokugan. Upon their return, the Shinjo Scouts traded knowledge with the Hiruma Scouts of the Crab and grew even more formidable.

- Replaces: Moto Bushi 2 or Shinjo Bushi 2. Requires: Stealth 3.

- Technique — The Swift Soul: Use the Stealth Skill while on horseback. Treated as having the Way of the Land Advantage no matter where you are. +1k0 to all attack rolls while Mounted.

**Unicorn Bariqu Wrestler [Bushi]:**

Bariqu wrestling dates back to the Ujik-Hai — the gaijin nomads who became the Moto family. Distinct from sumai, it focuses on throwing opponents using joint-locks and paralyzing holds rather than driving them from a ring.

- Replaces: Any Moto Bushi School 2, Shinjo Bushi 2. Requires: Jiujutsu (Bariqu) 3.

- Technique — Way of the Ujik-Hai: If your Grapple opponent does not have the Bariqu Emphasis of Jiujutsu, +1k1 when rolling to control the Grapple. If your Agility Rank is higher than your opponent’s in a Grapple, +2k0 when rolling to control the Grapple. Both bonuses stack.

**Unicorn Doomseeker [Shugenja]:**

A Rokugani adaptation of an ancient Burning Sands order dedicated to destroying immortal supernatural evils — originally khadi, later expanded to demons and rakshasa. Extraordinarily rare; never more than one Rokugani Doomseeker in the same generation.

- Replaces: Iuchi Shugenja 4, Horiuchi Shugenja 4, Moto Death Priest 4. Requires: Willpower 5. Must find a personal teacher who will share the secrets of the Doomseeker tradition.

- Technique — Path of the Doomseeker: In the presence of a hidden demon, monster, khadi, or similar entity, roll Willpower (TN 25) to sense its presence and location. If aware of such a creature (by any means), spend a spell slot as a Complex Action to make a Contested Willpower roll — on success, gain one key piece of information about how it can be unmasked or defeated (GM’s choice). Cannot target the same entity more than once per month.

**Unicorn Wardog Masters [Bushi]:**

The Unicorn brought back a breed of war-dog unknown within Rokugan from their gaijin travels. The Wardog Masters train these fierce, loyal animals to fight alongside their human companions.

- Replaces: Shinjo Bushi 4, Utaku Mounted Infantry 4, Moto Bushi 4. Requires: Awareness 3, Willpower 4, Animal Handling (War Dogs) 5. Special: Grants a pack of Unicorn War-Dogs; total pack size equals your Awareness.

- Technique — Ferocity of the Ki-Rin: Complex Action — roll Animal Handling (War Dogs)/Willpower (TN 20) to give your entire pack a simple command. Raise for more sophisticated commands (GM’s judgment). When your War-Dogs make attacks you have ordered, they gain +1k0 to attack and damage rolls.

**Unicorn Yomanri Archer [Bushi]:**

A gaijin archery style learned during the Unicorn’s travels — drawing the arrow to the cheek and sighting down the shaft before releasing. Relies on careful aiming rather than swift instinctive shooting.

- Replaces: Shinjo Bushi 4, Moto Bushi 4, Moto Vindicator 4, Utaku Battle Maiden 4. Requires: Agility 4, Kyujutsu 5.

- Technique — The Way of Yomanri: With a yumi or dai-kyu, spend one or more consecutive Simple Actions aiming before firing. Each Simple Action awards +1k1 to attack OR +1k0 to damage (your choice per action). Maximum aiming actions cannot exceed your Agility Rank. If interrupted, all aiming benefits are lost. Cannot be used in Center Stance.

**Utaku Horse Master:**

The men of the Utaku family are charged with raising and training the legendary Utaku warhorses. Many choose to eschew war entirely to devote their lives to this duty. A popular joke says the Horse Master marries twice — once to his spouse, the other to his stable.

- Replaces: Any Unicorn School 2. Requires: Must be a male of the Utaku family.

- Technique — Master of the Open Plains: +2k0 to all Animal Handling, Horsemanship, and Hunting rolls.
