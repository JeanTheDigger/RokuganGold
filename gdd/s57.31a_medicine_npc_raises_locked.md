## 57.31a Medicine NPC Raise Declaration — LOCKED

This section formalizes how the NPC decision engine determines how many Raises to
declare when executing TREAT_WOUND. GDD s57.31 specifies the Raise mechanic for
Medicine (each Raise adds +1 die to the healing roll, still keep 1, in exchange for
+5 to the skill check TN), but does not specify how NPCs decide how many Raises to
declare.

---

### Source Derivation

**GDD-confirmed mechanics (from s57.31):**

1. **Base**: success heals 1k1 Wounds (roll 1 die, keep 1 — average 5.5).

2. **Each Raise**: +1 die to the healing roll, still keep 1. "A healer calling 3 Raises
   rolls 4 dice and keeps the best 1." The skill check TN increases by +5 per Raise
   (standard L5R 4e Raise mechanic per s4.5).

3. **Rank 5 Mastery Ability**: base healing becomes 2k1. "At Rank 5 with 3 Raises:
   5k1 (base 1 + mastery +1 + Raises +3 rolled, keep 1)." This is the explicit GDD
   example of a Rank 5 healer working at full capability.

**Key GDD anchor:**
The Rank 5 mastery section uses 3 Raises as the canonical example of a Rank 5 healer.
This establishes that Medicine Rank 5+ healers are expected to call 3 Raises — the
+1k0 mastery bonus was specifically designed to combine with Raises.

**Design principle:**
NPC healers are almost exclusively treating high-priority targets (the triage system
in s57.31 routes to the most severely wounded ally first). When treating a Crippled or
Down ally, maximizing healing output is the dominant concern. NPCs declare as many
Raises as their skill supports.

---

### NPC Raise Declaration Table — LOCKED

| Medicine Rank | Raises Declared | Effective Skill Check TN |
|---------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| 0–2 | 0 | TN 15 (base) |
| 3–4 | 1 | TN 20 |
| 5+ | 3 | TN 30 |

**Healing dice at each tier:**

| Medicine Rank | Raises | Mastery Bonus | Healing Pool |
|---------------|--------|---------------|--------------|
| 0–2 | 0 | 0 | 1k1 (avg 5.5) |
| 3–4 | 1 | 0 | 2k1 (avg 7.0) |
| 5–6 | 3 | +1 | 5k1 (avg 8.6) — GDD explicit example |
| 7+ | 3 | +1 | 5k1 (avg 8.6) |

Rank 5–6 and Rank 7+ produce the same healing pool because 3 Raises is the maximum
shown in the GDD. Rank 7+ characters compensate via their larger skill check dice
pool, making the TN 30 requirement more reliable rather than increasing Raises.

---

### Rationale

**Medicine 0–2 (0 Raises).**
Low-skill healers are already uncertain of meeting base TN 15. Declaring Raises
increases the skill check TN without reliable benefit — these healers prioritize
not failing over maximizing output.

**Medicine 3–4 (1 Raise, TN 20).**
A developing healer with a (5–7)kX dice pool. TN 20 is achievable with reasonable
reliability. 1 Raise improves the healing roll from 1k1 (avg 5.5) to 2k1 (avg 7.0)
at manageable risk.

**Medicine 5+ (3 Raises, TN 30).**
The GDD Rank 5 mastery description explicitly uses "3 Raises" as the canonical expert
case: "At Rank 5 with 3 Raises: 5k1." Expert healers (Medicine 5+) have the dice pool
to meet TN 30 reliably (typically Intelligence 3+ for dedicated healers → 8k3+ pools).
The mastery ability exists to be combined with Raises — calling fewer than 3 at Rank 5+
underutilizes the mastery.

**No 2-Raise tier.**
The 4-step progression (0/1/2/3) is mechanically available but the GDD provides no
anchor for a dedicated 2-Raise tier. The progression from "developing healer (1 Raise)"
to "expert healer per GDD example (3 Raises)" is clean and consistent with L5R 4e's
Rank 5 as a major milestone threshold.
