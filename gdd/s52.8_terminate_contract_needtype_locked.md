## s52.8 TERMINATE_CONTRACT NeedType — LOCKED

This section specifies when an NPC lord will proactively select
`TERMINATE_CONTRACT` through the AP/civilian-order decision loop.

---

### Trigger Context

A lord considers terminating a contract only when the `ADJUST_TAX` NeedType
is active. `ADJUST_TAX` arises under financial pressure: koku reserves below
the `_decompose_prevent_shortage` threshold (5 koku), a low treasury ratio in
the seasonal strategic review, or the `_decompose_accumulate_wealth` and
`_decompose_control_trade` decomposition branches for lords who are cash-poor.

Contract termination is a fiscal lever — alongside `SET_TAX_RATE` and
`SET_STIPEND_RATE` — that reduces ongoing expense by removing the per-season
contract obligation. The 50% remaining-koku refund (Part G of s52.6) provides
an immediate koku injection in addition to stopping future expense.

---

### Objective Alignment Score

| NeedType | ActionID | Score | Rationale |
|----------|----------|-------|-----------|
| `ADJUST_TAX` | `TERMINATE_CONTRACT` | 35 | Below `SET_STIPEND_RATE` (60): contract termination carries −3 disposition cost and destroys an active service, making it a harder fiscal decision than adjusting stipends. Above `WRITE_LETTER` (30): it takes direct action on the financial problem rather than delegating it. |

**A77: TERMINATE_CONTRACT score under ADJUST_TAX = 35**

---

### Phase 4c Precondition

`TERMINATE_CONTRACT` must be gated by a Phase 4c precondition check so the
action never appears in the scoring pool when the lord has no active contracts.

A context key `has_active_contracts: bool` is injected into each lord's
`world_states` entry before wave resolution. It is `true` when at least one
character with `lord_id == this_lord.character_id` has a
`supply_ledger["contract_end_ic_day"] >= 0`. The key is cleared by the
daily stale-flag pass.

**A78: Phase 4c context key = `"has_active_contracts"`**
**A79: TERMINATE_CONTRACT is filtered out when `has_active_contracts == false`**

---

### Scope Note

Early termination for security reasons (contracted ronin turns hostile) is not
yet specified. If playtesting reveals lords retaining hostile contractors too
long, a separate INVESTIGATE_THREAT or REMOVE_THREAT trigger may be added.
Do not implement a security-driven trigger until a GDD section specifies it.
