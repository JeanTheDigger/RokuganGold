## 12.7a Letter Reply Values — LOCKED

This section formalizes numeric values for the letter reply system described qualitatively
in Section 12.7. The main section specifies reply factors ("disposition toward sender",
"personality profile", "high Courtesy") without numeric values. This addendum assigns the
canonical values used by the simulation.

---

### Source Derivation

**GDD-confirmed factors (from s12.7 text):**

1. **Disposition toward sender:** "A character with high disposition toward the sender
   is more likely to reply than one who is indifferent."

2. **Courtesy / Rei virtue:** "A character with high Courtesy is more likely to reply
   than one who is dismissive of correspondence."

3. **Hostile characters:** Implied by the disposition factor — characters at sufficiently
   negative disposition do not engage in correspondence with the sender.

4. **Games: Letters skill (Awareness):** "The Game of Letters skill (Awareness) referenced
   in Section 24 is directly relevant here — skilled correspondents write more persuasively
   and may have mechanical advantages in topic framing and reply rates." (s12.7 Design Notes).
   "Games: Letters" is confirmed as a skill sub-type in s15.4 (LOCKED) Play a Game action.

5. **Letters are the primary propagation mechanism for topics across Rokugan** (s12.7):
   Reply rates must be meaningful enough for topics to spread from well-connected NPCs,
   while the GDD's explicit constraint — "total daily disposition gain from correspondence
   remains deliberately small — a letter is a letter, not a personal visit" — requires that
   reply rates not make correspondence too powerful a relationship-building tool.

**Calibration anchors:**

| Source | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Completed exchange mutual disposition bonus | +1 per exchange |
| Letter quality success (TN 15): disposition bonus | +1 |
| Letter quality 1 Raise (TN 20): disposition bonus | +2 |
| Letter quality 2 Raises (TN 25): disposition bonus | +3 |
| Rival tier onset (s12.2 disposition scale) | −11 |

### Reply Constants — LOCKED

| Constant | Value | Meaning |
|----------|-------|---------|
| `BASE_REPLY_CHANCE` | 0.35 | 35% reply chance at neutral (0) disposition |
| `DISPOSITION_REPLY_BONUS` | 0.005 | +0.5% per disposition point above 0 |
| `COURTESY_REPLY_BONUS` | 0.15 | +15% for Rei (Courtesy) virtue |
| `GAME_OF_LETTERS_REPLY_BONUS` | 0.02 (PROVISIONAL) | +2% per rank in Games: Letters skill |
| `HOSTILE_REPLY_THRESHOLD` | −10 | Below this (Rival tier −11 and worse): no reply |
| `MEETING_ACCEPT_DISPOSITION` | 0 | Accept meeting proposals at neutral or positive |

**Resulting reply probabilities at key disposition tiers:**

| Disposition | Tier | Base character | Rei character |
|-------------|------|----------------|---------------|
| −11 | Rival | 0% | 0% |
| 0 | Stranger | 35% | 50% |
| +11 | Acquaintance | 40.5% | 55.5% |
| +31 | Friend | 50.5% | 65.5% |
| +61 | Devoted | 65.5% | 80.5% |
| +100 | (cap) | 85% (→95%) | 95% |

Maximum reply chance capped at 95% — no character replies to every letter.

---

### Design Rationale

**BASE_REPLY_CHANCE = 0.35.**
Rokugan samurai are bound by etiquette to reply to correspondence. A 35% baseline means
most samurai reply to most letters even from near-strangers over time, creating the topic
propagation the system requires while avoiding universal reply rates that would trivialize
the mechanism. At neutral disposition, across five letters, a character can expect about
1–2 replies — consistent with "deliberately small" daily gains.

**DISPOSITION_REPLY_BONUS = 0.005 per point.**
Linear scaling from 0 to 100 adds 0% to 50%, putting a Friend-tier correspondent at ~50%
and a Devoted correspondent near 65%. This ensures well-established relationships produce
reliable correspondence while strangers remain uncertain. The formula is: chance = base +
(disposition × 0.005) + virtue_bonus, clamped to [0.0, 0.95].

**COURTESY_REPLY_BONUS = 0.15.**
Rei (Courtesy) virtue is explicitly named in s12.7 as the virtue of reliable correspondence.
+15% represents a substantial but not total transformation of reply behavior — a Rei
character at neutral disposition replies to half of all letters received, consistent with
someone who treats correspondence as a social and moral obligation.

**HOSTILE_REPLY_THRESHOLD = −10 (below Rival tier onset).**
The Rival tier begins at −11 (s12.2 disposition scale). A character at exactly −10 (the
low end of Stranger) will still engage in correspondence — cold but not hostile. A character
at −11 (Rival) has sufficient antagonism to simply ignore letters from the sender. This
threshold applies before the reply chance formula; no reply is possible from Rival tier
or below.

**MEETING_ACCEPT_DISPOSITION = 0.**
A character with neutral or positive disposition toward the sender accepts meeting proposals
carried in replies. A character with any negative disposition declines. This is a binary
threshold — whether to accept a proposed meeting at all — not a probability modifier.
