## 12.11 Inventory System — LOCKED

Every named character has a persistent inventory tracking the items they possess. Items are politically significant objects — they can be gifted, stolen, presented as evidence, sent by letter, or destroyed. Items do not degrade or suffer condition loss through normal use or time. They exist at their quality tier until deliberately destroyed by their owner or removed through covert acquisition.

**Three Storage Tiers — LOCKED:**

On Person: Items physically carried right now. Limited by outfit capacity (see below). Vulnerable to Pickpocket (Sleight of Hand). Items in court formal outfits that exceed the outfit’s capacity must be concealed — requiring a Sleight of Hand roll. Failure means the item is visible, which may be a social violation depending on the item.

Current Quarters: The character’s room wherever they are currently staying — a guest room at court, an inn room, a tent on campaign. Capacity 20 slots. Vulnerable to Search Quarters (Sleight of Hand). Items in quarters travel with the character when they move — they are packed and taken along.

Home Storage: The character’s permanent room at their home castle or estate. Unlimited capacity. Not vulnerable to covert actions unless someone travels there specifically. Items here are safe but inaccessible until the character returns home or sends for them.

**Item Categories — LOCKED:**

- Documents: Letters, edicts, contracts, forged papers, travel papers. Usually Small size.

- Seals: Personal seals, clan seals, official stamps. Small size. High political value.

- Gifts: Items prepared for giving, tracked by quality tier (Section 49). Size varies.

- Weapons: Katana, wakizashi, tanto, bow, others. Size varies.

- Scrolls: Spell scrolls, books, poetry collections, reference materials. Small to Medium.

- Valuables: Koku pouches, rare objects, jewelry, tea ceremony implements, religious objects. Size varies.

- Evidence: Any item acquired through covert actions serving as physical proof. Tracked with is_evidence: true flag. Grants 1 Free Raise when used in Expose a Secret Publicly or Reveal a Secret Privately (Section 12.8).

**Item Sizes — LOCKED:**

- Small (1 slot): A letter, a scroll, a personal seal, a small knife, a pouch of koku, a tea bowl, a netsuke, a fan.

- Medium (2 slots): A book, a wrapped gift, a tanto, a bundle of scrolls, a tea ceremony implement set, a lacquered box.

- Large (3 slots): A katana, a full tea ceremony set, a large wrapped gift, armor components, a significant art object.

**On-Person Capacity by Outfit — LOCKED:**

- Court Formal (kimono, hakama, full ceremonial dress): 3 slots. Almost no carrying space. A few scrolls, a personal seal, one small concealed item at most.

- Casual/Informal Wear: 5 slots. Room for a few items in a bag or tucked away.

- Traveling Clothes: 8 slots. A pack, belt pouches, meaningful carry capacity.

- Light Armor (do-maru, light lamellar): 6 slots. Military practical. Cannot carry Large gifts or delicate items without risk of damage during movement.

- Heavy Armor (full o-yoroi): 3 slots. Weapons essentially only. A katana and wakizashi fill most of it.

**Item Transfer Mechanics — LOCKED:**

Give Directly (in person, no AP): Hand an item to another character in the same location. Immediate. No roll. Item moves from giver’s on-person inventory to recipient’s on-person inventory.

Send by Messenger (letter system, no AP): Attach a physical item to a letter. The item travels with the letter at the same delivery speed and is subject to the same interception risk. During transit the item exists in neither party’s inventory — it is in transit. If the letter is intercepted, the item is also intercepted and enters the interceptor’s inventory.

Deliver as Gift (Deliver Gift court action, 1 AP): The formal gifting action from Section 12.3. Uses an item from the giver’s inventory. On success, item transfers to recipient’s inventory with full disposition effect. On failure, item still transfers but without the full effect. On critical failure, item transfers but generates a negative disposition outcome.

Send to Home Storage: One IC day to arrange. A trusted servant makes the journey. The item enters home storage after the travel time. Vulnerable to interception on the road using the letter interception mechanic (Stealth/Agility roll).

Retrieve from Home Storage: Requires traveling home or sending a trusted messenger. Travel time applies. The item enters on-person or quarters inventory upon retrieval.

**Item Acquisition — LOCKED:**

- Purchase (Commerce System, Section 51): Items bought from merchants enter on-person or quarters inventory at Normal or Fine quality depending on availability.

- Craft (Section 49): Artisan creates an item. Enters their inventory at the crafted quality tier.

- Receive as gift: Item transfers from giver’s inventory to recipient’s on-person inventory.

- Covert acquisition (Section 12.8): Search Quarters or Pickpocket transfers an item from target’s inventory to actor’s inventory. The target’s inventory updates immediately — the item is gone.

- Discovery: During travel or exploration an item can be found and added to on-person inventory.

**Item Destruction — LOCKED:**

A character can deliberately destroy any item in their own inventory at any time. No roll required. The item ceases to exist permanently. This is the primary tool for eliminating evidence — destroying a letter or document before it can be used against you. A character cannot destroy an item they do not possess. To destroy evidence held by another character, they must first acquire it through covert means.

**Presenting Items as Evidence — LOCKED:**

In court (part of Expose a Secret Publicly, 1 AP): Present a physical document or object as proof. Grants 1 Free Raise on the roll (Section 12.8). The item remains in the presenter’s inventory after presentation — presenting evidence does not transfer it.

To a lord privately (Personal Visit): Present physical evidence as part of the betrayal consequence chain (Section 18.9). No additional AP cost beyond the visit. The lord’s response depends on the evidence quality and their disposition toward the accused.
