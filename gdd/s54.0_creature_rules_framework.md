## 54.0 Creature Rules Framework

**Creatures Without Void:**

Most non-human creatures do not have a Void Ring. This represents either their lack of intelligence and self-awareness (animals) or their lack of connection and harmony with the elements (Shadowlands creatures, undead, etc.). The ability to call on a moment of perfect harmony with the elements is overwhelmingly a human trait, and only a few non-human creatures even possess a Void Ring, let alone the ability to spend Void Points.

A creature without a Void Ring suffers a number of penalties and effects: it cannot spend Void Points, cannot assume the Center Stance, cannot perform an Iaijutsu duel, cannot meditate or perform other actions associated with Void, and its maximum Raises are limited by its lowest Trait. A creature without Void cannot be affected by spells, Techniques, or game effects that target the Void Ring or Void Points (such as the Void Release spell).

**Standard Special Abilities:**

**Fear:**

Many supernatural or grotesque creatures inflict Fear automatically at the beginning of any encounter. A character who fails to resist a Fear effect suffers a penalty to all die rolls equal to -Xk0, where X is the Fear Rank. See Section 4.6 for full Fear rules.

**Huge:**

A Huge creature is impossible to Grapple, due to its vast size and bulk. A Huge creature cannot be Grappled or targeted with the Knockdown maneuver, and will not initiate a Grapple with others unless it has ranks in the Jiujutsu skill.

**Invulnerability:**

A creature with Invulnerability takes normal damage only from spells, nemuranai, and weapons composed of jade, crystal, or obsidian. Against any other attack, the creature takes only a single Wound, no matter how powerful the blow. If a creature has both Reduction and Invulnerability, the effects of Invulnerability are applied after those of Reduction.

**Invulnerability, Partial:**

A creature with partial Invulnerability is Invulnerable only against certain kinds of attacks — for example, a creature might be Invulnerable to arrows, or to fire.

**Invulnerability, Superior:**

A creature with Superior Invulnerability has all the advantages of normal Invulnerability, but in addition is resistant to one or more of the attack forms that could harm a normal Invulnerable creature — for example, it might be Invulnerable to jade, or to Air spells.

**Magic Resistance:**

Functions in the same manner as the Magic Resistance Advantage in the Book of Fire.

**Spirit:**

A creature with the Spirit quality is from one of the spirit realms outside of Ningen-do, and is somewhat resistant to weapons and effects native to the mortal realm. Spirit creatures in Ningen-do take half damage from any weapon or other physical attack which does not contain jade, crystal, or obsidian, as well as from any spell or magical effect which does not have the Jade or Crystal quality.

**Swift:**

A Swift creature moves at a faster speed than its physical abilities would seem to permit. Creatures with Swift are considered to have a higher Water — one Rank higher for each Rank of Swift they possess — for purposes of calculating how far they can move with a Free or Simple Move Action.

**Undead:**

An Undead creature has no sense of pain and cannot suffer Wound penalties. Undead creatures remain fully functional with no Wound effects until they are literally hacked apart (reduced to Dead). Specific attacks such as removing their hands would however remove weapon or claw attacks. Since they are dead, they are also immune to Fear effects of any kind, as well as to any spell, Skill, or Technique that attempts to influence the mind or emotions.
