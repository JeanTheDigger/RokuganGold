## 27.8 The Unicorn Clan

Originally the Ki-Rin Clan — tasked by the Emperor to explore the world beyond Rokugan and return with knowledge of foreign threats. They departed in the early third century and did not return for roughly six hundred years, arriving back in the early ninth century. When they came back they were almost unrecognizable — changed by centuries of travel, foreign customs, and gaijin alliances. The Empire viewed them with suspicion and they struggled to reintegrate. They brought the finest horses in the Empire, knowledge of foreign peoples, and a directness that still makes other clans uncomfortable.

- Clan identity: Direct, honest, mobile, compassionate toward lower classes. Dislike confinement. Best cavalry in the Empire. Foreign customs still visible in their way of life.

- Key historical events: Shinjo's departure; 800 years of exploration; the return in the ninth century; Moto Tsume's fall to Shadowlands; the Kolat corruption of the Shinjo; Moto family's restoration of honor; Shinjo's return and purge.

- Inter-clan relations: Generally positive — seen as straightforward and trustworthy despite 'barbarian' reputation. Tension with Lion historically (territory disputes). Good relations with Phoenix.

**The Shinjo Family:**

Founded by Lady Shinjo herself — led the clan for over 1100 years. Embody compassion, especially toward lower classes. Optimistic, freedom-loving, wanderers at heart. Known for honesty — Shinjo bluntness is almost as famous as Crab crudeness. The family was severely damaged by the Kolat corruption scandal, and spent generations restoring their reputation. Their rule of the clan passed to the Moto after the disgrace.

**The Moto Family:**

Motto: 'Moto can die, but never yield.' Originally gaijin — descended from the Ujik-Hai steppe nomads who became the first people encountered by the Ki-Rin Clan. Tallest, darkest, most foreign-looking of any Rokugani family. Famous for aggressive cavalry charges and reckless courage. Devastated by the fall of Moto Tsume to Shadowlands — spent three centuries in penance, wearing white kabuki masks, forming the White Guard to destroy their fallen brethren. Now lead the clan after the Shinjo disgrace.

**The Utaku Family (formerly Otaku):**

Motto: 'Purity is my passion.' Led entirely by women — only women learn the Battle Maiden techniques and ride the Utaku warhorses into battle. Men of the family breed and care for the horses. The Utaku shiotome (battle maidens) are unanimously considered the most elite cavalry in the Empire. Silent in battle — charging in total silence honoring their ancestor Otaku. Renamed from Otaku to Utaku in honor of Otaku Kamoko, the Unicorn Thunder.

**The Ide Family:**

Motto: 'Violence is the language of defeat, swords the tongues of the lost.' The Unicorn's diplomats — founded by a patient, intelligent, peace-loving man who became the Ki-Rin's foremost mediator during their travels. Their philosophy 'wabukan' (the peaceful path) forms the basis of the Ide Emissary school. The most courtly and politically comfortable of the Unicorn families. Work to bridge the gap between Unicorn foreign ways and Rokugani customs.

**The Iuchi Family:**

Motto: 'Power is what you think it is.' The Unicorn's shugenja — transformed by centuries of exposure to foreign magic. Developed meishodo (name magic) from gaijin sources — inscribing ancient words on talismans to produce effects without using kami as intermediaries. Still viewed as strange by other clans' shugenja. Open-minded about magical practice, believe there is no single 'true way.' Battle-ready compared to other shugenja families — centuries of hostile travel made pacifism impractical.

**The Horiuchi Family:**

The newest of the Unicorn families — founded relatively recently. A small family that bridges the gap between the Iuchi magical tradition and the Shinjo scouting tradition. Known for elemental magic that enhances travel, communication, and mobility. Notable for their role in Yoritomo's ascension to Kami alongside Moshi Amika.
