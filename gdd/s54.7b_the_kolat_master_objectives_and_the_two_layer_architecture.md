## 54.7b The Kolat — Master Objectives and the Two-Layer Architecture

Masters do not receive extra Action Points. They do not run a separate decision loop. No engine changes are required. When a character is elevated to a Master seat, their standing objective is replaced by the Kolat mandate for their Sect (Section 54.7a). From that point forward, the engine runs identically to any other NPC — it just uses a different standing objective as input. The existing standing influence bonus (Section 55.26.2) flavours every scored action without overriding the primary objective, exactly as it does for all characters. There is one standing objective, one standing influence bonus, one engine. The Kolat mandate simply becomes who they are at a deep level, replacing whatever personal ambition they held before.

The standing objective replacement also affects how primary objectives are sourced. The opportunity scanner (Section 55.26.1) generates primary objectives by scanning world-state against the standing objective’s domain. For a Master, the scanner now operates against the Kolat mandate domain. Most Kolat mandates do not map to any of the scanner’s four domains (Political, Military, Economic, Personal) — so the scanner typically returns no self-selected candidates, and the Master waits. Kolat primary objectives arrive from Tiger via Tear: a direction, a name, an urgency level. Tiger-to-Lotus briefings: when Tiger routes intelligence to Master Lotus, the receiving topic is flagged source: tiger_briefing in Master Lotus’s IntelDB. Simple directives (target name, elimination priority) arrive by Tear and are processed immediately by ASSIGN_ELIMINATION_TARGET. Detailed threat assessments (context, known associates, location data, threat classification) arrive via Silk cipher document through the dead drop network. Master Lotus does not know who composed the assessment — only that it arrived through the authorised Silk channel and therefore carries Tiger’s implicit authority. The Master’s engine decomposes that directive into an ImmediateNeed on the next AP firing, exactly as it would decompose any lord-assigned primary. Tiger is, effectively, the Master’s lord for the purposes of primary objective assignment. Their public lord still assigns public primary objectives through the normal feudal chain, and those objectives remain active and pursued. The engine holds one primary objective slot. If both Tiger and the public lord assign a primary at the same time, the priority cascade resolves the conflict: a Tiger directive arriving as a reactive event fires at higher priority than a lord-assigned non-crisis objective. If both are crisis-grade, Tiger’s takes precedence. Tiger’s directive arrives as a reactive event, which the engine processes before AP-slot decisions per Section 55.2. The public primary is not suppressed — it is deferred until the reactive event resolves. No filter mechanism is involved.

### Kolat Standing Objectives by Sect

Each Master has exactly one Kolat standing objective drawn from their Sect’s mandate. Like all standing objectives it never completes — it is directional, shaping what primary objectives are generated from world state.

| **Sect** | **Kolat Standing Objective** |
| --- | --- |
| Tiger | Maintain the secrecy of the organisation and the safety of its members at all costs |
| Chrysanthemum | Ensure the Kolat has advance warning of every significant Imperial decision before it is enacted |
| Silk | Keep every active agent supplied with current intelligence and the physical network operational |
| Coin | Ensure the Kolat always has sufficient untraceable operational funds regardless of world conditions |
| Cloud | Preserve forbidden knowledge and ensure the right intelligence reaches the right people at the right time |
| Jade | Prevent supernatural corruption from destroying the Empire before humanity can claim it |
| Dream | Maintain the sleeper network, keep triggers current, and expand the network when targets of opportunity arise |
| Lotus | Ensure the organisation’s enemies do not survive long enough to become existential threats |
| Roc | Inactive at launch. Seat exists; no standing mandate assigned |
| Steel | Keep the Hidden Temple secure, undetectable, and operational at all times |

### Personality Compatibility — Hard Blocks

The Kolat standing objective does not override personality filters — it adds a new category of action to score, but the filters still apply. Some virtue combinations make an NPC ineligible for certain Sects entirely. They are never added to that pool. The weighted draw never sees them.

| **Virtue** | **Cannot Be Master Of…** | **Reason** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gi (Righteousness) | Tiger, Silk, Coin, Dream, Lotus | Active deception, financial fraud, torture, and unsanctioned assassination all require lying. Gi cannot. |
| Makoto (Honesty) | Tiger, Silk, Dream, Lotus | Sincerity cannot maintain a false persona or assign a killing through a pseudonym. |
| Rei (Courtesy) | Dream | Torture and psychological destruction are beneath courtesy. Rei has no emergency exception. |
| Jin (Compassion) | Dream, Lotus | Compassion cannot order torture or the assassination of someone who has not directly harmed others. |
| Chugi (Loyalty) | Tiger | Tiger’s mandate frequently requires acting against one’s own lord or clan. Chugi’s absolute loyalty to its feudal lord is irreconcilable with this. |

Shourido virtues carry no hard blocks. Seigyo, Ketsui, Ishi, Dosatsu, Chishiki, Kyoryoku, and Ishi shape how a Master pursues the mandate, not whether they can. Gi and Makoto are the most restrictive Bushido virtues in this system — an NPC holding either can only be Cloud, Jade, Roc, or Steel, the four Sects whose operational mandates do not require active deception. A Gi-virtue Master Jade is a genuinely excellent witch hunter who happens to serve the Kolat’s interest in keeping humanity alive long enough to inherit the world.

### Communication Architecture — The Oni’s Eye and the Silk Network

With both the Hidden Temple intact and the Oni’s Eye operational, the Kolat is a functioning centralised conspiracy with genuine coordination capacity. The Eye serves two functions. First, observation: a Master physically present at the Temple can direct it at any location in the Empire, watching events in silence — the Eye shows visual information but carries no sound. It cannot observe through wards against scrying, cannot observe reliably inside the Shadowlands, and by design cannot observe inside the Temple itself. Second, communication: the Eye produces small crystal teardrops — Tears of the Oni’s Eye — which trusted agents carry disguised as jewellery. The holder of a Tear can receive direct mental impressions from whoever controls the Eye at that moment. The communication is silent and leaves no observable sign.

All ten Masters hold a Tear. Tiger holds two. Master Silk distributes additional Tears to their most trusted senior field agents at their discretion. The total Tears in circulation at world generation is approximately fifteen to twenty. Masters communicate with each other through the Eye, not through the Silk network’s physical infrastructure. Silk handles everything below the Master level: moving information, agents, Koku, and materials through the Empire’s physical infrastructure. The mundane machinery the Eye cannot replace.

Tiger is the only Master who knows every other Master’s real identity. When a Master sends a directive through the Eye, it passes through Tiger as the routing node. The requesting Master never knows which NPC received the assignment. The receiving Master never knows who requested it. Tiger is the only node that sees both ends of every inter-Master communication. Tiger’s death or exposure is a crisis for any other Master; Tiger being Broken alive and interrogated is a catastrophe for the entire organisation — every real identity, every operation, and every dead drop Tiger has memorised becomes available to investigators.

The ten Masters convene at the Hidden Temple in person once per season. Meetings follow the original Kolat protocol: black robes, golden masks, no real names. Even in person, Masters are strangers to each other. They know code names, Sect roles, and capabilities. They do not know faces or true identities. Tiger’s mask looks identical to every other mask in the room. All Masters know the Temple’s location — seeing it is the final initiatory act of elevation to a Master seat. This makes every living Master a Temple exposure risk. The threshold for Tiger authorising the elimination of a compromised Master is lower precisely because of this: any Master taken alive and interrogated carries the Temple’s location in their memory.

### How Masters Are Recruited

All Masters are fully conscious of their role. No Master is a sleeper. Masters are never created through the Dream Sect’s conditioning process — they are the believers who hold the weapons, not the weapons themselves. Three recruitment paths exist, and the path taken shapes the Master’s psychology.

**The Long Ascent.** The most common path. The NPC was recruited as a junior agent years before world generation, proved themselves through increasingly sensitive operations, and was slowly read into deeper layers of the conspiracy. Elevation to Master was a culmination of a career. This NPC has had years to internalise the ideology and resolve whatever personal conflicts the Kolat mandate created for them. Those conflicts happened in backstory.

**The Direct Elevation.** Rarer. The NPC was identified as an ideal candidate and approached directly at a high level — shown enough of the Kolat’s structure and philosophy to make an informed decision and accepted. No years of junior work. This path is more likely for T1 candidates whose public position made junior agent work too risky. These Masters are ideologically newer, may carry more unresolved tension between their public values and Kolat mandate, and are more likely to produce the early behavioural anomalies that investigators detect.

**The Inheritance.** Some Masters inherited the seat when a predecessor died or was elevated elsewhere. They may have known the previous Master personally — a trusted lieutenant read into the full picture only when the seat went vacant. These Masters are loyal to a person as much as to an ideology. They understood what the Kolat was before they fully understood why. By world generation they understand both, but their emotional relationship to the conspiracy has a different root than pure ideological conviction.

### Detection — Anomaly Accumulation

Detection works through anomaly accumulation. No single signal is conclusive. Patterns build a case. Every Kolat action produces a trace. The question is whether anyone is looking and what they are looking for.

**Koku anomalies.** A Coin Master’s province accounts do not reconcile perfectly. The province generates X Koku; the tax cascade reports X minus something. A player with Commerce (Mathematics) 4+ and access to the records can find the gap. By itself it looks like administrative imprecision. Combined with other signals it looks like deliberate skimming.

**Behavioural inconsistencies.** The magistrate who always follows leads suddenly drops a promising investigation with no explanation. The abbot who never travels makes three trips to cities with no religious significance. The Emerald Champion who is known for thoroughness fails to note an anomaly they must have seen. Each of these generates a Tier 4 topic: “Something seems off about [character].” Not an accusation. A note.

**Association patterns.** This person keeps meeting privately with individuals who have no obvious connection to their public role. The Yasuki lord and the Phoenix scholar have no professional reason to meet. The Shosuro Actor and the Crab provincial lord have no social overlap. Investigation rolls to notice; higher rolls to identify the pattern rather than individual meetings.

**Tear usage.** A person receiving a Tear communication briefly loses focus — a few seconds of absence that looks like distraction. Almost never noticed under normal conditions. A player who has been watching someone closely and rolls Investigation (Notice) against TN 35 might catch the moment. Combined with other signals it becomes significant.

**Seasonal travel gaps.** Masters travel to the Temple once per season through cover identities and indirect routes. They still physically move through the world. A player who tracks a Master’s movements over several seasons may notice periods of absence that do not align with their public calendar — regular, roughly consistent duration, no adequate explanation.

The topic escalation tracks case-building. A first anomaly generates a Tier 4 topic. A second unrelated anomaly adds another. Once a pattern is identified, the topic escalates to Tier 3: “[Character] is hiding something significant.” Connecting the pattern to the Kolat generates a second Tier 3 topic. Confirmed Kolat connection triggers a Tier 2 crisis. Confirmed Master status triggers a Tier 1 crisis — and the Kolat responds at full force.

### The Broken Master — Response Cascade

Being Broken is a five-stage escalation with decision points at each stage. The Kolat responds with force first and eats its own only as a last resort. This is a persistent online world — all five stages play out across real server time. The investigator is a real player. The Master may be an NPC or a player. The Lotus contract Tiger eventually issues goes to a real operative who may not know why the target has been marked.

**Stage 1 — Discovery (investigator unaware of surveillance).** Evidence accumulates in the topic system. The Master does not know. The Kolat does not know. The Eye is not pointed at the investigators because Tiger has no reason to direct it there yet. This stage ends when the Master notices surveillance (Investigation / Awareness check) or when Tiger’s network intercepts intelligence suggesting an investigation is underway.

**Stage 2 — Awareness (the Master knows).** The Master’s Kolat standing objective now competes with cover-up actions for the same normal AP budget. They must maintain their public behaviour while destroying documentary evidence, surveilling the investigator, activating social cover, and flagging the threat to Tiger through their Tear. A Master who suddenly stops attending court and starts nervously burning documents is more suspicious, not less.

**Stage 3 — Tiger assessment.** Tiger receives the alert and directs the Eye at the investigators. Tiger evaluates on two axes: how much does the investigator actually have, and how capable are they. A lone player with circumstantial evidence is different from an Emerald Magistrate with Imperial backing. Responses scale accordingly: surveillance and preparation for low threats; a Lotus contract for medium threats; full crisis evaluation for high threats where the Master themselves may be unrecoverable.

**Stage 4 — The hard choice.** If Lotus’s elimination of investigators fails, or if the compromised Master has made serious operational errors, Tiger faces the hardest decision in the system: authorise the Master’s elimination. A Broken Master taken alive is infinitely more dangerous than a dead one. Their Sect’s operational details, dead drop addresses, and the Temple’s location are all at risk. Tiger routes a contract to Lotus. The cover story is managed by Silk. The seat goes vacant. A player character who is a Master and learns Tiger has ordered their elimination has one choice: run, fight, or defect. Defecting — going to Imperial investigators with everything they know — is the nuclear option. It exposes the Temple’s location, every operation they have personal knowledge of, and potentially Tiger’s identity if they somehow know it. The Kolat’s response is total and immediate.

**Stage 5 — Aftermath.** Whether the threat is neutralised by disruption, elimination of investigators, or elimination of the Master, the Kolat runs damage assessment. Dead drop addresses associated with the compromised Master are abandoned. Couriers who had contact are cut off or eliminated. Adjacent Sect operations coordinated through that Master are suspended pending Tiger’s evaluation. Tiger issues recall directives through the normal Kolat delivery channels: TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR for agents who hold one (carrying direction = “stand down,” urgency 3), and a Silk courier delivering a recall cipher document for agents without Tears. On receipt, the agent’s kolat_objective slot is cleared. Agents mid-execution of a Kolat objective who receive a recall halt immediately and treat the objective as abandoned. Tiger’s engine generates these recall directives as priority 3 ImmediateNeeds under SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE on the AP following damage assessment. Each recall costs 1 AP. A Master with many active field agents during a Stage 5 aftermath may require several consecutive APs to complete the recall sweep — a window investigators can exploit if they move fast. In the days and weeks after a major exposure event, the Kolat is partially blind and partially reconfiguring. Players who move fast in that window can exploit the confusion.

### The Collapse Condition

The Kolat does not collapse when a single Master is exposed. It does not collapse when two or three are exposed. It is designed to survive partial exposure. It collapses when the Temple is found and destroyed or the Eye is captured. Without the Temple there is no meeting place, no archive, and no Eye — Masters fall back on the Silk network for coordination, each running their Sect mandate on standing orders, unable to coordinate cleanly or receive new directives from Tiger. Without the Eye, Masters are isolated. Their Tears go silent. The organisation fragments into ten autonomous cells. This is not death — the Kolat survived degraded states before — but it is a catastrophically reduced organisation. A player faction that wants to destroy the Kolat must Break a Master, extract the Temple location, and raid the Temple before the Kolat can evacuate or destroy the Eye. Each step takes real time. The Kolat is attempting to stop them at every stage. Other players may be working for the Kolat without knowing it.
