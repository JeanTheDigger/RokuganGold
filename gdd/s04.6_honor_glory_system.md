## 4.6 Honor & Glory System

- Honor (0–10): represents personal integrity and adherence to Bushido. Affects social TNs, NPC reactions, and certain technique prerequisites.

- Glory (0–10): represents public reputation and fame. Affects how NPCs treat the player publicly, access to certain courts, and political influence.

- Both are tracked numerically and affected by in-game actions — performing honorable deeds, keeping oaths, winning duels honorably increases Honor; betrayal, cowardice, or breaching etiquette decreases it.

- Glory can be gained through public acts, winning tournaments, battlefield valor, or court recognition; it can be lost through public failure or disgrace.

**Scale Convention — LOCKED:**

Honor and Glory are tracked as decimal values on a 0–10 scale (e.g., 6.5, 3.2). Throughout this document and in the L5R 4th Edition source material, two units of measurement are used. “Points” are tenths: 1 point = 0.1 on the scale. “Ranks” are whole numbers: 1 rank = 1.0 on the scale. Example: “lose 2 Honor points” = −0.2. “Lose 1 Honor rank” = −1.0. “Gain 5 Glory points” = +0.5. This convention applies everywhere in the GDD — combat consequences, court events, technique costs, and all other systems that reference Honor or Glory values.

**Visibility — LOCKED:**

Honor is not publicly displayed. It is internal — only the character knows their exact Honor value. However, high Honor leaks through conduct and bearing. Any character may attempt to discern another’s Honor Rank with a Lore: Bushido / Awareness roll at TN 30. Success reveals the target’s Honor Rank. This is a Category 5 intelligence action costing 1 AP in a court context.

Glory is public by definition. It is reputation — widely known, no roll required. A character’s Glory Rank is visible to all who know of them. Infamy is similarly public.

**Timing — LOCKED:**

Honor and Glory changes apply immediately the moment they occur. There is no delay and no session batching. A masterful performance that raises Glory mid-court immediately benefits subsequent actions in that same session. A character who renege on a Public Declaration immediately loses Honor — the mechanical penalty applies to their next roll. This creates genuine real-time consequences and momentum within a court session.

**Honor as Court Credibility — LOCKED:**

Honor Rank provides Free Raises on Public Declarations and Offer a Favor actions — because a character with a track record of keeping their word is believed more readily. Low Honor characters face additional Raises required on the same actions — their word carries less weight.

- Honor Rank 1 (1.0–1.9): +2 additional Raises required on Public Declarations and Offer a Favor.

- Honor Rank 2 (2.0–2.9): +1 additional Raise required.

- Honor Rank 3–4 (3.0–4.9): No modifier.

- Honor Rank 5–6 (5.0–6.9): 1 Free Raise on Public Declarations and Offer a Favor.

- Honor Rank 7+ (7.0+): 2 Free Raises on Public Declarations and Offer a Favor.

Note: Honor benefits outside court are designed across multiple sections. The Strength of Honor (Section 32) establishes Honor Rank as a flat bonus to resist social manipulation and temptation. This applies to: all three intimidation contexts (Section 12.9), insurgency defection resistance (Section 11.11), Maho cult recruitment resistance (Section 11.11), and any contested Temptation roll. Additionally, Honor thresholds gate NPC willingness to use covert methods (Section 12.8), and Honor Rank is a weighted factor in heir evaluation (Section 22.5). Court credibility above is the most visible benefit; resistance to corruption and manipulation is the most mechanically significant.

**Court Honor and Glory Event Table — LOCKED:**

All values are in decimal increments. Changes apply immediately.

**Glory Gains:**

- Public Performance success: +0.3 Glory.

- Public Performance success with 2 or more Raises (masterful): +0.5 Glory.

- Public Debate decisive win (3+ Raises): +0.3 Glory.

- Publicly Praise Someone success: +0.1 Glory for you. +0.2 Glory for the praised character.

- Public Declaration made and later honored at court close: +0.2 Glory.

- Won a duel honorably in court context: +0.5 Glory.

- Perform Personally For masterful success: +0.2 Glory.

**Glory Losses:**

- Public Performance critical failure: −0.3 Glory.

- Public Debate decisive loss (opponent wins with 3+ Raises): −0.2 Glory.

- Publicly Insult Someone critical failure (backfired): −0.2 Glory.

- Expose a Secret Publicly critical failure (secret exposed as false): −0.3 Glory.

**Honor Gains:**

- Keeping a Public Declaration through to completion: +0.2 Honor.

- Offering a Favor and honoring it: +0.1 Honor.

- Refusing to use Gossip or false Disclose when your virtue (Gi, Makoto) prohibits it under pressure: +0.1 Honor. Passive and automatic — no AP cost.

**Honor Losses:**

- Reneging on a Public Declaration: −1.0 Honor. A genuine breach of the samurai’s word.

- Fabricating a secret that is later exposed as false: −0.5 Honor.

- Out-of-mandate proxy commitment, lord accepts: −0.3 Honor (lord), −0.5 Honor (proxy).

- Out-of-mandate proxy commitment, lord refuses: −0.2 Honor (lord), −1.0 Honor (proxy).

- Using Gossip or false Disclose when your virtue (Gi, Makoto) prohibits it: −0.5 Honor.

**PUBLIC_ATONEMENT — Full Mechanic:**

A samurai stands before an assembled court and publicly acknowledges a specific failing. They name the offense, express remorse, and accept the shame. This is not seppuku — the character continues to live. This is admitting fault publicly to demonstrate that they value honor above pride. ActionID: PUBLIC_ATONEMENT. Category 13 — Honor and Dueling. Cost: 1 AP. Requires AT_COURT. The character must name the specific offense they are atoning for — a topic, a secret, a scandal, a crime. Generic atonement is not permitted; the character must specify what they did wrong. A character can only atone for a given offense once — repeat atonements for the same offense are hollow and the system blocks them.

Roll: Sincerity / Willpower vs TN scaled by the severity of the offense. Tier 4 (minor scandal): TN 15. Tier 3 (serious — adultery, moderate dishonor): TN 20. Tier 2 (politically explosive): TN 25. Tier 1 (existential — treason, maho association): TN 30.

Success: Honor regained, scaled by tier. Tier 4: +0.3 Honor. Tier 3: +0.5 Honor. Tier 2: +0.8 Honor. Tier 1: +1.0 Honor. Each Raise adds +0.1 Honor. Glory loss: −0.3 regardless of tier — the public shame of confessing is the same whether the offense was minor or major. Disposition effect on observers present at court: characters with Gi (Honesty), Makoto (Sincerity), or Meiyo (Honor) dominant personality gain +3 to +5 disposition toward the atoning character — they respect the courage it takes to publicly admit fault. Characters with Seigyo (Control) dominant personality lose −2 to −3 disposition — they see exploitable weakness, not strength.

Failure: the atonement does not ring true. The court does not believe the sincerity. No Honor regained. Glory still drops −0.3 — everyone watched the attempt. Small disposition loss (−2) from observers who witnessed an unconvincing apology. Critical failure (miss TN by 10+): the attempt is perceived as performative or manipulative. Honor LOSS of −0.3 instead of gain — trying to fake sincerity is itself dishonorable. Glory loss −0.5. Disposition −5 from observers. The character made things worse.

NPC Behavior: the NPC Decision Engine fires PUBLIC_ATONEMENT through the RESTORE_HONOR NeedType when a character’s Honor has dropped below their personality-determined threshold. Meiyo and Gi NPCs are the most likely to atone voluntarily — their personal code demands it. Chugi NPCs atone when their lord demands it. Seigyo NPCs almost never atone voluntarily — they view the Glory loss as a worse cost than the Honor deficit. An NPC may also be compelled to atone by their lord through ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE — a lord who orders a vassal to publicly apologize for a scandal is using the existing operational hierarchy (Section 11.3.18) to force the action. Values PROVISIONAL pending balancing.
