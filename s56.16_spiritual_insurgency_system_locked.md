## 56.16 Spiritual Insurgency System --- LOCKED

Spiritual insurgencies are a distinct content source from the Jigoku-driven Taint system and the criminal/political insurgencies. They arise from worship neglect --- the failure to maintain the spiritual balance that keeps Ningen-do stable. A well-governed, Taint-free province that stopped maintaining its shrines is vulnerable. Good governance without spiritual observance is not enough.

Two types of spiritual insurgency exist: Spirit Realm Overlap and Elemental Imbalance. Both are triggered by aggregate worship failure in the existing Kami Worship System (Section 4.3.21). Both require a shugenja to resolve. Both produce ASCII map encounter content that is fundamentally different from every other quest seed --- a journey into a place that is becoming somewhere else, where the resolution is bringing it back.

**Trigger Threshold:** When 2 or more Great Fortunes are at Displeased or worse in a province, the spiritual barrier weakens enough for a bleed event or elemental imbalance to fire. Each additional Fortune at Displeased or Wrathful increases severity and frequency --- more spirits, more intense elemental phenomena, larger affected area. A province where all seven Fortunes are at Wrathful is in total spiritual collapse.

**Design Principle:** In Rokugani cosmology, the spirit realms do not rip open. They overlap. The boundary between worlds is like paper --- when it weakens, the two sides bleed through each other. A place does not get a portal. It gets thin. The world stops feeling like Ningen-do and starts feeling like somewhere else. The encounter is a place where the world has become wrong, and the player walks deeper into the wrongness until they find the heart of it and restore the boundary.

**Cross-Reference:** Section 4.3.21 (Kami Worship System --- thresholds, cascade, maluses). Section 2.6 (Spirit Realms). Section 4.5 (L5R Rules --- shugenja spellcasting). Section 42 (Shadowlands Taint --- separate system, Jigoku only).

### 56.16.1 Spirit Realm Overlap --- LOCKED

A spirit realm is bleeding into Ningen-do. The affected area gradually transforms --- the deeper the player walks, the less the world looks like the mortal realm and the more it resembles the overlapping spirit realm. Spirits from that realm are physically present. The place has not been invaded. It has become thin.

### 56.16.1a Realm Selection --- Weighted by Province Conditions --- LOCKED

Which realm overlaps is determined by a weighted random roll. The weights come from conditions the simulation already tracks. Local conditions shift the probabilities but do not guarantee the outcome. The barrier is thin everywhere --- whatever is closest pushes through, and closest has a random element.

- **Gaki-do (Realm of the Hungry Dead):** Weighted by: famine status (Section 4.3.6), consecutive seasons of food deficit, population starvation. Also weighted by: recent mass battle casualties in the province (Section 11.7), unperformed funeral rites after mass death. Starving provinces attract hungry dead. Mass battle dead are a feast for hungry ghosts. A major battle can directly trigger a Gaki-do overlap regardless of worship state.

- **Toshigoku (Realm of Slaughter):** Weighted by: War Status (Section 53), recent battles in the province, military casualties, provinces with historical battlefields. Blood-soaked ground attracts slaughter spirits.

- **Chikushudo (Realm of Animals):** Weighted by: proximity to major forests (Shinomen, Kitsune Mori), wilderness terrain type, hunting pressure on animal populations, provinces with strong natural features.

- **Sakkaku (Realm of Mischief):** Weighted by: active court intrigue in the province, number of secrets in circulation (Section 12.8), political deception events, Scorpion Clan presence. Deception attracts mischief.

- **Meido (Realm of Waiting):** Weighted by: recent population loss from any cause, unperformed funeral rites, PU drops. Mass death without proper spiritual handling attracts lingering souls that refuse to pass on.

- **Yume-do (Realm of Dreams):** Weighted by: shugenja activity surplus or deficit, provinces with thin-barrier history, proximity to spiritually significant locations. Baseline weight always present --- dreams push through anywhere.

### 56.16.1b The Gradient --- Visual Transformation --- LOCKED

There are no visible borders, no zones the player is told about, no mechanical boundaries on the map. The ASCII tiles themselves gradually shift as the player walks toward the heart of the overlap. The player reads the map and realizes something is wrong because what they are seeing does not match what a normal landscape looks like.

The generator assigns each tile an overlap intensity value from 0.0 to 1.0. Tiles at 0.0 use normal terrain assets. Tiles at 1.0 use fully transformed assets representing the overlapping realm. Everything in between blends. The player sees a smooth transition from normal to deeply wrong. There is no moment where the game announces the overlap --- the player decides for themselves how deep to push based on what they see.

**Gaki-do Gradient:** Normal forest becomes grey and lifeless. Trees lose leaves. The air feels heavy. Food in the player’s inventory starts a spoilage timer. Animal life disappears. At maximum intensity, the landscape is a hollow mockery of the real world --- everything looks like it should sustain life but sustains nothing. Gaki manifest as translucent figures with desperate, grasping movements.

**Toshigoku Gradient:** Normal terrain develops a reddish tint. Old weapons and armor fragments appear embedded in the ground. The wind carries sounds of distant combat. At maximum intensity, the field is littered with the detritus of ancient battles. Warrior spirits appear in the mist --- not transparent ghosts but figures in old armor who do not know the battle ended. They attack anything that moves.

**Chikushudo Gradient:** The forest becomes deeper than it should. Trees are older and larger than any in the province. Animal eyes watch from every shadow. Paths loop back on themselves. At maximum intensity, the player has walked further from civilization than any map says they should be. The forest has become more Chikushudo than Ningen-do. Animal spirits manifest with intelligence and hostility.

**Sakkaku Gradient:** Subtle at first --- things seem slightly off. A tree appears to be in a different place than a moment ago. Sounds come from wrong directions. At maximum intensity, nothing can be trusted visually. Illusions overlay the terrain. False paths lead to dead ends. Trickster spirits appear as friendly NPCs, helpful signs, or appealing objects that vanish or transform when approached.

**Meido Gradient:** Cold seeps in regardless of season. Fog thickens. Colors drain from the landscape. Faint figures stand at the edges of vision and disappear when looked at directly. At maximum intensity, the world is grey and still. The dead are visible --- not hostile, just present. They wander, confused, looking for something they cannot find. Some try to speak but produce no sound.

**Yume-do Gradient:** Reality becomes inconsistent. A building the player passed is suddenly somewhere else. The sky shifts color. NPCs repeat phrases or act out of context. At maximum intensity, the landscape changes between the player’s steps --- dream logic replaces physical logic. Things that should not exist appear normally. Things that should exist disappear. The player cannot trust the map because the map is changing.

### 56.16.1c Encounter Structure --- Spirit Realm Overlap --- LOCKED

The encounter is not a dungeon. It is a spiritual expedition. The player walks into the affected area, pushes through the overlap toward the heart, deals with whatever the realm produces, and protects the shugenja while they restore the boundary.

**The Heart:** The place where the overlap is strongest. At the heart, the player is functionally standing in the other realm while still technically in Ningen-do. The tile intensity is at maximum. The most dangerous spirits are concentrated here. The shugenja performs the restoration ritual at this location --- not closing a portal, but reminding the land that it belongs to Ningen-do. Restoring the identity of the place through prayer, offerings, and elemental communion.

**Enemies:** Spirits native to the overlapping realm. Their stat blocks come from Section 54.2 (Monsters and Nonhuman Races) and the spirit realm entries in Section 54. Gaki-do produces Gaki. Toshigoku produces warrior ghosts. Chikushudo produces animal spirits. Sakkaku produces trickster spirits. Meido produces confused dead. Yume-do produces dream entities. Each realm produces different encounter dynamics --- some are pure combat, some are navigation puzzles, some are social or spiritual challenges.

**Density scales with depth:** Outer tiles have no spirits. Middle tiles have occasional encounters. The heart is densely populated. The gradient in tile intensity also serves as a gradient in encounter density.

**Shugenja detection:** A shugenja character perceives the overlap more keenly than a bushi. They receive text prompts --- the kami feel disturbed here, the Water spirits are agitated, this place belongs to Gaki-do. A shugenja performing Divination (Section 4.3.21) before the mission identifies which realm and gives the party forewarning. A bushi going in blind relies entirely on reading the visual gradient.

**Resolution:** The shugenja performs the restoration ritual at the heart. Multiple rounds of prayer and offering while the bushi protects them. As the ritual succeeds, the world snaps back --- tiles revert to normal spreading outward from the heart. Grey trees regain color. Warrior ghosts fade. The forest stops looping. The temperature returns to normal. The transformation is the reward --- the player watches the world heal in real time.

**Post-battle trigger:** A major battle (Section 11.7) generating significant casualties can directly trigger a Gaki-do or Toshigoku overlap regardless of worship state. The sheer volume of death and violence thins the barrier locally. This means military commanders should arrange for shugenja to perform funeral rites after battles --- not just for honor but for spiritual security. Neglecting the dead creates content.

### 56.16.2 Elemental Imbalance --- LOCKED

An element has gone haywire in a physical location within the province. The kami of one element are agitated beyond their proper measure. This is not an invasion --- the Fire kami are not attacking. They are unbalanced. The place has always had Fire in it. Now it has too much.

### 56.16.2a Element Selection --- Random --- LOCKED

Which element becomes imbalanced is random. Worship failure weakens the spiritual barrier generally --- it does not predetermine which element breaks through. The player and the shugenja cannot predict what to expect based on which Fortunes were neglected. The shugenja must diagnose on-site, reading the signs of which element is agitated, and then prepare the correct counter ritual. Each of the five elements manifests differently:

- **Earth Imbalance:** The ground becomes unstable. Sinkholes appear. Fields crack and shift. Earthquakes that have no geological cause. Buildings settle and foundations split. At the heart: a place where the earth itself has risen or collapsed beyond natural explanation.

- **Fire Imbalance:** A hot spring that has been boiling for weeks. A forge district where fires will not extinguish. Mountain paths where stones are warm and the air shimmers. Spontaneous combustion of dry materials. At the heart: a place of unnatural sustained heat that a shugenja must cool.

- **Water Imbalance:** A river that changed course. A lake rising despite no rain. Wells that taste wrong. Mist that will not burn off by noon. Flooding in places that have never flooded. At the heart: a water source that has grown beyond its natural boundaries.

- **Air Imbalance:** Permanent localized storms. Wind that blows from impossible directions. Sounds carried from miles away or swallowed entirely. Suffocating stillness alternating with violent gusts. At the heart: a place where the air itself resists human presence.

- **Void Imbalance:** The rarest and strangest. Reality feels thin. Time moves inconsistently. People lose moments --- walking somewhere and not remembering the journey. The boundary between life and death blurs --- not undead, not ghosts, just a sense that the living and dead coexist without distinction. At the heart: a place that feels like it exists and does not exist simultaneously.

All five elements have equal base probability. The roll fires when the worship failure threshold triggers an elemental imbalance event. The same province can experience different elements on successive events --- a Fire imbalance this season, a Water imbalance next. The unpredictability is the point. A lord cannot prepare for a specific element. They must maintain overall spiritual health or deal with whatever comes.

### 56.16.2b The Gradient --- Elemental Transformation --- LOCKED

Same gradient system as Spirit Realm Overlap. No borders, no announced zones. The ASCII tiles shift gradually as the player approaches the heart. A Fire imbalance starts with tiles showing slightly scorched earth, then cracked ground with heat shimmer, then glowing rock and steam. The player reads the environment and judges how deep to go.

Each tile has an imbalance intensity from 0.0 to 1.0. At low intensity, the effects are cosmetic --- slightly warmer air, a faint rumble underfoot, unusually still water. At high intensity, the effects are mechanical --- Fire tiles deal damage to characters standing on them, Earth tiles can collapse, Water tiles flood and create difficult terrain, Air tiles impose movement penalties from wind, Void tiles disorient and cause random Perception failures.

### 56.16.2c Encounter Structure --- Elemental Imbalance --- LOCKED

The encounter is a journey to the heart of the imbalance. The environment is the primary threat, not creatures. There may be elementally agitated wildlife or minor elemental spirits, but the kami themselves are not enemies --- they are out of balance. The shugenja does not fight them. They soothe them.

**Environmental Hazards:** Tiles near the heart actively damage or impede characters. Fire tiles burn. Water tiles drown. Earth tiles collapse. Air tiles throw characters off balance. Void tiles cause confusion and lost actions. The player navigates around the worst tiles, finding a path through the imbalance to reach the heart.

**The Heart:** A physical location where the imbalance is concentrated. A boiling spring. A collapsing hillside. A flooding river source. A storm eye. A place that feels nowhere. The shugenja performs an elemental restoration ritual using the opposing or calming element. Fire imbalance requires Water-affinity restoration. Earth imbalance requires Air or Void. The shugenja must match the correct elemental counter. Wrong element does nothing --- the imbalance continues.

**No combat focus:** Unlike every other quest seed, the Elemental Imbalance encounter may involve minimal or no combat. The challenge is navigation through a hostile environment and protecting the shugenja long enough to perform the ritual. A Fire imbalance encounter is about finding a path through scorching terrain to reach the boiling spring without the party dying of heat. The difficulty is environmental, not martial.

**Resolution:** The shugenja performs the calming ritual. The elemental phenomena subside spreading outward from the heart. Boiling water cools. Cracked earth settles. Storms dissipate. The province’s worship deficit remains --- the imbalance can recur if worship is not restored. The mission treats the symptom. Sustained worship addresses the cause.

### 56.16.3 Severity Scaling --- LOCKED

Both spiritual insurgency types scale with the severity of worship failure.

- **2 Great Fortunes at Displeased:** Mild. One bleed event or imbalance per season. Small affected area. Low-intensity gradient. Manageable for a single shugenja with bushi escort. A warning sign.

- **3 Great Fortunes at Displeased:** Moderate. One or two events per season. Larger affected area. Higher intensity. May require multiple shugenja visits to fully resolve. The province is visibly spiritually sick.

- **4+ Great Fortunes at Displeased or any at Wrathful:** Severe. Multiple events per season. Large affected areas. Maximum intensity at the heart. Extended restoration rituals. The province is in spiritual crisis --- the worship system maluses are compounding with active realm overlap and elemental phenomena. Everything is going wrong simultaneously.

- **5+ Great Fortunes at Wrathful:** Catastrophic. Near-permanent overlap. Multiple realms may bleed through simultaneously in different locations. The province is barely Ningen-do anymore. Full spiritual reclamation campaign requiring sustained shugenja commitment over multiple seasons. This is the spiritual equivalent of PTL 9-10 Blighted --- the province has been spiritually abandoned.

### 56.16.4 Interaction with Other Systems --- LOCKED

Spiritual insurgencies are independent of and coexist with all other insurgency types. A province can simultaneously have a Ronin Bandit Uprising, a Maho Cult, elevated PTL with Taint Manifestation, AND a Gaki-do overlap from worship neglect. Each is a separate problem requiring separate solutions. A province in this state is a province that has failed at every level of governance --- military, spiritual, and political. The content volume is the punishment.

**PTL and Spiritual Barrier are separate axes:** PTL measures Jigoku specifically pushing in. Spiritual barrier weakness measures the general thinning of Ningen-do’s boundaries from worship neglect. A province can have PTL 0 and weak barriers (no Taint but spirits bleed through). A province can have PTL 8 and strong barriers (heavily Tainted but the non-Jigoku realms are held at bay by good worship). The two systems are independent. Both can be bad simultaneously --- the worst-case scenario.

**Mass battle trigger:** A major battle generating significant casualties (Section 11.7) can directly trigger a Gaki-do or Toshigoku overlap regardless of worship state. This is a separate trigger path --- not from worship neglect but from the sheer spiritual violence of mass death. Military commanders should arrange for shugenja to perform funeral rites after battles. Neglecting the dead creates content.

**Cross-Reference:** Section 4.3.21 (Kami Worship System --- all threshold and malus tracking). Section 2.6 (Spirit Realms --- lore and game effects for each realm). Section 11.7 (Army Combat System --- mass battle casualties). Section 54 (Bestiary --- spirit stat blocks).

### 56.16.5 Shared Encounter Framework --- LOCKED

This framework applies to all spiritual encounters --- both Spirit Realm Overlaps and Elemental Imbalances. Per-realm and per-element specifics (creatures, unique hazards, visual gradients) are defined in Sections 56.16.1 and 56.16.2. This section defines the shared mechanical rules.

### 56.16.5a Map Generation --- LOCKED

Spiritual encounters do not use the existing map templates (Sections 56.3 through 56.15). The terrain is whatever the province already has --- forest, plains, hills, a settlement. The generator takes the normal terrain and applies the spiritual overlay. A Gaki-do overlap in a forest is a forest becoming Gaki-do. A Fire imbalance near a hot spring is the existing landscape with Fire kami gone haywire. The base terrain provides the physical space. The overlay provides the wrongness.

**Heart Placement:** The heart is placed at a specific geographic feature in the province that makes sense for the overlap type --- a battlefield for Toshigoku, a famine-struck village center for Gaki-do, a natural water source for Water imbalance. The generator selects the heart location from province geography and history.

**Intensity Gradient:** From the heart outward, every tile gets an overlap intensity value from 0.0 to 1.0. Tiles closer to the heart have higher intensity. The radius of the affected area scales with severity (Section 56.16.3). Tile assets shift according to the intensity value. The player reads the visual gradient and judges how deep to push. No borders, no announcements.

### 56.16.5b The Restoration Ritual --- Shared Mechanics --- LOCKED

**Ritual Duration:** Measured in rounds. Base duration depends on severity. Mild (2 Fortunes Displeased): 10 rounds. Moderate (3 Displeased): 20 rounds. Severe (4+ Displeased or any Wrathful): 30 rounds. Catastrophic (5+ Wrathful): 50 rounds.

**Ritual Skill Roll:** The shugenja rolls each round. The skill combination depends on the encounter type (see 56.16.5c for realm-specific approaches, 56.16.5d for elemental counter-rituals). Success on a round counts as one round of progress toward completion. Failure means no progress --- the round is wasted but the ritual is not broken. Previous progress is not lost.

**Interruption:** If the shugenja takes damage during a ritual round, that round fails automatically. Previous progress is preserved --- the ritual pauses, not resets. But the round is wasted and the shugenja must survive the threat before continuing. Every hit the shugenja takes is a wasted round and a longer mission.

**Multiple Shugenja:** Each additional shugenja performing the ritual simultaneously counts their successful rounds toward the same total. Two shugenja working together complete a 20-round ritual in roughly 10 rounds. Three in roughly 7. Each rolls independently. Each can be interrupted independently. More shugenja means faster completion but also more people the bushi must protect --- and each shugenja at the heart is exposed to the full intensity of the overlap. A lord who sends three shugenja and a strong bushi escort resolves it fast. A lord who sends one shugenja and one bushi is in for a long, dangerous mission.

### 56.16.5c Spirit Realm Restoration Approaches --- LOCKED

Pushing a spirit realm back is not about elemental countering. Each realm has a nature --- a reason it is present, a meaning it carries. The shugenja addresses that meaning through the appropriate ritual approach. A shugenja who does not understand what the realm wants will fail. The diagnosis roll on arrival (Perception + Lore: Theology vs TN 15) tells the shugenja which realm is present. Their Lore: Theology knowledge tells them the approach.

- **Gaki-do (Hungry Dead):** The dead are here because something is unfulfilled. The shugenja performs feeding rites --- offerings of food, drink, incense. Acknowledging the dead’s hunger and giving them what they crave symbolically. Performing proper funeral rites for the neglected dead who were never properly sent on. The ritual satisfies the craving that drew Gaki-do close. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Awareness (perceiving what the dead need) vs ritual TN.

- **Toshigoku (Slaughter):** The violence was never honored. The shugenja performs memorial rites --- speaking the names of the fallen, acknowledging their sacrifice, honoring warriors who died without recognition. The ritual releases the battle’s spiritual memory. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Willpower (strength to calm the violence) vs ritual TN.

- **Chikushudo (Animals):** The natural world has been disrespected. The shugenja performs communion with the animal spirits --- offerings to the kami of the land, acknowledging that humans share Ningen-do with the natural world. The ritual restores the balance between civilization and nature. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Perception (sensing the animal spirits) vs ritual TN.

- **Sakkaku (Mischief):** Deception has become too prevalent. The shugenja performs truth rites --- speaking only absolute truth during the ritual, refusing illusion, stripping away pretense. The ritual is an act of radical honesty that the trickster spirits cannot abide. They retreat from sincerity. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Intelligence (seeing through deception) vs ritual TN.

- **Meido (Waiting):** The dead are confused. They do not know they are dead, or they cannot find the way forward. The shugenja guides them --- speaks their names if known, performs the rites of passage that should have been done, lights the way to proper judgment. The ritual sends them onward. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Awareness (finding the lost dead) vs ritual TN.

- **Yume-do (Dreams):** Reality has lost its grip. The shugenja performs grounding rites --- meditation on what is real, what is solid, what is true. Asserting the physical reality of Ningen-do against the fluidity of dreams. The ritual is an act of waking --- forcing the dreaming land to remember it is awake. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Willpower (holding onto reality) vs ritual TN.

### 56.16.5d Elemental Counter-Ritual --- LOCKED

Elemental imbalances require the shugenja to apply the correct countering element. The kami of one element are agitated beyond their proper measure. The shugenja soothes them by invoking the element that calms or opposes them. Using the wrong element produces no progress regardless of roll result.

- **Fire Imbalance:** Counter with Water. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Water Ring vs ritual TN.

- **Water Imbalance:** Counter with Earth. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Earth Ring vs ritual TN.

- **Earth Imbalance:** Counter with Fire. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Fire Ring vs ritual TN.

- **Air Imbalance:** Counter with Earth. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + Earth Ring vs ritual TN.

- **Void Imbalance:** Counter with any Ring at the shugenja’s choice. Void is the element of all and none --- any element can ground it. Skill roll: Lore: Theology + chosen Ring vs ritual TN.

**Diagnosis:** The shugenja identifies the imbalanced element on arrival through a Perception + Lore: Theology roll vs TN 15. Success reveals the element and therefore the counter. Failure means the shugenja must guess or spend additional time observing the symptoms before attempting the ritual. A wrong guess wastes rounds with no progress until the shugenja realizes and switches.

### 56.16.5e The Bushi's Role --- LOCKED

The bushi does not perform the ritual. They keep the shugenja alive while it happens. Spiritual encounters produce threats that intensify the longer the ritual takes. Creatures or phenomena emerge from the overlap, drawn to the ritual itself --- the restoration disturbs the spiritual presence and it fights back.

**Threat Escalation:** During the ritual, the encounter generates emergent threats. Not timed waves --- threats that increase in frequency as the ritual progresses. Early rounds: one or two minor encounters. Middle rounds: regular threats appearing every few rounds. Final rounds: the overlap is fighting back hard, the most dangerous creatures or phenomena appear. If the bushi cannot handle the threats, the shugenja takes hits, rounds are wasted, the mission drags on, which generates more threats. A death spiral if the party is too weak for the severity.

**Positioning:** The bushi positions between the shugenja and the direction threats emerge from. In an open field this means a defensive perimeter. In a forest this means watching the tree lines. The heart’s terrain determines the tactical challenge --- a heart in a clearing gives the bushi sightlines, a heart in dense forest means threats can appear from any direction. Multiple bushi can establish a wider perimeter. A single bushi must prioritize which direction to cover.

### 56.16.5f Resolution Spectrum --- LOCKED

- **Full Success:** Ritual completed, all rounds achieved. The overlap dissolves or the elemental imbalance calms. Tiles revert to normal spreading outward from the heart --- grey trees regain color, boiling water cools, warrior ghosts fade, the forest stops looping. The visual transformation reversing is the reward --- the player watches the world heal in real time. The province’s worship deficit remains. The overlap or imbalance can recur if worship is not restored. The mission treats the symptom. Sustained worship addresses the cause.

- **Partial Success:** The shugenja completed more than half the ritual rounds but had to stop --- too much damage, ran out of resources, party forced to retreat. The overlap weakens but does not fully dissolve. Affected area shrinks. Intensity drops. Fewer creatures, less hostile environment. Another mission can finish the job with fewer rounds needed. Progress persists between missions.

- **Retreat:** Less than half the ritual completed. The overlap noticed the attempt and is more agitated. Intensity increases slightly for one season --- the attempt stirred things up without calming them. Returns to baseline after one season. No lasting harm but no lasting progress either.

- **Failure:** The shugenja was killed or incapacitated during the ritual. The bushi may survive and retreat. The overlap is fully agitated --- intensity spikes for one season. Additional bleed events or imbalance events may fire during the spike. The worst outcome --- the mission made things temporarily worse.

**Post-Resolution:** Characters who spent time at the heart may carry spiritual effects. Not Taint --- that is Jigoku-specific. But exposure to Gaki-do might leave a lingering hunger. Exposure to Toshigoku might leave nightmares of violence. Exposure to Yume-do might cause confused dreams for a season. These are flavor consequences, not mechanical penalties, unless the severity was Catastrophic --- at which point the shugenja and bushi should make a Willpower roll vs TN 20 or carry a minor spiritual affliction for one season.

### 56.16.6 Gaki-do Encounter --- Full Design --- LOCKED

The Gaki-do overlap encounter uses the shared framework (Section 56.16.5) with the following Gaki-do-specific details. Trigger conditions: famine provinces, mass battle casualties, worship failure at 2+ Great Fortunes Displeased. Mass battle is a direct trigger regardless of worship state.

**Cross-Reference:** Shozai-Gaki stat block in Section 54.2. Buruburu, O-Toyo, Mokumokuren in Section 54 (Spirits of Gaki-do). Homebrew Gaki-do creatures (Muzai-gaki, Usai-gaki Swarm, Jikininki, Haraigaki, Fukuregaki, Kagaki, Gashadokuro) in Section 54 (Homebrew Spirits of Gaki-do).

### 56.16.6a Gaki-do Exposure Mechanic --- LOCKED

When a character is present in a Gaki-do overlap at high intensity tiles, the realm erodes their willpower. This is the mechanical cost of being somewhere you do not belong.

- **Willpower Erosion:** Each 10 minutes (approximately 100 rounds) spent within the overlap, the character must roll Willpower vs a TN that starts at 10 and increases by +1 for each subsequent check. Over a 1-hour encounter this produces 6 checks at TN 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15. Each failed roll temporarily reduces the character’s Willpower Rank by 1. This baseline is intentionally gentle --- the real Willpower pressure in Gaki-do comes from creature stacking: Muzai-gaki Swarm Presence adding to TNs, Haraigaki Wails inflicting direct Willpower loss, and Gashadokuro Bone Rattle adding +2 to erosion TNs. The passive timer is the background hum. The creatures are the storm.

- **Willpower 0:** If Willpower reaches 0, the character is temporarily transformed into a Muzai-gaki --- a formless, confused state of pure craving. They are not dead. They are briefly lost to the hunger. This state is terrifying but reversible.

- **Recovery:** Effects wear off within one hour after leaving the overlap. Willpower Ranks return at 1 per ten minutes once outside. A character reduced to Willpower 0 regains consciousness and humanity within an hour of removal from the overlap.

- **Stacking:** Muzai-gaki Swarm Presence adds to the Willpower TN. Haraigaki Wail adds temporary Willpower Rank loss on top of the erosion. Gashadokuro Bone Rattle adds +2 to the erosion TN. Multiple sources compound --- a character fighting near a Gashadokuro while Haraigaki scream and Muzai-gaki swarm is losing Willpower from four simultaneous sources.

- **Jade:** Jade has no protective effect against Gaki-do exposure. Gaki-do is not Jigoku. It is penance, not evil. The Celestial Order sanctions this realm. Jade recognizes no enemy here.

### 56.16.6b Gaki-do Roster --- LOCKED

**Environmental / Swarm Tier:**

- **Muzai-gaki:** Poltergeist clusters. The ambient spiritual fog. Not a combat threat individually but their Swarm Presence compounds Willpower erosion for anyone nearby.

- **Usai-gaki Swarm (Fujo-Ko-Byaku):** Flesh-eating insect swarms. Area denial. Fire is the counter. Blocks corridors and open areas.

**Mid-Tier Combatants:**

- **Jikininki:** Corpse-eating ghosts at burial sites. Pack feeders drawn to the dead. Recognizable faces. Night hunters.

- **Haraigaki:** Needle-throat screamers. Stationary area denial through Willpower-draining wail. The worst enemy to have near the heart during the ritual.

- **Kagaki:** Flame-mouth wanderers. Mobile fire hazard. Not aggressive but sets the terrain on fire. Water is the counter. Devastating in forested overlaps.

- **Fukuregaki:** Bloated gravity wells. Immobile but pull everything toward them. Grapple and absorb. Terrain hazard that reshapes movement around itself.

**Heavy Combatant:**

- **Shozai-Gaki:** The existing stat block from Section 54.2. Blood draining, invisible, possession. The most dangerous individual Gaki. Fast, stealthy, Fear 3.

**Boss Tier:**

- **Gashadokuro:** Giant skeleton from famine dead. Only in mass famine overlaps. Night only. 250 Wounds, Reduction 15, regeneration. Not a fight you win --- a fight you survive while the shugenja closes the overlap.

**Terrain-Specific:**

- **O-Toyo:** Forest overlap only. Cat-form ambush predator from the existing bestiary. Disguises as a normal cat.

- **Mokumokuren:** Settlement overlap only. Eyes in paper walls. Mental attack. Niche but devastating in urban Gaki-do overlaps.

**Post-Encounter:**

- **Buruburu:** Not an encounter enemy. A fear-feeding spirit that attaches to vulnerable characters after the mission and haunts them over weeks. Post-exposure consequence for characters who were reduced to low Willpower during the overlap.

### 56.16.6c Gaki-do Encounter Flow --- LOCKED

The player enters the overlap. The tiles shift gradually per the gradient described in Section 56.16.1b --- grey and lifeless, heavy air, food spoiling, animal life disappearing. No borders, no announcements. The player reads the map.

**Outer tiles:** Muzai-gaki clusters. Poltergeist activity --- objects moving, doors banging, stones shifting. Unsettling but not dangerous. The Willpower erosion begins here at low pressure.

**Middle tiles:** Jikininki at burial sites and corpse locations. Usai-gaki swarms blocking passages. Kagaki wandering and setting things on fire. The landscape is visibly Gaki-do --- grey, hollow, decaying. Willpower erosion intensifies. The player must choose routes carefully.

**Heart approach:** Fukuregaki sitting in the path, pulling debris and characters toward themselves. Haraigaki screaming, covering the approach with Willpower drain. Shozai-gaki lurking invisible, striking when the player is already weakened.

**The Heart:** The shugenja performs feeding rites (Lore: Theology + Awareness vs ritual TN). The bushi holds the perimeter. Threats escalate --- more Shozai-gaki, Jikininki converging on any corpses created during the fight, Haraigaki screaming from the edges. If the overlap was triggered by mass famine, the Gashadokuro manifests at nightfall and moves toward the heart. The shugenja must finish before it arrives or the bushi must hold it at bay.

**Resolution:** Per Section 56.16.5f. On full success, the world heals --- grey trees regain color, the air lightens, the Gaki fade. All Willpower erosion reverses within the hour. Characters check for Buruburu attachment if they were reduced below Willpower 2 during the encounter.

### 56.16.6d Jigoku-Corrupted Gaki --- LOCKED

Gaki-do exists close to Jigoku. The source material confirms that Jigoku’s corruption worms into Gaki-do like maggots in meat. Some Gaki encountered in an overlap --- particularly Shozai-gaki --- may have been recruited by Jigoku. These corrupted Gaki are more dangerous than their normal counterparts and have different vulnerabilities.

- **Probability:** In a province with PTL 0-2, the chance of any Shozai-gaki being Jigoku-corrupted is 5%. At PTL 3-5, 15%. At PTL 6-8, 30%. At PTL 9-10, 50%. The overlap between Gaki-do spiritual bleed and Jigoku Taint makes provinces with both problems simultaneously produce the most dangerous Gaki encounters.

- **Stat Changes:** A Jigoku-corrupted Shozai-gaki gains +1 Earth, +1 Strength, Taint Rank 2-4, and Reduction +3. It is visibly different --- darker coloring, blackened claws, visible corruption marks on translucent flesh. The hunger is the same but the malice is amplified by Jigoku’s influence.

- **Jade Vulnerability:** Jade weapons deal +1k0 damage against Jigoku-corrupted Shozai-gaki. This is the only Gaki-do creature type that jade affects. Jade has no effect on normal (non-corrupted) Gaki of any kind.

- **Detection:** A shugenja can distinguish corrupted from normal Shozai-gaki through Perception + Lore: Shadowlands vs TN 15. A bushi has to read the visual cues --- the darker coloring, the more aggressive behavior. Normal Shozai-gaki attack to feed. Corrupted ones attack to destroy.

- **Taint Exposure:** Unlike normal Gaki combat, melee with a Jigoku-corrupted Shozai-gaki is a Taint exposure event. Each hit received requires an Earth roll vs TN 15 or gain 1 Point of Taint. This is the Jigoku influence, not the Gaki-do nature. A province simultaneously experiencing Gaki-do overlap and elevated PTL is fighting two spiritual diseases at once.

### 56.16.6e Buruburu Post-Encounter Attachment --- LOCKED

The Buruburu (Section 54, Spirits of Gaki-do) is not an encounter enemy. It is a post-mission consequence. A fear-feeding spirit that attaches to vulnerable characters after the Gaki-do overlap encounter and haunts them over weeks.

- **Attachment Trigger:** Any character who was reduced below Willpower 2 during the Gaki-do overlap encounter has a 25% chance of attracting a Buruburu. The check fires once, after the mission, when the character returns to safety. The Buruburu followed them out of the overlap invisibly. The character does not know.

- **Onset:** Nothing happens for 2-3 days. Then the nightmares begin. The character cannot sleep properly. Per the Buruburu stat block: Willpower TN 20 each night. Failure means no rest. After three consecutive failures, +5 TN penalty on all rolls. Each consecutive day after adds +5. Twenty consecutive failures is death.

- **Discovery:** The character and their lord initially assume the nightmares are normal post-mission stress. After one week of persistent nightmares, a shugenja may suspect spiritual cause. Detecting the Buruburu requires Contested Awareness vs the Buruburu’s Awareness 5, or the spell By the Light of the Moon, or any magical means of detecting spirits. The Buruburu is invisible and physically attached to the victim --- it cannot be found by casual observation.

- **Removal:** Once detected, the Buruburu is fragile (20 Wounds, but Reduction 10 --- needs jade, crystal, or magic to damage efficiently). A shugenja banishing it with Earth magic is the cleanest solution. A monk performing purification rites can also drive it away. If the character overcomes their nightmares through sheer Willpower (making the TN 20 roll consistently for 7 consecutive nights after detection), the Buruburu gives up and departs on its own.

- **Narrative Function:** The Buruburu extends the Gaki-do encounter beyond the ASCII map. The player resolves the overlap, goes home, and thinks the mission is done. Then their best bushi starts having nightmares. The lord must recognize the problem, send for a shugenja, and resolve it --- or lose the character over weeks. It teaches the player that spiritual encounters have consequences that follow you home.

### 56.16.6f Willpower 0 --- Full Transformation --- LOCKED

The exposure mechanic in Section 56.16.6a states that a character reduced to Willpower 0 is temporarily transformed into a Muzai-gaki. This requires clarification on what happens mechanically and how recovery works.

- **Immediate Effect:** The character becomes incorporeal and formless. They lose control of their body --- it slumps to the ground empty. Their consciousness is pulled into the Gaki-do overlap as a Muzai-gaki. They experience formless, directionless hunger. They cannot speak, cannot act, cannot help their allies. On the ASCII map, the character’s tile shows an empty body and a new Muzai-gaki appears nearby.

- **Allies' Response:** The remaining party members can carry the empty body out of the overlap. The character’s Muzai-gaki form is drawn along with the body --- it cannot stray far from its physical form. Once outside the overlap, the Muzai-gaki form dissolves and the character’s consciousness returns. Willpower begins recovering at 1 Rank per ten minutes.

- **Left Behind:** If the body is not removed from the overlap within one hour of reaching Willpower 0, the transformation deepens. The connection between soul and body weakens. Recovery now requires shugenja intervention --- a specific ritual (Lore: Theology + Awareness vs TN 25) to call the soul back to the body. Without this intervention, the character remains a Muzai-gaki indefinitely, trapped in the overlap.

- **Permanent Loss:** If the overlap is closed while a character is in Muzai-gaki state and their body was not removed, the character’s soul is pulled back to Gaki-do with the closing overlap. The body dies. The soul is condemned to serve its own sentence in Gaki-do --- forced to fulfill a penance before reincarnation. The character is permanently lost. This is the worst possible outcome of the Gaki-do encounter and should be extremely rare.

### 56.16.6g Wealth-Gaki and Power-Gaki --- Design Note --- LOCKED

The source material identifies three Gaki motivation categories: food-gaki (hunger for sustenance), wealth-gaki (hunger for material possessions), and power-gaki (hunger for status and authority). The combat roster already covers the food-gaki extensively through the Shozai-gaki, Jikininki, and the homebrew variants. Wealth-gaki and power-gaki do not produce distinct combat enemies --- their cravings are abstract rather than physical. However, they manifest as encounter flavor:

- **Wealth-Gaki Presence:** In a Gaki-do overlap, items go missing. Equipment left on the ground vanishes. Koku in pouches disappears. Stored goods in nearby buildings are found empty. This is wealth-gaki activity --- formless spirits stealing objects to briefly hold before the objects dissolve in their incorporeal grasp. Mechanically: unattended items within the overlap have a 10% chance per round of vanishing. The player must keep everything on their person or lose it. This creates inventory pressure during the encounter.

- **Power-Gaki Presence:** In a Gaki-do overlap, characters with high Status or Glory feel watched. Uncomfortable. As if something is envying them intensely. Power-gaki are drawn to authority like Shozai-gaki are drawn to blood. Characters with Status 4+ within the overlap suffer an additional -1 to Willpower erosion rolls --- the power-gaki’s envy is a spiritual weight pressing specifically on the powerful. Ronin and peasant characters feel nothing additional. This inverts the normal social hierarchy --- high status is a vulnerability in Gaki-do.

### 56.16.7 Chikushudo Encounter --- Full Design --- LOCKED

The Chikushudo overlap encounter is fundamentally different from every other spiritual encounter. There is no horror. There is no malice. The danger is beauty. The forest is more alive, more perfect, more harmonious than anything in Ningen-do. The animals are magnificent. The air is clean. The light is golden. The danger is that the character does not want to leave.

**Cross-Reference:** Kitsune and Kitsune-tsuki stat blocks in Section 54 (Spirits of Chikushudo). Spirit Animal Overlay in Section 54 (Chikushudo Spirit Animal Overlay). Hengeyokai Spirit Lord in Section 54. Base animal stat blocks in Section 54.1.

### 56.16.7a Chikushudo Exposure Mechanic --- Temptation of Peace --- LOCKED

Chikushudo does not erode willpower through suffering. It erodes willpower through contentment. The character feels at home. The mortal world --- with its wars, politics, cruelty --- seems distant and ugly. Why would anyone go back?

- **Temptation Roll:** Willpower roll each minute (approximately 10 rounds) spent within the overlap. TN starts at 10, increases by +1 for each subsequent minute. This is not magical compulsion. It is the simple appeal of a perfect place. The character always has freedom of will. They can always choose to leave. It just gets harder to want to.

- **Failed Roll Effects:** Each failed roll gives -1 to all Initiative rolls and -1k0 to attack rolls against spirit animals. Stacking. The character becomes calm, content, reluctant to fight. They hesitate before attacking the beautiful spirit wolf. They pause to watch the light filtering through ancient trees. They forget why they came.

- **Pacification:** At 5 cumulative failures, the character is functionally pacified. They will not fight, will not move toward the heart, will not support the shugenja. They sit and watch. They are at peace. Allies can snap them out of it with a Contested Willpower roll --- shouting, shaking, reminding them of their duty, their family, their lord. Each successful contested roll removes one level of pacification.

- **Recovery:** Immediate once outside the overlap. The beauty fades and the real world reasserts itself. All Initiative and attack penalties vanish. The character remembers who they are and what they were doing. But they may carry a lingering sense of loss --- a faint sadness that the perfect forest is gone. This is flavor, not mechanical.

- **Prolonged Exposure (Source Material):** A mortal who voluntarily remains in Chikushudo for months undergoes a gradual transformation --- becoming more animalistic, eventually becoming a native animal spirit. This is not relevant to the encounter timeframe (minutes to hours) but applies if a character is left behind in the overlap. A character at maximum pacification who is not retrieved before the overlap closes may be pulled into Chikushudo permanently --- their body transforms over a period of days into an animal form. Recovery requires shugenja intervention within one week. After that, the transformation is permanent. The character becomes an NPC animal spirit.

### 56.16.7b Chikushudo Roster --- LOCKED

**Territorial Defenders:**

- **Spirit Wolves:** Packs of 3-5. Coordinated flanking, ambush tactics, howling communication. The primary combat encounter. They attack to drive intruders out, not to kill. If the party retreats, they disengage.

- **Spirit Boars:** Solitary or in pairs. Aggressive charges, hold ground at chokepoints. Tougher than wolves but less mobile. Found guarding specific areas --- clearings, streams, paths to the heart.

- **Spirit Bears:** Solitary. Heavy hitters near the heart. Earth 4 base becomes Earth 6 with overlay. Enormous, powerful, terrifyingly beautiful. The last line before the spirit lord.

- **Spirit Stags:** Fast, antler charges, lead other animals. A spirit stag appearing often signals that wolves are about to flank from another direction. They serve as field commanders for the territorial defense.

**Aerial Threats:**

- **Spirit Eagles and Hawks:** Dive attacks from above. Harass ranged characters and disrupt shugenja concentration during the ritual. Difficult to hit in flight. Irritating more than dangerous but they can interrupt ritual rounds.

**Ambush:**

- **Spirit Snakes:** Hidden in undergrowth. Poisonous bite. Strike from concealment. The only spirit animal that attacks without warning --- all others give visual or auditory signals before engaging.

**Trickster / Negotiator:**

- **Kitsune:** Existing stat block. Not necessarily hostile. May talk to the party. May lead them in circles. May test their intentions. A Kitsune encounter can be resolved without combat if the player shows genuine respect for the forest. A Kitsune who trusts the party might even guide them toward the heart --- bypassing territorial defenders. Or it might be lying. Kitsune are Kitsune.

**Dangerous Spirit:**

- **Kitsune-tsuki:** Dead fox spirit. Possesses characters. Partial Invulnerability to mundane weapons. Spirit Strike ignores armor. The one genuinely hostile Chikushudo denizen --- a tormented creature that does not share the realm’s harmony. Rare but dangerous. Encountered alone, never in groups.

**Boss Tier:**

- **Hengeyokai Spirit Lord:** Ancient animal spirit at the heart. 200 Wounds, Partially Invulnerable, Commanding Presence buffs all nearby spirit animals. Can be negotiated with. Diplomacy is a genuine tactical advantage --- a shugenja who gains consent receives Free Raises on the ritual. Forcing the ritual against the spirit lord means fighting it simultaneously. Full stat block in Section 54.

### 56.16.7c Chikushudo Mechanical Effects --- LOCKED

The Chikushudo overlap changes how certain game mechanics work within the affected area. These apply to both the player’s party and to any NPC present.

- **Elemental Spells:** All elemental spell casting rolls receive a Free Raise within the overlap. The kami are in harmony here and respond more readily to shugenja requests.

- **Animal Control Spells:** Any spell or ability that summons, binds, or controls animals does not function. The animals of Chikushudo cannot be commanded by mortals in their native domain. This includes Kitsune family abilities and similar school techniques.

- **Animal Handling:** Free Raise on all Animal Handling skill rolls. Communication with animals is easier. But communication does not mean obedience --- the animals understand you and choose their own response.

- **No Taint:** Taint cannot exist within a Chikushudo overlap. Characters with existing Taint Ranks feel their corruption suppressed --- Taint-related penalties are suspended while inside the overlap. This is temporary. The Taint returns when they leave. But for the duration, they feel clean. For a Tainted character, this makes the Temptation of Peace even harder to resist --- why leave the one place where the corruption stops hurting?

### 56.16.7d Chikushudo Encounter Flow --- LOCKED

The player enters the overlap. The tiles shift gradually per Section 56.16.1b --- the forest deepens, trees grow taller and older, the light becomes golden, animal sounds increase, paths become game trails. No borders, no announcements. The player reads the map and sees paradise growing around them.

**Outer tiles:** Normal forest transitioning to deeper growth. Spirit animals visible at a distance --- watching, not attacking. A spirit stag standing on a ridge, observing. Wolf tracks on game trails. The beauty begins. Temptation rolls start at low TN.

**Middle tiles:** The forest is unmistakably Chikushudo. Trees are ancient and vast. Light filters through impossibly thick canopy. Spirit wolves patrol territorial boundaries. Spirit boars guard stream crossings. A Kitsune might appear --- offering conversation or misdirection. Temptation intensifies. Characters begin failing rolls and losing Initiative and combat edge. The party has to push through creatures they feel guilty about fighting while resisting the urge to stop and stay.

**Heart approach:** Spirit bears. The most powerful territorial defenders short of the spirit lord. The forest here is older than the province. The light is golden and warm. The air smells of flowers that do not exist in Ningen-do. Characters at multiple failed temptation rolls may refuse to advance without being snapped out of it by allies.

**The Heart:** The spirit lord waits. The shugenja can attempt negotiation (Etiquette + Awareness vs TN 25) or begin the ritual immediately. If negotiations succeed, the ritual proceeds peacefully --- the spirit lord watches, the forest is still, the communion happens without interference. If negotiations fail or are not attempted, the spirit lord attacks and the territorial defenders converge. The shugenja performs the communion ritual (Lore: Theology + Perception vs ritual TN) while the bushi fights the most powerful being in the encounter.

**Resolution:** Per Section 56.16.5f. On full success, the forest recedes. The ancient trees shrink back to normal. The golden light fades to ordinary daylight. The spirit animals withdraw into Chikushudo. The spirit lord departs last --- and if negotiations succeeded, it departs with dignity rather than being banished. The world returns to normal. The province’s worship deficit remains. The overlap can recur if worship is not restored.

### 56.16.8 Toshigoku Encounter --- Full Design --- LOCKED

The Toshigoku overlap encounter is pure combat intensity. The overlap turns a place in Ningen-do into an active battlefield from an era that never ended. The danger is not beauty or hunger --- it is violence itself, and the encounter punishes the very aggression it provokes.

**Cross-Reference:** Toshigoku Encounter Archetypes (Musha Recruit, Musha Soldier, Musha Commander, Ancient General, Phantom Battle) in Section 54. Tsuno stat block in Section 54.2 (Jigoku-corrupted, special case). Section 2.6.8 (Toshigoku realm lore and game effects).

### 56.16.8a Toshigoku Exposure Mechanic --- Battle Rage --- LOCKED

Toshigoku erodes willpower through violence. The more a character fights, the faster they lose themselves. Every combat encounter within the overlap is an exposure check. Avoidance is rewarded --- every fight skipped is a Willpower check saved. The paradox: the player must fight through to reach the heart, but fighting feeds the overlap.

- **Passive Erosion:** Willpower roll every 10 minutes (100 rounds). TN 15, increases by +1 per check. Over a 1-hour encounter: 6 passive checks at TN 15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20.

- **Combat Trigger:** One additional Willpower roll per distinct combat encounter at the current passive TN. Each separate group of enemies fought costs one check. 5-6 combat encounters over a full dungeon means 5-6 additional checks. Sneaking past a group saves one check. Stealth matters more in Toshigoku than any other encounter.

- **Failed Roll:** Each failure reduces Willpower Rank by 1. Does not affect Earth Ring for Wound calculation purposes.

- **Crystal Protection:** A character carrying crystal adds +2k0 to all Toshigoku Willpower resistance rolls. Crystal degrades one quality tier per 30 minutes of exposure. Standard crystal lasts the full hour. Fine crystal provides a buffer. Crystal is the bushi’s lifeline.

### 56.16.8b Behavioral Thresholds --- LOCKED

- **Willpower reduced by 1:** No mechanical effect. The character feels aggressive, restless.

- **Willpower reduced by 2:** -1k0 to all Social rolls. +1k0 to all damage rolls. The violence seeps in --- hits harder, thinks less clearly.

- **Willpower reduced to 2:** The character cannot voluntarily retreat. Must make a Willpower roll vs TN 15 to disengage from combat or move away from an enemy. Failure means they keep fighting. Allies can physically drag them (Contested Strength roll).

- **Willpower reduced to 1:** The character attacks the nearest target --- friend or foe. No distinction. Each round they can attempt Willpower vs TN 20 to regain control for that round only.

- **Willpower 0:** Permanent transformation into a slaughter spirit. Joins the Musha. Allies have 5 rounds (30 seconds) to drag the body out. If removed within 5 rounds, the character is rescued but carries permanent consequences. If not removed, the character is permanently lost.

### 56.16.8c Post-Encounter Consequences --- LOCKED

- **Lowest Willpower was 3 or above:** Full recovery within one hour. No lasting effects.

- **Lowest Willpower was 2:** Gains the Brash Disadvantage for one season. Quicker to anger, more reckless. Removed early by shugenja purification ritual (1 AP, Lore: Theology + Willpower vs TN 20).

- **Lowest Willpower was 1:** Gains both Brash and Overconfident Disadvantages for one season. Picks fights, overestimates themselves. Same removal method but TN 25.

- **Willpower reached 0 but rescued within 5 rounds:** Gains both Brash and Overconfident permanently. The character was a slaughter spirit for a few heartbeats. That mark never fully heals. Can be reduced to one-season duration through an extended purification campaign --- 3 AP of shugenja attention over 3 seasons, TN 25 each.

### 56.16.8d Toshigoku Mechanical Effects --- LOCKED

The overlap changes how game mechanics work within the affected area.

- **Spellcasting Penalties:** The kami within the overlap are sparse, chaotic, and infused with violence. Water spells: +15 to casting TN. Earth and Air spells: +10 to casting TN. Fire spells: +5 to casting TN. Offensive (damage-inflicting) Fire spells function normally with no penalty. Void spells do not function at all. The shugenja is severely handicapped.

- **Crystal Protection:** Crystal operates against Toshigoku’s control similarly to jade against the Taint. +2k0 to Willpower resistance rolls. Degrades in hours, not days.

- **Self-Reinforcing Violence:** Every enemy killed within the overlap increases the overlap intensity by a negligible amount. Individually meaningless. Cumulatively, a party that fights through every encounter leaves the overlap slightly stronger than they found it. This is not a mechanical penalty the player tracks --- it is a background simulation effect. The lord who sends parties into Toshigoku repeatedly without closing the overlap makes the problem worse over seasons.

- **No Taint:** Toshigoku is not Jigoku. There is no Taint exposure. Jade has no special effect against Musha spirits.

### 56.16.8e Toshigoku Roster --- LOCKED

**Cannon Fodder:**

- **Musha Recruits:** Fresh souls, still confused. The weakest samurai-class enemies. Found at the outer edges of the overlap. Some might speak fragments mid-combat --- a name, a plea. Killing them feels different from killing the hollow older spirits.

- **Ashigaru Musha (Spear):** The most numerous spirits. Peasant soldiers. Individually weak but dangerous in formation --- 3 or more within 5 tiles gain +1k0 Attack from mob frenzy. Thinning their numbers drops the bonus. The spear wall the player hits first on the approach.

- **Bow Ashigaru Musha:** Ranged threat on high ground. Volley fire from 3+ archers. Fragile in melee but reaching them means crossing open ground under arrow fire. Forces the player to choose between the direct path (taking damage) and the long path (taking more Willpower checks).

**Standard Combatants:**

- **Musha Soldiers:** The core enemy. Centuries-old warriors stripped of identity. Fight in loose groups of 3-6. Simple Action attacks. They wear armor from every era. Dispersed on death --- every kill clears the path permanently.

**Elite:**

- **Musha Commanders:** Former officers. Rally nearby Musha (+1k0 Attack). Tactically intelligent --- identify and target the shugenja. Cannot be surprised or flanked. The biggest tactical threat because they direct the rank and file toward the ritual.

**Boss Tier:**

- **Ancient General:** Millennia-old warrior. 200 Wounds, Partially Invulnerable, adapts to the player’s fighting style (+1k0 after 3 rounds, +2k0 after 6). The only enemy that reforms during the encounter --- once, after 200 rounds (20 minutes). If killed early, it returns for the finale. If killed a second time, it stays down. May offer a formal duel --- if accepted, all other Musha cease fighting for the duration. The General wants a worthy fight. Full stat block in Section 54.

**Environmental Hazard:**

- **Phantom Battle:** 3x3 to 5x5 tile areas where ghostly armies clash. 2k2 spiritual damage per round to anyone standing on those tiles. Shifts position every 5 rounds. Cannot be fought or dispelled --- only avoided. The background noise of eternal war made visible.

### 56.16.8f Toshigoku Encounter Flow --- LOCKED

The player enters the overlap. The tiles shift per Section 56.16.1b --- the terrain develops a reddish tint, old weapons and armor fragments appear in the ground, the wind carries distant sounds of combat. Deeper: the red deepens, the sounds become louder, ghostly figures appear in the peripheral vision. At the heart: a full battlefield, blood-red sky, the Ancient General waiting.

**Outer tiles:** Musha Recruits wandering individually or in pairs. Confused, partially aware. Phantom Battles occasionally sweep through. The player can avoid most encounters with careful pathing.

**Middle tiles:** Musha Soldiers in groups of 3-6. Patrolling is the wrong word --- they drift toward conflict. If the player fights one group, the noise attracts others within several rounds. The self-reinforcing nature: violence draws more violence. A Musha Commander may appear, directing soldiers toward the party.

**Heart approach:** Dense Musha presence. Multiple Commander-led groups. Phantom Battles blocking direct paths. The player must navigate through active spirit warfare while managing Willpower erosion. Every unnecessary fight costs a check.

**The Heart:** The Ancient General. The shugenja performs memorial rites (Lore: Theology + Willpower vs ritual TN). The bushi faces the General. The General may offer a formal duel --- accepting means a one-on-one fight while the Musha hold back. Refusing means the General attacks alongside its Musha. The duel option is the tactical choice: one extremely dangerous enemy, or many moderately dangerous enemies plus the General. Each costs different numbers of combat Willpower checks.

**Resolution:** Per Section 56.16.5f. On full success, the violence drains from the land. The red fades from the sky. The ghostly armies disappear. Old weapons in the ground rust to nothing. The silence after the battle ends is deafening. The characters check their Willpower thresholds for post-encounter consequences.

### 56.16.9 Sakkaku Encounter --- Full Design --- LOCKED

The Sakkaku overlap encounter is fundamentally different from every other spiritual encounter. It is not a combat dungeon. It is a deception puzzle with lethal traps. The player cannot trust the map, the NPCs, or even the enemies they see. Some threats are real. Some are illusions. The player never knows which is which until contact. The bushi’s sword is almost irrelevant. The shugenja’s perception is the primary weapon. The encounter rewards investigation, patience, and the ability to resist the urge to engage with everything you see.

**Cross-Reference:** Mujina, Bakeneko, Konak Jiji, Pekkle stat blocks in Section 54 (Spirits of Sakkaku). Kappa stat block in Section 54.2. Section 2.6.6 (Sakkaku realm lore and game effects).

### 56.16.9a Sakkaku Exposure Mechanic --- Compulsion --- LOCKED

Sakkaku erodes self-restraint. The character starts behaving inappropriately --- first mildly, then worse, then completely. The realm does not destroy. It does not care enough. It finds destruction boring. It makes you ridiculous.

- **Compulsion Roll:** Awareness or Willpower (whichever is higher) roll every 10 minutes (100 rounds). TN 10, increases by +2 per check. Over a 1-hour encounter: 6 checks at TN 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20. Lower starting TN than Toshigoku because Sakkaku’s influence is subtle --- it does not feel like a threat until the character realizes they have been giggling for five minutes and cannot stop.

- **1 Failure:** Minor Compulsion. The character does something mildly inappropriate --- blurts out a secret, laughs at a serious moment, fidgets during concentration. Mechanically: -1k0 to the next skill roll requiring discipline or focus (Spellcasting, Investigation, Stealth, Etiquette).

- **2 Failures:** The character occasionally acts on impulse without player input --- touching things they should not, wandering off the path, speaking when silence is needed. The -1k0 penalty becomes persistent on all concentration-requiring rolls.

- **3 Failures:** The character actively undermines the mission. Gives away the party’s position. Triggers a Konak Jiji trap by picking up the baby despite knowing better. Starts a conversation with a Mujina. The character’s impulses override their judgment.

- **4+ Failures:** The character is functionally a Sakkaku native. Everything is hilarious. They cannot take anything seriously. They refuse to participate in the ritual because they think it is boring. They are not hostile --- they are useless. They sit down and watch with amusement while the rest of the party struggles.

**Recovery:** Outside the overlap, Compulsions fade at 1 per hour. Fast. Sakkaku’s grip is loose --- it does not really care if you leave. At 4+ failures, residual effects linger for one season --- the character gains the Obtuse Disadvantage (difficulty taking things seriously). If any Compulsion accumulated while the check TN was 20+, there is a 25% chance of a permanent minor Disadvantage: Compulsion (minor pranks), Can’t Lie, or Obtuse.

**No Death State:** Sakkaku does not destroy people. There is no Willpower 0 transformation, no permanent loss to the realm. The worst case is a character who comes out acting like a Mujina for a season and carries a minor permanent quirk. The punishment is embarrassment, not annihilation.

### 56.16.9b Sakkaku Mechanical Effects --- LOCKED

- **Air Spells:** +5 to casting TN. The Air kami are subverted by Sakkaku’s mischievous nature.

- **Void Spells:** +15 to casting TN. Sakkaku denies seriousness. The Void cannot be easily touched.

- **Void Points:** 50% chance of no effect when spent. The player spends the point but nothing happens. The realm finds the concept of perfect clarity hilarious.

- **Luck Advantage:** Considered one Rank higher in the overlap. Sakkaku rewards the lucky.

- **Unluck Disadvantage:** Functions normally. If a visitor earns Sakkaku’s enmity, their Luck Advantage ceases to function entirely.

- **Minor Annoyances:** Constant. Non-damaging but irritating. Ponytails come untied. Sandal straps break. Samurai trip over their own feet. Flavor text, not mechanical. The realm is having fun.

### 56.16.9c Sakkaku Roster --- Real Threats and Deceptions --- LOCKED

The roster splits into real threats the player must handle and deceptions designed to waste time and resources. The player never knows which is which until contact. Approximately 70% of encounters are real threats. 30% are Mujina-created illusions. A Perception + Investigation roll vs TN 20 before engaging reveals whether a threat is real. A shugenja casting By the Light of the Moon sees through all illusions within range.

**Real Threats:**

- **Kappa (Ambush Predator):** Per Section 54.2 stat block. The one Sakkaku creature that genuinely fights. Hides in water --- rivers, ponds, wells, puddles that should not exist. Grabs, drowns, pulls under. In the overlap, every body of water is suspicious. Some are illusions with nothing in them. Some have a Kappa waiting. The player approaches a stream crossing and has to decide: is it real, is there a Kappa, is the stream itself an illusion? The only way to know is to check or step in.

- **Bakeneko (Shapeshifting Deceiver):** Per Section 54 stat block. Air 2, Earth 2, Fire 2, Water 2. Reduction 5. Shapeshifter. Not a strong fighter but vindictive. Disguises as landmarks, friendly NPCs, signposts, or party members. Leads the player wrong. The most dangerous trick: a Bakeneko disguised as the shugenja walks ahead while the real shugenja falls behind. If the player follows the fake, the real shugenja is left unprotected. Perception + Investigation vs TN 20 spots the difference. If caught and attacked, the Bakeneko fights back poorly but wastes time and triggers Compulsion checks.

- **Konak Jiji (Lethal Trap):** Per Section 54 stat block. Looks like an abandoned baby. Cries to lure approach. Pick it up: weight increases to 400 pounds, paralysis venom injects, the creature eats you. Placed on tiles that look like they need investigation --- a crying sound from a side path, a small bundle near the heart. Characters with 2+ Compulsion failures may pick up the Konak Jiji without player input because their impulse control is compromised. The counter: do not pick anything up. The Compulsion mechanic undermines this simple rule.

- **Pekkle (Time Thief):** Per Section 54 stat block. Air 5, Earth 4, Awareness 7, Willpower 8. All damage halved, dice do not explode. Appears as a lost child asking for help. Plays on samurai duty to protect the innocent. If the party helps the child, the Pekkle strings them along deeper into the overlap, further from the heart. When bored, it reveals its true nature and vanishes laughing. Not worth fighting --- nearly impossible to hurt. Every minute spent on the Pekkle is another Compulsion check wasted.

**Environmental Deceptions (Mujina-Created):**

- **Illusion Tiles:** Each tile has two layers: display and reality. Some paths that look open are walls. Some walls that look solid are open. When the player steps on an illusion tile or uses a detection ability, the display updates to reality. A Perception + Investigation roll vs TN 20 when adjacent reveals the true tile. A shugenja using By the Light of the Moon sees all illusions within range. The map gradually becomes more reliable as the player reveals tiles --- but the Mujina can re-apply illusions to tiles the player has already passed.

- **False NPCs:** Mujina disguised as helpful villagers, fellow samurai, or authority figures. They give wrong directions, warn of dangers that do not exist, say nothing about real ones. Detection requires Awareness + Investigation vs TN 25. The Mujina are extraordinarily good actors.

- **Item Displacement:** Items left on the ground (not in inventory) have a 20% chance per 10 rounds of moving to a random adjacent tile. The player learns not to put things down. Anything held stays safe.

- **Laughter Compass:** Mujina laughter is always audible --- nearby, just out of sight. The laughter increases as the party makes mistakes and decreases when the party is on the correct path toward the heart. A clever player realizes the laughter is an inverted compass --- go where they laugh least. The Mujina would find this realization delightful.

- **Illusory Enemies:** Ghostly samurai, monsters, Oni --- things that look dangerous but are not real. They attack. The attack animation plays on screen. But the damage is zero. The player swings back and the sword passes through empty air. The illusion disperses. 30% of combat encounters in the overlap are illusory. The player cannot tell which is which until they take damage or do not. A Perception + Investigation roll vs TN 20 before engaging reveals the truth. A shugenja casting By the Light of the Moon sees through them. The tactical decision each time: engage immediately (might waste resources on nothing, might save your life from a Kappa), or spend a round checking (accurate information but a real enemy gets a free round).

### 56.16.9d Sakkaku Encounter Flow --- LOCKED

The player enters the overlap. The tiles look normal at first. Then subtle wrongness --- a tree that was on the left is now on the right. A path curves the wrong way. The player reads the map and feels uncertain. This is the point. Uncertainty is the weapon.

**Outer tiles:** Minor mischief. Paths curve slightly wrong. A False NPC offers cheerful but incorrect directions. Laughter from the undergrowth. The first Compulsion check fires. An illusory enemy charges from a treeline --- looks real, turns out to be nothing. Or does it? The player is already second-guessing.

**Middle tiles:** The map is actively lying. Illusion tiles redirect the party. A Bakeneko disguised as a party member tries to split the group. A Konak Jiji cries from a side path --- a character with Compulsion failures drifts toward it. A real Kappa lurks in a pond the party needs to cross. Another pond nearby is an illusion with nothing in it. Which is which? Investigation checks or trial and error.

**Heart approach:** Dense illusion coverage. The Mujina are working hardest here because the heart is where their fun ends. Multiple false paths. A Pekkle appears as a lost child blocking the correct route. Characters at 3+ Compulsion failures are actively hindering the party. The shugenja must see through the deception to find the real path --- By the Light of the Moon is nearly mandatory here.

**The Heart:** The shugenja performs truth rites (Lore: Theology + Intelligence vs ritual TN). The ritual is an act of radical sincerity in a place that finds sincerity unbearable. As the ritual progresses, illusions begin collapsing around the heart --- tile by tile, the true landscape is revealed. The Mujina scatter because honest truth is physically uncomfortable for them. The Kappa flee because the water they were hiding in was half-illusion. The Bakeneko revert to cat form and run. The world snaps back to clarity spreading outward from the heart.

**The Bushi's Role:** Different here than in any other encounter. The bushi cannot fight the Mujina. Cannot fight illusions. Cannot fight the map. Their role is protection and judgment --- keeping the shugenja safe from the real Kappa ambush that comes during the ritual, physically restraining Compulsion-affected party members from picking up the Konak Jiji, and providing the grounded discipline that resists Sakkaku’s chaos. A bushi with high Willpower and Perception is more valuable here than one with high Attack.

**Resolution:** Per Section 56.16.5f. On full success, the illusions collapse entirely. The real landscape is revealed --- often surprisingly mundane. The beautiful, confusing, dangerous forest was mostly a stage set. The Mujina depart, bored now that the game is over. The laughter fades. Silence. Characters check Compulsion levels for post-encounter consequences. The shugenja and bushi walk out of a perfectly normal forest that was never as deep or as strange as it appeared.
