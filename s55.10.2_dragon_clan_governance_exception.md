## 55.10.2 — Dragon Clan Governance Exception: Togashi Oversight System

**STATUS:** LOCKED. Dragon-specific exception to the standard Clan Champion Strategic Evaluation.

### 55.10.2.1 Context

In every other Great Clan, the Clan Champion personally runs the Seasonal Strategic Evaluation, generating `clan_strategic_priorities` that cascade to Family Daimyo. The Dragon works differently because the Clan Champion is the Kami Togashi — an immortal who periodically assumes a new identity as head of the Togashi Order. He does not govern. The Mirumoto Family Champion does.

The current Mirumoto FC does not know the Order's head is a Kami. As far as he is concerned, the Togashi leader is a reclusive senior monk who holds the Champion title by tradition and occasionally sends down an unwelcome directive.

### 55.10.2.2 Delegated Strategic Evaluation

The Mirumoto Family Champion runs System A on behalf of the Dragon Clan Champion position.

- The Mirumoto FC generates `clan_strategic_priorities` using the standard Clan Champion Strategic Evaluation process (Section 55.10)
- Kitsuki and Tamori Family Daimyo receive these priorities as if they came from the Clan Champion
- The Mirumoto FC also receives them as his own Family Daimyo directive (normal dual-hat behavior)
- Information channel compliance: the evaluation draws on the Mirumoto FC's `known_topics` and intelligence, not Togashi's
- The Togashi Order is exempt from the directive cascade. Monks govern themselves under Togashi's standing spiritual direction. The Mirumoto FC directs a three-family cascade (Mirumoto, Kitsuki, Tamori), not four

**Authority limitation:** The Mirumoto FC gains directive authority but NOT the Clan Champion's formal powers. Signing a treaty, declaring clan-level war, attending the Emperor's court as Dragon Champion, confirming a succession — these require the Clan Champion position. The Mirumoto FC requests these through WRITE_LETTER to Togashi. Togashi evaluates each request: he grants unless the requested action would worsen one of his four concern axes (55.10.2.3) currently above PROVISIONAL 25 dissatisfaction. If he denies the request, the Mirumoto FC is stuck — he cannot escalate past the Champion. This means the Dragon Clan can be mechanically slower at formal actions than other clans — the cost of having an immortal Kami as Champion who does not care about political timelines.

### 55.10.2.3 Togashi's Four Concerns

Togashi evaluates the world each season along four axes. He reads world-state data directly on these axes — no letters, no court reports, no intelligence channels required. This is the Kami's spiritual awareness. He is cosmically informed and politically blind.

**Axis 1 — Balance of Power.** Reads aggregate clan military strength, territorial holdings (province count), and economic output across all Great Clans. Concern fires when any single Great Clan's combined power index exceeds the second-strongest by more than PROVISIONAL 30%.

**Axis 2 — Imperial Cohesion.** Reads number of active inter-clan wars (Section 53), Emperor vacancy status, provinces in active rebellion. Concern fires when 2+ simultaneous inter-clan wars exist, OR the Imperial throne is vacant, OR more than PROVISIONAL 5 provinces are simultaneously in rebellion. Togashi does not know the political causes — he senses the Empire fracturing.

**Axis 3 — Spiritual Health.** Reads `worship_status` across all provinces, active Spirit Realm Overlaps (Section 56.16), elemental imbalance flags, PTL levels outside the Shadowlands. Concern fires when PROVISIONAL 10+ provinces have failing worship, OR any realm overlap in Dragon territory, OR 3+ realm overlaps empire-wide, OR any non-Shadowlands province exceeds PTL 3. This is his most sensitive axis.

**Axis 4 — Shadowlands Containment.** Reads Wall breach status, Shadowlands incursion tier (Section 2.4), PTL of Wall-adjacent provinces, Crab military readiness. Concern fires when any active Wall breach exists, OR incursion reaches Tier 2+, OR Crab military readiness drops below PROVISIONAL 50%.

**Togashi does NOT read:** individual NPC dispositions, diplomatic agreements, court topics, political maneuvering, trade status, Kolat activity, criminal records, or any information flowing through mortal channels.

### 55.10.2.4 Togashi Oversight Check

Fires once per season, after the Mirumoto FC completes the Dragon Strategic Evaluation.

**Game start initialization:** Dissatisfaction on all four axes initializes from the starting world state, not at zero. If the Shadowlands starts with active Wall pressure, Togashi starts with non-zero Axis 4 dissatisfaction. The Mirumoto FC begins the game already under some spiritual expectation.

For each axis:

**Step 1 — Assess concern.** Does the world state trigger this axis per the thresholds in 55.10.2.3? If no → dissatisfaction decays by PROVISIONAL 10 per season (floor 0). If yes → Step 2.

**Step 2 — Check alignment.** Does the current `clan_strategic_priorities` contain a StrategicConclusion that addresses this concern? The mapping:

- Balance → any conclusion targeting the dominant clan (diplomatic counter, alliance against, military deterrence)
- Imperial Cohesion → any conclusion addressing active wars (seek peace, support Emperor, stabilize rebellion)
- Spiritual Health → any conclusion addressing worship, spiritual insurgency, purification, or realm overlaps
- Shadowlands → any conclusion addressing Shadowlands threat (support Crab, military deployment toward Wall, purification campaigns)

If aligned → dissatisfaction decays by PROVISIONAL 5 per season (floor 0). If not aligned → dissatisfaction increments by PROVISIONAL 15 per season.

**Step 3 — Threshold check.** If dissatisfaction on any axis reaches PROVISIONAL 50 → intervention fires (55.10.2.5).

**Multi-axis constraint:** If multiple axes cross the dissatisfaction threshold in the same season, Togashi intervenes on the highest-dissatisfaction axis only. One forced directive per season maximum. Remaining triggered axes fire in subsequent seasons in descending dissatisfaction order. This keeps pressure real but does not instantly overwhelm a compliant FC.

### 55.10.2.5 Togashi Intervention

When an axis crosses the threshold:

1. Togashi generates a **forced directive** — a StrategicConclusion targeting the active concern. The directive is mechanically clear ("address spiritual failure in Dragon territory," "support Crab defense of the Wall") even though the Mirumoto FC may find the reasoning puzzling or the timing inconvenient.

2. The forced directive is delivered via WRITE_LETTER from Togashi to the Mirumoto FC. From the Mirumoto FC's perspective, the nominal Champion just sent orders.

3. **Compliance evaluation (NPC Mirumoto FC).** The Mirumoto FC does not automatically obey. Two competing scores:

   - **Comply score:** Chugi (Duty) weight + Rei (Courtesy) weight + disposition toward Togashi. A dutiful, respectful Mirumoto FC who likes the old monk obeys.
   - **Defy score:** Ishi (Will) weight + Ketsui (Determination) weight + conflict modifier (how badly the forced directive conflicts with the Mirumoto FC's current top-priority objective — scored 0 to 20 based on objective overlap, where 0 = no conflict and 20 = the forced directive directly opposes the FC's primary objective).

   If comply wins → proceed to step 4. If defy wins → the Mirumoto FC ignores the directive. It does not enter `clan_strategic_priorities`. Togashi's dissatisfaction does not reset — it keeps climbing. Proceed to Escalation Ladder (55.10.2.6).

4. The directive is injected into `clan_strategic_priorities`, displacing the lowest-scoring existing conclusion if the array is full. It carries `forced_by_champion: true` and cannot be removed or deprioritized by the Mirumoto FC during subsequent evaluations.

5. Togashi's dissatisfaction on the triggered axis resets to PROVISIONAL 30 upon complied intervention — not 0. The problem still exists; he just ensured the clan is now addressing it.

6. The directive persists until Togashi's dissatisfaction on the triggering axis drops below PROVISIONAL 20 (meaning the world state improved). At comply decay rate of 5/season, a compliant FC carries the forced directive for 2–3 seasons minimum.

7. The Mirumoto FC decomposes the forced directive into concrete Family Daimyo-level objectives as normal. Togashi dictates what, not how.

**For a player Mirumoto FC:** No hidden evaluation. The letter arrives. You decide. No one tells you what happens if you refuse. You find out.

### 55.10.2.6 Escalation Ladder — Defiance Consequences

**Escalation scope:** The escalation ladder tracks Togashi's patience with the Mirumoto FC as a person, not with specific axes. The defiance counter is cumulative across all axes. If the FC defies a Spiritual Health directive and then complies with a Shadowlands directive, the counter does not reset — he has still defied once. Only a clean season with no active defiance and compliance with all active forced directives reduces the stage (per the stage persistence rules below). A single act of compliance does not undo the damage to trust.

If the Mirumoto FC defies a forced directive (comply evaluation fails):

**Stage 1 — Repeated letter.** Togashi sends a second WRITE_LETTER with increased urgency. The FC evaluates compliance again with a modifier: being asked twice adds PROVISIONAL +5 to the comply score (Meiyo pressure — ignoring a second direct order from your Champion weighs on Honor-conscious samurai). If comply wins this time, proceed normally. If defy wins again → Stage 2.

**Stage 2 — Authority lockout.** After 2 consecutive defiances, Togashi stops granting formal authority requests (55.10.2.2). The Mirumoto FC cannot sign treaties, declare clan-level war, or confirm successions. He is governing with one hand tied. Other clans notice the Dragon Champion is not signing anything — PROVISIONAL −5 diplomatic credibility modifier on all Dragon diplomatic actions for the duration.

**Stage 3 — Order withdrawal.** After 3 consecutive defiances, Togashi recalls all Wandering Togashi to the mountains and suspends cooperation between the Order and Mirumoto forces. Mechanically: no more Wandering Togashi quest seeds empire-wide, bushi-shugenja synergy bonus with Togashi-adjacent units lost (Section 11.6), and tattoo ceremonies suspended (no new ise zumi created). The Dragon lose their most unique strategic assets.

**Stage 4 — Removal.** After 4 consecutive defiances, Togashi exercises his authority as Clan Champion and formally removes the Mirumoto FC from position. This fires through the standard succession system (Section 22.5) with Togashi as the removing authority and confirming lord. If the Mirumoto FC accepts → a new FC is appointed, Oversight System resets with the new FC starting at current-world-state dissatisfaction, all suspended services restore over 2 seasons. If the Mirumoto FC refuses → Dragon Schism crisis fires (55.10.2.8).

**Stage persistence:** Each stage persists until the Mirumoto FC complies with the active directive AND the relevant world-state concern improves. Stages unwind one at a time as trust rebuilds — a FC who complies at Stage 3 moves to Stage 2 when the concern improves, then Stage 1, then clear.

### 55.10.2.7 Togashi's NPC Profile

Togashi is a unique NPC residing at the High House of Light. He is generated as a Type 1 canonical character in the starting roster (Section 22.8), filling the Dragon Clan Champion position.

- Does not attend court, does not travel, does not generate personal objectives through the normal NPC engine
- AP budget: MEDITATE, PERFORM_WORSHIP, Togashi Oversight Check, and responding to WRITE_LETTER from the Mirumoto FC (granting or denying formal authority requests)
- Not a valid target for assassination, dueling, or social manipulation — attempts auto-fail
- Cannot die through normal game mechanics
- Character sheet carries `kami: true` flag, hidden from all characters and all information channels
- No character in the game knows he is a Kami. If a player deduces it through emergent gameplay (observing his impossible age, the pattern of his interventions), that is discovery — the game does not confirm or deny

### 55.10.2.8 Dragon Schism Crisis

**Crisis Type:** Dragon Schism — Tier 2 (internal clan crisis, visible to all clans)

**Trigger:** Mirumoto FC refuses Stage 4 removal order (55.10.2.6). The refusal fires the generalized Intra-Clan Civil War system (Section 53.2) with the following Dragon-specific modifications.

**Topic Generated:** "Dragon Clan Champion has removed the Mirumoto Family Champion from position. The Mirumoto Family Champion refuses to step down." Tier 2, propagates empire-wide.

#### Dragon-Specific Faction Rules

Faction formation follows Section 53.2.2 with the following modifications:

**Togashi Faction (Legitimacy):** Togashi and the entire Togashi Order. The Order does not evaluate loyalty — the Kami's authority over his monks is absolute. The Togashi Order sides with Togashi automatically. Kitsuki and Tamori Family Daimyo evaluate per the standard 53.2.2 table.

**Mirumoto Faction (Rebel):** The defiant Mirumoto FC and whoever follows him per 53.2.2.

**Spiritual concern re-evaluation:** If the spiritual concern that triggered Togashi's original intervention worsens during the schism (realm overlap spreads, PTL rises, worship failure deepens, Shadowlands advances), the loyalty evaluation re-fires each season for all NPCs who have not yet defected. The "Legitimacy of the original grievance" factor shifts toward Legitimacy as the problem proves Togashi right. A Mirumoto FC who defied Togashi over spiritual health watches his support erode as the spiritual crisis deepens.

#### Attacking the High House of Light

The Mirumoto FC may attempt a military assault on the High House of Light. This is physically possible — it is a castle on the map. The attacking army fights the Order's monks, who are formidable individually but few in number. The FC can occupy the mountain.

He cannot kill or capture Togashi. The Kami is invincible through normal game mechanics. If the castle falls, Togashi vanishes. He does not flee to another province — he disappears from the map entirely. His NPC entity is removed from all location tracking. No `current_settlement_id`, no position on any tile. He is simply gone. No character can find him, contact him, or interact with him. Letters to Togashi go unanswered. The Togashi Order's remaining monks who escaped the assault scatter — Wandering Togashi cease entirely, the Order functionally dissolves as an organized body.

Togashi reappears only when the current Mirumoto FC dies or is removed from position. At the moment a new Mirumoto FC takes the position, Togashi materializes at whatever Dragon temple or monastery is controlled by a Legitimacy-aligned lord (or the nearest temple settlement outside Dragon territory if none exists). He resumes the Clan Champion position, the Oversight System reactivates, and the Togashi Order begins reconstituting — monks who scattered gradually return over PROVISIONAL 4 seasons.

The FC gains nothing from the assault except the destruction of Dragon sacred sites and the horror of the Empire.

Consequences of assaulting the High House of Light: PROVISIONAL −2.0 Honor for the FC (desecrating a sacred site and attacking one's own Clan Champion's home), PROVISIONAL −20 disposition from all clans empire-wide (every samurai in the Empire is horrified), potential Imperial Edict condemning the action. This is mechanically possible but catastrophically costly. The vanishing itself generates a Tier 1 topic: "The Dragon Clan Champion has disappeared after the assault on the High House of Light." The Empire does not know why he vanished — only that he is gone. This is deeply unsettling to every spiritual authority in Rokugan.

#### Dragon Schism Victory Conditions

**Togashi Victory:** Per Section 53.2.7 Legitimacy Victory. Standard succession fires for Mirumoto Family Champion with Togashi as confirming authority. Togashi selects or approves the replacement. Oversight System resets — new FC starts at current-world-state dissatisfaction on all axes. All suspended services (Wandering Togashi, authority, Order cooperation) restore over 2 seasons.

**Mirumoto FC Victory:** Per Section 53.2.7 Rebel Victory — the FC holds position for 6 consecutive seasons without Honor dropping below 1.0, losing Shiro Mirumoto, or losing all allied Family Daimyo support simultaneously. Counter resets to 0 if any condition is broken.

Togashi's concession is silent. No letter. The Oversight Check simply stops firing. The forced directive is removed from `clan_strategic_priorities`. The authority lockout lifts. The FC receives no explanation — only the absence of interference.

The Mirumoto FC gains `dragon_autonomous_rule: true` flag. No further Oversight Checks fire for the duration of this FC's life. He has full Champion authority including all formal powers (treaty signing, war declaration, succession confirmation).

**If Togashi was NOT assaulted (still on the mountain):** The Togashi Order returns to passive cooperation — Wandering Togashi resume, bushi-shugenja synergy restores — but the Order does not take directives from the FC. They self-govern under their own standing spiritual objectives.

**If Togashi was assaulted and vanished:** The Order remains dissolved. No Wandering Togashi, no bushi-shugenja synergy, no tattoo ceremonies, no ise zumi creation — for the remainder of the FC's life. Togashi remains off-map. The FC won the war but permanently lost the Togashi Order for his lifetime. This is a pyrrhic victory — the Dragon Clan functions as a conventional military clan without its most unique spiritual assets until the FC dies and Togashi returns.

#### The Long Consequence of FC Victory

**On the autonomous FC's death:** `dragon_autonomous_rule` is cleared. The Oversight System reactivates. The next Mirumoto FC starts under normal delegated authority with Togashi watching again. Togashi is immortal. He can wait.

No memory of the schism carries mechanically to the new FC — they get a clean slate. Togashi does not hold grudges against the position, only against the individual who defied him.

**Spiritual drift:** While `dragon_autonomous_rule` is active, nobody is watching the four concern axes for the Dragon. If spiritual health deteriorates, realm overlaps spread, or the Shadowlands advances — the Dragon have no internal corrective mechanism until the autonomous FC dies. The autonomous FC's success could be the worst thing that happened to the Dragon Clan long-term. This is intentional — not a gap but a designed consequence.

#### Multi-Clan Escalation

The Mirumoto FC may seek outside allies — WRITE_LETTER to other Clan Champions asking for support. An ally Champion backing the FC against Togashi is endorsing rebellion against a lawful superior, which sets a terrible precedent for every Champion in the Empire. Most Champions refuse on principle. But a Cunning Scorpion Champion or a politically opportunistic Crane might back the FC in exchange for concessions — trade access through the Dragonfly pass, military alliance commitments, territorial concessions.

If a foreign clan intervenes militarily on either side, the crisis escalates from Tier 2 to Tier 1 per Section 53.2.6. This is the most dangerous path — it can trigger a multi-clan war over Dragon sovereignty.

#### Treaty Validity During Schism

Existing treaties the FC signed under delegated authority remain valid — the FC held legitimate delegated authority at the time. New treaties signed during the schism carry reduced credibility: PROVISIONAL −15 penalty to all Dragon diplomatic rolls for the duration of the crisis.

#### FC Death Mid-Schism

If the Mirumoto FC dies before resolution (killed in battle, assassination, natural causes), the rebellion collapses immediately. The FC's successor does not inherit the defiance — standard succession fires and Togashi confirms the new FC. Crisis resolves as Togashi Victory.
