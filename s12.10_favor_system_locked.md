## 12.10 Favor System — LOCKED

A favor is a tracked political obligation between two named characters. It is one of the primary currencies of Rokugani social life — more durable than disposition, more personal than alliance, and more dangerous to break than almost any other commitment. Favors are unlimited in number, strictly non-transferable, and personally bonded between two characters. When a character dies, their favor debts die with them.

**Two Favor Types — LOCKED:**

Specific Favor: The terms are defined at the time of offering. Both parties know exactly what is owed. Examples: ‘I will support your position on the Dragon alliance at the next court session.’ ‘I will send 10 Rice units when asked.’ ‘I will not attend the Lion court this season.’ Specific favors are more valuable disposition-wise, harder to dispute, and easier to enforce. When called in, no ambiguity exists — the debtor honors it or breaks it.

General Favor: Vague obligation. ‘I owe you a favor.’ The creditor defines what they want when they invoke it. More flexible for the caller but worth less disposition when offered. The debtor has some room to contest unreasonable requests. When called in, a contested Sincerity roll may resolve disputes about scope.

**Favor Tiers — LOCKED:**

The magnitude of what is promised determines the tier. Tier applies to both types — a specific favor and a general favor can both be Minor, Moderate, or Major depending on what is being offered.

Minor Favor (Tier 3): A piece of information. A social introduction. A letter of recommendation. Attendance at a specific court. A small gift. Low stakes, easily honored.

Moderate Favor (Tier 2): Political support on a specific topic at court. Access to resources below a threshold. Keeping a secret. Arranging a meeting with a difficult-to-access character. Meaningful commitment.

Major Favor (Tier 1): Military support. Significant resource transfer. Publicly backing someone in a dispute. Arranging a marriage. Withholding damaging information permanently. Life-altering commitment.

**Disposition Values for Offering a Favor — LOCKED:**

These replace the existing flat value in the Offer a Favor court action (Section 15.4). Values scale with tier:

- Minor Favor (Tier 3): +6 disposition on success. +2 per Raise.

- Moderate Favor (Tier 2): +10 disposition on success. +3 per Raise.

- Major Favor (Tier 1): +15 disposition on success. +4 per Raise.

Critical failure on any tier: −5 disposition. The target thinks you are insincere or manipulative. No favor debt created.

**Favor Disputes — Specific vs. General — LOCKED:**

When the creditor invokes a favor, the debtor must respond. Calling in a favor is a declaration, not a contested roll. The debtor either honors it or breaks it.

Specific Favors: Terms were defined upfront. No ambiguity. Honor it or break it with full consequences. No negotiation available.

General Favors: The creditor defines what they want. The debtor can argue the request is unreasonable — but ‘unreasonable’ has a narrow definition. It must be something that causes the debtor direct catastrophic harm, or something that directly violates their lord’s primary objective. Personal inconvenience does not qualify.

If the debtor contests a General Favor request as unreasonable: Contested Sincerity roll — Awareness + Sincerity for both parties.

- Creditor wins: the original request stands. The debtor honors it or breaks it.

- Debtor wins: the specific request is set aside. The debtor owes a lesser alternative of the same tier. The favor debt is not cancelled — it is renegotiated.

- The roll is public if in a court setting, private if in a personal visit. Witnesses to a creditor winning a contested favor dispute gain disposition toward the creditor.

**Breaking a Favor — LOCKED:**

Breaking a favor is one of the most serious social violations in Rokugan. The consequences scale with tier and are permanent. They replace the existing historical modifier entry for ‘offered a favor not honored’ in Section 12.2.

Minor Favor (Tier 3) broken:

- Historical disposition modifier with the owed character: −20 start, −15 floor. No decay.

- Honor loss: −0.5.

- Topic generated: Betrayal (Tier 4 Political).

Moderate Favor (Tier 2) broken:

- Historical disposition modifier with the owed character: −35 start, −30 floor. No decay.

- Honor loss: −1.0.

- Topic generated: Betrayal (Tier 4 Political) — spreads aggressively through social networks.

- Disposition loss with all who witness or learn of it: −5.

Major Favor (Tier 1) broken:

- Historical disposition modifier with the owed character: −50 start, −50 floor. No decay. Blood Enemy territory.

- Honor loss: −2.0.

- Topic generated: Betrayal — at Tier 2 crisis level. This is significant enough to become a formal political crisis, not merely a Tier 4 social topic.

- Disposition loss with all who learn of it: −10.

- Glory loss: −0.5.

**Favors and Supply Sharing — LOCKED:**

A favor can unlock supply sharing from characters at the Stranger/Acquaintance disposition tier (−10 to +30) who would otherwise refuse. Offering a Moderate or Major Favor in exchange for the supply request shifts the calculation — the lord now has a future obligation incoming rather than simply giving something away. The favor must be offered before the supply request resolves. A Minor Favor is insufficient to unlock supply sharing — the stakes are too low.

**Favors and Blackmail — LOCKED:**

Blackmail (Section 12.9) extracts favors as its primary currency. The number of favors extracted equals the number of Raises achieved on the blackmail roll. The tier of favor extracted is determined by the secret’s severity tier: Tier 1 secret can extract Major Favors. Tier 2 can extract Moderate Favors. Tier 3 can extract Minor Favors. Tier 4 (minor secret) can only extract a vague ‘goodwill’ that does not constitute a formal tracked favor.

Favors extracted through blackmail are tracked as General Favors — the debtor knows they owe something but the terms were not freely negotiated. Breaking a blackmail-extracted favor carries the same Honor consequences as breaking any other favor, but the creditor’s ability to enforce it publicly is limited — calling in a blackmail favor too openly risks exposing that the creditor used covert methods to obtain it.

**Favor Expiration — LOCKED:**

- Minor Favor (Tier 3): Expires after 1 in-game year (360 IC days) if never called in. The debt fades — it was a small thing and time moves on. No Honor loss for either party when it expires naturally.

- Moderate Favor (Tier 2): Expires after 3 in-game years (1,080 IC days) if never called in.

- Major Favor (Tier 1): Never expires. A Major Favor is a life debt. It persists until called in, broken, or both parties die. Exception: if the creditor dies, a Major Favor passes to their designated heir — the character who formally inherits the creditor’s position and name. Minor and Moderate favors die with the creditor and cannot be inherited. The designated heir has the full right to call in or forgive the inherited Major Favor.

**Gifts and Favors Are Distinct — LOCKED:**

A gift creates a social obligation — tracked by the temporary −2 disposition modifier (active until reciprocated with a gift of comparable quality, Section 12.2). This is informal and soft. It does not create a tracked favor debt in the Favor System. A formal tracked favor is a deliberate political commitment made through the Offer a Favor court action (Section 15.4). The two systems run in parallel and do not overlap. Reciprocating a gift with another gift resolves the gift obligation. It does not create or cancel a favor.

**Calling in a Favor — LOCKED:**

A favor is called in through one of three channels. The method determines social pressure and response window.

By letter (no AP cost): The creditor writes to the debtor invoking the favor. Subject to geographic delivery delay. The debtor has 90 IC days from receipt to honor the favor. Silence beyond this window is treated as breaking the favor with full consequences.

At court (1 AP): Public declaration that the favor is being called in. Everyone present knows. The debtor must respond before court closes. Refusing publicly is more costly than refusing privately — all witnesses observe the breach. Generates an immediate Betrayal topic if the favor is broken.

Personal visit (1 AP): Private invocation. Lower social pressure than a public court declaration. The debtor has until end of the visit or one IC season (whichever comes first) to honor the favor. If refused privately, the breach is known only to those present initially — though it will spread through the topic system.

- Calling in a favor costs no Honor regardless of method. It is the legitimate exercise of a political right.

- A debtor who honors a favor gains +0.1 Honor — duty fulfilled — and the favor is removed from both characters’ tracked lists.

- A debtor who breaks a favor takes the full consequences from the Favor Breaking table above, regardless of how the favor was called in.
