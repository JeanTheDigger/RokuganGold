## 56.6 Deferred Shared Outdoor Systems

The following two systems affect all outdoor ASCII map templates (Occupied Village, Forest Approach + Camp, Makeshift Stockade, Hilltop Position, Ravine Camp, Ruined Structure). They are noted here for future design and are not yet defined.

### 56.6.1 Regional Vegetation/Biome System --- LOCKED

Every province on the World Map carries a biome tag assigned at world generation. The ASCII map generator reads the biome tag when generating any map in that province and selects tile palettes accordingly. Seven biome zones exist.

NORTHERN_HIGHLAND (Dragon, Phoenix, Badger): Pine forests, bamboo groves at lower elevations, bare rock above the treeline. Sparse ground cover of moss and scrub. Mountain streams as water tiles. Snow ground tiles in winter. Wild animals: bears, wolves, mountain hawks, deer.

CENTRAL_PLAINS (Lion, northern Crane): Open grassland, rice paddies near settlements, scattered copses of deciduous trees. Flat terrain with wide sight lines. The farmland of the Empire. Wild animals: foxes, deer, rabbits, pheasants, wild boar.

WESTERN_STEPPE (Unicorn): Tall grass, open prairie, very few trees. The most open terrain in Rokugan. Sight lines extend to map edge in most directions. Wild animals: horses (wild herds), wolves, hawks, prairie dogs.

EASTERN_COAST (Crane, some Phoenix, Mantis islands): Temperate forest, cultivated gardens near settlements, coastal scrub and sand near the shore. Wetter and denser than the central plains. Mantis island provinces use a subtropical variant with tropical trees and heavier vegetation. Wild animals: cranes, herons, fish (in water tiles), monkeys (Mantis islands), snakes.

SOUTHERN_BORDER (Crab): Progressive decay. Healthy vegetation inland, thinning and browning closer to the Kaiu Wall, grey and rotting at the Shadowlands border. The vegetation palette shifts with Province Taint Level — at PTL 0 the province looks normal, at PTL 3+ trees are skeletal and ground cover is dead, at PTL 5+ the Shadowlands palette takes over entirely. Wild animals: normal inland, increasingly absent near the Wall, replaced by Tainted creatures (Section 54.9) at high PTL.

SHINOMEN: Ancient dense forest. Massive trees, heavy canopy, thick undergrowth. The densest natural terrain in the game — vision range is severely reduced and movement through undergrowth costs extra. Wild animals: Naga (sleeping, not encountered as animals), spirit creatures from Chikushudo, ancient predators.

SHADOWLANDS: Dead terrain. Black earth, skeletal trees, toxic pools as water tiles. No living vegetation. No natural animals — only Shadowlands creatures (Section 54.9). The palette is unmistakable: everything is wrong. This biome applies to all provinces beyond the Kaiu Wall.

Seasonal Interaction: Vegetation palettes shift with season (Section 4.3.2). Spring: budding trees, green ground. Summer: full canopy, tall grass, standing crops in rice paddies. Autumn: gold and brown foliage, harvested paddies. Winter: bare deciduous trees (pine stays green), snow ground tiles in Northern Highland and higher-elevation provinces, brown dormant grass elsewhere. Season effects on movement and concealment are defined in Section 56.6.2.

Spirit Realm Overlap Interaction: When a spirit realm overlap (Section 56.16) is active in a province, the vegetation palette is modified by the overlapping realm. Chikushudo: vegetation becomes wilder, denser, and overgrown — vines, massive leaves, flowers where none should grow. Toshigoku: vegetation dies and darkens — dead trees, blood-red ground, ash in the air. Gaki-do: colour drains from all vegetation tiles — grey, washed out, wilting. Sakkaku: vegetation becomes strange — impossible colours, trees growing sideways, ground cover that moves. Yume-do: vegetation shimmers and shifts — the palette changes subtly between turns, nothing looks the same twice. These modifications overlay the base biome palette and are removed when the overlap is resolved.

### 56.6.2 Weather and Season Effects on Outdoor ASCII Maps --- LOCKED

Weather and season modify the physical properties of outdoor ASCII map tiles. These effects layer on top of the base terrain and biome palette (Section 56.6.1). Indoor maps (caves, building interiors, urban hideouts) are not affected by weather. The Battlefield Bubble (Section 11.7) is affected when the battle occurs outdoors.

Weather is a sub-tile-level world state, determined once per IC day based on season and province biome. Both land ASCII maps and naval Army View engagements on the same sub-tile use the same weather — a land battle and a naval battle in the same province on the same IC day experience the same conditions. Each system reads the weather through its own modifier table: land reads through the table below (movement, ranged TNs, vision, stealth); naval reads through the Section 11.9 Weather at Sea table (Company Attack/Defense modifiers). States that do not appear in a system’s table are treated as the nearest applicable state. Weather persists for the duration of the mission or Heroic Opportunity — it does not change mid-map on land. At sea, weather can change mid-battle only through the Shift the Storm Naval Heroic Opportunity (Section 11.7 Category 9). The seasonal probability table in Section 11.9 Weather at Sea governs determination for all sub-tiles; biome modifiers in the land weather states below adjust the probabilities for specific terrain types.

Land weather interpretation: the five unified weather states (Clear, Wind, Rain, Storm, Typhoon) produce the following effects on land ASCII maps. Clear — no modifiers. The default. Wind — no modifiers on land. Minor visual flavor (flags, trees swaying). Rain — all exposed ground tiles convert to mud. Mud tiles cost double movement. Ranged attack TNs increase by +5 (wet bowstrings, poor visibility). Vision range reduced by 2 tiles. Stealth rolls gain +5 bonus (noise masked by rainfall). Storm — as Rain, but movement cost is triple on mud tiles. Vision range reduced by 4 tiles. Ranged attacks suffer +10 TN. Fires cannot be lit. Typhoon — as Storm, plus characters without shelter suffer 1k1 Wounds per 10 rounds of exposure from extreme wind and debris. Coastal and ocean sub-tiles only; does not fire on inland sub-tiles.

Biome conversions (land only): certain unified weather states convert to biome-specific variants based on season and province terrain. Rain → Snow in winter on NORTHERN_HIGHLAND provinces and deep winter on CENTRAL_PLAINS and WESTERN_STEPPE. Snow effects: all exposed ground tiles gain snow overlay. Snow tiles cost 1.5× movement. Characters leave footprints on snow tiles — any character moving through snow creates a visible trail that persists until the map ends. Tracking rolls against a character who moved through snow gain +10 bonus. Storm → Blizzard in winter on NORTHERN_HIGHLAND provinces. Blizzard effects: as Snow, but movement cost is double. Vision range reduced by 4 tiles. Ranged attacks suffer +10 TN. Characters without cold-weather gear suffer 1k1 Wounds per 10 rounds of exposure. Mist is a separate biome-specific state, not produced by the main weather roll. Mist fires as an independent check at dawn and in river valley or coastal provinces: 20% chance at dawn on NORTHERN_HIGHLAND, EASTERN_COAST, and river-adjacent sub-tiles; 0% otherwise. Mist effects: vision range reduced by 3 tiles. Stacks with forest density for extreme reduction. No movement penalty. Stealth rolls gain +5 bonus. Mist clears by mid-day — it does not persist for the full IC day the way the main weather state does.

Seasonal Ground State: These effects apply regardless of weather and modify the base terrain from the biome palette. Spring — soft ground from snowmelt. Open ground tiles cost 1.25× movement in NORTHERN_HIGHLAND and CENTRAL_PLAINS. Rice paddies are flooded (water tiles, impassable without wading). New growth provides light concealment in forest edges. Summer — standing crops in rice paddies provide concealment (characters in crop tiles gain +5 to Stealth, +5 to Armor TN from ranged attacks). Tall grass in WESTERN_STEPPE and CENTRAL_PLAINS provides similar concealment. Full canopy in forested biomes reduces vision. Autumn — harvested paddies are bare (no concealment). Fallen leaves in forested biomes reduce Stealth by −5 (noise from crunching). Dry grass in WESTERN_STEPPE is a fire hazard — fire-based attacks or spells can ignite adjacent grass tiles. Winter — bare deciduous trees open sight lines in forested terrain (vision range +2 in forests compared to summer). No crop concealment. Frozen water tiles become passable but slippery (Athletics TN 10 or fall prone when running).

### 56.6.3 Enemy Alert and Investigation System --- LOCKED

This system governs how enemies on ASCII maps detect the player and react. It builds on the existing Stealth Interaction rules (Section 4.4.2) which handle visual detection through contested Stealth (Sneaking) / Agility vs Investigation (Notice) / Perception rolls. This system adds noise propagation, enemy alert states, and investigation behavior.

Noise Levels: Every action on the ASCII map generates noise at one of five levels. Silent (0 tiles): stealth movement on soft ground, looking, waiting, hand signals. Quiet (3 tiles): normal movement on soft ground, stealth kill if target dies instantly, drawing a weapon slowly, opening a shoji door. Moderate (6 tiles): normal movement on hard ground, opening a wooden door, speaking at normal volume, a body hitting the floor. Loud (12 tiles): combat (any weapon strike, spell with verbal component, a target who survives an attack and cries out), kicking open a door, shouting. Very Loud (map-wide): alarm horns, explosions, building collapse. These base radii assume open air. Terrain modifies propagation.

Terrain Noise Modifiers: Walls block noise entirely — sound does not pass through solid walls but travels through open doors and corridors. Corridors extend noise range along their length by 50% — a Loud action in a corridor carries 18 tiles along the corridor instead of 12. Vegetation dampens noise — reduce radius by 25% in forested or heavy undergrowth tiles. Open air disperses noise normally (base radius). Ravines and canyons echo — all noise is treated as one level higher (Quiet becomes Moderate, Moderate becomes Loud). Water (rain, streams) masks noise — reduce radius by 25%, stacks with the Rain weather state bonus from Section 56.6.2.

Entering Stealth: a character enters stealth mode as a Free Action by pressing the “Go Stealth” button on the ASCII map interface. Precondition: no enemy currently has visual detection on the character — either the character is outside all enemy Perception radii, or the character is behind LOS-blocking terrain relative to all enemies in radius. If an enemy is actively watching the character (the character is in their radius and the enemy passed their passive detection roll), the character cannot enter stealth until they break line of sight. A character cannot enter stealth during combat — once engaged, stealth is not an option until the character breaks contact and leaves all enemy vision radii. Stealth Movement: while in stealth mode, the character rolls Stealth (Sneaking) / Agility each round they move. The TN is determined by the ground surface: soft ground (grass, earth, tatami) TN 10, hard ground (stone, wood floor) TN 15, noisy ground (gravel, dry leaves, shallow water) TN 20. Success reduces the character’s movement noise by one level (Quiet becomes Silent, Moderate becomes Quiet). Failure generates noise at the normal level for that action. Stealth movement costs double — the character moves at half their normal Water Ring movement rate while sneaking. A character not in stealth mode generates noise at the default level for their actions.

Stealth Breaking: stealth mode ends when the character is detected or reveals themselves. Stealth does not break from failure alone — it breaks from detection. A failed Stealth roll generates noise, but if nobody hears it, the character remains hidden. Stealth breaks when: an enemy succeeds on a visual or noise detection roll against the character (they see or hear you — stealth is broken for that enemy and any enemy who can see them react); the character attacks (the Ambush approach roll happens in stealth, but the attack itself reveals the character — stealth breaks after the strike resolves regardless of whether the target survives); the character casts a spell with a verbal component (generates Loud noise, stealth breaks immediately — exception: a character with the Spell Casting emphasis may contest vs observers’ Investigation/Perception to mask the spell’s origin, see Spell Casting emphasis below); the character performs any Loud or Very Loud action (combat, shouting, kicking a door — stealth breaks immediately); the character voluntarily exits stealth (Free Action, “Exit Stealth” button, resumes normal movement speed). A character whose stealth is broken by detection is immediately visible to the detecting enemy. Other enemies who did not detect the character remain unaware unless the detecting enemy generates noise (shouting an alarm, engaging in combat) that propagates to them through the noise system.

Spell Casting from Stealth (Spell Casting emphasis): when a stealthed character with the Stealth (Spell Casting) emphasis casts a spell, two things happen simultaneously. The spell effect is visible — everyone who can see the target tile sees the effect (fire erupts, water surges, an illusion appears). But the caster’s location is contested. The caster rolls Stealth (Spell Casting) / Agility vs each observer’s Investigation (Notice) / Perception (rolled individually per observer). Success against an observer: that observer sees the effect but cannot identify where it came from — the caster remains in stealth relative to that observer. Failure against an observer: that observer identifies the caster’s tile — stealth breaks for that observer. The verbal component noise still propagates normally through the noise system — enemies outside visual range hear the incantation and transition to Suspicious, investigating toward the sound source. The emphasis masks visual identification, not sound. A character without the Spell Casting emphasis who casts a spell from stealth breaks stealth automatically — no contested roll. The Soshi school technique The Kami’s Whisper (Section 29) is strictly superior for non-damaging spells — it eliminates all signs including noise at the cost of an extra spell slot. The Spell Casting emphasis is the fallback for damaging spells or non-Soshi casters.

Noise Detection: When noise propagates to a tile occupied by an enemy, that enemy makes an Investigation (Notice) / Perception roll. The TN depends on noise level at their position: Quiet TN 20, Moderate TN 15, Loud TN 10, Very Loud automatic detection. If the roll succeeds, the enemy registers the noise and transitions to Suspicious. If the roll fails, the enemy heard nothing. Sleeping enemies add +10 to all detection TNs. Enemies already in combat (Loud noise all around them) add +5 to detection TNs for distant noise — it is harder to pick out a specific sound in chaos.

Enemy Alert States: Unaware — the default. The enemy follows their patrol route or holds their post. They make passive visual detection rolls only when the player enters their vision radius (per Section 4.4.2). They do not actively search. Suspicious — the enemy heard something. They stop their current activity and orient toward the noise source. They move toward the source location at normal speed, actively scanning. For 3 rounds after becoming Suspicious, they make active visual detection rolls with a +5 bonus to their Investigation (Notice) / Perception. If they find nothing after reaching the noise source, they return to Unaware after 3 additional rounds. If they detect the player or find evidence (a body, an open door that should be closed, blood splatter), they transition to Alert. Alert — confirmed threat. The enemy knows an intruder is present. They engage in combat if the player is visible. If the player is not visible, Alert enemies actively search the area, check hiding spots, and may call for reinforcements (generating Loud noise that propagates to other enemies). Alert status does not decay — once Alert, an enemy stays Alert for the remainder of the mission unless killed. Alert enemies who cannot find the player after 5 rounds may sound an alarm (Very Loud — map-wide), transitioning all enemies on the map to Alert simultaneously. Fleeing — morale broken per the Morale Override Rule (Section 56.10.0). The enemy abandons their post and attempts to leave the map. A fleeing enemy may still generate Loud noise (screaming, crashing through obstacles), which can alert other enemies.

Stealth Kills: A character attacking from stealth mode first rolls Stealth (Ambush) / Agility to set up the strike — this is the approach, not the attack. If successful, the attack uses the character’s normal weapon skill (Kenjutsu / Agility, etc.) but the target’s Armor TN does not include their Reflexes — they are unaware and flat-footed. This dramatically lowers the TN to hit, which is what makes the one-hit kill achievable against tougher targets. If the target dies from the single attack, the kill generates only Quiet noise (body hitting the floor). If the target survives, they cry out — Loud noise, immediate propagation. The one-hit kill requirement makes stealth kills genuinely difficult against anything tougher than Bandit Rabble or Peasant Levy. A high-Earth samurai will survive the first hit and scream. This is intentional — silently eliminating trained warriors should be nearly impossible without overwhelming skill advantage. Body Discovery: A dead body on a visible tile is automatic detection — no roll required. Any enemy whose vision radius includes a corpse tile immediately transitions to Alert. An enemy who enters Suspicious state and investigates toward a noise source will discover any bodies along the way. Players can mitigate this by moving bodies out of patrol paths — dragging a corpse to a hidden tile costs a Simple Action and generates Quiet noise. A body left in the open will eventually be found.

Enemy Investigation Behavior by Unit Type: Bandit Rabble — investigate timidly. Move toward noise source at half speed, stop frequently, flee if they see anything threatening. Bandit Thug — investigate cautiously. Use cover, approach from an angle, hold position if uncertain. Ronin — investigate professionally. Move directly toward noise, weapon drawn, check corners. Samurai (any clan bushi) — investigate immediately and aggressively. Move at full speed toward noise, challenge loudly (generating Loud noise themselves), engage on contact. Shugenja — do not investigate alone. Call for guards (Loud noise), hold position, prepare defensive spells. Sentries and patrol guards — investigate immediately as their primary function. Move toward noise at full speed, raise alarm (Very Loud) if they confirm a threat. The Morale Override Rule (Section 56.10.0) applies to all investigation behavior — a coward ordered to investigate may flee instead, and a fanatic will charge regardless of risk.

Design note — cover and stealth: cover for stealth purposes is binary LOS only. A character is either behind an opaque obstacle (wall, mast, thick tree, boulder) and invisible to observers on the other side, or in the open and relying on their Stealth roll. No tiles provide partial concealment bonuses (+5 or +10 to Stealth). This is intentional — the Stealth roll, ground surface TN, noise system, and weather modifiers provide sufficient tactical depth without an additional cover layer.

⚙️ CROSS-REF: Visual detection in Section 4.4.2 (Stealth Interaction). Stealth skill in Section 24.4. Investigation skill in Section 24.1. Morale Override Rule in Section 56.10.0. Individual Variance Rule (Section 56.10.0a) applies to enemy Perception and Investigation values. Weather noise masking in Section 56.6.2. Per-template alert characteristics (Cave corridor propagation, Ravine instant echo, Village open-air dispersal, Ruined Structure fast alert slow response) are noted in individual template sections and use the terrain noise modifiers defined here.

### 56.6.4 Kansen Environmental Hazards on ASCII Maps --- LOCKED

Kansen are corrupted elemental kami drawn to places where maho has been practised. They are not enemies that occupy tiles and fight with stat blocks. They are environmental corruption — invisible to non-spiritual characters, perceptible to shugenja and monks as a spiritual fog of wrongness in the air. On the ASCII map, kansen manifest as hazard zones that affect all characters within them.

Kansen Density: Three density tiers exist on any given map, determined by the conditions that generated the mission. Low — faint spiritual discomfort. Typical in provinces with PTL 1–2 or near a dormant maho cell. Moderate — the air is thick with wrongness. Typical in provinces with PTL 3–4, near an active maho cell, or in an area where maho was recently cast. High — overwhelming spiritual corruption. Typical in provinces with PTL 5+, inside an active Bloodspeaker cell’s ritual chamber, or in the immediate aftermath of a major maho ritual. Density is assigned per tile or per zone within the map. A maho cult hideout might have Low density in the outer rooms, Moderate in the corridors, and High in the ritual chamber. The density map is generated alongside the ASCII map based on the quest seed that spawned the mission.

Taint Exposure: The primary hazard. Characters in kansen-dense tiles make Earth rolls to resist Taint (Section 42). Frequency and TN scale with density. Low: one roll per 10 rounds spent in the zone, TN 10. Moderate: one roll per 5 rounds, TN 15. High: one roll per round, TN 20. Failure on any roll = 1 Point of Taint as normal. Jade protection applies — a character carrying jade (Section 2.4.15) adds their jade’s bonus to the Earth roll. Jade Petal Tea’s +2k2 bonus applies. Characters who pass through a kansen zone quickly (fewer rounds than the exposure interval) may avoid rolling entirely. The threat is to characters who linger — fighting a prolonged battle in a High-density room is spiritually dangerous even if you win the combat.

Spell Disruption: Kansen interfere with a shugenja’s ability to commune with the elemental kami. All Spell Casting Rolls in kansen zones suffer a TN penalty. Low: +5 TN. Moderate: +10 TN. High: +15 TN. This represents the kami being drowned out by the spiritual noise of corrupted spirits. Maho casters are unaffected — kansen are their allies, not their enemies. Maho spells suffer no penalty in kansen zones. This creates a tactical asymmetry: the maho cultist casts freely in their ritual chamber while the player’s shugenja struggles to reach the kami through the corruption.

Whispers: Kansen whisper temptation, fear, anger, and despair into the minds of characters within their zone. Each round spent in a Moderate or High density zone, characters must roll Willpower at TN 10 (Moderate) or TN 15 (High). Failure imposes a −1k0 penalty to all rolls for that round — the character is distracted, unsettled, emotionally destabilised. This penalty does not stack across rounds — a fresh roll each round. Failed Whisper rolls do not cause Taint. They cause distraction. Low density zones do not trigger Whisper rolls — the kansen presence is too faint for active manipulation. Characters with Taint Rank 1+ are immune to Whispers — the kansen already have a hold on them and see no need to push further.

Detection and Banishment: Non-spiritual characters cannot perceive kansen at all — they feel uneasy but cannot identify the source. Shugenja and monks can sense kansen density by casting the Sense spell or rolling Spellcraft (Sense) / Void at TN 15. Success reveals the density tier of all tiles within the character’s vision radius. This allows the party to plan their route through the map, avoiding High-density zones or preparing jade protection before entering them. Banishment requires a shugenja or monk to spend a Complex Action on a tile and roll Spellcraft / Void (shugenja) or Theology / Void (monk) at TN 15 (Low), TN 20 (Moderate), or TN 25 (High). Success reduces the tile’s kansen density by one tier. A tile reduced from Low to zero is cleansed. Banishment affects only the target tile and its immediately adjacent tiles — clearing a High-density ritual chamber requires multiple rounds of sustained effort. Jade placed on a tile (consuming the jade) suppresses kansen in a 3-tile radius for the remainder of the mission, reducing density by one tier within that radius.

⚙️ CROSS-REF: Taint mechanics in Section 42. Jade protection in Section 2.4.15. Maho cult cell types in Section 56.14. Province Taint Level in Section 11.11. Sense spell in Section 32. Bloodspeaker spell list in Section 54.3. The Shugenja Requirement for Taint-related missions is in Section 56.10.10h.

### 56.6.5 Spellcasting on ASCII Maps --- RESOLVED

All spellcasting on the ASCII map uses L5R 4th Edition Rules As Written. Spells have defined range, casting time, area of effect, and targeting rules in the source material. The ASCII map provides the spatial representation. Tile scale conversion: 1 tile = 5 feet. A spell with range 50 feet targets anything within 10 tiles. Area of effect spells use the same conversion — a 15-foot radius spell affects all tiles within 3 tiles of the target point. Casting time follows RAW — most spells are a Complex Action. Line of sight follows the existing Field of Vision rules (Section 4.4.2). Maho spells from the Bloodspeaker spell list (Section 54.3) and elemental spells from Section 32 both use this same framework. The Kansen Environmental Hazard system (Section 56.6.4) applies TN penalties to non-maho casters in corrupted areas. No additional spellcasting system is required beyond the RAW rules and the tile scale conversion.

### 56.6.6 Fire Propagation Mechanics — PROVISIONAL

Fire on the ASCII map is an environmental hazard that spreads round by round, deals damage, and generates smoke. It interacts with the biome system (Section 56.6.1), weather system (Section 56.6.2), and noise system (Section 56.6.3).

Fire Sources: Any fire-element spell that deals damage to a tile, or any fire-based environmental effect (overturned lantern, deliberate arson). The target tile ignites if it contains flammable material.

Tile Flammability: Each tile carries a flammability state determined by terrain type, biome, and season. Highly Flammable: dry grass (autumn WESTERN_STEPPE and CENTRAL_PLAINS), standing crops (summer rice paddies), thatched roofing, fallen leaves (autumn forest floor). Flammable: undergrowth in non-winter forested biomes, light wooden structures (shoji walls, wooden fences), dead vegetation at PTL 3+ (SOUTHERN_BORDER). Non-Flammable: stone, water, mud, snow, bare earth, green/wet vegetation (spring and summer grass), metal, heavy timber walls. Suppressed: any tile currently affected by Rain or Storm weather is treated as Non-Flammable regardless of its base state. Existing fires on Rain-affected tiles extinguish within 1 round. Storm extinguishes all fires immediately.

Wind Direction: Wind direction is assigned at map generation as one of eight compass bearings (N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW), determined randomly. Wind direction is fixed for the duration of the mission. Wind direction is only mechanically relevant during Wind, Rain, or Storm weather states — during Clear weather, there is no wind and fire spreads uniformly.

Spread Mechanic: At the end of each combat round, each burning tile attempts to ignite adjacent flammable tiles. Spread depends on wind. No wind (Clear weather): fire may spread to any of the 8 adjacent tiles. Spread chance per adjacent flammable tile: 30% per round (PROVISIONAL). Wind active (Wind weather): fire spreads preferentially downwind. The three downwind tiles (directly downwind and the two diagonal-downwind) have a 60% spread chance (PROVISIONAL). The two lateral tiles (perpendicular to wind) have a 15% spread chance (PROVISIONAL). The three upwind tiles have 0% — fire does not spread against the wind. Rain and Storm: the Suppressed rule overrides — fire cannot ignite new tiles regardless of wind.

Burn Duration: A burning tile burns for a fixed number of rounds based on fuel type, then becomes Burned Out. Dry grass and fallen leaves: 3 rounds (PROVISIONAL). Standing crops: 4 rounds (PROVISIONAL). Undergrowth and light wood structures: 5 rounds (PROVISIONAL). A Burned Out tile is permanently changed for the remainder of the mission — ash terrain, non-flammable, no concealment bonus, normal movement cost. Fire consumes the tactical advantage of concealment terrain as well as creating the hazard.

Fire Damage: A character on a burning tile at the start of their turn takes 1k1 Wounds (PROVISIONAL). Armour does not reduce fire damage. A character who moves through a burning tile without stopping takes 0k1 Wounds (PROVISIONAL). Fire damage is environmental — no attack roll, cannot be parried or dodged.

Smoke: Each burning tile generates smoke that drifts downwind. With wind active, smoke extends 3 tiles downwind from each burning tile in a cone matching the wind direction (PROVISIONAL). During Clear weather (no wind), smoke rises vertically and affects only the burning tile itself — no extended smoke zone. Smoke effects: vision range reduced by 3 tiles for characters within smoke (PROVISIONAL). A character who spends a full round in smoke rolls Stamina at TN 10 (PROVISIONAL) or suffers a coughing fit — −1k0 to all rolls for that round and the cough generates Moderate noise (6 tiles, per Section 56.6.3). A character may hold their breath (Free Action at start of turn) to suppress the coughing check, but suffers −1k0 to all physical rolls from oxygen deprivation if breath is held for more than 2 consecutive rounds.

Noise: Ignition generates Loud noise (12 tiles). Ongoing burning generates Moderate noise (6 tiles) per burning tile per round. These integrate with the Enemy Alert system (Section 56.6.3) — a grass fire is not stealthy.

Weather Interaction Summary: Clear: fire spreads uniformly, smoke rises vertically, full flammability. Wind: fire spreads directionally downwind, smoke drifts downwind, full flammability. Rain: no ignition, existing fires extinguish in 1 round, smoke suppressed. Storm: all fires extinguished immediately, no ignition, smoke suppressed. Snow/Blizzard: all tiles Non-Flammable, no ignition. Mist: no effect on flammability or fire spread; smoke and mist vision penalties stack.

Spell Interaction: Water-element spells that create or move water extinguish fire on affected tiles and render them Non-Flammable for the remainder of the mission. Earth-element spells that create barriers (stone walls, earthen ramparts) create Non-Flammable obstacles that block fire spread. Fire-element spells targeting already-burning tiles deal spell damage normally but do not compound the ignition. Wind direction is a fixed map property and is not altered by Air-element spells.
