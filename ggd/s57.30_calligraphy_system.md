## 57.30 Calligraphy System

### 57.30.1 Overview

In Rokugan, letters are never direct. They are layered with implication, seasonal imagery, and carefully chosen words that convey meaning beyond the surface. The choice of seasonal reference, the quality of brushwork at emotional moments, the formality level shifting mid-letter, the things conspicuously not said &#x2014; all carry micro-signals of the writer&#x2019;s genuine feelings. The Calligraphy (Cipher) emphasis represents the ability to read between the lines &#x2014; to extract the writer&#x2019;s true disposition toward the recipient and their genuine stance on the letter&#x2019;s topic from the subtext of a written correspondence. This is not cryptography or secret codes. It is emotional and political intelligence extraction from the unconscious patterns embedded in calligraphic expression.

Every letter written in the game carries involuntary subtext. The writer&#x2019;s true feelings leak through their word choices, brushwork patterns, seasonal references, and compositional rhythm whether they intend it or not. A writer who hates the recipient but writes a polite letter still produces subtle markers of contempt &#x2014; a slightly aggressive seasonal metaphor, a fractionally hasty brushstroke in the honorific, a reference to autumn decay when spring would be more appropriate. The Cipher emphasis allows a reader to detect and interpret these signals.

### 57.30.2 Writer Side &#x2014; Involuntary Subtext

When WRITE_LETTER fires (Section 12.7), two rolls occur simultaneously. First, the existing Calligraphy + chosen Trait roll (Awareness, Intelligence, or Agility) vs TN 15 determines letter quality and disposition bonus on receipt &#x2014; this is unchanged. Second, a NEW automatic concealment roll fires: Sincerity / Awareness. The result of this roll becomes the concealment_tn stored on the letter_object. This roll is invisible to the writer &#x2014; they do not know how well they concealed their feelings. The concealment_tn represents how well the writer&#x2019;s surface composure masks their true emotional and political state. It is not an intentional act of deception &#x2014; it is the writer&#x2019;s natural self-control under the social pressure of correspondence.

Sincerity governs concealment because masking your true feelings is a Sincerity function &#x2014; it is the skill of presenting what you wish to present regardless of what you actually feel. A Scorpion with mediocre calligraphy but Sincerity 5 writes letters that reveal nothing &#x2014; their emotional control is impeccable. A Crab with beautiful calligraphy but Sincerity 1 leaks their feelings through every perfectly formed character. Awareness is the paired Trait because it reflects social self-awareness &#x2014; knowing how one&#x2019;s words will be perceived.

Three fields are stored on the letter_object at the time of writing, recorded from the writer&#x2019;s character state at that moment: writer_disposition_tier &#x2014; the writer&#x2019;s current disposition tier toward the recipient (Strong Ally, Ally, Friendly, Neutral, Rival, Enemy, or Strong Enemy per Section 19 disposition tiers). This is frozen at the time of writing &#x2014; if the writer&#x2019;s disposition changes after sending the letter, the letter still reflects what they felt when they wrote it. writer_topic_stance &#x2014; the writer&#x2019;s true position on the letter&#x2019;s topic payload: supports, opposes, or indifferent. Derived from the writer&#x2019;s disposition toward the topic&#x2019;s subject entity (positive disposition &#x2192; supports, negative &#x2192; opposes, neutral band &#x2192; indifferent). If the letter carries no topic, this field is null. writer_needtype &#x2014; the NeedType or objective that drove the NPC to write this letter (e.g., RAISE_DISPOSITION, DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP, MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION, routine correspondence). For player-written letters, this field is null &#x2014; player motivation is not mechanically tracked.

### 57.30.3 Reader Side &#x2014; Subtext Extraction

When a character reads a letter for the first time, the engine automatically evaluates a subtext extraction roll. This fires once per letter per reader on first reading &#x2014; no AP cost, no deliberate action. The reader either perceives the subtext or they don&#x2019;t. Requirement: the reader must possess the Calligraphy (Cipher) emphasis. Without Cipher, no extraction roll is made &#x2014; the reader takes the letter at face value. The letter&#x2019;s surface content (topic transfer, disposition bonus from calligraphy quality) fires regardless of whether Cipher is present.

Roll: Calligraphy (Cipher) / Intelligence vs the letter&#x2019;s concealment_tn. The reader&#x2019;s Intelligence is the analysis trait &#x2014; extracting subtext is an intellectual exercise in pattern recognition, not a social intuition. The Cipher emphasis provides +1k0 as the standard emphasis bonus. Rank 5 Calligraphy mastery provides an additional +10 to break the concealment (per the L5R mastery ability), applied as a flat bonus to the roll.

On success (met the concealment_tn): the reader learns two things. First, the writer&#x2019;s disposition tier toward them &#x2014; Strong Ally, Ally, Friendly, Neutral, Rival, Enemy, or Strong Enemy. This reveals whether the writer&#x2019;s polite words mask genuine warmth, indifference, or hostility. Second, the writer&#x2019;s true stance on the topic &#x2014; supports, opposes, or indifferent. This reveals whether the writer&#x2019;s stated position in the letter reflects their genuine opinion. Both results are added to the reader&#x2019;s knowledge about the writer and are usable in future social interactions.

On Raises (each Raise = margin of 5 above the concealment_tn): 1 Raise &#x2014; intensity within the tier. The reader learns whether the disposition is at the low end, middle, or high end of its band (e.g., &#x201C;low Rival&#x201D; = barely hostile, &#x201C;high Rival&#x201D; = approaching Enemy). 2 Raises &#x2014; deception detection. The reader detects whether the letter&#x2019;s surface content contradicts the writer&#x2019;s true position. A letter that says &#x201C;I support your proposal&#x201D; from a writer who opposes it is flagged as deliberately insincere. This is distinct from mere concealment &#x2014; it reveals active misdirection. 3 Raises &#x2014; motivation inference. The reader infers the NeedType or objective driving the letter. &#x201C;The writer sent this to build an alliance,&#x201D; or &#x201C;the writer is trying to damage your relationship with [third party],&#x201D; or &#x201C;this is routine correspondence with no ulterior purpose.&#x201D; For NPC letters, this reads the writer_needtype field directly. For player letters (writer_needtype null), this Raise is wasted &#x2014; the system cannot infer player motivation.

On failure (below the concealment_tn): the reader extracts nothing. The letter is taken at face value. The failure is invisible &#x2014; the reader does not know they missed subtext. They simply read a polite letter and move on. There is no &#x201C;I failed to read between the lines&#x201D; awareness. A character without Cipher emphasis and a character who failed the Cipher roll experience the same letter identically.

### 57.30.4 Clan Asymmetry

The concealment_tn is driven by Sincerity / Awareness &#x2014; which creates natural clan asymmetry in letter transparency. Scorpion (highest Sincerity training, Bayushi school starts with Sincerity): Scorpion letters are the hardest to read. Concealment TNs routinely reach 25&#x2013;35. Only the most skilled Cipher readers can extract anything. The Scorpion&#x2019;s mastery of emotional control extends to their correspondence &#x2014; their letters are socially impenetrable. Crane (high Sincerity, Doji school starts with Sincerity): nearly as opaque as Scorpion letters. Crane composure translates into disciplined correspondence. Crab (low Sincerity, direct speakers): Crab letters are the easiest to read. Concealment TNs are often 15&#x2013;20. A skilled Cipher reader can extract the Crab writer&#x2019;s true feelings effortlessly &#x2014; Hida samurai do not have the emotional discipline to mask their contempt in brushwork. Lion (moderate Sincerity, high Honor): Lion letters are moderately transparent. Their directness comes from Honor, not from lack of Sincerity &#x2014; they say what they mean because they believe it is right, but they are capable of concealment when duty demands it. Dragon, Phoenix, Unicorn: variable by individual. No strong clan-wide tendency.

### 57.30.5 NPC Engine Integration

Writer side: every NPC letter automatically generates a concealment_tn from Sincerity / Awareness. The three subtext fields (disposition_tier, topic_stance, needtype) are populated from the NPC&#x2019;s current state. No additional engine logic is needed &#x2014; the data is already available on the NPC&#x2019;s character sheet and in the Phase 5 decision that generated the letter.

Reader side: every NPC with Calligraphy (Cipher) emphasis who reads a letter automatically resolves the Cipher extraction. On success, the extracted information enters the NPC&#x2019;s knowledge about the writer. The NPC updates their internal model of the writer&#x2019;s disposition and political stance. If deception is detected (2 Raises), the NPC&#x2019;s trust toward the writer decreases &#x2014; disposition drops by &#x2212;3 (permanent, &#x201C;I know you lied to me in writing&#x201D;). If motivation is inferred (3 Raises), the NPC&#x2019;s understanding of the writer&#x2019;s strategy improves &#x2014; future social interactions with the writer gain +1k0 on PROBE and READ_CHARACTER rolls (the reader has insight into what the writer wants).

Kitsuki interaction: the Kitsuki Investigator school emphasises evidence-based deduction. A Kitsuki NPC who detects deception through Cipher treats the letter as evidence &#x2014; the deception enters the zone_event_log as a &#x201C;written deception&#x201D; record that can be referenced in future investigations or court proceedings. This connects Cipher to the investigation system without new mechanics.

### 57.30.6 Interaction with Poetry Letters

When a poem is delivered by letter (one of the three delivery channels for the Poetry system — RESOLVED: live recitation via PERFORM_FOR, letter delivery via WRITE_LETTER, gift scroll via DELIVER_GIFT), the Cipher extraction roll applies to the LETTER, not the poem. The letter’s concealment_tn reflects the writer’s emotional control in the surrounding correspondence — the greeting, the seasonal opening, the framing around the poem. The poem itself carries its own subject, dedication, and quality independently. A reader with Cipher can extract the writer’s true feelings from the letter’s calligraphic subtext AND experience the poem’s content — two layers of meaning from one correspondence. A tanka poem dedicated to the recipient paired with a letter whose subtext reveals genuine affection is a powerful social moment — the poem says “I wrote this for you” and the Cipher reading confirms “and they meant it.” Poem-in-letter mechanic: WRITE_LETTER gains an optional attached_poem_id field (int or null, default null). When set, the letter carries a poem object from the writer’s inventory (a Poetry Scroll produced by CRAFT with Artisan: Poetry / Awareness). On receipt, the poem applies the same disposition effect as PERFORM_FOR: the original CRAFT roll quality determines the disposition gain (+2 base, +1 per Raise achieved on the original roll). The poem is consumed — the scroll is now in the recipient’s possession. Festival poetry bonus: if a poem-letter is sent on a festival date that calls for poetry exchange (Lotus Blossoms, Festival of Leaves, Hotaru Matsuri), the sender gains +0.1 Glory. High-Status characters (Status 4+) who do not send at least one poem-letter on Festival of Leaves suffer −0.1 Glory (“cultural negligence”). PROVISIONAL.

### 57.30.7 Letter Object Amendments

Section 12.7 letter_object: three new fields added. concealment_tn (integer &#x2014; result of writer&#x2019;s Sincerity / Awareness roll at time of writing). writer_disposition_tier (enum &#x2014; Strong Ally, Ally, Friendly, Neutral, Rival, Enemy, Strong Enemy, frozen at time of writing). writer_topic_stance (enum &#x2014; supports, opposes, indifferent, or null if no topic payload). writer_needtype (NeedType identifier or null &#x2014; populated for NPC letters, null for player letters). These fields are populated automatically on every WRITE_LETTER action and are invisible to the writer. They are read-only &#x2014; they cannot be modified after the letter is sent. The letter is a snapshot of the writer&#x2019;s state at the moment of composition.

No new ActionIDs. The Cipher extraction is an automatic passive check on letter reading, not a separate action. Total ActionIDs remain 121.

### 57.30.8 High Rokugani — Formal Imperial Register

High Rokugani is the ancient, florid, and sophisticated style of language used in the Imperial Court. It is the formal literary register of the Empire — the language of ancient Imperial edicts, foundational legal texts, and the most elevated court correspondence. All samurai are literate in standard Rokugani, but composing in High Rokugani requires specialist training. The High Rokugani emphasis on Calligraphy represents mastery of this formal register. Without the emphasis, a character cannot attempt High Rokugani at all — their letters compose in standard Rokugani regardless of context.

Trigger: when WRITE_LETTER fires AND the recipient is currently located in the Inner City of the Imperial Capital AND the writer possesses the High Rokugani emphasis, an automatic Calligraphy (High Rokugani) / Intelligence roll fires. This is not a writer’s choice — a character trained in the formal register attempts to use it whenever the context calls for it. The roll either succeeds (the formal register is executed well enough to register as High Rokugani) or fails (the attempt wasn’t polished enough to land, and the letter functions as standard Rokugani).

Scaling TN by recipient prestige tier: TN 15 for general Inner City recipients (Otomo courtiers, Miya Heralds, Seppun Guards, resident Imperial bureaucrats). TN 20 for Imperial family members (branches of the Imperial line not currently holding high office). TN 25 for the Emperor’s Chosen (Imperial Chancellor, Imperial Advisor, Imperial Herald, Imperial Treasurer, Jeweled Champions). TN 30 for the Emperor himself. The register of writing must match the weight of the recipient — a letter to an Otomo courtier needs adequate High Rokugani, while a letter to the Emperor needs transcendent mastery of the formal register.

Success bonus scales with Raises, same pattern as the base Calligraphy roll (Section 12.7): +1 disposition on meeting the TN, +2 with 1 Raise, +3 with 2 Raises, +4 with 3 Raises. This bonus is added to the existing Calligraphy disposition bonus on receipt — the two rolls are independent and stack. A letter that rolls well on both base Calligraphy (+3) and High Rokugani (+4) delivers +7 total disposition on receipt, which represents the pinnacle of Imperial correspondence: perfectly composed in both content and register.

On failure: the letter functions as a standard letter. The base Calligraphy disposition bonus (Section 12.7) still applies normally — only the High Rokugani attempt goes unrecognized. The recipient perceives the letter as written in standard Rokugani. The writer does not know they failed — they believe they wrote High Rokugani, but the result wasn’t polished enough to register as the formal register to the recipient. There is no penalty for failure beyond the opportunity cost — no disposition loss, no social embarrassment, no indication to the writer that their attempt fell short.

Outside the Inner City: no High Rokugani roll fires. A letter written to a provincial daimyo, a Crab magistrate, or any recipient not currently in the Inner City is composed in standard Rokugani regardless of the writer’s emphasis. The formal register belongs to the Imperial Capital — it is culturally out of place elsewhere and confers no benefit. This naturally scopes High Rokugani’s impact: it is a specialised tool for Imperial correspondence, not a universal disposition amplifier.

NPC engine: NPCs with the High Rokugani emphasis automatically attempt High Rokugani when writing to recipients in the Inner City. No scoring decision required — the roll fires as part of the letter-generation pipeline. NPCs without the emphasis write standard Rokugani and never attempt High Rokugani. The distribution of the emphasis across the Empire is uneven: Otomo courtiers, Miya Heralds, Seppun Guards, and senior Phoenix scholars are most likely to possess it. Crane courtiers at the highest ranks typically have it. Crab, Mantis, and most provincial samurai do not.

Letter object amendment: Section 12.7 letter_object gains two new fields. high_rokugani_attempted (boolean, default false). Set to true when the writer has the emphasis and the recipient is in the Inner City at time of writing — records that the roll fired. high_rokugani_bonus (integer, default 0). The disposition bonus generated by the High Rokugani roll: 0 on failure, 1 on meeting TN, up to 4 with Raises. Applied to recipient disposition on letter receipt alongside the base Calligraphy bonus.
