## 11.6 Military Rank Structure, Unit Types & Named Characters — LOCKED

The Akodo military rank system is confirmed as the standard. Three distinct representations of armies exist in the game, each serving a different layer of play. The named character threshold is locked.

**The Three Army Representations — LOCKED:**

- World Map representation: armies as PU blobs showing position, size, and ownership only. No unit detail at this level.

- Army View (mass battle): Companies (Kaisha) are the basic tactical pieces. Players see and maneuver individual Companies on a battlefield view. Complex and intentionally so — massive battles are complicated.

- ASCII Map (individual combat): Player characters and named NPCs fight within the battle as individuals, operating inside the larger mass battle context.

**Military Rank Structure — LOCKED:**

- Hohei (Private) / Nikutai (Corporal): Rank and file soldiers. Not named.

- Gunso (Sergeant): Commands a Squadron (Guntai) of 20 soldiers. NAMED — lowest threshold for persistent characters.

- Chui (Lieutenant): Commands a Company (Kaisha) of ~153 troops (7 squadrons + command staff). NAMED.

- Taisa (Captain): Commands a Legion (Daibutai) of ~750 troops (6 companies + 1 reserve company). NAMED.

- Shireikan (Commander): Oversees 4–12 legions. NAMED.

- Rikugunshokan (General): Commands an Army (Go-hatamoto) of 48 legions. NAMED.

- Standard Great Clan maintains 1–4 armies. Crab maintains 5 (2 permanently on the Kaiu Wall).

**Named Character System (SUGGESTED — full design deferred):**

Every leadership position from Gunso upward is occupied by a named, persistent character — whether a player character, a notable NPC, or a procedurally generated individual. Anonymous units exist below this threshold (individual soldiers), but every commander has a name, a clan, a personality, and a history that persists across the game world.

- Named characters at the Gunso level and above exist as persistent entities — they can be killed, captured, promoted, dishonored, or befriended.

- The same principle extends beyond the military. Named characters should occupy every significant role in the game — Local Daimyo, Provincial Daimyo, magistrates, court officials, temple keepers. The world is populated by individuals, not abstractions.

- Procedurally generated named characters have randomized names, clan affiliations, personality traits, and histories. They are not player characters but they are not faceless either.

- The persistence of named characters at every level of the hierarchy is what gives the world life — a Gunso who survives three campaigns and rises to Taisa has a story. A rival who was dishonored and became ronin is still out there somewhere.

**Unit Types — LOCKED:**

The four original tiers (Peasant Levy, Ashigaru, Bushi Retainer, Elite Samurai) are revised as follows based on lore review and design decisions:

- Peasant Levy — unchanged. Improvised weapons, minimal protection. Temporary, levied from village PU.

- Ashigaru Spearmen — front line infantry. Spear and light armor. Defensive role, absorbs casualties, anchors battle lines.

- Ashigaru Archers — ranged support. Bow and light armor. Positioned behind spearmen, requires protection to be effective. Note: katana are samurai weapons only — Ashigaru carry spears or bows exclusively.

- Bushi Retainer — generic professional samurai unit. Reserved for Minor Clans that lack their own bushi school. All Great Clans replace this slot with their Tier 1 clan samurai unit (see Clan Elite Unit Roster below).

- Clan Elite Units — replace the generic Elite Samurai tier entirely. Every Great Clan has up to three unique unit types specific to their identity and lore, organized into three cost tiers (Baseline, Specialized, Elite). No generic elite tier exists. Full roster defined below.

- Ronin — auxiliary forces. Similar in capability to Bushi Retainers but slightly weaker and lacking clan loyalty. Hired with Koku, no levy required, no PU cost, no clan affiliation. Temporary by nature.

**Clan Elite Unit Roster — LOCKED:**

Each Great Clan replaces the generic Elite Samurai tier with up to three unique unit types. The generic Bushi Retainer is reserved for Minor Clans that lack their own bushi school. Every Great Clan fields at least one baseline samurai unit drawn from their primary bushi tradition. All units use standard Company size of 153 Health.

**Unit Cost Tiers:**

Clan elite units are Type 2 — Samurai Units (Permanent). They are not drawn from village PU. They represent the samurai caste and are always present as standing forces. Their costs are higher than generic Bushi Retainers to compensate for having no population impact. Rice upkeep is 0.35 per season for all samurai units regardless of tier.

**Tier 1 — Clan Samurai (Baseline):** Arms equip cost: 2.50 per unit. Iron upkeep: 0.25 per unit per season. Market price (Koku): 4.0 per unit. Every Great Clan’s primary bushi school unit. Slightly more expensive than generic Bushi Retainers (2.00 Arms / 0.20 Iron) due to superior training and equipment.

**Tier 2 — Specialized:** Arms equip cost: 3.50 per unit. Iron upkeep: 0.35 per unit per season. Market price (Koku): 5.5 per unit. Troops with specific tactical roles — scouts, spearmen, battle shugenja, poison specialists. More expensive to equip and maintain than baseline samurai.

**Tier 3 — Elite:** Arms equip cost: 5.00 per unit. Iron upkeep: 0.50 per unit per season. Market price (Koku): 8.0 per unit. The most powerful and prestigious units in each clan’s arsenal — Kenshinzen, Lion’s Pride, Crab Berserkers, Utaku Battle Maidens. Fielding these in large numbers is a serious economic commitment.

### Crab Clan

**Hida Bushi [Tier 1 — Baseline]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 5 | Defense: 7 | Morale: 20 | Morale Defense: 9

- Special: +2 Defense vs Shadowlands creatures.

- Notes: The heaviest line infantry in the Empire. Low offensive finesse but extremely difficult to destroy. They hold ground and do not break.

**Crab Berserkers [Tier 3 — Elite]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 8 | Defense: 3 | Morale: 20 | Morale Defense: 10

- Special: Cannot rout while above 25% Health. +2 Attack below 50% Health.

- Notes: Warriors who have lost everything to the Shadowlands and fight with nothing left to lose. Pure destruction — low Defense means brutal casualties, but they drag the enemy down with them.

**Hiruma Scouts [Tier 2 — Specialized]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 6 | Defense: 3 | Morale: 15 | Morale Defense: 6

- Special: +3 Attack when flanking. Enemy commander Survival Roll TN increases by +2 when this unit deals damage.

- Notes: Fast killers built to get behind enemy lines and eliminate leadership. Excel at flanking maneuvers and targeted assassination of commanders.

*Crab army identity: endurance and sacrifice. The Hida hold the line, the Berserkers break whatever the line cannot hold, and the Hiruma ensure the enemy’s commanders do not survive to try again.*

### Dragon Clan

**Dragon Talons [Tier 2 — Specialized]**

- Notes: No-dachi heavy infantry designed to crack open armored formations. That massive blade cleaves through armor that would stop a katana.

**Yamabushi [Tier 2 — Specialized]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 4 | Defense: 3 | Morale: 16 | Morale Defense: 8

- Special: +3 Attack to one adjacent allied Dragon unit per round (elemental magic). One adjacent unit gains +2 Defense for one round, once per battle (alchemical potions).

- Notes: Battle shugenja who serve as force multipliers rather than direct combatants. High Morale Defense reflects spiritual discipline despite physical fragility.

*Dragon army identity: unconventional synergy. Their divisions are smaller and less conventional than other clans, but the interplay between Mirumoto swordsmen, shugenja support, and specialized heavy infantry makes them unpredictable and dangerous when properly coordinated.*

**Deathseekers [Tier 2 — Specialized]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 8 | Defense: 2 | Morale: — | Morale Defense: —

- Special: No Morale stat — fights until destroyed. Cannot rout. +3 Attack below 50% Health.

*Lion army identity: overwhelming coordinated force. The Akodo line is the strongest conventional formation in Rokugan when properly assembled, the Lion’s Pride terrifies enemy leadership, and the Deathseekers are expendable shock troops that never break. The Lion field the largest and most professional armies in the Empire.*

- Special: +2 Defense when adjacent to allied shugenja units. Adjacent allied units gain +1 Morale Defense.

### Mantis Clan (Minor Clan)

**Yoritomo Bushi [Tier 1 — Baseline]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 5 | Defense: 4 | Morale: 15 | Morale Defense: 6

- Special: Each successful hit reduces the target’s Defense by 1 for the remainder of the battle (stacks up to -3). Naval: +2 Attack in boarding actions (tight-quarters multi-weapon fighting).

- Notes: Scrappy fighters who wear opponents down by finding and exploiting gaps in their guard. On land, outclassed by Great Clan forces.

**Storm Riders [Tier 2 — Specialized]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 3 | Defense: 3 | Morale: 14 | Morale Defense: 7

- Special: +2 Attack to one adjacent allied Mantis unit per round. Naval: +1 Attack to all adjacent friendly ships per round at sea (storm magic). Weather shift is not automatic — a Storm Rider character aboard may attempt Shift the Storm as a Naval Heroic Opportunity (Section 11.7 Category 9) when triggered by the Battle Table.

- Notes: Yoritomo shugenja who weaponize weather — storm magic buffs allies and batters enemies. Their true power will be realized at sea.

**Storm Legion [Tier 2 — Specialized]**

- Health: 153 | Attack: 7 | Defense: 4 | Morale: 16 | Morale Defense: 7

- Special: No terrain penalties. +2 Attack on first combat round. Naval: +2 Attack in boarding actions (veterans of boarding). No penalty on first round of boarding (ignore the standard −2 boarding penalty).

- Notes: The Yoritomo’s finest — hardened veterans of boarding actions and beach assaults who fight anywhere without hesitation.

*Mantis are a Minor Clan in this era. On land they are scrappy and competent but outclassed by Great Clan forces. Their true power will be realized in the naval combat system (Section 11.9), where weather magic, seamanship, and boarding expertise give them dominance at sea.*

**Design Notes — Clan Elite Units:**

Generic Bushi Retainer (A6 D5 M18 MD8, no special) is reserved for Minor Clans that do not possess their own bushi school. All Great Clans replace this slot with their Tier 1 clan samurai unit.

Universal units (Peasant Levy, Ashigaru Spearmen, Ashigaru Archers, Light Cavalry, Ronin, Garrison) remain available to all clans as defined above. Clan elite units supplement these, they do not replace them.

Balance philosophy: No clan should dominate in all situations. Each roster has deliberate weaknesses: the Crab lack mobility, the Crane lack magical support, the Dragon are small, the Lion are expensive to field at full coordination, the Phoenix collapse without their shugenja core, the Scorpion lack raw staying power, and the Unicorn struggle in tight defensive terrain. The cost tier system (1x / 1.5x / 2x) ensures that elite units represent a meaningful economic commitment.

Mantis naval bonuses: RESOLVED. All three Mantis units now have specific naval combat bonuses defined in their Company stat blocks. Yoritomo Bushi +2 Attack boarding, Storm Riders buff adjacent ships (weather shift via Heroic Opportunity, Section 11.7 Category 9), Storm Legion +2 Attack boarding + ignore first-round penalty

The four traditional Rokugani formation names from the Sword and Fan sourcebook map directly onto the deployment grid as lore flavor with no additional mechanical modifiers. Vanguard (Sakite): Forward Row center positions — the shock troops placed first into contact, typically the strongest Melee Companies. Main Body (Hongu): The bulk of the Forward Row — the professional core of the army filling the remaining front-line slots. These names are used in battle narration, commander dialogue, and court discussions of military affairs. They carry no bonus or penalty beyond their grid position.
