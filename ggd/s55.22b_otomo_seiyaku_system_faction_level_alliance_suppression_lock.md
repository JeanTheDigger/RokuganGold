## 55.22b Otomo Seiyaku System — Faction-Level Alliance Suppression — LOCKED

**Cross-references:** Section 2.1.19 (Otomo Family), Section 2.1.20 (Imperial Bureaucracy), Section 15.x (Otomo Institutional Behavior — LOCKED), Section 12.6 (Daily Conversation), Section 12.7 (Letter System), Section 17 (Personal Visits), Section 18.4 (Standing Objectives — “Maintain the balance of power”), Section 55.10.1 (Emperor Personality Archetypes), Section 55.22 (Political Standing Objectives).

**Source:** *Secrets of the Empire*, pp. 117–119 (Otomo family history, seiyaku tradition, “serpents in the garden”).

### 1. Core Concept

The Otomo Institutional Behavior (Section 15.x) defines what individual Otomo NPCs do across all interaction channels — court, visits, letters, and conversation. That behavior is the ground-level execution. The Seiyaku System is the institutional layer above it: a world-map-level AI behavior that runs on the Otomo family daimyo’s decision cycle, monitoring clan-to-clan disposition values and generating targeted intervention directives when any pair of Great Clans approaches a threshold that would threaten Imperial security.

Without the Seiyaku System, Otomo NPCs spread discord diffusely — they damage whatever relationships they happen to encounter. With it, the Otomo family concentrates its limited resources on the most dangerous alliances, assigning operatives to specific clan pairs and coordinating their efforts across every available channel.

### 2. The Alliance Alarm — Monitoring Threshold

**2.1 What the Otomo Watch:** The Otomo family daimyo’s AI maintains awareness of all clan-to-clan Champion disposition values — the disposition between each pair of Great Clan Champions. There are 21 unique pairings among the seven Great Clans. The Otomo do not care about individual samurai relationships or vassal-level dispositions — they care about the top of the hierarchy, because an alliance between two Clan Champions is the thing that can threaten the Throne.

**2.2 The Alliance Alarm Threshold:** Alliance Alarm fires when any clan-to-clan Champion disposition reaches **+45 or above** (mid-Friend range). At Friend disposition (+31 to +60), two clans are already cooperating: honoring supply requests, extending court invitations, supporting each other’s positions at Winter Court. At +45, the relationship is strong enough that it could develop into a formal military alliance or a coordinated political bloc within one or two more seasons of positive interaction. The Otomo consider this unacceptable. The threshold is deliberately below the Trusted Ally range (+61 and above). The Otomo act before alliances solidify — they are preventive, not reactive.

**2.3 Multiple Alarms:** Multiple clan pairs can be above the threshold simultaneously. The Otomo prioritize by magnitude: the pair with the highest disposition is targeted first. If resources permit, the Otomo address multiple pairs in parallel.

### 3. The Seiyaku Directive — Coordinated Intervention

When the Alliance Alarm fires for a clan pair, the Otomo family daimyo generates a **Seiyaku Directive** targeting that pair. The directive assigns an available Otomo courtier NPC as an operative. The operative receives priority overrides to their normal behavior across all interaction channels — not just court.

**3.1 Intervention Actions by Channel (all governed by the Otomo Institutional Behavior leans in Section 15.x):**

**Court (highest impact, limited availability):** The operative prioritizes attending any court where representatives of both targeted clans are present. At court, they receive +10 to all Social Skill rolls targeting disposition between the two clans (institutional support — the Otomo family feeds the operative information, contacts, and resources). They use Gossip, Disclose, Fabricate Accusations, and topic position manipulation focused exclusively on the targeted pair. Expected effect when at court with both clans: -3 to -8 disposition per season.

**Personal Visits (high impact, targeted):** The operative prioritizes visiting lords of the targeted clans at their holdings. During visits they conduct Category 3 actions (Gossip, Disclose, Reveal a Secret Privately) in the intimate setting, which is more effective for sensitive disclosures than public court. The operative alternates visits between Clan A and Clan B lords, poisoning each side’s view of the other. Expected effect: -2 to -5 disposition per season per visit conducted.

**Letters (low impact, daily, relentless):** The operative dedicates their letter allowance exclusively to representatives of the targeted pair, alternating between Clan A and Clan B contacts. A non-lord operative writes one letter per IC day (or more if they spend 1 AP for a batch of 4); a lord operative draws from their Civilian Order pool and may write multiple per IC day. Each letter carries a topic selected to frame the other clan negatively. Letters sustain constant downward pressure on the relationship even when the operative has no court access and is not visiting. Expected effect: −1 to −2 disposition per season from letters alone (more for lord operatives with larger letter budgets).

**Daily Conversation (passive, opportunistic):** When the operative shares a settlement with representatives of either targeted clan, daily conversation topic selection is locked to topics that damage the targeted relationship. This is automatic and costs no action points — it fires through the existing daily conversation system.

**Combined effect:** An operative using all four channels simultaneously against a targeted pair produces approximately -5 to -12 disposition per season. A strong alliance built on genuine shared interests can resist this pressure, but it requires both clans to actively maintain the relationship through their own positive interactions.

**3.2 Priority Escalation:** If the targeted disposition remains above +45 for two consecutive seasons despite intervention, the Otomo family daimyo escalates: the operative receives authorization to use Fabrication (Section 12.8) against representatives of either clan, regardless of the operative’s Honor cost. If no suitable secret is available for Fabrication, the operative may attempt to ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE (Section 55.21.4) against a representative of either clan specifically to obtain material for future Fabrication. This is the Otomo at their most ruthless — manufacturing the evidence they need to destroy a relationship.

### 4. Operative Pool and Resource Constraints

The Otomo cannot suppress all alliances simultaneously. They are a small family with limited courtier NPCs.

**4.1 Operative Pool:** Base pool: 3 operatives. This represents the minimum functional capacity of the seiyaku tradition. Additional operatives: +1 per 2 Otomo courtier NPCs currently alive and unassigned to other duties. Each Seiyaku Directive requires 1 operative. An operative assigned to a directive is committed for the duration — they cannot be reassigned until the directive is cancelled (the targeted disposition drops below +35, a 10-point buffer below the alarm threshold to prevent oscillation).

**4.2 Prioritization:** If more clan pairs are above the +45 threshold than the Otomo have operatives to cover: (1) Highest disposition pair first. (2) Pairs involving the strongest clan (by ASSESS_POWER_BALANCE composite). (3) Pairs involving clans with Champion disposition toward the Emperor below Stranger (below 0).

Unprioritized pairs receive no assigned operative but are still subject to opportunistic Otomo behavior — any Otomo NPC at court, visiting, or sharing a settlement with representatives of an uncovered pair will apply their standard Institutional Behavior leans (Section 15.x) against that pair. The system-level targeting is absent, but the ground-level instinct to sow discord remains.

**4.3 Operative Resource Exhaustion:** If every available operative is committed and new alarms fire, the Otomo family daimyo generates a Tier 4 topic at the next court they attend: “Otomo Resources Stretched — Alliance Activity Rising.” This signals to the Emperor and attentive players that the political landscape is shifting faster than the Otomo can manage.

### 5. Emperor Archetype Interaction

**Benevolent (Jin):** Alliance Alarm threshold raised to +55. Tolerates warmer inter-clan relationships. Otomo disposition toward Emperor: -1/year (institutional resentment). Base operative pool.

**Iron (Chugi/Meiyo):** Default. Threshold at +45. Base operative pool. The Otomo fulfill their duty as tradition demands.

**Cunning (Seigyo/Dosatsu):** Threshold lowered to +35. +1 operative added to pool. May issue specific directives targeting a particular clan pair regardless of disposition.

**Warlike (Yu/Kyoryoku):** Threshold at +45 (default). Base pool. Exempts clan pairs allied in an active Emperor-endorsed war. Suppresses only peacetime alliances.

**Tyrant (Seigyo/Ishi):** Threshold lowered to +25. +2 operatives added to pool. No one cooperates. The seiyaku tradition is weaponized beyond its original purpose. Clans that cannot cooperate also cannot coordinate relief during crises — this accelerates the cascade toward the Tyrant’s breaking point.

### 6. Detection and Counterplay

**6.1 Detection:** Any character at court, during a personal visit with an Otomo, or receiving an Otomo letter may attempt to detect manipulation. Roll: Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness vs. the Otomo operative’s Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness (contested). Success: the detecting character learns the Otomo are actively targeting a specific clan pair. Generates Tier 4 topic: “Otomo Manipulation Detected — [Clan A] / [Clan B] Targeted.” Effect: +5 sympathy disposition toward the targeted clans from all who learn the topic. Operative’s effectiveness halved for the remainder of that court session or visit. The operative is not expelled or punished — the Otomo’s duty is known and accepted, even if individual instances are resented.

**6.2 Formal Alliance:** Two Clan Champions who are aware of Otomo manipulation may jointly declare a Formal Alliance at Winter Court. Effects: Disposition floor of +31 (Friend) between the pair for as long as the alliance is active — Otomo manipulation can damage but cannot push below the floor. Political cost: Tier 3 topic fires at next Winter Court; Emperor response depends on archetype. Otomo escalation: 2 operatives assigned instead of 1; all four channels used at maximum intensity.

**6.3 Limitations:** The Otomo cannot suppress relationships between Imperial families and Great Clans. They cannot suppress intra-clan (family-to-family) relationships. They cannot act against explicit Imperial orders — if the Emperor commands two clans to cooperate, the Seiyaku Directive for that pair is suspended.

### 7. Interaction with Existing Systems

**Otomo Institutional Behavior (Section 15.x):** The Seiyaku System adds a targeting layer above the Institutional Behavior. The Institutional Behavior defines what Otomo NPCs do in every interaction channel; the Seiyaku System determines which clan pairs they focus on. Without an active directive, Otomo NPCs spread discord diffusely. With a directive, they concentrate.

**“Maintain the Balance of Power” Standing Objective (Section 55.22):** Complementary but distinct. That objective is available to any courtier with appropriate virtues and focuses on checking a dominant clan’s power. The Seiyaku System is Otomo-exclusive and focuses on preventing inter-clan alliances regardless of dominance. An Otomo NPC may have both simultaneously — they reinforce each other but operate independently.

**Disposition System:** The Seiyaku System operates entirely through the existing disposition system. No new disposition axis or modifier type is created. If the Otomo stop intervening, negative modifiers decay normally.

**Marriage System (if implemented):** Marriage alliances creating permanent positive disposition modifiers are the hardest for the Otomo to counter. The Otomo family daimyo assigns maximum priority to pairs with marriage ties and escalates to Fabrication more readily.

### 8. Edge Cases

Otomo family destroyed: Seiyaku System ceases entirely. Inter-clan alliances form unchecked until a new institution is established by Imperial Edict. This is historically accurate — the Gozoku conspiracy succeeded precisely because no one was performing the Otomo’s function.

Player is an Otomo character: The player sees the Seiyaku Directive in their objectives panel. They can pursue it or not — ignoring an active directive generates -5 disposition per season from the Otomo family daimyo and may result in recall or disgrace.

Otomo operative killed: The Otomo family daimyo immediately assigns a replacement and seeks to identify the responsible party. Killing an Otomo is killing an Imperial servant — Tier 2 political crisis if public.

Two clans at war: Clans at war have disposition well below the alarm threshold. The Otomo do not target pairs already in conflict. If a peace treaty pushes disposition back toward the threshold, monitoring resumes.

All operatives assigned: Topic generated: “Otomo Resources Stretched.” Signals that the political landscape is shifting faster than the Otomo can manage.

### 9. Design Notes

**9.1 Why Pervasive, Not Court-Limited:** The original Otomo Court Behavior restricted the seiyaku tradition to court sessions. This was insufficient for two reasons. First, the lore is clear: the Otomo are serpents in the garden at all times, not only during formal events. Second, mechanically, court sessions are periodic and limited — a system that only fires at court leaves large gaps where alliances can form unchecked. By extending Otomo behavior to letters, personal visits, and daily conversation, the system produces constant pressure that matches the lore’s description of an ever-present, relentless institutional force.

**9.2 Balancing Otomo Power:** The system is deliberately constrained. Limited operatives (3 base). Moderate disposition damage (-5 to -12/season at full intensity) that can be outpaced by two clans actively maintaining their alliance. Detection is possible. Formal Alliances create a floor. The goal is not to make inter-clan alliances impossible but to make them costly and fragile.

**9.3 Emergent Narrative:** A Crab-Crane alliance built on shared Shadowlands defense and cemented by marriage, slowly eroded by an Otomo operative whispering old grievances at every opportunity — at Winter Court, during a private visit to the Crane Champion’s castle, through daily letters to the Lion Champion emphasizing the Crane’s growing power, even in casual conversation at Otosan Uchi. Until one side’s Champion confronts the Otomo publicly and forces the Emperor to choose between his serpents and his allies. That is a Rokugan story. The system enables it mechanically.
