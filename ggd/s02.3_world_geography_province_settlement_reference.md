## 2.3 World Geography: Province & Settlement Reference

This section defines the vocabulary for settlement types and settlement infrastructure used throughout the geographic reference. All named settlements in Rokugan are classified using the settlement types below. Infrastructure within settlements is drawn from the infrastructure list. Both lists are closed vocabularies: new types require an explicit addition to this section.

### 2.3.1 Settlement Type Vocabulary

**Civilian Settlements**

Village (Mura): Agricultural settlement. The basic population unit of rural Rokugan. No inherent defenses. An army occupies it without siege. Can be founded by Provincial Daimyo or formed organically from surplus population (Section 4.3.22).

Town (Machi): Mid-scale civilian settlement. Larger than a village, with markets, multiple industries, and denser population. Exists at game start only. Cannot be built during play.

City (Toshi): Metropolis-scale settlement with full siege mechanics. Exists at game start only. Cannot be built during play.

Imperial Capital (Miyako): Otosan Uchi. Unique settlement type. The seat of the Emperor and the political heart of the Empire. One exists.

**Military Holdings**

Fortification (Yousai): Purely military structure. No civilian population. Can be built by Provincial Daimyo on their own territory, or by a Family Daimyo or Clan Champion on any vassal territory. Requires a deployed garrison to provide any defensive value (Section 4.3.22).

Keep (Torid-e): Small fortified holding with a modest civilian population. Typically a vassal family seat or a minor military holding. Walls and garrison, but limited in scale compared to a castle.

Castle (Shiro): Major fortified holding with a supporting town or significant civilian presence. Walls, garrison, and full siege mechanics (Section 11.7). The standard fortified seat of power for provincial daimyo and military commanders.

Family Castle (Kyuden): The seat of a Great Clan family. The largest and most important fortified holdings in the Empire. The ruling family’s castle (the clan capital) is a Family Castle of greater scale, not a separate type. When a Family Castle has a town or city around it, it is a single settlement entry. The description notes the internal division between castle and town, but they function as one unit for governance, siege, and economic purposes.

Wall Tower: Kaiu Wall-specific fortification. The twelve towers along the Kaiu Wall are a unique settlement type with their own mechanics (Section 2.4). Each tower functions as an independent garrison with a Taisa (Tower Commander), structural integrity, and jade/tea supply requirements.

**Religious Settlements**

Temple (Tera): Independent religious settlement on the World Map with resident monks and shugenja. Founded deliberately. Requires minimum 0.5 PU founding population. Generates 4 WP passive per season (general) or 12 WP to one Fortune (dedicated). Construction: 80 Koku, 4 seasons (Section 4.3.22).

Shinden: Great temple complex. The largest form of religious settlement. Independent World Map location. Requires minimum 1.0 PU founding population. Generates 8 WP passive per season (general) or 24 WP to one Fortune (dedicated). Construction: 250 Koku, 8 seasons (Section 4.3.22).

Monastery (Souiin): Brotherhood of Shinsei settlement. Independent World Map location housing exclusively monks. Generates WP primarily from its resident monk population performing active worship. Founded by Family Daimyo, Clan Champion, or the Brotherhood itself with lord’s approval (Section 4.3.22).

⚙️ CROSS-REF: Section 4.3.22 defines construction rules, costs, and founding requirements for all settlement types. Section 11.7 defines siege mechanics. Section 2.4 defines Kaiu Wall Tower mechanics. Section 57.36 defines Lesser Zone structures within settlements (interior zones scale by governance rank).

⚙️ RECONCILIATION NOTE: Section 4.3.22 uses “Castle Town” as a single type. This section refines that into a hierarchy of Keep, Castle, and Family Castle. The “Castle Town” label in Section 4.3.22 maps to Castle (Shiro) in this vocabulary. Keep (Torid-e) and Family Castle (Kyuden) are new distinctions not present in Section 4.3.22. At implementation, the settlement type field should use this vocabulary as the authoritative source.

### 2.3.2 Settlement Infrastructure

Infrastructure items are features within settlements, not standalone settlement types. Each infrastructure item exists as a Lesser Zone with its own ASCII map that players can enter for roleplay and mechanical interactions. Not every settlement has every infrastructure type. The geographic reference (Sections 2.3.3 onward) notes which infrastructure is present at each settlement at game start.

**Worship Infrastructure**

Roadside Shrine: 0.5 WP passive per season. Smallest worship structure. Construction: 5 Koku, 1 season. Can be general (split across all Seven Great Fortunes) or dedicated to one Fortune (12 Koku). Section 4.3.21.

Village Shrine: 1 WP passive per season. Construction: 15 Koku, 2 seasons. Dedicated version: 30 Koku, 3 WP to one Fortune. Section 4.3.21.

Local Shrine: 2 WP passive per season. Construction: 30 Koku, 3 seasons. Dedicated version: 60 Koku, 6 WP to one Fortune. Section 4.3.21.

**Military Infrastructure**

Garrison/Barracks: Military housing and training facility. Present at all castles, keeps, and fortifications. May also be present at strategically important towns and cities.

Dojo: Training facility for a specific school. The geographic reference specifies which school trains at each dojo by name. School identity determines what character types are generated at that location (Section 22.4). A settlement may have multiple dojo training different schools.

**Economic Infrastructure**

Forge: Metalworking and crafting facility. Feeds the Artisan and Crafting System (Section 49). Named forges (the Kaiu Great Forge, the Steel Crane Forge, the Tamori Furnace) have specific crafting capabilities and quality modifiers noted in their settlement entries.

Market/Marketplace: Commercial zone enabling trade. Feeds Koku generation and the material availability system (Section 42). Larger markets attract more trade traffic and provide access to rarer material tiers.

Port/Harbor: Coastal access point for naval and trade traffic. Required for COMMISSION_SHIP (Section 57.20) and REQUEST_PASSAGE (Section 57.42). Feeds the naval system (Section 11.9).

Shipyard: Naval construction facility. Required for building ships. The geographic reference specifies shipyard capacity where relevant (e.g., Fundai Mura can build up to 24 kobune simultaneously).

Mine: Resource extraction site. The geographic reference specifies resource type for each mine: iron, jade, gold, copper, or silver. Iron feeds the Arms production system (Section 4.3.10). Jade feeds the Crab supply system (Section 2.4.14). Mine output does not degrade or exhaust (Section 57, v462 decision).

Poppy Fields: Opium production facility. Primarily a Scorpion economic asset. Feeds the criminal economy (Section 11.4) and the opium trade.

**Social Infrastructure**

Okiya/Geisha House: Settlement feature present in settlements with a PLEASURE_QUARTER zone (Section 57.36). Three quality tiers assigned at world generation based on settlement size and clan cultural investment. Feeds the Geisha Intelligence System (Section 57.45) and the Wind-Down System (Section 57.44).

Library: Repository of scrolls, books, and records. Provides bonuses to Lore skill rolls and research actions when a character is co-located.

Embassy: Diplomatic facility maintained by a specific clan within another clan’s territory. The geographic reference specifies which clan operates each embassy. Provides a context for cross-clan diplomacy and intelligence gathering.

**Agricultural Infrastructure**

Stables: Horse breeding and care facility. Notable examples include the Utaku stables (finest horse breeders in the Empire) and the Miya stables. Provides mounts for cavalry units and individual characters.

**Leisure and Rest Infrastructure**

The following infrastructure items serve primarily as roleplay meeting zones and social spaces. They connect to the Wind-Down System (Section 57.44) for rest and recovery mechanics. Each exists as a Lesser Zone with an ASCII map.

Tea House: Establishment serving tea. Social gathering point for samurai. Wind-Down venue.

Sake House: Establishment serving sake and other drinks. Wind-Down venue. More casual atmosphere than a tea house.

Inn: Lodging and rest facility for travelers. Wind-Down venue. Present along major trade routes and in settlements that receive significant visitor traffic.

Theater: Performance venue for plays, kabuki, bunraku, and other theatrical arts. Feeds the Theater System (Section 57.22). Wind-Down venue.

Brewery: Sake and other beverage production facility. Economic asset producing trade goods.

Garden: Cultivated garden space. Requires creation by a named NPC with Artisan: Gardening skill (Section 57.23). Not built through lord authority like other infrastructure. Gardens connect to the Garden System (Section 57.23) for cultivation, maintenance, and visitor mechanics.

### 2.3.3 Crab Clan Provinces

#### Hida Family Provinces

##### Garanto Province

The northernmost Hida province and one of the largest, Garanto is the closest the Crab get to a breadbasket. Its lowlands contain the majority of the clan’s rice paddies, making it the single most important food-producing province in Hida territory. Separated from the other Hida provinces by the intervening Kuni lands, Garanto sits far enough from the Shadowlands that it has never faced direct assault, with Ishigaki Province serving as a buffer to the west. The province shares its name with provinces in both Phoenix and Unicorn territory, a recurring source of confusion for Imperial tax assessors.

Adjacent provinces: Ishigaki (Hida) to the west, Midakai (Kuni) to the south, Yoake (Kaiu) to the north

##### Ishibei Province

The southernmost Hida province, Ishibei is a rocky and desolate region dominated by the low, jagged peaks of the Twilight Mountains. The province has only a modest commoner population, as its agricultural productivity is minimal. Ishibei’s military importance far outweighs its economic value: it contains the Kaiu Pass, the easiest route through the Twilight Mountains and the primary corridor for moving troops between the Kaiu Wall and Kyuden Hida in Kyoukan Province. The southern border of the province is defined by the Kaiu Wall itself, and the five southernmost Wall Towers are garrisoned from this province. The River of the Last Stand flows through the province, marking the old boundary between Hida and Hiruma territory before the Maw’s invasion. From the eighth century onward, this river carries both strategic and spiritual weight as the site of Kuni Osaku’s legendary sacrifice. A narrow strip of coastline touches Earthquake Fish Bay at the province’s eastern edge, near the border with Kyoukan.

Adjacent provinces: Ishigaki (Hida) to the north, Kyoukan (Hida) to the northeast, Adauchi (Kuni) to the north-northeast, Ienikaeru (Hiruma) to the west (beyond the Wall), Kinbou (Hiruma) to the southwest (beyond the Wall)

##### Ishigaki Province

Ishigaki Province defines much of the Crab Clan’s border with the Shadowlands northwest of Ishibei. Like its southern neighbor, it is an underpopulated region dominated by the low, rugged peaks of the Twilight Mountains. The Kaiu Wall runs along its southwestern border, continuing north into Yoake Province in Kaiu territory. There are no major settlements or strongholds of note in Ishigaki Province. The Crab who dwell here do so at the Kaiu Wall, and the limited number of peasants inhabit scattered mining villages in the mountains.

Adjacent provinces: Yoake (Kaiu) to the north-north, Garanto (Hida) to the northeast, Midakai (Kuni) to the north-east-east, Adauchi (Kuni) to the east, Ishibei (Hida) to the southeast, Ienikaeru (Hiruma) to the southwest (beyond the Wall), Hissori (Hiruma) to the northwest (beyond the Wall)

##### Juuin Province

The farthest inland of all Crab provinces, Juuin Province is dominated by the Wall Above the Ocean Mountains, which stretch through the entire province north to south. A major trade route called the Mountain Road pierces the mountains at the southern end, while a smaller path crosses them farther north. The mountains are home to many iron mines and several jade mines, all vigorously exploited to supply the Crab war effort. There is also a fairly extensive network of caves and tunnels within the mountains, notorious for becoming periodically infested with Shadowlands creatures that must be cleared out by large-scale hunts. Crane territory lies to the east beyond unaligned lands and a river crossing, but the two are not directly adjacent.

Adjacent provinces: Kyoukan (Hida) to the south, Adauchi (Kuni) to the west-south, Midakai (Kuni) to the west-north, Sparrow Clan to the northeast

##### Kyoukan Province

The seat of power for the Hida family and the entire Crab Clan, Kyoukan Province is home to the great fortress of Kyuden Hida and the clan’s most sacred shrine, Koten. The province’s eastern border runs along Earthquake Fish Bay, and many fishing villages line the shore. Kyoukan has fair agricultural production and significant fishing output, but the need to base huge numbers of troops here means the province must regularly import food from elsewhere. The province directly borders Crane territory to the east, a proximity that has shaped both military planning and diplomatic tension throughout the clan’s history.

Adjacent provinces: Juuin (Hida) to the north, Adauchi (Kuni) to the northwest, Ishibei (Hida) to the west, Sunda Mizu (Yasuki) to the east-south, Anshin (Asahina, Crane) to the east

#### Kaiu Family Provinces

##### Hokufuu Province

The northernmost Kaiu province and for eleven centuries the most northern of all Crab territory, a status that changed when the Crab absorbed the Falcon Clan. The northernmost hills and peaks of the Twilight Mountains peter out here as the range comes to its end. Due to its great distance from the Shadowlands, the Crab long ago chose this remote province as their main site of diplomacy, a decision somewhat disrupted by the later admission of the Yasuki into the clan. Despite that, many outsiders must still make the lengthy trek to Hokufuu Province to conduct diplomacy at Face of the East Castle. The province is one of the safest in Crab territory, though Shadowlands forces have occasionally been active within the nearby Shinomen Mori, and those periods forced the Crab to relocate their diplomacy elsewhere. After the return of the Unicorn Clan, Face of the East gained a second role as a hub for trade between the two clans, since Unicorn caravans could reach it directly without passing through Scorpion tariff inspections.

Adjacent provinces: Yoake (Kaiu) to the southwest, Kuda (Kaiu) to the southeast, Toritaka Province to the north, Shinomen Forest to the east, Twilight Mountains to the west

##### Yoake Province

The southernmost of the three Kaiu provinces, Yoake is home to several of the more important Crab holdings, including Kaiu Shiro itself. Like neighboring Garanto, it is an agriculturally productive province, with rice paddies and grain fields responsible for feeding many samurai. The Kaiu Wall runs along the province’s southwestern border, continuing south from Ishigaki Province. Yoake is where the Kaiu Engineering Academy trains its students and where the Great Forge produces the weapons and armor that sustain the Crab war effort. Razor of the Dawn Castle anchors the western end of the Crab defensive line, looking out onto the Plains Above Evil from a highly defensible position between two peaks of the Twilight Mountains.

Adjacent provinces: Hokufuu (Kaiu) to the north, Kuda (Kaiu) to the east, Garanto (Hida) to the south, Ishigaki (Hida) to the southwest

##### Kuda Province

The easternmost of the three Kaiu provinces, Kuda is an open, lightly settled region. Much of its territory is part of the Quiet Wind Plain, so named for the soft but steady winds that blow across it on almost every day. The province borders unaligned territory to the south and east. Despite its remote location, Kuda contains some of the most fertile lands in Crab territory, and its single major settlement is responsible for more food production than any other Crab holding. The proximity of the Shinomen Mori to the northeast and the threat of bandits from the unaligned lands make security a constant concern despite the province’s distance from the Shadowlands.

#### Kuni Family Provinces

##### Midakai Province

The more northerly of the two Kuni provinces, Midakai borders Garanto to the north. Most of the province is part of the Kuni Wastes, barren gray earth without a single plant or animal, dotted with small huts and caves where Kuni shugenja live alone or in small groups conducting their experiments. Food and supplies must be brought in from outside the Wastes. However, Midakai is notable for containing the only two major Kuni villages to survive the Maw’s Taint, both located in pockets of land that escaped corruption. These villages sit in the foothills of the Twilight Mountains in the province’s far northwestern corner, supporting the extensive iron mine networks within the mountains. The mines have been in operation since the first or second century, winding ever deeper, and the workers dislike the sinister mountains where Shadowlands creatures often appear.

Adjacent provinces: Garanto (Hida) to the north, Ishigaki (Hida) to the west, Adauchi (Kuni) to the south, Juuin (Hida) to the east

##### Adauchi Province

The southern Kuni province is the more bleak of the two, composed almost entirely of the Wastes. It is home to Shiro Kuni, the family’s only holding of note, located just south of the Wastes on the border with Kyoukan Province. Shiro Kuni sits nearly dead center in the Crab lands, making it a natural staging area for the clan’s armies, a role it serves in addition to housing the most important of the Kuni family’s libraries and laboratories. After the creation of the Kaiu Wall, Shiro Kuni became the command center for the northern half of the Wall, with a high-ranking Crab general permanently assigned there alongside the Kuni researchers. The Barracks of the Damned sits just outside the castle walls, housing Tainted Crab warriors who choose to continue fighting for the Empire.

#### Hiruma Family Provinces (Beyond the Wall)

The three former Hiruma provinces lie beyond the Kaiu Wall in territory overrun by the Shadowlands after the Maw’s invasion in the eighth century. These provinces have no lord, no population units, no Rice production, no settlements, no tax revenue, and no governance mechanics. They exist in the province network as terrain accessible for ASCII map missions only. The Shadowlands overlay tag is active on all sub-tiles. Characters can enter these provinces for Hiruma scout patrols, artifact recovery expeditions, Shadowlands creature hunts, and other mission types generated by world state conditions.

##### Hissori Province

The northernmost of the three former Hiruma provinces, Hissori sits beyond the Kaiu Wall to the northwest of Ishigaki Province. Before the Maw’s invasion, Hissori was a sparsely populated region of low hills and scrubland used primarily as a training ground for Hiruma scouts. After the fall of Hiruma territory, the province was entirely consumed by the Shadowlands. The landscape is now a blighted expanse of gray earth, dead trees, and corrupted waterways. Tainted creatures roam freely and the air itself carries a faint wrongness that Kuni shugenja can detect from the Wall. Despite this, Hiruma scouts regularly cross the Wall into Hissori on reconnaissance missions, mapping the movement of Shadowlands forces and identifying threats before they reach the Wall. Of the three lost provinces, Hissori is considered the least dangerous, though that is a relative distinction that has killed many overconfident samurai.

Adjacent provinces: Ishigaki (Hida) to the east, Ienikaeru (Hiruma) to the south, Shadowlands to the west

Coastal: No

Rivers: None (pre-invasion waterways corrupted)

Roads: None (pre-invasion roads destroyed)

Governance: None (ungovernable territory, Shadowlands overlay active)

Settlements: N/A

Sub-tile subdivision: pending

##### Ienikaeru Province

The central of the three former Hiruma provinces, Ienikaeru sits directly beyond the Kaiu Wall between Hissori to the north and Kinbou to the south. Before the fall, Ienikaeru was the heartland of Hiruma territory, containing the family’s ancestral seat of power, Kyuden Hiruma. The great castle fell during the Maw’s invasion and has remained in Shadowlands hands ever since, its halls now home to oni and corrupted spirits. The ruins of Kyuden Hiruma are a source of enduring shame and longing for the Hiruma family, and there have been multiple failed expeditions to reclaim or at least recover sacred relics from within its walls. The province is more heavily infested than Hissori, as the presence of a corrupted major holding attracts greater concentrations of Shadowlands creatures.

Adjacent provinces: Hissori (Hiruma) to the north, Kinbou (Hiruma) to the south, Ishigaki (Hida) to the east-north, Ishibei (Hida) to the east-south

Coastal: No

##### Kinbou Province

The southernmost of the three former Hiruma provinces, Kinbou sits at the far southwestern edge of Crab territory beyond the Kaiu Wall. Before the fall, Kinbou was a coastal province on Earthquake Fish Bay, and its fishing villages supplemented the Hiruma diet. After the Maw’s invasion, the province was consumed by the Shadowlands like the rest of Hiruma territory. The coastline itself is now corrupted, with blackened cliffs dropping into waters that Crab fishermen refuse to approach. Of the three lost provinces, Kinbou is considered the most dangerous due to its proximity to the deeper Shadowlands to the south and west, and Hiruma scouts who venture this far rarely return in full health.

#### Yasuki Family Provinces

##### Junkin Province

The southerly of the two Yasuki provinces, Junkin is located along the eastern side of Earthquake Fish Bay and is home to the family’s seat of power, Yasuki Yashiki. The province is bordered on two sides by Asahina lands of the Crane, and for much of the Empire’s history the Daidoji settlement called Garden Under Shadow City looked down on it with hostility from the Crane side. The waters of Earthquake Fish Bay provide abundant fishing, while the land itself is devoted to producing goods for export rather than staple crops: tea, flowers, and sake rice. The Yasuki lands are strongly garrisoned despite their distance from the Shadowlands, due to the frequent risk of war with the Crane Clan. At the mouth of Earthquake Fish Bay, the Tidal Landbridge exposes a sandy crossing at low tide connecting Yasuki and Crane territory, a feature that has been the site of small battles, daring trade crossings, and at least one famous act of Daidoji heroism.

#### Toritaka Family Provinces

##### Toritaka Province

For most of their history the Toritaka are a Minor Clan, the Falcon. Their lands consist of a single valley and a considerable amount of forest and hillside at the far northern edge of the Twilight Mountains, close to the Shinomen Mori. The lands of the Toritaka are known to be some of the most haunted in the Empire, and the people there are deeply superstitious. The Valley of Spirits at the heart of the province is an intensely spiritually active place, with vengeful ghosts rising from untimely deaths and ancient spirits walking between the trees. A series of watchfires and lanterns surround the valley to help keep hostile spirits at bay. After the Toritaka joined the Crab Clan in the twelfth century, Kyuden Toketsu was granted to them, adding a second holding with pilgrim traffic and trade caravans passing through.

### 2.3.4 Crane Clan Provinces

#### Asahina Family Provinces

##### Anshin Province

The largest and most prosperous of the Asahina provinces in terms of agricultural wealth, Anshin Province is located between the Wall Above the Ocean Mountains and the ocean. Filled with shrines and temples, it is in many ways the perfect example of what other samurai imagine when they think of the Asahina lands. However, Anshin is marred by its lengthy shared border with the Crab Clan. The Daidoji maintain a strong garrison here in the keep of Zenshou Tetsu, but are constrained by the peace-loving Asahina. The province also contains the Heaven’s Wisdom Temple, an ancient Asahina center for astronomy and astrology built on a high open plain, and the ruins of Oni Mura, a village destroyed during the Clan War whose curse the Asahina have been unable to purge.

Adjacent provinces: Wakiaiai (Asahina) to the south, Kyoukan (Hida, Crab) to the west, Sunda Mizu (Yasuki, Crab) to the southwest, Ichigun (Daidoji, Crane) to the north

##### Wakiaiai Province

Much of Wakiaiai Province is marshland, hot and humid in the warm months, filled with fog in the winter. The land is nestled tightly between the endless ocean to the south and east, the Anshin province to the north, and the rocky Yasuki lands surrounding Earthquake Fish Bay to the west. Mantis sailors and disgruntled peasants often call this land the armpit of the Crane due to its humid climate and boggy stench, especially in the summer. Wakiaiai is a rural province with a relatively small total population, stuck between the two merchant strongholds of Jukami Mura and Yasuki Yashiki. Those who do live here are thus never far from commercial life. The province is home to Jukami Mura, the so-called Port That Never Sleeps, a cornerstone of trade in the Asahina lands and the entire southern half of Crane territory.

Adjacent provinces: Anshin (Asahina) to the north, Shinkyou (Asahina) to the south, Sunda Mizu (Yasuki, Crab) to the northwest, Junkin (Yasuki, Crab) to the southwest

##### Shinkyou Province

The heartland of the Asahina, Shinkyou Province is home to the blessed Fields of the Morning Sun which hold both the home temple of the Asahina family, Shinden Asahina, and a prominent Temple of Jurojin. The family views this as a land of sacred peace, built from the combination of philosophical ideas, political enforcement, and the supplication of spirits who promote nonviolence. Heimin and merchants, ji-samurai, and the rulers of the province all share an ever-present sense of calm and serenity. The province forms a peninsula, bounded on one side by Earthquake Fish Bay and on the other by the sea. A beach at the tip of this province connects it to the Crab Clan’s lands by way of the famous Tidal Landbridge. For much of its history the province is also home to Garden Under Shadow City, the largest Daidoji military stronghold in the southern Crane lands, destroyed during the Clan War.

#### Daidoji Family Provinces

##### Kosaten Province

Kosaten Province teems with activity all year long, and its storied history seems to offer only moments of peace as brief stopping points on the way to war. The people of Kosaten share a border on both the north and west with the Lion Clan, while to the southwest the Spine of the World Mountains and the Three Stone River protect them from conflict with the Scorpion. When war with the Lion takes place, Kosaten Province is the first to experience the heavy sandals of the Matsu soldiers tramping upon its roads and fields. However, even during times of war the land is so rich that it never sees a short harvest. ### 2.3.23 Otosan Uchi — The Imperial Capital (Miyako)

Settlement type: Imperial Capital (Miyako). The only settlement of this type in the Empire. Located within the Imperial Lands province. PU: 100 (canon figure, ~50,000 people). Coastal: Yes (Bay of the Golden Sun, also called Wan sano Kin Taiyo). Rivers: River of the Sun (Kawa sano Taiyo) runs through the city. Map type: Fully handcrafted (Section 4.3.4). ASCII map: handcrafted interior zones with specific building interiors, NPC placement, and unique features. Zone tier: each district is a Navigation Zone (Section 4.3.4 v564 amendment) containing child Lesser Zones for individual buildings and infrastructure.

Otosan Uchi, the Father’s Home, also known as Buzama ni Hirugaru Kyujin (the Sprawling Giant), is the capital of the Emerald Empire and the seat of the Emperor. The city has been the capital for over a thousand years and is home to every aspect of Rokugani culture — a microcosm of the Empire itself. The city sits on the shore of the Bay of the Golden Sun where the River of the Sun meets the sea. It is divided into three concentric sections: the Toshisoto (Outer Districts), the Ekohikei (Inner Districts), and the Forbidden City. Each section carries progressively stricter access requirements. 

The original city was built around the Emperor’s Palace in a flawless oval, designed in the first century by a group effort of all the Great Clans. As new districts formed and the city expanded, the shape became irregular and haphazard. The outer walls were never actually completed, and many sections of those walls crumbled over time. Architecture in the Imperial City is somewhat stunted by the limiting factor of the Imperial Palace itself — the Emperor’s castle is considered the epitome of architectural perfection, a symbol of purity and religious devotion, and no other structure in the city is allowed to rival it in height or splendor.

#### Access Restrictions

Access layer: TOSHISOTO. Open to all. Any character arriving at Otosan Uchi enters the Toshisoto. No permission required. Characters in the Toshisoto can move freely between all 11 Toshisoto zones.

Access layer: EKOHIKEI. Restricted. Entry requires permission from the Sentaku Tribunal or equivalent authority. Lower-ranking samurai need explicit permission just to enter. The Miwaku Kabe (Enchanted Walls) physically separates the Ekohikei from the Toshisoto. Entry is through the Southern Gate (primary, between Kanjo and Chisei districts) or the Necessary Gate (secondary, between Hito and Forbidden City, used by servants and low-ranking samurai). Gate guards (mostly Lion) enforce access. Characters in the Ekohikei can move freely between all 4 Ekohikei zones.

Access layer: FORBIDDEN CITY. Highly restricted. Entry requires specific authorization from the Emperor, the Emerald Champion, or the Sentaku Tribunal for a stated purpose. Two gates: the Road of the Most High (main entrance) and the Necessary Gate. Even samurai with Ekohikei access cannot enter the Forbidden City without separate authorization.

#### Governance

Otosan Uchi’s governance is unique. The city is not ruled by a single lord in the standard province model. Instead, the Sentaku Tribunal (a component of the Imperial Bureaucracy, dominated by the Otomo family) controls access to the Ekohikei and the Forbidden City and handles administrative matters too important for district governors but below the Emperor’s attention. The Tribunal draws its members from all four Ekohikei districts. Sentaku means “choice” — the Tribunal chooses who enters the inner city, which petitions reach the Emperor, and which administrative matters become law. Their statements and decrees are considered law in Rokugan and can only be overruled by the Emerald Champion or the Emperor himself.

Both the Ekohikei and the Toshisoto are divided into districts, each governed by a samurai chosen (somewhat arbitrarily) by the Emperor or a senior member of his court. These Governors report to the Sentaku Tribunal. Each Governor rules their district much as a provincial daimyo rules a province. Governors in the Toshisoto tend to rename their district upon taking power; citizens often refuse to learn the new names. In the Ekohikei, district names are permanent regardless of who serves as Governor. Each Governor has the right to command any of their citizens’ personal guards in an emergency. The military defense of each district is left up to its Governor, though the Lion Clan and Imperial Legions watch over the city and there is little need for independent defense.

#### Sentaku Tribunal Structure

The Sentaku Tribunal is a 5-member voting body. Each member is a named NPC with their own personality, dispositions, clan affiliation,. One member is designated as the Chair. The Chair has no extra voting power — still one vote, same as every other member — but serves as the administrative point of contact for the institution. All 15 Governors’ operational_superior_id points to the Chair’s character_id. If the Chair dies or is replaced, all Governors’ operational_superior_id fields update to the new Chair.

Decisions are made by majority vote: 3 of 5 votes required to pass. Each member votes independently based on their own personality, dispositions, clan loyalty, and objectives, processed through the NPC Decision Engine. When a petition arrives — such as a request for ekohikei_access or forbidden_city_access — all 5 members receive the petition and each casts a vote. The result is the majority outcome. Ties are impossible with 5 members.

Composition: the source material states the Tribunal is dominated by the Otomo and draws its members from all four Ekohikei districts. At world generation, the 5 seats are filled with pre-written NPCs. Expected composition: 2–3 Otomo (reflecting their dominance), with the remaining seats drawn from other Imperial families (Seppun, Miya) or from Great Clan samurai who have earned the Emperor’s trust. The Chair is typically the senior Otomo member. Exact roster is a pre-written character task (Section 22.8).

Vacancy and replacement: Tribunal members are appointed by the Emperor. When a member dies or is removed, FILL_VACANCY fires on the Emperor following the standard archetype-specific patterns (Section 55.10.1). Tribunal member is a position_type: “sentaku_tribunal_member.” The Chair is a sub-designation within that position_type: “sentaku_tribunal_chair.” Status: 6.5 (Chair), 6.0 (members). lord_id = Emperor for all 5.

Scope of authority: the Tribunal votes on ekohikei_access petitions (who may enter the Inner City), forbidden_city_access petitions (who may enter the Forbidden City), administrative decrees affecting the city (considered law, overruled only by the Emerald Champion or Emperor), and Governor performance reviews (the Tribunal can petition the Emperor to dismiss a Governor, but cannot dismiss directly). The Tribunal does not appoint Governors — that authority rests with the Emperor or his delegate (see Governor Appointment Mechanics below).

The Sentaku Tribunal operates through legitimate institutional authority only. No covert enforcement capability exists.

##### Access Petition Pipeline

Petition types: ekohikei_access (entry to the Inner City) and forbidden_city_access (entry to the Forbidden City). A character submits a petition via WRITE_LETTER to the Sentaku Tribunal Chair or via in-person request if already in the Toshisoto. The Chair distributes the petition to all 5 members. Each member votes independently through the weighted scoring formula below. 3 of 5 votes required to grant access.

Vote evaluation formula (per member): Petitioner Status (weight 35) — score = Status × 4.67, cap 35. Higher Status = more likely granted. Status 5+ passes easily for Ekohikei. Status 6+ passes easily for Forbidden City. Status 4 is the practical minimum for Ekohikei access without personal relationships. This is the heaviest weight because the Tribunal’s primary function is controlling who reaches the Emperor. Clan disposition (weight 30) — score = (clan_disposition + 50) × 0.30, range 0–30. The member’s disposition toward the petitioner’s clan. A member who dislikes the Scorpion votes against Scorpion petitioners. This is where Tribunal composition becomes politically significant. Personal disposition (weight 20) — if the member has met the petitioner (met_characters), score = (personal_disposition + 50) × 0.20, range 0–20. If they have not met, this defaults to 12 (benefit of the doubt — an unknown samurai receives a neutral-positive assessment, not a cold zero). Political climate (weight 15) — 15 if no active war involving the petitioner’s clan, 0 if the petitioner’s clan is currently at War Status with a clan the member favors. Active conflicts make the Tribunal hostile to belligerents seeking access to the capital’s inner sanctum.

Threshold: score ≥ 50 = YES vote for ekohikei_access. Forbidden City uses the same formula but with a +10 threshold increase (score ≥ 60 required), reflecting the stricter access standard. Status 5 characters need personal relationships with Tribunal members to reach the Forbidden City — this is the interesting gameplay. An explicit Imperial summons — from the Emperor, Emerald Champion, or any Emperor’s Chosen position — bypasses the Tribunal vote entirely and sets the access flag directly.

Denied petitions: the petitioner may resubmit after 1 IC season (4 OOC days). Resubmission within the same season is automatically denied without a vote. The petitioner can work to change conditions in the interim — raise disposition with specific Tribunal members, gain Status, or get a clan ally to advocate on their behalf via letter.

Duration: ekohikei_access persists until revoked by the Tribunal (3 of 5 vote to revoke) or by the Emperor or Emerald Champion. It does not expire. forbidden_city_access is per-visit — the petitioner declares a duration in IC days as part of the petition, and the flag auto-clears when that duration expires. Maximum permitted duration: 7 IC days per visit (PROVISIONAL). Overstaying is trespassing — a criminal offense per Section 11.3. Characters with permanent forbidden_city_access — Imperial family members, Emperor’s Chosen, anyone with a residence in the Forbidden City — skip the petition process entirely and hold a standing access flag that does not expire.

##### Governor Performance Review

Trigger: at each seasonal tick, the Tribunal evaluates all 15 Governors. A review fires for any Governor whose district has met any of the following in the past season: Stability dropped below 50, crime incidents exceeded 3 in a single IC season, the Governor generated a negative Tier 3+ topic, or the Governor has been in office less than 1 IC season and Stability has dropped at all (the Juramashi effect — new Governors are evaluated harshly for early failure).

Vote evaluation formula (per member): District Stability (weight 30) — lower Stability = more likely to vote for dismissal. Below 25 is near-automatic. Crime rate (weight 20) — more crime incidents in the district = more likely to vote for dismissal. Governor disposition (weight 20) — a member who likes the Governor will protect them; a member who dislikes them will push for removal. Governor clan (weight 15) — clan loyalty — a member from the same clan as the Governor will resist dismissal. Time in office (weight 15) — Governors who have served longer receive more patience. A Governor who has been in office 3+ seasons and is still struggling is judged more harshly than one who just arrived. Exception: in Juramashi, the inverse applies — lasting more than 1 season is itself impressive, and the Tribunal extends increasing patience the longer a Juramashi Governor survives.

Threshold: score ≥ 60 = YES (petition Emperor to dismiss). Below 60 = NO. If 3 of 5 vote YES, the Chair fires WRITE_LETTER to the Emperor recommending dismissal of the named Governor. The Emperor then evaluates the recommendation through his own personality archetype — a Benevolent Emperor gives second chances, a Tyrant fires anyone the Tribunal recommends removing (viewing the Tribunal’s recommendation as a loyalty test), an Iron Emperor evaluates the district metrics himself before deciding, a Cunning Emperor may keep a failing Governor in place to create political tension, and a Warlike Emperor dismisses immediately if the district’s problems affect military readiness but ignores purely civilian failures. The Emperor’s response fires DISMISS_FROM_POSITION (if agreeing) or DO_NOTHING (if declining). A declined recommendation generates no further action until the next seasonal review.

All threshold values and weight values PROVISIONAL pending balancing.

##### District Economics

Otosan Uchi’s 100 PU is subdivided across districts following the lore of each district’s population density, commercial activity, and size. Each district’s PU generates Koku independently via the standard Section 4.3.9 formula. The Governor’s SET_TAX_RATE determines the percentage they retain; the remainder flows directly to the Emperor’s stockpile. There is no Family Daimyo or Clan Champion intermediate tier — Governors are appointed officials, not feudal lords, so the cascade is Governor → Emperor only.

Toshisoto (82 PU across 11 districts): Juramashi 15 (largest district, two blocks, population alone could rival other districts individually), South Dock (Kosuga) 12 (handles over 2/5 of the city’s trade, never sleeps, Yatoshin warehouse district), Rich Crescent (Hojize) 10 (wealthiest Toshisoto district, controls half the river traffic, primary inland road), Gilded Hill (Hayasu) 8 (affluent residential, farms outside the walls), Emperor’s Road (Hidari) 7 (high-end shops and inns along the Road of the Most High), Spiritual (Ochiyo) 6 (temples and pilgrimage, well-maintained but low commerce), North Dock (Higshikawa) 6 (small but vital, shipping, pleasure houses, Imperial Guard kaisha), Brutal Flame (Tsai) 5 (lighthouse district, criminal element, moderate population), Prison/Moon (Toyotomi) 5 (dark and dirty, prison, gambling quarter), Tenari’s Ruin (Meiyoko) 5 (quarters overseen by gokenin, criminal refuge), Eta’s Island (Hinjaku) 3 (least respected, eta and outcasts, mostly outside the walls).

#### Governor Appointment Mechanics

Governor is a position_type in the position system (Section 11.3.18). 15 instances exist (one per governed district — the Forbidden City has no Governor). Each Governor’s lord_id = Emperor. Each Governor’s operational_superior_id = Sentaku Tribunal Chair (see Sentaku Tribunal Structure above). When a Governor vacancy occurs (death, dismissal via DISMISS_FROM_POSITION, resignation), FILL_VACANCY fires on the Emperor as the position’s lord. The Emperor’s archetype determines whether he fills the vacancy personally or delegates.

##### Archetype Routing

Appoints personally: Benevolent Emperor (fills quickly, merit-focused, cares about the people these Governors serve), Iron Emperor (fills quickly, strict merit evaluation, governance of the capital is the Emperor’s duty), Tyrant Emperor (fills based on loyalty, would never trust anyone else to vet Governor candidates). These archetypes decompose FILL_VACANCY directly to APPOINT_TO_POSITION targeting the vacant Governor slot.

Delegates to Imperial Advisor: Cunning Emperor (deliberately delays filling vacancies to keep candidates competing for his favor; delegation to the Imperial Advisor provides a layer of deniability and forces ambitious samurai to court both the Emperor and the Advisor), Warlike Emperor (deprioritizes political positions; district governance is beneath his attention while military matters dominate). These archetypes decompose FILL_VACANCY to ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE targeting the Imperial Advisor, passing the Governor position_id. The Imperial Advisor then runs FILL_VACANCY → APPOINT_TO_POSITION on the Emperor’s behalf. If the Imperial Advisor position is itself vacant, the delegation falls back to the Emperor personally — even a Cunning or Warlike Emperor must act when there is no one to delegate to.

Vacancy fill timing follows existing archetype patterns: Benevolent 14 ticks, Iron 14–30 ticks, Cunning 30–60 ticks, Warlike 45+ ticks, Tyrant variable (instant for loyal candidates, indefinite for positions that check his power — Governor is a loyalty-serving position, so the Tyrant fills quickly).

##### Candidate Evaluation Weights

Governor candidate evaluation uses APPOINT_TO_POSITION’s existing weighted scoring system. The following weights apply to Governor appointments specifically. Base weights: Skill relevance (default 15, modified by archetype — Iron increases to 25, Tyrant decreases to 5). Disposition toward appointing authority (default 15, modified by archetype — Tyrant increases to 30, Iron decreases to 10). Clan match (default 20, modified by archetype — Benevolent 20 [respects tradition], Iron 20 [tradition is part of the Celestial Order], Cunning 10 [sees value in occasionally breaking convention for political disruption], Warlike 20 [follows convention by default as the path of least resistance], Tyrant 0 [loyalty is everything, clan convention is irrelevant]; see District Clan Preference Table below). Note: Cunning and Warlike modifiers only apply in the edge case where the Imperial Advisor position is vacant and the Emperor must appoint personally — when the Advisor appoints, the Advisor reads the district’s clan_preference weight directly. Status appropriateness (default 10 — Governor is a Status 4.0–5.0 position; candidates far below are unsuitable, candidates far above would see it as a demotion). Current location proximity (default 5 — slight preference for candidates already in Otosan Uchi or nearby).

Relevant skills for Governor evaluation: Courtier (primary — governance is politics), Commerce (secondary — managing district finances and trade), Investigation (secondary — district law enforcement oversight), Lore: Law (tertiary — Governors report to the Sentaku Tribunal on legal matters), Battle (tertiary — emergency military command of citizen guards). The appointing authority evaluates the top-scoring candidate from their met_characters list who is available (not currently holding a position of equal or higher Status, not in active military deployment, not under criminal investigation).

##### Candidate Solicitation Fallback

The appointing authority (Emperor or Imperial Advisor) can only evaluate candidates from their met_characters pool (Section 22.3, Section 29.15.24). If no candidate in the pool meets the scoring threshold for the vacant Governor position, the appointing authority falls back to solicitation: WRITE_LETTER to the Clan Champion(s) of the clans listed in the district’s clan_preference, requesting a candidate recommendation for the Governor post.

Chain of resolution: (1) FILL_VACANCY fires on the appointing authority. (2) Appointing authority evaluates met_characters pool. No suitable candidate found — no character from a preferred clan scores above the appointment threshold, or the pool contains no candidates from preferred clans at all. (3) Appointing authority fires WRITE_LETTER to the Clan Champion of each clan in the district’s clan_preference list (1 letter per clan, standard letter delivery timing per Section 12.7). The letter carries the topic: “Governor vacancy in [district Sentaku name]” (Tier 4). (4) Each Clan Champion who receives the letter evaluates their own vassals for a suitable candidate. This is a standard FILL_VACANCY decomposition on the Champion’s end — the Champion identifies a vassal of appropriate Status, skill, and standing, then fires WRITE_LETTER back to the appointing authority with the recommendation. (5) The recommended character enters the appointing authority’s met_characters pool through the letter’s topic exchange (the letter names the candidate, which satisfies the “heard of them through topics or letters” clause of met_characters). (6) The appointing authority re-evaluates with the expanded pool and fires APPOINT_TO_POSITION.

Multiple solicitations: the appointing authority may write to more than one Clan Champion simultaneously if the district has multiple preferred clans. Each Champion responds independently. The appointing authority then evaluates all recommended candidates together and selects the best-scoring one. This naturally creates competition between clans for the posting and generates topics as each clan’s recommendation becomes known at court.

Juramashi special case: Juramashi has no clan_preference (weight 0 for all clans). If the pool contains no suitable candidate, the appointing authority writes to the Clan Champion of whichever clan currently has the highest disposition toward the Emperor. This is effectively a political favor — the Governor posting is a test or burden, and offering it to a favored clan is either a compliment (the Emperor trusts them with the impossible district) or a subtle insult (the Emperor is giving them a job that will embarrass whoever they send). The Cunning Emperor exploits this ambiguity deliberately.

Timing: the entire solicitation chain adds letter delivery time in both directions (appointing authority → Clan Champion, then Champion → appointing authority). For a Champion already at court in Otosan Uchi, this is near-instant. For a Champion in a distant province, this may take 10+ IC days each way. The vacancy remains open during this period — the district functions under the Sentaku Tribunal’s general authority and whatever bureaucracy the previous Governor left in place.

No new ActionIDs or NeedTypes required. The entire fallback chain uses existing infrastructure: WRITE_LETTER (Category 10), FILL_VACANCY (NeedType), APPOINT_TO_POSITION (Category 14), and the met_characters topic exchange rules (Section 22.3).

#### Law Enforcement

Otosan Uchi is the birthplace of all Imperial law. Each year during the month of the Dragon, after the Emperor returns from Winter Court, the Hashira Horitsu (Pillars of Law) convenes. The Emerald Champion, Imperial Magistrates, ranking courtiers and nobles, and a select few others discuss matters of law — this is where legal policy is made, reshaped, or discarded. Magistrates in Otosan Uchi are divided between wandering magistrates and district magistrates. Assignment to a district is more prestigious (greater stability, but also greater opportunity for corruption). Wandering magistrates can enforce law in any district but lack a power base. Under each magistrate are yoriki and doshin. Within the Ekohikei, yoriki are the lowest-ranking law enforcement. In the Toshisoto, many doshin (armed peasants) assist the yoriki. The city has two prisons: one in the Prison/Moon District for samurai, and one in the Eta’s Island District for all others. Neither prison is guarded by Imperial guards — they are policed by hajisherazu, or samurai who have been shamed and have voluntarily given up their freedom to serve.

Doshin allocation per Section 11.3: Otosan Uchi receives 15–20 city doshin per district, 1 doshin headman per district. Emerald Magistrate allocation: 1 per district (number per Section 11.3 pending exact district count — now confirmed at 17 zones, so 17 Emerald Magistrate posts across the city). Clan magistrate allocation: 1 per district (special case per Section 11.3).

#### The Miwaku Kabe (Enchanted Walls)

The Ekohikei is surrounded by a massive wall imbued with powerful Elemental magic. In its early history this wall represented the outer edge of the city’s territory. The walls were built during the First War when the Hantei assigned four of the Great Clans — the Phoenix, Crane, Crab, and Ki-Rin — to hold the city against the forces of Fu Leng. The entire circle of walls was completed in less than six years, and the efforts of shugenja from those clans imbued them with powerful guardian spirits and magical powers. Once the Empire was at peace, the Kakita re-worked the exteriors of the walls so their beauty would match their strength. The outer walls of the Toshisoto were never altogether completed and are poorly maintained, with many sections crumbling or pierced by gaps.

Eastern Wall (“Homare,” Glory): Built by the Isawa. The main gate into the Ekohikei. Brilliant golden kanji name every soul who has fallen defending it. The spirits of the fallen reside within the sacred stones, ready to take up arms in defense of the city again. The wall’s magical nature allows the Earth kami to repair any damage done to it by magic or steel. Exception: gaijin cannonball scars from the Battle of White Stag remain visible through all centuries.

Southern Wall: Consecrated by the shugenja Isawa Naigama and his pupils, all of whom gave their lives in the process. During the subsequent Shadowlands assault (the Battle of the Four Winds), the wall came to life — the Earth spirits formed limbs and struck out at the oni. The oni all disappeared, and speculation remains as to whether they were destroyed or consumed by the wall itself.

Western Wall: Contains bound Shadowlands creatures from the Battle of the Four Winds. The faint wails of these creatures, often called the Karada Voices, can be heard throughout nearby districts. Many shugenja have tried to research whether it still poses a threat. Some have gone mad. Generally considered safe, but anyone who ventures too close at night may feel hot, foul breath on the backs of their necks.

Northern Wall: Destroyed during the Battle of Four Winds and rebuilt afterward. Unlike the other three walls which are solid, the northern wall is hollow — filled with mazes, traps, and ambush zones. Anyone who breaches the ramparts must fight through the maze-like tunnels and passages that connect the wall’s various sections.

#### Toshisoto (Outer City) — 11 Zones

##### Zone 1: Brutal Flame (Tsai) District

Sentaku name: Tsai. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

Located at the tip of the Antlers of the White Stag, the two peninsulas that form the mouth of the Bay of the Golden Sun. The district’s tower, Zankoku Hon’O (the Brutal Flame), serves as a lighthouse guiding visitors to the capital in darkness. The tower is one of two at the tips of the Antlers and has the distinguished history of having never gone dark. It takes its name from the Battle of the White Stag, when a fire tensai of the Isawa stood atop it and called down the fire kami upon gaijin invaders. The district is a symbol of purity in an otherwise corrupt region. Governors here are selected either for their ability to manage the criminal element or their ability to stamp it out. Most governors have come from the Scorpion, the Yasuki, or the Tortoise. The governor’s home possesses one of the finest views of the Bay of the Golden Sun.

Infrastructure: Okiya (Bayushi’s Mask — legendary geisha house, near eastern Miwaku Kabe, Scorpion-run), Tea House (Leaves of Shosuro — famous for matcha, notorious for hidden opium den in back room), Theater (The Tear / Shosuro’s Tear — renowned playhouse, Scorpion-controlled), Local Shrine (shrine to Hofukushu, Fortune of Vengeance — hidden in back alleys, worshippers leave rice, sake, koku, and personal items related to crimes against them and their loved ones).

Notable holdings: Bayushi’s Bane (Agasha hospital, treats “strange ailments” i.e. poison), Dragon’s Mists (Kitsuki/Agasha hospital, vow never to interfere with the natural process of life but aid in all manmade troubles), Life’s Waterfall (sake house favored by citizens across the city for its beautiful interior fountains), Light as the Wind (Asahina shop specializing in kites and painted dolls), abandoned waterway houses (criminal hideouts, especially in heavy fog).

Handcrafted map landmarks: Zankoku Hon’O (lighthouse tower, never gone dark), Governor’s residence (bay view), Bayushi’s Mask (okiya, Scorpion-run), Leaves of Shosuro (tea house, hidden opium den), The Tear (theater, Scorpion-controlled), Shrine of Hofukushu (hidden shrine, Fortune of Vengeance), Bayushi’s Bane (Agasha hospital, poison treatment), Dragon’s Mists (Kitsuki/Agasha hospital), Life’s Waterfall (sake house, interior fountains), Light as the Wind (Asahina kite and doll shop), abandoned waterway houses (criminal hideouts).

##### Zone 2: Emperor’s Road (Hidari) District

Sentaku name: Hidari. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

The district referred to by the Sentaku as the Emperor’s Road is a region of prosperity and elegance, a display of beauty for the Imperial family to review. From the Imperial Palace, an arriving or departing Emperor walks upon the Road of the Most High, a massive wooden roadway designed specifically for the Son of Heaven. The Road of the Most High cuts through the Ekohikei, but when it reaches the Outer City, it is here, in this district alone, that the Emperor rests his gaze. This district follows along the Road of the Most High on both sides all the way to the southern gate, presenting the Imperial Presence with the best of everything the city has to offer. Nothing which might offend the eyes of Heaven may remain within sight of the Road of the Most High. The Emperor’s Road District changes hands perhaps more than any other district in the city because of the high profile of its Governor. Leadership of the district rarely allows unsightly structures to remain in place; any building with structural damage or even simply a poor reputation is razed to the ground rather than risk Imperial ire.

Infrastructure: Market (Emerald Coin — famous merchant plaza, favored by a passing Emperor who ordered a simple statue outside; multiple famous shops and tea houses), Tea House, Inn (Inn of the Last Rite — patrons drink late into the night in private rooms; back rooms are notorious for illicit gambling and opium dens), Okiya (Doji’s Children — Crane-run, draws members of the kuge).

Notable holdings: Light from Above (dining house with central room open to the sky for starry-night dining), Soshiuchi (House of Loss — a huge mansion built by a proud Crane lord for his bride, who perished in battle; his bride wandered the house in sorrow, never to leave; reputed to be haunted). Crane have a disproportionate number of holdings in this district. Kakita and Kaiu smiths often live here, crafting sacred weapons and armor. One street corner features an origami shop with tiny paper parodies of famous Rokugani plays. From the huge jade torii arches above the gate of the Ekohikei, the Emperor’s Road District is filled with the most famous shops, inns, tea houses, and other aesthetically pleasing holdings in the city.

Handcrafted map landmarks: Road of the Most High (massive wooden Imperial roadway), jade torii arches (gate to Ekohikei), Emerald Coin (market plaza, Imperial statue), Doji’s Children (okiya, Crane-run), Inn of the Last Rite (gambling and opium dens), Light from Above (dining house, open-sky central room), Soshiuchi / House of Loss (haunted Crane mansion), origami shop (paper parodies of famous plays), Governor’s residence.

##### Zone 3: Juramashi

Sentaku name: Juramashi (unique — never renamed). Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

The largest district in the city and a region of utter chaos and confusion, known for its dizzying variety in architecture, customs, and people. Juramashi stretches from the River of the Sun to the southern Miwaku Kabe, and spans outward to the southern edge of the Imperial City. It is so large it is divided into two blocks, Upper and Lower Juramashi, each of which could rival another district in size. The blocks are like two separate worlds, but no Governor would ever dream of splitting them. Juramashi is the only district the Sentaku refers to by its original name. The first Governor held power for more than a decade; every single Governor since has left office in one way or another in less than six months. To the people of the district, no matter who rules, it will always be Juramashi. The bureaucracy performs the day-to-day governance; a single transient Governor does not matter.

The district suffers from extremely poor planning. Streets veer off in random directions, rarely running parallel to one another. Building locations and designs follow no clear pattern. Even locals born in Juramashi can have difficulty navigating outside their own sections. The citizens actually enjoy this chaotic aspect of their district, believing it adds both character and fame.

Infrastructure: Market (Craftsman’s Quarter — constant construction projects), Tea House (Bright Wind Geisha House — visited by at least one major family daimyo or Clan Champion each year), Garden (Gardens of Juramashi’s Honor — largest garden complex in the Imperial City, over 30 Kakita-trained gardeners and scores of heimin assistants), Library (Kuroka Library — single largest library in the city, chaotic internally, some say the information of the entire world is housed here if you can find it), Inn (Glory’s Forgotten Rest Inn — gigantic structure where visitors to the Spiritual District come to rest), Sake House.

Notable holdings: Juramashi District Meeting Hall, Natsu-Togumara Shrine (Fortune of Travel and Experience), Maratu’s Origata (gift-wrapping artisan), Blessings of the Eternal Kami (mansion-turned-meeting house for annual shugenja pilgrimage, decreed by Hantei XXI), Lamplighters guild (Lower Juramashi — family of lamplighters oversees over 300 lamps), Jurojin’s Chance (only legal gambling den, located outside the outer walls). Upper Juramashi: residential neighborhoods, samurai, wealthy merchants. Lower Juramashi: lesser merchants, heimin, eta villages, Izaru farms.

Handcrafted map landmarks: Craftsman’s Quarter (market, constant construction), Bright Wind (geisha house), Natsu-Togumara Shrine (Fortune of Travel and Experience), Juramashi District Meeting Hall, Maratu’s Origata (gift-wrapping shop), Governor’s residence (if one currently survives).

##### Zone 4: Spiritual (Ochiyo) District

Sentaku name: Ochiyo. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

Otosan Uchi attracts religious pilgrims from all across the Empire because of its history, political significance, and spiritual connections. While the Forbidden City is the obvious choice for most tourists, few outside the upper crust of society are ever allowed inside the secure domain of the Son of Heaven. For the rest, there is the Spiritual District. Surrounded on all sides by other domains, the Spiritual District relies on its neighbors in the Gilded Hill and Juramashi. Far and away the most popular destination for visitors to the Toshisoto, the Spiritual District is the site of major temples to the founding Kami, the great Temple of Lady Sun, and dozens of smaller shrines. Monks, shugenja of all orders, and samurai of religious or philosophical mindset come here to pray, to seek the wisdom of their ancestors, or simply to bask in the joy of the Spiritual District’s connection to the higher realms. This region is known for its peaceful streets, its lack of shops, and its quiet residents. The district is well-maintained and constantly patrolled by Imperial and Lion soldiers.

Infrastructure: Temple (Temple of the Sun Goddess — largest building in the Spiritual District, rivals any temple in the Emerald Empire, glows with radiant light at all hours, no shadow falls near it), Local Shrine (Temples of the Six Great Kami along Seppun’s Path — Hida’s Temple [sturdy stone, Crab victories murals], Temple to Shinjo [suffered earthquake damage seven times, rebuilt each time], Temple of Bayushi [labyrinthine chambers, Scorpion-only access], Temple to Shiba [peaceful oasis, free-flowing ivy, central shrine], Temple of Doji [simple white-flower purity], Temple to Akodo [twin to the Hall of Ancestors]), Garrison (District Armory — full of weapons, supplies a small army and serves as a defensive location), Library (Asako Records — small old building, thousands of scrolls on the lineage of the Rokugani elite), Tea House (Shinsei’s Precious Memory Tea House — founded after Shinsei’s visit, solemn volunteer duty for Spiritual District residents), Stables, Dojo (fighting arena).

Notable holdings: Seppun’s Path (road connecting outer wall to southern Miwaku Kabe, winding through hidden gardens and secluded meditation sites), Simple Pleasures (single-story geisha house, okaa-san sends geisha out on call rather than inviting samurai in), Kin-iro no Sento (Golden Spire — largest public house, 46 expensive rooms with balcony views), Eishuri’s Compassion (tea-house and inn in the shadow of the Golden Spire), Merciful Spring Inn and Heaven’s Gift Inn (boarding houses for ji-samurai and heimin respectively). Note: No Temple to Togashi exists — an oversight the Dragon Clan never sought to rectify. Otosan Uchi is destroyed before Yoritomo ascends as the Kami of the Mantis Clan.

Handcrafted map landmarks: Temple of the Sun Goddess (largest building in district), Temple of Daikoku (Fortune of Wealth), Temple of Ebisu (Fortune of Honest Work), Temple of Benten (Fortune of Romantic Love), Simple Pleasures (okiya), Seppun’s Path (winding road through hidden gardens), meditation gardens (secluded, public access), Governor’s residence.

##### Zone 5: Gilded Hill (Hayasu) District

Sentaku name: Hayasu. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

Named for both its affluent nature and for the steep hill which dominates its landscape, the Gilded Hill is located just south of the River of the Sun and west of the Forbidden City. It is primarily a residential neighborhood for elite samurai, famous artisans, and wealthy merchants. The hill itself is the site of many elaborate private homes, while the district as a whole is known for its huge open spaces set aside for festivals. Space comes at a premium in the Imperial City, and open space is an obvious sign of wealth. Farms located just outside the crumbling outer walls, believed to be blessed by the great spirits of the Imperial City, provide the Gilded Hill District with good financial health. However, this district is not quite so splendid as it outwardly appears. Fortunes rise and fall, and some of the homes only give the appearance of wealth while inside they are empty, echoing with lost riches. The “gilded” Sentaku name is a subtle recognition of this reality. The Governor’s estate sits at the top of the hill, containing more than twenty extra bedrooms, offices, and meeting halls, surrounded by a huge Shintao rock garden. Each barracks at the base of the hill is dedicated to a single clan and assigned a specific region of the district to oversee.

Infrastructure: Market (Shining Square — grand merchant plaza, doubles as festival setting; Riverside Merchant Plaza — open-air markets selling bulk raw materials, grain, and luxury items to heimin), Okiya (Kankei neighborhood — thin avenue lined with fine-quality geisha houses and sake houses along the Way of the Elements), Tea House, Sake House.

Notable holdings: Cherry Blossom Row (strip of cherry trees along the waterway, cultivated by Kakita artisans), Chirping Crickets Neighborhood (merchants, modestly decorated shops selling tiny gilded cages with lucky crickets), Fortune’s Favored Neighborhood (wealthiest merchants, Sukichiba strip markets in crumbling wall gaps), Wild Rice Neighborhood (least expensive houses, heavily populated, still among the most sought-after in the city), Sheltered Swords (secluded samurai residential area behind the great hill, hidden between hill and walls, mainly Crane, Dragon, and Scorpion residents). Farms outside the outer wall provide food and are wealthy enough to attract infiltration by spy networks.

Handcrafted map landmarks: Shining Square (grand merchant plaza, festival setting), Riverside Merchant Plaza (open-air markets), Cherry Blossom Row (waterway-side cherry trees, Kakita-cultivated), Chirping Crickets Neighborhood (samurai residential, immaculate gardens), Governor’s residence (hilltop estate), farms outside the walls.

##### Zone 6: Rich Crescent (Hojize) District

Sentaku name: Hojize. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

This district is commonly referred to by the Sentaku as the Rich Crescent because of its unusual crescent shape, wrapping around its subsidiary, the Eta’s Island. The two districts were originally one, but a Lion governor in the second century split them. The Crescent is perhaps the wealthiest district of the Toshisoto, receiving tax funding, subsidies from merchant patrons, and an annual stipend from the Imperial families. It contains the primary inland road to the Imperial City. Governors of the Rich Crescent are responsible for creating the first impression of nearly every overland traveler. The Rich Crescent controls more than half the river traffic into Otosan Uchi, and its wharves are overseen by friendly but cunning portmasters. The streets are lined with manicured gardens and trees, and cultivated waterways and ponds dot the landscape.

Infrastructure: Port/Harbor (wharves — controls over half the river traffic), Market (well-stocked merchant shops), Inn (Clan Guide Houses — official arrival point where visiting samurai are brought upon first arrival, escorted along the Way of the Elements, each house equipped with the finest bathing facilities, food, and staff; also used by governors to deal privately with law violators), Garrison (Magistrate’s Memorial — where Soshi Takasho and Akodo Minobe uncovered the Bloodspeaker Iuchiban’s plots; active magistrate station, Emerald Magistrate posted here, usually a Lion, Scorpion, Isawa, or Kuni), Tea House (Morning Star Tea House — founded by a Kakita artisan who mapped the city, original map hangs in a place of honor).

Notable holdings: Kinjiren Tombs (graves from Iuchiban’s crimes, no longer used to inter bodies, all but forgotten), Chiken (Bloodsword) Temple (dedicated to Osano-Wo, Fortune of Fire and Thunder, built at the site of the Battle of Stolen Graves — the place seems haunted, with distant sounds of a smith working at his forge), Dark Cloud Monastery (nearby, monks’ constant chanting and drumming can be heard for blocks).

Handcrafted map landmarks: Wharves (river port, controls half the river traffic), Clan Guide Houses (inn, welcome committee for arriving samurai), Kinjiren Tombs (ancient graves from Iuchiban’s crimes, forgotten), Chiken / Bloodhawk Bridge (primary bridge to the Ekohikei, heavily guarded checkpoint), Governor’s residence, Koku Seal office (Miya-managed trade certification).

##### Zone 7: Eta’s Island (Hinjaku) District

Sentaku name: Hinjaku. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

In the city’s earliest history the Eta’s Island was part of the Rich Crescent, but after the second century it is governed separately to remove the city’s “dirty” inhabitants from the public eye. The Eta’s Island is the least-respected location in all of Otosan Uchi, and in fact most of it lies outside the city’s crumbling outer wall. The few samurai who live here are typically disgraced outcasts or ronin. In times of great strife, when refugees pour into the Father’s Home, this district fills up with unfortunate outsiders. Eta homes comprise a large portion of this district, and the untouchables here are in charge of cleaning not only the Rich Crescent but all the surrounding districts as well.

Infrastructure: (minimal formal infrastructure — no market, no garrison, no okiya of standard type).

Notable holdings: Takusanno Sakanaya (fishery processing plant, specializes in Pink Hamachi sushi, smell keeps samurai away), Shizukomen (Silent Release — crematorium, center of all business in the district, eta morticians prepare bodies in a huge stone building), Buichi’s Bunraku Dolls (puppeteer doll supplier, made from wood harvested outside the city), Durite Zoo (founded by a former governor named Kuni Durite, houses animals from all over the Empire, tends to become run-down between governors), Shudo Benten (all-female monastery dedicated to the Fortune of Romantic Love, women trained in fine arts), Temple of Eternal Breath (strange little shrine, it is said that whoever prays here and expresses true faith shall never die). High Lowlies neighborhood (skilled eta who serve as torturers and forensic assistants to magistrates), Wide Hat neighborhood (bridge between Rich Crescent and Eta’s Island, lowest of merchants who specialize in swindling visitors with cheap religious trinkets; also where forged documents and papers can be purchased).

Handcrafted map landmarks: Takusanno Sakanaya (fishery, Pink Hamachi sushi, terrible smell), Shizukomen (heimin residential, overcrowded), eta quarter (burakumin settlement outside the walls), Governor’s residence (undesirable posting).

##### Zone 8: Prison/Moon (Toyotomi) District

Sentaku name: Toyotomi. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

The district known to the Sentaku as both the Prison District and the Moon District is never called by those names outside the private halls of the Imperial Bureaucracy. Samurai, heimin, and hinin all consider this district to be cursed by both the presence of Otosan Uchi’s prison and by the presence of the sinister Shrine to Lord Moon. In most eras of the city’s history, the district is one of the least respectable domains, a dark and dirty place visited by few travelers. The Governor’s residence stands next to the district magistrate’s residence, both near the prison. The Governor’s domain is a palatial estate with an elaborate garden, and is perhaps the only impressive structure in the district. The magistrate station is quite large, the chief magistrate commanding twenty-five subordinate yoriki and more than a hundred doshin.

Infrastructure: Garrison (magistrate station — 25 yoriki, 100+ doshin), Okiya (Jumping Frog and others — cheap, relying on copious amounts of potent sake), Theater (Crying Peacock — the only theater in the district, richly decorated with paintings and sculptures of Noh and Kabuki artists), Sake House (Lion’s Rage — founded by a boisterous Lion samurai, attracts samurai from Lion, Mantis, and Crab), Tea House, Brewery.

Notable holdings: Kyuden Kokai (Palace of Remorse — the city’s primary prison, maintained by ~200 hajisherazu inhabitants and samurai guards, Governor’s estate and Makoto (Courtesy) Wall ensure samurai need not see it, four guard towers and a magistrate station keep watch), Shrine to Lord Moon (detested by residents citywide, high walls reduce its impact, monks within never leave, focused on forgiveness of wrath), Haven (rundown gambling quarter, oyabun criminal bosses run the neighborhood, Governor imposes some control by taxing criminal enterprises), All Words are One (bookstore / Scorpion-sponsored forgery front), House of Many Wonderful Things (Yasuki merchant, sells strange items from across the Empire and gaijin lands), Pampered Flower (bath house offering mochi and herbal treatments), Lucky Orchid Blossom (respectable geisha house), Everyone Dyes (reputed best dye shop in the city), Life’s Love (small rock garden where lovers court one another, rumored Shinsei once blessed it), Dawn Before Darkness (lamp shop), Fortune’s Wisdom (Imperial-sponsored Go house, home to the Naka Dainkei inner circle of elite Go masters, Yobigoeke sumai dojo), Suma’s Hot Spring (wooden structure around a hot muddy spring), Yum-Yum Mochi (best mochi in the district), Smiling Wok (longest-lived restaurant, extremely cheap, visitors never experience the same taste twice). A mysterious library stands near the center of the district — a tiny structure impossibly filled with scrolls, hangings, and written word of all kinds. Those who enter get the distinct impression the building could not possibly house all it does without magic.

Handcrafted map landmarks: Kyuden Kokai / Palace of Remorse (primary city prison, ~200 hajisherazu staff), magistrate station (garrison, 25 yoriki, 100+ doshin), Jumping Frog (okiya, cheap), gambling quarter (the Moon — notorious gambling houses), Governor’s residence.

##### Zone 9: Tenari’s Ruin (Meiyoko) District

Sentaku name: Meiyoko. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

Much like the Prison/Moon District, the Sentaku Tribunal keeps this region’s name hidden from the local populace. The name Tenari’s Ruin refers to the catastrophe of a long-dead governor. A brash young Yogo shugenja named Tenari governed the district and built a huge palatial estate in an enchanting grove at the southwestern edge, sinking untold thousands of koku into its construction. One year later, an earthquake ravaged the district, killing Tenari, his family, and his staff. The ruins remain to this day as testament to the folly of pride. The Tenari’s Ruin district covers the northeast corner of the Toshisoto, a sizeable region broken up into quarters, each quarter overseen by gokenin who face a constant struggle against the criminals who take refuge here between the North Dock and the Prison District.

Infrastructure: Garden (Hana Garden — iris flowers in Shintao rock gardens at the base of the governor’s hill, constructed in memory of the Battle of the Bloody Retreat, each stone bears the name of a fallen Scorpion who died fighting the Bloodspeakers).

Notable holdings: Tenari’s ruins (earthquake-destroyed palatial estate, heimin and samurai claim to hear the ghost of Tenari lamenting his foolish fate), statue of the ronin Kinryoku (protected a governor from assassination by throwing himself upon the killer’s blade; annual festival on the 14th day of the Serpent, no work is allowed). The modern governor’s estate stands atop high cliffs overlooking Golden Sun Bay and the entire district.

Handcrafted map landmarks: Tenari’s ruins (earthquake-destroyed palatial estate, haunted), Hana Garden (iris flowers, Shintao rock gardens, memorial), gokenin quarters (minor samurai retainers), criminal refuge areas, Governor’s residence (on the hill above the garden).

##### Zone 10: North Dock (Higshikawa) District

Sentaku name: Higshikawa. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

The North Dock District follows the northern bank of the River of the Sun as it becomes the Golden Sun Bay. Though small, North Dock plays a vital role in the economy of Otosan Uchi and boasts more pleasure houses per block than any other district. It is one of only two districts which deal with imports and shipping into the city. The roads of North Dock are often wet because of the heavy fogs and rains coming off the bay; the wealthier merchants have the major roads paved with gravel. Every residence and holding hangs large wood lanterns to guide travelers. North Dock tends to flood in the spring, and most houses along the waterway are built on stilts. Governors of North Dock funnel wealth into the district’s few unique holdings. The district stables and Imperial barracks maintain an entire kaisha (company) of the Imperial Guard and dozens of horses. Usually a Lion chui is given the responsibility of overseeing these forces, a tradition dating back centuries.

Infrastructure: Port/Harbor (Morning Star Wharves — three piers, can maintain up to thirty large kobune, warehouses typically full of Phoenix and Mantis goods, food, raw materials, and goods from across the Empire), Market (Trader’s Row — significant in size, lower-caste merchants, nearly any item can be found once), Inn (Endless Journey — old Agasha holding with a maze of shoji screens in a garden depicting tales of ancient times, the Fortunes, and heroes of old; Dragon replace the shoji screens each time it rains), Tea House (Iris Petal — governor’s private tea house), Sake House (White Stream Oolong — famous plum brandy, touted as the best beverage in the city), Stables (Imperial barracks stables — maintains a kaisha of the Imperial Guard).

Notable holdings: Takeo Library (Ikoma family backup, housing partial records of all the important events in the Emerald Empire’s history), Bochi Seido (Graveyard of Shrines — exotic mixture of shrines dedicated to old Fortunes, forgotten spirits, ancestors, and even Shinsei; two maintained shrines: one to Suitengu, Fortune of the Sea, and one to his wife Isora, Patron of the Shore), Gisei Ningen (Mortal Sacrifice — ambiguous statue worshipped by those who have lost a loved one), Maze of Wisdom (Togashi hedge maze, requires neither pruning nor care), Hameo’s House of Fish (sees highest yield of any fishery in the city), New Direction (offers travelers maps for Mantis and Crane coastal areas, some with a reputation for putting one in the way of coastal pirates), Time’s Gift (shop offering trinkets of silver, gold, and jade), Sweet Solace (candied breads and meat-filled pies). A stone Shintao temple is the tallest structure in the district; the Keeper rings the bell tower each hour in echo of the Spiritual District. The Keeper is blessed with secret knowledge of an old tunnel to the Temple of Eternal Redemption within the Ekohikei.

Handcrafted map landmarks: Morning Star Wharves (three piers, up to thirty large kobune), Takeo Library (Ikoma backup records), Imperial Guard kaisha (Lion-commanded barracks, centuries-old tradition), pleasure houses (sailor entertainment), Governor’s residence.

##### Zone 11: South Dock (Kosuga) District

Sentaku name: Kosuga. Zone type: CITY_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Toshisoto (open access).

South Dock District has significant influence over the myriad mercantile interests in Otosan Uchi. Merchants, traders, and moneylenders all come together each year to influence the value of the koku in the city and therefore the Empire. More than two-fifths of the city’s trade passes through the ports of Otosan Uchi, and nearly three-quarters of that comes through South Dock. It is the starting point of the commercial roadways in the Empire, the place where the flow of koku begins. South Dock borders the great Golden Sun Bay and most ship-bound travelers enter Otosan Uchi through its piers. The streets are much better maintained than in North Dock, and the district is dotted with houses of entertainment and rest. Some shops stay open quite late, and it is sometimes said that the South Docks never sleep. In certain eras, a sub-district called the Yatoshin exists, primarily made up of the huge warehouse neighborhood dominated by the Yotsu ronin family.

Infrastructure: Port/Harbor (primary trade port — handles 2/5 of the city’s total trade), Market (Flooded Merchant Bazaar — where any legal product in the city can be found; Yasuki Trading Grounds — center of merchant activity, can turn into a “merchant maze” of innumerable stalls), Forge (home of the Keeper of the Emperor’s Koku Seal — used to strike authentic koku during mintage, usually held by a member of the Miya family, protected by finest Imperial Legionnaires and Seppun wards), Sake House, Inn.

#### Ekohikei (Inner City) — 4 Zones

The four districts that make up the Inner City of Otosan Uchi have their people, establishments, and unique customs. Because the Ekohikei is so important and access is carefully restricted to the city’s elite, each of these districts is managed by its own Governor despite being far smaller than those in the Toshisoto. A posting as a Governor in the Ekohikei is an honor few ever attain.

##### Zone 12: Kanjo District

Zone type: EKOHIKEI_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Ekohikei (restricted access — requires ekohikei_access flag).

The southern district of the Inner City and the most populous of the four Ekohikei districts, since it is the main destination for those going to or from the Forbidden City. With hundreds, sometimes thousands, of visitors from all across the Empire coming and going each day, Kanjo is the busiest part of the Ekohikei. The seat of Kanjo government is a series of plain, almost spartan buildings facing the River of the Sun, housing administrative bureaucrats. One building is reserved for meetings of the Sentaku Tribunal. The Kanjo District takes special interest in the Festival of Triumph, celebrating the successful defense of Otosan Uchi against Fu Leng. The district also observes the Futatsu Shihai (“Two-Lord”) festival celebrating Shiba and Bayushi, the twin Kami who founded the Phoenix and Scorpion Clans.

The Southern Gate is surmounted by a massive fifteen-foot-tall torii arch crafted entirely out of jade and crystal. It is believed that the arch will glow in the presence of the Shadowlands Taint. The gate serves as the main artery for samurai to enter and leave the Ekohikei; all travelers must have permission from the Sentaku and must pass intense scrutiny from the gate guards (mostly Lion). The gate rests directly between the Kanjo District and the Chisei District, ensuring the nearby embassies of the Crane, Dragon, Lion, Phoenix, and Scorpion all have easy access.

Infrastructure: Embassy (Lion Embassy — two locations, the southernmost designed as a display of honor and martial prowess; Phoenix Embassy — modest building surrounded by a moat-like lake and beautiful gardens, one of the few places outside Kyuden Isawa where original materials from the Isawa Libraries are kept; Scorpion Embassy — the smallest of all embassies in the Ekohikei, and interestingly the most un-Scorpion building in the city, with no outer defenses, no moving walls, no secret passageways, and all rooms with windows to the outside — this is the one place where the Scorpion recognize that secrecy can only damage their reputation, so they have designed the building accordingly), Local Shrine (Temple of Eternal Redemption — managed by the Yogo family, officially dedicated to aiding the downtrodden lower caste, secretly a front for Scorpion activities within the Ekohikei), Garden, Inn (Inn of the Last Cherry Blossom — a pleasant inn open to all, famous for brews of tea from across the Empire).

Notable holdings: Sorrow’s Fall (a waterfall in the River of the Sun where a young Ikoma samurai-ko flung herself to death protecting dark secrets of the Hantei line; only a handful of Scorpion and Ikoma know the truth).

Handcrafted map landmarks: Lion Embassy south (martial display), Phoenix Embassy (arcane research), Scorpion Embassy (intelligence hub), Sorrow’s Fall (waterfall where Ikoma samurai-ko died), Sentaku Tribunal Hall, Imperial Treasury, Governor’s residence.

##### Zone 13: Chisei District

Zone type: EKOHIKEI_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Ekohikei (restricted access).

While the Kanjo District is where most visitors to the Inner City arrive and leave, the Chisei District is where they enjoy the highest life of the samurai. It is a place of beauty, art, and perfection, and only the bare necessary minimum of those from lower stations are allowed into the district at all. It is the location of the Road of the Most High, the special road on which the Emperor walks when he enters and leaves the Forbidden City. Duels of honor and even crimes have halted in the presence of the Emperor, and it is said that on more than one occasion he has passed judgement, ruled in favor of an offended party, or declared a marriage between two samurai on the spot as he was passing by. Because of heavy Crane influence, all festivals in Chisei are conducted with strict attention to proper custom, decorum, and etiquette. The largest and most important festival here is the Festival of Kakita. Due to the Crane influence, samurai from that clan are the traditional rulers of Chisei District.

Infrastructure: Embassy (Crane Embassy — the embodiment of all Crane ideals, expanded and renovated many times, one of the largest Story Gardens in all of Rokugan; Minor Clan Embassy — a great honor but very much overshadowed by the Crane, minor clans must share the same few buildings and are often cramped during times of high political activity; Phoenix Embassy — by contrast, a serene place that sees little activity), Garden (one of the largest Story Gardens in Rokugan, within the Crane Embassy grounds), Tea House (Field of Sharp Returns — not impressive in appearance but the tea served is unique and of truly legendary quality, brewed from a plant grown only in the garden surrounding the building), Dojo (Southern Guard Post — fortification atop the walls surrounding the Chisei District, highly defensible and difficult to infiltrate).

Notable holdings: Seppun Hill (the single most important landmark — the sacred site where Seppun first met the eight Kami and where Hantei won the tournament to become Emperor; it is the only piece of land in the entire city with no construction of any kind; its grandeur rivals any temple in the Empire), Road of the Most High (the Emperor’s personal road between the Forbidden City and the Outer City), Temple to the Seven Fortunes (contains small well-kept shrines to each of the Great Fortunes, plus a larger central temple where daily prayer services are conducted; bells ring each hour in time with those of the Temple District in the Toshisoto).

Handcrafted map landmarks: Seppun Hill (where Seppun met the eight Kami, Hantei crowned — most sacred site), Crane Embassy (largest Storyhouse in the Empire), Phoenix secondary embassy, Minor Clan embassy row, art galleries and performance halls, Governor’s residence.

##### Zone 14: Karada District

Zone type: EKOHIKEI_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Ekohikei (restricted access).

Each of the four Ekohikei districts boasts unique features that draw samurai to visit. The Karada district, however, is the least populated of the four, and its most significant feature is a sinister one — the massive earthquake crevice known as the Oni Warai (the Oni’s Smile). It is not a coincidence that the Crab embassy is located within this district. Though the Oni Warai does not pose any immediate or constant threat, few have returned alive from exploring its depths, and those who do are often blind or mad. Despite this, the Karada District is home to most of the lower caste residents of the Inner City — merchants, servants, laborers, and the like; the number of samurai residents is much lower than in the other three districts. The seat of Karada’s district government is probably the most run-down set of buildings in the Inner City. The Governors here are usually Crab and tend to be somewhat reclusive and anti-social.

Infrastructure: Embassy (Crab Embassy — the only major clan holding in the entire district, spartan and utilitarian, yet probably the most well-decorated Crab embassy in all of Rokugan since even they must be prepared to entertain important visitors when so close to the Emperor’s palace; Crab are the only clan with a significant presence in Karada), Local Shrine (Shinden Yaruki Jukko — a unique temple to the monastic tradition known as the Order of Courage’s Reflection, blending Shintao and Bushido, using mirrors as a means of meditative self-reflection), Library (Imperial Museum of Antiquities — houses many historical oddities and relics, including a mysterious creature “petrified” by the Museum’s original founder, Kuni Hazu).

Notable holdings: Oni Warai (the Oni’s Smile — massive earthquake crevice, few return alive from its depths), Yasuki Trading Grounds (extends from South Dock into this district, center of much merchant attention depending on the time of year, can turn into a merchant maze that lends itself to less honorable dealings when no magistrates are looking).

Handcrafted map landmarks: Crab Embassy (spartan, most defensible building in the Ekohikei), Oni Warai / the Oni’s Smile (massive earthquake crevice, deadly), Yasuki Trading Grounds (only commercial area in Ekohikei), lower-caste samurai residential quarters, Governor’s residence.

##### Zone 15: Hito District

Zone type: EKOHIKEI_DISTRICT (Navigation Zone). Layer: Ekohikei (restricted access).

The Hito District is a place where those within the Imperial City can feel safe — with constant patrols by the Lion and the Unicorn, as well as the tight security provided by the nearby Imperial Guards in the Forbidden City, it is one of the most crime-free regions in all of Otosan Uchi. The River of the Sun passes through this district, and much of the remaining space is taken up by the residences of samurai with sufficient rank to live here. Physically smaller than the other three Ekohikei districts. Festivals and ceremonies tend to focus on the river — the Bon Festival and the Festival of the River of Stars are especially popular. The Governor here is most commonly a Lion.

Infrastructure: Embassy (Lion Embassy second location — essentially a large, well-constructed barracks where soldiers who patrol the district are based and trained, standing midway between the Crab and Unicorn embassies as a sort of buffer; Unicorn Embassy — managed by the Ide family, uses mostly Rokugani architecture with only a few modest hints of gaijin influence; Fox Embassy — a separate embassy here because the Fox are descended from those of the Ki-Rin who remained in the Empire; the Fox Embassy serves as the base of operations for the Mondai Ketsu, a secretive last-resort organization that exacts justice and answers to the Emperor himself), Dojo (Imperial Training Grounds — just outside the Forbidden City, where the Ekohikei’s troops train alongside elite units such as the Empress’s Guard and the Seppun miharu), Local Shrine (Temple to the Kami — a unique holding with individual buildings dedicated to all five Elements, each shrine presenting challenges and puzzles that amaze and confuse visitors), Garden (Hito Water Gardens — huge maze-like water garden close to the River of the Sun, has grown considerably over the centuries).

Notable holdings: Road of Fast Hopes (one of two routes from the Forbidden City, exits through the Necessary Gate, used mainly by heimin servants and low-ranking samurai), Bridge of the Third Cut (crosses the River of the Sun from Hito to neighboring Kanjo District, named because the most dishonored and despairing samurai of the Ekohikei sometimes end their lives by jumping off it).

Handcrafted map landmarks: Lion Embassy second location (barracks, patrol base), Unicorn Embassy, Fox Embassy, Road of Fast Hopes (Forbidden City exit through Necessary Gate), samurai residential compounds (constant patrols), Governor’s residence.

#### Forbidden City — 1 Zone

##### Zone 16: Forbidden City

Zone type: FORBIDDEN_CITY (Navigation Zone). Layer: Forbidden City (highly restricted access — requires forbidden_city_access flag).

Located at the center of the Ekohikei, the Emperor’s personal lands are the site of the magnificent ten-story Imperial Palace, the scarcely less-impressive Otomo Palace, and smaller palaces used by the Seppun and Miya families. The grounds around these palaces contain beautiful gardens and a set of Guest Homes for housing important visitors to the Imperial presence — one for each of the Great Clans. The whole of the facility is surrounded by an irregular wall, low but well-defended, cutting it off from the rest of Otosan Uchi. Only two gates pierce this wall — the main gate where the Road of the Most High passes through, and the much smaller Necessary Gate that leads to the Road of Fast Hopes.

The Forbidden City is also home to the Shrine of the First Hantei. This shrine was erected in the first century by Hantei Genji to commemorate his father’s death from his wounds in the First War, and has ignored the passage of time completely, requiring no maintenance or reconstruction. A long bridge leads to the shrine, supported on the shoulders of statues of all the Emperors; as Emperors die and ascend into Tengoku, the bridge is redesigned to accommodate their statues. The shrine itself is quite small and simple — in fact, the main chamber is barely large enough for a single man to stand within.

### 2.3.24 Imperial Family Provinces

#### 2.3.24a Otomo Family Lands

The Otomo hold no provinces. This is deliberate — the family has maintained a policy of landlessness since its founding. The Otomo’s duty is to keep the Great Clans divided and focused on each other rather than on the Throne (Section 2.1.19). Governing territory would require retainers, armies, logistics, and all the administrative burdens that distract from that mission. The Otomo view land ownership as beneath their calling. They generate wealth through Imperial Treasury allocation, mercantile patronage, and artistic sponsorships — a revenue stream that requires no farmland, no tax collection, and no provincial governance. Despite controlling no territory, the Otomo maintain a lifestyle comparable to the wealthiest Great Clan families. Kyuden Otomo is located within the Forbidden City in Otosan Uchi (Section 2.3.23). No province entry exists for this family.

#### 2.3.24b Seppun Province

Of the three non-dynastic Imperial families, the Seppun hold the most extensive actual territory — though even so it amounts to only one sizable province. Seppun Province is located northeast of Otosan Uchi, stretching from the foothills north of the capital down to the shores of the Bay of the Golden Sun. It is one of the most lush and attractive plots of land in the Empire: wide verdant fields, rice paddies fed by many small streams prized for their quality and variety of fish, and gentle terrain that has been cultivated almost without interruption since the founding of the Empire. Wildlife survives only in certain sacred regions where no violence is permitted; otherwise, the entire province is tilled and maintained for one purpose or another of the Seppun family. The result is a province that is both beautiful and extremely quiet — only when Otosan Uchi itself is directly threatened does true violence reach Seppun lands.

The province is heavily populated, one of the most densely settled regions in Rokugan. The number of samurai retainers actually administering and defending it is low — considerably lower than the family could assign if it wished. The Seppun consider their sacred duty of defending the Emperor far more important than garrisoning their own lands, and they maintain the bulk of their soldiers within the Imperial capital at all times. This leaves the province underdefended by conventional standards, but the Seppun family’s close friendship with both the Lion and the Crane means its leaders seldom worry — both clans have interests tied closely to the Imperial Throne, and it is rare that they would refuse to send forces in the province’s defense. The sacred Grave of Seppun, one of the holiest sites in Rokugan, provides its own deterrent — it would take a desperate or foolish enemy to assault a region whose desecration would bring the wrath of the entire Empire.

Terrain: Flat Plains. Coastal: Yes (Bay of the Golden Sun). Rivers: multiple small streams throughout. Roads: major road SW to Otosan Uchi, road W to Yogasha Heigen (Emerald Champion’s Lands).

Adjacencies: Otosan Uchi (Imperial Capital) SW. Yogasha Heigen / Emerald Champion’s Lands (Imperial) W. Valley of the Centipede (Moshi, Centipede Clan) N [mountains between]. Additional adjacencies pending Atlas confirmation.

**Kyuden Seppun. **One might expect the personal defenders of the Imperial line to occupy a powerful military installation, but Kyuden Seppun is a sprawling estate of several conjoined smaller estates (yashiki), built over time by different Seppun daimyo and gradually fused together into a wondrous residence. It is built at the high point of a hill but lacks the high walls and fortified keep of a traditional castle. There are no parapets or walkways for guards — those who look closely will note small peg holes lining the upper walls, allowing the Miya’s vassal family, the Anou, to insert wooden platforms and create makeshift defenses if needed, but these have never been tested by anything greater than occasional banditry. The estate is serene, reverent, and quiet — a place where violence is almost unheard of and guests are treated to an experience that leaves even the most worldly visitors speaking glowingly of their time. The Seppun are a deeply religious family with equal roles as priests and soldiers, and Kyuden Seppun reflects that — a place that calls first on its roots in the priesthood. **The Four Temples. **A quartet of temples surrounding Kyuden Seppun: Daikandaiji, Yakushiji, Asukadera, and Kawaradera. Infrastructure type: Town (Machi) per Section 2.3.1, Coastal, with Port/Harbor per Section 2.3.2.

#### 2.3.24c Imperial Lands — Hub Villages

The four Hub Villages are located around Otosan Uchi, and while they are technically not within the Seppun Province, for most of history they are protected by the Seppun in the same manner as the Imperial capital. These four large villages — in truth, closer to small cities — serve both as places for excess samurai to live and as sources of specialized heimin labor for the capital’s needs. Because Otosan Uchi itself cannot grow beyond its outer walls and thus often has insufficient housing for all those who visit, many samurai acquire estates in one or another of the Hub Villages. Much of the Imperial bureaucracy lives here, and when the Emerald Champion’s magistrates bring in taxes in the fall, they are counted in the Hub Villages. In effect, these towns are the little capitals of the Empire, where those who do not have access to the Emperor’s Forbidden City can still meet to negotiate and trade. The Hub Villages are well-populated and heavily trafficked, known both for their many wealthy samurai and for their numerous heimin artisans, craftsmen, and shipwrights.

#### 2.3.24d Yogasha Heigen (Emerald Champion’s Lands)

The lands north and west of Otosan Uchi, beyond the Seppun Province, are assigned to the Emerald Champion — the first and most revered of the Jeweled Champions. The territory is dominated by the Yogasha Heigen, a wide empty plain surrounding the Castle of the Emerald Champion. The plains serve a strategic purpose: the flat, open terrain allows any advancing army to be spotted days in advance. No Rokugani army ever dares to threaten its borders, since all daimyo know that attacking the Emerald Champion’s personal fortress would be an invitation for all the other clans to unite against the aggressor.

The province is small and sparsely populated. It exists not as a productive economic territory but as the institutional seat of Imperial law enforcement. The Emerald Champion’s personal garrison is modest — the castle’s protection comes from its political inviolability rather than its military strength.

Terrain: Flat Plains. Coastal: No. Rivers: none noted. Roads: road SE to Hub Villages and Otosan Uchi.

Adjacencies: Hub Villages (Imperial) SE. Seppun Province (Imperial, Seppun family) E. Additional adjacencies pending Atlas confirmation.

**Shiro Yogasha (Castle of the Emerald Champion). **The castle sits on a high hill surrounded by the wide plains, allowing unobstructed sight lines in all directions. Despite its strategic position, the castle itself is a luxurious and sprawling estate with only modest fortifications — the Imperial Chrysanthemum displayed at the front gate is considered protection enough. Emerald Champion Yasuki Hachi orders defensive walls constructed around the castle and builds several lookout towers on the plains, providing the estate its first real military infrastructure. The castle houses the Emerald Champion’s personal court, administrative offices, and quarters for visiting Emerald Magistrates. Infrastructure type: Castle (Shiro) per Section 2.3.1.

**Ruby Dojo. **The Ruby Champion — master sensei of the Emerald Magistrates and the Imperial Legions — builds a small estate adjoining the Emerald Champion’s castle. This serves as the primary training facility for Emerald Magistrates and the sensei who will train the bushi serving the Empire. The Ruby Champion maintains roughly a dozen dojo throughout Imperial holdings, but this estate adjoining Shiro Yogasha is the largest. Infrastructure type: Dojo per Section 2.3.2. Cross-reference: Section 2.1.18 (Ruby Champion).

#### 2.3.24e Miya Province

In contrast to the religious significance of the Seppun and the wealth and influence of the Otomo, the Miya inhabit lands which are modest in size, poor in wealth, and relegated to a distant part of the Empire — almost as if the Emperor were embarrassed by the youngest and smallest Imperial family. Nothing could be further from the truth. The Miya chose their lands for themselves, selecting the humble region as a reminder of their duty and their family motto: “There are no Miya heroes.” That this forces them to travel long distances to fulfill their duties is an extra challenge they happily accept.

The lone province of the Miya is nestled against the foot of the Spine of the World Mountains, a place of rolling foothills that give way to wide open plains. Little of this land has been properly cultivated, and much of it is given over to natural beauty — trickling streams and waterfalls in the mountains, lightly-wooded forests in the hills, and swaying grasslands and wildflowers in the plains. A few farming villages are found scattered around the province, most of them located near Kyuden Miya itself; they are called Genmai Mura, or Brown-Rice Villages, a nod to the Miya’s embrace of commonality.

Terrain: Hills (foothills of the Spine of the World Mountains transitioning to plains). Coastal: No. Rivers: multiple streams and waterfalls in the mountain foothills. Roads: road connections to Unicorn territory.

Adjacencies: Eijitsu (Ide, Unicorn) W. Kaihi (Iuchi, Unicorn) E.

**Kyuden Miya. **The Miya castle is actually quite humble despite belonging to an Imperial family, and in truth the title of “Kyuden” is as much a gesture of respect as an assessment of the family’s ability to host the Emperor’s Winter Court. The castle itself is only three stories tall, a broad-based and squat keep in a solid style more akin to the Crab than the tall grace of the Crane or the Otomo. A second small castle, reserved for the Emperor and his travelling companions if they should ever visit, stands to the northeast of the main keep, still within the same walled courtyard and with the best view and the most protection. Unofficially known as “Kyuden Hantei,” this second castle is opulent and elegant, draped in wealth and works of art, and stays empty for decades at a time, save for the handful of Miya caretakers charged with its upkeep. The walls of Kyuden Miya are of stone, thick enough to repel attacking legions, but have never been tested by anything greater than occasional banditry. On either side of the castle are shrines: the Eastern Temple, dedicated to the Sun, and the Western Temple, dedicated to the Moon. The castle has one room set aside for each of six Great Clans at Winter Court (Crab, Crane, Dragon, Lion, Phoenix, and Scorpion) but has never added a place for the children of Shinjo after the return of the Unicorn Clan. Infrastructure type: Family Castle (Kyuden) per Section 2.3.1.

**The Miya Library. **The third-largest library in the Empire, behind the Isawa of the Phoenix and the Ikoma of the Lion. This library is nearly devoid of any writing by the Miya themselves, a further reinforcement of the family motto. Instead, the Miya Library is dedicated to everyone else, serving as a central repository for the heraldry and mon of every samurai in Rokugan, alive or dead. The Miya Library contains three specialized archives: the Archive of the Emperor (every Imperial proclamation), the Archive of Winter Court (guest lists, contest winners, treaties), and the Dead-Letter Archive (undelivered letters stored until the original recipient can claim them). Infrastructure type: Library per Section 2.3.2.

**The Miya Stables. **The largest horse-breeding area outside the Unicorn lands. After the return of the Unicorn, the Miya have cross-bred Unicorn riding horses with their own Rokugani ponies to create a unique breed of Miya ponies known for their swiftness and endurance. The Miya ponies are used to deliver emergency messages and to convey the Emperor’s annual decrees far and wide. They are exclusively property of the Miya and are never sold or given to any other Great Clan. Infrastructure type: Stables per Section 2.3.2.

**The Anou Lands. **The industrial region of the Miya, home to logging villages, stone quarries (including a unique pink marble quarry), and the five Disaster Villages used to train Miya in construction and rebuilding techniques. Earthquake Village (northernmost, destroyed by landslide), Wildfire Village (westernmost, burned down), Flooded Village (southernmost, flooded yearly), Tornado Village (easternmost, blown down by a nemuranai fan), and Plague Village (central, never destroyed, used for crisis management training with simulated plague, famine, and civil unrest). Infrastructure type: Village (Mura) per Section 2.3.1 for each Disaster Village.

### 2.3.25 Unaligned Territories

#### 2.3.25a City of the Rich Frog (Toshi sano Kanemochi Kaeru)

The settlement known as Toshi sano Kanemochi Kaeru is an independent ronin city for most of its history. Legend claims it is founded by a mischievous spirit-frog from Chikushudo, but other historical sources claim it is built by the Lion Clan during the centuries that they rule the lands of the departed Ki-Rin Clan. The city lies just north of the Drowned Merchant River, in lands close to the territory of the Lion, Dragon, and Unicorn but usually not claimed by any of them. For most of its history the city is independent, ruled by a secretive ronin family calling themselves Kaeru. An Imperial Magistrate is assigned to uphold law and collect taxes, and the Kaeru create their own militia, an otokodate called the Machi-Kanshisha, to serve as yoriki for the magistrate. The city earns a well-founded reputation for being open to any business possibility, including smuggled goods, dishonorable services, gaijin artifacts, and even drugs such as opium, all with the knowledge and tacit approval of the Kaeru family.

Terrain: Flat Plains. Coastal: No. Rivers: Drowned Merchant River (southern boundary). Roads: trade roads radiating to Lion, Unicorn, and Dragon territories.

Adjacencies: Kaihi (Iuchi, Unicorn) E. Shinten (Iuchi, Unicorn) SE. Ikoku (Moto, Unicorn) NE. Gisei (Ikoma, Lion) W. Shiranai (Ikoma, Lion) SW.

Governance: Independent. The Kaeru family rules as de facto local lords without formal clan or Imperial appointment. An Imperial Magistrate is permanently assigned. The Machi-Kanshisha otokodate serves as the city’s law enforcement under the magistrate’s authority. Taxes are collected and remitted directly to the Emperor through the Imperial Magistrate — no clan intermediary in the tax cascade.

Infrastructure type: City (Toshi) per Section 2.3.1. Infrastructure: Market/Marketplace, Inn, Sake House, Tea House per Section 2.3.2.

#### 2.3.25b Nanashi Mura (Nameless Village)

Nameless Village is located along but just outside the southern border of Dragon lands, roughly halfway between the City of the Rich Frog and Kyuden Tonbo. The village is founded in the late eleventh century by a ronin named Fuyari after he and his band are turned from evil deeds by a chance encounter with a mysterious hooded figure. Togashi Yokuni, Champion of the Dragon Clan, gives Fuyari permission to found a village solely for ronin, ruled by them under the distant protection of the Dragon — so long as they never build military defenses. The land is not well suited to farming — most food is imported, which gives the Dragon ultimate control. The town is divided into three loosely defined districts: Broken Wing (Crane/Phoenix ronin, veneer of civility), Bushi Row (Lion/Crab ronin, martial), and Highway’s End (disreputable). The Nanashi Dojo teaches the techniques developed by the Eyes of Nanashi, the village’s guardians. A shrine to Ebisu, the Fortune of Honest Work, dominates the settlement.

Terrain: Hills. Coastal: No. Roads: road N to Sinjutsu (Dragon), road SW to City of the Rich Frog.

Adjacencies: Sinjutsu (Kitsuki, Dragon) N [road]. City of the Rich Frog (unaligned) SW. Dragonfly Clan Lands (Tonbo, Dragonfly) S [pending confirmation]. Additional adjacencies pending Atlas confirmation.

Governance: Independent ronin settlement under Dragon Clan protection. No formal lord. The Dragon exercise indirect control through food supply dependency. No military fortifications permitted per the founding agreement with Togashi Yokuni. Taxes remitted to the Dragon.

Infrastructure type: Town (Machi) per Section 2.3.1. Infrastructure: Dojo (Nanashi Dojo), Shrine (Ebisu), Inn, Sake House, Market/Marketplace per Section 2.3.2.

#### 2.3.25c Koeru Mura

A small fortified village located between the Scorpion and Wasp lands, granted Imperial authority to govern itself. A governor is chosen from the leading families of the surrounding Scorpion and Wasp lands, and rules the town and surrounding territory with the help of a handful of ronin guards. The most prominent local shrine is dedicated to Inari, the Fortune of Rice, and the village is surrounded by fertile rice paddies and grain fields. The excess of each year’s harvest is sold to the highest bidders among the Minor Clans. In the mid-twelfth century, the Koeru Mura Dojo is established here as the training center for the army of the Minor Clan Alliance. A mixed force of Minor Clan samurai is permanently stationed, assuming responsibility for protecting the village.

Terrain: Flat Plains. Coastal: No. Rivers: River of Gold nearby. Roads: roads connecting to Scorpion and Wasp territories.

Adjacencies: Nezuban (Bayushi, Scorpion) N. Chuuou (Bayushi, Scorpion) W. Ashinagabachi (Tsuruchi, Wasp) E. Chuuhan (Tsuruchi, Wasp) S.

Governance: Independent under Imperial charter. Governor chosen from leading Scorpion and Wasp families. Minor Clan Alliance military presence provides de facto security authority.

Infrastructure type: Village (Mura) per Section 2.3.1. Infrastructure: Dojo (Minor Clan Alliance training center), Shrine (Inari), Garrison/Barracks, Market/Marketplace per Section 2.3.2.

#### 2.3.25d Zakyo Toshi (Pleasure City)

Pleasure City is founded as a trade city in the latter half of the seventh century, using its place on the Small Trade River, a tributary of the River of Gold, to act as a staging ground for goods moving to or from the Scorpion lands. Its position near the borders of the Scorpion, Fox, and Hare clans allows Zakyo Toshi to serve as a hub for illicit trade as well. For most of its existence it is ruled by a merchant council who are responsible for seeing that taxes find their way to the Emperor’s coffers — usually through a friendly Otomo intermediary who ensures the city keeps its nominal independence. The city is justly infamous for the criminal gangs who operate here: the Bawdy Carp (prostitution and gambling, identified by fish/water tattoos), the Silent Canary (smuggling and moneylending), and the Brotherhood of Autumn (members pose as monks, opium and illegal substances).

Terrain: Flat Plains. Coastal: No. Rivers: Small Trade River (tributary of the River of Gold), River of Gold (eastern boundary — no crossing). Roads: trade road N to Kawa (Scorpion), road S toward Hare Clan Lands.

Adjacencies: Kawa (Shosuro, Scorpion) N. Hare Clan Lands (Usagi, Hare) S.

Governance: Independent. Ruled by a merchant council with an Otomo intermediary ensuring nominal Imperial oversight. Taxes remitted directly to the Emperor — no clan intermediary in the tax cascade.

Infrastructure type: City (Toshi) per Section 2.3.1. Infrastructure: Market/Marketplace, Inn, Sake House, Tea House, Okiya per Section 2.3.2.

#### 2.3.25e Notable Holdings Within Existing Provinces

**Daidoji Yukan-se (The Valiant Daidoji Tower). **Host province: Douro (Tsuruchi, Wasp). A small keep held by ronin whose ancestors were once Crane Clan samurai. Originally an outlying Scorpion holding, the tower is captured by the Crane at some point in the Empire’s history, but some undefined sin or vice among the samurai stationed here results in their being exiled from the clan. The ronin who remain hold the tower, hunt bandits in the surrounding lands, and hope for the day they can rejoin the Crane — so far, to no avail. Infrastructure type: Keep (Torid-e) per Section 2.3.1.

### 2.3.26 Brotherhood of Shinsei Holdings

Monasteries and temples of the Brotherhood of Shinsei are not provinces — they are religious infrastructure scattered across clan territories. This section catalogs each holding with its host province, infrastructure type, and lore summary. Cross-references: worship system (Section 4.3.21), Brotherhood monk schools (Section 29.13), Fortune system (Section 2.5).

**Dangai. **Host province: pending confirmation (between Crane and Scorpion borders). A small monastery built in a barren section of land, primarily a library. The dry, silent, remote region is well-suited for the storage of scrolls, and Dangai is considered to have some of the earliest known editions of the Tao of Shinsei. Known for the serenity of its rock gardens. Pilgrims seeking quiet contemplation visit regularly. Infrastructure type: Monastery (Souiin) per Section 2.3.1.

**Monastery Among the Winds. **Unaligned territory between Kaihi (Iuchi, Unicorn) and Kinbou (Scorpion). Founded in the fifth century, the monastery stands in a small valley near a minor pass through the Spine of the World Mountains. The pass is sometimes used by pilgrims and messengers but is too small to allow the movement of large armies. The monastery makes the best use of the surrounding area, with terraced farms on the slopes and garden plots within the monastery walls. It serves as a retirement home for Seppun miharu and members of the Imperial Legions, who spend their remaining years training Brotherhood sohei to help defend the Empire. In violent eras, the monastery also finds use as a hospital and site of convalescence for wounded samurai. Infrastructure type: Monastery (Souiin) per Section 2.3.1. Infrastructure: Dojo (sohei training) per Section 2.3.2.

**Quiet Stone Monastery. **Host province: Ox Clan Lands (Section 2.3.20). Already documented in that section. Cross-reference only.

**Reihado Shinsei (Shrine of Shinsei / Crow Shrine). **No fixed location. The most famous and most mysterious shrine in Rokugan. Few can claim to have visited it. The shrine’s secondary name derives from Shinsei’s constant companion — a white crow that was forever blackened by the forces unleashed when the Thunders fought the Dark One. Tended by a handful of monks, some rumored to be shapeshifting Kenku. It is said that only those of pure heart can find the shrine. The exact location is uncertain and seems to change from one era to another. Infrastructure type: Shrine (unique, sacred). Dynamic Placement: placed by the engine in a randomly selected province at world generation. Position is fixed for the duration of the game. Discovery Prerequisites — PROVISIONAL: Discovery type is per-character. The shrine exists at its fixed location but is only perceptible to characters who meet all of the following conditions simultaneously: Honor 9.0 or above, AND Taint Rank 0 (no Shadowlands Taint whatsoever). A qualifying character who enters the shrine’s province receives a notification that they sense the shrine’s presence. The shrine becomes visible on that character’s local interface and they may visit it. Characters who do not qualify see nothing — the province appears normal. If a character’s Honor drops below 9.0 or they acquire any Taint after having discovered the shrine, they lose access — the shrine becomes invisible to them again until they re-qualify. No information pathway exists — no Lore roll, conversation, or topic reveals the shrine’s province. The only way to find Reihado Shinsei is to be in the right province while qualifying. This is intentional — “only those of pure heart can find the shrine” is a spiritual test, not a research problem.

**Shrine of the Seven Thunders. **No fixed location — placed at the base of a mountain in a randomly selected province with Mountains terrain. Located in a cave in the mountainside, this is actually seven shrines dedicated to the seven brave souls who faced down the Dark Kami. Hidden within the monastery is the Library of the Thunders — firsthand accounts from those who knew the Thunders personally. The monks each choose one shrine to tend exclusively. A seasonal tent city of trinket sellers and pilgrim lodgings waxes and wanes at the base of the mountain path. Infrastructure type: Monastery (Souiin) per Section 2.3.1. Dynamic Placement: placed by the engine at world generation in a randomly selected province with Mountains terrain. Position is fixed for the duration of the game. Discovery Prerequisites — PROVISIONAL: Discovery type is permanent — once any character discovers the shrine, it becomes a known location on the world map for all characters. Two discovery pathways exist. Information pathway: a character may learn of the shrine’s province through Lore: History / Intelligence at TN 25 (PROVISIONAL) or Lore: Theology / Intelligence at TN 20 (PROVISIONAL). Success reveals the province name. The character (or anyone they share this information with via conversation or letter) may then travel to the province. Exploration pathway: a character who enters the shrine’s province without prior information may discover it through Investigation (Notice) / Perception at TN 20 (PROVISIONAL) — representing noticing the pilgrim path, the tent city, or the cave entrance in the mountainside. This roll is attempted once automatically when a character enters the province. Monks and shugenja receive a +5 bonus to this roll (spiritual sensitivity to sacred sites). Upon discovery, the shrine is permanently revealed on the world map. The seasonal tent city of trinket sellers and pilgrim lodgings becomes a visitable location. The Library of the Thunders within the monastery becomes accessible.

**Shinden Osano-Wo. **Host province: Kinbou (Scorpion), southwestern side. The great Temple of Osano-Wo is the largest facility dedicated to the Fortune of Fire and Thunder in the Empire. Founded at the end of the third century, two hundred years to the day after Hida Osano-Wo’s death. The temple is constructed from limestone and looks as much like a fort as a temple. The roof is pierced with Kiau-made lightning rods. In front of the temple is a huge limestone maze filled with hazards (geysers, sharp stones) — a training ground for the monks. A special room is always kept ready for the Emerald Champion, who is viewed as blessed by Osano-Wo. The Test of the Emerald Champion is relocated here in the tenth century, with the abbot serving as main judge. The Order of Osano-Wo is the most militant and worldly of the Brotherhood sects. Infrastructure type: Temple (Tera) per Section 2.3.1. Infrastructure: Dojo (Order of Osano-Wo martial training), Garden (limestone maze training ground) per Section 2.3.2.
