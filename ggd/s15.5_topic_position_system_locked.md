## 15.5 Topic Position System — LOCKED

Every named character has a personal position on every topic they are aware of. This position is a number from −100 to +100, mirroring the Disposition scale. It represents where the character stands on a given matter — how much they support or oppose it. Position is distinct from personal relevance (Section 16.4), which measures how much a character cares. Position measures where they stand. A character can have high relevance on a topic and still be at position 0 — they care deeply but are genuinely undecided.

**Position Scale — LOCKED:**

- +50 to +100: YES — the character actively supports this topic or action. Will publicly commit if it is an action topic.

- +1 to +49: Leaning Yes — receptive but not yet committed. Can be pushed to commitment through court action.

- 0: Neutral — no initial bias. Either no anchors exist or they cancel out.

- −1 to −49: Leaning No — resistant. Requires significant effort to move toward commitment.

- −50 to −100: NO — the character actively opposes. Will work against this topic or action at court.

**Two Topic Types — LOCKED:**

Opinion Topics are events or situations that characters have positions on but are not being asked to act on directly. A duel between two samurai. A harvest failure in a distant province. A political appointment. Characters form positions on these topics organically — through disposition anchors, personality, and proximity. Opinion topics can spawn action topics.

Action Topics are specific decisions requiring a commitment. 'Join the coalition to send Rice to Region C.' 'Accept the ceasefire terms.' A character whose position on an action topic crosses +50 will publicly commit — triggering a real-world game action. Crossing −50 means active opposition — they will spend action points working against it.

**Topic Spawning — LOCKED:**

Opinion topics generate action topics. A famine in Region C (opinion topic) spawns multiple parallel action topics: 'The Crab will send Rice alone,' 'Join a court-decided coalition,' 'Do nothing.' Each action topic is a separate position calculation. A character's position on the parent opinion topic influences their starting position on child action topics — but each child calculates independently. The parent is a bias, not a transfer.

This means a character can hold conflicting positions across child topics spawned by the same parent. They may be at +60 on 'Crab sends Rice alone' (they dislike the Crab and want to burden them) and at −20 on 'Join the coalition' (they don't want to commit their own resources). Both positions are valid simultaneously.

**Starting Position Calculation — LOCKED:**

A character's starting position on a topic is calculated automatically from three inputs before any court action occurs.

Input 1 — Disposition Anchors: The topic involves named parties. The character's disposition toward each party contributes to their starting position. Disposition values are scaled at half value for this purpose — a +60 disposition toward a party contributes +30 to starting position. Multiple parties stack. Direction matters — positive disposition toward the benefiting party pushes position positive; positive disposition toward the harmed party pushes position negative. Crucially, negative disposition toward a party can push position in unexpected directions. A character who dislikes the Crab may start positive on 'Crab sends Rice' — because it burdens their rival. The calculation always asks: what outcome does this topic represent for each party involved, and how do I feel about those parties?

Input 2 — Personality Modifier: The character’s Bushido and Shourido virtues apply flat modifiers based on topic type — the instinctive lean a character has before personal relationships factor in. A Jin (Compassion) character receives a negative modifier on suffering topics and a positive modifier on relief topics regardless of who is involved. Seigyo (Control) reacts positively to power-shift and espionage topics. Each virtue fires only on the topic types it genuinely has a lean on — most virtue×topic pairings are 0. These modifiers apply before the anchors are summed. See Section 15.6 for the full virtue-to-topic-type modifier table covering all topic types across all tiers.

Input 3 — Personal Relevance as Amplifier: Personal relevance (Section 16.4) does not add to position directly. It amplifies whatever the other inputs produced. High relevance makes existing biases stronger — a character deeply affected by a famine will hold their position more firmly, be harder to move by court action, and more likely to spend action points on it. Low relevance means the character is present but largely passive — their position is weaker and easier to shift.

Formula: Starting Position = (Disposition Anchor Sum × 0.5) + Personality Modifier, then amplified by Personal Relevance. Capped at ±100.

**Position Movement — LOCKED:**

Court actions move position independently from disposition. The same roll produces both a disposition delta and a position delta, but at different magnitudes depending on the action type. The ratio reflects what each action actually does: Charm changes feelings without changing minds. Persuade changes minds deeply. Public Debate changes minds publicly across the room.

Position movement values per action on success:

- Charm: 0 position movement. Disposition gain only.

- Impress: +5 position. Also reduces TN on next action toward this person this session.

- Negotiate: +8 position. Per Raise declared: +4 additional position.

- Listen and Reflect: +10 position. Per Raise declared: +4 additional position. Also opens target to reduced TN on subsequent Persuade this session.

- Persuade: +12 position. Per Raise declared: +5 additional position. Personality mismatch can negate entirely — appealing to the wrong values produces no movement. A position moved by Persuade is more resistant to being moved back than one moved by Negotiate.

- Intimidate: +10 position (compliance-tracked separately from genuine position). Per Raise: +3. Compliance is not the same as genuine agreement — a character who was intimidated into position may reverse when pressure is removed. See compliance tracker design (Section 12.9).

Public Debate position shift per witness, based on combined score (roll differential + disposition tier modifiers):

- Score +1 to +2: +4 position shift toward winner's side per witness.

- Score +3 to +4: +8 position shift per witness.

- Score +5 to +6: +12 position shift per witness.

- Score +7 or more: +16 position shift per witness.

Position movement per action is deliberately sized so that moving a character from 0 to the +50 commitment threshold requires 4 to 6 successful targeted actions across a court session — achievable for a focused courtier but requiring sustained effort and good rolls, especially against high-relevance characters who resist movement more firmly.

**Commitment Threshold and Declaration — LOCKED:**

A character whose position on an action topic crosses +50 does not automatically commit. Commitment requires a Public Declaration action (Category 2). A character can be at +70 internally without having committed publicly — they support the action but have not yet spoken. The courtier's job includes not just moving positions but triggering the declarations that make commitments binding.

Once declared, the commitment is binding. Reneging triggers Honor loss and a permanent historical disposition modifier with all who witnessed the declaration.

A character at −50 or below on an action topic will actively oppose it at court — spending their own action points to move others away from commitment and potentially making a Public Declaration of opposition.

**Individual Commitments Only — LOCKED:**

Court produces no collective mandate. The outcome of any court session on any topic is the aggregate of what each lord individually crossed +50 on and publicly declared. No lord can be forced to commit except by Imperial Edict. A lord who never crosses +50 on any action topic simply does not commit — their inaction has disposition and Honor consequences but is not mechanically overridden.

Status weighting (a Clan Champion's position carrying more weight than a Local Daimyo's) applies in two specific contexts only: NPC decision-making, where a character reading the room is more influenced by high-Status opinions when deciding whether to commit; and the Emperor at Winter Court, where aggregate weighted positions inform what Imperial Edicts are issued.
