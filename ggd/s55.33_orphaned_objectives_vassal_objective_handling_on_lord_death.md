## 55.33 Orphaned Objectives — Vassal Objective Handling on Lord Death — LOCKED

STATUS: LOCKED

Section 22.5 handles succession — position vacancy, heir selection, confirmation authority. It does not address what happens to the dead lord’s vassal objectives. When a lord dies, their vassals may hold primary objectives assigned by that lord. Some objectives reference the dead lord directly (political missions assigned for the lord’s strategic benefit). Others target independent conditions that persist regardless of who assigned them (vengeance debts, province governance). This section defines how vassal objectives are evaluated, paused, or continued when the assigning lord dies.

Cross-references: Section 22.5 (Character Death and Replacement — Succession), Section 55.4.2 (Phase 2 — Priority Cascade), Section 55.6 (Information Transfer on Objective Assignment), Section 55.10 (Strategic Layer — Lord-Tier Decisions), Section 55.26 (Primary Objective Selection), Section 20 (Known Information).

### 55.33.1 Knowledge Delay — Section 20 Compliance

A vassal does not know their lord is dead until the information reaches them through legitimate channels. Lord death generates a Tier 2 topic: "[Lord Name] has died." This topic propagates through normal channels — characters present at the death learn immediately (FRESH confidence), others learn through letters, daily conversation, or court gossip. Delivery time varies by distance and information channel (Section 12.7 for letter delivery, Section 12.6 for daily conversation range).

Until the death topic enters the vassal’s knowledge pool, their objective remains ACTIVE. They continue pursuing it with full conviction. A vassal on campaign two provinces away may not learn for 1-2 seasons. During that time, they may complete the objective — conquering a province, exposing a secret, winning a duel — all on behalf of a dead lord. This is correct and intentional. The vassal acted on the last orders they received. The consequences of information delay create gameplay: a war started by a dead lord’s orders that the new lord must manage, a political operation completed after the strategic context changed.

### 55.33.2 Objective Validity Check

When the lord death topic enters a vassal’s knowledge pool, the engine runs an objective validity check on their active primary objective. The check evaluates whether the objective’s fulfillment condition still references something that exists and makes sense without the assigning lord.

**Lord-dependent objectives: **The objective’s completion condition, target, or strategic rationale directly references the dead lord or their authority. These are invalidated because the reason for the mission died with the person who ordered it. The objective status changes to ORPHANED. Examples: "Gain a Winter Court invitation for my lord" — fulfillment target is the dead lord, cannot be met. "Conquer Province X" — strategic war decision by the lord, needs reconfirmation from successor. "Have Character X appointed to Position" — lord’s political strategy, needs reconfirmation. "Resolve Clan War crisis through negotiation" — lord’s diplomatic mandate, needs reconfirmation. "Obtain an Imperial Edict on topic" — lord’s policy goal, needs reconfirmation.

**Target-dependent objectives: **The objective targets a person, place, or condition that exists independently of the dead lord. These persist as ACTIVE. The honor debt, the secret, the province — none of these changed because the lord died. Examples: "Avenge death of Togashi Haru" — the target is still alive, the debt still exists. "Expose Character X’s secret publicly" — the secret is still a secret. "Increase Koku output of Province X" — the province still needs governance. "Sabotage Clan X’s economic output" — the strategic context may have shifted but the target clan still exists.

**The distinction: **If you replace "my lord" in the objective text and it still parses, the objective is target-dependent and persists. If it breaks, the objective is lord-dependent and orphans.

### 55.33.3 Lord-Dependency Classification

Each objective type carries a lord_dependent flag in its definition. The 12 deep-dive objective types (Section 55.28) are classified as follows:

Lord-dependent (ORPHANS on lord death): "Break the alliance between X and Y" — strategic decision. "Isolate Character X politically" — strategic targeting. "Gain a Winter Court invitation for my lord" — literally references the lord. "Have Character X appointed to Position" — political strategy. "Remove Character X from Position" — political strategy. "Resolve Clan War crisis through negotiation" — diplomatic mandate. "Obtain an Imperial Edict on topic" — policy goal. "Conquer Province X" — war decision. "Sabotage Clan X’s economic output" — strategic hostility.

Target-dependent (PERSISTS on lord death): "Expose Character X’s secret publicly" — target exists independently. "Increase Koku output of Province X" — governance task, province unchanged. "Avenge Death/Disgrace of Character X" — personal honor debt, survives any lord.

Ratio: 9 lord-dependent, 3 target-dependent. This reflects the feudal reality — most missions a samurai undertakes are in service to their lord’s strategy, not personal quests. When the lord dies, the strategy dies. Personal debts endure.

### 55.33.4 ORPHANED Objective Status

When an objective’s status changes to ORPHANED, the decomposition recognizes this status and generates a new ImmediateNeed: REPORT_TO_NEW_LORD. This NeedType routes to SEND_LETTER or TRAVEL_TO targeting the confirmed successor (if known) or the next authority in the chain of command (if the successor is not yet confirmed).

The vassal’s report carries the objective details through Information Transfer (Section 55.6): "Lord Akodo Ren assigned me to conquer Province Beiden. He is dead. I await your orders." The letter includes: the objective type, target fields, current progress (from the progress function, Section 55.29.3), and the vassal’s current location and status.

Until the vassal reaches the new lord or receives a response, they fall back to their standing objective. They do not continue pursuing a potentially unwanted war. They do not sit idle — the standing objective keeps them productive. A vassal with standing objective "Protect those under my care" patrols their province. A vassal with "Advance my family name" seeks Glory at court. The primary objective slot holds the ORPHANED objective but the decomposition skips it in the priority cascade, treating it as suspended pending reconfirmation.

If the successor has not been confirmed (Section 22.5 confirmation process is still in progress), the vassal sends the report to the next authority up — the Family Daimyo or Clan Champion. The chain of command always has someone to report to, even during a succession crisis. If the entire chain is broken (cascading death — the Provincial Daimyo and Family Daimyo both killed in the same battle), the vassal reports to the Clan Champion. If the Clan Champion is also dead, the report goes to the highest-Status surviving character in the clan. There is always someone.

### 55.33.5 New Lord’s Response — Strategic Review Integration

The new lord’s first Strategic Review (Section 55.10) after succession includes an automatic scan: "Do I have vassals with ORPHANED objectives awaiting reconfirmation?" For each orphaned objective, the new lord evaluates three options:

**Confirm: **The new lord agrees with the mission. The objective status returns to ACTIVE. The vassal receives a letter: "Continue as ordered." The objective’s assigning_lord_id updates to the new lord. Progress and travel_redirects counters are preserved — no reset, because the mission is the same mission. This is the fastest resolution — one letter, no travel required.

**Modify: **The new lord changes the parameters. "Don’t conquer Beiden. Defend our border instead." A new objective replaces the orphaned one through standard ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE with Information Transfer. The orphaned objective is removed. The vassal receives the new objective and begins decomposition from scratch. Progress counters reset — this is a new mission.

**Cancel: **The new lord does not want this mission. The objective is removed from the vassal’s primary slot. The vassal returns to their standing objective until the next Strategic Review assigns a new primary. If the vassal has an army deployed for the cancelled objective, the new lord should also issue RECALL_ARMY or REASSIGN_ARMY — the Strategic Review handles this as part of its military assessment.

The new lord’s personality determines the likely response. If the deceased lord was Yu (aggressive, expansionist) and the new lord is Jin (compassionate, defensive), the new lord is very likely to cancel conquest objectives and confirm or modify governance objectives. If the new lord is also Yu, they confirm everything and the vassals barely notice the transition. The succession’s political impact emerges from the personality difference between the dead lord and their successor — same engine, different decisions.

### 55.33.6 Target-Dependent Objective Persistence

Target-dependent objectives do not orphan. The vassal continues pursuing them. When the vassal eventually learns about the succession (through the same death topic propagation), they note the new lord for reporting purposes. Their next success report — or their REASSESS_OBJECTIVE letter if they stall — goes to the successor instead of the deceased lord.

The successor may still choose to cancel a persisting objective during their Strategic Review. "Avenge Togashi Haru" persists automatically, but if Takeru reviews his vassals and decides the vengeance mission is a waste of resources, he can cancel it through ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE with a replacement. The persistence is a default, not a lock. The new lord always has authority over their vassals’ objectives.

Self-selected objectives (Section 55.26.1.5 — NPC chose their own primary because their lord was negligent) are always target-dependent by definition. They reference the NPC’s own goals, not the lord’s strategy. Lord death does not affect self-selected objectives at all. The NPC continues as if nothing changed — because for their personal mission, nothing did.

### 55.33.7 Interaction with Commitment Registry

A vassal with ORPHANED objectives may also hold active commitments (Section 55.31) related to the orphaned mission. For example: a vassal pursuing "Gain Winter Court invitation for my lord" may have a COURT_ATTENDANCE commitment at the target court and a SUPPORT_PLEDGE to an ally who was helping arrange the invitation.

When the objective orphans, the commitments do NOT automatically break. The vassal may still choose to attend the court (their standing objective might benefit from court attendance) or honor the support pledge (maintaining the relationship is valuable regardless of the original mission). The commitments remain PENDING and are evaluated at their deadlines through the normal commitment system (Section 55.31.4).

However, if the vassal’s standing objective routes them away from the commitment’s target (e.g., standing objective is "Protect my province" and the court is three provinces away), the commitment will likely break through normal mechanisms — the commitment_at_risk modifier (Section 55.31.7) competes with the standing objective’s urgency. If the standing objective wins, the vassal redirects and the commitment breaks. The force majeure clause (Section 55.31.11) does NOT apply here — lord death is not a qualifying crisis for the vassal’s commitments. The vassal chose to redirect based on their own priorities, not because of an external emergency.

Exception: if the lord died in a crisis that also qualifies as force majeure for the vassal (e.g., the lord died in a Shadowlands attack on the vassal’s province), then force majeure applies through the crisis, not through the lord death.

### 55.33.8 Worked Example — Death of Lord Akodo Ren

**Setup: **Lord Akodo Ren (Yu/Meiyo, Lion Family Daimyo) dies in a duel with Bayushi Aramoro. Three vassals hold active primary objectives assigned by Ren. Ren’s designated heir: Akodo Takeru (Jin/Gi, Rank 1, promoted from minor officer). Succession is confirmed by the Lion Clan Champion within 1 IC week.

**Vassal 1 — Matsu Ryota: **"Avenge death of Akodo Haru." Target-dependent — the vengeance target is still alive, the honor debt persists. Ryota is at court, two provinces away. He learns of Ren’s death within days through daily conversation (Ren’s death is major news at any Lion-attended court). Objective stays ACTIVE. Ryota continues pursuing vengeance. He notes Takeru as his new lord for future reports. Ironically, Ren’s death may generate a SECOND avenge objective — Ryota may now want to avenge Ren as well, depending on his disposition toward Ren and the circumstances of the duel.

**Vassal 2 — Ikoma Shu: **"Gain a Winter Court invitation for my lord." Lord-dependent — "my lord" is dead. Shu is at the Crane court, actively working to secure the invitation. He learns of Ren’s death through a letter that arrives 8 IC days after the event. Upon learning: objective status changes to ORPHANED. Decomposition generates REPORT_TO_NEW_LORD. Shu writes to Takeru: "Lord Ren assigned me to secure a Winter Court invitation for him. He is dead. I await your orders." Shu falls back to his standing objective ("Advance my family name") and uses his remaining court time to build personal Glory and relationships. Takeru’s first Strategic Review evaluates the orphaned objective. Takeru (Jin personality) does not need an aggressive court presence. He cancels the objective and assigns Shu: "Return home. I need you to help stabilize the province during the transition." Shu receives the new objective by letter and begins travel home.

**Vassal 3 — Akodo Kenji: **"Conquer Province Beiden." Lord-dependent — war decision. Kenji is on campaign, deep in enemy territory with an army of 30 PU. He is the last to learn — the death topic reaches him 1.5 seasons later through a courier who catches up with the marching army. During those 1.5 seasons, Kenji conquered Beiden. The province changed hands. When the topic arrives: the objective has already completed. It was fulfilled before the orphan check could fire. Kenji reports success to... Takeru, who didn’t order this conquest. Takeru now holds a province his father wanted but he does not. His Strategic Review must decide: hold Beiden (commit garrison, manage hostile population, risk escalation) or negotiate its return (sue for peace, return the province, restore relations). Takeru’s Jin personality strongly favors peace. He negotiates the return of Beiden in exchange for a formal apology and trade concessions. Ren’s aggressive strategy is undone by his successor’s compassion. The empire feels the succession. Same engine, different lord, different world.
