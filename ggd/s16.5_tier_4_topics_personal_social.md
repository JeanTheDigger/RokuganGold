## 16.5 Tier 4 Topics — Personal & Social

The existing Topic & Momentum System (Sections 16.1–16.4) governs crisis-level events — Shadowlands incursions, Clan Wars, Maho cults. These are Tiers 1 through 3: existential, major political/military, and local/regional threats that drive court agendas and demand formal response.

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Law ****&**** Order):** Magistrate investigations generate Betrayal topics when crimes are detected and attributed. A clan magistrate uncovering corruption in a vassal’s province generates a Tier 3 Betrayal topic. An Emerald Magistrate investigation into smuggling or treason generates a Tier 4 Political topic. The crime severity tiers (Section 57.47) map to topic tiers: minor crimes stay below the topic threshold, serious crimes generate Tier 3, and capital crimes (treason, maho) generate Tier 4 or higher. This is the primary mechanism by which the law enforcement system (Section 11.3) feeds into the court system (Section 15).

Tier 4 expands the Topic system downward into the fabric of daily Rokugani social life. Tier 4 Topics are not crises. They do not call courts into session or mobilize armies. They are the currency of conversation — the things characters talk about when they meet, write letters about, gossip over, and factor into their decisions. A Tier 4 Topic is anything noteworthy that named characters know about and care about.

Tier 4 Topics feed into the conversation system (designed separately). When two characters meet, the topics they hold influence what they discuss, how they react, and what decisions they make. A character who knows about a rival's recent disgrace will factor that into every interaction with that rival. A character who heard a rumor about an upcoming wedding will have opinions about it. Topics are the information layer that makes NPC decision-making feel alive and grounded.

**TIER 4 TOPIC CATEGORIES**

Tier 4 Topics are organized into five categories. Each category represents a domain of Rokugani social concern.

**Personal Topics**

Events involving named characters directly — their lives, relationships, honor, and fates.

- Duel: A formal challenge issued, accepted, or resolved. Outcome (victory, defeat, death, yield) generates a topic. The challenger, the challenged, and all witnesses hold this topic.

- Wedding: An arrangement announced, a ceremony held, or a marriage dissolved. Political implications elevate relevance for those with clan or family stakes.

- Birth: An heir born, a child acknowledged, an illegitimate birth revealed.

- Death: Natural death, battlefield death, seppuku, suspicious death. The manner of death affects relevance — an honorable death in battle is celebrated; a suspicious death is scrutinized.

- Disgrace: A public humiliation, a loss of face, a formal rebuke. Highly relevant to those with personal or clan connection to the disgraced.

- Promotion or Appointment: A named character is elevated in personal status or role — a samurai receiving a title, a courtier rising to a senior position, a warrior gaining command. This is personal news about a person. Distinct from New Appointment (Political), which concerns structural authority over territory.

- Retirement or Exile: A character stepping down or being removed. Power vacuums this creates are immediately relevant to those who might fill them.

- Romantic Scandal: An inappropriate relationship, a broken betrothal, an affair with political consequences.

- Adoption: A lord adopting an heir. Significant political implications.

- Gempuku: A notable coming-of-age ceremony. Relevant within the family and clan.

- Disappearance: A named character going missing without explanation.

- Injury or Illness: A significant wound or sickness affecting a named character of consequence.

**Political Topics**

Events involving the formal structures of Rokugani power — alliances, authority, and betrayal.

- Alliance Formed: A formal agreement between lords or clans is announced or ratified. The nature of the alliance — military, diplomatic, trade, or marriage-based — determines its political weight. See variant flag alliance_variant (Section 15.7).

- Alliance Broken: A formal agreement between lords or clans is dissolved, repudiated, or collapses. Carries a uniformly negative character — alliances break because trust failed, circumstances changed, or one party betrayed their commitment. No variant flag required — the disposition anchor calculation handles whether the breaking was justified.

- Betrayal: A political betrayal — broken oath, secret defection, sabotaged mission.

- New Appointment: A lord is newly assigned structural authority over a province or formal political position. This is a political change in the power structure, not merely a personal elevation. Distinct from Promotion or Appointment (Personal), which concerns an individual’s status. Both topic types trigger the same virtue modifiers (Seigyo: power shifts matter at all scales; Chishiki: knowing who holds what is always valuable) but represent different scales of event.

- Hostage Exchange: A hostage given or returned. Signals the state of relations between families.

- Formal Insult: A deliberate public slight between clans or lords.

- Significant Gift: A gift of unusual value or political implication — too large to be mere courtesy.

- Petition to the Emperor: A lord seeking Imperial attention or intervention.

- Edict Issued: A law or ruling handed down by a lord or champion.

- Spy Uncovered: An agent of another clan discovered operating in one's territory.

- Champion's Decision: A Clan Champion making a ruling that affects other families or clans.

**Military Topics**

Events on the battlefield and in the field — outcomes, movements, and the human cost of war.

- Battle Outcome: A victory or defeat in an engagement. Relevant to all involved clans and their neighbors.

- Provincial Raid: A lord raiding another's province. The seed of a potential war escalation.

- Army Movement: A significant army moving through or near one's territory.

- Commander Death: A named commander killed in battle. Power vacuum and morale implications.

- Unit Destroyed or Dishonored: A notable military unit annihilated or forced to retreat in disgrace.

- Siege Beginning or Ending: A castle placed under siege or a siege resolved.

- Desertion or Defection: A samurai abandoning their post or changing allegiance.

- Tactical Surprise: An unexpected or unusually clever military maneuver that becomes widely discussed.

**Supernatural Topics**

Events touching the spirit world, the Celestial Order, or the dark powers that threaten Rokugan.

- Omen Observed: An eclipse, unusual weather, strange animal behavior, or celestial event interpreted as significant. TRIGGER NOTE: Topic generated by the seasonal event system or specific worship/resource conditions. Exact trigger conditions deferred to World Map event design session.

- Spirit Manifestation: A kami, ancestor spirit, or other entity making itself known.

- Unusual Shugenja Casting: A shugenja performing magic of unusual power or disturbing nature. TRIGGER NOTE: Topic generated when a shugenja uses a spell above a power threshold or of a specifically restricted type. Exact trigger conditions deferred to Magic System design session.

- Cursed Location Discovered: A place found to be spiritually corrupt or haunted.

- Ancestor’s Displeasure: A family’s ancestral spirits communicating dissatisfaction — visible through ill fortune, failed harvests, or direct manifestation. TRIGGER NOTE: Topic generated by the Kami Worship System when a Family’s aggregate WP falls below the Family threshold (60 WP per Fortune per season — temporary value pending map design, Section 4.3.21). The Ancestor’s Displeasure topic represents the spiritual consequence of collective Family worship failure and fires as a Tier 3 crisis.

- Low-level Taint Discovered: Shadowlands corruption found in a person or location at a level not yet warranting Tier 1 crisis response. If unaddressed, may escalate.

**Economic Topics**

Events affecting the material resources of provinces, trade routes, and livelihoods.

- Harvest Failure: A province's crops failing. Relevant to neighbors who may be asked for aid and to lords who may exploit the weakness.

- Trade Route Disruption: A road or river route made unsafe or impassable.

- Unusual Gift or Bribe: Resources changing hands in politically significant quantities. TRIGGER: a legitimate gift with appraised value of 5 Koku or above generates this topic among all witnesses. Any detected bribe generates this topic regardless of amount (bribery is criminal per Section 11.3.11). Value PROVISIONAL.

- Banditry: Organized bandit activity affecting travel and trade on a road.

- Resource Shortage: A province running critically low on a key resource — Rice, Arms, Koku.

- Hunt Announcement: A samurai has announced they are organising a hunt at a specified province on a specified IC day. The topic carries host_id, target_province_id, hunt_date_ic_day, and host_status. Propagates via Daily Conversation and letters per Section 57.38. Removed from all topic pools when the hunt date arrives, regardless of remaining momentum.

**TOPIC RELEVANCE**

Not every character cares equally about every topic. A topic's relevance to a specific character is determined by two filters applied together:

- Personality: A character's virtue profile (Courage, Compassion, Courtesy, Honesty, Honor) shapes what they care about. A character with high Compassion is more affected by news of suffering. A character with high Honor is more disturbed by reports of disgrace. A character driven by Seigyo (Control) or Ketsui (Determination) is more alert to appointment, promotion, and power-shift news.

- Relationship to Subject: A character's connection to the topic's subject amplifies relevance. A topic about your lord is more relevant than a topic about a stranger. A topic about your rival is more relevant than one about a neutral party. Disposition values (Section 12.2) directly feed into this calculation — a character with strong positive or negative disposition toward a subject will have elevated relevance for topics involving them.

These two filters combine. A Lion samurai with high Honor who hears about a disgrace involving his direct commander has maximum relevance. The same Lion hearing about a distant trade route disruption has minimal relevance unless he has a personal connection to the parties involved.

**TIER 4 TOPIC DECAY**

Tier 4 Topics do not persist indefinitely. Unlike Tier 1–3 crisis topics which remain active until resolved, Tier 4 Topics decay over time. Each topic has a momentum-like counter that decreases each IC day when no one discusses it and holds or increases when characters actively bring it up in conversation.

A Tier 4 Topic that is discussed frequently — passed from character to character, referenced in letters, brought up at meetings — remains alive. A topic that no one discusses fades. Old news becomes old news. This mirrors real social dynamics: a duel that happened six months ago is no longer the talk of the court unless someone keeps bringing it up.

Decay rate is not fixed — it is driven by discussion activity. A topic with zero discussion loses momentum steadily each IC day. A topic being actively discussed across multiple characters holds its momentum. A topic spreading to new characters gains momentum. When a topic's momentum reaches zero, it is removed from all characters' active topic lists. It does not disappear from history — the event still happened — but it is no longer something characters spontaneously discuss.

**ESCALATION FROM TIER 4 TO HIGHER TIERS**

Tier 4 Topics are the seed layer from which crises grow. Most Tier 4 Topics will decay harmlessly — they are noted, discussed, and forgotten. But some Tier 4 Topics, left unaddressed or actively worsened, escalate into Tier 3, 2, or even Tier 1 crises.

- A Provincial Raid (Tier 4 Military) ignored or repeated becomes a Border Conflict (Tier 3), then a Clan War (Tier 2).

- Low-level Taint Discovered (Tier 4 Supernatural) uninvestigated becomes a Maho Cult Spreading (Tier 1).

- A Spy Uncovered (Tier 4 Political) that implicates a Clan Champion becomes a major political crisis (Tier 2).

- A Harvest Failure (Tier 4 Economic) in multiple provinces simultaneously escalates to Famine (Tier 2 or Tier 3 depending on scope).

Escalation is not automatic — it requires conditions in the world to worsen. A Provincial Raid that is diplomatically resolved stays Tier 4. A Harvest Failure that neighboring lords respond to with aid stays Tier 4. Player and NPC responses to Tier 4 Topics are the primary mechanism for preventing escalation. Ignoring small problems is how large crises are born.
