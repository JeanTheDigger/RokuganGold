## 14.2 Player Reaction Menu — LOCKED

The game processes the world in 4 IC day blocks corresponding to 1 real-world day. NPCs act during offline periods using their objective and decision systems. When a player logs in, everything that happened during their offline period is presented as a structured digest. This is both a UI feature and a mechanical system — the GDD defines which events require response, what the response windows are, and what happens if the player does not respond.

Events do not fully resolve until the 4 IC day block completes. This means a duel challenge issued on IC Day 2 of an offline period is held and presented when the player logs in, not processed in real time against an absent player.

**Category 1 — Requires Response (time-sensitive):**

These events need a player decision before consequences trigger. They are presented prominently at login. The player has until the end of their current OOC day to respond unless a longer window is specified.

Duel Challenge Issued Against You:

- Response window: 1 OOC day (4 IC days).

- Options: Accept or Decline.

- Declining has existing Honor implications depending on circumstances.

- No response within window: treated as declining with additional dishonor for failure to respond. Honor loss: −0.3. Disgrace topic generated.

- Issuing a duel challenge as a reaction to another character’s action does not cost AP. It is declared through the Reaction Menu.

Favor Called In:

- Response window: 7 real days from receipt. Prevents players from holding the system by not logging in.

- Options: Honor it or Break it with full Section 12.10 consequences.

- No response within 7 real days: auto-breaks. Full breaking consequences apply.

Proxy Out-of-Mandate Commitment:

- Response window: 1 OOC day.

- Options: Accept (commitment stands, lord pays Honor cost) or Refuse (commitment retracted, proxy pays severe Honor cost).

- No response within window: defaults to Refuse. Proxy suffers full consequences automatically.

New Primary Objective by Letter:

- No response required. Activates upon receipt. Player sees the new objective and knows their obligation from Section 18.9 onward.

Court Invitation Requiring RSVP:

- Response window: until court begins.

- Options: Accept, Decline, or Send Proxy (triggers proxy mandate assignment).

- No response: treated as declining with small disposition cost to host.

**Category 2 — Requires Presence (committed events):**

These are events the player already committed to. The game expects presence. Not participating has consequences.

Active Court Session:

- If the player accepted an invitation and the court is in session, they are expected to spend their AP.

- If the player logs in during an active court and spends no AP: they are present but passive. They gain no disposition changes, make no commitments, take no actions. No Honor loss — they showed up — but the session proceeds without their influence.

- If the player never logs in during the entire court session despite having accepted: treated as absence after acceptance. Small Honor loss (−0.1) and disposition cost with host.

Battle Assignment:

- Missing a battle the player was assigned to participate in is objective negligence under Section 18.9.

- Honor loss: −0.1 per IC season of negligence. Active opposition consequences if the player deliberately avoided a battle their lord needed them at.

**Category 3 — Notification Only:**

These events happened and the player needs to know. No response is required or available. They are presented as an informational digest below the time-sensitive items.

- Topics that entered the player’s pool through daily conversation or letter delivery.

- Disposition changes that occurred toward or from the player’s character.

- Crisis momentum changes affecting the player’s territory or relevant crises.

- Court outcomes from sessions the player did not attend — what was committed, who said what, what topics were moved.

- Deaths, appointments, duels, marriages, and other Tier 4 topics involving characters the player knows.

- NPC objective changes that became visible through Probe results or public declarations.

**UI Implementation Note — LOCKED:**

The Reaction Menu is the first screen a player sees upon logging in. Category 1 items are at the top, flagged clearly as requiring action. Category 2 items show active commitments and their status. Category 3 is a scrollable digest below. The player processes their responses before entering normal play. Unresolved Category 1 items remain flagged until addressed.
