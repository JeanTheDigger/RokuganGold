## 56.18 Ship Boarding Template --- LOCKED

The tenth map template. Generates when a boarding action (Section 11.9) involves a player character — either as attacker (boarding an enemy ship) or defender (enemy crew boards yours). The naval Army View resolves the broader engagement at Company level. The Ship Boarding Template generates the ASCII map for the player’s personal deck-to-deck fight. This is the naval equivalent of the Battlefield Bubble.

**56.18.1 Map Layout**

15×10 — the smallest template. Two ships side by side, each approximately 15 tiles long × 3–4 tiles wide (a Kobune is ~75 feet long, ~20 feet wide at 1 tile = 5 feet). A 1–2 tile water gap separates the ships. Narrow boarding planks (1 tile wide) connect the two decks across the gap — 2 planks for a standard boarding, 3 for a large ship (Sengokobune or Atakebune). Grappling hooks hold the ships together. The player’s ship occupies one side of the map, the enemy ship the other.

**56.18.2 Terrain Features**

Masts: impassable tiles, 1–2 per ship. Rigging: climbable (Athletics / Strength TN 10, 1 tile up per Simple Action). A character in the rigging gains the Mounted/Higher attack bonus (+1k0) and +5 Armor TN from ranged attacks below, but cannot use two-handed weapons. Archers in the rigging dominate the deck. Quarterdeck: elevated stern platform, 2–3 tiles. Provides +1k0 attack bonus against enemies on the main deck below (elevation advantage). The ship captain fights here. Cabin: enclosed tiles at the stern beneath the quarterdeck. Provides hard cover. Cargo/barrels: scattered on deck, provide light cover (+5 Armor TN). Wet decks: all deck tiles impose −1k0 on Athletics and movement-related checks due to rolling, wet footing. Yoritomo Bushi (Rank 1 — The Way of the Mantis: no penalties for rough or uneven terrain), Storm Legion (veteran boarding fighters), and characters with Sailing 3+ are immune to this penalty. Yoritomo Bushi Rank 4 (The Rolling Wave) is exceptionally powerful on ship decks — moving across a boarding plank grants +10 Armor TN, and the free Knockdown Raises can push enemies off planks into the water. Boarding planks: 1 tile wide, connect the two ships. A character on a plank can be pushed into the water (contested Strength roll, loser falls). Planks can be cut (Simple Action, weapon strike, plank destroyed in 1 hit). Cutting a plank while enemies are crossing it drops them into the water.

**56.18.3 Water Hazard**

The 1–2 tile gap between ships is open water. Any character knocked, pushed, or falling into the water must swim: Athletics / Strength TN 15 per round to stay afloat. Failure means sinking — drowning rules apply per RAW. Armored characters suffer: light armor −1k0 to swim rolls, heavy armor −2k0. This is why no one wears heavy armor at sea (Section 11.9 Lore Notes — salt air ruins armor, impedes swimming). A character in the water can climb back onto a ship (Athletics / Strength TN 20, Full Round Action, provokes free attacks from anyone on the deck edge above). Allies can throw a rope (Simple Action) reducing the climb TN to 10. Characters in the water cannot attack or defend normally — they are effectively out of the fight until they climb out.

**56.18.4 Assault Mode (Player Boarding)**

The player starts on their own ship with 6–10 friendly sailors. Objective: cross the planks and take the enemy ship. The enemy crew (6–12 depending on ship size) defends their deck. Attackers crossing the planks suffer −2 Attack on the first round they step onto the enemy deck (the standard boarding penalty from Section 11.9). Storm Legion ignore this penalty. The enemy captain on the quarterdeck is the high-value target — killing or capturing the captain breaks the enemy crew’s morale. The player must choose: rush the planks with numbers (fast but costly), send archers to the rigging first (slower but safer), or cut around via a second plank to flank the defenders.

**56.18.5 Defense Mode (Enemy Boarding)**

The player starts on their own ship with 6–10 friendly sailors. Enemy boarders cross the planks from the enemy ship. The player defends their deck. Defenders can hold the plank chokepoints (1 tile wide — only one enemy can cross at a time per plank), station archers on the quarterdeck or rigging, or cut the planks before too many enemies cross (stranding boarders already on the player’s deck, isolated from reinforcements). If the naval Army View shows the player’s ship losing, additional enemy waves cross. If winning, the flow of boarders slows and stops.

**56.18.6 Force Composition**

Both sides use the Pirate NPC Archetypes (Section 54.8) for wako engagements, or clan soldier stat blocks (Section 11.7) for clan naval battles. Friendly sailors are drawn from the ship’s crew — a Mantis Kobune has Yoritomo Bushi, a Crab vessel has Hida Bushi and sailors. Pirate crews fight with kama, masakari, and knives — the preferred sailor weapons (Section 11.9 Lore Notes). Named characters (ship captain, player’s allies) use their full character sheets.

**56.18.7 Resolution and Consequence**

Boarding succeeds (attacker takes the enemy deck, captain killed or captured): the ship is captured as a prize. Enemy crew surrenders. Prize value = half the ship’s construction cost in Koku. Boarding fails (attacker’s crew routed or planks cut with attackers stranded): surviving attackers must swim back to their own ship or surrender. Defender holds: the enemy boarding attempt is repelled. The naval Army View reflects the outcome. If the player’s ship is captured while defending: the player is captured (Hostage System, Section 22.9) or killed depending on circumstances. Pirates may ransom samurai prisoners. Glory rewards: +0.2 Glory for personally killing the enemy captain. +0.1 Glory for surviving a boarding action. Pirate suppression: a successful boarding against a pirate fleet reduces pirate Strength per the insurgency suppression rules (Section 11.11).
