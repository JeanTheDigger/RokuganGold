## 54.7 Factions: The Kolat

“Forget everything you have been told.” – Yasuki Taka, Humble Merchant, Twelfth Century

The Kolat is a vast and terrible conspiracy, dating back to the very founding of the Emerald Empire, conceived in absolute secrecy and maintained through ruthless practicality, amoral cruelty, and absolute fanaticism. When Hantei and his divine siblings fell from the Heavens, not all the human tribes welcomed them. Some malcontents met in secret, discussing ways in which the Kami might someday be defeated and their own rule restored. Their most prominent leader was a man known as Tora, a charismatic genius and philosopher. Tora and his fellow conspirators pored over Shinsei’s Tao, seeking wisdom and justification. Out of these early debates, lasting almost a quarter-century, the fledgling Kolat devised their own philosophy: the “Rule of Man.”

Tora argued the Celestial Wheel need not be an eternal circle – what if it was instead working to create some kind of final meaning, an ultimate pattern to the universe? In that case, if mankind was ruled by gods fallen from the Heavens, it would be those gods who determined the final pattern. But if men overthrew these gods and ruled themselves, it would be men who ultimately perfected the universe, forming it in their own image. On that basis, rebellion against the Kami was not merely justified but vitally necessary. Moreover, the entirety of human activity would need to be controlled and guided so that the final shape of the universe would be a desirable one. It might take hundreds or even thousands of years, but the ultimate goal would be more than worth the effort. His comrades agreed, and the Kolat was born.

### History of the Kolat

Tora and his fellows began the laborious work of building the Kolat into an Empire-spanning conspiracy. The leaders, now a council of ten Kolat Masters, began constructing a secret headquarters – a pyramidal structure which would come to be known as the Hidden Temple – deep within the Phoenix Mountains. Early in the second century, Kolat agents in the Crab lands learned of a mysterious object uncovered by Crab miners in the Twilight Mountains: a giant crystalline globe, larger than a man, with the outline of an eye carved into one side. It appeared to be magical, showing visions and images to those who gazed into its depths and “weeping” small crystal tears which seemed to contain some portion of its magical essence. They called it the Oni’s Eye. Kolat agents staged a bandit attack, stealing the object and quickly moving it to the Hidden Temple. The Kolat discovered the Eye permitted them to observe events from vast distances, almost anywhere they wished, and the strange crystal “tears” that oozed from it could be used to communicate over equally long distances. The Kolat suddenly had an intelligence source greater than anything the Empire could hope to match. The Eye also allowed them to regain contact with those of their brethren who had fled into the Burning Sands – the distant allies who called themselves Qolat.

One of the newly unified Kolat’s earliest victories came deep in the Burning Sands, where their agents, as part of a nomadic tribe known as the Ujik-Hai, arranged to join the Ki-Rin Clan, subtly infiltrating the clan under the very nose of the Kami Shinjo. They would spend the next several centuries slowly tightening their grip on the clan, and by the time the Unicorn Clan returned to Rokugan, the conspiracy was fully in control of the clan’s key leadership. Within Rokugan, the Kolat’s development of economic power gave rise to the Crab-Crane War of the third century, after which Kolat influence over the Yasuki family grew enormously. The return of the Unicorn Clan in the early ninth century gave the Kolat an entire clan at their beck and call, providing enormous economic and military leverage within the Empire. The Dragon Clan, however, controlled by its immortal Kami Togashi, defeated every effort to place agents of the conspiracy within its ranks for over a thousand years.

The Second Day of Thunder heralded the end of the Hantei Dynasty. When the Lying Darkness finally made its move, the Kolat stepped forward, unleashing hundreds of agents with crystal weapons to purge the Shadowspawn from the castles and courts of Rokugan – a key blow against the Darkness, but one that exposed the existence of the Kolat to the Empire as a whole. Even worse, the Kami Shinjo unexpectedly returned from the Burning Sands and unleashed a great purge against the Kolat infiltration of her clan, wiping out centuries of effort in a few weeks. To some of the Kolat leadership, these costs were acceptable – the end of the War Against the Darkness had elevated two mortals, Hitomi and Yakamo, to the position of Moon and Sun. Many Masters argued the loss of the Unicorn was more than compensated for by the ascension of mortals to the rulers of the Celestial Heavens themselves, and a considerable schism developed within the conspiracy’s leadership.

During the chaotic era known as the Race for the Throne, a traitor to the Kolat named Kaelung revealed the location of the Hidden Temple to the Scorpion Clan. The Scorpion assaulted the Temple and its Ox Clan defenders with all their might. The Oni’s Eye was captured and the majority of the Kolat perished, including all but three of the Ten Masters. Less than a year later, Yakamo and Hitomi were expelled from the Celestial Heavens by the new Jade and Obsidian Dragons. A new dynasty took power with the direct mandate of the Heavens, ending the influence of mortal man over Rokugan’s leaders. From the seeming heights of near-victory the Kolat had plunged to a position nearly as weak as at its founding, 1100 years before.

### Kolat Philosophy

Within a few generations Tora’s ideals of the “Rule of Man” had been firmly inculcated into the conspiracy’s recruits. The Kolat quickly became a veritable cult of true believers, men and women absolutely convinced they were doing the right thing for all mankind. This was a source of considerable strength, since it meant the members of the conspiracy were willing to do anything, without compunction or scruple, to pursue their ultimate goals. It also meant the conspiracy was from the beginning an organization dedicated to patient long-term planning. The Kolat admired Shinsei from the moment he appeared, seeing him not as a wise man and servant of the Kami but as a mortal who stood before gods and took command, briefly imposing his own will on the Celestial Order – just as the Kolat hoped to do for all eternity. The Tao of Shinsei was and remains the only religious text the Kolat respect, although they twist Shinsei’s words to justify their darkest ideals.

The Kolat pays no attention to the rules of Rokugan’s social hierarchy and will recruit eta as readily as it will samurai. For example, at the end of the eleventh century one of the Ten Masters was none other than the Champion of the Unicorn, while another was a geisha and still another was a captain in the Imperial Guard. Samurai are drawn to the conspiracy for a number of reasons: some are seduced by the simple draw of power, while many are drawn to the ideals of the Kolat itself. All too often samurai are forced to choose between their lord and themselves, and some begin to blame the Celestial Order for the tribulations in their lives. The Heavens dictated their suffering, so they turn against the Heavens.

### Organization: The Ten Masters and Ten Sects

The identity of the ten Kolat Masters is one of the conspiracy’s most closely guarded secrets, even from its own members. When the Masters met in the Hidden Temple they always wore full-body black robes and golden masks to conceal their forms and faces. When a Master dies, each of the remaining Masters is allowed to anonymously nominate a potential successor from within the Kolat ranks, and the candidates are tested rigorously by the entire leadership. When not visiting the Hidden Temple, the Kolat Masters live ordinary lives, behaving in all ways like the loyal and pious Rokugani they appear to be.

CHRYSANTHEMUM SECT: The Kolat’s eyes and ears within the Imperial City – first Otosan Uchi and later Toshi Ranbo. These agents had one of the most dangerous jobs within the conspiracy, for their operations were always liable to draw the attention of Imperial Magistrates or the Seppun Hidden Guard. Every agent in the Imperial City was required to keep a vial of poison at all times, to be taken in case of discovery. After the destruction of the Hidden Temple, the Chrysanthemum Sect continued to operate within Toshi Ranbo, albeit with far fewer resources.

CLOUD SECT: Preserves everything the conspiracy knows and distributes the information to the other sects as needed. Staffed heavily by shugenja as well as scholars and historians of many other stripes. The Cloud Sect is unique in that almost any member may directly contact its leader, Master Cloud, through a special spell taught to every shugenja in the sect. The fall of the Hidden Temple all but destroyed the Cloud Sect, an especially heavy blow to the Kolat organization.

COIN SECT: In charge of the Kolat’s extensive efforts in economic warfare. Raises vast sums through control and manipulation of trade within the Empire, with strong influence within the Yasuki, Daidoji, Yoritomo, and Ide families, among others. The Coin Sect survived the fall of the Hidden Temple with less damage than almost any other branch, and if the Kolat rise again it will likely be through Master Coin’s work.

DREAM SECT: Specializes in the strange and unnatural task of creating “sleepers” – Rokugani who have been brainwashed and reprogrammed to serve the needs of the conspiracy. When a sect needs a sleeper agent, they kidnap a suitable subject and subject the victim to weeks of physical and mental torture using both mundane and supernatural means. After conditioning, the sleeper agent’s memory is erased and he or she is returned home. Sleeper agents may be activated by a trigger word, phrase, or poem, by a set time passing, or any of a wide variety of other signals. Once activated, sleepers lose all sense of self and fulfill their mission at any cost, forgetting their deeds afterward. Like the Cloud Sect, the Dream Sect was largely wiped out when the Hidden Temple fell. The sect’s sleeper agents, however, continue to live out their lives, unaware of the programming hidden within their minds.

JADE SECT: Exists to oppose supernatural threats to the conspiracy’s goals – whether those threats be the Taint of Jigoku or the corruption of the Lying Darkness. Jade agents rarely take direct action; more often the Jade Sect utilizes the Kolat’s powerful network to bring allies to bear on the problem. The Kuni Witch Hunters, the Scorpion Kuroiban, the Phoenix Inquisitors, or the Jade Magistrates sometimes receive anonymous information pointing to dangerous threats. The Jade Sect’s most famous member was the former merchant Yasuki Taka, who was eventually encased in a prison of crystal with his memories mostly destroyed but his guile and hatred of corruption fully intact, running the Jade Sect for decades from within his crystal cocoon. He finally died when the Scorpion Clan stormed the Hidden Temple.

LOTUS SECT: Responsible for dispatching the enemies of the Kolat. Lotus assassins live in secluded monasteries in remote regions, training perpetually and waiting for orders. They are among the finest assassins in the history of Rokugan. The Lotus philosophy is that an assassin must be free of human contacts with the outside world, thereby ensuring the assassin can take life without hesitation when the time comes. Master Lotus keeps in close contact with Master Silk, with three couriers at each temple ready to run missives between the two, since most assassination targets are determined by information given to the Lotus by the Silk Sect.

ROC SECT: The seat that historically maintained the connection between the Kolat and its foreign counterpart. The Roc Sect exists as a Kolat Master seat, and a Master is selected at world generation following normal selection rules. However, the Roc Sect has no unique ActionIDs and no active mechanical pipeline. The Master operates using only the shared Kolat ActionID pool. The seat’s foreign connection exists as lore context but produces no mechanical effects at launch.

SILK SECT: Responsible for gathering intelligence for the conspiracy, collecting information in every possible way and conveying it back to the Hidden Temple for analysis. Many Silk agents are actors, geisha, or courtiers, and a large number are heimin or even eta – they are in every city, have agents in every court, and have ears almost anywhere a samurai could be expected to speak. A separate faction within the Silk Sect analyzes and disseminates the information collected by their field agents, and they also forge and falsify anything the Kolat might need, from legal documents to famous weapons.

STEEL SECT: The name given to the military force which guards the Hidden Temple – a small army of highly trained ronin called the “Hidden Guard.” Their responsibilities are simple: prevent any outsiders from approaching the Temple. When travelers stray too close, the Steel patrollers first attempt to cause “accidents” to turn the visitors away. If accidents fail, Steel Sect warriors take a more direct approach. The Steel Sect ultimately failed in its mission when the Scorpion Clan attacked the Hidden Temple directly.

TIGER SECT: Tasked with making sure the Kolat conspiracy remains a secret at all costs – thus the Tiger may be considered first among equals. Their members are the only Kolat agents who are routinely given the identities of other Kolat as part of their duties. They identify and execute those who detect or betray the conspiracy. Death at the hands of a Tiger agent is never a swift or painless affair, and when someone betrays the Kolat the punishment extends not only to that individual but also to his family and friends. Master Tiger often functions as the de facto leader of the conspiracy.

### Resources, Methods, and Weaknesses

The Kolat always prefers to act indirectly, using its influence to steer unknowing samurai toward an identified problem. Forged documents, seductions, bribery, blackmail – all of these are weapons in the conspiracy’s arsenal. The Silk Sect’s enormous information resources allow them to unleash incriminating evidence to shame a target publicly, usually resulting in exile or seppuku, if not execution. The power of the Oni’s Eye makes it pitifully easy for the Kolat to discover dark secrets about its targets once they draw its ire.

When assassination is necessary, the Kolat hides the cause and method of death rather than the death itself. Sleeper agents are a vital tool – they can be unleashed without warning to kill someone for no apparent reason or motivation. Poison is also popular, since poisons can be extremely difficult to trace back to their source. The Kolat generally uses slow-acting poisons, to lessen the chance of discovery beforehand and to create a confusing delay between the poisoning and subsequent death.

The Kolat’s greatest weakness was that it had to remain absolutely secret if it was to survive. Even at the peak of its power, the conspiracy’s real numbers were small and the Masters knew they could not survive full-scale exposure to the Empire. The Kolat’s most persistent foe throughout its history was the Dragon Clan. For over a thousand years the Dragon were ruled by the Kami Togashi, whose divine sight could identify every Kolat infiltrator, defeating every effort to gain a foothold in the Dragon. The rise of the Kitsuki family, whose strange methods of investigation made them capable foes of Kolat plots, further deepened the conspiracy’s fears.

The Oni’s Eye and the Tears of the Oni’s Eye: The Oni’s Eye is a giant crystalline globe, larger than a man, with the outline of an eye carved into one side. It permits the Kolat to observe events from vast distances, and “weeps” small teardrop-shaped crystal tears which trusted Kolat agents carry with them, usually disguised as a pendant or other item of jewelry. Those who control the Oni’s Eye can use it to communicate mentally with anyone carrying a Tear – there is no outward sign of this communication, for the person with the Tear will hear their voices directly inside his mind.

### New Advantages and Disadvantages

SHADOWED HEART [Mental, 5 Points]: You have trained your mind to be nigh-inscrutable to those who surround you. Rolls made to discern your true intentions or motivations, including Social Skill rolls and Spell Casting Rolls for any spells which might lay bare your thoughts, have their TN increased by +5.

SPY NETWORK [Social, 8 Points]: You have access to an intelligence network that can provide you with a variety of covert information. Once per session, if you can contact your spy network, you can acquire a piece of useful information which would otherwise be unavailable. The GM has the final say on what you learn, but it will always be something useful to your current endeavors. This Advantage cannot be used in environments where it would be impossible to make contact with your spy network. Daidoji family characters may purchase this Advantage for 7 points; Scorpion characters for 6 points; Kolat characters for 5 points.

SLEEPER AGENT [Mental Disadvantage, 5 Points]: Unknown to you, the Kolat have previously kidnapped you and programmed you to be one of their sleeper agents. A trigger word or phrase is implanted in your mind. Anyone who knows it may speak it aloud, followed by a simple command of five words or less. You will be compelled to carry out the command regardless of personal wishes. Sleeper agents are generally considered one-time-only assets, so their survival is not a factor in the tasks they are assigned. Some sleepers are given a fail-safe that causes them to kill themselves should the mission fail – if this is the case for you, this Disadvantage is worth 8 points.

### New Alternate Path: Kolat Master

The Ten Kolat Masters are among the most devious, manipulative, and ruthless individuals in the entire Emerald Empire. Only ten exist at any one time, and they generally achieve their position by rising through the ranks of the conspiracy. Technique Rank: 6 (can also be taken at Insight Ranks above 6). Replaces: Any Technique (or none) at Insight Rank 6 or higher. Required Rings/Traits: Two mental Traits at 5 or higher. Required Skills: Courtier (Manipulation) 5, Intimidation (Control) 4. Other Requirements: Must be a member of the Kolat conspiracy and chosen to lead one of the ten Sects. Cannot be Tainted or corrupted by the Nothing.

TECHNIQUE: WILL OF THE MASTER: The ten Kolat Masters exert total control over the conspiracy. You are considered to be Status 10 to any character who is a member of the Kolat conspiracy and who belongs to your Sect, and Status 9 to any character who is a Kolat but not of your Sect. You gain +2k0 on all Courtier and Intimidation Skill rolls.

### New Ronin Path: Guardian of the Hidden Temple (Bushi)

The ancient order of the Hidden Guard is tasked with protecting the Kolat’s headquarters. They are recruited from skilled ronin who have already joined the Kolat conspiracy and shown their loyalty and dedication. The Hidden Guard are taught defensive tactics to allow them to defeat the more specialized warriors of the Great Clans, fighting with tactics which most clan samurai would consider dishonorable – ambushes, surprise attacks, and similar methods. Technique Rank: 4. Requirement: Must be recruited by the Kolat for their Hidden Guard. Cannot be Tainted or corrupted by the Nothing.

TECHNIQUE: TIGERS DO NOT FALL: Any time an opponent in a skirmish declares a Maneuver against you (including the Increased Damage Maneuver), you may choose to immediately take a Free Action to spend a Void Point and negate the beneficial effects of the Maneuver. (The opponent must still make his Raises in order to hit you, but no longer gains any benefit from them.) In addition, at all times you may ignore the penalty to your Armor TN for being Grappled.

### New Advanced School: Kolat Assassin (Ninja)

The assassins of the Lotus Sect are among the deadliest killers in the Empire, swift and merciless. Much of their training is dedicated to infiltration and deceit, allowing them to disguise themselves and replace others, but they are also taught all possible methods of murder. Requirements: Agility 4, Reflexes 4. Skills: Acting 5, Knives 5, Stealth 5. Other Requirements: Must be a member of the Kolat and recruited into the Lotus Sect. Cannot be Tainted or corrupted by the Nothing.

RANK 1 – KISS OF THE LOTUS: The Kolat assassins are taught to slip through Rokugani society all but unnoticed. You are a master of deception and infiltration. When you spend a Void Point on a Stealth or Acting Skill roll, you gain a +2k2 bonus instead of the normal +1k1.

RANK 2 – TIGER’S CLAW: When striking on the first Round of a skirmish against an unaware opponent, or an opponent who does not realize you are a threat, you gain a +1k0 bonus to your attack rolls and may make melee attacks as a Simple Action.

RANK 3 – STEAL THE LIGHT: During a skirmish, once per opponent you may call 2 Raises to make a melee attack that keeps only 1 die of damage (regardless of the weapon used) but leaves the opponent either blind, deaf, or mute (your choice) for a number of minutes equal to your Water. Blinded characters suffer the Blind Conditional Effect from the L5R 4th Edition core rulebook. The GM has discretion to decide the exact penalties of being deafened or muted – for example, a muted character cannot cast spells.

Kolat Spells: Kolat shugenja draw on the following spells from the master spell compendium (Section 32 – Kolat Spells): Master Cloud’s Eyes (Air 3), The Mirror’s Smile (Water 4), The World is Truth (Air 6).

### Kolat NPC: Wily Trader

A vast network of agents and an equally vast arsenal of exotic weapons and tactics does not come cheap. Within the agents of the Kolat is a small army of merchants who provide the conspiracy with the money it needs. The Yasuki family has always had a large role to play in the Kolat’s mercantile ventures, and the conspiracy has also worked its way into the Daidoji, Yoritomo, and Ide trade networks. ### Sample Kolat Master: Yasuki Jinn-Kuen, Master Coin (Late Twelfth Century)

Bright and energetic despite his increasing age, Yasuki Jinn-Kuen is fortunate to look much younger than he is – merely one of many tools he employs to make sure others grossly underestimate him. Jinn-Kuen joined the Kolat so long ago he barely remembers life before the conspiracy. He recognized fairly early on that merit was highly relative within the Kolat. As one of the finest merchants in the entire Empire, he gravitated naturally to the Coin Sect, and quickly discovered his Master, Moshi Shanegon, was only competent by the barest of measures, vastly more concerned with his own well-being and comfort than the prosperity of the Coin Sect. Subtly guided by Jinn-Kuen, the former Unicorn general Moto Chen destroyed a large percentage of Shanegon’s most important operations, ruining the Master’s reputation so completely that he fled the Empire. After years of positioning himself, Jinn-Kuen was the natural replacement and rapidly became one of the most powerful members of the Ten Masters. He is dismayed by the schism between the Masters over Kolat policies and increasingly believes there is no need to destroy the last vestiges of Celestial involvement in the mortal realm.
