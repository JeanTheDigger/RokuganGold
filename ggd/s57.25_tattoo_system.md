## 57.25 Tattoo System

### 57.25.1 Overview

Tattooing is the only Artisan sub-skill whose output lives permanently on another character’s body. A tattoo cannot be carried, gifted, or placed in a zone. It exists as a persistent world object on the recipient’s character sheet and is visible only when the tattooed body part is exposed. The art form is most strongly associated with the Dragon Clan — nearly every Dragon samurai bears at least one. The Crab and Mantis embrace it as purely decorative art. The Crane admire fine work but rarely practice it. Among the Togashi Order, specially authorised elders apply ability tattoos that grant supernatural powers as school techniques at Ranks 1, 3, and 5. These are distinct from decorative tattoos and require authority beyond artistic skill. Tattooing has also migrated into the criminal underclass — pirates, bandits, and urban gangs tattoo affiliations and loyalties. That usage is outside this section’s scope.

### 57.25.2 Tattoo World Object

Each tattoo tracks the following fields: tattoo_id, recipient_id, artist_id, quality_tier, body_location, subject (text description of what is depicted), is_ability_tattoo (false for decorative, true for Togashi ability tattoos), ability_granted (null for decorative, ability_id for Togashi tattoos), date_applied, is_visible (computed dynamically), subject_type (image or topic), subject_description (text, for image type), topic_id (null for image type, links to a topic_id in the topic system for topic type). One tattoo per body part per character. A character who already has a tattoo on a specific body part cannot receive another on the same part. Subject type defines what the tattoo represents. Image type: a depicted creature, scene, symbol, natural element, or mon. The subject_description field is narrative only — it feeds READ_CHARACTER and PROBE results when the tattoo is visible but has no mechanical system interaction. Topic type: the tattoo represents a position, belief, allegiance, or event that exists as a topic in the topic system. The topic_id is set at application time. Topic tattoos carry their linked topic passively and permanently, even after the topic has faded from active world discourse.

Body locations and visibility thresholds: nine locations are defined. Left wrist / forearm and Right wrist / forearm — visible in most contexts, concealed only when wearing full formal sleeves or deliberate cover. Left upper arm / shoulder and Right upper arm / shoulder — visible without outer garment, concealed in formal dress. Chest / torso — visible only when upper garment removed. Back — visible only when upper garment removed. Left leg / thigh and Right leg / thigh — visible only when lower garments removed or in martial contexts (training, combat, bathing). Head — visible when the character is bald (is_bald: true on character sheet) and unhooded. If the character grows hair or wears a hood, head tattoos are concealed (is_visible: false). A character must be bald at time of application to receive a head tattoo. Each location uses its own visibility computation based on clothing state, is_bald, and context. is_visible is a dynamic boolean updated when any of these states change. Nine locations provide enough space for a full Togashi Tattooed Order monk (6 ability tattoos) plus additional decorative tattoos.

### 57.25.3 APPLY_TATTOO

ActionID: APPLY_TATTOO. Category 11 (Personal Development). Multi-AP single session. All AP must be spent within the same IC day — a tattoo cannot be left half-finished across days. Both artist and recipient must remain AT_ZONE throughout. Recipient consent is required — APPLY_TATTOO cannot fire against a character who has not accepted. Self-application exception: Togashi school monks of Rank 3 or above may apply tattoos to themselves. Artist_id equals recipient_id on self-applied tattoos. All other APPLY_TATTOO rules apply normally — the monk still spends the required AP, makes the roll, and both parties to the disposition bond are the same character (the bond fires as a self-reinforcing confidence in their craft, tracked but mechanically inert since self-disposition has no social effect). Cultural reluctance gate: before evaluating consent, the NPC engine checks the recipient’s cultural background against the artist’s disposition score. No reluctance (accepts at Neutral or above): Dragon, Crab, Mantis. Daidoji family specifically for wrist or forearm locations only, representing duty and family loyalty. Reluctant (requires Friendly, disposition 46+, to override cultural resistance): Lion, Unicorn, Phoenix, Scorpion, all Crane families except Daidoji. Scorpion exception: if the Scorpion NPC’s ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE NeedType is currently active toward the artist, the threshold drops to Neutral — the disposition bond is itself a calculated social asset. Very reluctant (requires Close, disposition 71+): Imperial families (Otomo, Seppun, Miya). Tattoos on Imperial line members are culturally transgressive; the relationship must be exceptionally intimate before consent fires. Subject matter modifier: if the tattoo subject is personally meaningful to the recipient — commemorates a battle they fought, depicts their family mon, honours their lord, references a topic_id from their own known history — the threshold drops one tier. A reluctant Lion who would require Friendly may accept at Neutral if the proposed subject depicts the specific battle that defined their career. This modifier applies when the artist includes the subject in the OFFER_ART_COMMISSION proposal and the NPC engine evaluates it against the recipient’s known_topics and character history. AP cost and TN by target quality: Normal = 2 AP, TN 15. Fine = 3 AP, TN 20. Exceptional = 4 AP, TN 25. Masterwork = 5 AP, TN 30. Legendary = 6 AP, TN 35. Roll: Artisan: Tattooing / Agility, one roll at completion. Raises upgrade quality tier one step per Raise, capped at Legendary. Skill gate: Artisan: Tattooing rank must equal or exceed the quality tier attempted (Normal = rank 1, Fine = rank 2, Exceptional = rank 3, Masterwork = rank 4, Legendary = rank 5). On success: the tattoo_object is created and added to the recipient’s character sheet. On failure: the session produced poor work; the tattoo_object is created at one tier below the target (minimum Normal). A failed Normal attempt produces a Mundane mark — aesthetically displeasing but not tracked as a tattoo_object. Decorative tattoo gate for tattooed monk schools: if the recipient belongs to the Togashi Tattooed Order, Kikage Zumi, or Hoshi Tsurui Zumi school path and has any unfilled ability tattoo slots from their current school rank (tattoos_granted_this_rank is less than the school allotment for that rank), APPLY_TATTOO for a decorative tattoo (is_ability_tattoo: false) is blocked. Refusal framing: when an OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (art_type: tattoo) is declined by this gate, the refusal is flagged as a contextual block rather than a social rejection. No disposition cost to either party. No declined_commissions entry is created for the artisan — the monk is not disinterested in the artisan or their work, they simply cannot accept at this time. The refusal topic reads as: “[Monk name] is not yet able to receive decorative work — they await their sacred marks.” The artisan may re-offer after the monk’s rank allotment is filled with no social penalty for the prior decline. The action is not available in Phase 5 for that recipient until all rank-appropriate ability tattoos are received. Ability tattoos always take precedence over personal aesthetic choices. Once the rank allotment is filled, decorative tattoos become available in any remaining body locations. Irreversible consequence: a monk who received decorative tattoos before joining a tattooed school path, or during a lapse of discipline, and has thereby occupied body locations needed for future ability tattoos, faces a permanent constraint on school progression. There is no canonical mechanism for tattoo removal. A Togashi elder cannot grant an ability tattoo when no eligible body location exists. This is a genuine consequence of poor prior choices, not a system gap to be resolved.

### 57.25.4 Disposition Bond

A permanent bidirectional disposition bond is created between artist and recipient on completion. Both the artist’s disposition toward the recipient and the recipient’s disposition toward the artist increase permanently by the quality tier amount: Normal +1, Fine +2, Exceptional +3, Masterwork +4, Legendary +5. The bond is per tattoo, fixed at the quality tier of that specific tattoo, and never degrades. Multiple tattoos from the same artist create multiple separate bonds — each body part carries its own bond at its own tier. A character with a Fine wrist tattoo and a Legendary back piece from the same artist holds two independent bonds at +2 and +5 toward that artist. The artist holds the same two bonds toward the recipient. There is no stacking cap — the bonds are distinct and additive.

### 57.25.5 Visibility and Social Read

A tattoo is socially legible only when is_visible is true. When visible, READ_CHARACTER and PROBE actions may surface it as a social detail. The subject field (what the tattoo depicts) provides narrative content for social interaction. Clan context affects how observers read a visible tattoo: Dragon characters treat tattoos as unremarkable. Crab and Mantis characters respond neutrally to positively — it is a common art form in their cultures. Crane characters note and admire fine work without practicing it; the Daidoji exception is wrist tattoos symbolising duty and family loyalty, which a Crane character may bear without social stigma. A visible tattoo in formal court from a non-Dragon, non-Crab, non-Mantis character is mildly notable — not penalised mechanically, but it registers as a social detail that READ_CHARACTER can surface. Concealing a visible tattoo (covering with clothing, gloves) is a deliberate act that may itself be noticed via Perception checks if the covering is conspicuous. Topic tattoo discovery: when a topic-type tattoo is visible, observers may learn the linked topic through two pathways. Passive roll: the observer makes Investigation / Perception or Lore: Heraldry / Intelligence against a TN reflecting how obscure the topic is. A major clan mon tattoo: TN 10. A well-known event or allegiance: TN 15. An obscure battle or regional topic: TN 20–25. A personal topic known only to a small circle: TN 30 or above. On success: the topic is added to the observer’s known_topics as if they had received it through standard topic propagation. On failure: the observer knows a tattoo is present and appears meaningful, but cannot read its significance. Active inquiry: the observer directly asks the wearer about the tattoo in conversation or through a court action. If the wearer chooses to explain, the topic transfers to the observer automatically with no roll required. The wearer controls whether personal topics are disclosed through this pathway. This creates a meaningful distinction: public or well-known topics can be read by a knowledgeable observer without the wearer’s consent; personal topics require either high Investigation skill or the wearer’s willingness to speak. A topic tattoo thus functions as a permanent passive topic carrier on the body — it can outlast the topic’s active life in the world, carrying meaning long after the original event has faded from common knowledge.

### 57.25.6 Togashi Ability Tattoos

Ability tattoos are distinct from decorative tattoos in three ways: they grant a named supernatural ability (ability_granted field set to an ability_id), they can only be applied by a Togashi school NPC of Rank 3 or above, and they require the session to take place within a Togashi province or castle. The ink used for ability tattoos incorporates the blood of Kami Togashi — a sacred secret kept entirely within Dragon territory. APPLY_TATTOO for is_ability_tattoo: true is context-gated: both artist and recipient must be AT_ZONE in a Togashi-held province or castle. If neither is in Togashi territory, the ability tattoo cannot be applied regardless of the elder’s skill or authority. Decorative tattoos have no geographic restriction and may be applied anywhere. This gate applies to both NPC elders and to Rank 3+ Togashi monks applying tattoos to themselves or others. Artisan: Tattooing rank 5 and technical mastery are necessary but not sufficient — the authority to grant an ability tattoo is a function of Togashi school rank, not artistic skill. A non-Togashi artisan of legendary skill cannot apply an ability tattoo regardless of roll result. The Togashi elder applies the ability_granted at session time through school authority, independent of roll quality. Quality tier still applies to artistry and determines the disposition bond. One ability tattoo may be active at a time per source material canon. Ability function requires exposure: is_visible must be true for the ability to be usable. A Togashi monk in full armour covering their tattoos cannot activate any of them. This is canonical and applies without exception.

Ability tattoos are granted in lieu of standard school techniques at Ranks 1, 3, and 5 of the Tattooed Monk school path. The ability_granted field references the tattoo ability table (canonical list in source material: Balance, Bamboo, Bear, Blaze, Centipede, Cloud, Crab, Crane, Dragon, Hawk, Ki-Rin, Lion, Mantis, Mountain, Ocean, Phoenix, Scorpion, Spider, Storm, Void, Volcano, Wave, Whisper, Wind, Wolf, and others). The specific ability assigned at each rank is determined by the Togashi elder applying it, within the school’s narrative logic — it is not freely chosen by the recipient. School-specific tattoo allotments: the number of ability tattoos granted per rank advancement is defined by the school, not a universal cap. Togashi Tattooed Order: 2 tattoos at Rank 1, 2 at Rank 3, 2 at Rank 5 — 6 total. Kikage Zumi: 1 at Rank 1, 1 at Rank 3, 1 at Rank 5 — 3 total. Hoshi Tsurui Zumi: 1 at game start, 1 at Rank 4 — 2 total. The system records tattoos_granted_this_advancement on the rank event and blocks further ability tattoo applications once the school allotment for that rank is reached. The one-per-body-part rule governs placement but does not override this gate — a Togashi at Rank 1 receives exactly 2 ability tattoos regardless of how many body parts remain available. Additional decorative tattoos may be applied through standard APPLY_TATTOO once all rank-appropriate ability tattoos have been received. For tattooed monk school members, decorative tattoos are blocked while any unfilled ability slot exists for the current rank (Section 57.25.3 decorative gate). This gate applies only to is_ability_tattoo: true objects for the allotment tracking; the decorative block is the separate consequence described above.

### 57.25.7 NPC Engine — Tattoo Evaluation

APPLY_TATTOO requires no commission infrastructure — no zone permission, no delivery window, no obligation record. It is a bilateral social act between two co-located consenting characters. OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (art_type: tattoo) may carry the proposal through the letter system if artist and recipient are not co-located, but no formal commission_record obligation fires. The acceptance is sufficient; both parties must subsequently be AT_ZONE together for APPLY_TATTOO to fire. NPC incitement: Dragon, Crab, and Mantis NPCs have elevated receptivity to tattoo offers (ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION NeedType scoring for the artist, disposition openness for the recipient). Togashi monks are the highest-priority recipients for ability tattoos from Togashi elders — this fires through the school rank advancement system, not through voluntary court interaction. A Togashi elder applies a tattoo when a student reaches the appropriate school rank; the trigger is school advancement, not social negotiation. Available body part check: before the NPC engine generates OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (art_type: tattoo) targeting a specific recipient, it checks the recipient’s tattoo_object list for occupied body locations. If all eligible locations are already tattooed, OFFER_ART_COMMISSION for that recipient is blocked — there is nowhere to put the work. If some locations remain available, the offer is generated and the proposed body_location is drawn from the unoccupied set. An artisan who has already tattooed every accessible location on a recipient will not continue offering to that character. SEEK_TATTOO NeedType: Togashi school monks with unfilled ability tattoo slots from their current school rank have a standing drive to seek a tattoo from a qualified elder. This NeedType activates when tattoos_granted_this_rank is less than the school’s allotment for the current rank. The NeedType deactivates when all slots for the current rank are filled and reactivates on the next rank advancement. A monk who has received all their rank allotment of tattoos is not driven by SEEK_TATTOO — the NeedType is specifically about ability tattoos, not decorative ones. Known elder pathway: if a qualified Togashi elder (school Rank 3 or above, Artisan: Tattooing rank 3 or above) exists in the monk’s met_characters, OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (art_type: tattoo) directed at that elder scores at 95. BEGIN_TRAVEL toward that elder scores at 90. This is the primary resolution path. Unknown elder pathway: if no qualified elder exists in met_characters, the monk does not simply wait. BEGIN_TRAVEL toward Dragon Clan territory (nearest Dragon province or known Togashi monastery) scores at 85 — the monk goes looking geographically even without a specific destination. ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION scores at 80 when AT_COURT or VISITING any settlement — the monk actively asks who in the area can grant tattoos. GATHER_INTELLIGENCE with “locate Togashi elder” as the query target scores at 75. The monk will travel to Dragon lands, ask at every court and monastery they pass through, and eventually find a qualified elder through the social network. Urgency scaling: the longer the monk has been at the current rank without receiving their allotment, the higher SEEK_TATTOO scores against competing objectives. Within 1 IC season of rank advancement: standard scoring. After 2 IC seasons unfilled: SEEK_TATTOO overrides most standing social objectives. After 3 IC seasons unfilled: SEEK_TATTOO scores at maximum, overriding all objectives except direct lord commands (ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE) and active military deployment. An ise zumi who has been waiting three seasons for a tattoo is spiritually incomplete and the engine reflects this urgency. BLOCKED state: if SEEK_TATTOO is active but all 9 body locations are occupied (no eligible location exists for a new ability tattoo), the NeedType transitions to BLOCKED. In BLOCKED state: urgency scaling stops. BEGIN_TRAVEL toward elders stops — the monk does not make futile journeys to elders who cannot help them. The monk accepts their spiritual ceiling. A BLOCKED flag is noted on the character sheet as a permanent state. The monk may still receive ability tattoos if a body location is freed — but since there is no canonical tattoo removal mechanism, BLOCKED is effectively permanent for a monk who has filled all 9 locations. GRANT_TATTOO NeedType: the reciprocal side of SEEK_TATTOO. Activates for Togashi elder NPCs (school Rank 3 or above, Artisan: Tattooing rank 3 or above) when a monk with an active SEEK_TATTOO NeedType is AT_ZONE with them in Togashi territory. While GRANT_TATTOO is active, APPLY_TATTOO scores at 95 in Phase 5 — the elder treats the session as their highest priority, above social actions and personal development. The elder cannot ignore a student who has come to them seeking their marks. GRANT_TATTOO deactivates when no monks with active SEEK_TATTOO are co-located in Togashi territory. If multiple seeking monks are present, the elder prioritises the one with the highest urgency score (most IC seasons at rank unfilled). The elder processes one tattoo session per IC day at their AP cost; additional seeking monks wait in sequence.

### 57.25.8 World Generation

At game start, tattoo_objects are procedurally seeded onto NPC character sheets according to clan, family, and school. Dragon NPCs (non-monk): non-Togashi Dragon samurai spawn with 0–2 decorative tattoos. Whether a specific NPC has a tattoo is probabilistic, weighted by family and personality — a Mirumoto bushi with elevated Chugi and a history of battle is more likely to have one than a Kitsuki magistrate. Togashi school monks are explicitly excluded from this rule and follow the monk rule below. Dragon tattoo artist NPCs are seeded in Dragon holdings to provide valid artist_id references: at minimum one artisan with Artisan: Tattooing rank 2+ per major Dragon province. Crab NPCs: Hida warriors and Mantis sailors have a 40–60% probability of spawning with 1–2 decorative tattoos at Normal to Fine quality, and a corresponding 40–60% probability of spawning with none. No NPC is guaranteed a tattoo. Crab and Mantis holding settlements each seed at least one tattoo artisan NPC to provide artist_id references. Crane NPCs: Daidoji family members have a 50% probability of spawning with a wrist or forearm tattoo (Normal to Fine quality, subject_type: image, duty or family loyalty theme). Not all Daidoji have the wrist tattoo — it is traditional but individual. Each major Daidoji holding seeds one Daidoji-affiliated artisan NPC with Artisan: Tattooing rank 1+. Other Crane families: no world-gen tattoos. Togashi school monks: spawn with their school-appropriate ability tattoos already applied, populated up to their current school rank allotment. A Rank 3 Togashi NPC spawns with 4 ability tattoos (2 from Rank 1, 2 from Rank 3). All world-gen ability tattoos are applied within Togashi territory — the location_of_application field is set to a Togashi castle or province. Artist_id for world-gen ability tattoos is set to a Togashi elder NPC generated alongside the monk. Decorative tattoos may additionally be present on Togashi monks based on the gap between rank advancements, applied by Dragon artisan NPCs. All other clans: no world-gen tattoos unless a specific NPC template specifies otherwise.

### 57.25.9 Provenance Investigation

A visible tattoo carries its artist_id in the world record, but a character observing the tattoo cannot directly query that field. They must investigate. Two investigation pathways exist for identifying a tattoo’s artist. Investigation (Search) / Perception: the observer studies the tattoo’s technique, style, and execution. TN by artist distinctiveness: a Legendary-quality tattoo by a master whose style is well-known = TN 15. Exceptional quality by a known regional artisan = TN 20. Fine quality, distinctive regional style = TN 25. Normal quality or deliberately generic execution = TN 30. Artisan: Tattooing rank 2+ grants 1 Free Raise — a practitioner recognises another’s hand. On success: the observer learns the artist’s identity (artist_id resolved to a named character or “unknown artisan from [region]”). Raises reveal additional details: date of application (1 Raise), subject description if not already visible (1 Raise). Lore: Heraldry / Intelligence: used when the tattoo depicts a mon, clan symbol, or affiliation marker. TN 10 for major clan symbols. TN 15 for minor clan or family symbols. TN 25 for obscure affiliations. On success: the symbolic meaning is identified. Does not reveal the artist, only what the symbol represents. Secret System cross-reference: if a topic tattoo is read via Investigation and the linked topic is a secret (Section 12.8), acquiring the topic through this pathway registers in the Secret System identically to any other method of secret acquisition. The tattoo bearer is not notified that their secret was read from their skin unless they have active Investigation or awareness actions running.

### 57.25.10 REQUEST_ART and Commission Obligation

REQUEST_ART (art_type: tattoo): a Daimyo may formally commission a tattoo artist for themselves or for a named recipient at their court. No zone_id required — the session happens wherever artist and recipient are co-located. Topic generated: “[Daimyo name] is seeking a tattoo artist for [self / recipient name].” Tier 4. Propagates through standard topic system. Any artisan NPC with Artisan: Tattooing rank 2 or above who receives the topic is incited toward OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (art_type: tattoo). Cultural reluctance applies to the recipient as normal — a REQUEST_ART does not override the recipient’s reluctance threshold. If the named recipient’s reluctance would block consent, the commission cannot proceed regardless of the lord’s desire.

Commission obligation: same structure as bonsai_gift. On acceptance of OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (art_type: tattoo), a commission_record is created. Delivery window: 1 IC season from acceptance. The artisan must travel to the recipient’s location and complete APPLY_TATTOO within the window. Window_start_date is set at acceptance. Travel pipeline: if the artisan is not co-located with the recipient at acceptance, BEGIN_TRAVEL fires automatically on the artisan’s next AP toward the recipient’s current location. Failure consequences: if the session does not occur within the window, commission_record → ABANDONED. Voluntary offer origin: disposition −3, no Honor cost. ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE origin: disposition −3 plus Honor −0.3. Failure topic fires: “[Artisan name] failed to deliver the commissioned tattoo for [Lord name].” Forgiveness mechanic: Sincerity (Honesty) or Sincerity (Deceit) / Awareness, TN 20/25/30 by timing, identical to garden and bonsai forgiveness (Section 57.23.5a). Completion: when APPLY_TATTOO succeeds, commission_record → COMPLETED. A small completion disposition bonus fires toward the commissioning lord: Normal = +2, Fine = +3, Exceptional = +5, Masterwork = +7, Legendary = +10. Smaller than garden and bonsai bonuses — the artisan created something intimate and personal rather than a displayed public work, but the lord still receives recognition for commissioning fine art.

### 57.25.11 Tattoo Ability Implementation — LOCKED

This subsection defines the mechanical implementation of each tattoo ability referenced in Section 57.25.6. All tattoos follow the general rules unless explicitly overridden: activated as Free Actions, only one tattoo active at a time, tattoos must be exposed (is_visible must be true) to activate, and default duration is 2 × School Rank rounds on the ASCII map. On the world map, the default duration is one AP slot (half an IC day) unless stated otherwise. Two tattoos have permanent passive effects that do not block other tattoos (Mantis, Ocean passive). Two tattoos fire instantaneously and do not count as “active” for the one-tattoo-at-a-time rule (Ocean active, Phoenix). Two tattoos function exclusively on the ASCII map (Dragon, Wind). Two tattoos function exclusively on the world map (Centipede, Whisper). All others function on both layers at their respective durations.

Balance: Modifies the TN of Spell Casting Rolls targeting the monk by (2 × School Rank) + 5. The system reads the spell’s spell_intent tag (beneficial or harmful) and automatically applies the correct direction: harmful spells have their TN increased (harder to land), beneficial spells have their TN decreased (easier to land). No player menu. Balance only applies to spells that explicitly select the monk as a target. Area-of-effect spells that include the monk in their area do not trigger Balance. If an enemy casts a beneficial-tagged spell, the TN still decreases — the tattoo reads the spell’s nature, not the caster’s intentions. The monk’s counter is to deactivate Balance by activating a different tattoo.

Bamboo: Armor TN increased by (2 × School Rank) + 5. Flat additive modifier to Armor TN, stacking into the standard formula: base (Reflexes × 5 + 5) + armor bonus + Bamboo modifier. Mutually exclusive with physical armor due to the tattoo exposure rule.

Bear: The monk chooses one of two effects at activation: Stamina increased by School Rank, or Strength increased by half School Rank (rounded up). Binary menu at activation; choice locked for the tattoo’s duration. The Stamina boost can cascade into Earth Ring if Willpower is equal to or greater than base Stamina — this is standard L5R Trait-to-Ring math and is intentional. All downstream calculations referencing Stamina or Earth automatically pick up the temporary modifier.

Blaze: Unarmed strikes deal additional fire damage equal to Fire Ring + School Rank. Single combined damage roll — base unarmed damage plus fire bonus, resolved together against the target’s Reduction. The fire bonus portion carries a fire tag. Creatures with fire-specific Reduction reduce only the fire bonus, not the base unarmed damage.

Centipede: World map only — cannot be activated on the ASCII map. Costs 2 AP (one full IC day). The monk moves to any land-connected destination on the world map. Ocean crossings block the movement — the monk cannot cross water or reach islands. No travel events fire during the movement (Section 57.50). The monk suffers 12 IC hours of exhaustion afterward, losing the next IC day’s Morning AP slot. Total effective cost: 3 AP (2 activation + 1 lost to exhaustion).

Cloud: Any physical attack (melee or ranged) that successfully hits the monk triggers a second attack roll from the attacker using the same modifiers. If the second roll fails to meet or exceed the monk’s Armor TN, the hit is negated entirely — no damage, no effects. While Cloud is active, the monk cannot make attacks of any kind. Spells that deal damage directly through a casting TN (no attack roll) are unaffected by Cloud. The counter to a Cloud monk is magic. Modified from source: expanded from melee-only to all physical attacks for thematic consistency.

Crab: Gain Reduction equal to Earth Ring. Flat Reduction rating. Standard Reduction stacking rules apply.

Crane: On activation, gain a pool of bonus dice equal to (School Rank + Air Ring). Each die adds +1k0 to any Social Skill Roll (Courtier, Etiquette, Sincerity, Temptation, and similar). Cannot add more dice to a single roll than Void Ring. Duration: one AP slot on the world map, standard tattoo duration (2 × School Rank rounds) on the ASCII map. Activating another tattoo loses all unspent dice. The pool forces a resource management decision: dump maximum dice on one critical negotiation, or spread them across several conversations. Modified from source: duration changed from one hour to one AP slot to match the world map time scale.

Dragon: As a Complex Action, breathe fire in a cone. On the ASCII map at 1 tile = 5 feet, the cone is 2–3 tiles long (10 feet + School Rank) and 1 tile wide at the end. The monk picks one of 8 cardinal or diagonal directions; the cone extends from the monk’s tile. Damage: Fire Ring k Fire Ring (DR equal to Fire Ring) against all targets in the cone. The monk takes flat damage equal to their Fire Ring on each use. Per-use ability — each use costs a Complex Action. The monk can breathe fire multiple rounds in succession while the tattoo remains active, paying the self-damage each time. ASCII map only.

Hawk: As a Complex Action, leap a distance equal to Water Ring × 5 tiles on the ASCII map. Per-use ability. The monk picks a destination tile within range and lands there. The monk is airborne during the leap — no intervening tile collision, leaps over enemies without provoking engagement. Must land on a valid surface tile (leap, not flight). Landing adjacent to an enemy triggers normal engagement rules. Vertical clearance is unlimited — rooftops, chasms, and walls are all valid leap targets.

Ki-Rin: Upon activation, the monk may re-roll any one of their own rolls per round, keeping the higher of the two results. Per-round flag: kirin_reroll_available resets to true at the start of each round and flips to false when used. Universal — applies to attack rolls, damage rolls, skill checks, resistance rolls, and any other roll the monk makes. Cannot force an enemy to re-roll. On the world map, applies once per ActionID roll.

Lion: Upon activation, the monk gains temporary ranks equal to School Rank in any single Bugei Skill of their choice. Menu at activation — the monk picks one Bugei skill (as defined in Section 24). Choice locked for the tattoo’s duration. The bonus stacks on top of existing ranks. Capped at Rank 10 — cannot exceed the L5R skill ceiling.

Mantis: Complete immunity to all Fear effects. Always active — does not require activation and does not prevent the use of other tattoos. Permanent passive flag: mantis_tattoo = true. Whenever a Fear effect targets the monk, the Fear roll is skipped entirely. Interacts with the Fear system in Section 46. This is the only tattoo that is permanently active and does not block other tattoos from its inception.

Mountain: All Wound Penalties are reduced by (School Rank + 2) while active. Flat modifier subtracted from the current Wound Penalty, minimum 0 — the penalty cannot become a bonus. Standard Wound Rank penalties: Nicked (−0), Grazed (−3), Hurt (−5), Injured (−10), Crippled (−15), Down (−20), Out (−40). A Rank 3 monk at Injured suffers −5 instead of −10.

Ocean: Two layers. Passive (always active, does not block other tattoos): the monk no longer requires food or drink. Permanent flag: ocean_tattoo = true. The monk is exempt from starvation and deprivation mechanics. During a siege, the monk does not consume food from the garrison stockpile. Active (instantaneous, does not count as “active” for the one-tattoo-at-a-time rule): the monk is instantly refreshed as if they had slept eight hours, and all spent Void Points are restored. Sets rested_last_night = true and refills Void Points to maximum. Cooldown: once per OOC day. Tracked via ocean_last_used_ooc_day field on the character sheet.

Phoenix: Reactive trigger — fires automatically when the monk is reduced to the Down Wound Rank or below. Requires at least 1 unspent Void Point. All unspent Void Points are lost. The monk immediately heals wounds equal to School Rank × 10. Phoenix counts as activating a tattoo — it replaces whatever tattoo was previously active. Cooldown: 1 IC week (2 OOC days). Tracked via phoenix_last_used_ic_day field on the character sheet. The 1 Void Point gate means a monk who has spent all Void Points cannot trigger Phoenix — resource management matters.

Scorpion: Two simultaneous effects while active. First: additional rolled dice on all Stealth Skill Rolls equal to School Rank (+School Rank k0). Second: unarmed attacks automatically Daze the opponent if any die in the damage roll explodes (rolls 10). The Daze condition is automatic — no resistance roll, no save. Both effects run simultaneously as part of the same tattoo for the standard duration.

Spider: While active, the monk automatically succeeds on all Athletics (Climbing) checks. No climbing roll is required. Movement rate while climbing is halved. If the tattoo deactivates while the monk is mid-climb, they must begin rolling Athletics (Climbing) normally from that point — failure means falling with standard falling damage. Simplified from source: reframed from wall-walking to automatic climbing success at half speed.

Storm: While active, the monk’s unarmed attacks require only 1 Raise to use the Knockdown Maneuver instead of the standard Raise cost. Applies to unarmed attacks only. The standard Contested Strength Roll for Knockdown still applies after the hit lands. Synergy with Wave (which boosts the Contested Roll), but only one can be active at a time.

Void: While active, the monk senses all living things within a radius of School Rank × 2 tiles on the ASCII map (School Rank × 10 feet). Detection penetrates walls, darkness, Stealth, and concealment. The system reports count and general category per direction using three categories: Humanoid (humans, Nezumi, Naga, Tsuno, and similar bipedal intelligent creatures), Small Animal (rabbits, foxes, birds, rats, snakes), and Big Animal (horses, bears, boars, large predators, ogres). Format: “3 Humanoid, north.” Characters in the monk’s met_characters list are identified by name within the detection radius. Living/undead distinction: oni register as living (alive but corrupted), skeletal undead and ghosts do not register.

Volcano: While active, the monk’s skin takes on the appearance of lava. Three weapon interaction branches determined by the attacking weapon’s material tag (requires weapons in the equipment system to carry an explicit material tag: wood, metal, or sacred/nemuranai). Wood or flammable weapons (including arrows): Reduction 5, and the weapon is destroyed (removed from the attacker’s inventory). Metal weapons: normal damage, but the attacker must make a Contested Fire Roll (attacker’s Fire vs monk’s Fire). If the attacker loses, the weapon drops to their tile. Sacred Weapons and nemuranai are immune to this disarm effect. The Contested Fire Roll fires on every successful hit, not once per engagement. Unarmed attacks: normal damage, no additional effects.

Wave: While active, the monk gains +Xk0 (where X is Insight Rank) on the Contested Strength Roll when performing a Knockdown maneuver with an unarmed attack. The same +Xk0 bonus applies defensively to resist all enemy Knockdown attempts, regardless of whether they are made unarmed or with weapons. Offensive bonus is unarmed Knockdown only; defensive bonus applies against all Knockdown sources.

Whisper: World map only — cannot be activated on the ASCII map. Mechanically identical to WRITE_LETTER (Section 12.7) in every way except three differences: delivery is instant (zero transit time), no letter object exists (nothing to intercept via INTERCEPT_LETTER, nothing to forge via FORGE_IMPERSONATION_LETTER, nothing to detect), and range is capped at 2 provinces per School Rank. The monk uses the same letter budget as any character: non-lord monks follow the standard letter allowance (1 free per IC day, optional 1 AP for 4 additional). Lord monks pay Civilian Orders per message per Section 57.34. One topic per message. Target must be in the monk’s met_characters list. The monk does not need to know the target’s current location — the whisper finds them if within range, fails silently if not. Calligraphy roll does not apply (no physical brushwork). No reply system — communication is one-way unless the target also has the Whisper tattoo. Orders (ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE) can be delivered via Whisper at the same Civilian Order cost as a letter. The tattoo slot is the additional cost beyond the normal letter budget — every AP slot with Whisper active is an AP slot without a combat or defensive tattoo. Modified from source: range changed from miles to provinces (2 per School Rank), reframed as instant WRITE_LETTER with same costs and budgets.

Wind: While active, the monk gains an additional Simple Action per round that cannot be used to make an attack. The monk’s maximum movement per round is no longer limited to Water Ring × 20 feet. Combined with the extra Move Action, the monk becomes the fastest character on the map. ASCII map only — no world map application (movement on the world map is governed by BEGIN_TRAVEL, not per-round actions). Standard duration (2 × School Rank rounds).

Wolf: While active, the monk gains Free Raises on Hunting (Tracking) Skill Rolls equal to School Rank. Duration is unique: School Rank hours (not the standard rounds-based duration). On the world map, this lasts School Rank hours. On the ASCII map, the hour-based duration effectively lasts the entire mission. The tattoo occupies the active slot for its full duration — no combat or defensive tattoos while tracking.

Equipment system dependency: Volcano requires weapons to carry an explicit material tag (wood, metal, sacred/nemuranai). This tag must be defined in the equipment data (Section 39) for Volcano’s branching logic to function. Most weapons have implicit materials (katana = metal, bo staff = wood, arrows = wood shaft) but the tag must be made explicit at implementation.

Character sheet fields required: mantis_tattoo (boolean, permanent), ocean_tattoo (boolean, permanent), ocean_last_used_ooc_day (int), phoenix_last_used_ic_day (int), crane_pool (int, transient), kirin_reroll_available (boolean, per-round), active_tattoo_id (ability_id or null). These fields are additions to Section 22.3.
