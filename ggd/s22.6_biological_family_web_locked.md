## 22.6 Biological Family Web — LOCKED

Every named character exists within a biological family web distinct from their institutional clan/family membership. The biological family tracks actual blood relationships — parents, siblings, grandparents, great-grandparents — across four generations. These relationships cross institutional boundaries through marriage, creating the intricate web of personal connections that defines Rokugani society.

**Two Layers of Family — LOCKED:**

- Institutional Family: The clan and family name a character belongs to by oath, adoption, or birth. Every Akodo is 'family' in the broad social sense. Already handled by the Clan + Family system.

- Biological Family: Actual blood relatives across four generations. Parents, siblings, grandparents, and great-grandparents. These create intimate personal connections that can cross institutional clan lines through marriage.

**Four Generation Depth — LOCKED:**

- Generation 1 (Active): The character themselves. Full character sheet. Lives in the world.

- Generation 2 (Parents): Usually living or recently deceased. May have full character sheets if still active in the world. Strong personal connection.

- Generation 3 (Grandparents): Mostly deceased. Lightweight records — name, clan, family, dates, spouse, children. Meaningful but distant connection.

- Generation 4 (Great-grandparents): Almost certainly deceased. Lightweight historical records only. Connection is historical rather than personal — you know who they were but likely never met them.

**Family Disposition Modifiers — LOCKED:**

- Siblings: Strong permanent positive disposition modifier — the closest biological bond.

- Parents/Children: Strong permanent positive disposition modifier.

- Grandparents/Grandchildren: Moderate permanent positive disposition modifier.

- First cousins: Small permanent positive disposition modifier.

- Cross-clan relatives through marriage: Small permanent positive disposition modifier — even if the institutional clans are enemies, blood ties create some warmth.

- These are permanent modifiers in the disposition system (Section 12.2) — they never decay.
