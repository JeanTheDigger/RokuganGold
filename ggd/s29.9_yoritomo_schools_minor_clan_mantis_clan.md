## 29.9 Yoritomo Schools (Minor Clan — Mantis Clan)

In the game’s default era the Yoritomo family exists as an independent Minor Clan (the Mantis Clan). The schools listed here represent their native traditions. Note: in later eras, after Yoritomo’s Alliance and elevation to Great Clan status, the Yoritomo schools absorb or affiliate with several additional family schools — see Section 29.10 for those families and their notation.

**BASIC SCHOOLS:**

**Yoritomo Bushi [CR]:**

Masters of shipboard combat. Centuries of fighting from ship to ship and on beaches have produced a highly unusual style that incorporates the pitching of waves into fluid movement. The Yoritomo have learned to exploit any weakness in an opponent’s defenses and to fight effectively with unconventional peasant-class weapons — a practical adaptation to the corrosive effect of sea air on steel. Jacks of all trades: potent attacks, unhindered movement, and the ability to strip Armor TN.

- Benefit: +1 Strength

- Skills: Commerce, Defense, Jiujutsu (Improvised Weapons), Kenjutsu, Knives (Kama), Sailing, any one Skill

- Honor: 3.5 | Outfit: Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 weapon (or pair of Knives), Traveling Pack, 10 koku

- Rank 1 — The Way of the Mantis: No penalties to movement or attacks for rough or uneven terrain. No Glory or Honor loss for using improvised weapons or Peasant keyword weapons in combat. No off-hand penalties for a Small or Medium Peasant keyword weapon. +1k0 to all attack rolls.

- Rank 2 — Voice of the Storm: Every time you hit an opponent in melee, their Armor TN is reduced by 5 (applies only to your attacks, lasts 2 Rounds, cumulative up to School Rank times; duration resets with each application).

- Rank 3 — Strike of the Mantis: Attack as a Simple Action with weapons with the Samurai or Peasant keyword.

- Rank 4 — The Rolling Wave: If you move at least 5 feet, gain +10 to Armor TN until your next Turn. May sacrifice this bonus before rolling any attack to gain two Free Raises usable only for the Knockdown Maneuver.

- Rank 5 — Hand of Osano-Wo: Spend a Void Point as a Free Action — keep additional damage dice equal to your Strength. Against Prone targets, also gain +0k2 to damage roll totals.

**Yoritomo Courtier [CR]:**

Built around intimidation and, frankly, bullying. Highly effective against opponents unaccustomed to such tactics or weak-willed targets — but more capable opponents like the Crane or Scorpion may be completely immune to their primary abilities. Since elevation to Great Clan status, the school has begun finding success in more varied political arenas.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower

- Skills: Commerce (Appraisal), Courtier, Defense, Etiquette, Intimidation (Control), Sincerity, any one Merchant or Lore Skill

- Honor: 2.5 | Outfit: Extravagant Clothing, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Calligraphy Set, Traveling Pack, 10 koku

- Rank 1 — Duty Before Honor: No Glory or Honor loss for using Commerce in public. No Honor loss for using Intimidation (Control). Against ronin, bandits, gang members, mercenaries, pirates, and other lawless types, gain Free Raises equal to your School Rank on all Social Skill Rolls.

- Rank 2 — Storm Heart: When using Intimidation (Control), your Willpower is considered one Rank higher. Against a samurai of lower Status Rank, two Ranks higher instead.

- Rank 3 — Command the Winds: School Rank times per session, if you have failed a Sincerity Social Skill Roll, reroll it as an Intimidation (Control) Social Skill Roll instead. Must keep the Intimidation result.

- Rank 4 — Will of the Storm: When initiating a conversation, make a Contested Intimidation (Control)/Willpower vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Willpower roll. On success, the target cannot spend Void Points against you for the next hour and suffers −3k0 on all Social Rolls made against you. Cannot be used again until the effect expires.

- Rank 5 — Strength in All Things: +5k0 on any Intimidation roll, and on any roll to resist Intimidation, Temptation, or a Fear effect.

**Yoritomo Shugenja:**

Descended from Kaimetsu-Uo’s daughter and her ronin husband, both gifted with the ability to speak to kami. No shugenja in the Empire is as at home on the seas as one from the Yoritomo family. Honed at the infamous Tempest Island — a small island renowned for the battering it receives during storm season.

- Benefit: +1 Perception

- Skills: Athletics, Calligraphy, Knives, Lore: Theology, Meditation, Sailing (Navigation), any 1 Skill

- Honor: 4.5 | Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 10 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Water / Earth | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Water, 2 Fire, 1 Air

- Technique — Child of the Sea: Expend one spell slot to alter wind conditions in your current location by one degree. Spending a Void Point instead shifts entire weather status one degree. Maximum combined spell slots and/or Void Points equal to School Rank per Round. Affects an area School Rank miles in all directions. Free Raise on all spells with the Thunder keyword.

**ADVANCED SCHOOLS:**

**Kobune Captain [Bushi] [EE]:**

The trusted servants of the Mantis Clan Champion — responsible for the kobune fleets that ferry materials and personnel across the seas. Masters of the wave in every sense: commerce, command, and battle aboard ships. Young samurai look upon them as inspiration; the arrogance of the Yoritomo is, at minimum, well earned.

- Requirements: Water 3 | Skills: Commerce 4, Knives 3, Sailing 4 | Other: Must possess the Leadership Advantage and have been appointed to a position of command within the Mantis Clan.

- Rank 1 — The Joy of Plunder: Status Rank considered one higher when interacting with all Mantis Clan members and heimin merchants. Spending a Void Point on any Merchant Skill grants +2k2 instead of +1k1. Once per month, make a Commerce/Intelligence roll (TN 25) and gain koku equal to the amount by which your roll exceeds the TN.

- Rank 2 — Strength of the Mantis: Gain 30 XP usable only to purchase the Servants Advantage (representing your crew). Budoka servants are instead considered Rank 1 Yoritomo Bushi. May spend 10 XP to gain a Rank 1 Moshi Shugenja navigator (Air 3 rather than all Rings at 2).

- Rank 3 — Master of the Seas: Spend a Void Point on any Merchant Skill Roll (excluding Craft Skills) to replace the normal dice rolled with 10k10. +3k0 to all Bugei Skill Rolls made while aboard a water-borne vessel.

**Storm Riders [Shugenja] [CR]:**

The elite shugenja unit at the heart of the Mantis armadas. Undisputed masters of the sea — their value in naval combat is fearsome to any who challenge the Mantis Clan’s dominance of the waters.

- Requirements: Air 3, Fire 3, Water 5 | Skills: Lore: The Sea 5, Sailing 3, Spellcraft 3, Lore: Theology 3 | Other: Elemental Blessing (Water) Advantage.

- Rank 1 — Strength of Suitengu: Shugenja School Rank considered one higher for casting Water spells. Spend a Void Point as a Complex Action to release a lightning bolt — one target within 10’ × Water Ring, dealing Air Ring kept damage dice. Expend additional spell slots for +1k0 damage per slot, up to your Storm Rider Rank.

- Rank 2 — The Raging Ocean: Shugenja School Rank increases by one. Water Ring considered 2 Ranks higher for determining number of Water spells castable per day.

- Rank 3 — Child of Osano-Wo: Shugenja School Rank considered one higher for casting Water spells. Spend a Void Point as a Free Action to gain immunity to all spells with the Thunder keyword for 3 Rounds (Mastery Level 3 and lower only).

**ALTERNATE PATHS:**

**Yoritomo Emissaries [Courtier]:**

The exception to the Mantis Clan’s general diplomatic habits — well-trained court functionaries who leaven Yoritomo aggression with the calm patience of the Earth, outlasting opponents’ anger to find peaceful solutions.

- Replaces: Yoritomo Courtier 4. Requires: Etiquette (Courtesy) 4.

- Technique — Intrepid Negotiator: When making an Etiquette or Sincerity roll, spend a Void Point to add Willpower in unkept dice to the roll. If specifically using Etiquette (Courtesy), add Willpower in kept dice instead.

**Yoritomo Scoundrel [Bushi] [CR]:**

Old habits from the Mantis’ history as smugglers, pirates, and mercenaries. The Scoundrel does what he must, despite what society says about his activities — because it works.

- Replaces: Yoritomo Bushi 2. Requires: Commerce 2, Sailing 2.

- Technique — Revel in Villainy: Whenever you spend a Void Point to enhance an Athletics, Commerce, Sailing, Sincerity, or Low Skill Roll, gain an additional bonus to the roll equal to twice your School Rank.

**Mantis Brawler [Bushi]:**

Born out of dirty shipboard fights and drunken bar-brawls. Passed down parent to child through direct instruction rather than formal dojo. Found among ronin, commoner wako pirate gangs, and smuggling cartels as much as within the clan itself.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Athletics, Commerce, Intimidation, Jiujutsu, Knives, Sailing, any 1 Weapon Skill

- Honor: 3.5 | Outfit: Rugged Clothing, any 2 weapons, Kama, Tanto, Traveling Pack, 4 koku

- Rank 1 — Way of Drunken Fists: While Prone, suffer no penalties to Armor TN or attacks with Small weapons or unarmed (still gain Prone bonus vs ranged). +1k0 to rolls to take control of Grapples and to damage rolls with unarmed strikes, improvised weapons, and Small weapons.

- Rank 2 — Drunk Loses His Sandal: After a successful Feint, reduce the extra damage by 5 to instead add +5 to Armor TN until the start of the next Turn (+10 if you were Prone when performing the Feint). Immediately after any enemy attack resolves against you, take a Free Action to become Prone.

- Rank 3 — Drunk Never Falls: Do not suffer penalties for Fatigued, Dazed, or intoxication. If Stunned, may still take one Simple Action per Round.

- Rank 4 — Two Drunks Dance: Attack as a Simple Action when fighting unarmed, with an improvised weapon, or with a Small weapon.

- Rank 5 — Drunk Pounds a Door: Spend a Void Point on a melee attack to gain +4k1 to both the attack roll and the damage roll. If Prone, the bonus is +4k2 instead.

**Mantis Navigator [Shugenja] [CR]:**

The shugenja who serve aboard Mantis vessels — capable of placating the elements even under extreme circumstances. Their whispered prayers can calm the roughest seas and clear the darkest skies.

- Replaces: Any Mantis Shugenja School 3. Requires: Sailing (Navigation) 3.

- Technique — The Fortunes’ Guidance: When confronted with difficult weather, spend a Void Point as a Simple Action to negate the conditions in an area School Rank × 20 feet in all directions. In a skirmish, effect lasts School Rank Rounds; outside combat, School Rank hours. If the weather effect is a spell, may only negate spells of Mastery Level equal to or less than School Rank, and must also expend spell slots equal to the Mastery Level in addition to the Void Point.

**Mantis Whirlwind Fighters [Bushi]:**

Credited to Yoritomo himself. Relentless motion combined with relentless attacks — obstructing enemy blows while overwhelming foes with rapid strikes. Often the preferred dueling form within the Yoritomo family.

- Replaces: Yoritomo Bushi 4. Requires: Knives (Kama) 5.

- Technique — Waves Rush to Shore: When fighting with a kama in each hand, add your Knives Skill Rank to your Armor TN and gain +3k0 to damage rolls with kama.

**Student of the Cliff’s Edge [Bushi]:**

The most advanced chain-weapon fighters in the Mantis Clan train at the Cliff’s Edge Dojo. The kusarigama was wielded by clan founder Kaimetsu-Uo himself. Many students undertake special pilgrimages to reach this level of mastery.

- Replaces: Tsuruchi Bounty Hunter 3 or Yoritomo Bushi 4. Requires: Agility 4, Chain Weapons 5.

- Technique — Howl of the Cliff’s Edge: When using a kusarigama to make a Knockdown or Disarm maneuver, gain +2k1 to the attack roll. Call three Raises on a kusarigama attack to Entangle an opponent up to 15’ away. The condition lasts until you voluntarily release the foe (Free Action) or the foe succeeds in a Contested Strength Roll against you (Simple Action).

**Mantis Orochi Rider [Bushi]:**

A small but extremely powerful element of the Mantis military in the latter half of the twelfth century — the closest thing the clan has to a cavalry tradition. The Orochi Riders form spiritual bonds with the great sea-serpents and ride them into battle.

- Replaces: Any Mantis Basic/Advanced School Technique at Insight Rank 6, or a “blank” Insight Rank after attaining Rank 5 in any Mantis Basic School. If trained entirely in Mantis Shugenja Schools, may take at Rank 5 (treated as Shugenja Path). Requires: Water 4, Athletics 5, Lore: Spirit Realms 5. Must be specially chosen and successfully bond with an Orochi.

- Technique — The Orochi Pact: Gain an Orochi sea-serpent as a mount (GM-controlled NPC that generally obeys your commands). +2k0 to Athletics rolls. Two Ranks of Magic Resistance against hostile Water Magic. While riding the Orochi, hold your breath underwater for twice the normal amount of time.

**Yoritomo Sculptors [Artisan]:**

Sailors’ penchant for wood-carving on long voyages developed over time into a modest school. Commercial ties with Crane and Phoenix helped the clan develop its casual skills into a true Technique used for political favor and personal leverage.

- Replaces: Any Mantis Bushi or Courtier 2. Requires: Artisan: Sculpture or Craft: Sculpture 4.

- Technique — Watanabe’s Legacy: +1k1 when using Artisan: Sculpture and Craft: Sculpture. May ignore all circumstantial or environmental penalties (distractions, weather, noise, etc.) on rolls with those two Skills.

**Soldier of the Three Man Alliance [Bushi] [IH]:**

Formed by combined efforts of experienced soldiers from the Sparrow and Wasp clans during the formation of the Three Man Alliance — the first step toward the Minor Clans banding together. The technique survives the Alliance’s supersession by Yoritomo’s Alliance, still occasionally taught in Suzume and Tsuruchi dojo decades later.

- Replaces: Tsuruchi Archer 2, Tsuruchi Bounty Hunter 2, or Suzume Bushi 2. Requires: Battle 2.

- Technique — Stand Against Oppression: When facing an opponent with a higher Glory Rank, spend a Void Point to gain bonus unkept dice on your attack roll equal to the difference in Glory Ranks. Cannot gain more dice than your School Rank from this Technique.
