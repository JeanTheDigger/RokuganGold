# 48. Insight, Rank Advancement & Progression

This section defines how player characters grow over time in the Rokugan game. Advancement is not a single resource — it is the product of two parallel systems working together: Training (time and discipline spent improving a trait) and Experience Points (rewards earned through deeds, quests, and achievement). Both feed the same underlying progress bars. Neither alone is sufficient for rapid advancement. A samurai who trains diligently but never risks himself will grow, but slowly. A samurai who seeks glory and danger but neglects his discipline will find his gains harder to consolidate. The samurai who does both will become a legend — if he survives.

**INSIGHT ****&**** RANK**

Insight is the numerical measure of a character's overall development. It is calculated as:

Insight = (sum of all five Ring values x 10) + total Skill ranks across all Skills

Insight Rank is determined by the character's total Insight score against the following thresholds:

- Insight 1–149: Rank 1

- Insight 150–174: Rank 2

- Insight 175–199: Rank 3

- Insight 200–224: Rank 4

- Insight 225–249: Rank 5

- Insight 250–274: Rank 6

- Insight 275–299: Rank 7

- Insight 300–324: Rank 8

- Every additional 25 Insight beyond 324: +1 Rank

Insight Rank determines what Techniques a character may learn, what Mastery Level spells a shugenja may cast, and how the world perceives and interacts with the character. A Rank 1 samurai is a young warrior fresh from his gempuku. A Rank 5 samurai is a seasoned, formidable figure — rare, respected, and dangerous. Rank 5 and above carries a 'wow' factor in the world: NPCs react differently, political weight shifts, and enemies think twice before engaging.

**SCHOOL RANK ****&**** TECHNIQUES**

School Rank tracks a character's progression within their specific School and is identical to Insight Rank for standard Schools. When a character's Insight crosses a Rank threshold, their School Rank advances with it — but they do not automatically learn the next Technique. To formally learn the new Rank Technique, the character must visit their School's dojo or seek out their Sensei and spend time in formal instruction. This visit represents the ritual acknowledgement of their advancement and the transmission of the School's closely guarded secrets. A character who has crossed the Insight threshold but has not yet made this visit is considered to be 'between Ranks' — their abilities reflect their new Insight, but the Technique remains locked.

Shugenja Schools are an exception. Rather than a new Technique at each Rank, a shugenja who advances gains: an increase in the number of dice rolled on Spell Casting Rolls (School Rank now applies directly), access to higher Mastery Level spells (new Rank determines the ceiling), and three new spells each of which must be of a Mastery Level accessible at the new Rank. These benefits are still received through formal Rank advancement at the dojo or with a Sensei.

**THE PROGRESS BAR SYSTEM**

Every Ring and every Skill has a hidden progress bar. Progress accumulates from Training and from spending Experience Points. When the bar fills completely, the Ring or Skill increases by one rank and the bar resets to zero. The amount of progress required to fill a bar scales with the rank being achieved — higher ranks demand proportionally more effort. Rings are significantly harder to advance than Skills, reflecting their nature as deep, fundamental aspects of a character's soul rather than learned techniques.

Progress required per rank-up:

**Skill Progress Costs**

- Skill rank 0 to 1: 1,000 progress

- Skill rank 1 to 2: 2,000 progress

- Skill rank 2 to 3: 3,000 progress

- Skill rank 3 to 4: 4,000 progress

- Skill rank 4 to 5: 5,000 progress

**Ring Progress Costs**

- Ring rank 0 to 1: 4,000 progress

- Ring rank 1 to 2: 8,000 progress

- Ring rank 2 to 3: 12,000 progress

- Ring rank 3 to 4: 16,000 progress

- Ring rank 4 to 5: 20,000 progress

Rings cost four times as much progress as a Skill of equivalent rank. This reflects the L5R 4th Edition tabletop design principle that raising a Ring Trait costs four times as much Experience as raising a Skill to the same rank. In the tabletop, raising Reflexes from 2 to 3 costs 12 XP while raising a Skill from 2 to 3 costs 3 XP. The progress bar system preserves this ratio exactly.

**TRAINING**

Training is the daily discipline of the samurai. A character may spend Action Points on Training as one of their available IC-day activities. Training applies progress directly to a chosen Skill or Ring's progress bar. A character may train any Skill or Ring they possess — but they may only train one trait per Training action.

Action Points and Training:

- Each IC day has 2 Action Point slots: Morning and Afternoon.

- One Training action costs 1 AP.

- A character may train twice per IC day (once Morning, once Afternoon) if they dedicate both slots to training — though most samurai will split their AP across training, quests, court duties, and personal activities.

- Since 1 OOC day equals 4 IC days, a character has 8 AP available per OOC day total across all activities.

Progress earned per Training AP:

- Solo training: 50 progress per AP

- Training under a Sensei (Sensei's Skill/Ring rank is 1 above student's): 75 progress per AP

- Training under a Sensei (Sensei's Skill/Ring rank is 2 or more above student's): 100 progress per AP

Training time reference — Solo:

- Skill 0 to 1: 20 AP (2.5 OOC days)

- Skill 1 to 2: 40 AP (5.0 OOC days)

- Skill 2 to 3: 60 AP (7.5 OOC days)

- Skill 3 to 4: 80 AP (10.0 OOC days)

- Skill 4 to 5: 100 AP (12.5 OOC days)

- Ring 2 to 3: 240 AP (30.0 OOC days)

- Ring 3 to 4: 320 AP (40.0 OOC days)

- Ring 4 to 5: 400 AP (50.0 OOC days)

Training time reference — With Sensei (1 rank above):

- Skill 0 to 1: 13 AP (1.7 OOC days)

- Skill 1 to 2: 27 AP (3.3 OOC days)

- Skill 2 to 3: 40 AP (5.0 OOC days)

- Skill 3 to 4: 53 AP (6.7 OOC days)

- Skill 4 to 5: 67 AP (8.3 OOC days)

- Ring 2 to 3: 160 AP (20.0 OOC days)

- Ring 3 to 4: 213 AP (26.7 OOC days)

- Ring 4 to 5: 267 AP (33.3 OOC days)

**THE SENSEI SYSTEM**

A Sensei is a teacher — a character whose Skill rank in the relevant trait exceeds the student's. The Sensei system applies only to Skill training, not Ring training. Rings are developed through personal discipline, meditation, and lived experience — they cannot meaningfully be taught by another person. Skills, by contrast, are techniques, forms, and bodies of knowledge that a master can actively transmit to a student.

Requirements for Sensei training:

- The Sensei's rank in the relevant Skill must exceed the student's current rank.

- Both Sensei and student must be in the same location.

- Both spend 1 AP for the training session.

- The Sensei gains a small amount of progress themselves at a reduced rate — teaching reinforces mastery.

Sensei multipliers apply based on the rank difference in the specific Skill being trained. A Sensei who is one rank above the student provides 75 progress per AP (1.5x solo rate). A Sensei who is two or more ranks above provides 100 progress per AP (2.0x solo rate). If the Sensei's rank equals or falls below the student's rank in that Skill, no bonus applies and the student trains at the solo rate.

The Sensei system is thematically central to Rokugani society. Finding a willing, qualified Sensei — especially for rare or restricted Skills — is itself a meaningful in-game objective. A Sensei from an opposing clan, a ronin master, or a retired legend willing to teach is a significant relationship worth pursuing and protecting.

**EXPERIENCE POINTS**

Experience Points (XP) are earned through quests, missions, and achievements. They represent the character's growth through deed and consequence — the lessons learned from success, failure, danger, and triumph. XP is spent directly on progress bars, filling them as an alternative to Training time. XP and Training both advance the same bar. A character may use XP to fill part of a bar and Training to fill the rest — there is no restriction on mixing the two.

XP to progress conversion: 1 XP = 200 progress on any Skill or Ring bar.

XP cost reference:

- Skill 0 to 1: 5 XP

- Skill 1 to 2: 10 XP

- Skill 2 to 3: 15 XP

- Skill 3 to 4: 20 XP

- Skill 4 to 5: 25 XP

- Ring 2 to 3: 60 XP

- Ring 3 to 4: 80 XP

- Ring 4 to 5: 100 XP

**QUEST XP TIERS**

XP is awarded upon quest completion. The amount varies by the danger and significance of the quest. High-risk quests are not available every day — they represent genuine events in the world (battles, crises, political emergencies) and are limited by world state. A player cannot simply choose to do dangerous quests endlessly; the world must generate the conditions for them.

- Minor (errand, minor court duty, routine patrol): 0.05 XP

- Standard (investigation, court challenge, meaningful mission): 0.10 XP

- Dangerous (skirmish, hostile negotiation, serious risk of injury): 0.20 XP

- High-risk (battle, assassination mission, major political crisis): 0.35 XP

- Exceptional (war arc, major story event, world-changing consequence): 0.50 XP

The risk-reward relationship is intentional. A samurai who seeks out danger and survives will advance significantly faster than one who plays cautiously. A samurai who dies advances nothing. This tension — grow faster by risking everything, or grow slowly and safely — is the central pacing dynamic of the progression system.

**ADVANCEMENT BENCHMARKS**

The following benchmarks describe expected progression timelines under different playstyles. These assume consistent daily play at 4 IC days per OOC day.

- Cautious player (standard quests + 2 AP training per OOC day): approximately 120 progress per OOC day. Expected time to Rank 5: approximately 2.7 OOC years.

- Aggressive player (high-risk quests + 4 AP training per OOC day): approximately 270 progress per OOC day. Expected time to Rank 5: approximately 1.2 OOC years.

- Aggressive player with Sensei (high-risk quests + 4 AP Sensei training per OOC day): approximately 370 progress per OOC day. Expected time to Rank 5: approximately 0.9 OOC years.

Rank 5 represents a master-level samurai — a figure of legend who has survived years of active service, warfare, court intrigue, or spiritual development. In the lore of Rokugan, such a character would be in their late 30s to late 40s, with decades of dangerous experience behind them. The rarity of Rank 5 characters in the game world is by design. Most samurai die before they get there. The few who do not are exceptional.

**IC TIME ****&**** DAILY RHYTHM**

1 OOC day equals 4 IC days. Each IC day has two Action Point slots: Morning and Afternoon. This means a player logging in each OOC day has 8 AP to distribute across all activities — training, quests, court duties, travel, personal interactions, and rest. The 4:1 ratio is fast enough to allow meaningful IC careers within a reasonable OOC timeframe, while still preserving the weight of IC time passing. A character who misses an OOC day has lost 4 IC days of their life — time their rivals, enemies, and the world did not pause for.

Players are expected to engage daily. The daily rhythm of Rokugan — morning duties, afternoon activities, evening court or personal time — maps naturally onto the Morning and Afternoon AP slots. The game is designed to be playable in short sessions. Spending 8 AP across 4 IC days does not require hours of play. A samurai's day is full, but not every moment needs to be narrated.
