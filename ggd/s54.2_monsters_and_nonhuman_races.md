## 54.2 Monsters and Nonhuman Races

### Bog Hag

Spirit Realm Association: Jigoku (Shadowlands creature)

Bog hags are among the more vile creatures of the Shadowlands, for they are able to infiltrate the Empire and spread both death and Taint far and wide. In their natural form they appear to be foul humanoid creatures, vaguely resembling an ancient human woman but with green skin, stringy matted hair, sagging or bloated flesh, monstrously ugly facial features, and sharp jagged teeth and claws. They are meat-eaters and enjoy the taste of human flesh, especially women and children. Most bog hags are solitary and territorial, although older ones sometimes form small groups for mutual protection.

Bog hags are amphibious and normally dwell in marshes. Their most dreadful power is the ability to wear the skin of any adult human they kill, allowing them to pass as a normal Rokugani. Even worse, they can absorb some of the memories of the person whose skin they wear, making it easier for them to maintain their deceptions. Their preferred targets for skinning are beautiful young women, allowing them to employ their skills at seduction and manipulation. They can wear anyone’s skin however — even a man, although they greatly dislike having to do so.

Bog hags are some of the most dangerous Shadowlands infiltrators and the Crab Clan watches carefully for them. The example below is one of the more potent types.

- Air: 3 | Earth: 3 | Fire: 3 | Water: 2

- Strength 3

- Initiative: 5k3

- Attack: Claws 5k3 (Simple)

- Damage: 3k2

- Armor TN: 20

- Reduction: 5

- Wounds: 16; +5; 32; +10; 48; +15; 72: Dead

- Taint Rank: 4

- Skills: Acting 4, Sincerity (Deceit) 4, Stealth 3, Temptation (Seduction) 4

**Special Abilities:**

- Aquatic: Bog hags swim in water at the same speed they move on land, and need not make a Skill roll to swim.

- Disease: Anyone wounded by a bog hag’s vile, encrusted claws has a 1 in 5 chance of contracting a foul wasting disease. This disease causes skin boils, blisters, and weakness, reducing the victim’s Stamina by 1 Rank per week until death at 0 Stamina. It can be cured with magic, or with a successful Medicine / Intelligence roll at TN

- Invulnerable (weaker bog hags do not always have this ability).

- Skin Wearing: A bog hag who wears the skin of her victim appears exactly like that victim in life, and acquires the victim’s memories and one rank in each Skill she possessed. Skins last a number of weeks equal to the victim’s Earth before they begin to rot away, exposing the monster beneath.

### Gaki (Shozai-Gaki)

Spirit Realm Association: Gaki-do (Realm of the Hungry Dead)

Gaki, sometimes called “hungry dead,” are spirits from the realm of Gaki-do who have entered the mortal world. Some Gaki are native to that realm, but many are actually the souls of humans who led extremely sinful lives but did not succumb to the Taint. Such souls are often condemned to Gaki-do, spending decades or centuries as one of the hungry dead so their karma can be cleansed.

The typical Rokugani often has difficulty telling a gaki apart from a true ghost. There are actually many different forms of gaki, depending on what sort of soul formed them. The unifying nature of all gaki is their relentless urge to feed on something — most typically either an impure physical substance like blood or human flesh, or an impure emotion like cruelty or pain.

The gaki presented here is the Shozai-Gaki, the most commonly encountered sort of gaki. These gaki are created from the souls of greedy and sinful humans, and are usually condemned to feed on an impure physical substance: blood, sweat, bile, flesh, etc. Each shozai-gaki will feed on only one particular substance of this sort, and if it cannot feed it will suffer agonizing pain. Physically, they appear to be grotesque, slightly translucent humanoid creatures with clawed hands and demonic features, often with a vaguely insectoid cast.

- Air: 2 | Earth: 2 | Fire: 2 | Water: 3

- Reflexes 4 | Agility 4 | Strength 4

- Initiative: 4k4

- Attack: Claw 4k4 (Complex), ignores physical armor

- Damage: 3k3

- Armor TN: 25

- Reduction: 5

- Wounds: 12; +5; 24; +10; 36: Dead

**Special Abilities:**

- Blood Draining: A gaki may forgo normal damage from a successful attack in order to drain the victim’s blood, drawing it out through translucent fingers. This inflicts 12 Wounds on the victim and heals the gaki by the same amount.

- Fear 3.

- Immortality: Gaki cannot actually be killed, and if their physical form is destroyed, they reform within Gaki-do.

- Invisibility: A gaki may become invisible as a Free Action, and while invisible may pass through walls and other solid inanimate objects (though not through living creatures).

- Shapeshifting: All gaki can take the form of a normal insect as a Complex Action. Regaining their true form is a Simple Action.

- Spirit.

- Superior Invulnerability: Gaki are immune to all illusion spells and to all spells, skills, and techniques which affect the mind or thoughts.

- Possession: A shozai-gaki can possess a mortal as a Complex Action by winning a Contested Willpower roll. This possession causes the victim to suffer high fever and wracking chills, losing 1k1 Wounds per day as the gaki feeds directly on their essence. The gaki can be driven out of its victim with the spells Ward of Purity or Bonds of Ningen-do.

CORRECTION --- JADE AND GAKI-DO (applies to Section 2.6.2 game effects). Section 2.6.2 states jade and obsidian weapons are effective against Gaki. This is only partially correct. Gaki-do is not Jigoku. It is a realm of kharmic penance sanctioned by the Celestial Order. Jade --- which is specifically anti-Jigoku --- has no inherent power against Gaki-do spirits. However, the source material confirms that Shozai-gaki are the Gaki type most likely to succumb to Jigoku’s corruption, and individual Shozai-gaki recruited into Jigoku’s service are encountered with alarming regularity. Jade IS effective against these Jigoku-corrupted Shozai-gaki specifically. The rule is: jade works against the Taint, not against the realm. A normal Shozai-gaki doing its kharmic penance --- no jade vulnerability. A Shozai-gaki that has been corrupted by Jigoku --- jade burns it like any Tainted creature. Earth magic banishes both types. Proper offerings and funeral rites address both types. Only jade discriminates.

### Ghost (Yorei)

Spirit Realm Association: Maigo no Musha (Realm of Thwarted Destiny) / Meido border

Rokugan is a superstitious land, and its myths and legends are replete with ghost stories. Although these stories vary widely in nature and contents, they share a general theme: ghosts are human souls who for whatever reason have remained in the mortal realm rather than traveling on to their next place in the Celestial Wheel. This is typically due to some powerful emotion — love, anger, regret, duty — drawing the soul back to Ningen-do when it should instead be traveling to Meido to accept the judgment of the Fortune of Death, Emma-O.

Ghosts are not always directly malignant, but they can still be dangerous to those they meet. Goryo (“vengeful ghosts”) are the souls of those murdered, kept in the mortal realm by their anger and need for vengeance. Another well-known type of ghost is the Ubume (“mourning woman”), ghosts of women who died in pregnancy or childbirth.

Ghosts have the same Rings, Traits, Skills, and other knowledge and abilities they had in life, but no longer have a Void Ring or Void Points. They can interact with physical objects, use weapons, and otherwise behave like living creatures. They generally cannot be physically harmed if they do not wish it. Jade and other sacred substances cannot actually kill a ghost — a Yorei which is reduced to zero Wounds will simply flee, probably returning later in anger to wreck vengeance on the one who hurt it. Convincing a ghost to leave for the afterlife is a matter of religion and conviction, not combat.

Note: Ghosts do not have a fixed stat block — they retain the Rings, Traits, and Skills they possessed in life. GMs should construct ghost stats based on who the individual was.

**Universal Ghost Special Abilities:**

- All ghosts have the ability to pass through solid objects, or to sink into the earth. They cannot be touched if they do not wish it.

- Jade, crystal, and obsidian do have some power to touch and harm ghosts, and the spell Ward of Purity can drive them away temporarily.

- Ghosts are largely immune to physical compulsion.

- Spirit.

### Goblin (Bakemono)

Spirit Realm Association: Jigoku (Shadowlands creature)

Goblins are small, vicious, semi-intelligent creatures who infest the Shadowlands in seemingly limitless numbers. They are humanoid, but with small bodies, spindly limbs, and oversized heads. Their hands and feet are clawed and their wide mouths are full of small sharp teeth. Goblins are only semi-intelligent, and are not individually much of a threat to an armed samurai. However, they have pack instincts and possess a dangerous cunning and malice. They also have a strange knack for imitation — they will often mimic human behavior, expressions, or language. Goblins easily fall under the sway of more powerful Shadowlands denizens, such as oni or Lost, who they serve willingly.

The Rokugani generally believe Fu Leng created goblins in an effort to replace men. Whether this is true or not, goblins have proven a persistent and tenacious danger to Rokugan. The statistics below represent a “typical” goblin — more powerful goblins appear from time to time, such as the leaders known as “warmongers,” as well as goblins who are a crude form of maho-tsukai.

- Attack: 4k2 (Simple)

- Damage: 4k2 (knife or sharp stick)

- Armor TN: 15

- Reduction: 3

- Wounds: 9; +5; 18: Dead

- Taint Rank: 2

- Skills: Stealth 2

**Special Abilities:**

- Swift 2.

### Kappa

Spirit Realm Association: Sakkaku (Realm of Mischief) / Chikushudo

Kappa are strange and malicious spirit creatures encountered in rivers and streams. They are amphibious and can travel underwater as quickly and easily as on land. Physically, they appear to be vaguely frog-like or turtle-like humanoids whose hands and feet are clawed and webbed. They have a beak-like mouth, a turtle’s shell on their back, and a distinctive bowl-shaped depression full of water atop their heads. Kappa are rare and usually malignant, subjects of superstition and fear among common folk. They are trickster spirits with little sense of restraint, often playing pranks that result in serious injury or death to the victim.

Kappa feed on blood, human and animal alike, but sometimes offer their prey the chance to escape if they can best the kappa at a puzzle or a test of wits. According to legend, the water in the kappa’s bowl-like head is the source of its power, and if that water is spilled, the creature is rendered powerless. A kappa which has lost its water is weak and vulnerable, and must retreat to the water for a full day to replenish itself.

- Air: 3 | Earth: 2 | Fire: 2 | Water: 3

- Intelligence 3

- Initiative: 4k3

- Attack: Claws 4k2 (Simple)

- Damage: 3k2

- Armor TN: 20

- Reduction: 15

- Wounds: 12; +5; 24; +10; 36: Dead

**Special Abilities:**

- Aquatic: Kappa swim in water at the same speed they move on land, and need not make a Skill roll to swim.

- Spirit.

- Trickery: The kappa can create visual illusions, similar to the Mists of Illusion spell, up to three times a day (more for an unusually powerful kappa).

- Water of Life: If the water in a kappa’s head-bowl is spilled, the kappa loses all Reduction and all of its Rings and Traits become 1 until it spends a full day underwater replenishing itself.

### Ogre

Spirit Realm Association: Jigoku (Shadowlands creature)

Ogres are huge, bestial humanoid creatures, eight feet tall or more, with massive limbs, tusk-like fangs, and thick, dirty hair. They are vicious and brutal predators, fighting with brute force and eating the flesh of those they kill. Ogres dwell within the Shadowlands, where they lead swarms of goblins or form shock troops for oni or Lost warlords. They also sometimes wander into the Empire, alone or in small packs, where they pose a lethal threat to samurai and peasants alike.

Ogres are actually an intelligent race, and once had a civilization of their own. However, Fu Leng enslaved the ogres at the dawn of the Empire, turning them into bestial creatures whose intellect is usually submerged within the violent impulses of the Taint. After Fu Leng died at the second Day of Thunder, some of the less Tainted ogres began to regain their will and intellect, becoming so-called “Free Ogres” who no longer made war on the Empire. The majority of the race, however, remained brutal Tainted monsters.

- Damage: 8k2 (club or small tree)

- Armor TN: 25

- Reduction: 10

- Wounds: 20; +5; 40; +10; 60; +15; 80: Dead

- Taint Rank: 3

**Special Abilities:**

- Fear 2.

- Huge.

### Oni (Ugulu no Oni)

Spirit Realm Association: Jigoku (Demon of Fu Leng’s realm)

Oni are the demons of Jigoku, among the most dreaded creatures known to the Empire. There are literally an infinite number of different oni within Jigoku, and only a tiny fraction of them have ever made it into the mortal realm. Oni can enter Ningen-do through physical portals from Jigoku — most infamously the Festering Pit of Fu Leng, located at the center of the Shadowlands — and can also be summoned by maho spells. Once in the mortal realm, an oni can spawn lesser versions of itself, many of which will eventually grow to be as strong as their “parent.” In this way entire races of oni can come into existence.

Physically, oni vary so widely that making any blanket statement about them is impossible. Some are humanoid, some bestial or even formless. Some are intelligent, others little more than mindless engines of destruction. Their universal trait is their extreme malevolence and evil. Oni seek always to corrupt, control, or destroy everything around them. They often enslave packs of lesser Shadowlands creatures or even Lost into their service.

The oni presented here, Ugulu no Oni, is one of the more powerful oni types known to the Rokugani, and Crab standing guard on the Kaiu Wall have learned to dread its appearance. It is a thirteen foot tall humanoid, with a swollen, grotesquely muscular body as if its purple skin can hardly contain the muscles beneath. Its hands sport claws as long as wakizashi, its demonic head is surmounted by a pair of large curving horns, and a long tongue lulls past the fangs of its great mouth.

- Air: 1 | Earth: 6 | Fire: 2 | Water: 2

- Agility 4 | Strength 8

- Initiative: 3k1

- Attack: Claws 7k4 (Simple)

- Damage: 9k3

- Armor TN: 10

- Reduction: 24 (12 against jade, crystal, or obsidian)

- Wounds: 32; +5; 64; +10; 96; +15; 124: Dead

- Taint Rank: 6

**Special Abilities:**

- Fear 3.

- Superior Invulnerability: Ugulu no Oni are completely immune to all arrows except for armor-piercing arrows.

- Spell Immunity: All Ugulu no Oni are immune to two spells, although the specific spells vary from one Ugulu to the next.

### Ratling (Nezumi)

Spirit Realm Association: Immune to Jigoku (Taint Immunity); Yume-do connection (Name magic)

The ratlings are a race of intelligent bipedal rats who dwell in the Shadowlands. Unlike other denizens of the Shadowlands, the ratlings are immune to the Taint, a phenomenon which fascinates those Rokugani who are aware of it. The ratlings claim to have once had a great civilization in ancient times, a civilization destroyed when Fu Leng fell from the sky. Whether this is true or not, the ratlings today are a race of primitive tribal creatures. They are notorious for their short attention spans and lack of long-term memory — many Rokugani have compared the typical ratling to a hyperactive six-year-old human child.

Ratlings are often seen as cowardly by the Rokugani, since they do not hesitate to flee from superior force or to employ stealth and ambush to gain an advantage over their enemies. However, the truth is that ratlings are quite courageous when need requires — a ratling female protecting her pups will fight as ferociously as any samurai. The ratlings simply do not see why they should fight without need, or deny themselves any advantages — after all, in the Shadowlands their enemies are often both larger and stronger.

Ratlings live in tribes of anywhere from a dozen to several hundred individuals. Most of these tribes are found in the Shadowlands, but several of them dwell within the Shinomen Forest or even within Rokugan itself. The statistics below represent a “typical” ratling without a school.

- Air: 1 | Earth: 2 | Fire: 2 | Water: 2 | Name: 1

- Reflexes 3 | Agility 3

- Initiative: 4k3

- Attack: Crude spear 4k3 (Complex)

- Damage: 4k2

- Armor TN: 20

- Reduction: None

- Wounds: Human-type Wound Ranks

- Skills: Athletics 3, Stealth 3

**Special Abilities:**

- Swift 2.

- Taint Immunity: Ratlings cannot gain the Shadowlands Taint from any source, and cannot cast maho spells.

- Name: Ratlings attach great importance to names, and the “stronger” a ratling’s name is, the more potent and lucky he or she will be. In game terms, ratlings have a “Name” trait which functions mechanically in the same manner as the Void Ring does for humans, including the ability to spend “Name Points” for the same effects humans can get from Void Points. However, ratlings cannot meditate or perform tea ceremonies to regain Name points, and cannot enter Center Stance.

**Chieftain Survival --- Special Rule:**

The Chieftain uses the standard Commander Survival System (Section 11.7). One exception: if the Chieftain's Guard Company routs, the Chieftain automatically survives --- he escapes with the fleeing unit. The rout-threshold survival roll (TN 25) is skipped. At all other thresholds (75%, 50%, 25% Health), standard survival rolls apply using the Chieftain's individual stats (Earth 3, Battle 2 → 5k3).

- Chieftain killed (Company destroyed to Health 0 and survival roll failed, or killed by a PC in an ASCII event): colony dies permanently. No respawn.

- Chieftain escapes (Company routs): new Nezumi Infestation spawns in an adjacent province at Strength 3 within 1–4 seasons. The new infestation enters at Phase 3 (Detection) --- the arriving tribe is immediately visible. It generates its own T4-85 and T3-36 topics and follows the normal insurgency lifecycle from that point.

**Force Composition at Strength 10:** 4–6 Warrior Pack, 1–2 Skirmisher, 1–2 Archer, 1 Broodmother Guard, 1 Chieftain's Guard. Total: 8–12 Companies. Fixed at transition --- no growth beyond Strength 10.

### Tsuno (Tsuno Warrior)

Spirit Realm Association: Toshigoku (Realm of Slaughter) — formerly; Jigoku (allied with Dark Daigotsu in the mortal era)

The Tsuno are a race descended from the Kitsu, the pre-human race whose last survivors joined the Lion Clan. In ancient times the Tsuno were exiled by their Kitsu cousins into the spirit realms. They spent centuries dwelling in Toshigoku, the Realm of Slaughter, adapting themselves to its cruel nature. During the time of the Four Winds in the twelfth century, the Tsuno re-entered the mortal realm and dwelled in the Shadowlands as allies of the Dark Lord Daigotsu, becoming Tainted by their own choice.

Tsuno appear to be bestial, furred humanoids with sharp claws on their hands and feet. Their long, slavering mouths are filled with sharp fangs, and they sport a pair of sharp horns atop their heads. Tsuno are highly intelligent and viciously cruel, with a martial culture that prizes strength and treats all other races with contempt. They wear armor and carry weapons — their swords are notorious for their hooked, barbed appearance. Tsuno fight in organized packs, led by powerful warriors called Ravagers and dangerous spellcasters known as Soultwisters. The sample Tsuno presented here is a basic warrior without such advanced training.

### Undead Revenant

Spirit Realm Association: Jigoku (created by maho)

Undead revenants are a more powerful form of zombie, created by potent maho spells. Like zombies, revenants are corpses animated by the malignant force of the Taint, seeking to attack and devour living things. However, they are usually under the control of the maho-tsukai who summoned them. Unlike zombies, revenants do not continue to rot once they are reanimated, and their sinews and muscles retain enough tone to allow them to move almost as quickly as they did in life. They also possess a certain animal intelligence that is greater than the mindless aggression of zombies. Like zombies, though, they are fearless, immune to pain, and lack any sense of empathy or humanity. Revenants are usually made from the corpses of samurai, and fight with the weapons they carried in life.

- Air: 0 | Earth: 3 | Fire: 1 | Water: 2

- Reflexes 3 | Agility 3 | Strength 3

- Initiative: 3k3

- Attack: Fist 5k3 (Complex) or Sword 5k3 (Complex)

- Damage: 3k1 (fist) or 5k2 (sword)

- Armor TN: 15 (revenants sometimes wear armor, with the appropriate increase in TN and Reduction)

- Reduction: 5

- Wounds: 72: Dead

- Taint Rank: 3

**Special Abilities:**

- Beheading: A revenant normally can only be destroyed by literally hacking it to pieces (reducing it to Dead). However, it can also be destroyed instantly by severing its head. This requires taking the Raises for a Called Shot and then inflicting at least 18 Wounds.

- Fear 3.

- Undead.

### Zombie

Spirit Realm Association: Jigoku (Shadowlands Taint / maho)

In Rokugan, undead are typically created when a corpse is suffused with the Taint. Any corpse left in the Shadowlands will reanimate as a zombie within ten to thirty minutes. A body that dies already Tainted will reanimate even faster. Corpses can also be reanimated by certain maho spells, or by sinister porcelain masks created by the Bloodspeaker cult.

Regardless of how they are created, all zombies are similar: rotting corpses animated by the malignant force of the Taint, seeking to attack and devour all living things. They feel no pain, no fear, nothing but the mindless urge to destroy. No vestige of their former humanity remains. To the Rokugani, these shambling monstrosities are both terrifying and blasphemous, violations of the Celestial Order that strike at the very essence of what it means to be human.

- Air: 0 | Earth: 3 | Fire: 0 | Water: 1
