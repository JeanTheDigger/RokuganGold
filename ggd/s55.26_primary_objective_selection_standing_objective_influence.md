## 55.26 Primary Objective Selection & Standing Objective Influence

**STATUS:** LOCKED

Two foundational systems that were missing from the engine: (1) how a lord or lordless character selects their own primary objective when no one assigns them one, and (2) how the permanent standing objective flavors the approach to whatever primary objective is active.

### 55.26.1 Part 1: Primary Objective Self-Selection

When does this fire? Five cases:

**Case 1 — Lordless character (Clan Champion, Ronin, independent lord):** No one above them to assign objectives. They must generate their own primary objectives from their standing direction and the world state. This fires at game start and whenever a primary objective completes.

**Case 2 — Lord evaluating their domain:** A Clan Champion’s standing objective is “expand territory.” At the seasonal Strategic Review, they survey the world and pick a concrete target. “Expand territory” becomes “Conquer Province Akodo.”

**Case 3 — Vassal completes objective, lord is negligent/unreachable:** Per Section 18.2, the vassal “defaults to standing objective until reassigned.” But if 2+ seasons pass with no reassignment, the vassal should self-select a primary derived from their standing direction.

**Case 4 — Crisis auto-generation:** Already handled (Section 18.7). A crisis in territory generates a primary automatically. Not covered here — this system handles the non-crisis case.

**Case 5 — Lord assigning to vassals:** The lord’s self-selected primary generates derived objectives for their vassals. This IS the assignment mechanism — the lord picks their own goal, then decomposes it into vassal tasks.

#### 55.26.1.1 The Opportunity Scanner

At each seasonal Strategic Review, a lord (or lordless character) scans the world state through their known information and identifies Opportunities — concrete situations where action would advance their standing objective. The scanner produces a ranked list of candidate primary objectives.

**Key principle:** The scanner reads ONLY known information (Section 20). A lord cannot identify an opportunity they don’t know about. This means intelligence gathering (letters, court reports, scout reports, Province Status updates) directly feeds objective selection. A well-informed lord picks better objectives.

##### 1.1.1 Opportunity Types by Standing Objective Domain

Each standing objective domain has its own opportunity scanner:

#### Political Opportunities

**Weak neighbor detected:** A province with low stability adjacent to your territory → candidate: “Conquer Province X” or “Secure alliance with Clan X (before they collapse).”

**Clan rising in power:** Known clan strength exceeding others → candidate: “Break alliance between X and Y” or “Isolate Character X politically.”

**Court scheduled:** Upcoming court with relevant attendees → candidate: “Secure Character X’s support on topic Y” or “Gain Winter Court invitation.”

**Secret discovered:** Holding leverage on a target → candidate: “Expose Character X’s secret” or “Remove Character X from position.”

**Alliance gap:** No formal alliance with a neighboring clan → candidate: “Secure formal alliance with Clan X.”

#### Military Opportunities

**Border weakness:** Enemy province under-garrisoned or at low stability → candidate: “Conquer Province X.”

**Insurgency detected:** Active insurgency in own territory → candidate: “Restore order in Province X.”

**Military imbalance:** Rival clan’s army significantly larger → candidate: “Raise military force of [size] by [date]” or “Secure military alliance with Clan X against Clan Y.”

**Shadowlands activity:** Taint detected or incursion warning → candidate: “Resolve Shadowlands Incursion crisis.”

#### Economic Opportunities

**Resource deficit:** Stockpile below safety threshold → candidate: “Acquire Resource X above threshold.”

**Famine in region:** Own or allied province at Hunger/Famine → candidate: “Resolve Famine crisis in Region X.”

**Koku output low:** Province generating below potential (banditry, conflict) → candidate: “Increase Koku output of Province X.”

**Trade partner available:** Clan with surplus resources and positive disposition → candidate: “Establish trade agreement with Clan X.”

#### Personal Opportunities

**Honor deficit:** Honor below starting value → candidate: “Restore Honor above Rank X.”

**Glory below role expectation:** Glory Rank below Status Rank → candidate: “Achieve Glory Rank X.”

**Vengeance target located:** Target of standing vengeance is at a known location → candidate: “Avenge death of Character X.”

**Vassal needs training:** A promising vassal at low Insight Rank → candidate: “Train Character X to Rank Y.”

#### 55.26.1.2 Opportunity Scoring

Each candidate objective is scored on four factors:

| **Factor** | **Weight** | **Description** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Standing Alignment | 40% | How directly does this objective serve the standing objective? Expand territory + Conquer Province = 100%. Expand territory + Resolve Famine = 20%. |
| Feasibility | 30% | Can I actually accomplish this with available resources? The War Readiness Feasibility Ledger (Section 4.3.17) is the model. A fully feasible objective scores 100. An impossible one scores 0. |
| Urgency | 20% | Is there a time pressure? A famine killing people scores higher than a political opportunity that will still be there next season. Active crises score 100. |
| Personality Fit | 10% | Does this objective suit my personality? A Yu lord prefers military objectives over diplomatic ones. A Rei lord prefers court objectives. Uses the same personality lean values from the engine. |

The highest-scoring opportunity becomes the lord’s new primary objective. Ties are broken by: urgency > standing alignment > feasibility > deterministic seed.

#### 55.26.1.3 The Selection Function

func _select_primary_objective(npc_id: StringName, ctx: ContextSnapshot) -> Objective:

var standing = ObjectiveSystem.get_standing(npc_id)

var candidates = []

# ═══ STEP 1: Scan for opportunities ═══

var scanner = OpportunityScannerFactory.get(standing.domain)

var opportunities = scanner.scan(npc_id, ctx)

# Each opportunity is a {objective_template, target_fields, raw_scores}

# ═══ STEP 2: Score each opportunity ═══

for opp in opportunities:

var score = 0.0

score += opp.standing_alignment * 0.4

score += _assess_feasibility(opp, ctx) * 0.3

score += opp.urgency * 0.2

score += _personality_fit(opp, npc_id) * 0.1

candidates.append({obj: opp, score: score})

# ═══ STEP 3: Select the best ═══

if candidates.is_empty():

return null # No opportunities found. Continue with standing objective.

candidates.sort_custom(func(a, b): return a.score > b.score)

var best = candidates[0]

# ═══ STEP 4: Create the objective ═══

var obj = Objective.new()

obj.type = best.obj.objective_template

obj.target_fields = best.obj.target_fields

obj.source = ActiveGoalSource.SELF_SELECTED

obj.assigned_ic_day = TimeSystem.current_ic_day

return obj

#### 55.26.1.4 Vassal Objective Derivation

Once a lord selects their own primary objective, they decompose it into vassal assignments. This happens as part of the Strategic Review:

func _derive_vassal_objectives(lord_id, lord_obj: Objective, ctx: ContextSnapshot):

var vassals = HierarchySystem.get_direct_vassals(lord_id)

# What tasks does this objective require?

var tasks = ObjectiveDecomposer.get_required_tasks(lord_obj)

# Example: 'Secure alliance with Dragon' produces:

# - 'Build disposition with Dragon contacts at court' (courtier task)

# - 'Maintain correspondence with Dragon family' (letter-writer task)

# - 'Commission appropriate gifts for Dragon delegates' (artisan task)

# - 'Gather intelligence on Dragon positions' (spy task)

# Match tasks to vassals by school type and skill

for task in tasks:

var best_vassal = _find_best_vassal_for_task(vassals, task, ctx)

if best_vassal:

ObjectiveSystem.assign_objective(

lord_id, best_vassal.id, task.as_objective())

# Information Transfer fires automatically (Section 6)

**Key insight:** The lord’s objective decomposes into MULTIPLE vassal objectives, each tailored to the vassal’s capabilities. A courtier vassal gets the court task. A bushi vassal gets the military task. An artisan vassal gets the craft task. The lord doesn’t assign the same objective to everyone — they delegate strategically.

This enables magistrates to assign investigation objectives to yoriki, military commanders to assign tactical objectives to subordinates, and delegation leaders to assign court objectives to delegation members --- none of whom appear in the feudal vassal list. #### 55.26.1.5 The Negligent Lord & Vassal Self-Selection

When a vassal completes their primary objective and their lord fails to reassign them:

**Season 1 after completion:** Vassal pursues standing objective directly. No self-selection. They wait for orders like a good samurai.

**Season 2:** Vassal sends a letter to their lord requesting a new assignment (WRITE_LETTER targeting lord). This is the “polite nudge.” The letter itself is a topic: “Vassal X requests new orders.”

**Season 3+:** If still no reassignment, the vassal runs their own Opportunity Scanner (limited to their role’s scope) and self-selects a primary objective derived from their standing direction. This objective is tagged self_selected: true. When the lord eventually notices, they may endorse or override it.

**Personality modifiers on self-selection timing:**

**Chugi:** Never self-selects. Waits indefinitely. Chugi characters pursue only their standing objective and send letters every season requesting orders. Maximum loyalty, minimum initiative.

**Seigyo:** Self-selects after 1 season (not 3). Control doesn’t waste time waiting. They pick an objective that serves both their lord’s interests AND their own standing ambition.

**Ishi:** Self-selects after 1 season. Will does not idle. They pick and commit fully.

**Makoto:** Self-selects after 2 seasons but announces it to their lord via letter before beginning. Sincerity is transparent about their intentions.

**Ketsui:** Self-selects after 2 seasons. Determination finds something to do.

#### 55.26.1.6 Edge Cases

**EDGE CASE:** No opportunities detected (world is peaceful, resources are fine, no threats): The lord’s scanner returns empty. They continue pursuing their standing objective through the standing decomposition (which handles maintenance behavior). A peaceful world means the lord trains troops, builds relationships, attends courts. This is not a failure — it’s stability.

**EDGE CASE:** Multiple equally good opportunities: Personality Fit (10%) is the tiebreaker. A Yu lord picks the military opportunity. A Rei lord picks the diplomatic one. If still tied: standing alignment breaks it. If STILL tied: deterministic seed prevents oscillation.

**EDGE CASE:** Lord’s self-selected objective conflicts with their superior’s interests: Possible for Family Daimyo whose Clan Champion has a different direction. If the Champion discovers the conflict (through court, letters, intelligence), they may override the Family Daimyo’s objective through reassignment. This creates emergent intra-clan politics.

**EDGE CASE:** Ronin with no territory, no vassals, no intelligence network: Their scanner is extremely limited — they only know what they’ve personally observed or heard through conversation. Their opportunities are personal: train, seek glory, seek employment. A ronin’s primary objective is typically: “Find a new lord” (RAISE_DISPOSITION with potential patrons), “Achieve Glory Rank X” (earn reputation to attract attention), or their vengeance target.

#### 55.26.1.7 Worked Example: Lord Self-Selection

**NPC:** Doji Satsume (Rei/Seigyo, Crane Clan Champion). Standing obj: “Maintain the balance of power.” Seasonal Strategic Review fires. Known information: Lion total military ~30 PU (dominant). Crab ~26 PU. Crane ~22 PU. Scorpion ~18 PU. Lion just conquered a Dragon border province.

**Opportunity Scanner (Political domain):**

Opportunity A: “Secure formal alliance with Crab against Lion.” Standing alignment: 90 (directly checks the dominant clan). Feasibility: 70 (Crab disposition +15, requires work). Urgency: 60 (Lion is expanding but not immediately threatening Crane). Personality: Rei +8. Score = 90×0.4 + 70×0.3 + 60×0.2 + 8×(10/15)×10×0.1 = 36 + 21 + 12 + 0.5 = 69.5.

Opportunity B: “Isolate Lion’s primary courtier politically at next Winter Court.” Standing alignment: 80. Feasibility: 85 (Crane are court specialists). Urgency: 40 (Winter Court is 2 seasons away). Personality: Seigyo +12. Score = 32 + 25.5 + 8 + 0.8 = 66.3.

Opportunity C: “Break Lion-Unicorn alliance.” Standing alignment: 95 (directly weakens dominant clan’s coalition). Feasibility: 50 (Unicorn disposition unknown, intelligence stale). Urgency: 70 (the alliance enabled the Dragon conquest). Personality: Seigyo +10. Score = 38 + 15 + 14 + 0.7 = 67.7.

**Result:** Opportunity A wins at 69.5. Satsume’s new primary objective: “Secure formal alliance with Crab against Lion.”

**Vassal derivation:** Satsume decomposes this into vassal tasks: Doji Hotaru (courtier) receives “Build disposition with Crab contacts at court.” Kakita Yoshi (letter-writer) receives “Maintain correspondence with Crab Family Daimyo.” Daidoji Uji (military advisor) receives “Evaluate joint defense posture with Crab border.” Each vassal’s engine fires, decomposes their assigned task, and begins work. The cascade is complete.

### 55.26.2 Part 2: Standing Objective Influence on Primary Pursuit

The primary objective tells the NPC WHAT to do. The standing objective tells them HOW to do it. This is not override — the primary always determines the goal. But when multiple paths lead to the same goal, the standing objective breaks the tie by favoring the path that also advances the standing direction.

#### 55.26.2.1 The Standing Influence Bonus

A new scoring component added to Phase 5. When the engine scores an action for primary objective alignment, it also checks: does this action ADDITIONALLY serve the standing objective? If yes, a Standing Influence Bonus is added.

**Standing Influence Bonus: 0 to +15**

Calculated as: objective_alignment.json lookup for the STANDING objective’s NeedType against the same ActionID, divided by 10, capped at +15. This means actions that score highly for BOTH the primary and standing objectives get a small but meaningful boost.

**The math:** If CHARM scores 90 for RAISE_DISPOSITION (primary need) and CHARM also scores 40 for SEEK_GLORY (standing need), the standing bonus is 40/10 = +4. Total action score gains +4. This is small compared to the primary’s ObjAlign (0–100) but enough to break ties and create a consistent behavioral flavor.

#### 55.26.2.2 How It Changes Behavior

Consider a courtier with:

Primary: “Secure alliance with the Dragon Clan” → NeedType: RAISE_DISPOSITION

Standing: “Accumulate political leverage”

Without standing influence, the courtier picks whichever social action best raises disposition: CHARM, LISTEN_REFLECT, DELIVER_GIFT. All serve the primary.

With standing influence, actions that ALSO gather leverage get a bonus:

| **Action** | **Primary ObjAlign** | **Standing Bonus (leverage ÷10)** | **Total Boost** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CHARM | 90 | CHARM for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 0 → +0 | +0 |
| LISTEN_REFLECT | 85 | LISTEN for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 0 → +0 | +0 |
| PROBE | 30 | PROBE for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 40 → +4 | +4 |
| READ_CHARACTER | 35 | READ for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 35 → +3.5 | +3.5 |
| DELIVER_GIFT | 85 | GIFT for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 0 → +0 | +0 |

**Effect:** CHARM still wins for raising disposition. But PROBE and READ_CHARACTER score slightly higher than they otherwise would. A courtier with “accumulate leverage” as their standing direction will occasionally choose PROBE over LISTEN_REFLECT — gathering intelligence on their target while building the relationship. They’re doing the primary job (alliance) but coloring it with their personal ambition (leverage). They learn the Dragon’s secrets while becoming their friend.

#### 55.26.2.3 Standing Influence Interaction Table

How standing objectives flavor different primary objective pursuits:

| **Primary Objective** | **Standing Objective** | **Behavioral Flavor** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Secure alliance | Accumulate leverage | Probes and reads the target while befriending them. Learns secrets along the way. |
| Secure alliance | Advance family standing | Prioritizes public-facing alliance building (court declarations, gifts) over private relationship building. Wants to be SEEN securing the alliance. |
| Secure alliance | Maintain peace | Approaches the alliance as a peace-building measure. NEGOTIATE and PERSUADE score higher than CHARM. Frames the alliance as mutual defense. |
| Defend province | Build strongest force | TRAIN_TROOPS and DRILL_TROOPS get a standing bonus even during crisis response. The defender trains between engagements. |
| Defend province | Protect those under care | OFFER_TERMS and SEEK_PEACE get a standing bonus. The protector looks for ways to end the threat with minimum casualties. |
| Defend province | Seek vengeance | STORM_ASSAULT and ORDER_SORTIE get a standing bonus. The avenger fights aggressively, not defensively. |
| Conquer province | Expand territory | Pure alignment. Every military action is boosted. |
| Conquer province | Maintain peace | Conflicting. Standing bonus is 0 or negative for aggressive actions. The NPC pursues the primary but with visible reluctance — they offer terms early, accept surrender readily. |
| Resolve famine | Accumulate wealth | PURCHASE_MARKET (sell rice at high prices) gets a standing bonus even while acquiring rice for the famine. The NPC profits from the crisis while resolving it. |
| Resolve famine | Protect those under care | SHARE_SUPPLIES gets a massive standing bonus. The protector gives generously. |

#### 55.26.2.4 Implementation

# In scorer.gd, Phase 5:

func _score_action(action: ScoredAction, goal: ActiveGoal, ctx: ContextSnapshot):

# ... existing 8 components ...

action.objective_alignment = _lookup_alignment(goal.immediate_need.need_type, action.action_id)

action.disposition_modifier = _lookup_disposition(action.target_npc_id, ctx)

action.personality_lean = _lookup_lean(ctx.npc_id, action.action_id)

action.competence_modifier = _lookup_competence(ctx, action.action_id)

action.urgency_bonus = _lookup_urgency(ctx, action.action_id)

# NEW: Standing Influence Bonus

var standing = ObjectiveSystem.get_standing(ctx.npc_id)

var standing_need = _get_standing_need_type(standing)

if standing_need:

var standing_alignment = _lookup_alignment(standing_need, action.action_id)

action.standing_influence = mini(standing_alignment / 10, 15)

else:

action.standing_influence = 0

**The total score becomes:** ObjAlign + DispMod + PersLean + Competence + Urgency + Standing Influence + TopicPosition + ResourceModifier. Eight components — topic_position_modifier added (Section 55.4, Annex H), resource_modifier added (Section 55.32). The standing influence is the weakest component (max +15 vs ObjAlign’s 100) but it creates the consistent behavioral coloring that makes each character feel like they have a personal agenda beyond their orders.

#### 55.26.2.5 Edge Cases

**EDGE CASE:** Standing objective and primary objective are in the same domain: The standing bonus is highest. “Expand territory” (standing) + “Conquer Province X” (primary) = military actions get double reinforcement. The NPC is single-minded and effective.

**EDGE CASE:** Standing objective actively conflicts with primary: “Maintain peace” (standing) + “Conquer Province X” (primary, assigned by lord). The standing bonus for aggressive actions is 0 or near-0. The NPC pursues the primary because it’s their duty, but their standing direction makes them favor OFFER_TERMS and NEGOTIATE over STORM_ASSAULT. They conquer reluctantly. This creates visible tension — a lord watching this vassal would notice they’re dragging their feet on the assault. This is emergent characterization.

**EDGE CASE:** Standing bonus causes a different action to win: Possible but unlikely (max +15 vs typical ObjAlign gaps of 20–30). The most likely scenario: two actions are within 10 points of each other for the primary, and the standing bonus tips the balance. This is EXACTLY the intended behavior — when two paths serve the primary equally, the NPC picks the one that also serves their personal ambition.

**EDGE CASE:** Chugi personality with conflicting standing direction: Chugi characters may feel conflicted when their standing objective colors them toward an approach their lord wouldn’t approve of. The personality filter handles this: Chugi blocks any action that contradicts the lord’s known position, regardless of standing bonus. The standing influence is suppressed by duty. This is exactly right for the archetype.

#### 55.26.2.6 Worked Example: Standing Influence in Action

**NPC:** Bayushi Kachiko (Seigyo/Dōsatsu, Scorpion Courtier, Rank 5). Primary objective (assigned by Scorpion Champion): “Secure Crane support on the Shadowlands crisis topic at Winter Court.” Standing objective: “Accumulate political leverage.” At Winter Court. Crane delegate Doji Takeshi present, disposition +22 (Acquaintance).

**Phase 2:** Primary active. Takeshi disposition +22 < +31 threshold for influence. Returns RAISE_DISPOSITION targeting Takeshi.

**Phase 5 WITHOUT standing influence:**

| **Action** | **ObjAlign** | **DispMod** | **PersLean** | **Comp** | **Urg** | **Total** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CHARM | 90 | 0 | +10+3=+13 | +10 | 0 | 113 |
| LISTEN_REFLECT | 85 | 0 | +8+5=+13 | +5 | 0 | 103 |
| PROBE | 30 | 0 | +12+12=+15 | +10 | 0 | 55 |
| DELIVER_GIFT | 85 | 0 | +5+3=+8 | +10 | 0 | 103 |

**Phase 5 WITH standing influence:**

Standing = “Accumulate political leverage” → standing NeedType approximation: ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE.

| **Action** | **ObjAlign** | **PersLean** | **Comp** | **Standing Bonus** | **New Total** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CHARM | 90 | +13 | +10 | CHARM for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 0 → +0 | 113 |
| LISTEN_REFLECT | 85 | +13 | +5 | LISTEN for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 0 → +0 | 103 |
| PROBE | 30 | +15 | +10 | PROBE for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 40 → +4 | 59 |
| DELIVER_GIFT | 85 | +8 | +10 | GIFT for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 0 → +0 | 103 |

**Result:** CHARM still wins at 113. The standing bonus didn’t change the outcome in this case — the primary’s alignment dominance is too strong. PROBE went from 55 to 59 — still not competitive.

**But consider Day 3,** when Takeshi’s disposition has reached +28 (close to Friend). The decomposition still wants to push to +31. But the gap is small — any social action will likely do it:

| **Action** | **ObjAlign** | **PersLean** | **Comp** | **Standing Bonus** | **Total** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CHARM | 90 | +13 | +10 | +0 | 113 |
| PROBE | 30 | +15 | +10 | +4 | 59 |
| READ_CHARACTER | 35 | +15 | +10 | +3 | 63 |

Still CHARM. But now consider Kachiko’s AFTERNOON AP. She already CHARMed this morning. Diminishing returns within a session (performing the same action twice in one day is less effective). CHARM drops by 15 (session penalty):

| **Action** | **Base** | **Session Penalty** | **Standing Bonus** | **Total** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CHARM | 113 | -15 | +0 | 98 |
| LISTEN_REFLECT | 103 | 0 | +0 | 103 |
| PROBE | 55 | 0 | +4 | 59 |
| DELIVER_GIFT | 103 | 0 | +0 | 103 |

**Result:** LISTEN_REFLECT and DELIVER_GIFT tie at 103. Tiebreaker: higher base ObjAlign = equal (both 85). Next tiebreaker: standing influence. Neither has any. Next: personality lean. Both at +13 vs +8. LISTEN_REFLECT wins. Kachiko listens attentively to Takeshi in the afternoon.

**Day 4:** Disposition crosses +31 (Friend). Primary decomposition advances to MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION. Now PROBE scores much higher for GATHER_INTELLIGENCE (ObjAlign 95). AND the standing bonus fires: PROBE for ACQUIRE_LEVERAGE = 40 → +4. Total: 95 + 15 + 10 + 4 = 124. Kachiko shifts to intelligence mode — she’s now Probing Takeshi to learn his true positions while also building leverage for her personal agenda. The primary job (learn his position to move it) and the standing ambition (gather secrets) converge naturally.

**This is the standing influence working exactly as intended:** it doesn’t override the primary. It waits for moments where the primary and standing objectives have overlapping paths, then nudges the NPC toward the overlap. Kachiko does her lord’s bidding faithfully. She just happens to pick up leverage along the way. That’s the Scorpion.

## Summary

| **System** | **What It Does** | **When It Fires** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opportunity Scanner | Evaluates world state through known info, produces ranked candidate objectives | Seasonal Strategic Review (lord-tier) or objective completion (vassal after 3 seasons) |
| Opportunity Scoring | Standing Alignment (40%) + Feasibility (30%) + Urgency (20%) + Personality Fit (10%) | During scanner evaluation |
| Vassal Derivation | Lord decomposes their objective into role-specific vassal tasks | After lord selects their own primary |
| Negligent Lord Timer | Vassal waits 1–3 seasons, then self-selects (modified by personality) | After primary objective completion with no reassignment |
| Standing Influence Bonus | 0 to +15 bonus on actions that also serve the standing objective | Every Phase 5 scoring, every action, always |

**Total score formula is now 8 components:** Objective Alignment (0–100) + Disposition Modifier (−20 to +20) + Personality Lean (−15 to +15) + Competence Modifier (−20 to +20) + Urgency Bonus (0 to +30) + Standing Influence (0 to +15) + Topic Position Modifier (−15 to +15) + Resource Modifier (0 to −40, Section 55.32). Maximum theoretical score: 215. Typical winning score: 90–140.

Selected method is written to lotus_network_record under the target operative’s entry as assignment_method. **ASSESS_SUPERNATURAL_THREAT.** “A supernatural threat has come to my attention. I need to classify it, determine whether existing institutions can handle it, and decide how to respond.” Fires for Master Jade when their IntelDB contains a topic matching the supernatural threat scope: Shadowlands taint topics (any tier), oni sighting or manifestation topics, maho use topics, Lying Darkness activity topics, spirit realm incursion topics, or any topic naming a character with confirmed Taint 3+. Priority scales with threat severity: Taint topics in a provincial capital or affecting a lord-tier NPC fire at priority 3; remote or unconfirmed sightings fire at priority 2; ambient monitoring fires at priority 1. ImmediateNeed fields: target_npc_id (the primary threat NPC or origin character if identifiable), target_province_id (the affected province), priority scaled by severity, threshold_type = “supernatural_threat_unresolved”. Decomposition runs a classification pass — querying IntelDB for all available information about the threat — and assigns one of three response tiers. Tier 1 (Institutional): existing Witch Hunters, Inquisitors, or Jade Magistrates can handle it with better information. Generate ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE targeting the most capable registered jade_asset_network contact near the threat province. Tier 2 (Assisted): the threat exceeds what local institutions can handle alone but Master Jade can supplement with resources. Generate ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE plus SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Tiger requesting Coin funding for an expedition or Silk intelligence support. Tier 3 (Escalation): the threat is existential, institutions are compromised, or a Kolat-connected character is involved. Generate SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE to Tiger at priority 3 with full threat assessment. Tiger coordinates cross-Sect response. The NeedType clears when the threat topic’s momentum reaches zero (resolved) or when Tiger acknowledges the escalation. If the threat escalates from Tier 1 to Tier 2 or 3 while the NeedType is active, it re-fires at the higher priority. Objective alignment: ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE 100, ANONYMOUS_TIP 85, GATHER_INTELLIGENCE 80, PROBE 75, SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE 70, BEGIN_TRAVEL 60, DO_NOTHING 0. Gap 1 — Institutional response monitoring: after routing a Tier 1 or Tier 2 tip, the NeedType does not immediately clear when the threat topic’s momentum reaches zero. Instead, it enters a monitoring state and checks IntelDB on each Seasonal Tick for a resolution indicator — a topic recording the threat as neutralised, a Jade Magistrate investigation-closed topic, or a confirmed death of the threat NPC. Only on confirmed resolution does the NeedType clear. If the threat topic fades without resolution confirmation, Master Jade generates a follow-up ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE to the same asset with a higher urgency flag, and re-assesses at the next tier. Gap 2 — No asset near threat province: if jade_asset_network contains no contact whose province_coverage matches or is adjacent to the threat province, the decomposition falls back to ANONYMOUS_TIP targeting the nearest institutional body by province distance, with no personalised asset relationship. Simultaneously, CULTIVATE_JADE_ASSET fires at priority 2 flagging the uncovered province as a priority region for the next asset recruitment. This creates a natural expansion driver — gaps in coverage push Master Jade to recruit in underserved areas. Gap 4 — Corrupted Kolat member: before generating ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE or ANONYMOUS_TIP, the decomposition checks whether the threat NPC ID matches any NPC in Tiger’s known_contacts, any other Master’s network record, or any known Kolat operative in IntelDB. If a match is found, the ANONYMOUS_TIP path is suppressed entirely. Instead, SEND_KOLAT_DIRECTIVE fires to Tiger at priority 3 with the full threat assessment. Tiger decides whether to route to Lotus for elimination, to attempt conditioning intervention, or to manage the situation internally. Master Jade never independently reports a Kolat-connected character to the institutions — that decision belongs to Tiger.

**CULTIVATE_JADE_ASSET.** [Standing NeedType — Master Jade’s primary standing loop.] “I need to build and maintain a network of institutional contacts who can be my eyes on supernatural threats across the Empire — contacts who trust my anonymous guidance and occasionally report back to me.” Fires for Master Jade when jade_asset_network in special_data has fewer than 2 registered contacts (below minimum operational coverage) or when an existing contact’s disposition toward Master Jade’s cover persona has fallen below Acquaintance. Priority = 1 (background cultivation, does not interrupt active threat responses). ImmediateNeed fields: threshold (asset_count 0–3), threshold_type = “jade_asset_count”. Decomposition: identify candidates matching the T2–T3 Jade Sect profile (Jade Magistrate, Witch Hunter, shugenja with Lore: Shadowlands 3+) in known_contacts or reachable through ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION. Build relationship through normal social interactions — RAISE_DISPOSITION, CHARM, shared interest in supernatural threat response. The asset never knows they are being cultivated for the Kolat. From their perspective, they are developing a relationship with a knowledgeable individual who shares their dedication to protecting the Empire. Design note — assets act independently: Jade Magistrates, Kuni Witch Hunters, and Phoenix Inquisitors already pursue supernatural threats as part of their own standing objective (RESPOND_TO_SUPERNATURAL_THREAT — see cross-reference below). They do not need Master Jade’s tips to begin investigating. The tip supplements an investigation already in progress — it improves the asset’s information quality, elevates their priority scoring, and may redirect them toward a specific target they had not yet identified. The asset’s own engine handles the response. Master Jade’s role is coordination and intelligence, not command. Once disposition reaches Friend (+31), the contact is registered in jade_asset_network as an unwitting asset. No RECRUIT_KOLAT_AGENT is used — these are never conscious agents. They receive ANONYMOUS_TIP deliveries and occasionally send informal intelligence back through normal letter correspondence. Maintenance: Master Jade sends at least one anonymous tip to each registered asset per season to maintain the relationship and keep the asset’s trust. If no tip is sent for two consecutive seasons, the asset’s perceived value of the relationship diminishes and they may stop sharing intelligence. Objective alignment: ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION 90, RAISE_DISPOSITION 85, CHARM 80, PROBE 70, ANONYMOUS_TIP 75, WRITE_LETTER 60, BEGIN_TRAVEL 55, DO_NOTHING 0. Inbound intelligence from assets: when an asset sends a field report — an informal letter describing unusual activity in their province — it arrives as a standard incoming letter. The engine tags any incoming letter from a registered jade_asset_network npc_id as source: jade_asset_report. A scanner rule processes these on the AP they arrive: content parsed for supernatural keywords (Taint, oni, maho, spirit, corruption). If found, a Tier 2 threat topic fires in Master Jade’s known_topics tagged source: jade_asset_report and province: the asset’s province_coverage, triggering ASSESS_SUPERNATURAL_THREAT on the next AP. last_report_ic_day updates on receipt. An asset who has reported within the last season is treated as an active intelligence node and weighted more heavily in subsequent ROUTE_ANONYMOUS_INTELLIGENCE routing. Supernatural topic intake: Jade does not automatically know about supernatural threats. Topics enter Jade’s IntelDB through the normal letter and conversation system. CULTIVATE_JADE_ASSET builds a trust relationship between Jade’s cover persona and each asset. Jade Magistrates, Witch Hunters, and Phoenix Inquisitors naturally write to trusted colleagues about concerning developments — a maho cult, an oni sighting, spiritual disturbances. Because Jade’s cover persona is one of those trusted colleagues, assets write to them naturally. The letter travels through normal channels and the topic enters Jade’s known_topics at whatever confidence the asset holds it. Jade’s last_report_ic_day in jade_asset_network tracks this. An asset that stops reporting may have nothing to report, may be dormant (asset_status “dormant” when Jade sends no tips for two seasons), or may be compromised. Without active cultivation, assets drift away and stop writing. Jade learns nothing he is not told. The quality of intelligence is entirely a function of how well relationships are maintained.
