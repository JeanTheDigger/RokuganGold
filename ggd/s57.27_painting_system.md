## 57.27 Painting System

### 57.27.1 Overview

Painting is the display art &#x2014; it creates objects that occupy zone slots and generate persistent effects on every named character who enters the space. The system is structurally a hybrid of the Tattoo system (Section 57.25 &#x2014; a visual object with a subject placed at a location, read by observers, carrying topic links) and the Theater Piece system (Section 57.22 &#x2014; a composed work in distinct formats with subject, framing, and disposition payload). The four painting formats parallel the four theater styles: kakemono (hanging scroll) is the standard workhorse, by&#x014D;bu (folding screen) is the prestige format, emakimono (narrative scroll) is the documentary/propaganda format, and fusuma (sliding door painting) is the permanent architectural installation.

Every painting depicts a specific subject. The subject determines the painting&#x2019;s mechanical effect when displayed or experienced. Subjects link to the topic system, the Fortune system, specific character_ids, clan_ids, or seasonal aesthetics. What you display on your walls is a political and cultural statement &#x2014; and the system treats it as such.

There is no canvas in Rokugan. All painting surfaces are washi (paper), silk, or wood panels &#x2014; the materials already defined in Section 49. The format determines the surface: kakemono are mounted on paper or silk, by&#x014D;bu panels use paper or silk stretched over wood lattice, emakimono are long paper or silk rolls, and fusuma are painted directly onto wooden sliding doors.

### 57.27.2 Painting World Object

Each painting tracks the following fields: painting_id, format (kakemono, byobu, emakimono, fusuma), creator_id, quality_tier (Normal through Legendary), style (yamato_e, sumi_e, or null — declared at composition start, determines emphasis applicability and provenance record), subject_type (seasonal, nature, portrait, religious, battle, clan, literary), subject_id (character_id, fortune_id, topic_id, clan_id, family_id, or null — null for nature and literary subjects), subject_description (text — narrative description of what is depicted), framing (positive or negative), season_affinity (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter, or null — only for seasonal subject_type), topic_ids (list, max 2 entries — primarily for emakimono; empty for most other formats), date_completed, craft_progress (int or null — null when complete, 0 when declared, accumulates during composition), display_zone_id (null or zone_id), display_slot (wall_art, displayed_art, fusuma, or null), copy_of (painting_id or null — emakimono copies only), is_original (true for originals, false for copies). Materials, provenance, and history accumulation follow standard Section 49 rules.

### 57.27.3 Subject System

Every painting carries a subject_type, a subject_id (where applicable), a framing, and optionally a season_affinity or topic_ids. The subject determines the painting&#x2019;s mechanical effect when displayed or experienced.

Seasonal landscape (shiki-e): depicts a specific season &#x2014; spring blossoms, summer mountains, autumn maples, winter snow. Carries season_affinity (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter). subject_id is null. The most common kakemono subject. Generates a seasonal harmony bonus when displayed during the matching IC season (Section 57.27.8). Framing is always positive &#x2014; seasonal landscapes are aesthetically contemplative, not polemical.

Nature (kach&#x014D;-ga): birds and flowers, animals, generic landscapes without strong seasonal marking. subject_id is null. season_affinity is null. Aesthetically safe, politically neutral. The default subject &#x2014; pleasant, inoffensive, appropriate everywhere. Always positive framing.

Portrait (nise-e): depicts a specific named character (subject_id = character_id). Carries framing: positive (flattering, heroic, dignified) or negative (mocking, unflattering, humiliating). The most politically charged subject type. A positive portrait of the lord flatters them. A negative portrait of a rival is a provocation. Dead characters can be depicted &#x2014; subject_id references the character record regardless of is_alive status.

Religious (butsu-e): Fortune, Buddha, bodhisattva, mandala, shrine scene, spiritual imagery. subject_id = fortune_id from the Fortune system (Section 2.5). When displayed at a shrine or temple zone, generates passive Worship Points (Section 57.27.12). Always positive framing &#x2014; depicting a Fortune negatively is blasphemy, not art.

Battle/historical (kassen-e): depicts a specific battle, campaign, or historical event. subject_id = clan_id or character_id — the clan or individual whose perspective the painting takes, whose victory it celebrates or whose defeat it mourns. This is the disposition target when polarization fires. The battle’s event itself is carried in the topic_ids list (max 2 entries, same as emakimono and Theater pieces) for topic amplification and passive topic delivery. A painting of the Lion victory at Beiden Pass has subject_id = Lion clan_id, framing = positive, and topic_ids = [Beiden Pass battle topic_id]. Disposition shifts target the Lion Clan. Topic amplification sustains the battle topic. Carries framing: positive (heroic victory from the subject’s perspective) or negative (depicting the enemy’s defeat humiliatingly — the subject is still the victorious party, but the framing emphasises the enemy’s suffering rather than the victor’s glory). The painting keeps the depicted event alive in the zone’s ambient topic pool — visitors who enter absorb the topic passively (Section 57.27.11). The most common emakimono subject.

Clan/family (mon-e): depicts a clan&#x2019;s founding story, iconic imagery, mon, or cultural identity. subject_id = clan_id or family_id. Carries framing: positive (tribute, celebration) or negative (caricature, mockery). Political signaling &#x2014; displaying Crane clan art at your court signals alignment. Displaying negatively framed clan art is a provocation.

Literary/court scene: depicts a scene from a famous tale, a poetic moment, a court tableau. subject_id is null. No mechanical target &#x2014; pure cultural prestige. Signals refined taste. Always positive framing. The Crane default for kakemono.

### 57.27.4 Zone Art Slots

Eligible interior zones support up to three independent painting slots. Each slot holds one painting and generates its own visitor effects. The slots are: wall_art_slot &#x2014; holds one kakemono. The tokonoma alcove or wall display position. Subject to seasonal rotation. displayed_art_slot &#x2014; holds one by&#x014D;bu. The floor-standing furniture piece. Already defined in Section 29.15 (Shiba Artisan R5 technique). fusuma_slot &#x2014; holds one fusuma painting. Architectural &#x2014; part of the building&#x2019;s sliding doors. Permanent until repainted or destroyed. All three slots may be occupied simultaneously. A fully decorated castle great hall could have Masterwork fusuma on the doors, an Exceptional gold leaf by&#x014D;bu as a room divider, and a seasonally-appropriate Fine kakemono in the tokonoma. Three different artists&#x2019; work, three different visitor effects firing simultaneously.

Emakimono occupy no slot &#x2014; they are inventory items that are read, not displayed. Exterior zones (CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD, TSUBONIWA) do not support painting slots &#x2014; those zones use the garden and bonsai_display_slot systems instead. Interior zone eligibility: Castle/Shiro, court locations, Temple/Shinden, estate/private quarters. Not supported on: open wilderness, military camps, roads, zones without permanent structures. Full Lesser Zone subtype definitions for painting slot eligibility are defined in Section 57.36 (Flag Matrix). The wall_art_slot, displayed_art_slot, and fusuma_slot flags are specified per zone subtype. Section 29.15 pending note also resolved.

### 57.27.4a Placement Permissions

Each zone art slot requires permission from the zone’s lord before an artisan may place or remove a painting. Three permission types exist, one per slot: wall_art_permission (kakemono placement), displayed_art_permission (byōbu placement), fusuma_painting_permission (fusuma composition and painting). Each is a simple flag — character-specific, zone-specific — following the same pattern as garden_permission (Section 57.23.2) and shide_placement_permission (Section 57.26.10).

Granting: the zone lord grants permission through direct conversation, court interaction, or acceptance of a REQUEST_ART / OFFER_ART_COMMISSION proposal. No ActionID required for the grant itself — it is a consent flag set when the lord agrees. For commissions through the formal pipeline (Sections 57.23.3–57.23.4), the commission acceptance automatically sets the appropriate permission flag for the specified zone. For voluntary offers outside the commission system, the artisan requests permission through social interaction (direct address or WRITE_LETTER), and the lord evaluates based on disposition toward the artisan and the artisan’s perceived skill.

Persistence: permission persists until explicitly revoked by the lord. The lord may revoke at any time. Revocation does not remove currently displayed paintings — it blocks future placement. A lord who revokes wall_art_permission cannot force the artisan’s kakemono off the wall through revocation alone; the lord uses the standard removal mechanic (0 AP, lord authority) to take down a displayed painting. Revocation prevents the artisan from hanging a replacement.

Lordship change: when a zone changes lords (death, succession, conquest), all three painting permissions lapse after a 1 IC season grace period — same pattern as garden_permission (Section 57.23.2). During the grace period, the artisan may still place or rotate paintings normally. After the grace period, the permission is void and must be re-granted by the new lord. Currently displayed paintings are not affected by permission lapse — they remain in their slots until the new lord removes them or the artisan removes them voluntarily. The grace period allows seasonal rotation to continue uninterrupted through a lordship transition.

The lord may place or remove paintings in their own zone without permission — they are the authority. A lord with Artisan: Painting skill may create and display their own work without a permission flag. The permission system exists to govern other characters’ access to the lord’s walls.

### 57.27.5 COMPOSE_PAINTING

ActionID: COMPOSE_PAINTING. Category 11 (Personal Development). All four formats use this ActionID with format-specific parameters. Roll: Artisan: Painting / Awareness vs TN 15 per AP spent. Progress per AP: roll result minus 15, minimum 1 on success. Declared Raises add +5 progress per Raise achieved. On failure (below declared TN): 0 progress, AP spent. Context: any for kakemono, by&#x014D;bu, emakimono. AT_ZONE at the target zone for fusuma &#x2014; the painter must be physically present at the doors they are painting. Skill gate: Artisan: Painting rank must equal or exceed the target quality tier at completion (Normal = rank 1, Fine = rank 2, Exceptional = rank 3, Masterwork = rank 4, Legendary = rank 5).

Declaration at start (cannot change mid-composition): format, style (yamato_e, sumi_e, or null), subject_type, subject_id (if applicable), framing, season_affinity (if seasonal), target quality tier, and for emakimono: target topic_ids. Declaration is 0 AP — it is a statement of intent.

Progress thresholds by format and quality: Kakemono &#x2014; Normal = 5 points (~2 AP at Painting 3), Fine = 10 (~4 AP), Exceptional = 18 (~6 AP), Masterwork = 28 (~10 AP), Legendary = 40 (~14 AP). By&#x014D;bu &#x2014; Normal = 15 (~5 AP), Fine = 30 (~10 AP), Exceptional = 50 (~17 AP), Masterwork = 75 (~25 AP), Legendary = 100 (~33 AP). Emakimono &#x2014; Normal = 20 (~7 AP), Fine = 40 (~14 AP), Exceptional = 65 (~22 AP), Masterwork = 95 (~32 AP), Legendary = 130 (~43 AP). Fusuma &#x2014; Normal = 15 (~5 AP), Fine = 30 (~10 AP), Exceptional = 50 (~17 AP), Masterwork = 75 (~25 AP), Legendary = 100 (~33 AP).

Composition degradation: applies to by&#x014D;bu and emakimono only. If 1 full IC season (90 IC days) passes without COMPOSE_PAINTING AP spent on the piece, accumulated progress halves. Each subsequent abandoned season halves again. Same pattern as Theater composition degradation (Section 57.22.5a). Does not apply to kakemono (progress threshold is low enough that resuming after any gap is trivial — the artist completes in a few sessions regardless). Does not apply to fusuma (the work is physically present on the doors in front of the painter — unlike a byōbu, which requires intellectual continuity across six separate panels composed in sequence, a fusuma painter resumes exactly where they stopped because the surface itself records their progress. Byōbu and emakimono degrade because their complexity is compositional — the artist must hold the multi-panel or multi-scene structure in their mind, and that mental architecture decays with time).

Raises at completion: when accumulated progress meets or exceeds the threshold, the painting is complete. Raises on a successful completing roll upgrade quality tier one step per Raise (capped at Legendary), following standard Section 49 crafting rules. For emakimono specifically, 2 Raises can add a topic_id to the topic_ids list (max 2 entries, same as Theater). On completion: painting_object is created in the world. craft_progress set to null. Provenance record established per Section 49.

### 57.27.6 Format: Kakemono (Hanging Scroll)

The workhorse format. A vertical scroll displayed in the tokonoma alcove. Materials: paper or silk + ink/pigments. Cost: 3 bu (canonical, Section 49). Silk-mounted kakemono use Uncommon material (+1 Free Raise on crafting roll). The kakemono&#x2019;s unique mechanic is seasonal rotation &#x2014; the expectation that displayed scrolls are swapped to match the current IC season.

Display: occupies the zone&#x2019;s wall_art_slot. Placement is 0 AP &#x2014; simply mounting or unmounting a scroll. Only the zone lord or a character with wall_art_permission may place or remove a kakemono. Removing a displayed kakemono returns it to the owner&#x2019;s inventory. Placing a new kakemono when one is already displayed automatically returns the existing one to the zone lord&#x2019;s inventory.

Seasonal harmony: when a displayed kakemono&#x2019;s season_affinity matches the current IC season, a seasonal harmony bonus fires. All visitor disposition effects from the painting are doubled. The zone lord gains +0.05 Glory per visitor tick instead of the standard +0.01. The painting is correct &#x2014; it demonstrates understanding of the aesthetic order. When the season_affinity does not match: no penalty, but NPCs with elevated Rei or Artisan: Painting rank 2+ who visit the zone register a Tier 5 aesthetic observation. No disposition loss, no Glory loss &#x2014; just a quiet social note that the art has not been rotated. Kakemono without season_affinity (nature, portrait, religious, etc.) are season-neutral &#x2014; always appropriate, never optimal for the harmony bonus. The system rewards maintaining a full four-painting seasonal set and rotating at each season boundary.

### 57.27.7 Format: By&#x014D;bu (Folding Screen)

The prestige format. A six-panel folding screen &#x2014; architectural furniture that dominates a room. Materials: silk or paper panels stretched over wood lattice, lacquered frame, metal hardware. Koku-priced: a standard by&#x014D;bu costs 5+ koku in materials. Gold leaf by&#x014D;bu require gold leaf (Rare material, +2 Free Raises, 10+ koku). Multi-AP composition using the COMPOSE_PAINTING progress track (Section 57.27.5).

Display: occupies the zone&#x2019;s displayed_art_slot. Placement is 0 AP. Only the zone lord or a character with displayed_art_permission may place or remove a by&#x014D;bu. Presence modifier: a by&#x014D;bu&#x2019;s physical size dominates the room. Visitor disposition effects from a by&#x014D;bu are calculated at quality tier +1 (capped at Legendary). A Fine by&#x014D;bu generates the visitor disposition of an Exceptional painting. This reflects the sheer visual impact of a six-panel screen versus a small hanging scroll.

No seasonal rotation. By&#x014D;bu are too large and expensive to swap seasonally. Season_affinity subjects on by&#x014D;bu do not generate harmony bonuses or rotation concerns &#x2014; the format is above seasonal convention. A by&#x014D;bu depicting all four seasons simultaneously (a common historical format &#x2014; Sessh&#x016B;&#x2019;s Landscape of the Four Seasons) is perpetually appropriate.

Gold leaf modifier: a by&#x014D;bu crafted with gold leaf (Rare material) gains a wealth signaling bonus. All visitors with Commerce rank 1+ who enter the zone automatically register the expense. The zone lord&#x2019;s Status is perceived as +0.5 higher by visitors while the gold leaf by&#x014D;bu is displayed &#x2014; a passive, continuous Status perception modifier, not a real Status change.

### 57.27.8 Format: Emakimono (Narrative Scroll)

The documentary format. A long horizontal scroll telling a story through sequential painted scenes &#x2014; battles, romances, religious tales, historical chronicles. Unrolled right-to-left on a low table. The format closest to a Theater piece: it carries subject, framing, and topic linkage, and it is experienced rather than passively displayed. Materials: paper or silk + ink/pigments. Cost: 5+ bu for a standard emakimono, koku-priced for ambitious works. Multi-AP composition using the COMPOSE_PAINTING progress track (Section 57.27.5).

Not displayed &#x2014; read. An emakimono does not occupy any zone art slot. It is stored rolled up in the owner&#x2019;s inventory and brought out to be read. ActionID: PRESENT_EMAKIMONO. Category 11. Cost: 1 AP. Context: AT_ZONE, any context. The owner shows the scroll to a specific recipient (targeted, 1 named character) or to a small gathering (up to School Rank witnesses present at the zone). All recipients must be co-located.

Reading effects: when a named character reads/views an emakimono, two effects fire. First, disposition toward the emakimono&#x2019;s subject shifts based on framing &#x2014; same polarization rule as Theater (Section 57.22.8 Step 2). Positive framing pushes positive opinions higher and negative opinions further negative. Negative framing does the reverse. Magnitude is determined by the emakimono&#x2019;s quality tier: Normal = 1, Fine = 2, Exceptional = 3, Masterwork = 4, Legendary = 5. Second, any topic_ids linked to the emakimono are added to the reader&#x2019;s known_topics if not already present. The scroll is a topic delivery vehicle &#x2014; a battle emakimono teaches the reader about the battle it depicts.

Immunity: same 30 IC day window as Theater. A character who has viewed the emakimono within 30 IC days is immune to a second viewing. pieces_seen field tracks this per painting_id, same as theater piece tracking.

Copying: a character with Artisan: Painting rank equal to or above the emakimono&#x2019;s quality tier can produce a copy. ActionID: COMPOSE_PAINTING (format: emakimono, copy_of: original painting_id). The copy uses half the original composition threshold (the narrative is already composed, the copier reproduces rather than invents). The copy is a new painting_object with is_original: false and copy_of set to the original. Subject, framing, and topic_ids are inherited. Quality is determined by the copier&#x2019;s roll &#x2014; a poor copy of a Legendary original may be Fine at best. Copies carry provenance noting both the original creator and the copier.

Suppression: destroying or stealing all copies of an emakimono destroys the narrative. Unlike Theater pieces (where suppression means eliminating all known_by characters), emakimono suppression means physically acquiring or destroying every copy &#x2014; a covert operations objective. A clan that commissions an emakimono of their great victory and distributes copies across the Empire is running a propaganda campaign. A rival that wants to suppress that narrative must find and destroy every copy.

### 57.27.9 Format: Fusuma (Sliding Door Painting)

The architectural format. A fusuma painting is part of the building &#x2014; painted directly onto sliding door panels. Cannot be carried, stored, or gifted. Exists as part of the zone. Multi-AP composition using COMPOSE_PAINTING at AT_ZONE &#x2014; the painter must be physically present for every AP spent. Materials: the wood panels are the surface (Common &#x2014; the doors already exist), pigments and decorative materials determine visual quality. Zone lord must grant fusuma_painting_permission before work begins, same pattern as garden_permission (Section 57.23.2).

Display: occupies the zone&#x2019;s fusuma_slot &#x2014; separate from wall_art_slot and displayed_art_slot. A zone can have fusuma paintings AND a kakemono AND a by&#x014D;bu simultaneously. Visitor effects fire from all three independently.

Cannot be removed without destruction. A fusuma painting is destroyed by: repainting (a new COMPOSE_PAINTING action over the same panels &#x2014; the old painting_object is destroyed and replaced), physical destruction of the doors (siege, fire, deliberate demolition), or explicit lord-ordered removal (the doors are replaced with unpainted panels, painting_object destroyed). Commissioning a new fusuma painting over an old one generates a topic if the old painting was Fine or above: &#x201C;[Lord name] has ordered [old artist name]&#x2019;s fusuma at [zone name] repainted.&#x201D; Tier 3 for Exceptional and above, Tier 4 for Fine. Erasing someone&#x2019;s work from your walls is a social statement.

Architectural persistence: fusuma survive zone lordship changes. A new lord inherits the previous lord&#x2019;s fusuma. Choosing to keep them signals respect for the previous administration. Choosing to repaint signals a clean break. This creates a political decision point on every lordship transition at zones with notable fusuma.

### 57.27.10 Visitor Effects

Any named character entering a zone with a displayed painting (kakemono in wall_art_slot, by&#x014D;bu in displayed_art_slot, or fusuma in fusuma_slot) automatically receives a disposition bonus toward the painting&#x2019;s creator. The creator themselves are excluded (character_id must not equal creator_id). Bonus scales with quality tier: Normal = +1, Fine = +2, Exceptional = +3, Masterwork = +4, Legendary = +5. By&#x014D;bu presence modifier: by&#x014D;bu visitor effects calculate at quality tier +1 (capped at Legendary). Duration: 4 IC months. Fires once per character per painting per visit &#x2014; a character holding an active disposition bonus from a specific painting does not receive it again until it expires and they re-enter the zone. Each painting fires its own independent visitor bonus &#x2014; a zone with kakemono, by&#x014D;bu, and fusuma generates three separate disposition effects toward three creators on a single visit.

Seasonal harmony (kakemono only): when the kakemono&#x2019;s season_affinity matches the current IC season, all visitor disposition effects from the kakemono are doubled. Duration remains 4 IC months. A Fine kakemono (+2) becomes +4 during its matching season. The zone lord&#x2019;s Glory tick rate increases from +0.01 to +0.05 per visitor tick during harmony.

Glory tick: each time visitor_count_since_last_tick reaches 5 for a specific painting, the creator receives +0.1 Glory. The zone&#x2019;s current Daimyo receives +0.01 Glory (hosting bonus). Cooldown: 1 IC season between Glory ticks per painting. The creator&#x2019;s own visits do not increment visitor_count. Each painting tracks its visitor count independently &#x2014; three paintings in the same zone generate three separate Glory tick streams.

painting_visit_memory flag: stored on any named character who received a disposition bonus from a specific displayed painting. Fields: painting_id, creator_id (at time of visit), zone_id, last_visit_date. Persists after the 4 IC month bonus expires. Capped at 200 most recent visitors per painting; entries older than 5 IC years purge. Used to route lifecycle topics to past visitors. Separate from garden_visit_memory and bonsai_visitor_memory — a character may hold all three types for objects in the same zone.

Familiarity decay (applies to all permanently displayed art — paintings, sculptures, gardens, and bonsai): a displayed work that has occupied the same slot continuously for more than 1 IC year begins losing visitor impact. The visitor disposition bonus reduces by 15% per IC year of continuous display beyond the first, flooring at 50% of the original value. A Fine kakemono (+2) displayed for 1 IC year: full +2. After 2 IC years: +1.7 (85%). After 3 IC years: +1.4 (70%). After 5+ IC years: +1.0 (50% floor, permanent). The decay reflects that regular visitors grow accustomed to art they see every season — the painting hasn’t changed, but their wonder has faded. Rotating the art resets the decay: removing a painting and replacing it with a different one starts the new painting’s decay clock at zero. Temporarily removing a painting and re-hanging the same one does NOT reset the clock — the visitors remember it. This creates a long-term incentive for lords to commission new art periodically rather than relying on a single installation forever. Ikebana is exempt from familiarity decay — arrangements die naturally before any decay could apply. Fusuma and religious statuary decay at half rate (7.5% per year, flooring at 75%) because architectural and sacred works are experienced differently from decorative art — the doors of a great hall and the Fortune statue in a shrine are part of the space’s identity, not just adornment. PROVISIONAL: decay rates pending playtesting.

### 57.27.10a Null and Deceased Creator Handling

When creator_id is null (ancient or unnamed creator, typical of world-generated paintings at game start), the visitor disposition bonus toward the creator does not fire — there is no living character to receive disposition. The same rule applies when creator_id references a dead character (is_alive: false): the visitor disposition bonus does not fire. In both cases, all other painting effects continue normally: lord hosting Glory ticks, seasonal harmony, clan/family/portrait/religious/battle topic effects, passive Worship Points, gold leaf Status perception modifier, negative framing provocation, and Event Ripple from any disposition effects that do fire (such as the ±1 clan painting modifier). The painting_visit_memory flag is still written for visitors at paintings with null or deceased creators — the visitor saw the painting even though no living creator benefits. Glory ticks for the creator do not fire when creator_id is null or deceased. Lord hosting Glory ticks fire regardless. This parallels the Theater system where author_id null on canonized pieces suppresses the authorship Glory mechanic but does not suppress performance effects (Section 57.22.9).

### 57.27.11 Painting and the Topic System

A displayed painting is a persistent topic presence in the zone. Unlike Theater performance (which fires a burst of topic amplification at the moment of performance and then ends), a displayed painting radiates topic content continuously to every visitor who enters. This is the core topic mechanic of the Painting system — the walls speak silently to everyone who walks through the room. The specific interaction depends on subject_type.

Battle/historical (kassen-e) — explicit topic link: paintings with battle/historical subject_type carry a non-empty topic_ids list linking to specific topics in the topic system. While displayed, two effects fire. First, passive topic delivery: any named character who enters the zone and does not already have the linked topic in their known_topics receives the topic automatically — the painting teaches them about the event, same as receiving a topic through conversation. This is the passive topic delivery parallel to the tattoo’s topic type (Section 57.25.5). Second, passive momentum sustain: each IC season, the painting adds topic_weight × 1 momentum to each linked topic for every 5 named character visitors who entered the zone this season. topic_weight by quality: Normal = 1, Fine = 1, Exceptional = 2, Masterwork = 2, Legendary = 3. This prevents the depicted event from fading to zero momentum through neglect — the painting keeps the memory alive as long as people see it. A battle painting at a busy court can sustain a topic indefinitely. A battle painting at an empty garrison sustains nothing. The momentum sustain does not add new information — it reinforces existing awareness.

Portrait (nise-e) — character topic generation: a portrait of a specific named character generates a passive topic about that character in the zone’s ambient topic pool. Any named visitor who enters the zone and does not have the depicted character in their known_topics receives a Tier 4 topic: “A [quality tier] portrait of [character name] is displayed at [zone name].” If the character is alive, the topic carries their current Status and clan affiliation — the visitor now knows this person exists and what they look like. If the character is dead, the topic carries their historical significance — the visitor learns about a past figure. For negatively framed portraits, the topic string is: “An unflattering [quality tier] depiction of [character name] is displayed at [zone name].” Both the positive and negative portrait topics enter the standard topic propagation system — visitors who learn about the portrait can spread it through conversation and letters. A portrait of the Clan Champion at their own court reinforces their presence. A portrait of a rival at your court is a statement that propagates through the social network.

Clan/family (mon-e) — collective identity topic: a painting depicting a clan or family generates a passive awareness topic about that group. Visitors who enter the zone absorb a Tier 5 ambient topic: “[Clan/family name] art is displayed at [zone name].” This is low-tier and does not propagate far — it is local atmosphere rather than news. The mechanical effect is subtle but cumulative: it pushes the depicted clan or family into the ambient awareness of every visitor, creating a baseline familiarity. For positively framed clan paintings, visitors receive a +1 disposition modifier toward the depicted clan for 30 IC days (temporary, Section 12.2 Category 3). For negatively framed clan paintings, the modifier is −1. These are small — far less than the visitor disposition toward the artist — but they compound with other influences. A castle decorated entirely with Crane clan art subtly pushes every visitor’s clan disposition toward the Crane, one point at a time.

Religious (butsu-e) — Fortune awareness: a religious painting generates a passive Fortune awareness topic for visitors who do not already know the depicted Fortune. Tier 5: “An image of [Fortune name] is enshrined at [zone name].” This supplements the passive Worship Point generation (Section 57.27.12) with a social awareness effect — visitors learn which Fortunes are venerated at this location. Characters with Theology rank 1+ who view a religious painting at a shrine also receive a +1 Free Raise on their next PERFORM_WORSHIP at that shrine if the worship targets the same Fortune depicted — the painting attunes their mind to the specific Fortune before the ritual. This stacks with shide and gohei bonuses (Section 57.26.10, 57.26.13). The +1 is a flat bonus, not quality-scaled — the presence of any image of the Fortune helps focus worship regardless of the painting’s artistic quality.

Seasonal (shiki-e), nature (kachō-ga), and literary — no topic generation: these subject types do not generate topics or modify dispositions toward any specific entity beyond the standard visitor disposition toward the creator. They are aesthetically pleasing, culturally correct, and politically neutral. Their value is in the seasonal harmony bonus (shiki-e only) and in the absence of political signal — a lord who displays only nature paintings is saying nothing about their allegiances, which is itself a choice.

Emakimono topic interaction: emakimono use the battle/historical topic system when their topic_ids are non-empty. On PRESENT_EMAKIMONO, all linked topics transfer to the reader’s known_topics (if not already present) and the reader’s disposition toward the subject shifts per the polarization rule (Section 57.27.8). Emakimono can also carry portrait, clan, or religious subjects — the reading effects follow the same subject-type logic as displayed paintings, but fired actively at the moment of reading rather than passively on zone entry. An emakimono depicting the founding of the Crane Clan with positive framing delivers the Crane clan awareness topic and a +1 Crane disposition modifier to each reader — same as the displayed mon-e painting but experienced as a deliberate social act rather than ambient background.

Design comparison — painting vs theater topic mechanics: Theater fires topic amplification as an immediate burst at performance time, scaling with audience size and topic_weight (Section 57.22.8 Step 4). Painting fires topic presence as a slow continuous drip, accumulating across all visitors over seasons. Theater is a political weapon — a concentrated strike of narrative force. Painting is political infrastructure — a persistent environmental pressure that shapes the baseline of what everyone in the room knows and feels. The two systems complement rather than compete. A lord who commissions both a Kabuki piece and a byōbu about the same battle is running a two-channel campaign: the performance creates an immediate burst of awareness and disposition shifts, the painting sustains and reinforces the message long after the actors have left.

Ambient art topic cap: painting-sourced topics (portrait awareness, clan awareness, Fortune awareness, battle topic delivery) accumulate in characters’ known_topics over time as they visit zones with displayed art. To prevent ambient art noise from overwhelming the topic system, each character may hold a maximum of 20 painting-sourced topics at any time. When a 21st painting topic would be added, the oldest painting-sourced topic (by date_acquired) is purged first. This cap applies only to topics generated by the passive zone-entry mechanism (Section 57.27.11), not to topics generated by active PRESENT_EMAKIMONO or by lifecycle events (completion, destruction, placement). Battle topic momentum sustain (the per-season momentum addition from displayed battle paintings) is also unaffected by the cap — momentum sustain operates on the topic itself, not on individual characters’ known_topics lists. The cap ensures that characters who visit many decorated zones accumulate a manageable set of ambient cultural awareness rather than an unbounded list. PROVISIONAL: cap of 20 pending topic system load testing.

### 57.27.12 Religious Paintings and Passive Worship

A painting with religious subject_type (fortune_id set) displayed at a shrine or temple zone generates passive Worship Points (WP) each IC season without any character performing PERFORM_WORSHIP. The painting’s depiction of the Fortune is itself a form of devotion — its presence sanctifies the space. Passive WP per season by quality: Normal = +0, Fine = +0.1, Exceptional = +0.25, Masterwork = +0.4, Legendary = +0.5. These values are deliberately modest — a painting supplements active worship but never replaces it. Even a Legendary religious painting generates only half a successful PERFORM_WORSHIP action per season. Art makes the shugenja’s job easier; it does not make the shugenja unnecessary. PROVISIONAL: all passive WP values pending worship threshold calibration.

The passive WP is directed at the fortune_id in the painting&#x2019;s subject_id. A painting of Daikoku at a shrine dedicated to Daikoku adds its passive WP directly to Daikoku&#x2019;s worship total for that province. A painting of Daikoku at a shrine dedicated to Inari still generates WP for Daikoku &#x2014; the Fortune depicted, not the shrine&#x2019;s primary dedication, determines where the WP flows. This means a shrine with multiple religious paintings depicting different Fortunes distributes WP across multiple Fortunes.

These values are deliberately modest. A Legendary religious painting generates +1.0 WP per season permanently &#x2014; equivalent to one successful PERFORM_WORSHIP action. The painting supplements active worship but does not replace it. A shrine with a Masterwork religious painting and an active shugenja performing PERFORM_WORSHIP with Exceptional shide and a Fine gohei is a well-maintained spiritual site. A shrine with only the painting and no active worship is being neglected &#x2014; the painting alone cannot sustain the Fortune&#x2019;s needs.

Religious paintings are never displayed at non-shrine/non-temple zones for worship purposes. A religious kakemono in a castle great hall generates standard visitor disposition effects toward the creator but no passive WP &#x2014; the space is not consecrated for worship. Passive WP fires only when the painting&#x2019;s display zone is a shrine or temple.

### 57.27.13 Negative Framing and Provocation

A painting with negative framing displayed at a zone is a passive provocation against the painting&#x2019;s subject. Two provocation triggers exist.

On placement: when a negatively framed painting is placed in any zone art slot, a provocation_flag is written against the subject_id. If subject_id is a character_id, the flag targets that character. If subject_id is a clan_id or family_id, the flag targets the current leader (Clan Champion or Family Daimyo). A placement topic fires: &#x201C;[Lord name] has displayed a [quality tier] painting depicting [subject] unfavorably at [zone name].&#x201D; Tier 3 for Exceptional and above, Tier 4 for Fine and below. Three-ring propagation. The subject (or their clan/family leader) receives the topic directly regardless of propagation scope.

On subject visit: when the subject (the character depicted negatively, or any member of the negatively depicted clan/family) enters the zone and sees the painting, a second provocation_flag fires. This is a direct personal insult &#x2014; the subject has been forced to witness their own humiliation. The visitor&#x2019;s disposition toward the zone lord decreases by the painting&#x2019;s quality tier value (Normal = &#x2212;1, Fine = &#x2212;2, Exceptional = &#x2212;3, Masterwork = &#x2212;4, Legendary = &#x2212;5) as a permanent historical modifier. This is a deliberate act by the lord &#x2014; they chose to display the insult and the subject walked into it.

The provocation_flag from displayed negative paintings connects to the existing provocation systems: Kasuga R2 reads it, Miya Herald R1b reads it, the legal system reads it. A lord who displays a mocking portrait of a rival clan&#x2019;s champion is committing a deliberate, sustained provocation that persists until the painting is removed. Removal of a negatively framed painting does not clear existing provocation_flags &#x2014; the act was committed. It only stops new flags from generating on future visits.

### 57.27.14 Commission Pipeline

All four formats are fully supported through REQUEST_ART and OFFER_ART_COMMISSION, following the existing commission infrastructure (Sections 57.23.3, 57.23.4). REQUEST_ART parameters include art_type (kakemono, byobu, emakimono, fusuma), subject_type, subject_id (if applicable), season_affinity (if seasonal kakemono), and zone_id (for kakemono, by&#x014D;bu, fusuma &#x2014; the zone where the work will be displayed). Emakimono commissions do not require a zone_id &#x2014; the work is an inventory item.

Commission obligation: follows the same structure as garden and bonsai commissions (Section 57.23.5a, 57.24.15). ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE commissions carry formal obligation with delivery window, failure consequences, and forgiveness mechanics. OFFER_ART_COMMISSION commissions are voluntary &#x2014; softer consequences on non-completion. Delivery window scales with format complexity: kakemono = 1 IC season, by&#x014D;bu = 2 IC seasons, emakimono = 3 IC seasons, fusuma = 2 IC seasons. CREATOR_DECEASED, SUSPENDED, ABANDONED, COMPLETED, CANCELLED, and FORGIVEN statuses follow the existing patterns.

Fusuma-specific: fusuma commissions require the artisan to be AT_ZONE for all composition AP. The commission implicitly grants fusuma_painting_permission for the specified zone. If the artisan is deployed elsewhere mid-composition, the commission suspends (same SUSPENDED pattern as garden commissions). Composition progress does not degrade for fusuma (Section 57.27.5) &#x2014; the work waits on the doors.

### 57.27.15 World Generation

Kakemono: at game start, eligible interior zones in castles, courts, temples, and noble residences are seeded with kakemono. Major courts (Imperial Court, Clan Champion courts): Exceptional to Masterwork kakemono, seasonally appropriate for the game start season. Provincial courts: Fine to Exceptional. Minor courts: Normal to Fine. Village shrines and minor temples: Normal or empty (50% probability). Creator_id set to a local artisan NPC or a historical unnamed artisan (creator_id null for ancient works). Crane and Phoenix territories seed at one quality tier higher than baseline.

By&#x014D;bu: major castle great halls and Champion courts are seeded with by&#x014D;bu at Fine to Masterwork quality. Gold leaf by&#x014D;bu appear only at the Imperial Court and the wealthiest Clan Champion courts. Provincial courts: 50% probability of a Fine or Exceptional by&#x014D;bu. Minor courts: no by&#x014D;bu at world start. Creator_id follows the same pattern as kakemono.

Emakimono: narrative scrolls are distributed as inventory items among major lords, historians, and temple libraries. Each Great Clan generates 2&#x2013;4 notable emakimono at world start depicting important clan history. The Imperial Archives (Section 2.1.20) hold a large collection. All world-gen emakimono are is_original: true. Some may have copies already distributed to allied lords. topic_ids are empty at world start &#x2014; they acquire topic linkage through DEDICATE_PIECE-equivalent actions during play or through the subject&#x2019;s inherent topic generation.

Fusuma: major castles and important temples are seeded with fusuma paintings at Fine to Masterwork quality. The Crane Champion&#x2019;s court and the Imperial Palace have Legendary fusuma. Provincial castles: Fine fusuma or empty. Minor holdings: no fusuma. Creator_id set to historical artisan NPCs.

Painting artisan NPCs: every clan seeds at least one NPC with Artisan: Painting rank 2+ per major province. Crane territory seeds the highest-rank painters (rank 3&#x2013;5), primarily Kakita Artisan school type with chosen_artisan_skill: Painting &#x2014; these receive Soul of Brilliance +2k2 on Void Point spend. Phoenix and Dragon territories seed rank 2&#x2013;4. Lion territory seeds rank 2&#x2013;3 (Ikoma historians who paint battle chronicles). Other clans seed rank 1&#x2013;2 as baseline.

### 57.27.16 DISPLAY_PAINTING

ActionID: DISPLAY_PAINTING. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 0 AP. Context: AT_ZONE at an eligible interior zone. The character places a completed kakemono or byōbu from their inventory into the appropriate zone art slot (wall_art_slot for kakemono, displayed_art_slot for byōbu). Requirement: the character must have the appropriate placement permission (wall_art_permission or displayed_art_permission) for the zone, OR be the zone’s lord. The painting must be in the character’s inventory. If a painting already occupies the target slot, it is automatically returned to the zone lord’s inventory and the new painting takes its place. Fusuma paintings do not use DISPLAY_PAINTING — they are created in place through COMPOSE_PAINTING (AT_ZONE) and occupy the fusuma_slot automatically on completion. Emakimono do not use DISPLAY_PAINTING — they are read, not displayed.

Removal: any character with placement permission or the zone lord may remove a displayed painting at 0 AP while AT_ZONE. The painting returns to the remover’s inventory (or the zone lord’s inventory if the remover is the lord). Removal of Fine or above paintings generates a Tier 4 lifecycle topic (Section 57.27.21). Lord-ordered removal follows the standard pattern — the lord has absolute authority over their walls.

### 57.27.16a Seasonal Rotation Trigger

At each IC season boundary, the NPC engine evaluates seasonal rotation for every zone with an occupied wall_art_slot. If the current kakemono has a season_affinity that does not match the incoming season, the engine checks whether the zone lord or an artisan with wall_art_permission has a kakemono in inventory with the correct season_affinity. If yes: DISPLAY_PAINTING fires automatically, swapping the old kakemono for the seasonally appropriate one. No AP cost, no scoring — this is a standing maintenance action parallel to checking shide degradation. If no matching kakemono is available in anyone’s inventory: the mismatched kakemono remains displayed. The lord’s NPC evaluation (Section 57.27.17) fires COMMISSION_WALL_ART to acquire one for next season. If the kakemono has no season_affinity (nature, portrait, religious, etc.): no rotation evaluated. The painting stays.

### 57.27.17 NPC Engine — NeedType Decomposition

Five NeedTypes decompose into painting actions. Each is defined below with its trigger conditions, personality weights, and action decomposition chain.

MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD (lord-tier NeedType — new): “My court lacks cultural refinement.” Trigger: the lord holds at least one eligible interior zone AND any of the following: a wall_art_slot is empty, a displayed_art_slot is empty at a major court zone, a fusuma_slot is empty at a castle/temple, or the current kakemono’s season_affinity does not match the current IC season. Personality weights: Rei (Courtesy) +25 (the strongest driver — a refined lord maintains their court’s aesthetic environment), Crane/Phoenix/Dragon clan +15, Artisan skill rank 1+ = +10, Chugi (Duty) +5 (maintaining the court is part of governance). Urgency: low by default. +10 if the zone is a Clan Champion court or Imperial Court (prestige venues demand higher standards). +5 if the zone has had at least 10 named visitors this season (a busy court with bare walls is more embarrassing than a quiet garrison). Decomposition: MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD decomposes into four sub-objectives, evaluated independently per empty or mismatched slot. COMMISSION_WALL_ART → REQUEST_ART (art_type: kakemono). Score: 60. COMMISSION_DISPLAYED_ART → REQUEST_ART (art_type: byobu). Score: 50 (byōbu are expensive; the threshold is higher). COMMISSION_FUSUMA → REQUEST_ART (art_type: fusuma). Score: 40 (rarest; only lords with strong cultural motivation). Seasonal rotation deficit → if a matching kakemono exists in inventory, DISPLAY_PAINTING fires at score 70 (immediate, no cost). If no match exists, COMMISSION_WALL_ART fires with season_affinity specified. Score: 65 (urgency from aesthetic mismatch). PROVISIONAL: all scores pending playtesting.

COMMISSION_EMAKIMONO (lord-tier sub-objective — fires from MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD or independently): trigger differs from zone-based commissions. A Tier 2 or Tier 3 topic involving the lord’s clan is in their known_topics, AND the lord’s cultural interest weight exceeds threshold, AND no active emakimono commission exists. REQUEST_ART (art_type: emakimono, topic_id). Score: 55. The lord wants a visual record of a significant event. Lion lords weight +15 for battle topics. Crane lords weight +15 for literary and cultural topics. PROVISIONAL.

ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE integration: a lord may order a vassal artisan to paint through ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (Section 12.7). The vassal objective specifies format, subject, and target zone (for kakemono/byōbu/fusuma) or topic_id (for emakimono). The vassal commission carries formal obligation with delivery window and failure consequences, following the same structure as garden ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE commissions (Section 57.23.5a). Delivery windows: kakemono = 1 IC season, byōbu = 2 IC seasons, emakimono = 3 IC seasons, fusuma = 2 IC seasons. The lord fires ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE when: MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD is active AND a vassal artisan with Artisan: Painting rank 2+ exists in the lord’s feudal chain AND no external commission from REQUEST_ART has been accepted within 1 IC season of the request. The lord prefers commissioning a vassal over soliciting outside artists because the vassal relationship carries obligation.

ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION (artisan-side NeedType — extended): “I am moved to paint.” Trigger conditions: character has Artisan: Painting rank 1+, no active COMPOSE_PAINTING in progress, no active military/siege/survival NeedType at urgency > 60, and character holds strong opinion on at least one subject (disposition ≥ +11 Ally or ≤ −11 Rival toward any clan, family, or named character). Same trigger structure as Theater ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION (Section 57.22.11). Personality weights: Jin (Compassion) +20, Rei (Courtesy) +15, Meiyo (Honor) +10, Ambition +10, Seigyo (Control) −15, Ketsui (Determination) −10. Same weights as Theater. Decomposition: COMPOSE_PAINTING enters the Phase 5 candidate pool. Format selection: AT_ZONE at a court with empty wall_art_slot → kakemono (score 55). Strong opinion + active topic momentum > 40 → emakimono (score 60). High SEEK_GLORY + major court → byōbu (score 60). AT_ZONE with empty fusuma_slot + permission → fusuma (score 55). Default → kakemono (score 55). Subject selection: scan disposition map, select strongest opinion, framing matches direction. Prefer subjects with active topic_ids in known_topics. Urgency: +20 if intended subject has active topic momentum > 60 (the NPC feels the political moment). PROVISIONAL.

Commission response: when an artisan receives a REQUEST_ART topic from a lord, the specific commission overrides ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION format and subject selection. Acceptance/decline follows OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (Section 57.23.4). Evaluation: disposition toward lord (Neutral+ = likely accept), skill vs requested quality, travel distance (fusuma), personal opinion on subject. An artist asked to paint a heroic portrait of someone they despise: high Chugi = obedient execution, high Makoto = honest refusal, high Manipulation = accept and produce negative framing (subversive compliance — the lord receives a painting that technically depicts the subject but with subtle unflattering treatment that a skilled observer can detect through provenance investigation). PROVISIONAL.

DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP (political painting — extended): when DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP is the active NeedType and the NPC has Artisan: Painting rank 1+, COMPOSE_PAINTING enters the candidate pool as an alternative to direct court actions. Alignment score: 50 (below GOSSIP at 80 but available in any context, same logic as Theater political composition in Section 57.22.12). Score modifiers: +20 if not AT_COURT (painting is available when social actions are not), +15 if active topic matching subject has momentum > 40, −20 if in true isolation (no audience for eventual display). Format preference: negatively framed portrait (score +10 — the most directly targeted political weapon), negatively framed clan painting (score +5 — broader but less precise), battle emakimono depicting the target’s defeat (score +10 — narrative weapon with topic linkage). The NPC chooses the format that best serves the political objective.

MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION (topic-driven painting — extended): when MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION is the active NeedType and the NPC has Artisan: Painting rank 1+, COMPOSE_PAINTING (emakimono or battle kakemono/byōbu) enters the candidate pool. Alignment score: 45. Score modifiers: +15 if the target topic has momentum > 40 (the painting will amplify something already moving), +20 if not AT_COURT. The NPC prefers emakimono format when the goal is topic amplification because emakimono carry topic_ids explicitly and can be copied for distribution — a propaganda tool. Battle byōbu is preferred when the goal is sustained ambient pressure at a specific location.

SEEK_GLORY (display and presentation — extended): when SEEK_GLORY is active and the NPC holds a completed painting in inventory, DISPLAY_PAINTING and PRESENT_EMAKIMONO enter the candidate pool. DISPLAY_PAINTING (kakemono at a high-traffic zone): score 60. DISPLAY_PAINTING (byōbu at a major court): score 70. PRESENT_EMAKIMONO (at a court with relevant audience): score 65. Score modifiers: +15 if the painting’s topic_ids contain active topics with momentum > 30 (political relevance increases the Glory payoff), +10 if the zone has > 10 named visitors per season (high-traffic venues generate more Glory ticks). If no display opportunity exists (no permission, no empty slot, no audience): BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the nearest viable venue scores at 55, competing against other travel objectives.

### 57.27.18 NPC Engine — Post-Completion Decision

When COMPOSE_PAINTING resolves and a painting is complete, the NPC immediately evaluates three competing disposition paths for the finished work. The highest-scoring path fires on the next Phase 5.

Path 1 — Commissioned delivery: if an active commission_record exists for this painting, delivery to the commissioning lord is the highest priority. BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the lord (or the specified zone for fusuma, which is already complete in place) scores at 90. On arrival: DISPLAY_PAINTING fires for kakemono/byōbu; DELIVER_GIFT fires if the commission specifies a gift delivery; fusuma is already installed. Commission_record transitions to COMPLETED.

Path 2 — Self-initiated display: if no commission exists, the artisan evaluates DISPLAY_PAINTING or PRESENT_EMAKIMONO. For kakemono/byōbu: the artisan targets the nearest zone where they hold placement permission and the relevant slot is empty. Score: 70 for byōbu (large investment warrants display), 60 for kakemono. If no permission exists anywhere, the artisan evaluates OFFER_ART_COMMISSION toward the nearest lord with an empty slot and disposition Neutral+. Score: 55. For emakimono: PRESENT_EMAKIMONO toward the nearest high-disposition named character. Score: 65. If topic_ids are non-empty and linked topics have momentum > 30: +15 (political urgency).

Path 3 — Gift: the painting enters the standard DELIVER_GIFT evaluation pool. The artisan targets the highest-disposition named character in met_characters for whom the painting is an appropriate gift. Score: 50 (lower than display because display generates ongoing Glory; a gift is a one-time disposition event). Quality-scaling: Masterwork or Legendary paintings score +10 as gifts because their provenance and History Point potential make them politically significant objects beyond their display value.

### 57.27.20 Emakimono &#x2014; Copying

A character with Artisan: Painting rank equal to or above the emakimono&#x2019;s quality tier may produce a copy using COMPOSE_PAINTING (format: emakimono, copy_of: original painting_id). The copier must have physical access to the original &#x2014; the emakimono must be in their inventory or in the inventory of a co-located character who grants access (same consent pattern as noshi wrapping, Section 57.26.7).

Copy threshold: half the original composition threshold (the narrative is composed, the copier reproduces). A Fine original (threshold 40) requires 20 progress to copy. Quality is determined by the copier’s completing roll — same Raise-at-completion rules. A copy’s quality cannot exceed the original’s quality tier regardless of the copier’s skill. A Legendary artisan copying a Fine emakimono produces at best a Fine copy. Generational degradation: each copy tracks a generation field (original = generation 0, copy of original = generation 1, copy of a copy = generation 2, etc.). The maximum quality tier a copy can achieve is reduced by one step per generation beyond the first. Generation 1 copies: maximum = original’s quality (no reduction). Generation 2 copies: maximum = original’s quality minus one tier. Generation 3: minus two tiers. Generation 4+: minimum Normal regardless of original quality. A Legendary emakimono (generation 0) can produce Legendary copies (generation 1), which can produce at best Masterwork copies (generation 2), which produce at best Exceptional copies (generation 3), which produce Normal copies (generation 4+). This creates a natural degradation chain — mass copying from copies of copies dilutes quality, incentivising access to the original or first-generation copies for high-quality reproduction. Provenance investigation (Section 57.27.22) can reveal a copy’s generation through the copy_of chain.

Copy provenance: the copy&#x2019;s provenance record notes both the original creator (inherited from the original&#x2019;s provenance) and the copier. Investigation of a copy can reveal it is not the original through Artisan: Painting / Awareness or Investigation / Perception vs TN scaled by copy quality (Fine copy = TN 25, Exceptional = TN 30, Masterwork = TN 35, Legendary = TN 40 &#x2014; better copies are harder to distinguish from originals). Artisan: Painting rank 3+ grants 1 Free Raise on this roll.

### 57.27.22 Provenance Investigation

Standard Section 49 provenance investigation rules apply to all paintings. Layer 1 (surface information, always visible): format, approximate quality tier, subject description, material type. Layer 2 (deep provenance, gated by rolls): creator identity, creation date, specific material origins, ownership history.

Creator identification: Lore: History or Artisan: Painting / Awareness vs TN by creator fame. Well-known Kakita master = TN 15. Regional artisan = TN 20. Unknown artisan = TN 25. Ancient or unsigned work = TN 30. Artisan: Painting rank 2+ grants 1 Free Raise. Emakimono copy detection: Artisan: Painting / Awareness or Investigation / Perception vs TN by copy quality (Fine = TN 25, Exceptional = TN 30, Masterwork = TN 35, Legendary = TN 40). Artisan: Painting rank 3+ grants 1 Free Raise. On success: the investigator learns the work is a copy and gains the original’s painting_id. On failure: the copy passes as an original.

Framing detection: a painting’s framing (positive or negative) is not always immediately obvious. Overtly negative works — a clear caricature, an explicitly mocking depiction — are self-evident (no roll required). Subtly negative works — a portrait that technically depicts the subject but with unflattering treatment in composition, expression, or symbolic detail — require investigation to detect. Roll: Artisan: Painting / Awareness or Investigation / Perception vs TN scaled by the painter’s skill at concealment. The TN is the painter’s original COMPOSE_PAINTING roll result at completion — a masterful painter conceals their mockery more skillfully. A painter who rolled 25 to complete the painting set TN 25 for framing detection. Artisan: Painting rank 2+ grants 1 Free Raise. On success: the investigator detects the negative framing and learns the painting is not what it appears. This information enters their known_topics as a Tier 4 topic: “[Painting] displayed at [zone name] contains concealed negative framing toward [subject].” On failure: the painting passes as positively framed. Subversive compliance context: when a painting was commissioned with positive framing through ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE and the artisan produced negative framing instead, detection exposes both the mockery and the disobedience. The lord who commissioned the painting receives the detection topic directly if it reaches them through propagation. The artisan’s commission_record shows the ordered framing (positive) alongside the painting’s actual framing (negative) — the discrepancy is evidence of deliberate subversion. Consequences: Honor −0.5 for the artisan (disobedience to a lord’s direct order), disposition −10 from the commissioning lord, and a Tier 3 topic: “[Artisan name] deliberately subverted [Lord name]’s commission.”

### 57.27.25 Siege, Sacking, and Zone Destruction

When a zone is captured, sacked, or destroyed, paintings in that zone are affected based on format. Kakemono and byōbu are portable objects — they can be looted. On zone capture (siege resolved, new lord takes control): the conqueror inherits all displayed paintings as inventory items. The paintings transfer to the conqueror’s possession with full provenance intact. Choosing to keep enemy art or destroy it is a political decision. Keeping a rival’s clan painting on your wall after conquest is a statement of dominance. Destroying it is a statement of contempt. Both generate topics. On zone sacking (destructive assault without intent to hold): all kakemono and byōbu have a quality-scaled survival probability. Normal = 20%, Fine = 30%, Exceptional = 40%, Masterwork = 50%, Legendary = 60%. Surviving paintings are looted by the attacking force (transferred to the attacking lord’s inventory). Destroyed paintings generate provenance records with destruction_cause: SACKED and are retained as historical records. On zone destruction (total destruction — fire, earthquake, deliberate demolition): all paintings in all slots are destroyed. No survival probability. Provenance records retained.

Fusuma: architectural — destroyed with the building in all three cases (capture, sacking, destruction). Fusuma cannot be looted because they are painted directly onto the doors. The conqueror of an intact zone inherits the fusuma in place (same as architectural persistence on lordship change). A sacked or destroyed zone loses its fusuma permanently. Emakimono: inventory items — they follow their owner. If the owner is killed during the siege, emakimono in their inventory follow standard estate resolution (inheritance system). If captured as a prisoner, their inventory (including emakimono) is seized by the captor.

Loot topics: looted paintings of Fine quality or above generate topics. “[Conqueror name] seized [quality tier] [format] depicting [subject] from [zone name].” Tier 3 for Exceptional+, Tier 4 for Fine. The original creator receives the topic directly regardless of propagation scope. Destruction topics: paintings of Fine quality or above destroyed during sacking or zone destruction generate topics. “[Creator name]’s [quality tier] [format] depicting [subject] was destroyed in the [siege/sacking/destruction] of [zone name].” Tier 3 for Exceptional+, Tier 4 for Fine. Cultural losses register in the world’s social memory.

Artist grief (cross-cutting rule — applies to all artisan systems): when a living creator’s work is destroyed, looted, or overwritten (fusuma repainted, painting removed and discarded, statue destroyed, garden bulldozed), the creator’s disposition toward the character who ordered or caused the destruction drops. The magnitude scales with the destroyed work’s quality: Normal = −2, Fine = −5, Exceptional = −10, Masterwork = −15, Legendary = −25. This is a permanent historical modifier — the destruction of a life’s work is not forgiven. The disposition drop targets the lord who ordered the destruction (for deliberate acts like repainting fusuma or removing paintings) or the commanding general (for siege/sacking). If the destroyer is unknown to the creator (never met, no met_characters entry), the disposition drop targets the destroyer’s clan instead — the creator blames the clan, not the individual. This connects to the existing provocation and topic systems: a creator who holds −25 disposition toward a lord who destroyed their Legendary work may seek revenge through the social system (DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP), the legal system (if the destruction was unlawful), or by refusing future commissions from that lord and their allies. The grief is not just emotional — it has mechanical consequences that ripple through the social network.

### 57.27.26 Painting Emphases

Artisan: Painting emphases grant +1k0 on COMPOSE_PAINTING rolls when working in the relevant format or style. Six emphases are defined. Format emphases: Kakemono (hanging scroll composition), Byōbu (folding screen multi-panel composition), Emakimono (narrative scroll sequential composition), Fusuma (architectural door painting). Style emphases: Yamato-e (polychrome, decorative, rich colour — associated with the Crane, the Imperial Court, and the classical tradition), Sumi-e (ink wash monochrome, minimalist — associated with the Dragon, Zen temples, and the Brotherhood of Shinsei). A painter may hold multiple emphases. Format emphases apply when composing that specific format. Style emphases apply when the painting’s aesthetic matches the style — declared at composition start alongside the other parameters. A painting can be both Kakemono format and Yamato-e style, receiving both emphases if the painter holds them. Emphasis acquisition follows the standard emphasis purchase rules (Section 24).
