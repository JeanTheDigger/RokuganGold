## 11.10 Flagged System — The Way of the Daimyo (High-Level Positions)

Source: Appendix Two (The Way of the Daimyo). An entire appendix defines six high-level samurai positions with distinct duties and gameplay hooks. These map to the lord character system and to roles player characters can hold on the World Map.

- Ambassador: Represents their clan at another clan's court. Primary tool for inter-clan diplomacy and information gathering.

- Governor: Administers a territory. Responsible for taxation, law enforcement, and managing the civilian population. Most directly connected to our resource systems.

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Law ****&**** Order):** The Governor position’s law enforcement responsibility is supported by the magistrate hierarchy documented in Section 11.3. Governors appoint clan magistrates, oversee doshin, and can be compelled to brief Emerald Magistrates operating in their territory (Section 11.3.6, Charter duties).

- Keeper of the Temple: Oversees a major religious site. Primary interface with the Kami worship system.

- Master Sensei: Runs a dojo. Trains and produces samurai units. Relevant to the military unit generation system.

- Merchant Patron: Officially sponsors a merchant operation without personally engaging in trade. The primary Koku-generating position for samurai who understand commerce.

- Warlord: Commands military forces. Primary interface with the army and war systems.

**☑ RESOLVED — CUT:** *CUT — The six Way of the Daimyo positions (Ambassador, Governor, Keeper of the Temple, Master Sensei, Merchant Patron, Warlord) will not be designed as formal mechanical roles. The existing feudal hierarchy (Local Daimyo through Clan Champion) already determines who can command armies, set taxes, manage provinces, and exercise authority. Adding a parallel layer of appointable positions would create complexity without meaningful gameplay value. The lore descriptions above are retained as flavor for the setting but carry no distinct mechanical hooks. No further design work required.*
