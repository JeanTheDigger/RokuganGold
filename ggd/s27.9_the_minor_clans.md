## 27.9 The Minor Clans

Minor Clans are samurai families granted official Imperial recognition by the Emperor. Unlike the Great Clans, who were founded by the Kami and assigned specific eternal duties, Minor Clans are created for specific purposes, in reward for great deeds, or simply on Imperial whim. They are second-class citizens of the Empire by definition — always outranked by a Great Clan samurai of equivalent position, less well-protected, and working harder for every political gain. Yet they endure, finding purpose in the cracks between Great Clan interests. Most Minor Clans align with the Minor Clan Alliance, headquartered at Kudo Mura, which provides a unified political voice. Each clan listed below exists as an independent entity in the game’s default era. Note that the Fox, Centipede, and Wasp Clans — later absorbed into the Mantis — are covered under Section 27.5.

### 27.9.1 The Badger Clan

Creed: ‘Individual strength allows our clan maximum flexibility.’ One of the oldest Minor Clans, founded in the year 110 by the Crab warrior Hida Domogu after winning a tournament of strength held by Emperor Hantei Genji. The Badger were tasked with guarding the Path of Woe — one of two passes through the Great Wall of the North Mountains — and have done so with stubborn dedication for over a millennium. They have been nearly destroyed twice: once by the demon Hideo no Oni in 1126, and again by the Army of Dark Fire in 1170. Both times they rebuilt. They are deeply self-reliant, contemptuous of magic, and largely forgotten by the rest of the Empire except as an annual line in the Imperial treasury records.

Clan identity: Physical strength is the highest virtue. Deeply competitive, stubbornly individualistic, old-fashioned even by the standards of the Crab. They have changed very little in a thousand years because they have rarely met anyone outside their own clan. Women are treated as martial equals if they can match the men in tests of strength. Shugenja are rare and regarded with mild disdain. The Badger do not ask for help — this has nearly killed them more than once.

Key historical events: The founding tournament between Hida Domogu and Kitsune Mako; the creation of the Shrine of Ryoshun (secret duty, rediscovered in 1166); the rampage of Hideo no Oni and near-annihilation of the clan; the Imperial inquiry of 1166 that nearly disbanded them; the Army of Dark Fire’s destruction of all but one of their fortresses in 1170; rebuilding in the Age of Exploration through adoption of ronin and expanded ties with the Crab and Dragon.

Inter-clan relations: Deep kinship with the Crab — the Badger are in many ways the Crab as they were a thousand years ago. Cordial with the Dragon, who are their nearest neighbors. Largely ignored by everyone else. Resentful of clans that tried to dissolve them in 1166. The Badger have no enemies because no one thinks about them enough to hate them.

**The Ichiro Family:**

Motto: ‘A wall can be torn down. Be the bedrock the wall is built on.’ The founding and only major family of the Badger Clan. Warriors and fortress-keepers. Deeply physical — prefer nodachi, ono, and masakari to katana; many fight unarmed. The annual Great Games tournament at Shiro Ichiro is the social and competitive centerpiece of Badger life. The Ichiro pass their name by blood but respect is earned through deeds, not birth.

**The Fureheshu Vassal Family:**

Founded by a fifth-century warrior who crushed an oni’s skull with his bare hands while it broke his back. The sumai wrestlers and extreme-strength specialists of the clan. Small in number but ferocious in reputation.

**The Tashimi Vassal Family:**

A tiny family of sensei, founded after the Hideo no Oni disaster to preserve the Ichiro Bushi School no matter what. Comprised almost entirely of teachers. Their sole purpose is ensuring the Badger techniques survive even if the rest of the clan does not.

#### Clan Special: The Path of Woe

The Badger have one job, and their mechanics reflect it. The Path of Woe is a strategic chokepoint on the world map — the only viable northern mountain pass in their territory. No army crosses it without Badger permission or a fight. It is irrelevant during peaceful eras and critically important the moment something moves through the northern mountains. The Badger control it absolutely.

#### Stubbornness

The Badger AI carries a hard suppression on REQUEST_AID regardless of threat level. They will not ask for help until they reach a near-destruction threshold, at which point pride breaks and they send word. A player watching the world map can see the Badger slowly losing ground and choose to intervene before the call comes — which earns significantly more Badger disposition than responding after the fact.

#### Shugenja Disdain

Badger NPCs carry a mild default disposition penalty toward shugenja characters — not hostility, but a quiet dismissiveness toward those who rely on the kami rather than their own strength. Badger-born shugenja are a specific NPC type: born into the clan, often fostered out or sent to train elsewhere, rarely returning. They make natural background characters with conflicted loyalties between their birth clan and their school.

#### The Great Games

Once per year, Shiro Ichiro hosts the Great Games — a sumai and strength tournament that draws participants from Minor Clans, occasional Great Clan samurai, and ronin. It functions as a world event: visitors arrive in Badger lands, disposition shifts occur between factions present, and the tournament champion gains Glory and a named reputation. It is one of the few moments the Badger are not invisible to the rest of the Empire, and the one occasion they actively welcome outsiders.

#### Ichiro Bushi [Tier 1 — Baseline] — Badger Clan

**Health: 153 | Attack: 7 | Defense: 5 | Morale: 16 | Morale Defense: 7**

**Special: **+2 Defense when deployed in a mountain pass or fortification tile. Ignores terrain movement and combat penalties.

**Notes: **Heavy weapon and unarmed specialists trained to hit harder than any other Minor Clan unit. High Attack reflects the Ichiro School’s philosophy of overwhelming damage over finesse. Low Morale reflects the clan’s small numbers and isolation, not their will to fight. They replace the generic Bushi Retainer for the Badger Clan.

*Badger army identity: one unit, one pass, one job. They have no cavalry, no shugenja, and no subtlety. What they have is the Path of Woe and the willingness to die holding it.*

### 27.9.2 The Bat Clan

Creed: ‘Knowledge travels on the wind.’ The youngest of the Minor Clans — founded by Yoritomo Komori, a half-spirit Mantis shugenja of extraordinary spiritual gifts, after he anchored the spirit of the deceased Empress Tsudao long enough for Emperor Toturi III to speak with her. The Bat Clan makes its home on the Island of Lost Wilderness, the southernmost and most dangerous island of the Silk and Spice archipelago — a place of poisonous jungle, thin barriers to the Spirit Realms, and a resident tribe of koumori bat-shapeshifter spirits. Their unique magic centers on long-distance communication and spirit interaction. They punch well above their weight politically through this ability.

Clan identity: Curious, inquisitive, open-minded. Most Bat samurai are shugenja. They have a deep reverence for bats as sacred animals and allies, conduct all important ceremonies at night, and have an unusually close relationship with their peasant population due to the shared struggle of taming their island. They approach knowledge the way other clans approach weapons — as the primary tool of survival. Visitors find them slightly eerie.

Key historical events: Founder Komori’s creation of the clan; the taming of the Island of Lost Wilderness with koumori aid; development of the long-distance message-sending magic; the Destroyer War expedition that discovered the Ivory Kingdoms ruins; establishment of Lost Traveler Village in the Colonies; Komori’s retirement and succession by Komori Junsaku.

Inter-clan relations: Strong ties to the Mantis, who consider the Bat a source of quiet pride — a Minor Clan that came from their own ranks. Good relations with Phoenix and Fox through shared spiritual interests. The Bat’s communication abilities have earned them allies among clans who would not normally bother with a Minor Clan.

**The Komori Family:**

Motto: ‘Even the smallest creature sees what others cannot.’ The founding and primary family. Overwhelmingly shugenja, with exceptional natural gifts for Air magic, spirit communication, and illusion. Descendants of the half-spirit founder carry subtle supernatural qualities. The few who lack magical talent train as bushi with the Yoritomo, ensuring the clan has at least minimal military capability. The Komori never speak when alone — Air carries all words, and nothing said is ever truly private.

**The Iongi Vassal Family:**

Founded by Yoritomo Iongi, a Mantis shugenja who discovered the Tetsubo of Thunder on a distant island and gifted it to the Bat for safekeeping. The Iongi watch over the relic’s shrine and maintain the Order of Osano-Wo’s presence on the island.

#### Clan Special: The Neutral Broker

The Bat Clan’s political power comes from the Komori Shugenja school technique — The Kami’s Whispers — which delivers a message to any known character within School Rank × 100 miles by expending a spell slot. In the context of the existing Letter and Topic Propagation systems, this does one specific thing: it removes delivery time entirely. Where a normal letter travels geographically and carries its topic on a delay measured in IC days, a Bat Clan shugenja delivers the same message and topic instantly. No transit time. No interception risk in transit.

The real value of this is not speed for its own sake. The Bat are politically non-threatening — too small to be a military target, allied with Mantis, Phoenix, and Fox, and trusted by all three. This makes them the clan other clans approach when they need to communicate with an enemy without being seen doing so: back-channel diplomacy, secret peace negotiations, warning a rival about a shared threat without admitting the warning was sent. The Bat function as a neutral intermediary broker inside the existing topic and letter network, giving them political leverage far exceeding their military weight.

The Bat have no bushi school. The few clan members who lack magical talent train with the Yoritomo and the clan fields the generic Bushi Retainer rather than a unique unit. Their military security depends entirely on the Mantis alliance.

#### ⚙️ AI Design Note — Shugenja Message Orders (LOCKED)

The Komori Shugenja Message — LOCKED: Komori Shugenja can transmit messages instantly across any distance using a spirit-communion spell. The mechanic uses the Letter System framework (Section 12.7) with the following modifications. Delivery is instant — the message arrives the same IC day it is sent, with no travel delay. No physical letter exists — the message is spoken through the kami, not written. This means INTERCEPT_LETTER cannot intercept it and SEARCH_QUARTERS cannot find evidence of it. The Calligraphy roll does not apply — there is no brushwork to evaluate, so no disposition bonus from letter quality. The message still carries one topic from the sender’s active topic pool, follows the one-message-per-IC-day limit per shugenja, and the met_characters constraint still applies to the recipient. A Komori Shugenja may send a message on behalf of another character if ordered to do so by their lord (operational_superior_id per Section 11.3.18). The Bat Clan Champion’s AI carries elevated weight on brokering communications between allied clans — offering Komori message services as a diplomatic asset in exchange for favours, protection, or political support. This makes the Bat Clan’s tiny shugenja corps one of the most strategically valuable Minor Clan resources in the game despite the clan’s negligible military and economic power.

### 27.9.3 The Dragonfly Clan

Creed: ‘Mountains are just mountains, and rivers are just rivers.’ Founded in the early eighth century when Mirumoto Asijin and Isawa Maroko — a Dragon warrior and a Phoenix shugenja — abandoned their duties for love, and the Dragon and Phoenix Clans united to protect them from Lion retribution. The Emperor, moved by this unprecedented unified action, blessed their union and granted them a family name. The Dragonfly exist in a shallow valley at the foot of the Dragon Clan’s mountains and serve as the official gatekeepers and emissaries of the Dragon — anyone seeking an audience with a Dragon lord must first approach the Dragonfly. This duty earns them the Dragon’s protection and the resentment of every other clan in the Empire.

Clan identity: Pacifistic, philosophical, deeply spiritual. The Dragonfly have not drawn the Ancestral Sword of their clan in over four centuries. They believe violence is a symptom of spiritual illness. They embrace impermanence and change as the fundamental nature of reality, distrust written doctrine as subjective, and encourage individual insight above received wisdom. Other clans find them insufferably serene, possibly cowardly, and diplomatically infuriating. The Lion hate them specifically.

Key historical events: The founding love story of Asijin and Maroko; Yakutsu’s revenge and the arranged duel that killed Asijin; Tonbo Kuyuden’s slaying of Akodo Yakutsu and subsequent vow of non-violence; the Lion invasion during the Four Winds era that razed Kyuden Tonbo; Toturi Sezaru’s restoration of the castle and lands; a second burning during the War of Dark Fire; unexpected rescue by Lion hero Ikoma Otemi during that same war.

Inter-clan relations: Protected by the Dragon, who consider the Tonbo’s gatekeeper role an enlightening test for petitioners. Deep historical enmity with the Lion — though the War of Dark Fire rescue created an uneasy thaw. Good spiritual ties with the Phoenix. Regarded with mild exasperation by most other clans who have had to wait in Tonbo lands for a Dragon audience.

**The Tonbo Family:**

Motto: ‘Understanding cannot be given, only found.’ The founding and primary family. Predominantly shugenja and scholars — in later generations they abandoned the swordsmanship dojo founded by Asijin and became a family of diviners and spirit-speakers. Their shugenja school is renowned for innovation over rote mastery. They are the Empire’s most willing practitioners of musha shugyo, and Dragonfly samurai are encountered throughout Rokugan on pilgrimages of learning. Love marriages are common and nakodo never arrange unions without consent.

**The Koshei Vassal Family:**

Founded by an Akodo lieutenant who burned Tonbo villages during the Four Winds invasion, then returned in guilt to rebuild what he had destroyed. A tiny family of bushi — anomalous within the peaceful Tonbo. They serve as yojimbo and frequently train with the Shiba.

**The Senkensha Vassal Family:**

Almost as old as the Tonbo themselves — founded after a woman named Tonbo Senkensha saved the clan from a killing winter through prophetic dreams. The family sometimes manifests the same gift. No specific duties assigned; they serve wherever their visions lead.

#### Clan Special: The Gate to the Dragon

The Dragonfly valley is the only viable approach to Dragon Clan territory. Any character who is not Dragon Clan themselves must pass through Tonbo lands to reach a Dragon lord. This is a world map routing constraint, not a diplomatic option — there is no route that bypasses it.

#### NPC and AI Characters

When a non-Dragon NPC arrives at Tonbo lands with a Dragon-directed objective, a social check fires against a Dragonfly NPC. Success grants passage. Failure stalls the character — the Dragonfly politely decline to facilitate the visit. The NPC waits, writes letters through the existing letter system, attempts again next season, or abandons the objective entirely. All of this resolves through the existing disposition and NPC objective systems with no new architecture required.

#### Player Characters — ASCII Map Event

A non-Dragon player character arriving at Tonbo lands triggers an ASCII map event — a face-to-face encounter with a Dragonfly NPC who evaluates the petitioner. The player makes a social check using the standard L5R 4th Edition skill system. Success earns an escort through Tonbo lands and onward to the Dragon. Failure results in the character being stranded in Tonbo lands for a set period before they may attempt again. Critical failure may result in being turned back entirely, or detained while the Dragonfly deliberate.

The check TN scales with the petitioner’s reputation. Arriving with a military escort, known hostile intent toward the Dragon, or a history of aggression against the Dragonfly all raise the TN. The Tonbo are pacifists who evaluate intent above all else — characters with high Jin, Rei, or Makoto pass more easily than those who project threat.

#### Dragon Sponsorship

A Dragon Clan lord may sponsor a non-Dragon character through the gate by sending formal word to the Dragonfly ahead of the visit. A sponsored character bypasses the social check entirely and is escorted through without incident. This makes Dragon sponsorship a meaningful political favour — a Dragon player offering to sponsor an ally’s delegation is granting them something no other clan can provide. The Dragon AI will use sponsorship as a diplomatic tool, extending it to clans with high disposition and withholding it from rivals.

#### Strategic Consequence

Destroying or isolating the Dragonfly does not only harm them — it severs every other clan’s access to the Dragon simultaneously. The Dragon AI will never bypass its own gatekeeper by sending heralds out directly; this is a matter of Dragon doctrine, not mechanical inability. Any clan considering aggression against the Dragonfly must weigh the diplomatic isolation that follows. The Lion have razed Kyuden Tonbo twice in history and paid for it both times.

### 27.9.4 The Hare Clan

Creed: ‘Survive. Hunt. Remember.’ Founded in the eighth century by the ronin Reichin — a thief, smuggler, and unlikely hero of Ryoko Owari who distinguished himself against Iuchiban’s Bloodspeaker army and saved the life of the Emerald Champion. The Emperor awarded him a family name and lands south of the River of Gold. The Hare have self-appointed as the Empire’s hunters of maho-tsukai and the Bloodspeaker Cult ever since. In the early twelfth century the clan was destroyed — their castle burned, their champion killed, their people scattered — through a Scorpion-backed frame-up over a Kolat scroll. Clan heir Usagi Ozaki spent years as a fugitive before exposing the conspiracy and winning reinstatement from Emperor Hantei XXXIX. They remain a small, scarred, vigilant clan.

Clan identity: Lean, pragmatic, survivors. Descended from a ronin gambler, they are not strictly honorable in the traditional sense — they value Courage and Duty above all but are comfortable with methods other clans find distasteful. The Usagi charge in; the Ujina assess and ambush. Both families agree that the end justifies means other clans wouldn’t consider. They respect peasants and ronin more than most samurai do, give credence to farmer testimony, and treat their commoners as genuine assets rather than resources.

Key historical events: Reichin’s founding and the first battles against the Bloodspeakers; the Scorpion siege of Shiro Usagi (1123) and false disbandment; Usagi Ozaki’s years as a fugitive and exposure of the Dajan Kolat cell; clan reinstatement and rebuilding; the Ujina family joining the clan; Jama Suru’s assault on Shiro Usagi (1165) beaten back with Daidoji aid; the Destroyer War.

Inter-clan relations: Deeply suspicious of the Scorpion — the clan that destroyed them — though they maintain professional dealings. Grateful to the Crane (Daidoji) for the cavalry that saved them in 1165. Wary alliance with the Yasuki. Good relations with the Minor Clan Alliance. Neutral to most others.

**The Usagi Family:**

Motto: ‘Strike before you are struck.’ The founding family. Quick, reckless, and brave to the point of recklessness. Trained to recognize maho and hunt its practitioners from childhood. Keep journals of every investigation — a practice learned from the Kitsuki. Funerals include three days of fasting and calligraphy scrolls recording the deceased’s deeds, burned so the soul rises on the smoke.

**The Ujina Family:**

Motto: ‘The survivor who does not fight today fights twice tomorrow.’ A ronin named Tokimasa who helped Ozaki destroy a Bloodspeaker cell in Otosan Uchi was granted a family name by Emperor Toturi I and swore fealty to the Hare. The Ujina are infiltrators, ambushers, and intelligence-gatherers. The Usagi find them cold; the Ujina find the Usagi wasteful. Together they are the most effective maho-hunting family pair in the Empire.

#### Clan Special: The Hunt

The Hare carry a standing cross-clan mandate to pursue maho-tsukai and the Bloodspeaker Cult anywhere in the Empire. Like the Asako Inquisitors they plug into the existing Maho_Cult_Spreading and Low_level_Taint_Discovered topic types with a high INVESTIGATE_THREAT weight. The difference is method. The Inquisitors work through proper social channels and are treated with deference. The Hare use approaches other clans find distasteful — the Ujina family specifically are infiltrators and ambushers who do not announce themselves and do not particularly care what other clans think of their methods. They are effective precisely because they are not constrained by appearances.

#### The Scar

The Scorpion destroyed the Hare once through deliberate fabrication. This is encoded as a permanent historical modifier: the Hare AI carries a deep baseline suspicion of Scorpion characters that makes them resistant to Scorpion courtiers and more likely to investigate anything Scorpion-adjacent when a maho topic is active. This is not a blood feud — the Hare maintain professional dealings with the Scorpion — but it is a clan that learned the hard way and does not forget it.

#### Usagi Bushi [Tier 1 — Baseline] — Hare Clan

**Health: 153 | Attack: 6 | Defense: 4 | Morale: 17 | Morale Defense: 7**

**Special: **+2 Attack when flanking or pursuing a routing enemy.

**Notes: **Light, fast hunters built for pursuit rather than line battle. Low Defense reflects minimal armour. The flanking bonus mirrors the Usagi Bushi school’s leap attack philosophy — they hit hardest when the enemy is already off balance. They replace the generic Bushi Retainer for the Hare Clan.

*Hare army identity: not a battle line but a hunting pack. They do not hold ground well but they run down anything that tries to flee, and they have been chasing things that do not want to be found for centuries.*

### 27.9.5 The Monkey Clan

Creed: ‘Every soul can be a hero.’ Founded by Toku — a peasant farmer’s son who fought in Toturi’s Army disguised as a samurai, confessed his deception directly to the Emperor, and was declared a samurai by Imperial fiat on the grounds that he already was one in everything but birth. Toturi I made him Captain of the Imperial Guard and Champion of a new Minor Clan. Toku eventually became the Fortune of Virtue after sacrificing his life against Asahina Yajinden. The Monkey have been Imperial loyalists of unshakeable conviction ever since. In the game’s default era the clan is small but prosperous, aligned with the Scorpion through an arranged marriage of Toku’s daughter to the Scorpion Champion.

Clan identity: Cheerful, sincere, almost aggressively virtuous. The Monkey take Bushido absolutely literally — reciting the seven virtues like a ward against doubt. They laugh at terrible circumstances because despair is a luxury. They defend peasants from samurai when the peasants are right. They give honest ronin honest work. Other clans find them naive; the Monkey consider this a failure of imagination. Their humor is famous throughout the Empire.

Key historical events: Toku’s founding and confession; the granting of Scorpion lands in the Vale of the Monkey; the War Against the Darkness assault on the clan; double destruction and rebuilding of their fortress; Toku’s death and ascension as Fortune of Virtue; the elevation of the Fuzake from vassal family to full family status; the Destroyer War; the appointment of Toku Hikaru as Imperial Chancellor under Iweko I.

Inter-clan relations: Warm alliance with Scorpion — an odd pairing that works through mutual respect and a Scorpion habit of treating the Monkey as a well-intentioned younger sibling. Strong loyalty to the Toturi dynasty’s memory. Generally well-regarded by smaller clans. The Lion admire their martial sincerity; the Crane find their informality exhausting.

**The Toku Family:**

Motto: ‘Virtue is not a rank, it is a choice.’ The founding family. Bushi and magistrates. Known for combining athletic training with philosophical education — students are tested by impossible situations designed to show them both their limits and their will. Many serve as Imperial Legionnaires or roving magistrates. Keep personal journals passed to their children on retirement. Every Toku Torid-e has shrines to the Fortune of Virtue and the three Toturi Emperors.

**The Fuzake Family:**

Motto: ‘All the Fortunes watch; none are more important than the rest.’ Originally a small order of ronin shugenja healers from Toturi’s Army led by Yasuki Garou (later Fuzake Garou). Elevated to full family status by Toturi III’s widow. The Fuzake venerate every Fortune — great and obscure alike — and operate Shinden Fuzake, a joyously chaotic temple complex near the Scorpion border that doubles as a clan hospital. Known throughout the Empire for eccentric humor and genuine compassion.

#### Clan Special: Imperial Loyalty and Popular Support

#### Imperial Loyalty

The Monkey are the most genuinely loyal Minor Clan to the Imperial line — their founder confessed deception directly to the Emperor and was declared samurai anyway, and a Toku has served as Imperial Chancellor. When a Monkey character speaks at court in support of an Imperial position or edict, they receive a mechanical advantage that punches above their Minor Clan status. The Emperor’s court remembers who Toku was. This is a flat bonus to relevant court action rolls when the Monkey position aligns with an active Imperial topic, representing institutional goodwill accumulated over generations of unflinching loyalty.

#### Popular Support

The Monkey founder was a peasant who gave ronin honest work and defended commoners from samurai. Monkey provinces carry a passive Stability bonus from genuine peasant loyalty — their commoners are treated as assets rather than resources, and the population responds accordingly. Additionally the Monkey hire ronin at reduced cost and draw from a larger availability pool than other clans. This plugs into the existing ronin hiring system: the Monkey’s reputation among wave-men means more ronin are willing to serve them, and at better terms.

#### Toku Bushi [Tier 1 — Baseline] — Monkey Clan

**Health: 153 | Attack: 6 | Defense: 5 | Morale: 19 | Morale Defense: 9**

**Special: **Does not suffer Morale penalties from being outnumbered. +1 Attack when facing a unit with higher Health remaining than their own.

**Notes: **The highest Morale Defense of any Minor Clan unit, reflecting the Toku Bushi school’s Willpower focus and wound penalty reduction. They are trained to absorb punishment, fight outnumbered, and keep going. The underdog bonus mirrors the school’s Rank 2 technique — they hit harder when the odds are against them. They replace the generic Bushi Retainer for the Monkey Clan.

*Monkey army identity: small, stubborn, and better than they look. A Toku unit that is losing is more dangerous than one that is winning.*

### 27.9.6 The Oriole Clan

Creed: ‘A sword is most desirable when it is sheathed.’ One of the youngest Minor Clans but containing one of the oldest families in the Empire — the Tsi, whose lineage traces to Tsi Wenfu, a Yobanjin smith of half-Phoenix ancestry who came to Rokugan seeking his father and became the personal smith of Emperor Hantei Genji. For centuries the Tsi were effectively stateless vassals of the Imperial house — creating blades of legendary quality for the Emperor alone. In 1159 they were enslaved by the Ninube shapeshifters after the fall of Otosan Uchi, and spent years making deliberately flawed weapons in silent defiance. After their rescue by the Jade Champion, Tsi Zutaka revealed he had hidden Emperor Toturi I’s sword from the enemy. Emperor Toturi III was so moved he granted the Tsi Minor Clan status as the Oriole Clan in 1168.

Clan identity: The forge is sacred art. To the Tsi, a sword’s beauty and its deadliness are the same quality — perfection of form cannot be separated from perfection of function. They are sculptors who happen to make weapons. They cringe when their blades are drawn in anger. Those born without the smith’s gift serve as bushi, diplomats, or armorsmiths — and are quietly looked down upon by their forge-mastering peers, though this attitude is slowly changing. The master-apprentice bond is the most sacred relationship in Tsi culture.

Key historical events: Wenfu’s arrival and recognition by Emperor Hantei Genji; centuries as Imperial vassals; Tsi Xing Guo’s ascension as the Fortune of Steel; the fall of Otosan Uchi and Ninube enslavement; Zutaka’s sabotage campaign; rescue and elevation to Minor Clan status; Zutaka’s political expansion through strategic marriages and school alliances; the ongoing construction of Kyuden Tsi near Toshi Ranbo.

Inter-clan relations: Sought after by everyone who wants a legendary blade — which is everyone. Good ties with Dragon (patrons of West Hub Village), Crane (aesthetic kinship, Kakita collaboration), Crab (the Hida Rezan marriage), and Phoenix (temple construction). The Tsi’s unique scattered territory means they interact with virtually every Great Clan as neighbor, trading partner, or craftsman. They have no enemies — no one wants to anger the only people who can make a perfect sword.

**The Tsi Family:**

Motto: ‘Even the Fortune of Steel is just an apprentice.’ The founding and only family of the Oriole Clan. Swordsmiths, armorsmiths, sculptors, and artisans of every kind — plus the bushi and diplomats they have been forced to become. The family name was historically earned through skill, not birth — those without the gift were cast out as ronin. This is no longer possible now that the Tsi are a clan and must retain all their children. Their holdings are scattered across the Empire, from Otosan Uchi’s ruins to the Utaku Plains, making them the most geographically dispersed Minor Clan in Rokugan.

#### Clan Special: The Sword Trade

The Tsi Smith school is an Artisan school, not a Bushi school. The Oriole’s entire identity is producing the finest weapons in the Empire. Within the existing Artisan and Crafting System they are the primary source of Masterwork and Legendary quality items. Any named character — player or NPC — who wants a weapon at those tiers must come to the Tsi. That single fact gives them political leverage with every clan simultaneously, including the Imperial house.

#### Universal Neutrality

The Oriole have no enemies — no one wants to anger the only people who can make a perfect sword. This is encoded as an AI constraint: the Oriole never declare war, and no clan AI will declare war on them because cutting off access to the Empire’s finest smiths is too costly a trade. If a clan does attack them, every other clan takes a disposition hit toward the aggressor. Threatening the Tsi is threatening everyone’s access to quality arms.

#### Scattered Territory

Tsi holdings are spread across multiple clan territories simultaneously. This makes them structurally difficult to isolate — any attack on the Oriole means operating inside someone else’s borders, requiring the aggressor to either ask permission or commit an additional diplomatic offence. The Oriole carry no bushi unit of their own and rely entirely on the generic Bushi Retainer. Their protection is political, not military.

### 27.9.7 The Ox Clan

Creed: ‘Strength without a master is still strength.’ Founded by Otaku Morito — a Unicorn samurai disgraced and exiled after trying to ride a Battle Maiden’s horse — who fought in Toturi’s Army, reclaimed his honor, and then led his followers to claim unaligned territory where the Dragon Heart Plain meets the Northern Wall Mountains. Their harassment of the Steel Chrysanthemum’s armies during the War of Spirits earned them Minor Clan status. What the rest of the Empire did not know was that Morito was also a Kolat Master, and the Ox Clan’s position secretly screened the approaches to the Kolat’s Hidden Temple. In 1170 the Scorpion stormed Shiro Morito, killed Morito and every identifiable Kolat member, and left the clan in the hands of the ignorant nephew Morito Garin. The Ox have spent the decades since fighting to earn back their reputation.

Clan identity: Direct, cavalry-heavy, and proud of their Unicorn roots. The Ox are one of the few Minor Clans with genuine military strength — their fortress is larger than some Great Clan installations, and their cavalry patrols cover an enormous territory. In the modern era they have redirected their Kolat-era knowledge of the criminal underworld toward dismantling organized crime throughout Rokugan. They operate without authorization where necessary and consider the ends worth the means.

Key historical events: Morito’s disgrace and exile from the Unicorn; Toturi’s Army service; the claiming of Dragon Heart Plain territory; Morito’s elevation to Kolat Master and the Ox Clan’s hidden role; the Scorpion purge of 1170 and Morito’s death; Garin’s redemption campaign during the War of Dark Fire; the siege of Shiro Morito against the Yobanjin; the Destroyer War; the modern anti-crime campaign under Morito Naname.

Inter-clan relations: Complex relationship with the Unicorn — kinship undermined by the Morito’s old shame and the Ox’s independent streak. Cautious but functional ties with the Scorpion, who know exactly what the Ox were and have chosen not to destroy them. Respected by the Phoenix as border partners. Growing reputation among the Minor Clan Alliance.

**The Morito Family:**

Motto: ‘Stand until there is nothing left to stand for.’ The founding and only family. Warriors, cavalry commanders, and underground intelligence operatives. Their bushi are trained in the Unicorn tradition of mounted combat adapted to the mountain-plain border terrain of their home. Fiercely protective of their lands and their slowly-rebuilt honor. The modern Morito are making their clan a scourge of organized crime — they know every smuggler’s route in the Empire, and are using that knowledge against its former beneficiaries.

#### Clan Special: The Unauthorized Hunt

The Ox have the most complete knowledge of Rokugan’s criminal underworld of any clan — a legacy of their Kolat years, when Morito used the clan to screen the approaches to the Hidden Temple and maintained extensive contacts across every smuggling route in the Empire. The current Champion Morito Naname is actively using this knowledge to dismantle organized crime, operating without magistrate authorization when necessary and seizing criminal funds and equipment to supply the clan. Where the Tortoise report intelligence up the chain, the Ox act on it unilaterally.

In game terms the Ox passively accumulate criminal intelligence in the same way as the Tortoise, but through land routes and underworld contacts rather than port presence. Their AI will act on this intelligence directly — dispatching forces to suppress criminal operations, seizing assets, eliminating targets — without waiting for court authorization. This occasionally generates diplomatic friction with local daimyo whose jurisdiction they have entered uninvited, but the Ox consider this an acceptable cost.

#### Flat Hierarchy

Outside of the Champion, there are almost no fixed positions of authority in the Ox Clan. Every samurai is allowed a voice in most decisions regardless of rank. Soldiers eat and drink alongside their officers. This is part of a broader philosophy of liberty — the Ox hate to bow to anyone who has not earned their respect. Mechanically this means the Ox Champion has weaker unilateral decision-making authority over their own clan than any other Clan Champion. Major decisions require broader internal consensus before the AI will commit, which makes the Ox slower to act but also harder to manipulate through a single point of influence.

#### The Scorpion Relationship

The Scorpion know exactly what the Ox were and have chosen not to destroy them. Garin actively sought Scorpion help to purge remaining Kolat influence after 1170. This is not leverage so much as uneasy mutual respect — encoded as a permanent positive historical modifier between the two clans that coexists with underlying wariness. Neither side fully trusts the other. Neither side wants to be the one to break the arrangement.

### 27.9.8 The Sparrow Clan

Creed: ‘Poverty teaches more than wealth ever could.’ Founded in the late eighth century when the Crane courtier Doji Suzume delivered an interpretation of Shinsei’s teachings that thrilled commoners and mortified the Crane Clan. The Emperor — amused or exasperated, scholars still debate which — commanded Suzume to take his admirers and form a Minor Clan. The Sparrow settled in the foothills between Crab and Crane territory, on land so poor it barely supports their small population. They have been genuinely impoverished for most of their history, and they have made a philosophy of it: the Sparrow believe material poverty clarifies the spirit and strips away pretension. They are the most Shinseist Minor Clan in the Empire and have better relations with the Brotherhood than with most samurai.

Clan identity: Humble, philosophical, surprisingly resilient. The Sparrow do not complain about their poverty — they embrace it. Their storytelling tradition is famous throughout the Empire; the Sparrow preserve old tales, songs, and legends that Great Clan scholars have long since forgotten. They farm, they fish, they record history no one else bothers with, and when threatened they fight with a ferocity that shocks enemies who underestimated them. They have no political ambitions. They want to be left alone with their philosophy and their stories.

Key historical events: Doji Suzume’s founding; the Three Man Alliance with the Wasp and Fox Clans (earliest predecessor of Minor Clan cooperation); the long border tension with the Crab over poor farmland; repeated near-destruction and survival through sheer stubbornness; the Destroyer War; continued role as keepers of oral tradition.

Inter-clan relations: Respectful ties to the Crane — their ancestral origin — though the Crane find them embarrassing. Warm relations with the Brotherhood of Shinsei. Neutral to most others. The Crab view them as a minor nuisance over border farmland disputes. The Sparrow have learned to be invisible to Great Clans, which suits them fine.

**The Suzume Family:**

Motto: ‘A full belly clouds the mind. We see clearly.’ The founding and only family. Philosophers, storytellers, and surprisingly capable warriors when forced to be. The Suzume Bushi School trains fighters who compensate for lack of equipment with speed, creativity, and utter commitment. Their storytellers travel the Empire collecting tales, and their oral archives contain history that exists nowhere else in written form.

#### Clan Special: Beloved and Forgotten

#### The Land Malus

Sparrow provinces carry a severe terrain modifier that keeps Rice production near or at subsistence level. There is no surplus, no meaningful tax cascade contribution, and no Koku generation worth speaking of. Population growth is minimal because the land cannot support it. The Sparrow are structurally poor and stay that way — this is not a recoverable situation but a permanent feature of who they are and where they live. They have made a philosophy of it.

#### Universal Goodwill

The Sparrow receive the highest passive starting disposition bonus of any Minor Clan with every other clan in the Empire. They have no political ambitions, no military capacity worth fearing, good stories, and genuine humility. Everyone likes them. No clan AI will ever prioritise the Sparrow as a military or political target — they are simply not worth the social cost of being seen to bully the poorest clan in Rokugan. Sparrow characters walk into any court already one step ahead socially, and the Sparrow AI can sustain diplomatic relationships that clans with far more resources would struggle to maintain.

### 27.9.9 The Tortoise Clan

Creed: ‘We carry the Empire’s secrets so it need not carry the shame.’ Founded after the Battle of White Stag, when the Empire first encountered and repelled a gaijin naval invasion. The Emperor tasked a newly-created Minor Clan with monitoring, managing, and if necessary dealing with the foreign traders and travelers who continued to appear at Rokugan’s shores — work too distasteful and dishonorable for a Great Clan. The Tortoise operate in the criminal underworld, maintain contacts among gaijin merchants, engage in smuggling when it serves Imperial interests, and gather intelligence through means no honorable samurai would claim. They are officially beneath notice and deliberately cultivate this status.

Clan identity: Secretive, pragmatic, and deeply aware of their role as the Empire’s necessary embarrassment. The Tortoise do things that need doing and cannot be acknowledged. They are not dishonorable by their own definition — they sacrifice their public reputation so the Empire’s reputation remains clean. This is their duty, and they accept it with the same grim sincerity the Crab accept the Wall. They have the most gaijin contacts of any clan, the deepest ties to Rokugan’s criminal underground, and the best knowledge of what is actually arriving on the Empire’s shores.

Key historical events: The Battle of White Stag and the founding mandate; centuries of operating in the shadows of East Hub Village and the ports; the development of extensive gaijin trade networks; the discovery and suppression of multiple illegal gaijin trade operations; the Destroyer War’s disruption of their networks; the Colonies’ opening creating both opportunity and chaos in their operating environment.

Inter-clan relations: The Tortoise are officially ignored by everyone, which is how they prefer it. Their closest working relationships are with the Imperial families — who authorize their activities — and the Mantis, who share their maritime world. The Scorpion respect them professionally. No Great Clan daimyo admits to knowing a Tortoise samurai personally.

**The Kasuga Family:**

Motto: ‘What you don’t see won’t keep you awake.’ The founding and only family. Smugglers, spies, intelligence brokers, and gaijin-contact managers. The Kasuga Smuggler school is the most practically dishonest school in the Empire, built entirely around deception and information control. They maintain a permanent presence in East Hub Village near Otosan Uchi and in every significant port city in Rokugan. Their most important skill is making powerful people believe the Tortoise are irrelevant.

#### Clan Special: The Port Network

The Tortoise maintain a permanent presence in every major port city in Rokugan. Through their underworld contacts and gaijin connections, they passively accumulate intelligence on criminal activity operating in those ports — smuggling operations, gang presence, corrupt officials, illegal gaijin trade. This information enters the existing Secret System as topic objects, available to any Tortoise character present in that port.

What a Tortoise character does with this intelligence is entirely their own decision. They may report it to the local daimyo — generating disposition and fulfilling their Imperial mandate. They may bring it to the Emerald Magistrates. They may use it as blackmail leverage through the existing Secret System. They may sell it to whoever pays. They may hold it as future currency. The Tortoise AI defaults toward reporting up the Imperial chain, consistent with their founding mandate. A player character Tortoise has full freedom to use the information however their objectives and personality dictate.

#### The Imperial Mandate

The Tortoise are authorized by the Imperial families to do things that would destroy any other clan’s reputation. When a Tortoise character is caught in dishonorable activity — underworld dealings, gaijin contact, smuggling — the default legal and Honor consequences are suppressed if they invoke their mandate. They cannot be formally prosecuted for what they are chartered to do. Other clans can resent them but cannot act against them without Imperial pushback. The Kasuga Smuggler school encodes this directly at Rank 2 — any samurai who attacks or slanders them without provocation immediately loses Honor equal to twice the Kasuga’s School Rank.

The Tortoise carry no unique bushi unit. Their protection is legal and political, not military. They use the generic Bushi Retainer.

### 27.9.10 The Boar Clan (Historical — Destroyed c. 500)

Creed: ‘What we built, we built alone.’ The Boar Clan existed for approximately a century before being annihilated by the Bloodspeaker maho-tsukai Agasha Ryuden, who sacrificed most of the clan to access solidified First Oni blood beneath their mountains and forge the cursed Anvil of Despair. The few survivors were pulled out of the mortal world entirely by the earth spirit Shakoki Dogu, with whom the clan had a secret pact since their founding. Founded by Crab survivors of a doomed expedition that became trapped in the Twilight Mountains — actually imprisoned in a spirit-realm pocket by the lonely Shakoki Dogu — and eventually released after Hida Heichi promised to stand at the spirit’s side forever. The Boar were cousins to the Crab in culture: defensive, physical, and deeply suspicious of outsiders. Their armor was legendary.

Relevance to play: The Boar are long dead in the game’s default era. Their ghost-haunted ruins in the Twilight Mountains remain dangerous and off-limits, their treasures legendary and never recovered. Characters with Boar ancestry are possible through survival or dispersal routes. The Heichi Bushi school techniques survive in scattered form among ronin who have found willing teachers. The Shakoki Dogu still watches its territory through stone effigies with wide eyes.

### 27.9.11 The Snake Clan (Historical — Destroyed c. 750)

Creed: Unknown — records were expunged. The Snake Clan was formed from the descendants of Isawa Chuda, honored for his battles against a maho cult. Within generations, his descendants became the very thing their ancestor fought — maho practitioners who summoned an oni so powerful it had to be sealed by the combined effort of multiple Great Clans. The entire Snake Clan was destroyed and their records purged from the Imperial histories. Their former territory on Dragon Heart Plain remains cursed and empty, bordered to the north by the Ox Clan, which maintains vigilant patrols against whatever still lingers there.

Relevance to play: The Snake Clan’s destruction is a cautionary reference point throughout the Empire — the standard example used when discussing what happens to Minor Clans that practice maho. Their lands are a named hazard on the world map. Any character claiming Snake Clan descent would face extreme suspicion.
