## 27.4 The Lion Clan

The Emperor's Right Hand. The Lion are the warriors and historians of the Empire — keepers of its military traditions and the living memory of its history. More than any other clan they embody the ideal of Bushido, especially Duty and Honor. Their armies are the largest and most disciplined in the Empire. Their libraries contain the most comprehensive histories. And their pride is legendary — both their greatest strength and their most dangerous vulnerability.

- Clan identity: Martial, honorable, proud, traditional. Honor above all. 'Victories won on the battlefield are given back in the courts' is a Lion lament — they often excel at war but lose in political arenas.

- Key historical events: Akodo's disbanding and restoration; the Time of Shame; Akodo Toturi becoming Emperor; the conflict with Lion traditions in the modern era.

- Inter-clan relations: Traditional blood enemies of the Crane (Matsu-Kakita rivalry, centuries of border wars). Respectful friction with Crab. Neutral to positive with Phoenix. Deeply suspicious of Scorpion.

**The Akodo Family:**

Descended from the founding Kami. Led the clan for almost all of its history. Warrior-scholars — they approach war as an art form, seeking perfection through precision and preparation. Ascetic and self-denying. Never act without preparation. Their fighting style is noted for self-control. Deeply pious and honorable. The family was disbanded, restored through divine mandate, and now acts with a more meritocratic approach to clan leadership.

**The Ikoma Family:**

Founded by a womanizing hard-drinking braggart — the Ikoma serve as the heart and soul of the clan, doing what honor prevents the other families from doing. They are the ones who express emotion, shed tears for fallen heroes, and record the deeds of the clan. Historians and bards (Omoidasu). Pragmatic where their cousins are rigid. Assist the Otomo in maintaining the Empire's official histories. Serve as scouts, wardens, and tacticians.

**The Kitsu Family:**

The most unusual family in the Lion Clan — descended from ancient lion-spirit creatures. The smallest of the Lion families. Serve as priests and spiritual advisors with unparalleled ability to commune with ancestor spirits. Follow almost incomprehensibly rigid traditions dating from the spirit-creature founders. Their connection to Yomi (the realm of honored dead) makes them uniquely powerful spiritual intermediaries.

**The Matsu Family:**

Motto: 'In my sword, the wind. In my heart, courage. In my eyes, death. I am Matsu.' The largest family in the Lion Clan — and for most of the Empire's history, the largest samurai family in the Empire. The backbone of Lion armies. To the Matsu, battle is everything — the core of their being. They harness war-rage, hone it, and turn it to their ends. Touchy, passionate, fanatically devoted to Bushido's martial aspects. Reserve special hatred for the Crane due to their founder's grudge with Kakita.
