## 55.31 Commitment Registry --- Social Obligation Tracking --- LOCKED

STATUS: LOCKED

The stateless decomposition correctly handles priority overrides --- a crisis should interrupt a planned court visit. But when an NPC accepts a social obligation and then fails to fulfill it, the world must react. Section 14.2 defines “absence after acceptance“ consequences for players (Honor loss, disposition cost with host). No equivalent system exists for NPCs. This section defines a general-purpose Commitment Registry that tracks all social promises and fires consequences at deadline regardless of why the NPC failed to honor them. The decomposition stays stateless. The commitment is game state.

Cross-references: Section 12.10 (Favor System), Section 14.2 (Player Reaction Menu --- absence after acceptance), Section 15 (Court System), Section 17 (Personal Visits System), Section 55.4 (Seven-Phase Decision Loop --- Phase 5 scoring), Section 55.29 (Travel Commitment and Oscillation Prevention).

### 55.31.1 Commitment Data Structure

Every social promise an NPC makes creates a Commitment entry on their character sheet. The commitment has a deadline. At the deadline, the engine checks fulfillment. If unfulfilled, consequences fire automatically regardless of the reason for failure.

Commitment fields: commitment_id (int, unique), commitment_type (enum --- see Section 55.31.2), source_action_id (string --- the action_log entry that created this commitment), creditor_npc_id (int --- who the promise was made to), debtor_npc_id (int --- who made the promise), deadline_ic_day (int --- when fulfillment is checked), fulfillment_condition (enum --- what counts as honoring it), fulfillment_target (variant --- specifics such as settlement_id, topic_id, resource amount), tier (int, 1-3 --- mirrors favor tiers from Section 12.10, determines consequence severity), status (enum --- PENDING, FULFILLED, BROKEN_NO_NOTICE, BROKEN_WITH_NOTICE, BROKEN_WITH_PROXY, EXPIRED), witnesses (Array of NPC IDs --- characters who know about this commitment), created_ic_day (int), created_by_action (string --- action_log reference).

### 55.31.2 Commitment Types

Six commitment types cover all social promises NPCs make. Each type defines when the commitment is created, what fulfillment means, and the default tier.

**COURT_ATTENDANCE: **“I will attend this court.“ Created when ACCEPT_INVITATION fires (reactive event response) or when ATTEND_COURT decomposition path commits the NPC to a specific court session. Deadline: court start date. Fulfillment: NPC’s context_flag is AT_COURT or VISITING at the commitment’s target settlement on the deadline date. Tier 3 (Minor) for regular courts. Tier 2 (Moderate) for Winter Court, Clan Champion courts, or any court where the NPC’s lord specifically ordered attendance. Witnesses: all characters who received invitations to the same court (public knowledge --- the guest list is socially visible).

**FAVOR_OBLIGATION: **“I owe you a favor.“ Created by the existing favor system (Section 12.10) when OFFER_FAVOR fires or when a favor is generated through NEGOTIATE/PERSUADE outcomes. Deadline and consequences are managed entirely by Section 12.10 (HONOR_FAVOR / BREAK_FAVOR). The Commitment Registry tracks FAVOR_OBLIGATION for visibility only --- the NPC and the player can see all obligations in one place. The registry does NOT fire its own consequences for this type. No double-dipping. Tier matches the favor tier from Section 12.10.

**VISIT_PROMISE: **“I will visit your holdings.“ Created when SEND_LETTER fires with visit intent --- the letter explicitly states the NPC will travel to the recipient’s location. Not every letter creates a commitment --- only letters where the content includes a visit pledge (determined at letter composition by the decomposition or player). Deadline: the season stated or implied in the letter, converted to the first IC day of that season. Fulfillment: NPC arrives at the target settlement and spends at least 1 AP in a non-TRAVELING context. Tier 3 (Minor). Witnesses: creditor and debtor only (private correspondence).

**SUPPORT_PLEDGE: **“I will support your position at court.“ Created when OFFER_FAVOR (specific, political) fires with a court-support condition, or when PERSUADE/NEGOTIATE produces a commitment outcome where the NPC explicitly promises to advocate for a position. Deadline: the relevant court session’s end date. Fulfillment: NPC takes at least one public action during the court session that supports the pledged position --- PERSUADE, PUBLIC_DEBATE, or MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION aligned with the promise. The NPC must be present and must act. Silent attendance does not fulfill. Tier 2 (Moderate). Witnesses: if pledged at court, all characters present at the moment of the pledge. If pledged privately, creditor and debtor only.

**RESOURCE_PROMISE: **“I will send supplies/troops/koku.“ Created by lord-tier NPCs during NEGOTIATE, REQUEST_ALLIED_AID acceptance, or ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE with resource transfer parameters. Deadline: the agreed delivery season. Fulfillment: the resource transfer action completes (SHARE_SUPPLIES, troop transfer, koku payment). Tier scales with quantity: below 10 koku or 5 PU = Tier 3. 10-50 koku or 5-20 PU = Tier 2. Above 50 koku or 20 PU = Tier 1. Witnesses: the two lords involved plus their direct vassals who would be informed through the chain of command (Information Transfer, Section 55.6).

**MEETING_ARRANGEMENT: **“We will meet at X location on Y date.“ Created when two NPCs exchange letters coordinating a meeting --- both NPCs receive a commitment entry. This is the only commitment type where both parties are simultaneously debtor and creditor. Deadline: the arranged meeting date. Fulfillment: both NPCs present at the target settlement on the deadline. If one arrives and the other does not, only the absent party’s commitment breaks. Tier 3 (Minor). Witnesses: the two parties only, unless the meeting was arranged publicly at court.

### 55.31.3 Commitment Creation

Not every social action creates a commitment. Only actions with an explicit or implicit promise to another character. The commitment is registered at action resolution time by the executor (Phase 7). No decomposition involvement --- the commitment is a side effect of action execution, not a planned output.

Creation triggers: ACCEPT_INVITATION fires a reactive event response -> COURT_ATTENDANCE commitment. OFFER_FAVOR fires -> FAVOR_OBLIGATION commitment (tracked for visibility, consequences delegated to Section 12.10). SEND_LETTER with visit_intent flag set by the composing decomposition -> VISIT_PROMISE commitment. NEGOTIATE or PERSUADE producing a pledge outcome -> SUPPORT_PLEDGE commitment. REQUEST_ALLIED_AID accepted by the target -> RESOURCE_PROMISE commitment on the acceptor. SEND_LETTER with meeting_coordination flag, confirmed by reply letter -> MEETING_ARRANGEMENT commitment on both parties.

The witness list is populated at creation time based on context. AT_COURT or VISITING: all characters in ctx.characters_present are added as witnesses. AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or TRAVELING (letter-based commitments): only the creditor and debtor. Lord-tier commitments (RESOURCE_PROMISE): the two lords plus direct vassals listed in each lord’s vassal roster. Witnesses are snapshot at creation --- characters who arrive later do not retroactively learn about the commitment unless informed through GOSSIP or topic spread.

### 55.31.4 Deadline Check and Fulfillment

At each daily tick, the engine scans all PENDING commitments where deadline_ic_day is less than or equal to current_ic_day. For each, the fulfillment condition is evaluated. If fulfilled, status changes to FULFILLED. If unfulfilled, status changes to the appropriate BROKEN variant based on whether the NPC sent advance notice (see Section 55.31.6).

func _check_commitments(npc_id, current_ic_day):

    var commitments = CommitmentRegistry.get_pending(npc_id)

    for c in commitments:

        if c.deadline_ic_day <= current_ic_day:

            if _is_fulfilled(c, ctx):

                c.status = CommitmentStatus.FULFILLED

            elif c.advance_notice_sent:

                c.status = CommitmentStatus.BROKEN_WITH_NOTICE

                _fire_broken_commitment(c)

            elif c.proxy_sent:

                c.status = CommitmentStatus.BROKEN_WITH_PROXY

                _fire_broken_commitment(c)

            else:

                c.status = CommitmentStatus.BROKEN_NO_NOTICE

                _fire_broken_commitment(c)

This runs regardless of what the NPC is doing. If they are mid-crisis, mid-travel, mid-anything --- the deadline arrives and the check fires. The commitment system does not care about reasons. It only cares about outcomes. This mirrors Rokugani social reality: a samurai who fails to appear at court is judged by the absence, not the excuse.

### 55.31.5 Broken Commitment Consequences

Consequences scale by tier and by how the commitment was broken. Three breaking modes exist: no notice (full consequences), advance notice (reduced), and proxy sent (minimal). FAVOR_OBLIGATION commitments are exempt --- they use Section 12.10 consequences exclusively.

**BROKEN_NO_NOTICE --- full consequences. **The NPC simply did not appear or fulfill the obligation. No explanation was given. The creditor and witnesses interpret this as disrespect or unreliability.

Tier 3 (Minor --- regular court attendance, visit promise, meeting arrangement): -0.1 Honor. -3 disposition with the creditor. Topic generated: “[NPC] failed to attend [event]“ --- Tier 4 topic, low momentum. Gossip fodder but not scandalous.

Tier 2 (Moderate --- Winter Court attendance, support pledge, moderate resource promise): -0.2 Honor. -6 disposition with the creditor. -2 disposition with all witnesses. Topic generated: “[NPC] broke their word to [creditor]“ --- Tier 3 topic, moderate momentum.

Tier 1 (Major --- military support, major resource promise): -0.5 Honor. -10 disposition with the creditor. -5 disposition with all witnesses. Topic generated: “[NPC] betrayed their commitment to [creditor]“ --- Tier 2 topic, high momentum. This is a scandal. The creditor’s lord may treat this as a diplomatic incident, potentially triggering the War Status escalation ladder if the broken commitment had military implications.

**BROKEN_WITH_NOTICE --- reduced consequences. **The NPC sent a letter of explanation before the deadline (see Section 55.31.6). The creditor is disappointed but not insulted. The NPC acknowledged the obligation and communicated their inability to fulfill it.

Tier 3: No Honor loss. -1 disposition with the creditor. No topic generated.

Tier 2: -0.1 Honor. -3 disposition with the creditor. No witness disposition penalty. No topic generated.

Tier 1: -0.2 Honor. -5 disposition with the creditor. -2 disposition with witnesses. Topic generated but at one tier lower momentum than the no-notice version.

**BROKEN_WITH_PROXY --- minimal consequences. **The NPC sent a representative in their place (see Section 55.31.6). The commitment is technically unfulfilled --- the NPC is not personally present --- but the social obligation is partially honored. Applies primarily to COURT_ATTENDANCE and MEETING_ARRANGEMENT.

Tier 3: No Honor loss. No disposition penalty. No topic.

Tier 2: No Honor loss. -1 disposition with the creditor. No topic.

Tier 1: -0.1 Honor. -2 disposition with the creditor. No witness penalty. No topic.

Note: SUPPORT_PLEDGE cannot be fulfilled by proxy unless the proxy has explicit authority to speak on the NPC’s behalf (lord-vassal relationship with ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE). A random representative cannot credibly pledge political support. If a proxy is sent for a SUPPORT_PLEDGE, it is treated as BROKEN_WITH_NOTICE, not BROKEN_WITH_PROXY.

### 55.31.6 Proactive Commitment Management

When the priority cascade overrides a primary objective and the NPC has a PENDING commitment that will become unfulfillable, the NPC should have the opportunity to mitigate consequences before the deadline arrives. The decomposition handles this through the existing action system --- no new actions are required.

**Advance notice: **SEND_LETTER targeting the creditor with apology/explanation intent. Costs 0 AP (existing rule --- one free letter per IC day). When the letter is sent, the commitment gains an advance_notice_sent flag and records the notice_ic_day. The letter must be sent before the deadline_ic_day. A letter arriving after the deadline provides no mitigation. The NPC does not need to explain the reason --- the act of notification is sufficient. A samurai who writes “I regret I cannot attend“ has honored the courtesy; a samurai who simply vanishes has not.

**Proxy dispatch: **ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE targeting a vassal with the sub-objective “Attend [court/meeting] as my representative.“ The vassal travels to the commitment’s target settlement and acts as proxy. When the vassal arrives and spends AP at the target location, the commitment gains a proxy_sent flag. The proxy must arrive before or on the deadline. Only lord-tier NPCs can send proxies (they have vassals to assign). Non-lord NPCs who cannot attend personally can only send advance notice.

**Decomposition integration: **When the priority cascade fires and selects a crisis or reassignment that would cause the NPC to redirect away from a commitment’s target, the decomposition considers mitigation actions before completing the redirect. The commitment_at_risk modifier (Section 55.31.7) makes SEND_LETTER highly attractive as an immediate action. The typical sequence: AP 1 --- crisis fires, SEND_LETTER (apology to creditor) scores high due to commitment_at_risk penalty avoidance, letter sent. AP 2 --- crisis action fires normally, NPC redirects. Total cost: 1 AP for the letter (or 0 if using the free daily letter), plus the redirect. A Rei-primary character almost always sends the letter (Rei lean on SEND_LETTER is positive, commitment penalty avoidance is strong). A Yu-primary character may skip the letter and charge toward the crisis (Yu lean on SEND_LETTER is low, and Yu’s urgency bonus for the crisis may overwhelm the commitment penalty).

### 55.31.7 Phase 5 Integration --- Commitment-at-Risk Modifier

The Commitment Registry feeds a scoring modifier into Phase 5. When the NPC is evaluating any action that would make an active PENDING commitment unfulfillable --- specifically CHANGE_DESTINATION, BEGIN_TRAVEL to a different settlement, or any action that consumes AP needed for the commitment --- the commitment_at_risk penalty applies.

Base penalty by tier: Tier 3 (Minor): -5 to commitment-breaking actions. Tier 2 (Moderate): -15. Tier 1 (Major): -25. Multiple at-risk commitments stack, but the total penalty is capped at -40 to prevent complete action lockout.

Personality modifiers to the commitment-at-risk penalty: Meiyo (Honor): additional -5 across all tiers. An honorable character treats every promise as sacred. Gi (Honesty/Righteousness): additional -8 to breaking commitments. A righteous character will not break their word. Chugi (Loyalty): additional -5 when the commitment is to their lord or a character in their lord’s direct service. Reduced by +3 when the commitment is to someone outside their loyalty chain --- duty to lord outweighs external promises. Rei (Courtesy): additional -5. Courtesy demands social obligations be honored. Seigyo (Control): reduced penalty --- -3/-10/-20 instead of -5/-15/-25. A calculating character weighs commitments pragmatically against strategic benefit. Ishi (Will): no modifier. Will is about following through on personal decisions, not external obligations. Kyoryoku (Strength): reduced penalty --- -3/-10/-20. Strength respects force and action, not social niceties.

Effect on behavior: a crisis with +15 urgency easily overrides a Tier 3 court attendance commitment (-5 base). The NPC redirects and takes the small Honor hit --- correct, because crises matter more than minor social obligations. A crisis with +15 urgency does not easily override a Tier 1 military support commitment (-25 base). The NPC is strongly pulled toward honoring the commitment. Only a truly overwhelming crisis (+30 urgency) would override it. A Gi-primary NPC with a Tier 2 commitment at risk faces -23 penalty (-15 base + -8 Gi) on any action that breaks the commitment. Almost nothing short of a province invasion overrides that. A Seigyo-primary NPC faces only -10 (-10 base, Seigyo reduction). They will break the commitment if something clearly better comes along.

### 55.31.8 Commitment-Aware Decomposition

The decomposition reads the Commitment Registry in two places. First, when generating ATTEND_COURT: if the NPC already has a COURT_ATTENDANCE commitment at a different court, the existing commitment feeds into scoring. Attending the committed court receives a bonus equal to the commitment_at_risk penalty that would apply if the NPC went elsewhere. This prevents accidental double-booking --- the NPC is pulled toward their existing commitment unless a stronger objective overrides it.

Second, when the priority cascade selects a crisis or reassignment: before the NPC fully commits to the redirect, the decomposition evaluates SEND_LETTER (apology) as a competing action for the current AP. The commitment_at_risk penalty on the redirect action, combined with the low cost of SEND_LETTER (0 AP for the daily free letter), often makes the letter the winning action for the first AP after the crisis fires. The NPC sends the apology, then redirects on the next AP. This is not a hard requirement --- if the crisis urgency is extreme (+30), even Gi-primary characters may skip the letter. But for routine reassignments, the letter almost always fires first.

### 55.31.9 Witness Knowledge Scope

Who knows about a commitment determines the blast radius of breaking it. The witness list is populated at creation time and does not change --- characters who learn about the commitment later through GOSSIP or topic spread may form opinions about the broken commitment topic, but they are not in the original witness list and do not receive the direct disposition penalty.

**Public commitments **(made at court or in the presence of other characters): witnesses = all characters in ctx.characters_present at the moment the commitment was created. This includes COURT_ATTENDANCE accepted publicly, SUPPORT_PLEDGE made at court, and any OFFER_FAVOR delivered publicly. A court with 15 attendees means 15 witnesses. Breaking a public Tier 2 commitment costs -2 disposition with each of the 15 witnesses --- a total of -30 disposition spread across the NPC’s social network. This is devastating and correct: public promises carry weight because public failure carries shame.

**Private commitments **(made by letter or in private conversation): witnesses = creditor and debtor only. This includes VISIT_PROMISE (letter-based), MEETING_ARRANGEMENT (letter-coordinated), and FAVOR_OBLIGATION offered privately. Breaking a private commitment costs disposition only with the creditor. The damage is contained but the relationship may be irreparably harmed.

**Lord-known commitments **(RESOURCE_PROMISE, military support): witnesses = the two lords involved plus their direct vassals who received the information through the chain of command (Information Transfer, Section 55.6). A lord who promises 20 PU of troops and fails to deliver loses disposition not just with the allied lord but with every vassal in the allied lord’s retinue who was counting on those reinforcements.

### 55.31.10 Interaction with Existing Systems

**Section 12.10 (Favor System): **FAVOR_OBLIGATION commitments are tracked in the registry for visibility. All consequences are delegated to Section 12.10 (HONOR_FAVOR / BREAK_FAVOR). The registry does not fire its own consequences for this type. No double-dipping.

**Section 14.2 (Player Reaction Menu): **The Commitment Registry applies equally to players and NPCs. For players, court invitation RSVPs continue to appear as Category 1 reactive events. The registry extends this by tracking all commitment types, not just court RSVPs, and by applying consequences to NPCs who were previously exempt. The Section 14.2 “absence after acceptance“ penalty (-0.1 Honor, disposition cost) is now the Tier 3 BROKEN_NO_NOTICE consequence for COURT_ATTENDANCE --- the values are identical, ensuring backward compatibility.

**Section 55.29 (Travel Commitment): **The travel_redirects counter (Section 55.29.1) and the commitment_at_risk modifier (Section 55.31.7) are complementary systems. The travel counter penalizes repeated redirects over time. The commitment modifier penalizes specific redirects that break specific promises. An NPC who redirects mid-travel to a court they accepted incurs both: the travel frustration penalty (-5 to -30 on future travel) AND the commitment-at-risk penalty (-5 to -25 on the redirect action). This double pressure strongly favors completing the journey unless a genuine crisis intervenes.

**Section 19.5 (Court Attendance Choice): **The existing wrong-court penalty (-0.1 to -0.5 Honor) applies when the NPC deliberately attends a different court than their lord ordered. The Commitment Registry adds a separate layer: even if the lord did not order attendance, the NPC’s own accepted invitation creates an obligation. A samurai who accepts two invitations and attends only one incurs the commitment penalty for the broken invitation AND potentially the wrong-court penalty if their lord preferred the other court.

**Section 20 (Known Information): **Commitments are known to their witnesses (Section 55.31.9). A broken commitment generates a topic that spreads through normal topic propagation. Characters who were not witnesses learn about the broken commitment through GOSSIP, daily conversation, or letter --- not through the commitment system directly. This maintains the information boundary: only direct witnesses experience the immediate disposition penalty; the broader social network learns through organic information flow.

### 55.31.11 Force Majeure — Crisis-Linked Commitment Breaking

Problem: a lord holding multiple commitments who is hit by a legitimate crisis (Shadowlands attack, province invasion, direct lord command) may break all commitments simultaneously. Under standard breaking rules, the cascading penalties destroy the NPC’s political network — a combined Honor loss of -0.7 or more and dozens of disposition penalties across witnesses. The NPC made the correct decision (defend their province) but is punished as if they were unreliable. The commitment system needs to distinguish between "broke a promise because they didn’t care" and "broke a promise because people were dying."

**Qualifying crises: **Any event that enters the priority cascade above the primary objective — Reactive Events and Crisis Overrides per Section 55.4.2. Specifically: Shadowlands attack on the NPC’s province or adjacent province, province invasion by enemy forces, active siege of the NPC’s castle, Tier 1-2 crisis in the NPC’s province or adjacent province (famine, plague, insurgency), and direct lord command (new objective by letter that explicitly overrides the primary). Routine reassignment does not qualify — only events that the priority cascade treats as higher than the NPC’s current primary objective.

**Crisis-commitment linking: **When the priority cascade selects a qualifying crisis and the NPC has PENDING commitments that will become unfulfillable, the engine stamps each affected commitment with the crisis_id before changing status. This link connects the specific commitment to the specific crisis. The link is permanent on the commitment record and is used by the retroactive forgiveness system.

#### 55.31.11.1 BROKEN_FORCE_MAJEURE Status

When a commitment breaks because of a qualifying crisis, the status is set to BROKEN_FORCE_MAJEURE instead of BROKEN_NO_NOTICE. This is an automatic upgrade — the NPC does not need to send a letter or take any action. The nature of the crisis is the explanation. If the NPC also sends advance notice (Section 55.31.6), the commitment status is still BROKEN_FORCE_MAJEURE (the better of the two statuses applies).

BROKEN_FORCE_MAJEURE consequences are equivalent to BROKEN_WITH_NOTICE:

Tier 3 (Minor): No Honor loss. -1 disposition with creditor. No topic generated. No witness penalty.

Tier 2 (Moderate): -0.1 Honor. -3 disposition with creditor. No witness penalty. No topic generated.

Tier 1 (Major): -0.2 Honor. -5 disposition with creditor. -2 disposition with witnesses. Topic generated at reduced momentum.

#### 55.31.11.2 Retroactive Forgiveness

The crisis that caused the force majeure generates a topic through normal crisis topic propagation (Section 16). When that topic reaches a character who suffered a disposition penalty from the linked broken commitment, a partial disposition recovery fires. The recovery is triggered by the information system — it fires when the receiving NPC learns about the crisis, not when the crisis occurs. If the crisis never becomes widely known (resolved quickly, local in scope), distant NPCs never learn the reason and the disposition penalty stands. This is consistent with Section 20 — characters act on known information.

func _check_retroactive_forgiveness(topic, receiving_npc_id):

    var linked_commitments = CommitmentRegistry.get_by_crisis(topic.crisis_id)

    for c in linked_commitments:

        if c.status == CommitmentStatus.BROKEN_FORCE_MAJEURE:

            var penalty_record = c.get_penalty_applied_to(receiving_npc_id)

            if penalty_record != null and not penalty_record.forgiveness_applied:

                var forgiveness_rate = _get_forgiveness_rate(receiving_npc_id)

                var recovery = abs(penalty_record.disposition_change) * forgiveness_rate

                DispositionSystem.modify(receiving_npc_id, c.debtor_npc_id, recovery)

                penalty_record.forgiveness_applied = true

The forgiveness rate is 50% by default. The recovery is half the original penalty — the receiving NPC understands the reason but still notes the broken commitment. Full forgiveness would make force majeure costless. In Rokugan, even a valid excuse does not fully erase a broken promise. The political damage is real, just softened.

#### 55.31.11.3 Personality-Modified Forgiveness Rate

Not all characters are equally understanding. The forgiveness rate varies by the receiving NPC’s personality primary:

**Jin (Compassion): **100% recovery. Compassion fully understands hardship. A Jin character who learns the reason forgives entirely — they would have made the same choice.

**Gi (Righteousness): **75% recovery. Righteousness recognizes that duty to protect one’s people is the highest obligation.

**Chugi (Loyalty): **75% recovery if the debtor is in the receiving NPC’s loyalty chain (same clan, allied lord). 25% recovery if the debtor is outside the loyalty chain. Loyalty forgives their own people readily but is less generous with outsiders.

**Rei (Courtesy): **50% recovery (default). Courtesy acknowledges the excuse and respects the circumstances, but notes the social failure.

**Meiyo (Honor): **50% recovery. Honor respects the warrior’s choice to defend their people but remembers that a word was broken.

**Yu (Courage): **50% recovery. Courage understands the call of battle but does not excuse the absence.

**Dosatsu (Insight): **50% recovery. Insight understands the circumstances intellectually.

**Seigyo (Control): **25% recovery. Control does not care about excuses — results matter. A commitment was broken. The debtor’s reliability assessment is updated regardless of the reason. Seigyo characters are the hardest to earn forgiveness from.

**Kyoryoku (Strength): **25% recovery. Strength respects power and action. The debtor failed to fulfill a commitment — the reason is irrelevant to the calculation of what was lost.

#### 55.31.11.4 Worked Example — Cascading Crisis

**NPC: **Yasuki Taka (Chugi/Dosatsu, Crab Provincial Daimyo, Rank 4). Late Autumn. Four active commitments: COURT_ATTENDANCE at Crane Winter Court (Tier 2, 12 witnesses), SUPPORT_PLEDGE to Doji Hotaru on trade topic (Tier 2, 8 witnesses), RESOURCE_PROMISE of 15 koku to Yasuki family treasury (Tier 2, 6 witnesses), MEETING_ARRANGEMENT with Isawa Kaede, Phoenix diplomat (Tier 3, 2 witnesses).

**Crisis: **Shadowlands horde attacks the Kaiu Wall adjacent to Taka’s province. Crisis Override fires in the priority cascade. Taka’s decomposition returns DEPLOY_ARMY. CHANGE_DESTINATION fires. Taka redirects from his travel to Crane Winter Court.

**Commitment resolution: **All four commitments are linked to crisis_id (the Shadowlands attack). Status set to BROKEN_FORCE_MAJEURE for all four.

**Immediate damage: **Tier 2 x3: -0.1 Honor each = -0.3 Honor. -3 disposition with each creditor = -9 total. No witness penalties. Tier 3 x1: no Honor. -1 disposition with Kaede. Total immediate: -0.3 Honor, -10 disposition across creditors. Compare to standard BROKEN_NO_NOTICE: -0.7 Honor, -21 creditor disposition, -52 witness splash. The force majeure clause reduced total damage by approximately 80%.

**Retroactive forgiveness: **"Shadowlands horde attacks Yasuki Province" enters the topic system as a Tier 2 crisis topic. High momentum — this is major news. Within 1-2 seasons, most politically active characters learn about it.

Doji Hotaru (Rei primary, creditor of SUPPORT_PLEDGE): learns about the Shadowlands attack at Winter Court through daily conversation. Forgiveness rate: 50% (Rei default). She suffered -3 disposition. Recovery: +1.5. Net: -1.5. Hotaru understands but is still slightly disappointed — she needed Taka’s support and didn’t get it.

Isawa Kaede (Jin primary, creditor of MEETING_ARRANGEMENT): learns about the attack through a letter. Forgiveness rate: 100% (Jin full compassion). She suffered -1 disposition. Recovery: +1.0. Net: 0.0. Kaede fully forgives — she would have done the same thing. The meeting can be rescheduled.

Bayushi Ogura (Seigyo primary, one of the 12 Winter Court witnesses): under force majeure, he suffered no witness penalty (Tier 2 force majeure has no witness splash). Under standard BROKEN_NO_NOTICE, he would have taken -2. The clause protected Taka’s broader network entirely.

**Net outcome: **Taka lost -0.3 Honor and approximately -5 net disposition across his network after forgiveness propagates. He can recover this in one season of active court work. His province is defended. His political network is strained but intact. Without force majeure, he would have lost -0.7 Honor and -73 total disposition — a catastrophic blow that would take years to recover from, punishing him for making the right decision. The force majeure clause ensures that the commitment system creates meaningful social pressure without making crises politically suicidal.
