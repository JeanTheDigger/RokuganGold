## 12.2b Clan & Family Collective Disposition — DESIGN FROZEN, VALUES PENDING PLAYTEST

Every named character’s personal disposition toward another character is seeded from three sources: the clan-to-clan baseline between their clans, the family-to-family baseline between their families, and their own personal history with that specific character. The clan and family baselines are shared values — collective sentiment built from the history of interactions between those groups. They are not the most important factor, but they are always present.

**CLAN-TO-CLAN BASELINE — LOCKED**

A single shared disposition value exists for each pair of Great Clans and Minor Clans. This value represents the collective historical relationship between those clans — their wars, alliances, trade relationships, and cultural affinities. The value shifts from large-scale events (see Event Ripple below).

When two characters from different clans interact for the first time, the clan-to-clan baseline applies as a permanent modifier to their starting personal disposition, weighted at 25% of its value. A clan-to-clan baseline of +20 contributes +5 to any individual meeting between members of those clans. A baseline of −20 contributes −5.

**STARTING DISPOSITION SEED FORMULA — LOCKED**

When a character meets another character for the first time, their starting personal disposition is calculated as:

- Clan-to-Clan baseline × 0.25

- + Family-to-Family baseline × 0.50

- + Personal history modifiers (favors, insults, shared events, etc.)

Example: Clan-to-Clan = +20, Family-to-Family = −10, no personal history. Starting disposition = (20 × 0.25) + (−10 × 0.50) = 5 − 5 = 0. The character starts neutral despite the positive clan relationship, because the family relationship counteracts it. Personal interaction then builds from this seed.

The personal disposition evolves through direct experience and grows to dominate the clan and family baselines over time. The baselines are a starting point, not a permanent weight.

**EVENT RIPPLE — LOCKED**

Events that generate personal disposition changes also generate a smaller ripple effect at the family and clan level. The ripple is proportional to the personal change but always smaller — one person’s actions shift collective sentiment only slightly.

- Personal disposition change × 0.20 = Family-to-Family baseline change

- Personal disposition change × 0.05 = Clan-to-Clan baseline change

Example: A Crab lord raids a Crane province. Personal disposition change: −20 (permanent). Family ripple: −4 to the Hida-Doji family baseline. Clan ripple: −1 to the Crab-Crane clan baseline. Repeated events compound — a clan that raids repeatedly accumulates significant negative clan-to-clan baseline over time.

Additional events that directly affect Family and Clan baselines beyond the ripple:

- Marriage between families: +5 permanent to both family baselines, +1 to clan baselines

- One family lord raids another family’s territory: −3 permanent to family baseline (on top of ripple)

- Betrayal between families: −10 permanent to family baseline. No decay.

- Intra-clan Rice sharing between families: +2 permanent to family baseline

- Duel between family members where one dies: −5 permanent to family baseline. No decay.

- Declared war between clans: −10 to clan baseline

- Formal peace treaty: +5 to clan baseline

- Clan Champion level marriage: +8 to clan baseline, +5 to participating family baselines

- Harvest destruction by one clan against another: −5 permanent to clan baseline. No decay.

Clan and family baselines do not decay on their own — they are collective historical memory, not fading personal impressions. Only deliberate diplomatic action (peace treaties, marriages, formal apologies at court) can improve a negative baseline. Events continue to push it further in either direction indefinitely.
