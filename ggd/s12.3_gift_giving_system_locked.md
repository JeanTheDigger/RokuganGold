## 12.3 Gift-Giving System — LOCKED

Gift-giving is a real political tool in Rokugan, not merely flavoring. Giving a gift raises disposition, creates social obligation, and signals intent. The quality and nature of the gift matters — a Kakita-crafted painting carries far more weight than a common object. Court is not just talking. It is beauty, skill, cultural expression, and the exchange of meaningful objects.

**Gift-Giving Mechanics (framework):**

- Giving a gift raises disposition between the giver and recipient. The magnitude of the disposition change depends on the quality and appropriateness of the gift.

- A gift creates a social obligation — the recipient owes goodwill or a favor in return. Failing to reciprocate damages disposition over time.

- Gift quality is tied to the artisan and crafting system — an item made by a renowned artisan from rare materials is a more powerful political gesture than an ordinary object.

- Appropriate gifts vary by recipient — a warrior values a fine weapon, a courtier values art or rare paper, a shugenja values ritual objects.

- An inappropriate or insulting gift can damage disposition rather than raise it.

✓ DONE — Gift-giving mechanics are fully designed across three sections:

- Quality tiers and Free Raises: Section 49 (Artisan & Crafting System). Normal: 0 Free Raises. Fine: +1. Exceptional: +2. Masterwork: +3. Legendary: +4. History Point bonuses stack on top.

- Deliver Gift court action — roll, outcomes, social obligation, inappropriate gift mechanics: Section 15.4.

- Disposition values per gift tier and social obligation temporary modifier: Section 12.2 temporary modifier table.

**What Constitutes an Appropriate Gift — LOCKED:**

Weapons and armor are not gifts in Rokugan. Gifting a sword implies the recipient lacks one, which is an insult. The sole exception is a legendary blade of extraordinary provenance — in that case the gift transcends the object and becomes a statement of singular honor. This is rare enough to be a once-in-a-generation gesture.

Appropriate gifts are objects of beauty, refinement, and cultural significance. They signal that the giver understands the recipient’s world and has invested thought in the gesture:

- Art objects — paintings, calligraphy, ceramics, lacquerware.

- Writing implements — fine paper, ink sticks, brushes. Valued by courtiers and scholars.

- Tea ceremony implements — universally valued across all clans and roles.

- Poetry collections, scrolls, books of lore — valued by scholars, shugenja, Dragon and Phoenix characters.

- Rare incense, perfumes, pressed flowers — valued in court contexts.

- Decorative accessories — netsuke, hairpieces, fans, fine silk cloth.

- Food and drink — rare teas, aged sake, regional delicacies. A thoughtful and personal choice.

- Religious and ritual objects — prayer beads, small shrine pieces. Valued by shugenja and devout characters.

What varies by recipient is taste within this range. A Crab bushi will not appreciate a Crane poetry scroll the way a Crane courtier would. A Phoenix shugenja values ritual implements over decorative accessories. A Dragon values scholarly works. The Deliver Gift action in Section 15.4 handles inappropriate gifts mechanically — they reduce or remove the Free Raises that gift quality would otherwise provide.
