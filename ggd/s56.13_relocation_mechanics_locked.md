## 56.13 Relocation Mechanics --- LOCKED

When an insurgency survives a player mission and its position is compromised, it relocates. This section defines when relocation triggers, where the insurgency goes, and what consequences follow in both the original and destination provinces.

### 56.13.1 Relocation Triggers --- LOCKED

The insurgency relocates when its current position is compromised. Not every mission outcome triggers relocation.

- **Full Success:** Always triggers relocation if any survivors remain. The camp is cleared. Survivors scatter.

- **Partial Success (player reached the objective space):** Triggers relocation. The player saw the layout, fought inside, knows the position. Staying is too risky.

- **Retreat (player was spotted inside the objective space):** Triggers relocation. The bandits know their position is known.

- **Retreat (player never reached the objective space):** Does not trigger relocation. A retreat from the forest zone, the base of a hill, or the ravine mouth does not necessarily reveal the camp itself. The bandits may not know they were scouted.

- **Failure:** Does not trigger relocation. The defenders held. Their position is secure.

### 56.13.2 Relocation Destination --- LOCKED

When relocation triggers, the insurgency either moves to a new location within the same province or flees to an adjacent province.

**Same-Province Relocation:** The default. The generator rerolls from the terrain probability pool (Section 56.2.1). A new template, a new procedurally generated layout, a new location in the province. The same template type can appear again but the specific map is freshly generated --- a different cave, a different clearing. No cooldown on template reuse.

**Adjacent-Province Relocation:** When the current province is heavily compromised --- the player knows the terrain, has scouts watching, garrison is active --- the bandits are more likely to flee further. Adjacent province relocation chance increases with the number of missions already conducted against this insurgency in the current province. Exact probability values pending playtest.

**Template-Specific Exceptions:**

- **Makeshift Stockade:** Delays same-province relocation by one season. A stockade takes time and materials to build. The insurgency operates without a fixed base for one season before establishing a new position.

- **Ruined Structure:** Can only reappear as a Ruined Structure if another ruin exists in the destination province. Otherwise the template pool excludes it and redistributes normally.

### 56.13.3 Adjacent Province Effects --- LOCKED

An insurgency relocating to an adjacent province creates consequences in both provinces.

**Destination Province:** Receives the insurgency at its current Strength minus any kills from the triggering mission. The insurgency bypasses the normal spawn roll entirely --- no Stability threshold check. A Stable province at Stability 85 can suddenly have a Strength 3 bandit group that fled from next door. That province immediately starts losing -1 Stability per season from active insurgency presence per the existing Section 11.11 rules, even though its conditions would never have produced the insurgency naturally. If the destination province already has active insurgencies, the relocating group adds to the province’s problems --- multiple insurgencies can coexist in the same province.

**Roster Composition Shift:** The insurgency’s roster composition adjusts to the destination province’s Stability. If the destination province is Restless rather than Volatile, the Bandit Rabble ratio drops --- some of the desperate peasants peel off during relocation, not following to unfamiliar territory. The ronin core and Bandit Thugs relocate intact. Only Bandit Rabble attrite during the move.

**Political Consequence:** The neighboring lord now has bandits in their territory that originated from the player’s province. This generates a Tier 4 diplomatic topic --- who is responsible, who failed to contain the problem, demands for assistance or compensation. The relationship between the two lords may suffer. A lord who repeatedly pushes insurgency problems into neighboring provinces instead of solving them will face political consequences through the existing court and disposition systems.

### 56.13.4 Original Province Recovery --- LOCKED

The insurgency leaving the original province removes the -1 Stability per season penalty from active insurgency presence (Section 11.11). But all other Stability losses remain --- famine, under-garrison, war damage, prior raid effects. Recovery is still capped at +3 Stability per season maximum (Province Clear +2, Peace Bonus +1 per Section 11.11). A province at Stability 40 that just lost its bandits still takes over a year of good governance to reach the Stable threshold at 76. The damage was done. The bandits were a symptom of poor governance --- removing them does not fix the underlying cause.
