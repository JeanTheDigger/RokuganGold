## 57.24 Bonsai System

### 57.24.1 Overview

A bonsai is a living world object cultivated over IC months through sustained patient attention. Unlike crafted items produced in a concentrated session, a bonsai exists continuously on its tender’s character sheet and requires 1 AP of tending per IC month to maintain and advance. Quality grows through accumulated Raises over time. A bonsai can be displayed in an eligible lesser zone as the zone’s art piece, generating visitor disposition bonuses and Glory for its tender. When displayed within a garden, it boosts the garden’s effective visitor tier. Bonsai are fully portable and giftable, carrying their complete history with them. World-generated bonsai exist at game start with age and quality already accumulated — ancient specimens produced by centuries of care that predate any player character.

### 57.24.2 Bonsai World Object

Each bonsai tracks the following fields: bonsai_id, owner_id (current owner and responsible tender — the same character; ownership and tending responsibility are never separated), species (cosmetic only — determines ASCII appearance and provenance string, carries no mechanical weight), collection_province_id (province where the specimen was collected or where world generation placed it), collection_date (IC date of collection or world gen timestamp), quality_tier (Normal through Legendary), quality_points (accumulated points toward next tier advance, resets on advance), last_tended_month (IC month of last successful TEND_BONSAI), consecutive_missed_months (counter for degradation), display_zone_id (null if not on display, zone_id if currently placed in a zone), is_dead (true if degraded past Normal to destruction), world_generated (true for game-start specimens). Provenance fields mirror the item system: a complete ownership history, collection record, and notable event log transfer with the object on gifting. Provenance access for living bonsai: the provenance record of a living bonsai is directly readable through the standard item Inspection Mode — no roll required. The object is physically present and its history is inscribed in its world record: collection province and date, all previous owners, quality progression, display history, and notable events. Section 57.24.19 covers investigation of bonsai that have died and no longer exist physically. A living bonsai needs no investigation — its history is simply there to read.

### 57.24.3 World Generation

At game start, bonsai are procedurally seeded into the world in two contexts. First, within existing gardens at eligible zones (CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD, TSUBONIWA) — a garden of Fine or above quality may have a bonsai already on display, integrated with the garden from its inception. Second, within lesser zones generally — a lord’s private room, a temple alcove, an artisan’s studio. Interior zone placement is tracked in the bonsai’s display_zone_id. Interior display follows the same zone eligibility as paintings per Section 57.36 (Flag Matrix) — Castle/Shiro, court locations, Temple/Shinden, estate/private quarters. World-generated bonsai are assigned to an NPC tender at generation time. Their quality_tier reflects their age and the calibre of their historical care: a 50-year-old bonsai in an ancient Dragon clan estate may be Masterwork or Legendary. Province determines species at generation per the same rule as player collection.

### 57.24.4 COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN

ActionID: COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 1 AP. Context: AT_ZONE in any non-city world map location. COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN is available at wilderness zones, mountain terrain, forest zones, road waypoints, temple grounds, rural settlements, and any other non-urban world map location a character passes through during travel. City zones and castle interior zones do not yield suitable specimens — no natural growing environment exists. Province determines what species are available. A character may opportunistically collect during travel without requiring a dedicated collection trip — the action fires naturally when passing through eligible terrain. Action_block note: COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN is subject to the standard action_block system. Prisoner status blocks the action — a captive does not have access to the natural terrain required for collection. This is handled by the broader action_block system (Section 29.15.24) rather than bonsai-specific rules; no special case is needed in this section. Roll: Artisan: Gardening / Perception vs TN by terrain difficulty. Common terrain (settled forest, temple grounds): TN 15. Difficult terrain (mountain, deep wilderness): TN 20. Rare terrain (ancient sacred grove, spirit-touched landscape): TN 25. The roll represents finding a specimen with the right form and potential — not every tree is suitable for bonsai. On success: a new bonsai world object is created at Normal quality_tier, assigned to the character as owner_id, with species set by province, collection_province_id and collection_date recorded. The bonsai enters the character’s inventory. On failure: no suitable specimen was found this AP. The character may try again with another AP in the same or different eligible terrain. Raises on collection: each Raise above success produces a provenance note about the specimen’s exceptional natural qualities — cosmetic only, no mechanical effect on starting quality_tier.

### 57.24.5 TEND_BONSAI

ActionID: TEND_BONSAI. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 1 AP per IC month. Context: AT_ZONE — the tender must be physically co-located with the bonsai. If the bonsai is on display in a zone, the tender must travel to that zone to tend it. If the bonsai is in the tender’s inventory (not on display), the tender may tend it at any zone they currently occupy. For player characters, tending a displayed bonsai requires entering it in the ASCII map view. Roll: Artisan: Gardening / Awareness vs TN 10. On success: last_tended_month is updated, consecutive_missed_months resets to 0. Bonsai is maintained. Each Raise called and achieved adds 1 quality_point toward the next tier advance. On failure (roll does not meet TN 10): counts as a missed month for degradation purposes — consecutive_missed_months increments.

### 57.24.6 Quality Progression

Quality advances when quality_points reaches the threshold for the next tier. Each Raise on a successful TEND_BONSAI roll adds 1 quality_point. On tier advance, quality_points resets to 0 and begins accumulating toward the following tier. Thresholds: Normal → Fine = 10 quality_points (minimum 10 months of single Raises). Fine → Exceptional = 20 quality_points. Exceptional → Masterwork = 35 quality_points. Masterwork → Legendary = 55 quality_points. A tender who reliably achieves 2 Raises per month reaches Legendary in approximately 6 IC years. A tender achieving only 1 Raise per month requires approximately 10 IC years. A tender who only maintains without Raises never advances in quality — the bonsai holds its current tier indefinitely. Skill gate: Artisan: Gardening rank must equal or exceed the quality tier to be reached (Normal = rank 1, Fine = rank 2, Exceptional = rank 3, Masterwork = rank 4, Legendary = rank 5). Quality_points accumulate regardless but tier advance is blocked until the rank requirement is met. Excess quality_points at Legendary: each quality_point accumulated past the Legendary threshold converts to +0.02 Glory for the tender, recognising the continued refinement of a work already at its peak.

### 57.24.7 Degradation and Death

Missing 1 consecutive month of tending (consecutive_missed_months = 1): a visible warning state appears in the bonsai’s ASCII representation. The quality_tier is unchanged. Missing 2 consecutive months (consecutive_missed_months = 2): quality_tier degrades one step. quality_points resets to 0. The ASCII simplifies accordingly. Missing further consecutive months: each additional consecutive missed month after the second continues to degrade quality_tier by one step per month. At Normal quality_tier with a further missed month: the bonsai enters a Mundane state — all mechanical effects cease, the ASCII shows a dying tree, and the tender has one final month to resume tending. If the following month is also missed, the bonsai dies (is_dead: true). A dead bonsai’s world object record is preserved indefinitely with the same philosophy as destroyed gardens — its history does not vanish. The record is flagged is_dead: true with a death_date. The physical bonsai is gone but the provenance history of what it was and who tended it persists.

### 57.24.8 DISPLAY_BONSAI

ActionID: DISPLAY_BONSAI. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 1 AP. No roll. Context: AT_ZONE in CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD or TSUBONIWA. The tender must be physically present and must own the bonsai (owner_id = character_id). The zone must have an available bonsai_display_slot — one slot per eligible zone. On display: display_zone_id is set on the bonsai world object. The bonsai’s Section 50 ASCII representation is embedded in the zone’s map at the display slot. The bonsai remains on the tender’s character sheet — ownership does not transfer to the zone. Removing from display: the tender may remove the bonsai at no AP cost while physically AT_ZONE. The bonsai returns to inventory, the display slot is cleared, and the ASCII feature is removed from the zone map. Visitor effects and Glory ticks begin firing once the bonsai is on display and the zone receives named character visitors (Section 57.24.10). The tender must still travel to the zone monthly to perform TEND_BONSAI. Unclaimed zones: if the zone has no current Daimyo (unclaimed wilderness shrine, abandoned structure, unoccupied waypoint), bonsai_display_permission is not required. The artisan may use DISPLAY_BONSAI freely at any unclaimed eligible zone. The Daimyo hosting bonus does not fire — there is no lord to benefit. Visitor effects and Glory ticks fire normally. If the zone later comes under a lord’s authority, the bonsai is treated as grandfathered — the new lord may order removal using the standard removal mechanic. A wandering artisan placing a bonsai at an unmaintained roadside shrine is a legitimate act requiring no lord’s consent.

### 57.24.9 Garden Integration

When DISPLAY_BONSAI is used in a zone that has an active garden_object, the bonsai is automatically integrated with the garden. Reverse case (BP): when CULTIVATE_GARDEN completes in a zone where a bonsai is already on standalone display, the newly created garden_object automatically integrates the existing bonsai. No additional action is required. The bonsai transitions from standalone visitor effects to garden-integrated mode at the moment of garden completion — its standalone Glory tick and disposition bonus stop firing, and the garden’s effective visitor tier gains the +1 boost from that point forward. Integration effect: the garden’s effective visitor disposition tier for the bonus calculation is raised by 1 (capped at Legendary). A Fine garden (+2 visitor bonus) with an integrated bonsai pays out as Exceptional (+3). The boost is flat regardless of the bonsai’s quality_tier — the presence of the living art object is what matters. The bonsai master remains responsible for monthly tending regardless of whose garden hosts the piece. The garden master and the bonsai master may be different characters, both visiting the zone independently on their respective maintenance schedules. When the bonsai is removed from display or dies, the integration ends and the garden’s effective tier returns to its current_tier. Garden destruction while bonsai is displayed (BM): when a garden_object is destroyed (through degradation or removal) while a bonsai is integrated in it, the bonsai automatically transitions to standalone display mode rather than being removed. The bonsai remains in the bonsai_display_slot, its display_zone_id is unchanged, and it begins generating standalone visitor effects from the next visitor tick. No action is required from the bonsai master. The integration simply ends — the garden is gone, the bonsai is still there, standing alone.

### 57.24.10 Standalone Display and Visitor Effects

When a bonsai is on display in a zone without a garden (standalone display), it generates its own visitor effects. Disposition trigger: any named character who enters the zone (VISITING or AT_COURT context) while the bonsai’s quality_tier is Normal or above automatically receives a disposition bonus toward the tender. The tender themselves are excluded (owner_id ≠ character_id rule, same as gardens). Bonus scales with quality_tier: Normal +1, Fine +2, Exceptional +3, Masterwork +4, Legendary +5. Duration: 4 IC months. Fires once per character per visit. Glory tick: each time visitor_count_since_last_tick reaches 5 (excluding the tender’s own visits), the tender receives +0.1 Glory regardless of their current location. Counter resets to 0. Cooldown: 1 IC season between Glory ticks. When a bonsai is garden-integrated (Section 57.24.9), standalone effects do not fire separately — the bonsai’s contribution is expressed through the garden’s boosted effective tier. Glory split by creator identity: if the bonsai tender and the garden creator are the same character, the garden’s standard +0.1 Glory tick (Section 57.23.7) covers both contributions — no additional tick fires. If the bonsai tender and the garden creator are different characters, a reduced bonsai integration tick of +0.05 Glory fires simultaneously to the bonsai tender each time the garden’s 5-visitor threshold is reached. The garden creator still receives their standard +0.1. The bonsai tender receives half that rate — their piece enhances the garden but the garden is not theirs. A Legendary bonsai integrated into a stranger’s Legendary garden generates meaningful, sustained Glory for its tender over time even though the tier boost appears to be absorbed. The Daimyo hosting bonus remains unchanged: +0.01 per tick regardless of how many creators are involved (Section 57.24.11).

### 57.24.11 Daimyo Hosting Bonus

The zone’s current Daimyo receives +0.01 Glory each time the bonsai’s Glory tick fires (visitor_count_since_last_tick reaches 5). This is the same hosting bonus as gardens (Section 57.23.7). Each art piece on display in the zone ticks independently — a zone with both a garden and a bonsai on standalone display generates two separate +0.01 Daimyo ticks when both reach their visitor thresholds. A zone with a garden-integrated bonsai generates one Daimyo hosting tick per garden Glory tick (the bonsai’s contribution is subsumed into the garden’s tick). The hosting bonus accrues to whoever currently holds the zone — it is not tied to the original commissioning lord.

### 57.24.13 Commission Pipeline

When a lord wants a bonsai — displayed or gifted — the same REQUEST_ART and OFFER_ART_COMMISSION ActionIDs that serve the garden system handle the bonsai commission, extended with two bonsai-specific art_type values. No new ActionIDs are required for the commission layer.

REQUEST_ART (art_type: bonsai_gift): the lord wants to receive a bonsai permanently. Full ownership transfer on delivery. No zone_id required. Topic: “[Daimyo name] is seeking a bonsai as a gift.” Tier 4. Any artisan with a Normal or above bonsai in their inventory may respond with OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (art_type: bonsai_gift). The artisan is offering one they already own — no new cultivation required. REQUEST_ART (art_type: bonsai_display, zone_id: [CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD or TSUBONIWA]): the lord wants an artisan’s bonsai placed and tended at a named zone. Artisan retains ownership throughout. Typically issued to a vassal artisan via ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. Zone knowledge prerequisite applies: the artisan must have visited the zone or received a topic naming it.

### 57.24.14 Permission and Declined Memory

Bonsai_display_permission: before DISPLAY_BONSAI can fire at a zone the artisan does not own, a bonsai_display_permission flag must be set (character-specific, zone-specific). Granted through REQUEST_ART or OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (bonsai_display type) acceptance. One bonsai per bonsai_display_slot per zone. Permission clears on: bonsai removed from display, bonsai dies while on display, commission cancellation, grace period expiry on zone transfer. Bonsai_gift commissions do not require display permission — DELIVER_GIFT is used instead. Same 1 IC season grace period on Daimyo death or zone transfer as garden permission (Section 57.23.2). Permission lapse does not auto-remove: when bonsai_display_permission lapses (grace period expires without renewal), the bonsai is not automatically removed from the display slot. It remains physically displayed and continues generating visitor effects and Glory ticks for the tender. Permission is required to place a bonsai (DISPLAY_BONSAI action gate) — not to keep it there once placed. The new zone lord may order removal at any time through the standard removal mechanic, generating a social topic if the bonsai is Fine or above. Without an active removal order, the bonsai simply remains, now hosted without a formal arrangement.

Declined memory: separate from garden fields. Zone commitment refusal → zone_id added to declined_bonsai_zones. Clears when slot becomes free or new REQUEST_ART names the zone. Lord disinterest refusal → (daimyo_id, art_type) added to declined_bonsai_commissions. Expires after 2 IC seasons or when disposition reaches Friendly or new REQUEST_ART overrides. These fields are fully separate from declined_garden_zones and declined_commissions — a declined garden offer does not block a bonsai offer, and vice versa.

          Commission status lifecycle: ACTIVE — commission accepted, delivery window running. SUSPENDED — a superseding ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE from the artisan’s operational superior chain (Section 11.3.18) has paused the commission; delivery window freezes. This includes orders from the commissioning lord directly OR from any lord higher in the operational hierarchy — a Clan Champion’s deployment order suspends a commission made by a subordinate Daimyo. Applies to ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE-origin commissions only. Biology continues during SUSPENDED: the bonsai degrades normally while the artisan is absent. The artisan is not penalised with failure consequences for degradation that occurs during SUSPENDED, but the physical state of the bonsai reflects the elapsed time. The artisan returns from deployment to whatever condition the bonsai is in. When the superseding duty ends, status returns to ACTIVE and the re-activation trigger fires (see below). Being idle at court or pursuing personal objectives does not qualify for suspension. ABANDONED — delivery window expired without fulfillment; failure consequences fire. COMPLETED — DELIVER_GIFT or DISPLAY_BONSAI fired within window. CANCELLED — lord or artisan cancelled before abandonment. Lord-ordered display removal explicitly triggers CANCELLED: when the zone’s Daimyo orders removal of a displayed bonsai, the bonsai_display commission_record (if any) immediately shifts to CANCELLED. No Honor cost to the artisan, no failure consequences — the lord terminated the arrangement. The artisan retains the bonsai. A Tier 3 social topic fires if the bonsai was Fine or above, as in standard removal. FORGIVEN — artisan failed but forgiveness granted per Section 57.24.15 forgiveness mechanic. BONSAI_LOST — the bonsai that was to be delivered or displayed no longer exists, is no longer in the artisan’s possession, or has died while on display under a bonsai_display commission. Death while on display triggers BONSAI_LOST, not ABANDONED — the delivery window is not the issue; the object is gone (see below). CREATOR_DECEASED — artisan died while commission was ACTIVE or SUSPENDED (see below).

Re-activation on SUSPENDED → ACTIVE: when the commission_record transitions from SUSPENDED back to ACTIVE, two effects fire automatically. If the commissioning lord is alive and holds the zone (bonsai_display) or is alive (bonsai_gift): the lord’s NPC generates WRITE_LETTER to the artisan with the bonsai commission topic as payload, re-activating it in the artisan’s known_topics. The commission_record is re-added to the artisan’s active_objectives, driving BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the target zone or lord on the artisan’s next Phase 5. Permission re-grant on retrieval: if the artisan voluntarily retrieved their bonsai before the SUSPENDED transition (bonsai is back in inventory, bonsai_display_permission was cleared by the removal), the re-activation letter automatically reinstates bonsai_display_permission for that zone. The permission was cleared by circumstances beyond the artisan’s control — reinstating it on re-activation prevents a soft deadlock where the artisan returns with their bonsai and no legal access to the display slot.

CREATOR_DECEASED: when the artisan dies while a commission_record is ACTIVE or SUSPENDED, the system checks all their bonsai commission_records. Each transitions to CREATOR_DECEASED immediately. No Honor cost — the dead cannot be held accountable. For bonsai_gift commissions: the bonsai reverts to the estate (follows character inheritance rules). The lord receives a topic: “[Artisan name] died before delivering the commissioned bonsai to [Lord name].” Tier 4. For bonsai_display commissions: if the bonsai is currently on display at the zone, it remains there but will degrade without tending. The lord receives a topic: “[Artisan name] has died; their bonsai at [zone name] will no longer be tended.” Tier 4. Bonsai_display_permission lapses immediately on creator death — no grace period, since no continuation is possible. The bonsai on display will degrade naturally season by season until destroyed or removed by the lord.

### 57.24.18 Gift Commission Mechanics

When a bonsai_gift commission is fulfilled through DELIVER_GIFT, two effects stack. First, the standard DELIVER_GIFT quality bonus applies — quality tier determines Free Raises per Section 49 item quality rules. Second, if the gift fulfills an active commission_record, a commissioned gift completion bonus fires: Normal = +3 disposition, Fine = +5, Exceptional = +7, Masterwork = +9, Legendary = +12 toward the recipient. This is smaller than the garden completion bonus (which reaches +20) because the artisan cultivated the bonsai independently before the commission existed — the commission formalised the transfer, not inspired the creation. After DELIVER_GIFT: owner_id updates to recipient, recipient becomes immediately responsible for monthly TEND_BONSAI, commission_record shifts to COMPLETED. Full provenance history is visible to the new owner through the item inspection system. Design note — converting a display commission to a gift: if a lord wishes to receive a displayed bonsai as a permanent gift rather than continue the bonsai_display arrangement, the artisan must first retrieve the bonsai from the display slot (0 AP, AT_ZONE), transitioning the bonsai_display commission_record to CANCELLED, then fire DELIVER_GIFT as a separate transaction. The commissioned gift completion bonus does not apply unless an active bonsai_gift commission_record exists — converting a display arrangement into a gift is a standard DELIVER_GIFT using quality-based Free Raises only. The gift is no less meaningful for lacking the commission bonus; a Masterwork bonsai arriving as a voluntary gift is a significant social act regardless of paperwork.

### 57.24.19 Post-Death Records and Historical Investigation

A bonsai’s record persists after its death. The bonsai_object record is retained indefinitely with is_dead: true and a death_date field. All provenance fields — owner history, collection province, quality progression, display history — remain intact. The operational fields are frozen at the moment of death. This follows the same philosophy as garden post-destruction records (Section 57.23.12) and item provenance.

Physical investigation at former display zones: Investigation (Search) / Perception AT_ZONE where the bonsai was displayed. Physical traces fade quickly compared to gardens — a display slot is a contained space, not soil shaped over years. TN scale: within 1 IC year: TN 15. 1–5 IC years: TN 20. Beyond 5 IC years: TN 25 (maximum difficulty). Physical traces are effectively absent beyond approximately 20–25 IC years — the display slot has been reused, repaved, or weathered to the point where no physical evidence remains regardless of roll result. For bonsai removed or deceased more than 25 IC years ago, physical investigation cannot succeed; only social and documentary investigation layers are available. Artisan: Gardening rank 3+ grants 1 Free Raise. On success: investigator learns a bonsai was displayed here. Raises reveal: owner identity (1), quality tier (1), display and death dates (1), cause of death or removal (1). Not applicable for bonsai that died in inventory — no zone trace exists.

Social investigation: Lore: History / Intelligence for characters with the bonsai’s last owner or zone’s former lord in their met_characters. TN 20 within 10 IC years (living memory). TN 30 beyond. On success: learns the bonsai existed and its owner. Raises reveal quality tier and circumstances of death. Documentary investigation: any living character who received a lifecycle topic about the bonsai carries that knowledge. bonsai_visitor_memory flags and commission_records constitute a persistent documentary trail. No new mechanics required — the existing topic and knowledge systems handle it.
