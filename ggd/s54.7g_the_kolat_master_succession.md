## 54.7g The Kolat — Master Succession

Masters cannot coordinate a succession process. They do not know each other’s identities. A vacancy cannot be resolved by committee vote or collective deliberation. The conspiracy’s anonymity architecture, which is its greatest protection, also makes collective succession impossible. The solution is individual preparation: each Master designates their own line of succession privately, and when the seat falls vacant the line activates automatically. The organisation never waits. The Sect never goes dark.

### Designation

**Three heirs in succession.** Each Master designates exactly three heirs, ranked in order of preference. All three must be conscious Kolat agents with kolat_sect matching the Master’s Sect. The Master chooses them personally — these are people the Master knows, trusts, and has assessed as capable of holding the mandate. The designations are filed with Tiger through SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT at the Hidden Temple or through a physically delivered cipher document via the Silk network. TRANSMIT_VIA_TEAR cannot carry this information — Tears transmit intent and urgency, not structured documents with ranked NPC identifiers. The designation record is a physical cipher document, stored in the Cloud archive under Tiger’s access key (see heir_designations data structure below). No other Master knows the designations exist. No heir knows they are designated unless the Master chooses to tell them.

**Heir conditions.** A designation is valid only if the heir is: alive, not currently Broken or under active investigation, a conscious Kolat agent in good standing with their Sect. Tiger evaluates these conditions when a vacancy triggers. Tiger does not evaluate capability, suitability, or readiness — that is the outgoing Master’s judgment, expressed in the ordering. Tiger only checks that the conditions are met.

**Maintenance.** Designations should be updated when circumstances change — an heir dies, is compromised, or becomes unsuitable. This is the Master’s responsibility. A Master who neglects to update their designations over many seasons runs the risk of all three heirs being unavailable when the seat falls vacant. This is a failure of planning, not a failure of the system. Tiger’s discretionary selection is the fallback, not the default.

**heir_designations data structure.** Stored in the Cloud archive as a cipher document, accessible only via Tiger’s personal access key. Schema: Dictionary[kolat_sect: string → Array[NPC_id, NPC_id, NPC_id]] — one entry per Sect, three ranked heir NPC IDs per entry. Tiger’s own succession record is stored as a separate sealed document within the same archive, accessible only via a secondary key Tiger holds alone and never transmits. The Cloud archivist has physical access to the archive but cannot read either document without the keys. Tiger’s NPC character sheet carries two hidden fields: heir_designations_key (string) and tiger_succession_key (string). Both are flagged is_hidden: true and are not accessible through any social interaction or engine action short of capturing Tiger alive and interrogating them.

### Vacancy Trigger

A Master seat becomes vacant when the Master dies by any cause: natural death, player action, Lotus elimination on Tiger’s order, or death in the field. A Broken Master who has not yet been eliminated does not trigger vacancy — they still hold the seat while alive, even if operationally compromised. Vacancy triggers only on confirmed death.

On the engine tick following confirmed death entering Tiger’s IntelDB, Tiger’s engine fires a direct system event: EVALUATE_SUCCESSION. The vacancy is real from the moment of the Master’s death. Tiger’s response is delayed by information travel time — a Master dying in a distant province may not be confirmed dead in Tiger’s IntelDB for days or weeks, depending on how quickly the Silk network carries the news. During that interval the seat is functionally vacant but Tiger does not yet know to act on it. This window is a genuine vulnerability: a player faction that kills a Master and then moves fast against the Sect before Tiger learns of the death is operating in a brief window of uncoordinated response.

EVALUATE_SUCCESSION is not a NeedType and does not generate an ImmediateNeed. It is a direct system call that executes Tiger’s heir evaluation logic automatically — reading the heir_designations record, checking conditions on each heir in order, and writing the elevation to the first valid heir’s character sheet. No AP is spent. No Phase 2–5 loop runs. The succession itself is a background system process. The follow-on actions — routing a Tear through the Silk network, submitting a succession report to the Cloud archive, notifying the new Master’s handler to arrange the first seasonal meeting — do cost AP and enter Tiger’s normal queue as ImmediateNeeds under existing NeedTypes on subsequent AP firings.

### Succession Cascade

**First heir evaluated.** Tiger checks the first designated heir against the three conditions. If valid: the heir is elevated. Their standing objective shifts to the Sect mandate immediately. Their Kolat objective slot is cleared of any active assignment — the first act of a new Master is orientation, not inherited tasks. If they do not already hold a Tear, Tiger routes one through the Silk network, arriving at the seasonal delivery speed. The heir receives the Temple location at the next seasonal meeting — they are a Master from the moment of elevation, but they do not know the Temple location until they attend in person. This is the final initiatory act, unchanged from world generation.

**Second and third heirs.** If the first heir fails the conditions, Tiger evaluates the second. If the second fails, the third. Each evaluation happens within the same reactive event resolution. The cascade runs instantly in engine time — the Sect does not experience a multi-season gap while Tiger deliberates.

**Tiger’s discretionary selection.** If all three designated heirs are unavailable, Tiger selects from the Sect’s senior conscious agents using the same weighted candidate pool logic from Section 54.7a, restricted to agents with kolat_sect matching the vacant Sect. Tiger applies the same minimums: Insight Rank 3+, relevant skill thresholds, personality hard block exclusions. The selection resolves within the same reactive event. This path is slower in narrative terms — Tiger is making a judgment call rather than executing a pre-prepared plan — but the engine resolves it on the same AP tick. The Sect never goes without a Master for more than one engine cycle.

### Operational Continuity During the Gap

Between the moment of death and the moment of elevation — which in engine terms is one AP tick, but in IC time may be days or weeks depending on Tiger’s current context and Silk network delivery speed — the Sect’s field agents continue running. Their standing objectives are unchanged. Their active Kolat objectives, if any, remain in their Kolat objective slot and continue competing for AP through normal scoring. No agent receives new Kolat objectives from the vacant seat during this period. DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE from the Silk network continues on its own rhythm. The Sect does not freeze. It simply stops receiving new direction until the new Master is in place.

The new Master’s first seasonal meeting is their real orientation. Until then they operate on the Sect mandate encoded in their standing objective, the intelligence in their kolat_positions field as it existed at elevation, and whatever knowledge their personal IntelDB already holds. A well-prepared heir who was trusted with sensitive information before designation transitions smoothly. An heir who knew almost nothing faces a steep first season.

### Tiger’s Succession

Tiger’s own succession follows the same three-heir cascade with one critical difference: Tiger’s designations are filed with no one. Tiger is the only node who knows all Master identities. There is no other Master who could hold Tiger’s succession records without compromising the entire identity architecture. Instead Tiger’s heir designations are stored in the Cloud archive under the highest Cipher classification — accessible only by a code Tiger carries and reveals to no living person until the moment of designation delivery.

When Tiger’s death enters the Cloud archivist’s IntelDB — through direct observation if Tiger dies at the Temple, or through the Silk network otherwise — the Cloud archivist’s engine fires EVALUATE_SUCCESSION as a system call. This is the same system call that handles all other Master successions, but here the Cloud archivist is the triggering agent rather than Tiger. The archivist’s engine reads the tiger_succession_key from their access credentials, retrieves the sealed record from the Cloud archive, and activates the first valid heir. The Cloud archivist does not know whose name is in the record until they open it. The new Tiger inherits Tiger’s records of all Master identities via the tiger_succession_key — this key unlocks the sealed succession record in the Cloud archive which contains a full copy of kolat_master_identities as of the last Conclave. If many seasons have passed, some entries may be stale. The new Tiger sends Tear transmissions to all known identities and flags non-respondents for MONITOR_KOLAT_SECURITY assessment immediately. The first Conclave corrects any stale entries through in-person attendance. This is the most sensitive moment in the conspiracy’s internal operation: a brief window where a single Cloud agent holds Tiger’s succession record before destroying it, knowing everything. The Dream Sect has historically maintained a conditioning protocol for Cloud archivists specifically to prevent this knowledge from surviving their tenure.

Cloud Master succession — archive handover: when a new Cloud Master is elevated, they face a unique inheritance problem. The personal working copy at the outgoing Master’s holdings was destroyed on compromise (or is simply gone if they died suddenly without triggering the compromise protocol). The master archive at the Hidden Temple is intact. The new Cloud Master’s first act after receiving the Temple location at their inaugural seasonal meeting is to visit the Temple archive and conduct a full orientation session — reading the cipher index, learning the archive’s organisation, and establishing their own personal cipher key for the working copy they will rebuild. Tiger provides the archive access credentials (stored in tiger_succession_key alongside Tiger’s own succession record) as part of the succession packet. The new Cloud Master begins their tenure with full access to the master archive but no working copy — they must rebuild their personal index from the Temple documents, which takes time. This rebuilding period is documented as cloud_archive_last_sync_ic_day being set to the day of their first Temple visit. A new Cloud Master is operationally limited for their first 1–2 seasons while they reconstruct their working knowledge. Tiger plans around this gap when possible — Cloud succession is the one event that meaningfully degrades the Kolat’s counter-intelligence capability.
