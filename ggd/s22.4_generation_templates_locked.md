## 22.4 Generation Templates — LOCKED

Procedurally generated characters are built using templates that combine Clan + Family + School + Insight Rank. A template defines the expected Ring values, skill distribution, starting Honor/Glory/Status, and equipment for a character of that combination at that Rank.

- Template variables: Clan → Family → School → Insight Rank. Each combination produces a distinct template.

- Example templates: Crab Hida Bushi Rank 2, Lion Ikoma Courtier Rank 4, Phoenix Isawa Shugenja Rank 3.

- Within each template, values are generated with small random variance — two Crab Hida Bushi Rank 2 characters will be similar but not identical.

- Personality virtues (Bushido + Shourido) are assigned randomly at generation from the full pool of 49 combinations, weighted by clan tendency — a Lion is more likely to have Yu (Courage) as their Bushido virtue than a Crane.

- Starting disposition values are assigned based on clan baseline modifiers (Section 12.2) — permanent modifiers applied at generation.

**HOW TEMPLATES ARE CONSTRUCTED**

A generation template is not a hand-authored file for every possible combination — there are too many schools, families, and ranks for that approach. Instead, a template is a procedural recipe that combines five data sources already present in the game:

- School data (Section 29): Starting skills, school benefit (+1 Trait), Honor starting value, outfit, and Techniques per Rank. This is the core of the template.

- Family data (Section 27): Each family grants a +1 bonus to a specific Trait. This stacks with the school benefit.

- Ring value ranges (defined below): Expected Ring values at each Insight Rank, based on how characters naturally advance.

- Personality weights (Section 19): Clan-specific probability weights for Bushido and Shourido virtue assignment.

- Disposition baselines (Section 12.2): Starting clan-to-clan disposition modifiers applied at generation.

The template engine reads these sources and combines them to produce a complete character sheet. Every Clan + Family + School + Insight Rank combination is handled automatically — no manual template authoring required. Small random variance is applied after base values are set.

**STEP 1 — IDENTITY ****&**** STARTING STATS**

The generation process begins with fixed identity fields and the school's starting values.

- Clan, Family, School: Set by the template combination being generated.

- School Type: Derived from school (Bushi, Courtier, Shugenja, Monk, Ninja, Artisan).

- Starting Honor: Taken directly from the school's listed Honor value (e.g. Hida Bushi = 3.5). This is the base — Insight Rank advancement applies a small modifier (see below).

- Starting Glory: 1.0 for all generated characters at Rank 1. Increases by 0.5 per Insight Rank above 1 (Rank 2 = 1.5, Rank 3 = 2.0, etc.).

- Starting Status: Derived from role/position assigned to the character (Local Daimyo = Status 3.0, provincial samurai = Status 1.0, etc.). Not determined by school.

- Starting Infamy: 0.0 for all generated characters unless a specific advantage/disadvantage overrides this.

- Age: Calculated from Insight Rank (see Age Calculation below).

**STEP 2 — TRAITS ****&**** RINGS**

All Traits start at 2 (the baseline for an adult samurai). Family bonus and school benefit each add +1 to their respective Traits. Rings are the average of their two constituent Traits.

Starting Traits at Rank 1:

- All Traits: 2 (baseline).

- Family bonus Trait: +1 (e.g. Hida family = +1 Stamina → Stamina 3, Earth Ring = 2.5 rounded to 2 for base, but Stamina tracks as 3).

- School benefit Trait: +1 (e.g. Hida Bushi school benefit = +1 Stamina, stacks with family → Stamina 4 if both apply to same Trait, or separate Traits each at 3).

- Note: Family bonus and school benefit are listed separately in Section 29. When they apply to the same Trait, the character has a notable strength in that area.

Ring value ranges by Insight Rank (expected values, before variance):

- Rank 1: Primary Rings 2–3, secondary Rings 2. Total Ring sum typically 11–13.

- Rank 2: Primary Rings 3, secondary Rings 2–3. Total Ring sum typically 13–15.

- Rank 3: Primary Rings 3–4, secondary Rings 2–3. Total Ring sum typically 15–17.

- Rank 4: Primary Rings 4, secondary Rings 3. Total Ring sum typically 17–19.

- Rank 5: Primary Rings 4–5, secondary Rings 3–4. Total Ring sum typically 19–22.

Primary Rings are those associated with the school's focus — Earth and Fire for Hida Bushi, Air and Water for Doji Courtier, Fire and Air for Isawa Shugenja. Secondary Rings advance more slowly. Void advances at half the rate of primary Rings for non-shugenja schools, and at primary Ring rate for shugenja schools.

**STEP 3 — SKILLS**

Skills are assigned from the school's listed starting skills at rank 1. Additional skill ranks are added based on Insight Rank — each Rank above 1 adds approximately 8–12 skill ranks distributed across school skills and secondary skills.

- Rank 1: School skills at rank 1. One school skill may be at rank 2 (the character's specialty).

- Rank 2: School skills average rank 2. One or two at rank 3. 2–3 non-school skills at rank 1.

- Rank 3: School skills average rank 3. Specialty skill at rank 4. 4–6 non-school skills at rank 1–2.

- Rank 4: School skills average rank 3–4. Specialty skill at rank 4–5. 6–8 non-school skills at rank 1–3.

- Rank 5: School skills average rank 4. Specialty skill at rank 5. 8–10 non-school skills at rank 2–4.

Non-school skills reflect the character's personal history and role. A Hida Bushi assigned to a river garrison may have Sailing at rank 2. A Doji Courtier who has served at Winter Court multiple times may have Tea Ceremony at rank 3. A small amount of random variance in non-school skills makes each generated character distinct.

**STEP 4 — TECHNIQUES**

Characters know all Techniques up to and including their current School Rank.

- Rank 1: School Rank 1 Technique.

- Rank 2: School Rank 1 and Rank 2 Techniques.

- Rank 3: School Rank 1 through Rank 3 Techniques.

- And so on.

Shugenja characters instead gain Spell Mastery Level access equal to their School Rank, and know spells as defined in Section 48 — 3 new spells per Rank advance. Starting spell pool at generation is filled from the school's elemental affinity, weighted toward lower Mastery Levels at lower Ranks.

**STEP 5 — PERSONALITY**

One Bushido virtue and one Shourido virtue are assigned from a weighted random selection. Weights are clan-specific, reflecting cultural tendencies without hard-locking any combination.

Bushido virtue weights by clan:

- Crab: Yu (Courage) and Chugi (Duty) heavily weighted. Jin (Compassion) and Rei (Courtesy) rare.

- Crane: Rei (Courtesy) and Gi (Honesty) heavily weighted. Yu (Courage) and Makoto (Sincerity) moderate.

- Dragon: Makoto (Sincerity) and Meiyo (Honor) heavily weighted. All others moderate.

- Lion: Yu (Courage) and Chugi (Duty) heavily weighted. Jin (Compassion) rare.

- Mantis: Yu (Courage) and Gi (Honesty) weighted. Rei (Courtesy) rare.

- Phoenix: Jin (Compassion) and Meiyo (Honor) heavily weighted. Yu (Courage) rare.

- Scorpion: All Bushido virtues equally rare — Scorpion publicly perform Bushido rather than embodying it. The few who genuinely hold a virtue are unusual.

- Unicorn: Yu (Courage) and Makoto (Sincerity) heavily weighted. Rei (Courtesy) moderate.

Shourido virtue weights by clan:

- Crab: Ketsui (Determination) and Kyōryōku (Strength) heavily weighted.

- Crane: Kanpeki (Perfection) and Dōsatsu (Insight) heavily weighted.

- Dragon: Chishiki (Knowledge) and Dōsatsu (Insight) heavily weighted.

- Lion: Kyōryōku (Strength) and Ketsui (Determination) heavily weighted.

- Mantis: Seigyo (Control) and Kyōryōku (Strength) heavily weighted.

- Phoenix: Kanpeki (Perfection) and Chishiki (Knowledge) heavily weighted.

- Scorpion: Seigyo (Control) and Dōsatsu (Insight) heavily weighted.

- Unicorn: Ishi (Will) and Kyōryōku (Strength) heavily weighted.

**STEP 6 — EQUIPMENT**

Equipment is assigned directly from the school's listed Outfit (Section 29). Higher Rank characters may have upgraded equipment — quality tier of weapons and armor scales with Rank.

- Rank 1–2: Normal quality equipment. Standard outfit as listed.

- Rank 3: Fine quality for at least one primary weapon or armor piece.

- Rank 4: Exceptional quality for primary weapon or armor.

- Rank 5+: Masterwork quality possible. May carry a named item with provenance.

Koku on hand at generation is calculated as: (1 month's stipend for their role) + 1d10 × Rank in additional savings. This reflects accumulated resources over a career.

**AGE CALCULATION**

A character's age is derived from their Insight Rank, reflecting how long it takes to advance through the progression system (Section 48). These are approximate ranges — variance of ±3 years is applied at generation to make characters feel distinct.

- Rank 1: Age 15–20. Just past gempuku.

- Rank 2: Age 18–28. A few years of active service.

- Rank 3: Age 23–35. An established samurai of some experience.

- Rank 4: Age 30–45. A senior samurai with a real career behind them.

- Rank 5: Age 38–55. A master-level figure. Has survived decades of active service.

- Rank 6+: Age 45+. Elder or exceptional. Their survival to this Rank is itself remarkable.

Age is purely narrative. It affects how other characters perceive and address the character, and feeds into the family web generation (parents' approximate ages, whether grandparents are living), but carries no mechanical bonuses or penalties.

**VARIANCE RULES**

Every generated character has small random variance applied after base values are set, ensuring that two characters from the same template are similar but never identical.

- Trait variance: ±1 on up to two non-primary Traits. No Trait may drop below 2 or rise above the Rank's expected ceiling.

- Skill variance: 2–4 non-school skills receive random ranks (0–2) reflecting personal interests and incidental training. These are in addition to the structured skill allocation above.

- Honor variance: ±0.5 on starting Honor. Reflects individual character within the school's general honor culture.

- Age variance: ±3 IC years from the Rank's base age range.

- Personality: Fully random within weighted pools — no two characters are predetermined to have identical virtue combinations even within the same school.

- Advantages/Disadvantages: 1–2 minor Advantages or Disadvantages assigned randomly, weighted by clan and school flavor. A Kuni Witch Hunter is more likely to have Lore-related Advantages. A Doji Courtier is more likely to have social Advantages. These are minor only — significant Advantages and Disadvantages are reserved for pre-written characters.

**WORKED EXAMPLE — LION MATSU BUSHI RANK 3**

Template input: Clan = Lion, Family = Matsu, School = Matsu Berserker, Insight Rank = 3.

- Identity: Generated Lion name. Age: 26 (Rank 3 base range 23–35, variance applied).

- Family bonus: +1 Strength (Matsu family). Strength = 3.

- School benefit: +1 Strength (Matsu Berserker). Strength = 4. Water Ring primary = 3.

- Starting Honor: 3.5 (Matsu Berserker school value). Glory: 2.0 (Rank 3 baseline).

- Rings: Earth 3, Fire 3, Water 3 (Strength 4, Perception 2), Air 2, Void 2.

- School skills: Battle 3, Defense 3, Kenjutsu 4 (specialty), Lore: History 2, Etiquette 2, Iaijutsu 2, Athletics 2.

- Non-school skills: Intimidation 2, Hunting 1, Jiujutsu 1.

- Techniques: Rank 1 — The Way of the Lion. Rank 2 — Strength of Purity. Rank 3 — Matsu Technique (whichever is appropriate).

- Personality: Bushido = Yu (Courage) — heavily weighted for Lion. Shourido = Kyōryōku (Strength) — heavily weighted. Decision profile: charges first, never retreats, low hostage likelihood.

- Equipment: Fine katana (Rank 3 quality upgrade), heavy armor, standard Lion outfit, 18 koku on hand.

- Disposition: Lion family baselines applied. Crane = −10, Unicorn = −5, Scorpion = −15 (historical modifiers). All others neutral.
