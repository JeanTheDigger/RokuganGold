# 42. Shadowlands Taint

The Shadowlands Taint is a manifestation of Jigoku’s corruptive influence on the mortal world. It functions as a disease, spreading on contact, but corrupts the soul as readily as the body. The Tainted slowly develop strange mutations, unnatural strength, unholy powers, and violent malice as the Taint subverts their thoughts and dreams. Corpses suffused with the Taint return as undead abominations.

**TAINT MECHANICS:**

- Taint is measured in Ranks and Points. 10 Points = 1 Rank.

- Exposure: Whenever a character is exposed to Taint from an outside source, make an Earth roll. TN ranges from 10 (simple contact) to 30 (swallowed alive by an oni). Failure = 1 Point of Taint.

- Monthly/Periodic rolls: Once per month (Rank 0–1), twice per month (Rank 2), once per week (Rank 3), once per day (Rank 4). TN = 5 at Rank 0, +5 per Rank thereafter. Failure = 1 Point of Taint.

- Jade Petal Tea: +2k2 on periodic Taint resistance rolls.

- Spiritual Purity: Living a life of spiritual purity grants +1k0 to +3k0 on such rolls.

- Embracing the Taint: A character may always add +1k1 to any physical roll in exchange for immediately gaining 1 Point of Taint. This cannot be prevented.

**SHADOWLANDS POWERS:**

- Minor Shadowlands Powers: Usable by the least powerful Tainted individuals. Using them forces an Earth Taint roll (TN 15) or gain 1 additional Point of Taint.

- Major Shadowlands Powers: Usable by the most powerful Tainted individuals. Using them forces an Earth Taint roll (TN 20) or gain 1 additional Point of Taint.

- Akutenshi Powers: Usable only by Akutenshi and exceedingly rare others. Otherwise treated as Major Shadowlands Powers.

- Mutations: The result of gaining Ranks in Taint. Represent difformities that must be concealed.

**TAINT RANKS:**

**Rank 0 — Seeds of Darkness (less than 10 Points)**

Not treated as actually Tainted. Spells targeting Tainted individuals do not register against them. At worst, occasional nightmares.

**Rank 1 — Passive Infection**

Recognized as Tainted by specific spells and abilities, but otherwise functions as normal. Occasional nightmares; may feel sick or tired.

**Rank 2 — Active Infection**

Experiences painful symptoms: nightmares, nausea and vomiting, muscle tremors, and mild hallucinations. Develops one Shadowlands Power (most often Minor).

**Rank 3 — Consuming**

Becomes paranoid and transformed. -1k0 to all Social Skill rolls. Develops one additional Shadowlands Power (Minor or Major) and one physical mutation.

**Rank 4 — Deadly**

Barely functions in normal society. Haunted by madness; obsessed with blood, death, and flesh. -2k0 to all Social Skill rolls. In stressful situations must make Willpower rolls (TN 15) or resort to violence. Maximum Void Points reduced by 1. Develops two Shadowlands Powers (at least one Major) and one additional physical mutation. If discovered, may be forced to commit seppuku or be killed as an enemy of the Empire.

**Rank 5 — The Lost**

A character with 5 Ranks of Taint becomes Lost unless exceptional (e.g. one Ring at Rank 6 or higher, in which case they hold on until 7 Ranks). Most often, the character is disintegrated, or becomes permanently mad as it is claimed by the Shadowlands — all mental Traits reduced to 1, treated as a creature under GM control.

Some survive as the Lost (Akutsukai): no longer suffer penalties from having Taint; gain 0–3 additional mutations and 0–3 additional Shadowlands Powers; add Taint Rank to all physical rolls; cannot embrace the Taint. Lost characters are in fact entirely driven by Jigoku — completely evil, without self-will, retaining only a twisted remnant of their former personality.

Akutenshi are truly self-willed abominations — rarer still. Claimed to bear the blood of Fu Leng or to be powerful Lost imbued with Jigoku’s power directly. Akutenshi exhibit a high level of free will and personality. They always have at least three Minor and three Major Shadowlands Powers, and between one and four additional Akutenshi-exclusive abilities.
