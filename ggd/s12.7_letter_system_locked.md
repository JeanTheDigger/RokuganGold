## 12.7 Letter System — LOCKED

The Letter System is the mechanism by which characters communicate across distance. Where the Daily Conversation System handles organic local interaction between characters who share a settlement, the Letter System handles deliberate long-distance communication across Rokugan. A letter is a one-way transmission of information — a character chooses to write to another character, selects what to communicate, and sends it. The recipient reads it when it arrives. The sender does not know when or if the letter has been received until a reply comes.

**SENDING A LETTER**

Letter allowance has two tiers depending on whether the character holds a lordship. Non-lord characters (courtiers, bushi, shugenja, monks, ronin — anyone without a lordship title): 1 free letter per IC day at no cost, representing the baseline correspondence that samurai conduct as part of daily life. Choosing who to write is itself a meaningful decision. If a non-lord character needs to write more than 1 letter in an IC day, they may spend 1 AP to write a batch of 4 additional letters that IC day. This represents sitting down for a dedicated writing session — grinding ink, composing multiple messages, sealing and dispatching them. A non-lord character may only spend AP on letters once per IC day, giving a maximum of 5 letters per IC day (1 free + 4 paid). Across a full OOC day (4 IC days), a non-lord character who dedicates 1 AP per IC day to letters can write up to 20 letters (4 free + 16 paid), burning 4 of their 8 AP on correspondence. This is the “dedicated correspondent” pattern — a Doji courtier assigned as correspondence secretary for their lord, or an Otomo operative running a sustained letter campaign. Lord characters (City Daimyo and above): all letters draw from the Civilian Order pool (Section 57.34). Each letter costs 1 Civilian Order. No free letter — the pool IS the lord’s letter budget, shared with verbal directives. No per-IC-day cap — a lord with 8 remaining civilian orders could write all 8 as letters in a single IC day if they chose. See Section 57.34 for full details.

A letter may be sent to any named character anywhere in Rokugan, regardless of disposition. Unlike daily conversation, which requires mutual acquaintance, a letter can be sent to a stranger, a rival, or an enemy. The sender chooses one topic from their active topic pool to include in the letter. The letter carries that topic to the recipient.

**LETTER QUALITY — CALLIGRAPHY ROLL**

A letter is not merely a vessel for information — in Rokugan, the physical act of writing is itself an expression of the writer's soul. The quality of brushwork, the elegance of composition, and the emotional resonance of the letter's form can deepen the recipient's regard for the sender. When writing a letter, the sender may make a Calligraphy roll paired with a chosen Trait to reflect the mood and intent of the letter.

The roll is: Calligraphy + chosen Trait vs TN 15. This roll is free — it does not cost Action Points. It is resolved at the moment of writing.

Trait choices and their meaning:

- Calligraphy + Awareness: A socially perceptive letter — the writer demonstrates understanding of the recipient's feelings and situation. Warm, attuned, diplomatic.

- Calligraphy + Intelligence: A clever, well-argued letter — the writer presents reasoning and insight with precision. Sharp, intellectual, persuasive.

- Calligraphy + Agility: A fluid, graceful letter — the brushwork itself is a statement of elegance. The form is as meaningful as the content. Refined, artistic, beautiful.

The writer chooses which Trait to use based on what they wish to convey. A letter of condolence calls for Awareness. A political argument calls for Intelligence. A letter to a Crane courtier may call for Agility to impress through sheer elegance of form.

Letter quality results:

- Failure (below TN 15): No bonus. The letter is serviceable but unremarkable. Standard delivery.

- Success (TN 15): +1 disposition granted to the recipient upon delivery.

- 1 Raise (TN 20): +2 disposition granted to the recipient upon delivery.

- 2 Raises (TN 25): +3 disposition granted to the recipient upon delivery. Maximum bonus — no further Raises improve the effect.

The disposition bonus is applied when the letter arrives, not when it is sent. The delay is geographic. A beautifully written letter sent to a distant province grants its bonus when it reaches the recipient's hands. The bonus stacks with the +1 disposition from a completed reply exchange. However, the total daily disposition gain from correspondence remains deliberately small — a letter is a letter, not a personal visit.

**DELIVERY TIME**

Letters do not arrive instantly. Delivery time is determined by the geographic distance between sender and recipient, computed by pathfinding across the World Map province structure. The courier network uses the Imperial road system and Miya relay stations (Section 2.1.19) to move letters at a base rate of 3 provinces per IC day — significantly faster than army movement (1 sub-tile per real day per Section 57.34) because couriers are single riders on horseback carrying nothing but the letter. Formula: delivery_time_ic_days = max(0, ceil(province_distance / 3) + mountain_provinces_crossed + warzone_provinces_crossed). Same province: 0 IC days (local delivery, arrives same day). Adjacent province: 1 IC day. 3 provinces: 1 IC day. 6 provinces: 2 IC days. 10 provinces: 3–4 IC days. Across Rokugan (~20 provinces): 7 IC days. Mountain province modifier: each mountain province on the route adds +1 IC day (steep terrain, narrow passes, no relay stations). This makes Dragon Clan territory deliberately slow to reach by letter — consistent with their geographic isolation (Section 2.2 Dragon Clan). Warzone modifier: each province on the route with active War Status (Section 53) adds +1 IC day (disrupted roads, checkpoint delays, courier risk). Miya Herald network: letters sent through the Miya courier system (available to characters with Miya family connections or Imperial authority) receive a −1 IC day reduction (minimum 0). The Miya maintain relay stations across Rokugan and their couriers have priority passage through all checkpoints. All timing values PROVISIONAL pending playtesting.

During transit, the letter exists in the world. It has been written and dispatched. The sender’s topic has been committed. If the sender’s opinion on the topic changes before the letter arrives, the letter still carries the original content — letters cannot be recalled.

Ocean letter delivery: on land, letters travel by courier network at a fixed rate based on province distance. When a letter’s route must cross ocean sub-tiles, the transport mechanism is ship traffic rather than overland couriers. The letter queues at the sender’s port and travels aboard the next ship crossing to the destination side. Major trade routes (Mantis Islands to mainland, inter-island routes) have regular ship traffic — under normal conditions, a ship crosses every few IC days, so ocean letters experience a small additional delay (the wait for the next crossing plus the voyage time) but reliably reach their destination. From the destination port, the letter continues overland by courier to the recipient’s location at the standard land delivery rate. Under crisis conditions — naval blockade, wartime disruption, hostile standing orders refusing a specific clan’s cargo — ocean letter delivery can be significantly delayed or blocked entirely. A clan whose ships are barred from Mantis ports cannot send letters to the Mantis Islands through normal channels. A character stranded on an island with no friendly ship traffic is truly isolated — their letters sit at the port with no vessel to carry them. This is a deliberate political consequence, not a system failure. Workarounds exist through the same channels available to stranded characters: smuggling a letter aboard a neutral vessel (a covert action), finding a Tortoise ship (the Kasuga operate independently), or sending a message through the Kolat Silk network (if available). Ocean crossing timing: each ocean segment on the route adds +2 IC days to delivery time (1 IC day waiting for the next ship + 1 IC day voyage). A letter from the mainland to the Mantis Islands crosses one ocean segment: +2 IC days on top of the overland distance to the port. Under blockade conditions (Section 53 War Status with naval component), ocean delivery is blocked entirely — the letter sits at the sending port indefinitely until the blockade lifts or a workaround is used. Full formula including ocean: delivery_time_ic_days = max(0, ceil(province_distance / 3) + ocean_segments × 2 + mountain_provinces + warzone_provinces − miya_bonus). All timing values PROVISIONAL pending playtesting.

**RECEIVING A LETTER**

When a letter arrives, the topic it carries is transferred to the recipient exactly as in a daily conversation topic exchange. If the recipient already knows the topic, the letter refreshes its momentum in their pool. If the recipient does not know the topic, it enters their pool at a relevance level shaped by their personality and relationship to the subject.

Receiving a letter does not grant a disposition bonus by itself. The relationship effect of correspondence comes through the reply system.

Deliberate examination: a character who suspects a received letter may be forged can spend 1 AP to deliberately examine it. The same reference precondition applies as for automatic detection — the examiner must have previously received at least one authentic letter from the apparent sender to have a basis for comparison. Without prior correspondence from the apparent sender, deliberate examination cannot detect the forgery (the AP is spent and the character finds nothing suspicious because they have no reference). With prior correspondence, roll Investigation / Perception vs the Forgery TN that was set when the letter was created (Section 12.8, FORGE_IMPERSONATION_LETTER and FORGE_ORDER). This is a separate check from the automatic silent detection that fires on receipt — the automatic check represents a casual first impression, the deliberate check represents sitting down with the document, studying the seal under lamplight, comparing brushstrokes to known samples. The Rank 5 Forgery mastery bonus (+1k0 to detect forgeries) applies to deliberate examination. If the letter is authentic (not forged), the AP is spent and the examination confirms the letter is genuine — no wasted AP notification, the character simply finds nothing suspicious. This action can be applied to any letter, not only those suspected of being forged.

**REPLIES**

Upon receiving a letter, the recipient has a chance to reply. The reply chance is influenced by the recipient's disposition toward the sender and their personality profile — a character with high Courtesy is more likely to reply than one who is dismissive of correspondence. A character with high disposition toward the sender is more likely to reply than one who is indifferent.

If a reply is generated, it functions as a new letter sent from the recipient back to the original sender. The recipient selects a topic from their own pool to include — it may or may not be the same topic that was sent to them. A reply that discusses the same topic represents an ongoing exchange of views on that matter. A reply that introduces a different topic represents the natural drift of correspondence — the recipient acknowledged the letter and shared something of their own in return.

A reply that arrives constitutes a completed exchange. Both sender and recipient gain +1 disposition — the equivalent of a successful daily conversation — representing the social warmth of maintained correspondence. Characters who write to each other regularly and receive replies slowly build their relationship across distance.

**LETTERS AND NEWS PROPAGATION**

The Letter System is the primary mechanism by which Tier 4 Topics — and Tier 1–3 crisis topics — spread across Rokugan beyond the local settlement level. A character in the Crab lands who witnesses a Shadowlands incursion writes to their contacts in the Crane and Lion. Those contacts receive the topic and may write to their own contacts. Within days or weeks, a topic that began in one corner of the Empire is known across multiple clans — not because anyone announced it formally, but because people wrote letters.

The speed and reach of news propagation is therefore a function of how many connections a topic's original witnesses have, how high their disposition values are toward those contacts (determining reply rates and continued forwarding), and the geographic distances involved. A topic held by a well-connected courtier with friends across multiple clans spreads faster and farther than the same topic held by an isolated provincial samurai.

**STRONGLY OPINIONATED TOPICS IN LETTERS**

Opinion Shifting via Letters — RESOLVED. The same resolution as Section 12.6 applies. When a letter delivers a topic to the recipient, their starting position on that topic is calculated using the Section 15.5 formula. The sender’s position does not directly transfer. The sender’s influence on the recipient is carried through their existing disposition value — a high-disposition sender passes topics through the information sharing threshold more freely, and their disposition feeds into anchor calculations. Letters carry slightly less weight than face-to-face conversation because disposition gains from correspondence (Section 12.7 reply system, +1 per completed exchange) accumulate more slowly than cohabitation and direct court action. No separate mechanic required.

**DESIGN NOTES**

- The one-letter-per-IC-day limit means players must choose who to write to. Writing to many people requires many IC days.

- NPC characters also send letters automatically, based on their disposition values, topic pools, and personality profiles. The letter system runs as a background simulation — the world is constantly corresponding with itself.

- Player characters receive letter notifications when a letter arrives. They see the topic that was shared and who sent it.

- The Game of Letters skill (Awareness) referenced in Section 24 is directly relevant here — skilled correspondents write more persuasively and may have mechanical advantages in topic framing and reply rates.

- Letter interception, forgery, and manipulation are fully designed in Section 12.8 (Secret System).
