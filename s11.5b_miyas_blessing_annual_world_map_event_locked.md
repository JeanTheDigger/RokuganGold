## 11.5b Miya’s Blessing — Annual World Map Event — LOCKED

**Cross-references:** Section 2.1.17 (Imperial Herald), Section 2.1.19 (Miya Family), Section 4.3.5 (Seasonal Event Sequence), Section 4.3.6 (Population Dynamics), Section 4.3.7 (Tax Cascade), Section 11.5 (Festival System), Section 27.10 (Miya family entry), Section 29.8 (Miya Herald school), Section 55.10.1 (Emperor Personality Archetypes).

**Source:** *Secrets of the Empire*, pp. 123–125 (Miya’s Blessing, Anou vassal family).

### 1. Core Concept

Miya’s Blessing is an annual charitable aid program administered by the Miya family under the authority of the Imperial Herald (Section 2.1.17). Each year, a portion of the Emperor’s Rice stockpile is earmarked for the Blessing. The Miya use this allocation to dispatch food, craftsmen (drawn primarily from the Anou vassal family), and supplies to three provinces they judge to be most urgently in need. This is not commerce and not politics — the Miya view it solely as duty. The Blessing is the single most reliable external aid mechanism in the Empire and a major reason the Miya are the most beloved of the Imperial families.

Mechanically, this is a once-per-year automated World Map event that withdraws Rice from the Emperor’s settlement stockpile at Otosan Uchi and deposits it into three target provinces. The Blessing is grounded in the real economy: good harvests across the Empire produce a larger imperial tax take, a bigger stockpile, and a more generous Blessing. Empire-wide famine or war depletes the stockpile, shrinks or cancels the Blessing, and leaves vulnerable provinces without relief — creating a visible feedback loop between Imperial health and provincial welfare.

### 2. Funding — The Emperor’s Stockpile

The Blessing draws Rice exclusively from the **Emperor’s settlement stockpile** at Otosan Uchi (or the current Imperial capital). No Rice is created — every unit delivered to a province is one fewer unit available for Otosan Uchi’s population, the Imperial Legions, and the Emperor’s emergency reserves.

**2.1 Allocation Calculation:**

Blessing Allocation = Emperor’s Rice income from the previous Autumn’s tax cascade x Blessing Rate.

The Emperor’s Rice income is the total Rice the Emperor collected at the top of the tax cascade (Section 4.3.7) in the most recent Autumn. This is the 15% of whatever reached the Emperor tier, aggregated across every Clan Champion in the Empire. The Blessing Rate is the percentage of that income earmarked for the Blessing. The default rate is 15%, reflecting the lore that the Miya set aside “at least fifteen percent.” This rate is modified by the Emperor’s personality archetype (see Section 5 below).

Example: The Emperor collected 12.0 Rice from the previous Autumn’s tax cascade. At the default 15% Blessing Rate, the allocation is 1.80 Rice, divided among three provinces = 0.60 Rice per province.

**2.2 Stockpile Constraint:**

The allocation cannot exceed what is physically present in the Emperor’s stockpile at the moment the Blessing tick fires (start of Spring). If the Emperor’s stockpile has been drawn down by Winter consumption, military supply, or other spending, the Blessing receives only what remains after a minimum Imperial reserve is protected.

Minimum Imperial Reserve: The Emperor’s stockpile must retain at least enough Rice to feed Otosan Uchi’s PU for one season (PU x 0.25) after the Blessing withdrawal. The Blessing cannot starve the capital. If the calculated allocation would drop the stockpile below this floor, the allocation is reduced to whatever amount keeps the stockpile at exactly the floor.

**2.3 Minimum Threshold:**

If the final allocation (after the stockpile constraint) is below 0.50 Rice total (meaning less than ~0.17 Rice per province), the Blessing does not fire that year. When the Blessing fails to fire, a Tier 4 topic is generated: “Miya’s Blessing Suspended — Imperial Reserves Insufficient.” This topic carries political consequences (see Section 7).

**2.4 Maximum Ceiling:**

The allocation per province is capped at 5.0 Rice. Total maximum Blessing expenditure: 15.0 Rice per year (5.0 x 3 provinces). Excess remains in the Emperor’s stockpile.

**2.5 The Transfer:**

The allocated Rice is physically removed from the Emperor’s stockpile and deposited into the settlement stockpiles of the three selected provinces, distributed proportionally by settlement PU within each province (larger settlements receive more). This Rice enters the existing system seamlessly — it is subject to normal consumption, starvation checks, and tax cascade in subsequent Autumns.

### 3. Timing

Miya’s Blessing fires once per year at the start of Spring, immediately after the Planting tick and before the first monthly event cycle of the season.

Insertion into the Full Seasonal Event Sequence (Section 4.3.5 Addendum): SPRING ONLY — fires at season start, in the following order: (1) Planting tick (existing). (2) Miya’s Blessing tick (NEW — calculates allocation from Emperor’s stockpile, selects three provinces, withdraws Rice, applies benefits). (3) Seasonal events fire (Rice consumption, starvation check, population adjustment, Iron production, Koku generation).

The Blessing fires before the Spring Rice consumption tick. This is deliberate: the injected Rice is available to absorb the Spring consumption draw, potentially pulling a settlement out of Shortage before the starvation check fires.

### 4. Province Selection — The Need Score

Each year, every province in the Empire receives a Need Score. The three provinces with the highest Need Scores are selected to receive the Blessing. Ties are broken by lowest current Stability (most unstable wins). If still tied, the province with the smaller population wins.

**4.1 Need Score Calculation:**

Province has any settlement at Shortage: +5. At Hunger: +10. At Famine: +20. Stability 51–75 (Restless): +2. Stability 26–50 (Volatile): +5. Stability 0–25 (Broken): +10. Province had Active War Status in previous year: +5. Province experienced a successful raid in previous year: +3. Active insurgency present: +3. Province PU declined by 10%+ over previous year: +5. Province PU declined by 25%+: +10 (replaces the +5). Province did not receive Blessing in previous year: +2. Province received Blessing in immediately preceding year: -5 (rotation malus).

**4.2 Exclusions:**

Shadowlands provinces (Taint Level above the Maho Cult spawn threshold): Excluded. Provinces in active rebellion against the Emperor: Excluded.

**4.3 Winter Court Influence:**

During Winter Court (Section 15.1), clan representatives may lobby for specific provinces to receive the Blessing. This is modeled as a Tier 4 Action Topic: Miya’s Blessing Petition. Any character with Status 3.0+ may raise a petition — Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness roll, TN 25. Success adds +8 Need Score to the petitioned province. Each Raise adds +2 more. The Miya daimyo (or representative) must be present. Only one petition per province per Winter Court.

### 5. Emperor Personality Archetype Interaction

Benevolent (Jin): Blessing Rate 20%. The most generous Blessing. Compounds with reduced tax rate (-5%). Iron (Chugi/Meiyo): Blessing Rate 15%. Default — fulfilled precisely as tradition demands. Cunning (Seigyo/Dosatsu): Blessing Rate 10%. Views the Blessing as a political tool. May add +10 Need Score to favored clan’s provinces and -10 to disfavored clan’s provinces. Miya disposition penalty: -2/year toward Emperor (hidden). Warlike (Yu/Kyoryoku): Blessing Rate 5%. Military eats first. Token gesture only. Tyrant (Seigyo/Ishi): Blessing Rate 0%. Blessing suspended entirely. Permanent Tier 4 topic: “Miya’s Blessing Denied by Imperial Order.” Contributes to Tyrant’s Empire-wide -2 Stability/season penalty.

### 6. Benefits Applied

**6.1 Rice Injection:** The province’s share of the allocation (total / 3) is deposited into its settlement stockpiles, distributed proportionally by settlement PU.

**6.2 Stability Bonus:** Each selected province receives an immediate +5 Stability. One-time injection, stacks with normal recovery bonuses.

**6.3 Population Recovery Boost:** Each selected province receives a one-season population growth bonus of +1% (additive, stacking with normal growth rate from Section 4.3.6). Represents the Anou vassal family craftsmen dispatched to rebuild infrastructure. Applies only for the Spring season of delivery.

### 7. Notification, Reputation, and Political Effects

**7.1 Blessing Delivered:** Tier 4 topic generated for each selected province. Miya family disposition: +2 from lord of each selected province and all lords within the same clan. Emperor disposition: +1 from lord of each selected province.

**7.2 Blessing Suspended:** Miya disposition toward Emperor: -3. Empire-wide disposition toward Emperor: -1 from every Clan Champion. All provinces with Need Score > 0 receive -1 Stability. Consecutive suspension (2+ years): penalties double. Three or more consecutive years: Miya daimyo raises formal grievance at Winter Court (Tier 3 Action Topic).

**7.3 Player Notification:** If the player controls or holds authority over a province that receives the Blessing, they receive a notification with exact Rice amount and Stability bonus. If the Blessing is suspended, notification explains why (insufficient reserves or Tyrant denial).

### 8. AI Interaction

**8.1 Miya NPC Behavior:** Miya NPCs treat the Blessing as a standing objective. During Autumn and Winter, they prioritize gathering information about provincial conditions. They do not reveal selections before Spring.

**8.2 Lord AI Response:** AI lords whose provinces receive the Blessing gain disposition boosts and are more likely to support Miya-aligned court positions. Lords whose provinces were not selected may raise the topic at Winter Court as a grievance.

**8.3 Emperor AI Interaction:** The allocation fires automatically based on Blessing Rate. The Emperor AI’s other spending decisions draw from the same stockpile and may inadvertently reduce the Blessing. This is an emergent consequence with real political fallout.

### 9. Edge Cases

Miya family destroyed: Blessing ceases. No other family takes over automatically. Imperial Herald position vacant: Blessing still fires — Miya family administers collectively. All provinces at Stable (76–100): Blessing still fires, targets three lowest-Stability provinces. Fewer than three eligible provinces: Blessing delivers to however many remain; unallocated Rice stays in Emperor’s stockpile. Player is Miya daimyo: May manually select the three provinces. Cannot modify Blessing Rate, exceed ceiling, or withdraw more than allocation. Player is Emperor: May adjust Blessing Rate within archetype range +/-5%. Imperial capital relocated: Blessing draws from current capital’s stockpile. Emperor’s stockpile at zero: Blessing cannot fire.

### 10. Design Notes

**10.1 Why the Emperor’s Stockpile:** The Blessing must cost something real. Prosperous Empire leads to generous Blessing leads to stable provinces leads to prosperous Empire (virtuous cycle). War-torn Empire leads to depleted stockpile leads to weak Blessing leads to unstable provinces (vicious cycle). Tyrant Emperor leads to no Blessing leads to mounting instability leads to breaking point.

**10.2 Miya Family Relevance:** The Seppun matter through the assassination TN system. The Otomo matter through court manipulation and the Seiyaku System (Section 55.22b). The Miya matter through the Blessing and the herald communication network. All three non-dynastic Imperial families have a visible mechanical footprint.

**10.3 Interaction with Existing Systems:** No new resource types, stockpile pools, or cascade mechanics. Uses existing Rice stockpile, Stability scale, population growth system, and topic/disposition systems. Implementation complexity is low relative to impact.
