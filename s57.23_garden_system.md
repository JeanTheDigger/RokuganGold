## 57.23 Garden System

### 57.23.1 Overview

A garden is a persistent world object installed in an eligible lesser zone by a character with Artisan: Gardening. Unlike crafted items, a garden cannot be carried or gifted — it belongs to the zone it occupies. Gardens are commissioned through REQUEST_ART (Daimyo initiates) or OFFER_ART_COMMISSION (gardener initiates), which establishes the garden_permission prerequisite before cultivation can begin. Once installed through CULTIVATE_GARDEN, any named character entering the zone automatically receives a small disposition bonus toward the gardener. The gardener accumulates Glory as visitors experience the space. Seasonal maintenance by the original creator is required to preserve it. Artisan: Gardening produces two distinct outputs: gardens (this section) and bonkei — portable miniature tray landscapes produced through the standard CRAFT action (Section 49, item entry in Section 50).

### 57.23.2 Zone Eligibility and Permission

Gardens may only be installed in two lesser zone subtypes. CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD: the open grounds between a castle’s outer walls and the keep itself — the first impression of the lord’s domain, walked through by arriving guests and visible from the ramparts. TSUBONIWA: the enclosed inner courtyard garden bordered on all sides by the residence’s corridors and viewed from the engawa. Guests in formal rooms look out onto it. Full zone subtype vocabulary is defined in the Section 4.3.4 Navigation Zone amendment (v564).

Before CULTIVATE_GARDEN becomes available in any zone, a garden_permission flag must be set for that character in that zone. Permission is established through REQUEST_ART acceptance (Section 57.23.3) or OFFER_ART_COMMISSION acceptance (Section 57.23.4). The flag is character-specific and zone-specific. One garden may exist per zone at any time — CULTIVATE_GARDEN is blocked while a garden_object already exists on the zone. Permission expiry on destruction: when a garden_object is destroyed for any reason (degradation, forced removal, voluntary removal), all garden_permission flags for that zone_id are automatically cleared for all characters. Any future cultivation requires fresh permission through REQUEST_ART or OFFER_ART_COMMISSION. This prevents stale permission from persisting after a commission relationship has ended and ensures the zone commitment check operates cleanly on a fresh state.

Permission lapse: if the Daimyo who granted garden_permission dies or loses control of the zone, the flag remains valid for one IC season (grace period). During the grace period CULTIVATE_GARDEN remains available. After the grace period expires garden_permission lapses and the gardener must re-negotiate with the new zone lord through OFFER_ART_COMMISSION. A garden completed within the grace period stands permanently under Section 57.23.9. The commission was a personal relationship — the new lord inherits the zone but not the agreement.

### 57.23.3 REQUEST_ART

ActionID: REQUEST_ART. Category 14 (Administration and Court Management). Lord-tier. Cost: 1 Civilian Order (not AP — per Section 57.34). No roll. Context: AT_COURT, AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, or delivered via WRITE_LETTER to a named artisan.

Two modes controlled by the art_type parameter. Open (art_type: open): a broadcast declaration of cultural patronage. The Daimyo announces they are seeking artistic work — any artisan NPC with a relevant Artisan or Craft skill who receives the topic is incited toward OFFER_ART_COMMISSION. Garden-specific (art_type: garden, zone_id: [specific CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD or TSUBONIWA in Daimyo holdings]): the Daimyo explicitly wants a garden cultivated in a named zone. Only characters with Artisan: Gardening are incited. On acceptance by the gardener, garden_permission is automatically set — the commission IS the permission, no separate negotiation required.

Topic generated: “[Daimyo name] is seeking [art type / artistic work] at [zone name / holdings].” Tier 4 topic. Propagates through the standard topic system. By letter: REQUEST_ART delivered via WRITE_LETTER contains the topic as its payload directed to a specific artisan; topic propagation is contained to the recipient rather than broadcast. Vassal case: if the target artisan is the Daimyo’s own vassal, ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE handles the commission as a direct order. REQUEST_ART is for non-vassals and open broadcasts.

### 57.23.4 OFFER_ART_COMMISSION

ActionID: OFFER_ART_COMMISSION. Category 1 (Court Actions — Raise Disposition, Targeted). Cost: 1 AP. No roll. Context: AT_COURT, public VISITING, or delivered via WRITE_LETTER. The artisan proposes to create a specific work for a specific Daimyo. Parameters: art_form, artisan_id, daimyo_id. For garden offers, zone_id must additionally be specified — a CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD or TSUBONIWA in the target Daimyo’s holdings. The artisan must have Artisan: Gardening for garden offers.

Incited by a REQUEST_ART topic in the artisan’s known_topics (base alignment score +30 on OFFER_ART_COMMISSION if the Daimyo’s own REQUEST_ART for the same art type is active). Also available proactively without incitement — an artisan may offer without being asked.

Zone knowledge prerequisite: before an NPC artisan can generate OFFER_ART_COMMISSION naming a specific zone_id, the engine verifies they have legitimate in-world knowledge of that zone. Two qualifying conditions: the artisan has a location history entry for that zone (they have physically been there, zone is in their visited_zones record), or they have received a REQUEST_ART topic that explicitly names the zone_id. Without one of these conditions, the artisan does not have in-world grounds to know that zone exists. In that case the NPC may still generate OFFER_ART_COMMISSION but only with art_type: open and no zone_id — a general offer of their services, letting the lord specify the location on acceptance. A player character has no such restriction, since players are expected to manage their own knowledge consciously.

Daimyo NPC resolution on receipt: if Daimyo’s own REQUEST_ART (matching art type or art_type: open) is active in their known_topics, accept weight +30 regardless of disposition. Disposition ≥ Neutral toward the artisan: accept, unless cultural_interest personality weight is below threshold. Disposition below Neutral: decline. A polite refusal letter is generated. No mechanical penalty to either party on decline. Artisan Glory modifier: artisan’s Glory Rank 3 or above adds +5 to the accept weight. Glory Rank 5 or above adds +10. A renowned artisan is a more attractive commission regardless of personal familiarity — the Daimyo recognises that hosting such a figure reflects well on their court. This modifier does not override disposition but tips the balance between comparable offers and may push a Neutral-threshold case toward acceptance.

          Zone commitment check: before the Daimyo evaluates any OFFER_ART_COMMISSION for a garden offer, the system checks whether garden_permission is already set for the named zone. Receiving a personal-disinterest refusal — where the Daimyo declined due to low cultural_interest or low disposition rather than zone commitment — adds the combination of (daimyo_id, art_form) to a separate declined_commissions set on the artisan’s character sheet. While this entry is active, OFFER_ART_COMMISSION targeting that daimyo_id for that art_form scores 0 in Phase 5. The entry expires after 2 IC seasons — preferences and relationships change. Early expiry triggers: if the Daimyo’s disposition toward the artisan reaches Friendly (disposition +46 or above), the entry clears immediately. If a new REQUEST_ART from that Daimyo arrives in the artisan’s known_topics, the entry clears — the lord’s explicit invitation overrides their prior disinterest. Without this check, a disinterested lord generates infinite polite refusals with no convergence.

Design note — vassal offers to their own lord, voluntary vs ordered: when a vassal artisan proactively sends OFFER_ART_COMMISSION to their own lord, the lord faces a choice that is not modelled by OFFER_ART_COMMISSION alone. Accepting the voluntary offer creates no failure consequence for the artisan. If the lord wants the formal obligation — with the completion window, neglect timer, and Honor consequences of Section 57.23.5a — they must follow acceptance with a separate ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. The NPC engine uses personality weights to determine which path a lord takes. Lords with elevated Chugi (Duty) weighting prefer the formal obligation: the commission is a duty, not a favour, and accountability is appropriate. Lords with elevated Jin (Compassion) or Rei (Courtesy) weighting prefer accepting the voluntary offer: demanding formal obligation in response to a generous proactive gesture would be socially graceless. A Hida garrison commander almost always formalises. A Doji courtier almost always accepts graciously. The difference has real downstream consequences for the artisan’s risk exposure.

Travel pipeline: when a gardener accepts a commission via letter and is not physically at the target zone, BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the target zone fires automatically on the gardener’s next AP. This follows the same pattern as MENTOR_CHARACTER — BEGIN_TRAVEL in Section 57.22.14. CULTIVATE_GARDEN is context-gated (AT_ZONE) and cannot fire until the gardener is present; the travel resolves naturally from the context gate without a special override. If the gardener is already at the zone when the letter is accepted, CULTIVATE_GARDEN becomes available immediately.

### 57.23.5 CULTIVATE_GARDEN

ActionID: CULTIVATE_GARDEN. Category 11 (Personal Development). Multi-AP progress track. Roll: Artisan: Gardening / Awareness vs base TN per AP. The gardener must be physically present in the zone (AT_ZONE context) for every AP spent — gardens cannot be worked on remotely.

Progress thresholds and TNs by target quality tier: Normal (TN 15) = 20 points. Fine (TN 20) = 40 points. Exceptional (TN 25) = 70 points. Masterwork (TN 30) = 110 points. Legendary (TN 35) = 160 points. Progress earned per AP = roll result minus base TN (minimum 1 on success, 0 on failure). Raises may be called before rolling, adding +5 progress per successful Raise. If Raises are called and the raised TN is not met, 0 progress is earned.

                Competing commission priority: when a character holds multiple ACTIVE commission_records simultaneously (multiple gardens commissioned but not yet complete), the NPC engine scores CULTIVATE_GARDEN at each zone using urgency as the primary tie-breaker. Urgency = completion_window minus neglect_timer (IC seasons remaining before auto-degradation). The zone with fewest seasons remaining scores highest and receives BEGIN_TRAVEL priority. If two zones have equal urgency, a commission_record originating from ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE ranks above one originating from OFFER_ART_COMMISSION — the formal order outranks the voluntary offer. If both originate from the same pathway, the older commission (earlier installation_date) ranks higher. The NPC travels to the most urgent zone, completes or maintains it, then re-evaluates for the next. This ensures an artisan with two obligations does not let one silently expire while optimising for prestige.

                Equal-urgency tiebreaker: when two bonsai_display commissions have identical urgency scores, higher quality_tier takes priority — a Masterwork bonsai approaching degradation is worth more effort than a Normal one at the same threshold. If quality_tier is also equal, the older commission (earlier commission_record creation_date) takes priority. NPC failed tending threshold: when an NPC’s consecutive_missed_months reaches 2 and their Artisan: Gardening rank is below 2, the engine evaluates DELIVER_GIFT to a character in met_characters with Artisan: Gardening rank 2 or above. Clan alignment weighting applies to recipient selection: same clan as the bonsai’s current owner — preferred, no disposition modifier. Allied clan — acceptable, standard Neutral or above required. Neutral clan — requires Friendly disposition or above. Opposing clan — requires Close disposition or above. These thresholds prevent culturally incoherent gifting — a Crane-lineage bonsai does not end up in Scorpion hands because a Scorpion happened to be nearby with Gardening skill, unless the relationship is genuinely Close. If no clan-appropriate recipient exists, the bonsai degrades naturally. If a viable clan-appropriate recipient exists and the bonsai is Fine or above, DELIVER_GIFT scores above continued futile TEND_BONSAI attempts.

Raises at completion: Raises called on a successful completing roll permanently upgrade the garden one quality tier per Raise to a maximum of Legendary. The Raise count achieved at completion is stored in completion_raises — required for maintenance (Section 57.23.8). On completion the garden world object is created on the zone and its ASCII representation is embedded in the zone’s map. Excess Raises at Legendary: if the garden’s target quality tier was already Legendary, or if Raises at completion push quality to Legendary with Raises still remaining, each excess Raise that cannot further upgrade quality converts to +0.2 Glory for the creator, applied immediately. A master artisan who completes a Legendary garden with 3 remaining Raises earns +0.6 Glory at the moment of completion — the exceptional execution is recognized by witnesses and the reputation effect is immediate. Gardens do not participate in the History Point system (Section 49) since they are not portable items; this Glory conversion is the garden-specific equivalent of outstanding craftsmanship recognition.

Commissioned garden completion bonus: when CULTIVATE_GARDEN completes and an active commission_record links this garden to a REQUEST_ART from the zone’s current Daimyo, the gardener receives a one-time disposition bonus from that Daimyo based on Raises at completion. Normal (0 Raises): +5. Fine (1 Raise): +8. Exceptional (2 Raises): +12. Masterwork (3 Raises): +16. Legendary (4+ Raises): +20. This bonus fires once only at completion and does not repeat on maintenance. If the commissioning Daimyo has since died or lost the zone, the bonus does not fire.

### 57.23.5a Vassal Commission Obligation

When a garden commission originates from ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE — a direct lord’s order rather than a voluntary OFFER_ART_COMMISSION — it carries a formal obligation. This obligation does not apply to voluntarily offered commissions (OFFER_ART_COMMISSION pathway), where the gardener made a free proposal and bears no penalty for non-completion.

          Commission status lifecycle: the commission_record status field tracks the obligation state. ACTIVE: the commission is in progress; the neglect_timer is running. ABANDONED: the neglect_timer has exceeded the completion_window; failure consequences fire. COMPLETED: CULTIVATE_GARDEN finished within the window; completion bonus fires and obligation is discharged. CANCELLED: the lord cancelled the commission before the window expired, whether through mutual agreement or a forgiveness request. Each ACTIVE or SUSPENDED commission_record transitions to CREATOR_DECEASED immediately. The lord receives a topic: “[Artisan name] died before completing the commissioned garden at [zone name].” Tier 4. Garden_permission is cleared for that zone. Any accumulated CULTIVATE_GARDEN progress is zeroed — there is no garden to complete and no continuation is possible.           Re-activation on SUSPENDED → ACTIVE transition: when the commission_record transitions from SUSPENDED back to ACTIVE (the superseding duty ends), the NPC engine generates two effects automatically. First, if the commissioning lord is alive and still holds the zone, the lord’s NPC generates WRITE_LETTER to the artisan with the garden commission topic as payload — a reminder that the outstanding work is still expected. This re-activates the commission topic in the artisan’s known_topics and re-incites CULTIVATE_GARDEN scoring. Second, the commission_record is added (or re-added) to the artisan’s active_objectives list, driving BEGIN_TRAVEL toward the commission zone_id on the artisan’s next Phase 5 if they are not already there. Without this re-activation, the artisan returns from war with no active prompt driving them back to the zone, and the commission silently lapses through inattention rather than deliberate choice.

Completion window: set at commission time equal to the target quality tier in IC seasons. Normal = 1 IC season. Fine = 2. Exceptional = 3. Masterwork = 4. Legendary = 5. The commission_record carries a window_start_date field (null at creation) which is set to the IC date of the first CULTIVATE_GARDEN AP spent on this zone. The neglect_timer only begins incrementing after window_start_date is set — a commission that has not yet been started is not yet failing. Once the window_start_date is set, the neglect_timer increments by 1 each IC season the commission is ACTIVE and the vassal spent zero AP on CULTIVATE_GARDEN. When neglect_timer exceeds completion_window, status shifts to ABANDONED and failure consequences fire. This prevents sequential work on parallel commissions from generating false abandonment penalties — an artisan who accepted two commissions and completed one before starting the second is not penalised for the time their work on the first commission occupied them.

Failure consequences on ABANDONED: Honor −0.5. A failure topic fires to the lord: “[Vassal name] failed to complete a commissioned garden for [Lord name].” The lord’s disposition toward the vassal decreases by −8 through standard topic processing. Partial mitigation: if progress_at_abandonment is greater than or equal to 50% of the completion_threshold for the commissioned quality tier, both penalties are halved — Honor −0.25 and disposition −4. The 50% threshold ensures mitigation reflects genuine sustained effort rather than a single token AP of work. A single AP spent to set window_start_date followed by immediate neglect does not qualify. Half the threshold represents the artisan having meaningfully engaged with the commission before circumstances or will failed them.

Forgiveness mechanic: the vassal may appeal to the lord through WRITE_LETTER (with commission_id in the payload) or direct address in person. No new ActionID — this is a specific use of the existing letter and court interaction infrastructure. The appeal resolves as a Sincerity / Awareness roll at a TN determined by timing. Proactive (before window expires, neglect_timer > 0): TN 20. Reactive immediate (ABANDONED this IC season): TN 25. Reactive late (ABANDONED, failure topic already propagated): TN 30. Two pathways exist with different risk profiles.

Sincerity (Honesty) / Awareness pathway: the vassal genuinely admits they cannot fulfil the obligation. On success: commission_record status → FORGIVEN or CANCELLED. Honor cost waived entirely. Disposition hit waived. Raises on a proactive success: 1 Raise = extension granted rather than cancellation, completion_window +1 IC season; 2 Raises on a reactive success = Honor cost waived and minor disposition recovery +3. On failure: no change, no additional penalty. The attempt itself is honourable and the vassal may try again next IC season at the same or worse TN tier.

### 57.23.6 Garden World Object

Each garden tracks the following fields: garden_id, zone_id, creator_id, quality_tier (completed tier at installation), current_tier (degrades over time), installation_date, last_maintained_season, completion_raises, visitor_count_since_last_tick, commission_record_id (null if the garden was not commissioned through REQUEST_ART or OFFER_ART_COMMISSION). The commission_record itself tracks additional obligation fields when the commission originated from ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE: status (ACTIVE / SUSPENDED / ABANDONED / COMPLETED / CANCELLED / FORGIVEN / CREATOR_DECEASED), neglect_timer (IC seasons ACTIVE with no CULTIVATE_GARDEN AP spent, only increments after window_start_date is set), completion_window (set at commission time per quality tier), window_start_date (null at creation, set on first CULTIVATE_GARDEN AP spent), progress_at_abandonment (null until ABANDONED, then snapshots accumulated progress).

ASCII map representation: on completion a persistent ASCII feature is embedded in the zone’s standard map layout. Quality tier determines visual complexity — Normal shows simple stone arrangements and trimmed plantings; Legendary fills more tile space with elaborate raked sand patterns, water features, shaped trees, and stone lanterns. As current_tier degrades the ASCII simplifies accordingly. When the garden object is destroyed the ASCII feature is removed.

### 57.23.7 Visitor Effects

Disposition trigger: any named character who enters the zone (VISITING or AT_COURT context) while the garden’s current_tier is Normal or above automatically receives a disposition bonus toward the creator. The creator themselves are excluded — character_id must not equal creator_id. Bonus scales with current_tier: Normal +1, Fine +2, Exceptional +3, Masterwork +4, Legendary +5. Duration: 4 IC months. Fires once per character per visit — a character holding an active garden disposition bonus does not receive it again until it expires and they re-enter the zone.

Glory tick: each time visitor_count_since_last_tick reaches 5, the creator receives +0.1 Glory regardless of their current location. The zone’s current Daimyo simultaneously receives +0.01 Glory — the hosting bonus for making the art available to guests. Counter resets to 0. Cooldown: 1 IC season between Glory ticks. The creator’s own visits do not increment visitor_count_since_last_tick — the creator entering for MAINTAIN_GARDEN does not count as a visitor for Glory tick purposes. A well-tended garden in a frequently visited zone generates Glory slowly but consistently for its creator over time. The Daimyo hosting bonus stacks with any bonsai hosting bonus if both are present in the zone (Section 57.24.9).

If the commission originated from ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE, the vassal obligation is cancelled with no Honor cost to the vassal — the failure was not theirs.

NPC voluntary removal evaluation: voluntary removal is a scorable action in the NPC engine, evaluated whenever the artisan generates BEGIN_TRAVEL to a destination that is not the current zone and a garden_object exists here. The engine runs a pre-departure check: can this artisan return within the coming season to perform MAINTAIN_GARDEN? If yes (destination is nearby, no blocking objectives), BEGIN_TRAVEL scores normally and removal is not evaluated. If no (destination is distant, duration indeterminate, or the artisan is being deployed elsewhere), the engine evaluates voluntary removal against abandonment using the following weights. Elevated Rei (Courtesy) personality weight: +40 toward removal — letting fine art degrade is aesthetically offensive to a refined artisan. Elevated Chugi (Duty) weight: +20 toward removal if commission_record exists, because an ordered commission that degrades reflects on their duty. garden quality_tier Exceptional or above: +30 toward removal — the artisan does not want a significant work to die neglected. quality_tier Normal: no additional weight either way. If voluntary removal scores above a threshold (PROVISIONAL: 60), the artisan removes the garden and generates a Tier 4 departure topic before leaving. Below the threshold, the artisan simply departs and the garden degrades naturally. This evaluation does not fire if the artisan intends to return within the season.

### 57.23.10 Garden Lifecycle Topics

Garden lifecycle events are not socially silent. Completion, degradation past meaningful thresholds, destruction, and removal all generate topics that propagate through the standard topic system (Section 16). These topics ensure the persistent world registers that a beautiful space appeared, declined, or disappeared — and that characters who would care about it find out. All garden lifecycle actions (CULTIVATE_GARDEN, voluntary removal) are named character actions in a lesser zone and therefore appear in the zone_event_log (Section 22.3), where they are discoverable through investigation for up to 1 IC season after the event.

Propagation scope: garden topics do not broadcast empire-wide. They propagate to three rings of relevant characters. Inner ring: characters currently AT_ZONE or who visited within the last IC season — they directly witnessed the change. Middle ring: characters who have the zone’s lord OR the creator in their met_characters — they hear through social networks. Outer ring: characters in the same province — local gossip radius. Tier 2 topics breach the outer ring and enter the general topic propagation pool. All lower tiers remain within the three-ring scope.

Garden completion topic: fires when CULTIVATE_GARDEN completes. String: “[Creator name], [Clan] artisan, has completed a [quality tier] garden at [zone name] in [Lord name]’s holdings.” Tier by quality: Normal = Tier 4. Fine = Tier 4. Exceptional = Tier 3. Masterwork = Tier 3. Legendary = Tier 2. Propagates to all three rings. A Legendary completion is a Tier 2 topic — news of it travels beyond the local province into the general topic pool. This is the first moment the garden enters the world’s social record.

Degradation topics: not every tier drop generates a topic — only thresholds with social legibility. Two triggers. First: current_tier drops from Exceptional or above to Fine. Fires only if the creator is alive (creator_id character status is alive) and the creator’s Glory is Rank 3 or above. String: “[Creator name]’s garden at [zone name] is showing signs of neglect.” Tier 4. Inner and middle rings only. Second: current_tier drops to Normal from Fine. String: “[Creator name]’s garden at [zone name] has fallen into disrepair.” Tier 4. All three rings. Both triggers check the creator’s alive status before applying. If the creator is dead when either fires, the dead-creator format applies instead (see below).

Dead creator format: when degradation or destruction fires and creator_id’s character status is dead, the topic string reflects the elegiac reality rather than gossip about neglect. First degradation after creator’s death: “[Creator name]’s [quality tier] garden at [zone name], left untended after their death, has begun to fade.” Tier shifts up by one from the standard degradation tier — a dying garden becomes more notable when there is grief attached to it. Destruction after creator’s death: “[Creator name]’s garden at [zone name], left untended after their death, has finally been lost.” Tier 3 if original quality_tier was Exceptional or above; Tier 4 otherwise. The creator’s death is checked at each degradation event — a garden may have survived its creator by several seasons before the first degradation fires, and the topic correctly reflects that time has passed since the death.

Destruction by neglect topic: fires when current_tier drops below Normal and the garden_object is destroyed. String (creator alive): “[Creator name]’s garden at [zone name] has been lost.” String (creator dead): uses dead creator format above. Tier: Tier 4 for Normal/Fine original quality. Tier 3 for Exceptional/Masterwork. Tier 2 for Legendary. A Legendary garden’s destruction is a cultural loss worth empire-wide attention. All three rings. This topic fires in addition to the final degradation topic — the disappearance of the garden is a distinct event from its last tier drop.

Daimyo-forced removal topic: fires when the zone’s Daimyo orders removal. String: “[Lord name] has removed [Creator name]’s garden from [zone name].” Tier 3 (per existing spec). Additionally, if the garden was Fine or above and the creator is alive, the creator receives the topic directly regardless of propagation scope — they will hear what was done to their work. The lord’s social cost (Tier 3 slight to creator) already covers the Honor implications in the existing spec; this topic entry specifies the string format and ensures the creator is notified.

Voluntary removal topic: fires when the creator removes their own garden. Silent for Normal quality gardens that attracted no visitors (visitor_count_since_last_tick was 0 in the last season). For Fine or above, or any garden that had active visitors: String: “[Creator name] has removed their [quality tier] garden from [zone name].” Tier 4 for Fine/Exceptional. Tier 3 for Masterwork/Legendary. Inner and middle rings. People notice a beautiful courtyard becoming empty. The absence is as notable as the presence was. Characters who had an active garden disposition bonus toward the creator and now find it discontinued are a likely source of curiosity-driven social inquiry — the topic gives them the explanation.

garden_visit_memory flag: stored on any named character who has received a garden disposition bonus from a specific garden. Fields: garden_id, creator_id, zone_id, last_visit_date. Persists after the 4-IC-month disposition bonus expires — the memory of the visit is permanent even when the mechanical bonus is gone. This flag is the mechanism by which destruction topics reach past visitors wherever they are. A Lion general who softened toward a Crane artisan after walking through their garden three IC years ago, and has since left the province, will learn when that garden is gone. The garden_visit_memory flag also feeds into historical investigation (Section 57.23.12) — a character investigating a zone where a garden once stood may find archived visit records as part of the evidence trail. Memory cap: garden_visit_memory entries for a given garden are capped at the 200 most recent visitors, and entries older than 5 IC years are purged automatically. A high-traffic court garden accumulating thousands of visitors over decades does not retain records for all of them — the connection fades. Destruction topics reach only characters holding an active, non-purged entry. Characters who visited more than 5 IC years ago or are beyond the 200-entry cap do not receive the destruction topic. This keeps propagation to a manageable scope while preserving meaningful reach for recently relevant visitors.

Zone_event_log integration: garden lifecycle events are world state changes rather than character actions, but they are explicitly added to the zone_event_log as an exception. Garden_object creation (CULTIVATE_GARDEN completion), degradation below Fine, and garden_object destruction each generate a zone_event_log entry alongside character action entries. This entry purges after 1 IC season per the standard rule (Section 22.3). During that window, Kitsuki investigators reading the zone log see the complete record of what happened — a garden completed, a garden declining, a garden lost — alongside character actions. For historical investigation beyond the purge window, garden_visit_memory flags and archived commission_records provide the recoverable evidence trail.

57.23.11 NPC Engine — Garden EvaluationSeasonal evaluation: once per IC season the NPC engine checks each Daimyo’s holdings for CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD and TSUBONIWA zones. Prerequisite (Fix M): if no CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD or TSUBONIWA zones exist in the Daimyo’s holdings at all, the check produces no action regardless of cultural_interest weight — a lord without eligible spaces cannot commission a garden. For each eligible zone found, the engine checks whether the zone is commission-free and garden-free (Fix N): lacking both an active garden_object AND an active commission_record with status ACTIVE or SUSPENDED. A zone with an active commission is already committed — issuing a new REQUEST_ART for it would create conflicting permissions. Only zones that are simultaneously garden_object-free and commission-free are considered empty for this purpose. If any such empty zone exists and the Daimyo’s cultural_interest weight exceeds the threshold, REQUEST_ART (art_type: garden, zone_id: [empty zone]) fires. The check fires per qualifying empty zone — a Daimyo with two eligible unoccupied courtyards may generate two REQUEST_ART actions in the same season.

### 57.23.12 Post-Destruction Records and Historical Investigation

A garden’s mechanical effects cease when it is destroyed, but its record does not. The item provenance system retains records indefinitely after destruction — a melted sword still has its provenance record. Gardens follow the same philosophy. Destruction means the ASCII feature is removed and all mechanical effects cease, but the garden_object record is retained permanently with a destroyed: true flag and a destruction_date field. This record is the foundation for historical investigation of garden sites.

Historical record fields retained after destruction: garden_id, zone_id, creator_id, quality_tier (original), installation_date, destruction_date, destruction_cause (NEGLECT / FORCED_REMOVAL / VOLUNTARY_REMOVAL / MAINTENANCE_FAILURE), destroyed: true. The operational fields — current_tier, visitor_count_since_last_tick, neglect_timer — are zeroed on destruction; they are irrelevant historically. The identity and provenance fields are retained indefinitely.

Physical investigation at the zone site: a character physically AT_ZONE may make an Investigation (Search) / Perception roll to read the physical traces a garden leaves behind — shaped stone arrangements, soil moisture patterns where plants were cultivated, raked sand marks worn into the ground. TN scales with time elapsed since destruction_date. Within 1 IC year: TN 15, traces are fresh. 1–5 IC years: TN 20, fading but present. 5–20 IC years: TN 25, requires expertise. 20–50 IC years: TN 30, subtle marks. 50+ IC years: TN 35, effectively beyond standard investigation. Artisan: Gardening rank 3 or above grants 1 Free Raise on this roll — a gardener knows what to look for in a way a magistrate does not. On success the investigator learns that a garden existed at this site. Each Raise beyond success reveals one additional detail from the historical garden_object record: creator identity (1 Raise), original quality tier (1 Raise), installation and destruction dates (1 Raise), destruction_cause (1 Raise). Physical trace limit: physical investigation cannot recover gardens older than approximately 75 IC years regardless of roll result. Past that threshold, soil patterns have normalized, stone arrangements have been moved or weathered away, and no physical evidence remains. TN 35 represents the practical ceiling for physical investigation, not a guaranteed floor — a character who rolls above TN 35 on a 200-year-old garden site finds nothing, because there is nothing physical to find. For gardens older than 75 IC years, only the social investigation layer (Lore: History) and the documentary layer (characters who received original lifecycle topics or hold commission_records) can provide information.

Social investigation: a character with the zone’s current or former lord in their met_characters may make a Lore: History / Intelligence roll to reconstruct what was there from social memory. TN 20 for gardens destroyed within the last 20 IC years (living memory). TN 30 for gardens older than that (hearsay and tradition). On success: learns the garden existed. Raises reveal creator identity and quality tier. This roll does not require AT_ZONE — it can be made anywhere by a character with the relevant social connections. A historian at Winter Court may reconstruct a garden’s existence from letters and court records without ever visiting the site.

Documentary investigation: if the garden generated lifecycle topics that were received by living characters, those characters may share the information through normal social interaction. This layer requires no new mechanics — it operates entirely through the existing topic propagation and conversation systems. A character who received the completion topic for a Legendary garden, and still lives, carries that knowledge. The garden_visit_memory flags and commission_records constitute a persistent documentary trail accessible to investigators who find the right people to ask. No additional mechanics required — this is the existing knowledge and topic system doing its work.

Design note — the Scenario 10 pipeline: a player character arriving at an ancient site where a Legendary garden stood fifty IC years ago needs Artisan: Gardening 3 for the Free Raise (putting their effective TN at 34 rather than 35), good dice, and persistence. On a full success with Raises, they can reconstruct the garden’s complete identity, original quality, and how it ended. Social investigation is harder at that age (TN 30) but possible through a sufficiently old or well-read court historian. Documentary evidence exists only if living characters received the original topics or hold the garden_visit_memory flag. A fifty-year-old Legendary garden’s story is recoverable but requires genuine effort — which is exactly right for something that beautiful and that old.

### 57.24.1 Overview

A bonsai is a living portable world object cultivated by a character with Artisan: Gardening. Unlike crafted items produced in concentrated sessions, a bonsai develops over IC months through patient, consistent care. It lives on the tender’s character sheet and travels with them. Quality advances through accumulated Raises over time rather than a single crafting event. Some bonsai exist at world generation with age, quality, and history already established. New bonsai begin when a character collects a suitable tree specimen in the field. A bonsai may be displayed in an eligible lesser zone as a standalone art piece or placed within an existing garden as a feature, boosting the garden’s effect. Full ownership and tending responsibility transfer permanently when a bonsai is gifted. The bonsai’s complete provenance history transfers with it.

### 57.24.2 Bonsai World Object

Each bonsai tracks the following fields: bonsai_id, owner_id (current owner and responsible tender — always the same character; gifting transfers both simultaneously), species (cosmetic only — determines ASCII appearance and provenance string, carries no mechanical weight), collection_province_id (province where the specimen was collected or world-gen origin province), collection_date (IC date of collection or world generation date), quality_tier (Normal through Legendary), quality_points (accumulated points toward next tier advance, resets to 0 on advance), last_tended_month (IC month of last successful TEND_BONSAI roll), consecutive_missed_months (counter for degradation), display_zone_id (null if not on display, zone_id if currently displayed in a lesser zone), is_dead (true if degraded beyond Normal to death), world_generated (true if spawned at game start, false if player-collected). Provenance history fields mirror the item provenance system: ordered list of previous owner_ids with transfer dates, and notable events registered against the bonsai_id (display events, Glory ticks, high-Raise tending sessions).

### 57.24.3 World Generation

At world generation, bonsai are placed in existing gardens and eligible lesser zones across the empire. The province of origin determines species — Dragon territory yields mountain pines and junipers, Phoenix territory produces flowering plum and cherry, Crane territory favors refined maple and wisteria, Crab territory sparse coastal varieties shaped by wind. Each world-generated bonsai is assigned a quality_tier, a collection_date predating the game start, and an initial owner_id (typically the Daimyo of the province or a named artisan NPC with Artisan: Gardening). These bonsai enter play with accumulated history — their provenance records may span decades. Players who acquire ancient world-generated bonsai through commerce, gifting, quest reward, or inheritance receive the full historical record.

### 57.24.4 COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN

ActionID: COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 1 AP. Context: AT_ZONE in eligible terrain. Eligible terrain is determined by province type — forested provinces, mountain provinces, temple grounds, and rural holdings all qualify. Urban zones and castle interiors do not. Roll: Artisan: Gardening / Perception vs TN 10. Finding a suitable specimen requires an eye for form and natural potential, not just horticultural knowledge. On success: a new bonsai_object is created on the character’s sheet at quality_tier Normal, quality_points 0, collection_province_id set to current province, collection_date set to current IC date, species assigned based on province type (cosmetic only). On failure: no specimen found this AP; the action may be attempted again. A character may hold multiple bonsai simultaneously — there is no ownership cap. Raises on the collection roll have no mechanical effect but may be narratively noted in the provenance record as an exceptional find.

### 57.24.5 TEND_BONSAI

ActionID: TEND_BONSAI. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 1 AP. Frequency: once per IC month. Context: AT_ZONE — the tender must be physically co-located with the bonsai. If the bonsai is on display in a zone, the tender must travel to that zone to tend it. If the bonsai is on the character sheet but not displayed, tending may occur anywhere the tender is physically present (they carry it with them). Roll: Artisan: Gardening / Awareness vs TN 10. Success with no Raises: the bonsai is maintained. last_tended_month updates, consecutive_missed_months resets to 0. Quality holds. Success with Raises: each Raise adds 1 quality_point toward the next tier advance (Section 57.24.6). Failure (TN not met): the month counts as a missed tending — consecutive_missed_months increments. See Section 57.24.7 for degradation consequences.

### 57.24.6 Quality Progression

Quality advances when accumulated quality_points meet or exceed the threshold for the next tier. Points reset to 0 on advance. Thresholds: Normal → Fine = 10 quality_points. Fine → Exceptional = 20. Exceptional → Masterwork = 35. Masterwork → Legendary = 55. At a steady pace of 1 Raise per month, a bonsai reaches Fine in roughly 10 IC months, Exceptional in roughly 30 months (2.5 IC years), Masterwork in roughly 65 months (5.5 IC years), and Legendary in roughly 120 months (10 IC years). A highly skilled tender calling multiple Raises monthly accelerates this significantly. Quality advancement is permanent — a bonsai that reaches Exceptional through years of cultivation retains that tier even if subsequently gifted to a less skilled tender, though the new tender must maintain it at the Exceptional maintenance standard or it degrades.

Legendary cap and excess quality_points: once the bonsai reaches Legendary, further Raises during TEND_BONSAI that would accumulate quality_points beyond the cap instead convert to +0.05 Glory per excess Raise, applied to the tender immediately. A master who tends a Legendary bonsai with sustained excellence is recognized for that excellence even when the tree itself cannot improve further.

### 57.24.7 Degradation and Death

Missing 1 IC month of tending (consecutive_missed_months = 1): the bonsai enters a visible warning state. Its ASCII representation shows distress — yellowing, drooping, sparse foliage. Any character examining the bonsai sees that it is being neglected. No mechanical penalty yet. Missing 2 consecutive IC months (consecutive_missed_months = 2): quality_tier degrades one step. The warning state clears and the new (lower) tier’s ASCII is shown. If the bonsai was at Normal and misses 2 consecutive months, it enters a Mundane pre-death state (below Normal). Missing a third consecutive month from Mundane: the bonsai dies. is_dead is set to true. The bonsai_object is removed from the tender’s active inventory but its historical record is preserved indefinitely — same philosophy as destroyed gardens (Section 57.23.12). A dead bonsai cannot be revived. The provenance record notes the date and cause of death.

### 57.24.8 DISPLAY_BONSAI

ActionID: DISPLAY_BONSAI. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 1 AP. No roll. Context: AT_ZONE in CASTLE_OUTER_COURTYARD or TSUBONIWA. The tender places their bonsai in the zone’s designated bonsai_display_slot. The bonsai remains on the tender’s character sheet — they retain ownership. The display_zone_id field on the bonsai_object is set to the current zone_id. The zone’s ASCII map updates to show the bonsai in the display slot, using the appropriate Section 50 ASCII representation for its quality_tier and species. Only one bonsai may occupy a zone’s display slot at a time. The tender must be physically AT_ZONE to place the bonsai and must return to the zone each IC month to perform TEND_BONSAI.

Visitor disposition bonus: while a bonsai is on display and its quality_tier is Normal or above, any named character who enters the zone receives a disposition bonus toward the tender. Bonus scales with quality_tier: Normal +1, Fine +2, Exceptional +3, Masterwork +4, Legendary +5. The tender is excluded (character_id must not equal owner_id). Duration: 4 IC months. Fires once per character per visit. The zone’s current Daimyo receives +0.01 Glory per visitor tick (per 5 qualifying visitors), the hosting bonus for making the art available. Glory tick: each time 5 qualifying visitors have experienced the displayed bonsai, the tender receives +0.1 Glory. Cooldown: 1 IC season between Glory ticks. Bonsai may also be displayed at interior zones eligible for art display per Section 57.36 (Flag Matrix) — Castle/Shiro, court locations, Temple/Shinden, estate/private quarters. Not supported at wilderness, military camps, roads, or zones without permanent structures.

Removing from display: the tender may voluntarily remove their bonsai from the display slot at no AP cost while physically AT_ZONE. The display_zone_id clears, the ASCII slot empties. The bonsai returns to non-displayed status on the tender’s character sheet. If the tender dies while their bonsai is on display, the bonsai remains in the slot until a new owner claims it or it degrades from missed tending.

### 57.24.9 Garden Integration

When DISPLAY_BONSAI is used in a zone that contains an active garden_object, the bonsai is associated with the garden. The garden’s effective visitor disposition tier for the bonus calculation increases by 1 (capped at Legendary). A Fine garden (+2 visitor bonus) with a displayed bonsai is experienced as Exceptional (+3) by visitors. This boost applies regardless of the bonsai’s quality_tier — the presence of living art within the garden is what matters, not the bonsai’s cultivation level. The bonsai tender and the garden creator may be different people. The Daimyo hosting bonus applies to both the garden’s Glory tick and the bonsai’s Glory tick independently — a zone with both a garden and a displayed bonsai generates +0.02 Daimyo Glory per respective tick threshold.

The bonsai tender is solely responsible for TEND_BONSAI regardless of whose garden hosts the bonsai. The garden creator’s seasonal MAINTAIN_GARDEN obligation is unaffected by the presence of a bonsai. The two obligations are independent. If the bonsai degrades or is removed from the garden zone, the garden reverts to its unaugmented effective tier immediately.

### 57.24.10 Gifting and Transfer

A bonsai may be permanently transferred via DELIVER_GIFT. On permanent gift: owner_id updates to the recipient. The full bonsai_object transfers — quality_tier, quality_points, species, collection_province_id, collection_date, and complete provenance history all pass to the new owner. The new owner immediately assumes tending responsibility. If they fail to tend the bonsai the following IC month, consecutive_missed_months begins incrementing from that point. The gifted bonsai’s history carries the original collector’s name, any previous owners, and the provenance of cultivation — who tended it, how long, and what quality was achieved during each tenure. An ancient Legendary bonsai that has passed through four owners over 40 IC years carries all of that history in its record. This provenance is readable through the item inspection system (Section 50 Inspection Mode) by any character who examines the bonsai in person.

### 57.24.11 NPC Engine — Bonsai Evaluation

NPC artisans with Artisan: Gardening who lack a bonsai may generate COLLECT_BONSAI_SPECIMEN when traveling through eligible terrain, scored under ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION and SEEK_GLORY NeedTypes. Once a bonsai is held, TEND_BONSAI fires monthly with high urgency when consecutive_missed_months approaches 1 (warning state). Urgency scoring mirrors MAINTAIN_GARDEN: base 85 in ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION, increasing as missed months accumulate. DISPLAY_BONSAI scores when the NPC is AT_ZONE in an eligible lesser zone with high visitor traffic and their bonsai’s quality_tier is Fine or above — a modest bonsai is not worth the display commitment. NPCs evaluate whether to display in a zone with an active garden (higher combined prestige and Glory return) versus standalone display. Gifting a bonsai via DELIVER_GIFT follows standard DELIVER_GIFT scoring under RAISE_DISPOSITION — a high-quality bonsai with notable provenance is a culturally significant gift.
