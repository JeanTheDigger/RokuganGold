## 4.8 Other Mechanics

### 4.8.1 Iaijutsu Dueling

The iaijutsu duel is the formal means of conflict resolution between members of the samurai caste. Such duels are most often to first blood, but matters of a truly grievous nature can result in a duel to the death. Once a challenge has been issued and accepted the process takes place over three combat rounds. Both duelists are considered to be in Center Stance throughout — no other actions may be taken other than those defined below.

**Round 1 — Assessment --- LOCKED**

Fires on the Initiative Turn of the faster duelist. Both duelists study each other carefully. Each makes an Iaijutsu (Assessment) / Awareness roll vs TN equal to (opponent’s Insight Rank × 5) + 10.

A successful roll reveals the first piece of information below. Each Raise beyond the TN reveals one additional piece, in order:

- The opponent’s Void Ring value.

- The opponent’s Reflexes Trait value.

- The opponent’s Iaijutsu Skill rank.

- Any Iaijutsu Emphases the opponent possesses.

- The opponent’s current Void Points remaining.

- The opponent’s current Wound Level.

If one duelist’s Assessment roll exceeds the other’s by 10 or more — regardless of whether they gained any information — that duelist gains a bonus of +1k1 on their subsequent Focus roll.

**Declining at Assessment:** Either duelist may concede defeat at the end of the Assessment round, recognizing their opponent as superior. This is considered honorable — no shame attaches to withdrawing when you have assessed your opponent and recognized you have no chance of victory. It is more shameful to deny your lord a loyal servant by dying pointlessly. However, if the duel was called to the death, declining may be considered tantamount to defeat and the declining duelist may be expected to commit seppuku.

**Round 2 — Focus --- LOCKED**

Fires on the Initiative Turn of the faster duelist. Both duelists make a Contested Iaijutsu (Focus) / Void roll. The duelists study one another for any sign of weakness, hands moving toward their blades.

- **Winner by 5 or more:** Earns the right to strike first. Gains 1 Free Raise on their Strike roll per additional increment of 5 by which they beat their opponent’s roll. These Free Raises apply to the attack roll in Round 3.

- **Neither wins by 5+:** Kharmic Strike — both duelists strike simultaneously in Round 3. See Kharmic Strike below.

**Round 3 — Strike --- LOCKED**

Fires on the Initiative Turn of the slower duelist. The first striker makes an Iaijutsu / Reflexes attack roll against the opponent’s normal Armor TN. All Free Raises gained from the Focus stage apply to this roll. The attack resolves normally including Wounds. The second duelist then makes their own Iaijutsu / Reflexes roll — if they are still alive and able to act.

- **Duel to first blood:** The loser is whoever was struck first. If the first striker hits, the duel is over — the second duelist has lost and striking after first blood is considered extremely dishonorable. If the first striker misses, the second duelist strikes and the duel resolves on that outcome.

- **Duel to the death:** If neither duelist is dead at the end of the Strike phase the duel becomes a standard skirmish, continuing until one combatant is dead. The act of striking counts as each character’s action for this Round.

- **Kharmic Strike:** If neither duelist won the Focus roll by 5+, both make their attack rolls simultaneously. The cause of the duel is considered dropped by both parties. Neither is the victor nor the defeated. Destiny has intervened.

**Honor and Glory Outcomes --- LOCKED:**

- Won a duel honorably in court context: +0.5 Glory (already in Section 4.6 event table).

- Striking after first blood in a first blood duel: −1.0 Honor. Extremely dishonorable — you have proven yourself without discipline or respect for the form.

- Declining honorably at Assessment: no Honor loss, no Glory loss. The act is recognized as wise rather than cowardly.

- Declining a duel to the death: −0.5 Glory. May also require seppuku depending on the terms and the superior’s judgment.

- Kharmic Strike outcome: no Honor or Glory change for either duelist. The matter is considered resolved by fate.

**Stare-Down (Optional Pre-Duel) --- LOCKED:**

Before the duel begins, either duelist may attempt a Contested Intimidation / Willpower roll against the other. Success does not end the duel but shakes the opponent — the loser of the stare-down takes a −1k0 penalty on their Assessment roll. This is optional and carries a social risk: a duelist who attempts a stare-down and loses it has revealed weakness before a single blade has moved.

### 4.8.2 Shugenja & Magic

- Shugenja commune with kami to cast spells — spell casting requires Complex Actions equal to the spell’s Mastery Level. Kiho are separate monk techniques, not spells.

- Spell Casting Roll: roll (Shugenja School Rank + relevant Element Ring) dice, keep (Element Ring) dice. TN = 5 + (5 × Mastery Level). The Ring used depends on the element of the spell being cast — not universally Fire.

- Void magic (Void spells, Void Ring usage) is distinct and rarer.

### 4.8.3 Character Progression

- Characters advance by spending XP (earned through play) to raise Rings, buy Skills, and learn Techniques (School abilities).

- Each character belongs to a School (e.g., Kakita Duelist, Mirumoto Niten, Kuni Witch Hunter) which defines their starting abilities and advancement path.

- Insight Rank (1–10) is derived from total Ring + Skill investment — higher Ranks unlock advanced Techniques.
