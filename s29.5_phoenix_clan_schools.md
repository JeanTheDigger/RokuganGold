## 29.5 Phoenix Clan Schools

**BASIC SCHOOLS:**

**Agasha Shugenja [CR]:**

Multi-elemental versatility. During their centuries with the Dragon Clan the Agasha pursued unconventional paths of magic — alchemy, and the creation of spells invoking kami of multiple elements simultaneously. Among the Phoenix they have been given leave to pursue this area as far as they wish, and have made tremendous strides. Their technique allows them to change the element of any spell they cast, giving unmatched flexibility in managing spell slots.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Calligraphy (Cipher), any one Craft Skill, Defense, Etiquette, Lore: Theology, Spellcraft, any one High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 4.5 | Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: Fire / Water | Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Fire, 2 Earth, 1 Air

- Technique — Elements of All Things: When casting a spell, spend a Void Point to use a different Ring of your choosing rather than the appropriate Ring for that spell. The spell uses a slot for the Ring actually used to cast it. Free Raise on any spell with the Craft keyword.

**Asako Henshin [Monk]:**

One of the most enigmatic groups in the Empire. Even among the Phoenix, few outside the Asako family understand the Henshin’s ways. They follow the Path of Man as revealed by Shiba to Asako — developing perfect unity with the universe, tricking the Elements and learning their secrets. Apprentices are known as michibuku; those inducted into the inner ranks become fushihai — essentially immortal masters who live far beyond the normal mortal span. Some fushihai ascend to the Celestial Heavens upon death, becoming minor Fortunes.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower

- Skills: Calligraphy, Jiujutsu, Lore: Elements (pick an emphasis), Lore: Theology, Meditation 2, any 1 Skill (must not be a Low Skill)

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Robes, Bo, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — The Four Mysteries: Simple Action — increase or decrease both Traits associated with one Ring by your School Rank. Lasts minutes equal to your Insight Rank; usable up to five times your Insight Rank per day. May alter another’s Traits by half School Rank (minimum 1). Altering an unknowing or unwilling target requires a Contested Willpower Roll. Trait reductions do not affect Ring values.

- Rank 2 — The Riddle of Earth: Simple Action — invoke Earth’s blessing; become immune to all Conditional Effects (excluding Grappled and Mounted) for hours equal to your Earth Ring.

- Rank 3 — The Riddle of Air: Simple Action — invoke Air’s blessing for hours equal to your School Rank. You cannot be deceived by any illusion or false image — you perceive them as transparent falsehoods. If an illusion was created by a spell of higher Mastery Level than your Insight Rank, engage in a Contested Air Roll with the caster; if you lose, the falsehood is not perceived.

- Rank 4 — The Riddle of Fire: Complex Action — invoke Fire’s blessing to increase kept dice on unarmed damage rolls to your Fire Ring for Rounds equal to your School Rank. Alternatively, when struck by a melee attack spend a Void Point to force a Contested Fire Ring Roll — on success, reduce the number of damage dice your opponent rolls by your Fire Ring.

- Rank 5 — The Riddle of Water: Roll Lore: Elements (Water)/Water (TN 20). On success, take one Complex and one Simple Action per Turn, or three Simple Actions per Turn.

**Asako Loremaster [Courtier] [CR]:**

Scholarly breadth as a social weapon. Extensive studies across many subjects give the Asako a frame of reference from which to examine any situation. Techniques use Lore Skills to augment Social Skills, gaining influence through learned depth rather than charm or manipulation.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Courtier, Etiquette (Courtesy), Lore: History, Lore: Theology (Fortunes), Meditation, Sincerity, any one Lore Skill

- Honor: 6.5 | Outfit: Sensible Clothing, Wakizashi, Calligraphy Set, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — Temple of the Soul: Free Raise on any Lore Skill Roll. When spending a Void Point on an Etiquette Skill Roll, gain +3k1 instead of the normal +1k1.

- Rank 2 — From the Ashes: After spending at least one day observing a particular court, roll Lore: History/Perception (TN 20) to gain +2k0 to all Social Skill Roll totals for the next two days while remaining in that court. Reactivate with another roll when it expires — no additional observation days required.

- Rank 3 — Voice of the Universe: Spend an hour conversing with an ally and roll Lore: History/Intelligence (TN 25). On success, that ally may add your Lore: History Skill Rank to any Social Skill Roll totals within the next twenty-four hours. Raise twice to affect one additional ally who also participates.

- Rank 4 — Invincible Mind: Any time you fail a Contested Social Skill Roll to resist influence on your emotions, opinions, or behavior, reroll using Intelligence as the Trait instead of whichever Trait was originally required. Must keep the second result. Cannot resist Fear with this Technique.

- Rank 5 — Wisdom of the Ages: +5k0 to the total of any Lore Skill Roll, including rolls for other School Techniques.

**Isawa Shugenja [CR]:**

The greatest shugenja school in the Empire. The Isawa are nearly twice the size of the next largest shugenja family — so many born with the gift that they have diversified into numerous areas of magical specialization. The school itself is the oldest of its kind in Rokugan. Rather than specializing, the Isawa are built around being the finest at spell-casting in general. The ability to choose any Affinity without a corresponding Deficiency, combined with a Free Raise on all Affinity spells, makes them the equal of virtually any other shugenja character.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Calligraphy (Cipher), Lore: Theology, any one Lore Skill, Medicine, Meditation, Spellcraft, any one High Skill

- Honor: 4.5 | Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Affinity/Deficiency: May select any element including Void as Affinity. No Deficiency.

- Starting Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 spells of any one element, 2 spells of any other element, 1 spell of a third element, 1 spell of a fourth element.

- Technique — Isawa’s Gift: Free Raise on all Spell Casting Rolls for spells of the element chosen as your Affinity.

**Shiba Artisan [Courtier] [EE]:**

Not as prestigious or politically connected as the Kakita Artisans, but much-admired across the Empire. Few Phoenix or Dragon daimyo will go without a sample of their work. Techniques use artistic mastery to build alliances, speak for the character in social situations, and restore Void Points through the presentation of craft.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Artisan (pick 2), Calligraphy, Etiquette, Lore (pick 1), Sincerity, any 1 High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 4.5 | Outfit: Sensible Clothing, Wakizashi, tools for chosen specialty, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — Soul of Brilliance: Select one Artisan Skill. When spending a Void Point on a roll using that Skill, gain +2k2 instead of the normal +1k1.

- Rank 2 — The Way of Sincerity: Once per month, make an Artisan Skill Roll (TN 15). Gain XP equal to the amount your roll exceeds the TN — these XP may only be spent to purchase the Allies Advantage (subject to GM approval). These Allies last weeks equal to your School Rank.

- Rank 3 — The Art Speaks: Choose one Artisan Skill. When called to make a Social Skill Roll, you may use your Artisan Skill Ranks in place of the required Social Skill. No Mastery Level benefits apply. GM has final say on applicability.

- Rank 4 — Bounty of the Craft: Once per day, make an Artisan Skill Roll (TN 30). On success, you and up to School Rank other people present all regain all Void Points. Requires at least fifteen minutes of ritualized presentation. Cannot be used in the Shadowlands.

- Rank 5 — The Touch of Destiny: When your work is on display in court or in a lord’s home, all present gain one additional Void Point. If you are present, you gain two additional Void Points instead and may deny the benefit to anyone in attendance. Maximum one additional Void Point per person per day (two for you).

**Shiba Bushi [CR]:**

Warrior-scholars devoted to the protection of life. The Shiba consider all options before committing to combat. If those they protect are threatened or combat is inevitable, they attack swiftly and without reservation. Those who mistake a Shiba’s desire to preserve life for an inability to take it are woefully unprepared. Techniques optimize Void Point use and specialize in protecting others, making Shiba excellent yojimbo with an affinity for polearms.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Defense, Kenjutsu, Kyujutsu, Meditation (Void Recovery), Spears, Theology, any one Bugei or High Skill

- Honor: 5.5 | Outfit: Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — The Way of the Phoenix: When spending a Void Point for +1k1 on a roll, you may instead spend 2 Void Points to gain +2k2. You may Guard as a Free Action; however, your target only adds +5 to his Armor TN instead of +10.

- Rank 2 — Dancing With the Elements: When you assume your Stance, choose a target within 30’. Whenever your target casts or is the target of a spell, you may increase or decrease the TN by 5. Additionally, whenever you are the target of a spell, you may increase or decrease the TN by 5.

- Rank 3 — One With the Void: Automatically activates during the Reactions Stage if any character spent a Void Point this Combat Round — you regain one Void Point (may temporarily exceed maximum; excess lost after combat). Activates only twice per skirmish.

- Rank 4 — Move With the World: Make attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action with Polearms, Spears, or weapons with the Samurai keyword.

- Rank 5 — Touch of the Void: For every Void Point you spend, gain the effects of spending two (when applicable). You may now spend Void Points on enhancements twice in one Turn.

**ADVANCED SCHOOLS:**

**Asako Inquisitors [Monk]:**

The Phoenix order devoted to eradicating corrupt and blasphemous elements from among the Empire’s shugenja. Peaceful in outward reputation, the Inquisitors embrace their mission completely and at any cost. They investigate not only maho and Shadowlands corruption but also gaijin magic and heretical religious groups. Members are recruited primarily from Phoenix shugenja, though bushi and Brotherhood Monks have also been inducted. All members receive a stylized eye tattoo — symbol of the order — somewhere on their body.

- Requirements: Void 4, any 2 other Rings at 3 | Skills: Lore: Law 4, Lore: Shugenja 3 | Other: Must be able to cast Mastery Level 4 spells in one element OR have Simple Action melee attacks and possess the Inquisitor’s Strike. May not be Tainted.

- Rank 1 — Eye of the Inquisitor: Considered one Rank higher in your original Shugenja School for casting purposes. Non-shugenja Inquisitors gain one Kiho (must meet prerequisites). Complex Action to prepare a disruption — later in the same skirmish, Free Action to force an opponent casting a spell to make 2 additional Raises on the casting roll (raises TN only).

- Rank 2 — The Trials of Jade: When casting any spell, spend a Void Point for the spell to count as jade or crystal for purposes of reducing or defeating Reduction or Invulnerability. Non-casters may instead make a melee attack as a Simple Action when facing an opponent known to have violated Imperial laws regarding legal forms of magic.

- Rank 3 — Conviction of Purity: Considered one Rank higher in your original Shugenja School for casting purposes. Non-shugenja Inquisitors gain one additional Kiho. May now prepare a disruption as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action; disrupted opponents require 3 additional Raises rather than 2.

**Elemental Guard [Shugenja] [CR]:**

Four separate schools — one for each non-Void element — for shugenja who have honed their entire soul to a single element. The Elemental Guard represents the pinnacle of devoted elemental study. Entry requirements are severe, ensuring only the very best shugenja in their chosen element reach this level.

- Requirements: Chosen Ring at 6 | Skills: Spellcraft 6, Lore: Theology 6 | Other: Must be able to cast spells of the chosen Element at Mastery Level 4.

- Rank 1 — Name of the Elements: Shugenja School Rank considered one higher for casting spells of the Chosen Element. Spend a Void Point as a Free Action to add twice your chosen Ring Rank to your Armor TN for minutes equal to your Shugenja School Rank (visible elemental effect — flames, swirling water, rocklike skin, etc.).

- Rank 2 — Touch of the Elements: Shugenja School Rank increases by one. Chosen Ring is considered two Ranks higher for determining spell slots in that Element per day.

- Rank 3 — Shape of the Elements: Shugenja School Rank considered one higher for casting spells of the Chosen Element. Select one spell of the Chosen Element at Mastery Level 3 or greater — a number of times per day equal to your Void Ring, cast that spell with a single Simple Action.

**ALTERNATE PATHS:**

**Acolytes of Snow [Shugenja]:**

Founded by a third-century Isawa shugenja fascinated by the legends of the Yuki no Onna (Snow Maiden). The Acolytes develop magic built around snow and ice, modifying spells from Water and other elements to fit their needs.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 2, any other Phoenix Shugenja 3. Requires: Lore: Yuki no Onna 3 or Water Affinity.

- Technique — The Maiden’s Icy Grasp: May cast the following Earth spells as Water spells: Armor of Earth, Armor of the Emperor, Be the Mountain, Bonds of Ningen-do, Earth’s Stagnation, Grasp of Earth, Major Binding, Minor Binding, Wall of Earth. Spells create their effects from ice rather than earth or stone.

**Agasha Alchemist [Shugenja]:**

The Ikakeya — a lesser-known Agasha sect who have deliberately avoided outside attention since joining the Phoenix. They continue alchemical studies from their Dragon Clan era, known for potions containing the essence of their magic and for transposing and combining elements.

- Replaces: Agasha Shugenja 3. Requires: Fire 3, Spellcraft 3.

- Technique — Fury of the Elements: Store a Fire spell with the Area of Effect “one target person” or “one target creature” in an alchemical preparation. Activated as a Simple Action (drink) or Complex Action (throw, using Athletics (Throwing)/Agility). By spending an additional spell slot of another element (not Void), also give the stored spell an additional elemental keyword or replace its Fire keyword with another. A spell may only gain one additional keyword this way.

**Asako Philosopher [Courtier]:**

Based in the Blue Tiled Room of Nikesake — a highly specialized philosophical library. Courtiers stationed there develop elaborate debates, frequently changing schools of thought mid-argument. The lords of the Phoenix call upon them when insight into specific families or clans is required.

- Replaces: Asako Loremaster 2. Requires: Courtier (Rhetoric) 2, Etiquette 3.

- Technique — The Winds of Rhetoric: +1k1 to the total of all Contested Social Skill Rolls using Etiquette.

**Asako Mediator [Courtier]:**

A near-monastic order dedicated to peace above all. Members must swear never to initiate violence against fellow Rokugani — violation entails Honor and Glory loss equivalent to a Blasphemous Breach of Etiquette and expulsion from the order.

- Replaces: Asako Loremaster 2, Shiba Bushi 2, Isawa Shugenja 2. Requires: Sincerity (Honesty) 3.

- Technique — Stand Down: When samurai are preparing to enter formal hostilities, approach either or both parties and make a Contested Sincerity (Honesty)/Awareness vs Etiquette (Courtesy)/Willpower roll. On success, that party backs down without any loss of Glory. The GM may require Raises (1–5) for deeply committed parties and may award bonuses (+1k0 to +5k0) for honorable proposed solutions. Large-scale conflicts may only be delayed rather than prevented.

**Asako Scholar [Courtier]:**

Those Asako who display the “Mind of Fire” — tremendous natural memory and intuitive grasp of connections between disparate fields of study — are trained in a special dojo where their gifts are honed for both scholarly research and courtly service.

- Replaces: Asako Loremaster 3, Asako Henshin 3. Requires: Investigation 3, Intelligence 4.

- Technique — The Hidden Patterns: After spending at least one day observing patterns within a court or social setting, roll Investigation/Intelligence (TN 25). On success, rivals and enemies cannot benefit from Raises on Social Skill Rolls against you and cannot spend Void Points on Social Skill Rolls against you for 24 hours. Raise to share the benefit with friends and allies (one person per Raise).

**Elemental Legions [Bushi]:**

For each of the four Elemental Guard units there exists a highly trained Shiba legion prepared to take the field alongside their shugenja brethren — the Legion of Wind, Legion of Stone, Legion of Flame, and Legion of the Wave. These bushi have received exhaustive training from shugenja and Brotherhood members in how to align their spirits with their element.

- Replaces: Shiba Bushi 3. Requires: Lore: Elements (appropriate Ring) 2. Must be selected to serve in one of the Elemental Legions.

- Technique — Strength of the Five: Select one non-Void Ring. When an allied shugenja within line of sight casts a spell of that Element, gain one bonus Void Point. Bonus Void Points may only be spent on rolls using that Element’s Traits, or element-specifically: Air — +10 Armor TN for one Round; Earth — reduce Wounds from one damage roll by 10; Fire — temporarily increase 1 Skill Rank from 0 to 1 for one roll; Water — exchange Initiative Score with one willing participant for the skirmish. Cannot exceed School Rank bonus Void Points; unused lost at skirmish end.

**Isawa Archaeologist [Shugenja]:**

The death of the Ivory Kingdoms has left the region rife with ruins and lost lore. The Phoenix — masters and keepers of ancient and forbidden knowledge throughout history — have taken up the pursuit of these secrets with particular eagerness. Many Phoenix in the Colonies train to make it their primary duty.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 2, Agasha Shugenja 2. Requires: Lore: Gaijin (Ivory Kingdoms) 3.

- Technique — There Are No Secrets: When casting a non-damaging spell on an object, creature, or willing individual (not yourself) for which you have at least 3 Ranks in an applicable Lore Skill, gain +1k1 on the Spell Casting Roll. GM has final say on Lore Skill applicability.

**Isawa Temple Guardian [Shugenja]:**

Countless temples and holy sites throughout the Empire have benefited from Temple Guardian protection over the centuries. They are welcome in the lands of virtually every clan and family in Rokugan. Although their expertise is rarely needed, the results are memorable when it is — many tales exist of a single Temple Guardian turning the tide during an unexpected attack.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 3, Agasha Shugenja 3. Requires: Earth 4, Lore: Theology 3.

- Technique — Never This Sacred Ground Shall Fall: When defending a sacred location (shrine, temple, or monastery), Free Raise on all Earth spells. When defending a sacred location, also spend a Void Point as a Free Action when casting any Earth spell that inflicts damage to grant it the Jade keyword.

**Isawa Seaguard [Shugenja]:**

Created in response to Yoritomo’s campaigns of raids and piracy. Lacking numbers for a naval build-up matching their Crane allies, the Phoenix instead placed shugenja on all ships and trained them to use winds and waves against Mantis vessels. Often recruited from shugenja who would normally have joined the Elemental Guard.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 3. Requires: Water 3, Air 3, Sailing 3.

- Technique — The Ward of the Sea: While on board a ship on water, expend a Water or Air spell slot as a Complex Action to create a barrier of water and air around the ship. Lasts three Rounds. While active: ship has Reduction 10 against physical attacks, Reduction 25 against natural or magical fire-based attacks, and arrows to and from anyone on ship are obstructed, replicating the mechanical effects of Summoning the Gale.

**Isawa Tensai [Shugenja] [CR]:**

Exceptional prodigies even among the Isawa. The Tensai focuses his entire attention on a single element — his command of those kami grows to an astounding level, as they seem to sing and dance at his call. This single-mindedness comes at a severe cost: complete deficiency in all other elements. A cost the Tensai are only too eager to bear.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 2. Requires: Lore: Elements 3, Spellcraft

- Technique — Embrace the Elements: Gain an additional Affinity in any one element for which you already possess an Affinity. Gain a Deficiency in all other elements. Example: a Rank 1 Isawa with Fire Affinity advancing to this Technique becomes capable of casting Mastery Level 4 Fire spells but only Mastery Level 1 spells of all other elements.

**Mist Legion [Shugenja]:**

A secretive group within the Hurricane Initiates — the Air-focused Elemental Guard unit — specializing in battlefield illusions. Their existence is seldom officially acknowledged; they can be recognized by a small gray cloud worked into the school mon on their kimono. Not solely warriors — in non-battle situations many use their abilities to create massive temporary works of art.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 3 (Air Affinity required).

- Technique — The World is a Canvas: A number of times per day equal to your Insight Rank, spend a Void Point to cast Summon Fog or False Realm as a Simple Action.

**Kawaru Sages [Shugenja]:**

A small sect that studies and refines the ancient kawaru texts and methods, offering guidance and divination to all who need it. They regard kawaru as a sacred art — a gift from the Fortunes — and blend kawaru methods with actual divination magic for more precise results. They wear robes embroidered with kawaru hexagrams.

- Replaces: Any Phoenix Shugenja 2, Tonbo Shugenja 2. Requires: Divination (Kawaru) 3.

- Technique — The Veil of the Future: At the start of each day, spend one hour in meditation and make a Divination Skill Roll (TN 15). On success, gain +1k0 bonus dice; Raise to gain additional bonus dice up to your Water Ring. Spend these dice on any Spell Casting Rolls made during the rest of the day. Unused dice lost at nightfall.

**The Inferno Guard [Shugenja]:**

Part of the Firestorm Legion — the most aggressive of the Elemental Legions. The Inferno Guard provides the defensive counterpart: defending positions with walls of fire, protecting against archery by immolating arrows mid-flight, and obscuring with thick plumes of smoke.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 3. Requires: Fire 3, Battle (Mass Battle) 3, Fire Affinity.

- Technique — Menacing Flames: When casting any Fire spell that does not list “One Target Creature” or “One Target Individual” as its Area of Effect, spend a Void Point or expend an additional spell slot to give the spell a Fear X effect, where X equals the Mastery Level of the spell.

**Nameless Ones [Shugenja] [IH]:**

Void-aspected shugenja who have embraced the power of Void against corruption and taint. Terrible foes of all corruption — especially the Shadowlands.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 3. Requires: Ishiken-do.

- Technique — Darkness Undone: When casting any spell targeting an opponent with the Shadowlands Taint, expend one additional spell slot to lower the opponent’s Reduction by your School Rank. May expend multiple spell slots this way up to a maximum equal to your Void Ring. The lowered Reduction lasts until the beginning of your next Turn.

**Order of Chikai [Bushi]:**

Among the oldest and most prestigious orders within the Shiba family. Named for Shiba Chikai — a yojimbo who sacrificed herself protecting the Phoenix Master of Water from ten assassins, none of whom survived. The Order is devoted to providing the finest yojimbo to protect all five Elemental Masters. Members are hand-selected from those with exceptional records of service.

- Replaces: Shiba Bushi 5. Requires: Must have taken the Shiba Yojimbo Path at Rank 3 and be selected for service in the Order of Chikai.

- Technique — None Must Fall: Designate one individual as your charge at the start of a skirmish. When your charge is targeted by an attack or spell, use the Shiba Yojimbo Technique as normal (Defense/Reflexes TN 25 to absorb the damage). Additionally, at the time of the roll you may spend a Void Point as a Free Action — if you succeed, the damage is negated entirely rather than simply transferred to you.

**Provincial Guard [Bushi]:**

Once known as the Sapphire Legion, a unit of distinction well known for defending the sacred City of Treaties, Nikesake. Renamed to Provincial Guard after confusion arose with an Imperial unit of similar name. Members are trained in etiquette in addition to their duties as sentries and yojimbo — the two aspects of training proving mutually beneficial.

- Replaces: Shiba Bushi 2. Requires: Defense 2, Etiquette 2.

- Technique — Maintaining the Peace: When in Defense or Full Defense Stance and spending a Void Point to augment your Armor TN, also add your Etiquette Skill Rank to your Armor TN.

**Sesai Ninja [Ninja]:**

Phoenix covert operatives willing to embrace dishonorable methods for the clan’s benefit.

- Replaces: Shiba Bushi 2. Requires: Stealth 3.

- Technique — Anything For the Phoenix: On a battlefield, +1k1 to all Stealth rolls and a Free Raise on all attack rolls made by surprise. When performing dishonorable acts on behalf of the Phoenix Clan, lose only half the normal Honor (rounded up).

**Shiba Advisor [Courtier]:**

Shiba who train with the Asako to become scholarly historians of war and conflict — a path the Asako normally frown upon. These Shiba view contemporary battles through the dispassionate eye of history, offering potent counsel during times of war. Every Shiba general ensures he has an Advisor on his command staff when the Phoenix must fight.

- Replaces: Asako Loremaster 3. Requires: Lore: History 3, Lore: War 3. Shiba family only.

- Technique — Lessons Never Forgotten: Prior to a military task (patrol, scouting mission, attack, or bandit hunt), roll Lore: War/Intelligence (TN 25) or Lore: History/Intelligence (TN 35). On success, one allied bushi on the mission gains one Rank of Luck for the mission’s duration. Raise to bestow Luck on additional allies (not yourself), up to a maximum equal to your School Rank.

**Shiba Armorsmiths [Bushi] [Artisan]:**

Some Shiba embrace both sides of their family’s nature — warrior and artist — taking up the path of the armorsmith. Phoenix Clan armor is both a beautiful artistic creation and an instrument of defense for what the Shiba regard as the tragic inevitability of war.

- Replaces: Shiba Bushi 2. Requires: Craft: Armorsmithing 3, Defense

- Technique — Brilliant Steel: +1k0 on Craft rolls to create armor. When wearing armor and spending a Void Point to increase Armor TN by 10, also increase Armor TN by an additional amount equal to twice your Earth Ring.

**Shiba Illusionists [Shugenja] [Artisan]:**

Founded by the unconventional shugenja Shiba Tsuna, who believed there is no sound the Air kami like more than the laughter of children. The Isawa regard their use of kami as frivolous but after the disgrace of Tsuna’s rival have avoided any direct attack on the organization.

- Replaces: Isawa Shugenja 1 or Shiba Artisan 1. Special: Use the Trait benefit, Skills, Honor, and outfit of whichever school they attend. Always treated as having Air Affinity; gain 3 Air, 2 Water, and 1 Earth spell if this is their first Shugenja Technique.

- Technique — The Tejina’s Art: Gain additional spell slots per day equal to your School Rank. These slots may only be used to cast Token of Memory, Flight of Doves, or Mists of Illusion.

**Shiba Yojimbo [Bushi] [CR]:**

Shiba bent the knee to man and swore to follow the Isawa at the inception of the Phoenix Clan. The Shiba Yojimbo is the epitome of that oath — he will die before he allows harm to his charge. Only the most honorable and selfless Shiba are selected, since the training requires a complete willingness to throw one’s life away without hesitation.

- Replaces: Shiba Bushi 3. Requires: Honor Rank 5.0+.

- Technique — Shiba’s Sacrifice: At the start of any skirmish, designate one ally as your charge. Any time your charge is hit by a physical or targeted non-area-effect damage-causing spell, if you are within 10’ make a Defense/Reflexes roll (TN 25). On success, you take the damage instead of your charge. If you spend a Void Point to reduce this damage, it is reduced by 20 instead of the normal 10.

**Sisters of the Sacred Light [Shugenja]:**

Founded shortly after the creation of the Centipede Clan, originally to deal with infestations of mischievous spirits. Although founded by the Centipede, they recruit women from several clans who are expected to forsake their former loyalties. They dress in traditional temple priestess garb — red hakama and white gi — with only a single clan mon to indicate ancestry.

- Replaces: Asahina Shugenja 2, Isawa Shugenja (Air) 2, Moshi Shugenja

- Technique — Light Banishes Lies: Specializes in exposing illusions. When attempting to Banish the kami of Air, you require only 2 Raises on the Sense spell (instead of the normal 3) and only 3 Raises on the Commune spell (instead of the normal 5).
