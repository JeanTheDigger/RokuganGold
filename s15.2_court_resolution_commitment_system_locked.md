## 15.2 Court Resolution & Commitment System — LOCKED

Court is not a democracy. It is a negotiation. Court produces individual commitments from each lord present — not a collective mandate. The outcome of a court session is a snapshot of what each lord decided to do, shaped by everything that happened during the session.

**How Courts End — LOCKED:**

- Courts have a time window. Decisions happen organically within that window — not at a formal closing vote.

- War/Peace: The moment both warring parties agree to terms, the war stops. No need to wait for court to formally close.

- Aid commitment: The moment lords pledge help, a short window opens for others to join. Then it closes. Those who committed send aid. Those who didn't, didn't.

- No decision reached: Court closes when time runs out. Nothing happens. The crisis continues accumulating momentum. If a new decision is needed, someone must invest in calling a new court.

- Winter Court: Closes after 120 in-game days regardless. Emperor issues Imperial Edicts on the top three global crises at closing. Edicts bind everyone — they are the only truly binding court outcome.

**Individual Commitments — LOCKED:**

Each lord present decides for themselves what they will commit to. No one can be forced to act except by Imperial Edict. The court's job is to move those individual decisions through political action, not to impose a collective verdict.

- Example — War between Clan A and B, hosted by Clan C: Only Clan A and Clan B can agree to stop the war. Clan C has no authority to impose peace — only to create the conditions where both parties choose it.

- Example — Shadowlands invasion, Crab hosts court: Only the clans that commit to helping will send troops. Those who decline are not bound. Their silence or refusal is itself a political statement with disposition consequences.

- Commitments are declared publicly — each lord makes a Public Declaration of what they will do or that they decline to act. This uses the Public Declaration action from Category 2, making it mechanically binding.

- Reneging on a public commitment: Honor loss. Significant disposition damage with all who witnessed the commitment. Permanent historical modifier in the disposition system.

**Consequences of Inaction — LOCKED:**

- Refusing to help when your clan is directly capable: disposition damage with those who asked.

- Staying silent: smaller but real disposition signal — everyone noticed.

- Declining while giving a diplomatic reason: smaller disposition cost than a flat refusal.

- The crisis continues accumulating momentum regardless — inaction is never neutral in the long run.
