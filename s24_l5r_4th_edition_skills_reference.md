# 24. L5R 4th Edition Skills Reference

All rolls use Trait + Skill format — the Trait determines dice rolled, the Ring determines dice kept. The Ring itself is almost never rolled directly. Skills are divided into four official categories: High Skills, Bugei Skills, Merchant Skills, and Low Skills.

## 24.0 Skills System Overview — LOCKED

**Skill Categories:**

- High Skills: Exclusively the province of the samurai caste. Used at daimyo's courts and the Emperor's court. Reflect sophistication and nobility. Require considerable education.

- Bugei Skills: The domain of the bushi — skills of a warrior. Samurai are not the only practitioners (ashigaru and budoka learn some) but are unquestionably the masters.

- Merchant Skills: Knowledge of practices not belonging to the samurai. Not inherently dishonorable but somewhat questionable — those who practice openly are looked upon with mild disdain.

- Low Skills: Deplorable crafts and practices no honorable soul would take up. Using these Skills usually results in Honor loss. Public use risks Glory loss as well.

**Emphases:**

Individual Skills may cover different activities — each distinct aspect is called an Emphasis. Emphases cost 2 Experience Points each. Number of Emphases allowed per Skill scales with Skill rank: 1 Emphasis at Rank 1, 2 at Rank 3, 3 at Rank 5, 4 at Rank 7, maximum 5 at Rank 9. Emphases are noted in parentheses after the Skill name (e.g. Kyujutsu (Yumi) 3).

- When making a Skill Roll with a relevant Emphasis: immediately re-roll any dice that resulted in a 1. This may only be done once per roll — dice that roll a 1 on the re-roll are not re-rolled again.

**Mastery Abilities:**

Traits are often considered more important than Skills in the Roll & Keep system — Traits determine both how many dice are rolled and how many are kept. Skills compensate through Mastery Abilities — additional benefits gained at higher ranks.

- Most Skills gain Mastery Abilities at Ranks 3, 5, and 7. Some Skills gain a single Mastery Ability at Rank 5 only.

- Mastery Abilities only apply when the actual Skill in question is being used, unless otherwise specified.

- All Skills possess a single universal Mastery Ability at Rank 10: one Free Raise on all rolls made using that Skill.

## 24.1 High Skills

Exclusively the province of the samurai caste. Used in courts and refined settings. Reflect sophistication and nobility.

**Acting (Awareness):**

Sub-types: Social Skill. Acting is considered a Perform Skill for the purposes of effects that target Perform Skills. Emphases: Clan, Gender, Profession.

Ability to convincingly pass oneself off as someone else — in theater or infiltration. Represents knowledge of Kabuki (flamboyant) and Noh (subdued) theater styles.

- Base TN to disguise: 15, assuming same Clan, gender, and profession. Each changed feature adds +5 TN. Impersonating a specific individual adds +15 TN total.

- Raises on the roll make the disguise harder to see through — Investigation / Perception vs TN 15 + 5 per Raise.

- Rank 3: TN to create disguise −5. Rank 5: −10 total. Rank 7: −15 total.

**Courtier (Awareness):**

Sub-types: Social Skill. Emphases: Gossip, Manipulation, Rhetoric.

The social offense skill of Rokugani court. Outmaneuvering opponents, influencing views, steering conversations, winning arguments, delivering put-downs. Gossip emphasis spreads and obtains rumors.

- Gossip TN: 10 + target's Glory × 5 − your Glory × 5. Raises can conceal you as the source.

- Rank 3: +3 Insight. Rank 5: +1k0 to all Contested Rolls using Courtier. Rank 7: +7 Insight total.

**Etiquette (Awareness):**

Sub-types: Social Skill. Emphases: Bureaucracy, Conversation, Courtesy.

The social defense skill. Resists manipulation and temptation. Courtesy allows offering opinions without offense and remembering social mores. Bureaucracy navigates governmental channels. Conversation finds appropriate discussion topics.

- Contested against Courtier, Temptation, and other social offensive skills.

- Rank 3: +3 Insight. Rank 5: +1k0 to all Contested Rolls using Etiquette. Rank 7: +7 Insight total.

**Perform (Varies):**

Sub-types: Macro-Skill, Social Skill. Includes: Biwa (Agility), Dance (Agility), Drums (Agility), Flute (Agility), Oratory (Awareness), Puppeteer (Agility), Samisen (Agility), Song (Awareness), Storytelling (Awareness), and others.

Performers maintain Rokugani culture. Musicians, orators, and actors are lauded in court. TN determined by audience mood and GM judgment.

- No Mastery Abilities.

Performance skill scope (_performance_skill): All Perform sub-skills qualify as _performance_skill for PERFORM_FOR and PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE. In addition, three Artisan sub-skills qualify: Artisan: Poetry (recitation is a live performance experienced in the moment), Artisan: Ikebana (a formalized demonstration where the act of arrangement is itself the aesthetic event), and Artisan: Origami (the act of folding in front of an audience is a live performance — the figure emerges from flat paper in real time, and the audience experiences the creation as it happens). All other Artisan sub-skills — Painting, Sculpture, Bonsai, Tattooing, Gardening — produce objects rather than performances and do not qualify. Finished Artisan items enter the court layer through DELIVER_GIFT or as props in school technique rolls, not through PERFORM_FOR or PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE. See Annex D (_performance_skill variable definition).

**Sincerity (Awareness):**

Sub-types: Social Skill. Emphases: Honesty, Deceit (Low Skill).

Convincing delivery of both truth and lies. Honesty for speaking truth convincingly. Deceit for lying while appearing truthful. Can avoid giving insult through careful phrasing. Opposed by Investigation.

- Rank 5: +5 to all Contested Rolls using Sincerity.

**Tea Ceremony (Void):**

Sub-types: None. Emphasis: None.

Ancient sacred tradition created by Lady Doji. Formal ritual restoring Void Points. Requires complete silence and a quiet location. Intended for two people, up to five total.

- Roll: Tea Ceremony / Void vs TN 15 + 5 per participant after the second. Success: all participants regain 1 Void Point. Takes 30 minutes.

- Rank 5: All participants regain 2 Void Points instead of 1.

**Calligraphy (Intelligence):**

Sub-types: Considered an Artisan Skill. Emphases: Cipher, High Rokugani.

Art form used by shugenja, artisans, and courtiers. Cipher emphasis — one clan cipher known, TN to break equals roll result used to create it. High Rokugani for the florid style of the Imperial Court. Directly relevant to the Game of Letters system.

- Rank 5: +10 when attempting to break a code or cipher.

**Divination (Intelligence):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Astrology, Kawaru.

Glimpsing fate through astrology or kawaru (carved stones thrown into a bowl). Results are vague — indistinct inklings rather than specific prophecy.

- Once per day. TN 15 (GM may increase). Second attempt immediately for a Void Point (TN +10).

- Rank 5: Second roll may be made without spending a Void Point.

**Investigation (Perception):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Interrogation, Notice, Search.

Assessing surroundings and gathering information from physical clues or extended inquiry. Can be re-rolled on failure after one hour at +15 TN. Locating hidden targets is Contested vs Stealth. Interrogation checks for lying vs Sincerity.

- Rank 3: Second Search attempt without TN increase. Rank 5: +5 to all Contested Rolls using Investigation. Rank 7: Third Search attempt even if second fails.

**Lore (Intelligence):**

Sub-types: Macro-skill. Includes: Anatomy*, Architecture, Bushido, Great Clan (choose one), Elements, Gaijin Culture, Ghosts, Heraldry, History, Maho*, Nature, Nonhuman Culture, Omens, Shadowlands*, Shugenja, Spirit Realms, Theology, Underworld*, War, and others. (* = Low Skills).

Scholarly knowledge of a chosen subject. Used to determine if a character possesses relevant information. History and Heraldry are most common and useful. Directly relevant to the Impress court action (Intelligence + Lore).

- No Mastery Abilities.

**Medicine (Intelligence):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Antidotes, Disease, Herbalism, Non-Humans, Wound Treatment.

Treatment of injuries and ailments. Wound Treatment (TN 15) removes 1k1 Wounds — each Raise adds a die rolled but only one die ever kept. One roll per person per day. Requires a Medicine Kit (10 uses). Antidotes: treating a poisoned character requires Medicine (Antidotes) / Intelligence against the poison’s Heal TN (listed per poison in Section 57.48). Success alleviates or ends the poison’s effects as described in each poison’s Heal entry. One treatment attempt per poisoned character per poison instance. Does not require a Medicine Kit — antidote preparation uses the healer’s own herbal knowledge and locally available materials.

- Rank 5: Amount healed increased by +1k0.

**Meditation (Void):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Fasting, Void Recovery.

Spiritual practice for recovering Void Points. Void Recovery (TN 20) recovers 1 Void Point after 30 minutes of uninterrupted meditation. Fasting ignores effects of going without food or water.

- Rank 3: Restores up to 2 Void Points. Rank 5: Fasting TN −5. Rank 7: Restores up to 3 Void Points.

**Spellcraft (Intelligence):**

Sub-types: Importune emphasis is a Social Skill when interacting with kami. Emphases: Importune, Spell Research.

Advanced shugenja knowledge. Spell Research formulates new spells: TN 10 + Mastery Level × 10, requiring weeks of study. Importune used as Social Skill when conversing with spirits.

- Rank 5: +1k0 on Spell Casting Rolls.

**Artisan (Awareness):**

Sub-types: Macro-skill. Includes: Bonsai, Gardening, Ikebana, Origami, Painting, Poetry, Sculpture, Tattooing, and others. Emphases vary by sub-skill.

Talent in one of the noble arts of Rokugani society. Artisan occupations are honorable pursuits. Skill Rolls create works of art with variable TNs determined by GM. Directly relevant to gift-giving and court performance systems.

- No Mastery Abilities.

Note: Artisan: Poetry pairs with Awareness for all standard item creation (CRAFT — Section 49, Artisan Track). When Poetry is used for theatrical composition (COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE), the governing Trait is Intelligence rather than Awareness — theatrical structure is an intellectual construction distinct from lyrical expression. See Section 57.22.5.

**Games (Various):**

Sub-types: Macro-skill. Includes: Fortunes & Winds (Awareness), Go (Intelligence), Kemari (Agility), Letters (Awareness), Sadane (Awareness), Shogi (Intelligence), and others.

Games are an important samurai diversion. Popular games include Kemari (ball-kicking), Go (strategic stones), Letters (elaborate written correspondence game), Shogi (chess-like strategy), and Sadane (impromptu criticism trading). Typically Contested Rolls between opponents.

- No Mastery Abilities.

- Note: Letters is a Games sub-skill (Awareness) — directly relevant to the Game of Letters diplomatic system.

## 24.2 Bugei Skills

Bugei Skills are the martial skills of Rokugan — combat, movement, and warfare. Primary skills of Bushi characters. All weapon skills use Agility as the primary Trait unless otherwise noted.

**Athletics (Strength):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Climbing, Running, Swimming, Throwing.

Physical conditioning and athletic capability. Covers climbing, running, swimming with greater ease than standard Trait rolls. Throwing Emphasis allows throwing items and weapons accurately — throwing a weapon offensively is an attack roll using Agility.

- Rank 3: Moderate Terrain no longer impedes movement. Difficult Terrain reduces Water Ring by 1 instead of 2.

- Rank 5: No movement penalties regardless of terrain.

- Rank 7: Add 5 feet to one Move Action per Round.

- ASCII map interpretation: this means +1 tile to one specific Move Action when multiple are taken in the same Round. The Round’s maximum total movement is unchanged.

- Climbing: mechanical rules for climbing on the ASCII map are in Section 4.5.6. Climbing uses Athletics (Climbing) / Strength with TNs from 10 (rope / ladder) to 30 (overhanging rock or a creature’s gullet). The Athletics Mastery Abilities at Rank 3, 5, and 7 apply to overland movement only — they do not reduce climb TNs.

**Battle (Perception):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Mass Combat, Skirmish.

The study of war — tactics, strategy, maneuvers. Highly regarded among all samurai castes. Directly relevant to our Army Combat System (Section 11.7) — commander Battle skill rank determines their bonus value.

- Rank 5: Character adds Battle Skill Rank to Initiative Score during Skirmishes.

**Defense (Reflexes):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: None.

Positioning blade and body to deflect incoming blows. Essential for Defense and Full Defense stances — both increase Armor TN.

- Rank 3: May retain previous Defense roll result rather than re-rolling in Full Defense Stance.

- Rank 5: Armor TN considered 3 higher in Defense and Full Defense Stances.

- Rank 7: One Simple Action may be taken while in Full Defense Stance (no attacks).

**Horsemanship (Agility):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Gaijin Riding Horse, Rokugani Pony, Utaku Steed.

Mounted combat and travel. All samurai can ride at normal speed on level ground without a roll — skill required only in stressful situations (bad terrain, storms, combat). Unicorn Clan considers inability to ride a crippling disability.

- Rank 3: May use Full Attack Stance on horseback.

- Rank 5: Mounting is a Simple Action, dismounting a Free Action.

- Rank 7: Mounting is a Free Action.

**Hunting (Perception):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Survival, Tracking.

Survival knowledge associated with the samurai sport of hunting. Survival forages for sustenance and shelter (TN varies by environment). Tracking locates and follows prey.

- Rank 5: +1k0 to all Stealth Skill Rolls in wilderness environments.

- Tracking: mechanical rules for tracking on the ASCII map are in Section 4.5.9. Tracking uses Hunting (Tracking) / Perception with TNs from 15 (same-day trail) to 30 (week-old), modified by terrain and weather.

**Iaijutsu (Reflexes):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Assessment, Focus.

The formal dueling system of Rokugan. When disputes cannot be resolved otherwise, iaijutsu duels determine who is correct. The winner is proven right — outcomes cannot be contested barring dishonorable conduct. Directly relevant to the duel system flagged throughout the GDD.

- Rank 3: Readying a katana is a Free Action.

- Rank 5: One Free Raise on Iaijutsu (Focus) / Void roll during the Focus Stage of a duel.

- Rank 7: +2k2 to Focus Rolls if Assessment exceeds opponent's by 10 or more (instead of normal +1k1).

**Jiujutsu (Agility):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Grappling, Improvised Weapons, Martial Arts.

Unarmed combat. Can be used as a Weapon Skill — roll Jiujutsu / Agility vs Armor TN, damage = Strength dice keeping 1. Also used for initiating and maintaining Grapples. Improvised Weapons Emphasis uses almost anything as a weapon.

- Rank 3: Unarmed damage +1k0. Rank 5: Free Raise toward initiating a Grapple. Rank 7: Unarmed damage +0k1 (+1k1 total).

**Kenjutsu (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Katana, Ninja-to, No-dachi, Parangu, Scimitar, Wakizashi.

The path of the sword — essential training for all bushi. The katana is mandatory training at virtually every dojo. Represents a warrior's soul.

- Rank 3: All sword damage rolls +1k0. Rank 5: Sword readied as Free Action. Rank 7: Damage dice explode on 9 and 10.

**Kyujutsu (Reflexes):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Dai-kyu, Han-kyu, Yumi.

The art of the bow. Rokugani archery does not rely on aiming — practitioners assess target then draw and fire as one fluid motion. −10 penalty to attack when firing at targets within melee range.

- Rank 3: Stringing bow is Simple Action. Rank 5: Maximum range increased 50%. Rank 7: Bow Strength increased by 1.

**Spears (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Mai Chong, Kumade, Lance, Nage-yari, Yari.

Yarijutsu — the art of the spear. Primarily thrusting weapons, extremely effective against cavalry. Traditional in many bushi families, notably the Daidoji of the Crane. Directly relevant to our Ashigaru Spearmen unit type (+3 Attack vs Cavalry).

- Rank 3: Ignore 3 points of Reduction against opponents in first round. Rank 5: Ranged range +5'. Rank 7: Ready as Free Action.

**Polearms (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Bisento, Nagamaki, Naginata, Sasumata, Sodegarami.

Long-reaching bladed weapons mounted on poles — between heavy weapons and katana in style. Keep opponents at bay while dealing damage. Sometimes called umayarijutsu.

- Rank 3: +5 Initiative in first round of skirmish. Rank 5: +1k0 damage vs mounted or significantly larger opponents. Rank 7: Ready as Free Action.

**Heavy Weapons (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Dai Tsuchi, Masakari, Ono, Tetsubo.

Hallmark of Crab Clan bushi. The tetsubo and ono are essential for defeating Shadowlands creatures that a katana cannot harm. Rarely seen in other clan armies.

- Rank 3: Opponent Reduction reduced by 2. Rank 5: Free Raise toward Knockdown. Rank 7: Damage dice explode on 9 and 10.

**Knives (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Aiguchi, Jitte, Kama, Sai, Tanto.

Tantojutsu — virtually every bushi is trained in knife use and carries one at all times. Not a priority weapon but universally present.

- Rank 3: No off-hand penalties with knives. Rank 5: Sai/jitte — Free Raise toward Disarm. Rank 7: Free Raise toward Extra Attack.

**War Fan (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: None.

Tessen — metal fans originally created for battlefield signaling. Used as weapons particularly by Lion Clan and the Tessen ronin group of Toshi Ranbo.

- Rank 3: No off-hand penalties. Rank 5: Armor TN +1. Rank 7: Armor TN +3.

**Chain Weapons (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Kusarigama, Kyoketsu-shofi, Manrikikusari.

Unconventional but accepted weapons. Higher use among the Mantis Clan who favor exotic weapons.

- Rank 3: Can initiate Grapple with chain weapons. Rank 5: +1k0 on Contested Rolls vs entangled/Grappled opponents. Rank 7: Free Raise toward Disarm or Knockdown.

**Staves (Agility):**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill. Emphases: Bo, Jo, Machi-kanshisha, Sang Kauw, Tonfa.

More common among monks and peasants than bushi. Armor bonuses to Armor TN are doubled against staff attacks.

- Rank 3: Armor bonus no longer doubled against staff attacks. Rank 5: Free Raise toward Knockdown. Rank 7: Large staves ready as Free Action; small staves +1k0 damage.

**Ninjutsu (Agility or Reflexes) — Low Skill:**

Sub-types: Weapon Skill, Low Skill. Emphases: Blowgun, Shuriken, Tsubute.

A disgraceful practice in the eyes of honorable warriors. Specialized weapons of ninja and shadow warriors. No honorable samurai would publicly demonstrate knowledge of this skill.

- Rank 3: Ninjutsu weapon damage +1k0. Rank 5: Damage dice explode normally (they do not normally). Rank 7: +0k1 additional damage.

## 24.3 Merchant Skills

Merchant Skills cover commerce, craftsmanship, and practical trade. Generally considered beneath the samurai caste but not inherently dishonorable — necessary for overseeing the practical workings of a domain.

**Animal Handling (Awareness):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: By animal (Dogs, Horses, Falcons most common).

Training and commanding animals vital to samurai endeavors — recreation, transportation, and war. Lion Clan uses warcats, Unicorn uses war dogs, many families use trained pigeons for correspondence. Difficulty varies widely by animal type.

- Rank 3: Commonly domesticated animals such as dogs, horses, or falcons may be trained for use by others.

- Rank 5: Trained animals still in possession of the trainer may be commanded to attack a chosen target. The animal flees if badly wounded regardless of commands.

- Rank 7: Animals trained by the character will not flee from combat even when badly wounded. The Rank 5 default flee-when-wounded behaviour is overridden.

**Commerce (Intelligence):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Appraisal, Mathematics.

Distasteful but necessary for samurai overseeing domain finances. Day-to-day trading left to merchant vassals — samurai practitioners are called 'merchant patrons.' Appraisal determines object value in any market. Mathematics calculates taxes, tariffs, and other financial figures. Directly relevant to our Trade Route and Koku systems.

- Rank 5: May increase or decrease the price of an item being bought or sold by up to 20%.

**Craft (Varies):**

Sub-types: Macro-skill. Includes: Armorsmithing#, Blacksmithing, Bowyer#, Brewing, Carpentry, Cartography, Cobbling, Cooking, Farming, Fishing, Masonry, Mining, Poison*, Pottery, Shipbuilding, Tailoring, Weaponsmithing#, Weaving, and others. (* = Low Skill. # = High Skill). Emphases vary by sub-skill.

Craftsmen create things of practical value — blacksmiths, armorsmiths, carpenters, shipwrights, farmers, fishermen. Valued and looked upon without stigma though less prestigious than artisan work. Directly relevant to our Arms production and Iron resource systems.

- No Mastery Abilities.

*Design note:* Four Craft sub-skills are mechanically active in the game: Weaponsmithing, Armorsmithing, and Bowyer operate through the Crafting System (Section 49) with full quality tier pipelines (Normal, Fine, Exceptional, Sacred), material rules, and equipment system integration (Section 39). Poison operates through the Poison System (Section 57.48) — Craft: Poison / Intelligence produces poison doses as consumable items with creation residue and detection mechanics. All other Craft sub-skills — Brewing, Carpentry, Cartography, Cobbling, Cooking, Farming, Fishing, Masonry, Mining, Pottery, Shipbuilding, Tailoring, Weaving — exist as character background flavor. They appear on character sheets, define what a character does for a living, and may be referenced in NPC descriptions or school prerequisites, but they have no dedicated game mechanics. Farming, Mining, and Fishing are handled at the PU abstraction level (Sections 4.3.4–4.3.8) rather than through individual character skill rolls. Implementers should not build mechanical systems for the flavor-only sub-skills.

**Engineering (Intelligence):**

Sub-types: Considered a Craft Skill. Emphases: Construction, Siege.

Rare and complex gift — understanding how things are constructed and how they can be deconstructed. The Kaiu family of the Crab Clan are unquestioned masters. Construction builds from towers to palaces. Siege Emphasis is the exact opposite — how to destroy such things. Directly relevant to our Siege System (Section 11.7).

- Rank 5: +5 to any Engineering Skill Roll made as part of a Cooperative or Cumulative Skill Roll.

**Sailing (Agility or Intelligence):**

Sub-types: Considered a Craft Skill. Emphases: Knot-work, Navigation.

Rokugan has a vast coastline with heavy trade and fishing activity. Mantis Clan ships make long voyages to the Islands of Silk and Spice even in winter. Navigation determines location via stars and direction to known destinations (land use +10 TN). Knot-work handles ropes and sails. Directly relevant to our Naval Trade system (flagged as deferred).

- Rank 5: +5 to any Sailing Skill Roll made as part of a Cooperative or Cumulative Skill Roll.

## 24.4 Low Skills

Low Skills are dishonorable or base skills — activities considered beneath the samurai caste or outright shameful. Possessing and using these skills carries social stigma and Honor loss in most circumstances.

**Forgery (Agility):**

Sub-types: Considered a Craft Skill. Emphases: By type of item forged (Artwork, Documents, Personal Seals, etc.).

Falsifying documents, seals, and official decrees. Documentation is vital in Rokugan — travel papers, lineage records, and Imperial Decrees can all be forged. The result of the Forgery / Agility roll sets the TN for Investigation / Perception to detect it. Directly relevant to our Secret System and court intelligence actions.

- Rank 3: +1k0 to Forgery roll result for purposes of setting detection TN.

- Rank 5: +1k0 on any roll to detect a forgery made by someone else.

- Rank 7: +0k1 (total +1k1) to Forgery roll result for purposes of setting detection TN.

**Intimidation (Willpower):**

Sub-types: Social Skill. Emphases: Bullying, Control, Torture.

Manipulation through threats — stated and implied. Bullying uses immediate threat of violence for short-term compliance. Control shapes behavior long-term through sustained pressure. Torture is used by hinin working for magistrates — no proper samurai tortures in person. Resisted by Etiquette (Courtesy) / Willpower — the resisting character adds their Honor Rank to the roll. Directly relevant to our Intimidate court action.

- Rank 5: +5 to all Contested Rolls using Intimidation.

**Sleight of Hand (Agility):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Conceal, Escape, Pick Pocket, Prestidigitation.

Dexterous manipulation of small objects for misdirection or concealment. Conceal hides small objects on person — Contested vs Investigation / Perception. Escape slips bonds (TN 15–30 depending on restraints, or Contested vs Sailing Knot-work). Pick Pocket uses same Contested roll as Conceal. Prestidigitation entertains or distracts.

- Rank 5: Conceal Emphasis may be used to hide small weapons.

**Stealth (Agility):**

Sub-types: None. Emphases: Ambush, Shadowing, Sneaking, Spell Casting.

Moving unseen and unheard. While concealed, only Simple Move Actions allowed — movement equal to Water Ring in feet. Sneaking causes no Honor loss. Ambush gains combat advantage in first round — Contested vs Investigation (Notice) / Perception. Spell Casting Emphasis conceals spell origin. Relevant to siege reconnaissance and assassination systems.

- Rank 3: Simple Move Actions while Stealthed allow movement equal to Water × 5 feet.

- Rank 5: Simple Move Actions while Stealthed allow movement equal to Water × 10 feet.

- Rank 7: Free Move Actions may be taken normally while Stealthed.

**Temptation (Awareness):**

Sub-types: Social Skill. Emphases: Bribery, Seduction.

Offering what others desire to gain what you desire. Bribery and Seduction are the two primary tools. Most effective against targets with Disadvantages such as Greed, Lechery, or Dark Secret. Resisted by Etiquette (Courtesy) / Willpower. Directly relevant to our court manipulation systems.

- Rank 5: +5 to all Contested Rolls using Temptation.
