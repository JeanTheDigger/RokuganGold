# 53. War Status System

The War Status System defines the formal state of armed conflict between clans, tracks each side's military position through a War Score, and governs the chain of authority through which wars are declared, escalated, and ended. It connects directly to the Crisis Tier system (Section 16), the Court System (Section 15), the Named Character System (Section 22), and the Honor/Glory/Status system (Section 4.6).

**WHAT IS WAR STATUS**

War Status is a formal tracked state between two or more clans. It is not the same as violence — provinces raid each other, bandits attack merchants, and skirmishes happen without triggering War Status. War Status is declared, recognized, and ended through formal mechanisms. It has specific mechanical consequences for trade, movement, court access, and NPC behavior that do not apply to informal violence.

War Status exists at different levels of the feudal hierarchy simultaneously. A Provincial Lord can be at war with a neighbor without their Clan Champion being involved. A Family Daimyo can be at war without it becoming a full Clan War. Each level has different authority, different consequences, and different mechanisms for resolution.

**THE ESCALATION AUTHORITY CHAIN**

Every war has an authority level that determines who can declare it, who can negotiate it, and who can end it. Wars escalate up the chain when lower authorities cannot contain the conflict.

- Provincial Raid (Tier 3): One Provincial Lord attacks another's territory. Only the two lords involved have authority. The conflict is handled directly between them. Family Daimyo are not yet involved — this is below their threshold unless it grows.

- Border Conflict (Tier 3 trending Tier 2): Raids escalate across multiple provinces. The Family Daimyo becomes aware and may intervene. The Family Daimyo can de-escalate the conflict or formally endorse it. Peace can still be negotiated between the Provincial Lords directly, but the Family Daimyo now has standing to participate.

- Family War (Tier 2): The Family Daimyo formally endorses the conflict. The entire family is now committed. Only the Family Daimyo or their designated proxy can make binding peace commitments on behalf of the family.

- Clan War (Tier 2): The Clan Champion formally declares war or officially endorses the conflict. The entire clan is now involved. Only the Clan Champion or their designated proxy can speak for the clan at peace courts. The Emperor may intervene at this level.

**ESCALATION TRIGGERS**

A conflict automatically escalates to the next authority level when any of the following conditions are met:

- The Provincial Lord's War Score drops below 25 (into the Losing tier) and they formally request aid from their Family Daimyo.

- Enemy forces enter provinces belonging to other families in the region — the conflict has spread beyond its original parties.

- The Provincial Lord's castle falls — a loss of that magnitude demands Family response.

- The conflict has lasted more than 3 IC seasons (approximately 10 OOC days) without resolution.

- The enemy is revealed to have allied with another clan — the threat level increases beyond what a single lord can address.

Escalation is not automatic in all cases — it requires the lower authority to formally request aid OR for the conditions to be so obvious that the higher authority intervenes on their own initiative based on their personality and objectives.

**THE WAR SCORE**

When War Status is formally established between two parties, both sides receive a War Score starting at 50. The War Score tracks each side's military position — it is not a relative measure, but an absolute one. Both sides can be above 50 (a hard-fought war with honors even) or both below 50 (a war that has ground both sides down). The score ranges from 0 to 100.

**War Score Shifts**

The following events shift War Scores. The winning party gains points, the losing party loses points:

- Minor battle victory/defeat: +3 / −3. Small engagement with limited forces.

- Major battle victory/defeat: +8 / −8. Significant engagement involving multiple companies.

- Decisive battle victory/defeat: +15 / −15. A battle that defines the campaign's direction.

- Province captured: +5 / −5 per province taken.

- Castle captured: +10 / −10. More significant than a plain province.

- Siege won — attacker: +12 / −8. Attacker gains more — an offensive achievement against a defended position.

- Siege repelled — defender: +8 / −5. Defender gains — held the line against assault.

- Named Gunso or Chui killed: +2 / −2.

- Named Taisa or Shireikan killed: +5 / −5. Significant commander loss.

- Rikugunshokan (General) killed: +10 / −10. A major strategic blow.

- Hostage taken (Rank 3 or higher): +3 / −3. Political leverage gained.

- Hostage taken (Rank 5+ or Champion's family member): +8 / −8. Major political leverage.

- Lord assassinated during active War Status (any lordship tier): +12 / −12. Disrupts the entire political and economic chain — triggers the succession process (Section 22.5) on top of the military impact. Exceeds a general killed (+10) because the consequences cascade through the tax system, Koku flow, vassal objectives, and potentially triggers a Succession Dispute crisis.

- Supply line cut: +3 / −3. Logistics disruption.

- Attrition (per IC season of active war): +1 / −1. The attacker gains slightly — sustained pressure is itself an achievement.

**War Score Interpretation**

- 80–100 (Dominant): Crushing victory. The opponent is broken. The dominant side can demand almost anything at the peace court.

- 65–79 (Winning): Clear advantage. Strong negotiating position. Unlikely to accept unfavorable terms.

- 50–64 (Ahead): Modest advantage. Open to negotiation but from a position of strength.

- 40–49 (Even or Behind): Near parity or slight disadvantage. Stalemate territory. The most fertile ground for negotiation.

- 25–39 (Losing): Clear disadvantage. Pressure to accept terms mounts. Personality and circumstances drive whether the lord accepts or fights on.

- 0–24 (Desperate): Catastrophic position. Most lords accept peace on almost any terms. Honor-driven or stubborn lords may still refuse — at great cost to their clan.

**War Score and Higher Authority**

When a higher authority formally joins the conflict, the War Score adjusts to reflect the boost in morale and resources:

- Family Daimyo commits forces to a Provincial Lord's war: +5 to the defending side's War Score.

- Clan Champion formally declares and commits the clan: +10 to the defending side's War Score.

- Allied clan joins the conflict: +8 per allied clan that formally commits forces.

**PEACE WILLINGNESS**

Peace willingness is not determined by a single threshold. A lord's willingness to accept peace terms is contextual — shaped by their War Score, the specific terms being demanded, their personality, and their circumstances. There is no score at which peace is automatic.

Factors that increase willingness to accept peace:

- War Score below 25 — Losing or Desperate. The pragmatic case for peace grows overwhelming.

- Terms do not require ceding currently held territory — a lord can accept peace without formal loss of face.

- A hostage from the lord's own family is held by the enemy.

- The Clan Champion or Family Daimyo is pressuring them to accept terms.

- Shourido virtue: Seigyo (Control) or Chishiki (Knowledge) — pragmatic lords accept tactical retreats.

- Bushido virtue: Gi (Honesty) or Makoto (Sincerity) — an honest accounting of the situation overrides pride.

Factors that decrease willingness to accept peace:

- Terms require formally ceding provinces that were held at the start of the war — formalizing territorial loss is deeply shameful even if the territory is already occupied by the enemy.

- War Score above 65 — winning. Why accept terms when victory is in reach?

- Bushido virtue: Yu (Courage) — fights longer than is tactically wise. Honor demands it.

- Shourido virtue: Determination or Will — cannot be redirected once committed.

- The lord's family has a significant historical loss connected to this enemy — revenge overrides pragmatism.

**THE HONOR STAKES OF ESCALATION**

Asking for help up the feudal chain is an admission of weakness. In Rokugan, admitting you cannot handle a threat alone is publicly shameful — it implies inadequacy. The honor loss is automatic and immediate regardless of whether the request is ultimately successful. However, the amount of shame scales with how overwhelmed the asker actually is — there is less shame in acknowledging an obviously impossible situation than in crying for help at the first sign of difficulty.

Honor loss for asking for aid (automatic, public):

- War Score 40–49 (slight disadvantage): −0.5 Honor. Minimal — a close fight, but asking looks like mild weakness.

- War Score 25–39 (Losing): −1.0 Honor. The worst position. Losing but not yet overwhelmed — asking at this stage looks like cowardice.

- War Score 0–24 (Desperate): No Honor loss. Survival overrides pride. No one expects a drowning man to refuse a rope.

The counterintuitive result is intentional: maximum shame falls on lords who ask for help when they are losing but not yet desperate. A lord who calls for aid at War Score 30 when they still have provinces and forces intact looks like they gave up. A lord who calls for aid at War Score 10 with their castle under siege is simply being realistic.

**THE HONOR STAKES OF REFUSING**

Refusing to help a vassal who has formally requested aid is one of the most dishonorable acts available to a Rokugani lord. The feudal bond is built on the exchange of service and protection — a lord who takes his vassal's taxes, levies, and loyalty and then abandons them in their hour of need has violated the fundamental contract of Rokugan's society.

Honor loss for refusing a vassal's formal request for aid:

- Family Daimyo refuses a Provincial Lord: −2.0 Honor. Massive — abandoning a sworn vassal.

- Clan Champion refuses a Family Daimyo: −3.0 Honor. Catastrophic — abandoning the clan.

- Additional loss if territory falls afterward: −2.0 Honor on top of the refusal penalty. The failure is made manifest.

Disposition consequences of refusing (applied immediately upon refusal becoming known):

- All direct vassals of the refusing lord: −15 disposition. They know they cannot count on their lord.

- All members of the abandoned lord's family: −20 disposition. Personal betrayal.

- Neighboring lords of all clans: −5 disposition. Word spreads — an untrustworthy lord is a danger to everyone.

- The Imperial Court if the refusal becomes known: −10 disposition. Shirking feudal duty is a public shame.

A lord who repeatedly refuses vassals will find their own position eroding — vassals begin seeking other patrons, allies grow distant, and enemies grow bold. The honor system enforces the feudal bond mechanically.

**ENDING WAR STATUS**

War Status ends through any of the following mechanisms:

- Formal surrender: One side's Clan Champion (or designated proxy at the relevant authority level) formally yields at a peace court. The war ends immediately. Terms are negotiated and binding.

- Negotiated settlement at a peace court: Both sides reach mutually acceptable terms through the court system. The most common resolution — pure military victory is rare.

- Imperial edict: The Emperor issues a binding order to cease hostilities. Both sides are legally obligated to comply. Violation of an Imperial peace edict is one of the gravest offenses in Rokugan.

- Annihilation: One side is militarily destroyed — their forces eliminated and their provinces occupied. Rare. The winning side's War Score is irrelevant at this point.

When War Status ends, the War Score at the moment of peace directly informs what terms can be demanded and what the losing side is willing to accept. A peace court between a Dominant side (80+) and a Desperate side (below 25) is a surrender negotiation. A peace court between two Even sides (both near 50) is a genuine negotiation where either side might gain or lose depending on courtier skill.

**MECHANICAL EFFECTS OF WAR STATUS**

While War Status is active between two clans, the following mechanical effects apply:

- Trade routes between the two clans are suspended. Materials from one clan's territory do not flow to the other's markets.

- Travel papers between the two clans are revoked. Characters cannot freely move through enemy territory.

- Court invitations between the two clans are suspended at the relevant authority level — a Provincial Lord at war cannot invite the enemy Provincial Lord to a social court.

- Disposition between all named characters of the two clans receives a passive negative modifier each IC season the war continues — prolonged conflict breeds generational hatred.

- Characters of the two clans in the same settlement cannot engage in daily conversation (Section 12.6) — they are enemies.

**ALLIANCE WARS**

When a clan formally calls allies into a war, the conflict becomes an Alliance War. Alliances operate on the same escalation chain — only a Clan Champion can commit their clan as an ally. A Family Daimyo cannot pledge the whole clan to another's war without Champion authorization.

Alliance structure:

- One clan is designated the Main Clan — the originator of the war or the clan whose territory is being defended. The Main Clan's Champion holds final authority over the alliance's war goals and peace negotiations.

- Allied clans fight under the Main Clan's banner. Their forces contribute to the War Score of the Main Clan's side. When the Main Clan's Champion accepts peace, the war ends for all allied clans simultaneously — unless an ally invokes the separate peace clause.

- The Main Clan cannot commit allied forces to terms without their consent. An ally's provincial forces are theirs to command — the Main Clan Champion cannot order an allied lord's troops directly. Coordination requires agreement.

Separate peace clause:

- If an allied clan is directly attacked on their own territory during the war — not as a participant in the main conflict but as a target of opportunistic aggression — they may negotiate a separate peace with their attacker independently of the Main Clan.

- Invoking the separate peace clause requires the allied clan's Champion to formally withdraw from the alliance on that front. This is diplomatically costly — it may damage the alliance relationship — but it is legally permitted.

- A clan that accepts a separate peace loses their War Score contribution to the Main Clan's side and is considered neutral for the remainder of the conflict.

- The Main Clan may feel betrayed by an ally who takes a separate peace. This generates a significant disposition loss between the two clans and may itself become a future casus belli.

**THE FORMAL REQUEST FOR AID**

When a Provincial Lord needs to escalate a conflict to their Family Daimyo, or a Family Daimyo needs to call upon their Clan Champion, they must make a formal request. This request is a deliberate act — it cannot happen accidentally and it cannot be denied without consequences.

The request can be made through three channels:

- In person: The requesting lord travels to their superior and makes the request directly. The most honorable method — it shows the lord takes the matter seriously enough to appear themselves. Fastest resolution — the superior must respond immediately.

- Through a letter: The requesting lord sends a formal written request via the letter system (Section 12.7). Delivery time applies based on geography. The superior has time to consider before responding — but also time to delay, which is itself a form of passive refusal with its own consequences.

- Through a courtier or envoy: The requesting lord sends a designated representative to present the request in person at the superior's court. Requires a courtier with sufficient Status and the formal designation to speak for their lord (Section 15 — Designated Proxy System). The envoy can negotiate terms of the assistance.

Once the request is made, the superior must respond. Silence is not neutral — a superior who does not respond within a reasonable time (one IC season, approximately 22.5 OOC days) is treated as having refused. The honor consequences of refusal apply. The requesting lord then has the standing to escalate further up the chain or seek aid elsewhere.

**WAR SCORE FLOOR AND CEILING**

The War Score is capped at 0 (minimum) and 100 (maximum). A score cannot go below 0 or above 100 regardless of further events.

- A clan at War Score 0 is in complete collapse — every possible military indicator is catastrophic. Further losses do not make the score worse mathematically, but they continue to have narrative and mechanical consequences (more provinces lost, more commanders dead, deeper disposition damage).

- A clan at War Score 100 has achieved total dominance — the enemy is broken in every measurable way. Further victories do not increase the score, but they may affect the peace terms the winning clan can demand.

At War Score 0, the Desperate peace willingness applies regardless of personality — even a Yu (Courage) virtue Clan Champion faces an impossible situation. The system does not force surrender, but the NPC decision-making profile will weigh survival of the clan against personal honor very heavily at this extreme.

**PLAYER CHARACTER INVOLVEMENT IN WARS**

War Status creates two primary roles for player characters: the warrior and the diplomat. Both directly affect War Score and peace outcomes.

The warrior role:

- Player characters who fight in battles during a War Status contribute to the Mass Battle system (Section 11.7) through Heroic Events. A player character's exceptional performance in battle — killing enemy commanders, holding a critical position, rallying broken troops — generates War Score shifts on top of the battle's standard result.

- A player character who kills a named enemy commander generates the commander death War Score shift (+2 to +10 depending on rank) for their side, in addition to the personal Glory gain.

- Player characters can be assigned to sieges, raids, supply line disruption missions, and other military operations — each of which generates War Score shifts when successful.

The diplomat role:

- Player characters can serve as designated proxies for their lord at peace courts (Section 15). A skilled courtier character can negotiate more favorable peace terms than an NPC courtier would achieve alone — their skill rolls directly affect what the losing side is willing to accept.

- Player characters can facilitate the formal request for aid — either as the envoy sent to deliver the request, or as a courtier working to persuade a superior to grant it. A successful Courtier roll on behalf of their lord can reduce the honor loss of asking, or improve the terms of the aid offered.

- Player characters can work to shift War Score through non-combat means — spreading damaging topics about enemy commanders (affecting morale), building alliances at court, or negotiating the defection of enemy vassals.

The war is the context. The player character is the hero within it — their individual actions can swing a war that armies alone cannot decide.

## 53.1 WAR JUSTIFICATION & CASUS BELLI

Before any AI lord initiates military action — from a provincial raid to a full clan war — the system must answer two separate questions. Section 4.3.17 answers: can they sustain it? This subsection answers the prior question: why would they? These are not the same check and must not be collapsed into one. A lord who lacks justification does not run the Feasibility Ledger. There is nothing to assess.

War justification in this system is not a named legal claim in the Crusader Kings sense. Rokugan does not have a formal mechanism for staking territorial titles. Justification is instead a function of the lord’s active objectives — their standing objective and primary objective together determine whether military action is a valid solution to what they are trying to accomplish. If war serves neither objective, the lord does not consider it regardless of personality, resources, or opportunity. Aggression without purpose is beneath a samurai.

The one exception is personality-driven aggression, addressed at the end of this subsection.

**THE THREE TIERS OF MILITARY ACTION**

Military action exists on a spectrum. The justification system treats each tier separately because the threshold for justification rises with the severity of the action. A lord who would never formally declare war might still authorize a raid. A lord who would raid willingly might resist crossing into Border Conflict. The three tiers map onto the escalation chain from the War Status system above:

- Tier 3 — Raid: Informal, targeted, short. A single strike against a specific province or supply line. Does not formally trigger War Status unless it escalates. This is the lowest threshold for military action and the most common.

- Tier 2 — Formal War: A declared, sustained campaign with War Status established at the appropriate authority level. Carries full mechanical consequences — trade suspended, travel revoked, disposition damage accumulating each season.

- Tier 1 — Total War: A Clan War formally endorsed by Clan Champions. The entire clan is committed. Reserved for existential objectives or catastrophic provocations.

Each objective entry below specifies which tiers it can justify. An objective that justifies only raiding does not justify formal war declaration — the AI will not escalate past the tier the objective supports unless a separate escalation trigger fires (per the Escalation Authority Chain above).

**STANDING OBJECTIVES THAT JUSTIFY MILITARY ACTION**

Standing objectives are the lord’s deep, persistent drives. When a standing objective makes war a valid tool, that validity is persistent — the lord will always consider military options in service of it, subject to the Feasibility Ledger. The following standing objectives (from Section 18.4) justify military action at the tiers noted.

- **Expand my clan’s territorial influence.** Justifies all three tiers. Territory is acquired through force or the credible threat of it. This is the most direct path from standing objective to military action in the game. The lord looks at adjacent provinces belonging to weaker clans and sees opportunity. Raid tier is used to probe defenses or seize a resource stockpile as a precursor to formal annexation. Formal war is declared when the lord is ready to press a territorial claim permanently. Personality gate: Jin (Compassion) lords will pursue this objective but prefer diplomatic expansion — they justify military action only if non-military options have been exhausted or the target has given provocation.

- **Keep my clan’s military the dominant force in the region.** Justifies Tier 3 and Tier 2. A lord with this objective monitors neighboring military strength. If a rival clan’s forces cross a perceived threshold — observable through intelligence, the size of levies raised, or the construction of fortifications — they may initiate military action to disrupt the buildup before it becomes a genuine threat. Raid tier is used to destroy supply stockpiles or eliminate a forming army before it is fully raised. Formal war may be declared if the rival’s military growth represents a sustained challenge to dominance. This objective does not justify Total War on its own — dominance in the region does not require annihilating a clan.

- **Eliminate the Shadowlands threat.** Justifies all three tiers, but only against Jigoku-touched targets. This standing objective never justifies military action against another Great Clan unless that clan has been credibly implicated in Shadowlands corruption — a Tainted lord, a province with a confirmed Jigoku bleed event, or intelligence suggesting maho activity. Against Shadowlands targets directly, this objective is the strongest possible justification — even lords who prefer peace accept that the Taint must be fought.

- **Strengthen the Kaiu Wall and Crab defenses.** Justifies Tier 3 and Tier 2 against Shadowlands targets only. Does not justify military action against other clans unless those clans are actively blocking resource or troop movement to the Wall — which would itself constitute a provocation generating its own primary objective (see primary objective list below).

- **Build the strongest possible fighting force under my command.** Justifies Tier 3 only, and only as a proving exercise — small raids against lightly defended targets to season troops and generate Glory. This objective does not justify formal war. A lord building their military for its own sake does not need a prolonged campaign; they need field experience and a battle reputation. Personality gate: Kyoryoku (Strength) and Ketsui (Determination) lords may use raid-tier action freely for this purpose. All other personality types require a secondary justification before initiating even a raid.

- **Seek vengeance for [historical wrong].** Justifies all three tiers against the specific target of the vengeance. The wrong is recorded in the character’s historical modifier table — the killed family member, the catastrophic betrayal, the public disgrace. Military action against the responsible party is always considered valid by this lord regardless of diplomatic context. They will not raid for resources unprompted, but against the target of their vengeance they will accept almost any pretext. Personality gate: Jin (Compassion) lords still feel the pull of vengeance but resist initiating Total War — they pursue it through formal war or targeted action, not clan-level annihilation. Makoto (Sincerity) lords pursue vengeance openly and declare their intent before striking, which removes the element of surprise but preserves their Honor.

- **Advance personal Glory.** Justifies Tier 3 only. A lord pursuing personal Glory may raid a lightly defended target to generate battle reputation and Glory gains. They are not seeking conquest — they want the narrative. Formal war is too expensive and too slow to be driven purely by a Glory objective; other objectives must be co-present to justify that escalation. Personality gate: Bushi-role characters with Kyoryoku or Ketsui virtues apply this justification most readily. Courtier-role characters with this standing objective pursue Glory through court performance, not military action, and this entry does not apply to them.

- **Undermine [Clan X]’s influence across the Empire.** Justifies Tier 3 and Tier 2 against the named clan. Economic raiding — hitting trade routes, seizing stockpiles, disrupting harvest — directly damages the target clan’s ability to project political influence. Formal war weakens them further. This objective does not justify Total War on its own — a weakened rival is more useful than an annihilated one. Personality gate: Seigyo (Control) and Dosatsu (Insight) lords strongly prefer covert disruption over open military action and will only escalate to raid tier if diplomatic and economic sabotage options are exhausted or unavailable. Gi (Honesty) and Makoto (Sincerity) lords will not pursue this objective through military action without a public, declared justification — covert raids violate their personal code.

- **Ensure my clan never faces resource shortage.** Justifies Tier 3 only, and only when the lord’s own settlements are at Shortage or worse. A lord who is not in shortage does not raid neighbors for preventive stockpiling — that is simple greed, not survival. A lord whose provinces are genuinely short may raid adjacent provinces for Rice, as already described in Section 4.3.17 Rung 6. Formal war is not justified by this objective alone — a prolonged campaign consumes more resources than it recovers and defeats the purpose entirely. Personality gate: Jin (Compassion) and Gi (Honesty) lords will not raid for resources under any circumstances regardless of shortage severity, as established in Section 4.3.17.

**STANDING OBJECTIVES THAT DO NOT JUSTIFY MILITARY ACTION**

The following standing objectives explicitly exclude military action as a valid solution. A lord with one of these objectives will never initiate military action in service of it. If a primary objective derived from one of these standing objectives is military in nature, it reflects a situational necessity — a crisis override — not a standing desire for war.

- Maintain the balance of power — no single clan dominates. War tips the balance rather than preserving it. Initiating military action in pursuit of this objective is a contradiction.

- Maintain peace — avoid war at all costs. A lord with this objective not only declines to initiate military action but actively resists escalation. They will pursue diplomatic resolution even from a position of strength.

- Strengthen the Imperial institution. Unsanctioned war undermines Imperial authority. A lord with this objective will not initiate military action without Imperial endorsement or a formal Imperial mandate. They may fight defensive wars when attacked.

- Accumulate political leverage — favors, secrets, and alliances. War destroys the court relationships that generate leverage. A lord with this objective treats military action as a catastrophic failure of diplomacy, not a tool.

- Maximize my province’s prosperity. War devastates Rice output and PU. No lord who genuinely prioritizes their province’s prosperity initiates military action against a neighbor.

- Protect those under my care. War puts them directly in harm’s way. Defensive military action is always valid for this lord — they will fight hard when attacked — but they will not initiate.

- Accumulate knowledge and wisdom. No military application. This objective is pursued entirely through non-violent means.

- Live by Bushido without compromise. Initiating unprovoked military action violates most Bushido virtues. A lord with this standing objective fights only when honor demands it — a direct insult, a formal challenge, a clear duty. They do not initiate wars of convenience or opportunity.

**STANDING OBJECTIVES WHERE WAR IS SITUATIONALLY VALID**

The following standing objectives do not intrinsically call for military action but do not exclude it either. Whether war is valid depends on circumstances — specifically, whether a military primary objective has been generated that serves the standing objective in a way that cannot be achieved otherwise.

- **Advance my family’s standing within the clan hierarchy.** War can raise a family’s standing through battlefield Glory and demonstrated competence — but only if the war is sanctioned by the lord’s superior. An unsanctioned military adventure that goes wrong destroys a family’s standing rather than advancing it. Valid only at Tier 3 (raid) unless the Clan Champion formally endorses the action.

- **Control the Empire’s key trade networks.** If a specific trade route is controlled by a rival and cannot be acquired through diplomacy or purchase, raiding to disrupt it — or formal war to seize the province it passes through — becomes a valid option. Requires an active primary objective targeting that specific route. Tier 3 and Tier 2 valid when the primary objective warrants it.

- **Honor my ancestors through my deeds.** If the lord’s ancestor history includes martial glory — a famous general, a clan-defining battle — then battlefield deeds are a valid path to honoring them. Valid at all tiers when a military primary objective is active, but only if the lord’s ancestor record supports it. A lord descended from scholars and courtiers does not honor their ancestors through unprovoked war.

- **Elevate my family name.** Same logic as “Advance my family’s standing” above. A famous, sanctioned victory elevates a family name. An unauthorized war that fails destroys it. Tier 3 valid freely; Tier 2 requires superior endorsement.

- **Achieve personal excellence in my role.** Valid at Tier 3 only for bushi-role characters. Personal excellence for a warrior is demonstrated in battle. Not applicable to courtier or shugenja role characters, who demonstrate excellence through their own domains.

**PRIMARY OBJECTIVES THAT DIRECTLY GENERATE WAR JUSTIFICATION**

While standing objectives establish whether a lord is generally willing to consider military action, primary objectives are what actually pull the trigger. These are the immediate, completable tasks a lord is actively pursuing. The following primary objectives (from Section 18.5) are inherently military — they cannot be completed without military action — and therefore constitute direct war justification at the tier noted.

- **Conquer [Province X].** Tier 2 required. Conquest is a formal act. A raid can soften a province but cannot hold it. This primary objective only enters the lord’s active list if their standing objective is one that justifies territorial expansion — it is never assigned in isolation.

- **Defend [Province X] against [Clan X].** All tiers valid. Defensive military action is always justified regardless of standing objective — it is not the lord choosing war, it is war being brought to them. A lord with “Maintain peace” as their standing objective will still fight to defend their own territory. This is the only case where the standing objective filter does not apply.

- **Resolve [Shadowlands Incursion crisis].** All tiers valid against Shadowlands targets. As with the defensive objective above, Shadowlands incursions bypass the standing objective filter — no Rokugani lord argues for peace with Fu Leng’s forces.

- **Destroy [Army X] in the field.** Tier 2 required. This objective requires a standing military force to be fielded and a deliberate engagement sought. Only generated when a standing objective justifying military action is active AND the target army represents a genuine threat or obstacle to the lord’s goals.

- **Relieve the siege of [Castle X].** All tiers valid. A siege of a friendly castle is a crisis that demands military response regardless of the lord’s standing objective. The standing objective filter does not apply when allied holdings are under direct attack.

- **Restore order in [Province X] — resolve active banditry or insurgency.** Tier 3 valid. Military action to suppress bandits or an insurgency is internal policing, not war against another clan. It does not trigger War Status unless the bandits are found to be operating on behalf of a rival clan — in which case a new primary objective is generated against that clan.

- **Secure [Trade Route X] — clear banditry or reopen blocked passage.** Tier 3 valid. Escorting or clearing a trade route is military action short of war. If the blockade is being maintained by another clan’s forces deliberately, this escalates to Tier 2 justification and a new primary objective (Conquer or Defend) is generated.

- **Sabotage [Clan X]’s economic output below [threshold].** Tier 3 valid when a standing objective justifying military action against that clan is active. Raiding to destroy stockpiles, burn supply convoys, or disrupt harvest is economic warfare, not formal military engagement. Personality gate: Gi and Makoto lords will not pursue this objective through covert military means — they either declare openly or do not pursue it militarily at all.

- **Avenge [Death/Disgrace of Character X].** All tiers valid against the responsible party. Vengeance is the most emotionally immediate justification for war in Rokugan. A lord whose father was killed, whose family was publicly disgraced, or whose castle was taken under dishonest circumstances has standing to pursue this objective militarily at any tier. The strength of the justification scales with the severity of the original wrong — a minor insult supports Tier 3 at most; a killed family member supports all tiers. Traced assassination is among the strongest triggers for this objective — a lord assassinated by another clan’s agent automatically generates this primary objective in the successor (Section 22.5) and justifies escalation to Total War if the victim was a Family Daimyo or above. See the assassination system (Section 12.8) for the full traced assassination consequence chain.

- **Eliminate [Character X] — through duel or other means.** Tier 3 valid. A lord who needs a specific person dead may use military action as cover — the target dies in a raid, in the chaos of battle, or in the siege of their home castle. Tier 2 (formal war) is only justified by this objective if the target is a lord of sufficient rank that killing them is itself the military goal, not a side effect of a larger campaign. Personality gate: Gi and Makoto lords will not use military action to mask an assassination — if they need someone eliminated, they use a duel or a formal accusation.

**PERSONALITY-DRIVEN AGGRESSION (THE EXCEPTION)**

For most lords, war requires an objective that war can serve. There is one exception: certain personality combinations produce lords who are intrinsically aggressive — who view military action as its own reward and require no further justification beyond opportunity.

A lord qualifies for personality-driven aggression if ALL of the following are true:

- Their Bushido virtue is Yu (Courage) or their Shourido virtue is Kyoryoku (Strength) or Ketsui (Determination).

- Their standing objective is not one of the peace-excluding objectives listed above (“Maintain peace,” “Protect those under my care,” etc.).

- The target province is observable as defensible weak. What “weak” means depends on the tier of action being considered, as defined below.

**Tier 3 Raid — Province-Level Assessment Only**

A raid is a provincial lord acting against a provincial lord. It does not formally involve the Family Daimyo or Clan Champion unless it escalates per the Escalation Authority Chain above. Because the aggressor is only facing the target province — not the target clan — clan-level military capacity is irrelevant to this assessment. A Lion border province with a minimal garrison is a valid raid target even though the Lion clan as a whole fields the largest standing military in Rokugan. As long as the raid stays contained, the Lion Clan Champion never enters the picture.

The Tier 3 weakness condition passes if ALL of the following are observable:

- The target province’s garrison is at or below its required minimum — 5% of total settlement PU, per Section 4.3.11. A garrison above minimum means the province has invested in its own defense. A garrison at or below minimum means it has not.

- No standing field army belonging to the target province’s lord is currently present in or adjacent to the province. A field army in the next province can respond within days. Its presence negates the weakness condition even if the garrison itself is minimal.

- The target province has no active alliance protection — no allied lord has a standing commitment to defend it, and no allied army is billeted there.

All three of these are observable facts. A lord can see garrison size from traders and travellers passing through. They can observe whether an army is encamped nearby. Alliance commitments are public record through the topic system. This assessment requires no espionage — it reflects what any reasonably attentive lord would know about their neighbours.

**Tier 2 Formal War — Clan-Level Assessment Required**

Formal war triggers the Escalation Authority Chain. The target’s Family Daimyo will become involved. Their Clan Champion may follow. Personality-driven aggression at Tier 2 therefore requires the aggressor to assess not just the target province but the target clan’s realistic response capacity. Attacking a weak Lion border province is one thing when raiding. Declaring formal war on it is another — the Lion will answer.

The Tier 2 weakness condition passes only if the Tier 3 condition above is met AND the following additional condition holds:

- The aggressor’s current fielded PU is at least 2× the observable standing military PU of the target clan within a two-province radius of the target. “Observable standing military PU” means samurai units and garrisoned forces that are physically present and visible on the map — not hypothetical levies that could be raised. The lord estimates what they can see, per Section 20. A two-province radius represents approximately one season’s march — the realistic window within which relief forces could arrive before a raid becomes entrenched. Forces beyond that radius are not a credible near-term threat and are excluded from the assessment.

This is what makes attacking the Lion at Tier 2 genuinely dangerous even from a position of local strength. Their standing samurai forces are large, permanently fielded, and distributed across their territory. The 2× threshold will almost never be met against a healthy Lion clan. Against a depleted or distracted clan — one already fighting elsewhere, or one whose forces have been observed moving to the far side of their territory — the window may open.

If all conditions are met at the appropriate tier, the lord may initiate military action without any supporting objective. The action is its own justification — they saw weakness and acted on it. This is the only case in the system where military action does not require a serving objective. It is intentionally narrow. The vast majority of lords never meet all conditions simultaneously. When it does fire, it should feel like the emergent behavior of a specific type of dangerous personality — the warlord, the conqueror, the bushi who finds peace unbearable.

Personality-driven aggression never justifies Tier 2 formal war through the weakness condition alone if the tier 2 threshold is not met. A lord who can raid does not automatically have grounds for a sustained campaign. Formal war demands either the Tier 2 condition above, or a supporting objective from the objective lists earlier in this subsection.

**THE FULL DECISION SEQUENCE**

When an AI lord considers military action, the system runs these checks in order. All must pass before the Feasibility Ledger (Section 4.3.17) is calculated.

- **Step 1 — Is war justified by objectives?** Does the lord have an active standing or primary objective that military action at the intended tier can serve? If yes, proceed. If no, check Step 2. If Step 2 also fails, stop. The lord does not consider military action.

- **Step 2 — Does personality-driven aggression apply?** Check all three personality-driven aggression conditions. If all are met, Tier 3 action is valid. If not all are met, stop. The lord does not consider military action.

- **Step 3 — Does the objective support the intended tier?** Confirm the tier the lord is considering is within the range the objective supports. A lord cannot justify formal war declaration from an objective that only supports raiding.

- **Step 4 — Do personality gates block this specific action?** Apply all personality gates relevant to the objective and action type. A Jin lord may have a valid objective but be gated out of the specific military method. If a gate blocks the action, the lord pursues non-military paths to the same objective instead.

- **Step 5 — Run the Feasibility Ledger.** Only now does the system ask whether the lord can sustain the military action they have decided to pursue. Section 4.3.17 takes over from this point.
