## 57.37 Tea Ceremony System

### 57.37.1 Overview

The tea ceremony is a second Void recovery path, parallel to MEDITATE (Section 57.32.3) but fundamentally different in character. MEDITATE is solitary and introspective — one character spending 1 AP to recover their own VP through a Meditation / Void roll. Tea Ceremony is social and ritualistic — one host spending 1 AP to recover VP for themselves and up to 4 guests through a Tea Ceremony / Void roll. Guests spend 0 AP. The host is a force multiplier: a skilled tea master gives the group free Void recovery that would otherwise cost each participant their own AP on individual MEDITATE actions.

This creates a genuine tactical role: the tea master. A Crane court with a skilled tea master has a structural advantage — their characters enter every critical roll with refreshed Void. Before a negotiation, the tea master conducts a ceremony and the lord, lead courtier, and yojimbo all regain VP. Before a dangerous mission, the party shares tea and enters at full capacity. The balance comes from skill rarity (Tea Ceremony is a niche skill — most bushi and shugenja do not invest in it), venue constraints (requires a refined interior space), and the co-location requirement (all participants must be present in the same zone).

### 57.37.2 CONDUCT_TEA_CEREMONY ActionID

ActionID: CONDUCT_TEA_CEREMONY. Category 11 (Personal Development). Cost: 1 AP for the host. 0 AP for participants — they attend but do not consume their own AP. Skill: Tea Ceremony / Void vs TN 15 + 5 per participant beyond 2. TN by participant count: 1 (solo) = TN 15, 2 = TN 15, 3 = TN 20, 4 = TN 25, 5 = TN 30. Participant cap: 5 total (host + up to 4 guests). The host is always a participant. The ceremony can be conducted solo (host only, TN 15, recovers VP for host alone).

Context: AT_ZONE in a zone where tokonoma is true OR shrine_eligible is true. Both host and all participants must be in the same zone. Invalid during active combat, siege assault, or forced march — same invalidity conditions as MEDITATE (Section 57.32.3). No new zone flag required — the tokonoma and shrine_eligible flags already identify the refined, quiet spaces where tea ceremony is appropriate. Eligible zones at a Provincial Daimyo’s castle: AUDIENCE_CHAMBER (tokonoma true), GUEST_WING (tokonoma true), TSUBONIWA (tokonoma true), CASTLE_SHRINE (shrine_eligible true). At a Family Daimyo’s castle: add CHASHITSU (tokonoma true, the purpose-built venue), LORD_QUARTERS (tokonoma true). At TEMPLE_GROUNDS (shrine_eligible true).

On success: all participants (including host) regain 1 spent Void Point. VP recovery cannot exceed max_void_points — a character already at full VP gains nothing. Rank 5 Tea Ceremony Mastery: all participants regain 2 VP instead of 1.

On failure: AP spent (host). No VP recovered for anyone. The ceremony was disrupted — the host’s preparation was inadequate, concentration was broken, or the spiritual resonance did not manifest. Participants’ time was wasted but no AP was consumed on their part.

No daily limit beyond AP cost. A host could theoretically conduct multiple ceremonies per IC day if they have the AP. Self-balancing: each ceremony costs 1 AP, and a character with 2 AP per IC day who conducts 2 ceremonies has no AP left for anything else.

### 57.37.3 Participant Selection (NPC Engine)

When the NPC engine scores CONDUCT_TEA_CEREMONY for a host, it selects participants from co-located characters using the following criteria. Eligible: character is in the same zone, current_void_points is below max_void_points, disposition toward host is Acquaintance (+1) or better. You do not share tea with strangers or enemies. Ineligible: VP already full (nothing to recover), disposition below Acquaintance (the invitation would be refused), character has an active urgent NeedType at urgency above 60 that blocks participation (same invalidity conditions as MEDITATE — a character about to fight, flee, or die does not stop for tea).

The host selects up to 4 eligible participants, prioritising: (1) characters with the lowest VP ratio (current/max) — those who need it most, (2) characters with active objectives that benefit from VP spending (same anticipation logic as MEDITATE scoring in Section 57.32.5), (3) highest disposition toward host (closer allies are invited first). The host does not invite participants who would push the TN beyond the host’s reasonable success threshold. A host with Tea Ceremony 3 + Void 3 = 6k3 should not attempt TN 30 (5 participants) — the engine caps participant count at the level where the host has at least a 50% success chance.

Engine pattern: host-initiated group effect. Tea Ceremony is not the first action where one character’s AP expenditure affects others. Two precedents exist: TREAT_WOUND (healer spends 1 AP, target receives healing at 0 AP — single target, selected by the healer) and PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE (performer spends 1 AP, all witnesses in the zone receive disposition effects at 0 AP — broadcast, no selection). Tea Ceremony is a hybrid: the host spends 1 AP, selects specific eligible recipients from the co-located pool (like TREAT_WOUND’s target selection but allowing multiple targets), and applies VP recovery to each selected recipient (like PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE’s multi-character effect). Phase 7 (Execution) for CONDUCT_TEA_CEREMONY runs a participant selection subroutine after the roll resolves: identify eligible co-located characters per the criteria above, apply VP recovery to each, log the ceremony in zone_event_log. No new engine phase is required — the logic sits entirely within Phase 7 execution.

Player participation: when an NPC tea master conducts a ceremony and a player character is in the same zone and eligible, the player receives a participation prompt — accept (receive VP recovery at no AP cost) or decline (excluded from the ceremony, no effect). NPC participants auto-accept per the eligibility criteria above. A player who accepts is present for the ceremony’s duration but loses nothing — the ceremony takes 30 minutes in IC time, far less than the half-day an AP slot represents. A player who declines may have personal reasons: distrust of the host, a desire to remain apart, or simple roleplay preference. The prompt is cosmetic — there is no mechanical reason to decline except zero VP deficit, in which case the prompt does not fire (the player is ineligible, not prompted).

### 57.37.4 Host-Side NPC Scoring

CONDUCT_TEA_CEREMONY fires under the RECOVER_VOID_POINTS NeedType (same as MEDITATE). When both MEDITATE and CONDUCT_TEA_CEREMONY are available candidates, scoring determines which fires. Base MEDITATE score: per Section 57.32.5. Base TEA_CEREMONY score: same base as MEDITATE, plus a social multiplier: +10 per eligible participant beyond the host. A solo tea ceremony (1 participant) scores identically to MEDITATE — if the host has higher Tea Ceremony than Meditation, tea ceremony wins; otherwise MEDITATE wins. A ceremony with 3 eligible participants scores +20 above the solo base — the group benefit justifies the host’s AP investment. If no eligible participants are co-located (all allies at full VP, or host is alone), the score reverts to solo and competes directly with MEDITATE on skill rank.

Personality leans: Rei (Courtesy) +10 — the tea ceremony is the quintessential expression of courtesy and refinement. Jin (Compassion) +5 — sharing VP recovery with allies is a compassionate act. Crane clan +10 — tea ceremony is culturally central to the Crane. Phoenix +5. Ketsui (Determination) −5 — a determined warrior prefers to push through rather than pause for ritual. Yu (Courage) −5 — impatient with ceremony.

### 57.37.5 Interaction with Other Systems

Void Point refresh (Section 57.32.2): Tea Ceremony VP recovery stacks with OOC Day Tick passive refresh. A character who receives VP from a ceremony during the OOC day still gets full pool refresh at the tick if rested_last_night is true. No interaction — the two systems operate independently, same as MEDITATE. Starvation: starvation blocks OOC Day Tick passive refresh but does NOT block Tea Ceremony VP recovery — same rule as MEDITATE. The ceremony is an active recovery action, not passive rest. void_refresh_blocked_until: this field blocks only passive OOC Day Tick refresh, not active recovery through MEDITATE or Tea Ceremony.

MEDITATE comparison: MEDITATE is personal (1 VP for self, 1 AP), available anywhere (no venue restriction), and uses Meditation / Void. Tea Ceremony is social (1-5 VP across the group, 1 AP from host only), requires a refined venue (tokonoma or shrine), and uses Tea Ceremony / Void. MEDITATE is the default; Tea Ceremony is the upgrade when a skilled host and willing participants are available. A character with both skills chooses based on context: alone or in a rough location, MEDITATE. At court with allies who need VP, Tea Ceremony.

Solo tea ceremony vs MEDITATE: a solo ceremony (TN 15, Tea Ceremony / Void, 1 VP) competes directly with MEDITATE (TN 20, Meditation (Void Recovery) / Void, 1 VP). Solo tea ceremony has a lower TN but requires a venue. MEDITATE has a higher TN but works anywhere. A character with Tea Ceremony 3 and Meditation 2 at court uses tea ceremony; the same character on the road uses MEDITATE. Both are valid choices; neither dominates in all contexts.
