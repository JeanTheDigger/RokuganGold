## 22.3 Character Sheet — LOCKED

Every named character has a full character sheet regardless of type. Canonical characters have pre-filled sheets. Generated characters have sheets produced by their generation template. Player characters have sheets they build at creation and develop through play.

**Known Sheet Components — LOCKED:**

- Identity: Name, Clan, Family, School, Insight Rank, Age.

- The Five Rings: Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Void — each with their two constituent Traits. Ring values determine dice pools for all actions.

- Skills: Ranked 0–10. Combined with Ring Traits for all rolls (Ring Trait + Skill rank vs TN).

- Insight: Calculated from Ring values × 10 + total skill ranks. Determines Insight Rank threshold.

- Personality: One Bushido virtue + one Shourido virtue (Section 19).

- Honor, Glory, Status: Tracked separately. Each runs 0.0 to 10.0 in Ranks and Points.

- Objective: Current active objective — always present, changes over time (Section 18).

- Physical location: Current zone in the MUD map.

- Role/Position: What position they currently hold (Gunso, daimyo, magistrate, etc.).

- Disposition values: Toward all named characters they have interacted with (Section 12.2).

**COMPLETE CHARACTER SHEET FIELDS**

The following defines every field on a named character's sheet. All characters — canonical, generated, and player — use the same sheet structure. Canonical characters have pre-filled values from lore. Generated characters have values produced by their generation template with small random variance. Player characters build their sheet at creation and develop it through play.

**IDENTITY**

- Name: Full formal name in Rokugani convention (Family name first, given name second). Generated characters receive procedurally generated names appropriate to their clan and family.

- Clan: Great Clan, Minor Clan, Imperial families, Brotherhood of Shinsei, or Ronin.

- Family: The specific family within the clan — Hida, Doji, Isawa, etc.

- School: The character's trained school — Hida Bushi, Doji Courtier, Isawa Shugenja, etc. Determines starting skills, techniques, and outfit.

- School Type: Bushi, Courtier, Shugenja, Monk, Ninja, or Artisan. Determines which mechanical systems primarily apply.

- Insight Rank: Derived from total Insight. Ranges 1–10+. Determines Technique access and world perception of the character.

- Age: Tracked in IC years. Purely narrative — age carries no mechanical bonuses or penalties. A 60-year-old Rank 5 samurai is as capable mechanically as a 30-year-old Rank 5.

- Gender: Tracked for social and narrative purposes.

**THE FIVE RINGS ****&**** TRAITS**

- Air Ring: Contains Reflexes and Awareness. Air × 10 contributes to Insight. Starting value typically 2.

- Earth Ring: Contains Stamina and Willpower. Earth × 10 contributes to Insight. Determines Wound levels.

- Fire Ring: Contains Agility and Intelligence. Fire × 10 contributes to Insight.

- Water Ring: Contains Strength and Perception. Water × 10 contributes to Insight.

- Void Ring: Single Trait — Void itself. Void × 10 contributes to Insight. Void Points refresh at the OOC Day Tick (Section 57.32.2).

Each Ring value is typically 2–4 at Rank 1, rising toward 5–6 at higher Ranks. Individual Traits within a Ring can differ by ±1 from the Ring value through expenditure. All Rings start equal unless a family or school bonus applies.

**DERIVED VALUES**

- Insight: (Air + Earth + Fire + Water + Void) × 10 + total Skill ranks. Determines Insight Rank.

- Initiative: Reflexes + Insight Rank (rolled at combat start).

- Armor TN: (Reflexes × 5) + 5 + any armor bonus. The base difficulty to hit the character.

- Reduction: From armor worn. Subtracted from damage taken.

- Void Points: Equal to Void Ring. Spent for bonuses, refresh at OOC Day Tick (Section 57.32.2).

**WOUND LEVELS**

Wounds are tracked in levels. Each level holds a number of Wound Points equal to Earth Ring × 2. When a level fills, the character takes the associated penalty and begins filling the next level. The penalty applies to all Trait rolls.

- Healthy: Earth × 2 Wound Points. No penalty.

- Nicked: Earth × 2 Wound Points. −3 to all Trait rolls.

- Grazed: Earth × 2 Wound Points. −5 to all Trait rolls.

- Hurt: Earth × 2 Wound Points. −10 to all Trait rolls.

- Injured: Earth × 2 Wound Points. −15 to all Trait rolls.

- Crippled: Earth × 2 Wound Points. −20 to all Trait rolls.

- Down: Earth × 2 Wound Points. −40 to all Trait rolls. Character cannot stand.

- Out: Earth × 2 Wound Points. Character is unconscious.

- Dead: Character is dead. Permanent. No recovery.

**SKILLS**

Every skill the character possesses is listed with its current rank (0–10). Skills are organized by category: High Skills, Bugei (Martial) Skills, Shugenja Skills, Low Skills, and Craft/Artisan Skills. Only skills at rank 1 or above are listed — rank 0 means untrained. Each skill entry also notes any Emphases the character has purchased.

- School skills: Listed at their school-granted starting rank.

- Purchased skills: Any additional skills bought with XP or through advancement.

- Emphases: Specific focus areas within a skill. Noted as sub-entries under the parent skill.

**TECHNIQUES**

Techniques are the secret abilities of the character's School, unlocked by advancing in School Rank. Each character lists every Technique they currently know.

- School Rank 1 Technique: Known at character creation.

- School Rank 2–5 Techniques: Unlocked as Insight Rank advances, formally learned at the dojo or with a Sensei (Section 48).

- Path Techniques: If the character has taken an Advanced School Path, those Techniques are listed separately.

- Kiho (Monks only): Kiho known are listed here rather than as Techniques. Each Kiho is listed with its name, Ring, and effect summary.

- Katas (Bushi only): Katas known are listed here. Each Kata is listed with its name, requirements, and effect summary.

**SPELLS KNOWN (Shugenja only)**

Shugenja characters track their known spells separately from Techniques.

- Affinity Element: The element the character has natural affinity with. Affinity spells cost one fewer Raise to cast.

- Deficiency Element: The element the character struggles with. Deficiency spells cost one additional Raise.

- Spells Known: Listed by element and Mastery Level. Format: Element — Spell Name (Mastery Level). Shugenja begin with spells from their school and gain 3 new spells per School Rank advance.

- Mastery Level Access: Current School Rank determines the highest Mastery Level spells the character may cast.

**ADVANTAGES ****&**** DISADVANTAGES**

Advantages and Disadvantages from Section 45 that the character possesses. Each entry lists the name, point cost, and a brief effect summary.

- Advantages: Positive traits, gifts, connections, and blessings. Purchased with XP.

- Disadvantages: Negative traits, flaws, and burdens. Some are taken at character creation for bonus XP. Others are acquired through play.

- Shadowlands Taint Disadvantages: Taint-derived mutations and corruptions are listed here and cross-referenced to the Taint tracker.

**HONOR, GLORY, STATUS ****&**** INFAMY**

Each tracked on a 0.0–10.0 scale in both Ranks (whole numbers) and Points (decimal tracking). See Section 4.6 for full rules.

- Honor: Moral and ethical alignment with Bushido. Affects what actions the character will take, how NPCs perceive them, and certain mechanical interactions.

- Glory: Public reputation and renown. Affects how widely the character is known and how they are treated in formal social situations.

- Status: Social position within the Celestial Order. Determines what the character can demand and what is expected of them.

- Infamy: Notorious reputation earned through criminal, dishonorable, or feared acts. Tracked separately from Glory on the same 0.0–10.0 scale. A character can hold both Glory and Infamy simultaneously. Infamy inspires fear and revulsion rather than awe. Combined Glory + Infamy Ranks determine recognition chance. See Section 4.6.

**SHADOWLANDS TAINT**

Taint is tracked as a separate value from 0 to 10 Ranks (with decimal points within each Rank). See Section 42 for full Taint rules.

- Current Taint: Numeric value (e.g. 2.3 = Rank 2, 3 points into Rank 3).

- Taint Manifestations: Any visible or detectable Taint effects the character currently exhibits.

- Taint is not present on most characters at generation. It accumulates through exposure to the Shadowlands, Maho, or corrupted items.

**EQUIPMENT ****&**** OUTFIT**

Every named character carries equipment appropriate to their school, rank, and current situation. Equipment is tracked as individual items, each potentially with its own provenance record (Section 49).

- Weapons: All weapons carried, with their DR, keywords, quality tier, and provenance if notable.

- Armor: Armor worn, with TN bonus, Reduction, quality tier, and provenance if notable.

- School Outfit: The standard equipment package from the character's school (as listed in Section 29).

- Personal items: Notable possessions beyond combat equipment — calligraphy set, musical instrument, tea set, jewelry, gifts received.

- Koku on hand: Physical currency currently carried. Separate from stipend tracking.

**PERSONALITY**

The character's virtue profile from Section 19. Drives NPC decision-making, reaction to topics, court behavior, and military choices.

- Bushido Virtue: The primary Bushido virtue the character embodies most strongly — Courage (Yu), Compassion (Jin), Courtesy (Rei), Honesty (Gi), Honor (Meiyo), Duty (Chugi), or Sincerity (Makoto). Drives honorable impulses and social behavior.

- Shourido Virtue: The primary Shourido virtue — Seigyo (Control), Ketsui (Determination), Dōsatsu (Insight), Chishiki (Knowledge), Kanpeki (Perfection), Kyōryōku (Strength), or Ishi (Will). Drives darker impulses and self-serving tendencies.

- Decision-Making Profile: The behavioral profile derived from the Bushido + Shourido combination. Governs NPC priorities, what they will never do, what arguments persuade them, how they behave at court, how they respond to siege end conditions, their hostage likelihood, and their reaction to crisis situations. See Section 19 for the full profile of each virtue combination. 49 possible combinations (7 Bushido × 7 Shourido).

**SOCIAL ****&**** DYNAMIC FIELDS**

Fields that change frequently during play.

- lord_id: int. The character ID of this character's feudal lord --- the daimyo, family daimyo, or clan champion they owe direct fealty to. Determines who can assign objectives through the feudal hierarchy (Section 55.10, HierarchySystem.get_direct_vassals). Updated on succession (Section 22.5), exile, or clan transfer. A ronin's lord_id is null. The Emperor's lord_id is null. Every other samurai has a lord_id pointing to their immediate feudal superior.

- operational_superior_id: int or null. The character ID of this character's current operational commander --- distinct from lord_id. Set when the character is assigned to an operational role: yoriki under a magistrate (legal), soldier under a military commander (military), or delegation member under a delegation leader (delegation). Null when no operational assignment is active. Objectives from operational_superior_id take priority over lord_id standing objectives for AP spending. See Section 11.3.18 for full operational hierarchy rules, assignment conditions, clearing conditions, and override rules.

- operational_hierarchy_type: string or null. The type of operational relationship: 'military', 'legal', 'delegation', or null. Determines which rules govern objective assignment and the scope of authority. Set and cleared alongside operational_superior_id. See Section 11.3.18a.

- Objective: Current active objective from Section 18. Always present. Changes when achieved, abandoned, or superseded.

- Physical Location: Current zone in the MUD/ASCII map. Updated as the character travels.

- Role/Position: Current institutional role — Gunso, Chui, provincial daimyo, magistrate, sensei, hostage, etc.

- Designated Heir: The named character formally designated as this lord’s successor. Only present for characters holding a lordship position (Local Daimyo or above). Set by a deliberate 1 AP action. Null if no heir has been designated. See Succession System (Section 22.5) for full heir evaluation and designation mechanics.

- Disposition Values: Tracked toward every named character this character has interacted with. Stored as a list of [character name: disposition value] pairs. Scale is −100 to +100 with named thresholds: Blood Enemy (−100 to −61), Enemy (−60 to −31), Rival (−30 to −11), Stranger (−10 to +10), Acquaintance (+11 to +30), Friend (+31 to +60), Trusted Ally (+61 to +90), Devoted (+91 to +100). The threshold name is displayed alongside the numeric value. See Section 12.2.

- Fear Rating: Some characters possess an innate Fear effect — a Rank from 1 to 10 that others must roll Willpower (TN = 5 + Fear Rank × 5) to resist. Most characters have no Fear Rating. Relevant for powerful oni, certain supernatural entities, and exceptionally intimidating NPCs with the Terrible Appearance Advantage or equivalent. If present, listed as Fear [Rank].

- Captive/Hostage Status: Whether the character is currently held as a hostage or prisoner, who holds them, and under what terms. Hostage status restricts available actions and movement. Affects disposition gain toward captor over time (proximity effect). See Section 22.9.

- Topic Pool: Active Tier 4 topics the character currently holds. Used by the daily conversation and letter systems (Sections 12.6, 12.7, 16.5).

- Active Quest/Mission: If the character is currently assigned to or undertaking a quest or mission, it is noted here.

- Met Characters: met_characters: [int]. Set of character IDs this character has encountered in person. Populated automatically when two named characters are co-located in the same zone with an ASCII map — whether a Lesser Zone or a Navigation Zone with an ASCII map (Section 4.3.4 v564 amendment). Global constraint: a character can only reference another character in conversation, topics, letters, or court actions if (1) the other character’s ID is in their met_characters set, or (2) the other character is named in a topic in their known_topics, referenced in a letter they received, or identified through an intelligence action. A character who has neither met nor heard of someone cannot target them with any social action. See Section 29.15.24 for full specification.

- Courtier Framework Fields: Additional character sheet fields required by courtier school techniques are defined in Section 29.15.24 (New Infrastructure Specifications). These include: self_reroll, granted_reroll, enhanced_void, timed_advantages, action_blocks, combat_modifiers_pending, and supply_ledger.

- Pieces Seen: pieces_seen: Dict[int → int]. Maps piece_id or painting_id to IC_day_last_seen. Shared immunity tracker for both the Theater System (Section 57.22) and the Painting System (Section 57.27). A character who has viewed a specific piece or painting within 30 IC days is immune to its disposition effects on subsequent viewings. The immunity window resets on each new viewing. Characters whose character_id appears in a piece’s known_by list are permanently immune and bypass this field entirely. Updated automatically on any successful PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE or painting viewing event.

- Learning Progress: learning_progress: Dict[int → int]. Maps piece_id to accumulated learning progress for theater pieces currently being learned via LEARN_THEATER_PIECE (Section 57.22.6). Absent entry = 0 progress. Entry removed on completion (piece moves to known_by) or piece loss. Progress accumulates across multiple LEARN_THEATER_PIECE sessions.

- Last Medicine Treatment: last_medicine_treatment_ic_day: int, default 0. IC day number of most recent TREAT_WOUND roll received (success or failure). Gates the one-treatment-per-IC-day validator. Source: Section 57.31.

- Rested Last Night: rested_last_night: bool, default true. Whether the character had sufficient rest across the current OOC day. Read at OOC Day Tick to determine natural healing (Section 57.31.7a) and Void Point refresh (Section 57.32) eligibility. Reset to true at start of each OOC day. Flipped to false by active combat, continuous travel, watch duty majority, starvation, or supernatural blocking effects.

- Civilian Order Budget: civilian_order_budget_max: int, derived from administrative rank (Provincial Daimyo: 5, City Daimyo: 8, Family Daimyo: 10, Clan Champion: 12, Emperor: 15). civilian_orders_remaining: int, resets to max at OOC Day Tick. Decremented by 1 per civilian order issued. Non-lord characters: both fields set to 0. Source: Section 57.34.

- Wind-Down State: last_wind_down_method: string, default “rest”. The wind-down method selected on the most recent OOC Day Tick. wind_down_void_modifier: float, default 1.0. Void Point refresh multiplier set by wind-down method: 1.0 for full refresh (sake house, geisha house, bathhouse), 0.75 for partial refresh (shrine prayer, temple stay, garden walking, tea house), 0.5 for rest. Source: Section 57.44.

Active Poisons: active_poisons: list of poison_instance objects, default empty. Multiple poisons can affect a character simultaneously — each instance tracks independently, effects stack. Each poison_instance contains: instance_id (unique int, auto-generated), poison_type (enum: dripping_poison, fire_biter, night_milk, venom), remaining_duration (int, ticks remaining until natural expiry — decremented each tick), onset_ic_day (int, IC day the poison was introduced), diagnosed (bool, default false — set to true when any character with Medicine 1+ succeeds on the diagnosis roll per Section 57.48.14), diagnosed_by (list of character_ids who have attempted diagnosis on this instance, success or failure — gates the one-attempt-per-observer-per-poison-instance rule), treated_by (list of character_ids who have attempted TREAT_WOUND with Medicine (Antidotes) on this instance, success or failure — gates the one-attempt-per-healer-per-poison-instance rule), source_character_id (int, the character who delivered the poison — used for CrimeRecord linkage, always known by the system regardless of whether characters have discovered it). A poison_instance is removed from the list when: remaining_duration reaches 0 (natural expiry), treatment succeeds (TREAT_WOUND with Antidotes, Section 57.48.15), three consecutive Stamina saves succeed (Venom only), or the character dies. The NPC reactive behavior trigger (Section 57.48.16) reads this list at each AP cycle — any non-empty active_poisons list fires the reactive check. Source: Section 57.48.

**FAMILY WEB**

The character's biological family connections across four generations. See Section 22.6 for full rules.

- Generation 1 (Self): The character.

- Generation 2 (Parents): Mother and father. Clan, family, and status noted.

- Generation 3 (Grandparents): Up to four grandparents. May be deceased.

- Generation 4 (Great-grandparents): Up to eight great-grandparents. Usually deceased, but historically significant.

- Siblings: Any brothers and sisters. May be full or half siblings.

- Children: Any children the character has produced or adopted.

- Spouse: Current spouse if married. Past spouses noted as deceased or divorced.

**PROGRESSION TRACKING (Section 48)**

Fields for the progression system.

- XP Total: Cumulative XP earned through quests, missions, and achievements.

- XP Spent: How much XP has been spent on progress bars.

- Progress Bars: One bar per Skill and Ring currently being advanced. Each bar shows current progress points out of the threshold needed for the next rank.

- Training Relationships: Any active Sensei relationships — who is teaching this character what skill, and the Sensei's relevant rank.

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Operational Hierarchy): **The lord_id and operational_superior_id fields listed above are governed by the Operational Hierarchy system in Section 11.3.18. That section defines the universal pattern for non-feudal command relationships used by three systems: legal (magistrate directing yoriki), military (Rikugunshokan through Gunso chain of command), and delegation (courtier delegation leader directing members at foreign courts). The engine's HierarchySystem.get_direct_vassals() function is extended to check both lord_id and operational_superior_id (Section 55.26.1.4, amendment in Section 57.17). All assignment, clearing, and override rules are in Section 11.3.18f-g.
