## 57.22 Theater Piece System

### 57.22.1 Overview

Theater pieces are world objects representing composed dramatic works. They exist in two states: private (known only to specific characters) and canonized (part of the world repertoire, accessible to any sufficiently skilled character). Every piece carries a style, a subject, a topic linkage, and a disposition payload. Performing a piece in court is a variant of PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE that fires additional political effects beyond the standard disposition gain toward the performer.

### 57.22.2 Piece Object Structure

Each theater piece is a world object with the following fields:

piece_id: Unique identifier. title: Generated name. style: Noh, Kabuki, Kyogen, or Bunraku. author_id: Character who composed it. Null for ancient or canonized pieces with no known author. subject: The entity the piece is about — clan, family, or individual character_id. This is what the disposition shift targets. framing: Positive or negative — whether the piece portrays the subject favorably or unfavorably. roles: List of role definitions. Every piece has at least one role. Each role has: role_id (identifier within the piece), subject_character (who this role portrays — named character_id, clan, family, or typed archetype such as “Crane Courtier Female”), framing (positive or negative — how this role portrays its subject_character, independent of other roles), casting_requirements (Clan, Gender, Profession — mismatch penalties per Section 57.22.4 apply per role individually), assigned_performer (character_id set at performance time, null when unassigned). Single-role pieces have exactly one entry in roles. For single-role pieces the top-level subject and framing fields mirror roles[0].subject_character and roles[0].framing respectively. For multi-role pieces the top-level subject and framing point to the lead role (roles[0]) and are otherwise superseded by the per-role values — each role carries its own subject and framing independently. A piece may portray the Crane heroically and the Scorpion villainously in the same work; these are two separate roles with opposite framings firing separate disposition effects. topic_ids: List of active topic_ids this piece is linked to. Maximum 2 entries. Empty list when no topic is linked — replaces the former nullable single topic_id field. A piece with an empty topic_ids list still fires disposition effects but has no topic amplification payload. When a referenced topic’s momentum reaches zero and the topic is purged, that entry is removed from the list automatically on the next engine pass. topic_weight: Integer 1–3. How strongly the piece amplifies each linked topic when performed. Set at composition time via Raises. A single topic_weight value applies to all entries in topic_ids. disposition_magnitude: Integer 1–5. How strongly the piece shifts audience dispositions. Set at composition time via Raises. known_by: List of character_ids who know this piece and can perform it. canonized: Boolean. True if the piece has entered the world repertoire. Canonized pieces cannot be marked lost. times_performed: Running count of successful performances. Serves two purposes: prestige metric for the piece and its author, and the trigger for authorship glory (+0.1 Glory to a living author each time someone else performs their piece). Does not feed a canonization threshold — canonization is a world-start property only (Section 57.22.9). craft_progress: Integer or null. Accumulated composition progress toward the threshold. Null when the piece is not in active composition (not yet started, or completed). Set to 0 when composition is declared. Set to null on completion. Halved each abandoned IC season per degradation rules (Section 57.22.5a). lost: Boolean, default false. True if the piece’s known_by list has been emptied by character deaths and the piece is permanently inaccessible. The piece record is retained as a historical world entry (piece_id, title, metadata intact) but cannot be performed, taught, or learned. Canonized pieces cannot be marked lost — their known_by lists are never populated, so the lost condition cannot apply. abandoned_incomplete: Boolean, default false. True if the author died while the piece was in progress (craft_progress > 0 but below threshold). The piece never completed and never entered the world. Distinct from lost — lost applies to completed pieces that lost all knowers; abandoned_incomplete applies to pieces that never completed.

### 57.22.3 Theater Styles

Style determines the default payload type, audience reach, and casting rules of a piece.

**Noh**: Subdued, masked, slow, spiritual. Deals with ghosts, ancestral grief, duty, and the supernatural. Aristocratic audience. Thematic tendency: pieces composed in Noh style naturally concern heavy ancestral and spiritual subjects — exactly the kind of material that carries cultural weight and amplifies active political topics. An NPC composing Noh will naturally produce pieces with high topic_weight relative to disposition_magnitude, because Noh subjects tend to be those worth debating in the abstract rather than those worth hating in the immediate. This is compositional guidance only — both disposition shifts (Steps 2–3) and topic amplification (Step 4) use identical formulas at performance time regardless of style. High prestige — performing Noh in court is never inappropriate regardless of subject matter. Noh mask rule: the mask abstracts the performer from the role entirely, removing all Clan-based TN modifiers — both mismatch penalties and same-clan bonuses. A Crane performing a Crane-subject Noh piece does not benefit from being Crane; the mask stands in for any Clan identity. Gender mismatch penalties also do not apply. Profession mismatch penalty still applies — the mask cannot disguise how a bushi moves versus how a courtier moves. Multi-role: Noh naturally supports two roles — the shite (lead, primary dramatic character) and the waki (supporting, often a traveling monk or observer who draws out the shite’s story). Pieces written with a waki role use the standard multi-role composition threshold modifier. Both roles benefit from the Noh mask rule.

**Kabuki**: Flamboyant, high-action, emotional, morally explicit. Thematic tendency: pieces composed in Kabuki style naturally concern moral confrontation and emotional clarity — subjects that produce immediate shifts in how the audience feels about specific people or clans. An NPC composing Kabuki will naturally produce pieces with high disposition_magnitude relative to topic_weight, because Kabuki subjects tend to be those worth hating or loving, not those worth debating. This is compositional guidance only — both payload types use identical formulas at performance time regardless of style. Broad audience appeal including lower-status witnesses. Full Clan, Gender, and Profession mismatch penalties apply. Multi-role: Kabuki is the natural home of grand multi-role productions — a full Kabuki piece depicting the founding of the Crane Clan with roles for Doji, Kakita, and the First Emperor is entirely within the style’s tradition. No role limit. Each additional role uses the standard threshold modifier at composition.

**Kyogen**: Comic, satirical, typically performed between Noh acts. Deals with social absurdity, hypocrisy, and the gap between stated ideals and actual behavior. Negative framing only — Kyogen cannot be written with positive framing. Its weapon is mockery. A Kyogen piece that witnesses find funny is more damaging than a direct insult because the audience participates in the humiliation by laughing. A successful Kyogen performance fires a provocation flag on the subject if they are present in the zone, connecting to PUBLIC_INSULT mechanics. Minimum Acting 3 to perform — this applies to the lead performer (and to each named performer in a 2-role Kyogen). Below this threshold the lead’s roll automatically fails regardless of dice result. Collaborative supporting performers have no minimum Acting requirement for Kyogen — they roll normally and simply contribute nothing on failure. Full Clan, Gender, and Profession mismatch penalties apply. Multi-role: Kyogen supports a maximum of two roles — the lord (shite) and the servant (ado). This two-character dynamic is the structural heart of Kyogen comedy: the servant outsmarts, embarrasses, or exposes the lord. Three or more roles breaks the satirical form. Kyogen cannot be written with more than two roles.

Archetype subject and provocation flag resolution: if a Kyogen piece’s subject is declared as a typed archetype (Clan/Gender/Profession combination rather than a specific character_id), the engine scans present zone characters against the archetype at performance time. If exactly one present character matches all three features, the provocation flag fires on them. If multiple characters match, it fires on the highest-Status match. If no present character matches, no provocation flag fires — the piece plays as general satire without a personal target. Declaring an archetype subject gives the author political deniability but surrenders targeting precision: the author does not choose who gets flagged, the room decides. Two resolution logics apply to archetype subjects and should be kept distinct: the +25 “subject present” scoring modifier uses “any match present” — the performance opportunity exists if at least one character matching the archetype is in the zone. The −40 social risk modifier and the provocation flag both use “highest-Status match” — the danger and the targeting are always calibrated to the most prominent person present who fits the archetype.

**Bunraku**: Puppet theater. Large puppets with visible operators, tragic narratives, longer performance than other forms. Requires a troupe — minimum three characters who all have the piece in their known_by list. Cannot be performed solo. Costs 2 AP instead of 1. Thematic tendency: Bunraku productions carry the deepest emotional investment of any theatrical form — the visible puppet operators, the deliberate artifice, the tragic weight of the narratives create a unique aesthetic experience that moves audiences more profoundly than any other style. This is compositional guidance only — both payload types use identical base formulas. Mechanical distinction: as a concrete expression of Bunraku’s greater impact, all Bunraku performances gain +1 effective disposition_magnitude over the piece’s stored value, applied before the quality tier multipliers and performance Raise bonuses. A stored magnitude 3 Bunraku piece fires at effective magnitude 4. This bonus justifies the 2 AP cost and the troupe requirement — the investment produces genuinely stronger results than any solo or duo performance could achieve at the same stored magnitude. For the performance roll, all three troupe members roll Acting / Awareness vs TN 15; the lowest Raise count among successful performers sets the quality tier, and any single failure collapses the performance. Full Clan, Gender, and Profession mismatch penalties apply per role assigned to each troupe member. Troupe coordinator: the known_by member who initiates the performance is the troupe coordinator. For NPC purposes, the coordinator is the known_by member with the highest NeedType urgency for a performance NeedType (SEEK_GLORY urgency ≥ 40, DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP, or MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION); ties broken by Status descending. The coordinator role has no mechanical field — it is an NPC engine designation that determines who drives recruitment and receives the initiating-performer tiebreaker in the casting algorithm.

Formation trigger: when a coordinator wants to perform a Bunraku piece but the piece has fewer than 3 known_by members OR those members are not co-located, the NPC engine evaluates troupe formation. Fires when ALL of the following are true: the character holds a Bunraku piece in known_by; a performance NeedType is active; known_by count for this piece is below 3; at least 2 characters in met_characters have Acting rank ≥ the piece’s disposition_magnitude.

Recruitment: the coordinator generates WRITE_LETTER with topic type REQUEST_JOIN_TROUPE to up to 2 eligible contacts — the 2 highest-Acting eligible characters in met_characters. The letter includes a script attachment. On receipt, the ACCEPT_TRAINING reactive event fires. Willingness gate for REQUEST_JOIN_TROUPE: disposition toward coordinator ≥ Ally (+11) AND Acting rank ≥ piece’s disposition_magnitude AND no military, siege, or survival NeedType at urgency > 60. The Kyogen minimum Acting 3 gate does not apply to Bunraku. If willing, the recipient begins LEARN_THEATER_PIECE and generates BEGIN_TRAVEL if not co-located. The KETSUI gate is bypassed for REQUEST_JOIN_TROUPE as it is for teacher-initiated teaching offers — the invitation itself constitutes the commitment signal.

Learning logistics: for canonized Bunraku pieces, each troupe member learns independently (no teacher required). For private Bunraku pieces, the coordinator teaches both students via MENTOR_CHARACTER, using the concurrent teaching rules from Section 57.22.14. Formation cooldown: the formation trigger fires at most once per IC day — preventing a burst of simultaneous recruitment letters from a single queue.

Performance gate: Bunraku requires at least 3 known_by members co-located at performance time (the troupe minimum), in addition to the standard minimum cast check. If fewer than 3 are present, PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE does not fire for this piece regardless of score.

Role assignment: the existing casting optimization algorithm (Steps 1–5, Section 57.22.13) assigns the 3 co-located known_by members to the piece’s named roles. The coordinator receives the standard initiating-performer tiebreaker in Step 4. Known_by members beyond 3 who are present become collaborative supporters per Section 57.22.7a.

### 57.22.4 Casting — Role Accuracy

Pieces that portray named or typed characters require the performer to embody that character’s Clan, Gender, and Profession. This uses the existing Acting skill mismatch system directly. Each mismatched feature adds +5 TN to the PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE roll. Possessing the relevant Acting Emphasis (Clan, Gender, or Profession) negates the penalty for that feature. Maximum penalty for a fully mismatched performer: +15 TN.

Profession for this purpose means five broad categories: Bushi, Courtier, Shugenja, Artisan, Monk. Monk covers Brotherhood of Shinsei monks, ascetics, and similar figures outside the standard samurai school hierarchy. School is irrelevant within a category — a Bayushi Bushi playing a Hida Bushi has no Profession mismatch, only a potential Clan mismatch. The Acting skill’s Profession Emphasis applies to all five categories.

Clan subject casting: when a role’s subject_character is a clan rather than a specific individual, there is no fixed Clan/Gender/Profession casting requirement — the role represents an entire people, not one person. Instead: if the performer belongs to the same clan as the subject, −5 to TN (embodying your own clan is natural). If the performer belongs to a clan holding traditional enmity toward the subject clan — defined as any pairing whose default starting inter-clan disposition is Rival (−11 or below) — +5 TN (cultural distance makes authentic portrayal harder). Family subject casting: when a role’s subject_character is a family, the same structure applies using the family’s parent clan. A performer belonging to the same family as the subject receives −5 TN. A performer whose parent clan holds traditional enmity toward the subject family’s parent clan receives +5 TN. Families within the same clan never generate enmity — a Bayushi performer playing a Shosuro role has no enmity penalty.

Abstract subject casting: when a role’s subject_character is a Fortune, spirit, mythological figure, or unnamed archetype with no clan affiliation, no casting requirements apply. No mismatch penalties, no bonuses. These are purely theatrical roles unconstrained by lived identity.

Noh exception: The Noh mask removes all Clan-based TN modifiers for the performer — both mismatch penalties and the same-clan TN bonus. The mask abstracts the performer from their Clan identity entirely. Gender mismatch penalties also do not apply. Profession penalty still applies.

### 57.22.5 Composition — Writing a Theater Piece

Action: COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE — a distinct ActionID from CRAFT (Category 11). It shares conceptual similarity with crafting (skilled creative work over time) but has theater-specific mechanics that CRAFT does not: a progress track with degradation, Raises-at-completion with topic linkage and magnitude upgrades, and ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION NeedType integration. “CRAFT variant (Artisan sub-system)” describes its conceptual lineage, not a shared ActionID. Uses Poetry / Intelligence vs TN 15 per AP spent. Note: this pairs Poetry with Intelligence rather than its standard governing Trait (Awareness) — theatrical composition is intellectual construction (dramatic structure, political subtext, narrative architecture), not lyrical expression. For standard poem creation as a gift item, use Poetry / Awareness via CRAFT (Section 49, Artisan Track). Composition uses a progress track — the piece does not exist until accumulated progress reaches the threshold for its target disposition_magnitude. The following parameters are declared at the start of composition and cannot be changed mid-work: style, subject, framing, target disposition_magnitude, target topic_weight, and number of roles. Number of roles is structural — it determines the threshold from the first AP, and changing it mid-composition would require retroactive threshold recalculation, which the system does not support. Kyogen validation at declaration: if style is Kyogen and framing is declared positive, composition cannot begin — the engine rejects the declaration immediately. Kyogen is structurally satirical and can only be written with negative framing. No AP is spent on a rejected declaration.

Progress each AP: roll Poetry / Intelligence vs TN 15. On success, progress earned = roll result minus base TN 15 (minimum 1). Raises may be called before rolling, increasing the TN by 5 per Raise as normal; each Raise that succeeds adds +5 progress points on top of base progress. If Raises are called and the raised TN is not met (even if the base TN of 15 would have been met), 0 progress is earned — the same risk/reward rule as Raises at completion. On outright failure (roll below base TN 15 regardless of Raises), 0 progress is earned. A failed or overreached AP is not a setback, only a lost opportunity.

Progress thresholds by target disposition_magnitude: magnitude 1 = 10 points (approximately 3 AP at Poetry 3). Magnitude 2 = 20 points (approximately 6 AP). Magnitude 3 = 35 points (approximately 10 AP). Magnitude 4 = 55 points (approximately 15 AP). Magnitude 5 = 80 points (approximately 22 AP). A more skilled poet earns more progress per AP and completes faster. These approximations assume Poetry 3 and average rolls. Multi-role threshold modifier: each role beyond the first adds a flat cost to the threshold, reflecting the structural complexity of writing for multiple characters. 2 roles: +10. 3 roles: +20. 4 roles: +35. 5 or more roles: +50 per additional role beyond 4. The author declares the number of roles at the start of composition. Bunraku uses the standard multi-role threshold modifier if multiple roles are declared — the threshold increases by +10 per role beyond the first, same as other styles. The troupe requirement (3 known_by members at performance time) is a performance gate, not a composition constraint. Kyogen is capped at 2 roles maximum.

Raises at completion: when accumulated progress first meets or exceeds the threshold, the piece is complete. Raises are called before rolling, as normal, increasing the TN. Raises made on a successful completing roll permanently upgrade the piece: +1 disposition_magnitude (maximum 5) costs 1 Raise each. +1 topic_weight (maximum 3) costs 1 Raise each. Adding a topic_id linkage to the topic_ids list costs 2 Raises — the list starts empty at composition and each 2-Raise purchase adds one entry, up to a maximum of 2. The first purchase adds entry 1; a second purchase (another 2 Raises) adds entry 2. The topic_ids list cannot exceed 2 entries — this Raise cannot be called if the list is already full. If Raises are called but the raised TN is not met: the piece does not complete this AP, but progress is still earned normally (roll result minus base TN 15, minimum 1). The piece will reach threshold again on a subsequent AP. The Raise opportunity is not lost — the author may call the same or different Raises on the next completing AP when the threshold is met again.

Skill gate: the writer’s Poetry rank must equal or exceed the target disposition_magnitude at the time of completion. If the writer declared magnitude 3 but their Poetry rank is 2 when the threshold is reached, the piece cannot complete — progress is frozen at the threshold until the skill requirement is met. If the writer’s Poetry rank drops below the target magnitude mid-composition due to injury or curse, progress continues to accumulate but completion is blocked until the rank is restored.

On completion the piece is added to the author’s known_by list with a craft_progress field set to null — the work is done. The piece object is created in the world at this moment. Multiple simultaneous compositions: a character may have more than one piece in progress simultaneously. When COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE fires in Phase 5, the engine advances exactly one piece per AP using this priority: (1) pieces serving an active political NeedType (DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP or MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION) over ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION pieces; (2) among same-priority pieces, advance the most-progressed piece toward its threshold (highest craft_progress / threshold ratio); (3) ties broken by most-recently-declared piece.

### 57.22.5a Composition Degradation

An unfinished theater piece degrades if the author neglects it. If one full IC season (90 IC days) passes without any COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE AP spent on the piece, accumulated progress is halved. Each subsequent abandoned season halves the remaining progress again. Progress never reaches zero through degradation alone — a long-abandoned piece retains a residual — but the residual becomes negligible after two or three abandoned seasons and the writer is functionally starting over.

Degradation reflects the reality of dramatic composition: the emotional thread goes cold, structural decisions lose their logic, and the work requires reconstruction. A poet interrupted by war and returning a year later is not continuing — they are remembering. Degradation does not apply to completed pieces or to item crafting (Section 49) — only to theater pieces in progress.

### 57.22.6 Learning a Piece — LEARN_THEATER_PIECE

ActionID: LEARN_THEATER_PIECE. Learning a piece uses a progress track, not a flat AP cost. Roll: Acting / Intelligence vs TN 15 per AP spent. Progress earned per AP = roll result minus TN (minimum 1 on success, 0 on failure). Learning thresholds scale with the piece’s disposition_magnitude at half the composition threshold: magnitude 1 = 5 points. Magnitude 2 = 10 points. Magnitude 3 = 18 points. Magnitude 4 = 28 points. Magnitude 5 = 40 points. When accumulated learning progress meets the threshold the character is added to known_by. Learning progress does not degrade — an interrupted student picks up where they left off.

For canonized pieces (canonized: true): no teacher required. The piece is culturally available. The character studies independently against the learning threshold. Requirement: Acting skill rank equal to or greater than the piece’s disposition_magnitude — checked at each LEARN_THEATER_PIECE AP. If the character’s Acting rank drops below the piece’s disposition_magnitude mid-learning (curse, injury, or other effect), they cannot spend APs on this piece until the rank is restored. Accumulated progress is retained while blocked. Pre-learning check: if the character’s character_id is already in the piece’s known_by list, LEARN_THEATER_PIECE is blocked. This applies to both canonized and private pieces. Learning a piece qualifies the character to perform any role in it — role assignment happens at performance time, not at learning time.

For private pieces (canonized: false): requires physical presence with a character in known_by during each LEARN_THEATER_PIECE AP. The teacher must be willing and present for each session — learning cannot continue if the teacher departs or becomes unwilling. Progress already accumulated is retained; the student simply cannot advance until a teacher is available again.

Script transmission by letter: a written script conveys the piece’s title, style, subject, and framing — sufficient for the recipient to know the piece exists and seek out a teacher. It does not grant learning progress. A character who receives a script and later finds a teacher begins learning from zero, not from a head start.

Post-learning venue-seeking: when a character’s accumulated learning progress meets the threshold and they are added to known_by, the engine immediately evaluates whether they have access to a performance venue — using the same logic as the post-composition hook in Section 57.22.11. If the character is already AT_COURT or in a public VISITING context with sufficient audience, PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE enters the normal scoring pool on the next AP. If not, the engine generates an ATTEND_COURT or TRAVEL_TO objective with weight derived from two factors: political relevance (high weight if the piece’s topic_ids list is not empty and any referenced topic has current momentum > 30 — the piece is still politically live) and personal disposition (higher weight if the character holds strong disposition toward the piece’s subject). A character who learned a politically live piece they care about strongly generates an urgent venue-seeking objective. A character who learned a piece with no live topics and neutral personal disposition drifts toward venues on their normal schedule. The objective competes normally in Phase 5 — it is not a forced override.

### 57.22.7 Performance — PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE

ActionID: PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE. Cost: 1 AP (2 AP for Bunraku). Context: AT_COURT or any public VISITING context with sufficient audience — defined as at least 1 named non-immune witness present. The context gate is a broad permission; the scoring system’s −30 modifier (fewer than 3 non-immune named witnesses) and hard gate (zero non-immune witnesses) provide fine-grained discouragement. Requirement: performer’s character_id must be in the piece’s known_by list.

Roll: Acting / Awareness vs TN 15, modified by casting mismatch penalties (Section 57.22.4).

Performance quality tiers: Bunraku bonus: before resolving any quality tier, Bunraku performances add +1 to effective disposition_magnitude. A stored magnitude 3 Bunraku piece fires at effective magnitude 4. This bonus is applied first, before quality tier multipliers and before performance Raise bonuses. Failure — standard PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE failure fires (small disposition loss toward the performer). For multi-role pieces, all named performers take the standard PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE failure disposition loss individually — each put themselves on the stage and shares in the embarrassment of a collapsed production. Piece effects do not fire, times_performed does not increment. Success — piece effects fire at base magnitude (with Bunraku +1 applied if applicable), standard PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE disposition gain toward performer fires, times_performed increments by 1. If the performer is not the author and the author’s character_id is alive in the world, the author gains +0.1 Glory once per performance event, regardless of how many named performers participated — the trigger is on the performance, not on each individual performer. Success with Raises — each called Raise adds +5 to the TN; if the roll still succeeds, each Raise adds +1 to effective disposition_magnitude for this performance only (does not permanently modify the piece). Critical success (exceed TN by 15+) — piece effects fire at effective disposition_magnitude +2 (where effective magnitude already includes the Bunraku +1 if applicable), topic amplification fires at maximum topic_weight regardless of the piece’s stored value. Authorship glory fires on critical success as on standard success, once per performance event.

Multi-performer quality determination: for pieces with multiple named roles, each performer rolls Acting / Awareness vs TN 15 plus their own role’s casting mismatch penalties. Each performer calls their own Raises independently before rolling. The performance quality tier is determined by the performer with the lowest Raise count on a successful roll. If any performer fails outright, the entire performance fails — one poor performer drags down the whole production regardless of how well the others did. Example: three performers call 2, 1, and 0 Raises respectively and all succeed — the performance fires at base magnitude (0 effective Raises). If the third performer had failed instead, the whole performance fails.

Bunraku troupe rule: Bunraku uses the same multi-performer quality determination — all three troupe members roll Acting / Awareness vs TN 15, lowest Raise count among successful performers sets the quality tier, any single failure collapses the performance.

### 57.22.7b Court Session Performance Queue

When multiple characters generate PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE on the same AP in the same zone, they do not resolve simultaneously. Performances are a witnessed sequential event — two pieces cannot play at the same moment. All PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE actions in the same zone on the same AP enter a performance queue and resolve in order.

Queue order: by Status descending — the highest-Status performer goes first. In Rokugan, higher Status commands the court’s attention first. Ties broken by Glory descending, then by the initiating NPC’s character_id (deterministic tiebreak).

Maximum performances per AP per zone: 3. A fourth or later performance that would fire in the same AP is deferred — the NPC’s AP is refunded, and PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE re-enters normal Phase 5 scoring on the subsequent AP. The NPC is not obligated to perform on the next AP and the action is not pre-committed — they re-evaluate in full scoring and may choose differently if conditions have changed.

Sequential disposition update: each performance’s disposition effects resolve fully before the next performance in the queue scores and fires. A piece is removed from the queue and its action is cancelled if either of the following becomes true after the preceding performance resolves: the piece’s score drops below 0 after updated dispositions are applied, OR non-immune witnesses drop to zero (the non-immune audience gate from Section 57.22.13 applies at queue-fire time as well as scoring time). The NPC’s AP is not refunded in either case — they chose to perform, the moment simply passed. They may attempt again next AP if conditions remain favorable.

Player character priority: if a player character generates PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE on the same AP as NPCs in the same zone, the player character enters the queue at their Status position. Player characters are not given automatic priority over NPCs — Status is the only ordering principle. Collaborative supporter queue entry: a collaborative performance (lead performer plus any supporters) is a single queue entry, ordered by the lead’s Status. Supporters do not generate separate PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE actions and do not enter the queue independently. Their rolls resolve as part of the lead’s queue entry after the lead’s position in the queue is confirmed. A supporter whose Status would normally place them ahead of the lead in the queue does not reorder the production — the lead determines the queue position for the entire ensemble.

### 57.22.7a Collaborative Performance

Any piece can be performed collaboratively — a lead performer and up to two supporting performers who share the stage without holding a named role. Supporting performers must have the piece in their known_by list. They do not need to meet the piece’s casting requirements — they are not playing a named character, they are supporting the lead’s performance through presence, timing, and energy.

Collaborative bonus: each supporting performer rolls Acting / Awareness vs TN 15 independently. For each supporting performer who succeeds, the lead gains +1k0 on their performance roll. This bonus applies before the lead’s roll is made. Maximum two supporting performers — beyond that the stage becomes crowded and returns diminish.

Authentic presence bonus: if a supporting performer meets the casting requirements of any named role in the piece (correct Clan, Gender, and Profession for that role), they add an additional flat +3 to the lead’s roll on top of the +1k0, provided their supporting roll succeeded. For single-role pieces this is simply matching the piece’s one role. For multi-role pieces, the supporter qualifies if they match any role’s casting requirements — genuine cultural resonance with any part of the production deepens the performance regardless of which role they are nearest to. A supporter may only earn the +3 once regardless of how many roles they match. For clan-subject roles (where subject_character is a clan identifier): the authentic presence bonus fires if the supporting performer belongs to the same clan as the subject — no Profession or Gender check applies. For family-subject roles: the same rule applies using the family identifier. Noh mask exemption does not apply to the authentic presence bonus check: the bonus evaluates raw casting requirements regardless of style.

Supporting performer failure: unlike named-role multi-performer pieces, a supporting performer’s failure does not collapse the performance. They simply contribute no bonus. The lead performs alone if both supporters fail. This makes collaborative performance lower-risk than multi-role performance — a weak supporter wastes their AP but does not harm the production.

Supporting performer benefit: each supporting performer spends 1 AP participating in a collaborative performance. A supporting performer gains the standard PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE social benefit and +0.05 Glory only if BOTH their individual roll succeeded AND the overall performance succeeded. If the lead’s roll fails (performance collapses), no supporter gains any benefit regardless of their individual roll result. They do not receive authorship glory — that fires only for named-role or solo performances.

Learning implication: collaborative performance gives actors an incentive to learn pieces they cannot perform solo due to casting mismatches. A Crab bushi who cannot convincingly play a Crane courtier can still support a Crane actor’s performance of that piece, contribute meaningfully to the production, and earn their share of the social moment.

Stacking with multi-role pieces: a multi-role piece can also have collaborative supporters beyond its named roles. Named role performers resolve their rolls per Section 57.22.7. Supporting performers beyond the named roles contribute the +1k0 bonus to the lead’s roll. Both systems apply simultaneously.

### 57.22.8 Disposition Effects

On a successful performance, every named character witness in the zone resolves the following sequence. “Witness” means named characters with tracked character sheets and individual disposition values, excluding the performers themselves. Named performers (lead performer, named-role co-performers, and collaborative supporters) are participants in the performance, not passive witnesses — they are excluded from witness counts for scoring, audience gate checks, and disposition effect resolution. A performer’s disposition toward the piece’s subject cannot be shifted through their own performance. Unnamed crowd NPCs, heimin without character sheets, and anonymous background figures are also not witnesses for mechanical purposes. Disposition effects, immunity checks, and topic amplification counts apply only to named non-performer characters. For multi-role pieces the sequence runs once per role independently. Topic amplification runs once for the piece regardless of role count.

Step 1 — Check existing disposition toward subject. For single-role pieces: retrieve the witness’s current disposition toward the piece’s subject. For multi-role pieces: retrieve the witness’s disposition toward each role’s subject_character separately. Each role resolves Steps 2 and 3 against its own subject. Dead subject resolution: if a role’s subject_character is a dead or historical character (is_alive: false or pre-game figure with no active character sheet), the disposition shift targets that character’s clan instead. If the dead character had no clan, Steps 2 and 3 do not fire for that role. Archetype subject resolution: if a role’s subject_character is a typed archetype (Clan/Gender/Profession combination), disposition effects target the archetype’s Clan component. A piece about “Scorpion Male Courtier” shifts dispositions toward the Scorpion Clan. The Clan field is always present in a valid archetype and provides the disposition target. Archetype pieces function as clan-subject pieces for disposition purposes — the archetype’s specificity is expressed through provocation flag targeting (Section 57.22.3), not through a unique disposition channel. Topic amplification in Step 4 still fires regardless of subject type.

Immunity check: before resolving any disposition shift, check whether the witness has seen this specific piece within the past 30 IC days. Each character carries a pieces_seen field — a dictionary of piece_id → IC_day_last_seen — on their character sheet (Section 22.3). If the piece_id is in pieces_seen and the current IC day minus IC_day_last_seen is 30 or fewer, the witness is immune: Steps 2, 3, and 4 do not fire for them this performance. They have already absorbed the piece’s message. Immunity is per witness per piece — seeing the same piece twice in 30 IC days at different venues from different performers still triggers immunity. The immunity window resets each time the witness sees the piece: a new viewing sets IC_day_last_seen to the current IC day, restarting the 30-day clock. On a successful performance, all non-immune witnesses in the zone have their IC_day_last_seen updated to the current IC day regardless of whether disposition effects fire — witnessing is witnessing even if the character is already immune. Permanent known_by immunity: any character whose character_id appears in the piece’s known_by list is permanently immune to that piece’s disposition effects. The 30-day window does not apply to them — knowing a piece implies complete familiarity that no performance can transcend. A performer who has spent weeks learning every nuance of a work cannot be meaningfully swayed by watching it staged. This immunity is computed at check time by consulting the piece’s known_by list directly — no separate storage entry is required, and no pieces_seen entry is created or updated for known_by members. If character_id is in known_by, the immunity check returns permanent immediately, bypassing the pieces_seen lookup entirely. Known_by members are excluded from the non-immune witness count for scoring and gate purposes.

Step 2 — Apply proportional shift. Shift magnitude = piece’s effective disposition_magnitude multiplied by a modifier based on existing disposition strength: Strong Ally (+51 to +100) ×2.0. Ally (+11 to +50) ×1.5. Rival (−11 to −50) ×1.5. Enemy (−51 to −100) ×2.0. The result is the number of points added to or subtracted from the witness’s disposition value on the −100 to +100 scale. Example: a magnitude 3 piece with an Ally witness: 3 × 1.5 = 4.5 points. A magnitude 5 piece with a Strong Ally witness: 5 × 2.0 = 10 points. Fractional results are applied as-is — the disposition scale supports decimal values. Disposition values are clamped at ±100. Polarization rule — both framings always push existing opinions further from center, never toward it: Positive framing pushes positive opinions higher (further positive) AND pushes negative opinions further negative — the hostile witness resents the favorable portrayal of something they dislike, deepening their opposition. Negative framing pushes negative opinions further negative (further negative) AND pushes positive opinions higher — the witness who loves the subject rallies to defend against the attack, deepening their loyalty. Theater does not convert; it polarizes. Both framings drive opinions toward the extremes of whatever position already exists. For multi-role pieces each role uses its own framing — a single performance can simultaneously push pro-Crane witnesses more pro-Crane and anti-Scorpion witnesses more anti-Scorpion, if the piece has a heroic Crane role and a villainous Scorpion role. Subject-as-witness exception: if a witness’s character_id matches the role’s subject_character, skip Steps 2 and 3 for that witness-role pair — a character’s disposition toward themselves is undefined and cannot be shifted. Their pieces_seen still updates normally.

Step 3 — Neutral handling. Witnesses at neutral disposition (−10 to +10) toward a role’s subject receive a flat push of +2 or −2 in that role’s framing direction. This is not proportional — it is a seed, not an amplification.

Step 4 — Topic amplification. Runs once per performance regardless of role count. For each topic_id in the piece’s topic_ids list: if the referenced topic is active (momentum > 0), the performance adds topic_weight × 2 to that topic’s momentum once for every witness who successfully processed any disposition shift in Steps 2 or 3. Each named witness is counted once toward topic amplification regardless of how many roles shifted their dispositions — the amplification reflects engagement with the performance as a whole event, not the sum of individual role reactions. A witness successfully processed a disposition shift if their disposition value changed by any amount — this includes both the proportional shifts from Step 2 and the flat seed pushes from Step 3. Neutral witnesses who received a flat +2 or −2 from Step 3 count toward topic amplification. If a topic_id in the list references a purged topic this entry contributes nothing. Each entry in topic_ids resolves independently using the same topic_weight value.

### 57.22.9 Canonized Pieces

Canonized pieces are ancient works that predate the game. They are generated procedurally at world start and exist in the repertoire from the first moment of simulation. The canonized flag is set to true at generation and is read-only thereafter — nothing written or performed during play can ever become canonized. A piece composed by a player character during play may become widely known and frequently performed, but it remains a private piece (canonized: false) regardless of its reach or fame. The distinction is cultural age, not popularity.

Canonized pieces have author_id null — their composers are lost to history. They can be learned by any character with Acting rank equal to or greater than the piece’s disposition_magnitude without a teacher. They cannot be suppressed. A private piece (canonized: false) can be suppressed by eliminating all characters in known_by — suppression is a valid covert objective connecting to the assassination and Secret systems.

Character death and known_by cleanup: when a character dies, their character_id is removed from the known_by list of every piece they belonged to. This fires as part of the standard death handler. If the removal empties a private piece’s known_by list entirely, the piece is marked lost: true — permanently inaccessible but retained as a world record. When a piece is marked lost: true, all stranded learning progress entries for that piece are removed from affected character sheets. In-progress composition cleanup: if the deceased had a piece with craft_progress > 0 and below threshold (composition started but never completed), the death handler sets craft_progress to null and marks the piece abandoned_incomplete: true. The piece never entered the world, cannot be performed, taught, or learned, and exists only as a historical record. No character can inherit or resume the composition. A piece authored by the deceased but still known by living characters is not lost — it survives through them. Author death does not stop the authorship glory mechanic; the +0.1 Glory is simply not delivered.

### 57.22.10 Procedural Generation at World Start and DEDICATE_PIECE

The starting world repertoire is generated procedurally, weighted by clan cultural output. All starting pieces are canonized: true with author_id null. Approximate piece counts by clan: Crane 12–15. Phoenix 10–12. Lion 7–9 (martial and historical subjects, Noh and Kabuki weighted). Scorpion 6–8 (politically pointed subjects, Kyogen weighted). Dragon 5–7. Unicorn 4–6. Minor Clans total 4–6. Imperial Families 3–5. Crab 2–4.

Each generated piece rolls randomly for style (weighted by clan culture), subject (clan, family, or notable historical figure), framing, and disposition_magnitude / topic_weight within the range a skilled poet of that era would have produced. Kyogen validation: any procedurally generated Kyogen piece must have negative framing — the framing roll is overridden to negative for any Kyogen style result. topic_ids is empty at world start — starting pieces carry no active topic linkage, and acquire it through the DEDICATE_PIECE action during play (see below).

DEDICATE_PIECE (ActionID, Category 17 — Theater Actions). A character formally frames a theater piece as commentary on an active political topic, adding that topic to the piece’s topic_ids list. Primary use case: canonized pieces, which have empty topic_ids at world start and acquire linkage only through this action.

AP cost: 1. Skill: Courtier / Awareness vs TN 10 + (piece’s disposition_magnitude × 2). TN by magnitude: 1 = 12; 2 = 14; 3 = 16; 4 = 18; 5 = 20. Higher-magnitude pieces have established canonical interpretations that resist reframing — it takes real rhetorical authority to declare that a revered ancient work speaks to a current crisis. Context: AT_COURT or any public VISITING context with sufficient audience. Requirements: character_id in piece’s known_by; piece’s topic_ids must have fewer than 2 entries; target topic must be in character’s known_topics with momentum > 0; the same character cannot dedicate the same piece to the same topic twice.

On success: target topic_id is added to the piece’s topic_ids. This is permanent — the dedication becomes part of the piece’s reception history. The act is public: a zone_event_log entry is written and a topic propagates in the format “[Character] dedicated [piece title] to [topic name]” through all witnesses’ known_topics. Witnesses learn both the piece’s existence and its political framing.

On failure: AP spent. No effect. The attempt was public but failed to resonate.

Political dimension: any known_by member may dedicate the piece to a topic without the author’s consent. A Scorpion who learns a Crane piece could dedicate it to an anti-Crane topic, appropriating the Crane’s own cultural work. The dedication is publicly attributed to the dedicating character, not the author.

NPC engine: when MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION is active, the NPC is in known_by for a piece with topic_ids < 2, and an active topic in known_topics has momentum > 40 and its subject matches any role’s subject_character — DEDICATE_PIECE enters the Phase 5 candidate pool. Alignment score: 55 (above the AT_COURT compose trigger at 40, below direct court actions at 80). See Annex A.

### 57.22.11 NPC Engine — ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION NeedType

New NeedType: ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION. “I am moved to compose a dramatic work.” Distinct from TRAIN_SKILL (self-improvement) and SEEK_GLORY (public performance). ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION is inward-facing creative motivation that produces an external artifact.

Trigger conditions — all must be true: character has Poetry rank ≥ 1. No active ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION objective already in progress. No active military, siege, or survival NeedType at urgency > 60. Character holds strong opinion on at least one subject (disposition ≥ +11 Ally or ≤ −11 Rival toward any clan, family, or named character).

Personality weights: Compassion (Jin) +20. Courtesy (Rei) +15. Honor (Meiyo) +10. Ambition +10. Control (Seigyo) −15. Determination (Ketsui) −10.

Subject selection: the NPC scans their disposition map and selects the subject they hold the strongest opinion about, positive or negative. Framing matches the disposition direction. If two subjects score equally, the one with an active topic_id in the NPC’s known_topics is preferred — the NPC writes about what is currently relevant to the world.

Style selection weighted by school type and personality: Courtier school — Noh and Kabuki equally weighted. Bushi school — Noh weighted (martial themes, ancestral grief). Shugenja school — Noh weighted (spiritual themes). High Seigyo or Ambition — Kabuki weighted (dramatic, visible). High Compassion — Noh weighted (subdued, emotional depth). Kyogen fires only when the NPC holds strong negative disposition AND has a sharp satirical personality profile — defined as Manipulation rank ≥ 3 or Deceit rank ≥ 3. This mirrors the Kyogen minimum Acting 3 performance requirement: the rank that qualifies someone to perform Kyogen also marks the personality inclination to write it. Kyogen is never the default style selection.

Urgency: low by default. ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION almost never carries urgency bonuses. Exception: if the intended piece’s subject is involved in an active topic with momentum > 60, urgency +20 — the NPC feels the political moment and wants to contribute while it matters.

Decomposition: COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE is the only action serving this NeedType. The NPC spends APs on COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE until the progress threshold is met. No co-location required — composition is a solo activity performable in any context except UNDER_SIEGE or active combat. The NeedType resolves when the piece completes. Role count selection: the default is a single-role piece. The NPC chooses 2 roles only if ALL of the following are true: personality Ambition weight ≥ +10 (the NPC wants maximum theatrical impact), Acting rank ≥ 3 (the NPC can credibly direct a production), and at least one other character in met_characters has Acting rank ≥ piece’s target disposition_magnitude and could serve as a co-performer. The NPC never declares more than 2 roles through ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION regardless of Ambition — grand multi-role productions require player coordination or deliberate commissioning, not autonomous engine generation. Second role subject selection: when 2 roles are chosen, the lead role uses the NPC’s strongest disposition subject (per normal subject selection). The second role uses the NPC’s second-strongest disposition subject on their disposition map — the next most extreme value after the lead role’s subject, whether positive or negative. Framing for the second role matches its disposition direction. If the second-strongest subject is the same entity as the first (the same clan appears twice), the engine falls back to single-role composition — two roles about the same subject is structurally incoherent. If no second distinct subject exists with a disposition that has cleared the neutral band, the engine also falls back to single-role. Raises-at-completion topic linkage: when the completing roll fires and the piece is about to resolve, the engine evaluates whether to spend Raises on topic_id linkage before the NeedType closes. For multi-role pieces this evaluation checks topics matching ANY role’s subject_character across all roles — not only the lead role. If one or more active topics in the NPC’s known_topics have momentum > 40 AND their subject matches any role’s subject_character — select the highest-momentum matching topic from across all roles and call 2 Raises on the completing roll to add it to topic_ids, provided the completing roll result is high enough to support the Raises. If multiple topics match across roles, always take the highest-momentum one first. If the completing roll cannot support 2 Raises, no topic linkage is attempted and topic_ids remains empty. A second topic linkage (another 2 Raises, next-highest-momentum matching topic from any role) is attempted only if two matching topics exist and the completing roll supports 4 total Raises — this is rare. The NPC does not spend Raises on topic_weight upgrades through ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION.

Post-completion venue-seeking: when ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION resolves and the piece is complete, the engine immediately evaluates whether the NPC has access to a performance venue. If the NPC is already AT_COURT or in a public VISITING context with sufficient audience, no further action is generated — PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE enters the normal scoring pool on the next AP. If the NPC is not at an appropriate venue, the engine generates an ATTEND_COURT or TRAVEL_TO objective with weight derived from the piece’s current political relevance: high weight if the piece’s topic_ids list is not empty and any referenced topic has current momentum > 30 — the piece is politically live and the NPC moves quickly toward the nearest appropriate court. Low weight if topic_ids is empty or all referenced topics have low momentum — the NPC drifts toward court on their normal schedule. Using current topic momentum rather than a stored historical value means venue-seeking urgency adapts to world state: a piece whose political moment has passed generates less urgency than one whose topic is still active. The venue-seeking objective competes normally in Phase 5 against other active objectives; it is not a forced override. An NPC with pressing military or political obligations may not travel immediately, but the intent to perform is queued and will fire when circumstances allow.

Proactive teaching trigger: after the piece has been performed at least once (times_performed ≥ 1) and the piece has not yet reached meaningful initial distribution (known_by count < 3 AND times_performed < 3), the engine evaluates whether to generate a proactive MENTOR_CHARACTER objective to spread the piece. The threshold of 3 on both counts represents a small initial network — once the piece has three knowers or has been performed three times, organic propagation through topic awareness and REQUEST_LEARN_PIECE letters can sustain it without the author pushing. Below that threshold, the piece risks stalling and the author’s proactive effort is the only reliable spread mechanism. This fires if ALL of the following are true: personality weight for Courtesy (Rei) ≥ +10 OR personality weight for Compassion (Jin) ≥ +15 — characters who value sharing and cultural contribution are the ones who proactively spread their work. The piece has positive framing OR the piece’s topic_ids list is not empty and any referenced topic has current momentum > 30 — authors of positive art and authors of politically still-relevant pieces both have strong motivation to propagate. At least one character in the NPC’s met_characters set or who has appeared in a letter the NPC received (per Section 22.3 met_characters clause 2) has Acting rank ≥ piece’s disposition_magnitude — no point teaching if no one can perform the piece. If all conditions are met, the engine selects the target student: the highest-disposition ally among eligible contacts. The author generates WRITE_LETTER attaching the script and flagging the letter as a teaching offer, which the student receives as a REQUEST_LEARN_PIECE with roles reversed — the teacher is initiating, not the student. The letter is flagged teacher_initiated: true. The student’s ACCEPT_TRAINING reactive event evaluates willingness with the following modification for teacher-initiated offers: the KETSUI prior-objective gate is bypassed. KETSUI normally declines unless the student has a pre-existing MENTOR_CHARACTER objective targeting the teacher — this gate was designed for reactive learning where the student has already committed. A teacher-initiated offer does not require prior student commitment; the offer itself is the initiation. All other ACCEPT_TRAINING personality gates apply normally. The proactive teaching trigger re-evaluates each time times_performed increments, so the author continues seeking students as long as conditions are met and eligible contacts exist. Cooldown: the trigger fires at most once per IC day regardless of how many performances occur in the same AP queue. After sending one teaching offer letter on a given IC day, the trigger does not re-fire until the next IC day rollover — preventing a burst of simultaneous offers when multiple performances happen in a single queue.

### 57.22.12 NPC Engine — Political Composition

When DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP or MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION is the active NeedType and the NPC has Poetry ≥ 1, COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE is added to the action candidate pool. Base alignment score: 60 — lower than direct social actions such as GOSSIP (80), but available anywhere, making it valuable when the NPC is not AT_COURT or lacks social access to the target.

Score modifiers: +20 if not AT_COURT — writing is available regardless of location. +15 if an active topic matching the intended subject has momentum > 40 — the piece will amplify something already moving. “Matching the intended subject” means the topic’s subject field is an exact identifier match to the piece’s declared subject field (same clan_id, family_id, or character_id). Subset relationships do not constitute a match — a piece about the Doji Family does not match a Crane Clan topic. −20 if the NPC has no audience available in their current location — defined as no co-located named characters present in the same zone. Any named character regardless of disposition counts as potential audience. The −20 fires only in true isolation: wilderness travel, solo siege, solitary confinement.

### 57.22.13 NPC Engine — PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE Piece Selection

PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE serves three NeedTypes: SEEK_GLORY (theater as public performance and reputation-building, competing with PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE as an alternative path — when the theater piece score from this section exceeds PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE’s SEEK_GLORY alignment score defined in Section 15.4, the NPC chooses theater; when below, they choose direct performance), DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP (theater as political broadcast weapon), and MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION (theater as topic amplification tool). When any of these NeedTypes is active and the NPC is AT_COURT or in a public VISITING context, the engine scores each piece in the NPC’s known_by list. The highest-scoring piece is selected. If no piece scores above 0 after all modifiers, PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE does not fire this AP.

Base score: 50 for any performable piece. Modifiers: +30 if piece’s topic_ids contains an active topic in NPC’s known_topics with momentum > 30. +20 if strictly more than half of current non-immune witnesses (excluding known_by members) hold disposition toward the piece’s subject that aligns with the piece’s framing — performing to a receptive audience. For multi-role pieces, this modifier checks across all roles: it fires if the majority alignment check passes for any role’s subject/framing combination. “Aligns with framing” means the witness already holds a disposition in the same direction as the framing: positive disposition (≥ +11) for positive framing, negative disposition (≤ −11) for negative framing. Neutral witnesses (−10 to +10) do not count as aligned for this check — they have no declared side. “Majority” throughout this section means strictly more than 50%: an exact 50/50 split does not qualify. The modifier fires once regardless of how many roles satisfy the check. +20 if the NPC holds strong personal disposition toward the piece’s subject — defined as any disposition that has cleared the neutral band: Ally (≥ +11) or stronger, or Rival (≤ −11) or stronger. For multi-role pieces, this modifier fires if the NPC holds strong personal disposition toward any role’s subject. Fires once regardless of how many roles satisfy the check. +15 if the NPC is the author — pride in performing own work. −20 if the NPC is the author and has already performed this piece on the current IC day (“court session” = one IC day; the modifier resets at IC day rollover, allowing the same piece to be performed again the following day). −25 if strictly more than half of current named witnesses (excluding known_by members) have active immunity to this piece (seen it within the past 30 IC days) — performing for a saturated audience is wasteful, the message has already been delivered. −30 if fewer than 3 non-immune named witnesses are present — a near-empty or fully-saturated room is not worth the effort. Kyogen additional modifiers: +25 if the piece’s subject is physically present in the zone — mockery lands hardest when the target witnesses it. For archetype subjects, this fires if at least one character matching the archetype is present in the zone. −40 if the subject holds higher Status than the performer without a clear provocation pretext — a Kyogen attack on a social superior without cause is a serious risk that only high-Ambition or high-Manipulation NPCs will accept. When the subject is a specific character, their Status is used directly. When the subject is a clan or archetype, the Status proxy is the highest-Status member of that clan or archetype currently present in the zone — performing Kyogen against the Lion Clan when the Lion Champion is watching is as dangerous as attacking him directly; performing it when only a junior Lion observer is present carries far less social risk. For multi-performer Kyogen, “the performer” in the Status comparison is the highest-Status named performer in the production — the most socially prominent person taking responsibility for the piece. This always produces the most favorable Status comparison for the ensemble, which is correct: the highest-Status participant bears the social accountability.

Provocation pretext — machine-evaluable definition: a clear provocation pretext exists if ANY of the following conditions are true at the time of performance: (1) the subject has a hostile-tagged action against the performer recorded in the zone_event_log within the current or previous IC season; (2) the subject has an active provocation_flag targeting the performer; (3) the subject executed PUBLIC_INSULT or PUBLIC_DEBATE targeting the performer in the current court session; (4) the subject holds Enemy disposition (≤ −51) toward the performer AND has taken any court action against them in the past season. For multi-performer Kyogen, “the performer” in all four conditions means ANY named performer in the production — if the subject has a legitimate grievance with any participant, the pretext exists for the whole production. This differs from the −40 Status comparison (which uses the highest-Status performer only) — pretext is about collective grievance, danger is about the most prominent person taking responsibility. If none of these conditions are true for any named performer, the −40 modifier applies. If any are true for any named performer, the modifier is suppressed.

Multi-role casting algorithm: when a multi-role piece is selected for performance, the engine runs a casting optimization across all co-located characters in the piece’s known_by list. The goal is to minimize total TN penalties across all roles while concentrating the highest Acting skill where it is needed most.

Step 1 — Build the compatibility matrix. For each co-located known_by character, score them against each role: 0 if Clan, Gender, and Profession all match; −5 per mismatch feature. Acting Emphases negate their respective penalty as normal. Noh exception: for Noh pieces, Clan and Gender are treated as always matching (0 penalty contribution) in the compatibility matrix — the Noh mask removes these as factors, so they should not influence casting optimization. Only Profession mismatches score −5 for Noh roles. Result: a [performer × role] matrix of compatibility scores.

Step 2 — Identify easy, medium, and hard roles. Sort roles by the best compatibility score any available performer achieves for that role. Easy: at least one performer scores 0. Medium: best available score is between −1 and −9 inclusive — no perfect match but no severe mismatch either. Hard: no performer scores better than −10 — every available performer has at least two mismatches for this role.

Step 3 — Assign best-fit performers to easy roles. For each easy role, assign the highest-compatibility performer. If multiple performers tie on compatibility for an easy role, hold them as candidates and resolve in Step 3a.

Step 3a — Assign remaining performers to medium roles greedily. After easy roles are filled, assign remaining performers to medium roles in descending order of compatibility score. No special Acting-skill override — medium roles are handled the same way as Step 5 (greedy best-fit). If performers remain after medium roles are filled, proceed to Step 4.

Step 4 — Assign highest Acting skill to the hardest role. The hardest role is the one with the worst best-available compatibility score (≤ −10). Assign the performer with the highest Acting skill rank to that role — high skill compensates for TN penalty better than low skill does. If two performers tie on Acting skill for the hardest role, the initiating performer (the one who generated the PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE action) takes it.

Step 5 — Fill remaining roles greedily. Assign remaining performers to remaining roles in descending order of compatibility. Performers who know the piece but are not assigned a named role become collaborative supporters per Section 57.22.7a.

Minimum cast check: if the number of co-located known_by characters is fewer than the number of named roles, PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE does not fire for this piece this AP. The engine scores other pieces or defers to a different action entirely. Non-immune audience gate: if the number of non-immune witnesses present (excluding characters in known_by who are permanently immune) is zero, PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE does not fire for this piece at this venue regardless of score. Performing to a fully saturated room produces no effect and the engine does not waste the AP. This gate is checked at queue-fire time as well as at scoring time — a piece that passes the gate at scoring but reaches zero non-immune witnesses by the time it fires in the performance queue is cancelled at that point.

Venue-exhaustion travel trigger: when the saturation modifier (−25) fires for a piece AND non-immune witnesses are 2 or fewer, the engine evaluates whether a fresh venue exists within reasonable travel distance. “Recently performed at” means within the past 30 IC days — matching the immunity window, so that a venue is “fresh” again when most of its witnesses’ immunity has naturally expired. If an accessible court or public gathering is available that the NPC has not performed at within the past 30 IC days, the engine generates a BEGIN_TRAVEL objective with weight derived from the piece’s current political relevance: high weight if topic_ids is not empty and any referenced topic has current momentum > 30; low weight otherwise. This trigger fires once per saturation event and does not re-fire until the NPC has traveled to a new venue and performed there. It competes normally in Phase 5 and is not a forced override.

High-value fresh witness modifier: +15 if at least one non-immune named witness present (excluding known_by members, who are permanently immune) has Status ≥ 3. A Family Daimyo, Imperial official, or Clan Champion attending a performance for the first time is a meaningful political opportunity regardless of the broader audience composition. This modifier stacks with the standard audience alignment modifier — a Status 4 witness who is also disposed favorably toward the piece’s subject contributes to both the +20 alignment check and the +15 high-value bonus.

When no piece is viable — alternative evaluation: the engine’s scoring loop already evaluates every piece in the NPC’s known_by list simultaneously. If piece A is blocked or scores below 0, piece B naturally surfaces as the highest scorer without any additional logic. No explicit alternative-scan is needed — the loop handles it. What requires explicit design is the fallthrough when no piece at all is viable.

Compose trigger on piece unavailability: if no piece in known_by scores above 0 or passes the hard gate, AND the NPC’s active NeedType is DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP or MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION, AND the NPC has Poetry ≥ 1 — COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE is added to the Phase 5 candidate pool with alignment score 40. This places it behind direct court actions (GOSSIP scores 80, INTIMIDATE and PUBLIC_INSULT score similarly) but above inaction, reflecting that composition is a medium-term investment rather than an immediate strike. This is distinct from the standard political composition trigger in Section 57.22.12 (which fires when the NPC is not AT_COURT and scores 60 with modifiers). This trigger fires specifically because existing pieces are unavailable — the NPC recognizes their theatrical arsenal is depleted and begins building a new one. The lower score of 40 versus the standard 60 reflects that composition at court is competing against immediate social options; when off-court, composition is more relatively valuable because direct social actions are unavailable. The composed piece will be fresh, unperformed, and aimed at the current political target. Raises-at-completion for political composition: when a politically composed piece (originating from DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP or MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION) completes, the engine evaluates two Raise investments before resolving the NeedType. First, topic_id linkage: the engine checks topics matching ANY role’s subject_character across all roles (not only the lead role). If one or more active topics in the NPC’s known_topics have momentum > 40 and their subject matches any role’s subject_character, select the highest-momentum matching topic across all roles and spend 2 Raises to add it to topic_ids. If a second matching topic exists from any role and the roll supports 4 total Raises, add it as well. Highest momentum first — that is the most politically relevant topic at the moment of completion. Second, disposition_magnitude upgrade: if the completing roll supports 1 additional Raise beyond topic linkage costs and the NPC’s Poetry rank exceeds the declared target magnitude, spend 1 Raise to push magnitude +1. A politically motivated NPC wants the most powerful piece possible — they maximize what the completing roll allows.

Court action fallthrough: when theater is entirely unavailable — all pieces blocked, composition not yet viable — the engine falls through to normal Phase 5 action scoring. Direct court actions that serve the same NeedType will naturally score higher in theater’s absence: GOSSIP and PUBLIC_INSULT for DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP; CHARM, NEGOTIATE, and PERSUADE for RAISE_DISPOSITION; MOVE_TOPIC_POSITION for its own NeedType via standard court actions; PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE for SEEK_GLORY (the baseline performance action theater was competing against). Theater is a broad-reach medium-efficacy tool. Direct court actions are higher-efficacy single-target. The NPC pivots naturally — no special override needed. This fallthrough is emergent from existing Phase 5 scoring and is documented here for implementation clarity.

### 57.22.14 NPC Engine — Teaching and Learning Coordination

Teaching a theater piece uses the existing MENTOR_CHARACTER NeedType and MENTOR ActionID (Section 55.21.13) with piece_id as the objective parameter in place of a skill. All coordination infrastructure — co-location requirement, BEGIN_TRAVEL to reach the student, WRITE_LETTER to arrange a meeting, LOCATE_CHARACTER when the student’s location is unknown, ACCEPT_TRAINING reactive event on the student — is inherited from the MENTOR system without modification. Both teacher and student spend 1 AP per MENTOR session. Student gains learning progress per AP per the thresholds in Section 57.22.6. Concurrent students: a teacher may hold multiple active MENTOR_CHARACTER objectives for the same piece (teaching multiple students simultaneously). However, each MENTOR session uses 1 AP — a teacher with 2 AP per IC day can teach at most 2 students per day, one AP per student. The teacher allocates APs to active students in Phase 5 priority order; lower-priority students simply wait for a future AP. All students make progress at the same rate per session — there is no dilution from teaching multiple students. Competing teachers: if two teachers both hold MENTOR_CHARACTER objectives targeting the same student for the same piece on the same AP, the student’s Phase 5 scoring selects the session from the teacher they hold higher disposition toward. The other teacher’s AP is not spent for that session. If dispositions are equal, the student selects the teacher who joined known_by first.

Knowledge propagation — how a student learns a piece exists: witnessing a performance generates a topic in all witnesses’ known_topics, propagating through the normal topic system. Topic string format varies by piece type. For private pieces with a living author and a single role: “[Author] performed [piece title] about [subject].” For private pieces with a living author and multiple roles: “[Author] performed [piece title] about [subject 1] and [subject 2]” — all roles’ subjects are enumerated so witnesses receiving the topic via propagation understand the full political scope of the piece. For canonized pieces (author_id null): “[Performer] performed the ancient piece [piece title] about [subject]” — with multi-role enumeration applied as above. Archetype subject format: if any role’s subject_character is a typed archetype, that role’s subject descriptor is omitted from the topic string entirely. The string uses the piece title and style tag only: “[Author/Performer] performed [piece title] ([style]).” where [style] is the piece’s actual style (Noh, Kabuki, Kyogen, or Bunraku). This preserves the author’s political deniability and prevents exposure of targeting specificity through propagation. Witnesses present know whom the piece targeted through direct observation; witnesses absent learn only that a performance of that style occurred. An author who sends a letter with a script attached transmits piece awareness directly to the recipient (met_characters constraint applies — the author must know the recipient). A character who learns of a piece through either path can then seek a teacher.

REQUEST_LEARN_PIECE — new letter topic type. A character who knows a piece exists and wishes to learn it generates WRITE_LETTER with topic payload REQUEST_LEARN_PIECE targeting any character in the piece’s known_by list. On receipt the teacher evaluates willingness: willing if disposition toward student ≥ 0 (Neutral) and no active blocking objective. If willing, teacher generates MENTOR_CHARACTER targeting the student with the piece_id, and replies by letter confirming — both characters then orient toward a meeting location using the standard travel system. Unwilling if disposition < 0 or if teaching this student would be politically unwise given the piece’s subject and the student’s clan. If unwilling, teacher sends a declining letter and the student may approach another character in known_by. Cover identity clause: the political evaluation uses the teacher’s perceived clan of the student — the clan the student presents publicly, or their active cover clan if operating under a Shosuro infiltration persona. The engine queries the student’s displayed_clan field, not their real clan. A spy who has successfully established a Crane cover identity will be evaluated as Crane for this check. Teachers can only act on what they know — the system does not give NPCs access to hidden character sheet fields they have no in-world means of knowing.

Suppression implication: a politically dangerous piece can be suppressed by ensuring all known_by characters either decline to teach or are removed from the world before knowledge spreads wide enough to be unstoppable. A clan that wants to prevent a damaging piece from circulating has a window between composition and wide dissemination. Once enough characters know it, coordination becomes impossible and suppression fails.

Intelligence discovery of known_by membership: suppression requires knowing who to target. Four existing actions provide this intelligence at graduated precision, with no new ActionIDs required. Witnessing a performance automatically adds the performer(s) to the witness’s awareness — no roll needed, they saw it. PROBE on a known performer (Courtier / Perception, standard mechanics) may reveal who else they have taught: each margin of 5 above TN reveals one additional known_by character the performer has personally taught. SEARCH_QUARTERS on a known performer (Sleight of Hand / Agility) — scripts and teaching materials are physical objects; a successful search against the performer’s concealment TN reveals known_by characters they hold scripts for. GATHER_INTELLIGENCE with the piece as the named target (Investigation / Perception) — systematic inquiry across contacts; low success returns the author’s identity only, high success returns a partial known_by list proportional to roll margin. Suppression targeting is inferential at low intelligence investment and increasingly precise at high investment. A single witnessed performance tells the Scorpion who performed; extensive investigation tells them who was taught privately.
