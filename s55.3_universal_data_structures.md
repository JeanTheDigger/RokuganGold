### 55.3.2a Two-Character Targeting — Secondary Target & Intent System

**STATUS: **LOCKED

**Problem Statement**

19 actions and objectives in the engine involve two characters beyond the thinker. The original ImmediateNeed struct carries a single target_npc_id, which is insufficient. Without a secondary target slot and explicit intent signaling, Phase 3 (Generate Options) cannot build the correct candidate pool, and Phase 5 (Score) cannot correctly interpret the relationship between the two targets.

**Structural Addition to ImmediateNeed**

Two new fields are added to the ImmediateNeed struct (Section 55.3.2):

**target_npc_id_secondary: int or null. **The secondary character involved in the action. Populated either by the decomposition function (when both targets are decided upstream) or by Phase 3’s candidate evaluation (when the secondary target must be dynamically selected). Null when the action involves only one target. Fill only what applies — same rule as all other ImmediateNeed fields.

**target_intent: StringName or null. **Tells the engine how to interpret the relationship between target_npc_id (X) and target_npc_id_secondary (Y), and whether Y requires dynamic evaluation. One of four values:

**LEVERAGE_POSITIVE — **X is decided upstream. Y must be dynamically evaluated from the NPC’s available pool (contacts, vassals, retinue, or characters present — determined per ActionID by Phase 3’s context generators). Y is scored on capability, access to X, and willingness. High Y-to-X disposition is an asset. Example: ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION (Y is my contact who has good access to X).

**LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE — **X is decided upstream. Y must be dynamically evaluated from the NPC’s available pool (same ActionID-specific candidate generation as LEVERAGE_POSITIVE). Y is scored on damage potential, exploitability, or combat capability against X. High Y-to-X disposition means more damage when exploited — it is a reason to select Y, not a reason to avoid them. Example: GOSSIP (Y is the listener where poisoning Y against X causes the most fallout).

**TRIAGE_TARGET_NETWORK — **X is decided upstream. Y must be dynamically evaluated from X’s relationship network, not the NPC’s own pool. Phase 3 generates Y candidates from X’s known allies, vassals, and associates. Y is scored on relationship vulnerability — the weakest or highest-impact link to sever. Example: DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP as a NeedType (triage X’s allies to find which relationship to attack first).

**FIXED_PAIR — **Both X and Y are decided upstream by the decomposition function or objective text. No dynamic evaluation of Y is needed. Phase 3 does not generate Y candidates. Both IDs are passed through to Phase 5 and Phase 7 as-is. Example: “Break the alliance between Clan X and Clan Y” where both clans are named in the objective.

Null when the action involves only one target (the majority of actions). When null, the engine ignores target_npc_id_secondary entirely — no candidate generation, no secondary scoring.

**How the Intent Flows Through the Seven Phases**

**Phase 1 (Build Context): **No change. ContextSnapshot already includes known_contacts, dispositions, and characters_present.

**Phase 2 (Resolve Goal ****&**** Decompose): **The decomposition function sets target_intent based on the objective type and the current decomposition step. It always sets target_npc_id (X). It sets target_npc_id_secondary (Y) only for FIXED_PAIR. For the other three intents, Y is left null — Phase 3 will populate it.

**Phase 3 (Generate Options): **When target_intent is non-null and not FIXED_PAIR, the context generator builds a candidate list for Y. For LEVERAGE_POSITIVE and LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE: candidates come from the ActionID’s natural pool. GOSSIP draws from characters_present. ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE draws from the NPC’s vassals. ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION draws from known_contacts. The ActionID determines the pool, not the intent. For TRIAGE_TARGET_NETWORK: candidates come from X’s known relationship graph. The engine reads X’s disposition list from the NPC’s knowledge (known through Probe, daily conversation, or public knowledge — Section 20 applies). Only relationships the NPC actually knows about are candidates. Each ActionID + Y candidate combination becomes a separate ScoredAction entry. If GOSSIP has 4 possible listeners (Y candidates), Phase 3 generates 4 GOSSIP entries, each with a different target_npc_id_secondary.

**Phase 4 (Personality Filter): **No change. Filters apply to the ActionID, not to the secondary target. If GOSSIP is blocked by personality, all 4 GOSSIP entries are removed regardless of Y.

**Phase 5 (Score All Options): **The disposition_modifier component (currently −20 to +20) is extended. When target_intent is null: disposition_modifier uses the NPC’s disposition toward target_npc_id. No change from current behavior. When target_intent is LEVERAGE_POSITIVE: disposition_modifier uses the NPC’s disposition toward Y (willingness of Y to cooperate). An additional sub-score is computed: Y’s known disposition toward X, scaled to 0 to +10. This sub-score is added to the ScoredAction’s total. Y candidates who have strong positive relationships with X score higher. When target_intent is LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE: disposition_modifier uses the NPC’s disposition toward Y (can I approach Y at all?). An additional sub-score is computed: Y’s known disposition toward X, scaled to 0 to +10. High Y-to-X disposition means more damage potential — this sub-score rewards strong relationships, not weak ones. When target_intent is TRIAGE_TARGET_NETWORK: disposition_modifier uses the NPC’s disposition toward Y (am I willing to act against this person?). An additional sub-score is computed: X’s known disposition toward Y, scaled to 0 to +10. High X-to-Y disposition means a more painful severance — higher impact. When target_intent is FIXED_PAIR: disposition_modifier uses the NPC’s disposition toward X (the primary target). No secondary scoring.

The additional sub-score (0 to +10) extends the maximum theoretical ScoredAction total from 215 to 225. This is a minor range extension and does not affect the relative weighting of the seven core components.

**Phase 6 (Selection): **No change. Highest total wins. Tiebreaker cascade is unchanged.

**Phase 7 (Execution): **The executor receives both target_npc_id and target_npc_id_secondary on the chosen ScoredAction. It passes both to the owning system. For GOSSIP, the court action system receives “tell Y about X.” For ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE, the objective system receives “assign Y to handle X.”

**Structural Addition to ScoredAction**

**One new field: target_npc_id_secondary: int or null. **Mirrors the ImmediateNeed field. Populated by Phase 3 during candidate expansion or carried through from a FIXED_PAIR decomposition. Passed to Phase 7 for execution.

**SEND_INVITATION:** Pattern C. X = Court objective. Y = Person to invite. Intent = LEVERAGE_POSITIVE.

**DISPATCH_COURTIER:** Pattern B. X = Target daimyo. Y = Courtier to send. Intent = LEVERAGE_POSITIVE.

**MENTOR (arranging):** Pattern B. X = Student. Y = Sensei to assign. Intent = LEVERAGE_POSITIVE.

**ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (constructive):** Pattern B. X = Task/target. Y = Best-suited vassal. Intent = LEVERAGE_POSITIVE.

**GOSSIP:** Pattern A. X = Person to damage. Y = Listener for max impact. Intent = LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE.

**REVEAL_SECRET_PRIVATE:** Pattern A. X = Secret holder. Y = Recipient for max fallout. Intent = LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE.

**ASSIGN_ELIMINATION_TARGET:** Pattern B. X = Elimination target. Y = Operative for the job. Intent = LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE.

**ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (hostile):** Pattern B. X = Hostile task/target. Y = Best-suited vassal. Intent = LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE.

**DAMAGE_RELATIONSHIP (NeedType):** Pattern D. X = Person to weaken. Y = Triage X’s allies for impact. Intent = TRIAGE_TARGET_NETWORK.

**EAVESDROP:** Pattern F. X = Person of interest. Y = Incidental (not scored). Intent = null (single-target).

**Interaction with Existing Systems**

**Information Transfer (Section 55.6): **When an objective with a FIXED_PAIR is assigned to a vassal, both X and Y knowledge transfers. The vassal receives the lord’s known intelligence on both characters.

**Contact Discovery (Section 55.7): **Two-character actions still require the NPC to know X before decomposition produces a secondary target evaluation. If X is unknown, IDENTIFY_CONTACT fires first as usual. Y candidates are drawn from known characters only — Section 20 applies.

**Province Triage (Section 55.9): **Unchanged. Province triage selects a province, not a character pair.

**Personality Filter (Section 55.4.4): **Filters apply to the ActionID. If an action is blocked, all Y-candidate expansions of that action are blocked.

**AI Coder Instructions Addendum**

Add to CLAUDE.md: Two-character actions: X is always the reason (decided by decomposition). Y is always the lever (evaluated by Phase 3 unless FIXED_PAIR). Never reverse this. The intent field tells scoring how to read Y-to-X disposition. Same disposition value, opposite interpretation between LEVERAGE_POSITIVE and LEVERAGE_NEGATIVE. Candidate pool for Y is always determined by the ActionID’s context generator, not by the intent field. Intent tells scoring how to evaluate, not where to look.

### 55.3.3 ScoredAction — Eight Components

Fields: action_id, target fields (npc/settlement/province/topic), ap_cost. Score: objective_alignment (0–100) + disposition_modifier (−20 to +20) + personality_lean (−15 to +15) + competence_modifier (−20 to +20) + urgency_bonus (0 to +30) + standing_influence (0 to +15) + topic_position_modifier (−15 to +15) + resource_modifier (0 to −40, Section 55.32) = total_score. Maximum theoretical score: 215 (resource_modifier is negative-only and does not increase the maximum). All eight components fire for every action in every ContextFlag — the scoring formula is context-independent. Topic positions and standing objectives shape decisions on the world map, at court, on campaign, and everywhere else. No separate court scoring pipeline exists.

### 55.3.6 WallTowerStatus — LOCKED

For Crab lord-tier NPCs only. Extends ContextSnapshot with Wall-specific intelligence gathered through legitimate knowledge channels (scouts, Taisa reports, Shireikan communications). All fields reflect known information only — confidence degrades on stale reports exactly as ProvinceStatus does. Assembled fresh each engine firing. Fields:

- tower_id: StringName — unique identifier for the Tower (Tower 1 through Tower 12).

- si: int (0–10) — last known Structural Integrity from Taisa report or direct inspection. Stale reports may not reflect recent assault hits or seasonal decay.

- scout_deployed: bool — whether a scout is currently active in the adjacent Shadowlands province. Known only if Taisa has reported it.

- scout_report_age: int (seasons) — how old the last scout report is. 0 = this season, 1 = last season, 2+ = stale. Triggers DEPLOY_SCOUTS if above 1.

- scout_report_elevated_activity: bool — whether the most recent scout report indicates elevated Shadowlands activity in the adjacent province. Primary trigger gate for ORDER_SHADOWLANDS_SORTIE.

- max_taint_rank: int (0–5) — highest Taint Rank held by any named character in the garrison as known to the Kuni or equivalent. Unknown if no Taint-detection capable character is present. Triggers MANAGE_TAINT at Rank 3+, emergency removal protocol at Rank 4.

- tea_stockpile_seasons: float — estimated seasons of Jade Petal Tea remaining at current Tainted troop count. Below 1.0 triggers MANAGE_TAINT with Tea shortage flag.

- jade_stockpile_critical: bool — whether Tower jade has dropped below minimum sortie allocation threshold. Blocks ORDER_SHADOWLANDS_SORTIE. Triggers ACQUIRE_RESOURCE target=jade.

- garrison_above_minimum: bool — whether garrison Health is above minimum defensible threshold (1 Company, provisional) after accounting for any sortie detachment. Blocks ORDER_SHADOWLANDS_SORTIE if false.

- last_report_ic_day: int — IC day of the last Taisa report. Used to assess whether WallTowerStatus data is current. Mirrors ProvinceStatus confidence model.
