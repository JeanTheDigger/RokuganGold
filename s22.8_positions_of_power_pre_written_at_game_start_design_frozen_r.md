## 22.8 Positions of Power — Pre-Written at Game Start — DESIGN FROZEN, ROSTER PENDING

Every position of significance in the world is filled by a pre-written character at game start. Not just canonical lore characters — every daimyo, every Champion, every major courtier, every temple head, every significant military commander. The world starts fully populated with intentional people, not purely random generation.

- Canonical characters (Type 1) fill the highest-profile positions — Clan Champions, major family daimyo, Imperial figures.

- Pre-written non-canonical characters fill every other significant position — provincial daimyo, Shireikan, senior courtiers, temple heads, school masters.

- Pre-written characters are built with the same Clan + Family + School + Insight Rank template system but are individually crafted rather than randomly generated — they have specific names, specific personality virtue combinations, specific family connections, and specific starting objectives.

- The family web is constructed across all pre-written characters first — ensuring the web of relationships is coherent and intentional before positions are assigned.

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Law ****&**** Order):** The following law enforcement positions require pre-written characters at game start: at least one Clan Magistrate per province, Emerald Magistrates in each major city and the Imperial Capital, Jade Magistrates (when the Jade Champion’s office is active), and representatives of the three anti-maho organizations (Asako Inquisitors, Kuni Witch-Hunters, Scorpion Kuroiban). Each magistrate should have assigned yoriki. See Section 11.3 for the full hierarchy and charter. The current Emerald Champion is already listed in Section 2.1.17–2.1.18 as a required pre-written character.

Pre-written non-canonical characters fill all others.

ESTIMATED TOTAL: approximately 200–300 named characters at game start depending on World Map size, plus dynamic generation of military commanders, magistrate yoriki, and religious figures as the simulation runs.
