## 12.6 Daily Conversation System — LOCKED

When two named characters share the same settlement — the same castle, town, village, or waystation — they have the opportunity to speak. A ship on voyage also counts as a qualifying co-location for this system: characters aboard the same ship while aboard_ship_id is non-null (Section 57.42.8) are treated as sharing a settlement for Daily Conversation trigger purposes, with the ship’s Lesser Zone (Section 57.43) serving as the shared location. The Daily Conversation System models the organic, unscheduled social interaction that happens between characters who know each other and happen to be in the same place. It is distinct from formal court actions, personal visits, and deliberate diplomatic maneuvers. It is simply two people who know each other, talking.

**TRIGGER CONDITIONS**

A daily conversation check occurs once per IC day for every pair of named characters who share the same settlement. The check only fires if both characters have a mutual disposition of +11 or higher toward one another — the start of the Acquaintance tier. Below +11, characters are Strangers — they do not seek each other out for casual conversation. The disposition threshold reflects the minimum social warmth required for spontaneous interaction.

The probability of conversation triggering scales with disposition:

- Disposition +11 to +30 (Acquaintance): 10% chance per IC day

- Disposition +31 to +45 (Friend, lower range): 15% chance per IC day

- Disposition +46 to +60 (Friend, upper range): 20% chance per IC day

- Disposition +61 to +75 (Trusted Ally, lower range): 28% chance per IC day

- Disposition +76 to +90 (Trusted Ally, upper range): 35% chance per IC day

- Disposition +91 to +99 (Devoted): 50% chance per IC day

- Disposition +100 (Devoted, maximum): 65% chance per IC day

The check uses the lower of the two characters' mutual disposition values. Friendship must be mutual to generate conversation — a character who deeply admires someone who barely knows them does not spontaneously seek them out at the confidant rate.

**WHAT HAPPENS IN A CONVERSATION**

When a conversation triggers, two things occur: a topic exchange and a disposition effect.

Topic Exchange: Each character holds a pool of active Tier 4 Topics (Section 16.5) and active Tier 1–3 Crisis Topics (Section 16.1–16.4) they are aware of. During the conversation, each character shares one topic from their pool with the other. The topic selected is weighted by relevance — the most personally relevant topic to the speaker is most likely to be raised, though not guaranteed. Characters do not always lead with the most important thing on their mind.

If the receiving character already knows the topic, the exchange is simply mutual acknowledgement — they discuss something they both already know. No new information is transferred, but the topic's momentum is refreshed for both characters (Section 16.5 Decay rules).

If the receiving character does not know the topic, the topic is transferred to them. It enters their active topic pool at a relevance level shaped by their personality and relationship to the topic's subject (Section 16.5 Topic Relevance). The topic now begins decaying on its own schedule for the new character.

Disposition Effect: Every conversation that triggers grants +1 disposition to both characters. This represents the simple social value of shared company — two people who enjoy each other's presence and make time to talk grow incrementally closer. This is a small but persistent effect. Characters who are frequently in the same settlement and have high mutual disposition will slowly but steadily deepen their relationship through accumulated small interactions.

**STRONGLY OPINIONATED TOPICS**

Opinion Shifting — RESOLVED. When a character shares a topic during daily conversation, the receiving character’s starting position on that topic is calculated using the full formula from Section 15.5: disposition anchors toward the parties involved, virtue modifiers from Section 15.6, and personal relevance as amplifier. The sharer’s own position does not directly transfer — but it influences the listener indirectly through the disposition system. A character with high disposition toward the sharer (+61 and above) will receive sensitive topics freely (Section 12.2 information sharing threshold), and their accumulated disposition toward the sharer feeds into future anchor calculations. A trusted confidant’s opinion therefore carries more weight not through a separate mechanic but through the existing disposition system. No additional mechanic required.

**DESIGN NOTES**

- Daily conversations are automatic and passive — neither character needs to spend AP for this to occur. It is background social life, not a deliberate action.

- A character who is traveling or in isolation (no other qualifying characters in their settlement) generates no conversation checks.

- Player characters participate in the same system. When a player character shares a settlement with an NPC they know, the daily conversation check fires for them as well. The player sees the result — what topic was exchanged — as a notification.

- This system is the primary organic mechanism for news propagation within a settlement. Topics spread person to person through daily life without anyone deliberately spreading them.

- Per-character cap on Daily Conversations per IC day: a character (PC or NPC) participates in at most 5 Daily Conversations per IC day. Once a character has participated in 5 conversation fires that IC day, further Daily Conversation checks involving them fail silently — the check does not fire, no notification is generated, no topic is transferred, no disposition shift occurs. The check simply does not happen. This throttles topic propagation in dense court settings (Winter Court with many co-located courtiers) without constraining quiet provincial castles where the 5-per-day cap is rarely reached. The cap applies symmetrically to both participants — each character’s daily count increments by 1 per conversation they participate in. Value PROVISIONAL pending balancing.
