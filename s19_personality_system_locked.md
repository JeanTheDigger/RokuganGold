# 19. Personality System — LOCKED

Every named character in the game has a personality defined by two independent virtues — one from Bushido and one from Shourido. These two virtues operate independently, never as a combined archetype. A character's Bushido virtue drives their honorable impulses and social behavior. Their Shourido virtue drives their darker drives and self-serving tendencies. The two can align, pull in the same direction, or conflict — creating the internal tension that makes characters feel like real people rather than archetypes.

**System Rules — LOCKED:**

- Every named character has exactly one Bushido virtue and one Shourido virtue.

- The two virtues operate independently — each generates its own set of mechanical modifiers separately.

- No combinations are forbidden — any Bushido virtue can pair with any Shourido virtue.

- 49 possible combinations (7 × 7) — enough variety without being unmanageable.

- Mechanical effects of each virtue to be designed in a dedicated session. Definitions below establish the lore foundation.

## 19.1 The Seven Virtues of Bushido — LOCKED

Bushido is the ethical code of the samurai — a set of virtues handed down by Akodo that defines how an honorable samurai should live, fight, and die. A character's favored Bushido virtue is the tenet they most embody and most prize. Definitions sourced from L5R 4th Edition core rulebook.

**Jin — Compassion:**

"Through intense training the samurai becomes quick and strong. He is not as other men. He develops a power that must be used for the good of all. He has compassion. He helps his fellow men at every opportunity. If an opportunity does not arise, he goes out of his way to find one." — Akodo's Leadership

Compassion teaches samurai that, as the warrior elite of society, it is their duty to protect and guide the lesser folk of Rokugan. In its most obvious form this means offering military protection — guarding commoners against bandits, criminals, foreigners, and monsters. Bullying or abusing those of lower station is an act unworthy of a samurai, even if the social order allows it. Some clans take Compassion more fully to heart and seek to offer spiritual guidance — the Phoenix are known for educating their peasants in the ways of the Tao, seeing spiritual support as just as important as physical protection.

**Yu — Courage:**

"Rise up above the masses of people who are afraid to act. Hiding like a turtle in a shell is not living at all. A samurai must have heroic courage. It is absolutely risky. It is dangerous. It is living life completely, fully, wonderfully. Heroic courage is not blind. It is intelligent and strong. Replace fear with respect and caution." — Akodo's Leadership

Courage is in many ways the most basic and universal of all the Bushido virtues — every samurai is expected to be ready and able to die at a moment's notice. A samurai must be prepared to fight and die without hesitation, whether at his lord's command or simply due to unavoidable circumstances. It is popular to say that a samurai lives at all times three feet from death, since that is the reach of a katana. Importantly, courage does not mean foolhardiness — a samurai's life belongs to his lord, not to him. A samurai who throws his life away uselessly is failing in his duty. There are many times when retreating requires more courage than merely staying and dying.

**Rei — Courtesy:**

"Samurai have no reason to be cruel. They do not need to prove their strength. A samurai is courteous even to his enemies. Without this outward show of respect, we are nothing more than animals. A samurai is not only respected for his strength in battle, but also by his dealings with other men. The true inner strength of a samurai becomes apparent during difficult times." — Akodo's Leadership

Samurai are civilized men and women, not barbarians, and are expected to behave with courtesy and proper manners at all times. A samurai who shows undue emotion or rudeness is violating Courtesy and losing face, disrespecting those around him and shaming himself. A true samurai remains courteous even when facing his bitterest enemy or provoked with vile insults. Rokugani courtiers endlessly practice the art of the subtle and indirect insult precisely because open rudeness reveals weakness. Conversely, when confronted with failures of Courtesy by those of higher station, a samurai's own honor is demonstrated by enduring such provocations without drawing attention to others' failures.

**Chugi — Duty/Loyalty:**

"For the samurai, having done some 'thing' or said some 'thing,' he knows he owns that 'thing.' He is responsible for it and all the consequences that follow. A samurai is intensely loyal to those in his care. To those he is responsible for, he remains fiercely true." — Akodo's Leadership

A samurai must always be ready to serve his lord in whatever way is required, no matter what the cost. Death is the least a samurai may face — he must be prepared to endure humiliation, dishonor, shame, and failure for the sake of Duty. Duty is the reason why love is so problematic for samurai, since a samurai in love will feel a conflicting loyalty which may disrupt the fulfillment of Duty. The Scorpion Clan practically makes a fetish of Loyalty — in a clan where treachery is a way of life, there has to be one thing that can be counted on. Violations of Loyalty are punished in the most horrific way the Scorpion can manage — the terror of Traitor's Grove, where the souls of those who betray the clan are imprisoned for eternity.

**Gi — Honesty/Righteousness:**

"Be acutely honest throughout your dealings with all people. Believe in justice, not from other people, but from yourself. To a true samurai, there are no shades of gray in the question of honesty and justice. There is only right and wrong." — Akodo's Leadership

Honesty is in principle the simplest of the virtues but perhaps the most troublesome. Many samurai who serve in court find that Honesty is often a virtue which must be danced around or even violated to fulfill their duties. Almost all samurai who serve in politics practice the art of deceiving opponents while remaining technically truthful. The Kitsuki family of the Dragon Clan was founded on the search for truth above all else. The Scorpion ignore Honesty altogether and exhibit open contempt for samurai who strive to tell the truth.

**Meiyo — Honor:**

"A true samurai has only one judge of his honor, and that is himself. Decisions you make and how those decisions are carried out are a reflection of who you truly are. You cannot hide from yourself." — Akodo's Leadership

Both the subtlest and the most basic of the virtues, Honor teaches that every samurai stands in judgment over himself at all times. Bushido is not merely enforced by social convention but by each samurai's own heart and soul. A samurai without Honor cannot truly follow the other virtues, for he is merely acting as others expect. Conversely, a samurai with true Honor will follow Bushido even when society becomes corrupt and superiors command dishonor. Only the Scorpion reject Honor — and those few Scorpion who actually embrace it within their hearts are regarded with deep suspicion and labeled derisively as junshin, 'not of the blood.'

**Makoto — Sincerity:**

"When a samurai has said he will perform an action, it is as good as done. Nothing will stop him from completing what he has said he will do. He does not have to 'give his word.' He does not have to 'promise.' The action of speaking alone has set the act of doing in motion. Speaking and doing are the same thing." — Akodo's Leadership

Samurai are taught from childhood that they must express absolute sincerity in both word and deed. A samurai who speaks on behalf of his lord in court but does so in a lackadaisical or unconvincing manner is serving his lord as badly as if he refused to speak at all. The Dragon respect Sincerity's principle of unifying word and action. Even the Scorpion recognize that Sincerity is tactically valuable — if they say and do everything with complete conviction, their enemies will never know when they are lying. The Crab are known for delivering the sneering insult 'Oh, how sincere' whenever they feel someone is trying to manipulate them.

## 19.2 The Seven Virtues of Shourido — LOCKED

Shourido is the dark code associated with the Spider Clan and the Shadowlands — an antithesis of Bushido that prizes self-mastery, personal excellence, and the will to prevail above all social obligations. A character's favored Shourido virtue is not necessarily an evil impulse in isolation — the darkness lies in what it displaces. Definitions sourced from L5R 4th Edition sourcebooks.

**Seigyo — Control:**

Allows one to manipulate circumstances so that there may be only one outcome. A samurai of Control is a consummate strategist and political operator. They are never satisfied leaving outcomes to chance or to the goodwill of others — they engineer situations, manage information, and position people so that events unfold precisely as intended.

**Ketsui — Determination:**

Seeing a thing through to its conclusion. A samurai of Determination refuses to abandon a course of action regardless of obstacles, setbacks, or the counsel of others. They push through what should be impossible through sheer persistence. Their independence makes them powerful but isolated — they struggle to build the alliances that Bushido demands.

**Dōsatsu — Insight:**

The ability to see the truth behind facts. It brings foresight. A samurai of Insight is never satisfied with surface appearances — they observe, analyze, and infer until they understand the true nature of people, situations, and events. In court they are extraordinarily difficult to deceive and equally difficult to read, sharing nothing they have learned until the moment it serves them best.

**Chishiki — Knowledge:**

The basis of right thinking. Knowing the facts of a situation. A samurai of Knowledge collects information, histories, and secrets the way other samurai collect weapons — because knowledge is power. They are the most dangerous intelligence-gatherers in court, the most effective at reading and probing others, and the most likely to hold information in reserve until precisely the right moment.

**Kanpeki — Perfection:**

The encompassing goal that surrounds and envelops all other actions. A samurai of Perfection cannot accept adequate — every action must be executed with absolute excellence. This drives extraordinary skill development and genuinely impressive accomplishments, but makes them difficult to work with, slow to act when they cannot act perfectly, and deeply contemptuous of those who accept mediocrity.

**Kyōryōku — Strength:**

Acting upon knowledge with insight and control brings strength, and thus victory. A samurai of Strength believes that raw power — physical, political, or military — is the only reliable currency. They have little patience for finesse or the careful maneuvering of court. They believe that ultimately everything resolves through the application of force, and those who cultivate subtlety are simply delaying the inevitable.

**Ishi — Will:**

Will is the force which causes one to take the actions that they must. A samurai of Will cannot be intimidated, rarely changes course once decided, and resists social pressure that would bend lesser characters. They are extraordinarily difficult to manipulate through court actions. Their rigidity makes them reliable and formidable but can also make them unable to adapt when adaptation would serve them better.

## 19.3 Bushido Virtue Decision-Making Profiles — LOCKED

Each Bushido virtue defines how an NPC makes decisions when faced with choices — not what stats they have. The virtue shapes their priority hierarchy, what they will never do, what arguments persuade them, and how they respond to crisis end conditions. Mechanical connections to existing systems are noted below. Further elaboration will occur as connected systems are completed.

**Jin — Compassion:**

- Resource decisions: When a Famine crisis exists within their personal relevance range, commits to sending Rice without requiring political incentive — decision is automatic. Will not levy taxes above the minimum required (connects to tax cascade system, Section 4.3).

- Military decisions: Will never use Forbidden Tactics — specifically destroying farmlands or salting fields. Always offers surrender terms before initiating a storm assault (connects to siege end conditions, Section 11.7).

- Court: When receiving a Persuade action invoking civilian welfare or protection of the weak — acting character gets 1 Free Raise. Will never commit to Gossip or Disclose actions that harm innocent third parties.

- Siege end condition: When garrison Health drops below 25% and no relief is coming — chooses negotiated surrender over suicidal sortie or seppuku.

- Hostage likelihood: High — a Jin character prioritizes the lives of those under their care over personal honor. They will surrender and accept hostage status to spare their people further suffering.

**Yu — Courage:**

- Siege: Honor cowardice threshold reduced by 10 daily ticks — feels the shame of inaction sooner than other personality types (connects to Section 11.7 Honor cowardice system).

- Siege end condition: Strongly favors suicidal sortie over surrender or seppuku — fights to the last.

- Hostage likelihood: Very low — a Yu character fights until death rather than surrendering. Will not become a hostage unless physically incapacitated.

- Court: When receiving a Persuade action framing inaction as cowardice — acting character gets 1 Free Raise. Last to commit to peace negotiations while military strength remains — requires the strongest possible argument.

- Crisis: Among the first NPCs to commit troops to Shadowlands Incursion or Oni Manifestation crises regardless of odds.

**Rei — Courtesy:**

- Court: When receiving any court action that violates proper etiquette — a crude Intimidate, an open direct insult — that action automatically fails regardless of roll. When receiving a Persuade or Charm action delivered with exceptional courtesy — acting character gets 1 Free Raise.

- Court: Will never commit to Gossip actions — too crude for their sensibilities.

- Siege end condition: Prefers negotiated surrender with proper terms of honor — will not accept terms that strip all dignity from the defeated.

- Hostage likelihood: Moderate — a Rei character will accept hostage status if proper terms and courtesies are observed. Will refuse if the captor's conduct is dishonorable.

- Court: Will not make a Public Declaration that causes a public scene or breaches decorum even when their position demands it.

**Chugi — Loyalty/Duty:**

- Court: Declared objective always aligns perfectly with their lord's known position — no independent deviation possible. Cannot be moved by Persuade or Negotiate actions that conflict with their lord's known position — those actions require 1 additional Raise.

- Siege end condition: Fights to the last if their lord has not given explicit permission to surrender — duty overrides personal survival.

- Hostage likelihood: Entirely dependent on their lord's decision. If their lord permits surrender, they accept hostage status without complaint. If not, they fight to the last regardless of personal survival.

- Supply sharing: Follows their lord's supply decisions exactly — no independent judgment applied.

- Crisis commitment: Automatically commits to whatever action their lord has already publicly committed to.

**Gi — Honesty/Righteousness:**

- Court: Will never use Gossip, Disclose with false information, or Fabricate a Secret — these actions are unavailable to them. Public Declarations are always truthful — cannot declare a false position.

- Court: When receiving a Persuade action grounded in verifiable facts and clear justice — acting character gets 1 Free Raise.

- Crisis: When presented with evidence of wrongdoing, will act on it regardless of political inconvenience — cannot look the other way.

- War: Will support peace negotiations when the war is clearly lost rather than maintain false bravado — acknowledges military reality honestly.

- Hostage likelihood: Moderate — a Gi character accepts the honest reality of defeat. If surrender is clearly the right and just outcome, they accept hostage status without false pride.

**Meiyo — Honor:**

- Court: Immune to shame-based public attacks — Publicly Insult actions against them require 1 additional Raise to have any effect. Social pressure and peer opinion carry no weight in their decision-making.

- Siege end condition: Chooses whichever end condition their internal code judges most honorable — determined at character generation and fixed thereafter. Not driven by public perception.

- Hostage likelihood: Determined entirely by their internal code — if their Honor demands fighting to the last, they will. If their Honor accepts surrender, they accept it. Unpredictable from the outside.

- Will refuse orders from their lord that violate their personal code — unique among Bushido personality types in this capacity.

- Court: Their own Public Declarations are always consistent with their private position — no gap between stated and actual objective.

**Makoto — Sincerity:**

- Court: Once they make a Public Declaration, their objective is locked for the remainder of the court session — cannot be changed regardless of changed circumstances.

- Court: Probe actions against a Makoto NPC always succeed at partial success minimum — they cannot help but convey their genuine position. When receiving a Persuade action delivered with genuine conviction — acting character gets 1 Free Raise.

- Supply commitments: Once committed to sending resources, will always follow through — reneging is not possible for this personality type.

- Siege end condition: Once they declare their intention (fight, surrender, or sortie) they will not reverse course regardless of what changes.

- Hostage likelihood: Determined entirely by their declaration. If they declared surrender, they accept hostage status absolutely and will not attempt escape — their word is given. If they declared to fight, they fight regardless of consequences.

## 19.4 Shourido Virtue Decision-Making Profiles — LOCKED

Shourido virtues are narrower in scope than Bushido virtues but hit harder in the specific areas they affect. Less breadth, more impact. Like Bushido profiles, these define decision-making behavior — not stat modifiers.

**Control:**

- Will never commit to a position at court until they have observed every other lord's position first — always the last to make a Public Declaration.

- Will never reveal their true objective through conversation — Probe actions return only what they choose to show.

- In any crisis, will first assess how they can shape the outcome to their advantage before committing to any course of action.

**Determination:**

- Will never request aid from another lord regardless of need — would rather suffer deprivation than ask. Connects to supply source hierarchy in Section 11.7.

- Will never accept a relief army during a siege if it creates a debt or obligation — would rather hold out alone.

- Hostage likelihood: Very low — accepting hostage status creates an obligation to the captor, which is anathema to Determination. They will fight or die before becoming indebted.

- Once committed to a course of action, will see it through regardless of changed circumstances or counsel from others.

**Insight:**

- Will never act on surface appearances — always seeks the deeper truth before committing to any position.

- In court, withholds what they have observed until the moment it serves them best — never reveals intelligence prematurely.

- Will not make a commitment until they believe they understand the true motivations of all relevant parties.

**Knowledge:**

- Will seek information before committing to any action — will use at least one intelligence action before making a Public Declaration.

- Will never act on incomplete information when more can be gathered — delays commitment until they judge they have enough.

- Hoards what they learn — shares intelligence only when it serves a specific and deliberate purpose.

**Perfection:**

- Will never commit troops to a battle they assess as unwinnable — an imperfect outcome is worse than no outcome at all.

- Will not make a Public Declaration until they are certain of their position — will not speak until ready to speak with complete conviction.

- Will withdraw from a commitment rather than execute it poorly — an incomplete action is preferable to a flawed one.

**Strength:**

- Will always respond to a crisis with military commitment first — diplomatic solutions are considered only after military options are exhausted.

- Will not engage in subtle court maneuvering when direct confrontation is available — prefers the blunt approach in all situations.

- Responds to pressure with counter-escalation rather than compliance or retreat — pushing them harder makes them dig in deeper.

**Will:**

- Once decided on a course of action, will not change it regardless of new information, changed circumstances, or arguments from others.

- Will not be drawn into negotiation once they have committed — their position is fixed and further court actions targeting that position are futile.

- In a siege with no hope, will fight to the last regardless of what any other personality element would suggest — Will overrides all other end condition preferences when active.

- Hostage likelihood: Near zero — a Will character cannot be broken or redirected. They fight to the last. The only circumstance under which they become a hostage is physical incapacitation.

## 19.5 Personality Tax & Stipend Modifier Tables — LOCKED

Each named character has a Tax Lean and a Stipend Lean derived from their Bushido and Shourido virtues. The two lean values are summed to produce the final Tax Modifier and Stipend Modifier, soft-capped at ±20%. These plug directly into the Tax Cascade (Section 4.3.7) and Koku Flow (Section 4.3.9) hooks.

### Design Rules — LOCKED

Each virtue contributes a lean value in the range −10 to +10.

Tax Modifier = Bushido Tax Lean + Shourido Tax Lean, capped at ±20%.

Stipend Modifier = Bushido Stipend Lean + Shourido Stipend Lean, capped at ±20%.

Positive Tax Modifier: lord collects more than default, passes less upward, loses loyalty from vassals below over time.

Negative Tax Modifier: lord collects less, gains loyalty below, but passes less upward — superiors may notice.

Positive Stipend Modifier: lord distributes more than default downward, builds vassal loyalty, retains less personally.

Negative Stipend Modifier: lord retains more, weakening loyalty below.

Chugi (Duty) and Makoto (Sincerity) are exceptions: their lean is 0 because they execute their declared position exactly rather than having an independent economic tendency. Makoto’s tax rate is fixed at character generation and does not drift.

The subsistence floor (1.00 Rice per PU) cannot be breached by any Tax Modifier under any circumstances.

### Bushido Virtue Leans — LOCKED

Bushido virtues reflect the samurai’s ethical code. Their leans are generally negative on tax (honorable lords do not over-extract) and positive on stipend (honorable lords provide for those below).

Jin — Compassion: Tax Lean −10, Stipend Lean +10. Explicit in §19.3: will not levy above minimum. Most generous distributor.

Gi — Honesty: Tax Lean −5, Stipend Lean +4. Will not fabricate surplus or skim; also will not over-squeeze.

Meiyo — Honor: Tax Lean −4, Stipend Lean +4. Aggressive extraction and miserly distribution both feel beneath their honor.

Yu — Courage: Tax Lean −3, Stipend Lean +5. Invests resources in military capacity; rewards those who fight for them.

Rei — Courtesy: Tax Lean −2, Stipend Lean +3. Extortion is uncouth; proper form demands fair treatment below.

Chugi — Duty: Tax Lean 0, Stipend Lean 0. No independent lean — executes lord’s directive exactly on both axes.

Makoto — Sincerity: Tax Lean 0, Stipend Lean 0. Collects and distributes exactly what declared at generation; immovable.

### Shourido Virtue Leans — LOCKED

Shourido virtues are narrower but hit harder. They generally push toward extraction on tax and retention on stipend — self-serving lords keep more. Exceptions exist where the virtue’s logic demands strategic generosity (Seigyo) or principled correctness (Kanpeki).

Kyōryōku — Strength: Tax Lean +10, Stipend Lean −7. Takes what they need by force of position; keeps resources as power.

Ketsui — Determination: Tax Lean +8, Stipend Lean −8. Takes what they need; expects vassals to be equally self-reliant.

Ishi — Will: Tax Lean +6, Stipend Lean −2. Collects what they decided; underlying tendency is retention, not generosity.

Seigyo — Control: Tax Lean +7, Stipend Lean +3. Extracts strategically; distributes enough to keep vassals loyal and dependent.

Chishiki — Knowledge: Tax Lean +5, Stipend Lean +2. Knows every rule and loophole on collection; gives the minimum that maintains loyalty.

Dōsatsu — Insight: Tax Lean +4, Stipend Lean +3. Calculates maximum sustainable extraction; calibrates exactly what keeps vassals stable.

Kanpeki — Perfection: Tax Lean +2, Stipend Lean +2. Pursues the correct amount on both axes — neither punishing nor generous.

### Worked Examples — LOCKED

**Jin + Kyōryōku: **Tax: −10 + 10 = 0. Stipend: +10 − 7 = +3. Does not over-tax (compassion overrides the strength impulse), but keeps resources rather than distributing freely. Reads as a protective but not especially generous lord.

**Ketsui + Gi: **Tax: +8 − 5 = +3. Stipend: −8 + 4 = −4. Collects firmly and honestly — no skimming but no softness either. Keeps most of what flows through. Vassals feel the pinch.

**Seigyo + Chugi: **Tax: +7 + 0 = +7. Stipend: +3 + 0 = +3. Chugi defers to lord’s directive; Seigyo sets an extractive-but-controlled baseline. A strategically calculating lord who uses both levers deliberately.

**Ishi + Yu: **Tax: +6 − 3 = +3. Stipend: −2 + 5 = +3. Firm on collection, generous with reward. A respected if inflexible commander — hard to negotiate with on tax policy, reliably good to those who serve.

**Jin + Jin (impossible — illustrates the cap): **Not a valid combination (one Bushido, one Shourido required). Included to confirm that the ±20% cap would bind extreme combinations if two high-magnitude virtues aligned: e.g. Kyōryōku + Ketsui = Tax +10 + 8 = +18, capped at +20% if Stipend also ran high.

Both hooks are already built into those systems and read the modifier value from the lord’s character record. Personality-driven supply sharing willingness is covered by the behavioral gates in Sections 19.3–19.4 and does not require a separate numeric modifier.
