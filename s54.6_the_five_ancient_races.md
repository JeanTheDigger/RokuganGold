## 54.6 The Five Ancient Races

The Five Races came to prominence long before the rise of Rokugan or even humanity. They preceded the Nezumi Empire and perhaps even the ancient Naga. Their civilization is so ancient that little evidence remains to mark its existence or passing. Long ago, there was only chaos – the realm of Ningen-do did not exist as mankind knows it. Everything was a tumultuous maelstrom of elemental energy, seething between the boundaries of the Spirit Realms. Then, somehow, something changed. Intelligent creatures came into being within the turmoil, each marked by the power of a particular element: the doughty Zokujin represented the strength of Earth; the fickle Ningyo represented the impermanence of Water; the clever Kenku represented the subtlety of Air; the fierce and inventive Trolls represented the power of Fire; and the wise Kitsu represented the mysteries of Void.

Using their natural magic the Five Races each carved tiny bubbles of stable reality within the maelstrom. All of this changed when the races found one another. Each possessed strengths the others lacked. The Five Races determined to combine their will to make a world that would last forever. In the center of chaos they created a vast city – the City of Night – and in the heart of this city they combined their most powerful magic in the form of a great crystal focus. Within the focus all five elements were balanced in perfect harmony. The Five Races used the focus to impose their wisdom on the maelstrom, awakening the elemental spirits and importuning them to cease their mindless destruction. Thus, by the will of the Five Races, the first elemental spirits were born, and Ningen-do, the mortal realm as man knows it, was created.

The creation of this new world was not without consequence. Deeper in the Spirit Realms, the dark realm of Jigoku stirred awake – a sinister and intelligent realm that looked upon the stability of Ningen-do with terrible hunger. It extended its dark tendrils into the mortal world and began to corrupt what it found there, sending forth its servants, the ruthless oni, and waging war upon Ningen-do. The Five Races had never faced such a threat as this.

Tsuno Kishenku, leader of a militant order of Kitsu known as the Soultwisters, believed only the crystal focus could defeat Jigoku. Soli Xiaomin, the elder Kitsu mystic who ruled the City of Night, refused to risk using its power. Kishenku agreed to Xiaomin’s wishes… but only so he could marshal his forces in secret and attack the City of Night while Xiaomin was distracted. Kishenku seized the crystal focus for himself – the power was so great even Jigoku’s forces could not stand against it. Most of the twisted creatures who had invaded the mortal world were incinerated. A handful fled back into their shadow realm to plot vengeance. But Kishenku could not maintain control. A wave of energy ripped through the City of Night, killing its inhabitants and turning the city itself into a strange purple crystal. The shockwaves rippled across the world, bringing death and ruin to all the cities of the Five Races. The survivors hunted down Kishenku and his followers. For their crimes they were cast into the Realm of Slaughter – Toshigoku – where, over time, they were twisted into the creatures now known as Tsuno.

Descendants of the other races, however, survive to this day. All of them have declined from their former lofty status as creators of the mortal realm – some more than others – but all of them yet survive.

### The Kenku

The Kenku are quick and agile creatures, small by Rokugani standards. In their natural forms they resemble humanoid crows and stand a little over four feet high. Around humans they prefer to utilize disguise, combining magical and mundane means to conceal their true nature. Many Kenku live among humans unnoticed, usually disguised as individuals of humble status. They are particularly fond of passing themselves off as monks, and many Rokugani tell stories of wandering monks on remote roads who reveal themselves as Kenku to worthy individuals.

The Kenku lost much of the power they used to help create the City of Night, but its legacy remains. The Kenku do not age and are immune to ordinary maladies – they are effectively immortal, though they can still die due to violence, hunger, or mishap. After the City was destroyed, the few remaining Kenku retreated to remote regions to contemplate their place in the universe. Some even removed themselves from the mortal realm altogether, withdrawing to Sakkaku, the Realm of Tricksters. A Kenku will always recognize another of his kind and greet him with courtesy and respect, but they inevitably soon go their separate ways. The Kenku rarely mate or reproduce, and over the centuries their numbers have gradually declined. Despite this bleak outlook, the Kenku are surprisingly optimistic creatures – they believe their immortality is proof of the responsibility the universe has placed on them, and they shall continue to endure so long as they are needed to guide those who have inherited the world they helped create.

Modern Kenku are teachers. They watch the world from secrecy, biding their time and searching for those who seem worthy. The Phoenix Clan histories feature tales of Kenku teachers dating back to their time as the Tribe of Isawa, and the Crane claim that Kakita himself learned the art of Iaijutsu from a Kenku. A Kenku’s choice can often be quite random – from time to time a hero simply strikes a Kenku’s fancy. But a Kenku will never accept a student unless he has been properly tested, and tests are central to the Kenku philosophy. Tests can be quite dangerous – or humiliating – for their subjects. Kenku have been known to steal valuables, seduce wives, and even defile temples, all with the intent of teaching someone a lesson, earning them a reputation as malicious pranksters as much as enlightened teachers.

The Kenku are greatly renowned not only for their wisdom but also for their extraordinary swordsmanship. The Kenku Kensei (“sword saint”) style has persisted since the time of the Five Races, making it the oldest known martial style in Rokugan. Their style focuses on speed and precision over strength, patiently waiting for the proper moment to deliver a single devastating blow while distracting enemies with careful feints. Kenku swords are of extremely high quality, and on the rare occasions they are given to humans as gifts they become treasured family heirlooms. The Kenku require three conditions before they will teach their swordsmanship to a human: the student must have the talent for it, the student must actually need the skill, and the student must swear to absolute secrecy – a human who learns Kenku techniques is expected to teach no one else, on pain of his own death.

In the days before the Kami fell, Shinsei was just a man – but an extraordinarily wise one. The Kenku recognized this and knew the forces of Jigoku were growing in power. A Kenku named Takashi came to Shinsei early in life, seeking to teach him and prepare him for Jigoku’s inevitable attack. To Takashi’s surprise, Shinsei not only passed his tests but taught the Kenku a great deal in turn. Over the centuries, Shinsei’s wisdom and deeds have earned him great respect among the Kenku, and this is likely one of the reasons they prefer to disguise themselves as Taoist monks.

### The Ningyo

A Ningyo is the Rokugani equivalent of a mermaid. They are elegant creatures with smooth silver skin covered with shimmering scales. From the waist up they are vaguely humanoid, though their fangs and long webbed fingers betray a distinctly nonhuman origin. From the waist down, their bodies taper into serpentine shapes up to twelve feet long, marked with several broad fins. The Ningyo have always been naturally reclusive. When the Five Races shaped the mortal realm, the Ningyo chose to live beneath the water, building their cities in the lakes, rivers, and oceans. When Jigoku came forth, the Ningyo chose to hide in their remote underwater homes rather than fight alongside their allies – a decision that would prove catastrophic.

When the Soultwisters seized the City of Night and unleashed its power against Jigoku, Kishenku also turned it against the Ningyo traitors. The Soultwister’s curse drove the majority of them insane, devolving them to a near-feral state. Even those who maintained some bit of sentience became a shadow of their former selves – unable to survive outside of the water, their once-great civilization forever lost. They also ceased to age and now suffer their madness forever. Worse, any creature who consumed the flesh of a Ningyo could potentially inherit their immortality – when this secret was discovered, other races began to hunt them. The Ningyo race might have eventually met a grim fate if not for an intervention from the serpentine spirits known as the Orochi.

The Orochi are serpentine spirits native to Sakkaku who had long endured torment from the malicious mujina of that realm. When the creation of Ningen-do finally gave them hope for escape, a handful managed to cross into the mortal realm under King Ryujin before the Mujina sealed the borders of Sakkaku. Ryujin journeyed to the Ningyo and, unable to remove Kishenku’s curse, instead made it stronger – any living being who ingested Ningyo flesh would now also suffer the Orochi King’s curse, transforming any who had harvested Ningyo into crazed monstrous beings. This protected the Ningyo from hunters. In return, the Ningyo offered the Orochi safe haven within their cities, and the friendship between Ningyo and Orochi has endured ever since.

The Orochi continue to have a calming effect on the Ningyo, greatly mitigating the darker effects of Kishenku’s curse. Ningyo who spend a great deal of time in the presence of Orochi become less savage and bestial than their cousins. Those who dwell closest to land are usually the most feral and savage, while those who dwell in the deepest regions are intelligent and frequently practice pearl magic. The handfuls of intelligent Ningyo who still practice pearl magic use it in the same manner as a Naga jakla, but can only use it to cast Rank 1 Water spells.

The Ningyo Curse: Any creature who eats Ningyo flesh gains immortality – immune to all diseases, needing no longer to eat, drink, or breathe, and ceasing to age entirely. However, the mind of any individual who eats Ningyo flesh is twisted and distorted by the curse. To determine the curse, roll 1d10: 1–2 Curse of Air (can only speak or hear a wailing lament of how you murdered the Ningyo); 3–4 Curse of Earth (transform into the shape of a tree or boulder, forever aware but unable to speak); 5–6 Curse of Fire (cannot eat normal food – must feed on a specific substance chosen by GM at least once a day or immediately die); 7–8 Curse of Water (become a Ningyo; can only breathe water); 9–10 Curse of the Void (grow claws and develop monstrous carnivorous appetites; become an NPC under GM control). These curses can normally only be removed by Ryujin himself, though in theory a Fortune’s power could also end them. They cannot be undone without removing the immortality they confer.

### The Trolls

Modern trolls are large, green-skinned humanoids covered in layers of flabby muscle and thick hide, dwelling in vicious packs within the Shadowlands and its coastal waters. Their vile modern state is a great tragedy considering what they once were. In the civilization of the Five Races, the Trolls possessed the keenest scientific minds – expert architects and master craftsmen, creating buildings and weapons of great beauty and skill. They were also clever diplomats who managed to maintain peaceful relations between their many allies despite wildly divergent philosophies and personalities.

When the City of Night’s power was unleashed, the Trolls’ territories in the south were relatively unaffected. While the other races struggled to find a place for themselves afterward, the Trolls continued more or less as they had before, building several more great cities including their capital, Volturnum, whose ruins still stand deep in the Shadowlands. Alone, however, the Trolls were not nearly as great as they had been in league with the others of the Five Races. Their culture began to stagnate and decay, falling into moral corruption and self-indulgence. Eventually, they were conquered by wandering tribes of Ogres, who in turn were conquered by the Nezumi. By the time Fu Leng conquered them a third time, there was hardly anything left of the once-great Troll culture. According to Ogre legends, the Trolls willingly offered their servitude to Fu Leng. The Trolls of today are primitive and barbaric shadows of their ancestors, with no remaining members who remember their former greatness.

The King of Trolls – Sichi was an ancient troll chieftain, one of the original architects of the City of Night. When the Soultwisters returned and unleashed the crystal’s power, Sichi tried to stop them. The Soultwisters channeled great amounts of magical power into him, transforming him into a mindless rampaging beast. It was the Zokujin who eventually brought Sichi’s madness to an end, drawing on his connection with the earth to transform his body into stone and sealing him into an inert slumber. The King of Trolls slumbered until the tenth century, when he was accidentally awakened by Imperial Cartographers venturing too closely to the City of Night. After a brief rampage, Sichi returned to slumber. He awoke once more one hundred years later, only to be defeated again by an alliance of human and Zokujin heroes. He now rests near the mouth of Seikitsu Pass, waiting until he can rampage once again.

### The Kitsu and the Tsuno

The Kitsu were skilled shapeshifters whose forms were limited only to their imagination. They favored feline forms – often appearing as great lions or humanoid cats – with one consistent feature: naturally bright yellow-gold eyes. After the renegade Soultwisters unleashed the power of the crystal and destroyed the City of Night, the Kitsu blamed themselves for their race’s failure and retreated entirely into seclusion. They hid themselves from the other races, taking bestial forms and living as enormous feline creatures deep in the mountains of central Rokugan.

When mankind rose to dominate what became Rokugan and Jigoku began to stir once again, the Kitsu determined they would not sit idly by. They stepped forward to offer their aid – but the first humans they met were those who followed Akodo. The Lion Clan mistook the Kitsu for monsters in Fu Leng’s service and attacked them unmercifully, nearly exterminating the entire race. Only when the last five remaining Kitsu stepped forth to beg for their lives did Akodo realize what a terrible mistake he had made. He offered the Kitsu a place within his own clan and marriage to his own followers. The surviving Kitsu shifted their shapes one final time and became human. Swearing fealty to Akodo, they founded the Kitsu family and intermarried with Akodo’s human followers. Though modern members of the Kitsu family still exhibit a connection to the spirits and ancestors far greater than normal human shugenja, they are no longer the Kitsu of old. They are, for better or worse, human.

The Soultwisters who had been cast into the Realm of Slaughter – Toshigoku – did not remain there forever. Centuries there changed their forms to become monstrous, covered with intimidating spikes and horns, their long slavering mouths filled with sharp fangs. They retained their race’s intelligence and added to it a vicious cruelty, embracing a martial culture that prized strength and treated all other races with contempt. They lost the shapeshifting ability that was the legacy of their Kitsu blood – and did not regret the loss. They developed their own fighting techniques (the so-called Ravager School) and refined the magic their Soultwister founders had discovered, a magic based on the powers of the various Spirit Realms. These transformed Kitsu became the Tsuno.

Late in the twelfth century, the Tsuno allied with Daigotsu and entered Ningen-do, accepting the Taint into themselves and joining his sinister armies. After the City of Night’s great crystal was destroyed by Toturi III, the Tsuno’s interest in the mortal realm began to dwindle, and within a couple of decades most of them had returned to Toshigoku. There they continue their old goal of expanding their power base and extending their dominion across the Spirit Realms.

Tsuno Nintai: The tale of Tsuno Nintai is both noble and tragic. He was a powerful Soultwister and leader among the Tsuno who launched the plot that turned the Nezumi tribe known as the Stained Paw to the service of Jigoku. However, in a desperate combat with the Nezumi Shaman Te’tik’kir, Nintai was overcome by Nezumi Name Magic and regressed to a younger and nobler version of himself. He became a true Kitsu once more – the last true Kitsu. When the Nezumi passed into Tomorrow, Nintai promised to help ensure they would not be forgotten and watched over the shrine built in Te’tik’kir’s memory within the Shinomen Forest. Unfortunately, this made him a target for the forces of Jigoku, and Nintai was brutally tortured and murdered by members of the Spider Clan.

### Tsuno: New Mechanics, Schools, and Spells

All Tsuno have the creature qualities of Fear 2, Spirit, and Swift 3, reflecting their unnatural gifts as malignant creatures who traverse the Spirit Realms. Their unarmed attacks have a DR of 0k2 due to their sharp claws, and they have a natural Reduction of 5. Those Tsuno who have accepted the Taint have a Taint Rank of 4.

Tsuno Blades: The preferred Tsuno weapon is a large, heavy sword with a jagged and hooked appearance. Tsuno Blades use the Heavy Weapons skill and have a base Damage Rating of 3k3. A Tsuno Blade gains a +1k0 attack bonus against any opponent who has at least one Rank of Taint, who has the Spirit quality, or who has the Touched by the Realms Advantage.

Tsuno Honor: The Tsuno retain a dark and twisted form of the Kitsu nobility – a code of conduct that respects authority and strength while rejecting empathy and compassion. Their “Honor Rank” functions mechanically the same as the Honor of samurai characters, enhancing their resistance to Fear and temptation. However, they never gain Honor for acts of compassion or mercy – instead, they lose Honor for those acts. The only other behaviors that cause them to lose Honor are disobedience and cowardice.

### Tsuno Ravager School (New Basic School: Bushi)

Tsuno Ravagers are the undisputed leaders of the Tsuno military. They are battle-hardened veterans who learn their School Techniques by performing dangerous quests into different Spirit Realms. Benefit: None. Skills: Athletics, Battle, Defense, Iaijutsu, Lore: Spirit Realms, Kenjutsu (Tsuno Blade), any one Bugei Skill. “Honor”: 4.5. Outfit: Tsuno Blade, Light Armor.

RANK 1 – KILL YOUR WEAKNESS: When a Ravager is injured, he gains a Reduction rating equal to his current Wound Penalties. (This works off of base Wound Penalties, ignoring modifications for spells, Advantages such as Strength of the Earth, and similar effects.) This bonus changes as the Ravager’s Wound Penalties change, and ceases to function once he is at Down or Out.

RANK 2 – HOWL OF THE DISPLACED: Once per day a Ravager can take a Simple Action to unleash a terrifying howl. Any foes who hear the howl are affected by Fear 5, and any allies within earshot gain a +1k0 bonus to their attack rolls. Both effects last for a number of Rounds equal to the Ravager’s School Rank. Multiple Ravagers cannot stack their bonuses, although they can stack the duration and each generates their own Fear effect.

RANK 3 – TSUNO TECHNIQUE: The Ravager may make melee attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action.

RANK 4 – UNDYING VENGEANCE: If a Ravager has wounded or been wounded by a foe, he may take a Simple Action to make an Awareness Roll (TN 15 if the foe is within the same Spirit Realm, TN 25 if the foe has moved to another Spirit Realm). With a success, the Ravager learns the direction, distance, and physical condition of the foe. If the Ravager fails the roll, he loses track of his foe until the next time they meet. Magic that shields someone from detection will protect against this Technique. The Ravager may track multiple foes at once if he has been wounded by more than one enemy.

RANK 5 – SLAUGHTER IS IN THE BLOOD: If a Ravager kills an opponent, he immediately gains a number of individual +1k1 bonuses equal to his School Rank. These bonuses may be used on attack rolls, damage rolls, or any Skill or Trait roll, and must be used before the end of the next Round or they are lost. This Technique can be triggered a number of times each skirmish equal to the Ravager’s School Rank.

### Tsuno Soultwister School (New Basic School: Shugenja)

Tsuno Soultwisters are a combination of sage, thief, and warrior. They do not gain their magical abilities by calling on the kami – instead, they steal the power of the spirits and use it to accomplish what they desire. Benefit: None. Skills: Athletics, Battle, Defense, Intimidation (Torture), Kenjutsu (Tsuno Blade), Lore: Spirit Realms, any one Bugei skill. “Honor”: 3.5. Outfit: Tsuno Blade, light armor, satchel with ritual components. Affinity/Deficiency: Soultwisters have an affinity for spells related to any Spirit Realm for which they possess the Touch of the Spirit Realms Advantage. If the Soultwister has the Cursed by the Realm Disadvantage, he has a deficiency with spells related to the Realm associated with the Disadvantage.

TECHNIQUE: THE POWER OF THE SPIRITS: Soultwisters travel across the Spirit Realms, learning tricks and stealing power. No Soultwister can master the magic of all spirit realms – each Tsuno must choose three Spirit Realms when he becomes a Soultwister. He may only use spells related to those realms, beginning by knowing one spell from each. Every time the Soultwister increases in School Rank he may choose to either gain three new spells from his chosen Spirit Realms, or learn magic from a new Spirit Realm and gain one new spell. For spell casting, each Spirit Realm is associated with a Ring: Air – Yomi and Chikushudo; Earth – Gaki-do and Meido; Fire – Sakkaku and Tengoku; Water – Yume-do and Toshigoku. A Soultwister rolls School Rank/Ring at TN 5 + (5 x Mastery Level). A Soultwister has spell slots and conforms to the same rules regarding casting time, Raises, and so forth as a Rokugani shugenja. On those occasions when Tsuno become Tainted, their Soultwisters can cast Maho Spells as though they are Soultwister spells without the need to spill blood (though they still gain Taint as normal).

Soultwister Spells: Soultwisters draw their spells from the following list in the master spell compendium (Section 32 – Tsuno Spells): Mastery Level 1 – Benevolence of Tengoku, Bloodlust of Toshigoku, Dream Messages of Yume-do, Gaki-do’s Grasping Hunger, Meido’s Lethargic Introspection, Primal Senses of Chikushudo, Sakkaku’s Vandalism, Yomi’s Ancestral Separation. Mastery Level 3 – Banished from Yomi, Consumed by Gaki-do, One with Chikushudo, Questioned by Meido, Spurned by Sakkaku, Tengoku’s Grace, Trapped in Yume-do.

### The Zokujin

The Zokujin are the most peculiar of the Five Races in both demeanor and appearance. They are squat, dense creatures who live deep within the earth. They have metallic green skin resembling burnished copper, and huge gem-like yellow or silver eyes (with no visible pupils) that often glow faintly. At a brief glance they have a vague resemblance to large metallic-colored goblins, which has earned them the rather demeaning human nickname “copper goblins.” Though Zokujin are slow to anger and generally rather passive, even the least among them possess startling magical powers – all Zokujin live in such harmony with the earth kami that they can push earth and stone aside as if it were soft mud, allowing it to reform just as solidly once they turn their attention away. They possess many qualities often associated with the earth: they are slow-moving, wise, patient, and extremely resilient.

When the City of Night was built, none of the respective participants fully understood its workings – save the Zokujin. As they watched the crystal’s creation, the basic workings of the elements became obvious to them. This understanding led them to create a second such crystal focus: the Bloodwhite Stone, the heart of the mortal realm and the source of all magic. It was considered the most sacred artifact of the Zokujin race. When the Soultwisters unleashed the City of Night’s energies upon the world, the Zokujin sensed what had happened and felt the massive outbreak of power. Only by focusing the collective effort of their shamans on their Bloodwhite Stone were they able to counter the worst of what Tsuno Kishenku had unleashed, directing the brunt of the chaotic energies against Jigoku while struggling to prevent Ningen-do from being destroyed.

Centuries later, the Zokujin came under attack by creatures from Jigoku. Thousands perished and the survivors were forced to flee deeper into the earth. Their once-unified civilization was reduced to scattered, wandering tribes. The Bloodwhite Stone was lost, and their race fell into a primitive state – though unlike the Trolls, they escaped Jigoku’s control and maintained at least some sense of their history and culture. The belief that recovering the Bloodwhite Stone will unite the Zokujin tribes and bring back the greatness of old drives many of the deep tribes, and groups are regularly dispatched to search for it. These search parties typically avoid all contact with surface dwellers, responding to any attempts to communicate with either retreat or violence.

The few Zokujin tribes who live near the surface have met with mixed results in their interactions with humans. The Lion Clan forced the non-violent Zokujin into slavery, compelling them to mine metals and other minerals. The Zokujin made little effort to free themselves – their shamans encouraged a philosophy of patience and non-aggression. They are patient and long-lived creatures, confident they will outlast their human bondage while using the opportunity to learn about these strange creatures that have inherited the world they helped create. In the Dragon lands, by contrast, a Zokujin shaman helped repel an attack by a dangerous supernatural being, and the Dragon came to regard these creatures with far more respect. Kjgkt, a Zokujin shaman of considerable wisdom and power, has since served as an advisor to the Tamori family on many occasions.

### Zokujin: Miner

Describes a typical Zokujin working in a Lion Clan mine, or a Zokujin living in one of their tribal settlements. These creatures are passive and slow to anger, but will fight in self-defense or to protect their families and tribe. These Zokujin rarely speak any tongue save their own.

Air 3 | Earth 3 | Fire 2 | Water 4 | Reflexes 4 | Agility 3

Initiative: 5k4 | Attack: Claws 5k3 (Complex) | Damage: 4k1 | Armor TN: 25 | Reduction: 10 (see Special Abilities) | Wounds: 18: +5; 36: +10; 64: Dead

Skills: Athletics 3, Stealth 3

Special Abilities: Burrowing – A Zokujin can move at normal speed through solid earth or stone, leaving no tunnel behind. A Zokujin who moves at Water 2 can leave a tunnel that will last for one minute before collapsing. A Zokujin who moves at Water 1 can leave a permanent tunnel. Earthshaping – A Zokujin can mold earth or stone in its bare hands as if they were clay. Any earth or stone molded by a Zokujin will automatically revert to its previous form a Round after the Zokujin ceases contact, unless the Zokujin consciously wills it to remain in its new form. Mineral Resistance – A Zokujin’s Reduction functions only against weapons made from stone or metal. Wooden weapons ignore it.

### Zokujin: Stonehunter

The strongest and most capable Zokujin are chosen to seek for the missing Bloodwhite Stone. These warriors can be surprisingly capable, and while they prefer to avoid conflict when they can, a samurai who attacks them may be in for a rude surprise.

### Zokujin: Shaman

Zokujin communities are led by shamans, wise and magical individuals whose counsel is universally obeyed. Their magical powers are loosely similar to those of human Shugenja, but they cast their spells instinctively, without any need for scrolls or prayers.
