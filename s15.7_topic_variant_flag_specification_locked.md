## 15.7 Topic Variant Flag Specification — LOCKED

Topic variant flags are enum fields on the topic data object. They are set at topic generation time based on the circumstances that created the topic. They determine which personality modifier from Section 15.6 fires. Only the topic types listed below require variant flags — all others have a single uniform modifier for their type.

Principle: variants exist only where the same topic type genuinely produces different instinctive reactions in characters. A death in battle and a suspicious death are the same topic type but trigger different virtue responses. A variant flag is not narrative colour — it is a mechanical distinction that changes the calculation.

**DEATH — field: death_variant**

- honorable: Died in battle or formal duel, fighting for their lord, no disgrace attached.

- seppuku: Ritual suicide. Chosen death with full ceremony.

- battlefield: Killed in mass combat. Not a formal duel. Honorable but not ceremonial.

- suspicious: Circumstances unclear. Possible foul play. No confirmed cause.

- violent_murder: Killed by another person outside formal combat or duel.

- natural: Illness, age, or accident. No particular honor or dishonor.

**BATTLE OUTCOME — field: battle_variant**

- victory_clean: Won without atrocity, dishonor, or controversy.

- victory_persistence: Won after sustained difficult campaign against the odds.

- defeat_honorable: Lost but fought with full honor. No disgrace.

- defeat_cowardice: Lost due to retreat, abandoned position, or failure of nerve.

- heavy_casualties: Significant losses regardless of outcome.

- forbidden_tactics: Farmlands burned, civilians targeted, poison used, or other dishonorable methods employed.

- poor_tactics: Lost due to demonstrably bad decisions by the commander.

**DISGRACE — field: disgrace_variant**

- unjust: Disgraced through false accusation or political manipulation.

- justified: Disgraced through genuine failure or wrongdoing.

- ambiguous: Circumstances unclear — whether justified or not is contested.

**EDICT ISSUED — field: edict_variant**

- proper_order: Standard lord’s ruling maintaining the hierarchy. No controversy.

- just_ruling: Resolves a genuine wrong with clear righteousness.

- restricting_action: Limits what specific characters or clans can do.

- overriding_personal: Directly overrides a character’s declared commitment or personal decision.

**ROMANTIC SCANDAL — field: scandal_variant**

- deceptive: Involved active deception — false identity, hidden marriage, concealed relationship.

- improper_conduct: Inappropriate relationship without active deception.

- broken_betrothal: A formal arrangement was dissolved.

**FORMAL INSULT — field: insult_variant**

- false_fabricated: Insult based on false or fabricated information.

- genuine: Insult grounded in real grievance or documented failure.

- political: Calculated insult issued for strategic purposes.

**CHAMPION’S DECISION — field: decision_variant**

- power_shift: Changes who holds authority, position, or territory.

- just_ruling: Resolves a matter with clear righteousness and proper process.

- honorable: Conducted with proper ceremony, honor, and observance of custom.

- improper: Conducted without proper process or in breach of established custom.

**WEDDING — field: wedding_variant**

- genuine: Arranged with mutual goodwill. Families genuinely aligned.

- political_only: Pure strategic arrangement. No personal warmth between parties or families.

**RETIREMENT OR EXILE — field: departure_variant**

- voluntary_honorable: Stepping down with dignity intact. Chosen transition.

- forced_weakness: Removed due to failure, political defeat, or loss of standing.

- lord_departure: The departing character held a position of authority — creates a power vacuum.

**UNIT DESTROYED OR DISHONORED — field: unit_variant**

- cowardice: Unit fled, surrendered without fighting, or abandoned post.

- overwhelmed: Destroyed by superior force with no dishonor — fought to the last.

- atrocity: Unit committed dishonorable acts before or during destruction.

**ALLIANCE FORMED — field: alliance_variant**

- military: Primarily a military pact — shared force commitment.

- ending_conflict: Specifically resolves an ongoing war or active crisis.

- sincere: Both parties genuinely committed beyond political calculation.

- political_only: Strategic arrangement without genuine goodwill.

NOTE: Alliance Broken is a separate topic type from Alliance Formed and requires no variant flag — the breaking of an alliance is uniformly negative regardless of circumstances. If circumstances matter (e.g. a justified breaking of a treacherous alliance), this is handled through the disposition anchor calculation, not through a variant flag.

**SIEGE ENDING — field: siege_end_variant**

- successful_defense: The garrison held. The siege was lifted or the besieging army withdrew.

- negotiated_peace: Terms were agreed between besieger and garrison. No final assault.

- surrender_forced: The garrison capitulated under pressure. The castle fell.

- relief_army: An external force broke the siege before either of the above outcomes.

**ADOPTION — field: adoption_variant**

- clear_succession: A legitimate heir adopted with broad acceptance. Succession secured.

- questionable_legitimacy: Adoption contested, circumstances suspicious, or claim disputed.
