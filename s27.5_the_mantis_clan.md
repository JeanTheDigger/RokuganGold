## 27.5 The Mantis Clan

The youngest of the Great Clans — proclaimed a Great Clan by Emperor Toturi I after Yoritomo's Alliance proved decisive in the Clan Wars. The Mantis rose from minor clan status through sheer force of will, naval dominance, and mercenary muscle. They are wealthy, aggressive, and deeply proud of what they have built without the advantages of ancient lineage. Their founder Yoritomo eventually ascended to become their founding Kami, granting them the legitimacy they always lacked.

- Clan identity: Aggressive, mercantile, proud of their low origins. Dominant at sea. Willing to do what other clans won't. The Tsuruchi are among the Empire's finest archers.

- Key historical events: Yoritomo's Alliance during the Clan Wars; elevation to Great Clan status; Yoritomo's ascension as Kami; absorption of the Fox Clan (Kitsune) and Centipede Clan (Moshi).

- Inter-clan relations: Economic rivalry with the Crane. At odds with Scorpion over Ryoko Owari Toshi. Generally allied with Phoenix through the Moshi family's shared Sun goddess worship.

**The Kitsune Family:**

Motto: 'Fall down seven times, stand up eight.' Originally the Fox Clan — descendants of Ki-Rin samurai left behind when Shinjo departed. Drove out of their lands by Lion, resettled in Kitsune Mori, forged alliance with kitsune spirits. Shugenja school based on spirit-communication techniques. Joined Mantis in 1169 after the Shadow Dragon attacked them and Mantis rescued them. Retain their original colors (red-brown and silver) by agreement.

### Fox Clan (Pre-Mantis) Special: The Forest and the Bridge

#### The Kitsune Mori

The Kitsune Mori is already flagged in the GDD as a permanent Chikushudo overlap zone. This is the Fox Clan’s territory and their power base. Any character entering the forest encounters spirit activity regardless of magical ability. The Kitsune are the only clan that can reliably navigate it — everyone else is at the mercy of the fox spirits inside. Non-Kitsune characters attempting to travel through the Mori trigger a spirit encounter check that the Kitsune can suppress through their existing relationships with the forest’s inhabitants.

#### Chikushudo Bridge

The Kitsune Shugenja school can affect animal spirits directly using basic spells — no other school can do this. In game terms Kitsune shugenja generate Worship Points toward nature Fortunes more effectively than any other school, can commune with animal kami as an intelligence source, and are the Empire’s foremost experts when strange animal behaviour or Chikushudo spirit manifestations occur anywhere in Rokugan. When such a topic activates, the Kitsune AI is notified and generates an INVESTIGATE_THREAT response weighted toward spiritual rather than martial resolution.

#### The Emperor’s Protection

After the Lion drove the Fox from their lands, the Emperor issued a formal decree restricting Great Clans from warfare against Minor Clans. This decree is a permanent world-level constraint: any Great Clan that attacks a Minor Clan without Imperial sanction generates an immediate Honor loss and a Tier 2 political topic. The Fox Clan’s suffering is the historical reason this protection exists. It benefits every Minor Clan simultaneously.

#### Ryosei’s Legacy

During the Clan War, Fox Champion Ryosei was considered the moral heart of Yoritomo’s Alliance. The Kitsune carry this legacy as a high baseline disposition with all former Alliance members and a personality profile weighted heavily toward Jin and Makoto. They have no bushi unit of their own and rely on the generic Bushi Retainer. Their strength is entirely spiritual and diplomatic.

**The Moshi Family:**

Originally the Centipede Clan — founded by Isawa descendants who settled in a remote valley. Became a matriarchy within three generations. Devoted primarily to the worship of Amaterasu (the Sun). Their shugenja specialize in Fire and Air magic. Joined Yoritomo's Alliance and eventually the Mantis Clan proper.

#### Centipede Clan (Pre-Mantis) Special: The Valley and the Sun

#### The Natural Fortress

The Centipede valley sits atop a cliff in the Seikitsu Mountains overlooking the Sea of Amaterasu. A single narrow pass provides the only landward approach. Visitors arriving by sea must scale the sheer cliffs. On the world map this functions as a routing constraint identical in structure to the Dragonfly gate — any overland assault must come through the pass, and any naval assault is an amphibious operation against a fortified height. No clean approach exists. The Centipede are nearly impossible to attack conventionally despite having almost no military force of their own.

#### Disposition Bonus

The Centipede carry the highest baseline disposition with Phoenix and Imperial family NPCs of any Minor Clan. Their Sun goddess devotion, deeply formal traditions, and descent from Isawa blood make them culturally legible to the most conservative factions in the Empire in a way that few Minor Clans are. They carry no bushi unit beyond the generic Bushi Retainer. Their protection is entirely geographic and diplomatic.

**The Tsuruchi Family:**

One of the youngest Great Clan families. Founded by a brilliant archer who abandoned bushido formalities in favor of pure martial effectiveness. The finest archers in the Empire — no other clan or family can match the Tsuruchi at range. Originally a minor clan (the Wasp Clan), joined Yoritomo's Alliance and were absorbed into Mantis.

#### Wasp Clan (Pre-Mantis) Special: The Hunt Never Stops

#### Bounty Hunting

The Wasp fund themselves through bounty work rather than the tax cascade. Their AI carries a high INVESTIGATE_THREAT weight against active banditry, criminal, and fugitive topics, and maintains a standing objective to pursue any rogue ronin or criminal topic that enters their awareness. They do not wait to be hired — they actively scan the topic pipeline for work and route toward it on their own initiative. Income follows from completed objectives. A lord who wants a fugitive found and cannot or will not use their own magistrates can quietly approach the Wasp, and the Wasp will find them.

#### The Wasp Code

Many Tsuruchi refuse to carry swords. The Wasp Code explicitly deprioritises Bushido formalities in favour of martial effectiveness, which draws consistent criticism from traditionalist clans. This is encoded as a permanent mild disposition penalty with Phoenix, Lion, and Imperial family NPCs. The corresponding sympathy from clans that understand being looked down on — Mantis, Minor Clan Alliance — offsets this with a modest positive baseline toward those factions.

**The Yoritomo Family:**

The founding family of the Mantis Great Clan. Descended from Kaimetsu-uo, a son of Hida who was exiled. Built the clan from pirates and minor-clan misfits into a naval empire. The Yoritomo are aggressive, commercially dominant, and willing to bend rules that other clans follow rigidly. Naval combat is their absolute domain — no Great Clan can challenge them at sea.

#### Mantis Clan (Minor Clan Era) Special: The Sea Belongs to Us

#### Naval Dominance

The Mantis control the seas in a way no other clan matches. Their trade routes are maritime, their military projection is naval, and their island provinces are connected by sea rather than land. Other clans can field ships but the Mantis own the ocean commercially and militarily. Any clan that wants to move goods or armies by sea in significant volume must either deal with the Mantis or risk Mantis interference. This gives them economic leverage over every coastal and island province in Rokugan and makes them functionally unblockable by land-based military pressure alone — you cannot besiege an island chain from the shore.

#### Multiple Provinces

The Mantis are the only Minor Clan with a territorial footprint comparable to a small Great Clan. They generate meaningful Rice and Koku, field real armies, and carry political weight that other Minor Clans cannot match. In the existing resource systems they operate at a scale closer to a Great Clan than a Minor Clan — which is precisely the point. They are the Minor Clan that should not be one, and every other clan knows it. Their unit roster is fully defined in Section 11.6 and carries over unchanged into the Minor Clan era.
