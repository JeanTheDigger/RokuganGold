# 3. Story & Narrative

## 3.1 Synopsis

**NOT APPLICABLE:** *This is a persistent emergent simulation. There is no pre-authored story synopsis. Narrative emerges from the NPC Decision Engine (Section 55), the court system (Section 15), and player interaction with a living world. See Core Design Philosophy (Section 20).*

## 3.2 Player Character

**RESOLVED:** *Player character creation is defined in Section 22 (Named Character System) and Section 4.5 (L5R 4th Edition Rules Integration). The player controls a samurai of any Great Clan, with clan, family, and school selected at creation. Background emerges from the generation template (Section 22.4).*

## 3.3 Main Characters & NPCs

**RESOLVED:** *Named NPCs are procedurally generated per Section 22. Pre-written positions of power (Clan Champions, Imperial officials) are in Section 22.8. NPC motivations emerge from the Personality System (Section 19) and the NPC Objective System (Section 18). There is no fixed cast — the roster is unique to each world generation.*

## 3.4 Story Structure

**NOT APPLICABLE:** *No act structure or scripted story beats. The world simulation runs continuously (Section 13), generating crises, wars, court politics, and personal conflicts through emergent systems. Player experience is shaped by their position in the feudal hierarchy, their province, and their relationships — not by a pre-authored narrative arc.*

## 3.5 Endings

**NOT APPLICABLE:** *The simulation has no scripted ending. PC death is permanent (Section 22.5). The world persists. A player's story ends when their character dies — a new character starts fresh at Rank 1 with no connection to the previous one.*
