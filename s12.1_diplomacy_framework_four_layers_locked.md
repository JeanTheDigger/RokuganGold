## 12.1 Diplomacy Framework — Four Layers — LOCKED

**Layer 1 — Formal Courts:**

Large formal gatherings, invitation-based, with strictly hierarchical access. The grand political arena where wars begin and end, harvests are negotiated, marriages arranged, military appointments debated, and insults answered with steel.

- Imperial Winter Court: The highest court in the Empire. The Emperor visits a chosen clan each Winter — enormous prestige, fierce competition for the honor. Crane dominate this competition historically. Clan Champions and their best courtiers attend. Declarations of war, major treaties, harvest imbalances all resolved here.

- Clan Champion Courts: Attended by prominent clan members, allies, and enemies. Military appointments, resource distribution, inter-clan conflicts are primary topics. Champions offer distractions (hunting trips, performances) to create private meeting opportunities for vassals.

- Family Courts: More intimate than Champion courts but more important than Minor Clan courts. Variable in scope and attendance.

- Provincial Courts: Erratic scheduling. Lower stakes but meaningful at the local level. A promising courtier who wins here may earn a place in higher courts.

- Minor Clan Courts: Surprisingly important as training grounds. Minor Clans prefer peaceable solutions — anything larger than a border skirmish could devastate them.

- Seasonal pattern: Winter is the primary season for courts. Smaller courts occur in other seasons. Most major courts are inactive during Spring and Summer when campaigns are active.

- Invitation system: Invitations are sent out. Access is hierarchical — the Emperor's Winter Court invites Clan Champions and high-rank delegations. Smaller lords hold smaller courts with correspondingly limited guest lists.

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Operational Hierarchy): **When a clan sends a delegation to a formal court, the delegation leader directs the other members' court actions through the Operational Hierarchy system (Section 11.3.18e). Delegation members' operational_superior_id is set to the delegation leader. The leader assigns court objectives --- who to approach, what intelligence to gather, which gifts to deliver. Members execute assignments using their own skills and personality. The delegation leader's operational authority clears when the delegation returns home. See Section 11.3.18e for the full delegation hierarchy pattern and Section 11.3.18h for what happens when a delegation member refuses an order.

**Layer 2 — Personal Visits:**

Intimate interactions at someone's hold — either self-invited or by formal invitation. Could be 1-on-1 or a small private group. This is where private deals are made, favors exchanged, intelligence gathered, and relationships deepened or damaged away from public scrutiny.

- Can be initiated by either party — a lord inviting a courtier they want to cultivate, or a courtier inserting themselves into someone's court seeking access.

- Smaller and more personal than formal courts — the stakes of individual interaction are higher because there are fewer witnesses and less political theater to hide behind.

- Outcomes of personal visits can feed directly into formal court dynamics — a relationship built here determines how a lord votes, who they support, what information they share in public.

**Layer 3 — Disposition System:**

The underlying relationship layer. Every named character has a disposition value toward every other named character they have interacted with. This value shifts based on the history between those characters — favors granted, insults delivered, promises kept or broken, debts accumulated. Disposition affects everything else in the diplomatic system. See Section 12.2 for the full system design.

- Disposition is tracked between any two named characters who have met.

- Positive disposition: invitations extended, supply requests honored, political support offered, duels avoided.

- Negative disposition: invitations withheld or refused, supply requests declined or sabotaged, political opposition, duels provoked.

- Disposition can be built slowly through repeated positive interactions or destroyed instantly by a single major insult or betrayal.

- Connects directly to the military supply system — a lord's willingness to share Rice and Arms depends on their disposition toward the requesting lord.

**Layer 4 — The Game of Letters:**

Written communication between named characters across distance. Letters carry political meaning far beyond their words — the quality of paper, the style of folding, the accompanying object, the scent, the brushwork, the choice of servant to deliver it all communicate intent. Letters are almost never sealed — everyone reads what servants carry. This is accepted and expected.

- Paper quality signals status and esteem — sending correspondence on cheap paper is an insult. High-quality paper signals respect.

- Folding style carries meaning — casual folding implies lack of care, elaborate folding implies great significance.

- Accompanying objects add layers — a sprig of bamboo implies strength, a flower implies a particular emotion, a stick of incense carries its own meaning.

- Brushwork conveys emotion — messy calligraphy suggests insult or lack of emotional control.

- The choice of servant delivering the letter has political meaning.

- Skilled courtiers write letters several times to achieve the exact effect they want — a message that on its surface says one thing but communicates something entirely different.

- Letters keep relationships active between formal courts and personal visits — the Game of Letters is the between-seasons political layer.
