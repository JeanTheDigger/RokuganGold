# 20. Core Design Philosophy — LOCKED

**Characters Act on Known Information, Not Meta-Knowledge:**

This is a foundational principle of the entire game — NPCs and players alike operate based on what their characters actually know, not on information the player or system happens to possess behind the scenes. Court, diplomacy, military decisions, and political maneuvering all flow from this principle.

- A lord who has not been informed of another lord's position on a topic will not act as if they know it. They will gather intelligence, make assumptions based on history, or act on incomplete information.

- A Public Declaration removes ambiguity — it makes a character's position a known fact in the game world that all witnesses can now factor into their decisions.

- Intelligence-gathering actions exist precisely because information is not freely available. Spending action points to learn someone's position, disposition, or objective is meaningful because that information is otherwise hidden.

- NPCs behave believably because they respond to what they know. An NPC who witnessed a Public Declaration will approach the declarer as an ally, adversary, or persuasion target based on that known fact — not on meta-knowledge of the player's true intentions.

- This principle keeps the game honest without requiring staff enforcement — the mechanical systems reward playing with known information and penalize acting on knowledge your character could not realistically possess.
