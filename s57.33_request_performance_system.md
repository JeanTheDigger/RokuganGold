## 57.33 REQUEST_PERFORMANCE System

### 57.33.1 Overview

A lord may commission a live performance at their court. Unlike REQUEST_ART (which produces a persistent physical object), REQUEST_PERFORMANCE produces an ephemeral event — a single performance that happens once, generates disposition and Glory effects, creates a topic, and is then done. The lord who arranges the event receives patron Glory for their role as a cultural host, distinct from and more substantial than the passive Glory trickle from displayed art objects. A performance is a statement of cultural investment: “I am a lord who provides beauty and entertainment for my court.”

### 57.33.2 REQUEST_PERFORMANCE ActionID

ActionID: REQUEST_PERFORMANCE. Category: 14 (Administration and Court Management, Lord-Tier). Cost: 1 Civilian Order (not AP — per Section 57.34, this is an administrative directive, not a personal action). No roll — the lord issues the request; no skill check is required to ask for entertainment. Context: AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or AT_COURT (lord must be at their own court or a court they control).

Request parameters: the lord specifies performance_type (any _performance_skill sub-type: biwa, dance, drums, flute, oratory, samisen, song, storytelling, or any qualifying Artisan performance such as poetry recitation, ikebana, or origami). Optionally, the lord specifies target_performer_id (a specific named performer they want; if null, any qualified performer may fulfill). The target_performer_id must satisfy the met_characters constraint (Section 22.3 and Section 29.15.24) — the lord can only target a performer they have met in person or heard of through topics, letters, or intelligence actions. A lord cannot request a performer they have never encountered. The lord specifies venue_mode: public (fulfilled through PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE, broadcast to all court attendees) or private (fulfilled through PERFORM_FOR, targeted at the lord personally). The venue zone must have performance_permitted: true for public requests; private requests have no venue restriction.

Output: the action creates a pending_performance_request entry on the court. Fields: requesting_lord_id, performance_type, target_performer_id (or null), venue_mode (public or private), venue_zone_id, created_on_ic_day, expires_after_ic_season (1 IC season = approximately 90 IC days). The request is a standing invitation, not an instant resolution — it waits for a performer to accept and travel to the court if necessary.

### 57.33.3 Performer Acceptance (NPC Engine)

Invitation delivery — how the performer learns of the request: information does not teleport. A performer three provinces away has no way of knowing a lord wants them unless they are told. The mechanism depends on whether the request targets a specific performer or is an open invitation.

Targeted request (target_performer_id is set): when the lord fires REQUEST_PERFORMANCE with a specific performer named, the system auto-generates a letter of invitation through the existing SEND_INVITATION infrastructure (Section 12.7). The letter travels at normal letter speed (1–3 IC days per province, per the letter delivery system). No additional AP cost to the lord — the invitation is bundled into the REQUEST_PERFORMANCE action. The letter contains: the requesting lord’s identity, the performance type requested, the venue (court and zone), and a general timeframe (“at your earliest convenience” or “before the close of winter court”). The performer receives the letter and evaluates whether to accept. This means targeted requests have delay — a lord requesting a performer 5 provinces away must account for letter travel time plus the performer’s own travel time. Requesting a performance “for tonight” only works if the performer is already at court.

Open request (target_performer_id is null): the request is visible to any performer already present at the court OR any performer who arrives at the court during the request’s lifetime. No letter is sent. The open invitation is effectively posted at the court itself — like a notice on the wall: “Lord [X] desires a biwa performance at the next court session. Qualified performers may present themselves.” This means open requests are fulfilled quickly if a performer is already present, but may sit for days or weeks if no one with the right skill is at court.

Reply letter: when a targeted performer accepts the invitation, the system auto-generates a reply letter through the letter system: “I am honoured by your invitation and will arrive within [estimated travel time].” This lets the lord plan — they know the performer is en route and can schedule court sessions accordingly. If the performer declines, the reply letter says so: “I regret that I am unable to attend at this time.” The lord then knows the request will not be fulfilled by that performer and may issue a new REQUEST_PERFORMANCE targeting someone else or leave it open.

When a performer NPC evaluates their ImmediateNeeds — either upon receiving a targeted invitation letter or upon arriving at a court with an open request — a pending_performance_request generates a FULFILL_PERFORMANCE_REQUEST candidate under the SEEK_GLORY NeedType. The performer must possess the requested _performance_skill at rank 1+. If target_performer_id is set and does not match this NPC, the candidate scores 0 and is skipped — the lord asked for someone specific.

Scoring factors for acceptance: disposition toward the requesting lord (Strong Ally +20, Friend +15, Acquaintance +10, Neutral +5, Rival −10 — few performers refuse a lord’s invitation, but hostile disposition reduces enthusiasm). Travel distance (−3 per province of distance — a performer already at court scores much higher than one three provinces away). Performer’s SEEK_GLORY priority (artisan and performer NPCs score higher; bushi with hobby Perform skill score lower). Skill rank match (higher rank in the requested skill = higher score; Rank 5+ performer scores +15 over a Rank 2 performer). Status of the requesting lord (a Great Clan Champion’s request scores +20; a minor vassal lord’s request scores +5).

Travel resolution: if the performer accepts and is not already at the requesting court, BEGIN_TRAVEL fires automatically on their next AP, targeting the court location. On arrival, the performer fires the appropriate performance action (PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE for public venue_mode, PERFORM_FOR for private venue_mode) on their next available AP. This follows the same commission-then-travel pipeline as REQUEST_ART (Section 57.23.3).

One request, one performance: a pending_performance_request is consumed upon fulfillment. One request produces one performance, one set of patron Glory effects, one topic, and then it is done. The performance is an event, not a retainer contract. A lord who wants entertainment across an entire winter court season fires REQUEST_PERFORMANCE repeatedly — once per OOC day, or once per IC week, whatever their civilian order budget allows. Each request is a deliberate cultural investment.

Post-fulfillment: after the single performance, the performer’s obligation is complete. They are at the lord’s court with no standing engagement. Several natural paths follow: (1) the performer may fire spontaneous PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE or PERFORM_FOR for their own SEEK_GLORY benefit — no patron Glory for the lord from these, but the performer builds their own reputation at the court. (2) The lord may fire another REQUEST_PERFORMANCE on a subsequent OOC day, and the performer (now present at court) accepts instantly with no travel delay. (3) The performer may pursue other objectives at the court — social actions, intelligence gathering, court intrigue, even training or mentoring. (4) The performer may accept other lords’ pending requests at nearby courts and fire BEGIN_TRAVEL onward — creating the “traveling performer circuit” pattern where a skilled biwa player moves from court to court fulfilling requests, building Glory and disposition across the Empire. Each stop is a discrete event, not an ongoing contract.

### 57.33.4 Fulfillment and Patron Effects

Fulfillment: when the performer fires PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE or PERFORM_FOR at the requesting lord’s court AND does so in explicit fulfillment of a pending_performance_request (linked by request_id), the pending request is consumed. The standard performance effects fire normally for the performer (disposition, Glory per Section 15.4). Then the patron effects fire for the requesting lord.

Critical distinction — spontaneous vs requested: if a performer spontaneously fires PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE at a court that happens to have a pending_performance_request, the lord does NOT receive patron Glory. Patron effects fire only when the performer explicitly fulfills the linked request. The lord who actively curates entertainment (spends AP on REQUEST_PERFORMANCE) is rewarded; the lord who passively benefits from visiting performers is not. This creates the incentive to actually use the ActionID rather than relying on spontaneous performer behaviour.

Patron Glory by outcome (public venue_mode):

- Successful performance hosted: +0.2 Glory to the lord. “A cultured lord who provides entertainment for his court.”

- Masterful performance hosted (performer called 2+ Raises): +0.3 Glory to the lord. “A lord with excellent taste who attracts the finest performers.”

- Failed performance (performer rolled below TN but not critical): +0 Glory to the lord. Unremarkable — no credit, no blame.

- Critical failure performance: −0.1 Glory to the lord. “Who invited this incompetent?” The lord’s judgment is questioned.

Patron Glory by outcome (private venue_mode): the lord gains half the public patron Glory values — +0.1 on success, +0.15 on masterful, +0 on failure, −0.05 on critical failure. The reduced values reflect that fewer people witnessed the event and thus less reputation is generated. Private performances are primarily about the PERFORM_FOR disposition gain with the lord, not about public cultural display.

For comparison with the performer’s own Glory: the performer gains +0.3 / +0.5 / 0 / −0.3 Glory on public performances respectively (per Section 15.4). The lord receives roughly two-thirds of the performer’s reward on success — they arranged the event, the performer did the work.

### 57.33.5 Topic Generation

A successful hosted performance generates a topic: “[Lord X] hosted a [performance_type] performance by [Performer Y] at [Court Z].” The topic enters the topic system and propagates normally through letters, gossip, and court conversation. A masterful performance (2+ Raises) generates a Tier 1 topic with wider reach. A standard success generates a Tier 2 topic with local reach. A critical failure generates a negative topic about both the lord’s judgment and the performer’s incompetence. Failed performances (below TN but not critical) generate no topic — unremarkable events are not discussed.

### 57.33.6 Lord-Side NPC Scoring

Lord NPCs issue REQUEST_PERFORMANCE through the MAINTAIN_CULTURAL_STANDARD NeedType (which already drives art display, garden maintenance, and other court prestige actions) and the SEEK_GLORY NeedType (patron Glory is a direct Glory source). The scoring evaluates whether the lord’s court currently has adequate entertainment scheduled.

Personality modifier: high Rei (Courtesy) lords request performances frequently — Rei × 5 added to the REQUEST_PERFORMANCE alignment score. A Crane daimyo with Rei 4 adds +20, making performance requests a high-priority cultural investment. Low Rei lords (Crab frontier commanders, aggressive Lion generals) rarely fire REQUEST_PERFORMANCE — their Rei 1 adds only +5, and competing military or administrative objectives easily outrank it.

Court season modifier: during winter court (the major political and cultural event of the year), REQUEST_PERFORMANCE scoring receives +15 — winter court is when lords are expected to provide entertainment. During summer (military campaign season), REQUEST_PERFORMANCE scoring receives −10 — the lord has more pressing concerns. Spring and autumn are neutral.

Pending request check: if the court already has an unfulfilled pending_performance_request, the lord does not issue a second one for the same performance_type. Different performance types may stack — a lord may request both a biwa performance and a dance simultaneously, but not two biwa performances.

### 57.33.7 Performance Fatigue Interaction

The hosted performance is still a PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE (or PERFORM_FOR) and still subject to the performance fatigue rules in Section 15.4. A lord who requests three performances from three different performers in one OOC day gets three full-effect patron Glory bonuses (independent fatigue counters per performer). A lord who requests three performances from the same performer gets full → half → zero patron Glory scaling alongside the performer’s own fatigue. Patron Glory scales with the performer’s effect: if the performer’s disposition/Glory effect is halved by fatigue, the patron Glory is also halved; if zeroed, the patron Glory is also zeroed. A lord who arranges a zero-effect performance gains nothing — the audience was already saturated.

### 57.33.8 Unfulfilled Requests

If no performer accepts the request within 1 IC season (approximately 90 IC days, roughly 22–23 OOC days), the pending_performance_request expires and is removed. The lord’s 1 AP spent on the request is a sunk cost — no refund, no penalty. This models the reality that not every request can be fulfilled: the desired performer may be busy, travelling, or uninterested. A lord who repeatedly issues unfulfilled requests is wasting AP, which the NPC engine naturally avoids through scoring (low expected value from historically unfulfilled request patterns).

### 57.33.9 Player Interaction

Player as lord: a player character who controls a court may fire REQUEST_PERFORMANCE specifying performance_type, optional target_performer_id, and venue_mode. The request enters the pending queue and NPC performers evaluate it normally. If the player targets a specific performer, that NPC’s acceptance is driven by the scoring formula — a hostile performer may decline.

Player as performer: when a player character with a _performance_skill arrives at a court with a pending_performance_request matching their skill, the UI surfaces a prompt: “[Lord X] has requested a [performance_type] performance. Accept?” If the player accepts, they fire PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE or PERFORM_FOR on their next AP with the patron bonus applied to the requesting lord. If they decline, the request remains pending for other performers. A player performer fulfilling a request receives the standard performer Glory and disposition effects; the patron Glory applies to the requesting lord regardless of whether the performer is a player or NPC.

### 57.33.10 Theater Scope Exclusion

REQUEST_PERFORMANCE covers _performance_skill actions only (Section 24 Perform sub-skills plus qualifying Artisan performances). It does NOT cover PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE (Section 57.22), which has its own elaborate pipeline: compose → learn → perform, with piece selection scoring, dedication mechanics, and multi-AP compositions. RESOLVED: REQUEST_THEATER_PERFORMANCE (1 Civilian Order, no roll, AT_OWN_HOLDINGS or AT_COURT, lord-tier). Creates a pending_theater_request on the lord’s court record. Theater performers with learned pieces score this request through the NPC Decision Engine — elevated weight on TRAVEL_TO targeting the requesting lord’s court, then PERFORM_THEATER_PIECE fires on arrival using the standard piece selection scoring (Section 57.22.13). The request persists until fulfilled or cancelled. Category 14 (Administration and Court Management). New ActionID — total updated. PROVISIONAL.
