## 57.31 Medicine System

### 57.31.1 Overview

Medicine is the out-of-combat wound treatment system. A samurai who has taken Wounds from damage recovers over time through natural healing (governed by Stamina, Section 24), but a character with the Medicine skill can accelerate that recovery by treating an ally’s wounds directly. This system implements the L5R 4e core Medicine (Wound Treatment) mechanic as an ActionID integrated into the normal AP economy. It is distinct from the in-combat ASCII map “Tend the Wounded” Category 5 battle action (Section 11.7), which heals Company Health during mass combat, not individual character Wounds.

The system has three components: the TREAT_WOUND ActionID itself, the Medicine Kit inventory constraint (characters carry one Medicine Kit at a time), and the per-target daily treatment limit. Together these implement faithful RAW mechanics while respecting the game’s persistent simulation, inventory, and AP systems.

### 57.31.2 TREAT_WOUND ActionID

ActionID: TREAT_WOUND. Category: 11 (Personal Development), alongside TRAIN, CRAFT, MEDITATE, MENTOR, and the artisan actions. Cost: 1 AP. Context: any context — AT_COURT, AT_OWN_HOLDINGS, VISITING, TRAVELING, ON_CAMPAIGN, UNDER_SIEGE — wherever a wounded ally is present. Not valid during active ASCII map combat turns (the in-combat equivalent is Tend the Wounded, Section 11.7).

Roll: Medicine (Wound Treatment) / Intelligence vs TN 15. Per L5R 4e core rules. Characters without the Wound Treatment emphasis roll base Medicine / Intelligence at TN 15 without the +1k0 emphasis bonus, per the standard emphasis pattern used throughout the document.

Success: target heals 1k1 Wounds (roll 1 die, keep 1 — range 1–10 Wounds healed, average 5.5). Each Raise: +1 die rolled, still keep 1 die. A healer calling 3 Raises rolls 4 dice and keeps the best 1 (range 1–10, but heavily weighted toward higher rolls due to keeping the best). This is explicit RAW — the keep count never increases from Raises.

Rank 5 Mastery Ability: “The amount of Wounds healed on a successful Medicine roll is increased by +1k0.” Base becomes 2k1 (roll 2 dice, keep 1). Each Raise continues to add +1 die rolled, still keep 1. At Rank 5 with 3 Raises: 5k1 (base 1 + mastery +1 + Raises +3 rolled, keep 1).

Failure: no Wounds healed. Medicine Kit charge is still consumed (the healer attempted the treatment — bandages applied, salves used — it simply did not help). The daily treatment limit on the target still applies: a failed attempt prevents any further Medicine treatment of that target that IC day. This is per RAW: “An injured individual may only benefit from a single Medicine Skill Roll per day.”

### 57.31.3 Target Restrictions

TREAT_WOUND targets an ally — per RAW: “When you treat Wounds that an ally has suffered from damage...” The target is a separate character, not the healer themselves. Self-treatment is not supported under RAW and is not implemented in this system. A character who wishes to heal their own wounds must rely on natural Stamina-driven recovery or on magical healing through a shugenja ally.

Target must be in the same zone as the healer. Medicine treatment requires physical access to the wound — inspecting, cleaning, bandaging, applying poultices. Remote treatment through letters or messengers is not possible.

Target must have at least 1 Wound. A character at full health cannot be meaningfully treated — the healer has nothing to heal. The validator rejects TREAT_WOUND candidates targeting unwounded characters before scoring.

Target must consent. Treatment is not forcible. A character who refuses treatment (captured enemy, hostile stranger, someone who distrusts the healer) cannot be targeted. In practice this is almost never restrictive — wounded allies and friendly NPCs accept competent medical care — but it matters in rare cases: an unconscious enemy prisoner cannot be forcibly treated to recover them for interrogation.

### 57.31.3a Wound Rank Visibility

Wound Rank is public information within a zone. Every character in the same zone as another character automatically knows that character’s current Wound Rank: Healthy, Nicked, Grazed, Hurt, Injured, Crippled, Down, or Out. No perception roll, no Investigation check, no Medicine inspection is required. This information is broadcast as part of the normal zone-awareness state.

Rationale: Wound Ranks produce observable consequences that cannot be hidden. A character at Hurt suffers −10 on all rolls — their movements are stiff, their swordwork off, their voice strained. A character at Injured (−15) is obviously suffering. A character at Crippled (−20) can barely function; Down (−40) is unable to stand; Out is incapacitated. These effects are visible in real time to everyone present. Treating Wound Rank as a public variable matches this observable reality without requiring a separate detection subsystem.

Validator implication: the TREAT_WOUND validator checks target.wounds greater than 0 and target in same zone. No additional knowledge check is required. Any character in the zone with a wounded ally can fire TREAT_WOUND against them (subject to consent, daily limit, and Medicine Kit availability). Whether the observer possesses Medicine skill or Investigation skill is irrelevant to noticing that help is needed — those skills matter only for providing the help, not detecting the need.

Triage prioritisation: when an NPC healer has multiple wounded allies in zone, the TEND_WOUNDED_ALLY NeedType generates one candidate per wounded target. The scoring layer naturally prioritises by Wound Rank severity: a target at Crippled scores higher urgency than a target at Nicked. Combined with disposition weight (lord or Strong Ally first, then Friend, then Acquaintance), this produces realistic triage behaviour without requiring a dedicated triage algorithm. A healer walks into a post-battle camp with three wounded allies — one at Injured, one at Hurt, one at Nicked — and treats the Injured ally first, then the Hurt ally the next IC day, then the Nicked ally the IC day after. Each target locks out for their own day after treatment; severity drives order.

Out-of-zone visibility: Wound Rank is broadcast within a zone, not across zones. A character in a different zone from the wounded ally has no knowledge of the wound condition unless informed through letters, messengers, or direct report. This matters for distant lords wondering whether their vassal survived a skirmish, for family members separated by distance, and for messengers carrying news. A wounded character’s condition becomes known to new observers as soon as those observers enter the same zone.

Pride and concealment — narrative note: a samurai may attempt to hide their suffering for reasons of honour or pride (refusing to appear weak, not wanting to burden comrades). This is purely narrative and does not block detection. Everyone still knows the Wound Rank. What the proud samurai can refuse is treatment itself — see Section 57.31.3b for the mechanical refusal evaluation. The wounded character who refuses help is making a visible choice, not concealing a hidden condition.

### 57.31.3b Treatment Refusal — Pride Evaluation

When a character is targeted by TREAT_WOUND, they may refuse treatment. Refusal is driven by Rokugani cultural values — appearing weak in front of others, enduring hardship stoically, not burdening comrades. It is not universal: most samurai accept help from allies in most contexts. But a proud bushi offered treatment in public for a minor scratch may decline, and a rival offering help to a character with strong Bushido convictions may be waved off.

Scope: the refusal evaluation applies automatically to NPCs. Player characters are not subject to the formula — a TREAT_WOUND offer to a player character surfaces as a prompt, and the player decides freely whether to accept or decline according to their own roleplay. The mechanical formula drives autonomous NPC decisions in the persistent simulation; it is not a forced constraint on players.

Refusal formula: the target’s refusal_pressure is computed as the sum of the factors below. If refusal_pressure is greater than 10, the target refuses TREAT_WOUND. The validator treats refusal as a target-side consent failure (Section 57.31.3) — the caster’s action is rejected before the roll fires. No Medicine Kit charge consumed, no daily lockout applied.

Factors increasing refusal_pressure (pride side):

- +5 × target’s Yū (Courage) score. High-Courage characters endure hardship rather than showing weakness. Yū 4 — 5 characters feel strong pride pressure.

- +2 × target’s Honour Rank (whole number, Honour 4.7 = Rank 4). Traditional samurai values reinforce stoicism. Honour 4+ characters inherit cultural pressure toward endurance.

- +5 per witness present in zone beyond the caster and target. Witnesses are other named characters in the same zone who are neither the healer nor the wounded character. Maximum witness contribution: +20 (caps at 4 witnesses). Public contexts drive refusal; private contexts do not.

Factors decreasing refusal_pressure (acceptance side):

- −4 × target’s Chūgi (Duty) score. “I cannot fail my lord through death from infected wounds.” High-Duty characters override personal pride in favour of service.

- Wound Rank severity modifier. A severe wound is too obviously dangerous to refuse — refusing would be stupidity, not courage. Severity weights are non-linear: minor wounds produce little acceptance pressure, severe wounds produce overwhelming acceptance pressure. Severity values by Wound Rank: Nicked 0 (no acceptance pull — minor scratch, pride dominates), Grazed −3, Hurt −6, Injured −15 (substantial jump — this wound could fester and kill), Crippled −30 (overwhelming pull — even proud bushi recognise they cannot function), Down automatic acceptance (unconscious, cannot refuse). These weights are applied directly to refusal_pressure as listed, not multiplied by a constant. The non-linear curve models the cultural reality that refusing treatment for a scratch is noble endurance, refusing treatment for a crippling wound is suicide.

- Disposition toward healer: Strong Ally (+50+) −15, Friend (+25 to +49) −10, Acquaintance (+10 to +24) −5, Neutral (+0 to +9) 0, Rival (−10 to −24) +10, Enemy (−25 to −49) +20, Strong Enemy (−50 or worse) automatic refusal (refusal_pressure set to +100, target refuses regardless of other factors).

Threshold: refusal_pressure greater than 10 = target refuses. Refusal_pressure 10 or less = target accepts.

Worked examples:

- Example A: Matsu Kenji (Yū 3, Honour 4.2, Chūgi 2), wounded at Nicked, disposition toward healer +25 Friend, private context (no witnesses). refusal_pressure = (3×5) + (4×2) − (2×4) − (0×3) − 10 + 0 = 15 + 8 − 8 − 0 − 10 = 5. Accepts. A Friend offering help in private for a minor scratch — Kenji accepts gladly.

- Example B: same Kenji, same wound, but at winter court with 3 Scorpion witnesses. refusal_pressure = 15 + 8 − 8 − 0 − 10 + 15 = 20. Refuses. Public context with hostile observers makes accepting help socially costly — Kenji waves off the offer with a smile, claims it is nothing.

- Example C: same Kenji, now at Injured (severe), still at winter court with 3 witnesses. refusal_pressure = 15 + 8 − 8 − 15 − 10 + 15 = 5. Accepts. The wound is too serious to refuse — Chūgi and the steep severity weight override pride. Even in public, refusing treatment for an Injured wound would look foolish rather than brave.

- Example D: Bayushi Hana (Yū 1, Honour 2.0, Chūgi 2), at Hurt, disposition +15 Acquaintance, at winter court with 2 witnesses. refusal_pressure = (1×5) + (2×2) − (2×4) − (2×3) − 5 + 10 = 5 + 4 − 8 − 6 − 5 + 10 = 0. Accepts. Low-Courage Scorpion isn’t driven by public pride — pragmatic acceptance.

- Example E: Hida Oyabun (Yū 4, Honour 5.0, Chūgi 4), at Injured, disposition −5 Neutral toward healer, private context. refusal_pressure = 20 + 10 − 16 − 15 + 0 + 0 = −1. Accepts. High-Courage Crab bushi but severe wound plus own Chūgi win out.

- Example F: same Oyabun, now at Hurt (not Injured), disposition −20 Rival toward healer, private context. refusal_pressure = 20 + 10 − 16 − 6 + 10 + 0 = 18. Refuses. “I do not need your charity.” A rival offering help to a Crab with high pride and moderate wound — the Crab endures. Pride wins at moderate severity.

- Example G: same Oyabun, same Rival healer, but now at Crippled. refusal_pressure = 20 + 10 − 16 − 30 + 10 + 0 = −6. Accepts. Even high-pride Oyabun cannot refuse treatment for a crippling wound from a Rival — duty to his lord and the overwhelming severity pull crush his pride. The refusal that held at Hurt collapses at Crippled. This demonstrates the key design principle: the more wounded, the more likely the character accepts.

Strong Enemy auto-refusal: when disposition toward the healer is −50 or worse (Strong Enemy tier), refusal is automatic regardless of all other factors. A Crab bushi captured by the Shadowlands Horde and offered healing by a Tainted healer will refuse even at Crippled — death is preferable to accepting care from something of Jigoku. An Imperial hostage held by rebel forces may refuse treatment from the rebel healer on principle, accepting only from their own attending physician.

Witnesses specifically: witnesses are other named characters in the same zone besides the caster (healer) and target (wounded). Unnamed bystanders, peasants, ashigaru, and non-character presences do not count. An ikebana master alone in her garden with her niece who wishes to treat her minor cut faces no witness pressure. The same master with three rival courtiers watching at winter court faces the full +15 witness pressure. This naturally routes healing to private contexts — a dignified retreat from public view to a private chamber for treatment is the cultural solution, matching historical Japanese samurai practice.

No Honour/Glory effects: the refusal itself carries no direct Honour or Glory consequence. The wounded character’s subsequent choices — continuing to function while injured, eventually dying from untreated wounds, or later accepting treatment privately — may generate topics through the Event Ripple system. Witnesses to a dignified refusal may generate a “saw them endure” topic that slowly contributes to the character’s reputation for stoicism, but this is emergent narrative rather than a mechanical Honour/Glory tick tied to the refusal event.

Repeat offers: if a target refuses TREAT_WOUND, the healer is not prevented from offering again later in the IC day — the target’s last_medicine_treatment_ic_day is NOT set on refusal (no roll fired, no kit charge consumed). However, the context must change meaningfully for a second offer to succeed. If Kenji refuses at court with Scorpions watching, the healer may later offer again privately when the rivals are no longer present; the witness count drops, refusal_pressure drops, Kenji now accepts. This models the natural social dynamic of “later, in private” conversations. NPCs re-evaluate the refusal each time TREAT_WOUND fires; the formula is state-dependent, not sticky.

Non-human characters use a simplified refusal rule: the samurai Bushido personality system (Yū, Chūgi, Honour Rank) does not cleanly apply to nezumi, naga, zokujin, kenku, trolls, or other non-human characters. For these targets, the full refusal formula is bypassed. Non-humans refuse TREAT_WOUND only when their disposition toward the healer is Rival or worse (disposition −10 or lower). Otherwise they accept. A nezumi scout with Friend disposition toward a Crab ally accepts unhesitatingly — nezumi culture places no value on stoic endurance of wounds. A naga with Neutral disposition toward a Scorpion healer accepts pragmatically. A zokujin with Rival-or-worse disposition refuses on principle (distrust of human medicine rather than pride). Strong Enemy auto-refusal still applies to non-humans at disposition −50 or worse. This simplified rule models the cultural reality that non-human races have their own healing traditions and don’t share samurai pride codes — their acceptance or refusal is driven by trust of the specific healer, not by honour performance.

Out wound status: the severity weight table covers Nicked through Down. Characters at Out (mortal peril, beyond Down) automatically accept treatment from any non-Strong-Enemy healer. Out is not unconsciousness — an Out character may be conscious but dying. They cannot meaningfully maintain pride when bleeding to death. The refusal formula is bypassed for Out targets in the same way it is bypassed for Down (unconscious) targets: treatment is accepted without roll. Only the Strong Enemy auto-refusal (disposition −50 or worse) can block treatment of an Out target — a Tainted captive dying of wounds may still refuse a Witch Hunter’s mundane aid, preferring death to defilement by the enemy’s touch. Magical healing remains available in that case through separate channels.

Strong Enemy persistence through unconsciousness: when a character’s disposition toward a potential healer is Strong Enemy (−50 or worse), the auto-refusal persists even when the character becomes unconscious (Down or Out). Consent is not something the character actively refreshes each moment — it is a pre-committed state driven by categorical opposition to the healer (ideological, ethnic, spiritual, or personal enmity at the deepest level). An unconscious Bloodspeaker does not become willing to accept aid from a Kuni Witch Hunter simply because they cannot speak. An Imperial hostage held by rebel forces does not become willing to receive treatment from the rebel physician just because they have been knocked senseless. The refusal holds. The only paths to treating such targets are magical healing (which operates through its own consent rules, typically none for spells) or narrative resolution (the target is moved to a neutral or friendly healer whose disposition is not Strong Enemy).

Player characters are exempt: when TREAT_WOUND is offered to a player character (by an NPC healer or another player), the UI presents an accept/decline prompt. The player chooses freely based on their own roleplay preference. The refusal formula is not applied to player characters. Similarly, when a player character offers TREAT_WOUND to an NPC, the NPC’s refusal evaluation fires normally — the player sees the NPC accept or refuse based on the formula. The formula governs NPC behaviour; players have agency.

### 57.31.4 Daily Treatment Limit

Per RAW: an injured individual may only benefit from a single Medicine Skill Roll per day. This limit is tracked on the target’s character sheet via a new field:

- last_medicine_treatment_ic_day (integer, default 0) — stores the IC day number on which the target last received a TREAT_WOUND attempt, successful or not. Updated on every TREAT_WOUND roll regardless of outcome. Purpose: prevents multiple Medicine treatments of the same target within a single IC day.

Validator rule: if target.last_medicine_treatment_ic_day equals current_ic_day, TREAT_WOUND against that target is rejected. The limit is per-target, not per-healer — a healer may treat multiple different wounded allies in a single IC day (each gets their own roll), but each wounded ally receives at most one treatment per day even if multiple healers are present. A party of three wounded samurai attended by one healer with Medicine takes three IC days to treat all three, or requires three healers working simultaneously for a single-day full treatment.

The field resets naturally at each new IC day tick — no explicit reset is needed. The validator simply checks equality against the current IC day. Any prior day’s value is effectively unlocked.

### 57.31.5 Medicine Kit Inventory Rules

The Medicine Kit is a physical inventory item. It is a substantial satchel of herbs, bandages, needles, salves, and ground minerals — not a pocket item. Per RAW: a Medicine Kit allows for ten Medicine Skill Rolls before being consumed. Each TREAT_WOUND roll (success or failure) consumes one charge from the healer’s Medicine Kit. When the kit’s remaining_uses reaches 0, the kit is consumed and removed from inventory.

Inventory limit — new rule: a character may carry at most one Medicine Kit at a time. This is enforced at the inventory layer: attempting to add a Medicine Kit to a character who already holds one is rejected. Receiving a Medicine Kit as a gift, looting one from a body, or purchasing one at a market all fail if the recipient already carries one. The kit must be used up (all 10 charges consumed) or deliberately discarded, transferred to an ally, or stored at a holding before another can be obtained.

Rationale: the Medicine Kit is bulky and not easily stockpiled. The one-kit limit creates meaningful logistical pressure on extended missions — Shadowlands expeditions, long campaigns, sieges — where resupply is not immediately available. A field medic who treats 10 wounded allies exhausts their kit and must return to a settlement to obtain another. Parties planning long expeditions can distribute Medicine Kits across multiple characters so that each carries one, giving the party a combined 20 or 30 charges without any individual character exceeding the limit.

Kit object structure (inventory record):

- item_type: medicine_kit

- remaining_uses: integer 1–10 (a new kit starts at 10; removed from inventory when reaches 0)

- acquired_ic_day: integer (IC day the kit was obtained — for narrative records and possible herb degradation in a future pass)

Medicine Kits are standard equipment purchasable at markets. Existing pricing is unchanged (see Section 49). Phoenix settlements receive −30% pricing (per existing Section 49 clan modifier table) reflecting the Asahina and Isawa apothecary traditions. Dragon settlements also receive −30% (per existing table) reflecting the Tamori alchemical tradition.

### 57.31.6 Non-Human Targets

Per RAW: non-human races may be treated using the Non-human Emphasis, but it must be purchased separately for each race (Nezumi Medicine, Naga Medicine, Zokujin Medicine, etc.). When the target of TREAT_WOUND is a non-human character, the roll uses Medicine (Non-Humans: [target race]) / Intelligence rather than Medicine (Wound Treatment) / Intelligence.

Design extension (not explicit RAW but consistent with RAW emphasis structure): characters lacking the appropriate Non-Humans sub-emphasis for the target’s race may still attempt TREAT_WOUND but suffer +10 TN (effective TN 25) reflecting unfamiliar anatomy, different organ placement, poisonous blood chemistry, and unknown herbal sensitivities. A Crab bushi with Medicine (Wound Treatment) 4 but no Non-Humans sub-emphasis attempting to treat a wounded nezumi ally rolls at TN 25 rather than TN 15. With the Non-Humans: Nezumi sub-emphasis, TN reverts to 15 and the +1k0 emphasis bonus applies normally.

Non-human races for which the emphasis can be purchased: Nezumi (Ratling), Naga, Zokujin, Kenku, Kitsu (spirit), Trolls, and any other non-human race with statted anatomy present in the game. Each race is a separate emphasis purchase.

### 57.31.7 NPC Engine Scoring

TREAT_WOUND fires automatically for NPCs who have Medicine 1+, a Medicine Kit with charges remaining, and a wounded ally (disposition ≥ Neutral) in their current zone. The NeedType driving this action is TEND_WOUNDED_ALLY, which generates as an ImmediateNeed when: (a) a character with disposition ≥ Neutral from the NPC’s perspective is in the same zone with 1+ Wounds and has not received Medicine treatment today; (b) the NPC has Medicine skill rank 1+; (c) the NPC has a Medicine Kit in inventory with remaining_uses greater than 0.

Priority scaling: combines disposition tier and Wound Rank severity. Base priority by disposition: priority 3 (high) when the wounded character is a family member, lord, direct vassal, or Strong Ally (disposition +50+). Priority 2 (normal) for Friend and Ally tier (disposition +25 to +49). Priority 1 (low but present) for Acquaintance and Neutral tier (disposition 0 to +24). Hostile targets (disposition below 0) do not generate this NeedType — the NPC does not voluntarily treat enemies. Wound Rank modifier: add +1 priority if target is at Hurt or worse, add +2 priority if target is at Injured or worse, add +3 priority if target is at Crippled or Down. A Friend at Injured (base priority 2 + Wound Rank modifier +2) fires at priority 4 — higher than a Strong Ally at Nicked (base priority 3 + 0 = priority 3). Severity dominates when critical. Priority ceiling: 4 (critical).

Personality modifiers: Jin (compassion) +15, Gi (righteousness) +10, Chugi (loyalty, when target is lord or direct superior) +20, Rei (courtesy) +5. Personality-driven healers fire TEND_WOUNDED_ALLY more aggressively than average characters.

Schools with elevated TREAT_WOUND weights: Asahina Shugenja (pacifist healers), Kitsu Shugenja (when tending to Lion family), Monkey Clan Healer (Toku Bushi ronin tradition established post-Iuchiban), Crab Berserker medics, and any NPC with Medicine 4+ regardless of school. These NPCs autonomously triage wounded allies after combat without requiring lord direction.

Objective alignment: TREAT_WOUND 100, ASK_FOR_INTRODUCTION 10, DO_NOTHING 0. Extremely narrow alignment — only TREAT_WOUND meaningfully addresses a wounded-ally need. If no Medicine Kit is available, the NeedType falls back to BEGIN_TRAVEL targeting the nearest settlement with an apothecary.

### 57.31.7a Natural Healing Baseline

Natural healing is the passive baseline recovery mechanism that operates for all characters regardless of Medicine skill or medical attention. A character who rests through a full night recovers Wounds automatically each morning — the body’s own capacity to heal, strong in the well-conditioned samurai of Rokugan, drives this recovery. This is separate from and stacks with TREAT_WOUND: a wounded character may receive a Medicine treatment during the day AND recover naturally overnight.

Formula: at each OOC Day Tick (once per real-world 24-hour cycle), any character whose rested_last_night flag is true recovers Wounds equal to:

- Wounds recovered = (Stamina × 2) + Insight Rank

This is the L5R 4e RAW natural healing formula. Stamina is the target’s current Stamina Trait (not affected by Wound Rank penalties — penalties apply to rolls, not Trait values). Insight Rank is the target’s current Insight Rank (1–10).

Worked examples:

- A Rank 1 Crab bushi with Stamina 3: recovers (3×2) + 1 = 7 Wounds per OOC day.

- A Rank 3 Crane courtier with Stamina 2: recovers (2×2) + 3 = 7 Wounds per OOC day.

- A Rank 5 Lion bushi with Stamina 4: recovers (4×2) + 5 = 13 Wounds per OOC day.

- A Rank 7 Asahina shugenja with Stamina 2: recovers (2×2) + 7 = 11 Wounds per OOC day. High-rank characters recover faster because their Insight Rank adds directly to recovery.

Rest requirement — what counts as a full rested OOC day: the character must have had sufficient rest across the OOC day (not necessarily at a single moment — the OOC day spans 4 IC days with normal work and sleep patterns). The following conditions disqualify natural healing for that OOC day by setting rested_last_night to false:

- Active combat at any point during the OOC day (ambush, siege assault, bandit raid, any ASCII map combat turn resolution). If the character participated in or was present at active combat during any of the OOC day’s 4 IC days, rested_last_night is set to false for the next OOC Day Tick. No natural healing that OOC day.

- Continuous travel during the OOC day (forced march, urgent journey, night travel to avoid pursuit — any travel state that denies the character a proper stationary rest period within the OOC day). No healing that OOC day.

- On watch duty for the majority of the OOC day (standing guard, patrol rotation, sentry duty). No healing that OOC day. A character on a light rotation (short watches interspersed with long rest periods) still rests sufficiently and heals normally; the rule applies only when watch duty is the dominant activity.

- Starvation conditions: characters who have gone without food or water for 2+ days cannot recover Wounds naturally (per existing starvation mechanic in Section 23). No healing that OOC day.

- Certain spell effects or supernatural conditions that specifically block healing (Jigai’s Curse, Lingering Agony, Kansen bleeding wounds without treatment, etc.). Each such effect specifies its own block.

- Character is at Out (mortal peril). Characters at Out do not heal naturally — their condition is critical and worsening. An Out character requires stabilisation through Medicine, magical healing, or an Earth roll to survive the night; natural healing does not fire until they recover to Down or higher.

Down characters DO heal naturally if the OOC day counts as rested — they are unconscious but not dying. A Down character resting safely through an OOC day recovers Stamina × 2 + Insight Rank Wounds at the next OOC Day Tick and may return to consciousness at a higher Wound Rank (Crippled or better).

OOC Day Tick resolution: natural healing does NOT fire at each IC Morning Tick. Because 4 IC days occur within 1 OOC day in the game’s compressed timeline, applying healing at every IC Morning Tick would compress natural recovery into four doses per real-world day — trivialising combat consequences. Instead, natural healing fires once per OOC day at a single OOC Day Tick event. This anchors wound recovery to the player’s real-world rest cycle rather than the accelerated in-game day cycle. A character who takes 30 Wounds on IC Day 1 of an OOC day remains wounded throughout IC Days 2, 3, and 4 of that same OOC day — their natural recovery happens only when the real-world day closes and the next OOC day begins. This matches the design principle that rest-driven mechanics follow player-real-world time, not compressed in-game time.

The engine iterates all characters at the OOC Day Tick, checks the rested_last_night field on the character sheet, and if true, applies the healing formula. The rested_last_night field resets to true at the start of each OOC day and is flipped to false by any disqualifying event that occurs at any point during the OOC day (any of its 4 IC days). Multiple disqualifying events across the OOC day do not need to be tracked individually — any one of them setting the flag false prevents natural healing at the next OOC Day Tick.

Stacking with TREAT_WOUND: natural healing and Medicine treatment are independent and operate on different cadences. TREAT_WOUND fires per IC day (daily lockout per target), while natural healing fires per OOC day. A character wounded in combat may receive TREAT_WOUND treatments across multiple IC days within a single OOC day (each against a different target, or same target across OOC days), AND also receive the single OOC Day Tick natural healing. The TREAT_WOUND per-IC-day limit (Section 57.31.4) does NOT apply to natural healing — that limit is for Medicine Skill Rolls only, not baseline recovery.

Stacking with magical healing: natural healing, TREAT_WOUND, and magical healing (Path to Inner Peace, Regeneration kiho, Asahina rituals) all operate independently. A well-served wounded samurai in a friendly settlement could theoretically receive: a shugenja’s Path to Inner Peace during the day (magical healing), a Medicine treatment that evening (TREAT_WOUND), and natural healing overnight. All three apply.

Character sheet field addition: rested_last_night (boolean, default true). Reset to true at the start of each OOC day. Flipped to false by any disqualifying event occurring during the OOC day: active combat, continuous travel, watch duty majority, starvation, or specific spell/condition effects. Read by the OOC Day Tick resolution to determine natural healing eligibility. The field is NOT reset per IC day — the disqualification persists through all 4 IC days of the OOC day in which it was triggered.

Implication for gameplay: natural healing is paced against the OOC day rather than the IC day, meaning wounds persist meaningfully across multiple IC days within a single OOC session. A Rank 2 bushi with Stamina 3 (recovering 7 Wounds per OOC day) who took 20 Wounds in a skirmish recovers fully in 3 OOC days (about 12 IC days) without any Medicine attention. Medicine treatment can accelerate this — TREAT_WOUND fires per IC day, so up to 4 Medicine treatments could occur within the same OOC day (one per IC day), providing meaningful mid-session recovery while natural healing catches up on the OOC tick. This means serious injury scenarios persist within a playing session, giving wounded characters tactical incentive to seek healers or rest, rather than shrugging off damage within an IC morning. The drama of wounds comes from their immediate tactical effects and their persistence across the IC timeline until the real-world day closes. DESIGN INTENT: the asymmetry between TREAT_WOUND (per-IC-day) and natural healing (per-OOC-day) is intentional. Action-driven mechanics (Medicine treatment, combat, court actions) follow IC day cadence. Rest-driven mechanics (natural healing, Void refresh, Civilian Order refresh) follow OOC day cadence. This is a core architectural principle — see Section 57.32.2 for the parallel Void Point design.

### 57.31.8 Interaction with Other Systems

Kansen bleeding wounds (Section 23, maho spells): a wound inflicted by a kansen that causes continuous bleeding (1 Wound per Round) can be stopped two ways. (1) A Medicine (Wound Treatment) / Intelligence roll at TN 20, resolved as an in-combat action (parallel to Tend the Wounded in Section 11.7) — this is NOT the TREAT_WOUND ActionID, which is specifically out-of-combat. During combat, a character with a Medicine Kit may spend a Complex Action attempting the TN 20 bandaging roll; success stops the bleeding but does not heal existing Wounds. (2) Magical healing that restores the target to full Wounds — Path to Inner Peace or similar spells that heal the target to 0 Wounds end the kansen effect. Once combat ends and the bleeding is either stopped or the target has died, subsequent wound treatment follows normal TREAT_WOUND rules (TN 15, out-of-combat, AP-based). The TN 20 in-combat kansen stop and the TN 15 out-of-combat TREAT_WOUND are two separate Medicine uses with different contexts — they are not variants of the same action.

Taint: Medicine does not remove Taint. A character with Taint Rank 1+ can have their Wounds healed normally by TREAT_WOUND, but the Taint itself persists and continues to generate its own effects. Taint removal requires shugenja purification rituals (Section 4) or specific Kuni Purifier interventions — not mundane Medicine.

Wound Ranks and Wound Penalties: TREAT_WOUND heals Wounds from the top of the damage track. A character who has taken 25 Wounds and is in the Nicked Wound Rank heals downward as Wounds are removed — once their total Wounds drop below the Nicked threshold, the Nicked penalty lifts. The system does not track “which specific Wound is healed” — Wounds are a pool, not individual injuries.

Magical healing: Path to Inner Peace (Water spell), Regeneration kiho, Asahina healing rituals, and similar magical effects heal Wounds through their own mechanics and do not interact with the TREAT_WOUND daily limit. A character may receive Path to Inner Peace AND a TREAT_WOUND roll in the same IC day — the daily limit applies to mundane Medicine treatments, not to magic. This is per RAW: the one-per-day rule references Medicine Skill Roll, not all healing.

Natural healing (Stamina-driven recovery): continues normally regardless of TREAT_WOUND. A character with high Stamina who also receives TREAT_WOUND benefits from both — natural recovery ticks on the daily cycle, and the Medicine roll provides an additional one-time boost.

Poison treatment: the Antidotes emphasis applies to poison remediation. When TREAT_WOUND targets a character whose poison has been diagnosed (Section 57.48.14), the roll uses Medicine (Antidotes) / Intelligence vs the poison’s Heal TN. One attempt per healer per poison instance. No Medicine Kit consumed. Wound treatment and poison treatment are tracked separately — both can occur on the same IC day. Full poison diagnosis, treatment, and NPC reactive behavior rules: Section 57.48.14–16.

### 57.31.10 Character Sheet Field Additions

Section 22.3 character sheet amendment (added v484): (1) last_medicine_treatment_ic_day (integer, default 0) — IC day number of the most recent Medicine treatment received. Updated by TREAT_WOUND on every roll (success or failure). Used by the TREAT_WOUND validator to enforce the RAW one-treatment-per-IC-day limit. (2) rested_last_night (boolean, default true) — whether the character had sufficient rest across the current OOC day. Read at the OOC Day Tick to determine natural healing and Void Point refresh eligibility (Section 57.31.7a and Section 57.32). Reset to true at the start of each OOC day; flipped to false by active combat, continuous travel, watch duty majority, starvation, or specific blocking effects occurring anywhere within the OOC day’s 4 IC days.
