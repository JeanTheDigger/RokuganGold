## 2.5 Religion — The Fortunes

Rokugan is a deeply spiritual empire. The Fortunes — divine entities who govern every aspect of mortal life — are not abstract concepts but real and active presences whose favor and displeasure manifest in tangible ways. Every farmer prays before planting. Every soldier invokes a Fortune before battle. Every birth, death, marriage, and harvest has its patron deity. The Fortunes are divided into two tiers: the Seven Great Fortunes, who are the most powerful and widely venerated, and the Lesser Fortunes (Mikokami), who govern more specific domains.

### 2.5.1 The Seven Great Fortunes

The Seven Great Fortunes are the most powerful divine entities in Rokugan outside of the Celestial Dragons and the Kami themselves. They existed before the rise of Rokugan and were worshipped by the scattered tribes of the ancient world. Every settlement in the Empire maintains at least a small shrine to the Seven.

**Benten — Fortune of Romantic Love**

The lone female among the Seven Fortunes, Benten is beautiful beyond description and speaks for the group on the rare occasions they appear together — a testament to her gifts for oratory and diplomacy. She is not cruel or spiteful, and her wrath is rarely roused by any mortal. Benten is particularly beloved by the Crane Clan, who she blesses frequently, especially in marriages between the Crane and other clans. The Scorpion also maintain a devoted following to her, though their adoration is less openly displayed.

**Bishamon — Fortune of Strength**

Great Bishamon is the warrior of the Seven Fortunes — powerful, direct, and the quickest to anger of his peers. Despite his tempestuous nature he is also a patron of philosophers and idealists, and turns to war only when no other solution exists. He is always depicted as a huge man in full armor bearing a massive weapon. Bishamon is particularly venerated by the Crab and Lion clans, though bushi of all clans pay him homage.

**Daikoku — Fortune of Wealth**

The most approachable of the Seven, Daikoku is beloved by the lower classes above all others — not only is he the patron of farmers and merchants, but countless peasants pray to him for relief from poverty. He appears as a pleasantly fat man in fine clothing, carrying a bag of rice that perpetually trickles away though never runs out. His companion is a large rat that consumes the rice he leaves behind — the two are great friends. Daikoku is mischievous and frequently travels the Empire in disguise to observe mortals. He favors the Unicorn Clan above all others, but also frequently blesses the Crane and Mantis. One of his greatest temples is found in the Scorpion city of Ryoko Owari.

**Ebisu — Fortune of Honest Work**

Unassuming in appearance and distant in temperament, Ebisu's most favored subjects are the peasants of the Empire — those who work hardest and receive the least reward. In his view, the fruits of honest labor are their own reward. He appears as a man in rough common garb, though his otherworldly presence ensures no one mistakes him for a mortal. Ebisu favors the Mantis heavily, because they earned their place among the Great Clans rather than inheriting it.

**Fukurokujin — Fortune of Wisdom**

The Fortune most closely associated with enlightenment in Rokugani theology. Many scholars equate him with Shinsei in some way, though others consider this blasphemous. He appears as an older man with long sharp features and bright alert eyes, and has fewer documented appearances in the mortal realm than any of his peers. Fukurokujin favors the Dragon and Phoenix clans for their abundance of theologians and scholars — those who seek wisdom for its own sake rather than as a means to an end.

**Hotei — Fortune of Contentment ****&**** Serenity**

Hotei blesses those who have accepted their place in the universe. He will not grant his gift of genuine serenity to those who constantly seek something more — only those who can endure and enjoy their life as it is will receive his favor. He abhors violence and is unique among the Seven for having virtually no wrath whatsoever. He genuinely likes all mortals and has animosity toward none. Those rare individuals who incur his disfavor find themselves perpetually out of place, never fitting in no matter where they go. Hotei always appears as a heavyset monk-like figure with a perpetual smile.

**Jurojin — Fortune of Longevity**

The Fortune of Longevity represents health, vitality, and long life, and is also considered a keeper of sacred wisdom — making him either a rival or closest ally of Fukurokujin depending on the sect consulted. Some believe Jurojin can speak to the animals of Rokugan and knows all they see, though this is debated. The Scorpion revere him greatly for the secrets he is believed to possess, and Jurojin appears to return their favor.

### 2.5.2 The Lesser Fortunes (Mikokami)

The Lesser Fortunes govern more specific aspects of mortal life. Many were once mortals themselves — heroes, scholars, officials, or craftsmen elevated to Fortunehood by Imperial decree or by the will of the Celestial Heavens. Others are primal entities who existed long before mankind. The mikokami are more likely than the Seven to visit the mortal realm directly, and are more approachable in their dealings with mortals.

Daikoku — Fortune of Wealth

See Seven Great Fortunes above.

**Emma-O — Fortune of Death**

Usually represented as a tall bearded man with an unkind countenance — perhaps bitter that his duties have permanently removed him from the Celestial Heavens. He holds dominion over Meido, the Realm of Waiting, where those who perish await his judgment. He weighs the sum of each person's worth and decides their fate: a period of waiting before reincarnation, condemnation to Jigoku or Gaki-do, or the rare honor of ascending to the Realm of Blessed Ancestors. Emma-O is known to have fathered at least one child with a mortal woman.

**Goemon — Fortune of Heroes**

Formerly Matsu Goemon, a Lion Clan soldier who fought in the Clan War, the War against the Darkness, the War of Spirits, and countless other conflicts. He was renowned for the extraordinary morale and success rate of his unit. Killed during the battle for the City of the Rich Frog, his death was feared to damage army morale so greatly that the Kitsu family petitioned Emperor Toturi III to elevate him as a Fortune. He now serves the Thunder Dragon.

**Hachiman — Fortune of Battle**

Particularly revered among soldiers serving in their clan's armies and military officers of all sorts. Hachiman is seen as a vassal of either Bishamon or Fukurokujin depending on the individual's inclination. He has shrines at the sites of many major battles from the Empire's past, and armies frequently invoke his favor en masse immediately before taking the field.

**Hamanri — Fortune of Stability ****&**** Government**

Formerly Otomo Hamanri, a quiet and unassuming bureaucrat who devoted his life to improving the efficiency of the Imperial government. Others frequently took credit for his innovations, which never deterred him — he considered duty more important than glory. After his death, documentation of his contributions was presented to the Emperor, who levied punishments against those who had exploited him and elevated Hamanri to Fortunehood in recognition of his devotion.

**Haruhiko — Fortune of Fishermen**

A simple peasant fisherman who worked tirelessly to feed not only his own family but also others in need. He once shared his last fish with a hungry stranger — who turned out to be the Emperor traveling in disguise. For this act of selfless kindness the Emperor elevated him to Fortunehood upon his death. Haruhiko was later killed by Fu Leng during the Dark Kami's assault on the Celestial Heavens.

**Hikora — Fortune of Oak**

A stoic and removed entity entreated by those who dwell in forested regions or depend on the forest for their livelihood. Carpenters revere Hikora and offer prayers when cutting lumber and constructing wooden goods, asking for durability and beauty in their work.

**Hujokuko — Fortune of Fertility**

She has few shrines or temples but is frequently invoked by those who wish to bear children or who have struggled to begin a family. She is considered a vassal of Benten and many worshippers invoke both at once. Nuns and shugenja devoted specifically to Hujokuko are rare but extremely popular, always in demand for their rituals and blessings. Her adherents are almost invariably female.

**Inari — Fortune of Rice**

Unquestionably one of the most venerated of all Lesser Fortunes, Inari is invoked regularly by virtually every farmer in the Empire. Depicted primarily as female, she is considered benevolent — even when harvests are poor it is generally assumed the farmers failed to be sufficiently pious, or did not offer enough sacrifice to earn her blessing. Her rarely depicted wrathful aspect is known as the Frost Dragon, a creature that brings untimely frosts that can devastate the crops of the arrogant or disobedient.

**Jizo — Fortune of Mercy**

An extremely popular Fortune entreated most often by those suffering from illness or misfortune. There is theological debate over whether Jizo is a unique entity or an aspect of Fukurokujin, as no formal records exist of any mortal named Jizo being elevated to Fortunehood. Detractors counter that Jizo is a primal Fortune who predates humanity entirely.

**Jotei — Fortune of Morning Dew**

A Fortune of very little influence. Jotei is most famous for a peculiar story: a farmer who passed a dilapidated shrine dedicated to him misheard the name and believed it devoted to Hotei. Knowing nothing of proper ritual, the farmer spent much of his life tending the shrine anyway. Though he eventually discovered his error, he died without regret, having lived happily. Jotei blessed the farmer's grave, and the site became known as the Shrine of Reverse Fortunes — now receiving many visitors.

**Kenro-ji-jin — Fortune of Soil**

It is possible that no other Fortune has more shrines devoted to them than the Fortune of Soil. Virtually every farmer and gardener in the Empire maintains a small shrine to Kenro-ji-jin near their fields, offering prayers at the start and end of each day. He is notoriously impartial — it is extremely difficult to gain his attention, making regular and consistent devotion essential. He is one of several Fortunes involved in crop production alongside Inari and Kuroshin.

**Kisada — Fortune of Persistence**

The legendary Crab hero Hida Kisada, remembered for his tactical brilliance and tragic poor judgment during the Clan War. His most remarkable feat came at the end of his life when Fu Leng stabbed him through the stomach with the Ancestral Blade of the Hantei — a weapon reputed to instantly slay all it struck — and Kisada survived another two years, helping the Crab Clan redeem itself. Emperor Toturi I declared him the Fortune of Persistence. He later briefly returned to mortal flesh by passing through Oblivion's Gate, spending several years with the Crab before falling to Scorpion assassins and returning to the Celestial Heavens.

**Ko-no-hama — Fortune of Flowers**

A petite and delicate entity mirroring the plants of her domain, childlike in appearance. She is favored by artisans of all sorts, particularly ikebana artists and gardeners, and is a special favorite of the Kakita and Shiba families. All samurai families who embrace outdoor beauty maintain small unobtrusive shrines to her in the most beautiful regions of their homelands.

**Koji — Fortune of the Kitchen**

Sometimes jokingly called the Fortune of Housewives, Koji's likeness is omnipresent in the kitchens of all castes throughout the Empire. Housewives and mothers pray to her with great fervor asking her to protect their homes from fire, which can devastate even the wealthiest estate. She is depicted as a plump and happy woman with nimble hands, and also oversees the art of cooking — which some artisans consider a ritualized process rivaling the tea ceremony or formal flower arrangement.

**Koshin — Fortune of Roads**

The patron of all who travel extensively — Unicorn riders, Miya heralds, merchants, scouts, magistrates, and tax collectors all maintain devotion to the Fortune of Roads. Caravan masters frequently keep small mobile shrines to Koshin to ensure their journeys are safe and prosperous.

**Kuroshin — Fortune of Agriculture**

An extremely patient and kind Fortune closely associated with Inari, Kenro-ji-jin, and Toyouke-omikama. Kuroshin favors all who are truly pious and patient in learning proper farming — those who take pains to ensure each year's harvest also protects the land's long-term vitality earn his particular blessing. Those who are careless and leave the land depleted or barren earn his ire. The largest shrines to Kuroshin can be found in Unicorn and Crane holdings, which boast some of the most fertile plains in the Empire.

**Megumi — Fortune of Heroic Guidance**

A mischievous entity prone to interference in mortal affairs in ways the more powerful Fortunes cannot indulge. Megumi tests the worth and honor of samurai by appearing in mortal form as an aged commoner, always carrying one item of brilliant blue color no matter how out of place it seems. Those who pass his tests receive his blessings; those who do not are left to their own devices. He appears even more frequently in the form of a crane, making him very well regarded by the Crane Clan.

**Musubu-no-Kami — Fortune of Marriage**

The second of the three Unnamed Fortunes, Musubu-no-Kami is a primal entity intricately bound to the tradition of marriage among mortals. It has no fixed form and can appear as male or female in any manner imaginable. Especially venerated by matchmakers who invoke its favor to ensure arranged unions are happy, prosperous, and politically beneficial. The Otomo family maintains an enormous temple to the Fortune, invoking it constantly in the course of their duty as the Empire's most prominent matchmakers.

**Muzaka — Fortune of Enigmas**

As mysterious as might be expected, Muzaka is poorly understood and has almost no significant following — typically only theologians and scholars are even aware of his existence. He has a tradition of entering the Empire periodically, seeking out a worthy mortal to engage in some contest — usually a game or a riddle competition. Those found truly worthy are granted a vision of things to come, though only as a sense of fate the individual can alter through very careful choices. These visions frequently lead to ruin — but the truly strong-willed can emerge stronger and more prosperous.

**Nagiroko-to — Fortune of Forgiveness**

Invoked by the penitent and the guilty — those who have committed shameful acts that cannot or will not be forgiven by mortal minds may pray to Nagiroko-to in hope that she will place forgiveness in the hearts of the wronged. The only group known to entreat her en masse are the Deathseekers of the Lion Clan, who pray that their acts of heroic death will bring forgiveness from the spirits of their ancestors.

**Natsu-togumara — Fortune of Travel ****&**** Experience**

The patron of those who crave adventure and purpose beyond a minor role in an obscure holding. More than one samurai who felt trapped by circumstance has prayed fervently to the Fortune of Travel and Experience for a greater duty. Magistrates often pray for his blessings to better fulfill their duties. Those undertaking an artist's journey or the warrior pilgrimage of musha shugyo frequently invoke his name.

**Osano-Wo — Fortune of Fire ****&**** Thunder**

Perhaps the most powerful of all the mikokami. Formerly the mortal Hida Osano-Wo, son of the Kami Hida and the mortal avatar of the Thunder Dragon. His death was avenged by his elder son Kaimetsu-uo — who founded the Mantis Clan — when lightning struck down the gates of the Phoenix fortification hiding his father's murderer. This display of power from beyond death persuaded the Phoenix to petition the Emperor to declare Osano-Wo a Fortune.

**Sadahako — Fortune of Geisha ****&**** Artists**

Sadahako was a geisha favored by a member of the Hantei dynasty, elevated to Fortunehood following her death. Her existence is a quiet controversy, as geisha occupy a low position in the Celestial Order. She was also made Fortune of Artists — an aspect that receives considerably more public attention. Artisans revere Sadahako and pray for her inspiration, particularly among the Kakita and Shiba families.

**Saibankan — Fortune of Magistrates ****&**** Judges**

Formerly Soshi Saibankan, a key figure in the Empire's older system of law enforcement. He was approached by the Emperor’s Champion, Doji Hatsuo, and together the two developed the Imperial Magistrate system that has endured for over a thousand years with almost no change. Ironically, members of the Scorpion Clan also pray to him regularly — seeking his wisdom in avoiding detection by the very magistrates he created.

**Sengen — Fortune of Mount Sengen**

**⚙️ CROSS-REF (Law ****&**** Order):** The full history of Soshi Saibankan and Doji Hatsuo’s creation of the Emerald Magistrate system is documented in Section 2.8.3. Magistrates are still called “saibankan” in his honor.

One of the oldest known primal Fortunes. Despite early identification, little is understood about Sengen's true function or role in the Celestial Order. Shugenja agree the kami around Mount Sengen are particularly powerful and receptive to mortal entreaties, but no one fully understands why. When the Naga were briefly awake in the twelfth century, Rokugani scholars attempted to consult their records of the region — the Naga were strangely reluctant to discuss it.

**Sudaro — Fortune of Perseverance**

A minor Fortune whose position was somewhat complicated by the ascension of Kisada as the Fortune of Persistence. Sudaro lacks the sheer force of will Kisada possesses and may be somewhat resentful of the newcomer's greater prominence, yet has accepted his role as kindred spirit — perhaps even subservient to Kisada given the latter’s vastly greater following. There is an obvious irony in the fact that the Fortune of Perseverance has been placed in a difficult position requiring him to persevere.

**Suitengu — Fortune of the Sea**

One of the primal Fortunes, having existed long before mankind. Unlike many primal Fortunes, Suitengu always manifests the same way — as a rather unassuming samurai with wind-blown hair and robes, and a wild wrathful look in his eyes. He is not kind or merciful. Most prayers to him are to avoid his wrath rather than entreat his mercy, for those most familiar with the sea know it is absolutely merciless. Shrines appear all across Rokugan’s coastline. The largest shrine is in Clear Water Village in Crab lands, where hundreds of ships arrive daily and thousands of sailors pray for permission to cross his domain.

**Tengen — Fortune of Writing ****&**** Literature**

A quiet and reserved Fortune who remains largely absent from prominent displays of divine power in the mortal realm, preferring to quietly bless those who prove worthy. Particularly revered by shugenja, priests, historians, and those who create works of fiction. The largest temple to Tengen was once in the Ikoma provinces of the Lion Clan until it was destroyed in war — an ambitious Miya family member ensured an even larger shrine was constructed in the Miya provinces, a small but pointed political victory at the Lion's expense.

**Tsi Xing Guo — Fortune of Smiths ****&**** Swordsmiths**

Associated with a series of Celestial Swords granted to the Great Clans by the Heavens. Despite allegedly having something of a temper in mortal life, he is known as a calm and even-tempered Fortune content to focus on his craft and fulfill requests made of him. Revered by families with strong smithing traditions — particularly the Kaiu of the Crab Clan, the Dragon Clan's Tamori, and the Tsi family itself. Bushi also pray for his blessings on their weapons as they prepare for battle.

**Yama-no-kami — Fortune of Stone**

The third of the three Unnamed Fortunes, and by far the most mysterious. Yama-no-kami appears to have chosen to be forgotten by the majority of mortals, for reasons no one can say. A very small sect of monks — the Order of Stone — tends to the Fortune's one and only known temple, hidden deep within the mountains far outside normal travel routes and well away from any Great Clan holdings. The Order insists that Yama-no-kami once had a true name, but allowed it to be forgotten — the reason for this they will not reveal to outsiders.

**Yozo — Fortune of Vengeance**

One of the most unique Fortunes in Rokugani cosmology. Yozo was a mortal shugenja who retired to a monastery, but in a moment of weakness and regret committed an unforgivable act — summoning an oni. Immediately overcome with remorse, he destroyed the beast and spent the rest of his life fighting tirelessly against corruption and evil. Upon his death his spirit spontaneously ascended to Tengoku as the Fortune of Vengeance. A fragment of his soul had remained with the demon he created, however, and when Fu Leng assaulted the gates of Tengoku the demon was among his forces. It consumed numerous smaller Fortunes before being destroyed in combat with Yozo — who likewise perished. The fate of his soul remains unknown, unprecedented in all of Rokugani theology.
