## 55.10.3 — Phoenix Clan Governance Exception: Elemental Council Authority System

**STATUS:** LOCKED. Phoenix-specific exception to the standard Clan Champion Strategic Evaluation.

### 55.10.3.1 Context

In every other Great Clan, the Clan Champion holds supreme authority. The Phoenix inverts this. The Shiba Clan Champion holds the title officially, but true governing authority rests with the Isawa Elemental Council — five Masters, one for each Ring. The Champion's role is ceremonial leadership, diplomatic representation, and military command of an army the Council must first authorize.

This is not a dormant arrangement like the Dragon. The Council actively governs. Every major decision passes through a majority vote. The Champion proposes, the Council approves or rejects. This makes the Phoenix militarily slow but politically resilient — and it means the Champion's relationship with the Council is the central tension of Phoenix governance.

### 55.10.3.2 Council-Gated Strategic Evaluation

The Shiba Champion runs System A, generating `clan_strategic_priorities` through the standard Clan Champion Strategic Evaluation process (Section 55.10). However, any StrategicConclusion involving a **major decision** requires Council majority vote (3 of 5 Masters) before it becomes operational.

**Major decisions requiring Council vote:**
- Declaring or entering war
- Deploying the Phoenix Go-hatamoto outside Phoenix territory
- Signing treaties or formal diplomatic commitments
- Major resource expenditure above PROVISIONAL 500 koku or equivalent
- Enacting grand rituals
- Committing shugenja to another clan's campaigns

**Decisions the Champion handles alone:**
- Internal province governance and administration
- Diplomatic representation at court (attending, speaking for the clan)
- Routine military positioning within Phoenix territory
- Tax and stipend adjustments
- Filling vacancies below Family Daimyo level

The Asako Family Daimyo receives directives from the Champion directly — no Council gate. The Shiba family's internal military structure answers to the Champion directly for routine matters. The Council collectively administers all Isawa resources — there is no Isawa Family Daimyo. Isawa provincial lords report to the Council as a body, not to any individual Master.

### 55.10.3.3 Council Vote Mechanic

When a major decision reaches the Council, each Master evaluates the proposal independently and votes YES or NO. 3 of 5 votes required for approval.

**Per-Master vote evaluation:**

Each Master scores the proposal on two axes — elemental alignment (does this proposal match my element's temperament?) and practical assessment (does this serve the clan based on current world state?). The elemental alignment is the dominant factor, making votes partially predictable but never certain.

**Fire Master — Aggressive, decisive, volatile.**
Votes YES on: military action, offensive campaigns, decisive responses to threats, punishing enemies, grand rituals of destruction. Votes NO on: defensive postures, prolonged negotiation, patience, inaction in the face of provocation. Disposition modifier: +10 toward proposals involving direct action, −10 toward proposals counseling restraint.

**Water Master — Adaptive, pragmatic, opportunistic.**
Votes YES on: proposals that exploit current advantage, flexible responses, alliances of convenience, trade agreements, anything with clear material benefit. Votes NO on: rigid commitments, ideologically-driven actions with poor strategic returns, honorable but costly gestures. Disposition modifier: +10 toward proposals with clear practical benefit, −10 toward proposals driven by principle over pragmatism.

**Air Master — Subtle, political, deceptive.**
Votes YES on: diplomatic solutions, court maneuvers, intelligence operations, actions that increase Phoenix political influence, indirect approaches. Votes NO on: direct military action, blunt displays of force, anything that exposes Phoenix to political embarrassment. Disposition modifier: +10 toward proposals involving court or diplomacy, −10 toward proposals involving open warfare.

**Earth Master — Conservative, defensive, stubborn.**
Votes YES on: defensive measures, fortification, maintaining the status quo, protecting Phoenix territory and resources, cautious responses. Votes NO on: offensive war, risky ventures, change for its own sake, committing resources outside Phoenix territory. Disposition modifier: +10 toward defensive or conservative proposals, −10 toward aggressive or expansionist proposals.

**Void Master — Inscrutable. Driven by omens and prophecy.**
The Void Master does not evaluate proposals through conventional reasoning. Their vote is determined by a weighted random roll modified by the current spiritual state of the Empire: active realm overlaps, PTL levels, worship failures, and celestial omens. The Void Master's vote cannot be reliably predicted or bought through disposition manipulation. PROVISIONAL: 40% chance of YES on any proposal, modified by +20% if active spiritual crisis exists related to the proposal, −20% if no spiritual dimension exists. The Void Master may also abstain — creating the possibility of a 2-2 deadlock (see 55.10.3.4).

**Additional vote modifiers (all Masters):**
- Master's disposition toward the Champion: Friend or above adds +5 to YES. Rival or below adds +5 to NO.
- Active Tier 1 crisis threatening Phoenix: all Masters gain +15 to YES on proposals addressing the crisis (survival overrides temperament).
- The proposal directly threatens the Master's element's domain (e.g., a Fire shrine is threatened): that Master gains +20 to YES regardless of elemental alignment.

### 55.10.3.4 Council Deadlock

If the Void Master abstains, a 2-2 split is possible. This is a recognized crisis state.

**Deadlock resolution:** The deadlocked proposal is tabled for one season. At the next seasonal review, the vote is called again. If the Void Master abstains a second consecutive time on the same proposal, the Champion may break the tie — but exercising this authority costs PROVISIONAL −0.3 Honor (acting against the spirit of the compact) and generates a Tier 4 topic ("Phoenix Champion overrode Council deadlock").

If the Void Master votes on the re-vote, the result stands. A proposal that fails twice is dead — it cannot be resubmitted for PROVISIONAL 2 seasons.

### 55.10.3.5 Champion Defiance Path

The Champion may grow frustrated with Council vetoes — particularly when the Champion believes action is urgently needed and the Council refuses. The defiance path triggers when the Champion takes a major action without Council vote.

**Escalation scope:** Like the Dragon (55.10.2.6), the escalation ladder tracks the Council's collective patience with the Champion. The defiance counter is cumulative across all unilateral actions. A single act of compliance between defiances does not reset the ladder.

**Stage 1 — Formal censure.** The Council issues a public statement condemning the Champion's unilateral action. PROVISIONAL −0.3 Honor for the Champion. The action stands — the Council cannot undo a military deployment already in progress — but the political damage is done. A Tier 4 topic propagates: "Phoenix Champion acted without Council approval."

**Stage 2 — Diplomatic suspension.** After 2 unilateral actions, the Council suspends the Champion's diplomatic representation authority. The Champion can no longer attend court as the official Phoenix representative — the Council appoints an Isawa courtier instead. The Champion retains military command but loses the political face of the clan.

**Stage 3 — Shugenja withdrawal.** After 3 unilateral actions, the Council withdraws all Isawa shugenja support from the Phoenix military. The Phoenix Go-hatamoto operates without shugenja integration — devastating for a clan whose military relies entirely on shugenja coordination. Reserve Companies (shugenja units) are recalled to Isawa temples. The Phoenix military becomes the weakest conventional force in the Empire.

**Stage 4 — Declaration of unfitness.** After 4 unilateral actions, the Council declares the Champion unfit and demands they step down. If the Champion accepts → the Champion retires or takes a monastery vow. The Champion position becomes vacant immediately. However, reincarnation only triggers on death — the soul of Shiba remains in the living retired Champion. No new Champion can emerge until the retired one dies. This creates an indefinite vacancy during which the Council governs alone with no military commander, no diplomatic representative, and no check on their authority. The Council may find this arrangement suits them — or they may quietly arrange the retired Champion's death to trigger reincarnation into a hopefully more compliant vessel. If the Champion refuses → Phoenix Schism (55.10.3.7).

**Stage persistence:** Stages unwind one at a time when the Champion complies with Council votes and submits major decisions through proper channels. One season of full compliance reduces the stage by one.

### 55.10.3.6 Council Overreach Path

The Council may abuse its authority — a corrupt, arrogant, or power-hungry Council may try to strip the Champion's remaining role entirely. The overreach path triggers when the Council acts against the Champion's legitimate functions.

**Overreach triggers (any of the following):**
- The Council vetoes PROVISIONAL 3 consecutive Champion proposals that directly address an active Tier 2+ crisis threatening Phoenix territory. One veto is the Council exercising judgment. Three consecutive vetoes of crisis-response proposals constitutes overreach — the Council is endangering the clan through inaction.
- The Council issues direct military orders to Shiba commanders, bypassing the Champion's military authority entirely. The Champion is the military commander — the Council authorizes deployment, they do not command troops.
- The Council unilaterally strips the Champion of diplomatic representation without prior defiance from the Champion (no Stage 2 trigger from 55.10.3.5). This is a power grab, not a response to misconduct.
- The Council refuses to authorize ANY Champion proposal for PROVISIONAL 3 consecutive seasons regardless of content. Total obstruction.

**Champion response escalation:**

**Stage 1 — Formal protest.** The Champion formally protests the Council's overreach. A Tier 4 topic propagates: "Phoenix Champion protests Elemental Council overreach." No mechanical consequence yet — this is a political signal.

**Stage 2 — Appeal to the Emperor.** After 2 overreach triggers, the Champion may appeal to the Emperor for mediation. The Emperor's response depends on archetype (Section 55.10.1). The appeal generates a Tier 3 topic and may result in an Imperial Edict — but the Emperor may also decline to intervene in internal Phoenix affairs.

**Stage 3 — Champion declaration.** After 3 overreach triggers (or if the Emperor declines to intervene), the Champion declares the Council has violated the compact. The Champion asserts full governing authority and refuses to submit further decisions to Council vote. This is the mirror of the Council declaring the Champion unfit.

**Stage 4 — Schism trigger.** If the Council does not restore the Champion's authority within 1 season of the declaration → Phoenix Schism (55.10.3.7).

### 55.10.3.7 Phoenix Schism Crisis

**Crisis Type:** Phoenix Schism — Tier 2 (internal clan crisis, visible to all clans)

**Trigger:** Either the Champion refuses Stage 4 removal by the Council (Champion Defiance Path), OR the Council refuses to restore Champion authority after Stage 3 Champion declaration (Council Overreach Path). The schism fires the generalized Intra-Clan Civil War system (Section 53.2) with the following Phoenix-specific modifications.

**Topic Generated:** Either "Phoenix Elemental Council has declared the Shiba Champion unfit. The Champion refuses to step down." OR "Phoenix Champion has declared the Elemental Council in violation of the compact. The Council refuses to yield." Tier 2, propagates empire-wide.

#### Ambiguous Legitimacy

Unlike the Dragon Schism, where Togashi is clearly the lawful authority, the Phoenix Schism has no clear legitimate side. Both factions have valid claims:

- Champion faction: "I am the reincarnation of Shiba. The Kami's soul chose me. My authority is divine."
- Council faction: "Shiba himself swore the oath. The compact grants us governing authority. The Champion serves us, not the other way around."

This ambiguity modifies the loyalty evaluation from 53.2.2: the "Legitimacy of the original grievance" factor (15% weight) is replaced by **"Which side do I believe is right?"** — scored by the NPC's personality:

- Chugi-dominant NPCs favor the Council (the compact is duty, the oath must be honored)
- Meiyo-dominant NPCs favor the Champion (the divine mandate outweighs mortal compacts)
- Gi-dominant NPCs evaluate which side acted more honestly in the lead-up
- Seigyo-dominant NPCs favor whichever side they can personally benefit from

This factor is genuinely contested — reasonable NPCs disagree, and the split is less predictable than in other civil wars.

#### Honor Hemorrhage Override

The generalized civil war system (53.2.6) applies −0.3 Honor/season to the Rebel Faction leader. The Phoenix Schism modifies this based on which path triggered the schism:

**Champion Defiance Path triggered:** The Champion is clearly the rebel — they bypassed lawful Council authority. The standard −0.3 Honor/season hemorrhage applies to the Champion.

**Council Overreach Path triggered:** Neither side is clearly in the wrong. The Champion defended their legitimate role against Council abuse. No automatic Honor hemorrhage applies to either side. Honor loss during the schism comes only from specific actions — killing Masters (−0.5 per Master), using the grand ritual against Phoenix territory (−2.0 per Master), assaulting sacred sites, or other direct acts. The state of rebellion itself carries no ongoing penalty.

#### Phoenix-Specific Faction Rules

**Champion Faction:** The Shiba family core — the Champion's own family. Military forces, bushi, yojimbo. Shiba provincial lords evaluate per 53.2.2 with the modified legitimacy factor above.

**Council Faction:** The Isawa — all five Masters (or however many survive) and all Isawa shugenja, provincial lords, and resources. The Council commands the most powerful concentration of shugenja in the Empire. Individual Isawa NPCs do not evaluate loyalty — the Council's authority over the Isawa is institutional and absolute, mirroring Togashi's authority over the Togashi Order.

**Asako — The Swing Family:** The Asako Family Daimyo evaluates independently using the standard 53.2.2 table with the modified legitimacy factor. The Asako have ties to both sides — scholars who value the compact's tradition (Council-favoring) but also the Inquisitors who respect military authority and practical action (Champion-favoring). The Asako's choice often decides the balance of power.

#### Military Balance

The Phoenix Schism produces a lopsided military split:

**Champion Faction strength:** The Phoenix Go-hatamoto's conventional bushi forces. Without shugenja support, this is the weakest conventional army in the Empire. The Champion's advantage is military organization and command structure.

**Council Faction strength:** All Isawa shugenja — the most powerful magical force in the Empire. The Council's weakness is that shugenja are not soldiers. They have no organized military command structure, no bushi, no tactical formations. But they have the grand ritual deterrent: a unanimous Council (if all surviving Masters agree) can unleash catastrophic elemental effects on a province.

The asymmetry means neither side can easily crush the other. The Champion has soldiers but no magic. The Council has magic but no soldiers. A prolonged conflict favors the Council (magic is more versatile than swords), but a quick decisive strike by the Champion before the Council organizes could capture key Masters and end the war.

#### Assassinating or Capturing Council Masters

Individual Masters can be killed or captured during the schism. Each lost Master reduces the Council's voting power AND magical capability. If 3 or more Masters are killed or captured, the Council faction collapses — they no longer have a majority and their magical deterrent is critically weakened.

Consequences of killing Masters:
- Each Master killed generates PROVISIONAL −0.5 Honor for the Champion (killing the spiritual leaders of the clan)
- PROVISIONAL −10 disposition from all shugenja NPCs empire-wide (every shugenja school is horrified)
- A Master killed by the Champion faction cannot be replaced during the schism — the position stays vacant
- The grand ritual requires unanimity among surviving Masters. Killing even one Master eliminates the grand ritual option permanently until a replacement is appointed post-resolution

#### Grand Ritual Threat

If all surviving Council Masters are present and unanimous, they can threaten or execute the grand ritual — catastrophic elemental destruction targeting a province. This is the Council's ultimate deterrent.

Using the grand ritual against Phoenix territory (targeting the Champion faction's provinces) is an act of desperation that devastates the Council's legitimacy: PROVISIONAL −2.0 Honor for every surviving Master, PROVISIONAL −20 disposition from all clans empire-wide, automatic Tier 1 crisis escalation, and the targeted province is devastated (all Stability to 0, major infrastructure destroyed, massive civilian casualties).

The Council can also threaten without executing — a bluff that may force the Champion to negotiate. The Champion must evaluate whether the Council is willing to destroy their own clan's territory. Earth Master personality makes execution unlikely. Fire Master personality makes it plausible.

#### Phoenix Schism Victory Conditions

**Council Victory (Champion removed):** The Champion capitulates, is killed, or has Honor drop below 0.0. Per Section 53.2.7 Legitimacy Victory — but with the reincarnation twist: if the Champion dies, the soul of Shiba reincarnates into a new vessel (55.10.3.8). The new Champion may be more compliant. The Council resumes normal governance with the new Champion. If the Champion capitulated alive, they retire or take a monastery vow — the soul does not transfer until death.

**Champion Victory (Council concedes):** Per Section 53.2.7 Rebel Victory — the Champion holds position for 6 consecutive seasons meeting all three conditions (Honor above 1.0, seat of power held, at least one allied Family Daimyo). The Council concedes governing authority.

Result: the Champion gains `phoenix_champion_authority: true` flag. The Council vote requirement is suspended. The Champion governs like a normal Clan Champion — full authority, no gates. The Council retains control of Isawa shugenja and resources but serves the Champion rather than directing him. This is a fundamental inversion of Phoenix tradition.

Unlike the Dragon's `dragon_autonomous_rule`, this flag does NOT automatically expire on Champion death — because Phoenix succession is through reincarnation, not appointment. The soul of Shiba carries the victory.

**Compact Restoration:** The flag can only be cleared through voluntary action by the Champion. RESTORE_COUNCIL_COMPACT is a 1 AP action available to any Champion holding the `phoenix_champion_authority: true` flag. The Champion reinstates the Council vote requirement and submits to the traditional power structure. No Council can force this — only the Champion can give back the authority the soul won.

**Reincarnation with active flag:** When a Champion holding the flag dies and the soul reincarnates, the new Champion inherits the flag. During their first season, the new Champion evaluates whether to restore the compact based on personality: Chugi-dominant Champions (Duty score above PROVISIONAL 60) voluntarily restore the compact within their first season — the oath weighs on them. Ishi-dominant or Seigyo-dominant Champions keep autonomous authority — they see no reason to surrender power. All other Champions evaluate based on their disposition toward the current Council Masters. This means the Council cannot force restoration, but reincarnation may produce a Champion who restores it willingly.

**Championship Seizure — DOES NOT APPLY:** Per Shiba reincarnation rules (55.10.3.8), the Phoenix championship cannot be seized by an outside rebel. Killing the Champion triggers reincarnation. An outsider claiming the title holds a hollow position — the real Champion (the reincarnated soul) exists somewhere else and the Council recognizes only the reincarnated vessel. This exception is absolute and cannot be overridden by War Score.

#### Shiba Reincarnation During Schism

If the Champion dies during an active Phoenix Schism, the soul of Shiba reincarnates into a randomly selected living Shiba character per 55.10.3.8. The new Champion's personality and disposition determine what happens next:

- If the new Champion's personality favors the Council (high Chugi, low Ishi) → the new Champion likely capitulates. Schism resolves as Council Victory.
- If the new Champion's personality favors independence (high Ishi, low Chugi) → the new Champion may continue the defiance. The schism continues with a new Champion at the helm.
- The new Champion evaluates independently — they inherit the position but not the previous Champion's grudges. Their fresh personality creates genuine uncertainty about the schism's outcome.

This makes assassination of the Phoenix Champion a gamble for both sides. The Council cannot engineer a compliant replacement. The Champion faction cannot guarantee continuity. Reincarnation is random and the result is unpredictable.

### 55.10.3.8 Shiba Reincarnation Mechanic

When the Shiba Clan Champion dies — by any cause — the soul of the Kami Shiba reincarnates.

**Selection:** A living Shiba character is selected randomly from all Shiba NPCs currently alive and not in captivity. No weighting by rank, age, school, or position. A Rank 1 ashigaru-born Shiba is as likely as a Rank 5 general. The selection is truly random — this is a divine act, not a political one.

**Transfer:** The selected character immediately becomes the Shiba Clan Champion. Their existing stats, personality virtues, skills, Insight Rank, Honor, Glory, disposition values, and relationships all carry over unchanged. Only the title and position transfer. The character's `lord_id` updates to reflect the Champion position. Their previous position (if any) becomes vacant and triggers succession (Section 22.5).

**Recognition:** The Elemental Council (specifically the Void Master) confirms the reincarnation. In normal circumstances, this confirmation is immediate and uncontested — the Void Master senses the soul's transfer. During a schism, confirmation may be politically charged: a Council at war with the Champion faction might delay recognition or dispute the reincarnation's validity, generating a Tier 3 topic.

**Emperor confirmation bypass:** Section 22.5 states that Clan Champion succession is confirmed by the Emperor. Shiba reincarnation is an exception — the succession is supernatural, not political. The Emperor acknowledges the new Champion but does not gate the appointment. The soul chooses; the Emperor accepts. If the Emperor refuses to acknowledge the reincarnation (possible for a Tyrant or Cunning Emperor seeking leverage), the Champion still holds the position — Imperial non-recognition creates diplomatic complications but does not invalidate a divine act.

**Mechanical consequence:** The new Champion may be completely unsuited for the role — a young courtier with no military skill suddenly commanding the Phoenix armies, or a provincial administrator thrust onto the Imperial stage. This capability gap is intentional and mirrors the design philosophy — the Phoenix pay a cost for their supernatural succession. The new Champion must grow into the role.

**Player interaction:** If the randomly selected character is a player's Shiba PC, the player becomes the Phoenix Clan Champion. No warning, no preparation. This is one of the most dramatic possible events for a Phoenix player.

### 55.10.3.9 Council Master Vacancy and Succession

When an Elemental Master dies or is removed, the vacant seat must be filled. The remaining Masters select the replacement from eligible Isawa shugenja — the candidate must have the appropriate elemental affinity (a Fire shugenja for the Fire seat, etc.). Selection requires majority vote among surviving Masters.

If fewer than 3 Masters survive, they cannot achieve majority vote. In this case, the Shiba Champion gains temporary appointment authority for Council seats — the Champion selects replacements until the Council can self-govern again. This is a significant power shift and creates an incentive for the Champion faction during a schism to eliminate Masters — reducing the Council below quorum hands appointment power to the Champion.

**Council extinction:** If all five Masters are killed, the Elemental Council ceases to exist. The Champion holds sole governing authority over the entire Phoenix Clan — including all Isawa resources, shugenja, and territories. The Champion must reconstitute the Council from scratch by appointing five new Masters. All appointees are personally selected by the Champion, creating a puppet Council loyal to the Champion who built it. This is the ultimate Champion victory path during a schism: don't win through the 6-season endurance condition, win by eliminating the Council entirely. The consequences are severe — PROVISIONAL −2.5 cumulative Honor for the Champion (−0.5 per Master killed), PROVISIONAL −50 cumulative disposition from all shugenja NPCs empire-wide, and the Phoenix Clan's magical capability is devastated for a generation (new Masters start at lower Insight Ranks than those they replaced). But the Champion holds absolute power over a broken clan. Whether that power is worth the cost is the question.
