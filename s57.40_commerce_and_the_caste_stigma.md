## 57.40 Commerce and the Caste Stigma

### 57.40.1 Design Intent

The Commerce skill (Section 24.3) is mechanically essential to the persistent world — tax calculation, tribute assessment, Rice market posting, Kolat laundering, provincial economic assessment, and many other systems draw on Commerce as a primary or secondary skill. Yet within Rokugani caste culture, commerce itself is beneath the dignity of the samurai class. The buke are meant to govern and fight, not count coins. A samurai overseeing their clan’s financial affairs does necessary work, but doing it visibly — haggling in a marketplace, negotiating prices in open court, conducting trade where other samurai can see — carries a social cost. This section implements that cost. A character who uses the Commerce skill in a public context takes a rank-scaled Honor penalty and a flat Glory penalty, reflecting the caste stigma attached to mercantile work. The Ide Trader school (Section 29) is the single formal exception: the Ide family has negotiated cultural immunity for their Trader specialists, and the Ide Trader technique waives both penalties. All other mercantile schools — Yasuki, Yoritomo, Ide Emissary, Ide Caravan Master, Mantis Brawler, Ichiro, Kasuga, Tsi Smith, Yotsu, Daidoji Trading Council, Yasuki Extortionist, Yoritomo Scoundrel — operate under the stigma despite their professional necessity to use the skill.

The design goal is to produce the correct social texture. A Crab bushi can oversee his province’s tax collection without shame (private, administrative). The same bushi haggling over rice prices at a public market takes a visible hit to his reputation (public, mercantile). The stigma is not punishment; it is the cost of breaking caste expectations in public, and higher-Honor characters feel it more acutely than lower-Honor characters because they have more reputation to protect. A character who has already lost most of their Honor through other failures has less to lose when they fall further, and the stigma therefore scales down with low Honor. The system produces accurate social dynamics without forcing the player into specific choices — it makes visible commerce expensive but not forbidden.

### 57.40.2 Public vs Private Commerce Rolls

The stigma fires only on public Commerce rolls. Determination of public vs private is deterministic, based on the action being fired and the character’s current context.

**Always public (stigma fires):** PURCHASE_MARKET in any context — the act of buying resources at a market is inherently visible. CONDUCT_COMMERCE in AT_COURT or VISITING contexts — mercantile activity witnessed by other samurai. Haggling rolls during court scenes where a Commerce roll is requested (GM-triggered or system-triggered), regardless of the wrapping action.

**Always private (stigma does not fire):** SET_TAX_RATE and SET_STIPEND_RATE — lordly administration conducted behind closed doors. CONDUCT_COMMERCE in AT_OWN_HOLDINGS context — a lord managing their own estate’s books is normal caste behaviour. All Kolat Category 16 operations using Commerce (BRIBE_GARRISON_COMMANDER, APPROACH_FOR_RECRUITMENT for Coin operatives, LAUNDER_KOKU, UNDERREPORT_KOKU) — these are covert by design and carry their own Honor costs through the Kolat action framework; they do not additionally trigger the public-Commerce stigma.

**Commerce as secondary skill:** Actions where Commerce is the secondary skill rather than the primary do NOT trigger the stigma. EVALUATE_WAR_READINESS (Battle primary, Commerce secondary), ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS (Battle primary, Commerce secondary), and MAINTAIN_SIEGE (Battle primary, Commerce secondary) treat Commerce as supporting calculation, not mercantile activity. The character is using their numerical skill, not conducting trade. The stigma attaches to mercantile behaviour, not to arithmetic competence.

**General rule:** if Commerce is the primary skill of the roll AND the context is AT_COURT or VISITING (or a public market scene), the stigma fires. Otherwise it does not. Implementers adding new Commerce-using actions should classify each as public or private following this rule and document the classification in the action’s mechanics.

### 57.40.3 Honor Stigma Penalty

The Honor penalty for a public Commerce roll scales with the character’s current Honor Rank at the moment of the roll, using the canonical Honor Rank tiers from Section 4.6. The penalty applies once per IC day per character — the first public Commerce roll of an IC day triggers it; subsequent rolls the same day are free. This reflects the reality that a samurai’s reputation for mercantile activity is a state established by the first visible act of the day, not incrementally worsened by each additional haggling session.

**Honor penalty by rank:** Honor Rank 7+ (7.0 or higher) — −0.3 Honor. Honor Rank 5–6 (5.0 through 6.9) — −0.2 Honor. Honor Rank 3–4 (3.0 through 4.9) — −0.1 Honor. Honor Rank 2 (2.0 through 2.9) — no penalty. Honor Rank 1 (1.0 through 1.9) — no penalty. A character with Honor below 3.0 is already considered morally loose by their peers; one more commerce roll does not materially change that perception, and the stigma produces no additional reputation damage.

Calibration note: these values fit naturally into the existing Section 4.6 Honor event table. The −0.3 Honor cost at Honor Rank 7+ matches the scale of “Out-of-mandate proxy commitment, lord accepts” (−0.3 for the lord) — a meaningful but recoverable cost for a paragon who acts outside caste expectations. The −0.1 cost at Honor Rank 3–4 matches “Offering a Favor and honoring it” in reverse — the same magnitude that single virtuous acts carry, applied here as a single stigma cost. The scaling is intentional: the higher the character’s standing, the more weight visible commerce carries against them.

### 57.40.4 Glory Stigma Penalty

In addition to the Honor penalty, a public Commerce roll costs −0.1 Glory. The Glory penalty is uniform regardless of the character’s current Glory or Honor rank, and like the Honor penalty, fires only once per IC day per character on the first public Commerce roll. Subsequent public Commerce rolls the same day do not incur additional Glory loss.

Rationale for uniformity: Glory reflects public reputation as perceived by others. The perception of a samurai dealing in commerce is the same regardless of how famous or obscure they already are. A daimyo seen haggling and a minor retainer seen haggling both produce the same category of gossip — “a samurai was conducting commerce at market.” The social judgement does not scale with current Glory, so the penalty does not either. What differs between the daimyo and the retainer is how widely the gossip propagates (the topic system handles that via topic relevance and character Status), not how severe the judgement is.

### 57.40.5 Ide Trader Technique Override

The Ide Trader school (Section 29, Unicorn Clan alternate path) grants the Brisk Economy technique: “Cannot lose Honor or Glory for using the Commerce Skill in public.” For Ide Trader characters, the stigma penalties of Sections 57.40.3 and 57.40.4 do not fire. Both the Honor table and the −0.1 Glory penalty are waived entirely, regardless of context or rank. The Ide Trader remains subject to all other consequences of their actions (topic generation per Section 57.40.6 still fires; the trade effects of CONDUCT_COMMERCE or PURCHASE_MARKET still apply normally); only the stigma costs are removed.

This override is unique to the Ide Trader path. Other mercantile schools do not receive the same protection. A Yasuki Courtier with Commerce 5 takes the full stigma on public rolls; so does a Yoritomo Courtier, an Ide Emissary, an Ide Caravan Master (despite being nominally on the same Unicorn economic path), a Mantis Brawler, an Ichiro Bushi, a Kasuga Smuggler, a Tsi Smith, a Yotsu Bushi, a Daidoji Trading Council member, a Yasuki Extortionist, and a Yoritomo Scoundrel. The Ide family’s cultural immunity was negotiated specifically for the Trader role through the Imperial court as part of the Unicorn’s long reconciliation with Rokugani norms after their return from the Burning Sands; no other mercantile school has successfully obtained equivalent formal acceptance.

Implementation: the character sheet flag school_paths contains the character’s school history. If “Ide Trader” is present in school_paths, the stigma-calculation function returns zero for both Honor and Glory penalties and does not fire the per-IC-day flag. The Brisk Economy technique has other effects (the Courtier (Manipulation)/Awareness persuasion bonus with Commerce rank multiplier for recruiting mercantile partners) which remain handled by the school technique system in Section 29 and are outside this section’s scope.

### 57.40.6 Topic Side-Effect

A public Commerce roll generates a Tier 4 Economic topic in the local zone_event_log. The topic reads “[Character name] was seen conducting trade at [zone name]” or equivalent framing based on the specific action. The topic is low-relevance by default, subject to standard decay and propagation rules per Section 16.5. It feeds the daily conversation system (Section 12.6) and provides narrative texture for witnesses who heard about the event through the normal topic network.

The topic fires even for Ide Trader characters — the Trader’s technique waives stigma penalties but does not render their commerce invisible. Other characters still see an Ide Trader doing mercantile work; they simply do not judge it as stigma-worthy. The witnessed behaviour is the same; the social interpretation is different. This preserves the topic network’s role as a truthful record of public events regardless of how those events are judged.

If the same character fires public Commerce rolls repeatedly across many IC days — consistent enough that the behaviour becomes defining — the accumulated topic trail builds an informal reputation as a “merchant patron” in local gossip. This reputation is narrative only, emergent from the topic system; it is not a character sheet field and has no direct mechanical effect. Lore: the informal merchant-patron designation is a Rokugani social category that exists in the fiction; characters who earn it through sustained public commerce are recognized as having crossed a social boundary, regardless of whether they took Honor penalties each time (an Ide Trader can become a merchant patron through consistent visible activity even without stigma loss).

### 57.40.7 NPC Decision Engine Personality Filter

The personality filter (Annex C) adjusts NPC willingness to fire public Commerce actions based on school and virtue alignment. Mercantile schools lean toward public Commerce because it is their professional identity; high-caste ritual schools lean against it because the stigma conflicts with their social role.

**School leans (applied to public Commerce actions):** Mercantile schools whose professional identity is commerce receive a +5 lean: Yasuki Courtier, Yoritomo Courtier, Ide Emissary, Ide Caravan Master, Kasuga Smuggler, Tsi Smith, Yasuki Extortionist, Daidoji Trading Council, Yoritomo Scoundrel. These are the schools whose samurai are understood by their peers to conduct commerce as part of their role, and whose training explicitly prepares them for mercantile work. The Ide Trader receives a +10 lean — no stigma to offset, so they fire public Commerce more readily than any other school. Several schools have Commerce as a starting skill without mercantile professional identity — Yoritomo Bushi (shipboard fighters who encounter commerce but lead with combat), Mantis Brawler (enforcers of mercantile interests, not merchants themselves), Ichiro Bushi (Badger strength-focused bushi from a minor clan needing to run their own affairs), and Yotsu Bushi (urban protector-guardians of Otosan Uchi); these schools receive no adjustment (neutral). High-caste ritual schools receive a −10 lean: Doji Courtier, Kakita Artisan, Otomo Courtier, Seppun Miharu, all Phoenix shugenja schools (Isawa, Asako, and Agasha-as-shugenja), Kitsu Sodan Senzo. Miya Herald receives a moderate −5 lean — Imperial family but more practical than purely ritual schools, as the heralds travel widely and administer charitable aid (Miya’s Blessing). All other schools (Crab bushi, most Dragon schools, most Scorpion schools, Unicorn cavalry schools, Crane bushi schools without Commerce, the Brotherhood of Shinsei) are neutral with no adjustment.

**Virtue leans:** Bushido Makoto (Sincerity) applies a −3 lean on public Commerce — Makoto samurai feel the stigma more acutely as a conflict with their sincere self-image. Bushido Rei (Courtesy) applies a −2 lean — commerce is somewhat discourteous in public. Shourido Chishiki (Intellect) applies a +3 lean — the pragmatic calculation of Chishiki sees the skill as a tool to be used. Shourido Seigyo (Control) applies a +2 lean — Seigyo values resource control and commerce supports that goal. Other virtues are neutral. These are cumulative with school leans.

**Honor-based self-regulation:** the engine applies an additional avoid lean on public Commerce for NPCs at Honor Rank 5+ — these characters value their reputation and weigh the stigma cost more heavily. Honor 5–6 characters receive −3 lean, Honor 7+ characters receive −5 lean. This is cumulative with school and virtue leans. The result: a high-Honor Doji Courtier with Makoto virtue is strongly averse to public Commerce (combined lean of −10 school + −3 Makoto + −5 Honor = −18), while a low-Honor Yasuki Courtier with Chishiki virtue actively seeks it (+5 school + +3 Chishiki + 0 Honor = +8).

### 57.40.8 Rank 5 Mastery — Deferred Design Note

The Commerce skill’s Rank 5 Mastery Ability per Section 24.3 (“may increase or decrease the price of an item he is buying or selling by a maximum of 20%”) is NOT currently implemented. This is a deliberate deferral, not an oversight. The existing Rice Market system (Section 4.3.18) and Iron Market system (Section 4.3.10) use seller-posted prices with seasonal drift, not per-transaction haggling. Implementing the Rank 5 ability cleanly requires either adding a per-transaction haggling layer to PURCHASE_MARKET (non-trivial scope involving buyer-seller contested rolls and new market state), or restricting the mastery to seller-side price posting only (a partial implementation that does not match the RAW “buying or selling” scope).

Until a haggling layer exists, Commerce 5+ characters experience the skill’s mechanical value through other channels: larger dice pools on all Commerce-using rolls (SET_TAX_RATE, CONDUCT_COMMERCE, ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS as secondary, EVALUATE_WAR_READINESS as secondary, Kolat laundering rolls), school path prerequisites (Yasuki Extortionist Commerce 4, Daidoji Trading Council Commerce 3, Ide Caravan Master Commerce 2, Ide Trader Commerce 3, Yoritomo Scoundrel Commerce 2), and dispositional signaling (Commerce rank visible to NPCs evaluating a character’s mercantile expertise). The skill remains mechanically useful at all ranks even without the Rank 5 mastery implemented; the mastery is pure upside deferred to a future market-layer implementation pass.

When implementation happens: the haggling layer should preserve the existing Rice Market seasonal-drift pricing as the baseline, and add per-transaction adjustment of up to 20% via Commerce-rank-gated roll. Disposition between buyer and seller should factor into the adjustment (a friendly disposition giving buyer more negotiating room; an enemy disposition allowing the seller to refuse adjustment entirely). The Rank 5 ability should produce ordinary market behaviour tempered by character skill, not a guaranteed discount.

### 57.40.9 Appraisal Emphasis — Deferred Design Note

The Commerce (Appraisal) emphasis per Section 24.3 (“determine the approximate value of an object in any given market”) is NOT currently implemented as a mechanical ActionID. This is deferred because the underlying item-quality system it would operate on does not yet exist. The GDD currently tracks resources (Rice, Iron, Koku) and specific named items with explicit values (weapons per Section 11.8, certain artifacts), but does not model generalized item quality tiers with appraisable value variance. Implementing Appraisal as an APPRAISE_ITEM action would produce a roll without a well-defined output.

Current interim behaviour: a character with Commerce (Appraisal) can describe item value in fiction. They can narratively assess the worth of a sword, a painting, a tea set — and other characters recognize their expertise — but there is no mechanical output beyond the narrative flavour. The emphasis contributes to the Commerce dice pool on any Commerce roll where appraisal would be relevant, but does not itself fire discrete rolls. When item-quality infrastructure is designed, APPRAISE_ITEM can be added as an ActionID with roll mechanics tied to the item-quality tier system. Until then, Appraisal remains a flavour specialization.
