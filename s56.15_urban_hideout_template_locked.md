## 56.15 Urban Hideout Template --- LOCKED

The Urban Hideout template generates a hidden underground space beneath a functioning settlement. The eighth map template and the only one that operates inside civilization rather than outside it. Two-zone structure: a surface level (the settlement) and a hidden level (the lair beneath). The unique gameplay element is that finding the entrance is the first challenge. Primarily used for Maho Cult cells in towns and cities (Section 56.14.2 specifies the Bloodspeaker Cult prefers urban locations). Also applicable to urban gang suppression (Section 11.4.2), Kolat operations (Section 54.7), and any quest seed that hides inside a populated settlement.

**Not Part of Terrain Probability Pools:** Unlike the seven wilderness templates (Sections 56.3 through 56.12), the Urban Hideout is not selected through the terrain probability pool in Section 56.2.1. It is selected when the quest seed is settlement-based --- specifically when a Maho Cult cell established in a town or city becomes Active and the suppression mission fires. The generator checks whether the cell is in an urban settlement. If yes, Urban Hideout is used. If no, the standard terrain pool applies.

### 56.15.1 Size Categories --- LOCKED

- **Single Basement:** One building with a hidden room or cellar underneath. Tight. The cult meets in one space. One or two rooms below. Can house up to Strength 3.

- **Connected Basements:** Tunnels connecting the cellars of multiple buildings. Several rooms spread under a block. Can house up to Strength 6.

- **Catacombs:** An extensive underground network beneath the settlement. Old burial tunnels, a forgotten foundation layer, or deliberately excavated passages. Can house up to Strength 9.

### 56.15.2 Entry and Flow --- LOCKED

Two distinct phases: the search phase on the surface, then navigation of the hidden level below.

**The Search Phase:** The player arrives in a settlement district knowing a cult operates here --- intelligence from investigation, PTL anomalies, Kansen sightings. They do not know which building hides the entrance. The player interacts with the surface level: talking to locals, observing who enters which buildings at odd hours, detecting spiritual contamination with shugenja abilities, following suspicious figures. This is investigation gameplay, not combat. The search phase ends when the player locates the hidden entrance.

**Finding the Entrance:** Multiple paths. A civilian tips them off. A shugenja detects concentrated Kansen activity emanating from a specific building. The player follows a cultist from a distance and watches them enter. They detect a PTL spike at a particular location. Or brute force --- searching building by building, which is slow, loud, and alerts the cult. The better the investigation, the less warning the cult gets.

**Entry Points:** Once found, the entrance is a transition from surface to hidden level. A trapdoor in a shop’s back room. A concealed passage behind a temple altar. A cellar stairway in an unremarkable house. Narrow, one person at a time. Usually only one entrance --- the cult wants controlled access. Catacombs may have a second entrance the cult does not know about --- an old collapsed tunnel discovered through exploration.

**Hidden Level Flow:** Depends on size. Single Basement is barely a dungeon --- one room, maybe a side alcove. Connected Basements play like a small Cave --- corridors between rooms, branching possible, but all at the same depth. Catacombs play like a full Cave or Ravine --- extensive, branching, potentially multiple levels, dead ends.

**The Ceiling is the Surface:** Unlike a Cave, the space above the hidden level is occupied --- buildings, streets, people. No fire allowed. Smoke rises into buildings above. Loud combat is heard on the street. The player and the cult both have reasons to keep quiet.

**Escape Upward:** The cult can flee through the entrance into the surface level. A cultist who reaches the street blends into the crowd instantly --- they look like everyone else. Pursuit becomes an urban chase, not a dungeon fight. The leader’s escape plan is always: reach the surface, disappear. Controlling the entrance is critical.

⚙️ CROSS-REF (Settlement Building Framework): The surface level described in this search phase is the settlement Navigation Zone ASCII map defined in Section 4.4.1. The search behaviours described above --- talking to locals, observing who enters which buildings, detecting spiritual contamination, following suspicious figures --- use the Observe Building, Follow NPC, and Question Local mechanics defined in the Settlement Building Framework and Criminal Network Presence Module (Section 11.11), supplemented by shugenja-specific detection (Sense spell, Kansen detection per Section 56.6.4). When the Urban Hideout surface search phase and the Criminal Network Presence Module are both active in the same settlement, both operate on the same street map --- the player may encounter both criminal activity and cult indicators during the same visit.

### 56.15.3 Population Placement --- LOCKED

Split between surface and hidden level. Almost all enemies are below.

**Surface Level:**

- **Lookout:** One or two cultists posing as normal civilians near the entrance building. A shopkeeper, a beggar, a laborer. They watch for investigators. If they spot the player asking questions or searching buildings, they slip down to warn the cult. They do not fight on the surface --- that would reveal the operation. They blend and report.

- **Sympathizer:** Not a combatant. A building owner, a merchant, someone who knows the cult is there and either supports them or is too afraid to talk. They misdirect, deny knowledge, waste the player’s time during the search phase. If pressed hard enough --- Intimidation, evidence --- some crack.

**Hidden Level:**

- **Door Guard:** One or two at the entrance from above. They hear someone opening the trapdoor and either fight or alert below. If the player enters quietly, the Door Guard is the first combat encounter. Cult Initiate or Maho Cultist.

- **Zombie Screen:** In Connected Basements and Catacombs. Zombies standing in corridors between the entrance and the ritual space. Passive defense --- always present, never asleep, never distracted. They stand in the dark and wait.

- **Cultist Groups:** Maho Cultists in the rooms. Smaller groups than bandit Camp Groups because cells are small. Two or three per room at most. Casting from behind zombie screens.

- **Leader:** In the ritual space. The deepest, most central room. Behind maximum zombie and cultist layers.

**No Patrols:** The hidden level is too small and too secret for patrol routes. Everyone stays in position.

**No Sentries Below:** The Lookouts on the surface are the sentries. The Door Guard is the threshold. Below that, static defense.

**Density Pattern:** Immediate. Small spaces. The player descends and is in the thick of it within one or two rooms. No long approach, no gradual escalation. The search phase is the approach. Once below, the encounter is compressed and intense.

**Alert Behavior:** Sound below is muffled by the surface but within the hidden level, the spaces are small enough that any combat alerts the entire lair almost instantly. The cult knows the player is there the moment the first fight starts. They prepare: the leader begins emergency procedures, cultists take defensive positions behind zombies, and someone may attempt to flee upward to the surface.

### 56.15.4 Environmental Features --- LOCKED

- **Low Ceilings:** Basements and tunnels are not built for comfort. Close ceilings restrict movement. Spear and polearm users are at a disadvantage --- not enough room to swing. Short weapons and unarmed fighting gain relative advantage.

- **Constructed Walls:** Stone or packed earth. More uniform than cave walls. Doorframes, support pillars, load-bearing beams. Easier to read than natural cave layouts because the space follows architectural logic. The cult may have modified the layout --- bricked up a doorway, dug a new connecting passage, collapsed a section deliberately.

- **Darkness:** No natural light. Occupied rooms have light per the Cave lighting rule. The ritual space may use unusual light --- candles in specific patterns, colored oil lamps, or no light at all if the cultists have adapted through Taint mutation.

- **Ritual Contamination Gradient:** The closer to the ritual space, the worse the Taint presence. Outer rooms near the entrance are relatively clean --- faint unease. Middle rooms have noticeable Kansen presence --- whispers at the edge of hearing, cold spots, objects that feel wrong. The ritual space itself is heavily contaminated --- Taint check on entry (Stamina + Willpower vs TN 20), visible spiritual corruption, possible physical manifestation of Jigoku’s influence. A shugenja can sense this gradient and use it as a compass pointing toward the ritual space.

- **Evidence:** Unique to this template. The cult lives and operates here. They leave traces. Bloodstained tools in a side room. Scrolls with maho notation. A list of names. Personal belongings of victims. This is a crime scene as well as a dungeon. Anything found feeds back into the World Map as intelligence. Letters connect to other cells. Names connect to Sleepers.

- **Stored Goods Above:** The surface buildings contain normal inventory. If the player searches the surface building before descending, they might find hidden compartments, concealed wealth funding the cult, or evidence linking the building owner to the operation.

- **No Fire:** Critical restriction. Fire in a basement fills the space with smoke and rises into buildings above, potentially burning down a city block. Civilian casualties, infrastructure destruction, massive political consequence. Fire is effectively forbidden unless the player accepts catastrophic collateral damage.

- **Noise Restriction:** Extended loud fighting eventually draws attention from above --- civilians, doshin, magistrates. If the player takes too long, surface authorities arrive. Good if the player has authority. Bad if they do not.

- **Escape Routes:** The entrance is the primary escape for both sides. In Catacombs, old collapsed tunnels may provide secondary exits --- emerging in a different building or outside the settlement. The cult may have prepared an escape route the player does not know about.

### 56.15.5 Objective Placement --- Urban Hideout Template --- LOCKED

- **Locate the Entrance:** First objective, unique to this template. No other template requires finding the dungeon before entering it. Achieved through investigation, shugenja detection, following suspects, civilian information, or brute force. The method matters --- quiet investigation gives no warning, loud searching alerts the cult.

- **Suppress the Cell:** Standard objective once below. Kill or capture cultists. Reduce Strength.

- **Kill or Capture the Leader:** High-value target in the ritual space. Capturing alive is more valuable --- interrogation yields intelligence about the Bloodspeaker network, other cells, Sleepers. But a Bloodspeaker Adept may cast maho at point-blank range or suicide with a final blood spell rather than be taken.

- **Destroy the Ritual Space:** Purify or demolish the ritual space so the location cannot be reused for maho. Requires shugenja purification or physical destruction of ritual implements, blood circles, and contaminated materials. Without destruction, a new cell could reoccupy the same space.

- **Recover Evidence:** The intelligence objective. Scrolls, letters, names, financial records, ritual notes. Every document feeds back into the World Map. Evidence connects to other cells, reveals Sleepers, exposes sympathizers. A thorough post-combat search is as valuable as the combat itself.

- **Prevent Escape:** Time-sensitive. Activates once combat begins. The leader and surviving cultists try to reach the surface and disappear. The player must control the entrance or move fast. A cultist who escapes becomes a Sleeper carrying cult knowledge. The player has to decide: push deep fast, or secure the exit first.

**Secondary Objectives:** Identifying surface sympathizers. The merchant who rented the building. The doshin who looked the other way. Evidence found below connects to people above. This generates court topics, magistrate actions, and disposition consequences on the World Map.

**Placement Rule:** The ritual space is always the deepest room. Evidence is scattered throughout --- some in the ritual space, some in side rooms, some in the surface building above. The entrance is the critical chokepoint for preventing escape.

### 56.15.6 Resolution and Consequence --- Urban Hideout Template --- LOCKED

- **Full Success:** Cell suppressed, leader killed or captured, ritual space destroyed, entrance secured. Evidence recovered feeds into the World Map --- connected cells exposed, Sleepers identified, sympathizers named. The surface building is flagged as a former cult site. The lord gains Glory. Shugenja purification prevents reuse. The most valuable outcome of any quest seed because of the intelligence yield.

- **Partial Success:** Player fought below but did not complete all objectives. Leader escaped means a Sleeper is loose. Ritual space intact means the location can be reoccupied. Evidence not recovered means connected cells remain hidden. Each incomplete objective has specific consequences.

- **Retreat:** Cult knows they have been found. They abandon the lair within hours --- every surviving member scatters to the surface and disappears. The lair is empty when anyone returns. Evidence may have been destroyed. Cult members become individual Sleepers spread across the province. Worse than never finding them because now they are scattered and paranoid instead of concentrated.

- **Failure:** Player could not find the entrance, or was killed below. If the search failed, the cult goes dark --- Concealment resets to maximum, they become Dormant, the insurgency vanishes from the World Map. It reactivates later. If the player was killed, the cult has a samurai’s body and equipment.

**Template-Specific Consequences:**

- **Intelligence Cascade:** The primary reward. Evidence from an Urban Hideout can expose other cells, Sleepers, sympathizers, and network connections. A single successful mission can trigger follow-up missions across multiple provinces. Highest strategic value quest in the system.

- **Civilian Exposure:** The settlement knows maho was practiced beneath their feet. Fear, spiritual anxiety. Temporary Stability hit from the revelation. A Tier 4 topic generates: maho discovered in the settlement.

- **Surface Sympathizer Consequences:** Identified sympathizers face the legal system --- magistrate investigation, trial, punishment per Section 2.8. Political and legal content generated by the quest, playing out through court and law systems.

- **Purification Requirement:** The ritual space remains spiritually contaminated even after the cell is destroyed. If not purified, PTL continues ticking from residual corruption. A new cell could reoccupy the same space and skip setup --- the ritual infrastructure already exists.

- **No Relocation:** A discovered urban lair is abandoned, not relocated. Cult members scatter as individuals. They may reform elsewhere through Sleeper and propagation mechanics but the specific lair is done.
