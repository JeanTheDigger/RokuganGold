## 54.7f The Kolat — Dual Stance and Topic Awareness

Every NPC in the engine holds a set of topic positions in known_positions — their stance on active topics, scored from −100 (maximum opposition) to +100 (maximum support). These stances feed into Phase 5 scoring through the topic_position_modifier component, shaping every decision the NPC makes in every context. A single stance per topic per NPC is sufficient for most characters. For conscious Kolat agents it is not.

A Silk field agent whose primary persona is a traveling merchant holds a public stance on the topic “Jade Magistrates investigating unusual merchant routes”: mild concern, perhaps −15. Their true stance as a Kolat agent is existential threat: −100, respond immediately. The engine currently has no way to hold both, and would score the agent’s actions from the mild concern position — producing behavior that is far too calm for what the agent actually knows.

### The kolat_positions Field

**Definition.** A second topic position dictionary on the character sheet, structured identically to known_positions: topic_id → position value (−100 to +100). Present only on NPCs with kolat_sect set (conscious agents). Null for all other characters. Hidden in the same way as kolat_sect and the sleeper fields — not visible through normal social interaction, accessible to the engine and to investigators with Lore: Kolat 4+ who succeed on an Investigation roll vs TN 35. Cleanup: when a topic’s momentum reaches zero and it is removed from all characters’ active topic lists (Section 16), any kolat_positions entry for that topic_id is also cleared simultaneously, following the same trigger as known_positions cleanup. Stale Kolat stances on closed topics do not persist.

**Phase 5 integration.** When the engine scores actions for a conscious Kolat agent and a topic_position_modifier lookup fires, the scorer checks kolat_positions first. If the active topic has an entry there, that value is used instead of the known_positions entry. If no kolat_positions entry exists for that topic, the engine falls back to known_positions as normal. This is a lookup substitution — one line of change in scorer.gd. The rest of Phase 5 is unchanged.

**Personality still governs.** The kolat_positions entry shifts the topic_position_modifier component of the score. It does not override the other six components. A Gi-virtue agent whose kolat_positions entry on a threat topic is −100 will score covert actions very highly from that component — but the personality filter still blocks GOSSIP, EAVESDROP, and FABRICATE_SECRET. Their response to the threat will be urgent but constrained to actions their virtue permits: WRITE_LETTER, PROBE, LOCATE_CHARACTER, SUBMIT_KOLAT_REPORT. The urgency is real. The limits are real. Both emerge from the system without special cases.

### How kolat_positions Gets Populated

Three sources feed the field through different channels. All three write to the same dictionary.

**Master assignment and Tiger directives.** When a Master assigns a Kolat objective through the delivery channel (Section 54.7d), the assignment package can include kolat_positions updates for topics relevant to that objective. Tiger assessing a threat and routing a Lotus contract also pushes stance updates to affected agents through the Silk network: the investigator topic acquires a kolat_positions entry of −100 for every agent flagged as operationally exposed. This is how an agent can suddenly start treating a previously neutral figure as a maximum-urgency threat without having met them personally.

**DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE.** Master Silk’s intelligence distribution pushes stance data alongside operational intelligence. An agent receiving a DISTRIBUTE_INTELLIGENCE update on a new topic also receives the Kolat’s assessed position on that topic. Their kolat_positions entry is written at the same time as the intelligence entry in their knowledge pool. The two travel together through the same physical Silk channel.

**Direct observation.** A conscious agent who personally witnesses something Kolat-relevant and has enough context to recognise it generates a kolat_positions entry themselves — exactly as any NPC generates a known_positions entry through observation (Section 55.12). A Silk agent who sees an Emerald Magistrate copying down the dead drop location they just serviced does not need Tiger to tell them this is a threat. Their kolat_sect flag and their direct observation of the event combine to generate a kolat_positions entry at maximum urgency for that investigator topic. This is emergent. It requires no special trigger.

### Scope and Limits

**Not every topic has a kolat_positions entry.** Most topics are irrelevant to the Kolat and the field stays empty for them. The entry only exists when the agent has received information, a directive, or made an observation specifically in their Kolat capacity. A Coin agent has kolat_positions entries on tax audit topics, financial investigation topics, and reserve exposure topics. They likely have no kolat_positions entry on a war between two distant clans unless Tiger has specifically flagged it as operationally relevant.

**The field does not know everything the Kolat knows.** An agent’s kolat_positions is populated by what has reached them through their specific position in the network. Master Silk has exhaustive kolat_positions entries. A courier three layers removed from the Master has sparse ones. Intelligence travels through physical channels that take time and can be intercepted. An agent in a remote province may be operating on kolat_positions entries that are seasons out of date while the Temple’s assessment has moved on entirely. This is consistent with the information asymmetry design principle throughout the engine (Section 20).

**Unconscious assets have no kolat_positions.** A bribed contact or unwitting informant has no Kolat awareness and therefore no kolat_positions field. They respond to topics from their normal known_positions only. The Kolat manages them through other means — the conscious agents who interact with them, the standing objectives that keep those agents in the right places, the primary objectives their handlers assign through the feudal chain without their knowledge.
