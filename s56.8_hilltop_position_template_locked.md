## 56.8 Hilltop Position Template --- LOCKED

The Hilltop Position template generates an elevated defensive position: a hill or bluff with a camp on top. Two-zone structure like Forest Approach + Camp but vertical instead of horizontal --- the slope is the challenge zone, the hilltop camp is the objective zone. The elevation itself is the defense. Applicable to Ronin Bandit Uprising and potentially military forward positions. Primary terrain: Heavy Hills and Mountains.

### 56.8.1 Size Categories --- LOCKED

Same Strength floor rule as other templates.

- **Low Rise:** A modest hill with a flat top. Room for a few shelters and a campfire. Can house up to Strength 3.

- **Steep Hill:** A proper hill with meaningful elevation. Flattened top with space for multiple structures, good sightlines in all directions. Can house up to Strength 6.

- **Ridge/Bluff:** An extended elevated position or a steep-sided bluff. Large flat area on top, possibly with rocky outcrops for additional cover. Can house up to Strength 9.

### 56.8.2 Entry and Flow --- LOCKED

The player starts at the base of the hill. They can see the camp above them --- or at least the smoke and silhouettes. The slope is the challenge, the hilltop camp is the objective space.

**Entry Points:** The hill can be approached from any side, but the slopes are not uniform. The generator assigns each face a different grade: one or two gentler slopes that are easier to climb but more exposed, and steeper faces that are harder to climb but offer more cover from rocks and terrain folds.

**The Path:** Like the Forest trail --- most hilltop camps have a worn route the bandits use to go up and down. Easiest ascent but watched. Leaving it means scrambling up uneven ground: slower, risk of loose rocks creating noise, but less predictable.

**Slope Zone Flow:** Open and exposed like Stockade approach, but with terrain working against the player. Uphill movement is slower. Defenders above have ranged advantage --- they can see further and shoot downhill. Scattered rocks and outcrops on the slope provide intermittent cover the player moves between.

**Hilltop Camp Flow:** Once the player crests the hill, it is an open camp like Forest’s clearing. Flat ground, tents, firepits. The elevation advantage disappears --- it is even ground. But getting there is the hard part.

**No Depth Rule:** Like Stockade --- the leader is at the center of the camp on top, behind the most fighters. No deep room, just distance from the edge.

### 56.8.3 Population Placement --- LOCKED

Split between slope and hilltop, heavily weighted to the top.

**Slope Zone:**

- **Lookout:** One or two posted partway up the slope at vantage points. Sitting on a rock, leaning against an outcrop. They watch downhill. Better vision than any other sentry type because elevation gives them range. If they spot the player, they shout uphill --- the alarm carries fast.

- **Path Guard:** One or two on the worn path, closer to the top. They control the easy route up. Not at the bottom --- positioned where the path narrows or turns so they have the advantage if someone comes up the obvious way.

**Hilltop Camp Zone:**

- **Camp Group:** Clustered around firepits and shelters on the flat top. Most of the roster. Relaxed because the hill itself is their security.

- **Leader Group:** Center of the hilltop camp. Same 20-25% concentration. Best fighters.

- **Edge Defenders:** Two or three fighters positioned near the hilltop edge, not in the camp interior. Rapid response --- if alarm sounds, they move to the crest and fight downhill against anyone climbing up. They hold the lip of the hill as a natural chokepoint.

**No Patrols:** Same logic as Stockade. Nobody patrols downhill when you can see everything from up top.

**The Crest as Chokepoint:** The moment of cresting the hill is the most dangerous point. The player is exhausted from climbing, Edge Defenders are waiting, and the Camp Groups are alerted and moving to support. Breaking over the top is the key tactical challenge of this template.

### 56.8.4 Environmental Features --- LOCKED

The terrain itself is the primary feature.

- **Slope Grade:** Varies around the hill. The generator assigns each face a grade: gentle, moderate, or steep. Gentle slopes are faster to climb but more exposed --- less cover, defenders see the player the whole way. Steep slopes are slower and physically demanding but offer more dead angles where defenders above cannot see.

- **Rocks and Outcrops:** Scattered on the slopes. Hard cover the player can shelter behind during the ascent. Larger outcrops break line of sight from above. Smaller rocks provide partial cover only. The generator distributes these unevenly --- some slopes are bare, others have decent cover chains the player can move between.

- **Loose Ground:** Scree, gravel, dry earth on steeper sections. Moving through it is loud --- rocks sliding downhill alert anyone listening. Also a physical hazard --- a failed movement could mean sliding back down, losing progress, generating noise. Ties into the alert system.

- **The Crest:** The lip where slope meets flat top. A natural defensive line. Defenders standing at the crest have full sightlines downhill while the climber is exposed. But the crest also limits defender vision of the far slope --- they can only watch one side at a time. Approaching from the far side of the hill means the crest blocks the defenders’ view during most of the ascent.

- **Wind:** Hilltops are exposed. Wind affects ranged attacks at the top --- arrows in wind are less accurate. Also carries sound --- the player might hear camp noise from the base, giving information about activity level. But wind also carries the player’s noise away from defenders if approaching from downwind.

- **Firepits and Shelters:** Same as Forest camp. On the flat top. Light sources visible from below at night. Smoke visible during the day. The camp announces itself from a distance.

- **No Trees:** Hilltops are exposed by nature. If there were trees, it would be a Forest template instead. The hilltop is open sky. No canopy, no concealment on top.

- **Time of Day:** Same significance as Stockade. Night reduces the Lookouts’ vision advantage dramatically. A night ascent through rocks with no light from above is dangerous but far more survivable than a daylight climb under observation.

### 56.8.5 Objective Placement --- Hilltop Position Template --- LOCKED

Same types, shaped by the elevation dynamic.

- **Kill the Leader:** Leader is at the center of the hilltop camp. The player must crest the hill to reach them. Escape is possible --- the leader can run down the opposite slope. But running downhill is visible from a long distance and the leader is abandoning their defensive advantage. Unlike Forest where the leader disappears into trees, a fleeing leader on a hillside is exposed. Pursuit is viable if the player has the stamina after the climb.

- **Recover Stolen Goods:** Stored on the hilltop near shelters. Getting them down the hill is the complication. The player has to carry goods downhill across open slope. Heavy loads on steep terrain are slow. If the player has not cleared the defenders, extracting goods under fire while descending is brutal.

- **Burn the Camp:** Works here. Same as Forest --- bandit construction, not civilian infrastructure. No PU cost. Fire on an exposed hilltop is visible for miles --- neighboring settlements and patrols will see the smoke. Could attract attention both helpful and unwanted. Wind on the hilltop makes fire unpredictable --- it spreads faster and in shifting directions.

- **Rescue Hostages:** Held on the hilltop. Same extraction problem as Recover Stolen Goods --- getting hostages safely down a slope under pressure. Hostages cannot move fast on rough terrain. The descent is the hard part, not the rescue itself.

**Secondary Objectives:** Found on the hilltop. Unique possibility: the Lookout positions on the slope might carry messages or orders that reveal intelligence about the wider insurgency. Killing a Lookout quietly and searching them before ascending could yield useful information.

**Placement Rule:** Everything is on the hilltop. The slope has nothing except enemies and terrain. The player climbs to reach every objective.

### 56.8.6 Resolution and Consequence --- Hilltop Position Template --- LOCKED

Same spectrum, shaped by the elevation dynamic.

- **Full Success:** Primary objective completed. Strength reduction same as other templates by objective type. The hilltop position is abandoned. Unlike Stockade, the hill itself cannot be destroyed --- it is terrain. The bandits could theoretically reoccupy it, but a cleared hilltop with dead defenders is a known position. Reoccupying it is risky --- the attackers know the slopes, the cover points, the easy routes up.

- **Partial Success:** Player crested the hill and fought but did not complete the primary objective. Strength reduced by kills. The hilltop is harder to partially succeed at than other templates because the crest acts as a commitment point --- once over the top, the player is generally in a fight to the finish. Partial success usually means the leader fled downhill during the chaos.

- **Retreat:** Player could not crest the hill or got pushed back over the edge. Strength reduced only by kills on the slope, which may be few. The defenders’ advantage held. Next mission is not necessarily harder --- the hill does not change, the defenders just stay alert. But the player now knows the slope layout, which cover points work, and which faces are weakest.

- **Failure:** No Strength reduction. The insurgency remains at current Strength. See Section 56.10 for seed-specific failure consequences.

**Template-Specific Consequences:**

- **Slope Knowledge Persists:** Like Forest knowledge --- the player remembers which routes worked on the ascent. Second attempt is better informed.

- **Visible Outcome:** A burning hilltop camp or a battle on an exposed hill is visible from surrounding areas. Could generate a Tier 4 topic about the engagement. Nearby lords know something happened whether the mission succeeded or failed.

- **No Civilian Impact:** Wilderness position. No PU loss, no infrastructure damage.

- **Downhill Pursuit:** If the leader fled, pursuit is a separate encounter --- a chase across open ground downhill. Fast, chaotic, exposed. Could be resolved as a simple contested roll or a brief ASCII map chase sequence. Detailed pursuit mechanics deferred.
