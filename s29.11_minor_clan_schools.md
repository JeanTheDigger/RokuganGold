## 29.11 Minor Clan Schools

Schools of the independent Minor Clans. Fox, Centipede, and Wasp schools are listed under Section 29.10 (Mantis Minor Clan Era). The Falcon/Toritaka school is listed under Section 29.1 (Crab). The Tiger/Yotsu school is included here as an appendix clan. All schools marked [CR] are from the Core Rulebook; [SE] from Secrets of the Empire; [IH] from Imperial Histories; [EE] from Enemies of the Empire.

**BASIC SCHOOLS:**

**Ichiro Bushi [CR] — Badger Clan:**

The founder of the Ichiro Bushi School was a Hida. It is no surprise, then, that the School remains similar to its Crab roots. The school’s philosophy is simple: hit the enemy so hard he has no time for fancy tactics. The Ichiro Bushi uses strength as the answer to almost any problem. Others may move faster or strike with more precision, but the Ichiro answers with overwhelming damage.

- Benefit: +1 Strength

- Skills: Athletics, Commerce, Defense, Heavy Weapons, Jiujutsu, Kenjutsu (Katana), any High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 3.5

- Outfit: Light or Heavy Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any Heavy Weapon or Polearm, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — Transcend the Mountain: You may re-roll any Skill or Trait roll using Strength, keeping the second result. You suffer no movement or combat penalties for Moderate terrain, and only half the normal penalty for Difficult terrain.

- Rank 2 — Strength of the Badger: A number of times per session equal to your Strength Rank, you may re-roll a damage roll, keeping the better result.

- Rank 3 — Crushing Blow: You may ignore the effects of armor on an opponent’s Armor TN. Your unarmed attacks may ignore 1 Rank of your target’s Reduction.

- Rank 4 — Crashing Stones: Your attacks with Samurai weapons or unarmed strikes are Simple Actions.

- Rank 5 — Return the Strike: At the start of a skirmish, before Initiative is rolled, you may lower your Initiative total by 20 for the duration. In exchange, add your Strength Rank in unkept dice to both attack and damage rolls for the first two Rounds.

**Komori Shugenja [CR] — Bat Clan:**

When Yoritomo Komori was granted the honor of forming his own Minor Clan, he gathered the brightest shugenja to his side. He decided to honor his bat spirit heritage of swift communication and bring that resource to the Empire. The Komori Shugenja have tapped into the magic of the koumori bat spirits — benevolent creatures from Chikushudo whose powerful magic has never been fully understood — giving them strange and wondrous abilities that have never been seen before.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Calligraphy (Cipher), Lore: Ancestors, Lore: Theology, Meditation, Spellcraft, Tea Ceremony, any one High Skill

- Honor: 4.5

- Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Affinity / Deficiency: Air / Earth

- Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Air, 2 Water, 1 Fire

- Technique: The Kami’s Whispers — The Komori practice the rituals of the koumori spirits and ferry messages via the kami. Expend one spell slot to send a whispered message to any individual known to you. The message may be no longer than thirty seconds per School Rank in length, and may be delivered a maximum distance of School Rank x 100 miles.

**Heichi Bushi [CR] — Boar Clan (Historical):**

Shadowy dangers always threatened the Boar lands, and the Heichi Bushi School formed to face them. The Boar developed techniques to endure the worst their enemies could deliver and keep fighting. The school emphasizes the Mai Chong — a uniquely shaped spear with a curved head, rarely seen elsewhere — which the Heichi Bushi uses to devastate enemies at range while maintaining a strong defense, waiting for the perfect moment to break the enemy’s momentum. Historical school — survives among ronin in scattered form after the clan’s destruction c. 500.

- Benefit: +1 Strength

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Hunting, Intimidation, Kenjutsu, Spears (Mai Chong), any one Bugei Skill

- Honor: 3.0

- Outfit: Light or Heavy Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any Spear, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — The Charge of the Boar: If you are in Full Attack stance, you may ready a medium weapon or spear as a Free Action. When wielding a spear, you gain +0k1 to damage rolls.

- Rank 2 — The Strength of Opposition: You may call two Raises to Entangle an opponent with a Mai Chong, or three Raises to Entangle with another spear-class weapon.

- Rank 3 — The Speed of the Boar: You may attack as a Simple Action when wielding a spear.

- Rank 4 — The Anger of the Boar: Spend a Void Point during the Reactions segment to reduce your Wound TN penalty by one Wound Rank for the remainder of the skirmish.

- Rank 5 — Beyond the Mountains: If you are in Full Defense Stance, you may make one normal attack with a spear or Samurai-keyword weapon as a Free Action, ignoring the restriction of Full Defense. This attack gains +2k0 to the attack roll.

**Tonbo Shugenja [CR] — Dragonfly Clan:**

The Tonbo are a deeply spiritual people. As intermediaries between the Dragon Clan and the rest of Rokugan, they are acutely aware of the political responsibilities that rest on their shoulders. The Tonbo Shugenja School uses all the resources at its disposal to prepare its students for the courts — a trained Tonbo can use his connection to the spiritual world to heighten his social graces, a necessity when opponents such as the Lion are always watching for a social faux pas.

- Benefit: +1 Perception

- Skills: Calligraphy, Courtier, Etiquette, Meditation (Void Recovery), Sincerity, Spellcraft, any one High Skill

- Honor: 4.5

- Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, Knife, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Affinity / Deficiency: Water / Fire

- Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Water, 2 Air, 1 Earth

- Technique: Guided by Fate — The kami’s whispers can be powerful tools in court. Expend one spell slot to gain +1k0 on one Social Skill Roll. You may never spend more spell slots than your School Rank to augment a single roll. You gain a Free Raise on any spell with the Divination keyword.

**Usagi Bushi [CR] — Hare Clan:**

The Hare Clan has seen more combat than most Minor Clans — their centuries of war against the Bloodspeaker Cult have perfected the Usagi Bushi School in blood. It takes the athleticism of the Hare and channels it into combat advantage: the Hare learn to move faster than the average samurai, darting in to deliver a punishing blow then dashing out of range, attacking from what seems like all directions at once.

- Benefit: +1 Reflexes

- Skills: Athletics (Running), Defense, Hunting, Jiujutsu, Kenjutsu, Lore: Maho (may replace with Lore: Kolat after Clan War era), any one High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 4.0

- Outfit: Ashigaru or Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, Bow (with 20 Arrows) or Knife, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — Speed of the Hare: In any situation where you have room to maneuver (GM’s discretion), add your Athletics Skill Rank to your Armor TN except when in Full Attack or Center stance. Your Water Ring is considered 1 higher for Move actions.

- Rank 2 — Leap of the Hare: In Full Attack stance, you may leap forward and attack an opponent up to 15’ away without taking a Move Action. All attacks this turn must target that opponent.

- Rank 3 — Swift as Lightning: Your attacks with unarmed strikes, knives, or Samurai-keyword weapons are Simple Actions.

- Rank 4 — Kick of the Hare: You may now perform your Rank 2 Technique while in the Attack stance instead of Full Attack. If performed in Full Attack, your second attack may target another opponent within 15’ of the first.

- Rank 5 — Reichin’s Style: You may perform the Feint Maneuver for only 1 Raise, and your damage bonus from a successful Feint is not limited to your Insight Rank x 5.

**Toku Bushi [CR] — Monkey Clan:**

The Monkey Clan has the blessing of the Imperial line and access to skilled experts in the field of war. Hundreds of ronin teachers have stepped through the dojo doors, and their diverse skills were distilled into a cohesive fighting style containing the best aspects of each lesson. The Toku Bushi School teaches techniques to fight against multiple enemies, keeping calm in the face of overwhelming odds and fighting on without hesitation.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower

- Skills: Athletics, Defense, Hunting, Investigation (Search), Kenjutsu, Lore: History, any one High or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 5.5

- Outfit: Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 Weapon, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — Toku’s Lesson: Add +1k0 to all Skill Rolls with a TN of 25 or higher (including attack rolls). All Wound Penalties are reduced by an amount equal to your Willpower plus double your School Rank.

- Rank 2 — The Strength of One Man: While facing multiple opponents or an enemy with a higher Insight Rank, add +1k1 to attack and damage rolls.

- Rank 3 — Courage Above All: You may make melee attacks as a Simple Action instead of a Complex Action.

- Rank 4 — Forge Your Own Fate: Spend a Void Point when suffering Wounds to force your opponent to drop his two highest damage dice, even if this reduces kept dice (minimum 1k1).

- Rank 5 — Fortune Favors the Mortal Man: Once per Round, spend a Void Point to reroll any roll, adding +2k1 to the new roll. You may keep either result.

**Fuzake Shugenja [SE] — Monkey Clan:**

The Fuzake originated as a small order of ronin shugenja within Toturi’s Army, led by Yasuki Garou (later Fuzake Garou), a close friend to Toku. When the Monkey Clan was formed he immediately swore fealty to it. The Fuzake dedicate themselves to healing and to an intense but rather eccentric veneration of the Fortunes.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Calligraphy, Defense, Etiquette, Lore: Theology, Medicine, Spellcraft, any one High or Merchant Skill

- Honor: 5.5

- Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, Knife, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 2 koku

- Affinity / Deficiency: Earth / Fire

- Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Earth, 2 Water (one automatically Path to Inner Peace), 1 Air

- Technique: The Sideways Path — Fuzake Garou was originally trained by the Kuni but spent his life protecting and healing those around him. When casting a spell that heals Wounds or cures poison or illness (such as Path to Inner Peace or Peace of the Kami), you may expend one or more Earth spell slots in addition; each such slot awards a Free Raise on your Spell Casting Roll.

**Tsi Smith [Artisan] [CR] — Oriole Clan:**

Tsi was the name of an ancient gaijin blacksmith who impressed Emperor Hantei Genji with his works. Centuries later his legacy was reclaimed by the ronin who would found the Oriole Clan. Artisans trained in the Tsi Smith School learn everything to increase their skills crafting weapons and armor — they learn martial styles to understand how experts will treat the weapons they create, and they have little use for social pleasantries.

- Benefit: +1 Intelligence

- Skills: Any one Artisan Skill, Commerce, two ranks in any one Craft Skill, Defense, any one Weapon Skill, any one High/Bugei/Merchant Skill

- Honor: 4.5

- Outfit: Sensible Clothing, Wakizashi, Calligraphy Set, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — Tools of the Fortunes: All Craft Skills are School Skills for you. Whenever you make a Craft Skill Roll against a TN of 10 + (School Rank x 5) or higher, gain 1 Glory for succeeding, plus 1 for every increment of 5 by which you exceed the TN.

- Rank 2 — Tsi Xing Guo’s Blessing: When wielding or using one of your own creations, you gain +2k1 for Void Points spent on relevant Skill Rolls instead of the usual +1k1. When an item you created is given as a gift among samurai, both you and the gift-giver gain Glory equal to your School Rank.

- Rank 3 — A Crafter’s Dedication: When crafting, halve the price for purposes of determining creation time. When someone in your presence uses an item you created, take a Complex Action to grant them +xk1 (where X is your School Rank) on the relevant Skill Roll.

- Rank 4 — Exhaustive Knowledge: Spend a Void Point as a Free Action to make all attacks with a weapon you created as Simple Actions for a number of Rounds equal to your School Rank.

- Rank 5 — Star-filled Steel: Once per day, upon completing a weapon, spend all your Void Points to create a Sacred Weapon (Tsi Blade, Tsi Tetsubo, etc.) with one of the following qualities: Balanced (+1k0 to attack rolls), Swift (+5 Initiative while wielding), or Unbreakable (weapon cannot be broken). At GM’s option, a truly gifted Tsi smith may also make a weapon Radiant (treat as jade for harming Invulnerable creatures).

**Morito Bushi [CR] — Ox Clan:**

The Ox Clan earned its stripes in combat against a spirit army filled with the wisdom of centuries. The Morito Bushi School teaches the samurai to maximize the benefit of mountain and plain combat. The bulk of the Ox Clan’s force is heavy cavalry intended to swiftly crush the core of an infantry army. The Morito most often uses a polearm, though he is trained to adapt to any situation.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Defense, Horsemanship, Hunting (Tracking), Kenjutsu, Lore: Kolat, Lore: Underworld, any one Skill

- Honor: 3.5

- Outfit: Light or Heavy Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, Heavy Weapon or Polearm, Horse (Rokugani Pony), Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — Legacy of the Four Winds: Add +1k0 to your Initiative if Mounted at the start of combat. Add +1k0 to attack rolls while mounted.

- Rank 2 — The Wind Blows Many Ways: At the start of combat, declare a number of Bugei Skills equal to your School Rank. Add +1k0 to all rolls involving the chosen Skills.

- Rank 3 — Thunder and Fury: You may make attacks as a Simple Action while Mounted.

- Rank 4 — The Blade Upon the Wind: Once per skirmish, spend a Void Point as a Free Action to set your Initiative Score equal to that of any other participant. Afterward your Initiative Score is reduced by 5 during each Reactions Stage until the encounter ends.

- Rank 5 — Fast and Furious: While attacking an opponent with lower Initiative, add +2k2 to all attack and damage rolls.

**Chuda Shugenja [CR] — Snake Clan (Historical):**

The Chuda Shugenja School was formed with techniques the Phoenix Inquisitors still use today. The founder Isawa Chuda was an Imperial Magistrate sworn to destroy maho-tsukai — tragically, his descendants became exactly what he fought. The original school was designed to do one thing: destroy the forces of the Shadowlands. Historical school — the Snake Clan was destroyed c. 750 and their records expunged from Imperial history. This school survives only as forbidden knowledge.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower

- Skills: Calligraphy (Cipher), Etiquette, Lore: Maho, Lore: Shadowlands, Spellcraft, any 2 Skills

- Honor: 4.5

- Outfit: Robes, Wakizashi, Knife, Scroll Satchel, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Affinity / Deficiency: Earth / Air

- Spells: Sense, Commune, Summon, 3 Earth, 2 Fire, 1 Water

- Technique: To Punish the Wicked — Isawa Chuda was a ruthless persecutor of the Shadowlands, and his techniques live on in those who bear his name. Spend a Void Point as a Free Action to negate an amount of Reduction equal to your School Rank from one targeted opponent. Add your School Rank to the total of any damage roll made by a spell you cast against a target with the Shadowlands Taint.

**Suzume Bushi [CR] — Sparrow Clan:**

The Sparrow Clan has never been a martial power, but Sparrow samurai will march into combat when the call comes. Their fighting style displays their philosophy on life — they take as much time as needed to make a perfect strike. The Suzume Bushi’s love of storytelling gives him insight into battle, letting him see the ebb and flow of combat and exploit his opponent’s weaknesses. The school has evolved far from its Crane roots, creating unique abilities that center on Initiative.

- Benefit: +1 Willpower

- Skills: Calligraphy, Kenjutsu (Katana), Lore: History, Lore: Theology, Perform: Storytelling, any one Lore Skill, any one Skill

- Honor: 6.5

- Outfit: Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 1 weapon, Traveling Pack, 3 koku

- Rank 1 — All Things in Time: At the beginning of the Combat Round, before the first character has taken his Turn, you may reduce your Initiative by 5 to add +1k0 to all attack and damage rolls for the Round.

- Rank 2 — Purity of Chi: Add +5 to your Armor TN against creatures and against human opponents with lower Honor.

- Rank 3 — Wisdom is the Greatest Weapon: Spend a Void Point to add your Honor Rank to any Perform or Lore Skill Roll.

- Rank 4 — Quiet Spirit, Steady Blade: You may make melee attacks as a Simple Action while in the Attack Stance.

- Rank 5 — Slow and Deadly: During the Round following your adoption of the Center Stance, add +10 to your attack and damage rolls. Does not apply during iaijutsu duels.

**Yotsu Bushi [IH] — Tiger Clan (Ronin Family / Otosan Uchi Guardians):**

The original Yotsu technique was based on the family’s self-appointed role as protectors of the common people in Otosan Uchi. The later evolution of a full bushi School came from fighting the Spider Clan in the city’s ruins — the Yotsu were forced to fight with stealth and ruthless aggression in an environment of ruined buildings. Although the resulting school is not one more rigidly honorable samurai would admire, it has kept Otosan Uchi’s people alive.

- Benefit: +1 Agility

- Skills: Commerce, Hunting, Kenjutsu, Kyujutsu, Lore: Gaijin or Lore: Shadowlands, Stealth (Sneaking), one Low or Bugei Skill

- Honor: 5.5

- Outfit: Ashigaru or Light Armor, Sturdy Clothing, Daisho, any 2 weapons, Traveling Pack, 4 koku

- Rank 1 — The Tiger’s Tread: You do not lose Honor for using Ambush or Shadowing against enemies of the Empire (GM’s discretion). In urban environments (including ruins), gain +1k0 to Stealth rolls and add your Stealth Skill Rank to your Armor TN.

- Rank 2 — The Tiger’s Pounce: On the first Round of a skirmish, add your Stealth Skill Rank to your Initiative roll total (ends at Reactions stage of Round 1).

- Rank 3 — Rending Claws: When fighting with a Samurai weapon you may make attacks as a Simple Action.

- Rank 4 — Shelter the Blameless: During any Round when you are actively defending or protecting others (GM’s discretion), gain one bonus Void Point for that Round. Unspent Void Points from this Technique are lost at end of Round.

- Rank 5 — The Tiger’s Fangs: On the first Round of a skirmish, you may declare Maneuvers for one less Raise.

**Kasuga Smuggler [Courtier] [CR] — Tortoise Clan:**

The Kasuga are as true to the Imperial cause as any other clan — though one often wouldn’t know this from watching their activities. They perform illegal acts with murderers and thieves, monitoring conspiracies in the Emperor’s name. The Kasuga Smuggler must mingle with the criminal riffraff of the Empire, and their abilities reflect that need: ruthless in all dealings, knowledgeable of the underworld, finding allies wherever they can.

- Benefit: +1 Awareness

- Skills: Commerce, Etiquette, Investigation (Notice), Lore: Underworld, Sincerity (Deceit), Stealth, any one High/Merchant/Low Skill

- Honor: 2.0

- Outfit: Extravagant Clothing, Wakizashi, any 1 weapon, Calligraphy Set, 5 koku

- Rank 1 — Way of the Tortoise: Learn an additional gaijin language free with every purchase of the Languages Advantage. No Honor loss for Low Skills or Glory loss for Commerce in public when acting on clan duty. +2k0 to all Social Skill Rolls when dealing with heimin or hinin.

- Rank 2 — The Shell of the Tortoise: Any clan or Imperial samurai who physically attacks or openly slanders you without provocation immediately loses a number of Honor points equal to twice your School Rank.

- Rank 3 — The Eyes of the Emperor: When acting on behalf of your clan, roll additional unkept dice equal to your School Rank on all Low Skills in which you have at least one Skill Rank (including School Skills used as Low Skills, such as Sincerity with Deceit Emphasis).

- Rank 4 — Hand in Hand: Once per session, if you have access to local heimin or hinin, roll Lore: Underworld / Awareness at TN 25 to learn one useful piece of information (GM’s choice) that cannot be learned any other way.

- Rank 5 — The Tortoise Smiles: When making a Skill Roll with any of your six School Skills, your maximum Raises are not limited by your Void. A number of times per session equal to your Void Ring, gain +5k0 on any School Skill roll (stacks with Rank 3).

**ADVANCED SCHOOLS:**

**Minor Clan Defender [Bushi] [EE]:**

The samurai of the Minor Clans begin at a significant disadvantage compared to Great Clan counterparts — fewer resources, fewer allies, vastly less prestige. There are a few who refuse to accept such a fate and commit themselves fully to championing the cause of their clans. These individuals occasionally come into contact and form strong alliances, passing down secrets through family lines and apprenticeship. These secrets are shared among all who share the same passion, regardless of Minor Clan affiliation.

- Requirements — Rings/Traits: Agility 5, Strength 4

- Requirements — Skills: Any 1 Weapon Skill at Rank 5

- Requirements — Other: Only members of Minor Clans may take ranks in this Advanced School; must possess one Paragon Advantage.

- Rank 1 — Know No Boundaries: Spend a Void Point to temporarily gain 2 Ranks in Status, forcing others to recognize your talents. This effect lasts 1 hour.

- Rank 2 — The Speed of Certainty: Once per Round, spend a Void Point to make one melee attack as a Free Action. You may make no other attacks this Round.

- Rank 3 — The Strength of Humility: Make melee attacks as a Simple Action with Samurai-keyword weapons. If you already have this ability with such weapons, choose any one other melee weapon type — you may also attack with those as Simple Actions.

**ALTERNATE PATHS:**

**Dragonfly Advisor [Courtier/Shugenja] [SE]:**

- Replaces: Tonbo Shugenja 3

- Requirements: Intelligence 3, Meditation 5

- Technique — Seeing the Pattern: The Dragonfly Advisors are trained to understand all the patterns of cause and effect around them, letting them perceive the potential consequences of their actions. Gain +2k0 to all Divination and Etiquette Skill Rolls.

**Ichiro Pass Warden [SE]:**

- Replaces: Ichiro Bushi 3

- Requirements: Strength 4, Spears 4, Athletics 4

- Technique — Hold the Passes: Your range with hand-hurled missiles is doubled, and you add +1k0 to damage rolls with thrown weapons; if the weapon is a nage-yari or a rock, the bonus is +1k1 instead.

**Minor Clan Alliance Diplomat [Courtier]:**

- Replaces: Any Minor Clan School 4

- Requirements: Character must belong to a Minor Clan

- Technique — The Courts of Kudo Mura: The diplomats of Kudo Mura are masters at dealing with the needs, desires, and whims of their superiors. When conversing with a samurai from one of the Great Clans or the Imperial Families, gain +3k0 on all Contested Rolls using Etiquette or Sincerity.

**Ox Clan Vigilant [SE]:**

- Replaces: Morito Bushi 4

- Requirements: Investigation 5

- Technique — Seek the Guilty: When investigating someone for possible lawbreaking or Kolat activity, make a Contested Roll of Investigation (Interrogation) / Perception against the target’s Sincerity (Deceit) / Awareness. If you succeed and the target is actually guilty (GM’s judgment), gain +1k1 to all Intimidation rolls and attack rolls against that target for the next 24 hours.

**Soldier of the Three Man Alliance [Bushi] [IH]:**

- Replaces: Tsuruchi Archer 2, Tsuruchi Bounty Hunter 2, or Suzume Bushi 2

- Requirements: Battle 2

- Technique — Stand Against Oppression: When facing an opponent with a higher Glory Rank than you, spend a Void Point to gain bonus unkept dice on your attack roll equal to the difference in your respective Glory Ranks. You may not gain more dice than your School Rank from this Technique.

**Suzume Storytellers [Courtier/Artisan] [SE]:**

- Replaces: Suzume Bushi 2

- Requirements: Perform: Storytelling 3, Precise Memory

- Technique — The Heart of the Story: The Suzume Storyteller can use a skillfully crafted story to make virtually any point. When making a Courtier Skill roll, spend a Void Point to add your Perform: Storytelling Skill Rank to the total.

**Tortoise Guard [Bushi] [IH]:**

- Replaces: Kasuga Smuggler 4

- Requirements: Strength 3, any 1 Weapon Skill 4

- Technique — The Path of One: From their gaijin ancestors and commoner allies, the Tortoise Guards have learned that samurai Honor can be a hindrance to victory; practicality is more useful. You may attack as a Simple Action with any non-Samurai melee weapon, and gain +1k0 to damage with any Peasant Weapon.

**Tortoise Killers [Bushi] [SE]:**

- Replaces: Kasuga Smuggler 3

- Requirements: Agility 3, Stealth 4, any two Weapon Skills at 4

- Technique — The Poisoned Frog: Once per opponent per skirmish, when making an attack roll spend a Void Point to cause your Raises for damage on that roll to award +1k1 rather than +1k0. You may never take more Raises than your Void Rank when using this Technique.

**Ujina Skirmishers [Bushi] — Hare Clan:**

- Replaces: Usagi Bushi 2

- Requirements: Knives (Tanto) 3

- Technique — Master of the Quick Blade: The Ujina are trained to fight with a tanto in each hand, cutting their foes to death with swift and deadly strikes. When fighting with a knife in each hand, gain +1k0 to Initiative rolls and +1k1 to damage rolls with knives.
