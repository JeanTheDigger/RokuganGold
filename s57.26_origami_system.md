## 57.26 Origami System

### 57.26.1 Overview

Origami is the most accessible, fastest, and most ephemeral of the noble Artisan sub-skills. Paper is cheap, tools are unnecessary &#x2014; only the artist&#x2019;s hands &#x2014; and a skilled folder can produce a finished work in minutes. The art form&#x2019;s mechanical identity in this system is as a support skill: origami output enhances other characters&#x2019; actions (DELIVER_GIFT through noshi wrapping, PERFORM_WORSHIP through shide and gohei) rather than creating standalone objects requiring lifecycle management. The one exception is the senbazuru &#x2014; a thousand-crane devotional project that tracks long-term progress.

The system has four mechanical layers. Noshi (formal gift wrapping) enhances DELIVER_GIFT rolls as a preparatory CRAFT action and creates a service relationship between wrapper and gift-giver. Shide (shrine streamers) are zone-placed objects that enhance PERFORM_WORSHIP at the shrine where they hang. Gohei (purification wands) are portable objects that enhance the wielder&#x2019;s PERFORM_WORSHIP wherever they are. Senbazuru (thousand cranes) is a long-term devotional project with dedication types, Raise-based progress, and completion effects scaled by quality.

Origami also qualifies as a _performance_skill (Section 24 amendment in Section 57.26.24), unlocking PERFORM_FOR and PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE &#x2014; the act of folding in front of an audience is a live performance experienced in the moment, identical in justification to Ikebana&#x2019;s existing qualification.

### 57.26.2 Noshi World Object

Each noshi wrapping attaches as a record to an existing inventory item. Fields: noshi_id, item_id (the wrapped item), wrapper_id (character who performed the wrapping), wrap_quality_tier (Normal through Legendary, determined by CRAFT roll), date_wrapped. The noshi_record persists on the item until consumed by DELIVER_GIFT or manually removed (0 AP). Only one noshi_record may exist per item &#x2014; rewrapping replaces the existing record.

### 57.26.3 Shide World Object

Each shide set is a zone-placed object at a shrine. Fields: shide_id, zone_id (the shrine zone), crafter_id, quality_tier (Normal through Legendary), date_placed, current_tier (degrades seasonally, starts equal to quality_tier), shide_placement_permission_holder_id (the character granted permission to place shide at this zone). One shide_object per shrine zone. Placing new shide replaces the existing set &#x2014; the old shide_object is destroyed and the new one takes its place.

### 57.26.4 Gohei World Object

Each gohei is a portable inventory item. Fields: gohei_id, crafter_id, quality_tier (Normal through Legendary), date_crafted, uses_remaining (set at creation based on quality tier), owner_id (current holder). One gohei may apply its bonus per wielder per PERFORM_WORSHIP roll &#x2014; a character carrying multiple gohei in inventory applies only the highest-quality one. When uses_remaining reaches 0, the gohei_object is destroyed.

### 57.26.5 Senbazuru World Object

Each senbazuru is a progress-tracked devotional project. Fields: senbazuru_id, folder_id, dedication_type (Healing, Protection, Remembrance, Atonement), recipient_id (null for Atonement), crane_count (0 to 1000), total_raises (cumulative Raises achieved across all successful sessions), successful_session_count (number of sessions where TN was met), declaration_date, is_complete (false until crane_count reaches 1000), completion_date (null until complete), presentation_date (null until presented). A character may only have one active senbazuru at a time. The one-at-a-time gate clears on presentation (presentation_date set), not on completion &#x2014; a completed but unpresented senbazuru is still an active commitment. The project is not done until the cranes are delivered to their intended purpose. After presentation, the senbazuru_record is retained permanently as a historical record but no longer blocks new declarations.

### 57.26.6 CRAFT (Noshi) &#x2014; Gift Presentation Wrapping

Standard CRAFT action. Artisan: Origami / Awareness vs TN 15. Cost: 1 AP. Context: any &#x2014; the wrapper needs only the item and their hands. The crafter targets a specific item in their own inventory or in the inventory of a co-located character who has granted consent (Section 57.26.7). Skill gate: Artisan: Origami rank 1 minimum.

On success: a noshi_record is attached to the item. Wrap_quality_tier is determined by Raises: 0 Raises = Normal, 1 Raise = Fine, 2 Raises = Exceptional, 3 Raises = Masterwork, 4+ Raises = Legendary.

On failure (below declared TN): the wrapping is Mundane &#x2014; visibly poor work. The wrapper may strip the Mundane wrapping (0 AP, noshi_record deleted, item returns to unwrapped state) or leave it. If a Mundane-wrapped item is delivered via DELIVER_GIFT, the gift-giving roll takes &#x2212;1k0 &#x2014; bad wrapping is worse than no wrapping because it signals pretension the artisan could not back up.

On DELIVER_GIFT: the noshi_record&#x2019;s wrap_quality_tier provides Free Raises on the gift-giving roll. Normal = +0, Fine = +1, Exceptional = +2, Masterwork = +3, Legendary = +4. These stack with item quality Free Raises (Section 49). The noshi_record is consumed &#x2014; wrapping is opened, gift received, presentation done.

A character may always choose not to declare noshi even if they have the skill. Without training, gifts are delivered in standard fashion &#x2014; no roll, no bonus, no risk. Origami knowledge is intrinsic to the skill &#x2014; TN 15 represents both technical competence and correct occasion-appropriate conventions. A character who succeeds folded the right style for the occasion. A character who fails got the technique, the social context, or both wrong.

### 57.26.7 Noshi &#x2014; Wrapping for Others

The wrapper must be co-located (AT_ZONE) with both the item and the character who holds it. The item owner grants consent &#x2014; a simple flag, same pattern as tattoo recipient consent (Section 57.25.3). The wrapper rolls their own Artisan: Origami / Awareness. The noshi_record records the wrapper&#x2019;s identity as wrapper_id.

The wrapper&#x2019;s identity is discoverable through provenance investigation on the noshi itself (Section 57.26.22). A character examining the gift&#x2019;s wrapping before or after opening may identify the wrapper&#x2019;s style. This is social information &#x2014; knowing who wrapped the gift reveals a relationship between the gift-giver and the artisan. A Doji courtier who always presents gifts in Kakita noshi is making a public statement about their clan&#x2019;s cultural infrastructure every time they give a gift.

### 57.26.8 Noshi &#x2014; Wrapper Disposition Bonus

When someone wraps a gift for another character, the gift-giver gains a temporary disposition modifier toward the wrapper. This fires when the CRAFT (noshi) action completes targeting another character&#x2019;s item, not when the gift is delivered. Self-wrapping exclusion: if the wrapper and the item owner are the same character (wrapper_id equals the item&#x2019;s owner_id), no wrapper disposition bonus fires. The bonus exists because someone did you a favour &#x2014; wrapping your own gift is preparation, not a service. A character who wraps their own item and delivers it receives the noshi Free Raises on the DELIVER_GIFT roll but no disposition feedback to themselves.

Bonus by wrap quality: Normal = +2, Fine = +4, Exceptional = +6, Masterwork = +8, Legendary = +10. Duration follows Section 12.2 Category 3 temporary modifier conventions: Normal = 30 IC days, Fine = 45 IC days, Exceptional = 60 IC days, Masterwork = 75 IC days, Legendary = 90 IC days. The bonus is full strength for the duration, then expires cleanly.

Event Ripple (Section 12.2b): the personal disposition change from the wrapper bonus ripples into family and clan baselines at the standard rates (personal &#x00D7; 0.20 = family-to-family, personal &#x00D7; 0.05 = clan-to-clan). An artisan who wraps gifts regularly for members of other families and clans generates cumulative collective disposition shifts over time.

Section 12.2 Category 3 table amendments required: (1) Add entry &#x201C;Recently had a gift wrapped by another character&#x201D; with quality-scaled values and durations above. (2) Add entry &#x201C;Recently received a senbazuru (Healing/Protection)&#x201D; &#x2014; Normal = +3, duration 30 IC days; Fine = +5, 45 IC days; Exceptional = +8, 60 IC days; Masterwork = +12, 75 IC days; Legendary = +15, 90 IC days. (3) Add entry &#x201C;Recently witnessed a senbazuru presentation (Remembrance)&#x201D; &#x2014; Normal = +2, duration 30 IC days; Fine = +4, 45 IC days; Exceptional = +6, 60 IC days; Masterwork = +8, 75 IC days; Legendary = +10, 90 IC days.

### 57.26.9 CRAFT (Shide) &#x2014; Shrine Streamers

Standard CRAFT action. Artisan: Origami / Awareness vs TN 15. Cost: 1 AP. Materials: paper (Common, negligible cost &#x2014; 5 zeni per shrine set). Produces a shide_object in the crafter&#x2019;s inventory. Quality tier determined by Raises: 0 Raises = Normal, 1 Raise = Fine, 2 Raises = Exceptional, 3 Raises = Masterwork, 4+ Raises = Legendary.

### 57.26.10 Shide &#x2014; Placement and Permission

Placement: 0 AP action while AT_ZONE at a shrine with shide_placement_permission. The shide_object transfers from inventory to the zone&#x2019;s shide slot. The zone&#x2019;s ASCII map updates to show shide on the shrine gate. If shide already exist at the zone, placement replaces them &#x2014; old shide_object destroyed, new one takes its place.

Permission: the shugenja assigned as custodian of the shrine grants shide_placement_permission to a specific character for that zone. This is a simple consent flag &#x2014; same lightweight pattern as tattoo consent. No formal commission pipeline, no obligation record, no delivery window. The origami practitioner asks, the shugenja agrees or declines.

NPC consent logic: a shugenja evaluates the request based on disposition toward the artisan and the artisan&#x2019;s perceived skill. Disposition Neutral or above with an artisan at Artisan: Origami rank 2+ = grants permission readily. Below Neutral or with a rank 1 artisan = may decline. A shugenja who has observed the artisan&#x2019;s previous work at another shrine is more willing. Permission persists until revoked. The shugenja may revoke at any time. Revocation is not hostile unless the existing shide are Fine or above, in which case a Tier 4 topic fires.

A shrine without a custodian shugenja has no one to grant permission. Shide cannot be placed at unstaffed shrines &#x2014; decorating an untended sacred space is spiritually presumptuous.

Custodian change: when the shrine&#x2019;s custodian shugenja dies, is reassigned, or is replaced, shide_placement_permission lapses after a 1 IC season grace period &#x2014; same pattern as garden permission lapse on daimyo change (Section 57.23.2). During the grace period, the artisan may still refresh shide normally. After the grace period, the permission is void and the artisan must seek permission from the new custodian. Existing shide_objects are not affected by custodian change &#x2014; they remain in place and continue providing their bonus until seasonal degradation destroys them. The permission governs future placement, not current presence. If the shrine becomes unstaffed (custodian dies with no replacement), the grace period runs and then permission lapses permanently. The existing shide degrade naturally and cannot be replaced until a new custodian is assigned.

Mechanical effect: while a shide_object is placed at a shrine zone, all PERFORM_WORSHIP actions at that zone receive Free Raises from the shide&#x2019;s current_tier: Normal = +0, Fine = +1, Exceptional = +2, Masterwork = +3, Legendary = +4. Shide bonus stacks with the gohei bonus (Section 57.26.13) when a character wielding a gohei worships at a shrine with active shide &#x2014; the two objects are complementary. A Fine gohei (+1) at a shrine with Exceptional shide (+2) provides +3 total Free Raises on the PERFORM_WORSHIP roll.

### 57.26.11 Shide &#x2014; Seasonal Degradation

At each IC season boundary, the shide&#x2019;s current_tier drops one step. Fine &#x2192; Normal after one season. Exceptional &#x2192; Fine &#x2192; Normal after two. Masterwork lasts three seasons at any useful bonus level. Legendary lasts four. When current_tier drops below Normal, the shide_object is destroyed &#x2014; tattered remnants that provide no benefit and must be replaced. This creates recurring demand for origami practitioners at active shrines.

### 57.26.12 CRAFT (Gohei) &#x2014; Portable Worship Implement

Standard CRAFT action. Artisan: Origami / Awareness vs TN 20 (higher than shide &#x2014; the wand requires precision construction, with shide attached to a carved wooden handle and properly balanced). Cost: 1 AP. Materials: paper and wood (Common, negligible cost &#x2014; 1 bu total). Produces a gohei_object in the crafter&#x2019;s inventory. Quality tier determined by Raises: 0 Raises = Normal, 1 Raise = Fine, 2 Raises = Exceptional, 3 Raises = Masterwork, 4+ Raises = Legendary. Uses_remaining by quality: Normal = 3, Fine = 5, Exceptional = 8, Masterwork = 12, Legendary = 20.

### 57.26.13 Gohei &#x2014; Use and Degradation

A character wielding a gohei receives Free Raises on their PERFORM_WORSHIP roll: Normal = +0, Fine = +1, Exceptional = +2, Masterwork = +3, Legendary = +4. Each PERFORM_WORSHIP roll where the gohei bonus is applied ticks uses_remaining down by 1. When uses_remaining reaches 0, the gohei_object is destroyed.

One gohei applies per PERFORM_WORSHIP roll. A character carrying multiple gohei applies the highest-quality one. Spare gohei in inventory are not consumed &#x2014; only the active one ticks down.

Gohei bonus stacks with shide bonus at the same shrine. A monk with an Exceptional gohei (+2) performing worship at a shrine with Fine shide (+1) receives +3 total Free Raises. The shrine&#x2019;s decoration and the priest&#x2019;s implement are complementary, not redundant.

A gohei may be gifted to another character through DELIVER_GIFT, following standard gift mechanics. Ownership transfers. Uses_remaining carries over. Noshi stacking note: a gohei wrapped in noshi before DELIVER_GIFT receives both the gohei&#x2019;s item quality Free Raises and the noshi wrap Free Raises on the gift-giving roll. An Exceptional gohei (+2 from quality) in Masterwork noshi (+3 from wrapping) = +5 Free Raises on the gift-giving roll for an item worth 1 bu. This is intentional &#x2014; the social weight comes entirely from the artisan skill invested, not the material cost. The gohei is a cheap consumable elevated into a significant gift by the craftsmanship of both the gohei-maker and the wrapper, who may be the same person or two different artisans.

### 57.26.14 Senbazuru &#x2014; Declaration

The character formally dedicates a senbazuru by creating a senbazuru_record on their character sheet. Cost: 0 AP &#x2014; declaration is a statement of intent, not an action. Dedication types: Healing &#x2014; for a specific named character who is ill, wounded, or Tainted. Protection &#x2014; for a specific named character about to face great danger (military deployment, Shadowlands expedition, dangerous mission). Remembrance &#x2014; for a specific named character who has recently died (within the last IC season). Atonement &#x2014; the folder dedicates it to their own spiritual restoration, no recipient.

A character may only have one active senbazuru at a time. Declaration generates a Tier 4 topic: &#x201C;[Character name] has begun folding a senbazuru for [recipient name / purpose].&#x201D; Three-ring propagation scope (inner: co-located characters, middle: met_characters of folder or recipient, outer: same province). The project is publicly known from the start.

### 57.26.15 Senbazuru &#x2014; Progress

Each AP spent folding uses the standard CRAFT action &#x2014; Artisan: Origami / Awareness vs TN 15. No new ActionID required. The CRAFT action is tagged with the senbazuru_id.

On success: base 10 cranes produced. Each declared Raise produces an additional 5 cranes. 0 Raises (TN 15) = 10 cranes. 1 Raise (TN 20) = 15 cranes. 2 Raises (TN 25) = 20 cranes. 3 Raises (TN 30) = 25 cranes. 4 Raises (TN 35) = 30 cranes. The number of Raises achieved is added to total_raises. successful_session_count increments by 1.

On failure (does not meet declared TN): 0 cranes produced, AP spent, session wasted. Session does not increment successful_session_count and does not add to total_raises. The cost of failure is time, not quality degradation.

AP does not need to be consecutive. The cranes accumulate across days, weeks, and seasons. Paper cranes stored in a box do not degrade &#x2014; there is no abandonment timer. A character who declared a senbazuru two IC years ago and folded 400 cranes before deployment can resume where they left off. The social consequence of non-completion is self-enforcing: the declaration topic is out there, and an unanswered promise is visible in the world&#x2019;s memory without mechanical punishment.

### 57.26.16 Senbazuru &#x2014; Quality Determination

At completion (crane_count reaches 1,000), the average Raises across all successful sessions determines the final quality tier: total_raises &#x00F7; successful_session_count. Average 0 Raises = Normal. Average 1 Raise = Fine. Average 2 Raises = Exceptional. Average 3 Raises = Masterwork. Average 4+ Raises = Legendary. Fractional averages round down (e.g. average 1.7 = Fine). Failed sessions do not count toward the average.

### 57.26.17 Senbazuru &#x2014; Completion and Presentation

When crane_count reaches 1,000, is_complete is set to true and the senbazuru becomes a completed item in the folder&#x2019;s inventory. Presentation is a 1 AP action at the appropriate location.

Healing &#x2014; presented at a shrine on behalf of the recipient, or directly to the recipient if co-located. Two effects fire on presentation. First, the recipient gains disposition toward the folder regardless of their current condition: Normal = +3, Fine = +5, Exceptional = +8, Masterwork = +12, Legendary = +15. Temporary modifier, Section 12.2 durations (Normal = 30 IC days, Fine = 45, Exceptional = 60, Masterwork = 75, Legendary = 90). The emotional weight of the gesture is independent of whether the mechanical benefit applies. Second, if the recipient is still wounded, ill, or Tainted at the time of presentation, they receive Free Raises on their next wound recovery roll, illness resistance roll, or Taint resistance roll (whichever is relevant): Normal = +0, Fine = +1, Exceptional = +2, Masterwork = +3, Legendary = +4. One-time use &#x2014; consumed on the next qualifying roll. If the recipient has already recovered before presentation, the second effect does not fire &#x2014; there is no qualifying roll to enhance. The disposition gain from the first effect still applies.

Protection &#x2014; presented directly to the recipient before departure. Two effects fire on presentation. First, the recipient gains disposition toward the folder using the same quality scaling and durations as Healing: Normal = +3, Fine = +5, Exceptional = +8, Masterwork = +12, Legendary = +15 (Section 12.2 durations). Second, the recipient recovers all spent Void Points. If already at full Void, the recipient instead gains +1k0 on their first dangerous roll during the mission (combat, Taint resistance, Athletics for survival &#x2014; the first life-threatening roll). Consumed on use. If the recipient&#x2019;s mission is cancelled or the danger passes before departure, the second effect does not fire &#x2014; there is no departure to bless. The disposition gain from the first effect still applies.

Remembrance &#x2014; presented at the funeral, memorial, or the deceased&#x2019;s ancestral shrine. Public act. All named characters who witness the presentation gain disposition toward the folder: Normal = +2, Fine = +4, Exceptional = +6, Masterwork = +8, Legendary = +10. Temporary modifier, Section 12.2 durations. The folder gains Glory: Normal = +0.1, Fine = +0.2, Exceptional = +0.3, Masterwork = +0.4, Legendary = +0.5.

Atonement &#x2014; presented at a shrine. The folder recovers Honor: Normal = +0.05, Fine = +0.1, Exceptional = +0.15, Masterwork = +0.2, Legendary = +0.3. Modest but meaningful &#x2014; this is meditative penance through sustained craft, not a loophole for rapid Honor farming.

Completion topic: &#x201C;[Character name] has completed a [quality tier] senbazuru for [recipient / purpose].&#x201D; Tier 3 for Exceptional and above. Tier 4 for Fine and below. Three-ring propagation.

Creator deceased: if the folder dies while the senbazuru is in progress, the record transitions to CREATOR_DECEASED. No penalty. Topic fires: &#x201C;[Character name] died before completing the senbazuru for [recipient / purpose].&#x201D; Tier 4.

Dedication shift: if the recipient of a Healing or Protection senbazuru dies while the project is in progress, dedication_type automatically shifts to Remembrance. Progress is unaffected. Topic updates: &#x201C;[Character name]&#x2019;s senbazuru, begun for [recipient]&#x2019;s [healing/protection], continues as a memorial.&#x201D; Tier 4.

### 57.26.18 Commission Pipeline

Noshi: no commission pipeline. Wrapping is a service or favour resolved through social interaction between co-located characters. REQUEST_ART and OFFER_ART_COMMISSION do not apply.

Shide and gohei: no formal commission pipeline. These are cheap consumable items, not prestige works. A lord or shugenja who wants shide or gohei communicates directly with the artisan through conversation or WRITE_LETTER. The request generates a topic that incites the artisan toward CRAFT and BEGIN_TRAVEL, but no commission_record, delivery window, or obligation mechanics are created.

Senbazuru: REQUEST_ART (art_type: senbazuru) is supported. A lord may formally request a senbazuru for a specific purpose &#x2014; typically for a dying vassal, a departing warrior, or a funeral. The request generates a topic: &#x201C;[Lord name] is seeking an origami artisan to fold a senbazuru for [recipient / purpose].&#x201D; Tier 4. Any NPC with Artisan: Origami rank 1+ who receives the topic evaluates whether to take on the project. No formal obligation attaches &#x2014; senbazuru is a devotional act, and coerced devotion is spiritually empty. ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE may not override this &#x2014; a lord cannot order a vassal to fold a senbazuru. The commission exists as an invitation, not a command.

### 57.26.19 World Generation

Shrine shide: at game start, shrines are seeded with shide appropriate to the shrine&#x2019;s importance. Major temples and clan capital shrines: Exceptional shide. Provincial shrines: Fine shide. Village shrines: Normal shide or empty (50% probability). Crafter_id for world-gen shide is set to a local NPC with Artisan: Origami rank 2+. Custodian shugenja at each shrine are seeded with shide_placement_permission already granted to that crafter.

Gohei: shugenja NPCs who regularly perform worship (stationed at shrines or temples) have a 60% probability of spawning with a gohei in inventory. Quality scaled to the shugenja&#x2019;s school rank: Rank 1&#x2013;2 = Normal, Rank 3&#x2013;4 = Fine, Rank 5+ = Exceptional. Uses_remaining set randomly between 50% and 100% of the quality tier&#x2019;s maximum. Crafter_id set to a local origami artisan NPC.

Origami artisan NPCs: every clan seeds at least one NPC with Artisan: Origami rank 2+ per major province. Crane territory seeds the highest-rank origami artisans (rank 3&#x2013;5), primarily Kakita Artisan school type with chosen_artisan_skill: Origami &#x2014; these NPCs receive the Soul of Brilliance +2k2 on Void Point spend for all Origami rolls, making them mechanically superior to origami practitioners from any other school. Crane provinces also seed Doji Courtier school type NPCs with Origami as a secondary trained skill (rank 2&#x2013;3), representing courtiers who fold for social purposes rather than artistic mastery. Phoenix and Dragon territories seed rank 2&#x2013;3 artisans: Phoenix primarily Shiba Artisan school type (chosen_artisan_skill: Origami grants Soul of Brilliance), Dragon primarily Courtier or Monk school types with Origami as a secondary skill. Other clans seed rank 1&#x2013;2 as baseline, using whatever school type the NPC generation assigns &#x2014; these are competent folders, not specialists.

Noshi: no world-gen state. Noshi wrapping is a per-event action, not a persistent object. Senbazuru: no world-gen state. Senbazuru are personal devotional projects that emerge from play.

### 57.26.20 NPC Engine &#x2014; Origami Evaluation

Noshi pre-delivery evaluation: when an NPC with Artisan: Origami rank 1+ generates DELIVER_GIFT in Phase 5, the engine evaluates whether to spend 1 AP on CRAFT (noshi) before delivering. Scoring: gift quality Fine or above = +30. RAISE_DISPOSITION is the active NeedType driving the DELIVER_GIFT = +20. Artisan: Origami rank 1&#x2013;2 = &#x2212;10 (failure risk), rank 3+ = +0, rank 5+ = +10 (reliable with Raises). Elevated Rei personality = +15. Target is higher Status = +10. AP pressure from competing high-priority actions = &#x2212;20. Threshold: score exceeds 40 triggers CRAFT (noshi). Raise declaration: rank 3+ = 1 Raise (TN 20), rank 5+ = 2 Raises (TN 25), rank 7+ = 3 Raises (TN 30). On Mundane result: NPC always strips and delivers unwrapped. PROVISIONAL: threshold pending playtesting.

Wrapping for others: detection hook — during Phase 5 pre-scan, the engine identifies all NPCs at the same settlement who have pending DELIVER_GIFT actions targeting Fine+ items in their inventory. For each such NPC, the engine checks whether any co-located NPC has Artisan: Origami rank 1+ and disposition Neutral+ toward the gift-giver. If a match exists, the artisan’s Phase 5 adds CRAFT (noshi for another) to the scored action pool before the gift-giver’s DELIVER_GIFT resolves. This pre-scan runs once per Phase 5 cycle, not per-artisan — it is a batch check on co-located gift-giver/artisan pairs. Scoring: ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION: 55. RAISE_DISPOSITION toward the gift-giver: 65. SEEK_GLORY: 40. Gift-giver accepts at Neutral+ disposition toward the artisan. If the artisan’s CRAFT (noshi for another) scores above competing actions, the artisan wraps first, then the gift-giver’s DELIVER_GIFT fires on their next AP with the noshi_record attached. If the artisan’s CRAFT does not win, DELIVER_GIFT fires unwrapped. PROVISIONAL.

Shide &#x2014; artisan side: once per IC season, any NPC with Artisan: Origami rank 1+ who is AT_ZONE within the same settlement as a shrine zone evaluates that shrine&#x2019;s shide status. &#x201C;Same settlement&#x201D; means the artisan and the shrine are Lesser Zones within the same settlement node on the World Map &#x2014; not merely the same province. An artisan in a castle town can evaluate the town shrine; an artisan in a neighbouring village cannot evaluate the castle town&#x2019;s shrine without traveling there first. No shide_object present: CRAFT (shide) scores at 60 under ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION, 50 under PERFORM_WORSHIP alignment. Shide at Normal tier (one season from destruction): CRAFT (replacement) scores at 70. Shide at Fine or above: no action. Permission-seeking fires through standard social interaction if the artisan lacks shide_placement_permission and knows the shrine&#x2019;s custodian shugenja (met_characters, Neutral+ disposition). Remote shrine maintenance: an artisan NPC who previously placed shide at a shrine evaluates BEGIN_TRAVEL toward that shrine as seasonal degradation approaches. ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION: 60 + previous shide quality bonus (Fine = +5, Exceptional = +10, Masterwork = +15). PROVISIONAL.

Shide &#x2014; shugenja side: once per IC season, a shugenja NPC assigned as shrine custodian checks shide status. No shide and a known origami artisan (rank 2+) in met_characters: outreach scores at 65 (co-located = direct request, otherwise WRITE_LETTER). Shide present but at Normal tier: same outreach, urgency +10. PROVISIONAL.

Gohei: shugenja and monk NPCs who regularly perform PERFORM_WORSHIP evaluate gohei acquisition. No gohei and known origami artisan in met_characters: outreach scores at 55. Gohei with 2 or fewer uses remaining: urgency +15. Gohei depleted: urgency +10 above baseline. Quality preference: NPCs prefer the highest-quality gohei available and approach the highest-rank artisan they know. PROVISIONAL.

Senbazuru &#x2014; declaration evaluation: fires when an NPC has ALL of: Artisan: Origami rank 1+, a character in met_characters at disposition 46+ (Friendly or above), and that character is severely wounded, ill, Tainted, or recently deceased. Scoring by dedication type: Healing &#x2014; base 50 under RAISE_DISPOSITION, Jin +25, Chugi +15 if recipient is lord or operational superior, Close disposition +10, Devoted +20, severely wounded +10, Tainted +15, actively dying +25. Protection &#x2014; base 45 under RAISE_DISPOSITION, same personality and disposition modifiers as Healing, Shadowlands expedition +15, Tier 2+ crisis +10. Remembrance &#x2014; base 55 under SEEK_GLORY, Jin +20, Chugi +15, Rei +15, deceased was lord +25, family member +20, Friendly+ friend +10, ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION 60. Atonement &#x2014; base 40 under self-directed honor recovery, Honor deficit 0.5+ below school baseline = +20, 1.0+ = +35, Rei +15, Chugi +10. Declaration threshold: PROVISIONAL 65.

Senbazuru &#x2014; continuation scoring: each Phase 5 after declaration, CRAFT (senbazuru) scores at base 55 under ARTISTIC_EXPRESSION. Modified by crane_count progress: 250&#x2013;500 = +5, 500&#x2013;750 = +10, 750&#x2013;999 = +15 (completion pull). Recipient condition worsening +15. Standard Phase 5 competition handles priority &#x2014; military orders and crisis actions naturally outscore senbazuru folding. Raise declaration: rank 3+ = 1 Raise, rank 5+ = 2 Raises, rank 7+ = 3 Raises. NeedType alignment shift on dedication change: when dedication_type shifts from Healing or Protection to Remembrance (recipient died mid-project), the continuation scoring&#x2019;s NeedType alignment switches immediately. RAISE_DISPOSITION toward the recipient is no longer valid &#x2014; the recipient is dead. SEEK_GLORY becomes the primary NeedType (Remembrance generates Glory at presentation). ### 57.26.22 Provenance Investigation

Noshi: Investigation (Search) / Perception or Artisan: Origami / Awareness to identify the wrapper&#x2019;s style from a gift&#x2019;s presentation. TN by wrapper fame: well-known Kakita master = TN 15, regional artisan = TN 20, unknown artisan = TN 25. Artisan: Origami rank 2+ grants 1 Free Raise. On success: the investigator learns the wrapper&#x2019;s identity (wrapper_id resolved). On failure: the observer notes the wrapping is skillful but cannot identify the artisan. Investigation may be performed on preserved wrapping if the recipient kept the paper.

Shide: Investigation (Search) / Perception or Artisan: Origami / Awareness to identify the crafter of shrine shide. TN by quality: Legendary = TN 15, Masterwork = TN 20, Exceptional = TN 25, Fine = TN 30, Normal = TN 35. Higher-quality shide carry more distinctive craft signatures. Artisan: Origami rank 2+ grants 1 Free Raise. On success: crafter identity resolved. Investigation of destroyed shide: within 1 IC season of destruction, TN +5. Beyond 1 IC season: physical investigation cannot succeed &#x2014; paper traces disintegrate rapidly.

Gohei: same investigation framework as shide. The wand is a physical object carrying the crafter&#x2019;s style. TN by quality follows the same scale. A gohei in someone&#x2019;s possession can be examined at any time.

Senbazuru: a completed senbazuru presented publicly is attributed to its folder through the completion topic &#x2014; no investigation needed. A senbazuru found in someone&#x2019;s possession (inherited, discovered) can be investigated: Artisan: Origami / Awareness vs TN 20 to identify the folder. Each Raise reveals additional details: dedication type (1 Raise), approximate age (1 Raise).
