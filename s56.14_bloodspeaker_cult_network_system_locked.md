## 56.14 Bloodspeaker Cult Network System --- LOCKED

The Bloodspeaker Cult is not an insurgency. It is a persistent empire-wide faction that operates through distributed, compartmentalized cells. The insurgency system (Section 11.11) models the local manifestation --- what happens when a cell becomes active and detectable. This section models the network behind it: how cells are distributed, how they grow, how they propagate, and why they can never be fully destroyed.

**Design Principle:** The Bloodspeaker Cult is a permanent source of content. Good governance does not prevent it. Suppressing one cell does not eliminate the network. A single named NPC can restart a cell. The cult is the fire that is always burning somewhere in the empire. Players and lords can fight it locally but never win globally. This is both lore-accurate (Section 54.3) and gameplay-necessary.

**Cross-Reference:** Section 54.3 (Bloodspeaker Cult faction lore, philosophy, technique, artifacts). Section 11.11 (Maho Cult insurgency type --- the local detection and suppression pipeline). Section 11.11 Maho Cult Recruitment and Sleeper Aftermath (named NPC corruption and post-suppression seeding). Section 2.6.3 (Jigoku, maho mechanics, Kansen).

### 56.14.1 Cell States --- LOCKED

Every Bloodspeaker cell exists in one of four states at any given time.

- **Dormant:** The cell exists. Members live normal lives. They meet in secret but do not practice actively. No PTL impact. No detectable activity. No insurgency registered on the World Map. They are waiting for instructions, opportunity, or desperation. This is most cells most of the time.

- **Active:** The cell is practicing maho, recruiting, pursuing an objective. PTL rises. Kansen accumulate. Jigoku bleed events fire. The cell crosses into the Section 11.11 insurgency system --- detectable, trackable, suppressible. An Active cell is a Maho Cult insurgency on the World Map.

- **Propagating:** A mature Active cell sends one member to seed a new cell elsewhere. The parent cell loses one member (Strength decreases by the equivalent of one person). A new Dormant cell appears in the target province with Strength 1 and Concealment at maximum. The parent cell continues operating at reduced Strength.

- **Destroyed:** All members killed or captured. The cell no longer exists. But any Sleepers who escaped carry the cult_affiliation flag and can restart the cycle per the existing Sleeper Aftermath mechanics (Section 11.11).

### 56.14.2 World Generation --- Initial Distribution --- LOCKED

**Total Cells at Game Start:** 25-35, randomized within that range. The empire has been hunting the Bloodspeakers for generations. The cult is diminished but persistent.

**Placement Logic:** Cells are not placed randomly. The cult is strategic. Placement is weighted toward:

- **High population provinces:** More recruitment targets, easier to hide in crowds.

- **Provinces with eta communities:** Historically the most fertile recruitment ground per Section 54.3 lore.

- **Provinces near the Shadowlands border:** Jigoku’s influence is stronger. Kansen are more present.

- **Provincial capitals and castle towns:** The lore specifies the cult prefers towns and cities over rural villages.

- **Provinces with low garrison presence:** Less chance of discovery.

**Cell State at Game Start:** Approximately 75-80% of cells start Dormant. The remaining 20-25% start Active --- these produce the early-game Maho Cult content. Active cells at game start enter the Section 11.11 insurgency pipeline immediately with Strength 2-4 (randomly assigned) and Concealment equal to the Maho Cult base Concealment of 8.

**Cell Leader Selection:** Where possible, the cell leader is a named NPC selected during world generation. NPCs with high Susceptibility (Section 11.11), low Honor, existing grievances, Shourido virtue leans, or appropriate personality profiles are eligible. Their character sheet gains the hidden cult_affiliation flag. Not every cell has a named NPC leader --- some are led by generic Bloodspeaker cultists. The Kolat Master Selection system (Section 54.7a) provides the design pattern: weighted selection from the NPC pool, tiered by suitability.

**Player Visibility:** None. The player does not know how many cells exist, where they are, or who leads them. They discover cells through the insurgency detection pipeline --- Kansen activity, Jigoku bleed events, PTL anomalies, investigation. The empire-level cell count is never displayed.

### 56.14.3 Propagation Mechanics --- LOCKED

An Active cell at Strength 4 or above has a 10% chance per season of propagating. When propagation fires:

- **Agent Dispatch:** One member is sent to a target province. They travel through the simulation like any character --- they can be intercepted, spotted, questioned. If they arrive safely, a new Dormant cell appears in the target province with Strength 1 and maximum Concealment.

- **Parent Cell Cost:** The parent cell loses one member. Strength decreases by the equivalent of one person. Propagation is a real cost --- the cult sacrifices local power for network expansion.

- **Target Selection:** Deliberately distant. Not adjacent. At least 3 provinces distant, preferring different clan territories. If a Crab province cell propagates, the new cell targets Crane or Phoenix lands. The cult knows adjacent cells are vulnerable to sweep operations.

- **Target Weighting:** Same criteria as world generation placement --- high population, eta presence, Shadowlands proximity, urban centers, low garrison. The cult plants where conditions favor survival.

### 56.14.4 The Hydra Rule --- LOCKED

When a cell is suppressed through the Section 11.11 pipeline, the system checks whether the cell propagated before detection.

- **Active for 4-7 seasons before suppression:** 60% chance it already sent an agent. A new Dormant cell exists somewhere in the empire.

- **Active for 8+ seasons before suppression:** 90% chance. Near-certain that a seed was planted.

- **Active for fewer than 4 seasons:** No propagation check. The cell was caught early enough.

The player suppresses the cell, the lord receives Glory for the victory, and there is no way of knowing whether a seed was already planted. The new Dormant cell is invisible until it activates years later.

Additionally, any Sleepers who survived suppression undetected carry the cult_affiliation flag. Per the existing Sleeper Aftermath mechanics (Section 11.11): +15% Maho Cult spawn chance after 4 seasons in a province, +30% after 8. One person restarts the cycle. The Bloodspeaker network regenerates through two independent mechanisms --- deliberate propagation and Sleeper seeding --- ensuring that even the most thorough suppression campaign leaves roots behind.

### 56.14.5 Activation Triggers --- What Makes a Dormant Cell Go Active --- LOCKED

A Dormant cell does not activate randomly. Something pushes it.

- **PTL rises above 3 in the province:** Jigoku’s influence strengthens. Kansen become more present. The cell’s members feel the pull. Activation chance 20% per season while PTL is 3+.

- **Province enters Volatile or Broken Stability:** Desperate people are easier to recruit, authorities are distracted. Activation chance 15% per season.

- **A named NPC in the province falls to Maho:** Whether they join the cell or not, the act of someone nearby using blood magic emboldens the cell. Automatic activation.

- **Instruction from a propagating cell:** A cell that propagates can also send activation orders to a known Dormant cell instead of planting a new one. This means the network can strategically activate cells in response to empire events.

- **Passage of time:** Even without triggers, a Dormant cell has a 2% base activation chance per season. Some cells just get restless. Over decades, every Dormant cell eventually activates.

### 56.14.6 Maho Use and PTL --- Correction and Expansion --- LOCKED

**CORRECTION (applies to Section 11.11 PTL Gains):** Section 11.11 states +1 PTL per confirmed maho use event. The word confirmed is incorrect. Any maho use in a province raises PTL whether detected or not. The Kansen do not wait for a magistrate to notice. Every blood spell, every kansen summoning, every forbidden ritual poisons the spiritual fabric of the land. The +1 PTL fires on the act itself. Detection is separate --- the Jigoku bleed event is the observable symptom. The PTL rise is the invisible reality.

This has cascading implications for the Bloodspeaker network:

- **A Dormant cell that occasionally practices in secret:** Raises PTL at a negligible rate. Perhaps +0.25 PTL per season from occasional minor rituals. Slow enough to be invisible for years but it accumulates.

- **An Active cell:** +1 PTL per season as already locked in Section 11.11. Regular practice, recruitment rituals, kansen summoning.

- **A lone Sleeper:** +0.25 PTL per season from passive Taint spreading (already locked in Sleeper Aftermath). Not actively practicing but spiritually radiating corruption.

- **A lone named NPC maho user (not Bloodspeaker):** +1 PTL per use event. Each individual act of blood magic scars the land. A single rogue shugenja practicing maho is a PTL source even without a cult.

- **Kansen accumulation:** Kansen are corrupted elemental kami summoned by maho use. Outside the Shadowlands they are virtually nonexistent unless someone practices blood magic. When a cell operates, Kansen accumulate in the area --- haunting villages, homes, disturbing the spiritual peace. This is what Jigoku bleed events mechanically represent. Monks and shugenja can detect and banish minor Kansen. Major Kansen presence indicates sustained maho activity. Kansen presence is the bridge between hidden cult activity and player-detectable symptoms.

**Cross-Reference:** Section 2.6.3 (Jigoku game effects --- maho use generates Jigoku bleed events). Section 11.11 PTL Thresholds (PTL 3+ spawns Province Taint Manifestation). Section 11.11 Maho Cult Recruitment (active cults recruit named NPCs through Temptation). Section 42 (Shadowlands Taint --- character-level Taint accumulation).

### 56.14.7 Cell Establishment Paths --- LOCKED

A new Maho Cult cell can be established through four distinct paths. Each produces a different kind of cell with different characteristics. The Stability-based spawn roll in Section 11.11 is one mechanism among several, not the sole origin.

- **Path 1 --- Bloodspeaker Agent Infiltration:** An agent from an existing cell is sent to the province through the propagation mechanics above. This is the most common path. The cell arrives organized, with knowledge of cult practices, and with a connection to the parent cell. The most dangerous variant from day one because the leader is a trained Bloodspeaker.

- **Path 2 --- PTL Corruption:** Province Taint Level rises and Jigoku’s influence begins actively corrupting locals. A shugenja hears whispers from Kansen. A desperate person makes a bargain they do not understand. Tied to the PTL system, not Stability. A province at PTL 3+ is vulnerable regardless of governance quality. This path produces amateur cultists fumbling with power they do not understand --- dangerous but disorganized.

- **Path 3 --- Named NPC Falls to Maho:** A specific named character turns to blood magic through the existing Character Corruption and Susceptibility Framework (Section 11.11). A disgraced shugenja, a desperate lord, someone who lost everything and found forbidden power. The cult is built around one individual. Without them it collapses. This path produces a personality-driven cell --- the leader IS the cell. Their skills, school techniques, and resources define what the cell can do.

- **Path 4 --- Artifact Discovery:** A Maho artifact surfaces in the province --- a Bloodsword, a porcelain mask (Section 54.3), a cursed scroll, a kansen-infused relic. Someone finds it, uses it, gets corrupted. The artifact is the seed. The cult grows around it. This path produces a cell organized around a specific object. Destroying or recovering the artifact cripples the cell immediately.

Each path feeds into the Section 11.11 Maho Cult insurgency type once the cell becomes Active and detectable. The path determines the cell’s composition, leadership quality, and vulnerability --- information that shapes the ASCII map encounter when the player is sent to suppress it. Cell-level roster composition and ASCII map encounter design are deferred to Section 56.10 when the Maho Cult quest seed is fully detailed.
