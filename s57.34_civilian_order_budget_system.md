## 57.34 Civilian Order Budget System

### 57.34.1 Design Principle — AP vs Orders

SUPREMACY CLAUSE: this section defines the authoritative distinction between Action Points and Orders for all lord-tier actions across the document. Where any other section lists an AP cost for an action reclassified here as an Order, this section supersedes. The reclassification table in Section 57.34.4 is binding.

The game distinguishes two resource pools for characters in positions of authority:

Action Points (AP): what the character does personally. Negotiating face-to-face. Leading a charge. Meditating. Training. Worshipping at a shrine. Writing a letter. Performing a tea ceremony. Every NPC and player character has 2 AP per IC day (8 per OOC day). AP represents the character’s personal time, skill, and engagement. If the character must be personally present, personally skilled, or personally at risk for the action to succeed, it costs AP.

Orders: what the character tells others to do. Raising taxes. Commissioning a painting. Requesting a performance. Assigning a vassal to a task. Garrisoning troops at a gate. Ordering a levy. These are verbal directives, administrative commands, and delegated instructions. The lord speaks; a subordinate, retainer, steward, or staff member executes. The lord’s personal skill is not tested. The lord’s personal time is negligible — saying “raise taxes to 40%” takes a moment, not half a day. Orders represent the lord’s administrative bandwidth — how many things they can direct their household and staff to do in a given period.

The test for classification: “Could a competent steward or retainer execute this if the lord just told them what to do?” If yes, it is an Order. If the lord’s personal involvement, skill, social weight, or physical risk is required, it is an AP action. A lord saying “arrange a biwa performance at court tonight” is an Order — the steward handles the logistics. A lord personally negotiating a peace treaty with a rival clan is an AP action — no one else can do it for them.

The Military Commander Orders system (Section 7, line 7625 in the military operations chapter) already established this distinction for battlefield directives. Military commanders receive 5–15 orders per OOC day to direct troops, separate from their personal AP. This section extends the same principle to civilian governance.

### 57.34.2 Civilian Order Budget by Rank

Lords receive a Civilian Order Budget based on their administrative rank. The budget refreshes each OOC day at the OOC Day Tick (same tick as natural healing and Void Point refresh, Section 57.31.7a and Section 57.32.2). Unused orders do not carry over — they expire at the end of the OOC day.

- City Daimyo / Minor Holding Lord: 5 civilian orders per OOC day. Small household, limited staff. Averages 1.25 orders per IC day.

- Provincial Daimyo: 8 civilian orders per OOC day. Larger administration, more retainers. Averages 2 orders per IC day.

- Family Daimyo: 10 civilian orders per OOC day. Major household with specialised staff. Averages 2.5 orders per IC day.

- Clan Champion: 12 civilian orders per OOC day. Imperial-scale administration. Averages 3 orders per IC day.

- Imperial positions (Emperor, Emerald Champion, Jade Champion, Imperial Advisor): 15 civilian orders per OOC day. Maximum administrative capacity. Averages 3.75 orders per IC day.

These values parallel the Military Commander Order budgets (Chui 5, Taisa 10, Shireikan 10, Rikugunshokan 15). A character who holds both a civilian lordship and a military command rank has two separate order budgets — civilian orders for governance, military orders for troop direction. The two pools do not cross — a Clan Champion cannot spend civilian orders on troop movements, nor military orders on tax adjustments.

Budget values are PROVISIONAL pending playtesting. The key constraint is that the budget should be generous enough that routine governance is not bottlenecked (a lord should never run out of orders on a quiet day) but scarce enough that an active crisis (war preparations + winter court management + economic adjustments) forces prioritisation. A Provincial Daimyo with 8 orders per OOC day can comfortably handle 2 tax adjustments, 1 garrison reassignment, 1 performance request, 1 art commission, and 3 vassal objectives — a busy but manageable day. A Clan Champion juggling a war, a winter court, and an economic crisis across multiple provinces may find 12 orders tight and must delegate to subordinate lords.

### 57.34.3 Constraint: Present in Same Area

Civilian orders require the recipient to be physically present in the same settlement or holding as the lord. A lord at Kyuden Doji can order their steward (who is at Kyuden Doji) to raise taxes, but cannot order their steward at a distant provincial holding to do the same — that requires a letter (WRITE_LETTER, 0 AP, subject to letter delivery delay). This matches the military order system: orders to subordinates in the same location are instant; orders to subordinates at different locations require a messenger.

Exception — standing governance orders: certain governance actions (SET_TAX_RATE, SET_STIPEND_RATE) apply to the lord’s holdings regardless of the lord’s physical location because they are policy decisions, not operational commands. A lord who sets the tax rate at 40% while physically present at their holding uses 1 civilian order. A lord who sends a letter to their steward saying “raise taxes to 40%” from a distant court uses WRITE_LETTER (1 Civilian Order) and the steward implements the change on receipt, subject to letter delivery delay. Both achieve the same game state change; the difference is timing. The civilian order budget governs both in-person directives and lord correspondence; the standard letter system governs non-lord correspondence.

For REQUEST_PERFORMANCE specifically: if the lord targets a performer who is already present at court, the order resolves immediately (the performer is told in person). If the lord targets a performer at a distant location, the request auto-generates an invitation letter per Section 57.33.3. Both cost 1 civilian order from the lord’s budget — the administrative decision is the same regardless of the performer’s location. The delivery mechanism differs, not the cost.

### 57.34.4 Action Reclassification Table

The following table reclassifies lord-tier actions from AP cost to Civilian Order cost. This table is binding — where any other section in the document lists “1 AP” for an action listed here, this section supersedes. The original section’s mechanics (roll, TN, effects) remain unchanged; only the resource cost changes from AP to Order.

Actions reclassified from 1 AP to 1 Civilian Order:

- SET_TAX_RATE (Category 9). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order. Rationale: telling the steward a number.

- SET_STIPEND_RATE (Category 9). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order. Rationale: adjusting household budget.

- REQUEST_ART (Category 14). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order. Rationale: commissioning an artisan to create something. The artisan does the work.

- REQUEST_PERFORMANCE (Category 14). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order. Rationale: telling a performer to play. The performer does the work.

- ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE (Category 10). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order. Rationale: giving a vassal instructions. The vassal executes.

- ASSIGN_GARRISON (Category 7). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order (or 1 Military Order if the lord holds military rank). Rationale: directing troops to a position. The troops move themselves.

- ORDER_LEVY (Category 7). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order (or 1 Military Order). Rationale: calling up ashigaru. An administrative mobilisation command.

- ORDER_DEPLOY (Category 7). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Military Order (or 1 Civilian Order for lords without military rank). Rationale: marching troops to a location. A troop movement directive.

- ORDER_RETREAT (Category 7). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Military Order. Rationale: withdrawal directive.

- ORDER_FORTIFY (Category 7). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Military Order (or 1 Civilian Order). Rationale: engineering directive. The engineers do the work.

- ORDER_PATROL (Category 7). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Military Order (or 1 Civilian Order). Rationale: assigning a patrol route. The troops walk it.

- ASSESS_PROVINCE_STATUS (Category 9). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order. Rationale: asking the steward for a report. The steward compiles the data.

- EVALUATE_WAR_READINESS (Category 9). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order (or 1 Military Order). Rationale: asking the military staff for an assessment.

- ASSIGN_TO_MILITARY_SERVICE (Military Service Assignment, Section 7). Formerly 1 AP. Now 1 Civilian Order. Rationale: the lord selects a retainer and assigns them to military duty. The retainer travels to the army; the lord issues the directive.

- WRITE_LETTER (Category 10). Two-tier system. For lord characters: formerly 0 AP with 1-per-IC-day limit; now 1 Civilian Order per letter with no per-IC-day limit (pool is the only constraint). Rationale: writing a letter and giving a verbal order consume the same directive bandwidth. For non-lord characters: 1 free letter per IC day (unchanged) plus a new option to spend 1 AP for a batch of 4 additional letters per IC day. See Section 12.7 and Section 57.34.7 for full details.

Actions that REMAIN at AP cost (lord’s personal involvement required):

- CALL_COURT (Category 14). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally presides over court — sits on the dais, opens proceedings, sets the agenda. Major personal time commitment.

- INVESTIGATE_PROVINCE (Category 14). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally inspects for Shadowlands taint. Requires their skill and physical presence.

- DEMAND_TRIBUTE (Category 7). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally intimidates another character. Requires their social weight and physical presence.

- REQUEST_ALLIED_AID (Category 7). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally asks another lord for help. Diplomatic action requiring personal credibility.

- CONDUCT_COMMERCE (Category 9). Remains 1 AP. The lord (or merchant-role character) personally trades. Hands-on economic activity.

- PURCHASE_MARKET (Category 9). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally assesses and purchases. Quality judgment requires personal attention.

- CONDUCT_RAID (Category 7). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally leads a raid. Military action with personal risk.

- RAID_HARVEST (Category 7). Remains 1 AP. Same as CONDUCT_RAID — personally led.

- DRILL_TROOPS (Category 7). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally drills troops. Training requires their physical presence and expertise.

- ORDER_BATTLE (Category 7). Remains 1 AP. The lord personally commits to battle — a strategic decision requiring their judgment and presence on the field. Directing individual units during the battle uses Military Orders; the decision to fight costs AP.

- All Categories 1–6 court actions (CHARM, NEGOTIATE, GOSSIP, PUBLIC_PERFORMANCE, etc.). Remain at 1 AP. These are personal social actions where the character’s own skill is tested. A lord who CHARMs someone does it personally. A lord who performs does it personally. Orders cannot substitute for personal social engagement.

- All Categories 11–13, 15, 17 personal actions (TRAIN, MEDITATE, PERFORM_WORSHIP, TREAT_WOUND, COMPOSE_THEATER_PIECE, BEGIN_TRAVEL, etc.). Remain at AP cost. Personal skill, personal presence, personal time.

Siege actions (Category 8): MAINTAIN_SIEGE, CONDUCT_STORM_ASSAULT, NEGOTIATE_SURRENDER, CONDUCT_SORTIE all remain at 1 AP. These are major strategic decisions with personal stakes — the lord commits their army and their own safety.

### 57.34.5 NPC Engine Integration

The NPC Decision Engine (Section 55) must track civilian orders as a separate resource from AP. When the engine evaluates a lord NPC’s available actions in Phase 3 (Generate Options), it checks both resource pools. Actions classified as Orders draw from the civilian order budget; actions classified as AP draw from the AP budget. Within each wave, lord characters resolve TWO actions: the engine runs Phase 3–5 once for AP-only candidates and once for Order-only candidates, executing both winners sequentially (AP first, then Order). See Section 55.13 (AP Wave Resolution) for full implementation. A lord with 0 remaining civilian orders but 2 remaining AP can still CALL_COURT, NEGOTIATE, DEMAND_TRIBUTE, or DRILL_TROOPS — but cannot SET_TAX_RATE, REQUEST_PERFORMANCE, or ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE. Conversely, a lord with 0 remaining AP but 5 remaining civilian orders can still issue directives but cannot personally engage in court politics or military leadership.

This separation means that a lord’s governance work does not compete with their personal political and social engagement for the same resource. A Crane Champion at winter court can spend all 8 AP on NEGOTIATE, CHARM, PUBLIC_DEBATE, and CALL_COURT — doing the personal work of a great politician — while simultaneously spending civilian orders on SET_TAX_RATE, REQUEST_PERFORMANCE, ASSIGN_VASSAL_OBJECTIVE, and REQUEST_ART through their household staff. The two activities run in parallel, reflecting the reality that a powerful lord has both a personal agenda and an administrative apparatus.

Non-lord characters have no civilian order budget. They have only AP. A vassal, a courtier, a bushi without a lordship — they act solely through their personal AP. The civilian order budget is a privilege of authority: you can direct others only if you hold a position that gives you people to direct.

### 57.34.6 Character Sheet Field Additions

Section 22.3 character sheet amendment (added v484): civilian_order_budget_max (integer, derived from administrative rank per Section 57.34.2 — 5/8/10/12/15). civilian_orders_remaining (integer, resets to civilian_order_budget_max at OOC Day Tick). Decremented by 1 each time the lord issues a civilian order. Non-lord characters have these fields set to 0. When a character gains or loses a lordship, civilian_order_budget_max updates immediately to reflect their new rank.

### 57.34.7 Interaction with Existing Systems

WRITE_LETTER unification for lords: for lord characters, WRITE_LETTER draws from the same civilian order budget as verbal directives. Each letter written costs 1 civilian order. This replaces the standard “1 letter per IC day” rule for lords — a lord has no per-IC-day letter cap. Instead, their total directive bandwidth (civilian order budget) is the shared constraint. A Provincial Daimyo with 8 civilian orders per OOC day could theoretically write all 8 as letters in a single IC day, leaving 0 orders for the remaining 3 IC days. Or they could write 2 letters and give 6 verbal orders across the OOC day. The allocation is entirely flexible within the pool.

Rationale: writing a letter and giving a verbal order are the same administrative act — the lord deciding what to tell someone. Whether the instruction travels by voice (verbal order to a present subordinate) or by paper (letter to a distant subordinate) consumes the same mental and directive capacity. A lord who spends the morning writing five letters to distant allies has less bandwidth to govern their household that afternoon. A lord who spends all day issuing verbal orders at court has no time left to write correspondence. The unified pool captures this trade-off naturally.

Non-lord characters use a separate letter system: the standard WRITE_LETTER rule gives non-lords 1 free letter per IC day (4 per OOC day) at no cost. If a non-lord character needs to write more than 1 letter in an IC day, they may spend 1 AP to write a batch of 4 additional letters that IC day — a dedicated writing session. Maximum: 5 letters per IC day (1 free + 4 paid), 20 per OOC day if 1 AP per IC day is spent on correspondence. This creates the “dedicated correspondent” pattern for courtiers, scribes, and operatives running sustained letter campaigns. Full details in Section 12.7.

SEND_INVITATION (Category 10, currently 1 AP): this is a borderline case. The lord personally writes a formal invitation — the calligraphy matters, the phrasing matters, the lord’s personal touch is part of the social weight. SEND_INVITATION remains at 1 AP (personal action) AND also consumes 1 civilian order from the lord’s pool (the directive bandwidth). A formal invitation is both personal work and an administrative act. Auto-generated invitation letters created by REQUEST_PERFORMANCE (Section 57.33.3) are NOT SEND_INVITATION — they are administrative correspondence generated by the order itself and cost nothing additional beyond the 1 civilian order for REQUEST_PERFORMANCE.

Military Order overlap: actions like ASSIGN_GARRISON, ORDER_LEVY, ORDER_DEPLOY, ORDER_PATROL, ORDER_FORTIFY, and ORDER_RETREAT are listed above as reclassified to “1 Civilian Order or 1 Military Order.” If the lord holds a military command rank (Chui through Rikugunshokan), these actions draw from the military order budget. If the lord is a civilian authority without military rank (a Provincial Daimyo who is not also a military commander), they draw from the civilian order budget. A lord who holds both ranks may choose which budget to draw from. This prevents civilian lords from being unable to garrison their own holdings simply because they lack a military title.

Offline lords: when a lord’s controlling player is offline, the NPC engine issues civilian orders autonomously through the standard standing-orders system (Section 13). The civilian order budget still constrains the NPC — an offline lord does not receive unlimited governance capacity.
