## 56.5 Forest Approach + Camp Template --- LOCKED

The Forest Approach + Camp template generates a two-zone map: a dense forest the player navigates through as an approach phase, and a clearing where the occupants have established camp. High reuse --- applicable to Ronin Bandit Uprising, military forward positions, Maho cult gatherings, and Shadowlands forward camps. The unique element is that the approach is its own challenge before the player reaches the objective space. The forest is tension and preparation. The camp is action.

### 56.5.1 Size Categories --- LOCKED

The camp clearing size determines the objective space. The forest zone scales proportionally --- a larger camp implies a deeper forest approach. As with Cave, the Strength value sets a minimum floor for camp size but does not directly determine it.

- **Small Clearing:** 1-2 tents, a firepit. Tight. Can house up to Strength 3.

- **Medium Clearing:** 3-6 shelters, organized loosely around a central area. Can house up to Strength 6.

- **Large Clearing:** 7+ structures, possibly multiple firepits, a command tent, supply area. Can house up to Strength 9.

### 56.5.2 Entry and Flow --- LOCKED

The two-zone structure creates unique pacing. The player enters at the forest edge and must navigate through the forest to reach the camp.

**Entry Points:** The player enters at the forest boundary --- the tree line. The player can enter from any point along this boundary. Once inside the trees, movement is constrained by density. There is no road leading in unless the bandits have worn a trail from use, and that trail is the obvious approach, meaning it is watched.

**The Trail:** Most camps have one. A worn path through the forest that the occupants use for supply runs and movement. Following it is faster but predictable --- sentries watch it. Leaving it means pushing through dense forest: slower, harder to navigate, but less likely to be spotted.

**Forest Zone Flow:** No rooms or corridors. Instead, tree density creates natural channels. The generator places trees with density rules that create open gaps, tight squeezes, and natural clearings. Some paths between trees are passable, others are not. The player reads the terrain and picks a route. Visibility is limited by trees in every direction --- short sightlines, constant uncertainty about what is ahead.

**Transition:** The forest thins at the clearing edge. The player reaches a point where they can see the camp through the last line of trees. This is the decision moment --- they can observe from the tree line before committing to the open space. The tree line itself is cover for the approach but also the last cover available.

**Camp Zone Flow:** Open like a Village but smaller and less structured. Tents and shelters do not block sightlines the way buildings do. The camp interior is mostly exposed once entered. No buildings to duck into. The tactical challenge shifts from navigation to commitment --- once the player leaves the trees, they are visible.

### 56.5.3 Population Placement --- LOCKED

The two-zone structure splits the roster between the forest and the camp.

**Forest Zone:**

- **Outer Sentry:** One or two posted along the trail or at the forest edge. Watching for anyone approaching. If bypassed, the player reaches the camp without early warning. If alerted, they send a runner back to the camp.

- **Forest Patrol:** Moves along a route through the trees. One patrol in Small and Medium clearings, two in Large. They know the forest and move faster through it than the player does. If combat happens in the forest, the patrol is the most dangerous element because they can approach from an unexpected direction through trees the player has not scouted.

**Camp Zone:**

- **Camp Group:** Clustered around firepits and shelters. Most of the roster is here. They are relaxed --- the forest is their security buffer. They trust the sentries and patrols to give warning.

- **Leader Group:** In the command tent or largest shelter. Center or back of the clearing. Same 20-25% concentration of best fighters.

**No Guard Posts:** The forest itself is the defense. No chokepoints to hold inside the camp --- it is an open clearing.

**The Security Buffer Effect:** Most enemies are in the camp, not the forest. The forest has light coverage --- just enough to detect and delay. A stealthy player who gets through the forest without alerting anyone reaches a camp full of enemies who are not expecting trouble. A loud player who fights through forest sentries arrives at a camp that is already preparing for them.

### 56.5.4 Environmental Features --- LOCKED

Features split across both zones.

**Forest Zone:**

- **Tree Density:** The core terrain feature. Blocks sightlines, constrains movement. The generator places trees at varying density --- some areas tight enough that only one-tile gaps exist, others open enough to see a few tiles ahead. Density varies within the same forest, creating natural pockets and channels.

- **Undergrowth:** Bushes, fallen branches, ground cover. Provides concealment when crouching or moving slowly. Moving through it quickly generates noise. Different from trees --- trees block movement entirely, undergrowth slows it and makes it louder.

- **Natural Clearings:** Small gaps in the forest that are not the camp. A fallen tree, a stream crossing, a rocky outcrop. These are ambush points --- a patrol might rest here, or the player might use one to get bearings. Not every clearing has enemies.

- **Streams:** Small water features running through the forest. Difficult terrain, noise when crossing. But also a navigation aid --- following a stream is a reliable way to move without getting disoriented.

- **Wild Animals:** The forest is a real forest --- things live in it. Boar, bear, wolves, snakes. Pulled from the Section 54.1 natural creatures bestiary based on the province’s geographic location. They are not quest enemies. They are environmental hazards that exist because the forest exists. The bandits avoid them. The player might stumble into them, especially off the trail. A fight with a wild animal generates noise, which could alert the patrol. Or the player spots animal tracks and knows to go around. Which animals appear depends on where on the world map the province is located, per the Regional Vegetation/Biome System (Section 56.6.1).

**Camp Zone:**

- **Firepits:** Light sources. Mark occupied areas. Smoke visible above the tree line during the day, glow visible through the trees at night. The player can spot the camp before reaching it by seeing smoke or light through the canopy.

- **Tents and Shelters:** Soft cover only. Unlike village buildings, these do not block movement or provide hard protection. A sword cuts through a tent wall. No doors to funnel through. Enemies inside can be engaged from any side.

- **Supply Stacks:** Stolen goods, food stores, weapons. Placed near shelters. Provide minor cover but also mark where objectives might be located.

- **Tree Line Edge:** The boundary between forest and clearing. Last hard cover before the open camp. The player’s observation point and decision moment. Enemies in the camp are visible from here but so is the player if they step out.

**Time of Day and Weather:** Same systemic effects as Occupied Village. Night raids mean campfire glow is visible but enemies beyond it are in darkness. Rain makes the forest floor muddy and loud. Full details deferred to the shared outdoor weather system.

### 56.5.5 Objective Placement --- Forest Approach + Camp Template --- LOCKED

Same primary objective types adapted for the two-zone structure:

- **Kill the Leader:** Leader is in the command tent at the center or rear of the clearing. Straightforward once the player reaches the camp. But unlike Cave and Village, the leader has a direct escape route --- straight into the forest on the far side. If alerted, they can run into trees the player has not explored. Pursuit through unknown forest is dangerous.

- **Recover Stolen Goods:** Stored in the camp near supply stacks. Visible once the player reaches the tree line edge and observes. Grabbing and extracting means crossing the clearing twice --- in and out --- or finding a route through the forest to the camp’s far side.

- **Burn the Camp:** Works here unlike Occupied Village. This is the bandits’ construction, not civilian infrastructure. Tents, shelters, and supply stacks burn easily. No provincial PU cost. Fire in a forest clearing is controllable if the clearing is wide enough. If the clearing is tight, fire could spread to the tree line --- creating chaos for both sides.

- **Rescue Hostages:** Held in a tent at the camp edge or in a makeshift shelter separate from the main group. The forest offers a potential extraction route --- free the hostages and pull them into the trees rather than fighting through the whole camp.

**Placement Rule:** The objective is always in the camp zone, never in the forest. The forest is the approach, not the destination. Secondary objectives can be in the forest --- a hidden supply cache, a dead drop for communication with a supplier, a deserter’s campsite off the main trail.

### 56.5.6 Resolution and Consequence --- Forest Approach + Camp Template --- LOCKED

Same spectrum with template-specific factors:

- **Full Success:** Primary objective completed. Strength reduction same as other templates by objective type. Camp destroyed or cleared. The forest location is known --- if the insurgency survives at reduced Strength, they cannot reuse this spot. They relocate.

- **Partial Success:** Player reached the camp and did damage but did not complete the primary objective. Leader fled into the forest. Goods identified but not extracted. Strength reduced by kills. The insurgency relocates --- next mission generates a different map at a different location in the province.

- **Retreat:** Player pulled out. Could mean retreating from the forest before reaching the camp, or retreating from the camp back into the trees. Strength reduced only by kills. If the player never reached the camp, the insurgency may or may not know they were scouted --- they may not relocate. If the player was spotted in the camp, they definitely relocate.

- **Failure:** Same as other templates. No reduction, Strength increases by 1.

**Template-Specific Consequences:**

- **Forest Knowledge:** If the player successfully navigated the forest, that route knowledge persists. A second mission against the same camp --- if the insurgency did not relocate --- starts with the player knowing the forest layout. The approach is easier the second time.

- **No Civilian Impact:** No PU loss, no infrastructure damage, no displacement. This is wilderness. The only consequence is to the insurgency itself and the player’s own casualties.

- **Escape Problem:** The forest makes total kills unlikely. Enemies scatter into trees when the fight turns. The player will not chase every fleeing bandit through dense forest. Forest camps tend toward partial suppression --- reducing Strength significantly but rarely wiping the insurgency in one mission. Repeated missions may be needed.
