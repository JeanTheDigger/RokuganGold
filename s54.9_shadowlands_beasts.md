## 54.9 Shadowlands Beasts

When Fu Leng fell from the Heavens, the monstrous impact ruined the lands and tore open a gate to Jigoku – the Festering Pit of Fu Leng – releasing the supernatural power of the Taint to spread into the mortal world. Most of the natural life in the affected area quickly died. Those plants and animals which did not perish were warped by the Taint into cruel, dangerous parodies of their former selves. In the early years after his fall, Fu Leng swiftly corrupted the ancient races of the ogres and trolls, destroying their cultures and reducing them to slaves. He also created the race of goblins in a foul mockery of humanity.

The general environment of the Shadowlands is extremely hostile and malignant. First and foremost is the presence of the Taint, which makes it extremely hazardous simply to enter the realm. The heavy cloud cover and dim sunlight makes the passage of time hard to gauge. The air is cold in all seasons and smells of ashes and putrefaction. Fire is very difficult to make in the Shadowlands, and when it does ignite, it burns only weakly with a pale unnatural green or blue light. Water and food are widespread but fully Tainted, of course, and usually diseased as well. Even the kami, the fundamental spirits of the world, are corrupted and unnatural within the Shadowlands – instead the kansen, the elemental spirits of Jigoku, hold sway.

One of the most disturbing aspects of the Shadowlands’ environment is that the landscape moves. Some Crab scholars have suggested the entire Shadowlands moves in a spiral pattern from the Festering Pit. As such, it is impossible to truly map the deeper regions of the Shadowlands. Ultimately, it can never truly be understood or predicted.

Shadowlands environment rules: Food and drink brought into the Shadowlands quickly becomes suffused with Taint and lethal disease. Characters who sleep in the Shadowlands do not recover Wounds, Void Points, or spell slots from rest (though Meditation can recover Void and spell slots). All Spell Casting Rolls in the Shadowlands suffer a +10 TN penalty; if a Spell Casting roll is failed due to this penalty, a kansen will answer the prayer instead, offering to fulfill the desired spell – if the shugenja agrees, the spell is successfully cast, but the shugenja gains a number of points of Taint equal to the spell’s Mastery Level, and this cannot be prevented with jade.

### Aka-name

One of the more disgusting and reviled creatures of the Shadowlands. The Aka-name looks like a bloated, misshapen human with red skin and eyes, moving on all fours with long distorted arms and a twisted neck. Its back usually displays a huge hunch, and swollen muscles ripple under its tight skin. They seem to be scavengers and ambushers, traveling alone or in small groups and feeding on the weak, the wounded, and the dead. They seem to have a special preference for the foulest of substances, and will eat filth and excrement when no flesh is available. On the rare occasions when an Aka-name infiltrates Rokugan, their taste for filth often leads them to eta villages, where they can cause great damage before samurai finally arrive to deal with them.

### Dokufu, the Mountain Spider

Dokufu are terrifying but mercifully very rare creatures. Their natural form is a bloated seven-eyed spider the size of a small house, with a slobbering toothless mouth, sharp claws at the end of each of its eight legs, and a rocky and jagged outer carapace. Their vast size and stony exterior gives them the nickname “mountain spider.” What makes them a truly terrible threat is their shapeshifting ability – the dokufu craves non-Tainted human flesh and enters Rokugan in search of it. They always assume the form of the same woman no matter how many times they change form, and always appear as aged as they really are; since dokufu can live for centuries, they often appear as decrepit old women. The dokufu reproduces with eggs that need human flesh to incubate, preferring to trap human victims in its webs and implant an egg sac in the belly, then swallow the victim whole. The victim suffers a slow and agonizing death as the egg grows and hatches. A dokufu can also vomit forth its young to defend itself against threats. They prefer to target unarmed or helpless victims in bathhouses, geisha houses, and other establishments where customers are habitually unarmed.

### Garegosu no Bakemono

Living proof of the Shadowlands’ lack of rationality, the Garegosu no Bakemono look like nothing so much as land-dwelling giant octopi. They have a pair of small sinister black eyes and a large mouth filled with gnarled and uneven teeth. The rest of their bodies constantly change as they create and absorb tentacles while moving around. Despite the fluid nature of their changing bodies, they are covered in solid plate-like scales. Although the average specimen is usually the size of a large room, the creatures seem to add to their mass each time they consume food. The peculiar name derives from the fact that despite their prodigious appetite they never eat goblins, and even seem to enjoy a friendship of sorts with the smaller creatures.

### Goblin (Bakemono) Variants

Goblins (bakemono) are by far the most common creatures of the Shadowlands, and may number in the millions. They are not too bright, prone to both mass and individual hysteria, easily manipulated, and while stronger than their skinny limbs would suggest, are hardly a match for a typical samurai. Their prodigious birth rate (a female gives birth to a litter of four to six goblings after approximately four months of gestation, and is usually pregnant again within two weeks) and innate cunning ensure their survival despite constant infighting. Goblin society is a crude and twisted imitation of humanity – social hierarchy is established by a simple rule: the strong dominate the weak. In battle, goblin tactics are typically extremely simple, relying on their near-endless numbers – an attacking mob of bakemono will almost never retreat; it has to be destroyed to the very last goblin to be defeated.

### Hanemuri

Hanemuri are crow-sized reptilian flying creatures with scrawny bony bodies covered with thin brown skin, bat-like wings, and oversized jaws lined with thousands of tiny needle-like teeth. Hanemuri usually lurk in treetops and other high perches, waiting for prey. Alone or in small numbers they are cowardly, but when a dozen or more gather together they become aggressive and will swarm larger prey with voracious ferocity.

### Kumo

The kumo are not actually native to the Shadowlands – they are a race of shapeshifting spiders from Chikushudo, the realm of animal spirits. Long ago they were seduced by Fu Leng’s promises of power and human flesh, and accepted the Taint into themselves willingly. In their natural form, kumo are giant spiders measuring between four and six feet long and standing about three feet high, possessing human-level intelligence and their own language despite their Taint. They have lost the ability to shapeshift into human form, though GMs may optionally allow a limited shapeshifting ability – in which case the kumo can take human form as a Simple Action, but always appears as an extremely elderly and wizened individual with skinny limbs and a bloated stomach. The kumo go out of their way to kill other shapeshifters from Chikushudo (kitsune, tanuki), as they are deeply embittered about being stranded in the realm of mortals. The kumo race possesses a king – the Great Sea Spider – which was imprisoned long ago in the Sea of Amaterasu.

### Mamono

The mamono is a rare and dangerous shapeshifting creature that is extremely difficult to kill. They have only one human form and cannot change their shape as quickly or easily as other infiltrators, so mamono prefer to create a long-term persona and travel the Empire in that guise, preying on anything that crosses their path. They are cunning and cautious, hunting and killing only in their true monstrous form while using their human form to avoid attention. Mamono have an intense dislike of sunlight, which pains them. During the night, however, they are extremely resilient and almost impossible to kill permanently – if killed during daylight hours they will appear to be dead, but as soon as the sun is down their bodies begin regenerating. The only reliable way to kill a mamono permanently is to tear it into at least three pieces and bury the parts separately, preventing them from ever reuniting.

### Mountain Goblin

Mountain goblins resemble true goblins but are more thickset, with gray skin and a flat knobby head sporting a mane of filthy hair. Strictly speaking, they are not the same species as true goblins. They are usually encountered in mountainous areas in the northern parts of the Shadowlands, although there have been some reports of small packs in the Seikitsu Mountains. The mountain goblin is reclusive, hiding in caves and scavenging whatever food it can find. The mountain goblin’s most striking trait is its ability to reattach severed limbs – a mountain goblin that suffers such a grievous wound will simply pick up the lost limb and hold it up to the stump; moments later it will be reattached and fully functional.

### Nikumizu (Heart Grubs)

Nikumizu are some of the smallest creatures in the Shadowlands, but that does not make them any less dangerous. These brown worms are about the size of a human forefinger, with no obvious organs except for a mouth filled with sharp mandibles. They commonly hide in dead logs, cracks in the floor, and other locations where they can attack without warning. Once under the skin, they slowly burrow their way to their victim’s heart – a very painful process. Once it reaches the heart, the victim dies and the nikumizu lays its eggs, which mature for 24 hours before hatching and erupting from the body. This process does not prevent the body from being reanimated as a zombie, making for a very nasty surprise when a dozen worms emerge from a freshly-killed undead.

### Obake

The Obake is the horrifying reminder that not all evil comes from the depths of Jigoku. Some of the most hateful old men naturally draw kansen spirits, who whisper in their ears of ways to cheat death and prolong their malevolent lives. This unnatural pact transforms an old man into an obake, attracting vicious wasps that burrow into the flesh of his back to form great bulbous hives. The obake appears as a spindly old man with ragged clothes covering a grotesque hump. He will often rudely demand food or money from passersby – if rebuffed, he becomes enraged. The obake does not usually directly attack those who enrage him – instead he prefers to flee and then release a vengeful swarm of wasps.

### Ogres

Ogres are one of the most well-known races of the Shadowlands. They are massive humanoid creatures averaging 8’ in height, with thick skin and bulging muscles. They commonly have small horns or bulges over their eyebrows and may have a length of black hair tied in a topknot. Most Rokugani would be shocked to learn that ogres may once have been a proud race with a rich culture. According to free ogre legends, the progenitor of their race was Muhonarak, who came from the north of Rokugan and built an ogre empire – but it was vanquished and enslaved by the Nezumi. After Fu Leng fell, the ogre king Muhomono son of Muhonarak refused to bow to Fu Leng even after the trolls had already done so. In retaliation, the Ninth Kami robbed them of their souls to make them his powerful, if stupid, servants.

FREE OGRES: Those ogres who travel outside the Shadowlands sometimes begin to slowly change their nature once beyond the direct influence of the Tainted realm. After about a month in Rokugan, these ogres find their intelligence increasing and discover the ability for elaborate language. The so-called free ogres became much more common after the Second Day of Thunder. Free ogres consider humans to be evil but pitifully weak creatures. They will sometimes ally with ronin to form bandit groups, but consider the humans to be their slaves. The only race they respect is the ferocious Tsuno.

### Onibaba (Demon Crone)

Onibaba are thankfully an extremely rare, perhaps even unique, creature – thought to be a highly evolved bog hag that finally found a skin that became fully theirs. Unlike bog hags, the onibaba does not wear others’ skins – its own natural skin is bone white. It has long unruly hair that seems to have a will of its own, using the strands like tentacles to both attack and defend. The onibaba also possesses limited shapeshifting abilities, can alter its apparent age at will to look young and beautiful, and is a potent spellcaster. Its preferred hunting grounds are in the mountains and forests of the Empire, where it lures travelers away from main roads and devours them. It can infiltrate human society but usually only for the purpose of enticing victims to follow it out to its lair: a rickety wooden shack or a dark cave. Witnesses claim to have seen kumo around onibaba lairs, suggesting some manner of partnership between the two species.

### Onikage (Demon Steed)

Whenever horses die or are Lost to the Taint in the Shadowlands, they are reborn as onikage. Their forms vary greatly: some look reptilian with black scaled skin, a forked tongue, and a mane of spines; others look like zombie horses with green rotting flesh; and still others are animated skeletons. Most onikage have glowing red eyes, and all have clawed hooves used to attack. Onikage are carnivorous and feed on their victims. Most encountered in the Shadowlands are wild and travel in herds. When a rider breaks an onikage, a supernatural bond is established: the onikage marks the rider, giving him a subtle deathly stench that frightens normal horses. In return, the onikage is extremely loyal and will come to the rider from wherever it might be in the Shadowlands when called. To break an onikage, a potential rider must first mount it (Athletics/Agility or Horsemanship/Agility at TN 25), then stay astride it for 3 Rounds with three consecutive successful Horsemanship/Agility rolls at TNs of 25, 30, and 35.

### Sanshu Denki (Muck Monster)

The sanshu denki is an amphibious creature that dwells in the marshes of the Shadowlands. ### Swamp Goblin

Swamp goblins are goblins only in name – they share a similar general physical form but are actually a very different species. They look like the hideous merge of a humanoid frog and a goblin, with large glowing yellow eyes, long pink-frilled gills where their ears would be, and thin scales covering their body. They need to keep their delicate skin constantly moist and rarely leave their native swamps. Swamp goblins are smarter than true goblins – the smartest of them have been able to learn basic Rokugani – and their behavior is sometimes more like a Nezumi’s. They are not aggressive and prefer to avoid fighting whenever possible. Swamp goblins are extremely curious by nature and attracted to anything shiny or brightly colored; a savvy samurai could possibly barter baubles for information. However, one should never forget that swamp goblins bear the mark of Jigoku, and trusting them can prove a fatal mistake.

### Trolls

The trolls are an ancient race, one of the Five Races that pre-dated humanity. They had a vast but decadent civilization and built many cities, most notably Volturnum, their capital. By the time the ogres came from the north, however, the troll civilization was in decline and they were soon conquered and enslaved. Centuries later, when Fu Leng fell from the Heavens, the trolls were all too eager to swear fealty to the Dark Lord, embracing the Taint and becoming slaves once more, this time to Jigoku. In modern times, the trolls have degenerated so much that none remember anything of their former greatness.

Trolls are large, flabby, green-skinned humanoids with yellow glowing eyes, slimy skins, and webbed feet. They live deep in the Tainted swamps and seas of the Shadowlands. They cannot breathe water but can stay submerged for several hours. For the most part, trolls are reclusive – they avoid other denizens of the Shadowlands and behave submissively toward oni, Lost, and other powerful beasts – the legacy of centuries of slavery. Troll shamans are so rare that even most Kuni scholars consider them to be legends.

### Tsumunagi (Blood Eel)

The tsumunagi is some sort of a cross between a monstrous leech and an eel, found only in the Shadowlands’ most Tainted regions. Its rubbery black body is almost three feet long and nearly flat, with two suckling mouths (one at each end) that look like lengthwise gashes filled with tiny serrated teeth. The sides and ends of the creature are lined with tiny hooks and claws with which it digs into the flesh of its victims. The tsumunagi dwells in shallow pools of murky water or in rotting trees near water, often found in colonies of a dozen or more individuals. When hot-blooded prey approaches, the tsumunagi springs upon the victim’s nearest limb and wraps itself tightly around it, biting into the victim’s flesh and draining blood until death. The creature’s saliva thins the blood, so even a freed victim will continue to bleed profusely unless properly bandaged. As with a regular leech, a flame or burning ember applied to its skin will make it release its grasp.

### Yamauba (Mountain Ogress)

The creature known as the Mountain Ogress is a solitary hunter with a singular power: any person who gazes upon it will see their own mother. Naturally, it prefers to avoid crowds. The only giveaway of its true form is its icy-cold skin and a small fanged mouth on the back of its neck. The yamauba reveals its true form when it attacks its prey (or when it dies): a foul humanoid creature with eyeless snakes for hair and six-inch claws on its fingers. The yamauba is an infiltrator and prefers to slip into Rokugan rather than remain in the Shadowlands. It prefers to live among clans with little Shadowlands knowledge, such as the Crane or Lion, rather than among the paranoid Crab.
