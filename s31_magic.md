# 31. Magic

**QUICK REFERENCE: Spell Casting**

- Spell Casting Roll: Roll (Ring + School Rank) dice, keep (Ring) dice. TN = 5 + (5 × Mastery Level).

- Spell Slots per day: Equal to Ring Rank in that element. Bonus slots equal to Void Ring (usable in any element).

- Casting Time: Mastery Level Complex Actions. First action = Spell Casting Roll. Each Raise on that roll reduces Actions by 1 (minimum 1).

- Failed Roll: Spell fails and slot is consumed.

- Interrupted Before Completion: Spell lost, slot is NOT consumed. Willpower Roll TN 10 (or TN 5 + damage) to resist interruption.

- Affinity: Cast as if School Rank is 1 higher. Deficiency: Cast as if School Rank is 1 lower (Rank 0 = cannot cast that element).

- Memorizing a Spell: Spend XP equal to Mastery Level to cast without a scroll.

**SPELL CASTING FUNDAMENTALS:**

**The Nature of Rokugani Magic**

Rokugani spells are prayers and invocations to the kami, spoken in a mystical tongue the kami can understand. All shugenja study this speech as part of their schooling. Most must read these prayers from scrolls while casting. A shugenja may memorize a spell by spending XP equal to its Mastery Level, allowing them to cast without a scroll. Because the kami must hear the prayers, spells are normally spoken aloud. Spells may be concealed by muttering under one’s breath using the Stealth Skill.

**Spell Casting Rolls ****&**** Target Numbers**

When casting a spell, a shugenja rolls dice equal to the relevant Ring + Shugenja School Rank, and keeps dice equal to the Ring. The result is compared to the spell’s TN: 5 + (5 × Mastery Level).

- Spell Slots: A shugenja has spell slots equal to their Ring in each element. A shugenja with Fire 3 can cast up to 3 Fire spells per day.

- Bonus Slots: All shugenja have bonus spell slots equal to their Void Ring. These may be used to cast additional spells in any element of choice.

- Failed Roll: A failed Spell Casting Roll still consumes the spell slot — the kami are angered by failure.

- Interrupted Casting: A shugenja who succeeds the roll but is interrupted before completion does not lose a spell slot.

**Actions ****&**** Casting Time**

A spell requires Complex Actions equal to its Mastery Level to cast. The first Complex Action is spent making the Spell Casting Roll; each Round thereafter one Complex Action maintains the process. A Mastery Level 3 spell therefore takes three full Rounds. The spell takes effect immediately upon completion of the final Complex Action.

- Reducing Casting Time: Each Raise made specifically for this purpose on the Spell Casting Roll reduces the required Complex Actions by one. Cannot be reduced below one Complex Action.

- Interruption: A shugenja who suffers damage or significant distraction while casting must make a Willpower Trait Roll (TN 10, or TN 5 + damage suffered if damaged). Failure means the spell is lost — but the spell slot is not consumed.

**Banishing and Importuning the Spirits**

Banishing: A shugenja may dismiss elemental kami from an area (typically to prevent Commune from revealing their activities) by casting Sense with three Raises to identify all spirits, then Commune with five Raises to persuade them to leave. Must be done separately for each Element.

- Importuning: An especially devout shugenja can attempt to cast almost any spell without a scroll by asking the spirits with sufficient piety. Requires casting Commune and speaking with the kami for 5 minutes per Mastery Rank of the desired spell. Then roll Spellcraft (Importune)/[relevant Ring] at TN 15 + 5 per Mastery Level. On success, the shugenja may attempt to cast the spell once within the next hour at TN 15 + (Mastery Level × 5). Cannot importune for a spell of higher Mastery Level than the shugenja could normally cast.

**Affinities ****&**** Deficiencies**

Virtually all shugenja Schools have an Affinity for one element and a Deficiency for another. Air and Earth are opposed; Fire and Water are opposed; Void has no opposing element.

- Affinity: A shugenja casts spells of their Affinity element as if their Shugenja School Rank were one higher.

- Deficiency: A shugenja casts spells of their Deficiency element as if their Shugenja School Rank were one lower. If this reduces effective School Rank to zero, they cannot cast spells of that element.

**COUNTERSPELLING (OPTIONAL RULES):**

**Overview**

L5R 4th Edition does not include Counterspelling in its base rules due to timing complexity. The following are optional rules for GMs who wish to include it. Only normal Rokugani spells may be Counterspelled — Maho, Nothing spells, and gaijin magic of all kinds cannot.

**Who Can Counterspell ****&**** When**

- Eligibility: A shugenja with at least 5 Ranks in Spellcraft may attempt to Counterspell any normal Rokugani spell.

- Awareness: The shugenja must be aware a spell is being cast (any shugenja can generally recognize the sights and sounds of spellcasting) and within suggested maximum range of 50’ × School Rank.

- Multi-Action Spells: If the enemy spell takes more than one Complex Action, the Counterspelling shugenja may attempt as long as the Initiative order gives them a Turn between the spell’s start and completion.

- Single-Action Spells: If the spell takes only one Complex Action (Mastery 1 spell, or Raises reduced it), Counterspelling is only possible if: (a) the Counterspelling shugenja acts before the caster in Initiative and delays their Turn specifically for this purpose, or (b) they are tied with the caster in Initiative.

- Limit: A specific shugenja may only attempt to Counterspell each enemy spell once, even if they have multiple Turns during the casting period.

**Counterspelling Procedure**

- Action: The Counterspelling shugenja takes a Complex Action and expends a spell slot in the Element opposed to the spell being cast (Fire opposes Water; Air opposes Earth; Void opposes Void).

- Roll: Make a Spell Casting Roll using the opposing Element (Affinity/Deficiency apply). If the total exceeds the caster’s Spell Casting Roll, the Counterspell succeeds and the spell fails.

- Combined Attempts: Multiple shugenja may combine a single Counterspell attempt using the highest Ring and highest School Rank among them, plus +1k1 per additional shugenja beyond the first.

**When Counterspelling Is Impossible**

- Effects that store spells for later discharge cannot be Counterspelled: this includes the spell Silent Waters, Agasha/Tamori alchemical potions, and Yogo paper wards.

- Unicorn Meishodo magic does not involve a Spell Casting Roll and cannot be Counterspelled.

**Additional Counterspelling Options (Optional)**

- School Bonuses: Phoenix Inquisitors gain +1k1 to Counterspelling. Yogo Wardmaster and Seppun Shugenja gain +1k0.

- Powerful Spells: If the spell being Counterspelled is a higher Mastery Rank than the Counterspelling shugenja can normally cast, they must call one Raise on the Contested Roll per Mastery Rank by which the spell exceeds their capacity.

**TARYU-JIAI (SHUGENJA DUELING):**

**Overview**

Duels of magical power between shugenja date to the early Empire, when the Isawa used them to settle differences. Taryu-Jiai is rare overall — shugenja typically defer to yojimbo for matters of honor — but plays an important role in tournaments involving shugenja, particularly the Tournament of the Jade Champion. Socially treated the same as any alternate dueling form: acceptable if both parties agree. May be conducted to first blood or to the death.

**Taryu-Jiai Mechanics**

Conducted in three Rounds (GMs may vary this). Does not use Center Stance; duelists are typically in Attack Stance throughout.

- Step 1 — Selection: Each duelist chooses which Element to call upon. Typically their Affinity element but not required. Must be one of the four normal Elements — there are no Void duels, even between Ishiken.

- Step 2 — Assessment: Contested Spellcraft/Void roll. Winner learns the Ring their opponent selected and their Rank in that Ring. Winning by 10+ also reveals either the opponent’s School Rank or Void Rank (winner’s choice). Either shugenja may concede after Assessment.

- Step 3 — Empower: Both shugenja pray and gather elemental energy. This stage is full concentration — only Free Actions are possible. Can be disrupted by outside attacks or distractions.

- Step 4 — Strike: Both shugenja unleash gathered power in a Contested Spell Casting Roll using their chosen Element (Affinity/Deficiency and spell slots apply as normal). Winner inflicts 2k2 damage + 1k1 per 5-point differential in their favor. Loser inflicts half as many dice (rounded down) on the winner.

- Non-lethal: Winner is whoever inflicts more Wounds in the Strike. Lethal: Repeat Empower and Strike until one duelist falls — a duelist reduced to Down or Out in a lethal duel is immediately overwhelmed and slain.

**Taryu-Jiai Additional Options (Optional)**

- Disruption: During the Empower step, duelists may attempt to disrupt each other’s kami control via a Contested Spellcraft/[chosen Element] roll. Winning by 10+ grants +1k1 to the subsequent Strike roll.

- Elemental Effects: Air — half damage is non-lethal buffeting. Fire — +1k0 to damage. Water — +1k0 to Assessment rolls. Earth — opposing duelist becomes Fatigued.

**Avatar Duels**

High-powered shugenja duels may involve summoning Elemental avatars (via Rank 6 Rise spells such as Rise Earth, Rise Water, etc.) to wage battle by proxy. Summoned avatars are infused entirely with elemental energy and can damage each other ignoring Invulnerability. The duel continues until one avatar is reduced to zero Wounds and dispelled.
