## 4.2 The Large Living World Map

Inspired by Romance of the Three Kingdoms and Mount & Blade, this is Rokugan as a breathing political and military organism. It is not directly controlled by any one player — it evolves according to NPC faction AI, seasonal events, and the consequences of player actions at the local level.

### 4.2.1 Scale & Representation

- The map covers all of Rokugan, divided into provinces and major cities, faithful to L5R canon geography.

- Each province is owned by a clan (or contested), has resource values, a garrison, and a loyalty rating.

- Major cities are nodes of political power — they host courts, markets, dojo, and potential quest content.

- The Shadowlands occupy the southern border as a persistent threat zone with its own expansion logic.

**RESOLVED:** *Strict canon. Section 2.3 (284K) documents every province and settlement faithful to L5R 4th Edition geography.*

### 4.2.2 Living World Systems

- Clan AI: Each Great Clan pursues political goals — expansion, alliances, vendettas — independent of player action.

- Seasons: The in-game calendar advances, triggering seasonal events (Winter Court, harvest, Shadowlands incursions).

- Wars & Diplomacy: Clans can go to war, forge alliances, or be diplomatically humiliated. Players can influence but not fully control this.

- Province Status: Provinces track stability, resource output, and military strength. Neglected or contested provinces degrade.

**RESOLVED:** *✅ — Fully emergent simulation. There is no separate “Clan AI” system. Clan behavior emerges from the NPC Decision Engine (Section 55) running on every named character in the feudal hierarchy. Each lord, courtier, general, and magistrate pursues their own objectives through the seven-phase decision loop, filtered by personality (Section 19). The aggregate result of hundreds of NPCs making individual decisions IS the clan’s political, military, and economic strategy. Seasonal events fire through the game tick system (Section 13). Wars and diplomacy resolve through the War Status System (Section 53), disposition system (Section 12.2), and court mechanics (Section 15). Province status is tracked through the resource systems (Section 4.3). No additional clan-level AI layer is required.*

### 4.2.3 Player Interaction with the World Map

- Players observe the world map to understand the political situation and plan their next moves.

- Players can travel between regions via the world map — selecting a destination triggers fast travel or a journey event.

- Player actions at the local level (e.g., assassinating a daimyo, burning a village, forging an alliance) ripple back into world map state.

**RESOLVED:** *✓: The world runs as a persistent online simulation with continuous daily ticks (1 IC day = 6 OOC hours) and seasonal ticks. Real-time with ticks, not turn-based. Fully specified across Sections 4.3.5 (seasonal event sequence), 13 (server architecture), and 57.34 (tick timing).*
