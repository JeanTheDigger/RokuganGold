## 27.3 The Dragon Clan

The Dragon Clan spent over a thousand years holding itself apart from the Empire — watching, waiting, and pursuing enlightenment in its mountain fastness. The tattooed Togashi monks, the warrior-scholars of the Mirumoto, and the experimental shugenja of the Agasha/Tamori give the clan an air of inscrutability and mystery that the rest of the Empire finds both fascinating and irritating. In the modern era, after Fu Leng's defeat, the Dragon have begun to forge a new role for themselves within the Empire.

- Clan identity: Philosophical, individualistic, enigmatic. Encourage personal paths to wisdom. Speak in metaphor and riddle. Often appear to act on hidden knowledge.

- Key historical events: Togashi's thousand-year vigil; the Hitomi-Hoshi schism; the Agasha defection to Phoenix; the creation of the Tamori family; Togashi Satsu becoming Voice of the Empress Iweko.

- Inter-clan relations: Historical rivalry with Crane (Mirumoto-Kakita dueling tradition). War of Silk and Steel against Crane over the Daidoji Harriers. Generally neutral toward others. Scorpion distrust the Kitsuki Method.

**The Kitsuki Family:**

Founded in 820. Masters of Kitsuki's Method — logical deduction and physical evidence over samurai testimony. Will question a samurai's word, examine minute clues, draw inferences. Considered outrageous or heretical by most of the Empire. Serve as clan magistrates. The Empire's premier investigators, particularly renowned for their investigation of the 'ninja myth.' Include a faction called the Justicars who use iaijutsu duels to prove guilt.

**The Mirumoto Family:**

Motto: 'I have spent many years on the road, and I have fought forty-seven duels, and I have never lost.' — Mirumoto Hojatsu. Created the Niten two-sword style. The practical face of the Dragon Clan — handle day-to-day matters, the largest and most conventional family. Share the clan's poetic worldview but in a more martial frame. Their rivalry with the Kakita has endured for a thousand years.

**The Tamori Family (formerly Agasha):**

The original Agasha family mastered alchemy, multi-elemental magic, and crafting. Most defected to Phoenix under Hitomi's reign. The small remnant that stayed renamed themselves Tamori. Tamori Shaitung rebuilt the family into a militant and powerful force. The modern Tamori retain alchemy and crafting expertise, added martial intensity, and stand off the entire Phoenix Elemental Council in battle to earn their respect. Tend to wear tattoos openly as family tradition.

**The Togashi (Tattooed Men):**

Not a traditional family — a spiritual brotherhood. Recruited through adoption, almost none marry. Tattoos are inked with the Kami's blood, granting mystical powers. Monks pursuing enlightenment through physical, mental, and spiritual training. Three sub-groups: ise zumi (balanced path), kikage zumi (physical/violent path), tsurui zumi (spiritual/meditative path). Rarely leave their mountains but have outsized impact when they do.
