## 56.11 Ravine Camp Template --- LOCKED

The Ravine Camp template generates a camp inside a natural gorge, dried riverbed, or cut between ridges. The defining feature is natural walls on both sides funneling movement into a linear space. The bandits did not need to build walls --- the terrain provides them. They only need to control the chokepoints. This is the most linear template and the one that most rewards scouting and preparation before committing. Primary terrain: Heavy Hills, Rough Terrain, and Mountains. Applicable to Ronin Bandit Uprising, Nezumi Infestation (adapted), and potentially Maho Cult lairs.

### 56.11.1 Size Categories --- LOCKED

Same Strength floor rule as other templates.

- **Narrow Gully:** A short, tight cut. One bottleneck, a small open area beyond it. Room for a few tents. Can house up to Strength 3.

- **Ravine:** A longer gorge with some width variation. Wider sections where the camp sits, narrow sections that serve as natural chokepoints. Can house up to Strength 6.

- **Canyon:** An extended formation with multiple wide sections connected by narrows. Room for a substantial camp spread across the wider areas. Possibly a water source running through. Can house up to Strength 9.

### 56.11.2 Entry and Flow --- LOCKED

The ravine forces linearity but smart players find alternatives.

**Primary Entry:** The ravine mouth. The obvious way in and the way the bandits use. It is a chokepoint --- narrow, watched, the defenders’ strongest position. Walking in the front door is a fight on their terms.

**Rim Approach:** The player can climb to the top of the ravine walls and move along the rim. From above, they can observe the camp layout, enemy positions, everything. The rim gives total information advantage. But getting down into the ravine from above is the problem --- the walls are steep. The player needs to find a climbable section: a crumbled wall, a slope of loose rock, a place where a tree root offers a handhold. The generator places two or three descent points along the rim. Climbing down is slow, noisy, and leaves the player exposed mid-wall.

**Back Exit:** Ravines often have a second opening --- the other end of the gorge. Narrow Gullies are dead ends with no back exit. Ravines have a 40% chance. Canyons always have one. If it exists, the bandits use it as an escape route and supply path. Less defended than the mouth but not unguarded.

**Interior Flow:** Linear with rhythm. Narrow sections alternate with wider sections. The narrows are chokepoints --- one or two tiles wide, defenders hold them easily. The wider sections are where the camp sits --- room to fight, room to maneuver, but bounded by walls on both sides. The player pushes through a sequence: narrow, wide, narrow, wide. Each narrow is a gate. Each wide section is an arena.

**The Funnel Problem:** This is the only template where the terrain actively works against flanking. The player cannot go around. They go through or over. Frontal assault through chokepoints is brutal. The rim approach is the tactical answer but requires climbing skill and time. This makes Ravine the template that most rewards scouting and preparation before committing.

### 56.11.3 Population Placement --- LOCKED

The linear layout creates layered defense in depth. Each chokepoint is a defensive line.

- **Mouth Guard:** Two or three at the ravine entrance. The first and strongest defensive position. They do not need to be good fighters --- the terrain does the work. One or two men holding a narrow gap can stall a much larger force. The toughest opening encounter of any template.

- **Chokepoint Holders:** One or two at each narrow section deeper in the ravine. Same principle as Mouth Guard but thinner. They are the second and third defensive lines. If the mouth falls, they hold the next narrow. The player fights through one chokepoint only to face another.

- **Camp Group:** In the wider sections between narrows. Same as other templates --- eating, resting, clustered around fires. But the ravine walls mean they can hear fighting at the chokepoints clearly. Sound carries and echoes in a gorge. Alert propagation is fast --- faster than any other template. A fight at the mouth is heard throughout the entire ravine almost instantly.

- **Leader Group:** In the deepest wide section, furthest from the mouth. Same 20-25%. If a back exit exists, the leader is near it. Escape route planned.

- **Rim Watcher:** Unique to this template. One enemy posted on the rim above the camp, watching the top edge. Not always present --- 50% chance in Ravine size, always present in Canyon. If the player tries the rim approach and the Rim Watcher spots them, the entire camp knows someone is above them. Every advantage of the rim approach evaporates.

**No Patrols:** The ravine is too linear for patrol routes to make sense. Enemies hold positions, they do not walk circuits.

**Density Pattern:** Front-loaded defense, back-loaded value. The mouth and chokepoints have the most defenders per tile of any template. The deeper camp sections are sparser because the chokepoints are supposed to stop anyone from getting there. A player who breaks through the narrows finds progressively easier fights --- until the Leader Group.

### 56.11.4 Environmental Features --- LOCKED

The ravine walls dominate everything.

- **Walls:** Sheer or near-sheer rock on both sides. They define the space completely. Height varies --- a Narrow Gully might have walls two or three times a character’s height, a Canyon could be towering cliffs. The walls block all lateral movement and create permanent shadow on the ravine floor.

- **Echoes:** Unique to this template. Sound does not just propagate, it amplifies. Combat noise at the mouth reaches the deepest section almost instantly. There is no such thing as a quiet fight in a ravine. The alert system should treat every combat encounter as immediately audible to the entire camp. Stealth is only viable before the first fight starts.

- **Floor Terrain:** The ravine floor is not flat. Rocks, gravel, uneven ground. A dry streambed running through the center in most cases --- smooth stones, sometimes standing water in deeper sections. The streambed might be the easiest path to walk but also the most exposed. Edges along the walls offer uneven footing but put the player closer to potential climbing points.

- **Chokepoint Features:** The narrow sections are not just narrow. The generator can place boulders partially blocking the gap, a makeshift barricade the bandits wedged between the walls, or a raised lip of rock that gives the defender height advantage over anyone pushing through. These are the hardest positions to assault in any template.

- **Climbing Points:** Specific locations on the ravine walls where descent from the rim is possible. Crumbled sections, root systems, rock shelves forming crude steps. The generator places two or three along the length. Not every climbing point leads to a useful position --- some drop into empty stretches, others drop right into a camp section. The player must assess from the rim which descent point serves their plan.

- **Overhead Exposure:** Anyone on the ravine floor is vulnerable to attack from the rim. Rocks, arrows, anything dropped from above. This works both ways --- if the player reaches the rim, they can rain attacks down onto enemies in the ravine. The Rim Watcher exists to prevent exactly this.

- **Water:** If present, a stream or standing pools on the ravine floor. Movement through water is loud and slow. In a ravine the sound bounces off the walls and amplifies. Water crossing in a ravine is louder than anywhere else.

- **Darkness:** Ravines are naturally shadowed. Deep ravines with high walls may have reduced vision even during daylight, especially at floor level. Not full darkness like caves, but a permanent dusk that reduces effective Perception by one or two points. At night, ravines are extremely dark --- walls block moonlight and starlight. Campfires in the wider sections create pools of light with hard shadow boundaries against the walls.

### 56.11.5 Objective Placement --- Ravine Camp Template --- LOCKED

Shaped by the linear layout and the depth problem.

- **Kill the Leader:** Leader is in the deepest wide section. The player must fight through every chokepoint to reach them --- or descend from the rim into that section directly, bypassing the front entirely. If a back exit exists, the leader flees through it the moment the fight reaches the last narrow. Pursuit out the back exit puts the player in open terrain behind the ravine --- a different tactical situation entirely.

- **Recover Stolen Goods:** Stored in a wide section, probably the middle one. Not the deepest --- the bandits need to move goods in and out, so they keep them accessible from the mouth. The player does not need to clear the entire ravine to reach the goods. But extracting them means carrying them back through chokepoints that may still have defenders behind them. The rim is no help --- goods cannot be hauled up a cliff face.

- **Burn the Camp:** Limited effectiveness here. The ravine walls are stone. Tents and shelters burn but the ravine itself does not. A fire in a ravine creates a chimney effect --- smoke and heat funnel upward between the walls, making the ravine floor unbreathable fast. This could drive everyone out through mouth and back exit, achieving the objective of clearing the camp but in an uncontrolled way. Dangerous for hostages. Dangerous for the player if they are still inside.

- **Rescue Hostages:** Held deep, same as the leader. The chokepoint problem is worst here. A loud fight at the mouth gives the guards in the deep section time to do whatever they want with the hostages before the player reaches them. The rim descent is the best approach for hostage rescue --- drop directly into the section where they are held, eliminate guards fast, extract upward or out the back exit.

**Secondary Objectives:** The chokepoint barricades themselves might hold useful items. Defenders at chokepoints carry orders, maps, communication from the wider insurgency. Each chokepoint cleared is a chance to search the bodies and the barricade.

**Placement Rule:** Primary objectives are always past at least one chokepoint. Never in the first wide section. The player must commit to depth.

### 56.11.6 Resolution and Consequence --- Ravine Camp Template --- LOCKED

Same spectrum, shaped by the linear chokepoint structure.

- **Full Success:** Primary objective completed. Strength reduction same as other templates by objective type. The ravine itself cannot be destroyed --- it is terrain. But a cleared ravine with known chokepoints is compromised. Any surviving bandits know the attackers understand the layout. Reoccupying is possible but the defenders lose their information advantage --- the attacker knows every narrow, every wide section, every climbing point.

- **Partial Success:** Player pushed through one or more chokepoints but could not reach the primary objective. Strength reduced by kills. Chokepoint fights tend to produce decisive casualties on both sides --- the narrow space means fewer enemies but harder fights. The player might have killed a third of the roster just getting through two narrows. The insurgency survives but the front defense is gutted. Next mission faces weaker chokepoint resistance.

- **Retreat:** Player could not break through a chokepoint or chose to pull back. Strength reduced only by kills. In a ravine, retreat is straightforward --- back out the mouth. The narrows that made attack hard also make pursuit hard. The bandits will not chase through their own chokepoints. Clean disengagement is easier here than any other template.

- **Failure:** No Strength reduction. Insurgency remains at current Strength. See Section 56.10 for seed-specific failure consequences.

**Template-Specific Consequences:**

- **Chokepoint Attrition:** Even failed or partial missions permanently thin the chokepoint defenders. The bandits can reassign Camp Group members to chokepoint duty, but Camp Group fighters are worse at holding a narrow than dedicated Chokepoint Holders. Repeated missions degrade the ravine’s defensive quality even without reaching the primary objective.

- **Rim Knowledge:** If the player scouted from the rim, they know the full layout --- every enemy position, every wide section, every climbing point. This intelligence persists and makes the next mission dramatically easier regardless of whether the first attempt succeeded.

- **Clean Retreat Option:** The ravine is the most forgiving template for retreat. The player can probe, test the defenses, kill a few at the mouth, and pull out with minimal risk. This encourages hit-and-run attrition across multiple missions rather than a single decisive assault.

- **No Civilian Impact:** Wilderness position. No PU loss, no infrastructure damage.
